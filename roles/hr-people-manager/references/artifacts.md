## 2. Performance triage worksheet (filled example)

| Signal | Skill? | Will? | Fit? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Can produce high-quality work when engaged, but frequently late | No — demonstrated capability | Yes — engagement/motivation gap | No |
| Struggles even with clear instructions and support | Yes — needs training | Possible, check after training | No |
| Consistently prioritizes work that conflicts with role's actual scope | No | No | Yes — check if role expectations match actual strengths |

**Diagnosis for this case:** Will problem — capability is demonstrated, but delivery consistency is the gap. Intervention: clarify expectations and incentives (not a training plan), with a documented conversation about specific missed deadlines and what changes.

## 3. Structured 1:1 conflict-diagnosis template (filled example)

> **Question set (both parties, separately):**
> 1. "What's unclear about how work is divided between you two on [specific deliverable]?"
> 2. "Is there a metric or goal where you feel you're being measured against each other rather than working toward the same outcome?"
> 3. "What information do you wish the other person had that they don't currently have?"
>
> **Findings (filled example):** Both parties cited unclear ownership of the same client account, with individual quota credit creating implicit competition for the same deals. Root cause: structural (ownership + incentive design), not personality.
> **Action:** Manager clarifies account ownership split and adjusts credit-sharing rule for shared accounts — not a mediation/personality session.

## 4. PIP documentation trail (filled example)

| Date | Type | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 1:1 feedback, documented | "Two deadlines missed this sprint without advance flag — let's talk about workload and blockers." |
| Month 2 | 1:1 feedback, documented | "Same pattern continued — third missed deadline. Need a plan for flagging risk earlier." |
| Month 3 | Formal PIP issued | Specific expectations: flag any at-risk deadline 48 hours in advance, weekly check-in, 60-day timeline, clear success/failure criteria stated. |

**Rule applied:** the PIP in month 3 builds directly on two months of prior documented feedback — it isn't the first time the issue has been raised, and the person already knew the bar before the formal plan started.
