# HR/people management working vocabulary

Terms an HR/people manager uses precisely. Format: definition → how a practitioner says it → common misuse.

**Skill/will/fit triage** — A performance-issue diagnostic distinguishing whether someone can't do the work (skill, coachable), won't (will, a motivation/incentive/expectations problem), or is fundamentally mismatched to the role (fit, likely not fixable in this role).
In use: "This is a will problem, not a skill problem — she clearly can do the analysis, she's just not prioritizing it under the current incentive structure."
Misuse: applying a generic performance improvement plan regardless of which of the three is actually present, wasting months on training (for a will or fit problem) or a motivation conversation (for a genuine skill gap).

**Compa-ratio** — An individual's pay divided by their band's midpoint, the standard check for whether an individual pay decision is consistent with the intended structure, used before approving any exception.
In use: "The retention offer puts her at a 1.05 compa-ratio against the updated band — consistent with where our other strong performers sit."
Misuse: approving an individual comp decision without checking its compa-ratio against band and peers, missing whether it creates an unexplained inconsistency.

**Iceberg model of conflict** — The idea that a stated complaint is the visible tip of a conflict, with the real driver (unclear priorities, unaddressed resentment, competing incentives) sitting underneath, unaddressed if only the visible complaint is resolved.
In use: "The complaint is 'they don't respond to messages' — the iceberg underneath is unclear ownership of the same deliverable."
Misuse: resolving only the stated, visible complaint (e.g., mediating "communication style") without investigating the underlying structural or informational cause, which then resurfaces in a different form.

**Radical candor** — A feedback framework requiring both genuine care for the person and directness about the actual issue simultaneously, as opposed to either withholding hard truths to spare feelings or blunt criticism without underlying care.
In use: "That feedback needs radical candor — specific about the three missed deadlines, but framed with genuine investment in her success, not just a critique."
Misuse: treating "candor" as license for blunt, uncushioned criticism without care, or treating "kindness" as license to soften feedback into vague language the recipient can't actually act on — both fail the framework's actual requirement of combining the two.

**Precedent (in people decisions)** — The unstated policy that an individual decision (a discipline call, a comp exception, an accommodation) creates for how similar future cases will be expected to be handled, regardless of whether it was intended as a one-off.
In use: "Before approving this exception, what precedent does it set for the next person in a similar position who asks for the same thing?"
Misuse: treating every people decision as fully independent and case-specific, missing that inconsistent handling of similar cases accumulates into a fairness problem even when no single decision was made in bad faith.

**Calibration (performance review)** — A cross-manager review process comparing performance ratings and comp recommendations across teams, designed to reduce individual manager bias and inconsistency in how ratings are applied.
In use: "Calibration flagged that Manager A rates noticeably higher than peers for comparable work — that's exactly what the process is meant to catch."
Misuse: running performance reviews purely within a single manager's judgment with no cross-manager calibration step, allowing manager-to-manager rating inconsistency to go unchecked.

**Structured interviewing** — Using consistent questions and scoring rubrics across all candidates for a given role, as opposed to an unstructured, interviewer-discretion-driven conversation, specifically to reduce interviewer-to-interviewer variance and bias.
In use: "We moved to structured interviewing for this role after noticing wildly different pass rates depending on which interviewer ran the loop."
Misuse: allowing each interviewer to ask different questions and apply their own informal criteria, which introduces bias and inconsistency that structured interviewing is specifically designed to reduce.

**Performance improvement plan (PIP)** — A written document specifying clear, measurable expectations and a defined timeline for a performance issue, intended as a genuine improvement tool with real documented history behind it, not a formality preceding a predetermined termination.
In use: "The PIP builds directly on the specific feedback and missed-deadline documentation from the last two months — she already knew the bar before this started."
Misuse: issuing a PIP as the first formal documentation of a performance concern, with no prior feedback trail, which makes the eventual outcome (whether improvement or termination) look — and often actually be — procedurally unfair.

**Pay equity audit** — A statistical analysis (typically regression, controlling for role, level, tenure, and performance) checking whether pay differences correlate with a protected characteristic, run proactively on a regular cadence rather than only after a complaint.
In use: "The quarterly equity audit is what caught this gap before it became a complaint or a lawsuit."
Misuse: only investigating pay equity reactively after a specific complaint or legal event, rather than running the analysis proactively and regularly.

**Org design (following strategy)** — Structuring teams and reporting lines around what the business needs to execute on currently, as distinct from reorganizing to resolve an interpersonal conflict or as a generic periodic "refresh" with no strategic driver.
In use: "This reorg is specifically to align the team with the new product line's ownership structure — not a general refresh."
Misuse: proposing or approving a reorganization to fix an interpersonal problem or for its own sake, without a clear business/strategy rationale driving the specific structural change.
