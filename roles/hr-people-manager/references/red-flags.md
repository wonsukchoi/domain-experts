# Red flags — what an HR/people manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A comp exception requested "quietly" or "just this once" for a specific individual
- **Usually means:** the request is a policy decision in disguise — approving it quietly teaches the organization that leverage comes from external offers and threats to leave, rewarding negotiation assertiveness over role/market/performance.
- **First question:** "Would this same call be made, visibly and documented, for any other person at this level with this scope and performance?"
- **Data to pull:** current band and compa-ratio for the individual and peers at the same level, any independent market data justifying a band-wide update.

### A conflict between two employees framed immediately as a "personality clash" with mediation requested before checking structure
- **Usually means:** unclear ownership, competing incentives, or missing information is likely the actual root cause, and mediation treats the symptom while leaving the structural cause to produce the next conflict.
- **First question:** "Do these two have overlapping, unclear ownership of the same deliverable, or metrics that put them in implicit competition?"
- **Data to pull:** role/ownership documentation for both individuals, their individual performance metrics and how they interact.

### A performance improvement plan issued with no prior documented feedback or expectation-setting conversation
- **Usually means:** the PIP may function as a pre-decided termination step rather than a genuine improvement tool, and the lack of prior documentation makes the eventual outcome look — and possibly be — unfair.
- **First question:** "What specific, documented feedback and expectations did this person receive before the PIP, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** prior feedback/1:1 documentation, dates of any expectation-setting conversations relative to the PIP issuance.

### A disengagement signal (declining participation, missed deadlines, flat affect) with no proactive conversation initiated
- **Usually means:** a regrettable departure risk is developing with visible warning signs that are being left until the resignation letter to address.
- **First question:** "Have we asked this person the exit-interview question now — what would make them consider leaving, what's currently frustrating?"
- **Data to pull:** engagement survey trend for this individual/team, timeline of observed disengagement signals versus any proactive check-in.

### A stated cultural value (collaboration, quality) with a reward/measurement structure that doesn't actually reinforce it
- **Usually means:** the actual incentive structure, not the stated value, is what's driving behavior — a "culture problem" diagnosis may really be an incentive-design problem.
- **First question:** "What does the actual reward/measurement system incentivize, and does it match what we say we value?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific metrics or criteria used for performance ratings, bonuses, or recognition, compared against the stated cultural value.

### A pay equity or leveling review finding an unexplained gap with no follow-up remediation plan
- **Usually means:** a real, potentially legally significant issue is being noted but not acted on, risking it compounding or being discovered externally later.
- **First question:** "Does this gap persist after controlling for role, level, tenure, and performance, and if so, what's the remediation timeline?"
- **Data to pull:** the equity analysis with controls applied, any documented remediation plan and timeline.

### A reorg proposed to resolve an interpersonal conflict or as a generic "refresh" with no stated business/strategy driver
- **Usually means:** the reorg is likely solving the wrong problem and may create more confusion than it resolves, since org design should follow strategy, not interpersonal friction.
- **First question:** "What does the business need to execute on now that this reorg specifically enables, separate from the interpersonal issue?"
- **Data to pull:** the stated business rationale for the reorg, the underlying interpersonal or structural issue it's actually meant to address.

### A hiring process with inconsistent interview questions or evaluation criteria across candidates for the same role
- **Usually means:** interviewer-to-interviewer variance and bias are likely affecting who gets hired, independent of actual candidate quality.
- **First question:** "Are all candidates for this role being asked the same core questions and evaluated against the same rubric?"
- **Data to pull:** interview questions and scoring rubrics used across different interviewers for the same role/req.
