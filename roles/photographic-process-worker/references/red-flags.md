# Red flags

### Processing time held at the standard duration despite tank/chemistry temperature differing from the process standard
- **Usually means:** under- or over-development from an uncompensated temperature deviation.
- **First question:** what's the actual measured temperature versus standard, and was a compensated time calculated?
- **Data to pull:** the temperature log compared against the process standard and the time actually used.

### Chemistry condition judged by how recent finished prints or negatives look rather than a process control strip read on a densitometer
- **Usually means:** a numeric drift outside control limits that isn't yet visually obvious could be missed.
- **First question:** what do the control strip densitometer readings actually show against the control limits?
- **Data to pull:** control strip densitometer readings compared against control limits for the relevant run period.

### Fixer used past its rated capacity/volume without verifying fixing completion
- **Usually means:** residual undeveloped silver risking long-term image degradation not visible immediately after processing.
- **First question:** how does the volume processed compare to the fixer's rated capacity, and was a fixer-check test performed?
- **Data to pull:** fixer volume processed compared against rated capacity, and any fixer-check test result.

### Control strip densitometer reading drifts outside control limits and production continues without correcting chemistry
- **Usually means:** subsequent production is being processed with chemistry already known to be out of specification.
- **First question:** what did the control strip trend show, and at what point (if any) was a correction made?
- **Data to pull:** control strip readings over the run compared against the point of any correction made.

### Shared processing equipment switched between different chemical processes without a documented rinse/purge sequence
- **Usually means:** cross-contamination risk that can degrade both chemistries and is hard to trace back to this cause later.
- **First question:** was the required rinse/purge procedure actually followed between process types?
- **Data to pull:** the equipment usage log compared against the required rinse/purge procedure.

### A recurring density/color-shift pattern appears across a specific time period or equipment line without being traced to a specific cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process control issue rather than isolated one-off variation.
- **First question:** does the control strip trend data correlate with a specific temperature or replenishment pattern over the same period?
- **Data to pull:** control strip trend data compared against process parameter logs (temperature, replenishment rate) over the same period.

### Replenishment rate applied doesn't match the actual volume of film/paper processed
- **Usually means:** chemistry becoming progressively exhausted or over-diluted relative to actual usage.
- **First question:** what does actual processed volume show against the replenishment rate being applied?
- **Data to pull:** actual processed volume compared against the replenishment rate applied.
