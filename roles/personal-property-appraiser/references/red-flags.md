### An appraisal engagement begins before the intended use (insurance, estate, donation, lending) is confirmed
- **Usually means:** The wrong value premise could be applied, since fair market value, replacement cost, and liquidation value are distinct standards tied to specific purposes.
- **First question:** What is the specific intended use of this appraisal, and has the corresponding value premise been confirmed before analysis began?
- **Data to pull:** Engagement letter or client instructions specifying intended use.

### A unique or collectible item's authentication or attribution hasn't been resolved before comparable-sales analysis begins
- **Usually means:** Any value conclusion built before this threshold question is resolved is not yet meaningful — authentication can change value by an order of magnitude.
- **First question:** What documentation (provenance records, authentication certificate, expert opinion) supports the item's attribution, and has it been resolved before valuation?
- **Data to pull:** Provenance documentation, any existing authentication or attribution reports.

### A comparable sale is used without documenting why it's relevant to the subject item
- **Usually means:** The comparable may not actually be a good match (different maker, period, or condition), undermining the value conclusion's support.
- **First question:** Does this comparable match the subject on maker/artist, period, and condition, and is that basis documented?
- **Data to pull:** Comparable sale details, subject item's specific attributes for comparison.

### A charitable donation appraisal doesn't confirm the appraiser's qualified-appraiser status or the appraisal's timing relative to the donation date
- **Usually means:** The deduction could be disallowed by the IRS on compliance grounds, independent of whether the value conclusion itself is accurate.
- **First question:** Does the appraiser meet the qualified appraiser credentialing/experience standard, and was the appraisal completed within 60 days of the donation date?
- **Data to pull:** Appraiser's credentials, appraisal completion date, donation date.

### An asset-based lending or bankruptcy appraisal uses fair market value in continued use instead of a liquidation value premise
- **Usually means:** The resulting value may overstate what the collateral would actually realize in the lending or liquidation scenario the appraisal is meant to inform.
- **First question:** Does the engagement call for orderly liquidation value, forced liquidation value, or fair market value in continued use, and does the value premise used match that specific requirement?
- **Data to pull:** Engagement terms specifying the required value premise, current appraisal's stated premise.

### Machinery or equipment is valued using sales comparison when the resale market for that specific type is known to be thin
- **Usually means:** A cost approach (replacement cost new less depreciation) may be more reliable than sparse or non-comparable sales data.
- **First question:** How many genuinely comparable sales exist for this specific equipment type, and is that sample size sufficient to support a sales comparison approach?
- **Data to pull:** Available comparable equipment sales, equipment age/condition/specification details for cost approach depreciation calculation.

### Multiple comparables are simply averaged without weighting by match quality
- **Usually means:** A comparable with a poor match (requiring large adjustments) may be distorting the reconciled value if weighted equally with a closer match.
- **First question:** Which comparable has the closest documented match on maker/period/condition, and has it been weighted more heavily in reconciliation?
- **Data to pull:** Adjustment magnitude for each comparable, reconciliation weighting rationale.

### An appraisal report doesn't state which specific value premise (FMV, replacement cost, liquidation value) was used
- **Usually means:** A reader could misapply the figure for a purpose it wasn't intended to serve.
- **First question:** Does the report explicitly state the value premise and the intended use it corresponds to?
- **Data to pull:** Appraisal report's stated value premise and intended use section.
