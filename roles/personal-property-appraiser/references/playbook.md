# Personal and Business Property Appraiser — Playbook

## Value premise selection table

| Intended use | Applicable value premise |
|---|---|
| Charitable donation, estate tax, gift tax | Fair market value (FMV) |
| Insurance coverage | Replacement cost (new or like-kind) |
| Asset-based lending collateral | Orderly liquidation value (OLV) |
| Bankruptcy / urgent forced sale | Forced liquidation value (FLV) |
| Divorce settlement / litigation (net proceeds relevant) | Marketable cash value (FMV less costs of sale) |

**Use:** Confirm intended use before starting analysis — this table determines which value definition governs the entire engagement, and the wrong premise answers a different question than the one being asked.

## Comparable sales adjustment worksheet (filled example — art donation appraisal)

| Comparable | Sale price | Adjustment 1 | Adjustment 2 | Adjusted value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Comp 1 (closest match: similar size, period, condition) | $18,000 | Condition: −5% (−$900, conservator-documented craquelure) | Size: −$2,000 (subject 40 sq in smaller, $50/sq in premium) | **$15,100** |
| Comp 2 (excellent condition, slightly larger) | $22,000 | Condition: −8% (−$1,760, excellent vs. good) | Size: −$2,000 | **$18,240** |
| Comp 3 (later period, similar size) | $15,500 | Period: +15% (+$2,325, subject's earlier/more desirable period) | None | **$17,825** |

**Reconciliation:** Weight Comp 1 (closest overall match) and Comp 3 (strong period-adjusted comparable) most heavily; weight Comp 2 less given its larger adjustment magnitude. **Reconciled FMV ≈ $16,500.**

## IRS qualified appraisal compliance checklist (donations over $5,000)

1. Confirm the appraiser meets IRS qualified-appraiser standards (recognized credentialing, verifiable relevant experience, not a party to the transaction or otherwise disqualified).
2. Confirm the appraisal is completed no more than 60 days before the donation date.
3. Confirm the appraisal report includes all required elements: description of property, date of contribution, terms of any agreement relating to use/disposition, appraiser's qualifications, and the specific valuation method and basis used.
4. Confirm Form 8283 Section B is completed and signed by the qualified appraiser.

**Use:** Complete this checklist before finalizing any donation appraisal over $5,000 — missing any one of these can risk deduction disallowance regardless of how accurate the value conclusion is.

## Cost approach worksheet for machinery/equipment (illustrative structure)

| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Replacement cost new (current market price for equivalent new equipment) | $250,000 |
| Physical depreciation (age/wear-based, e.g., 40% of useful life consumed) | −$100,000 |
| Functional obsolescence (outdated technology vs. current equivalent) | −$20,000 |
| Economic obsolescence (reduced demand for equipment's output in this market) | −$15,000 |
| **Indicated value (orderly liquidation basis)** | $250,000 − $100,000 − $20,000 − $15,000 = **$115,000** |

**Use:** Always check for functional and economic obsolescence separately from physical depreciation — equipment can be in good physical condition but still significantly devalued by outdated technology or reduced market demand for what it produces.
