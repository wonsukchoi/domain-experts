### Visible sand in discharge after development is complete
- **Usually means:** screen slot too large for the gravel pack (or formation, if naturally developed), or gravel pack ratio too coarse relative to formation D50 — second most likely: development stopped before fines were fully removed.
- **First question:** what slot size and pack D50 (or formation D50, if no pack) were used, and what ratio does that produce?
- **Data to pull:** the well's sieve analysis and completion report slot/pack specification.

### Specific capacity below 50% of nearby wells screened in the same aquifer
- **Usually means:** incomplete development leaving fine material clogging the pack/formation interface — second most likely: incrustation or biofouling on an older well, or a poorly matched pack/slot design on a newer one.
- **First question:** when and by what method was the well last developed (or, for a new well, was it developed at all before testing)?
- **Data to pull:** step-drawdown test data and the development log, compared against completion reports for nearby wells in the same formation.

### Static or pumping water level rising unexpectedly while drilling
- **Usually means:** the bit has entered a confined (artesian) aquifer under pressure — second most likely: cross-connection with a shallower flowing zone through an uncased annulus.
- **First question:** do any offset well logs in the section already flag artesian conditions at this depth?
- **Data to pull:** area well completion reports and static water level history for the section.

### Sudden, near-total loss of drilling fluid circulation
- **Usually means:** the bit has broken into a cavern, solution channel, or highly permeable gravel zone — second most likely: an induced fracture from excessive mud weight.
- **First question:** at what exact depth and formation contact did returns stop?
- **Data to pull:** the real-time driller's log entry for that interval and any offset logs noting the same feature.

### Grout returns fail to reach surface during tremie placement
- **Usually means:** bridging of the grout in the annulus or an enlarged/washed-out section of hole absorbing more volume than calculated — second most likely: an undersized tremie pipe or pump rate too slow, allowing the slurry to set before reaching the top.
- **First question:** how much grout volume was actually pumped versus the calculated annulus volume for that interval?
- **Data to pull:** the grout tally sheet against the calculated gal/ft annulus volume for the seal interval.

### Coliform-positive or nitrate-elevated bacteriological sample shortly after completion
- **Usually means:** annular seal failure (too shallow, voided, or bridged) or contaminated construction water/equipment — rarely the aquifer itself, since a properly sealed well isolates surface influence.
- **First question:** does the logged seal depth reach the confining layer found in the cuttings, or only the code minimum?
- **Data to pull:** the completion report's seal depth and the driller's log lithology at and below that depth.

### Pump short-cycling with fast recovery between cycles
- **Usually means:** pump rated well above the well's tested continuous specific capacity, dewatering the well below the pump intake repeatedly — second most likely: pressure tank sizing or pressure switch settings, not the well itself.
- **First question:** what continuous rate was the well actually tested at, versus the pump's rated output?
- **Data to pull:** the constant-rate pump test report and the installed pump's performance curve.

### Well completion report shows only a "blow yield" (air-lift estimate) and no pump test
- **Usually means:** the well was never run through a controlled, measured test — the reported gpm figure is a rough field estimate, not a design number.
- **First question:** was a step-drawdown or constant-rate test ever performed, and if not, why is a pump already installed?
- **Data to pull:** the completion report's test section versus the installed pump's rated capacity.

### Casing fails a plumbness-and-alignment check before pump installation
- **Usually means:** the hole deviated during drilling (common in bouldery or interbedded formations) and the casing followed the deviation — second most likely: casing was set off-center in an oversized borehole.
- **First question:** at what depth interval does the deviation begin, per the drilling log?
- **Data to pull:** the plumbness/alignment test results and the driller's log for that depth range.

### Turbidity spike coinciding with seasonal high water table
- **Usually means:** the annular seal doesn't extend below the seasonal high water table, letting near-surface infiltration reach the casing — second most likely: a deteriorated sanitary seal at the wellhead.
- **First question:** what is the seal depth relative to the highest recorded seasonal water table for the site?
- **Data to pull:** the completion report seal depth and local seasonal water table records.
