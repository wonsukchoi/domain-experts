# Red Flags

Smell tests for studies and live procedures. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### EF reported with no biplane Simpson's trace saved in the study
- **Usually means:** measured under time pressure by visual estimate; second most likely: endocardial borders were too poor to trace and contrast wasn't used to salvage the window.
- **First question:** "Is there a traced biplane Simpson's loop saved, or just a freeze-frame?"
- **Data to pull:** the stored clips — confirm a traced end-diastolic/end-systolic pair exists for both apical 4- and 2-chamber views.

### ABI reported as ">1.4, normal"
- **Usually means:** noncompressible, calcified vessels (diabetes, ESRD) misread as reassuring; second: cuff or Doppler probe placement error.
- **First question:** "What's the toe-brachial index?"
- **Data to pull:** TBI value and waveform morphology at the ankle and toe.

### Contrast running total not documented on the procedure log mid-case
- **Usually means:** end-of-case bookkeeping instead of live tracking — elevated contrast-nephropathy risk went unmanaged in real time; second: injector log not transcribed to the written note.
- **First question:** "What was this patient's calculated Cigarroa max, and where are we against it right now?"
- **Data to pull:** injector-logged contrast volume by timestamp, baseline creatinine used in the calculation.

### ACT not rechecked in over 45-60 minutes during an ongoing PCI
- **Usually means:** heparin redosing was missed as the case ran long; second: ACT device/reagent lot issue producing unreliable readings.
- **First question:** "When was the last ACT drawn, and what was the value?"
- **Data to pull:** ACT log timestamps alongside the heparin bolus/redose record.

### ICA PSV >230 cm/s but ICA/CCA ratio <2.0
- **Usually means:** Doppler angle-correction error or sample-volume misplacement; second: contralateral compensatory flow elevation from a high-grade lesion on the other side.
- **First question:** "Was the angle ≤60° and re-verified on this waveform?"
- **Data to pull:** the raw spectral trace and the angle-correction value actually used.

### E/e' >14 driven by septal e' alone in a patient with LVH, LBBB, or a paced rhythm
- **Usually means:** septal e' is technically unreliable in this substrate, inflating the apparent filling-pressure estimate; second: genuine diastolic dysfunction.
- **First question:** "What's the lateral or averaged e', and what's the LA volume index?"
- **Data to pull:** septal vs. lateral e' values, LA volume index, TR jet velocity.

### Bubble study positive with bubbles appearing more than 5-6 cardiac cycles after right atrial opacification
- **Usually means:** pulmonary or intrapulmonary shunt rather than an intracardiac PFO/ASD; second: inadequate Valsalva release timing muddying the cycle count.
- **First question:** "How many cardiac cycles after RA opacification did bubbles appear in the left heart?"
- **Data to pull:** frame-by-frame cycle count on the stored loop.

### Cumulative fluoroscopy air-kerma dose crosses the lab alert threshold without a verbal callout
- **Usually means:** dose monitoring is being logged but not communicated in real time; second: a complex or prolonged case going unflagged as it extends.
- **First question:** "Has the operator actually been told the cumulative dose number, or just seen it on the display?"
- **Data to pull:** dose-area product/air-kerma log against case duration.

### Same abnormal segment reported differently across serial echo studies with no noted reason
- **Usually means:** measurement technique or view changed between studies (different software, different apical foreshortening), not a real interval change; second: genuine progression.
- **First question:** "Were the same views and measurement method used as the prior study?"
- **Data to pull:** prior study images side-by-side with the current study, and the measurement-method flag on each.

### Closure device deployed without a documented access-site confirmation angiogram
- **Usually means:** puncture-site anatomy (level, calcification, vessel diameter) wasn't confirmed before device selection, raising retroperitoneal bleed or device-failure risk; second: anatomy was genuinely borderline and the device was used off-protocol.
- **First question:** "Was a femoral angiogram obtained before selecting the closure device?"
- **Data to pull:** the access-site angiogram image and the sheath size relative to vessel diameter.

### Stress echo read as negative despite a wall-motion segment that "looked a little sluggish"
- **Usually means:** a genuine new regional wall-motion abnormality was talked out of the read rather than flagged, often under pressure to finish the study list; second: image quality at peak stress was too poor to call confidently either way.
- **First question:** "Side-by-side, does that segment thicken and move inward at peak the same way it does at rest?"
- **Data to pull:** rest-vs-peak quad-screen loops for the segment in question.
