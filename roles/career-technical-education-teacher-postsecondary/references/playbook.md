# Playbook

Filled operational sequences a CTE program coordinator actually runs — adapt the numbers, keep the structure.

## 1. Annual program review (the one that decides funding)

Run this before, not during, any budget conversation with the dean — walking in with the table already filled changes the conversation from "defend your program" to "here's the data, here's the ask."

**The core table (filled example — Precision Machining Technology, FY24):**

| Indicator | Formula | This program | Benchmark | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Completion rate | completers ÷ concentrators | 18/24 = 75% | 70% | pass |
| Credential attainment (4S1) | credentialed completers ÷ completers | 17/18 = 94% | 70% | pass |
| Placement (3S1) | placed (job or further ed) ÷ completers, within 6 mo. | 16/18 = 89% | 80% | pass |
| Cost per completer | program budget ÷ completers | $198,000/18 = $11,000 | $8,500 (system avg.) | +29%, flag |
| Wage premium | occupation median − regional median | $54,000 − $41,000 = $13,000 | n/a | +32% |

**Reading order:** outcomes first (rows 1–3) — a program failing these gets a remediation plan, not a funding defense. Cost (row 4) only matters once outcomes clear the bar, and only in light of the wage premium (row 5); a $2,500 cost overrun against a $13,000 wage premium is a rounding error for the region, not a deficit.

**If any outcome row fails:** don't go to the funding conversation yet. Diagnose first — pull section-level pass rates on the credentialing exam, check whether the failure clusters on one instructor/cohort/skill domain, and bring a remediation plan with a re-review date to the meeting, not just the bad number.

## 2. Credential-backward-mapping worksheet

Use when building a new program or revising one against a stale curriculum. Filled example: NIMS Machining Level 1 (Measurement, Materials, and Safety credential).

| Exam domain | Exam weight | Current course hours | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Measurement & inspection | 35% | 28 hrs (of 120 total) = 23% | under by 12 pts |
| Job planning, benchwork & layout | 25% | 30 hrs = 25% | on target |
| Safety | 20% | 40 hrs = 33% | over — acceptable, safety floor |
| Materials | 20% | 22 hrs = 18% | under by 2 pts, minor |

**Rule:** any domain more than 8 points under its exam weight gets displaced hours from the next curriculum revision, sourced from the domain furthest over its weight that isn't the safety domain — safety hours never get cut to fund another domain, even when the exam underweights it relative to shop-floor risk.

**Cadence:** re-run this worksheet whenever the credentialing body updates its exam blueprint (NIMS/NOCTI/ASE post updates every 3–5 years) or when the advisory committee flags new equipment adopted by two or more member employers.

## 3. Advisory committee agenda (filled)

Twice a year, 90 minutes, decisions required by the end — not a status update read aloud.

```
ADVISORY COMMITTEE — Precision Machining Technology — Spring 2025

ATTENDING: 5 employer reps (Acme Fabrication, Regional Tooling, TriState
  Manufacturing, Bolt Aerospace, Delta CNC), program coordinator, dean's designee

1. CURRICULUM DELTA SINCE LAST MEETING (10 min)
   Added: 8 hrs of CAM software (Mastercam) training — 3 of 5 member
   companies now run it on the shop floor; program had none as of Fall 2024.
   Cut: 4 hrs of manual lathe theory redundant with the practical block.

2. CREDENTIAL/EXAM BLUEPRINT UPDATE (10 min)
   NIMS updated the Measurement & Inspection domain weight from 30% to 35%
   for the 2025 exam cycle — curriculum hours adjusted per backward-mapping
   worksheet (see above).

3. EQUIPMENT GAP CHECK (15 min) — decision required
   Question to committee: what does your shop floor run that this program's
   equipment list doesn't? (Standing agenda item, every meeting.)
   FY25 finding: 2 of 5 companies now require CNC 5-axis experience; program
   has 3-axis only. Decision: request $45,000 capital line for one 5-axis
   trainer, FY26 budget cycle.

4. PLACEMENT DATA REVIEW (15 min)
   FY24: 16/18 completers placed within 6 months (89%). 2 non-placements:
   1 relocated out of region (excluded from indicator per Perkins V), 1
   still job-searching (counted against the indicator, no exclusion).

5. WORK-BASED LEARNING SLOTS FOR FY26 (15 min) — decision required
   Committee members commit slot counts: Acme (2), Regional Tooling (1),
   TriState (2), Bolt Aerospace (1), Delta CNC (0 this cycle — hiring
   freeze). Total 6 slots against 24 concentrators — coordinator flags
   gap, requests committee help recruiting 2 additional host employers.

6. NEXT MEETING: Fall 2025 — bring updated equipment gap findings and
   FY25 outcome indicators.
```

The two items that make this a working committee instead of a courtesy meeting: the standing equipment-gap question (item 3) and requiring a committed slot count for work-based learning (item 5), not a vague "let us know if you have openings."

## 4. Work-based learning placement checklist

A placement that can't check every box here doesn't start, no matter how eager the student or employer is.

```
STUDENT: _____________  EMPLOYER: _____________  PROGRAM: _____________

[ ] Signed training agreement on file (employer + college + student)
[ ] Named on-site supervisor identified, contact info on file
[ ] Competency list for this placement mapped to program's skill
    standards (which check-offs does this placement satisfy?)
[ ] Hours-logging method assigned (timesheet, supervisor sign-off, or
    LMS check-in) and student briefed before day one
[ ] Liability/insurance coverage confirmed (student accident coverage,
    employer general liability — per institution's WBL policy)
[ ] Safety orientation completed and documented (site-specific, in
    addition to the program's general OSHA training)
[ ] Minimum hour threshold for 4S1 credit confirmed with state CTE
    office (varies by state; do not assume the program's default)

Start date: _______  Review date (midpoint check-in): _______
```

**Rule:** the training agreement and the hours-logging method are the two fields most often missing when a placement gets challenged during a Perkins monitoring visit — confirm both are filled before the first day, not retroactively.
