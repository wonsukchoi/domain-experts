# Red Flags — Compensation, Benefits, and Job Analysis Specialist

Signals worth an audit pass before a market slip, a pay-equity complaint, or a bad benefits renewal forces the question. Each maps to a specific pull, not a vibe.

### A tenured (2+ years) employee's compa-ratio sits below 80% of midpoint
- **Usually means:** the structure moved past them (a midpoint refresh with no corresponding individual increase) or they were hired in below range and never corrected — either way, past the early-career "developing zone" window, this is an unexplained-gap or flight-risk signal, not normal positioning.
- **First question:** "When was this person's last increase relative to the last time this job's midpoint was refreshed?"
- **Data to pull:** compa-ratio history for the employee across the last 3 structure refreshes, cross-referenced against their increase history.

### A job family's survey benchmark hasn't been refreshed in 18+ months while the survey's P50 has moved more than 5%
- **Usually means:** the annual market-pricing cycle skipped this family (often a smaller or non-standard job family that falls outside the default refresh list), not a deliberate decision to hold the structure flat.
- **First question:** "What was the last date this specific benchmark match was re-pulled from the survey, not just the general market-pricing cycle date?"
- **Data to pull:** the family's survey match history log and the current survey's P25/P50/P75 for the same benchmark job.

### A pay-equity regression model's R² is below 0.70
- **Usually means:** a missing legitimate compensable factor — most commonly job level/grade (if the cohort spans levels), function, or a cleaner tenure measure — not that no gap exists or that a flagged gap is real.
- **First question:** "What compensable factors are in this model, and which obvious ones (level, function, location, performance) are missing?"
- **Data to pull:** the model specification (variable list) and a level/function breakdown of the cohort to check whether it should have been split into separate models.

### A protected-class coefficient in a pay-equity regression is statistically significant (p<0.05) and exceeds 3%
- **Usually means:** a real unexplained gap requiring remediation budget — but only if the R² clears 0.70 first; a significant coefficient from a low-R² model is not yet trustworthy evidence.
- **First question:** "What is this model's R², and does the coefficient hold once [missing factor] is added?"
- **Data to pull:** full regression output including R², plus a re-run with any obviously missing factor added.

### More than 15% of a job level's incumbents fail the primary-duty question in a duties-test sampling audit
- **Usually means:** the level's job-architecture classification itself has drifted from its written duties test (scope creep across the whole level, not one employee) — a reclassification project, not an individual correction.
- **First question:** "Is this concentrated in one region/manager/business unit, or spread evenly across the level?"
- **Data to pull:** duties-test sampling audit results by level, cross-tabbed against region and reporting manager.

### A proposed midpoint move exceeds 8% in a single cycle without a phased rollout plan
- **Usually means:** the market genuinely moved that much (real, but needs phasing to avoid a wave of red-circled incumbents and a surprise remediation bill) or the prior cycle's survey match was wrong and this cycle is over-correcting.
- **First question:** "Is this move consistent with the prior 2–3 cycles' trend, or a sudden jump versus last cycle specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** the job family's midpoint history for the last 3 cycles alongside the survey P50/P75 for each cycle.

### More than 20% of a job family sits below the new range minimum after a structure refresh
- **Usually means:** the structure moved up faster than individual increases could follow, converting what looks like a routine market-pricing update into a de facto mass pay-compression event.
- **First question:** "What's the total remediation cost to bring everyone below-min up to at least the minimum, and is that budgeted separately from the general increase pool?"
- **Data to pull:** post-refresh compa-ratio distribution for the family, filtered to below-minimum, with a summed remediation cost.

### A job's point-factor evaluation score changed with no corresponding change to the written job description
- **Usually means:** grade inflation tied to the current incumbent's performance or tenure rather than a genuine change in job content — the two are supposed to move independently.
- **First question:** "What specifically changed in the job's duties, scope, or reporting relationships to justify a new score?"
- **Data to pull:** the prior and current job description side by side, and the factor-by-factor scoring worksheet showing which factor changed and why.

### A fully-insured benefits plan's loss ratio exceeds 85% for two consecutive renewal cycles
- **Usually means:** the block's claims experience has genuinely worsened (aging population, a few high-cost claimants) and the carrier's renewal ask reflects real cost, not just margin — this is the trigger to model self-funding or re-shop, not to just accept the increase.
- **First question:** "Is the loss ratio increase driven by claim frequency or a small number of large individual claims (stop-loss territory)?"
- **Data to pull:** the broker's large-claim report (claims above the stop-loss attachment point) alongside the aggregate loss-ratio trend for the last 3 renewals.

### A market-pricing recommendation cites a survey benchmark match below 70% duty overlap with the actual job description
- **Usually means:** no clean survey match exists for this job and the analyst forced a fit to the closest available title rather than flagging it as a "no clean match" case needing point-factor evaluation instead.
- **First question:** "What specifically doesn't overlap between the survey job description and our actual job description?"
- **Data to pull:** the survey benchmark's full job description text next to the internal job description, with the non-overlapping duties highlighted.
