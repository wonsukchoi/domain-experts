# Office Machine Operator — Vocabulary

### Ascending register
The postage meter's cumulative lifetime total of postage used — only increases, never resets. **In use:** "Ascending was $18,442.10 before the run and $20,058.50 after — that's the actual postage used, $1,616.40." **Common misuse:** Confusing it with the descending register (funds remaining), which moves the opposite direction.

### Descending register
The postage meter's remaining funded balance — decreases as postage is used, refilled by a meter reset/funds transfer. **In use:** "Descending's down to $180 — we need a reset before the next big run." **Common misuse:** Treating a low descending balance as a machine fault rather than a funding/reset need.

### Duty cycle
A machine's rated maximum volume (pages/impressions) over a period, usually monthly — running consistently near or above it accelerates wear. **In use:** "We're at 90% of rated duty cycle most months — that's why we're seeing more service calls than the older, lower-volume unit." **Common misuse:** Assuming duty cycle is a hard limit the machine physically can't exceed, rather than a wear-rate rating it can exceed short-term at a reliability cost.

### Click charge
The per-page cost billed under a copier/printer service contract, separate from the machine's lease/purchase cost. **In use:** "Color clicks run three times mono — that job should've been quoted mono." **Common misuse:** Ignoring click charges when comparing job costs, focusing only on paper/toner cost.

### Grain direction
The alignment of paper fibers in a sheet, which affects how it folds, feeds, and curls; specified as short-grain or long-grain relative to the sheet's longer dimension. **In use:** "This stock's short-grain and the machine wants long-grain feed — that's your jam pattern." **Common misuse:** Assuming all paper of the same weight/finish behaves the same regardless of grain.

### Registration
The precise alignment of print/imaging on a sheet, especially critical for multi-part forms, pre-printed stock, or multi-pass color work. **In use:** "Registration's off by a millimeter on the invoice number field — that's a setup issue, not a jam." **Common misuse:** Treating registration drift as a paper-jam symptom rather than a setup/calibration issue.

### Makeready
The setup time before a print run begins — loading stock, setting registration, running test sheets — distinct from run time once production starts. **In use:** "Makeready ate twenty of the twenty-eight minutes on that job; the actual run was fast." **Common misuse:** Quoting or scheduling a job by run time alone, ignoring makeready.

### Weight break
The postage rate threshold where a mailpiece's weight crosses into a higher rate class or a surcharge. **In use:** "A few pieces in that batch were right at the weight break — worth spot-weighing before we assume it's a duplicate-run problem." **Common misuse:** Assuming every piece in a batch is priced identically without checking outliers near a weight break.

### Piece counter
The postage meter's count of individual pieces run through it on a job, independent of the dollar registers — used to reconcile against the mailing manifest's expected count. **In use:** "Piece counter shows 2,908, manifest says 2,850 — that's the actual discrepancy, not the rate." **Common misuse:** Checking only the dollar registers on a reconciliation and never the piece counter, which misses duplicate/skipped-run errors that happen to net close to the expected dollar total.

### Jam-history log
The machine's internal record of jam events by code, location, and timestamp, distinct from a single visible jam in front of the operator right now. **In use:** "Pull the jam-history log — if this code's fired four times this week, it's not today's paper's fault." **Common misuse:** Diagnosing from the current jam alone without checking whether it's part of a recurring pattern.

### Preventive maintenance (PM) interval
The manufacturer-specified impression count or time interval at which service parts (fuser, rollers, developer) should be replaced before they fail. **In use:** "We're 8,000 impressions past the PM interval on the fuser — that's almost certainly today's repeat jam." **Common misuse:** Treating PM intervals as optional until something visibly breaks, rather than as a scheduled prevention point.

### Folder-inserter
Finishing equipment that folds printed sheets and inserts them into envelopes as part of a mailing run, often the bottleneck stage after printing. **In use:** "Print's done in twenty minutes, but the folder-inserter's the real constraint on this job — that's forty-five." **Common misuse:** Scheduling a mailing job by print time alone without accounting for the finishing-equipment stage.
