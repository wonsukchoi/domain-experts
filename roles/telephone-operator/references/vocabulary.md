# Telephone Operator — Vocabulary

### Communications Assistant (CA)
The FCC's formal term for the person relaying a TRS call — not "operator" or "interpreter" in the regulatory text, though those terms are used colloquially.
**In use:** "The CA relayed the call verbatim per FCC neutrality requirements."
**Common misuse:** Calling the role a "translator," which implies converting meaning between languages rather than relaying content in the same functional exchange (even VRS, which involves ASL/English, is relay, not translation in the legal sense).

### TRS (Telecommunications Relay Service)
The umbrella federal program requiring relay services for people who are deaf, hard of hearing, deaf-blind, or have speech disabilities, funded and regulated under FCC rules.
**In use:** "This center handles TTY and STS call types under our TRS contract."
**Common misuse:** Treating "TRS" and "VRS" as interchangeable — VRS (video relay) is one mode of TRS, not a synonym for the whole program.

### Verbatim relay
Reproducing exactly what was said or typed, without summarizing, cleaning up, or omitting content, unless the caller has specifically requested a summary mode.
**In use:** "Verbatim relay is the default — summary mode only applies if the caller's profile requests it."
**Common misuse:** Assuming "verbatim" means word-for-word transcription is always literal for every relay type — VRS interpretation is necessarily not word-for-word (ASL and English have different grammar) but must still be complete and accurate, which is the actual standard.

### Speed of answer
The FCC-mandated metric: TRS providers must answer 85% of calls within 10 seconds, measured daily.
**In use:** "We missed speed of answer during the 8:45 window because of a staffing gap."
**Common misuse:** Averaging speed of answer over a full shift or day and concluding the target is met, when a sub-window well below target can be masked by strong performance elsewhere.

### CA neutrality
The requirement that the CA not counsel, advise, interject opinions, or otherwise participate in the substance of a relayed call.
**In use:** "Neutrality means I don't answer when either party asks what I think."
**Common misuse:** Treating small helpful additions (clarifying a mumbled word, offering an opinion on a trivial matter) as exceptions to neutrality rather than violations of it — the rule has no size threshold.

### STS (Speech-to-Speech)
A TRS mode where the CA revoices a speech-disabled caller's words for the hearing party, rather than typing/reading text.
**In use:** "STS calls need a CA trained to understand a wider range of speech patterns."
**Common misuse:** Confusing STS with VRS — STS is voice-to-voice for speech disabilities, VRS is sign-language-to-voice for deaf/hard-of-hearing callers.

### Call refusal
FCC rules narrowly restrict when a CA/provider may decline to relay or complete a call — content or subject matter offensiveness alone is not sufficient grounds.
**In use:** "This call doesn't meet any of the refusal-eligible criteria, so it proceeds."
**Common misuse:** Assuming personal discomfort with a call's content is grounds for refusal — the actual bar is narrow and defined by employer policy tied to FCC rules, not individual judgment.

### Confidentiality (relay-specific)
The requirement that no record of a relayed call's content be kept beyond the call itself, distinct from general workplace confidentiality about not gossiping.
**In use:** "I can't tell you what that call was about — we don't retain content, only metadata."
**Common misuse:** Treating this as a "don't repeat it to coworkers" norm rather than a hard rule against retaining notes on content at all, even for the CA's own reference.

### Disambiguation (directory assistance)
Asking a targeted follow-up question to resolve which of several plausible directory matches the caller wants, before connecting.
**In use:** "I disambiguated with the street name since there were three J. Smiths listed."
**Common misuse:** Skipping disambiguation and connecting to the most prominent-looking match, treating "most likely" as "confirmed."

### Call-type mode preference
A caller's standing preference (verbatim vs. summary, or a specific relay type) that may be on file and should be confirmed at call start rather than assumed.
**In use:** "Checked the caller's profile — they're set to verbatim mode, no summary requested."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a caller's preference from a previous call still applies without confirming, since relay calls are handled by whichever CA answers and preferences can change.

### Operator-assisted call completion
A call type where the operator places a collect call, person-to-person call, or bills a call to a third party — a distinct legacy service from relay or directory assistance.
**In use:** "This is an operator-assisted collect call, not a relay session."
**Common misuse:** Conflating this with directory assistance — one connects a specific requested call type with billing arrangements, the other looks up a number.
