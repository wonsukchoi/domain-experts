# Telephone Operator — Red Flags

### A hearing party asks the CA to "just tell me what they said" instead of relaying live
- **Usually means:** the hearing party doesn't understand the relay format, or is trying to get the CA to summarize/interpret rather than relay verbatim.
- **First question:** has this been explained at call start, or is this happening mid-call?
- **Data to pull:** the call's requested mode (verbatim vs. summary) on file, if any.

### CA catches themselves about to answer a direct question from either party
- **Usually means:** conversational momentum is pulling the CA into participant mode — common on long or emotionally engaging calls.
- **First question:** did I actually answer, or catch it before responding?
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a real-time self-check, not a logged event unless it happened.

### Directory query returns more than 5 plausible matches with no disambiguating detail offered
- **Usually means:** caller has incomplete information, or the name/business is genuinely common in the area.
- **First question:** can the caller provide a street name, or is this a business with a well-known main number?
- **Data to pull:** the full match list to see if one is dramatically more likely (e.g., only one is a business with a public storefront).

### A relay call's content includes a specific, credible threat of violence
- **Usually means:** this may cross into a duty-to-report category depending on jurisdiction and employer policy — distinct from ordinary offensive language, which does not trigger reporting.
- **First question:** does this meet the employer's specific escalation criteria (credible, specific, imminent), or is it hyperbole/venting?
- **Data to pull:** the employer's written escalation policy for threat content — this is not a judgment call to make from general instinct.

### Speed-of-answer dips below target for a sustained period, not a single spike
- **Usually means:** a scheduling/coverage gap (breaks, shift-change overlap) rather than a one-off volume surge.
- **First question:** is the shortfall concentrated in a specific time window, or spread evenly across the shift?
- **Data to pull:** speed-of-answer broken into 15-30 minute windows, cross-referenced against the staffing schedule.

### A caller requests the CA remember something from a previous call
- **Usually means:** the caller doesn't realize relay calls carry no continuity — each call is handled by whichever CA answers, with no retained history.
- **First question:** has this been explained to the caller before?
- **Data to pull:** none — confidentiality rules mean there's nothing to pull; explain the policy instead.

### A CA is asked by a supervisor "what was that call about"
- **Usually means:** either an innocent check-in that needs the standard confidentiality reminder, or (rarely) a genuine policy question that should route through the compliance/legal process instead of an informal answer.
- **First question:** is this a casual question or does it relate to an actual complaint/investigation?
- **Data to pull:** if it's a real investigation, the compliance team's formal record-request process — not an informal recollection from the CA.

### Directory-assistance connection rate to wrong numbers is trending up
- **Usually means:** disambiguation script isn't being followed consistently, possibly due to time pressure from speed-of-answer targets.
- **First question:** is this concentrated with specific operators or specific query types (common surnames, multi-branch businesses)?
- **Data to pull:** a sample of recent connections against the disambiguation-script checklist.
