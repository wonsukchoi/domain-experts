# Producer/Director — Red Flags

### Contingency drawn >30% of total by the schedule's midpoint
- **Usually means:** a single recurring risk (weather, one difficult location, one unreliable department) is compounding rather than a series of unrelated one-off overages.
- **First question:** is the overage concentrated in one location/department, or spread evenly across the shoot?
- **Data to pull:** cost report by category and by shooting day; DOOD report for any cast member with unplanned hold days.

### More than 2 weather-hold days on a single exterior location
- **Usually means:** the location was scheduled without a weather-contingency plan, or the shoot dates were locked against an unfavorable seasonal window.
- **First question:** are the remaining exterior days at the same location, and does the forecast window close before them?
- **Data to pull:** remaining schedule for exterior days at that location; long-range forecast; standby-retainer cost per held day.

### Below-the-line day rate more than 15% above the topsheet estimate
- **Usually means:** the original bid underestimated crew size, equipment rental duration, or location fees — or scope crept after the budget locked (added VFX, added cast, added locations).
- **First question:** was this a bidding error or a scope addition after lock?
- **Data to pull:** original line-item bid vs. actual crew/equipment invoice; any change orders approved after budget lock.

### A "name" cast member's availability window is shorter than the scenes assigned to them
- **Usually means:** casting was finalized before the schedule was locked, or the schedule wasn't re-stripped after casting closed.
- **First question:** can the actor's scenes be clustered into their available window without breaking continuity the role needs?
- **Data to pull:** DOOD report for that actor; scene list requiring their presence; contractual availability dates.

### Coverage plan calls for more setups per day than the crew has historically completed
- **Usually means:** the shot list was built against an idealized pace, not the crew's demonstrated setup-per-day rate on this production.
- **First question:** what was the average setups-per-day on the first week of this shoot, and does today's plan exceed it?
- **Data to pull:** call sheets and script supervisor reports from completed days; planned setup count for the day in question.

### A completion bond company has requested a schedule review mid-shoot
- **Usually means:** the bonding company's own risk model has flagged the production as trending toward a bond call — this is an external, not internal, signal.
- **First question:** what specific variance triggered the bonder's review — schedule, budget, or both?
- **Data to pull:** the bonder's own variance report; the production's most recent cost report for the same period.

### Post-production budget line hasn't moved despite principal photography running over schedule
- **Usually means:** the post budget was never re-forecast after a schedule slip, and will be understated when reconciled — a classic place overages hide until it's too late to correct.
- **First question:** has the post-production budget been re-forecast since the last schedule change?
- **Data to pull:** original post budget vs. current schedule-adjusted estimate; delivery date commitments to distributor/financier.

### A department head requests overtime approval more than twice in the first week
- **Usually means:** the daily schedule is structurally too ambitious for the crew size or setup complexity, not a one-off bad day.
- **First question:** is this the same department each time, or rotating across departments?
- **Data to pull:** daily wrap times for the first week; planned vs. actual setups completed per day.

### Greenlight is being requested before the financing structure is fully closed
- **Usually means:** pressure to lock cast/crew/locations before their holds expire is being used to justify starting production ahead of financing certainty.
- **First question:** what percentage of the total budget is currently backed by closed (not "in negotiation") financing?
- **Data to pull:** financing waterfall showing closed vs. pending sources against the total topsheet.
