# Producer/Director — Playbook

## Budget topsheet (filled example, 24-day independent feature)

| Category | Line item | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Above-the-line | Director fee | $150,000 |
| Above-the-line | Lead cast (2) | $350,000 |
| Above-the-line | Producer fee | $100,000 |
| Above-the-line | Writer/rewrite | $50,000 |
| **ATL subtotal** | | **$650,000** |
| Below-the-line | Crew day rate × 24 days ($42,000/day avg) | $1,008,000 |
| **BTL subtotal** | | **$1,008,000** |
| Post-production | Editorial, color, mix, VFX finishing | $280,000 |
| Contingency | 10% of BTL | $100,800 |
| **Total budget** | | **$2,038,800** |

Financing must close against the total before greenlight — not against ATL+BTL alone, and not against a budget that assumes zero contingency draw.

## One-line schedule (stripboard excerpt)

| Day | Scenes | Location | Cast called | Int/Ext, D/N |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 12 | 34, 35 | Pier 7 (exterior) | Lead A, Lead B, 4 supporting | Ext, Day |
| 13 | 36 | Pier 7 (exterior) | Lead A | Ext, Day |
| 14 | 22, 23 | Studio Stage 2 | Lead B, 2 supporting | Int, Day |
| 15 | 40 | Location B (warehouse) | Lead A, 6 background | Int, Day |
| 16 | 41 | Location C (rooftop) | Lead A, Lead B | Ext, Day |

Scenes are grouped by location and cast availability, not script order — Scenes 34-36 (Pier 7) shoot together even though 36 comes much later in the story, because moving the whole company back to Pier 7 a second time costs a full company move.

## Day-out-of-days (DOOD) report (excerpt)

| Cast member | First day | Last day | Total days worked | Days on hold (paid, not shooting) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lead A | Day 1 | Day 24 | 20 | 2 |
| Lead B | Day 3 | Day 20 | 14 | 1 |
| Supporting — Detective | Day 8 | Day 11 | 4 | 0 |

Lead A's 2 hold days are the direct cost driver behind the mid-shoot overage — every hold day pays the actor's full rate for zero footage.

## Cost report (actual vs. budgeted, day 12 checkpoint)

| Category | Budgeted through Day 12 | Actual through Day 12 | Variance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Below-the-line | $504,000 (12 × $42,000) | $549,000 | +$45,000 |
| Contingency drawn | $0 planned at this point | $45,000 (44.6% of $100,800) | — |

Rule of thumb applied here: contingency draw above 30% of total by the schedule's midpoint triggers a formal compression review (see SKILL.md Decision framework, step 5) rather than waiting to see if the back half self-corrects.
