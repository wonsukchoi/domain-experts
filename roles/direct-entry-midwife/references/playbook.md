# Direct-Entry Midwife — Playbook

Filled protocols for out-of-hospital eligibility, transfer/transport decisions, postpartum hemorrhage, and pre-birth planning. Populate the bracketed fields per client; the thresholds and sequence are the reusable part.

## 1. Out-of-hospital eligibility table

Re-run at intake, at 36 weeks, at labor admission, and at any new finding — never treated as decided once.

| Category | Finding | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Absolute exclusion (do not plan/continue OOH birth) | Multiple gestation, non-vertex presentation at labor onset, placenta previa/vasa previa, category III fetal heart pattern, active genital herpes lesion at labor onset, prior classical cesarean | Refer to physician-led care; not eligible for planned home/birth-center birth |
| Practice-dependent exclusion (varies by practice/state) | VBAC, breech, twins under a specifically trained provider, GDM diet-controlled | Only proceed if this specific practice/state permits it and additional consult/documentation criteria are met |
| Postdates | ≥42w0d (or practice's earlier written limit) | Outer limit reached → transfer for induction per protocol, not negotiated case-by-case |
| Relative — increased surveillance, not automatic exclusion | Prior postpartum hemorrhage, BMI >35, GBS positive | Continue with a documented enhanced-monitoring plan specific to the finding |

## 2. Transfer / transport trigger table

| Trigger | Category (NARM) | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Arrest of progress against practice's written labor-curve criteria, adequate contractions confirmed | Non-emergent | Arrange transport (private vehicle acceptable if stable), call ahead to receiving facility |
| Non-reassuring FHR pattern not resolved by repositioning within 2 auscultation cycles | Emergent | 911 transport, continuous positioning/O2 en route, call ahead |
| Retained placenta >30–60 min (practice-defined) without separation signs | Non-emergent unless bleeding | Arrange transport; upgrade to emergent if EBL crosses 500mL threshold below |
| Cumulative EBL ≥500mL (vaginal) with ongoing bleeding | Emergent-track (start protocol immediately, transport decision at step below) | See hemorrhage protocol §3 |
| Maternal temp ≥38.0°C intrapartum with ROM >18h | Non-emergent (unless fetal status also non-reassuring) | Transport for antibiotics/evaluation, call ahead |
| Shoulder dystocia after birth of head | Emergent (regardless of resolution) | Complete HELPERR sequence, document head-to-body interval; transport mother/newborn for evaluation even if resolved without injury |
| Newborn not breathing spontaneously by 60 seconds after stimulation | Emergent | Begin NRP sequence immediately; call 911 in parallel, do not wait to see if resuscitation succeeds first |
| Transport time to backup facility >30 min for any of the above | Any | Move every trigger point above 10–15 min earlier; call ahead as soon as the finding is confirmed, not after attempting to resolve it |

## 3. Postpartum hemorrhage protocol (out-of-hospital, quantified)

1. **0 min (birth of placenta):** Confirm active management given per state scope (IM oxytocin where permitted). Place calibrated collection drape.
2. **5 min:** Read drape. If <500mL and uterus firm — continue routine fourth-stage monitoring (q15min vitals/fundal check x1h).
3. **At any point EBL (drape + weighed pads, 1g≈1mL) reaches 500mL:** Start hemorrhage protocol immediately — fundal massage, bimanual compression if boggy, catheterize/empty bladder, second-line uterotonic per state formulary (oral/sublingual misoprostol or methylergonovine), and call the backup facility to give early notice (decide on transport separately, per step 5).
4. **Re-quantify every 10 min** using weighed pads; track running cumulative total and vitals trend against each client's own baseline (not population norms).
5. **Activate transport (911) at whichever comes first:** cumulative EBL ≥1000mL sustained despite intervention, OR any instability sign — HR up ~20 from baseline, systolic BP down ~10–15 from baseline, altered mental status, or persistent uterine atony unresponsive to two uterotonic doses.
6. **En route:** continue bimanual compression/fundal massage; second attendant manages newborn and family; prepare SBAR handoff (see SKILL.md worked example for the exact format).

## 4. GBS-positive intrapartum management by scope

| This state's CPM scope includes... | Action |
|---|---|
| IV antibiotic administration | Offer standard IV penicillin/ampicillin per CDC risk-based protocol at labor onset or ROM |
| IM antibiotics only | Offer IM alternative if available in formulary; document that IV route (preferred) isn't in scope |
| No parenteral antibiotic access | Document a three-way conversation: transfer for IV prophylaxis, accept oral/no prophylaxis with a defined newborn-observation plan, or transfer electively before labor onset — client chooses, midwife documents the specific numbers discussed, not just "GBS+ discussed" |
| Any of the above | Enhanced newborn observation (temp, feeding, tone checks at closer intervals) for 12–24h regardless of route chosen, since prophylaxis effectiveness varies with timing and route |

## 5. Pre-birth transport-time planning checklist

- [ ] Backup hospital identified and confirmed still accepting transfers this practice period.
- [ ] Drive time measured (not estimated) under typical traffic/weather conditions for this specific address.
- [ ] If drive time >30 min: written note in chart of adjusted (earlier) trigger points for this specific birth.
- [ ] EMS jurisdiction and address/access instructions confirmed (rural addresses, gate codes, GPS pin — not just a street address).
- [ ] Client and support person briefed on what "non-emergent transport" vs. "911 transport" will look like in practice, before labor starts.
- [ ] Second attendant's role in a transport scenario (stays with newborn/family vs. rides with client) agreed in advance.
