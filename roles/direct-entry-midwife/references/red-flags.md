# Direct-Entry Midwife — Red Flags

### Cumulative EBL ≥500mL by weighed/quantified measurement after vaginal birth
- **Usually means:** Uterine atony (most common), retained placental fragment, or an unrepaired genital-tract laceration
- **First question:** Is the uterus firm or boggy on palpation right now?
- **Data to pull:** Running total from calibrated drape + weighed pads, fundal exam finding, time since placenta delivered

### Meconium-stained fluid, moderate or thick, at rupture
- **Usually means:** A fetal stress response ranging from benign (postdates, vagal) to a sign of evolving compromise
- **First question:** Was the fetal heart rate reassuring on the last two auscultation checks?
- **Data to pull:** Time of ROM, fluid description, last three auscultation readings with maternal position noted

### Second stage exceeding the practice's written outer limit without descent
- **Usually means:** Fetal malposition (OP, asynclitic) or inadequate expulsive effort
- **First question:** What position is the fetal head in on the last exam?
- **Data to pull:** Serial cervical/station exams, position changes attempted, contraction pattern

### Postdates approaching 42w0d
- **Usually means:** Dating may be imprecise, or placental function is declining
- **First question:** Is dating confirmed by an early (<20wk) ultrasound, or LMP-only?
- **Data to pull:** Dating-confirmation method, last fetal movement/fluid assessment

### Maternal temperature ≥38.0°C intrapartum
- **Usually means:** Chorioamnionitis, or (less likely if isolated) dehydration/exertion
- **First question:** How long has rupture of membranes lasted?
- **Data to pull:** ROM duration, GBS status, fetal heart rate trend

### Non-reassuring fetal heart pattern on intermittent auscultation (persistent deceleration after a contraction, baseline <110 or >160)
- **Usually means:** Cord compression, or evolving hypoxia
- **First question:** Does repositioning resolve it on the next check?
- **Data to pull:** Last three auscultation readings with position noted, trend over the last 30 minutes

### Newborn without spontaneous respirations by 60 seconds after stimulation
- **Usually means:** Transitional depression from labor/cord events, or a more significant compromise
- **First question:** Is the heart rate above or below 100?
- **Data to pull:** 1-minute Apgar components, response to initial stimulation and positioning

### Retained placenta beyond 30–60 minutes (practice-defined) without separation signs
- **Usually means:** Partial accreta, uterine atony preventing separation, or a trapped placenta
- **First question:** Are any separation signs present — cord lengthening, gush of blood, rising fundus?
- **Data to pull:** Time since birth, cumulative EBL so far, separation-sign checklist

### Client declines a standing transfer indication (postdates limit, non-reassuring FHR, etc.)
- **Usually means:** Fear of hospital or loss of autonomy, or a genuine risk-tolerance difference — rarely a misunderstanding of the risk itself
- **First question:** Can you tell me in your own words what we're worried about right now?
- **Data to pull:** Prior informed-consent documentation on this exact topic, specific numbers previously discussed

### Transport time to backup facility >30 minutes for a developing complication
- **Usually means:** The decision window a hospital-based guideline assumes doesn't apply at this distance
- **First question:** Has the receiving facility already been called?
- **Data to pull:** Pre-identified drive time, current trend (worsening vs. stable), time of last vitals check

### Shoulder dystocia
- **Usually means:** Fetal macrosomia or maternal pelvis/position factors
- **First question:** Has McRoberts positioning already been attempted?
- **Data to pull:** Head-to-body delivery interval, sequence of maneuvers used (HELPERR order)

### No fetal movement reported by client in the days before labor onset
- **Usually means:** A benign quiet period, or evolving placental insufficiency/fetal compromise
- **First question:** When was movement last clearly felt?
- **Data to pull:** Kick-count results if done, most recent Doppler check or NST if any exists

### Maternal instability signs (HR up ~20 from baseline, systolic BP down ~10–15) before cumulative EBL reaches 1000mL
- **Usually means:** The raw-volume threshold understates severity for this client; bleeding is being tolerated worse than the number alone suggests
- **First question:** What was this client's baseline HR/BP earlier in labor, not a population norm?
- **Data to pull:** Baseline vitals from admission, current vitals, cumulative EBL at time of vitals change
