# Direct-Entry Midwife — Vocabulary

### CPM (Certified Professional Midwife)
A national credential issued by NARM after passing its written exam and skills assessment, obtainable via apprenticeship (PEP) or a MEAC-accredited program.
**In use:** "She's a CPM through the PEP pathway, not a school graduate — same NARM exam either way."
**Common misuse:** Conflating CPM with CNM (certified nurse-midwife) as if they're the same license and scope — CPM is a non-nursing credential with a different, narrower, and state-variable legal scope.

### Direct-entry midwife
A midwife who entered midwifery training without first becoming a nurse, via apprenticeship or a dedicated midwifery program.
**In use:** "I came in direct-entry — did my PEP portfolio, never went through nursing school."
**Common misuse:** Assuming "direct-entry" means informally trained — the PEP pathway has documented supervised-birth counts and a standardized exam, not an ad hoc apprenticeship.

### PEP (Portfolio Evaluation Process)
NARM's apprenticeship-based route to CPM eligibility, requiring a documented portfolio of supervised births and skills competencies.
**In use:** "Her portfolio's done — she's just waiting on her NARM exam date."
**Common misuse:** Treating PEP as a lesser or shortcut path compared to a MEAC-accredited school — both lead to the same CPM exam and the same skills requirements, just different delivery methods.

### MANA Stats
The Midwives Alliance of North America's outcomes-data registry that midwives contribute individual birth data to, the source of the largest published planned-home-birth outcome studies.
**In use:** "We entered that birth into MANA Stats before month's end, same as every birth."
**Common misuse:** Citing "a MANA Stats number" without naming which cohort or dataset year — methodology and inclusion criteria changed across versions, and the figures aren't interchangeable.

### Quantitative blood loss (QBL)
Blood loss measured by weighing pads or reading a calibrated collection drape, distinct from a visually estimated number.
**In use:** "That's QBL, not EBL — we weighed it, we didn't eyeball it."
**Common misuse:** Writing "EBL" in a chart when the number actually came from weighing — the two terms aren't interchangeable, and using the wrong one undercuts the documentation's defensibility.

### Non-emergent vs. emergent transport
NARM's categorization distinguishing an urgent-but-stable transfer (can be arranged calmly, private vehicle acceptable) from a true emergency requiring 911/ambulance.
**In use:** "This is non-emergent — get the car, I'll call ahead, no need for a siren."
**Common misuse:** Calling every transfer "an emergency" regardless of actual acuity, which erodes a receiving hospital's trust over repeated low-acuity transports labeled urgent.

### Scope of practice
The specific set of actions a CPM/direct-entry midwife is legally permitted to perform in a given state — which may or may not include IV placement, specific medications, or newborn resuscitation drugs.
**In use:** "IV oxytocin isn't in my scope here — that's an automatic transfer point if she needs it."
**Common misuse:** Assuming scope is uniform nationally; it's set state-by-state and can differ enormously even between adjacent states.

### Out-of-hospital eligibility
The ongoing determination of whether a specific pregnancy or labor remains appropriate for a planned birth outside a hospital — distinct from a general "low risk" label.
**In use:** "She was OOH-eligible at intake, but the breech presentation last week took her off that list."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as synonymous with hospital-based "low risk," when out-of-hospital eligibility criteria are typically narrower — a finding a hospital would manage as merely higher-risk can be an outright exclusion at home.

### Active management of third stage
Administering a uterotonic (and controlled cord traction, where in scope) immediately after birth to reduce hemorrhage risk, as opposed to physiologic/expectant management.
**In use:** "We're doing active management here — IM oxytocin at the anterior shoulder, per her state's scope."
**Common misuse:** Assuming "active management" always means an injection — in a CPM's practice it may mean an oral or sublingual agent rather than IM/IV, depending on that state's formulary.

### HELPERR
The fixed mnemonic sequence (Help, Evaluate for Episiotomy, Legs/McRoberts, suprapubic Pressure, Enter maneuvers, Remove posterior arm, Roll to hands-knees) for managing shoulder dystocia.
**In use:** "We went through HELPERR in order — McRoberts resolved it at the third step."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as a menu to pick from rather than a fixed sequence, and skipping the head-to-body interval documentation because "it resolved fine."

### Calibrated collection drape
A graduated under-buttocks drape with printed volume markings, used to directly measure blood loss at birth.
**In use:** "Drape's reading 260 at five minutes — that's quantitative, not an eyeball guess."
**Common misuse:** Treating any absorbent birth pad as a "calibrated" measure — only a drape with printed markings, read per its documented method, counts as quantitative.

### Licensed Midwife (LM)
A state-issued license type some states use to regulate direct-entry practice, distinct from the CPM, which is a national certification.
**In use:** "I'm CPM-certified nationally, but I also needed the state LM license to practice here legally."
**Common misuse:** Using "LM" and "CPM" interchangeably — a midwife can hold the CPM credential without being licensed in a given state, and in a few grandfather-clause states can be licensed without holding the CPM.

### Backup/consulting arrangement
A referral relationship with a physician or hospital for consultation and transfer, distinct from a CNM's collaborative-practice agreement.
**In use:** "My backup OB will take a transfer call, but she's not co-managing — this is a referral relationship only."
**Common misuse:** Assuming this arrangement grants any prescriptive or admitting authority — it doesn't; it's strictly a referral pathway.

### Chorioamnionitis (out-of-hospital)
Intra-amniotic infection, typically flagged by maternal fever plus supporting signs (fetal tachycardia, uterine tenderness, foul-smelling fluid).
**In use:** "One temp reading isn't chorio on its own — but with ROM at 20 hours and FHR climbing, we're transferring."
**Common misuse:** Either treating a single isolated temperature as automatically diagnostic, or waiting for lab confirmation that isn't available on-site before acting on the full clinical picture.

### MEAC (Midwifery Education Accreditation Council)
The accrediting body for formal direct-entry midwifery education programs, as opposed to the apprenticeship/PEP pathway.
**In use:** "She went the MEAC-accredited school route instead of PEP, but sits the same NARM exam at the end."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a MEAC-accredited degree is a licensing credential by itself — it's an educational accreditation that feeds into NARM certification and state licensure, not a substitute for either.
