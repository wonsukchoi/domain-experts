# Optometrist — Red Flags

### Sudden-onset flashes or a new shower of floaters
- **Usually means:** posterior vitreous detachment (common, benign) that has caused, or risks causing, a retinal tear; second most likely is a stable, longstanding floater the patient is newly noticing.
- **First question:** "When exactly did this start, and has it been getting worse, staying the same, or improving?"
- **Data to pull:** same-day dilated fundus exam with scleral depression to check the peripheral retina for a tear or detachment.

### Sudden, painless vision loss in one eye
- **Usually means:** retinal artery or vein occlusion, or an ischemic optic neuropathy — a vascular event, not a refractive or routine finding.
- **First question:** "Exactly when did the vision change, and did it come on over seconds, minutes, or hours?"
- **Data to pull:** immediate dilated exam and same-day or emergent referral; this is a stroke-equivalent timeline for the retina.

### Red, painful eye with halos around lights and nausea
- **Usually means:** acute angle-closure glaucoma — an ophthalmic emergency, not a routine red-eye.
- **First question:** "Is the pain severe, and are you seeing rainbow-colored rings around lights?"
- **Data to pull:** IOP immediately (expect markedly elevated, often >40 mmHg) and emergent ophthalmology referral same day.

### IOP ≥30 mmHg on any single reading
- **Usually means:** significant risk of acute optic-nerve damage; second most likely is a measurement artifact (thick cornea, patient squeezing) that still needs same-visit confirmation.
- **First question:** "Is this reading consistent on repeat measurement, and what does the optic nerve look like right now?"
- **Data to pull:** repeat tonometry same visit, immediate optic-nerve exam, same-day or urgent referral if confirmed.

### Cup-to-disc-ratio asymmetry >0.2 between the two eyes
- **Usually means:** early glaucomatous damage in the eye with the larger cup, occurring regardless of what the IOP reads; second most likely is a congenitally asymmetric but stable disc anatomy.
- **First question:** "Has this asymmetry been documented and stable on a prior exam, or is this new?"
- **Data to pull:** visual-field test and OCT of both eyes this visit, comparison against any prior imaging on file.

### Contact lens wearer with pain, redness, and light sensitivity
- **Usually means:** microbial keratitis (corneal infection) — a sight-threatening emergency requiring same-day management, not routine follow-up; second most likely is a sterile infiltrate from lens-related hypoxia or a foreign-body abrasion.
- **First question:** "Are you still wearing the lens, and when did you last take it out?"
- **Data to pull:** slit-lamp exam with fluorescein staining same day; if an infiltrate or ulcer is present, culture and same-day treatment initiation or ophthalmology referral.

### Diabetic patient overdue for a dilated exam
- **Usually means:** diabetic retinopathy risk accumulating silently — the disease is frequently asymptomatic until advanced, which is why the exam interval exists independent of symptoms.
- **First question:** "When was your last dilated exam, and how has your blood sugar control been?"
- **Data to pull:** dilated fundus exam this visit regardless of chief complaint; document any microaneurysms, hemorrhages, or neovascularization and grade severity.

### Child with new-onset esotropia and significant hyperopia on refraction
- **Usually means:** accommodative esotropia — the eye is over-accommodating to compensate for uncorrected farsightedness, pulling the eye inward.
- **First question:** "Is the eye turn present all the time, or only sometimes — and does it change when the child is looking at something close up?"
- **Data to pull:** cycloplegic refraction to reveal the full hyperopic error (manifest refraction alone will underestimate it), full correction prescribed, recheck alignment after glasses are worn consistently.

### Contact lens power beyond ±4.00D ordered without a documented vertex-distance conversion
- **Usually means:** the spectacle power was transcribed directly onto the CL order — a systematic under- or over-correction is about to be dispensed.
- **First question:** "Was the vertex distance conversion applied before this power was finalized?"
- **Data to pull:** the original spectacle Rx and vertex distance measurement; recalculate before dispensing.

### Anisometropia (significant power difference between the two eyes) newly discovered in an older child
- **Usually means:** likely uncorrected for some time, risking amblyopia in the more ametropic eye if not caught and corrected promptly.
- **First question:** "Has vision in one eye ever seemed worse to you or the child, even briefly?"
- **Data to pull:** best-corrected visual acuity in each eye separately, and a same-visit decision on full correction plus, if acuity doesn't improve with correction alone, referral for amblyopia treatment (patching or atropine penalization).
