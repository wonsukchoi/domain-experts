# Optometrist — Playbook

## Vertex-distance conversion table (filled examples)

Formula: F(contact) = F(spectacle) / (1 − d × F(spectacle)), d = vertex distance in meters (standard 12mm = 0.012m).

| Spectacle power | Below ±4.00D threshold? | Contact lens power (calculated) | Rounded to nearest 0.25D step |
|---|---|---|---|
| −2.00 D | Yes — conversion negligible | −2.00 / (1 − (0.012×−2.00)) = −2.00 / 1.024 = −1.95 D | −2.00 D (use spectacle power as-is) |
| −4.00 D | Borderline — check both ways | −4.00 / 1.048 = −3.82 D | −3.75 D |
| −6.00 D | No — convert | −6.00 / 1.072 = −5.60 D | −5.50 D |
| −8.00 D | No — convert | −8.00 / 1.096 = −7.30 D | −7.25 D |
| +4.00 D | No — convert (plus powers shift the other direction) | +4.00 / (1 − (0.012×4.00)) = +4.00 / 0.952 = +4.20 D | +4.25 D |
| +6.00 D | No — convert | +6.00 / 0.928 = +6.47 D | +6.50 D |

**Rule of thumb below ±4.00D:** the calculated shift is under 0.25D, smaller than the smallest available lens-power step — use the spectacle power directly. Above ±4.00D, always calculate.

## IOP / cup-to-disc-ratio referral-decision table

| IOP (mmHg) | CDR finding | Visual field | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10–21 (statistical normal) | Symmetric, <0.5, no rim thinning | Full | Routine annual recheck |
| 22–29 | Symmetric, <0.5 | Full | Repeat IOP same visit (diurnal variation); if confirmed elevated, baseline OCT + visual field, recheck in 1–3 months — ocular hypertension, monitor |
| Any value, including normal | Asymmetric CDR >0.2 between eyes, or either eye >0.6 | Not yet tested | Order visual field + OCT this visit — normal-tension glaucoma is on the differential regardless of IOP |
| ≥30 | Any | Any | Same-visit referral or urgent ophthalmology consult — risk of acute damage rises sharply above this range |
| Elevated + rim thinning/notching on exam | Abnormal CDR | Defect present | Diagnose glaucoma, refer for management per state scope-of-practice rules or co-manage with ophthalmology |

## Contact lens base-curve selection sequence

1. Take keratometry (flat and steep corneal curvature readings) — this is the starting reference, not the final base curve.
2. For a standard soft spherical lens, select a base curve approximating the flat-K reading plus the manufacturer's stated steepening offset (typically 0.6–1.0mm flatter than K in the finished lens's base-curve spec, varies by material) — do not fit "average" base curve without checking the specific lens line's fitting guide.
3. Dispense a trial lens; assess movement on blink (target ~0.5–1.0mm), centration, and comfort after a settling period (minimum 10–15 minutes on eye).
4. If the lens moves excessively or decenters, steepen the base curve; if it's tight (minimal movement, corneal staining after removal), flatten it — adjust in the smallest available step, not a full curve change.
5. Confirm over-refraction matches the intended power before finalizing the order; a base-curve change can shift effective power slightly and needs a final check.

## Referral note template (filled example)

> **Ophthalmology Referral — Suspected Normal-Tension Glaucoma**
> Patient: [name], DOB [date]. IOP OD 16 / OS 15 mmHg (within statistical normal both visits). CDR OD 0.4, OS 0.7 — 0.3 asymmetry exceeds referral threshold. Visual field (Humphrey 24-2): OS shows a superior arcuate defect consistent with glaucomatous damage; OD full field. OCT RNFL: OS thinning in the superior quadrant correlating with the field defect; OD within normal limits.
> **Assessment:** likely normal-tension glaucoma, OS, given optic-nerve and field findings despite normal IOP.
> **Requesting:** glaucoma specialist evaluation for diagnosis confirmation and treatment planning. Copies of OCT and visual-field results attached.
