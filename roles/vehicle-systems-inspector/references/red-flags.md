### Pushrod stroke measured within 1/8 in of the OOS threshold for that chamber's stamped type/size
- **Usually means:** The reading was taken without full brake application, or the chamber genuinely sits near the end of its adjustment range.
- **First question:** Was the stroke measured with the brake fully applied at 90–100 psi, or at rest?
- **Data to pull:** Chamber type/size stamp, stroke reading at full application, maintenance file's last brake-adjustment date.

### Tire tread readings vary by more than 2/32 in across points on the same tire
- **Usually means:** Irregular wear from misalignment or a mismatched dual-tire pairing, not a measurement error.
- **First question:** Which point gave the shallowest reading, and is that the one being reported?
- **Data to pull:** Tread depth at 3 points around the tire's circumference, axle position, dual-mate tire's reading for mismatch.

### Placard mounted on the vehicle doesn't match the hazard class on the shipping paper
- **Usually means:** The prior load's placard was never removed, or the current load was misclassified or under-declared.
- **First question:** Does the shipping paper's hazard class and aggregate weight actually match what's placarded on the vehicle right now?
- **Data to pull:** Shipping paper hazard class and quantity per package, placard hazard class as mounted, 172.504 threshold table for that class.

### Aggregate hazmat weight sits within a few hundred pounds of the 1,001-lb Table 2 threshold
- **Usually means:** The shipper or driver calculated against a single container's weight instead of the aggregate across all packages of that hazard class.
- **First question:** What is the total gross weight of every package of this hazard class aboard, not any one container?
- **Data to pull:** Itemized shipping-paper weights per package, summed aggregate by hazard class.

### 20% or more of a vehicle's brake positions show any defect, even if none individually looks severe
- **Usually means:** A fleet-wide adjustment or maintenance-interval issue rather than one isolated bad brake.
- **First question:** How many of the total brake positions on this vehicle or combination show a defect, counted individually across every axle?
- **Data to pull:** Full brake-by-brake stroke tally for every position, not just the axle where the first defect surfaced.

### Driver presents a CVSA decal issued more than 3 months prior as current proof of inspection
- **Usually means:** The driver is conflating the decal cycle with the 12-month DOT annual inspection cycle, or is offering an expired decal as if it were still valid.
- **First question:** Is this decal being offered in place of a current annual inspection sticker, and what is the decal's actual issue date?
- **Data to pull:** Decal issue date, separate annual-inspection sticker date, 49 CFR 396.17 12-month requirement.

### A fifth wheel, kingpin, or glad-hand shows visible movement or a capacity mismatch
- **Usually means:** Incorrect equipment pairing for the trailer's rated weight, not simple wear.
- **First question:** Does the fifth wheel's rated capacity match the trailer's GVWR, and is the kingpin fully locked and not just seated?
- **Data to pull:** Fifth-wheel and kingpin rating plates, manual lock-engagement check, trailer GVWR.

### Cargo securement uses fewer tie-downs than the load's weight and length appear to require
- **Usually means:** The driver or shipper applied a rule of thumb instead of computing the required aggregate working load limit for that weight and length.
- **First question:** What is the cargo's actual weight and length, and does the tie-down count and rated working load limit meet the securement formula for it?
- **Data to pull:** Cargo weight, tie-down count and rated WLL per unit, load length.

### A lighting or reflector defect appears on only one side of the vehicle
- **Usually means:** An isolated bulb failure, or a wiring-harness fault taking out an entire circuit at once.
- **First question:** Is only the one lamp out, or does the whole circuit — turn signal, brake — fail together?
- **Data to pull:** Which specific lamps are out, a continuity check of the affected circuit, whether the same defect was cited on a prior inspection.

### Carrier's current Vehicle Maintenance BASIC percentile is elevated going into the stop
- **Usually means:** This specific vehicle is statistically more likely to carry an undetected systemic defect, not that today's truck is necessarily bad.
- **First question:** Does the carrier's inspection history show a repeated defect type or terminal pattern that justifies a Level I instead of a faster level?
- **Data to pull:** Carrier's current BASIC percentile, recent violation history by defect type and terminal.

### A defect that was cited and marked corrected on a prior inspection reappears within a short interval
- **Usually means:** The corrective action was cosmetic (adjusted just enough to pass) rather than a durable repair, or the underlying cause (parts, maintenance interval) was never addressed.
- **First question:** What corrective action was documented on the prior citation, and does today's defect trace to the same root cause?
- **Data to pull:** Prior inspection report and citation, maintenance record showing the corrective action taken, interval between the two inspections.
