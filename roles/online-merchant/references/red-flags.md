### A SKU's profitability is reported using gross margin only
- **Usually means:** True net margin may be substantially thinner once payment processing fees, shipping cost, and expected return cost are included, especially for high-return categories.
- **First question:** What is this SKU's category return rate, and has the full cost stack (payment fees, shipping, expected return cost) been calculated against gross margin?
- **Data to pull:** COGS, payment processing fee structure, shipping cost, category return rate, return processing cost per unit.

### Chargeback ratio is trending upward but still below the card network's monitoring threshold
- **Usually means:** Without intervention, the ratio could cross into a monitoring or excessive-chargeback program, risking fines or processing termination — worth investigating root cause now rather than waiting until the threshold is breached.
- **First question:** What is driving the upward trend — fraud, product dissatisfaction, or friendly fraud — and is it concentrated in specific SKUs, channels, or customer segments?
- **Data to pull:** Chargeback ratio trend over recent months, chargeback reason codes, affected SKU/channel breakdown.

### A marketing channel is evaluated using only first-order contribution margin against CAC
- **Usually means:** The channel's real value (or lack of it) may only become clear once actual repeat-purchase and retention data for that specific channel is factored in.
- **First question:** What is this channel's actual repeat purchase rate and average number of repeat orders, and how does the resulting LTV compare to CAC?
- **Data to pull:** Channel-specific CAC, repeat purchase rate, average repeat order count and margin.

### A new SKU or product line is launched on a marketplace channel without comparing owned-store unit economics
- **Usually means:** The full cost of marketplace fees (referral plus fulfillment) and the qualitative cost of not owning the customer relationship may not have been weighed against the reach benefit.
- **First question:** What would the unit economics look like on an owned storefront channel, including customer acquisition cost, compared to the marketplace's fee structure?
- **Data to pull:** Marketplace fee schedule (referral fee %, fulfillment fee), estimated owned-channel CAC and conversion rate for comparison.

### Inventory reorder points are set to minimize stockouts with no consideration of overstock/holding cost
- **Usually means:** This likely leads to chronic overstocking, tying up working capital and risking markdown or write-off on slow-moving inventory.
- **First question:** What is the holding cost and markdown-risk profile for this SKU if it's overstocked, compared to the cost of a stockout (lost sale plus at-risk customer loyalty)?
- **Data to pull:** Current reorder point and safety stock level, holding cost per unit per period, historical stockout and overstock incidence.

### A CAC:LTV ratio is below roughly 3:1 but ad spend is being scaled up anyway
- **Usually means:** The channel/SKU combination may not be clearly profitable at current unit economics, and scaling further spend into it compounds a marginal or unprofitable position.
- **First question:** What specifically is driving the decision to scale — is CAC expected to decrease, or is retention expected to improve, or is this scaling on an unexamined assumption?
- **Data to pull:** Current CAC:LTV ratio, any planned changes to targeting/creative/retention efforts that would justify scaling despite the current ratio.

### A high-return-rate category's return processing cost hasn't been updated recently
- **Usually means:** The true net margin calculation may be using a stale return cost figure, understating or overstating actual profitability.
- **First question:** When was the return processing cost (shipping, restocking labor, markdown risk) for this category last recalculated?
- **Data to pull:** Current return processing cost components, date of last recalculation.

### Free shipping is offered on a SKU without the shipping cost being included in the net margin calculation
- **Usually means:** The SKU's true profitability is being overstated if merchant-paid shipping cost isn't subtracted from the margin.
- **First question:** Is the actual outbound shipping cost for this SKU (not just the customer-facing "free shipping" label) included in the net contribution margin calculation?
- **Data to pull:** Actual outbound shipping cost per unit, current net margin calculation for the SKU.
