### Crime-count comparison across years or jurisdictions cited with no population figure
- **Usually means:** the student (or a generalist analyst) is treating a raw count as a rate; second most likely, the underlying source only reported counts and the denominator wasn't pulled
- **First question:** what was the population of the jurisdiction in each year being compared
- **Data to pull:** Census/ACS population estimates for the jurisdiction and years cited

### Trend comparison spans January 1, 2021 with no note on the NIBRS transition
- **Usually means:** unfamiliarity with the hierarchy-rule-to-incident-based counting change; second most likely, the data source itself doesn't flag the break
- **First question:** was the pre-2021 data converted to a NIBRS-equivalent estimate, or is this two counting systems presented as one line
- **Data to pull:** the FBI's NIBRS transition documentation for that state/agency and the agency's reporting-system change date

### Research protocol names parolees, probationers, or pretrial-supervised subjects and is submitted as exempt or minimal-risk with no Subpart C discussion
- **Usually means:** the applicability question was never asked because the subjects aren't "incarcerated" in the literal sense; second most likely, a genuine good-faith read of 45 CFR 46.303(c) that stops at the confinement definition
- **First question:** does any subject group's supervision status create a coercion or confidentiality risk the IRB would want flagged even if Subpart C doesn't formally apply
- **Data to pull:** the IRB protocol's subject-population description and draft consent language

### Ride-along or corrections-practicum roster shows a student without a dated background-check clearance within the agency's required window
- **Usually means:** a paperwork lag, not an active risk yet — but it's a hold, not a note
- **First question:** has the host agency confirmed the clearance in writing, or is this still pending
- **Data to pull:** the agency's clearance confirmation letter or email for that student

### Course syllabus for a POST-affiliated academy shows fewer contact hours than the state's mandated minimum for a specific topic
- **Usually means:** semester schedule compression traded against a mandated topic; second most likely, a state standard update the syllabus hasn't caught up to
- **First question:** which specific mandated-hour line is short, and by how much
- **Data to pull:** the state POST lesson-plan hour crosswalk and the actual course calendar

### Grade distribution on a case-analysis assignment is sharply bimodal
- **Usually means:** one cluster relied on primary case documents (police report, court record, exoneration registry entry) and the other relied on secondary media coverage of the same case
- **First question:** did the low-scoring cluster cite a primary source at all, or only news/documentary coverage
- **Data to pull:** student bibliographies or citation lists for the assignment

### A wrongful-conviction teaching module rests on a single dramatized secondary account (documentary, podcast) with no primary case record cited
- **Usually means:** the module was built from what's compelling to teach, not what's been checked against the case file
- **First question:** has the case been checked against the National Registry of Exonerations or the original court record
- **Data to pull:** the registry entry or original case file for the case being taught

### A discussion-forum thread on an active or recent use-of-force case escalates into a personal exchange referencing a specific student's law-enforcement background
- **Usually means:** the documented discussion ground rules weren't distributed before the module opened, or weren't enforced when the thread turned personal
- **First question:** were the ground rules posted and acknowledged before this module started
- **Data to pull:** the syllabus discussion-policy section and the thread's posting timestamps relative to the module's start date

### ACJS self-study or program review cites a placement or pass-rate percentage with no cohort size given
- **Usually means:** a small cohort makes the percentage volatile and potentially misleading (e.g., "80% job placement" from a 5-student cohort)
- **First question:** what is the actual headcount behind the percentage
- **Data to pull:** the raw cohort roster for the years cited

### A ride-along or practicum host agency requests a change to what students may discuss or publish about the placement after the term has already started
- **Usually means:** no agency-specific rules of engagement were signed before placement, so the agency is defining terms retroactively
- **First question:** what did the original placement agreement specify about confidentiality and publication
- **Data to pull:** the signed practicum MOU or waiver from before the term started

### A capstone or thesis draws a causal "the program worked/failed" conclusion from a single city with no comparison jurisdiction or pre-program baseline
- **Usually means:** the design can show association at best, not causation, and the write-up outran the design
- **First question:** what would the rate likely have been without the program — is there a comparison jurisdiction or a longer baseline trend available
- **Data to pull:** a comparable jurisdiction's data over the same period, or a longer pre-program trend line for the same city

### A student's public-facing paper or presentation includes juvenile-record or victim-identifying detail
- **Usually means:** the detail came from a source the student didn't recognize as protected (a sealed juvenile record, a victim's name in an old news article)
- **First question:** is this detail from a record that's sealed or otherwise confidentiality-protected under state law
- **Data to pull:** the original source of the detail and its confidentiality classification
