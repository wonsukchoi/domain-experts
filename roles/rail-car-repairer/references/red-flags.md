# Rail Car Repairer — Red Flags

### Wheel flange thickness at or below 7/8 in (22 mm)
- **Usually means:** normal wear-out on a high-mileage wheelset, less commonly accelerated wear from a misaligned truck or persistent flange-contact condition
- **First question:** what's the mileage/time since this wheelset was last serviced or mounted, per Umler?
- **Data to pull:** flange thickness gauge reading, wheelset mount date and mileage since, truck alignment/hunting history if flagged before

### Single flat spot ≥2.5 in, or two adjacent flats each ≥2 in
- **Usually means:** a wheel-slide event during an emergency or dynamic brake application, less commonly a stuck brake cylinder holding one wheel locked
- **First question:** does the chatter-mark pattern match a matching brake shoe wear anomaly on the same truck?
- **Data to pull:** flat-spot length measurement, brake shoe thickness on same truck, any logged emergency-application or derailment event for this car

### Composition brake shoe remaining thickness at or below roughly 3/8 in
- **Usually means:** normal wear reaching end-of-life, less commonly a stuck or dragging brake rigging accelerating wear on one shoe
- **First question:** is the wear even across all shoes on the truck, or concentrated on one?
- **Data to pull:** shoe thickness on every shoe on the truck, brake cylinder piston travel, rigging adjustment history

### Coupler knuckle crack found under visual or magnetic-particle inspection
- **Usually means:** fatigue cracking from cumulative service loading, less commonly a casting flaw present since manufacture
- **First question:** has this knuckle's service history or mileage been unusually high for its type?
- **Data to pull:** MPI inspection result, knuckle date-of-manufacture stamp, prior coupler-related repair history on this car

### Wheel diameter difference between wheels on the same axle exceeding roughly 1/2 in
- **Usually means:** one wheel replaced individually rather than as a matched set, less commonly uneven wear from a persistent brake or alignment issue
- **First question:** were both wheels on this axle mounted at the same time, per Umler?
- **Data to pull:** wheel diameter measurement both sides, mount date for each wheel, truck alignment history

### Single-car air brake test leakage exceeding the shop's pass threshold (commonly cited around 5 psi/min)
- **Usually means:** a loose hose connection, worn gasket, or leaking angle cock, less commonly a cracked brake pipe or fitting
- **First question:** does the leak location isolate to the car itself or to the test connection setup?
- **Data to pull:** SCTD leakage reading, visual/audible leak-check results at each connection point, prior brake system repair record

### Same defect code cited on two consecutive interchange inspections within roughly 90 days
- **Usually means:** the underlying cause wasn't addressed at the prior repair, only the symptom (e.g., shoe replaced without checking why it wore unevenly)
- **First question:** what was actually done at the last repair order for this defect code?
- **Data to pull:** prior repair order for the same component, current measurement against the same limit, brake rigging/alignment check

### Same defect type appearing on multiple cars off the same train or same yard track
- **Usually means:** a yard or train-handling practice issue (over-speed humping, rough coupling), not car-specific wear
- **First question:** did these cars all pass through the same yard, hump, or handling event recently?
- **Data to pull:** train consist and yard routing history for the affected cars, hump speed logs if available, prior defect rates for that yard

### Thermal crack or heat-check pattern on wheel tread or plate
- **Usually means:** a dragging or stuck brake shoe holding continuous friction contact, less commonly prolonged dynamic braking on a steep grade
- **First question:** is there a corresponding shoe or rigging anomaly on the same wheel?
- **Data to pull:** brake shoe condition and rigging travel on the affected wheel, route/grade profile if grade-braking is suspected

### Journal or bearing discoloration consistent with overheating ("hot box" signature)
- **Usually means:** bearing failure in progress from lubrication loss or seal damage, an urgent defect regardless of how the rest of the car looks
- **First question:** does a hot-bearing detector (wayside or handheld) confirm elevated temperature right now?
- **Data to pull:** wayside detector reading if available, bearing seal/lubrication inspection, axle/bearing service history

### Repair bill disputed by the billed road within the interchange dispute window
- **Usually means:** the original classification lacked documented physical evidence, not necessarily that the classification was wrong
- **First question:** what evidence was recorded at time of inspection — chatter marks, mileage-since-service, logged handling event — versus what's being asserted now?
- **Data to pull:** original repair order and defect classification notes, car's Umler service history at time of inspection, any logged handling events on the disputing road
