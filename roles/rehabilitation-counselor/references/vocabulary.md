# Rehabilitation Counselor — Vocabulary

### Functional Capacity Evaluation (FCE)
A standardized, therapist-administered test measuring what a client can physically do — lift/carry limits by time band, postural tolerance, endurance — as opposed to what a diagnosis alone implies.
**In use:** "The FCE puts him at Light PDL, occasional 20 / frequent 10 — that's what the TSA has to work within, not the MRI findings."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a permanent lifetime ceiling rather than a time-stamped snapshot that needs re-evaluation as the condition changes.

### Physical Demand Level (PDL)
A five-tier classification (Sedentary, Light, Medium, Heavy, Very Heavy) of how much lifting/carrying an occupation requires, defined by occasional/frequent/constant weight thresholds.
**In use:** "Her prior job was Medium PDL; the FCE now caps her at Light — that's a one-tier drop, not a career-ending one."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with job title ("desk job" assumed Sedentary) rather than checked against the actual physical requirements of the specific role.

### Specific Vocational Preparation (SVP)
A 1–9 scale estimating how long it typically takes to learn to perform a job proficiently, from short demonstration (SVP 1–2) to multi-year training (SVP 7–9).
**In use:** "Jumping from SVP 3 to SVP 7 without a funded training plan is the part of this IPE that's going to fail at placement."
**Common misuse:** Ignored in favor of matching only physical demand level, missing that a large SVP gap is often the bigger transferability barrier.

### Transferable Skills Analysis (TSA)
The process of decomposing a client's work history into underlying worker traits (PDL, SVP, GED, aptitudes, certifications) and cross-referencing them against occupational databases to identify jobs the client can realistically move into.
**In use:** "The TSA surfaced Shipping/Receiving Clerk — not because it sounds like his old job, but because the inventory-software and certification skills transfer directly."
**Common misuse:** Done as a job-title-matching exercise off a resume instead of a true worker-trait decomposition.

### Individualized Plan for Employment (IPE)
The funded, time-bound vocational plan required for public VR clients under the Rehabilitation Act/WIOA, naming a specific employment goal, the services needed to reach it, responsible parties, and a timeline.
**In use:** "The goal statement alone isn't an IPE yet — we need the funding source and timeline for the certification renewal before this is complete."
**Common misuse:** Written as a goal statement without a concrete, funded services plan, which looks complete but fails at implementation.

### Reasonable accommodation
A modification to a job or work environment that enables a qualified employee with a disability to perform essential functions, required under the ADA unless it imposes undue hardship.
**In use:** "The lift-assist device is a reasonable accommodation for the restocking task — it's the conveyor-loading volume that can't be accommodated."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to mean "whatever the employee requests," when it's specifically tied to enabling performance of essential job functions, not a general workplace preference.

### Undue hardship
The legal standard (ADA) an employer must meet to deny an accommodation — significant difficulty or expense, judged against the four-factor test (accommodation cost/nature, employer's overall resources, facility size, type of operation), not a fixed dollar amount.
**In use:** "A single manager saying 'we don't have budget for that' isn't an undue-hardship determination — that requires the four-factor analysis against the company's actual resources."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a simple cost ceiling set unilaterally by the employer, rather than the specific four-factor legal test.

### Essential job function
A fundamental duty of a position — as opposed to a marginal one — that an accommodation must enable the employee to perform, distinguishing what must be accommodated from what could be restructured away entirely.
**In use:** "Is the 50 lb occasional lift an essential function of this role, or could it be reassigned to another team member without fundamentally altering the job?"
**Common misuse:** Assumed to include every listed duty on a job description, when courts weigh actual time spent, consequences of not performing it, and whether other employees could absorb it.

### Wage replacement ratio
The percentage of a client's pre-injury earnings that a candidate occupation's wage would replace, used as a screening flag (not a hard cutoff) in vocational goal-setting.
**In use:** "93% wage replacement on the Shipping/Receiving option — that's well above the flag line, so we don't need to push training-funded alternatives here."
**Common misuse:** Ignored entirely in favor of "any job within PDL," producing a technically valid but financially unrealistic IPE goal.

### Labor-market information (LMI)
Wage, employment, and job-growth data — ideally local or regional, not just national — used to validate that a candidate occupation actually has openings before it enters an IPE.
**In use:** "National BLS growth for this occupation looks great, but the local LMI shows almost no postings here — that's a demand problem, not a skills problem."
**Common misuse:** Substituting national occupational-outlook data for local job-posting reality, producing plans built on jobs that don't exist in the client's actual labor market.

### Goal inflation / goal collapse
Two mirror-image grief responses in vocational counseling: insisting on a target beyond documented functional capacity (inflation), or rejecting every realistic option as inadequate (collapse).
**In use:** "This is goal collapse, not a real objection to the Inventory Control Clerk option — the wage and skills match are both solid."
**Common misuse:** Read as simple stubbornness or unreasonableness rather than as a predictable stage in adjusting to a changed functional capacity, which changes how the counselor should respond.

### Job Accommodation Network (JAN)
A federally funded resource providing accommodation-cost data and specific accommodation ideas by disability and job type, used to ground undue-hardship discussions in actual cost distributions.
**In use:** "JAN's data shows the median accommodation costs nothing or close to it — that's worth citing before the employer assumes this will be expensive."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a legal authority that settles an undue-hardship determination, when it's a data/idea resource, not a binding standard.
