# Rehabilitation Counselor — Playbook

## Transferable Skills Analysis (TSA) — worksheet template

**Step 1 — Decompose prior work into worker traits (not job titles).**

| Trait | Prior job value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Physical Demand Level (PDL) | Sedentary / Light / Medium / Heavy / Very Heavy | FCE or DOT-equivalent job description |
| Specific Vocational Preparation (SVP) | 1–9 scale (1 = short demonstration, 9 = 10+ yrs) | DOT/O*NET job code |
| GED reasoning / math / language | 1–6 scale each | DOT/O*NET job code |
| Aptitudes (verbal, numerical, spatial, motor) | High/Medium/Low per aptitude | GATB or self-reported skill inventory |
| Certifications/licenses held | List (e.g., forklift, CDL, first aid) | Client history, still current? |

**Step 2 — Establish current functional capacity ceiling.**

| FCE dimension | Result | Time band |
|---|---|---|
| Occasional lift/carry | ___ lbs | 0–33% of shift |
| Frequent lift/carry | ___ lbs | 34–66% of shift |
| Constant lift/carry | ___ lbs | 67–100% of shift |
| Standing/walking tolerance | ___ min before position change | — |
| Sitting tolerance | ___ min before position change | — |
| Cognitive/sensory limitations | List if any | — |

PDL mapping from lift capacity (illustrative DOT convention):
- Sedentary: occasional ≤10 lbs
- Light: occasional ≤20 lbs, frequent ≤10 lbs
- Medium: occasional ≤50 lbs, frequent ≤25 lbs
- Heavy: occasional ≤100 lbs, frequent ≤50 lbs

**Step 3 — Generate candidate occupations within current PDL, ranked by trait match.**

| Candidate | PDL match? | SVP delta from prior job | Certifications transfer? | Local demand (openings/yr) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| — | Y/N | +/- steps | Y/N/partial | # |

Discard any candidate with SVP delta greater than +2 steps unless a funded training plan is in the IPE — that gap is the most common reason placements fail.

**Step 4 — Wage-replacement table.**

| Candidate | Hourly wage (local median) | Annual (× 2,080 hrs) | Wage replacement vs. prior earnings |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prior job (baseline) | $___ | $___ | 100% |
| Candidate A | $___ | $___ | __% |
| Candidate B | $___ | $___ | __% |

Flag any candidate under ~80% wage replacement to the client explicitly before ranking it out.

## Undue-hardship four-factor test — worksheet

For any accommodation request with material cost (rule of thumb: over ~$500):

1. **Nature and cost of the accommodation** — one-time vs. recurring; net cost after any tax credit or external funding (e.g., state VR agency cost-share).
2. **Overall financial resources of the facility/employer** — not the department budget, not one manager's discretionary authority; the legal entity's resources.
3. **Number of employees and effect on operations** — a $1,200 accommodation reads differently at a 5-person shop than a 500-person facility.
4. **Type of operation, including facility structure** — a single-site retailer vs. a multi-site chain with shared resources across locations.

Document each factor explicitly, even when the answer is obviously "not undue hardship" — the documentation is what protects the determination if it's later challenged.

## IPE goal-setting checklist

- [ ] Employment goal named as a specific occupation, not a field ("Shipping/Receiving Clerk," not "warehouse work")
- [ ] Goal's PDL confirmed within current FCE limits (or accommodation gap explicitly closed)
- [ ] Wage-replacement ratio calculated and disclosed to client
- [ ] Local labor-market demand confirmed (not just national BLS trend)
- [ ] Services list names specific actions (training, certification renewal, job placement) with a responsible party for each
- [ ] Timeline stated with a target start date
- [ ] Fallback goal named in case primary goal has no openings within a stated window
- [ ] Re-evaluation trigger stated (date or symptom-change condition)
