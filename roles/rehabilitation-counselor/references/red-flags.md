# Rehabilitation Counselor — Red Flags

### FCE more than 12–18 months old anchoring a new IPE goal
- **Usually means:** Condition may have improved, worsened, or fluctuated since the evaluation; using stale numbers risks either an unsafe placement or an unnecessarily restrictive one.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed in your symptoms, treatment, or daily function since this evaluation?"
- **Data to pull:** FCE date, most recent physician progress note, any interim work-attempt or symptom-diary data.

### Client's stated target occupation exceeds documented PDL by more than one level
- **Usually means:** Denial of the functional limitation, outdated self-image of capacity, or a genuine belief an accommodation will close the gap.
- **First question:** "What would need to be true — an accommodation, more recovery time — for this to be realistic?"
- **Data to pull:** Current FCE, essential-functions list for the target job, any prior accommodation attempts.

### Employer states a fixed dollar ceiling for "any accommodation" before the specific request is evaluated
- **Usually means:** Employer is applying an informal budget rule rather than the legally required four-factor undue-hardship test.
- **First question:** "Is that ceiling based on this specific accommodation's cost against the company's overall resources, or a general policy?"
- **Data to pull:** Employer size/revenue (if disclosable), the specific accommodation's actual quoted cost, any available tax credit or state cost-share.

### Candidate occupation's wage replacement falls below ~80% of pre-injury earnings
- **Usually means:** The realistic options within the client's functional capacity pay materially less, or the client hasn't yet considered a higher-SVP candidate requiring training.
- **First question:** "Is this a tradeoff you're choosing for stability, or should we look at training-funded options that pay closer to your prior wage?"
- **Data to pull:** Full wage-replacement table across all shortlisted candidates, training-funded alternatives and their timelines.

### Transferable-skills analysis is matching job titles instead of worker traits
- **Usually means:** The analysis was done from a resume/job-title list rather than a true decomposition into PDL/SVP/GED/aptitudes, and is likely missing valid candidates.
- **First question:** "What specific skills — not job titles — did the prior role actually require day to day?"
- **Data to pull:** DOT/O*NET worker-trait codes for the prior occupation, not just its title.

### Client cycles through rejecting every candidate occupation as "not good enough"
- **Usually means:** Grief over the pre-injury identity is driving goal collapse, not an objective flaw in the candidates.
- **First question:** "What does this job loss mean to you beyond the paycheck?"
- **Data to pull:** None yet — this is a conversation, not a data problem; revisit the TSA after the grief conversation, not instead of it.

### IPE lists a training program with no named funding source or timeline
- **Usually means:** The goal was set collaboratively but the services plan wasn't actually built out — a common way IPEs look complete but fail at implementation.
- **First question:** "Who is paying for this training, and by what date does it need to start to hit the target start date?"
- **Data to pull:** Training program cost and duration, funding source (VR agency, workers' comp, client self-pay), enrollment deadlines.

### No local labor-market openings for a candidate occupation despite strong trait match
- **Usually means:** National BLS growth data was used without checking local/regional demand — a common gap in TSA that only checks trait fit.
- **First question:** "How many actual openings for this occupation have posted in this labor market in the last 90 days?"
- **Data to pull:** Local/state LMI job-posting data, not just national occupational outlook.

### Placement attempt fails and the IPE is not revisited
- **Usually means:** The plan is being treated as fixed rather than as a hypothesis to be tested and revised — a failed placement attempt is itself new functional or labor-market information.
- **First question:** "What did we learn from this attempt that changes the plan?"
- **Data to pull:** Reason for the failed placement (functional mismatch vs. hiring-market factors vs. other), updated FCE if functional capacity is in question.
