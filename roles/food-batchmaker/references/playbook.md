## Ingredient-tolerance-by-risk-category table

| Risk category | Example ingredients | Typical tolerance approach |
|---|---|---|
| Bulk structural | Flour, water, base carbohydrate/protein | Wider tolerance (e.g., ±2-5% batch weight) acceptable for most products |
| Functional/process-critical | Leavening, emulsifiers, stabilizers | Tighter tolerance; verify functional behavior at actual process timing, not just weight accuracy |
| Safety-critical | Preservatives, acids/pH-adjusters | Tight tolerance tied to regulatory limits (e.g., legal maximum preservative %) |
| Allergen-declared | Any labeled allergen ingredient | Tightest tolerance and control — deviation affects label accuracy and cross-contact risk, not just product quality |

## Allergen sequencing checklist

1. Confirm the day's production schedule sequences allergen-free/lower-risk products before allergen-containing products.
2. If the schedule must change (supply issue, equipment failure), re-verify the sequencing rule still holds for the revised order — don't assume the original logic still applies.
3. Before any allergen-containing batch: confirm full CIP or equivalent cleaning cycle completed and verified (not just visually clean) on all shared equipment.
4. After any allergen-containing batch: confirm full CIP or equivalent cleaning cycle completed and verified before the next (different-allergen-profile) product runs.
5. Document changeover completion with timestamp and verification method in the batch record before the next product starts.
6. Never shorten steps 3-4 for schedule-recovery purposes — if time doesn't allow the full protocol, delay the next batch rather than shortening the clean.

## Mixing functional-endpoint quick reference

| Product type | Under-mixed signal | Over-mixed signal | Endpoint indicator |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bread dough | Slack, tears easily, poor gas retention | Dense, tough, dull surface | Windowpane test (stretch test) passes |
| Emulsified sauce | Separated/broken appearance, oil visible on surface | Over-thickened, grainy texture | Visual homogeneity + viscosity check |
| Cake batter | Under-aerated, dense crumb after bake | Tough crumb from over-developed gluten | Target specific gravity/volume reading |
