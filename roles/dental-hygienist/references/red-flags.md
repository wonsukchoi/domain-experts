# Dental Hygienist — Red Flags

### Probing depth increases ≥3mm at a previously stable site since the last charting
- **Usually means:** Active disease progression at that site, or a measurement/technique inconsistency between visits (different clinician, different probe angulation).
- **First question:** Who charted the prior visit, and was the same probing technique/angulation used?
- **Data to pull:** Prior two periodontal charts for that tooth, radiograph if not taken in the last 12 months.

### New furcation involvement not present on the prior chart
- **Usually means:** Disease has progressed into the root trifurcation/bifurcation, which changes both staging and prognosis for that tooth.
- **First question:** Is this Class I (early, detectable with probe tip), Class II (probe enters but doesn't pass through), or Class III (through-and-through)?
- **Data to pull:** Current radiograph, prior charting for that tooth, mobility grade.

### Full-mouth BOP percentage rising across two consecutive recall visits despite unchanged home care
- **Usually means:** The current recall interval isn't holding, or a systemic factor (new medication, uncontrolled diabetes, pregnancy, stress) is affecting the periodontium independent of plaque control.
- **First question:** Has anything changed medically or in medications since the last visit?
- **Data to pull:** Updated medical history, BOP percentage trend across the last three visits.

### Localized bone loss disproportionate to visible plaque/calculus (heavy destruction, light biofilm)
- **Usually means:** Possible aggressive/rapidly-progressive periodontitis (Grade C) rather than typical chronic periodontitis, or an undiagnosed systemic condition affecting periodontal tissue.
- **First question:** Is there a family history of early tooth loss or periodontal disease?
- **Data to pull:** Radiographic bone-loss rate if serial films exist, family/medical history.

### Tooth mobility increase of one grade or more since last visit
- **Usually means:** Loss of periodontal support, occlusal trauma, or a periapical process — not something hygiene should treat around without dentist input.
- **First question:** Is the mobility isolated to one tooth or affecting adjacent teeth in a pattern consistent with occlusal trauma?
- **Data to pull:** Occlusal analysis, current radiograph, prior mobility grade.

### A lesion, ulceration, or mucosal change lasting >2 weeks on oral exam
- **Usually means:** Could range from minor trauma to a condition requiring biopsy; hygienists are trained to notice, not diagnose.
- **First question:** Does the patient recall trauma to the area, and how long has it been present?
- **Data to pull:** Photo/documentation of location and appearance, patient's timeline.

### Patient reports a home-care routine inconsistent with the plaque/calculus level observed
- **Usually means:** Either technique is wrong despite correct frequency, or the self-report is inaccurate (common, not necessarily dishonest — patients overestimate compliance).
- **First question:** Can the patient demonstrate their brushing/flossing technique chairside?
- **Data to pull:** Plaque-index score, disclosing-agent photo if available.

### SRP quadrant not showing expected pocket-depth reduction at 4–6 week re-evaluation
- **Usually means:** Incomplete calculus removal, a site requiring periodontal referral, or a non-plaque-related cause (systemic, genetic) that SRP alone won't resolve.
- **First question:** Was the re-evaluation done at the correct interval, and is the same site still bleeding?
- **Data to pull:** Pre- and post-SRP probing depths at the same site, referral history if any.

### Radiographic bone loss visible on a tooth with normal clinical probing depths
- **Usually means:** Either the radiograph is showing an old/healed defect, or there's a discrepancy worth flagging — probing depth can miss buccal/lingual bone loss that a periapical or bitewing captures differently than a probe angle does.
- **First question:** Is this a new radiograph or comparison against an old one, and does the clinical picture match?
- **Data to pull:** Prior radiograph for comparison, full six-site charting at that tooth.
