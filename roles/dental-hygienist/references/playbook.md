# Dental Hygienist — Playbook

## Full-mouth periodontal charting template (six sites per tooth)

| Tooth | MB | B | DB | ML | L | DL | Worst CAL | Furcation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| #3 | 6/1/7 (PD/rec/CAL) | 5/0/5 | 7/2/9 | 4/0/4 | 3/0/3 | 4/0/4 | 9mm | Class II (DB) |
| #14 | 4/0/4 | 3/0/3 | 3/0/3 | 4/0/4 | 3/0/3 | 3/0/3 | 4mm | None |
| #19 | 5/1/6 | 4/0/4 | 4/0/4 | 5/1/6 | 4/0/4 | 4/0/4 | 6mm | Class I (MB) |

Format per cell: probing depth (PD) / recession (rec) / CAL = PD + rec. A negative recession value (coronally positioned margin, e.g. gingival hyperplasia) subtracts from CAL instead — CAL can be less than PD.

## Staging worksheet (2017 AAP/EFP classification)

| Stage | Interdental CAL at worst site | Radiographic bone loss | Tooth loss from periodontitis |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | 1–2mm | Coronal third (<15%) | None |
| II | 3–4mm | Coronal third (15–33%) | None |
| III | ≥5mm | Extends to mid-third of root or beyond | ≤4 teeth |
| IV | ≥5mm | Extends to mid-third of root or beyond | ≥5 teeth, or major additional complexity (bite collapse, drifting, <10 teeth in occlusal function) |

Stage is set by the single worst qualifying site in the mouth, not an average — a mouth that is otherwise Stage I with one Stage III tooth is charted Stage III (localized).

## Grading worksheet (rate of progression)

| Grade | Bone loss % ÷ age (worst site) | Risk factors | Default recall interval |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (slow) | <0.25 | None | 6 months |
| B (moderate) | 0.25–1.0 | None or one minor factor | 4 months |
| C (rapid) | >1.0, or disproportionate to biofilm/calculus level present | Smoking, uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c ≥7%), or heavy biofilm with minimal destruction (suggests aggressive form) | 3 months |

If direct evidence of progression exists (prior radiograph showing bone loss over a known time interval), use the measured rate directly instead of the age-ratio proxy — the proxy is a substitute for missing longitudinal data, not the preferred method when real data exists.

## SRP quadrant-sequencing template

| Visit | Quadrant | Rationale | Anesthesia | Re-eval scheduled |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | UR (worst: tooth #3, Stage III localized) | Highest disease burden, furcation involvement needs earliest intervention | Local (lidocaine 2% w/ epi 1:100,000) | 4–6 weeks |
| 2 | UL | Second-highest BOP count | Local | 4–6 weeks |
| 3 | LL | Stable, lower priority | Local or none if tolerated | With visit 4 |
| 4 | LR | Stable, lower priority | Local or none if tolerated | With visit 3 |

Re-evaluation at 4–6 weeks checks pocket-depth reduction and BOP resolution at the treated quadrant before deciding whether the site needs re-treatment, a referral to periodontics, or moves to maintenance interval.

## Filled chairside note to dentist (escalation)

> Patient [name], recall exam [date]. BOP positive at both sites. Radiograph pending to confirm bone-level change. Holding on SRP at this quadrant pending your review — requesting exam before proceeding.
