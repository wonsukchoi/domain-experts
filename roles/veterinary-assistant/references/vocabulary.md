# Veterinary Assistant — Vocabulary

### TPR
Temperature, pulse, respiration — the three core vital signs checked together at each monitoring interval.
**In use:** "TPR at T+30 was within normal limits, but let's keep watching the trend."
**Common misuse:** Treating TPR as a single pass/fail check rather than three separate values that each need their own trend tracked.

### CRT (capillary refill time)
Time for color to return to a mucous membrane (usually gum) after firm pressure is applied and released; normal is under 2 seconds.
**In use:** "CRT is at 3 seconds now, up from under 2 at the last check."
**Common misuse:** Estimating CRT visually without actually pressing and timing it, or reporting it as "fine" without a number.

### Six rights
The medication-administration verification checklist: right patient, drug, dose, route, time, documentation.
**In use:** "I ran the six rights before giving it — weight was updated since yesterday, so the dose changed."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as a one-time training concept rather than a check performed at every single administration.

### Scruff-and-wrap (towel burrito)
A restraint technique for cats combining a scruff hold with a towel wrapped around the body to control limbs.
**In use:** "Let's try scruff-and-wrap before we escalate to sedation for the blood draw."
**Common misuse:** Applying it as the default first step for every cat regardless of the individual animal's temperament or the procedure's needs.

### Floor-space allowance
The minimum square-inch (or metric equivalent) of cage floor space required per animal under an approved husbandry protocol, scaled to body weight.
**In use:** "These mice have outgrown the juvenile allowance — we need to split the cage."
**Common misuse:** Checking only that the animal count matches the approved group size, without re-checking that the weight class (and therefore required space) still matches.

### IACUC
Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee — the body that reviews and approves animal-research protocols, including husbandry standards.
**In use:** "The cage density has to match what IACUC approved for this protocol, not just what looks reasonable."
**Common misuse:** Treating IACUC approval as a one-time checkbox rather than an ongoing compliance standard checked at every husbandry round.

### Extra-label use (off-label)
Using an approved drug outside its labeled species, dose, or indication — legal under AMDUCA when directed by a veterinarian, but the assistant only administers, never decides.
**In use:** "That's an extra-label dose — make sure it's on the treatment sheet with Dr. Alvarez's initials before giving it."
**Common misuse:** Assuming any dose that "seems high" must be an error, rather than checking whether it's a documented extra-label order.

### Compensatory shock
An early physiologic state where the body maintains blood pressure via rising heart rate and vasoconstriction, before decompensation causes a drop in blood pressure.
**In use:** "The trend looks like compensatory shock — heart rate climbing while temp falls."
**Common misuse:** Waiting for a blood-pressure drop or other late/decompensated sign before escalating, missing the earlier compensatory-stage signal.

### Mucous membrane (MM) color
The color of gum tissue, used as a quick perfusion/oxygenation indicator; normal is pink.
**In use:** "MM went from pink to pale pink between checks — flagging that."
**Common misuse:** Checking MM color once at baseline and not rechecking it at every subsequent vital-sign interval.

### Standing order
A pre-authorized set of instructions from the veterinarian (e.g., monitoring interval, escalation criteria) that doesn't require a new order for each instance.
**In use:** "The standing order says escalate on two adverse parameters — we hit that at T+60."
**Common misuse:** Treating a standing order as optional guidance rather than the actual authorization boundary for what this role can do without asking first.

### Husbandry round
A scheduled check of animal housing, feeding, and welfare status, distinct from a clinical exam.
**In use:** "Flag the cage density issue at the next husbandry round, don't wait for protocol renewal."
**Common misuse:** Treating husbandry rounds as purely feeding/cleaning tasks rather than also a compliance-verification checkpoint.

### Twitch
A restraint device applied to a horse's upper lip or ear to produce a calming/analgesic effect for brief procedures.
**In use:** "Twitch is holding, but we're past the 10-minute mark — let's reassess."
**Common misuse:** Using it as an indefinite restraint method rather than a time-limited tool for brief procedures.

### Adverse trend
A pattern where a vital-sign parameter moves in the clinically worse direction across two or more sequential checks, as distinct from a single abnormal reading.
**In use:** "One elevated HR isn't an adverse trend by itself — we need to see the next check before deciding."
**Common misuse:** Using "trend" loosely to describe any single data point, collapsing the distinction between one reading and a pattern across time.
