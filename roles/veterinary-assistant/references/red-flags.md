# Veterinary Assistant — Red Flags

### Heart rate and temperature trending in opposite directions across two checks
- **Usually means:** Compensatory shock (rising HR, falling temp) or, less commonly, an early febrile response masked by peripheral vasoconstriction.
- **First question:** What was the trend, not just the current reading — has this happened across two or more consecutive checks?
- **Data to pull:** Full TPR/CRT flowsheet for the monitoring period, not just the latest entry.

### Capillary refill time >2 seconds on any check
- **Usually means:** Reduced peripheral perfusion — early shock, hypovolemia, or hypothermia.
- **First question:** Is this the first prolonged CRT reading, or part of a trend with HR/temp also moving adverse?
- **Data to pull:** Concurrent HR, temp, and mucous-membrane color at the same check.

### Patient struggling harder against restraint after 1-2 repositioning attempts
- **Usually means:** The restraint method is escalating the patient's fear response rather than calming it.
- **First question:** Has a lower-intensity technique or chemical restraint been tried before increasing physical force?
- **Data to pull:** Patient's chart for prior restraint-response notes or known aversions.

### Treatment sheet shows no prior dose logged, but the patient's demeanor suggests it may have already been given
- **Usually means:** A documentation gap, not necessarily a missed or duplicate dose — but must be resolved before administering, not assumed either way.
- **First question:** Who administered the last shift's doses, and did they log in real time or at end of shift?
- **Data to pull:** Full treatment-sheet history for the shift, cross-checked with staff who administered prior doses.

### Lab-animal cage appears crowded relative to what the protocol originally specified
- **Usually means:** Litter growth, combined litters, or an unreported breeding event has pushed the cage past its approved floor-space allowance.
- **First question:** What is the current animal count and average weight, and does that match the group size the protocol was approved for?
- **Data to pull:** IACUC protocol's approved group size and floor-space minimum for the current weight class.

### Patient refuses food/water or shows lethargy beyond the expected post-anesthetic window
- **Usually means:** Normal anesthetic recovery lasting longer than typical, pain not adequately controlled, or an early surgical complication.
- **First question:** How many hours post-procedure, and does the timeline exceed this patient's/species' typical recovery window?
- **Data to pull:** Anesthesia record (drugs/doses used) and time elapsed since extubation.

### Mucous membranes pale, white, or blue rather than pink
- **Usually means:** Anemia, poor perfusion, or inadequate oxygenation — an escalation-worthy finding regardless of other vitals.
- **First question:** Is this a new finding compared to the baseline check, or has it been present throughout?
- **Data to pull:** Baseline MM color at T+0, current CRT, and respiratory rate/effort.

### A medication vial's concentration doesn't match what the dose calculation assumed
- **Usually means:** A restocking error, a look-alike vial mix-up, or a calculation done against the wrong concentration.
- **First question:** Does the vial label's concentration match what the treatment sheet's mg/mL assumption was based on?
- **Data to pull:** Vial label, treatment-sheet order, and the dose calculation's source concentration.
