# Veterinary Assistant — Playbook

## Restraint-technique selection table

| Species | Standard technique | Escalate to chemical restraint when |
|---|---|---|
| Cat | Scruff + body wrap (towel burrito) for exams/injections; scruff alone for brief handling | Struggling continues after 2 attempts at repositioning, or handler safety is at risk (claws/teeth engaged) |
| Dog | Muzzle + standing/lateral restraint; towel restraint for small breeds | Muzzle refused/unsafe to apply, or aggression escalates despite calm approach |
| Cattle | Chute or halter + head restraint | Procedure requires full immobility (e.g., surgery) beyond what physical restraint provides |
| Horse | Halter + lead, twitch for brief painful procedures | Twitch response inadequate, or procedure duration exceeds twitch tolerance (~10-15 min) |
| Small rodent (mouse/rat) | Tail-base + scruff restraint | Rarely needed; anesthesia is standard for any procedure beyond brief handling |

Rule: escalate restraint intensity only after a documented failed attempt at the lower level — do not skip straight to chemical restraint or maximum physical hold on a first encounter with an unfamiliar-but-calm patient.

## Post-procedure TPR/CRT monitoring flowsheet (filled example)

Patient: canine, post-anesthetic recovery. Check interval: every 30 min x 2 hr per standing order.

| Time | Temp (°F) | HR (bpm) | RR (/min) | CRT (sec) | MM color | Trend flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| T+0 | 99.8 | 88 | 16 | <2 | Pink | Baseline |
| T+30 | 99.2 | 132 | 24 | <2 | Pink | HR ↑50%, temp ↓0.6°F — 1 parameter adverse |
| T+60 | 98.6 | 156 | 32 | 3 | Pale pink | HR ↑77% from baseline, temp ↓1.2°F, CRT prolonged — 3 parameters adverse across 2 checks → **escalate now** |

Escalation rule: two or more parameters trending adverse across two consecutive checks (not just one abnormal reading) triggers immediate escalation, regardless of scheduled interval.

## Six-rights medication-administration check (filled example)

Order on treatment sheet: "Cefazolin 22 mg/kg IV, patient #4417, 0800 and 1600."

| Right | Check performed | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Patient | Cage card #4417 matches chart name/photo | Match confirmed |
| Drug | Vial label reads Cefazolin, concentration 100 mg/mL | Match confirmed |
| Dose | Patient weight 28 kg x 22 mg/kg = 616 mg = 6.16 mL | Calculated, double-checked against chart weight (28.2 kg logged yesterday — used current weight, not chart default) |
| Route | Order specifies IV; catheter patent and flushed | Confirmed before administration |
| Time | Treatment sheet shows no 0800 dose logged yet | Confirmed not already given |
| Documentation | Logged immediately after administration: time, dose, route, initials | Completed same time as administration, not end of shift |

## Laboratory-animal cage-density check (filled example)

Protocol approved for 5 adult mice per cage, standard shoebox cage (~75 in² floor space, meeting the Guide's ≥15 in²/mouse for mice 15-25g).

| Check | Value | Compliant? |
|---|---|---|
| Approved group size | 5 mice/cage | — |
| Current cage count | 7 mice (2 litters combined after weaning) | Exceeds approved count |
| Current animal weight | 22g average (past juvenile stage) | Requires full adult floor-space allowance |
| Required floor space | 7 x 15 in² = 105 in² | Cage provides only 75 in² — **non-compliant** |

Action: split into two cages per protocol before next husbandry round; do not wait for scheduled protocol review — floor-space compliance is checked at every husbandry round, not just at renewal.
