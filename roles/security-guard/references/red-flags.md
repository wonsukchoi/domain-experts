# Red flags — what a security guard notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Format per flag: signal → usually means → first question → data to pull.

### Concealment observed but the subject leaves your sightline for more than a few seconds before reaching the exit

- **Usually means:** the continuous-observation chain required for a lawful shopkeeper's-privilege stop is broken — the strongest single reason a clean-looking theft stop later becomes a false-imprisonment claim.
- **First question:** "Did anyone else maintain unbroken observation during that gap?"
- **Data to pull:** camera coverage map for that specific blind spot, any other employee's line of sight during the gap, exact duration of the gap from timestamped footage.

### A detention has run past 20–30 minutes with no police contact yet

- **Usually means:** either police response is genuinely delayed (call again, document the call time) or the "detention" has drifted into something the guard's authority doesn't cover anymore.
- **First question:** "What time did I actually place the call, and has it been confirmed received?"
- **Data to pull:** dispatch call log/timestamp, the exact time physical contact or the request to remain began.

### A subject's account of an injury or a claim of a search doesn't match what the report documents

- **Usually means:** a search occurred that wasn't authorized (no consent obtained) or force was used beyond what was reported at the time — this is exactly the discrepancy a plaintiff's attorney looks for first.
- **First question:** "Did I get explicit consent before touching any bag or pocket, and is that consent documented?"
- **Data to pull:** body-worn or fixed camera footage of the entire contact, any witness statement taken at the scene.

### Same access-control alert (forced door, denied badge) recurring at the same location more than twice in a week

- **Usually means:** a configuration gap (wrong door group, badge not deactivated) rather than repeated intrusion attempts — but only the log across multiple shifts reveals the pattern.
- **First question:** "Has this exact alert, same door, happened on other shifts this week?"
- **Data to pull:** access-control system log filtered by door ID for the past 7–14 days, HR termination list cross-checked against active badges for that door group.

### A fire watch patrol log has a gap longer than the AHJ-set interval

- **Usually means:** either the patrol was missed or it wasn't logged — both are a life-safety compliance failure regardless of which, and both need a documented explanation, not a silent backfill.
- **First question:** "What was happening during that gap that prevented the patrol or the log entry?"
- **Data to pull:** the impairment notice specifying the required interval, the actual patrol log with timestamps, any radio traffic during the gap.

### A subject is unusually calm and immediately compliant during what was reported as a violent incident

- **Usually means:** could be genuine non-involvement, or could be someone experienced enough with the process to know calm compliance reads as credible — this alone is not evidence either way.
- **First question:** ask for a detailed sequence of events rather than accepting a short denial — detail, not demeanor, is where inconsistency surfaces.
- **Data to pull:** any camera footage of the actual incident, prior incident history for the same subject at that site.

### Report draft uses "aggressive," "combative," or "irate" with no specific act in the same sentence

- **Usually means:** the narrative is being reconstructed from overall impression rather than the specific sequence — exactly what a claims adjuster or attorney will target first.
- **First question:** "What did I actually see or hear, in order, that made me use that word?"
- **Data to pull:** any available footage, reviewed before finalizing the report, to reconstruct the specific sequence.

### A post order instruction conflicts with what the situation on the ground seems to require

- **Usually means:** either the post order is stale (client's practice changed and the document wasn't updated) or the situation is being misread — either way, improvising without flagging it removes the guard's own liability protection.
- **First question:** "Is this genuinely outside what the post order covers, or does it just feel that way under pressure?"
- **Data to pull:** the current signed post order, the date it was last updated, prior incident reports at the same post referencing the same conflict.

### Force was used and the subject sustained a visible injury

- **Usually means:** documentation and evidence preservation now determine the entire outcome of any later claim — the guard's own account is worth far less without corroboration.
- **First question:** "Is there camera coverage of the entire sequence, not just the moment of contact?"
- **Data to pull:** full camera footage from before contact through after, photographs of any injury to both parties, names and statements of any witnesses before they leave.
