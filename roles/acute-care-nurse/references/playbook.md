## Early warning score trending worksheet

Fill throughout the shift for any patient showing a new or worsening finding.

| Time | Temp | HR | RR | BP | SpO2 | NEWS2 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8am | 99.8°F | 92 | 18 | 118/76 | 97% | 2 | Baseline for shift |
| 12pm | 100.6°F | 104 | 20 | 112/72 | 96% | 3 | Rising |
| 4pm | 101.4°F | 116 | 22 | 106/68 | 95% | 5 | Escalated to surgical team |

**Rule:** compare each new reading against the trend, not against "normal" in isolation — a score climbing from 2 to 5 over 8 hours is a different clinical picture than a single reading of 5 with no trend context.

## Post-operative recovery timeline reference (illustrative — verify against the specific procedure/service line's actual guideline)

| Time post-op | Expected findings | Concerning if instead |
|---|---|---|
| 0-24h | Mild fever, moderate pain, expected | Fever >102°F, uncontrolled pain |
| 24-36h | Fever resolving, pain decreasing | Fever still climbing, pain increasing |
| 36-72h | Vitals trending to baseline | New tachycardia, wound changes, rising WBC |
| 72h+ | Near-baseline vitals, mobilizing | Any new deterioration this late — atypical |

**Rule:** the same vital sign reading means something different depending on where the patient is in their specific expected recovery timeline — always check the time point, not just the value.

## Multi-patient re-triage guide

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| No new acuity signals across assignment | Continue scheduled task order |
| One patient's trend/NEWS2 rising | Reprioritize to assess that patient next, inform charge nurse of the shift in plan |
| Two or more patients showing concerning signals simultaneously | Triage by acuity/trend severity first; request additional support if needed |
| A scheduled task (e.g., routine med pass) conflicts with an urgent reassessment | Delay the routine task briefly and document the reason, rather than delaying the urgent reassessment |

**Rule:** re-triage the whole assignment continuously against real-time acuity signals — the original schedule is a starting plan, not a fixed commitment regardless of what changes during the shift.
