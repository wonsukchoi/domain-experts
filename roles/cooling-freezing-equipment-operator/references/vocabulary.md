# Vocabulary

### Latent heat (freezing)
The heat that must be removed during the phase change from water to ice at a constant temperature, distinct from sensible heat removal, which changes temperature.
**In use:** "That plateau is latent heat removal — the product's not cooling temperature-wise right now, it's changing phase."
**Common misuse:** Assuming freezing time is governed mainly by sensible cooling, missing that latent heat removal during the phase-change plateau typically dominates total freezing time.

### Thermal center (core temperature)
The point within a product, typically the geometric center of its largest dimension, that is the last to reach target temperature during freezing, used as the verification point for freezing completeness.
**In use:** "Surface probe reads well below freezing, but the thermal center's still the one that has to hit target before this counts as frozen."
**Common misuse:** Verifying freezing completion via surface temperature rather than the actual thermal center, which lags significantly behind the surface.

### Freezing curve (temperature plateau)
The characteristic temperature-vs-time profile during freezing, showing an extended plateau near the freezing point as latent heat is removed, before temperature continues dropping toward the target frozen temperature.
**In use:** "This freezing curve's plateau matches what we'd expect for this product's water content — nothing wrong with the equipment."
**Common misuse:** Interpreting a temperature plateau during freezing as an equipment malfunction rather than the expected latent-heat removal phase.

### Plank's equation (freezing time estimation)
A foundational, simplified equation relating freezing time to product thickness, thermal properties, and freezing conditions, commonly showing freezing time scaling with the square of characteristic thickness for larger items.
**In use:** "Ran the Plank's-equation-based estimate for the new thickness — that's what the 360-minute figure comes from, not a doubled guess."
**Common misuse:** Assuming freezing time scales linearly with product thickness or size, rather than via the disproportionate, roughly squared relationship that governs conduction-dominated freezing.

### Residence time (continuous freezer)
The actual time a product spends within a continuous freezer, determined by belt speed and freezer length, which must match the calculated freezing time requirement.
**In use:** "Belt speed's up for throughput, but check residence time still covers the required freezing time for this product."
**Common misuse:** Increasing belt speed to raise throughput without verifying the resulting residence time still meets the freezing time the product actually requires.

### Cold chain
The continuous temperature-controlled process from initial processing/freezing through storage, transport, and distribution, within which time-temperature limits apply for food safety.
**In use:** "Cold chain compliance covers the whole path, not just whether the freezer itself ran correctly."
**Common misuse:** Treating cold chain compliance as satisfied by the freezing step alone, without tracking cumulative time-temperature exposure across the full chain.

### Danger zone (food safety, freezing context)
The temperature range in which bacterial growth accelerates, relevant to the time a product spends transitioning through it during freezing or thawing, with defined cumulative time limits.
**In use:** "Track danger-zone time on the way into the freezer too — this isn't just a hot-holding concept."
**Common misuse:** Assuming danger-zone time limits only apply to cooking or hot-holding processes, missing that freezing processes also have a transition period through this range that must be tracked.

### Loading density (freezer)
The amount of product loaded per unit area or volume in a freezer, which affects airflow and heat removal around individual product units.
**In use:** "Loading density's up this run — check whether airflow around each unit still matches what the freezing time calc assumed."
**Common misuse:** Increasing loading density to process more product per batch without reassessing whether airflow/heat removal per unit still matches the assumptions the freezing time calculation was based on.

### IQF (Individually Quick Frozen)
A freezing method designed to freeze individual product units rapidly and separately, rather than in a mass block, typically requiring specific size, spacing, and airflow conditions to achieve.
**In use:** "This needs true IQF spacing — crowding the units together defeats the individual-freezing part of the process."
**Common misuse:** Assuming any fast freezing process qualifies as IQF, when it specifically requires conditions preventing product units from freezing together or freezing too slowly due to inadequate individual exposure.

### Belt speed (continuous freezer)
The rate at which a conveyor moves product through a continuous freezer, the primary control variable determining residence time.
**In use:** "Belt speed's the lever here — speeding it up for throughput directly cuts the residence time the product actually gets."
**Common misuse:** Treating belt speed purely as a throughput/production-rate lever without recognizing it directly and inversely determines residence time, which must satisfy the freezing time requirement.
