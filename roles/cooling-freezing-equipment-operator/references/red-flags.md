# Red flags

### Freezing completion verified by surface temperature or elapsed time rather than actual core/thermal-center temperature
- **Usually means:** the product's thermal center may remain above target frozen temperature despite the surface or freezer air appearing fully cold.
- **First question:** what does an actual core-temperature probe measurement show against target?
- **Data to pull:** core temperature measurement compared against target specification, for a representative sample.

### A temperature plateau observed during a product's freezing cycle treated as an equipment malfunction rather than expected latent-heat removal
- **Usually means:** unnecessary troubleshooting or downtime investigating normal freezing physics.
- **First question:** does the plateau occur at a temperature and duration consistent with this product's expected latent heat removal phase?
- **Data to pull:** temperature-vs-time freezing curve for this product compared against expected latent-heat plateau characteristics.

### Belt speed/throughput increased without verifying resulting residence time still meets the calculated freezing time requirement
- **Usually means:** product may exit under-frozen even though overall production rate looks improved.
- **First question:** what does the new residence time at increased belt speed show against the calculated required freezing time?
- **Data to pull:** new residence time at increased belt speed compared against the calculated required freezing time for this product.

### A larger/thicker product variant introduced with residence time increased only linearly rather than via the thickness-squared relationship
- **Usually means:** significant underestimation of required freezing time, risking a substantially under-frozen product.
- **First question:** what does the recalculated (non-linear) freezing time requirement show against the residence time actually set?
- **Data to pull:** new product thickness compared against original, and the recalculated freezing time requirement against the residence time actually set.

### Cumulative time-temperature danger-zone exposure not tracked/verified independently of freezing-rate or product quality checks
- **Usually means:** a food-safety compliance issue could exist even if the final frozen product otherwise seems fine.
- **First question:** what does the cumulative danger-zone time log show against the food-safety time limit for this product/process?
- **Data to pull:** cumulative danger-zone time log compared against the food-safety time limit for this product/process.

### A recurring under-freezing or quality complaint pattern for a specific product/process appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — residence time, product size change, freezer air temperature, loading density — rather than isolated one-off run variation.
- **First question:** does the complaint/defect log correlate with a specific process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** complaint/defect occurrence log compared against process parameter logs (residence time, product size, freezer conditions) over the same period.

### Freezer loading density increased without reassessing whether airflow/heat removal per unit product still meets the freezing time calculation's assumptions
- **Usually means:** actual freezing time may be longer than calculated if increased loading density reduces effective air circulation around individual product units.
- **First question:** what does current loading density show against the density assumed in the original freezing time calculation?
- **Data to pull:** current loading density compared against the density assumed in the original freezing time calculation.
