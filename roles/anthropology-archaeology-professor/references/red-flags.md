# Red flags

Smell tests a senior anthropology/archaeology professor catches in the first pass over a dossier, a program review packet, a grant proposal, or a fieldwork plan.

### Program review packet citing only the majors count (e.g., "38 → 11 over 9 years") with no SCH data attached

- **Usually means:** the reviewing committee built its case off enrollment/majors data alone, without the department's cost-per-SCH figure — a gap the department needs to fill, not accept.
- **First question:** what is the department's current cost-per-SCH, and how does it compare to the institution's own policy threshold?
- **Data to pull:** registrar/IR office's SCH-by-course report for the past 3-5 years; the institution's written program-viability policy and its stated threshold.

### Fieldwork involving probable human remains, funerary objects, or human-subjects interviews begun before NAGPRA consultation or IRB approval

- **Usually means:** the PI treated compliance review as a scheduling formality rather than a substantive gate, often under field-season time pressure.
- **First question:** has fieldwork actually started at this location, or is this still pre-approval planning?
- **Data to pull:** IRB protocol number and approval date, or documented SHPO/THPO/Tribal consultation correspondence with dates.

### Tenure dossier self-statement claims subfield-leading standing that fewer than half the external letters corroborate, or letters read as notably hedged ("solid," "promising," "developing")

- **Usually means:** the self-statement was drafted before the letters arrived and never reconciled against what reviewers actually said.
- **First question:** does the self-statement's framing match the letters' language, term for term, or does it overstate relative to them?
- **Data to pull:** all solicited letters side by side with the self-statement's claims about standing.

### Grant proposal submitted to a single funder within weeks of a hard fieldwork-season deadline, with no parallel or backup submission

- **Usually means:** the funding-rate risk (roughly 8-15% for NSF Cultural Anthropology/Archaeology and Wenner-Gren Dissertation Fieldwork Grants) wasn't modeled into the timeline.
- **First question:** if this proposal is declined, does the field season still happen, and on what budget?
- **Data to pull:** the funder's published funding rate for the relevant program cycle; the applicant's submission calendar for the past 2 years.

### Field school projected enrollment under its break-even headcount two seasons running

- **Usually means:** fixed costs (permit, faculty summer salary, insurance) are being absorbed by department discretionary funds without anyone stating that subsidy explicitly.
- **First question:** what is the break-even headcount this year, and who signed off on running below it last year?
- **Data to pull:** the field school's fixed-cost budget, per-student tuition-equivalent revenue, and enrollment for the past 2-3 seasons.

### Teaching evaluation scores compared across course types with no control for section size, time slot, or required-vs-elective status

- **Usually means:** a declining evaluation average is being read as a teaching-quality signal when it may simply reflect a shift toward larger, required, early-slot gen-ed sections.
- **First question:** what is the section-level breakdown — enrollment, time, required vs. elective — behind the aggregate number being cited?
- **Data to pull:** per-section evaluation data with enrollment, time slot, and course-requirement status attached.

### Adjunct/contingent faculty teaching more than roughly half of the department's total course sections

- **Usually means:** the department's SCH-generating capacity has quietly shifted onto non-tenure-track labor, which is both a program-review vulnerability and a labor-conditions issue.
- **First question:** what share of total sections and total SCH is adjunct-taught, and has that share grown over the past 3 years?
- **Data to pull:** the department's staffing roster by appointment type, cross-referenced against the SCH-by-course report.

### Syllabus missing required institutional boilerplate (accommodation process, academic integrity procedure, Title IX reporting statement)

- **Usually means:** the syllabus was copied forward from a prior term without checking for updated institutional language, which exposes both the instructor and the institution.
- **First question:** does this syllabus match the current template the provost's office or curriculum committee circulates each term?
- **Data to pull:** the current institutional syllabus-policy template, dated to this term.

### Grant-funded course buyout charged to a sponsored project with no corresponding teaching-load reduction filed with the department chair

- **Usually means:** the faculty member is being credited with a reduced load on the grant's books while the department still counts them at full teaching load, double-counting the effort.
- **First question:** is there a signed effort-reallocation form on file matching the buyout percentage in the grant budget?
- **Data to pull:** the grant's personnel budget/effort certification and the department's internal teaching-assignment record for the same term.

### A publication, dataset, or field-school teaching collection drawn from excavation with no documented repatriation or community-consultation plan for associated human remains or sacred objects

- **Usually means:** repatriation obligations were treated as a museum-collections issue rather than something attaching to teaching and research collections too.
- **First question:** does this collection include human remains or NAGPRA-eligible objects, and if so, has consultation with the affiliated Tribe(s) begun?
- **Data to pull:** the collection's accession/curation records and any documented Tribal consultation correspondence.
