# Red flags — what a personal financial advisor notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Format per flag: the signal → what it usually means → first question to ask → data to pull.

### Portfolio turnover exceeding 100%/year in an account billed as "buy and hold"
- **Usually means:** the account is being actively traded to generate commissions or to justify an advisory fee, or a prior advisor's tactical strategy never got reconciled with the client's stated goals.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the thesis for each trade in the last quarter — what changed that justified selling?"
- **Data to pull:** trade blotter for the last 12 months, realized short-term vs. long-term gains split, fee/commission total vs. account value.

### A withdrawal rate above 5% in year one of retirement with a 25+ year horizon and no guardrail rule attached
- **Usually means:** the plan was built to hit a spending target the client wanted, not one the portfolio can actually sustain; nobody modeled a bad first-five-years sequence.
- **First question:** "What's the plan if the market is down 20% in year two — does spending change, or does the plan just fail quietly?"
- **Data to pull:** Monte Carlo success probability and 10th-percentile ending balance, not just the median case.

### A single employer stock position exceeding 20% of net worth after vesting, with no diversification plan on file
- **Usually means:** the client (or a prior advisor) treated "it's done well" as a reason to hold rather than a reason to size down; often paired with an unexamined tax hit that's being used as an excuse not to act.
- **First question:** "If this stock were cash today, would you buy this much of it back at this price?"
- **Data to pull:** cost basis and unrealized gain, vesting schedule, insider trading window/blackout calendar, existing 10b5-1 plan if any.

### A 25%+ RMD excise exposure sitting unaddressed past the December 31 deadline
- **Usually means:** an account (often an inherited IRA or a newly RMD-eligible traditional IRA) was missed in the annual review process; SECURE 2.0 reduced the penalty to 10% if corrected within two years, but only if someone catches it.
- **First question:** "Which accounts in this household are RMD-eligible this year, and has each one's distribution actually been confirmed, not just calculated?"
- **Data to pull:** account-by-account RMD calculation and confirmed distribution date, prior-year filing for any missed-RMD correction already in process.

### Advisory fee plus underlying fund expense ratios totaling more than ~1.5% of AUM with no clear service beyond fund selection
- **Usually means:** the client is paying for a service (comprehensive planning, tax coordination, behavioral coaching) that either isn't being delivered or was never explained as the thing they're paying for.
- **First question:** "If we stripped this down to index funds and a flat planning fee, what would the client actually lose?"
- **Data to pull:** all-in fee schedule including 12b-1 fees and platform costs, a list of planning deliverables provided in the last 12 months.

### A major life event (divorce, remarriage, new dependent, death of a spouse) more than 12 months old with beneficiary designations unchanged
- **Usually means:** the plan document was updated but the actual account-level beneficiary forms weren't — beneficiary designations override the will, so this is a live estate-planning defect, not a paperwork nitpick.
- **First question:** "Have you checked the beneficiary form on this account directly with the custodian since [event], not just the estate plan?"
- **Data to pull:** current beneficiary designation on every retirement account, life insurance policy, and TOD/POD account.

### A wash-sale-eligible repurchase inside a spouse's IRA or 401(k) within 30 days of harvesting a loss in the taxable account
- **Usually means:** the tax-loss harvesting process only checked the account where the loss was realized, not every account the household owns.
- **First question:** "Did either spouse's retirement account buy anything similar to what we just sold at a loss, in the last 30 days either direction?"
- **Data to pull:** full household holdings across every taxable and tax-advantaged account, cross-referenced against the harvested security's ticker and close substitutes.

### No will, power of attorney, or healthcare directive on file for a household with net worth above ~$2M or with minor children
- **Usually means:** estate planning was deferred as "not urgent" past the point where intestacy or a court-appointed guardian becomes a real, not theoretical, risk.
- **First question:** "If something happened to both of you this month, who has legal authority over the kids and the accounts on Monday morning?"
- **Data to pull:** current estate documents and their last-reviewed date, guardian designation, state of residence (intestacy rules and document validity are state-specific).

### Cash-value life insurance recommended to a client who hasn't maxed available tax-advantaged retirement accounts
- **Usually means:** an insurable need got matched to the highest-commission product before the lower-cost solution (term life plus continued 401(k)/IRA contributions) was ruled out.
- **First question:** "What's the specific insurable need this solves that term coverage plus maxed retirement contributions doesn't?"
- **Data to pull:** current term coverage in force and its expiration, current retirement account contribution rate vs. the annual limit, illustrated policy's internal rate of return net of fees over 10/20/30 years.

### A lump-sum "sudden money" event (inheritance, business sale, settlement) invested in a single transaction within days of receipt
- **Usually means:** the advisor prioritized capturing the assets before a competitor could over giving the client time to build an emergency reserve and a considered allocation; also skips the step of confirming the funds aren't needed for a known near-term liability (taxes owed on the event itself, for one).
- **First question:** "What does this household need in the next 12 months from this money before any of it goes into the market?"
- **Data to pull:** tax liability estimate on the windfall itself, existing emergency fund balance, any near-term known expenses.

### Stated risk tolerance and actual portfolio allocation diverging by more than one risk band on the same questionnaire
- **Usually means:** the allocation was set once, years ago, and never revisited as the client's actual capacity for risk changed (new pension, job loss, approaching retirement), or the questionnaire was never actually re-administered.
- **First question:** "When was this client's risk tolerance last re-assessed, and does the current allocation still match it — and match their actual capacity, not just their comfort?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent risk questionnaire result and date, current allocation, a capacity assessment (time horizon, income stability, other assets).

### An account converted from commission-based to fee-based advisory ("reverse churning") for a client whose trading activity would clearly cost less under the prior structure
- **Usually means:** the conversion optimized the advisor's revenue predictability over the client's actual cost, since a low-activity account often costs the client more under an ongoing AUM fee than it would have under occasional commissions.
- **First question:** "Model this account's cost over the last 3 years under both fee structures — which one would the client actually have paid less under?"
- **Data to pull:** trade frequency for the prior 3 years, fee paid under each structure side by side.
