# Interior Designer — Red Flags

### Furniture layout finalized before ADA clearance check run
- **Usually means:** clearances were assumed adequate by eye rather than measured against the 30"×48" clear-floor-space and 60" turning-radius tables.
- **First question:** "Where's the dimensioned clearance check for the accessible route and every fixed-approach point?"
- **Data to pull:** the block plan with clearance dimensions called out, cross-referenced to ICC A117.1 section numbers.

### Finish selection exceeds the per-square-foot allowance by more than 10% at the room level
- **Usually means:** a finish was chosen for appearance before its extended cost was checked against the client's stated allowance for that room.
- **First question:** "What's the lower-cost alternative already priced for this selection?"
- **Data to pull:** the finish schedule's extended-cost column against the room-by-room allowance breakdown.

### Furniture/finish item with 8+ week lead time not flagged until purchase-order stage
- **Usually means:** the concept-approval review didn't separate long-lead items from stock items, or the lead time wasn't confirmed with the vendor before specification.
- **First question:** "When was this lead time confirmed, and was it flagged at concept approval?"
- **Data to pull:** the vendor quote/lead-time confirmation date against the concept-approval date on the project timeline.

### Lighting plan finalized from fixture appearance without a footcandle calculation
- **Usually means:** fixtures were selected in a showroom or catalog review and never checked against the task-specific footcandle target for the zone.
- **First question:** "What's the calculated footcandle level at the task surface, not the room average?"
- **Data to pull:** the photometric calculation output by zone, compared against the task-specific target table.

### Commercial finish specified at residential wear-rating
- **Usually means:** a sample selected for appearance in a low-traffic showroom setting was specified without checking its wear class against the actual traffic pattern of the space.
- **First question:** "What's the wear rating on this spec, and what's the projected foot-traffic or use-cycle for this zone?"
- **Data to pull:** the manufacturer's wear-class or double-rub rating against the space's occupancy/traffic classification.

### Wall-mounted control or fixture height specified from manufacturer standard without site verification
- **Usually means:** the standard mounting height on a cut sheet was assumed compliant without checking it against the specific project's reach-range requirement (forward vs. side approach changes the allowable range).
- **First question:** "Is this a forward or side approach at this location, and does the mounting height fall inside that specific reach-range table?"
- **Data to pull:** the shop drawing's dimensioned mounting height against the applicable forward/side reach-range table.

### Finish schedule and furniture plan not cross-checked against each other before issue-for-construction
- **Usually means:** the two documents were developed by different team members or at different phases and never reconciled against a single current floor plan.
- **First question:** "When was the finish schedule last checked against the current furniture plan revision?"
- **Data to pull:** the revision dates on both documents, confirming they reference the same plan version.

### Punch-list conducted for aesthetic fit and finish quality only, no accessible-route re-check
- **Usually means:** the punch-list checklist doesn't include a clearance/accessible-route line item, treating it as a one-time check completed at design approval rather than a final-condition verification.
- **First question:** "Has the turning radius and clear-floor space been re-measured as-built, not just as-designed?"
- **Data to pull:** the punch-list checklist itself, confirming an accessible-route line item with an as-built measurement.

### Budget contingency line item missing or under 10% on a project with any custom millwork
- **Usually means:** the schedule was priced optimistically without accounting for field-verification dimension changes or vendor-quote fluctuation on custom items.
- **First question:** "What's the contingency percentage, and does it scale with the proportion of custom vs. stock items in the schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** the contingency line item's percentage against the ratio of custom-fabricated to off-the-shelf items in the schedule.
