# Red flags

Smell tests a veteran prevention specialist checks in the first ten minutes of an inspection, permit review, or cause investigation.

### ERC sitting within 2 points of a tier boundary with no wetting system in the 5-day forecast

- **Usually means:** the district is about to cross into the next precaution tier regardless of today's technical compliance, and operators planning around "today's rule" are about to be caught by a mid-week shutdown.
- **First question:** "What does the 3-5 day forecast show, and is there any frontal system bringing sustained rain, or is this a dry ridge holding?"
- **Data to pull:** 5-day ERC trend line for the station, NWS extended forecast for the zone, any Predictive Services outlook for the GACC.

### Spark arrester is physically present but the exhaust configuration doesn't match the listed unit

- **Usually means:** an aftermarket muffler or removed baffle that voids the tested spark-arrester certification even though a screen is bolted somewhere in the exhaust path.
- **First question:** "Can you show me the make/model tag, and does the exhaust match the Spark Arrester Guide listing for this engine?"
- **Data to pull:** current NWCG/USFS Spark Arrester Guide listing for the equipment's engine model, physical exhaust photos, maintenance log for any exhaust work.

### Witness says the fire "started right where the discarded cigarette/campfire was" but the burn pattern points uphill and upwind of that location

- **Usually means:** witness memory is anchored to where the fire was first noticed, not where it started — smoke and flame visibility, not ignition point, drove what the witness actually saw.
- **First question:** "What was the wind direction and slope aspect at the reported time, and does that match the direction of the V-pattern from the claimed origin?"
- **Data to pull:** contemporaneous wind data for the exact time window (not the daily average), slope/aspect map of the reported origin, photographs of char/ash indicators at and around the claimed point.

### A permit applicant requests a burn window that starts before the prior day's Red Flag Warning has formally lifted

- **Usually means:** either the applicant is watching the calendar instead of the actual bulletin, or is aware conditions are marginal and is testing whether the office will catch it.
- **First question:** "Has the Red Flag Warning for this zone officially expired, and what's the RH/wind forecast for the requested window specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** current NWS Red Flag Warning bulletin and expiration time, hourly forecast for the requested burn window.

### Cause investigation converges on "human-caused, incendiary" primarily because no accidental source was found near the origin

- **Usually means:** negative-corpus reasoning — absence of an identified accidental cause is being treated as evidence of intentional cause, which is not a valid inference and will not survive challenge.
- **First question:** "What specific physical evidence points to incendiary cause, independent of the fact that we couldn't find an accidental one?"
- **Data to pull:** full origin-area evidence log, any incendiary device or accelerant indicators actually recovered, comparison against known accidental-cause patterns for the fuel type and terrain.

### District's citation count is rising but the human-caused ignition rate isn't falling year over year

- **Usually means:** enforcement is hitting easy, visible violations (roadside debris burns, uncovered campfires) while the actual ignition sources — equipment sparks on industrial operations, vehicle-related ignitions on remote roads — are going uninspected because they're harder to reach.
- **First question:** "Break down this year's ignitions by cause category and location type — does it match where patrol and citation effort has actually gone?"
- **Data to pull:** ignition cause/location breakdown for the season, patrol-hour allocation by area, citation log by violation type.

### Defensible-space inspection passes on a quick walk of the immediate zone (0-5 ft) without checking the extended zone (30-100+ ft)

- **Usually means:** the inspection stopped at the easiest zone to assess visually (noncombustible material near the structure) and skipped the zone most predictive of ember-cast ignition and crown-fire exposure.
- **First question:** "What's the canopy spacing and understory fuel load 50-80 feet out, on the uphill/prevailing-wind side specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** three-zone assessment checklist per NFPA 1144, aerial or ground photos of the extended zone, slope/aspect relative to the structure.

### Industrial operator requests a variance to run cable yarding during IFPL III daytime hours "because the tower's already rigged"

- **Usually means:** a sunk-cost argument being used to push a schedule that the current tier doesn't permit — rigging cost is real but doesn't change the fire-behavior risk the hours restriction exists to manage.
- **First question:** "What does the current tier's posted hours restriction say, and is there a written variance process, or is this a request to waive the rule informally?"
- **Data to pull:** current IFPL tier and posted hours restriction for the operation type, any prior variance history for this operator, gust forecast for the requested window.

### A prescribed-fire unit's burn plan trigger points are less conservative than the general Red Flag Warning threshold for the zone

- **Usually means:** the plan was written for a different set of typical conditions (often adapted from another unit or an older plan) and hasn't been re-checked against current NWS criteria for this specific zone.
- **First question:** "When was this burn plan's weather prescription last validated against the current zone's Red Flag criteria, and who signed off?"
- **Data to pull:** the burn plan's weather-trigger table, current NWS Red Flag criteria for the zone, plan revision date and approving official.
