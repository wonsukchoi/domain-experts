# Red flags — what a program administrator notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### Completion rate more than ~15-20 percentage points below enrollment/target, with no exit-cause data collected
- **Usually means:** a real attrition problem exists but its actual cause is unknown, risking a misdiagnosed and ineffective fix.
- **First question:** "Do we have exit survey or interview data on why non-completers actually left, broken down by cause?"
- **Data to pull:** completion rate trend, exit survey/interview data with cause categories, if any exists.

### A proposed fix (tightening admissions, adding academic remediation) that targets academic causes when exit data shows academic difficulty is a minority of total attrition
- **Usually means:** the intervention is being aimed at the most visible or most institutionally comfortable cause rather than the actual documented driver of attrition.
- **First question:** "What percentage of documented non-completion causes does this proposed fix actually address?"
- **Data to pull:** exit-cause breakdown by category, the proposed intervention's target cause and its share of total attrition.

### A program's outcome/value claim (job placement rate, credential recognition) that hasn't been validated with current data in the last 1-2 years
- **Usually means:** the claim may be based on historical performance that no longer reflects the current job market or employer recognition of the credential.
- **First question:** "When was this outcome claim last validated with current placement/wage data, and does it still hold?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent placement/outcome data, date of last validation, any changes in the relevant industry since.

### A program schedule built entirely around traditional weekday daytime hours for a population primarily composed of working adults
- **Usually means:** the schedule may structurally exclude or disadvantage a large share of the intended population, showing up later as attrition rather than as a visible access barrier.
- **First question:** "What share of our target population's likely work schedules conflict with our current class times?"
- **Data to pull:** current schedule format and options, participant work-schedule data or exit-survey responses citing scheduling conflict.

### No employer/industry engagement or curriculum review in the past 12-18 months for a program tied to a specific job/skill outcome
- **Usually means:** the curriculum may be drifting from current employer need, producing a credential with declining real market value even if the content is internally coherent.
- **First question:** "When did we last validate this curriculum directly with employers in the field, and has anything material changed in the field since?"
- **Data to pull:** date and content of last employer engagement/advisory review, any industry/technology changes since.

### Support service budget (advising, financial aid navigation, childcare/transportation support) flat or declining while completion rates lag target
- **Usually means:** practical barriers, which disproportionately affect this population's completion, may be under-resourced relative to their actual effect on outcomes.
- **First question:** "What share of documented non-completion causes are practical/logistical, and does the support-service budget reflect that share?"
- **Data to pull:** support service budget trend, exit-cause breakdown showing logistical/financial share of attrition.

### A generic, one-size-fits-all retention program applied without first diagnosing the specific attrition cause for this population
- **Usually means:** the intervention may not address the actual driver of dropout for this specific program's population, wasting resources on the wrong lever.
- **First question:** "What does our own exit data say is the top cause of attrition here, and does this specific intervention target that cause?"
- **Data to pull:** program-specific exit-cause data, the generic intervention's actual mechanism and intended target cause.

### Enrollment marketing materials emphasizing outcome claims not matched by the program's own completion or placement data
- **Usually means:** prospective participants may be making a cost-benefit decision based on a claim the program's own data doesn't currently support.
- **First question:** "Does our current completion/placement data actually support the outcome claim in this marketing material?"
- **Data to pull:** current marketing/recruitment materials, actual completion and placement data for recent cohorts.
