# Red flags

### Callback rate under 5% across 20+ submitted self-tapes
- **Usually means:** materials (reel/headshot) no longer match what the actor is actually being seen for, or a recurring technical problem with the tapes themselves
- **First question:** "when did I last update my reel and headshots to match what I'm actually getting submitted for?"
- **Data to pull:** the last 20 submission notes against roles booked in the past 12 months; audio/lighting spot-check on the last 5 tapes

### Booking rate is healthy but no reel-quality footage after 12 months
- **Usually means:** the actor is only landing background or under-5-line roles that don't generate usable footage
- **First question:** "of the last 10 bookings, how many produced footage I could actually put on a reel?"
- **Data to pull:** booking log with role tier and line count per job

### Agent or manager hasn't submitted in 6+ weeks with no explanation
- **Usually means:** materials are stale in the rep's eyes, or the actor has quietly been deprioritized after a missed self-tape deadline or unreliable behavior on a past job
- **First question:** "when did we last sit down and update my materials together?"
- **Data to pull:** the rep's submission log for the period, compared to submission volume for actors of similar type at the same agency

### Same casting director books once, never recasts, across 3+ years of submissions
- **Usually means:** the performance was competent but unmemorable — it solved that one casting problem without leaving an impression that solves the next one
- **First question:** "what specifically did that CD's notes say, and did I ever get to see them?"
- **Data to pull:** callback/booking notes if the rep kept them; the specific credit and its billing tier

### Commission stack exceeds ~25–28% of gross on a single deal
- **Usually means:** an unusual manager fee, a packaging fee, or a double-dip where both agent and manager are billing on the same gross without a clear split agreement
- **First question:** "what exactly is each party taking, and on what gross number — before or after taxes withheld at source?"
- **Data to pull:** the deal memo and both commission agreements side by side

### Verbal offer with no deal memo 48+ hours before the start date
- **Usually means:** the production's financing or scheduling isn't as locked as the offer implied
- **First question:** "has a wardrobe fitting or hair/makeup test actually been scheduled yet?" — real bookings generate logistics fast
- **Data to pull:** production coordinator contact and confirmed call sheet, not just the casting director's verbal

### Director repeats the identical note ("bigger," "more real") across 3+ takes
- **Usually means:** the actor is playing a result at a fixed ceiling rather than raising the actual stakes of the obstacle underneath it
- **First question:** "what specifically changes for the character if this doesn't land — have I actually raised that, or just the volume?"
- **Data to pull:** the take log; compare what tactic changed take-to-take, if anything did

### Same-day tape rejection before the role is even fully cast
- **Usually means:** a technical disqualification — wrong file name, missing room tone, bad audio — not a judgment on the performance
- **First question:** "did the file follow the exact naming and framing spec in the breakdown?"
- **Data to pull:** the original casting breakdown's technical specs, checked line by line against the submitted file

### Cast only within one narrow type for 3+ years with no rep effort to break the pattern
- **Usually means:** the type is genuinely paying the bills and reps have no incentive to risk it, or reps have quietly filed the actor as a one-note booking
- **First question:** "is this type actually building toward something, or is it just the path of least resistance for my reps?"
- **Data to pull:** annual income by role type over the period, and how many submissions outside that type were ever sent

### Chronic lateness or missed calls converting callbacks into no-offers despite strong reads
- **Usually means:** a reliability flag has entered the casting office's private notes and is now outweighing the read itself
- **First question:** "has any casting associate or AD mentioned timing or professionalism directly, even in passing?"
- **Data to pull:** call sheet arrival times from the last few bookings; any informal notes passed back through the rep
