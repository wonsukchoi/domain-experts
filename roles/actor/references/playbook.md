# Playbook

Filled templates an actor executes directly — not descriptions of them.

## Scene breakdown worksheet (filled example)

Scene: two-page dialogue scene, self-tape audition for a co-star role, sides only (no full script provided).

| Field | Answer |
|---|---|
| Who am I | Younger sister, early 30s, has been financially bailed out by the other character twice before |
| Where/when | Sibling's kitchen, the morning after a family funeral |
| Relationship | Loves him, resents needing him, has never said either out loud |
| What just happened | She found out he's been paying their mother's rent for two years without telling her |
| Want (this scene) | Get him to admit he did it to make her look bad, not to help |
| Obstacle | He genuinely didn't do it for that reason and won't take the bait |
| Tactic, beat 1 | To needle him |
| Tactic, beat 2 (after his flat denial) | To shame him |
| Tactic, beat 3 (after he says her name gently) | To retreat / to disarm herself |
| One invented private detail (not in text, stays inside given circumstances) | She rehearsed this exact confrontation in the car before coming in |

Rule of thumb: if the "want" line can't be written as one sentence with a verb aimed at the other person, the breakdown isn't done — go back to given circumstances, not to line readings.

## Self-tape technical checklist (with thresholds)

1. **Frame**: medium close-up, eyeline just off-lens, headroom consistent with the breakdown's stated preference (network dramas typically want tighter than indie features).
2. **Slate order**: name → height → role/project name → "take one." Casting directors read energy off the slate itself — treat it as the first beat of the read, not paperwork.
3. **Audio**: no background hum audible above -50dB in a quiet room; if in doubt, record 10 seconds of room tone separately and label it `roomtone_[lastname]_[project].mov`.
4. **File naming convention**: `LastName_FirstName_RoleName_ProjectName.mp4` — anything else risks the file being unopened in a folder of 200 identical-looking submissions.
5. **Turnaround**: 24–48 hours from receiving sides to uploaded tape is the industry-standard expectation for self-tape auditions; if the deadline is tighter, confirm receipt with the reader/casting associate rather than guessing.
6. **Reader**: use a real off-camera reader who can take direction on pacing — never read both parts, and never use a monotone placeholder reader for a scene with real stakes.

## Booking-conflict decision table

When two offers land on overlapping dates, score each on the same four axes before calling reps back:

| Axis | Network Co-Star | ULB lead |
|---|---|---|
| Net pay this cycle | $848.25 | $519 |
| Credit tier (Lead > Recurring > Guest Star > Co-Star > Background) | Co-Star | Lead |
| Usable reel minutes | ~1 min | ~15 min |
| Relationship value (verifiable director track record, chance of recast) | Low (one-day set) | High (named festival history) |

**Decision rule**: if gross pay gap is under ~3x, weight credit tier and reel minutes over net pay; past ~3x, take the money for that cycle and look for the relationship value elsewhere. Document which axis actually decided it — that record is what makes the next version of this decision faster.

## Rehearsal note-to-adjustment log

Track director notes across takes so a note doesn't get silently dropped or re-litigated:

| Take | Note given | New tactic applied | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "Too much sympathy in beat 2" | Swap tactic to shame, drop the softening | Landed — director moved on |
| 2 | "Slow down the turn at 'you knew'" | Hold the discovery beat one extra count before speaking | Landed |
| 3 | "Bigger" (repeated third time) | Raise the stakes of the obstacle, not volume — obstacle becomes "if I don't get this out now I never will" | Landed; if it hadn't, next step is to ask directly what "bigger" means in the director's terms |
