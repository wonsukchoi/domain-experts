### Cohort edTPA first-submission pass rate drops below ~80%
- **Usually means:** either the state raised its cut score this cycle, or a specific task (often Instruction) is tanking across multiple candidates, pointing to a curriculum gap rather than individual weakness.
- **First question:** is the drop spread evenly across tasks, or concentrated in one — and did the state's published cut score change since last cohort?
- **Data to pull:** rubric-level score reports by task, aggregated across the whole cohort, compared to the prior two cohorts.

### Cooperating-teacher year-over-year retention in partner districts under ~60%
- **Usually means:** compensation/stipend for supervising is not competitive, or the university side (site-visit frequency, paperwork load) is making the partnership more burdensome than it's worth to the district.
- **First question:** are the departing cooperating teachers citing compensation, workload, or a specific university-side friction in exit conversations?
- **Data to pull:** the partnership MOU's stipend terms against comparable districts, and any placement-coordinator exit notes.

### Danielson midpoint observations clustering at "Basic" for the same domain across multiple candidates
- **Usually means:** the program's coursework isn't preparing candidates for that domain (commonly Domain 2, classroom environment) rather than a coincidence of individual placements.
- **First question:** do the affected candidates share a course section, a supervising faculty member, or a placement district?
- **Data to pull:** midpoint Danielson score distributions by domain, cross-tabbed against course section and supervisor.

### Praxis Core first-attempt pass rate below ~70% for an admitted cohort
- **Usually means:** the admission criteria's academic-ability screen (GPA, prior test scores) isn't correlating with Praxis readiness, or the cohort needs earlier test-prep support than currently offered.
- **First question:** is this cohort's admitted-GPA average lower than prior cohorts, or is the pass-rate drop showing up despite comparable admitted GPAs?
- **Data to pull:** admitted-cohort GPA distribution and Praxis Core subtest scores broken out by subtest (reading/writing/math).

### CAEP self-study evidence gap flagged for Standard 2 (clinical partnerships)
- **Usually means:** placement-quality documentation (cooperating-teacher qualifications, caseload data, observation-alignment records) hasn't been systematically collected, even if placements themselves are fine.
- **First question:** does the program have caseload and qualification data for every current cooperating teacher, or only for a sample?
- **Data to pull:** the clinical-partnerships office's placement roster with cooperating-teacher caseload and credential data for the current and prior academic year.

### A candidate has two or more dispositions concern reports filed in a single semester
- **Usually means:** either a genuine professionalism/ethics pattern, or a supervision mismatch generating repeated friction with one specific cooperating teacher/instructor.
- **First question:** are the reports from the same source, or independent observers describing the same behavior?
- **Data to pull:** the full dispositions log for the candidate, dated and sourced, not summarized.

### District MOU renewal stalls more than 60 days before the semester's placement start
- **Usually means:** a district-side budget, staffing, or liability-insurance issue that will not resolve in time for the planned candidate cohort.
- **First question:** is the stall on the district's legal/business-office side, or still sitting in the university's own contracts office?
- **Data to pull:** the MOU's current negotiation status and the district's stated blocking issue, in writing.

### Course syllabi haven't been remapped to InTASC/CAEP standards since before the last self-study cycle (7+ years)
- **Usually means:** courses have drifted from their standards-mapped design as instructors iterate content, leaving the accreditation evidence stale even if the teaching itself improved.
- **First question:** which courses changed instructors or major content since the last mapping, and were those changes re-mapped at the time?
- **Data to pull:** the standards-to-course crosswalk currently on file, checked against the actual current syllabi.

### Admitted-cohort average GPA trending below 3.0 for two consecutive cohorts
- **Usually means:** either admissions criteria loosened under enrollment pressure, or the applicant pool itself shifted — both matter differently for the CAEP Standard 3 response.
- **First question:** did the program change its admission GPA floor or multiple-measures policy in the same window, or is the pool composition shifting with GPA policy unchanged?
- **Data to pull:** admitted-cohort GPA distributions for the last 4 cohorts against any documented policy changes.

### An external syllabus audit (e.g., NCTQ) flags a standards-alignment gap not corroborated by internal review
- **Usually means:** the review methodology scored the syllabus document alone and missed content delivered through readings, guest sessions, or field assignments not listed on the syllabus.
- **First question:** does the reviewer's published rubric criteria match what the course actually requires the syllabus to state, and does the full course record (not just the syllabus) show the content?
- **Data to pull:** the reviewer's specific rubric item cited, against the complete course folder (readings, assignments, field-based tasks).

### edTPA targeted-retake usage above ~30% of a cohort
- **Usually means:** a systemic prep gap in the task most candidates are retaking (commonly Instruction), not scattered individual weaknesses.
- **First question:** are the retakes concentrated in the same task and the same 1-2 rubrics across candidates?
- **Data to pull:** rubric-level first-submission scores across the full cohort, not just the candidates who failed.

### A candidate's student-teaching placement subject/grade doesn't match their licensure endorsement area
- **Usually means:** a placement-scheduling shortcut taken under district capacity pressure, which risks the placement not counting toward the specific endorsement being sought.
- **First question:** does the state licensure office's endorsement requirement specify subject/grade-band matching, and does this placement meet it?
- **Data to pull:** the candidate's declared endorsement area against the placement's actual grade/subject assignment, checked against state licensure rules before the placement starts, not after.
