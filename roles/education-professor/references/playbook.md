# Playbook

Filled operational sequences for the recurring decisions in a teacher-preparation program. Populate the tables with real course/candidate data; the structures below are ready to execute as-is.

## 1. Backward-design a licensure course unit from standards

Map every graded assessment to the standard it's meant to evidence *before* writing the assignment, not after, so the artifact is citable in the next CAEP self-study.

| Unit | InTASC standard(s) | edTPA task/rubric it evidences | Graded artifact | CAEP standard it supports |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Formative assessment cycle | Standard 6 (Assessment) | Task 3, rubrics 11-13 | Candidate designs a 3-lesson assessment plan with a rubric and a sample of annotated student work | Standard 1 (Content & Pedagogical Knowledge) |
| Differentiation for language learners | Standard 2 (Learning Differences) | Task 2, rubric 7 (Engaging Students in Learning) | Candidate submits a lesson adapted for a specified EL proficiency level with a rationale memo | Standard 1 |
| Classroom routines & environment | Standard 3 (Learning Environments) | Task 2, rubric 6 | Candidate video-records and self-analyzes a 10-minute classroom-management segment against Danielson Domain 2 | Standard 2 (Clinical Partnerships & Practice) |
| Reflective practice on instructional decisions | Standard 9 (Professional Learning & Ethical Practice) | Task 2, rubric 8 (Analyzing Teaching Effectiveness) | Candidate writes a reflection citing specific student evidence for one instructional pivot | Standard 1 |

**Fallback if a course predates this mapping:** retro-map existing assignments to the nearest standard first (cheapest fix); only redesign the assignment itself if no existing artifact evidences the standard at all.

## 2. Student-teaching observation cycle (Danielson-based)

Standard placement: 12-16 weeks full-time, minimum 3 formal observations (early, mid, final) plus informal cooperating-teacher check-ins weekly.

| Checkpoint | Week | What's scored | Escalation trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early observation | Week 3-4 | Domains 2 & 3 only (environment, instruction) — establishing baseline | Any domain rated Unsatisfactory → immediate improvement-plan meeting, not wait for midpoint |
| Formative (midpoint) | Week 7-8 | All 4 domains, full Danielson instrument | 2+ domains at "Basic" → written improvement plan with a 2-3 week checkpoint |
| Improvement-plan checkpoint | Week 10 | Re-score only the flagged domain(s) | No measurable movement (same or lower rating) → placement-continuation review with program coordinator |
| Summative (final) | Week 14-15 | All 4 domains, recommendation decision | Below "Proficient" average across domains → hold licensure recommendation, route to remediation-semester decision |

**Fallback order if a placement is failing:** (1) written improvement plan with specific, observable targets — (2) added cooperating-teacher co-observation and coaching — (3) placement change to a different cooperating teacher/context if the mismatch (not the candidate) looks like the driver — (4) removal from the placement and remediation-semester plan, reserved for safety/ethics issues or no movement after step 1-2.

## 3. edTPA retake decision

| Total score vs. cut score | Task-level pattern | Recommended path |
|---|---|---|
| Within 0-2 points | One task 1-2 points below its proportional share | Targeted retake of that task only (check state permits it — roughly 20 states do as of recent SCALE state-policy summaries) |
| Within 3-5 points | One task clearly the outlier (5+ points below its 25-point share) | Targeted retake of the outlier task; identify the 2 weakest rubrics within it and rewrite with added student-evidence citations |
| 6+ points short, or two tasks both weak | No single-task fix closes the gap | Full retake; treat as a program-level flag if 2+ candidates in the same cohort show the same task-pattern — that's a curriculum gap, not an individual one |
| Any score, dispositions concern also on file | — | Do not resubmit until the dispositions review concludes — score alone doesn't clear a candidate for recommendation |

Cost/time reference: targeted retake ≈ $100, 2-3 week rescoring window. Full retake ≈ $300, 5-6 week window. Both figures vary by state/edTPA administration — confirm current fee schedule before advising a candidate.

## 4. CAEP self-study (7-year cycle) evidence timeline

| Year in cycle | Milestone | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1-2 | Standards gap analysis against current course/assessment map; flag any standard with thin or stale evidence | Program coordinator + standards committee |
| Year 3-4 | Close identified gaps (redesign assessments, renegotiate MOUs for placement-quality evidence under Standard 2) | Course instructors + clinical-partnerships office |
| Year 5 | Draft self-study report (SSR); collect completer outcome data (employment, retention, in-service evaluation ratings) for Standard 4 | Unit head + assessment office |
| Year 6 | Internal mock site-visit review; revise SSR against reviewer feedback | Full faculty |
| Year 7 | Submit SSR; host site visit; respond to any Areas for Improvement (AFIs) | Unit head |

**Fallback if a standard has a genuine gap with no time to fully close it:** document it as a Plan for continuous improvement with a specific timeline and interim data point, rather than omitting or overstating the evidence — CAEP site visitors weigh a credible improvement plan far better than a claimed-but-unevidenced strength.

## 5. Dispositions concern escalation ladder

1. **First report** (any faculty/cooperating teacher): documented in the candidate's file with the specific observed behavior, not a characterization — date, context, exact statement or action.
2. **Second report, same semester:** program coordinator convenes a review meeting with the candidate; written improvement plan with specific, observable behavior changes and a re-check date.
3. **Third report, or no movement on the improvement plan:** unit-level dispositions committee review — this is the CAEP Standard 3-required formal process, and its outcome (continue, conditional continue, program dismissal) becomes part of the candidate's permanent program file regardless of concurrent content/edTPA performance.
