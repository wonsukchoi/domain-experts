### edTPA
A subject-specific, portfolio-based performance assessment (Planning, Instruction, Assessment tasks; 15 rubrics, 1-5 scale, 75-point max) developed by Stanford's SCALE and scored by Pearson/Evaluation Systems, used by many states as a licensure requirement.
**In use:** "Her total is 39 against a cut score of 42 — before we call that a fail, check whether the state allows a targeted retake of just the Instruction task."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a pass/fail content test rather than a rubric-scored portfolio where the task-level breakdown, not just the total, determines the right remediation path.

### InTASC standards
The Interstate Teacher Assessment and Support Consortium's 10 Model Core Teaching Standards (CCSSO, 2013), organized under Learner and Learning, Content, Instructional Practice, and Professional Responsibility.
**In use:** "This assignment evidences InTASC Standard 6 — make sure the rubric language actually maps to assessment practice, not just 'good teaching' generally."
**Common misuse:** Cited as a general aspirational list rather than as the specific standard-numbered evidence a CAEP self-study will need traced to individual assignments.

### CAEP
The Council for Accreditation of Educator Preparation, the national accrediting body for most US teacher-preparation programs, operating on a 7-year cycle with a self-study report and site visit against 5 standards.
**In use:** "We can't just say the placement went well — Standard 2 needs cooperating-teacher qualification and caseload data on file, not a narrative claim."
**Common misuse:** Referred to as "the accreditation people" in the abstract, rather than by the specific standard number a piece of evidence is meant to satisfy.

### Dispositions
The professionalism, ethics, and responsiveness-to-feedback traits CAEP requires programs to formally monitor across a candidate's whole program, separate from content or performance-assessment scores.
**In use:** "Content and edTPA are both strong, but this is the second dispositions report this semester — that goes to committee regardless of her scores."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a soft, subjective add-on rather than a formally documented, program-level gate that can independently block a licensure recommendation.

### Clinical practice / clinical placement
The supervised, in-classroom teaching experience (student teaching) a candidate completes in a partner K-12 school under a cooperating teacher, typically 12-16 weeks full-time for final placements.
**In use:** "The clinical placement is the evidence base for Standard 2 — a great syllabus doesn't substitute for placement-quality data."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably with "field experience" in general, when clinical placement specifically denotes the culminating, evaluated teaching assignment rather than earlier observation-only hours.

### Cooperating teacher
The in-service K-12 teacher who hosts and directly mentors a candidate during clinical placement, operating under the partnership MOU's caseload terms.
**In use:** "She's already at her MOU caseload cap this term — we decline the placement request rather than double-book her."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to have unlimited capacity to take on candidates as a professional courtesy, ignoring the caseload limits that actually govern placement quality.

### High-leverage practices (HLPs)
A defined set of core teaching practices (e.g., eliciting student thinking, explaining and modeling content) identified by Ball & Forzani and operationalized by TeachingWorks as the practices novices most need explicit coursework and rehearsal on, rather than general pedagogical philosophy.
**In use:** "Redesign this methods course around 3-4 HLPs with rehearsal cycles, not a survey of pedagogical theories."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with "best teaching practices" generally, rather than the specific, named, rehearsal-based set the research identifies as highest-leverage for novices.

### Self-study report (SSR)
The comprehensive standards-mapped evidence document a program submits to CAEP once per 7-year accreditation cycle, ahead of the site visit.
**In use:** "We're in year 5 of the cycle — the SSR draft needs completer outcome data for Standard 4, not just course-level evidence."
**Common misuse:** Started the year of the site visit rather than built incrementally across the cycle, producing thin, last-minute evidence gathering.

### Targeted retake (edTPA)
A state-permitted option (not universal — roughly 20 states as of recent SCALE summaries) to resubmit only the single task that scored well below its proportional share of the cut score, rather than all three tasks.
**In use:** "Her Instruction task is the clear outlier — check if this state allows a targeted retake before we tell her to redo the whole portfolio."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be universally available; the option and its terms vary by state and must be confirmed against current policy before advising a candidate.

### Praxis / Praxis Core
ETS's standardized licensure testing series — Praxis Core (basic academic skills, often an admission gate) and Praxis II subject-area assessments (often a licensure gate), with state-specific cut scores.
**In use:** "Her Praxis Core writing score is 3 points under this state's cut — check whether a superscore across attempts is allowed before recommending a full retest."
**Common misuse:** Treated as one uniform national test with one national cut score, when both the required subtests and the passing scores are set state-by-state.

### Science of reading
The evidence base (largely from cognitive-science and reading-research literature) on phonics-based, structured literacy instruction, which by 2024 roughly 40 US states had codified into legislation mandating aligned instruction in teacher-prep and K-12 curricula.
**In use:** "This isn't a future-cycle curriculum item — the state statute means every reading-methods course needs auditing against it this academic year."
**Common misuse:** Referred to as a pedagogical trend or preference, rather than a statutory compliance requirement in the many states that have legislated it.

### Program completer
A candidate who has finished all program requirements and clinical placement, as distinct from one who is merely enrolled — the unit CAEP Standard 4 outcome data (employment, retention, in-service ratings) is measured against.
**In use:** "Our Standard 4 evidence tracks completers three years out, not just program-exit satisfaction surveys."
**Common misuse:** Used loosely to mean "graduate" in general, blurring the specific accreditation-defined population whose outcomes must be tracked.

### Endorsement (licensure)
The specific subject/grade-band authorization (e.g., "Elementary Education K-6," "Secondary Mathematics 7-12") attached to a teaching license, which a candidate's clinical placement and assessments must match.
**In use:** "Her endorsement is K-6 — a 7th-grade placement doesn't satisfy the state's grade-band requirement even if the district needed the coverage."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a formality that any placement can satisfy, rather than a specific matching requirement the state licensure office checks before granting the license.
