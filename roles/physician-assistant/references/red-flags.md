# Physician Assistant — Red Flags

### Delegation agreement doesn't explicitly list the condition or drug class being managed
- **Usually means:** the agreement was drafted generically and never updated for this practice's actual case mix, or the case genuinely sits at the boundary.
- **First question:** "Is this condition on the explicit independently-manageable list, or am I inferring it's similar to something that is?"
- **Data to pull:** the current signed collaboration/supervision agreement, not a remembered summary of it.

### Controlled-substance prescription approaching or at the days'-supply/refill threshold
- **Usually means:** either a legitimate acute-pain case needing a short course, or early drift toward a chronic-management pattern that needs physician involvement.
- **First question:** "Does this specific prescription cross the agreement's or the state's cosignature threshold?"
- **Data to pull:** prior prescription history for this patient, the specific threshold language in the agreement.

### Patient not improving on schedule for a condition being managed independently
- **Usually means:** wrong diagnosis, wrong treatment, or a complication outside the original scope assumption.
- **First question:** "Is the lack of response itself now a reason this case needs physician input, separate from whether it started in scope?"
- **Data to pull:** the original diagnosis rationale and decision-rule score, current symptoms vs expected trajectory.

### New specialty rotation, less than ~3-6 months of independent case volume in this area
- **Usually means:** clinical pattern recognition for this specialty hasn't caught up to the PA's general competence yet.
- **First question:** "Would I have consulted on this exact case a year ago, in my prior specialty?"
- **Data to pull:** case log/volume in this specific specialty area since the rotation started.

### Collaborating physician hasn't reviewed charts in longer than the agreement's stated interval
- **Usually means:** the retrospective-review safety net specified in the agreement isn't actually functioning.
- **First question:** "Is this a paperwork gap or has independent practice actually been running unreviewed?"
- **Data to pull:** chart-review log, last documented review date.

### A clinical decision rule wasn't applied where one exists for the presentation
- **Usually means:** reliance on gestalt where a validated, chart-defensible threshold was available.
- **First question:** "Is there a named decision rule (Ottawa, Wells', Centor, HEART) for this exact presentation that wasn't used?"
- **Data to pull:** the chart note for a documented rule application, or its absence.

### Diagnosis is a listed "requires consultation" item but management proceeded without documented contact
- **Usually means:** either an urgent situation forced action before contact was possible, or a scope violation.
- **First question:** "Is there a documented attempt to reach the collaborating physician, and a reason action couldn't wait?"
- **Data to pull:** the chart's timeline of contact attempts vs treatment initiation.

### Patient explicitly asks whether they're seeing "a real doctor"
- **Usually means:** normal patient uncertainty about the PA role, not a red flag about the care itself — but handled poorly it damages trust.
- **First question:** "Has the collaborative-practice model been explained plainly, without defensiveness?"
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a communication check, not a clinical one.

### Retrospective chart review flags an unexpected outcome on an independently-managed case
- **Usually means:** either a genuine clinical miss, or a scope-of-practice question that needs to inform future agreement updates.
- **First question:** "Was this outcome due to the clinical decision itself, or to a gap in what the agreement should have required consultation on?"
- **Data to pull:** the original decision rationale, the review findings, and whether the agreement's scope list needs revision.
