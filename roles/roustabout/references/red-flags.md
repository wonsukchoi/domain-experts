# Red flags

### H2S reading above 10 ppm at the breathing zone
- **Usually means:** a localized flash-gas pocket near a hatch, pit, or low point; second most likely, a genuine sour-gas breakout from a line or fitting.
- **First question:** is this steady or a transient puff from something that was just opened?
- **Data to pull:** multi-gas meter datalog, site H2S contingency plan zone map.

### LEL reading trending upward over a shift with no new task started
- **Usually means:** a slow leak or vapor buildup — a vent partially blocked, a valve seeping — building since the last clean reading.
- **First question:** what changed on this site since the last clean reading?
- **Data to pull:** gas detector trend log, record of the last valve or line worked.

### O2 reading below 19.5% in an enclosed or below-grade space
- **Usually means:** displacement by another gas (hydrocarbon vapor, CO2, a recent inert-gas purge/blanket), not consumption.
- **First question:** has this space been purged or blanketed with an inert gas recently?
- **Data to pull:** confined space entry log, purge/blanket record for the vessel or pit.

### Trench sidewall showing tension cracks or a fissure line parallel to the excavation
- **Usually means:** the spoil pile is closer than the 2 ft minimum setback, or the soil is losing cohesion — a precursor to sidewall collapse.
- **First question:** how long has this trench been open, and has it rained since it was dug?
- **Data to pull:** competent-person daily excavation inspection log.

### Standing sheen or discoloration inside a containment berm
- **Usually means:** an active or recent seep from a tank seam, valve, or flowline connection.
- **First question:** is the source still flowing, or is this residual from an earlier event?
- **Data to pull:** last tank strapping/gauge reading, berm inspection log.

### Pump jack or rotating equipment still warm or moving after a "stop" call
- **Usually means:** the lockout wasn't actually applied, or was applied to the wrong disconnect.
- **First question:** whose lock and tag is on the energy-isolating device right now?
- **Data to pull:** LOTO log, energy control procedure for that specific unit.

### Thief hatch left open or gasket visibly degraded on a routine gauge round
- **Usually means:** a gauging shortcut (hatch not re-closed after the last round) or a hatch that has been venting continuously.
- **First question:** how long has it been open, and who was last at this tank?
- **Data to pull:** gauge round sheet, vapor recovery unit status for the battery.

### Crew member standing in the swing or drop path of a suspended load during rig-up
- **Usually means:** the rigging call wasn't verbally confirmed before the move started.
- **First question:** who called this move, and did everyone hear it before it began?
- **Data to pull:** JSA sign-off, rigging plan for the specific lift.

### A single gas reading, taken at the opening, used to clear an entire pit or tank for entry
- **Usually means:** the crew tested only at the rim, not at depth — heavier-than-air gas at the bottom goes undetected.
- **First question:** was the space tested at multiple heights, or only at the opening?
- **Data to pull:** permit worksheet showing reading locations and times.

### Pressure gauge reads zero but the line "feels" full when handled
- **Usually means:** a plugged, frozen, or failed gauge, not an actually depressurized line.
- **First question:** has the line been bled and verified at a second point, or only assumed from the one gauge?
- **Data to pull:** bleed-off log, valve position record for that line.

### Crew on hour 10+ of a shift still on the same repetitive manual task (pipe racking, tong work)
- **Usually means:** fatigue-driven attention narrowing, a known contributor to caught-between injuries.
- **First question:** when was the last break, and is this the same two people who've been on this task all day?
- **Data to pull:** shift/rotation log.

### New hire or contractor on site without a logged site-specific orientation
- **Usually means:** the visitor/contractor orientation step was skipped under schedule pressure.
- **First question:** has this person been through today's site-specific orientation, not a generic company one?
- **Data to pull:** visitor/contractor sign-in and orientation log.

### Confined space posted "non-permit" with no competent-person signature or date
- **Usually means:** the posting was copied from a similar structure elsewhere on the lease rather than assessed on this one.
- **First question:** who classified this space, and when?
- **Data to pull:** competent-person classification record for this specific structure.
