# Red flags — what a media buyer notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### CTR declining 20%+ week over week on the same creative in the same channel
- **Usually means:** creative fatigue — the audience has seen it enough times that response is dropping, independent of targeting quality.
- **First question:** "What's the average frequency per person right now, and is it past the effective range we planned for?"
- **Data to pull:** frequency distribution by week, CTR trend by creative asset, week the decline started relative to flight start.

### A single channel carrying more than ~65-70% of planned impressions or spend
- **Usually means:** either the audience genuinely concentrates there (fine, but should be a stated decision) or the plan drifted toward the easiest channel to execute rather than the best-fit one.
- **First question:** "Was this concentration planned from the audience data, or did it happen during buying?"
- **Data to pull:** original planned channel split vs. actual delivered split, audience media-consumption data behind the original plan.

### Platform-reported conversions running 20%+ above a holdout or incrementality-adjusted estimate
- **Usually means:** last-click/self-reported attribution is over-crediting the platform — a real but partly inflated number.
- **First question:** "Do we have a holdout group or incrementality test for this channel, and what does it say the true lift is?"
- **Data to pull:** platform dashboard conversion count, holdout-group conversion rate, spend level (to judge if a formal incrementality test is warranted).

### CPM up 15%+ from the prior comparable flight with no stated market or seasonality reason
- **Usually means:** either genuine demand-side inflation (e.g., competitive event, seasonal spike) or a vendor quietly reducing the effective rate they're passing through.
- **First question:** "Is this CPM move consistent with what other buyers in this category are seeing this period?"
- **Data to pull:** CPM trend for the same channel/audience over the trailing 4-6 flights, third-party benchmark data for the category and period.

### Budget pacing 15%+ ahead of the flight's elapsed-time share
- **Usually means:** the buy is front-loading delivery, which either burns the budget before the flight ends or is deliberately shaped that way (e.g., a launch-week push) — needs to be which one on purpose.
- **First question:** "Is this front-loading intentional, and will the remaining budget still hit the frequency target through the full flight?"
- **Data to pull:** daily pacing report against the planned delivery curve, remaining budget vs. remaining flight days.

### Viewability reported by the vendor diverging more than ~10 points from third-party verification (e.g., MRC-accredited measurement)
- **Usually means:** a measurement methodology gap, or in rarer cases inventory quality issues (bot traffic, below-the-fold placements) the vendor's own numbers don't surface.
- **First question:** "Can we get the third-party verification pixel/tag live on this placement before scaling further?"
- **Data to pull:** vendor-reported viewability rate, third-party verified viewability rate, placement-level breakdown if available.

### A brief that lists more than one primary objective or more than one "the main thing this needs to do"
- **Usually means:** the brief wasn't forced through the single-audience/single-message discipline before it reached creative — it will produce work trying to do everything.
- **First question:** "If we could only measure one outcome from this campaign, which one, and does the brief say that?"
- **Data to pull:** the brief document itself, and whether a single success metric was defined alongside it.

### No documented success threshold before launch
- **Usually means:** the team is planning to judge the campaign qualitatively after the fact, which allows any result to be narrated as a win.
- **First question:** "What specific number, set before we saw any results, would have made this a failure?"
- **Data to pull:** the pre-launch plan or brief; absence of a stated number is itself the finding.

### Creative going into a scaled buy with zero small-scale test data
- **Usually means:** either the flight window was too short for a test-and-scale cycle (a real constraint, should be flagged) or the test step was skipped under time pressure.
- **First question:** "Was there a test phase, and if not, was that a timeline constraint or an oversight?"
- **Data to pull:** flight timeline, any A/B test results if one existed, sample size if a test was run.

### Agency-reported media cost with an undisclosed rebate or volume-discount arrangement
- **Usually means:** the effective price paid for media differs from the rate card price shown, which distorts CPM comparisons and ROI reporting if not surfaced.
- **First question:** "Is the CPM we're being shown the gross rate, or does it already reflect any rebate the agency receives?"
- **Data to pull:** the agency's disclosed fee/rebate structure in the contract, gross vs. net media cost reconciliation.
