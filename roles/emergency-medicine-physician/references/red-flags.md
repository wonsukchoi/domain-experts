# Red Flags

## Lactate ≥4 mmol/L, or a rising serial lactate, in a normotensive patient
- **Usually means:** Occult sepsis or hypoperfusion masked by compensated blood pressure ("cryptic shock"); second: a hemolyzed or delayed sample producing a falsely elevated result.
- **First question:** Has the 30 mL/kg fluid bolus and a repeat lactate already been ordered?
- **Data to pull:** Serial lactate trend, blood culture and antibiotic administration timestamps, sample collection/processing time.

## Boarded patient with a trending vital sign and no reassessment in >1 hour
- **Usually means:** The admission decision froze the clinical reassessment clock even though the patient is still physically in the ED; second: a bed-placement queue prioritizing wait time over acuity.
- **First question:** Who last laid eyes on and re-examined this patient, and when?
- **Data to pull:** Nursing reassessment timestamps, current vitals trend, updated acuity/ESI if re-triaged.

## HEART score 4–6 discharged without serial troponin or objective testing
- **Usually means:** Bed or boarding pressure drove a discharge that doesn't meet the HEART Pathway's low-risk (≤3) plus two-negative-troponin criteria; second: a miscalculated score (e.g., risk-factor count undercounted).
- **First question:** What were each of the five HEART components, and when was the second troponin drawn relative to the first?
- **Data to pull:** Documented HEART components, troponin timestamps, discharge instructions.

## PERC or Wells applied to a patient outside the rule's derivation population
- **Usually means:** The clinician used a rule-out tool (PERC especially) on a patient with an exclusion criterion present — active malignancy, prior VTE, recent surgery — where the tool's negative predictive value doesn't hold; second: an incomplete criteria checklist.
- **First question:** Does this patient actually meet every inclusion/exclusion criterion for the rule being applied?
- **Data to pull:** Documented PERC/Wells checklist, oncology and VTE history.

## LWBS (left without being seen) rate >5% on a shift
- **Usually means:** An input or throughput bottleneck (understaffed triage, slow room turnover); second: an output bottleneck where boarding is backing up the whole department, not a triage-nurse performance issue.
- **First question:** What's the current NEDOCS score and boarder count?
- **Data to pull:** NEDOCS score, hourly LWBS log, door-to-room time distribution.

## Discharged patient returns within 72 hours with the same complaint and a new or missed diagnosis
- **Usually means:** The first visit's can't-miss ruleout was incomplete or the return-precaution instructions weren't specific enough to act on; second: a genuinely early-stage presentation not detectable at visit one.
- **First question:** What was explicitly documented as ruled out at the first visit, and what were the actual return precautions given?
- **Data to pull:** First-visit chart (HPI, MDM, discharge instructions), second-visit findings.

## Transfer request or insurer pushback before the medical screening exam is documented complete
- **Usually means:** External non-clinical pressure (bed availability, payer preference, referring facility urgency) is being allowed to substitute for the EMTALA screening/stabilization obligation.
- **First question:** Has the MSE been documented as complete, and has an emergency medical condition been ruled in or out?
- **Data to pull:** MSE timestamp and findings, stabilization note, on-call specialist contact log.

## Vital signs crossing an ESI danger-zone threshold (adult HR >100 with SBP <100, or SpO2 <90%) in a patient not reassessed in over an hour
- **Usually means:** Unnoticed decompensation in a boarded or crowded department where reassessment frequency hasn't kept pace with acuity.
- **First question:** When was this patient's last full reassessment, and does the acuity level need to change?
- **Data to pull:** Vitals trend, nursing acuity/reassessment log, current bed and staffing assignment.

## Sepsis hour-1 bundle antibiotics given >3 hours after the recognition timestamp
- **Usually means:** Sepsis wasn't flagged at triage or on initial assessment (recognition delay); second: a pharmacy or nursing workflow delay compounded by department crowding.
- **First question:** When was sepsis first suspected or documented, versus when was the first antibiotic dose actually administered?
- **Data to pull:** EHR sepsis-alert trigger timestamp, medication administration record, blood culture draw time.

## Chief-complaint note with no pertinent negative tied to the relevant can't-miss diagnosis
- **Usually means:** The differential was never explicitly built before closing the workup (premature closure); second: a documentation shortcut taken under time pressure with the reasoning actually done but unrecorded.
- **First question:** What's the single worst diagnosis this presentation could be, and is it addressed anywhere in the note?
- **Data to pull:** The chart's HPI and medical decision-making (MDM) section.
