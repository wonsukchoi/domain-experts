# Red flags

### Wear or chipping concentrated on only some of a multi-flute/multi-edge tool's cutting surfaces
- **Usually means:** a process condition (interrupted cut, excessive feed, misalignment) rather than normal, uniform abrasive wear.
- **First question:** does the wear pattern show uniform distribution across all cutting edges, or is it concentrated on specific ones?
- **Data to pull:** wear pattern location/distribution, the tool's application/process conditions.

### A regrind performed to eliminate visible damage without measuring actual damage depth
- **Usually means:** the regrind may not remove enough material to clear subsurface micro-cracking or a work-hardened layer beneath the visible defect.
- **First question:** was chip/damage depth actually measured, or was regrind depth set by visual judgment of the surface alone?
- **Data to pull:** measured damage depth, actual regrind depth performed.

### A tool returned to service without verifying restored angle geometry against its original specification
- **Usually means:** the tool may be sharp but cutting with the wrong geometry, changing chip formation and cutting force.
- **First question:** was relief/rake/cutting angle measured and compared to spec, or was sharpness alone confirmed?
- **Data to pull:** the tool's specified geometry, post-regrind measurement if performed.

### A tool failing again shortly after a regrind, in the same failure mode as before
- **Usually means:** either insufficient regrind depth left residual damage, or an unaddressed process condition caused the same failure to recur.
- **First question:** was the original wear pattern diagnosed as process-related, and was that flagged/addressed before the tool returned to service?
- **Data to pull:** the original wear diagnosis and any process flag raised, time-to-failure after the regrind vs. normal service interval.

### A wear pattern showing discoloration or a built-up edge, without investigating heat/lubrication in the original application
- **Usually means:** a heat generation or lubrication issue in the tool's actual use, not primarily a geometry or sharpness problem.
- **First question:** does the discoloration/built-up-edge pattern suggest a specific heat or lubrication issue in the application?
- **Data to pull:** wear pattern appearance, cutting parameters/lubrication used in the application.

### A tool showing signs of overheating (discoloration, hardness change) after a regrind operation itself
- **Usually means:** the grinding process itself generated excessive heat, potentially reducing the tool's restored hardness.
- **First question:** was adequate coolant/light-pass technique used during this regrind, and does the ground surface show any heat discoloration?
- **Data to pull:** grinding parameters used, visual inspection of the ground surface for heat signs.

### A tool retired or continued in service without tracking cumulative regrind count
- **Usually means:** a decision about remaining useful life may be made without the actual cumulative history informing it.
- **First question:** does this tool's record show cumulative regrinds and estimated remaining life, or is retirement/continuation decided ad hoc?
- **Data to pull:** the tool's regrind history log.

### A regrind performed significantly deeper than needed to clear observed damage
- **Usually means:** unnecessary tool life is being sacrificed beyond what the actual damage required.
- **First question:** does the regrind depth correspond to the measured damage depth plus a reasonable clearance margin, or is it excessive?
- **Data to pull:** measured damage depth, actual regrind depth performed.

### A process engineering flag raised about a tool's wear pattern with no follow-up documented
- **Usually means:** the diagnostic information from the wear pattern may not have led to an actual process investigation or correction.
- **First question:** was the flagged process condition (feed rate, interrupted cut, etc.) actually investigated, and what was found?
- **Data to pull:** the flag/report, any process investigation follow-up record.

### Multiple tools from the same application showing the same wear pattern across different regrind cycles
- **Usually means:** a persistent, unaddressed process condition rather than isolated tool-to-tool variation.
- **First question:** does the wear pattern recur consistently across tools used in this specific application?
- **Data to pull:** wear pattern history across multiple tools from the same application/process.
