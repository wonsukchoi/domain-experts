# Dragline and Excavating/Loading Machine Operator — Red Flags

### Swing angle sustained above ~110–120° on the current face-to-dump geometry
- **Usually means:** face advance has drifted the actual dig-to-dump arc away from the pit plan's assumed angle without a plan update, quietly depressing production
- **First question:** what's the current measured swing angle, and when was the spoil-placement plan for this face last confirmed against it?
- **Data to pull:** current pit survey/face position, spoil-placement plan revision date, shift cycle-count log

### Loading pass count outside roughly 3–5 passes for the truck/bucket combination in use
- **Usually means:** a bucket/truck fleet-sizing mismatch — too few passes risks impact overload on the truck body, too many wastes cycle time and truck queue capacity
- **First question:** what's the calculated pass count for this specific bucket/truck pairing, and is it a one-load anomaly or the standing combination?
- **Data to pull:** bucket rated capacity and fill factor, truck rated payload, material loose density, recent pass-count log

### Fill factor running below ~0.65–0.70 on material logged as virgin bank
- **Usually means:** the face material is actually rehandled or blocky cast spoil being logged as virgin bank, or digging technique/face condition has degraded
- **First question:** is this face virgin bank or previously cast/rehandled material, and does the logged fill factor match which one it actually is?
- **Data to pull:** blast/cast plan for this face, recent fill-factor history for the machine, face material log

### Berm at an active dump point measuring below the mid-axle height of the largest unit dumping there
- **Usually means:** berm degradation from traffic or weather since last build/inspection, or a berm built to a smaller unit's spec than what's currently using the point
- **First question:** what's the measured berm height right now, and what's the mid-axle height of the largest self-propelled unit dumping at this point?
- **Data to pull:** current berm measurement, largest-unit mid-axle spec, dump-point inspection log (30 CFR §77.1605(k) or Part 56 parallel)

### Dragline tub or shovel/excavator crawler repositioning onto fill, recent spoil, or visibly wet ground without a bearing-capacity confirmation on record
- **Usually means:** the crew is treating compacted-looking material as equivalent to engineered, confirmed ground rather than requesting an actual bearing check
- **First question:** has pit engineering confirmed bearing capacity for this specific position, or is the crew relying on how the surface looks?
- **Data to pull:** bearing-capacity confirmation record for this position, recent fill/spoil placement history at the location

### Machine settlement, list, or unusual resistance observed during a walk or track move
- **Usually means:** the ground bearing capacity at the current position is lower than assumed, and continued movement risks a stability event
- **First question:** has all movement stopped, and has pit engineering assessed the position before movement resumes?
- **Data to pull:** movement log with timestamps, position coordinates, any prior bearing confirmation for this spot

### Digging depth for the current cut requiring reach beyond the boom/bucket's rated working range
- **Usually means:** the cut hasn't been benched down into lifts within rated reach, risking a short dump or an overloaded boom on an overreach pass
- **First question:** does the current cut depth fit within the machine's rated digging range at this position, or does it need benching?
- **Data to pull:** OEM rated digging depth/reach spec, current cut geometry, pit design cross-section

### Highwall crack, spall, or visible movement near an active digging face
- **Usually means:** localized highwall instability that can precede a larger failure, independent of the digging operation itself
- **First question:** has the highwall examination required before shift work at this face been completed and logged, and does it show this condition?
- **Data to pull:** highwall examination log (30 CFR §§77.1000–77.1004 or Part 56 parallel), geotechnical assessment if available

### Cast-blast fragmentation unusually coarse or blocky, material being loaded as if it were standard virgin bank
- **Usually means:** the blast plan under-performed on fragmentation or cast distance for this pattern, changing both the fill factor and the volume actually within reach
- **First question:** does the fragmentation at this face match the blast plan's design intent, and has the fill-factor assumption been adjusted to match the actual material?
- **Data to pull:** blast design/cast plan for this pattern, post-blast fragmentation assessment, current fill-factor log

### Shift production trending materially below plan (roughly >15%) for more than one consecutive shift without a named cause
- **Usually means:** one of swing angle, fill factor, ground condition, or effective-hours loss is depressing output and hasn't been isolated and reported
- **First question:** has the shortfall been broken down by cause (swing angle vs. fill factor vs. ground condition vs. downtime), or is it being logged as a general "ran slow"?
- **Data to pull:** shift production log against plan, cycle-time log, downtime log, current face swing-angle measurement
