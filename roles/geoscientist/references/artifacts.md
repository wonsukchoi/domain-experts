## Reserve-category classification criteria (SPE-PRMS, condensed)

| Category | Confidence level | Typical evidence bar |
|---|---|---|
| 1P (Proved) | >=90% probability the actual quantity recovered will equal or exceed the estimate | Multiple wells with production or well-test data; commercial viability demonstrated under current economic conditions |
| 2P (Probable) | >=50% probability | Limited well control; recovery factor often borrowed from analog; geologic model reasonably supported but not production-validated |
| 3P (Possible) | >=10% probability | Sparse data; interpretation extends beyond directly supported area; higher-risk assumptions on continuity or fluid contacts |

A compartment moves from 2P to 1P only when new data (production history, additional wells, pressure-transient tests) directly validates the parameters currently borrowed from analogy — not through elapsed time or management preference for a higher category.
