# Red flags

### Two sections graded by different raters show a >8-10 percentage point gap in mean speech score on a comparable prompt
- **Usually means:** rater calibration drift (a missed norming session), less often a genuine cohort or scheduling difference (e.g., an early-morning section).
- **First question:** did both raters attend the same anchor-speech calibration session this term?
- **Data to pull:** rubric-criterion-level breakdown per section; a blind cross-rater re-score of a random sample.

### A speech scores fluent, well-organized, and confident, but the student cannot answer a direct follow-up question about a cited source
- **Usually means:** a purchased or reused speech-bank script, or heavy outside editing of the outline the student didn't internalize; less often, well-rehearsed delivery over genuinely thin research.
- **First question:** does the submitted outline/works-cited page match what was actually delivered?
- **Data to pull:** outline draft timestamps, source URLs and publication dates, delivery transcript or recording.

### A rubric competency shows near-zero or negative discrimination (speeches that score well elsewhere score no better, or worse, on this one item)
- **Usually means:** the competency's descriptor is ambiguous or two raters are silently interpreting "satisfactory" differently.
- **First question:** did raters ever discuss, out loud, what "satisfactory" looks like for this specific competency before scoring independently?
- **Data to pull:** item-level score distribution across all raters for the assignment.

### A student scores above 80 on the PRCA-24 (or an equivalent screening) and requests a grading accommodation
- **Usually means:** genuine high trait communication apprehension, present in roughly the top fifth of any given class, not assignment avoidance.
- **First question:** is the request about delivery conditions (audience size, rehearsal slot) or about waiving a rubric criterion?
- **Data to pull:** PRCA-24 subscale scores (public, meeting, group, dyadic), prior-term accommodation history.

### Every member of a group rates one teammate meaningfully lower on a peer evaluation (e.g., more than 1 point below on a 5-point scale) than the rest of the group
- **Usually means:** a genuine free-rider; occasionally a personality conflict unrelated to actual contribution.
- **First question:** does that member's individual portion of the deliverable match the peer feedback, or only the peer narrative?
- **Data to pull:** individual contribution log or timeline, peer-evaluation comments from every group member.

### A major-assignment class mean falls more than roughly 10-15 points below the department's 3-term rolling average for the same course
- **Usually means:** the assignment or rubric shifted without being normed, or a genuine prerequisite gap in this cohort; rarely "a weak class" as a standalone explanation.
- **First question:** pull the criterion-level breakdown before drawing any conclusion about the students.
- **Data to pull:** criterion/item-level score analysis, comparison against the last three terms.

### A rhetorical-criticism or media-analysis paper applies the same critical method to every artifact regardless of genre
- **Usually means:** the course presented one method as the default rather than as an artifact-matched choice; less often, the student defaulted under time pressure to whichever method they remembered best.
- **First question:** was more than one critical method actually presented as viable during instruction, or is this a curriculum gap?
- **Data to pull:** syllabus reading list and method coverage, the assignment prompt's wording.

### A GTA reports spending under roughly 15 minutes of written feedback per graded speech across a full section
- **Usually means:** feedback has become templated/copy-pasted boilerplate, or the per-rater grading load is unsustainable for the number of speeches assigned.
- **First question:** how many speeches is this rater grading per week, and at what average time per speech?
- **Data to pull:** the grading-load roster across all raters, a sample of written feedback for comparison.

### The basic course's pass rate drops more than 5 points versus a 3-term rolling average right after a syllabus or rubric change
- **Usually means:** the new rubric wasn't normed with all raters before rollout, or the change genuinely raised the bar without a matching change in instruction.
- **First question:** did every rater score the same anchor set under the new rubric before grading live speeches?
- **Data to pull:** DFW (D/F/withdraw) rate by term, rubric version history.

### A student's live extemporaneous delivery is markedly weaker than their submitted outline or manuscript quality
- **Usually means:** the written material had significant outside help (tutor, AI drafting tool, purchased script) the student can't reproduce live; less often, severe state anxiety suppressing otherwise-solid preparation.
- **First question:** can the student explain, unscripted, why they chose this particular main-point order?
- **Data to pull:** outline draft history and timestamps, a brief unscripted oral follow-up.
