# Playbook

Filled templates for the recurring operational moments in a communication-studies teaching load: rubric scoring, apprehension screening, rater calibration, and originality checks.

## 1. Competent Speaker Speech Evaluation Form (filled example)

8 competencies, each scored 0 (unsatisfactory) – 1 (marginal) – 2 (satisfactory) – 3 (excellent). Raw max 24, converted to percentage as raw ÷ 24 × 100.

| # | Competency | Observable behavior scored | Score (0-3) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Topic selection | Topic is narrowed to fit time limit and audience/occasion | 3 |
| 2 | Thesis/specific purpose | A single, clearly stated central claim, stated early | 2 |
| 3 | Supporting material | At least 2 distinct sources cited by name/date, not just asserted | 2 |
| 4 | Organizational pattern | Main points signposted (first, second, finally) and previewed | 3 |
| 5 | Language | Concrete, audience-appropriate word choice, no unexplained jargon | 2 |
| 6 | Vocal delivery | Rate/volume/pauses support meaning, not monotone or rushed | 2 |
| 7 | Nonverbal delivery | Eye contact and gesture support content, not distracting | 2 |
| 8 | Adaptation to audience | Explicit reference to this audience's stake in the topic | 1 |
| | **Raw total** | | **17 / 24 = 70.8%** |

Score every competency independently before computing a total — a rater who starts from a gut-feel overall grade and reverse-engineers criterion scores is the single most common norming failure.

## 2. PRCA-24 scoring and interpretation

Four 6-item context subscales (public speaking, meetings, group discussions, dyadic/interpersonal), each subscale summed 6-30 after reverse-scoring the appropriate items, total range 24-120.

| Total score | Interpretation | Default response |
|---|---|---|
| < 51 | Low trait CA | No accommodation needed; typical range |
| 51-80 | Moderate trait CA | Standard course support (rehearsal slots, feedback) sufficient |
| > 80 | High trait CA (~top 20% of a typical class) | Offer delivery-condition accommodations (smaller audience, extra rehearsal); do not adjust rubric criteria |
| Subscale-specific spike (e.g., public-speaking subscale > 24/30 while others are moderate) | Context-specific apprehension, not generalized | Target accommodation to that context only (e.g., public speaking) rather than blanket-adjusting group or interpersonal assignments |

Administer at week 1-2, before the first graded speech, so accommodations are in place ahead of the assignment that would trigger them.

## 3. Cross-rater calibration session (agenda, ~90 minutes, before live grading opens)

| Step | Time | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 10 min | Distribute the rubric and review each competency's observable-behavior definition; no scoring yet |
| 2 | 15 min | All raters independently score anchor speech 1 (a mid-range recorded speech, ~17-19/24 historically) |
| 3 | 15 min | Compare scores live; discuss any competency with a >1-point spread across raters until consensus |
| 4 | 15 min | Independently score anchor speech 2 (a strong speech, ~21-23/24 historically) |
| 5 | 10 min | Compare and discuss |
| 6 | 15 min | Independently score anchor speech 3 (a weak speech, ~10-13/24 historically) |
| 7 | 10 min | Compare, discuss, and confirm the anchor set is retained for next term's session |

A rater who misses this session should not grade live speeches until a make-up session (same three anchors) is completed, or should be excluded from grading entirely for that round.

## 4. Cross-section grade-gap investigation (threshold-driven)

1. Compute each section's mean speech score for the assignment in question.
2. If the spread between any two raters' section means exceeds **8-10 percentage points** on a comparable prompt, do not accept the gap as a real performance difference yet.
3. Draw a random sample of **10 speeches** (or 20% of the smaller section, whichever is larger) from the lower-scoring rater's section.
4. Blind re-score the sample with 2 raters who attended calibration, using the same rubric; average their scores per speech.
5. Compute the correction: (calibrated mean − original mean) as both raw points and a percentage.
6. Apply the correction to the full section, not the flat difference between section averages — the flat difference conflates rater bias with any real cohort/scheduling effect.
7. Route any residual gap (post-correction) to the scheduling/section-composition data before deciding it's a genuine performance difference.

## 5. Originality/speech-bank verification checklist

- [ ] Outline and works-cited page submitted at least 48 hours before delivery (not day-of).
- [ ] Every cited source in the outline is traceable to a real, checkable URL or publication with a date.
- [ ] One unscripted follow-up question asked live, tied to a specific cited source ("why did you choose that particular study over a more recent one?").
- [ ] Delivery register matches the outline's content level — a mismatch (fluent generic phrasing paired with an inability to explain a specific claim) is the strongest single tell, stronger than a plagiarism-detection tool score for spoken content.
- [ ] Topic connects to a personal angle or local example the student had to generate themselves, reducing the fit of a purchased generic script.

## 6. Basic-course model choice (sequencing decision)

| Model | Best fit when | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| Public-speaking-only basic course | Program's gen-ed requirement is specifically oral communication; most students are one-and-done | Simpler to norm (one skill, one rubric) but doesn't expose students to interpersonal/small-group competencies |
| Hybrid public speaking + interpersonal/small-group basic course | Department wants the basic course to also seed majors toward interpersonal/organizational tracks | Broader competency coverage, but splits limited class time and requires two rubrics normed instead of one |

Default to public-speaking-only when GTA staffing turnover is high (new raters each term benefit from norming one rubric well); default to hybrid only when the department has a stable, multi-term GTA core that can absorb norming two rubrics without drift.
