# Red flags

Smell tests a specialist or a reviewer catches on a first pass over an inspection report, complaint log, or remediation record.

### TCS food out of temperature range with no time-stamped log entry

- **Usually means:** the establishment isn't tracking time in the danger zone at all, which makes the 2-hour/4-hour disposition rule impossible to apply correctly — the food's actual risk status is unknown, not just undocumented.
- **First question:** is there any record (kitchen log, delivery timestamp, prior inspection reading) that can establish how long the food has actually been out of range?
- **Data to pull:** the establishment's temperature log (if any) and the time the item was last confirmed in range on a prior visit or delivery receipt.

### Cooler ambient thermometer reads compliant but food probe reads high

- **Usually means:** the unit's air temperature and the food's internal temperature have decoupled — overloading, poor air circulation, or food recently added and not yet cooled — and the ambient reading is masking the real risk.
- **First question:** how long has the specific food item been in this unit, and does its internal temperature match what the loading pattern would predict?
- **Data to pull:** the probe reading on the specific food item (not the unit's built-in gauge) and a note on when that item entered the unit.

### Fecal/vomit pool incident logged as a routine shock treatment with no cyanuric acid reading

- **Usually means:** the operator applied the formed-stool protocol to a diarrheal or vomit event, or applied hyperchlorination without checking whether CYA level would defeat the CT target — either way the stated FC/hold time may not represent actual *Cryptosporidium* inactivation.
- **First question:** was the incident diarrheal/vomit or formed stool, and what was the CYA reading before dosing?
- **Data to pull:** the incident report's stool/vomit classification and the pre-treatment water chemistry log (FC, pH, CYA, temperature).

### Percolation test result reported without a soil boring log or water-table check

- **Usually means:** the site was approved on the perc number alone, with no independent verification of the water-table depth or bedrock separation that could override a favorable rate.
- **First question:** does the file contain a boring log showing water table and restrictive layer depth, separate from the timed percolation reading?
- **Data to pull:** the percolation test worksheet and the soil boring/site evaluation log for the same test hole location.

### Same establishment named in two or more complaints within a short window, logged as separate independent items

- **Usually means:** the complaints share an exposure window and should be treated as a potential cluster, not two isolated food-illness reports — the investigative approach (pulling logs, supplier records, employee-illness data) is materially different for a cluster.
- **First question:** do the reported exposure dates and symptom onset windows overlap enough to suggest a common source?
- **Data to pull:** both complaints' reported meal/exposure dates and symptom onset times, compared side by side.

### Repeat critical or Priority violation cited on the two most recent prior inspections, current visit shows the same finding

- **Usually means:** the correction was never actually implemented, or was implemented and abandoned — a pattern that changes the appropriate response from a standard correction deadline to escalated enforcement.
- **First question:** what corrective action did the operator report after each prior citation, and is there any evidence it was carried out (not just promised)?
- **Data to pull:** the prior two inspection reports' violation and corrective-action sections for the same code section.

### Closure or embargo action taken with the code section field left blank or generic

- **Usually means:** the citation was written from impression rather than from the specific regulatory trigger, which weakens the agency's position if the action is appealed.
- **First question:** which specific code section and observed condition authorizes this specific closure or embargo action?
- **Data to pull:** the closure notice's citation field and the code text for the section cited.

### Chlorine or pH reading taken with a test strip only, used as the sole basis for a closure or reopening decision

- **Usually means:** a high-stakes decision (closing or reopening a facility) is resting on a lower-precision method — strips are adequate for routine monitoring but carry more reading error than DPD titration or a photometer at the margins that matter for a closure call.
- **First question:** was a confirmatory titration or photometer reading taken before the closure/reopening decision was finalized?
- **Data to pull:** the water chemistry log entry's stated test method alongside the reading.

### Private well sample shows total coliform positive, E. coli negative, reported to the owner as "contaminated" without distinction

- **Usually means:** the two results indicate different things — total coliform alone often reflects environmental influence (surface intrusion, aging well seal) while E. coli specifically indicates fecal contamination — and conflating them either alarms the owner unnecessarily or, in reverse, understates a genuine fecal-contamination finding.
- **First question:** which specific organism(s) came back positive, and does the report distinguish total coliform from E. coli in the recommendation to the owner?
- **Data to pull:** the lab report's itemized results and the cover letter or notice sent to the well owner.

### Mosquito trap counts flat or declining while human arbovirus case counts in the same district are rising

- **Usually means:** a gap between the trapping program (location, timing, or trap type) and where actual transmission is occurring — the surveillance data and the epidemiologic data have decoupled.
- **First question:** do trap locations and collection dates align with where and when the human cases were exposed?
- **Data to pull:** the vector surveillance program's trap-site map and collection log, compared against case investigation exposure locations.
