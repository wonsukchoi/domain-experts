# Red flags

Smell tests a bell captain or senior porter catches fast, with the first question to ask and the record to pull before assuming the worst about staff or guest.

### Guest disputes bag count at claim ("I had 4, you gave me 3") with no matching ticket stub

**Usually means:** a claim ticket wasn't issued at receipt, or the storage-room log wasn't reconciled at the last shift change — assume a process gap first, not guest error or theft.

**First question:** "Do we have the guest's stub, and does it match a ticket number in our storage log?"

**Data to pull:** claim-ticket stubs issued that shift, storage-room sign-in/out log, loading-dock or bell-stand camera timestamp if available.

### A cart tips over or a bag arrives damaged

**Usually means:** a top-heavy or unbalanced load, often loaded fast during a surge without following the base-heavy/centered-weight order.

**First question:** "What was on that cart, and in what order was it loaded?"

**Data to pull:** incident-log entry, the cart's rated stack limit, staffing level and bag volume at the time (was this during a surge with no on-call called in?).

### A bellhop reports repeated lower-back strain or files more than one workers'-comp claim in a season

**Usually means:** solo-lifting bags above the safe single-lift range — soft-sided, no handle, off-center weight — without a hand truck or second person, normalized under time pressure over months.

**First question:** "Walk me through how that specific bag was lifted — solo, hand truck, or two-person?"

**Data to pull:** injury/workers'-comp log frequency for that staffer, whether hand trucks are stocked and accessible at the bell stand, average bag weight trend for the property (convention and ski-resort properties skew heavier).

### Group-arrival delivery time consistently exceeds the property's stated target

**Usually means:** staffing wasn't scaled ahead of a scheduled group arrival, or the call-in threshold existed on paper but wasn't actually triggered.

**First question:** "What was the bag-to-staff ratio at peak, and was on-call actually called — and when?"

**Data to pull:** group manifest bag count, staff on floor at arrival time, average cart-cycle time that day, on-call call-in timestamp if any.

### Pooled tips per staffer vary more than ~30% shift-to-shift for similar hours worked

**Usually means:** the pool is being split by an inconsistent method (sometimes headcount, sometimes hours/points), or the points log isn't being kept accurately.

**First question:** "How was this week's pool actually calculated, and against what hours or points log?"

**Data to pull:** tip-pool ledger, shift schedule with actual hours, the property's stated pooling formula, whether surge hours were flagged for the multiplier.

### Luggage storage room holds a stub older than 24 hours with no matching active checkout activity

**Usually means:** a bag placed in storage was never followed up for delivery, or a guest checked out without claiming it.

**First question:** "Which stubs in the room are past 24 hours, and do they match an active or departed reservation?"

**Data to pull:** storage log with intake timestamps, PMS reservation status for each unclaimed stub, front-desk notes on guest departure.

### A top-tier loyalty guest's bags were delivered after standard-tier guests during a surge

**Usually means:** carts were dispatched in arrival order instead of triage priority order — the priority list wasn't checked before the first cart moved.

**First question:** "What order were the carts actually dispatched in — was the priority list pulled?"

**Data to pull:** cart-dispatch log with timestamps, the guest-tier list for that arrival window, whether mobility-assist flags were checked.

### A guest reports an item missing from a bag after it changed hands

**Usually means:** either the item was never in the bag when it was received (undocumented) or there's an actual custody gap during storage or transit — neither can be resolved without a condition record from receipt.

**First question:** "Do we have any condition documentation — photo or note — from when the bag was received?"

**Data to pull:** receipt-condition log or photo if one was taken, the claim-ticket custody trail (who handled it, when), storage-room access log for the window the bag was held.

### Bell staff turnover spikes shortly after a scheduling or tip-pool policy change

**Usually means:** the new formula redistributed income in a way the busiest-shift staff experience as a pay cut, even if the average payout looks unchanged.

**First question:** "How does take-home under the new policy compare to the old one specifically for whoever worked the busiest shifts?"

**Data to pull:** per-staffer earnings before and after the change, shift-assignment patterns, exit-interview notes if any exist.

### A guest complains the room orientation/reveal never happened

**Usually means:** a rushed delivery during a surge skipped the standard walkthrough, or staff assumed a returning guest didn't need it and guessed wrong.

**First question:** "Was this during a peak period, and had staff flagged the guest as a repeat visitor?"

**Data to pull:** arrival volume at the delivery time, guest stay history (repeat vs. first-time), any delivery notes staff logged for that room.
