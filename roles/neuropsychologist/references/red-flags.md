# Red flags

### TOMM Trial 2 (or equivalent PVT) below 45/50
- **Usually means:** invalid effort or non-credible presentation; second most likely, a severe, independently documented condition (advanced dementia, IQ <60) genuinely producing a failure at that cutoff.
- **First question:** is this evaluation forensic, disability, or litigation-related, or is there documented severe global impairment that could explain a genuine failure?
- **Data to pull:** the full PVT/SVT battery (not just one instrument), litigation/disability claim status, MMSE/MoCA or other global cognitive screen.

### More low scores than the battery's chance-expected base rate, scattered across unrelated domains
- **Usually means:** multivariate base-rate noise from a large battery, not a true deficit; second, fatigue, pain, or a genuinely diffuse process.
- **First question:** do the low scores share a cognitive domain, or a session-timing/fatigue pattern, or neither?
- **Data to pull:** raw and standard scores across the full battery grouped by domain, session notes on breaks and time of day.

### Free recall impaired, recognition memory within normal limits
- **Usually means:** a retrieval/effort-mediated deficit (depression, anxiety, ADHD, sedating medication), not a consolidation failure; second, a sedating medication effect.
- **First question:** does the patient's mood/anxiety screen (PHQ-9/GAD-7) clear the clinical cutoff?
- **Data to pull:** recognition-trial hit and false-alarm rates (d'), current medication list, PHQ-9/GAD-7 scores.

### >15-point (1 SD) gap between reading-based premorbid estimate and current FSIQ in a patient with a reading disorder history or non-native English
- **Usually means:** the premorbid estimate itself is invalid, not true decline; second, genuine severe decline masking reading ability enough to also depress the estimate.
- **First question:** was English this patient's first language, and is there documented history of a learning disorder predating the current complaint?
- **Data to pull:** school records, prior testing, language-acquisition history.

### Serial retest gain smaller than the test's published practice-effect estimate for that interval
- **Usually means:** no real change — the apparent gain is entirely practice effect; second, true decline masked by a practice effect large enough to make a stable score look like improvement.
- **First question:** what interval elapsed, and what does the test manual's retest reliability/practice-effect table say for that interval?
- **Data to pull:** the manual's practice-effect table, a computed reliable change index against the prior scores.

### Forensic/disability referral where self-reported symptom severity far exceeds objective test findings
- **Usually means:** symptom exaggeration or somatic amplification; second, a genuine condition (chronic pain, PTSD) that current instruments have limited sensitivity to.
- **First question:** what do the symptom/response-bias validity scales show (PAI NIM, MMPI-3 FBS-r)?
- **Data to pull:** SVT/validity scale scores, litigation or disability claim documentation, collateral informant report.

### Informant reports functional decline; all cognitive test scores within normal limits
- **Usually means:** early-stage process below the current tests' floor sensitivity; second, informant overreporting driven by caregiver burden or family conflict.
- **First question:** is the informant's report specific and behavioral (missed medications, got lost driving a familiar route) or global and vague ("just not himself")?
- **Data to pull:** a functional-status questionnaire (e.g., FAQ), dated specific behavioral incidents, informant's relationship and any secondary motive.

### Executive-function tests impaired, but the patient shows no executive-domain problems in daily life
- **Usually means:** the low score reflects a co-occurring processing-speed or motor deficit driving the same test, not true dysexecutive syndrome; second, compensatory strategies masking real-world impact.
- **First question:** is the low executive score fully explained by a low processing-speed or motor score on the same battery?
- **Data to pull:** processing speed index score, motor exam findings, informant ADL/IADL checklist.

### Intact bedside screener (MoCA/MMSE) despite a strong referral concern
- **Usually means:** a ceiling effect in a high-premorbid-functioning patient masking real decline; second, the presenting complaint is non-cognitive (anxiety, sleep disorder).
- **First question:** what was this patient's estimated premorbid/occupational functioning level relative to the screener's ceiling?
- **Data to pull:** WTAR/TOPF or education/occupation-based premorbid estimate, full battery scores compared against the bedside screen.

### >2 SD gap between two subtests measuring the same underlying construct within one battery
- **Usually means:** an administration or scoring error, not a genuine neurocognitive discrepancy; second, differing item difficulty or ceiling between the two instruments.
- **First question:** have both tests been re-scored and checked for administration error before the discrepancy is interpreted as real?
- **Data to pull:** raw item-level responses, the scoring worksheet, a second scorer's independent check.
