# Red flags

Smell tests a working musician learns to catch before signing, showing up, or ignoring a symptom.

### "Exposure" offered instead of payment

- **Usually means:** the promoter's budget is zero and they're testing what they can get away with; occasionally it's a genuine showcase where a specific decision-maker will be present.
- **First question:** "Who specifically will be there — can you name one confirmed industry contact (booker, A&R, press) attending?"
- **Data to pull:** guest list or confirmed attendee names; verified attendance numbers from a comparable past date at the same room.

### Door deal with no printed ticket count or box-office access

- **Usually means:** the promoter can under-report net revenue after the show with nothing to check it against; sometimes it's just a small operation with no formal ticketing system.
- **First question:** "Can I get read access to the ticketing dashboard, or a signed house count within 24 hours of doors?"
- **Data to pull:** ticketing platform export, comp-list size, paid count vs. house count reconciliation.

### Door-deal draw projection well above the venue's own recent history

- **Usually means:** the promoter is quoting an optimistic ceiling, not a realistic expectation, to make a percentage deal look competitive with a flat guarantee.
- **First question:** "What did the last three comparable shows in this room actually draw?"
- **Data to pull:** past three shows' verified ticket counts at the same venue/day-of-week/genre; compute the breakeven draw the door deal needs to match a flat-guarantee alternative.

### Verbal-only agreement on song or band splits once a song starts getting traction

- **Usually means:** an unresolved authorship dispute waiting for a royalty check large enough to fight over — the classic setup behind most band-breakup lawsuits.
- **First question:** "Is there a signed split sheet from the writing session, and is each writer's percentage registered with a PRO?"
- **Data to pull:** split sheet, PRO registration records, session date logs.

### Recurring hoarseness, breathiness, or narrowed vocal range lasting more than two weeks

- **Usually means:** vocal fold pathology (nodules, polyps, edema) developing from cumulative overuse or technique breakdown — not ordinary post-gig fatigue, which resolves in 24–48 hours.
- **First question:** "How many performances this month, and has pitch range or breath support noticeably changed?"
- **Data to pull:** gig-frequency/vocal-load log over the past 4–6 weeks; referral to an ENT/laryngologist for stroboscopic exam if symptoms persist past two weeks.

### Ringing in the ears (tinnitus) lasting more than 24 hours after a show

- **Usually means:** acoustic trauma from stage or monitor SPL exceeding safe exposure — a warning sign of cumulative noise-induced hearing loss, not something to "sleep off" and ignore.
- **First question:** "What's the monitor/stage SPL typically running, and is attenuating hearing protection worn?"
- **Data to pull:** SPL meter reading at the mix or in-ear position; frequency of tinnitus episodes over recent months; audiogram if episodes recur.

### Booking agent or manager commission applied to a gig they didn't source

- **Usually means:** the agency or management contract has a blanket "all engagements" commission clause rather than a "engagements booked by agent" clause — contract ambiguity more often than deliberate overreach.
- **First question:** "Does the agreement specify commission only on engagements the agent/manager actually sourced?"
- **Data to pull:** the signed agency/management contract's commission clause; a log of who sourced each booking on the calendar.

### An all-in "360" deal offered to a act with no touring or merch track record yet

- **Usually means:** the label or manager is hedging against a low-probability breakout by taking a percentage of every revenue stream up front, whether or not they contribute meaningfully to all of them.
- **First question:** "What specific service justifies a cut of each revenue stream listed — recording, touring, merch, publishing?"
- **Data to pull:** the contract's percentage by revenue stream; comparable deal terms for acts at a similar stage (via an entertainment attorney or manager network).

### Merch settlement done by venue staff alone with no witnessed starting count

- **Usually means:** shrinkage or miscounting, whether deliberate or just sloppy end-of-night bookkeeping with no baseline to check against.
- **First question:** "Can a band member count merch in at load-in and out at settlement, both signed by a venue rep?"
- **Data to pull:** signed load-in count, signed settlement sheet, unit cost vs. gross-sold reconciliation.

### Large corporate or private client wants to pay in full only after the event, no deposit

- **Usually means:** either a legitimate large-organization net-30 payment process, or genuine cancellation/no-show risk with nothing securing the booking.
- **First question:** "Is there a signed contract with a deposit and cancellation clause, or is this verbal-only?"
- **Data to pull:** signed contract/deposit invoice; client's booking history or references if this is a new client.

### Streaming numbers "going viral" with no matching growth in ticket or merch sales

- **Usually means:** bot traffic, playlist-farm placement, or passive algorithmic pushes generating raw stream counts with no actual fan conversion behind them.
- **First question:** "Where geographically are the streams concentrated, and does it match upcoming tour markets?"
- **Data to pull:** Spotify for Artists (or equivalent) geographic and source breakdown; save-rate and follow-conversion vs. raw stream count; ticket pre-sale data in the top streaming markets.

### Practice sessions are all full run-throughs, no isolated trouble-spot work

- **Usually means:** practice time that feels productive but reinforces already-solid material while the specific failing passage never gets targeted repetition (Ericsson's "naive practice" pattern).
- **First question:** "What exact bar or phrase failed last time, and how many isolated reps at reduced tempo were done on just that passage?"
- **Data to pull:** practice log with tempo markings per passage, not just total minutes logged.

### New sideman or touring hire with no written rate agreed before the first rehearsal

- **Usually means:** the rate gets renegotiated downward once travel and rehearsal time are already sunk and the player has lost the leverage to walk away.
- **First question:** "Is day rate, per diem, and travel/backline responsibility in writing before rehearsal one?"
- **Data to pull:** the sideman agreement or AFM contract; comparable session/tour day rates for the market and instrument.
