### Basis quoted more than 15 cents off the 5-year average for this location and month
- **Usually means:** A stale basis board that hasn't been updated against today's rail/freight or local demand conditions, or a genuine local supply shock (nearby plant outage, rail congestion) that hasn't been communicated to the desk.
- **First question:** When was the basis board last updated today, and against what freight/rail cost assumption?
- **Data to pull:** Basis history report (5-year rolling average by location and delivery month), current rail/truck freight quotes.

### Grower disputes a moisture or grade reading on the scale ticket
- **Usually means:** A probe sample taken from a single point in the load rather than a composite sample, or a miscalibrated moisture meter.
- **First question:** Was the sample a composite pull across the load, and when was the meter last calibrated?
- **Data to pull:** Calibration log for the moisture meter, the original probe sample, USDA grade-standard reference for the commodity.

### A forward contract's delivery window has passed with no delivery and no communication
- **Usually means:** The grower is under-delivering because the market moved against the contract price (grower would rather sell elsewhere or default than deliver at a below-market price).
- **First question:** Where is today's cash price relative to the contract price, and has the grower been contacted about remaining bushels?
- **Data to pull:** Open contract report showing quantity delivered vs. contracted, current cash bid.

### Unhedged forward-contract volume exceeds the company's standing open-position limit
- **Usually means:** Contracts were booked faster than the hedge desk executed offsetting futures trades, or a hedge was placed against the wrong contract month.
- **First question:** What is today's net open position by delivery month, and does it reconcile to the daily hedge log?
- **Data to pull:** Daily position/hedge report, futures trade blotter.

### A single grower's discount pattern looks systematically different from others delivering the same day
- **Usually means:** Inconsistent application of the grade-discount schedule (a courtesy waiver, or a data-entry error on that ticket).
- **First question:** Was the same discount schedule and moisture baseline applied to every ticket from today's intake?
- **Data to pull:** All settlement tickets for the delivery day, the posted discount schedule in effect.

### Storage utilization is above ~85-90% during active harvest intake
- **Usually means:** Intake pace is outrunning outbound shipping or processing capacity, and continuing to buy at the posted price will force either refused loads or off-site emergency storage.
- **First question:** What is today's net intake minus outbound movement, and how many days of headroom remain at the current pace?
- **Data to pull:** Daily storage utilization report, outbound shipment schedule.

### A basis contract or HTA has an open leg past its stated pricing deadline
- **Usually means:** The grower hasn't called in to set the unpriced leg (futures or basis) and is either waiting for a better level or has lost track of the deadline.
- **First question:** What is the contract's stated deadline for setting the open leg, and has the grower been reminded ahead of it?
- **Data to pull:** Open HTA/basis contract log with pricing deadlines.

### A counterparty (grower) requests contract terms that deviate from standard company paper
- **Usually means:** The grower is trying to shift quality risk, delivery timing risk, or basis risk onto the buyer in a way the standard contract wouldn't allow.
- **First question:** Which specific clause is being changed, and has it been reviewed by legal or the merchandising manager?
- **Data to pull:** Standard contract template, the proposed redline.

### Test weight or foreign material readings cluster suspiciously close to the discount threshold across many loads
- **Usually means:** Either the elevator's grading equipment is systematically biased just inside the no-discount line, or growers/haulers have learned exactly where the threshold sits and are blending loads to stay just under it.
- **First question:** What is the distribution of readings relative to the threshold across the last two weeks of intake?
- **Data to pull:** Grade-reading log across all tickets, equipment calibration records.

### A delivered load's grade is materially better or worse than the same grower's historical pattern
- **Usually means:** A change in field conditions, storage practice, or handling — or, less commonly, co-mingling with another grower's or another field's grain.
- **First question:** Has anything changed in how this grower is storing or handling grain since the last delivery?
- **Data to pull:** That grower's delivery history (grade trend over past deliveries).
