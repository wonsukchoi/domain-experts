# Red Flags — Smell Tests a Social Work Professor Catches Instantly

Each entry: what the smell usually means, the first question to ask, and what to actually pull. None are automatic verdicts — they're triggers for a specific check that routinely changes the decision.

---

### Competency benchmark under 75-80% for two consecutive cohorts

**Usually means:** either a real curriculum gap (the skill isn't being taught where the assessment says it is) or an instrument problem — the evaluation form is measuring something adjacent to the named competency (e.g., documentation speed standing in for clinical judgment).

**First question:** "Did this drop across every field site and liaison, or is it concentrated in one section?"

**Data to pull:** the Tevera/IPT competency evaluation form's item wording, broken out by field site and by liaison — a program-wide drop points to curriculum or instrument; a single-site drop points to that field instructor's calibration.

### A field instructor's credential wasn't verified before a student's start date

**Usually means:** the field office is tracking placements by agency relationship, not by instructor-level credential (MSW + 2 years post-degree per EPAS M3.2), and treats the agency as pre-vetted once, permanently.

**First question:** "When was this specific instructor's credential last verified — not the agency's standing, the person's?"

**Data to pull:** the field-instructor roster with credential-verification dates; agencies routinely rotate staff faster than the affiliation file is updated.

### An affiliation agreement is expired, unsigned, or "in progress" but hours are already being logged

**Usually means:** someone treated the paperwork as a formality that could catch up after the placement started, usually under enrollment-deadline pressure.

**First question:** "Who authorized the student to start before the agreement cleared counsel?"

**Data to pull:** the agreement's actual signature/counsel-review dates against the student's logged hour-start date in the field-management platform.

### A dean or provost proposes an enrollment increase with no field-capacity number attached

**Usually means:** the proposal was built from tuition-revenue math alone, treating field placements as an admissions/marketing problem rather than a lead-time-bound pipeline.

**First question:** "How many additional field-agency slots does this require, and what's our onboarding rate per year?"

**Data to pull:** three years of new-agency onboarding counts from the field office, and the current agency-to-student ratio.

### IRB protocol for a practicum- or dissertation-linked study lists "clients" as subjects with no vulnerable-population language

**Usually means:** the researcher is applying general Common Rule review when Subparts B-D (pregnant women, prisoners, children) actually apply, often because the client population wasn't described precisely in the initial submission.

**First question:** "Does any subset of this sample include minors, incarcerated individuals, or pregnant women — even a handful?"

**Data to pull:** the actual intake/eligibility criteria for the study population, not the protocol's summary description of it.

### A tenure or reappointment packet has zero quantification of field-liaison hours

**Usually means:** the candidate (or the packet template) is treating field liaison work as invisible background administration rather than a countable service load, which reads to a committee as "did less" rather than "did unmeasured work."

**First question:** "How many students, how many site visits each, and what did each visit actually take in hours?"

**Data to pull:** the field office's liaison assignment roster for the review period, cross-referenced against the candidate's packet.

### A publication counted toward tenure has no named peer-review venue

**Usually means:** an op-ed, blog post, or invited commentary is being positioned as equivalent to a peer-reviewed article — sometimes a legitimate part of a Boyer "application" or "integration" case, but only if labeled as such, not folded into the discovery count.

**First question:** "Which Boyer category is this claimed under, and does the committee's rubric actually credit that category?"

**Data to pull:** the venue's peer-review policy and the packet's own category labeling for each item.

### Student evaluations of teaching crater in one specific practice-course sequence, semester over semester

**Usually means:** either an instructor-calibration problem in that sequence, or a curriculum redesign (e.g., a new competency-mapped assignment) that hasn't been piloted before full rollout and is landing badly.

**First question:** "Did this coincide with a specific curriculum or instructor change, or has it always been this course?"

**Data to pull:** evaluation trend data by section and instructor, cross-referenced against the curriculum-map revision history.

### A new field placement is approved because "the agency has hosted our students for years"

**Usually means:** agency-level trust is substituting for placement-level verification — the specific instructor assigned this cycle, the current liability coverage, and this year's supervision plan haven't been independently checked.

**First question:** "Who is the named field instructor this semester, and when was their credential last verified?"

**Data to pull:** the current-cycle affiliation checklist for that agency, not last year's file.

### A CSWE self-study or benchmark report is drafted by one administrator alone, close to the submission deadline

**Usually means:** the data (competency scores, field rosters, faculty qualifications) wasn't pulled from each department/concentration in time, so the report risks either stale numbers or an administrator's estimate standing in for measured data.

**First question:** "Which of these numbers came from a live pull this cycle, versus carried over from the last report?"

**Data to pull:** the timestamp and source system for every data table in the draft report.
