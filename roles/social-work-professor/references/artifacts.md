Real work products, filled in with plausible examples. Copy the structure, replace the content. The example program throughout is a mid-size MSW program (100-140 students) at a regional state university.

## 1. Field-agency affiliation checklist

Gates whether a student can start hours at an agency. Every box unchecked is a reason to hold the placement, not a formality to backfill.

```
Agency: Riverside County Family Services
Proposed field instructor: D. Alvarez, MSW (2019), 3 yrs post-degree practice

[x] Affiliation agreement signed by both university counsel and agency (dated 2026-05-12)
[x] Certificate of liability insurance on file, coverage >= university minimum ($1M/$3M)
[x] Field instructor credential verified: MSW + 2+ yrs post-degree (EPAS M3.2) — 3 yrs, PASS
[x] Field instructor completed program's field-instructor orientation (EPAS-required training)
[ ] Task supervisor identified for daily on-site supervision (if field instructor is off-site)
[x] Placement hours plan on file: 900 hrs across 2 semesters, 15 hrs/week average
[ ] Prior-cohort incident review clear (no unresolved complaints from this agency)

STATUS: HOLD — task supervisor and incident review outstanding.
Do not assign a student until both close. Target: 2 weeks before semester start.
```

What makes this work: two boxes are unchecked and the status says HOLD, not "pending, proceeding anyway." A field office that green-lights placements with open items is where the liability actually lives.

## 2. EPAS competency-to-assignment curriculum map (excerpt)

Every graded assignment in a practice course traces to a specific EPAS competency — the artifact CSWE site visitors ask for first.

| Course | Assignment | EPAS Competency | Assessment method | Benchmark (cohort %) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SW 612 Advanced Clinical Practice | Biopsychosocial assessment paper (client composite) | 7 — Assess Individuals, Families, Groups, Orgs, Communities | Rubric, 4-point behaviorally-anchored scale | 80% at "competent" or above |
| SW 612 | Treatment plan + case presentation | 8 — Intervene | Rubric + faculty observation of presentation | 80% |
| SW 614 Field Seminar | Field instructor's mid-semester competency evaluation | 4 — Practice-informed research | Field instructor rating form (Tevera) | 75% |
| SW 620 Policy Practice | Policy analysis memo to a named legislator | 5 — Engage in Policy Practice | Rubric, argument + evidence quality | 80% |
| SW 601 Foundations | Reflective ethics journal (3 entries) | 1 — Ethical and Professional Behavior | Pass/fail on NASW Code of Ethics application | 90% |

**Reading the benchmark column honestly:** an 80% target that the cohort clears at 96% every year without variance is worth checking — either the assignment stopped discriminating between competent and not-yet-competent work, or the rubric has drifted toward automatic passes. A benchmark that's never been failed is a benchmark that isn't measuring anything.

## 3. CSWE benchmark report excerpt (competency outcome data)

What actually gets submitted at the 8-year reaffirmation cycle (and at the year-4 interim report).

```
## Competency 7 — Assess Individuals, Families, Groups, Organizations, Communities

Assessment method: Field instructor evaluation (Tevera form, 4-point scale),
administered at end of advanced-practice field placement.

Cohort data (last 3 cycles):
  2023-24 cohort (n=42): 34/42 = 81.0% rated "competent" or above — MEETS benchmark (80%)
  2024-25 cohort (n=45): 28/45 = 62.2% rated "competent" or above — BELOW benchmark
  2025-26 cohort (n=39): 27/39 = 69.2% rated "competent" or above — BELOW benchmark

Finding: Two consecutive cohorts below the 80% benchmark on Competency 7.

Corrective action (per program's own decision framework — curriculum-level,
not instructor-level, since the shortfall spans multiple field sites and liaisons):
  1. Added a dedicated assessment-skills lab (4 sessions) to SW 612, piloted
     Spring 2026 with one section (n=18) before full rollout.
  2. Revised the Tevera evaluation form's Competency 7 items — prior wording
     conflated "assessment" with "intake paperwork completion," which field
     instructors were rating on documentation speed, not clinical judgment.
  3. Re-benchmark target: 78% for the pilot cohort (partial-semester exposure),
     80% for the first full cohort under the revised curriculum.

Revisit: report interim progress in next year's annual assessment cycle,
ahead of the 8-year reaffirmation site visit.
```

The reconciling numbers matter: 28/45 and 27/39 are stated as raw counts, not just percentages, so a reviewer can verify the math and the denominators are given as full cohorts, not filtered to exclude anyone.

## 4. Tenure-packet scholarship table (Boyer-mapped)

Built to argue the case to a committee that defaults to counting only "discovery" output.

| Boyer category | Evidence | Why it counts as scholarship, not service |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | 2 peer-reviewed articles, *Journal of Social Work Education* & *Families in Society* (n=210 client outcome study) | Original empirical contribution; standard tenure currency |
| Integration | Book chapter synthesizing trauma-informed care literature across 3 practice domains for a CSWE curricular guide | Cross-domain synthesis used by other programs — the discipline's version of a review article |
| Application | Community-based program evaluation for county behavioral health department (contracted, $45k, produced a public report cited in county budget hearings) | Practice-informed research with real institutional consumers, per Boyer's "application" category — not counted as consulting/service |
| Teaching | Peer-reviewed pedagogy article in *Journal of Teaching in Social Work* on the assessment-skills lab redesign (see benchmark excerpt above) | Scholarship *of* teaching — a competency-benchmark fix documented and published, not just a service memo |
| Field liaison service (quantified) | 18 students/semester × 2 site visits × ~3 hrs (travel + observation + write-up) = 108 hrs/semester, 6 semesters = 648 hrs over the review period | Not scholarship, but explicitly quantified in the service narrative so it isn't invisible next to a bench scientist's grant-writing hours |
