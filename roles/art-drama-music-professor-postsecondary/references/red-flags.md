# Red Flags — Smell Tests in Studio/Applied Postsecondary Arts Teaching

Each entry is a trigger for a specific, minutes-long check — not an automatic verdict.

---

### Jury/crit score more than ~15 points below the semester formative average

- **Usually means:** performance-day circumstance (illness, short repertoire/project prep window, unfamiliar panel) rather than a real skill ceiling; second most likely is a panel-calibration problem if several students show the same gap that week.
- **First question:** "Did anything happen in the 72 hours before this, and how much prep time did this specific piece/project actually get?"
- **Data to pull:** practice-room/rehearsal log, health-center note if any, date the repertoire/project was assigned versus the department's minimum prep window.

### More than one student in the same jury/crit week shows the same large formative/terminal gap

- **Usually means:** the panel wasn't calibrated before the session, or the rubric's sub-criteria were applied inconsistently across jurors.
- **First question:** "Did the panel run a norming conversation with the rubric before today?"
- **Data to pull:** individual juror score sheets broken out by sub-criterion, not just the averaged total.

### A tenure dossier lists exhibitions/productions with no juried/invitational distinction

- **Usually means:** the faculty member (or whoever assembled the CV) didn't realize the distinction carries the tenure-equivalent weight of peer review, or is hoping volume substitutes for review status.
- **First question:** "For each entry, was there a blind selection panel, and is there a published or written review?"
- **Data to pull:** the call-for-entry or curatorial invitation for each venue; any published review or catalog essay.

### Applied-lesson adjunct grading distribution sits noticeably tighter or looser than the program norm (e.g., all A-/A range, or a 20+ point spread from the FT faculty's sections)

- **Usually means:** the adjunct is grading to a private-studio personal standard rather than the shared program rubric, most common when they haven't sat through a calibration session.
- **First question:** "When was the last time this instructor scored a reference recording alongside the rest of the applied faculty?"
- **Data to pull:** grade distribution by instructor across the last two terms; calibration-session attendance record.

### A studio area has zero full-time faculty and enrollment above roughly 10 students

- **Usually means:** the area grew past its original staffing plan on adjunct labor alone, which is an accreditation-site-visit risk (NASM/NASAD expect identifiable resident coverage per major applied area).
- **First question:** "Who is the one person a site visitor would interview about this area's curriculum continuity?"
- **Data to pull:** enrollment trend by area over three years; current faculty roster by area and appointment type.

### A crit session's peer-feedback time is dominated by the same 2–3 students across multiple weeks

- **Usually means:** the crit is running open-floor rather than a structured rotation, and the loudest students are absorbing feedback time meant to be distributed.
- **First question:** "Who spoke in the last three sessions, and for how long each?"
- **Data to pull:** a simple speaking-time tally for the last three crit sessions.

### A student's practice-room log shows a sharp drop (e.g., from 5x/week to 1x/week) in the weeks before a terminal assessment

- **Usually means:** a health, financial, or personal issue is displacing practice time, or the student has quietly disengaged from the major — worth a direct conversation before jury/crit week, not after a low score.
- **First question:** "Has anyone talked to this student about what changed in the last three weeks?"
- **Data to pull:** the log itself, and whether the assigned advisor has met with the student this term.

### A required repertoire/project piece was assigned inside half the department's stated minimum prep window

- **Usually means:** a mid-semester substitution (voice-fit issue, injury, a dropped collaborator) compressed the timeline, and the student is walking into the terminal assessment under-prepared through no fault of technique.
- **First question:** "When was this specific piece/project assigned, against the handbook's minimum?"
- **Data to pull:** the assignment date and the handbook's stated minimum preparation window for that assessment type.

### A first barrier-jury or qualifying-crit failure gets escalated straight to a change-of-major conversation

- **Usually means:** whoever is handling it isn't following the accreditor-aligned or department-bylaws probation pathway, either from unfamiliarity with the handbook or from treating the terminal score as dispositive.
- **First question:** "Is this the student's first failure at this barrier, and does the handbook specify a probation-and-remediation step first?"
- **Data to pull:** the student's full jury/crit history and the program handbook's barrier-assessment policy section.

### A tenure-track studio/performance faculty member's CV shows years of extensive course-materials redesign but a thin exhibition/production/recording record

- **Usually means:** time is going into artisanal, non-transferable teaching prep that the tenure committee (which reads creative production, not teaching-materials polish, as the currency) won't credit the same way.
- **First question:** "What of this redesign work is documented in a form an external tenure letter-writer could evaluate?"
- **Data to pull:** the annual activity report's time allocation between teaching-prep hours and creative-production hours over the review period.
