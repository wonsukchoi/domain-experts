# Playbook: Barrier Jury, Structured Crit, Adjunct Staffing, Studio-Art Tenure Dossier

Filled process for the four recurring executions of the role. Reference context: a mid-size university School of the Arts with separate art, theatre, and music divisions.

## 1. Barrier-Jury / Qualifying-Crit Diagnosis (filled example)

Trigger: sophomore vocal-performance barrier jury, pass threshold 70/100, one student scores 61 against a semester formative average of 82.

**Step 1 — pull the split.** Compare formative record against terminal score for every student who scored below threshold that jury week, not just the one flagged case:

| Student | Formative avg (15 wks) | Jury score | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| A (case above) | 82 | 61 | -21 |
| B | 58 | 55 | -3 |
| C | 74 | 68 | -6 |

Student A's -21 gap is an outlier against B and C's small, consistent gaps — a large formative/terminal mismatch is the signal that something event-specific happened, not a studio-wide grading problem.

**Step 2 — circumstance check for the outlier.**

| Factor | Finding |
|---|---|
| Health note on file | Laryngitis, documented, 48 hrs pre-jury |
| Repertoire assigned | 3 weeks before jury (handbook minimum: 8 weeks) |
| Reason for late assignment | Prior piece dropped after voice-fit issue found in coaching |
| Practice-room log | 5 sign-ins/week, full semester, no gaps |

**Step 3 — decide the pathway.** Handbook's barrier-jury policy: first failure → one-semester probation with a faculty-approved remediation plan; second consecutive failure → mandatory major-change conversation. This is a first failure with a documented circumstance gap, so probation applies, not major change.

**Step 4 — remediation plan, with the three checkpoints.**

| Checkpoint | Week | Repertoire | Panel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mock jury 1 | 6 | Previously prepared piece | 2 unfamiliar faculty |
| Mock jury 2 | 12 | Same piece, performance polish | 2 unfamiliar faculty |
| Re-jury | 15 | Same piece | Full 3-person barrier panel |

**Step 5 — track and log.** Scores: 68 → 74 → 79 (passes 70 threshold). Logged in the department's barrier-jury exception file regardless of outcome, so the next accreditation self-study has the base rate of first-failure-to-pass conversions on file (this cohort: 1/1).

## 2. Structured Critique Rotation (filled example)

Context: 14-student junior painting studio, weekly 3-hour crit session.

**Fixed allocation per student (9-minute rotation, 14 students = 126 minutes of the 180-minute block, remainder for transitions and a closing synthesis):**

| Segment | Minutes | Who talks |
|---|---|---|
| Student states intent | 1 | Presenting student only |
| Silent looking | 1 | Whole class, no talking |
| Peer observations | 4 | 3–4 designated peers, round-robin, not open floor |
| Faculty synthesis | 2 | Instructor — names the one technical issue and the one conceptual issue worth carrying forward |
| Student response | 1 | Presenting student |

**Rotation roster (excerpt, prevents the same 2–3 students dominating discussion slots week to week):**

| Week | Peer-observer group |
|---|---|
| 1 | Students 1–4 respond to presenters 5–14 in rotation |
| 2 | Students 5–8 respond to presenters 9–14, 1–4 |
| 3 | Students 9–12 respond to presenters 13–14, 1–8 |

**Fallback if a session runs over:** cut the peer-observation window to 3 minutes before cutting faculty synthesis — the named technical/conceptual note is the piece most likely to change what the student does next, not the fifth peer opinion.

## 3. Applied-Lesson / Studio Staffing Plan (filled example)

Context: music division, 42 students across five applied areas (voice, piano, violin, guitar, percussion) for fall term.

| Area | Students | Current FT faculty | Staffing plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Voice | 18 | 1 | 1 FT (12 students) + 1 adjunct (6 students) |
| Piano | 12 | 1 | 1 FT, no adjunct needed |
| Violin | 6 | 0 | 1 adjunct (working orchestra professional) |
| Guitar | 4 | 0 | 1 adjunct |
| Percussion | 2 | 0 | 1 adjunct, shared load with ensemble director |

**Rule applied:** every area keeps at least one full-time faculty voice where enrollment justifies it (voice, piano); areas with no FT line (violin, guitar, percussion) still get a resident-adjunct with a multi-year reappointment expectation, not a rotating one-off hire, so students get continuity across the degree — and so the area has one identifiable person for a NASM site visit to interview.

**Adjunct calibration session (before term start):** all five applied instructors review the shared jury rubric together and score one recorded reference performance independently; scores are compared and discussed until the spread across instructors is under 8 points on a 100-point scale before the term's actual grading begins.

## 4. Studio-Art Tenure Dossier (filled example)

Context: 5th-year tenure-track review, studio art (sculpture), CAA-aligned department.

**Exhibition/production record, annotated by review type (the annotation is the whole point — an unmarked list reads as a flat CV):**

| Year | Venue | Type | Selection basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2021 | Regional Contemporary Art Center | Juried | Blind panel, 340 submissions, 22 accepted |
| 2022 | University faculty gallery | Invitational | Departmental showcase, not peer-reviewed |
| 2023 | State Triennial | Juried | Blind panel, published catalog with critical essay |
| 2024 | Solo show, commercial gallery | Reviewed | 800-word review in a regional arts publication |
| 2024 | Group show, artist-run space | Invitational | Curated by department colleague |

**Weighting note for the dossier narrative (per CAA standards):** the two juried entries and the reviewed solo show carry the tenure-equivalent weight of peer-reviewed publications; the two invitational entries are listed as service/visibility but explicitly flagged in the narrative as not peer-reviewed, so the all-campus committee doesn't average all five into an undifferentiated "5 exhibitions" line that reads thinner than it is next to a five-publication CV from another department.

**Dossier structure:** teaching statement → annotated exhibition/production table above → student outcome data (studio course grade distributions, crit-rubric trends) → external letters from the juried venues' curators where available → committee-facing one-page summary translating "2 juried, 1 reviewed" into the publication-count-equivalent framing the committee already knows how to read.
