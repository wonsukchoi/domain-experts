# Playbook

Filled templates and step sequences, not descriptions of them. Defaults, not laws — deviate consciously and note why in the record you keep.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| First-time takers | 178 |
| First-time passers | 121 |
| First-time non-passers | 57 |
| Total needed to pass ultimately (75% of 178) | 134 (178 × 0.75 = 133.5, round up) |
| Additional passes needed from the 57 non-passers | 13 |
| Required retake success rate | 13 / 57 = 22.8% |

Rule: never report a first-time rate to a committee or site-visit team without the ultimate-rate table for the last three closed cohorts sitting next to it — a first-time number with no ultimate-rate context invites exactly the panic-read the naive interpretation produces.

## 2. Cold-call panel policy template (1L doctrinal course)

Post at the start of the term, not adjusted mid-semester without notice:

- **Default mode:** on-call panel of 5 students per class session, panel list posted 48 hours in advance via the course platform.
- **Unannounced cold-call carve-out:** reserved for named sessions only (state explicitly in the syllabus which weeks) — typically the week simulating a client interview or negotiation, where the pedagogical objective is performance under unpredictable pressure.
- **Pass mechanism:** each student may pass once per term without penalty, must state readiness on the next question.
- **Documentation:** keep the posted panel schedule and the syllabus language identifying which sessions are unannounced — this is the record a bias or well-being complaint gets checked against.

## 3. Law review submission triage

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| No existing offer, piece has broad general-interest appeal | Submit simultaneously to a tiered list of 30–50 journals (top-30, 31–75, specialty as backup); do not expedite yet |
| No existing offer, piece is a strong specialty fit (e.g., a tax piece to a tax journal) | Submit to the specialty journal alone first; hold 2–3 weeks before going broad |
| Offer received from a lower-tier journal on the list | File expedite requests immediately to every journal ranked above it, with a firm decision deadline (typically 2 business days) stated in the request |
| Expedite deadline passes with no response from higher-ranked journals | Accept the offer in hand — a lapsed expedite window is a "no," not a pending "maybe" |
| Rejected everywhere on first submission cycle | Revise for a named, specific weakness (not a global rewrite) and hold for the next cycle (typically August or February) rather than resubmitting the identical draft mid-cycle |

## 4. Faculty-track reappointment evidence structure

Build the file against the criteria that actually govern the track — mixing them is the single most common reappointment-committee error.

| Track | Governing standard | Core evidence | Do NOT weight heavily |
|---|---|---|---|
| Doctrinal, tenure-track | ABA 405(b); AAUP norms | Publication log (venue, placement tier, SSRN rank); teaching evaluations with response rate stated; committee service log | Clinical caseload metrics (not this track's job) |
| Clinical | ABA 405(c) | Student case outcomes and supervision logs; peer classroom/clinic observation; client and court feedback where available; program administration record | Law review placement (not the deliverable this track produces) |
| Legal writing | ABA 405(d) | Student writing-portfolio improvement evidence; program-level curriculum design contribution; teaching evaluations | Traditional scholarship output, unless the school's own written policy explicitly requires it for this track |

| Year 3 | Doctrinal | Placements (tier) | 1 top-30, 2 specialty | 1 top-50 | No |

## 5. Bar-passage academic-support triage (by 1L GPA percentile)

| Fall 1L GPA percentile band | Historical first-time pass rate for band | Default action |
|---|---|---|
| Top 50% | ~92% | No mandatory intervention |
| 50th–75th percentile (from bottom) | ~81% | Voluntary academic-support referral |
| 75th–90th percentile (from bottom) | ~64% | Mandatory academic-support enrollment |
| Bottom 10% | ~41% | Mandatory enrollment plus individualized subject-area remediation plan, reviewed each semester |

Bands and rates are school-specific historical data to be rebuilt from the institution's own cohort records each year — the table above is the shape to populate, not a transferable number.
