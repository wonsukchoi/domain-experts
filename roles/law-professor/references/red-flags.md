# Red flags

### First-time bar passage rate more than 10 points below the jurisdiction average for a single sitting
- **Usually means:** a weak cohort's LSAT/UGPA entering credentials, a curriculum-content gap versus what's tested, or a one-time disruption (exam software failure, a subject not covered before a scope change) — rarely an accreditation violation by itself.
- **First question:** what does the ultimate two-year rate look like for the last three closed cohorts, and what's the historical retake success rate in this jurisdiction?
- **Data to pull:** the Standard 316 compliance table (last 3 closed cohorts), the retake-need calculation for the open cohort, and the subject-area score breakdown for the sitting.

### Ultimate two-year bar passage rate for a closed cohort under 78%
- **Usually means:** the school is inside the 75% floor's margin of error and one bad retake cycle away from a Standard 316 finding.
- **First question:** is the trend across the last three closed cohorts declining, flat, or a single-year outlier?
- **Data to pull:** three-cohort ultimate-rate trend line, entering-credential data (LSAT/UGPA) for the affected cohort versus prior cohorts.

### A tenure or clinical-review file with no evidence table, only narrative self-assessment
- **Usually means:** the file was assembled to read well rather than to survive committee scrutiny, or the candidate genuinely lacks the quantified record the track requires.
- **First question:** does the department's written tenure/clinical-review document specify required evidence categories, and is each one populated with a number or a log, not a sentence?
- **Data to pull:** the department's written review-criteria document and the candidate's raw logs (publication, supervision hours, evaluations) before any narrative is drafted.

### A clinical or legal-writing faculty review scored against traditional scholarship output
- **Usually means:** the reviewing committee defaulted to the doctrinal-track rubric out of habit rather than consulting the school's 405(c)/(d) policy.
- **First question:** does the written faculty-track policy for this position require law-review-style scholarship, or does it specify supervision/pedagogy evidence instead?
- **Data to pull:** the school's faculty handbook section defining the specific track's review criteria.

### A classroom cold-call complaint citing bias or unfair targeting
- **Usually means:** either a genuine pattern in who gets called (worth checking), or a single unannounced cold-call session the student experienced as arbitrary because the syllabus didn't flag it as a planned unannounced week.
- **First question:** was the on-call panel policy posted in advance, and does the syllabus name which sessions (if any) were unannounced by design?
- **Data to pull:** the posted panel schedule, the syllabus method-policy language, and a tally of which students were called over the term to check for an actual pattern.

### A single term's overall teaching-evaluation score dropping alongside a grading-curve dispute
- **Usually means:** students conflating dissatisfaction with the mandated curve outcome for dissatisfaction with the instructor, especially in a required 1L course with a hard median.
- **First question:** does the item-level breakdown show the drop concentrated in "fairness of grading" specifically, or spread across unrelated items too?
- **Data to pull:** item-level evaluation breakdown, the section's actual grade distribution against the school's mandated curve, and the department mean for the same course.

### A manuscript held at a single journal for more than 3 weeks with no other submissions out
- **Usually means:** sequential single-journal submission instead of the field's simultaneous-submission norm, which is losing placement time for no corresponding gain in acceptance odds.
- **First question:** has the piece gone out simultaneously to a tiered list, or is it sitting with one journal alone?
- **Data to pull:** the submission log (journal, date submitted, tier, status) and the current point in the placement cycle (August or February).

### An expedite request filed with no offer in hand
- **Usually means:** the request will be ignored or read as a bluff by higher-ranked journals, since editors generally only move on expedites backed by a real competing offer.
- **First question:** is there an actual offer letter, and from which tier of journal?
- **Data to pull:** the offer letter and its stated acceptance deadline before any expedite email goes out.

### A 1L student in the bottom GPA decile who was only voluntarily referred to academic support
- **Usually means:** the school's academic-support triage table wasn't consulted, or was consulted and the mandatory-enrollment threshold was treated as optional.
- **First question:** what is this student's actual percentile band, and what does the school's own historical pass-rate-by-band table say for that band?
- **Data to pull:** the student's fall 1L GPA percentile and the school's academic-support triage table with historical pass rates per band.

### A curriculum committee memo asserting Standard 316 compliance with no supporting table
- **Usually means:** the assurance is a narrative read of the newest first-time number rather than a check against the actual two-year ultimate-rate metric.
- **First question:** which cohort's ultimate rate is this claim based on, and has its two-year window actually closed?
- **Data to pull:** the Standard 316 compliance table for the last three closed cohorts, cited by graduation year.
