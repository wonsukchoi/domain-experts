# Red flags

### Effluent BOD5 or TSS trending above 80% of the permit limit for 2+ consecutive months
- **Usually means:** organic/solids loading has crept up (new customer, seasonal load) faster than the plant's biological capacity, or a unit process (clarifier, aeration blower) is degrading.
- **First question:** has influent loading (not just flow) increased over the same period?
- **Data to pull:** 12-month DMR history for influent and effluent concentration and flow, plus any maintenance log for the aeration/clarification train.

### Three or more valid exceedances in a rolling 12-month window
- **Usually means:** most state agencies and NPDES permits treat this as an automatic escalation trigger regardless of exceedance magnitude — a pattern of small exceedances draws more scrutiny than one large one.
- **First question:** were all counted exceedances validated against the permit's specified sample type and method, or does one drop out on a technicality?
- **Data to pull:** DMR submissions for the trailing 12 months with sample-method annotations.

### A process or production change that increases flow but not obviously loading
- **Usually means:** hydraulic capacity looks fine while biological/mass loading capacity is quietly exceeded — the most common blind spot in capacity sign-offs.
- **First question:** what is the mass loading (lb/day), not just the flow (gpd), before and after the change?
- **Data to pull:** influent concentration sampling bracketing the change, original design report's rated mass loading.

### Potential-to-emit calculation that lands within 10% of a major-source threshold
- **Usually means:** the facility is at real risk of becoming a major source under PSD/NSR on its next permitted increase, even a small one.
- **First question:** is the facility currently operating under a synthetic minor source limit, and does it have documented headroom?
- **Data to pull:** current air permit's PTE limits per pollutant, county attainment designation, most recent emissions inventory.

### A remediation site with contamination above screening levels but no completed exposure pathway analysis on file
- **Usually means:** the site may be over-remediated (spending on excavation where monitoring would satisfy the regulator) or under-assessed (missing a vapor intrusion pathway to an occupied structure).
- **First question:** has a conceptual site model (CSM) been completed identifying source, pathway, and receptor?
- **Data to pull:** most recent RI/FS or site characterization report, nearest occupied structure distances.

### Ammonia or nitrate rising in effluent while BOD5/TSS stay in compliance
- **Usually means:** nitrification is failing — often from insufficient sludge age (SRT) after an MLSS or loading change, a temperature drop, or a toxic influent slug (industrial pretreatment upset upstream).
- **First question:** has sludge retention time (SRT) or MLSS changed in the same window as the ammonia rise?
- **Data to pull:** SRT/MLSS operating logs, industrial user monitoring data if any upstream pretreatment discharges feed the plant.

### A sampling result relying on a grab sample where the permit specifies composite (or vice versa)
- **Usually means:** the result is not defensible as a compliance determination regardless of the number — this is a common way an apparent exceedance turns out to be invalid, or a compliant result turns out not to count.
- **First question:** what sample type does the specific permit condition require for this parameter?
- **Data to pull:** the permit's monitoring and reporting requirements section, not the general boilerplate.

### A vendor-provided treatment system design guarantee with no site-specific influent characterization behind it
- **Usually means:** the vendor sized to a generic wastewater profile, not this facility's actual loading — a frequent cause of underperformance discovered only after installation.
- **First question:** what influent data (concentration, flow variability, temperature) did the vendor use to size the system?
- **Data to pull:** the vendor's design basis memo and at least 90 days of actual influent sampling.

### A air dispersion model (AERMOD or similar) run with default meteorological data instead of site-representative data
- **Usually means:** the modeled ambient impact may not reflect actual site conditions (terrain, nearby structures, local meteorology), which regulators increasingly flag on review.
- **First question:** was 5-year National Weather Service data from the nearest representative station used, or a generic default dataset?
- **Data to pull:** the model input file's meteorological data source and station ID.
