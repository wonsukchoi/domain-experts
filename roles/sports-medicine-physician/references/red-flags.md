# Red Flags

## Rectal temperature >40°C with altered mentation, and cooling not yet started at minute 5 post-collapse
- **Usually means:** Transport was prioritized over on-site cold-water immersion, or immersion equipment wasn't pre-positioned for the event; second: temperature was measured by an unreliable route (oral/tympanic) masking the true core temperature.
- **First question:** Is a cold-water immersion tub on-site right now, and has it been started?
- **Data to pull:** Rectal temperature trend, time-of-collapse to time-cooling-started interval, ambient wet-bulb globe temperature at event time.

## Same-day return-to-play request after a diagnosed concussion
- **Usually means:** Coach, parent, or athlete pressure to return before any graduated-protocol stage has been completed; second: a genuinely mild presentation being conflated with "no concussion occurred."
- **First question:** Which GRTS stage has actually been completed and confirmed symptom-free for 24 hours?
- **Data to pull:** SCAT6 symptom score trend, GRTS stage log with dates, any medication given that could mask symptoms (analgesics, stimulants).

## Positive item on the AHA 14-element screen without a completed ECG or cardiology referral
- **Usually means:** The screen was administered but a positive item wasn't escalated before clearance was signed; second: a borderline family-history item was judged "probably nothing" by the examiner rather than referred.
- **First question:** Which of the 14 elements was positive, and has it been escalated to ECG/echo and sports cardiology?
- **Data to pull:** Completed PPE form, ECG if obtained, cardiology referral status.

## Second bone stress injury at the same or a different site within 12 months
- **Usually means:** An underlying RED-S/energy-availability problem was never worked up after the first fracture; second: a training-load error (rapid volume increase) repeated without a load-management change.
- **First question:** Was a menstrual history and training-log review done after the first stress fracture, or was it treated as an isolated event?
- **Data to pull:** Prior injury history, menstrual cycle history, training log volume trend, DEXA if obtained.

## Team physician signing a clearance the same week as a conflict-of-interest-relevant event (playoff game, contract year, scholarship decision)
- **Usually means:** External pressure influenced the timing or threshold of a borderline clearance; second: coincidental timing with no actual threshold change, which still needs to be documented as such.
- **First question:** Would this exact clearance have been given on the same objective findings in a meaningless mid-season game?
- **Data to pull:** Clearance note with dated objective criteria, prior similar decisions for the same finding type at this program.

## Sickle cell trait-positive athlete placed on full activity restriction rather than a graduated conditioning plan
- **Usually means:** Blanket disqualification substituting for the standard graduated acclimatization and hydration protocol; second: a genuinely symptomatic prior sickling event that does warrant more caution, not addressed as a distinct case.
- **First question:** Is this a graduated-conditioning management plan or a blanket exclusion, and is there a prior exertional sickling event on record?
- **Data to pull:** Sickle cell trait screening result, conditioning plan on file, any prior exertional collapse history.

## Preparticipation ECG read as "abnormal" using non-Seattle-criteria interpretation
- **Usually means:** The reading physician isn't using athlete-specific interpretation standards and is flagging normal training-related findings (isolated voltage-only LVH criteria, early repolarization) as pathologic.
- **First question:** Was this ECG re-read against the Seattle criteria specifically, and by someone with sports-cardiology ECG experience?
- **Data to pull:** Original ECG tracing, interpreting physician's credentials/experience, any subsequent echo or cardiology re-read.

## Amenorrhea documented in the chart with no bone density or dietary workup ordered
- **Usually means:** The finding was noted but not connected to the RED-S framework, especially if there's no concurrent injury prompting a "cluster" read; second: the athlete declined further workup and that decline isn't documented.
- **First question:** Has a training log, dietary screen, or DEXA been ordered, or does the chart stop at "amenorrhea noted"?
- **Data to pull:** Menstrual history, training log, LEAF-Q or equivalent screen, DEXA order status.

## Athlete cleared to return based on pain-free status alone, with no functional/objective test performed
- **Usually means:** RICE-phase comfort was mistaken for rehabilitation completion; second: time pressure (game day) shortcut the objective testing step that would normally gate clearance.
- **First question:** What objective test (hop test, strength symmetry, range of motion) was performed, versus relying on the athlete's subjective report alone?
- **Data to pull:** Clearance note, any documented functional testing results, comparison to contralateral limb or baseline.

## Exertional collapse in a young athlete with a family history item never asked about at the original PPE
- **Usually means:** The 14-element screen was administered as a checklist without follow-up depth on a vague-sounding family history answer (e.g., "grandpa died young, not sure why").
- **First question:** Was the family history item pursued to an actual cause of death, or accepted at face value?
- **Data to pull:** Original PPE form, any follow-up documentation on ambiguous family-history answers.
