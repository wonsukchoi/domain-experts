# Sports Medicine Playbook

Filled tools a sports medicine physician actually runs — protocol tables with real stages and thresholds, screening checklists, and a clearance note template. Populate with the specific athlete's numbers; don't describe the protocol, run it.

## 1. Graduated Return to Sport (GRTS) — concussion, 6 stages, minimum 24h each

| Stage | Activity | Goal | Advance criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Symptom-limited activity (daily life, no exercise) | Gradual return to typical activity | Tolerates activities of daily living without symptom exacerbation |
| 2 | Light aerobic exercise (walk/stationary bike, <70% max HR) | Increase heart rate | No symptom increase during or after |
| 3 | Sport-specific exercise (running/skating drills, no head-impact risk) | Add movement | No symptom increase |
| 4 | Non-contact training drills (passing drills, resistance training) | Exercise, coordination, cognitive load | No symptom increase; may start light resistance training |
| 5 | Full-contact practice (following medical clearance) | Restore confidence, assess functional skills | Normal neurocognitive/balance testing, full-contact tolerated |
| 6 | Return to sport (competition) | — | Full clearance only after stage 5 completed symptom-free |

Any symptom recurrence at a stage: drop back to the prior symptom-free stage for a minimum 24 hours before re-attempting. No stage may be skipped. No same-day return once concussion is diagnosed — this is a multi-day minimum protocol regardless of how symptom-free the athlete reports feeling at stage 1.

## 2. AHA 14-element preparticipation cardiovascular screen

Personal history (items 1–5): exertional chest pain/discomfort; exertional syncope/near-syncope; excessive/unexplained exertional dyspnea or fatigue; prior recognition of a heart murmur; elevated systolic blood pressure.

Personal history (items 6–8): prior restriction from sports; prior testing for cardiac reasons (ECG, echo).

Family history (items 9–12): premature death (sudden or otherwise, <50 years old) in ≥1 relative; disability from heart disease in a close relative <50; specific knowledge of hypertrophic or dilated cardiomyopathy, long-QT or other ion channelopathy, Marfan syndrome, or clinically important arrhythmia in a family member; premature (<50) sudden cardiac death in ≥1 relative or unexplained near-drowning/single-vehicle accident.

Physical exam (items 13–14): heart murmur (supine and standing/Valsalva), femoral pulses to exclude coarctation, physical stigmata of Marfan syndrome, brachial artery blood pressure (sitting).

**Any positive item → ECG plus sports-cardiology referral before clearance**, not physician discretion alone.

## 3. Seattle criteria — borderline ECG re-read categories

| Finding | Category | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Isolated QRS voltage criteria for LVH | Normal training-related | No further workup required if isolated |
| Sinus bradycardia >30 bpm, 1st-degree AV block, incomplete RBBB, early repolarization | Normal training-related | No further workup if isolated and asymptomatic |
| T-wave inversion beyond V1–V2, pathologic Q waves, ST depression, complete bundle branch block, profound axis deviation, ventricular pre-excitation | Abnormal — refer | Echocardiogram + sports cardiology before clearance |

Isolated normal-training-related findings should not trigger disqualification; ≥1 abnormal-category finding requires full cardiology workup before any RTP decision.

## 4. Fredericson MRI grading — bone stress injury and graduated return

| Grade | MRI finding | Typical return timeline (guideline, adjust to clinical trend) |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Periosteal edema only, no marrow edema | 3–4 weeks progressive loading |
| 2 | Periosteal + mild-moderate marrow edema on T2 | 4–6 weeks |
| 3 | Marrow edema on T1 and T2 (more severe) | 6–9 weeks |
| 4a | Discrete linear fracture line, marrow edema present | 9–12+ weeks, orthopedic co-management |
| 4b | Fracture line at a high-risk site (anterior tibial cortex, femoral neck tension side, navicular) | Non-weight-bearing until healing confirmed; surgical consult |

Return to running only after a pain-free walk-to-jog progression at the grade-appropriate week mark; a repeat stress fracture at the same site within 12 months triggers a RED-S / bone-health workup (below), not just a longer rest period.

## 5. RED-S / energy-availability screen (LEAF-Q anchored)

Screen when any two of the following co-occur: menstrual irregularity (>3 months of irregular cycles or primary/secondary amenorrhea), a bone stress injury (any grade), and a training load increase without a matched increase in caloric intake.

| Component | Threshold flag |
|---|---|
| Menstrual history | <9 cycles/year, or primary amenorrhea by age 15 |
| Bone density (DEXA, if obtained) | Z-score below −1.0 for age-matched athletes is a flag; below −2.0 is a diagnostic criterion |
| Training log | Volume increase >10%/week sustained over >3 weeks without matched fueling increase |
| Eating behavior (LEAF-Q or clinical interview) | Any restrictive pattern, skipped meals around training, or reported energy availability concern |

**Two or more flags present → manage as one RED-S case**: sports dietitian referral, bone density baseline, and training-load reduction together, not as three independent referrals.

## 6. Exertional heat stroke — cold water immersion sequence

1. Recognize: altered mentation + hyperthermia in an exertional/heat context. Confirm with rectal thermometry (oral/tympanic/axillary are unreliable in this setting).
2. Immerse to the neck in an ice/cold-water tub (35–59°F / 1.7–15°C water) within minutes of recognition — do not wait for ambulance arrival.
3. Recheck rectal temperature every 5 minutes; continue immersion until 38.9°C (102°F) is reached — field cooling rates run roughly 0.15–0.2°C/min, so a 3°C drop takes on the order of 15–20 minutes.
4. Extract at 38.9°C (overcooling past this risks iatrogenic hypothermia) and transport, or continue monitoring on-site if transport isn't otherwise indicated.
5. Labs on arrival at definitive care: CK, renal panel, hepatic panel; admit for cardiac monitoring for 24 hours given rhabdomyolysis and arrhythmia risk.
6. No return to training for minimum 7 days; return via a graduated heat-acclimatization protocol (progressive duration/intensity in the heat over 1–2 weeks) with physician sign-off at each stage, not a fixed calendar date alone.

## 7. Return-to-play clearance note template (filled example)

> "Diagnosis: [specific finding, e.g., 'Grade 2 tibial bone stress injury, Fredericson MRI grading']. Protocol stage completed: [e.g., 'Week 5 of 6, pain-free walk-jog progression, no point tenderness on exam']. Objective criteria met: [e.g., 'Single-leg hop test within 10% of contralateral limb, no pain at deep palpation']. Clearance status: [e.g., 'Cleared for full-intensity training, competition clearance pending one additional pain-free week']. Restrictions: [named, specific — e.g., 'No plyometric loading until week 6 recheck']. This clearance reflects objective criteria only and does not change based on competition schedule."
