# Red flags

### Sustained, continuous grinding contact on hardened steel with no cooling pauses
- **Usually means:** heat is building up at the contact point beyond what's visible, risking localized temper drawing.
- **First question:** has the part's temperature been checked periodically, or is contact continuing based only on the absence of visible discoloration?
- **Data to pull:** grinding duration and contact pattern, any temperature checks performed, material's heat sensitivity classification.

### A hardened, hardness-spec-critical part released based on dimensional/visual inspection alone after hand grinding
- **Usually means:** hardness at the ground surface hasn't been independently verified, and heat-affected softening wouldn't show in a dimensional or visual check.
- **First question:** has a hardness test been performed at or near the ground surface, or only dimensional/visual inspection?
- **Data to pull:** the part's hardness specification, any hardness verification test result.

### An abrasive grit stage skipped to save time
- **Usually means:** the scratch pattern from the prior, coarser stage may not be fully removable by the next stage in a reasonable time, risking a defect hidden under an apparently polished surface.
- **First question:** does the surface show a consistent scratch pattern from the current grit, or remnants from a skipped stage?
- **Data to pull:** the specified grit progression sequence, actual stages performed.

### A tight-tolerance part hand-finished with dimensional checks only at the end of the process
- **Usually means:** uneven material removal during the process wasn't caught early, risking an out-of-tolerance part discovered only after finishing is complete.
- **First question:** were dimensional checks performed periodically during grinding, or only at final inspection?
- **Data to pull:** dimensional check frequency and results during the process, part's tolerance specification.

### Grinding/polishing media used across different metal types without dedicated separation
- **Usually means:** cross-contamination risk — iron particles from one metal embedding into a dissimilar metal (e.g., stainless steel), risking a later corrosion defect.
- **First question:** has this specific media been used exclusively on this metal type, or shared across dissimilar metals?
- **Data to pull:** media usage/tracking log, metal types processed with this specific wheel/belt.

### Rust spots or surface corrosion appearing on a stainless steel part after some time in service or storage
- **Usually means:** likely cross-contamination from shared grinding/polishing media during finishing, not an inherent material defect.
- **First question:** was dedicated, stainless-only media used during this part's finishing process?
- **Data to pull:** finishing process record for this part, media dedication log if available.

### A surface finish measurement not performed, with pass/fail determined by visual/tactile judgment alone
- **Usually means:** the actual roughness value relative to spec hasn't been confirmed, only a subjective assessment.
- **First question:** has a profilometer or comparator measurement been taken, or is finish acceptance based on appearance/feel alone?
- **Data to pull:** surface finish specification, actual measurement if performed.

### A part with both a tight surface finish spec and a tight dimensional tolerance, finished without checking their combined effect
- **Usually means:** achieving the specified finish may have consumed more of the dimensional tolerance allowance than accounted for.
- **First question:** does the material removed to achieve the specified finish still leave the part within its dimensional tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** dimensional measurement after finishing, material removal estimate for the finishing process used.

### A hand-finishing job handed off between workers without specifying the current grit stage or heat-sensitive areas already worked
- **Usually means:** the incoming worker lacks the information needed to continue the process correctly (risk of skipping/repeating a grit stage, or reheating an already-worked area).
- **First question:** does the handoff specify exactly which grit stage was last completed and any heat-sensitive handling notes?
- **Data to pull:** the handoff record, current part status.

### A worker reporting they "always use" the same media/wheel regardless of the material being processed
- **Usually means:** media dedication by material type isn't being actively maintained, creating ongoing cross-contamination risk.
- **First question:** does this worker's typical media use account for the different metal types actually processed on this bench?
- **Data to pull:** the range of materials processed at this workstation, actual media dedication practice.
