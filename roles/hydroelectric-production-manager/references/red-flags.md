# Red flags — what a production manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A generation plan built to hit an output target with no explicit check against remaining irrigation and ecological flow obligations
- **Usually means:** the plan may be treating generation as the sole objective rather than one of several legally constrained obligations on the same water.
- **First question:** "What are the remaining binding water obligations for the rest of the season, and does this generation plan leave enough storage to meet them?"
- **Data to pull:** current reservoir storage, remaining contracted irrigation delivery, ecological minimum flow requirement for the remaining period.

### Reservoir operating decisions based only on the median hydrologic forecast with no dry- or wet-scenario check
- **Usually means:** the plan is unprepared for either a drought (insufficient water for commitments) or flood (insufficient storage) scenario that a wider forecast range would have anticipated.
- **First question:** "What does this operating plan look like under the dry-scenario forecast, not just the median case?"
- **Data to pull:** the hydrologic forecast's scenario range (wet/median/dry), current storage against each scenario's implied need.

### A dam safety-relevant inspection or repair deferred for budget or scheduling reasons
- **Usually means:** a categorically different risk (structural failure, uncontrolled release) is being treated with the same risk tolerance as routine equipment maintenance, when the consequence asymmetry doesn't support that.
- **First question:** "Is this specifically a dam-safety-relevant item, and if so, why is it competing with budget-negotiable maintenance items?"
- **Data to pull:** the item's safety classification, current budget/scheduling status relative to safety-critical prioritization.

### Reserved flood control storage capacity being used for generation optimization during a period when storm risk is still present
- **Usually means:** a standing flood-control obligation is being treated as a flexible buffer, undermining the facility's actual flood-risk-management function.
- **First question:** "Is storm risk still present for this period, and does current storage still respect the flood control reservation?"
- **Data to pull:** current reservoir level against the flood control storage requirement, storm-risk forecast for the remaining period.

### Ecological minimum flow being met at exactly the minimum threshold with no margin, especially during a sensitive biological period (spawning, migration)
- **Usually means:** the requirement may be being minimally, technically satisfied rather than genuinely meeting its ecological intent, inviting regulatory and reputational risk.
- **First question:** "Does this release plan meet the letter of the minimum flow requirement, or does it also account for the specific ecological timing (spawning, migration) it's meant to protect?"
- **Data to pull:** actual release data against required minimum flow, timing of any sensitive biological periods this season.

### A structural monitoring anomaly (seepage, movement, spillway condition change) noted but not immediately escalated for investigation
- **Usually means:** slow-developing structural deterioration can progress invisibly until it becomes a serious safety issue — any anomaly deserves immediate investigation, not routine-cycle attention.
- **First question:** "Has this anomaly been investigated immediately, or is it queued for the next routine inspection cycle?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific monitoring data/anomaly, current investigation status and timeline.

### A generation revenue shortfall from a water-availability constraint discovered mid-season rather than communicated proactively when the constraint became known
- **Usually means:** stakeholders are learning about a tradeoff after the fact rather than being told as soon as the water-balance math made it clear, risking a larger trust and planning problem.
- **First question:** "When did we know this constraint would affect generation output, and has that been communicated to stakeholders yet?"
- **Data to pull:** timeline of when the water-availability constraint became evident, stakeholder communication log.

### A coordination agreement with another water user (irrigation district, municipal authority) that hasn't been reviewed against actual current-year conditions
- **Usually means:** an operating decision may unintentionally conflict with another party's legitimate claim on the same water if the agreement's assumptions haven't been checked against this year's actual hydrology.
- **First question:** "Does this year's actual water availability still support the assumptions this coordination agreement was built on?"
- **Data to pull:** the coordination agreement's terms and underlying assumptions, current-year actual versus assumed water availability.
