# Private Detective and Investigator — Playbook

## Engagement-letter scope template (filled example)

**Client:** [Attorney/individual name] | **Matter:** Support-modification pre-litigation investigation
**Retainer:** $3,000 ($2,750 field/research + $250 flat background-research fee)
**Rate:** $85/hr field surveillance, billed in 15-minute increments; mileage at $0.67/mi

| Scope item | Included | Excluded |
|---|---|---|
| Public-records research (property, voter, court, UCC) | Yes | Sealed/expunged records |
| Utility/service-connection records | Yes, where legally accessible | Direct utility-company account access without subpoena |
| Vehicle registration lookup | Yes, DMV public-record tier | Real-time GPS tracking of non-client-owned vehicle |
| Surveillance — public vantage points | Yes, up to 30 hrs | Private-property vantage points, drone overflight |
| Audio recording | No — jurisdiction is two-party consent | — |
| Social media research | Public-profile content only | Pretexting to gain private-profile access |
| Pretext phone calls | Yes, non-fraudulent locate confirmation only | Impersonating law enforcement or a government agency (always excluded, all jurisdictions) |

**Reporting format:** Written summary with dated log excerpts; photographs/video provided as separate exhibit; no conclusions of fact beyond what is directly observed.

## Skip-trace corroboration checklist

For a located-subject conclusion to move from "lead" to "confirmed location," at least **2 of the following must independently agree**:

1. Utility/service connection at the address, dated within 90 days
2. Vehicle registration or insurance address, dated within 12 months
3. Recent (within 30 days) social-media geotag or check-in
4. USPS-confirmed mail delivery (via public collection-point observation, never mail interception)
5. Direct visual confirmation (surveillance)

A single data-broker hit (people-search aggregator) **never counts alone** — these sources frequently carry stale or purchased-list data 6–24 months out of date.

## Background-check permissible-purpose gate (FCRA)

Before running any consumer-report-style search (credit header, criminal-records aggregator with FCRA-covered content):

| Client's stated purpose | Permissible under FCRA? | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Employment screening | Yes, with disclosure/authorization on file | Proceed, require signed FCRA disclosure form |
| Tenant screening | Yes, with disclosure/authorization on file | Proceed, require signed disclosure |
| Personal curiosity ("is my neighbor a felon") | No | Decline consumer-report search; offer public-records-only research |
| Pre-litigation discovery support (attorney client) | Case-by-case — often falls outside FCRA if attorney-directed and not employment/credit/insurance-related | Confirm with retaining attorney in writing before proceeding |
| Dating/personal-safety check on an individual | No | Decline; offer public sex-offender registry search only (separately permissible) |
