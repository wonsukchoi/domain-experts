# Red flags

Smell tests a head chef checks in the first ten minutes of a P&L or line review, before assigning blame to a person or a supplier.

### Actual food cost running >3 points over theoretical (recipe-costed) food cost

- **Usually means:** unweighed portions at the line, unlogged comps/voids, or a receiving shortfall — in that order of likelihood. Rarely a menu-pricing problem on its own.
- **First question:** "Pull the last two weeks of comp/void logs and do a spot portion-weigh on the three highest-mix items right now."
- **Data to pull:** POS comp/void report by server and reason code; scale-weighed portions vs. recipe spec on the top 3 sellers; last four weeks of receiving logs for scale-check gaps.

### Prime cost above 65% of revenue

- **Usually means:** either the menu is underpriced/miscosted for its labor intensity, or the schedule is built to habit instead of forecast — check both, because fixing only one can make the other worse.
- **First question:** "Show me food cost % and labor % separately for the last four weeks — which one moved, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly food cost % and labor % trend; sales-per-labor-hour by shift; theoretical vs. actual food cost for the same period.

### One item is 40%+ of a category's unit mix

- **Usually means:** the item is either a genuine Star carrying the menu, or a Plowhorse whose margin is quietly subsidizing everything around it — the classification depends entirely on its contribution margin versus the category average, not its popularity.
- **First question:** "What's this item's contribution margin against the category average, and has its recipe been re-costed in the last 90 days?"
- **Data to pull:** current plate-costing card for the item; category average CM from the latest menu-engineering worksheet; ingredient price history for its top 2 cost components.

### A walk-in or reach-in fails a temperature check twice in 24 hours

- **Usually means:** a door seal, compressor, or thermostat fault — not "the door was open a lot" past the first failure. A second failure on the same unit is a maintenance ticket, not a coaching moment.
- **First question:** "What's in that unit right now, and has any of it been out of range for more than 4 cumulative hours?"
- **Data to pull:** the unit's temp log for the last 48 hours; a list of product currently stored in it with received dates; the maintenance/repair history for that unit over the last 6 months.

### A vendor's invoice price has crept up over three consecutive deliveries with no notice

- **Usually means:** a price increase being absorbed quietly rather than communicated, or a spec substitution (lower grade, smaller case count) at the same nominal price — check the spec before assuming it's a straight price hike.
- **First question:** "Pull the last three invoices for this item side by side — same case size, same grade, same unit price basis?"
- **Data to pull:** trailing three invoices; the original contract/quote sheet; competing quotes from at least one alternate vendor for the same spec.

### A dish gets 86'd more than twice in a month for the same ingredient

- **Usually means:** the par level is set below actual velocity, or the vendor's delivery schedule doesn't match the kitchen's prep cycle — not that the dish is simply "popular," which is a symptom, not a cause.
- **First question:** "What's the par on that ingredient, and what's the actual weekly draw against it for the last month?"
- **Data to pull:** par/max sheet for the ingredient; POS mix data for every dish that uses it; vendor delivery schedule vs. the kitchen's prep and ordering calendar.

### Kitchen labor scheduled flat week-to-week regardless of forecasted covers

- **Usually means:** the schedule is being copied forward from habit rather than built from a reservation/forecast number, which is the most common driver of prime-cost drift on the labor side.
- **First question:** "Walk me through how this week's schedule was built — off what forecast, and against what SPLH target?"
- **Data to pull:** forecasted covers vs. actual covers for the last 4 weeks; scheduled labor hours vs. SPLH target by shift; overtime hours by employee.

### A new hire's health-card/food-handler certification isn't on file before their first shift on the line

- **Usually means:** onboarding paperwork is trailing behind staffing urgency — an operational gap that becomes a legal liability the moment an inspector asks for it, not just a paperwork delay.
- **First question:** "Is this person certified, and if not, are they on a supervised station only until they are?"
- **Data to pull:** the certification file for every active kitchen employee; the jurisdiction's required certification and renewal interval; the onboarding checklist showing where the gap occurred.

### A signature or long-running menu item hasn't been re-costed in over 6 months

- **Usually means:** ingredient prices and yields have moved since the last costing pass, and the item's actual food cost % is now unknown rather than simply stable — "it's always been fine" is not a costing cycle.
- **First question:** "When was this item's plate cost last recalculated, and against which vendor's current prices?"
- **Data to pull:** the item's costing card date vs. today; current AP prices for its top components; yield % test results if trim/waste hasn't been re-measured recently.
