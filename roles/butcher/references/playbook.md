# Playbook

## Value-allocation cost calculation (filled example)

100 lb ribeye subprimal, $9.00/lb purchase price, $900 total cost.

| Product | Weight (lb) | Market value/lb | Potential revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boneless ribeye steaks | 60 | $18.00 | $1,080.00 |
| Trim (ground) | 25 | $5.00 | $125.00 |
| Fat (rendering) | 10 | $0.50 | $5.00 |
| Bone/loss | 5 | $0.00 | $0.00 |
| **Total** | **100** | — | **$1,210.00** |

| Product | Revenue share | Allocated cost | Cost per lb |
|---|---|---|---|
| Steak | 1,080 / 1,210 = 89.26% | $900 × 0.8926 = $803.31 | $803.31 / 60 = $13.39/lb |
| Trim | 125 / 1,210 = 10.33% | $900 × 0.1033 = $92.98 | $92.98 / 25 = $3.72/lb |
| Fat | 5 / 1,210 = 0.41% | $900 × 0.0041 = $3.72 | $3.72 / 10 = $0.37/lb |
| Bone/loss | 0% | $0.00 | — |
| **Check total** | 100% | $803.31 + $92.98 + $3.72 = $900.01 | matches $900 within rounding |

Naive cost per pound of steak using purchase price alone: $9.00/lb. Actual value-allocated cost: $13.39/lb — 48.8% higher.

## Dry-aging weight-loss pricing (filled example)

Ribeye subprimal enters dry-aging at 60 lb (green weight), $13.39/lb allocated cost basis (from above) = $803.40 total cost tied up in this piece.

| Stage | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Start of dry-age (green weight) | 60 lb | Cost basis: $803.40 total |
| End of dry-age, 28 days | 46.8 lb | 22% weight loss from moisture evaporation |
| Additional trim loss (dried pellicle removed) | 42.1 lb | Further 10% loss from removing the hardened exterior crust |

**Naive pricing:** Price the finished, trimmed product ($803.40 total cost) against the original 60 lb green weight → $13.39/lb, ignoring that only 42.1 lb is actually sellable.

**Correct pricing:** $803.40 total cost ÷ 42.1 lb final sellable weight = $19.08/lb — a 42.5% increase over the green-weight figure, reflecting the real cost of the moisture and pellicle-trim loss during aging.

## Cutting spec vs. yield deviation check (filled example)

Historical average yield for this primal/grade: 62% sellable product. This week's actual yield: 54%.

| Check | Historical spec | This week's actual | Deviation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fat trim depth | 1/4" max | 1/2" observed on sample cuts | Excess fat left in trim category, reducing steak yield |
| Bone-in vs. boneless | Boneless | Boneless (unchanged) | Not the cause |
| Portion size | 12 oz standard | 12 oz (unchanged) | Not the cause |

Conclusion: the 8-point yield gap traces to fat trim depth doubling from spec (1/4" to 1/2"), not to the animal itself — correct the cutting technique before treating this week's yield as the new baseline.
