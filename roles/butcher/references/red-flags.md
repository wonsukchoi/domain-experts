# Red flags

### Actual cutting yield falls significantly below the historical average for this primal and grade
- **Usually means:** a cutting spec or technique deviation (excess fat left on, different bone-in/boneless approach) rather than this specific animal being lower-yielding.
- **First question:** does the actual cut spec used match what established the historical average yield?
- **Data to pull:** the cutting spec sheet compared against the actual trim/waste weight recorded for this breakdown.

### Retail pricing is set from the primal's purchase price per pound rather than a yield-adjusted cost per sellable pound
- **Usually means:** systematic underpricing of the finished product relative to its actual production cost.
- **First question:** has yield percentage actually been factored into this price at all?
- **Data to pull:** the yield/cost worksheet for this specific primal and cut.

### Dry-aged product is priced at its pre-aging (green) weight rather than its finished aged weight
- **Usually means:** the aging weight loss is being absorbed as an unrecovered cost instead of priced into the finished product.
- **First question:** what was the product's weight at the start of aging versus at the end?
- **Data to pull:** the aging log with start and end weights and dates.

### Product spends more than the allowed cumulative time in the temperature danger zone during or after breakdown
- **Usually means:** a cold-chain discipline break specifically at the highest-risk step of the process.
- **First question:** how long was the product actually out of refrigeration during and immediately after cutting?
- **Data to pull:** the HACCP/time-temperature log for that specific batch.

### Trim or byproduct is sold or used at a value well below current market rate
- **Usually means:** byproduct value isn't being tracked or updated, quietly reducing overall recovered cost.
- **First question:** what's the current commodity price for trim or fat versus the rate actually being applied internally?
- **Data to pull:** current market/commodity pricing compared against the internally applied byproduct rate.

### Cost is allocated equally by weight across cuts of significantly different market value
- **Usually means:** the allocation model doesn't reflect actual value distribution, mispricing both the premium and the cheap cuts.
- **First question:** what does the relative market value of each product category actually look like versus the allocation method being used?
- **Data to pull:** the market value of each resulting product category compared against the cost-allocation method applied.

### Supplier's delivered primal weight or grade doesn't match the invoice or purchase spec
- **Usually means:** a receiving discrepancy that needs resolving before it gets absorbed into yield and cost calculations.
- **First question:** does the receiving scale weight and grade stamp actually match the invoice?
- **Data to pull:** the receiving record (scale weight, grade stamp) compared against the invoice.
