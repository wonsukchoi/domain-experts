# Playbook

Filled sequences and tables for the four recurring decisions: what fits inside a turnaround window by vehicle type, the timed bloodborne-pathogen response, chemical/surface compatibility, and pressure-washer settings by zone.

## Turnaround checklist by vehicle type

| Vehicle type | Window | Do inside the window | Defer to next deep-clean cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Transit bus (end-of-run, nightly) | ~40 min/bus within an 8-hr overnight shift (12-bus garage) | Trash pull, sweep/mop floor, seat and stanchion wipe-down, window spot-clean, driver area reset, exterior rinse | Carpet extraction, seat-fabric shampoo, full disinfection fog — cadence ~every 45 days |
| Aircraft cabin (narrow-body quick-turn) | 15–25 min, run in parallel with refuel/boarding | Seat-pocket and overhead-bin trash, tray-table/armrest wipe, lavatory reset (paper, soap, surface wipe), visible-floor spot pickup | Carpet shampoo, seat-fabric deep clean, galley deep-sanitize — deferred to overnight/maintenance-check clean |
| Rental car (ready-line) | Heuristic ~20–30 min per vehicle between return and next reservation [industry-typical, not a cited standard] | Trash removal, vacuum, dash/console wipe, window clean, fuel/mileage check handoff to maintenance | Interior shampoo, odor treatment, paint correction — routed to a separate reconditioning bay |
| Rail car (end-of-run layover) | Heuristic parallel to bus: full clean at terminus, quick reset at short layover stops [heuristic] | Trash pull, seat wipe, vestibule/door-area clean | Deep floor scrub, upholstery treatment — scheduled maintenance cycle |

## Bloodborne-pathogen spill response — timed sequence

Trigger: any blood or bodily fluid, regardless of visible severity (Universal Precautions — treat as presumptively infectious).

1. **Don PPE** — nitrile gloves, eye/face protection, apron if splash risk. (~2 min)
2. **Contain and remove bulk material** — absorb/scoop visible material into a disposable barrier before wiping; wiping first spreads contamination. (~3 min)
3. **Apply EPA-registered disinfectant carrying a tuberculocidal claim at its labeled dwell time** — commonly 1–10 minutes depending on the product; the label is the controlling number, not a house rule. (label-specified, ~10 min typical for a tuberculocidal claim)
4. **Wipe and rinse the treated surface.** (~3 min)
5. **Double-bag contaminated material as regulated/biohazard waste, label it, and place it in a closed container** separate from general trash. (~4 min)
6. **Log the incident** — date, location, vehicle ID, material type, response taken — into the exposure/incident record. (~3 min)

Total added time: ~20–25 minutes on top of the vehicle's standard clean baseline, largely independent of vehicle size.

## Chemical / surface compatibility

| Surface | Compatible product class | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vinyl / leatherette seating | Quat disinfectant labeled for vinyl/upholstery, ~150–400 ppm active per label | Full-strength bleach, high-alcohol-content cleaners used repeatedly | Breaks down the surface coating and printed grain pattern over repeated exposure, then attacks the substrate — shows up as cracking/discoloration months later, not during the clean |
| Cloth seat fabric | Fabric-safe enzymatic or quat-based cleaner at label dilution | Solvent-heavy degreasers | Solvents can set stains and degrade fiber, and don't achieve the disinfection claim needed for a bio-event |
| Touchscreens / electronics (fareboxes, entertainment units, rental-car infotainment) | Isopropyl-alcohol wipe at manufacturer-approved concentration (commonly ~70%) | Ammonia-based glass cleaner, saturating spray application | Ammonia and excess liquid can degrade anti-glare coatings and migrate into bezels/connectors |
| Tinted windows | Ammonia-free glass cleaner | Ammonia-based cleaner | Ammonia degrades window tint film, clouding or bubbling it over repeated use |
| Hard non-porous surfaces (floors, stanchions, tray tables) | Quat or bleach-based disinfectant per site protocol | — | These are the surfaces the stronger chemicals are actually formulated for |

## Pressure-washer PSI by zone

| Zone | Setting | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Body panels / general exterior (tolerant surface) | Standard wash PSI per site protocol (commonly 1,200–2,000 PSI for vehicle exteriors) | Full face protection required above ~2,000 PSI per OSHA-referenced guidance |
| Wheel wells / undercarriage | Reduced pressure, wide-angle nozzle | Debris packed near suspension and brake components can hide seals not rated for direct high-pressure contact |
| Electrical connectors, junction boxes, sensors, exterior lighting harnesses | Low-pressure or hand-wipe only — never direct spray | Forced water intrusion causes corrosion and intermittent failures that surface days to weeks later, not during the wash |
| Door seals / window seals | Reduced pressure, angled away from the seal edge | High pressure directed into a seal gap forces water past it into the cabin or door cavity |
| Any surface at close range | Increase standoff distance rather than pressure to remove stubborn residue | Reduces both surface damage risk and high-pressure water-injection injury risk to the operator's hands |

## Lost-and-found chain of custody

| Step | Action | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Discovery | Log time, exact location (seat/row, vehicle ID), and description immediately, before moving the item | The finder's record is the only one that exists until the item changes hands |
| 2. Securing | Place in a sealed bag/envelope with a claim tag matching the log entry | Prevents commingling with other found items |
| 3. Escalation trigger | High-value items (electronics, cash, jewelry, firearms) go to a supervisor or site security within the shift, not at shift end | Some sites transfer to police/security custody within as little as 14 days of the retention window; earlier escalation protects both the passenger and the finder |
| 4. Retention | Hold per site policy — commonly on the order of 30–90 days for standard items before donation/disposal, longer (up to ~6 months) for high-value items; exact windows vary by operator | Confirm the specific site's written policy — this varies enough that assuming a number is itself a risk |
| 5. Release/disposal | Release only against matching claim documentation; log disposal/donation with date and method | Closes the custody chain with a record, not a verbal confirmation |
