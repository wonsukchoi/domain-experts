# Red flags

Smell tests worth catching before they show up as an exposure incident, a damaged vehicle, or a lost-property dispute.

### A bio-event vehicle gets released to service in under standard bloodborne-pathogen protocol time (~20 min faster than the ~20–25 min the full sequence takes)
- **Usually means:** dwell time or PPE steps were skipped under turnaround pressure; second most likely, the spill was misclassified as a routine stain rather than a bodily fluid.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the sequence on this one — PPE, containment, disinfectant dwell time, waste bagging, log entry. Which step actually happened?"
- **Data to pull:** incident log entry (or its absence) for that vehicle/shift, disinfectant dispensing record, turnaround timestamp vs. standard bio-event duration.

### Vinyl or leatherette seating across a fleet segment shows cracking or discoloration well inside expected service life
- **Usually means:** a disinfectant or dilution incompatible with vinyl/leatherette is in routine use, most often a bleach-based or over-concentrated product substituted for speed or availability.
- **First question:** "What product and dilution is being used on seating in this depot, and does the label list vinyl/upholstery compatibility?"
- **Data to pull:** chemical purchase/usage log by site, product label/SDS, seat-replacement or reupholstery cost trend by depot.

### Quat disinfectant solution testing below label-stated ppm (commonly below ~150–300 ppm active)
- **Usually means:** dispenser miscalibration or manual over-dilution to stretch product volume, both of which quietly fail the tuberculocidal or sanitizing claim on the label.
- **First question:** "When was this dispenser last verified with a test strip, and against what target ppm?"
- **Data to pull:** test-strip logs, dispenser calibration/maintenance record, product reorder frequency vs. expected usage rate.

### The same vehicle or route slot runs over its turnaround budget on a recurring basis
- **Usually means:** the budgeted window doesn't match the vehicle's real cleaning load (frequent bio-events, higher passenger volume, worse fouling), not that the cleaner is slow.
- **First question:** "Pull the last eight turnaround times for this slot — is the overage clustered around a specific cause or spread evenly?"
- **Data to pull:** per-vehicle turnaround time log, incident log cross-referenced by vehicle/slot, dispatch override/delay notes.

### Pressure-washer setting is unchanged regardless of the zone being cleaned (interior vs. undercarriage vs. electrical panel)
- **Usually means:** the operator is running one shop-standard PSI for speed rather than adjusting per zone, or nozzle-switching has been skipped as an unnecessary step.
- **First question:** "Show me the PSI/nozzle setting used near the electrical harness and wheel well versus the body panel — same or different?"
- **Data to pull:** wash-bay equipment settings log if automated, maintenance records for corrosion/electrical faults on washed vehicles, operator training record on zone-specific settings.

### A vehicle develops an intermittent electrical fault (lighting, sensor, infotainment) shortly after a scheduled exterior wash
- **Usually means:** water was forced past a seal or connector during pressure-washing; the fault surfaces days to weeks later, which is why it's rarely connected back to the wash.
- **First question:** "When was this vehicle last pressure-washed, and at what setting near the affected component?"
- **Data to pull:** wash schedule log, maintenance fault report with timestamp, wash-bay PSI setting history for that vehicle.

### Found passenger items are logged only at end of shift, in a batch, rather than at the moment of discovery
- **Usually means:** logging has become a shift-end paperwork task rather than a discovery-time habit, which erodes exactly the record a disputed or high-value item needs.
- **First question:** "For the last five items logged, was the timestamp the discovery time or the end-of-shift log time?"
- **Data to pull:** lost-and-found log timestamps vs. shift schedule, discrepancy reports or passenger complaints about missing items.

### A high-value found item (electronics, cash, firearm) sits with the finder or in an unsecured location for more than a few hours before escalation
- **Usually means:** the escalation path isn't clear to line staff, or informal handling has become normalized because nothing has gone wrong yet.
- **First question:** "What's the written escalation trigger and timeline for high-value finds at this site, and was it followed here?"
- **Data to pull:** site lost-and-found policy, escalation timestamps for recent high-value finds, any prior missing-item claims involving this depot/route.

### Regulated/biohazard waste is found in general trash during a bin audit
- **Usually means:** bagging and labeling steps from the bloodborne-pathogen protocol are being skipped, most often on spills judged (wrongly) as minor.
- **First question:** "What made this spill get treated as general cleanup rather than a bio-event?"
- **Data to pull:** waste-stream audit findings, incident log for the period, disinfectant usage record for the vehicle/shift in question.
