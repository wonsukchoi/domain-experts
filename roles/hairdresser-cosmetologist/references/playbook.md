# Hairdresser / Cosmetologist — Playbook

## Level-and-tone lift chart (starting level → achievable lift by developer volume, standard timing)

| Developer volume | Typical lift (levels) | Primary use | Max processing (on-scalp) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10-volume | 0 (deposit only) | Gray coverage/tone-on-tone at same level | 20–30 min |
| 20-volume | 1–2 | Standard color with modest lift | 30–45 min |
| 30-volume | 2–3 | Noticeable lift, resistant gray | 30–45 min |
| 40-volume | 3–4 | Maximum single-session lift; highest damage risk | 20–30 min (shorter due to damage risk) |

Underlying-pigment sequence exposed as natural level lifts (darkest to lightest): black/brown → red → red-orange → orange → orange-yellow → yellow → pale yellow. A target tone that ignores which pigment sits at the target level produces the wrong result even at the correct level.

## Strand-test protocol (filled example)

1. Section a hidden area (nape or interior crown) of roughly 1cm width.
2. Apply the planned formula and developer volume exactly as intended for the full service.
3. Check at 3 intervals: 50% of standard time, 75%, and full standard time — record level/tone/condition at each check.
4. Rinse and evaluate: does the result match the target level and tone, and is the hair's texture/elasticity intact (not gummy, not snapping when gently stretched)?

Example filled strand-test log:

| Check point | Time | Level reached | Tone observed | Condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 50% | 12 min | 5.5 | Orange-red | Firm, elastic |
| 75% | 18 min | 6.5 | Orange | Slightly soft |
| 100% | 24 min | 7 | Orange-yellow | Soft, still elastic (pass) |

Result: target level reached at full standard time with acceptable condition — proceed to full-head application at 24-minute processing, re-checking at 18 minutes for early signs of over-lift.

## Chemical-service compatibility check (fallback order)

1. **No prior chemical service in the compatibility window (typically 4+ weeks for color, longer for relaxer/keratin)** — proceed with standard formulation.
2. **Prior color only, same chemical family, outside the manufacturer's minimum interval** — proceed with a strand test before full application; treat porosity from the prior service as a formulation input (may need shorter processing).
3. **Prior relaxer, keratin treatment, or unknown/metallic-salt-suspected product, or inside the minimum interval** — do not proceed with a new chemical service today. Recommend a clarifying treatment and a follow-up strand test at a later visit.
4. **Client cannot confirm history and visual/tactile assessment is ambiguous** — treat as case 3 (stop and verify) rather than case 1 (proceed) — the ambiguity itself is the red flag, not a reason to default to "probably fine."

## Multi-client processing-window scheduling (filled example, one chair, one afternoon)

| Client | Service | Application time | Processing time | Chair-free window | Next check-in |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Full color, 20vol | 1:00–1:20 | 1:20–2:00 (40 min) | 1:20–1:45 (25 min free) | 2:00 |
| B | Cut only | 1:20–2:00 | — | — | — |
| C | Root touch-up, 20vol | 1:45–2:00 | 2:00–2:35 (35 min) | 2:00–2:20 (20 min free) | 2:35 |

Reconciliation: Client A's processing window (1:20–2:00) overlaps Client B's full cut appointment — feasible only because B needs no chemical-check attention during that window. Client C's application (1:45–2:00) is scheduled inside A's processing window, using the chair-free time productively, with both processing checks (A at 2:00, C at 2:35) staggered by 35 minutes so neither is missed.
