# Hairdresser / Cosmetologist — Red Flags

### Client history says "nothing done" but hair is gummy, mushy, or unusually elastic when wet
- **Usually means:** an undisclosed or forgotten at-home chemical treatment (box dye, keratin kit, relaxer), or metallic-salt/henna product buildup
- **First question:** "Have you used any at-home color, straightening, or 'natural' hair products — including ones you might not think of as chemical, like henna?"
- **Data to pull:** visual/tactile porosity assessment; strand test with a metallic-salt indicator check if bleach/lightener is planned

### Strand test shows uneven lift, a green/metallic cast, or a heat/fizzing reaction during processing
- **Usually means:** metallic-salt dye contamination reacting with peroxide-based lightener
- **First question:** "Has this client used a boxed 'gradual' or 'natural' color product in the past several months?"
- **Data to pull:** strand-test log with timestamped observations; product-history questionnaire response

### Client requests more than 3–4 levels of lift in one visit from previously color-treated or relaxed hair
- **Usually means:** the requested result isn't achievable in one session without unacceptable breakage risk
- **First question:** "What's the last chemical service this hair had, and how long ago?"
- **Data to pull:** strand-test result at planned formula/volume; elasticity (stretch) test result

### Hair snaps or shows visible breakage during a wet-stretch test before any new service starts
- **Usually means:** existing structural damage from a prior service or prolonged heat styling that limits what any new chemical service can safely add
- **First question:** "When was the last time this hair had a relaxer, perm, or high-heat styling treatment?"
- **Data to pull:** elasticity-test result; visual porosity read at root vs. mid-shaft vs. ends

### Client wants a relaxer touch-up and a color service in the same visit
- **Usually means:** same-visit chemical stacking risk — the two services compound structural stress even if each looks individually safe
- **First question:** "Which service is the priority today, and can we schedule the other for [manufacturer-interval] out?"
- **Data to pull:** date of last relaxer application; manufacturer's stated minimum interval between chemical services

### Strand test at 50% of standard processing time already shows the target level reached
- **Usually means:** hair is more porous/resistant-to-processing than the "standard" formula assumed, and full standard time will over-process
- **First question:** "Does this match what we saw on this client's card from the last visit, or is this new?"
- **Data to pull:** current strand-test timestamp log; prior visit's service-record card

### Client's reference photo shows a tone the client's underlying pigment can't produce without additional toning
- **Usually means:** the photo shows a finished, toned result — not the raw lift color this client's hair will show at that level
- **First question:** "Do you want [target tone] as the final result, which means a toning step after lift — or are you expecting the lift color itself to look like this?"
- **Data to pull:** target level-and-tone breakdown; underlying-pigment expectation at that level for this client's natural level

### Skin or scalp shows redness, bumps, or irritation at a prior color-service site, or client reports a "tingling" during a past service
- **Usually means:** possible allergic sensitivity to PPD (para-phenylenediamine) or another color-formula component
- **First question:** "Have you ever had a reaction — itching, swelling, redness — during or after a color service, even a mild one?"
- **Data to pull:** patch-test result (48-hour skin patch per manufacturer instructions) before proceeding

### Client is pregnant or has a documented scalp condition (psoriasis, eczema, open lesions) and wants a chemical service
- **Usually means:** standard formulation timing/product choice may need modification, or the service should be deferred
- **First question:** "Has your doctor said anything about chemical services during this, or about this scalp condition?"
- **Data to pull:** scalp-condition location relative to planned application area; any medical guidance the client can share
