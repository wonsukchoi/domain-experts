# Red flags — signals a loss prevention manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can be innocent; two or more on the same store, employee, or period is a pattern worth opening a case over.

### No-receipt refund rate at a store running 3x or more the chain average
- **Usually means:** either a genuine customer-service pattern worth reviewing for policy clarity, or refund fraud — possibly employee-assisted given how consistently it clears any override threshold in place.
- **First question:** "Pull the override approvals behind these refunds — is one employee ID approving a disproportionate share, and are manager-PIN requirements actually being enforced?"
- **Data to pull:** refund transaction log with approver ID, override-threshold compliance rate, comparison to the same store's rate in prior periods.

### A single employee ID responsible for a disproportionate share of voids, overrides, or no-sale transactions
- **Usually means:** either that employee's role legitimately requires more overrides (verify against job function) or the employee is exploiting override authority to cover theft (sweethearting, fake returns, drawer manipulation).
- **First question:** "What's this employee's void/override rate compared to peers in the same role at the same store, and does their schedule correlate with the flagged transaction timestamps?"
- **Data to pull:** transaction log by employee ID, shift schedule, peer comparison within the same role and store.

### Cycle count variance at a specific store consistently running high on the same SKU category quarter over quarter
- **Usually means:** either a recurring administrative/receiving error specific to that category, or theft concentrated on a specific high-resale-value item line.
- **First question:** "Is the variance concentrated at receiving (miscount on arrival) or at the shelf (post-receiving shrink), and has this SKU category been the same one every quarter?"
- **Data to pull:** receiving audit records vs. POS-recorded sales for the category, prior-quarter variance reports, EAS alarm data for the same category.

### EAS (electronic article surveillance) alarm rate rising without a corresponding rise in recovered merchandise
- **Usually means:** either tag placement/deactivation process has a gap (legitimate purchases triggering false alarms, weakening staff response to real alarms over time) or theft methods are defeating the tags without triggering recovery.
- **First question:** "What's the false-alarm rate specifically, and has staff response to alarms measurably slowed as the false-alarm rate rose?"
- **Data to pull:** EAS alarm log with disposition (recovered/false alarm/no response), tag-deactivation process audit at point of sale.

### Same high-theft SKU category showing elevated loss across 3 or more stores in the same region within the same window
- **Usually means:** organized retail crime (ORC) rather than isolated shoplifting — coordinated across locations, which changes the appropriate response from store-level security to task-force escalation.
- **First question:** "Does video from any of these stores show the same individual, vehicle, or group across multiple locations?"
- **Data to pull:** cross-store video correlation, loss trend by SKU category and store, any secondary-marketplace resale matches for the SKU pattern.

### An apprehension or detention logged without all evidentiary-checklist elements completed
- **Usually means:** a stop was made on suspicion rather than confirmed observation — the single highest-liability pattern in the program, regardless of whether the suspicion turned out to be correct.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the checklist for this stop — which element wasn't met, and why did it happen anyway?"
- **Data to pull:** apprehension log with checklist completion status, video timestamp confirming (or failing to confirm) continuous observation, incident report narrative.

### Employee interview conducted without HR or a second witness present
- **Usually means:** the investigation's evidentiary integrity is compromised regardless of outcome, and the organization is exposed to a wrongful-termination or coerced-confession claim.
- **First question:** "Who else was in the room, and was there a pre-planned question script, or did this happen informally?"
- **Data to pull:** interview log/notes, HR notification timestamp relative to interview timestamp, any recording or contemporaneous documentation.

### A terminated or disciplined employee's flagged transaction pattern stops immediately but the store's overall shrink number doesn't improve
- **Usually means:** either the employee wasn't actually the primary driver of the shrink (a coincidental correlation), or there's a second undetected source — possibly a collusion partner who's now working alone.
- **First question:** "Did the specific flagged transaction type this employee was investigated for actually stop, or did a different pattern simply take its place?"
- **Data to pull:** shrink trend for 60–90 days post-action, EBR pattern comparison pre- and post-termination, any new anomalies emerging in the same category.

### Return/refund frequency for a single customer account or loyalty ID exceeding a set threshold in a short period
- **Usually means:** either a legitimate pattern (a customer who buys and returns frequently in a specific category) or return fraud — wardrobing, receipt reuse, or resale-then-return schemes.
- **First question:** "Are the returned items the same SKU repeatedly, and do the return timestamps cluster suspiciously close to a known resale or reseller pattern?"
- **Data to pull:** customer/loyalty return history, SKU repetition, time-between-purchase-and-return distribution.

### Loss-prevention technology (cameras, EAS, analytics) purchased or upgraded with no corresponding change in investigation case volume
- **Usually means:** the technology is generating flags or footage nobody is reviewing or acting on — a sunk cost that measures theft without reducing it.
- **First question:** "How many cases were opened directly from this system's output in the period since it went live, versus the volume of flags it actually generated?"
- **Data to pull:** system flag/alert volume, case-open rate against those flags, investigator headcount allocated to review this system's output.
