|---|---|---|---|

| **This case** | — | Yes (3 prior reports) | Matches the 9-case pattern → 1 day / 5 days + conference |

**Rule applied:** the 2 outlier cases in the "documented history" row that received the flat 5/5 default are themselves flagged for review — they're the inconsistency this process is designed to catch, not a justification for a third inconsistent decision.

## 2. Incident response protocol (containment vs. investigation, filled example)

| Phase | Actions | Timeframe | Who decides |
|---|---|---|---|
| Containment | Separate students, address immediate physical/emotional needs, notify families of the incident (facts only, not conclusions) | Within 1 hour | On-duty administrator |
| Investigation | Interview involved students and witnesses, pull discipline log for precedent check, review any documented prior history | 24-48 hours | Assigned administrator + counselor if relevant |
| Determination | Finalize consequence against precedent, document rationale, communicate to families | After investigation complete, not before | Administrator, reviewed by principal for consistency |

**Rule applied:** the determination phase does not start until the investigation phase is complete — pressure to skip ahead is the exact failure mode this protocol exists to prevent.

## 3. Family communication template (filled example)

> **Initial notification (within 1 hour, facts only):** "Your child was involved in an incident with another student today at approximately [time]. Both students are safe. We are investigating what happened and will follow up with more information within 48 hours."
> **Follow-up (after investigation, with determination):** "Following our investigation, we found [factual summary appropriate to share]. The consequence is [specific outcome], based on [general rationale — e.g., 'our discipline policy for this type of incident, taking into account documented history']. We're also implementing [restorative/support step] to address the underlying issue."

**Rule applied:** the initial notification goes out within the containment window with facts only — no premature conclusions — and the follow-up with the actual determination doesn't go out until the investigation is genuinely complete.

## 4. Resource allocation vs. stated priorities check (filled example)

| Stated priority | Budget/staffing evidence this cycle | Aligned? |
|---|---|---|
| "Supporting students with additional needs" | Added 0.5 FTE reading specialist, extended intervention block by 20 min/day | Yes |
| "Teacher development and retention" | Professional development budget unchanged for 3rd consecutive year; turnover up 8 points | No — flagged for next budget cycle |
| "School safety" | Added 2 additional counselor hours/week, no change to physical security budget | Partial — check whether this matches what the safety review actually recommended |

**Rule applied:** each stated priority gets checked against an actual line-item change, not assumed aligned by default — "No" or "Partial" findings go into the next planning cycle as an explicit gap, not left unaddressed.
