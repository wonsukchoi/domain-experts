# Red flags — what an administrator notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A disciplinary decision being finalized before a precedent check against the discipline log
- **Usually means:** the decision risks being inconsistent with how comparable past cases were actually handled, which reads as unfair once compared side by side later.
- **First question:** "Have we pulled comparable past cases from the discipline log, and does this decision match how those were resolved?"
- **Data to pull:** discipline log for comparable incident types over the past 12-24 months, outcomes and any distinguishing factors (documented history, first offense, etc.).

### Discipline outcome rates by demographic group diverging meaningfully from that group's enrollment share (commonly flagged at a gap of 10+ percentage points)
- **Usually means:** a real disproportionality signal that warrants a policy-level review, independent of whether any single case was decided in bad faith.
- **First question:** "What's this group's share of enrollment versus their share of this discipline category, and has this gap been reviewed at a policy level?"
- **Data to pull:** discipline outcomes by demographic group over multiple years, enrollment composition for the same period.

### A disciplinary consequence issued within hours of a safety/behavioral incident, before the fuller investigation is complete
- **Usually means:** the urgency appropriate for the immediate safety response is being applied to the determination step too, risking a premature or wrong conclusion once fuller context (a documented history, an unreported provocation) emerges.
- **First question:** "Is this consequence final, or is it a containment action pending a fuller investigation?"
- **Data to pull:** incident timeline (containment actions vs. investigation completion), any relevant prior-incident history for the students involved.

### A parent/family communication about a negative incident sent later than, or with materially less detail than, the school's own internal incident report
- **Usually means:** disclosure is being minimized or delayed relative to what's actually known, which tends to damage trust more than the underlying incident once families discover the gap.
- **First question:** "Does what we've told the family match what's actually documented in the incident report at this point?"
- **Data to pull:** timestamp and content of family communication versus the internal incident report/timeline.

### A budget or staffing cycle where a stated priority (student support, safety, teacher development) shows no corresponding allocation change
- **Usually means:** the stated priority may be aspirational rather than reflected in actual resource decisions.
- **First question:** "What specifically changed in staffing, schedule, or budget to reflect this stated priority, and if nothing did, why not?"
- **Data to pull:** budget/staffing allocation by category over 2-3 cycles, the school's stated priorities for the same period.

### Teacher turnover or working-conditions survey scores declining while student-facing initiatives continue to be funded at the same or increased level
- **Usually means:** teacher support may be being treated as a lower, separable priority rather than as an upstream driver of the same student outcomes the funded initiatives are meant to improve.
- **First question:** "What's driving the turnover/survey decline, and does any current initiative address it, or is all new investment going to student-facing programs only?"
- **Data to pull:** teacher turnover rate and working-conditions survey trend, budget allocation trend across teacher-support versus student-program categories.

### A completed compliance report or policy checklist being cited as evidence of program quality with no separate outcome data attached
- **Usually means:** meeting a compliance floor is being conflated with actually achieving the underlying educational goal — two different questions being treated as one.
- **First question:** "Beyond compliance, what outcome data shows this program is actually working for students?"
- **Data to pull:** the compliance documentation itself, separate student outcome/quality data for the same program.

### An accommodation or schedule exception granted for one student/family with no documented rationale that would extend to a similar future request
- **Usually means:** an individually-reasonable exception that isn't checked against consistency, risking a fairness problem the next time a similar request comes from a different family.
- **First question:** "If a different family made this same request tomorrow, would we grant it the same way — and is that written down anywhere?"
- **Data to pull:** the exception's documented rationale, any policy language it should update if it reflects a genuine reconsideration rather than a one-off.
