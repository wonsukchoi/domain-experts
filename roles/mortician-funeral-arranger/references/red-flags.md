# Mortician / Funeral Arranger — Red Flags

### A family member says "whatever you think is best" and the conversation moves on without itemized options being shown
- **Usually means:** deference under grief being mistaken for informed consent — a selection made without real input risks becoming a grievance discovered during estate settlement.
- **First question:** "Would you like me to walk through the options first, even briefly, before we decide?"
- **Data to pull:** the GPL, presented item by item regardless of the deferral.

### Death-certificate informant details are being accepted verbally without a primary-source check
- **Usually means:** stress-dictated details (birth date, SSN, spelling) carry real risk of transposition or misremembering.
- **First question:** "Do we have an ID or document I can verify this spelling/date against?"
- **Data to pull:** driver's license, Social Security card, or birth certificate from the decedent's effects.

### Multiple family members disagree and no one has confirmed legal decision-making authority
- **Usually means:** risk of a decision later challenged or unwound by the person who actually holds authority.
- **First question:** "Is there a named executor, or who is the closest legal next-of-kin here?"
- **Data to pull:** will/executor documentation if available; state next-of-kin hierarchy if not.

### A same-day or rapid-timeline religious/cultural requirement surfaces after a standard processing timeline is already set
- **Usually means:** the requirement wasn't asked about early enough, and now conflicts with permits/logistics already in motion.
- **First question:** "Is there a religious or family timing requirement I should know about before we finalize this schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** the family's stated tradition/requirement and the actual legal minimum processing time in this jurisdiction.

### An itemized total lands significantly above or below what the family seems to have expected, and no explanation is offered
- **Usually means:** an informal budget expectation (from a prior experience or hearsay) not reconciled against what's actually selected, risking a surprised or upset family at final payment.
- **First question:** "Does this total match what you were expecting, or should we walk through what's included?"
- **Data to pull:** the itemized GPL selections next to the family's stated budget, if mentioned.

### An obituary or service program is sent to print without being read back to the family
- **Usually means:** risk of a factual error (name spelling, dates, survivor list) becoming a permanent, public, painful correction.
- **First question:** "Can I read this back to you before we send it to print?"
- **Data to pull:** the drafted obituary/program text, cross-checked against the family's confirmations.

### A cremation or burial authorization is about to be signed by someone other than the confirmed legal authority
- **Usually means:** the signature won't hold up if challenged, and the cremation/burial can't be un-done afterward if it turns out the signer lacked standing.
- **First question:** "Is [this person] the legally authorized next-of-kin or named agent for this decision?"
- **Data to pull:** the confirmed legal-authority determination from earlier in the conference.

### The fourth (or any) immediate family member is entirely absent from the conference and no one has mentioned why
- **Usually means:** potential for a later dispute from someone with equal or superior legal standing who wasn't consulted.
- **First question:** "Is there anyone else who should be part of this conversation or notified before we finalize?"
- **Data to pull:** the full immediate-family list against who is actually present.
