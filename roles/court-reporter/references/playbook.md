# Court Reporter — Playbook

## QC-triage log (filled format)

Every draft transcript gets one of these before certification. Non-substantive rows close the transcript's QC; substantive rows must hit zero corrected before signature.

| # | Page:Line | Draft text | Flag type | Correction | Source verified |

| 3 | 126:03 | "she was aware of the issue" | Substantive | "she was **not** aware of the issue" (omitted word) | Backup audio 00:19:07 |
| 4 | 130:22 | `SKWRAOEUS` (untranslated) | Non-substantive | "voir dire" (dictionary added) | Steno notes |
| 5 | 138:09 | "approximately ten thousand" | Substantive | "approximately ten thousand **dollars**" (omitted unit) | Backup audio 00:31:15 |

**Close-out rule:** substantive column must show 0 open rows before the certification statement is signed. Non-substantive rows close to a dictionary-update backlog, not to the transcript itself.

## Errata sheet (FRCP 30(e) format, filled)

| Page:Line | Original testimony | Changed to | Reason stated by deponent |
|---|---|---|---|
| 45:12 | "I don't recall the exact date" | "The meeting was on March 3rd" | "I reviewed my calendar after the deposition and located the date." |
| 52:08 | "No, I did not sign that document" | "Yes, I signed a similar document, not that exact one" | "Clarification of prior answer." |

**Flag rule:** a change from a flat "no" to "yes" (or the reverse) on a thin reason ("clarification") gets routed to the engaging attorney before processing — this is the fact pattern most likely to draw a motion to strike the errata sheet.

Original answer is never deleted or overwritten — both rows exist permanently in the transcript record, cross-referenced.

## Read-back procedure checklist

1. Confirm the exact page:line range requested by the bench or examining attorney.
2. Pull from steno notes as struck — not from memory, not from the realtime feed's on-screen text if it differs from the notes.
3. Read verbatim, including false starts, incomplete sentences, and audible fillers if present in the notes.
4. If the notes are ambiguous or damaged for that passage, cross-check against backup audio timestamp before reading back — do not guess.
5. Log the read-back request and the range delivered in the session notes, for the record.
