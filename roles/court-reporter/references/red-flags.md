# Court Reporter — Red Flags

### Substantive-error rate above 0% at the point of certification
- **Usually means:** a resolved-but-wrong word or omission slipped past the QC pass because it read as grammatically valid text, or the backup-audio cross-check was skipped on an ambiguous passage.
- **First question:** which specific pages/lines carry the substantive flags, and has each one been checked against the backup audio independently of the steno notes?
- **Data to pull:** the QC-triage log, the backup audio timestamps for each flagged passage.

### Realtime feed untranslated-outline rate climbing assignment over assignment for the same reporter
- **Usually means:** the CAT dictionary isn't being updated with new proper nouns, jargon, or recurring brief conflicts — the reporter's underlying skill hasn't degraded, the dictionary maintenance has lapsed.
- **First question:** when was the case or personal dictionary last updated, and which specific outlines are recurring?
- **Data to pull:** the dictionary-conflict tracker, a count of repeat-flagged outlines across the last several assignments.

### Errata sheet reverses a "no" to "yes" (or the reverse) with a thin stated reason
- **Usually means:** the deponent's counsel is using the errata process to correct a substantively damaging answer rather than a genuine transcription or recollection error — a use FRCP 30(e) permits but courts scrutinize.
- **First question:** does the stated reason describe what was misheard or mistranscribed, or does it describe a change of position?
- **Data to pull:** the original testimony, the proposed change, and the deponent's stated reason side by side.

### Backup audio has a gap longer than a few seconds during a contested passage
- **Usually means:** an equipment issue (battery, storage, or connection) coincided with testimony that later becomes disputed — the steno notes are now the sole record for that passage.
- **First question:** does the gap overlap with any passage already flagged as substantively uncertain in the QC pass?
- **Data to pull:** the audio log's gap timestamps cross-referenced against the QC-triage log and steno notes for the same range.

### A sealing or in-camera order is applied to the final transcript but not to the rough draft or realtime log
- **Usually means:** the sealing protocol was set up around the deliverable, not around every artifact the proceeding generated — draft files and realtime session logs are sitting outside the order's protection.
- **First question:** which artifacts from this proceeding exist outside the certified transcript, and are they stored under the same access restriction?
- **Data to pull:** a full artifact inventory for the proceeding (rough draft, realtime log, backup audio, personal notes) with storage/access location for each.

### A single steno conflict is driving more than a handful of QC flags on one transcript
- **Usually means:** one missing or wrong dictionary entry (commonly a proper noun) is generating repeat non-substantive flags that look like a larger error count than the actual risk warrants.
- **First question:** how many of the total flagged discrepancies trace back to that single conflict, and has the dictionary entry been added yet?
- **Data to pull:** the QC-triage log grouped by root cause, not by raw count.

### Read-back requested for a passage where the realtime on-screen text and the steno notes disagree
- **Usually means:** a live-session correction or a CAT auto-translation choice diverged from what the steno strokes actually captured — the notes are authoritative, but the discrepancy itself is a signal worth logging.
- **First question:** which source is being read from, and has the divergence been reconciled against backup audio?
- **Data to pull:** the steno notes, the realtime session log, and the backup audio for the specific passage.

### A party requests the transcript be delivered on an expedited basis that compresses the QC pass below its normal window
- **Usually means:** legitimate deadline pressure, but also the exact condition under which a substantive error is most likely to reach a certified transcript uncaught.
- **First question:** can the substantive-error triage step be preserved in full even on the compressed timeline, or does something get cut?
- **Data to pull:** the standard QC-pass time budget for a transcript of this length, compared against the requested delivery window.
