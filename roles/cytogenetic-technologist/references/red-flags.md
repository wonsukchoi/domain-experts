# Red Flags — Cytogenetics Bench and Reporting

### A single metaphase shows loss of a chromosome, no other cells affected
- **Usually means:** Random chromosome loss during slide fixation/drop (mechanical artifact) — the single most common cause of apparent monosomy in an otherwise normal count.
- **First question:** Have I screened enough additional cells to know whether a second and third cell share the identical loss?
- **Data to pull:** Full metaphase-by-metaphase count log; ISCN clonality criteria (3 cells required for loss).

### Two or more metaphases show an identical extra chromosome or identical structural rearrangement
- **Usually means:** A real clonal abnormality (meets ISCN's 2-cell gain/structural threshold), not artifact.
- **First question:** Does the karyotype percentage need confirmation/quantification by FISH given culture-selection bias?
- **Data to pull:** Full ISCN string with cell counts in brackets; interphase FISH order status for the same abnormality.

### Karyotype clone percentage and FISH/molecular clone percentage disagree by more than ~1.5–2x
- **Usually means:** Culture-selection bias — normal or abnormal cells proliferating disproportionately in vitro relative to their true marrow/tissue prevalence.
- **First question:** Which method's cell population is less culture-selected, and does the report need a caveat rather than a single number?
- **Data to pull:** Both raw counts (metaphase n and FISH nuclei n), the specimen's culture growth notes.

### Band quality on the majority of metaphases is below the resolution target for the case
- **Usually means:** Harvest timing (colcemid exposure too long/short) or synchronization protocol mismatch for the requested resolution.
- **First question:** Should this culture be re-harvested rather than analyzed at the available quality?
- **Data to pull:** Harvest log (colcemid exposure time, synchronization agent/timing), prior successful-harvest parameters for this specimen type.

### Prenatal specimen shows an unexpected abnormal or mosaic result
- **Usually means:** True fetal finding, or maternal cell contamination (especially in amniotic fluid/CVS with visible maternal decidua).
- **First question:** Has a maternal blood comparator (STR markers) been requested to rule out maternal cell origin?
- **Data to pull:** Maternal STR profile, original specimen collection notes (visible maternal tissue contamination at receipt).

### CVS direct-prep result and long-term culture result disagree
- **Usually means:** Confined placental mosaicism (abnormality present in placenta, not fetus) versus a true discordant/mosaic fetal result — the direct prep samples cytotrophoblast, the culture samples mesenchymal core, and they can differ biologically.
- **First question:** Does this case need amniocentesis follow-up to resolve which tissue reflects the fetus?
- **Data to pull:** Both direct-prep and cultured karyotype results side by side; ACMG guidance on CPM follow-up.

### A stat oncology karyotype comes back "no abnormality detected" from a low-cellularity or poor-quality harvest
- **Usually means:** Insufficient dividing cells or poor banding masked a real abnormality rather than a true normal result.
- **First question:** Was the count/resolution actually adequate to exclude the abnormality the clinician is asking about, or does this need FISH as a safety net?
- **Data to pull:** Metaphase count achieved vs. protocol minimum, band quality score, reflex FISH panel results if run.

### FISH result sits within 1–2 percentage points of the laboratory's validated cutoff
- **Usually means:** A borderline call that the fixed cutoff wasn't designed to resolve cleanly — could be low-level true positive or upper range of normal variation.
- **First question:** Does this need a repeat FISH run, a different probe, or correlation with the karyotype/morphology before finalizing?
- **Data to pull:** Original validation data (mean, SD, n of normal controls) for this specific probe, repeat-run results if available.

### A "normal" microarray is reported for a case where a balanced rearrangement was clinically suspected
- **Usually means:** Test-selection mismatch — microarray cannot detect balanced structural events by design, this isn't evidence of no abnormality for that question.
- **First question:** Was karyotype (or FISH targeted to the suspected rearrangement) also performed, or only microarray?
- **Data to pull:** Original test order and clinical indication; whether karyotype was run in parallel or substituted.

### Same abnormality appears across multiple unrelated specimens processed in the same batch
- **Usually means:** Cross-contamination between specimens (culture, harvest, or slide-labeling error), not a biological coincidence.
- **First question:** Do specimen identifiers, culture vessel logs, and slide labels all trace cleanly with no batch overlap?
- **Data to pull:** Batch processing log, specimen chain-of-custody records, STR identity confirmation if contamination is suspected.
