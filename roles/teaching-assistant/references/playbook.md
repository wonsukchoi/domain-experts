# Playbook

Filled operational routines a teaching assistant runs directly, with real structures and example numbers to adapt.

## 1. Small-group rotation setup (guided reading, 20-minute block)

**Before students arrive:**
- Confirm with the teacher: target skill for today, materials, and what "done" looks like — independent completion or supported completion.
- Pull each student's most recent data sheet; set today's starting prompt level per student from their last two sessions, not from memory.

**Rotation table (4-student group, example):**

| Student | Target skill | Last 2 sessions (independent rate) | Today's starting prompt level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Marcus | CVC word decoding | 2/20, 3/20 (10-15%) | Verbal |
| Priya | Sight word fluency | 14/20, 16/20 (70-80%) | Natural cue (fading from verbal) |
| Deja | Blend segmenting | 8/20, 9/20 (40-45%) | Gestural (stepped down from verbal) |
| Owen | CVC word decoding | 5/20, 4/20 (20-25%) | Verbal |

**During the block:** deliver instruction → 5-second wait → prompt only if no response or an error → log prompt level and correct/independent on each trial. Do not skip the wait even under time pressure; a rushed block that abandons wait time undoes weeks of fading progress in a day.

**After:** total each student's independent rate for the session; flag to the teacher any student who's held flat (±5 points) for 3+ sessions — that's a fading-plan review, not a "give it more time" situation.

## 2. Prompt-fading plan (individual student)

**Step sequence, in order:**

1. **Baseline** — 3-5 sessions at the student's current prompt level, 15-20 trials each, no changes. Record independent rate per session.
2. **Set the step-down rule** — e.g., "move down one prompt level (verbal → gestural) after 2 consecutive sessions ≥40% independent." Write the rule down before starting; deciding in the moment invites drifting the goalposts when a session goes badly.
3. **Insert the wait** — 5 seconds minimum before any prompt at every level, timed, not estimated.
4. **Track every trial**, not a summary impression: prompt level used, response (independent/prompted/error), reinforcement given.
5. **Review weekly** against the step-down rule. If two sessions clear the bar, move down a level next session; if a session dips more than ~15 points below the recent average, hold the current level and check for an environmental cause (fatigue, schedule change, new material) before assuming regression.
6. **Escalate to the team**, not just adjust solo, if 4+ consecutive sessions show no movement at any prompt level — that's a goal or method review, above a TA's authority to solve by trying harder.

## 3. ABC data collection (behavior)

**When to use:** any behavior that repeats across 3+ occasions in a way that disrupts instruction or safety — not a one-off bad moment.

**Log structure (filled example):**

| Time | Antecedent | Behavior | Consequence | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10:14 | Asked to stop iPad, transition to math | Threw iPad, yelled "no" | Removed from group, sent to calm corner (adult attention, task removed) | 4 min |
| 11:40 | Peer took his pencil | Grabbed it back, pushed peer | Redirected verbally, pencil returned | 30 sec |
| 1:05 | Independent worksheet, no adult nearby | Put head down, no response to name x3 | Teacher came over, sat with him 1:1 | 6 min |

**Pattern read (after 5+ entries):** two of three entries above follow a demand or transition and end in either task removal or adult attention — a functional-behavior hypothesis worth naming to the case manager: "possible escape-plus-attention function around transitions," not a diagnosis, a hypothesis for the team to test.

**Data to hand off:** raw log, trial counts, and the hypothesis — never just "he had a rough day."

## 4. Non-instructional supervision (recess/hallway/lunch) — incident escalation ladder

1. **Verbal redirect** — name, clear one-step instruction, from a normal distance (not looming).
2. **Proximity + choice** — move closer, offer two acceptable options ("finish here or take a break at the wall").
3. **Space + time** — if escalating, give space, remove the audience of peers if possible, narrate calmly what happens next.
4. **Trained physical intervention** — only if there is immediate risk of injury to the student or others, and only within the bounds of certified training (e.g., CPI); document immediately after per building policy.

**Rule:** any incident that reaches step 3 or 4 gets a same-day written note to the teacher/administrator with time, antecedent, and steps taken — not a verbal mention in passing.

## 5. Weekly data handoff to teacher/case manager (template)

```
STUDENT: Marcus              WEEK OF: Oct 6-10
TARGET SKILL: CVC decoding, guided reading rotation
SESSIONS TRACKED: 5 (Mon-Fri, 20 trials each)
INDEPENDENT RATE: 10%, 15%, 15%, 20%, 20% (5-session trend: +10 pts)
PROMPT LEVEL USED: Verbal (80-90% of trials, no change from last week)
FLAG: Independent rate rising but prompt rate flat — recommend
  5-second wait protocol before any prompt, review in 1 week.
BEHAVIOR NOTES: none this week.
```

The two fields a rushed handoff usually skips: **prompt level trend** (not just the outcome rate) and an explicit **recommendation**, not just raw numbers with no read on them.
