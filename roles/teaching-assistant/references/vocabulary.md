# Vocabulary

Terms generalists flatten together that a teaching assistant keeps sharply separate.

### IEP vs. 504 plan

**IEP** (Individualized Education Program) is a legally binding document under IDEA for a student found eligible for special education, specifying goals, services, and accommodations. A **504 plan** is under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, for a student with a disability who needs accommodations to access general education but doesn't require specialized instruction.

**In use:** "He's on a 504 for ADHD — extended time and preferential seating, no specialized instruction — don't confuse that with an IEP goal we'd be tracking data against."

**Common misuse:** treating the two as interchangeable "paperwork," or assuming a 504 student needs the same level of direct support as an IEP student with a service-minutes requirement.

### Prompt hierarchy (least-to-most vs. most-to-least)

A structured order of assistance levels, used to give the minimum help needed for a correct response. **Least-to-most** starts with the smallest prompt (natural cue) and increases only if needed; **most-to-least** starts with heavy support and fades down, used when errors are costly to unlearn.

**In use:** "We're least-to-most on decoding — start with the wait, not the verbal prompt, even if it's slower."

**Common misuse:** starting every task at whatever prompt level is fastest for the adult, rather than the level the data says is actually necessary that day.

### Prompt dependency vs. scaffolding

**Scaffolding** is temporary, planned support explicitly designed to be removed as skill grows, with the removal built into the plan. **Prompt dependency** is the same support continuing past the point it's needed because no one planned or tracked its removal.

**In use:** "This isn't scaffolding anymore, it's dependency — we never wrote a step-down rule, so the verbal prompt just became permanent."

**Common misuse:** calling any ongoing support "scaffolding" as a compliment, when the defining feature of real scaffolding is a plan for its own removal.

### ABC data (antecedent-behavior-consequence)

A structured behavior log capturing what happened immediately before a behavior, the behavior itself in observable terms, and what happened immediately after — used to generate a functional hypothesis, not a diagnosis.

**In use:** "Three of five ABC entries this week show the behavior right after a transition demand, followed by task removal — that's an escape-function hypothesis, not proof, but it's enough to bring to the team."

**Common misuse:** writing subjective labels ("he was being disrespectful") into the behavior column instead of an observable description ("threw pencil, said 'no'"), which erases the data's value.

### Related services vs. accommodations vs. modifications

**Related services** (e.g., speech therapy, OT) are direct services delivered by a specialist under the IEP. **Accommodations** change *how* a student accesses the same content/expectations (extended time, seating, read-aloud). **Modifications** change *what* is expected (fewer problems, different grade-level content).

**In use:** "Extended time is an accommodation — same test, more time. A shortened assignment with different content standards is a modification, and that's a bigger decision than I can make solo."

**Common misuse:** treating any adjustment as equally minor "help," when modifications carry different legal and instructional weight than accommodations.

### Paraeducator/paraprofessional vs. teaching assistant vs. instructional aide

Largely overlapping job titles that vary by state/district; **paraeducator/paraprofessional** is the formal term used in IDEA-adjacent policy and ESSA qualification rules, **teaching assistant** and **instructional aide** are common on-the-ground titles for the same role in general education settings.

**In use:** "Our district calls the position 'instructional aide' on the job posting, but the ESSA paraprofessional qualification rules still apply to it."

**Common misuse:** assuming "teaching assistant" always means a general-education-only role with no IEP-related responsibilities — in practice, general-ed TAs routinely support included students with IEPs without being their dedicated one-to-one aide.

### FERPA (Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act)

Federal law protecting the confidentiality of student education records, including IEP/504 content, grades, and disciplinary records; access is limited to those with a legitimate educational need to know.

**In use:** "The case manager needs that behavior data — the parent down the hall asking about it doesn't, and neither does the staff room."

**Common misuse:** treating FERPA as only about grades on paper, rather than any identifiable student information shared in casual conversation.

### Positive reinforcement vs. bribery

**Reinforcement** is anything delivered contingent on a behavior that increases the future likelihood of that behavior, ideally tied to a plan and faded over time. **Bribery**, in common (if imprecise) classroom-management usage, refers to offering a reward *during* an escalating behavior to stop it in the moment, which reinforces the escalation itself, not the desired behavior.

**In use:** "Give the sticker after he transitions calmly, not while he's mid-tantrum — offering it now teaches him the tantrum is what earns the reward."

**Common misuse:** assuming all rewards are equally fine regardless of timing, when contingency timing is the entire mechanism that makes reinforcement work or backfire.

### Least restrictive environment (LRE)

The IDEA principle that students with disabilities are educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, with removal from general education only as the level of need requires.

**In use:** "Pulling him out for every math block isn't automatically 'more support' — check whether the IEP actually calls for that much removal or whether it's become the default because it's easier to schedule."

**Common misuse:** treating more separate/pulled-out instruction as inherently more supportive, when LRE explicitly frames removal from general education as a cost to be justified, not a default.
