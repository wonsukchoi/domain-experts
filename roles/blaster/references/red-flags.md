# Red flags

Smell tests a certified blaster catches before signing off on a shot. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before firing or before responding to a complaint.

## 1. Entire row tied to a single surface delay via detonating cord

**Usually means:** the crew defaulted to the fastest tie-in method without checking it against the per-delay charge weight cap — every hole on that trunkline fires within the same window, summing to a charge weight that can be 5–7× what any individual hole carries.

**First question:** "What's the total charge weight of every hole tied to this one delay, and is that number under the per-delay cap — not the per-hole number?"

**Data to pull:** the timing sheet as physically tied (photograph the hookup, don't trust the design drawing), per-hole charge weights, the compliance basis (scaled-distance cap or seismograph-monitored limit) for this shot.

## 2. Powder factor below the site's rolling average with no geology change noted

**Usually means:** cost pressure led to a charge cut without a matching burden/spacing reduction — the pattern is now under-confined for its geometry, producing oversize, poor muckpile shape, or a longer flyrock throw than the pattern's stemming was designed for.

**First question:** "What changed — rock type, joint frequency, moisture — or did we just cut the load?"

**Data to pull:** last 10 shots' powder factor for this rock type and bench, geology log for this specific bench section, fragmentation photos or crusher throughput data from the most recent shots at the lower factor.

## 3. Stemming material is drill cuttings or round pit-run gravel, not crushed angular stone

**Usually means:** whatever was on hand got used instead of properly sized crushed stone — round or fine material confines gas worse at the same length, and this is the single most correctable cause of collar-zone flyrock.

**First question:** "What's actually in the stemming column on this shot, and does the stemming length compensate for using a weaker confining material?"

**Data to pull:** stemming material spec vs. what was loaded, stemming length as a fraction of burden, any prior flyrock incidents on shots using the same material.

## 4. Nearest structure distance taken from a site map or plat, not a field measurement

**Usually means:** the scaled-distance compliance calculation is only as good as the distance input, and map-scaled distances are frequently off by enough to matter — especially for structures added after the site map was last surveyed.

**First question:** "Has anyone physically measured or GPS-verified the distance to the nearest occupied structure this quarter?"

**Data to pull:** current GPS survey of all structures within the permit's survey radius, date of last field verification, any new construction near the property line since the last survey.

## 5. A misfire is being treated as resolved because "it probably fired"

**Usually means:** someone is substituting a plausible story (burned-through leg wire, visible crater matching expected size) for the mandated wait-and-approach procedure — this is how misfire incidents become injury incidents.

**First question:** "Did we run the full wait period and the from-the-firing-point investigation, or are we inferring it went off?"

**Data to pull:** time of last firing attempt, elapsed wait time against the applicable minimum, circuit continuity check results, hole count vs. detonation count from the post-blast inspection.

## 6. Complaint of "cracking" arrives with no pre-blast survey on file for that structure

**Usually means:** either the structure was outside the surveyed radius at the time (worth re-checking whether the radius itself needs updating) or the survey step was skipped for that phase — either way, there's no baseline to compare the claimed damage against.

**First question:** "Do we have a dated pre-blast condition log and photos for this specific structure?"

**Data to pull:** survey log and photo dates, blast log entries and seismograph traces (if monitored) for every shot since the structure was occupied, distance from the structure to each of those shots.

## 7. Fragmentation complaints (oversize, poor dig rate) trace to a specific bench section repeatedly

**Usually means:** a localized geology feature — a harder band, a change in joint orientation, a void — that the standard pattern isn't accounting for, not a general design failure.

**First question:** "Is the oversize concentrated in one part of the bench, or spread evenly across the whole shot?"

**Data to pull:** fragmentation photos or a sieve analysis by bench location, geology/joint mapping for that section, drill logs (any hard-drilling zones, air/water loss indicating voids) from that area.

## 8. A shot's actual tie-in doesn't match its filed design before firing

**Usually means:** a field substitution (out of a detonator type, a hole abandoned and not accounted for, a last-minute pattern change) happened without updating the compliance calculation — the number that was checked against the cap is no longer the number being fired.

**First question:** "What changed between the filed design and what's actually loaded and tied right now?"

**Data to pull:** original design charge weight and delay plan, as-built tie-in (photographed), recomputed per-delay charge weight against the compliance cap using the as-built numbers.

## 9. Air overpressure complaints with no stemming or exposed-detonating-cord check done

**Usually means:** exposed detonating cord on the surface (uncovered trunklines) or short stemming venting gas is generating airblast independent of the ground vibration reading — a shot can be within PPV limits and still generate a noise/overpressure complaint from this cause.

**First question:** "Was any detonating cord left uncovered on the surface, and what was the stemming length on the holes closest to the complainant?"

**Data to pull:** photos of the surface hookup before firing, stemming length by hole, wind direction and any temperature inversion at shot time (both affect how far airblast carries).
