# Red Flags

Signals that warrant an immediate reassessment, protocol step, or physician call — not an exhaustive symptom list.

### MAP <65 mmHg despite a first-line vasopressor at its ordered ceiling
- **Usually means:** the patient needs the next agent on the escalation ladder (e.g., fixed-dose vasopressin), not more of the first drug; second most likely is unaddressed source control (undrained abscess, ongoing hemorrhage) keeping shock refractory.
- **First question:** "Is this drip actually at the ordered ceiling, or can it still be titrated within range?"
- **Data to pull:** current order set/protocol range, drip rate history over the last hour, most recent lactate and source-control status.

### Lactate not clearing ≥10–20% over a 2-hour window despite resuscitation
- **Usually means:** ongoing tissue hypoperfusion the MAP number isn't capturing; second most likely is a non-perfusion lactate source (seizure, severe liver dysfunction, some drug effects).
- **First question:** "Is the MAP/UOP picture actually improving, or just the one number I'm anchored on?"
- **Data to pull:** serial lactate values with timestamps, UOP trend, most recent volume status assessment.

### Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/hr for 2 consecutive hours with MAP reported "at goal"
- **Usually means:** under-resuscitation masked by an adequate blood pressure number; second most likely is an evolving acute kidney injury independent of perfusion.
- **First question:** "What's the lactate trend doing while the UOP stays low?"
- **Data to pull:** hourly UOP log, cumulative fluid balance, creatinine trend.

### Plateau pressure >30 cmH2O with tidal volume already at 6 mL/kg PBW
- **Usually means:** worsening lung compliance (ARDS progression, auto-PEEP, abdominal distension) that needs a further Vt reduction, not acceptance of the higher pressure; second most likely is a correctable mechanical issue (breath-stacking, patient-vent asynchrony).
- **First question:** "Did peak pressure rise with it (compliance) or stay flat (resistance)?"
- **Data to pull:** peak vs. plateau pressure trend, current Vt in mL/kg PBW, auto-PEEP check.

### Peak pressure rising while plateau pressure stays flat
- **Usually means:** an airway resistance problem — secretions, bronchospasm, a kinked or bitten tube; second most likely is condensation/obstruction in the circuit itself.
- **First question:** "Has this patient been suctioned in the last hour, and when was the circuit last checked?"
- **Data to pull:** suctioning log, circuit inspection, recent chest exam/auscultation note.

### RASS more negative than the ordered sedation target with no charted reason
- **Usually means:** a titration gap — the sedative wasn't weaned as the patient stabilized; second most likely is drug accumulation (renal/hepatic clearance change) not yet reflected in the rate.
- **First question:** "What's the ordered RASS target, and when was the drip last reduced toward it?"
- **Data to pull:** ordered sedation target, current infusion rate and duration at that rate, renal/hepatic function trend.

### CAM-ICU positive with sedation dose increased in response
- **Usually means:** delirium treated as agitation — the underlying driver (hypoxia, new infection, withdrawal, the sedative itself) hasn't been worked; second most likely is undertreated pain misread as agitation.
- **First question:** "What's on the delirium driver checklist that hasn't been ruled out yet?"
- **Data to pull:** CAM-ICU components, recent labs/vitals for infection or hypoxia, medication administration record for sedative/opioid exposure.

### SAT or SBT skipped for 2+ consecutive shifts on a hemodynamically tolerable patient
- **Usually means:** "too unstable" became a default answer that was never re-tested; second most likely is a documentation gap where the trial happened but wasn't charted.
- **First question:** "What specific hemodynamic or respiratory parameter is failing the safety screen right now?"
- **Data to pull:** SAT/SBT safety-screen checklist results for the current shift, vasopressor requirement trend over the last 4 hours.

### New arrhythmia during a vasopressor or vasopressin titration
- **Usually means:** the current agent or dose is arrhythmogenic for this patient (common with epinephrine, high-dose norepinephrine); second most likely is an electrolyte disturbance (potassium, magnesium) unmasked by the hemodynamic stress.
- **First question:** "What changed in the last 30 minutes — the drip rate or something else?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent electrolyte panel, drip rate change log with timestamps, telemetry strip at onset.

### Mobility deferred 3+ shifts running on a patient meeting the stability gate
- **Usually means:** the stability gate is being assumed rather than re-checked each shift; second most likely is a staffing/workflow barrier (no PT availability, line management concerns) rather than a clinical contraindication.
- **First question:** "Does this patient currently meet today's stability gate, independent of yesterday's note?"
- **Data to pull:** current vasopressor trend over the last 2 hours, FiO2, arrhythmia history this admission, PT/OT scheduling log.
