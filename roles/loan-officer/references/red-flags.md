# Red flags — what a loan officer notices instantly

Signals that a file will bounce in underwriting, blow a closing date, or trip a compliance issue. Any one can be innocent; two together on the same file usually isn't.

### Back-end DTI recalculates >2 points higher after a hard-pull or VOE update than the initial soft-pull estimate
- **Usually means:** an undisclosed debt (co-signed loan, new auto loan opened after pre-approval, IRS installment agreement) surfaced on the hard pull, or the borrower's income was overstated at intake.
- **First question:** "What changed between the soft pull and the hard pull — is this a new account or one that existed and wasn't disclosed?"
- **Data to pull:** side-by-side credit report comparison (soft vs. hard), borrower's original 1003 debt list, most recent paystub/VOE.

### Appraisal comes in below contract price
- **Usually means:** the purchase price was set by a competitive-offer market, not comparable sales; LTV must be recomputed off the lower appraised value, which can push the loan above the program's LTV ceiling or eliminate a no-PMI structure.
- **First question:** "Does the purchase contract have an appraisal contingency, and will the seller renegotiate or does the borrower have cash to cover the gap?"
- **Data to pull:** appraisal report, comparable sales used, purchase contract contingency language.

### A single deposit >25% of one month's qualifying income appears in bank statements without a clear, documented source
- **Usually means:** an undocumented loan from a third party (creates undisclosed debt), a non-arm's-length gift not properly letter-and-sourced, or funds that can't be used for down payment/reserves under investor guidelines.
- **First question:** "Where did this specific deposit come from, and can the source provide a paper trail (transfer statement, gift letter, sale of asset)?"
- **Data to pull:** two months of bank statements, gift letter template if applicable, source-of-funds documentation.

### Rate lock is within 10 business days of expiration with open underwriting conditions still outstanding
- **Usually means:** the file is behind the timeline the lock was priced against — appraisal delay, title issue, or a slow-turning condition — and will need a paid extension or a reprice.
- **First question:** "Which specific condition is the long pole, and what's the realistic clear date?"
- **Data to pull:** LOS condition-status report, lock expiration date, current market rate vs. locked rate (to size the extension-vs-reprice decision).

### Borrower's employment or income source changed during processing (new job, switched from salary to commission, employment gap)
- **Usually means:** the income used to qualify is no longer verifiable the way it was documented, and may require a new averaging method or a probationary-period wait.
- **First question:** "Is this a same-line-of-work change (usually fine) or a new industry/self-employment start (usually requires new documentation and often a wait period)?"
- **Data to pull:** updated VOE, offer letter if new job, year-to-date paystubs under new arrangement.

### Self-employed borrower's most recent year's net income is lower than the prior year by more than a modest margin
- **Usually means:** income must be trended down, not averaged flat or annualized off the better year — qualifying income will be materially lower than the borrower expects.
- **First question:** "What drove the decline — one-time or structural — and does the trend continue into current-year P&L?"
- **Data to pull:** 2 years of business and personal returns, YTD profit-and-loss, CPA letter if available.

### Condo purchase where the HOA has pending litigation, >X% investor-owned units, or >Y% commercial space (project-specific thresholds vary by investor)
- **Usually means:** the project may be non-warrantable for the intended program, which can eliminate financing options entirely or force a shift to a portfolio/non-QM product with a different rate and down payment.
- **First question:** "Has this project's warrantability been checked against this specific investor's guidelines, not just 'condos generally'?"
- **Data to pull:** condo questionnaire, HOA budget and litigation disclosure, master insurance policy.

### A changed circumstance affecting fees or APR is discovered but not disclosed within the required window
- **Usually means:** a compliance violation is forming — either the change wasn't caught in time or someone is delaying disclosure to avoid resetting the CD clock and missing a closing date.
- **First question:** "When was this changed circumstance identified, and has compliance been notified today?"
- **Data to pull:** date-stamped change log, current LE/CD versions, APR delta calculation.

### Borrower requests a temporary buydown or seller credit specifically framed as "helping me qualify"
- **Usually means:** a misunderstanding of how qualification works — temporary buydowns qualify at the note rate under agency guidelines, so the buydown doesn't move the DTI decision at all.
- **First question:** "Is this being used to lower year-one cash flow (valid) or to change the qualifying DTI (won't work under agency rules)?"
- **Data to pull:** buydown agreement terms, agency guideline citation for the specific investor.

### Cash-to-close figure on the CD is higher than what was quoted on the initial LE by more than a small, explainable amount
- **Usually means:** a fee tolerance was breached, a per-diem interest or escrow estimate was off, or a late changed circumstance wasn't fully priced through to the borrower before disclosure.
- **First question:** "Walk the LE-to-CD fee comparison line by line — which category moved, and is it in-tolerance?"
- **Data to pull:** LE/CD comparison (loan estimate vs. closing disclosure tolerance cure worksheet), title/escrow final figures.

### Two or more borrowers on the same purchase with a non-arm's-length relationship to the seller (family sale, employer-employee, LLC affiliate)
- **Usually means:** stricter appraisal and down-payment-sourcing rules apply, and some programs (FHA identity-of-interest, certain investor overlays) cap LTV lower or require additional documentation regardless of otherwise-clean ratios.
- **First question:** "Is this relationship disclosed on the 1003, and has the applicable program's non-arm's-length overlay been checked?"
- **Data to pull:** purchase contract parties, 1003 disclosures, program-specific non-arm's-length policy.
