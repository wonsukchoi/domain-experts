# Red flags

### Two glass pieces joined/fused without verifying COE compatibility for that specific combination
- **Usually means:** cooling-stress cracking at the joint regardless of how clean the fusion looked while hot.
- **First question:** what are the actual tested or documented COE values for both pieces?
- **Data to pull:** COE test/spec for each piece compared against the compatibility tolerance for this joint type.

### Annealing schedule calculated from average or thinnest wall thickness rather than the piece's thickest cross-section
- **Usually means:** residual internal stress remains in the thicker sections even after the schedule completes.
- **First question:** what does the annealing schedule's basis show versus the piece's actual thickest measured cross-section?
- **Data to pull:** annealing schedule parameters compared against the actual thickest measured cross-section of the piece.

### A piece is treated as fully annealed based on total time in the kiln rather than confirming the actual temperature profile was followed
- **Usually means:** the piece may retain stress despite appearing to have gone through a normal-length process.
- **First question:** does the kiln's actual temperature log match the calculated schedule (soak time, cooling rate)?
- **Data to pull:** the kiln temperature log compared against the calculated annealing schedule.

### Working (torch/furnace) temperature parameters reused from a different glass type without adjustment
- **Usually means:** either failing to properly work the glass or overheating it past a safe, controllable viscosity state.
- **First question:** what glass type is actually in use versus the working temperature parameters being applied?
- **Data to pull:** glass type in use compared against the working temperature parameters actually applied.

### A finished piece shows a crack or check appearing some time after forming/annealing, with no polariscope check performed beforehand
- **Usually means:** a stress-related failure that a pre-use stress check could have caught.
- **First question:** was a polariscope reading ever taken on this piece before the crack appeared?
- **Data to pull:** polariscope stress reading, if performed, compared against the crack pattern and timing.

### A piece with significantly uneven wall thickness is annealed on a standard/default schedule rather than one adjusted for its specific thickest section
- **Usually means:** the standard schedule assumption doesn't hold for this specific piece's geometry.
- **First question:** does the piece's actual wall thickness variation match what the standard default schedule assumes?
- **Data to pull:** the piece's actual wall thickness variation compared against whether the schedule was adjusted from the standard default.

### A batch of glass material lacks clear labeling or verification of its COE or type before being used in a joint or mixed batch
- **Usually means:** a joint or batch could unknowingly combine incompatible glass types.
- **First question:** is there a reliable material certification, or does this batch need an actual COE test before use?
- **Data to pull:** material labeling/certification compared against an actual COE test if labeling is uncertain.
