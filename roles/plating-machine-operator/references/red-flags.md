# Red flags

### Plating time calculated from a theoretical deposition rate without adjusting for the bath's actual cathode efficiency
- **Usually means:** actual achieved thickness will fall short of target by roughly the efficiency shortfall percentage.
- **First question:** does the deposition rate figure used already account for actual bath efficiency, or is it a theoretical 100%-efficiency figure?
- **Data to pull:** deposition rate basis (efficiency-adjusted or theoretical) compared against the bath's actual measured cathode efficiency.

### Current set based on a fixed amperage regardless of the specific part's actual surface area
- **Usually means:** current density falls outside the bath chemistry's effective range, risking poor deposit quality (too low) or burning (too high).
- **First question:** what does the calculated current density (current ÷ area) show against the bath's specified effective range?
- **Data to pull:** calculated current density compared against the bath's specified effective current density range.

### Plated thickness verified only at easily-accessible/exposed surface locations, not in recesses or complex geometry
- **Usually means:** under-plated recessed areas from poor throwing power could go undetected while average/exposed thickness looks acceptable.
- **First question:** what do thickness measurements at recessed/less-accessible locations actually show against target?
- **Data to pull:** thickness measurements at recessed/less-accessible locations compared against target, not just exposed-surface readings.

### Surface preparation not verified against specification before plating begins
- **Usually means:** an adhesion failure (peeling, blistering) that traces back to inadequate prep, not the plating process itself, regardless of correct plating thickness achieved.
- **First question:** what does the surface prep verification result show for this specific part?
- **Data to pull:** surface prep verification result compared against specification, for this specific part.

### A plating bath's actual cathode efficiency not periodically re-verified, with time calculations relying on an outdated or assumed figure
- **Usually means:** plating time calculations may be based on a bath condition that no longer reflects current reality (contamination, chemical depletion).
- **First question:** what does a current bath efficiency test actually show against the figure used in recent time calculations?
- **Data to pull:** current bath efficiency test result compared against the efficiency figure used in recent time calculations.

### A recurring under-thickness or adhesion failure pattern for a specific part geometry or bath appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — throwing power, current density, surface prep, bath efficiency — rather than isolated one-off part variation.
- **First question:** does the defect occurrence log correlate with a specific part geometry, bath, or process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** defect occurrence log by part geometry/bath compared against process parameter logs over the same period.

### Plating parameters validated for one part geometry reused directly for a significantly different geometry without accounting for throwing power differences
- **Usually means:** the new part's less-accessible areas may not receive adequate thickness even though the parameters worked for the prior, simpler geometry.
- **First question:** was throwing-power-specific verification performed for this new geometry?
- **Data to pull:** part geometry comparison compared against whether throwing-power-specific verification was performed for the new geometry.
