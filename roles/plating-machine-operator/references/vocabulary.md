# Vocabulary

### Faraday's law (electroplating)
The electrochemical relationship governing how much metal is deposited based on current, time, and the specific metal's electrochemical properties — the basis for calculating required plating time.
**In use:** "Plating time's calculated off Faraday's law with this bath's actual efficiency, not just a stopwatch on the last batch."
**Common misuse:** Calculating plating time from a target thickness and elapsed time alone, without applying the actual current-density-and-efficiency-based relationship.

### Current density
The amount of electrical current per unit of part surface area, which must be controlled within a specific range for a given plating chemistry to achieve quality deposits.
**In use:** "Current density comes out to 4 A/dm² on this part's actual surface area — that's within the bath's effective range."
**Common misuse:** Setting current based on a fixed amperage value regardless of the specific part's surface area, rather than calculating and controlling actual current density.

### Cathode efficiency
The percentage of applied current that actually results in metal deposition, as opposed to side reactions like hydrogen evolution, typically well under 100% and specific to bath chemistry and condition.
**In use:** "This bath's running at 95% cathode efficiency — that 5% loss has to be built into the time calculation."
**Common misuse:** Assuming all applied current converts directly to deposited metal (100% efficiency) when calculating required plating time.

### Throwing power
A plating bath's ability to deposit metal uniformly into recesses, corners, and complex geometry, rather than concentrating deposition only on the most exposed surfaces.
**In use:** "Throwing power's mediocre on this bath — check thickness specifically in the recessed bore, not just the flat exterior."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a bath's average or exposed-surface plating thickness represents the thickness achieved throughout a part's more complex geometry, when poor throwing power can leave recessed areas critically under-plated.

### Burning (plating defect)
A rough, poorly-adherent, often dark or powdery deposit caused by excessive current density, especially concentrated at edges, points, or other high-current-density locations.
**In use:** "Edges are burning at this current — that's the upper limit for this bath chemistry, not a setting to push past."
**Common misuse:** Assuming higher current density always plates faster with no quality tradeoff, ignoring the upper limit beyond which deposit quality degrades.

### Hull cell testing
A standardized test method used to evaluate a plating bath's current density range, throwing power, and overall condition using a single test panel.
**In use:** "Ran a Hull cell panel to confirm bath condition before committing the full production run to these parameters."
**Common misuse:** Relying only on production part results to judge bath condition, rather than using Hull cell testing as a controlled, standardized diagnostic method.

### Strike plating
A thin, initial plating layer, often a different metal than the final finish, applied specifically to improve adhesion of a subsequent plating layer.
**In use:** "This substrate needs a nickel strike before the final gold plate — skipping it risks adhesion failure."
**Common misuse:** Skipping a specified strike layer to save time, assuming the final plating layer will adhere adequately without it on substrates that specifically require one.

### Anode
In a plating bath, the electrode, often made of the metal being deposited, that dissolves to replenish the bath's metal ion concentration as plating proceeds.
**In use:** "Check anode condition on a long run — dissolution rate affects bath metal concentration over time."
**Common misuse:** Assuming bath metal concentration remains constant throughout a run without considering anode condition and dissolution rate, particularly relevant for extended production runs.

### Coating thickness gauge (plating context)
An instrument, often XRF, magnetic, or eddy current based depending on substrate/coating combination, used to directly measure actual plated thickness on a finished part.
**In use:** "Gauge reading confirms 25 microns on the actual part — that's what clears it, not the calculated theoretical value alone."
**Common misuse:** Relying on calculated theoretical thickness from process parameters alone without direct gauge measurement verification on the actual part.

### Activation (pre-plating)
A surface preparation step that chemically or electrochemically prepares a substrate surface, often by removing a thin oxide layer, immediately before plating, critical for adhesion.
**In use:** "General cleaning's done, but this substrate still needs activation to strip the oxide layer before plating."
**Common misuse:** Treating general cleaning as equivalent to activation, when activation specifically addresses the surface oxide/passivation layer that general cleaning alone may not remove.
