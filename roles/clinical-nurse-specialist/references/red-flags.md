# Clinical Nurse Specialist — red flags

### Bundle audit compliance under 80%, sustained across 2+ weeks of direct observation
- **Usually means:** a workflow or supply barrier at the bedside (stockout, cart location, extra documentation steps), not a knowledge gap.
- **First question:** what, specifically, is blocking the step for the nurse standing at the bedside right now?
- **Data to pull:** direct-observation audit log (not self-report or chart documentation) plus the supply par-level report for the same period.

### Charted compliance and directly observed compliance disagree by more than ~15 points
- **Usually means:** nurses are documenting the expected action rather than performing it, or the flowsheet field is being auto-populated/copy-forwarded.
- **First question:** when did anyone last watch this step happen versus read that it happened?
- **Data to pull:** paired chart-review vs. direct-observation audit on the same sample of episodes.

### New protocol rolled out house-wide with no unit-level pilot behind it
- **Usually means:** leadership or regulatory pressure to show speed overrode the small-test-of-change step.
- **First question:** what would a 4-week, one-pod pilot have caught that we're about to find out the expensive way?
- **Data to pull:** any pilot data that exists (even informal), and the post-rollout audit from week one.

### An outcome metric only looks good while the CNS is physically present on the unit
- **Usually means:** the change was never hardwired into a structure (order set, flowsheet, staffing policy) — it's being held up by presence, not design.
- **First question:** what happens to this metric on the CNS's next vacation or the champion's next day off?
- **Data to pull:** metric broken out by shift, by day of week, and by CNS on-unit vs. off-unit days.

### Compliance monitoring for a protocol depends on one named "champion" nurse
- **Usually means:** the change is still a pilot, not a system-sphere program, and carries full turnover risk.
- **First question:** who does this when that person leaves, rotates, or takes extended leave?
- **Data to pull:** unit turnover history for the role; whether the audit task appears on any job description or council charter besides that person's.

### CNS spending the visible majority of shift time performing tasks personally rather than coaching or auditing staff
- **Usually means:** understaffing or a hiring gap is pulling the CNS into direct-care backfill, at the cost of nurse- and system-sphere work.
- **First question:** what nurse-sphere or system-sphere deliverable hasn't moved in the last month as a result?
- **Data to pull:** CNS time-and-motion log or calendar audit across a representative 2-week period.

### An outcome is reported as a raw count with no denominator
- **Usually means:** the number can't be benchmarked or trended and is being used rhetorically rather than diagnostically.
- **First question:** per how many line-days, patient-days, or procedures — and against what benchmark?
- **Data to pull:** the relevant denominator (NHSN device-days, patient-days) for the same period.

### A new responsibility or workflow assumes CNS authority (prescribing, admitting, independent diagnosis) without checking the state practice act
- **Usually means:** CNS scope is being conflated with NP scope, which differs by state and is not uniform even among full-practice-authority states.
- **First question:** what does this specific state's board of nursing say about CNS scope, separate from NP scope?
- **Data to pull:** the state nurse practice act / board of nursing CNS scope-of-practice document, not a national association's general summary.

### A national guideline is adopted verbatim without checking its study population against the local unit
- **Usually means:** the evidence is real but doesn't transfer — different acuity, age range, device dwell time, or comorbidity burden.
- **First question:** who was actually studied in the source evidence, versus who is on this unit?
- **Data to pull:** the original study's or guideline's stated inclusion/exclusion criteria.

### CNS position flagged in a budget review with no outcome data tied to cost or length of stay in the last 2–4 quarters
- **Usually means:** sphere work was done but never translated into the rate-and-cost terms that survive a budget conversation.
- **First question:** what is the last quarterly outcome report this role produced, and does it show a dollar or LOS figure?
- **Data to pull:** the last 2–4 quarterly outcome reports (or the absence of them).

### Staff refer to a practice standard as "the CNS's project" rather than "our unit's standard," a year or more after rollout
- **Usually means:** ownership was never formally transferred to the unit council or nurse manager — it's still personally attributed, hence fragile.
- **First question:** whose name is on the audit and the policy now — the CNS's, or the council's?
- **Data to pull:** unit practice council minutes and the policy's listed owner of record.
