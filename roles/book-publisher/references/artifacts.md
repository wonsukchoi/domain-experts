# Artifacts

Filled templates for the three documents a book publisher produces most: the acquisition P&L model, the print-run sizing worksheet, and the auction bid-tracking sheet.

## Acquisition P&L model (filled example)

```markdown
# Acquisition P&L — "The Long Ledger" (debut literary fiction)

Comp titles: "Northfield" (2024), "The Salt House" (2023), "Everything We Buried" (2024)
  — avg. year-1 hardcover units: 12,000

Scenario          | Net units Y1 | Contribution/unit | Y1 contribution
------------------|--------------|--------------------|-----------------
Conservative       | 7,000        | $5.40              | $37,800
Expected            | 12,000       | $5.40              | $64,800
Hit case            | 28,000       | $5.60 (escalator)  | $156,800

Marketing budget (debut literary, standard tier): $40,000

Subsidiary rights estimate (from comp title sales):
  UK/territory rights: $22,000
  Audio rights: $13,000
  Film/TV option: not modeled (speculative, excluded from ceiling calc)

Advance ceiling calc (expected case, 2-year earn-out target):
  ($64,800 contribution - $40,000 marketing + $35,000 sub-rights) = $59,800
  x 2 years = $119,600 -> rounded ceiling: $120,000

Decision: bid up to $120,000 as advance, or restructure to $150,000 total
value via escalated royalty above 10,000 units if outbid on advance alone.
```

## Print-run sizing worksheet (filled example)

```markdown
# Print Run — "The Long Ledger", first printing

Comp title first-week BookScan sales (3 titles, similar platform/review profile):
  1,850 / 2,100 / 1,700 units — avg. 1,880 first-week units

Pre-order tracking at 4 weeks out: 2,400 units (28% above comp avg — starred
reviews in Kirkus + PW driving early signal)

Distributor sell-in request: 9,500 units across accounts

First printing decision: 11,000 units
  = distributor sell-in (9,500) + 15% buffer for reorders (1,500)
  Reprint plates held ready at printer; quoted reprint turnaround: 12 days.
  Rationale: pre-order signal justifies printing above comp-title base rate,
  but NOT to the full pre-order-implied demand — hold the excess as fast-
  reprint capacity rather than upfront inventory risk.

Trigger for reprint order: sell-through >60% of first printing within first
3 weeks, confirmed against BookScan weekly reporting.
```

## Auction bid-tracking sheet (filled example)

```markdown
# Auction Tracker — "The Long Ledger"

Pre-set ceiling (from P&L model, documented before bidding opens): $150,000
  total value ($120,000 advance + escalated royalty structure)

Round | Bidder count | Current high bid | Our position
------|--------------|-------------------|---------------------------
1      | 6            | $85,000           | In (bid $90,000)
2      | 5            | $135,000          | In (bid $140,000)
3      | 4            | $180,000          | OUT — exceeds ceiling
        |              |                    | Final offer sent: $150,000
        |              |                    | structured as above, agent
        |              |                    | declined, auction closed
        |              |                    | at $310,000 to competitor.

Post-mortem note: winning bid was 2.6x our modeled expected-case ceiling.
Consistent with winner's-curse pattern — no adjustment to our modeling
approach warranted; log comp data for this category to refine future
first-week benchmarks.
```
