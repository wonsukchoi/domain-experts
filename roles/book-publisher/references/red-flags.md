# Red flags

### Advance offer exceeds 2x the P&L model's expected-case earn-out projection
- **Usually means:** auction adrenaline or editorial conviction has substituted for the model, and the deal is being justified against the hit-case scenario instead of the expected case.
- **First question:** "what does the expected-case earn-out actually support, and why are we bidding past it?"
- **Data to pull:** the acquisition P&L model's three scenarios, with the current bid marked against each.

### Print run set without referencing comp-title BookScan first-year sales data
- **Usually means:** the run was sized on author confidence, internal excitement, or a round number, rather than actual demonstrated demand for comparable titles.
- **First question:** "which comp titles were used, and what did they actually sell in their first year?"
- **Data to pull:** BookScan first-week and first-year figures for the 2–3 closest comp titles.

### Pre-order numbers tracking >30% below comp-title benchmark at 4 weeks pre-launch
- **Usually means:** either the positioning/marketing isn't landing, the comp titles were poorly matched, or the book's actual market appetite is smaller than modeled.
- **First question:** "is this a marketing execution problem or a demand problem, and how do we tell which?"
- **Data to pull:** pre-order tracking by retail channel compared against the comp set's pre-order trajectory at the same point.

### Marketing budget is backloaded past the first 6 weeks post-launch
- **Usually means:** the spend plan wasn't built around how bestseller-list and algorithmic momentum actually compounds — most of the leverage exists in the launch window, and spend after it works far less hard.
- **First question:** "what percentage of the total marketing budget lands in the first 6 weeks?"
- **Data to pull:** the marketing spend plan broken out by week against launch date.

### Returns reserve held below industry-standard percentage on a title with thin sales history
- **Usually means:** royalty payments are being calculated against a "sold" number that hasn't accounted for the real chance of returns, risking overpayment that becomes a clawback dispute later.
- **First question:** "what reserve percentage are we holding, and does it match this title's risk profile (pre-order-heavy, debut, no sales history)?"
- **Data to pull:** the royalty statement's reserve-against-returns line and the title's actual return rate once available.

### Subsidiary rights not modeled or valued separately before the advance is finalized
- **Usually means:** the acquisition decision was made on print P&L alone, potentially underpricing or overpricing the deal relative to its full economic value.
- **First question:** "what's the estimated sub-rights value for this title, and is it in the ceiling calculation?"
- **Data to pull:** comparable recent sub-rights sales (foreign, audio, film option) for similarly positioned titles.

### Backlist title sales decline >15% year-over-year with no marketing or repositioning review triggered
- **Usually means:** attention and marketing budget have been fully redirected to frontlist, and a quietly profitable backlist title is being allowed to erode without anyone deciding that tradeoff explicitly.
- **First question:** "when was this title's positioning or marketing last reviewed, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** year-over-year BookScan trend for the title alongside its marketing spend history.

### Sell-through at weeks 2–4 diverges materially from the model with no reprint or pull-back decision made
- **Usually means:** the launch plan is running on inertia rather than being actively managed against real sales data as it comes in.
- **First question:** "given actual sell-through so far, does the original print run and marketing plan still make sense?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly BookScan sell-through against the model's projected sales curve for the same weeks.

### Editor pushes to acquire past the auction ceiling citing prestige, without a revised P&L
- **Usually means:** editorial conviction is being treated as sufficient justification on its own, bypassing the financial discipline the ceiling was set to enforce.
- **First question:** "what's changed in the comp data or rights valuation that would justify revising the ceiling, versus just wanting the book more?"
- **Data to pull:** the original P&L model versus any proposed revision, with the specific input that changed identified.

### Author royalty statement disputes recur due to unclear or undisclosed returns-reserve policy
- **Usually means:** the reserve policy wasn't communicated clearly at contract signing, so the author's agent is discovering the reserve mechanism only when a statement comes in lower than expected.
- **First question:** "was the reserve-against-returns policy disclosed in the contract and explained at signing?"
- **Data to pull:** the contract's reserve clause language and the history of statements for this author/title.
