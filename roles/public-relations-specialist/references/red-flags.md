# Public Relations Specialist — Red Flags

### Open rate under 25% across a pitch batch of 20+ reporters
- **Usually means:** subject line or first sentence isn't landing the hook, or the list is poorly targeted (wrong beat, stale contacts).
- **First question:** "Would I open this if I got 40 other pitches that morning?"
- **Data to pull:** subject-line text, list beat-fit ranking, send-time-of-day.

### Reply rate under 10% despite a healthy open rate (40%+)
- **Usually means:** the pitch was opened and read but the hook isn't compelling enough to act on, or the ask (interview, call) is too heavy for the story's newsworthiness.
- **First question:** "What am I actually asking the reporter to do, and is that proportional to the story?"
- **Data to pull:** pitch body text, ask specificity, comparable placement rate from prior campaigns.

### A reporter references a fact from the pitch that isn't in the press release
- **Usually means:** pitch and release drifted out of sync during drafting, or a stat was updated in one document and not the other.
- **First question:** "Do the pitch and release cite the exact same numbers?"
- **Data to pull:** side-by-side diff of pitch email and release draft.

### Embargo publish-time missed by a reporter, even by minutes
- **Usually means:** either the reporter jumped early (rare, serious) or the embargo terms weren't confirmed in writing before the briefing.
- **First question:** "Do I have written confirmation of the exact publish time this reporter agreed to?"
- **Data to pull:** embargo log, email thread confirming the specific time.

### A crisis inquiry sits unanswered more than 60-90 minutes during business hours
- **Usually means:** the org is still "gathering facts" without issuing even a holding statement, which reads as stonewalling regardless of intent.
- **First question:** "Can I send an honest acknowledgment-plus-timeline right now, even without full facts?"
- **Data to pull:** time since inquiry received, what's already confirmed internally vs still pending.

### Same pitch angle sent unchanged across three or more campaigns with declining response
- **Usually means:** no one is tracking angle-level performance, so a stale hook keeps getting reused by habit.
- **First question:** "What was the placement rate on this exact angle last time, and did I check before reusing it?"
- **Data to pull:** historical pitch-tracking table, angle-by-angle placement rate over time.

### A reporter's question touches financial, legal, or safety materiality
- **Usually means:** the inquiry has moved outside this role's authority and into disclosure/materiality judgment territory owned by the PR manager or legal.
- **First question:** "Does this question require a materiality or disclosure-timing call I'm not authorized to make?"
- **Data to pull:** the standing crisis/disclosure escalation plan, who's authorized to approve this specific type of statement.

### A press release boilerplate paragraph hasn't been updated in 2+ quarters
- **Usually means:** customer counts, funding stage, or other boilerplate facts are now stale and will be quoted as current by whoever picks up the release.
- **First question:** "When was the boilerplate last checked against current company facts?"
- **Data to pull:** boilerplate paragraph, current company metrics, date of last boilerplate revision.

### Follow-up pitch sent with zero new information
- **Usually means:** the follow-up is pure pressure ("just checking in") rather than news, which reads as nagging and reduces future open rates from that reporter.
- **First question:** "What's actually new in this follow-up that wasn't in the original pitch?"
- **Data to pull:** original pitch text vs follow-up text, diff for new facts.

### A single outlet or reporter accounts for the majority of a campaign's placements
- **Usually means:** the list is over-concentrated in one relationship rather than genuinely diversified by beat-fit, creating fragility if that one relationship sours.
- **First question:** "How many of my placements this quarter came from the same 1-2 reporters?"
- **Data to pull:** placement log by reporter over a rolling period.
