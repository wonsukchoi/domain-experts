# Red flags

Smell tests a CRM archaeologist catches in the first pass over a survey design, a prior report, or field data.

### Ground surface visibility under roughly 30% with no subsurface testing performed

- **Usually means:** the survey relied on pedestrian-only methods when the conditions couldn't support a defensible negative finding.
- **First question:** what was the documented visibility estimate, and does the report's methodology section justify skipping shovel testing given it?
- **Data to pull:** field visibility log/photos, the governing SHPO's minimum-visibility threshold for pedestrian-only survey.

### Positive STP delineation stopped before two consecutive negative STPs bound it in every direction

- **Usually means:** the site boundary was truncated to save field time or budget, understating the site's actual extent.
- **First question:** in which direction(s) does the boundary rely on a single negative STP instead of two consecutive ones?
- **Data to pull:** the delineation-testing map, STP log showing positive/negative sequence along each transect.

### Site recommended "not eligible" under Criterion D based solely on plow-zone disturbance

- **Usually means:** the evaluator conflated surface disturbance with subsurface destruction, without checking for an intact buried component.
- **First question:** was subsoil below the plow zone actually examined for features or intact deposits, or was testing stopped at the plow zone's base?
- **Data to pull:** STP/unit profile drawings showing depth below plow zone actually reached, any soil-horizon or feature notes from subsoil levels.

### Site on a floodplain, terrace, or colluvial landform reported as a clean Phase I negative with no geomorphological assessment

- **Usually means:** standard-depth shovel testing under-sampled a site buried by later alluvial or colluvial deposition.
- **First question:** what is the depositional history of this landform, and how deep would a buried A-horizon or occupation surface plausibly sit?
- **Data to pull:** soil survey mapping, any regional geomorphological studies, maximum depth reached by the STPs relative to known deposition rates.

### Radiocarbon sample submitted from a context with no clear stratigraphic association

- **Usually means:** the dated material (charcoal, shell) may be intrusive (root disturbance, rodent burrow) or residual (reworked from an earlier deposit), producing a date that doesn't actually date the feature of interest.
- **First question:** what is the sample's Harris matrix position relative to the feature it's meant to date, and is there any visible disturbance at that context's boundary?
- **Data to pull:** excavation unit profile/plan showing the sample's exact provenience, Harris matrix diagram, field notes on soil disturbance near the sample.

### Calibrated radiocarbon date reported as a single point estimate

- **Usually means:** the calibration output (a range, possibly multimodal) was collapsed to its midpoint for narrative convenience, discarding real chronological uncertainty.
- **First question:** what did the calibration software actually output — a single unimodal range, or multiple disconnected ranges with different probabilities?
- **Data to pull:** the OxCal/CALIB output file or printout, lab report with conventional age and error.

### APE defined as only the direct project footprint on an undertaking with likely indirect effects

- **Usually means:** visual, auditory, or atmospheric effects (e.g., a wind farm's viewshed, a highway's noise footprint near a historic district) were left out of the identification effort.
- **First question:** does this undertaking type have a plausible indirect-effects pathway, and was it evaluated before the APE was finalized?
- **Data to pull:** the APE justification memo, any viewshed or noise-modeling analysis, agency correspondence on APE scope.

### Adverse effect finding issued without documented SHPO/THPO consultation

- **Usually means:** the review-period step was skipped or the finding was made unilaterally by the agency or consultant without the required 30-day consultation.
- **First question:** is there a dated SHPO/THPO response letter or documented lack of response within the review period on file?
- **Data to pull:** the Section 106 correspondence log, SHPO/THPO transmittal and response letters.

### Testing continues near probable human remains without a documented halt

- **Usually means:** field pressure (schedule, curiosity) overrode the mandatory stop-work protocol under NAGPRA or the state burial law.
- **First question:** was work halted at the location the moment remains were suspected, and was the appropriate authority (SHPO, coroner, Tribal representative) notified before any further excavation there?
- **Data to pull:** the field director's incident log, notification timestamps, any photographs or notes taken before the halt.

### Artifact density claimed as evidence of a discrete activity locus with no background-density comparison

- **Usually means:** a high raw count was characterized as significant without checking whether it's actually higher than the site's or parcel's typical background scatter.
- **First question:** what is the density (not raw count) at the STP or unit in question, and what's the background density computed from negative/low-yield testing on the same grid?
- **Data to pull:** per-context artifact counts and excavated volumes across the full testing grid, not just the high-count location.
