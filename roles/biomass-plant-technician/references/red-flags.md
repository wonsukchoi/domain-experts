# Red flags

### A new fuel delivery run at standard combustion parameters without sampling actual moisture content
- **Usually means:** combustion parameters calibrated for a different moisture level may produce incomplete combustion and elevated emissions.
- **First question:** has this specific delivery's actual moisture content been tested, or is it assumed to match the design basis?
- **Data to pull:** fuel moisture test result, design basis moisture content, current combustion parameters.

### A fuel feed interruption addressed by adjusting combustion controls rather than checking for mechanical bridging
- **Usually means:** the actual cause may be a mechanical fuel-handling issue (bridging, jamming) rather than a combustion parameter problem.
- **First question:** does the feed interruption pattern match a mechanical bridging signature, or is it being treated as a combustion issue?
- **Data to pull:** fuel handling equipment status, feed rate/flow data at the time of interruption.

### Soot blowing/cleaning performed on a fixed calendar schedule regardless of observed slagging/fouling conditions
- **Usually means:** actual fouling rate (which varies with fuel batch ash chemistry) may not match the fixed schedule's assumption.
- **First question:** does current heat transfer surface condition or steam temperature trend indicate fouling faster or slower than the fixed schedule assumes?
- **Data to pull:** steam temperature/heat transfer trend data, current soot-blowing schedule basis.

### Emissions monitored on a fixed, infrequent schedule despite known fuel batch variability
- **Usually means:** a fuel-driven emissions excursion between scheduled checks could go undetected.
- **First question:** does the monitoring frequency account for the actual variability in fuel batches being burned, or is it set for a hypothetically consistent fuel source?
- **Data to pull:** monitoring frequency, recent fuel batch variability data.

### CO or particulate emissions trending upward with no corresponding investigation into fuel moisture/composition change
- **Usually means:** a fuel batch characteristic shift may be the cause, requiring combustion parameter adjustment rather than a different corrective action.
- **First question:** has recent fuel moisture/composition been checked against what combustion parameters are currently calibrated for?
- **Data to pull:** recent fuel batch test data, current combustion parameter settings.

### A fuel handling bridging event resolved by manual intervention without investigating the underlying cause (particle size, moisture, storage condition)
- **Usually means:** the same bridging issue is likely to recur if the underlying fuel characteristic driving it isn't identified.
- **First question:** does this fuel batch show characteristics (moisture, particle size distribution) known to increase bridging risk?
- **Data to pull:** fuel batch characteristics, bridging event frequency/pattern.

### Boiler output reduced without a documented explanation tied to fuel characteristics
- **Usually means:** an operator may have made an ad hoc adjustment without recording the fuel-driven reasoning, losing traceability for future reference.
- **First question:** does the reduced-output decision reference the specific fuel moisture/composition that necessitated it?
- **Data to pull:** operating log entry for the output change, fuel batch data at the time.

### A recurring slagging/fouling issue at a specific location in the boiler correlating with a specific fuel source
- **Usually means:** that fuel source's ash chemistry may have characteristics driving more aggressive fouling than other sources.
- **First question:** does the fouling pattern correlate with fuel from a specific supplier/source?
- **Data to pull:** fouling location/severity data, fuel source traceability.

### A new fuel source or supplier introduced without characterizing its typical moisture/composition range before full-scale use
- **Usually means:** combustion parameters may not be appropriately calibrated for this new source's actual characteristics.
- **First question:** has this new source's typical fuel characteristics been sampled/established, or is it assumed similar to prior sources?
- **Data to pull:** new source's fuel characterization data if available, prior source's typical range for comparison.
