# Anesthesiologist Assistant — Red Flags

### ETCO2 rising >10 mmHg over <10 minutes with unchanged ventilator settings
- **Usually means:** Malignant hyperthermia (especially if paired with rising temperature and tachycardia); second most likely is a partial rebreathing/soda-lime exhaustion issue or a genuine hypermetabolic state (thyroid storm, sepsis).
- **First question:** "Is the capnography waveform well-formed, or flat/erratic?" — a well-formed rising waveform points to the patient; a flat or noisy one points to equipment.
- **Data to pull:** Soda-lime canister color/change interval, temperature trend over the same window, prior MH family history in the chart.

### Core temperature rising >0.5°C in 15 minutes intraoperatively
- **Usually means:** Malignant hyperthermia if paired with ETCO2 rise and tachycardia; second most likely is over-aggressive active warming or an infectious process already present preoperatively.
- **First question:** "Is this rise paired with an ETCO2 or heart-rate change, or isolated?"
- **Data to pull:** Warming device settings/duration, MH family history, preoperative temperature baseline.

### More than 4 concurrent rooms open under one directing anesthesiologist, even briefly
- **Usually means:** A scheduling overlap has silently converted the model from medical direction to medical supervision for the overlap period — reduced real-time backup, not a documentation footnote.
- **First question:** "For how long, and is anyone actively managing the fifth room, or is it just open?"
- **Data to pull:** OR scheduling board, anesthesiologist's actual physical location during the overlap window.

### Unexplained tachycardia or hypertension mid-case with no new surgical stimulus
- **Usually means:** Light anesthesia (check depth/MAC first); second most likely is pain from an inadequate block, or early hypovolemia.
- **First question:** "What is the current MAC or infusion rate relative to what this stimulus typically requires?"
- **Data to pull:** Volatile agent concentration or TIVA infusion rate trend, surgical step log, blood loss estimate.

### STOP-BANG score ≥3 on a patient with a low ASA Physical Status class
- **Usually means:** Clinically significant, possibly undiagnosed, obstructive sleep apnea being masked by an otherwise healthy presentation.
- **First question:** "Does the postoperative disposition plan already account for OSA risk, or only for the ASA class?"
- **Data to pull:** STOP-BANG component answers, home sleep study if any, planned opioid load for the case.

### Any drug drawn up without an independent label check immediately before injection
- **Usually means:** Time pressure or high case volume overriding the verification step — the single highest-frequency precursor to a look-alike/sound-alike swap (succinylcholine/neostigmine, phenylephrine/ephedrine).
- **First question:** "Was this syringe labeled and read aloud at draw-up, and re-checked at the point of injection?"
- **Data to pull:** Syringe labeling per APSF color-code standard, count of drugs drawn up simultaneously in this induction.

### Case not responding as expected within the typical window for the intervention given
- **Usually means:** Wrong diagnosis/differential, not an underdosed correct one; second most likely is a mechanical issue (line infiltration, disconnected circuit).
- **First question:** "Has the differential been re-opened, or has the same intervention just been escalated?"
- **Data to pull:** Time-stamped intervention log, response curve for each dose/action taken so far.

### Anesthesiologist not physically present for a scheduled induction or emergence, non-emergent reason
- **Usually means:** A concurrency or scheduling conflict is eating into the medical-direction requirement.
- **First question:** "Is this a one-off delay, or a pattern for this anesthesiologist's room load today?"
- **Data to pull:** Medical-direction documentation log (seven-step template), anesthesiologist's schedule for the day.

### Post-regional-block perioral numbness, tinnitus, or metallic taste
- **Usually means:** Early local anesthetic systemic toxicity (LAST); second most likely is vasovagal response to the injection itself.
- **First question:** "What was the total local anesthetic dose and was there an inadvertent intravascular injection?"
- **Data to pull:** Total local anesthetic mg given (compare to lean-body-weight-based maximum), injection technique notes (aspiration performed Y/N).

### Chart documentation being written retrospectively at end of case rather than in real time
- **Usually means:** Case volume or fatigue is compressing documentation into a single end-of-day pass, which loses exact timing needed for both safety review and the medical-direction seven-step requirement.
- **First question:** "Can the exact minute of the first abnormal trend be reconstructed independently of memory?"
- **Data to pull:** AIMS auto-captured vitals timestamps versus manual note entry timestamps.

### Repeat vasopressor or fluid boluses without a re-assessment of the underlying cause
- **Usually means:** Ongoing blood loss or a developing anaphylaxis being treated symptomatically instead of diagnostically.
- **First question:** "What is the cumulative dose so far, and has a cause been reconsidered since the second bolus?"
- **Data to pull:** Cumulative pressor/fluid totals, estimated blood loss, any new rash/bronchospasm noted.
