# Red Flags

Smell tests for cataloging, circulation, ILL, and serials work. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Copy-cataloging match rate under ~90% on a routine trade order
- **Usually means:** either the order genuinely skews toward small-press/self-published titles with thin OCLC coverage, or the search technique is missing matches that exist (searching by title/keyword instead of ISBN first).
- **First question:** "Of the unmatched titles, how many have a valid ISBN that was actually searched?"
- **Data to pull:** the batch's unmatched-item list with ISBNs, re-searched directly by ISBN in OCLC.

### Match rate under ~70%
- **Usually means:** a real search-technique or system-access problem (wrong OCLC database selected, missing search privileges), not "this batch is just unusual."
- **First question:** "Has this technician's OCLC search default or database scope changed recently?"
- **Data to pull:** OCLC Connexion session/search logs for the affected technician or terminal.

### A single OCLC holdings count of 1 (this library) on a mainstream trade title
- **Usually means:** the record was misattached to the wrong bibliographic record, or the title was miscataloged as a rare/local item.
- **First question:** "Is this actually a rare or local-only printing, or did the wrong record get selected?"
- **Data to pull:** the attached record's ISBN, publisher, and edition statement compared against the physical item.

### ILL lending fill rate below ~60% over a quarter
- **Usually means:** items marked "available" in the catalog are actually missing, checked out, or non-circulating and not flagged as such, so requests get declined after the fact.
- **First question:** "Of the declines, how many were 'not on shelf' vs. 'not lendable by policy'?"
- **Data to pull:** the ILL decline-reason report broken out by cause.

### Serial issue more than 90 days late with no claim filed
- **Usually means:** the check-in routine has a gap — either nobody is reviewing the expected-vs-received report, or the title was missed when check-in responsibilities were last reassigned.
- **First question:** "When was the last time the expected-issues report was reviewed for this title?"
- **Data to pull:** the serials module's expected-vs-received report, sorted by days overdue.

### Same patron tech question 3+ times in one shift
- **Usually means:** a documentation gap (no help sheet, or an outdated one) rather than a training gap in any one staff member.
- **First question:** "Is there a current one-page help sheet for this task, and when was it last updated?"
- **Data to pull:** the reference-desk question log for repeat topics over the past week, not just the shift.

### Backlog of "in process" items past the shelf-ready SLA (commonly 5 business days)
- **Usually means:** either an ambiguous-match bottleneck (items awaiting librarian original cataloging) or a staffing/volume mismatch, not a general "we're behind" problem.
- **First question:** "How many of the backlogged items are awaiting original cataloging vs. sitting untouched in the copy-cataloging queue?"
- **Data to pull:** the in-process report filtered by cataloging status, not just age.

### Weeding list generated by publication-date cutoff alone
- **Usually means:** circulation data wasn't cross-checked, risking removal of titles that are old but still actively used (backlist classics, perennial reference).
- **First question:** "Was last-circ date and checkout count pulled for every title on this list before finalizing?"
- **Data to pull:** the weeding candidate list joined against circulation history for the past 24–36 months.

### Local call number outside the expected classification range
- **Usually means:** a shelving-convenience override that will misfile the item relative to its actual subject, confusing both patrons browsing the correct range and future staff reclassifying nearby items.
- **First question:** "Is there a documented local exception policy for this range, or was this a one-off decision?"
- **Data to pull:** the classification scheme's expected range for the item's subject heading, compared to the assigned number.

### Duplicate bibliographic records attached for the same ISBN in the local ILS
- **Usually means:** two staff members cataloged the same title independently without checking for an existing local record first, splitting holdings and circulation history across two records.
- **First question:** "Do both records show the same ISBN, and which one has the active circulation history?"
- **Data to pull:** both records' 020 fields and item-level circulation counts, to decide which to merge into.

### ILL borrowed item open past the lending library's due date with no renewal or recall check
- **Usually means:** the due-date tracking relies on the local system's default loan period rather than the actual terms the lending library set, which can differ.
- **First question:** "What due date did the lending library's paperwork or transaction record actually specify?"
- **Data to pull:** the original ILL transaction record, not the local circulation system's auto-assigned due date.

### "Core-level" or below OCLC record accepted with zero subject headings
- **Usually means:** the record was accepted purely on ISBN/pagination match without checking the 6xx fields, leaving the item undiscoverable by subject browse or search.
- **First question:** "Does this record have at least one subject heading, and if not, was that checked before acceptance?"
- **Data to pull:** the record's full field list as attached, specifically the 6xx block.
