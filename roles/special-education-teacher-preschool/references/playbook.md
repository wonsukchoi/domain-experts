# Playbook

## Domain-by-domain screening worksheet

Fill for every referral before drafting an eligibility recommendation.

| Domain | Child's age-equivalent | Chronological age | Delta (months) | Within normal variation? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Communication (expressive) | 22mo-equivalent (score 17.5/60, cutoff 24.16) | 34mo | -12 | No — flagged |
| Communication (receptive) | 30mo-equivalent | 34mo | -4 | Yes |
| Gross motor | 33mo-equivalent | 34mo | -1 | Yes |
| Fine motor | 31mo-equivalent | 34mo | -3 | Yes |
| Social-emotional | 32mo-equivalent | 34mo | -2 | Yes |
| Adaptive/self-help | 33mo-equivalent | 34mo | -1 | Yes |

**Rule:** a domain delta beyond roughly 25-33% of chronological age (context-dependent on the specific tool's SD bands) merits a flag; a delta within that band across multiple domains simultaneously is what distinguishes a global delay from an isolated one — never label global delay off a single flagged domain.

## Delay-vs-variation decision table

| Pattern observed | Likely read | Action |
|---|---|---|
| One domain flagged, all others within ~3mo of chronological age | Isolated delay in that domain | Targeted referral/goal in the flagged domain only |
| Two or more domains flagged across two independent settings (home + classroom/clinical) | Corroborated multi-domain delay | Broader multi-domain evaluation warranted |
| One domain flagged, but only in one setting/language and not replicated elsewhere | Possible testing-condition artifact | Re-screen in child's dominant setting/language before acting |
| All domains near cutoff but none clearly below it | Normal variation, monitor | Watchful waiting with a defined recheck date (e.g., 8-12 weeks), not immediate eligibility |

## IFSP-to-IEP goal conversion worksheet

| IFSP outcome (Part C, family-centered) | Converted IEP goal (Part B, milestone-framed) | Baseline | Target | Timeline | Measurement tool |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Mateo will use words to ask for what he wants during mealtime" | "During snack/mealtime, Mateo will request a desired item using a 2-word phrase" | 22 words expressive vocabulary | 75 words | 6 months | Caregiver word-count log + monthly clinician check |
| "Mateo will play near other children without hitting" | "During free play, Mateo will engage in parallel play for 5+ minutes without a physical aggression incident" | 2 min average before incident | 8 min average | 3 months | Direct classroom observation, 2x/week |

**Rule:** every converted goal keeps the functional context (setting, routine) from the IFSP outcome — dropping the setting when moving to IEP language is how goals drift toward generic academic phrasing.

## Caregiver coaching plan template

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Target strategy | Model 2-word phrase, wait 5 seconds, expand child's 1-word attempt |
| Embedded routine(s) | Mealtime (10 min), bath time (10 min) |
| Frequency | 7 days/week, both routines |
| Coaching cycle | Teacher models strategy live (session 1) → caregiver attempts with teacher observing (session 2) → specific feedback given → caregiver reports independent use (session 3+) |
| Adherence tracking | Caregiver log: routine completed Y/N per day |
| Adherence at 12-week check | 6/7 days average (85%) |
| Overload check | Only 1 strategy taught until adherence >70% sustained for 4 consecutive weeks |

**Rule:** never introduce a second strategy until the first is being used independently at a sustained frequency — stacking strategies before the first sticks is the most common cause of caregiver-coaching plans failing.

## Progress trajectory tracking table

| Checkpoint | Expressive vocabulary (words) | Gain since last checkpoint | Rate (words/month) | Compared to own baseline rate (2 words/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Intake (week 0) | 22 | — | — | baseline |
| Week 12 (3mo) recheck | 61 | +39 | ~13 | 6.5x acceleration |
| Week 24 (6mo) recheck (target) | 75 | +14 (from wk12) | ~4.7 | 2.4x baseline (deceleration expected as approaching typical range) |

**Rule:** plot against the child's own baseline rate, not a population norm rate — a deceleration toward a still-above-baseline rate near a target is expected and not itself a red flag; a drop back to baseline rate (2 words/month) before reaching target is.
