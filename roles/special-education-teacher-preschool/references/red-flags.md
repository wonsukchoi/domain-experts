# Red flags

### A global developmental delay label assigned from one flagged screening domain
- **Usually means:** the score reflects an isolated domain issue, a testing-condition artifact, or (less often) a true multi-domain delay that hasn't been corroborated yet.
- **First question:** did the other developmental domains score within normal range on the same screening administration?
- **Data to pull:** full domain-by-domain screening scores (ASQ-3 or equivalent), not just the summary/flagged result.

### An IEP or IFSP goal written as academic subject content for a child under 5 (e.g., "identify 10 letters")
- **Usually means:** the goal writer defaulted to a school-age goal template instead of a developmental-milestone framing appropriate to this age band.
- **First question:** does this goal describe an observable developmental milestone in a functional setting, or a subject-matter skill?
- **Data to pull:** the present-levels section the goal is drawn from, and the goal bank/template used.

### A caregiver coaching plan introducing three or more new strategies in a single session
- **Usually means:** the plan is set up to overload the caregiver, which predicts low adherence regardless of how sound each individual strategy is.
- **First question:** what is the caregiver's current adherence rate on strategies already taught?
- **Data to pull:** the caregiver coaching log and adherence percentage over the last 2-4 weeks.

### A Part C-to-Part B transition conference not yet scheduled inside the 90-day window before the child's third birthday
- **Usually means:** a procedural deadline is at risk of being missed independent of whether the eligibility decision itself will be correct.
- **First question:** has the transition conference been scheduled, and does it fall at least 90 days before the birthday?
- **Data to pull:** the IFSP transition timeline and current conference scheduling record.

### Two consecutive progress-monitoring data points flat against the child's own established trajectory
- **Usually means:** either the intervention isn't genuinely embedded in a preferred activity, the dosage/frequency has slipped, or the goal's target rate was set unrealistically given the child's baseline.
- **First question:** was the "play-based" session audited to confirm the target skill is embedded in an activity the child is actually motivated by, versus drill relabeled as play?
- **Data to pull:** session logs describing the actual activity structure, and the caregiver adherence log for the same period.

### A "play-based" activity that is seated instruction or flashcard drill conducted near toys
- **Usually means:** the intervention dose is effectively lower than logged, since a disengaged child isn't practicing the target skill even if the session is recorded as delivered.
- **First question:** is the target skill embedded inside the activity's own motivating structure (a turn-taking game, requesting a preferred item), or delivered alongside it?
- **Data to pull:** a direct observation or video review of a recent session.

### Eligibility determined from a single caregiver-report checklist with no direct observation
- **Usually means:** the eligibility decision rests on one source and one setting, which is a weaker basis than IDEA's intent for a multi-source determination.
- **First question:** has the child been directly observed in at least one additional setting (classroom, clinical) beyond the caregiver report?
- **Data to pull:** the full evaluation record listing sources and settings used.

### An IEP written at Part B transition with no functional link back to the prior IFSP present levels
- **Usually means:** the writer started the IEP from a blank template rather than carrying forward what was already known about the child's functioning and what interventions had already been tried.
- **First question:** does the new IEP's present-levels section reference the specific IFSP outcomes and their status at transition?
- **Data to pull:** the prior IFSP document and the transition conference notes.

### A formal diagnostic label (autism, specific learning disability) used in team discussion before a completed, documented evaluation supports it
- **Usually means:** the team is treating a suspected pattern as a confirmed diagnosis, which can prematurely narrow the intervention plan or create a record inconsistent with the actual eligibility category on file.
- **First question:** does the evaluation record actually support this label, or is the current eligibility category still "developmental delay" pending further assessment?
- **Data to pull:** the eligibility determination paperwork and the specific evaluation reports it cites.
