# Vocabulary

### Permission to Operate (PTO)
The utility's formal authorization to energize and export from an interconnected system, granted after both AHJ inspection and the interconnection agreement are complete.
**In use:** "We're PTO-pending on #114, don't count that revenue yet."
**Common misuse:** Treating "inspection passed" as equivalent to PTO — a system can pass the AHJ's field inspection and still be weeks away from utility PTO.

### AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction)
The local government body (usually a county or municipal building/electrical department) enforcing code for permitting and inspection.
**In use:** "Check with the AHJ before assuming SolarAPP+ covers this scope."
**Common misuse:** Treating "the county" and "the utility" as the same approval gate — they are separate processes, separate desks, and separate timelines.

### Installer of Record
The specific individual — often required to hold a NABCEP certification — named on the permit, and where applicable an incentive program, as legally accountable for a given installation.
**In use:** "Confirm who's listed as installer of record before we swap crews on that job."
**Common misuse:** Assuming any certified tech on the crew satisfies the requirement, when the permit application names one specific individual.

### Rapid shutdown (NEC 690.12)
The code requirement that PV system conductors de-energize to a specified low-voltage state within a set boundary and time window, for firefighter safety during a forced roof entry.
**In use:** "Label placement failed 690.12/690.56(C) — that's a same-day fix, not a design problem."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as purely an equipment spec question, when inspectors separately check labeling and documentation compliance under 690.56(C).

### Interconnection agreement
The contract between the system owner and the utility governing how a distributed generation system connects to and exports onto the grid.
**In use:** "Don't schedule final payment collection until the interconnection agreement is fully executed, not just submitted."
**Common misuse:** Treating submission of the application as equivalent to approval — the agreement isn't binding, and PTO doesn't follow, until it's executed.

### Fast-track (interconnection tier)
A simplified, faster utility interconnection review path for systems below a size or grid-impact threshold, commonly modeled on FERC's Small Generator Interconnection Procedures.
**In use:** "This one's over the fast-track threshold — budget for a full impact-study timeline."
**Common misuse:** Assuming every residential system automatically qualifies for fast-track regardless of its actual kW rating and the local feeder's condition.

### Cost per watt ($/W)
Total installed cost divided by the system's nameplate DC wattage — the standard unit for comparing project economics across system sizes.
**In use:** "We closed #109 at $2.91/W against a $2.85 budget."
**Common misuse:** Comparing $/W across system classes (residential vs. commercial, roof vs. ground) as if soft costs scale identically — they don't, and the comparison hides which segment is actually losing margin.

### Change order
A documented, priced modification to the original scope of work, agreed after the contract was signed.
**In use:** "Get the added string written up as a change order before the crew touches it."
**Common misuse:** Treating a verbal customer request as already-approved scope and absorbing the extra labor into the original job budget, which quietly erodes margin and hides the real driver of a cost overrun.

### First-pass yield
The percentage of installed jobs that pass AHJ or utility inspection on the first attempt, without a callback.
**In use:** "Crew A's first-pass yield dropped to 80% this month — something's off."
**Common misuse:** Not tracking it at all, or tracking one fleet-wide pass rate instead of splitting it by crew and jurisdiction, which hides which crew or site condition is actually driving the failures.

### SolarAPP+
A DOE/NREL-developed automated permitting platform that instantly approves code-compliant residential PV (and increasingly storage) permit applications in jurisdictions that have adopted it.
**In use:** "That county's on SolarAPP+ — we can promise a same-day permit."
**Common misuse:** Assuming every jurisdiction has adopted it, or that every design (nonstandard equipment, unusual structural conditions) qualifies for the automated path rather than falling back to manual review.

### Warranty/callback reserve
A budgeted percentage of a job's contract value set aside specifically to cover post-install rework, tracked apart from the original install labor budget.
**In use:** "That label fix comes out of the callback reserve, not the install budget."
**Common misuse:** Pulling callback labor from "whichever crew is free" without charging it against the reserve, which hides a real quality trend behind a normal-looking install-labor number.

### Plan review
The AHJ's technical review of submitted permit drawings and calculations prior to permit issuance — a distinct stage from the later field inspection.
**In use:** "It's stuck in plan review, not field inspection — different desk, different timeline."
**Common misuse:** Using "still in permitting" as a catch-all status when the two stages have different bottlenecks, different contacts, and different expected turnaround times.

### Interconnection application
The formal request submitted to the utility (separate from the AHJ building/electrical permit) to connect a distributed generation system to the grid.
**In use:** "The permit's approved, but the interconnection application hasn't even gone in yet."
**Common misuse:** Treating the AHJ permit and the utility interconnection application as one submission, when they are filed with different entities on different timelines and both must clear before PTO.
