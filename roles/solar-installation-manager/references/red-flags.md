# Red flags

### Crew idle-day count >1 per crew per week
- **Usually means:** the schedule was built off the install calendar without cross-checking each job's permit/interconnection stage, or a systemic AHJ slowdown is going untracked.
- **First question:** which pipeline stage did the job stall at — submitted, under plan review, or awaiting inspection?
- **Data to pull:** pipeline-stage report cross-referenced against the crew dispatch calendar for the week.

### Cost-per-watt variance >3% over budget on a closed job
- **Usually means:** a callback or rework got absorbed into new-install labor instead of the warranty reserve, or an approved change order was never billed.
- **First question:** was there a failed inspection or a scope change on this job that isn't reflected in the budget line it hit?
- **Data to pull:** job-costing detail by labor category, change-order log for the job.

### A jurisdiction's average permit turnaround running >20% above its trailing-quarter baseline
- **Usually means:** an AHJ staffing change, a code-cycle adoption, or a backlog from a local policy shift — not a one-off slow week.
- **First question:** has anyone called the plan-review desk this month, or has the team just been waiting on the portal?
- **Data to pull:** permit tracking log by jurisdiction, dated AHJ correspondence.

### Near-zero reinspection failures across the crew fleet for several months
- **Usually means:** either genuinely clean installs, or failures are being fixed informally in the field without being logged, which understates the warranty reserve's real utilization.
- **First question:** are field techs logging every AHJ callback, including minor label/documentation fixes, or only the ones that require a return trip?
- **Data to pull:** inspection pass/fail log compared against the warranty reserve ledger for the same period.

### Warranty/callback reserve utilization above 100% of its budgeted percentage for a segment
- **Usually means:** a systemic install-quality issue concentrated in one crew, one equipment SKU, or one jurisdiction's inspection standard.
- **First question:** is the callback pattern concentrated on one crew, one product line, or one AHJ?
- **Data to pull:** callback log filtered by crew, SKU, and jurisdiction.

### A completed-and-inspected job sitting >10 business days without PTO
- **Usually means:** the utility interconnection paperwork stalled, or a required document is missing from the application.
- **First question:** has the utility acknowledged receipt of a complete interconnection packet, or is a piece still missing?
- **Data to pull:** interconnection application/portal status, dated utility correspondence.

### The same crew repeatedly pulled onto "rush" jobs outside normal dispatch
- **Usually means:** the schedule is being built reactively off hope rather than off actual install-ready status.
- **First question:** how many jobs in the pipeline are genuinely install-ready this week versus scheduled optimistically?
- **Data to pull:** install-ready job count for the week versus total crew capacity.

### A single equipment SKU appearing in more than 2 callback tickets in a month
- **Usually means:** a batch or manufacturing defect, not installer error.
- **First question:** do the affected units share a lot or serial-number range?
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer defect/RMA log cross-referenced against the affected units' serial numbers.

### Crew day-rate cost trending up without a matching increase in kW or SF installed per day
- **Usually means:** overtime creep, or productivity loss from staging and material availability, not a rate change.
- **First question:** is the crew losing hours to travel or material pickup instead of actual install time?
- **Data to pull:** daily crew-hour log versus kW/SF installed, by crew.

### A NABCEP-certified installer of record not actually present on a job that requires one
- **Usually means:** dispatch didn't cross-check the job's certification requirement against the crew roster before assigning it.
- **First question:** which jobs this week legally require a certified installer of record, and is that specific person assigned to each?
- **Data to pull:** job requirements list versus crew roster and current certifications.

### A permit application resubmitted more than once for the same correction type
- **Usually means:** a design or documentation error is systemic to the design team's template, not a one-off drafting mistake.
- **First question:** is this the same rejection reason (e.g., missing structural calc, wrong label spec) as a prior resubmission?
- **Data to pull:** AHJ rejection/comment history for the application, compared against other recent submissions from the same designer.
