**Pre-session verbal check-in (client on table, before starting):**

**Classification:** Systemic-adjacent urgent finding (not a simple local contraindication) — full stop-work on the affected limb, refer out, do not substitute "lighter pressure" for "no work."

**Technique/pressure applied:** Deep-tissue on back (client's stated preference), moderate-to-deep on neck/shoulders, checked against tissue guarding at 10-minute intervals — no guarding observed, pressure held at requested level.

## Technique-selection reference (filled)

| Presenting complaint | First-choice technique | Why | When to escalate to a different approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generalized stress, poor sleep, first-time client | Swedish, moderate pressure | Broad relaxation goal, unknown tissue tolerance | If client requests more targeted work after assessing response |
| Chronic, localized adhesion (e.g., upper trapezius knot present for months) | Deep tissue, localized | Adhesion needs sustained, focused pressure to release | If pressure produces guarding rather than release, back off before continuing |
| Sharp, reproducible referred pain from a specific point (e.g., pressing left rhomboid reproduces pain down the arm) | Trigger-point | Reproducible referral pattern is the specific indication for this technique | If pressure doesn't reproduce the referral pattern, it's not a trigger point — reassess |
| Restricted range of motion without acute injury (e.g., limited shoulder rotation, no recent trauma) | Myofascial release | Addresses fascial restriction rather than muscle-belly tension directly | If restriction doesn't improve over 2-3 sessions, refer to PT for a fuller assessment |
| Pregnant client, third trimester | Side-lying Swedish, light-to-moderate pressure | Avoids supine hypotensive syndrome risk; lighter pressure standard for pregnancy | If any swelling, headache, or visual disturbance reported, refer to OB before proceeding — possible preeclampsia signs, outside scope |
| Post-chemotherapy client, in active treatment | Light pressure, modified Swedish, avoid port site and affected-side lymph nodes | Site-specific modification per current oncology-massage practice, not blanket refusal | If client reports new/unexplained bruising beyond the norm for their treatment, defer to their oncology team before continuing regular sessions |
