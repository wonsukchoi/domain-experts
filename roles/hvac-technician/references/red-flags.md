# Red flags

Field smell tests a senior tech catches in the first ten minutes on site. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before charge, capacity, or duct work gets touched.

## 1. Temperature split under 14°F or over 22°F across the evaporator

**Usually means:** under 14°F with high static usually means restricted airflow (dirty filter, undersized return, closed dampers); over 22°F usually means low charge or a metering-device fault, not an airflow problem.

**First question:** "What's the total external static pressure, and when was the filter last changed?"

**Data to pull:** TESP against rated static, filter condition, indoor wet bulb, superheat/subcooling against target — split alone doesn't distinguish the cause, it only flags that something needs to.

## 2. Second "topped off refrigerant" ticket within 12 months

**Usually means:** an active leak, not normal charge drift — sealed systems don't lose refrigerant in ordinary operation. The first visit's fix addressed the symptom (low superheat/subcooling) and skipped the cause.

**First question:** "How much was added last time, and was it logged against a weight, or just added until the vents felt cold?"

**Data to pull:** prior service ticket's recorded weight added (if any), current superheat/subcooling vs. target, nameplate full charge, percentage of total charge lost since last service.

## 3. Total external static pressure above ~0.8 in. w.c. against a 0.5 in. w.c. rated system

**Usually means:** duct restriction (undersized returns, crushed flex, excessive fittings) or a dirty coil/filter — the blower is fighting the duct system, which caps both delivered capacity and motor life regardless of refrigerant charge.

**First question:** "Where's the static drop concentrated — filter, coil, or the duct runs themselves?"

**Data to pull:** static readings segmented across filter, coil, supply trunk, return trunk; delivered CFM against the Manual D design airflow for this tonnage.

## 4. Oversized-feeling complaint: short cycling plus high indoor humidity at setpoint

**Usually means:** equipment capacity exceeds the Manual J design load — the thermostat is satisfied before the coil has run long enough to remove moisture. Frequently traces to a "bump it up a size" install with no load calc on file.

**First question:** "Do we have a Manual J on file for this house, and what's this unit's nominal tonnage against it?"

**Data to pull:** Manual J load (existing or re-run), installed equipment nominal capacity, cycle length and cycles-per-hour on a design-condition day, indoor RH log if available.

## 5. Superheat reading "normal" on a TXV/EEV system with other overcharge symptoms

**Usually means:** the valve is holding superheat near its setpoint regardless of actual charge — superheat is the wrong diagnostic for this metering device. Subcooling hasn't been checked, and the system may be significantly over or under its rated charge.

**First question:** "What's subcooling reading against the nameplate target — not superheat?"

**Data to pull:** liquid line pressure and temperature for subcooling calculation, nameplate subcooling target, high-side pressure trend, sight glass condition if equipped.

## 6. Sight glass shows bubbles despite an adequate or high subcooling reading

**Usually means:** non-condensables (air/moisture) in the system rather than low charge — bubbles are frequently misread as "needs refrigerant" when subcooling already says the charge is adequate.

**First question:** "Was this system evacuated to spec after the last service that opened it, and do we have a vacuum-hold record?"

**Data to pull:** last evacuation record and micron level held, subcooling and superheat readings, non-condensable purge history if any.

## 7. Vacuum won't pull below ~1,000 microns, or rises quickly after isolating the pump

**Usually means:** moisture in the system (rising and stalling) or an active leak (rising and continuing) — recharging over either produces an immediate comeback and, if moisture, acid formation that damages the compressor over months.

**First question:** "Does the vacuum rise and plateau, or rise and keep climbing?"

**Data to pull:** micron gauge log over a 10–15 minute isolated hold, moisture source candidates (recent line replacement, burnout history), leak-search results if the rise doesn't plateau.

## 8. No line-set length or nameplate charge recorded on the install ticket

**Usually means:** the original installer didn't do (or didn't record) a charge-by-weight startup — any future subcooling/superheat check has no factory baseline to reconcile against, and every subsequent tech is charging by chart alone with no cross-check.

**First question:** "What's the nameplate charge, and how long is the actual line-set run versus the base length it assumes?"

**Data to pull:** condenser data plate charge, measured line-set length, manufacturer's per-foot adjustment factor, any prior startup or commissioning report.

## 9. Refrigerant recovery or recharge with no cylinder weight logged

**Usually means:** a recordkeeping gap that's also a Section 608 compliance gap — without before/after weights, neither the amount added nor the leak-rate math for next time can be reconstructed.

**First question:** "What did the recovery cylinder weigh before and after, and what's logged against the work order?"

**Data to pull:** cylinder weight log, technician certification number on file, equipment full-charge nameplate value to determine whether the 50 lb regulatory threshold applies.

## 10. High head pressure with low compressor amp draw

**Usually means:** non-condensables or overcharge on the high side while the compressor itself isn't working harder to produce it — points away from a compressor mechanical problem and toward charge or a condenser airflow/fouling issue.

**First question:** "Is the condenser coil clean and getting full airflow, and what's subcooling reading?"

**Data to pull:** condenser coil condition, condenser fan CFM/RPM, subcooling, ambient temperature at time of reading, amp draw against nameplate RLA.
