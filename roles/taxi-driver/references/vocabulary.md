# Vocabulary

Terms generalists conflate that a taxi driver or dispatcher keeps sharply separate. Each: definition, a sentence a practitioner would actually say, and the common misuse.

### Deadheading

Driving with no revenue passenger aboard — cruising between drop-off and next pickup, or repositioning toward a feeder point — which burns fuel and shift-lease time without producing fare revenue.

**In use:** "We spent the last hour deadheading around downtown instead of taking app pings — that's the whole reason utilization tanked."

**Common misuse:** treating deadheading as unavoidable overhead rather than a number to actively manage and reduce through positioning decisions.

### Utilization (revenue-mile ratio)

The fraction of total miles driven in a shift that had a paying passenger aboard, computed as revenue miles ÷ total miles. The actual profitability metric, as opposed to counting fares taken.

**In use:** "Sixty-seven revenue miles out of a hundred ninety-five total — that's 34%, well under where we want to be."

**Common misuse:** judging a shift's success by gross fares or fare count alone, without ever computing what fraction of the miles driven actually carried a passenger.

### Flat rate vs. metered fare

**Flat rate** is a fixed, pre-filed price for a specific origin-destination pair in a specific direction (most commonly airport-to-core-city trips), set by the licensing authority rather than computed by the meter. **Metered fare** is the default, computed live from distance and time by the certified meter, used whenever no filed flat rate matches the trip.

**In use:** "This is a filed flat route — charge the seventy, don't run the meter just because traffic would make it higher."

**Common misuse:** assuming a flat rate applies in the reverse direction of the filed trip, or running the meter on a trip that actually matches the filed flat itinerary.

### Street hail vs. e-hail vs. dispatch

**Street hail** is a passenger flagging the cab directly with no prior matching or identity record. **E-hail** is an app-based request (Curb, Arro, FREENOW) that matches a licensed taxi to a passenger with an account and logged trip, distinct from a rideshare-network trip. **Dispatch** is a phone/radio-routed pickup through a base or fleet dispatcher.

**In use:** "Utilization's been weak the last hour — switch to e-hail pings instead of cruising for a street hail."

**Common misuse:** treating all three as equally "just getting a fare," when the risk profile and utilization economics differ meaningfully between an unmatched street hail and a logged e-hail or dispatch pickup.

### Medallion

The transferable license (in medallion-system cities) that authorizes a specific vehicle to operate as a licensed taxi and pick up street hails; distinct from the driver's personal license/permit.

**In use:** "The medallion's leased to three different shift drivers — check whose gate this is before assuming the lease cost."

**Common misuse:** conflating the medallion (attached to the vehicle/operating right) with the driver's own license, which is a separate credential with its own renewal cycle.

### Gate / lease (shift lease)

The flat fee a driver pays to a medallion or fleet owner for the right to operate the vehicle for a shift or period, regardless of how much the driver earns in fares that shift. Distinct from a commission model, where the owner takes a percentage of fares instead of a flat fee.

**In use:** "Gate's $130 for the ten-hour shift — that's owed whether tonight's a $290 night or a $150 night."

**Common misuse:** treating the lease/gate fee as a variable cost that scales with how good the shift was, when it's actually a fixed cost the driver owes regardless of fares earned.

### Holding lot / trip pass

An airport-specific dispatch queue where taxis wait in a designated lot and are released to the terminal curb in arrival order; a **trip pass** is the credential or scan that enforces that order and can flag drivers who skip the queue for short in-airport trips.

**In use:** "Don't cut back into the holding lot line after a short terminal-to-terminal run — that's a trip-pass violation."

**Common misuse:** treating the holding lot as a first-come informal line rather than an enforced queue with its own rule set and penalties for gaming it.

### Cruising

Actively driving through streets looking for a street hail, as opposed to staging (waiting at a known feeder point) or app-matching. One of three distinct positioning strategies, not a synonym for "working."

**In use:** "Cruising residential blocks after 1 AM isn't paying off — stage at the hotel line instead."

**Common misuse:** assuming cruising is always the default positioning strategy, when staging or app-matching often has a better return depending on zone and hour.

### Staging (feeder point)

Parking and waiting at a location with reliably recurring demand — a transit hub, hotel taxi line, or airport holding lot — rather than actively driving in search of a hail.

**In use:** "Utilization's been weak all shift — stage at the train station for the evening rush instead of cruising."

**Common misuse:** staging at a feeder point without accounting for the deadhead cost of getting there, or without checking whether that point's actual wait time still beats cruising at that hour.

### Hail-acceptance screen

The quick, per-hail risk assessment (hour, location, passenger presentation, whether a destination is stated before doors lock) a driver runs before accepting a street hail, as distinct from a one-time blanket policy about which hails to take.

**In use:** "Two factors stacked on that one — late hour and no destination stated — that's a verify-first, not an automatic accept."

**Common misuse:** either skipping the screen because recent hails have been uneventful, or overcorrecting into refusing most late-night hails outright instead of running the screen each time.

### De-escalation ladder

The ordered response to an escalating fare dispute or aggressive passenger: verbal redirect, cite the printed meter/flat-rate record, move to a safe public stop, then involve dispatch or authorities — run in sequence, not skipped.

**In use:** "Don't argue the amount parked on that block — cite the receipt and get to the gas station on the corner before this goes further."

**Common misuse:** jumping straight to confrontation or negotiating the fare amount on the spot in an isolated location, instead of working the ladder in order.

### TPEP-style equipment (GPS / camera / payment system)

The mandated in-vehicle hardware bundle — GPS trip logging, camera, and electronic payment terminal — named after New York's Taxicab Passenger Enhancement Program but standard in similar form across many licensing regimes; the evidentiary and safety record for a trip.

**In use:** "Pull the GPS log and camera footage for that trip before taking either side's account of what happened."

**Common misuse:** treating this equipment as optional or as something to disable for driver convenience, rather than as the record that protects the driver in a dispute or incident.
