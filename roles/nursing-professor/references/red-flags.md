# Red Flags

### NCLEX-RN first-time pass rate below 80% (or the state's minimum) cumulative, or down more than ~10 points year over year
- **Usually means:** a curriculum, staffing, or clinical-placement gap concentrated in one cohort, campus, or content area — rarely a program-wide decline in student quality
- **First question:** is the drop uniform across every campus/section, or concentrated in one cut?
- **Data to pull:** disaggregated pass-rate table by campus/cohort/section, plus the vendor's item-analysis report broken out by NCJMM cognitive-skill layer

### Clinical group instructor-to-student ratio exceeds the state's approved cap (commonly 1:8–1:10)
- **Usually means:** a scheduling shortcut taken to cover an unreplaced faculty vacancy, not a considered pedagogical decision
- **First question:** is this a one-time substitute-coverage gap, or has it been the standing schedule for more than one rotation?
- **Data to pull:** the clinical schedule roster for the term versus the state Board's approved staffing ratio on file for that program

### Standardized exit-exam (HESI E2 / ATI Comprehensive Predictor) predicted-pass rate trending down two consecutive cohorts
- **Usually means:** a curriculum-sequencing or content gap that will surface in the NCLEX pass rate a semester later
- **First question:** which specific content category or NCJMM layer is driving the predictor decline?
- **Data to pull:** item-level predictive-exam breakdown by content category, cross-referenced against the corresponding course's syllabus and exam blueprint

### Student dismissed from the program after a single high-stakes exam score, with no documented remediation attempt on file
- **Usually means:** a due-process exposure that will not survive an appeal or state administrative review, regardless of whether the academic judgment was correct
- **First question:** does the signed handbook policy in effect at the student's matriculation date specify single-attempt dismissal, or does it require a remediation cycle first?
- **Data to pull:** the student's signed policy acknowledgment from matriculation and their complete remediation/advising file

### Clinical evaluation comments use subjective language ("unsafe," "poor attitude," "unprofessional") without a named rubric criterion attached
- **Usually means:** the documentation will not survive a grade appeal or a licensure-board referral, independent of whether the underlying concern was real
- **First question:** which specific rubric dimension (e.g., a Lasater Clinical Judgment Rubric item) does this behavior map to?
- **Data to pull:** the clinical evaluation form and rubric in use for that course, and whether the comment was entered the same clinical day

### A clinical partner site reduces available slots, beds, or preceptor hours mid-cycle
- **Usually means:** the program needs to expand simulation substitution (within the state's cap) or pursue a dedicated education unit — not quietly absorb the loss by increasing group sizes
- **First question:** what is the program's current simulation-substitution percentage relative to the state's cap, and is there room to expand it?
- **Data to pull:** current clinical-hour allocation by site, simulation-hour log, and the state's substitution-cap rule

### Same accreditation standard (CCNE or ACEN) flagged non-compliant across two consecutive review cycles
- **Usually means:** the prior cycle's corrective action was written but not actually executed or verified
- **First question:** does the corrective-action entry for that standard have both an implementation date and a named verifier, or just a plan?
- **Data to pull:** the SPEP corrective-action table for that standard across both cycles

### A new campus, site, or delivery mode was added without a substantive-change filing to the accreditor before students enrolled
- **Usually means:** an unreported change that is independently grounds for adverse accreditation action, regardless of program quality at the new site
- **First question:** has the accreditor been notified yet, and if not, how long has the site been operating?
- **Data to pull:** the accreditor's substantive-change policy and the program's internal record of when the site began enrolling students

### Faculty vacancy rate high, with adjuncts covering the majority of clinical sections for more than one consecutive term
- **Usually means:** a leading indicator of ratio violations and pass-rate volatility roughly a year out, consistent with the national faculty-shortage pattern AACN tracks
- **First question:** how many clinical sections this term are adjunct-covered versus full-time-faculty-covered, and for how many consecutive terms?
- **Data to pull:** faculty roster with FTE/adjunct status by section, compared against the prior two terms

### Simulation hours counted toward the clinical-hour requirement exceed the state's substitution cap (commonly 50%, sometimes lower)
- **Usually means:** the program's recorded clinical hours will not hold up under a state Board audit, invalidating them for licensure purposes
- **First question:** what is this specific state's substitution cap, and has it been confirmed against the current rule rather than assumed to be the NCSBN study's 50%?
- **Data to pull:** the clinical-hour log broken into supervised-clinical versus simulation hours per student, and the state Board's current substitution rule
