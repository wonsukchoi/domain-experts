# Vocabulary

### NCJMM (NCSBN Clinical Judgment Measurement Model)
The framework the Next Generation NCLEX uses to score six cognitive-skill layers — recognize cues, analyze cues, prioritize hypotheses, generate solutions, take action, evaluate outcomes.
**In use:** "This item only tests recognize-cues; we need more analyze- and generate-solutions items to match the actual test-plan weighting."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a synonym for "critical thinking" in general, rather than a specific six-layer scoring structure that dictates how items should be written and clinical performance evaluated.

### Next Generation NCLEX (NGN)
The exam version implemented April 2023 that scores clinical judgment via NCJMM-aligned item types (extended multiple response, matrix/grid, bowtie, trend items), some of which carry partial or unscored-experimental credit.
**In use:** "Our exit-exam predictor still uses legacy item formats; it's not benchmarked against NGN item types."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be simply a harder version of the prior exam rather than a structurally different scoring model requiring different item-writing and teaching approaches.

### LCJR (Lasater Clinical Judgment Rubric)
An 11-dimension, 4-domain (noticing, interpreting, responding, reflecting) rubric for scoring clinical judgment demonstrated in simulation or live clinical practice, each dimension rated on a 4-point developmental scale.
**In use:** "The student scored 'beginning' on the interpreting domain — prioritized the wrong finding — so the remediation plan targets that specifically."
**Common misuse:** Used as a generic clinical grade sheet by faculty who haven't been trained/calibrated on it, producing inter-rater scores that don't agree closely enough to survive a grade appeal.

### Dedicated Education Unit (DEU)
A clinical partnership model where a specific unit's staff nurses, trained as adjunct clinical instructors, supervise students in a 1:1 or near-1:1 preceptor structure, distinct from a standard group clinical rotation.
**In use:** "We're converting the med-surg rotation to a DEU model to absorb the lost clinical slots without exceeding the ratio cap."
**Common misuse:** Any clinical site partnership is loosely called a "DEU" even when it's actually a standard group-supervised rotation with no dedicated, trained unit staff.

### SPEP (Systematic Program Evaluation Plan)
The accreditor-required (CCNE/ACEN) continuous data-collection and corrective-action process tracking program outcomes — pass rates, completion rates, employment rates — against benchmarks, standard by standard, across the full review cycle.
**In use:** "Pull the SPEP data across both review cycles for Standard IV before we draft the self-study narrative."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a document assembled once before a site visit rather than a continuous, dated data-collection process the accreditor expects to see updated every review period.

### High-stakes testing policy
A program policy that ties academic progression (course passage, graduation eligibility) to a single standardized exam score above a set cut point.
**In use:** "The high-stakes policy requires an 850 on the HESI E2 to sit for the final clinical; anything below triggers the remediation track before a retake."
**Common misuse:** Applied without a published rubric, remediation pathway, or appeal process the student agreed to at matriculation — the single most common source of successful legal challenges to nursing-program dismissals.

### Clinical remediation
A documented, structured plan addressing a specific deficiency identified against a named rubric criterion, with defined timelines and reassessment.
**In use:** "The remediation plan requires two supervised medication-pass sessions focused on the MAR-verification step before the student returns to the unit."
**Common misuse:** An informal verbal warning or a general "do better" conversation, undocumented, counted as remediation when a dismissal is later challenged.

### Preceptor vs. clinical instructor
A preceptor is a practicing staff nurse supervising a student 1:1 in their own patient assignment under a collaborative agreement; a clinical instructor is faculty (or faculty-credentialed adjunct) directly supervising a group under the program's ratio and liability structure.
**In use:** "That capstone rotation runs on preceptor hours, not clinical-instructor ratio, so it's governed by a different section of the affiliation agreement."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably, which obscures that the two models carry different supervision ratios, liability allocation, and hour-counting rules under most state Board regulations.

### Concept-based curriculum
A curriculum organized around a limited set of recurring concepts (e.g., perfusion, oxygenation, clinical judgment) applied across body systems and settings, rather than organized body-system-by-body-system or disease-by-disease.
**In use:** "We're mapping the med-surg course to the perfusion and oxygenation concepts so students transfer the reasoning across cardiac and respiratory content instead of re-learning it each unit."
**Common misuse:** Treated as simply relabeling the same body-system chapters under concept headings, without actually restructuring how the reasoning transfers across contexts.

### Formative vs. summative simulation
Formative simulation is practice-and-debrief with no grade consequence, intended to build clinical judgment through reflection; summative simulation is graded and counts toward course or progression outcomes.
**In use:** "That was a formative sim — the debrief is for learning, not for the gradebook — so don't penalize the hesitation during the code."
**Common misuse:** Grading a formative debrief punitively, which suppresses the psychological safety the debrief depends on and undermines the entire simulation's learning value.

### Item analysis (p-value and point-biserial)
Post-exam statistical review of each test item: the p-value (proportion who answered correctly) and point-biserial coefficient (how well the item discriminates between high- and low-scoring students overall).
**In use:** "Item 41 has a negative point-biserial — pull it from scoring before we finalize grades, it's probably miskeyed."
**Common misuse:** Skipped entirely, with exam scores finalized and grade disputes handled item-by-item after the fact instead of catching flawed items before scores post.

### Substantive change
An accreditor-defined change (new campus, new delivery mode, ownership change, significant curriculum change) that must be reported to and often approved by the accreditor before implementation.
**In use:** "Opening the satellite campus is a substantive change — file it with CCNE before we enroll the first cohort there, not after."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to only cover major structural events like a merger, when adding a new instructional site or delivery format also qualifies and is frequently reported late or not at all.

### Clinical hour ratio
The state Board of Nursing's regulatory limit on instructor-to-student supervision ratio in direct clinical practice, distinct from a classroom or lab ratio.
**In use:** "We're at 1:10 in the peds rotation — at the cap, with no buffer if an instructor calls out."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a program-level pedagogical guideline the faculty can flex for scheduling convenience, when it is a state-regulated compliance requirement that can invalidate clinical hours if exceeded.
