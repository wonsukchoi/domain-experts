# Occupational Therapy Assistant — Red Flags

### Standardized measure regresses >10% from the prior session with no clear cause
- **Usually means:** an unreported incident (fall, overexertion at home), an intercurrent illness, or early signs of a complication (e.g., adhesion, infection, reinjury) rather than measurement noise.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed since your last visit — an injury, illness, missed home exercises, or anything different about how you've been using it?"
- **Data to pull:** the last 3 sessions' measures, home-program compliance log, any interim provider visits noted in the chart.

### Patient reports pain during an activity that was previously pain-free
- **Usually means:** the activity has been progressed too quickly, a new issue has developed, or the patient is compensating differently than before, changing the load on the area.
- **First question:** "Where exactly, and does it feel like the same kind of discomfort as before or something different?"
- **Data to pull:** the plan's stated precautions and pain-response protocol, prior sessions' pain reports if any.

### Two consecutive sessions with no meaningful change on the plan's standardized measure
- **Usually means:** the activity level is no longer providing enough stimulus (needs grading up), the patient has reached a natural plateau requiring a plan change, or an unaddressed barrier (pain, fear, home-environment issue) is limiting progress.
- **First question:** "What's changed here — is the same drill not working anymore, or is something making this harder than the numbers show?"
- **Data to pull:** the full progress-tracking table, home-program compliance, any missed sessions in the window.

### Patient or caregiver asks to add, change, or skip part of the plan
- **Usually means:** the plan isn't matching the patient's real priorities or home context, but it's also a common point where a well-meaning COTA improvises outside authorized scope.
- **First question:** "Let me note that request for the OT — can you tell me more about why, so I can pass along the full picture?"
- **Data to pull:** the current plan of care's stated goals and activities, so the request can be compared against what's actually authorized.

### Patient reaches the plan's stated goal earlier than the expected timeline
- **Usually means:** genuinely faster recovery, or the goal was set conservatively — either way it's a reassessment trigger, not a reason to keep running the same sessions unchanged.
- **First question:** "Has the OT's plan said what happens once this goal is hit, or does this need a check-in first?"
- **Data to pull:** the plan's stated goal and re-evaluation criteria, the date of the last OT evaluation.

### A precaution in the plan would be violated by what the patient wants to do that day
- **Usually means:** the patient feels better than the precaution accounts for, which is exactly the situation precautions exist to guard against — subjective improvement doesn't override a stated clinical precaution.
- **First question:** "I know it feels ready, but the plan has us holding off on [X] until [condition] — let's stick with that and I'll flag how you're doing to the OT."
- **Data to pull:** the specific precaution language and its stated duration/condition for lifting.

### Patient shows a cognitive or behavioral presentation different from their documented baseline
- **Usually means:** could be as benign as poor sleep or as significant as a new medical issue (medication interaction, infection, neurologic event) — not something to guess at.
- **First question:** "Is this how you've been feeling the last few days, or is today different?"
- **Data to pull:** baseline cognitive/behavioral notes from the OT's evaluation, any recent medication changes noted in the chart.

### Home-program compliance has clearly dropped (patient can't demonstrate exercises, reports not doing them)
- **Usually means:** a barrier the plan didn't account for — pain, confusion about instructions, physical inability to do them alone, or a motivation issue — not laziness by default.
- **First question:** "What's making it hard to get to these at home — is it the instructions, the time, or something about how it feels?"
- **Data to pull:** the home-program handout given to the patient, prior compliance notes.
