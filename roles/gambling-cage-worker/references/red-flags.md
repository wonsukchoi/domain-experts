# Gambling Cage Worker — Red Flags

### Patron's cumulative cash-out for the gaming day crosses $10,000 across multiple transactions
- **Usually means:** legitimate large win/redemption pattern; occasionally deliberate structuring to evade a single-transaction threshold.
- **First question:** what's the patron's cumulative cash-IN and cash-OUT total for today, checked against player-card history, not just this transaction?
- **Data to pull:** patron's gaming-day transaction log by tracking ID.

### Patron asks how much they can cash out "without paperwork" or "without it being reported"
- **Usually means:** attempted structuring — a federal offense regardless of whether the underlying funds are legitimate.
- **First question:** has this patron shown this pattern on prior visits?
- **Data to pull:** patron's transaction history across recent gaming days, not just today.

### Marker request would push outstanding balance above the patron's approved credit line
- **Usually means:** patron is either unaware of their remaining headroom or testing whether the limit is enforced.
- **First question:** what's the current outstanding balance, and does this request exceed the line even by a small margin?
- **Data to pull:** credit-limit approval on file and current outstanding-marker ledger.

### Fill or credit slip presented without all three required signatures
- **Usually means:** rushed floor process under time pressure; rarely malicious but still a control failure if accepted.
- **First question:** which signature is missing, and can it be obtained now before chips/cash move?
- **Data to pull:** the slip itself and the pit supervisor on duty.

### Cage bank reconciliation shows a variance at shift change
- **Usually means:** a specific miscounted or mis-keyed transaction; less commonly a physical count error; rarely theft.
- **First question:** does retracing the shift log by timestamp isolate the variance to one transaction window?
- **Data to pull:** shift transaction log with timestamps, denomination breakdown of the physical count.

### Same patron requests multiple markers or redemptions each just under a reporting threshold
- **Usually means:** possible structuring pattern, especially if the sub-threshold amounts recur across visits.
- **First question:** does the pattern repeat across multiple gaming days, not just today?
- **Data to pull:** patron transaction history across the property's tracking system, cross-referenced by date.

### A check presented for cashing has a hold flag or exceeds the property's standard cashing limit
- **Usually means:** either a legitimate large-value check needing manager approval, or a check-kiting/counterfeit risk.
- **First question:** does the check's issuing bank and account have any prior returned-check history with this property?
- **Data to pull:** check-verification system record and property's check-cashing policy limit.

### Chip fill requested for an unusually large amount relative to the table's normal volume
- **Usually means:** legitimate high-volume play; occasionally an attempt to move chips off a table without a corresponding buy-in (chip-passing/collusion risk).
- **First question:** does the fill amount correlate with observed table activity, or does it look disconnected from buy-ins?
- **Data to pull:** table's buy-in log for the shift, cross-checked against the fill request.
