# Red flags — program and resourcing signals a compliance manager notices instantly

Signals about the health of the compliance function itself (staffing, budget, coverage), not about a single case. Any one alone can be explainable; two or more together across a review cycle is a resourcing gap worth a budget action.

### Alert or case backlog aging past its SLA, growing two consecutive quarters
- **Usually means:** headcount hasn't scaled with alert/case volume, or a recently purchased monitoring tool increased alert volume without a paired headcount funding line.
- **First question:** "Is the SLA breach concentrated in one risk tier or spread evenly — and did volume or headcount change first?"
- **Data to pull:** alert-age distribution by month, headcount timeline, alert-volume timeline, any tooling go-live dates in the window.

### Compliance headcount flat while regulated business volume or obligation count grew materially
- **Usually means:** the coverage-ratio math has drifted below target without anyone recomputing it — headcount requests get deferred year over year until the ratio silently breaks.
- **First question:** "What's the current accounts-per-analyst ratio in the top-risk-tier unit, and when was it last recalculated?"
- **Data to pull:** headcount history by year, business volume/obligation-count history, current risk-tiered coverage worksheet (see `references/playbook.md` §1).

### Training completion reported only as a single org-wide percentage
- **Usually means:** a low-completion business unit is being averaged out by high-completion units, hiding exactly the pocket that needs attention.
- **First question:** "Break this out by business unit — which one is actually below 90%, and is it the same unit every quarter?"
- **Data to pull:** completion rate by business unit and by quarter, correlation with that unit's exam/audit findings.

### Compliance budget cut in the cycle immediately following a zero-finding audit
- **Usually means:** the program's "success" was read as "risk is low" rather than "we tested and found nothing this cycle," with no coverage-ratio evidence to contest the framing.
- **First question:** "What was the testing coverage percentage behind that zero-finding result, and was it presented to the board alongside the finding count?"
- **Data to pull:** coverage percentage for the audited period, board-deck language from that cycle, budget-request history for the following year.

### Monitoring or case-management platform licensed with no corresponding headcount line in the same budget cycle
- **Usually means:** the purchase was framed and approved as a risk-reduction spend without anyone costing the analyst hours needed to work its output — the alert-aging metric will surface the gap within one to two quarters.
- **First question:** "Who is funded to review what this tool generates, and at what ratio?"
- **Data to pull:** vendor contract and go-live date, current headcount assigned to review its output, alert-age trend since go-live.

### Embedded compliance analyst's compensation or performance review controlled by the business unit they monitor
- **Usually means:** an incentive-capture risk — findings against that unit get softened or delayed because the reviewer's raise depends on the reviewed unit's leadership.
- **First question:** "Who signs this analyst's performance review — the compliance manager, or the business-unit head?"
- **Data to pull:** current org chart and reporting lines, review-and-compensation authority documentation, finding-severity trend for this analyst's coverage area versus peers.

### Regretted attrition among case-handling analysts exceeding roughly 20% in a single year
- **Usually means:** either caseload per analyst is past the workable span-of-control ratio, or compensation has fallen behind market for a role with rising licensing/certification demands.
- **First question:** "What was each departing analyst's caseload relative to the target ratio in their exit quarter?"
- **Data to pull:** caseload-per-analyst at time of departure, exit-interview themes, compensation benchmarking data for the role.

### A new regulatory obligation rolled out without a defined coverage metric baseline
- **Usually means:** six months from now there will be no way to demonstrate the rollout worked, which weakens the next budget ask for the same program area.
- **First question:** "What metric will show this obligation is being met, and when was it first measured?"
- **Data to pull:** rollout project plan, any metric defined at launch (or its absence), first measurement date if one exists.

### Board deck reports coverage as a raw count with no stated denominator
- **Usually means:** "142 reviews completed" sounds like progress but is meaningless without the population size it was drawn from — a manager under budget pressure has an incentive to report the number that looks best.
- **First question:** "142 out of how many in scope, and what tier were they drawn from?"
- **Data to pull:** total in-scope population for the period, tier breakdown of what was actually reviewed, prior-period coverage percentage for trend comparison.

### Share of interim/consultant compliance headcount rising as a percentage of total compliance FTE
- **Usually means:** permanent headcount requests have been deferred and the gap is being papered over with contractors, which costs more per hour and doesn't build institutional case-history knowledge.
- **First question:** "What's the fully-loaded cost of the current contractor mix versus the permanent-hire request that's been deferred?"
- **Data to pull:** contractor spend trend by quarter, headcount-request history and approval/denial dates, cost-per-FTE comparison (contractor vs. permanent, loaded).
