# Kindergarten Teacher — Playbook

## Daily schedule block (full-day program, 8:00-2:45, 20 children, 1 teacher + 1 aide)

| Time | Block | Length | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8:00-8:15 | Arrival / unpacking / soft start (choice table) | 15 min | Not instructional — regulation time before the day's first demand |
| 8:15-8:30 | Morning meeting (greeting, calendar, message) | 15 min | Responsive Classroom structure — same sequence daily, predictability is the point |
| 8:30-8:45 | Whole-group phonemic awareness routine | 15 min | Oral, no print — segmenting/blending practice, e.g., Heggerty-style daily sequence |
| 8:45-9:00 | Movement transition | 15 min | Not filler — resets attention before the next direct-instruction block |
| 9:00-9:15 | Whole-group letter-sound / phonics mini-lesson | 15 min | Direct instruction capped at 15 min; longer produces "talked at," not taught |
| 9:15-9:55 | Literacy centers (rotating groups of 4-5) | 40 min | Teacher pulls a small group for targeted work each rotation; aide floats/supervises |
| 9:55-10:10 | Snack | 15 min | Full group seated — never split for this age band |
| 10:10-10:40 | Outdoor / gross motor | 30 min | |
| 10:40-10:55 | Whole-group number-sense mini-lesson | 15 min | Same 15-min cap as literacy mini-lesson |
| 10:55-11:35 | Math centers (rotating small groups) | 40 min | Mirrors literacy-center structure |
| 11:35-12:15 | Lunch | 40 min | |
| 12:15-12:30 | Rest / quiet time | 15 min | Not nap-mandatory at this age, but a regulation reset after lunch |

| 1:15-1:45 | Choice / dramatic play / blocks | 30 min | Deliberately unscheduled-feeling; still carries embedded academic targets in materials |

| 2:30-2:45 | Pack-up / dismissal | 15 min | Sign-out checked against authorized-pickup list, no exceptions |

## Kindergarten entry screening cycle (first 3 weeks of year)

1. **Administer whole-child entry assessment** across literacy, number sense, self-regulation, fine motor, and social-skill domains — never literacy alone.
2. **Score each domain independently.** Literacy splits further into letter-knowledge and phonemic-awareness sub-scores, scored separately (they measure different things).
3. **Flag any domain below the entry-level benchmark band**, and note whether it's isolated (one domain) or broad (multiple domains, which points toward a developmental-pace conversation rather than a single-skill intervention).
4. **For an isolated flag, confirm with a second short task tapping the same construct** within the first month before starting a Tier 2 plan.
5. **For a broad flag across most domains**, route to a developmental conversation with the family and a lower-stakes observation period (4-6 weeks) before any formal intervention or referral discussion — a broad flag this early is often a maturity-and-exposure story, not yet a diagnostic one.
6. **Log baseline scores per domain** — this is the comparison point for every progress-monitoring check the rest of the year.

## Tier 2 intervention decision (mid-year screening)

1. **Split the literacy screen into its component constructs**: letter-sound fluency and phoneme segmentation/blending, scored and read separately.
2. **Below-benchmark on segmentation, at-or-above on letter-sound** → oral phonemic-awareness intervention (segmenting/blending, no print), 10-15 min/day, 4-5 days/week, sourced from choice time.
3. **Below-benchmark on letter-sound, at-or-above on segmentation** → direct letter-sound instruction with multisensory practice (tracing, sound-sorting), same time allocation.
4. **Below-benchmark on both** → sequence phonemic awareness first for 3-4 weeks, then layer in letter-sound instruction — teaching both simultaneously to a child missing the prerequisite tends to produce memorization over decoding.
5. **Recheck with a short, targeted probe on the same construct in 4-6 weeks**, not a full re-administration of the entry/mid-year screen.
6. **No movement after one full cycle** → adjust the intervention (frequency, group size, specific activity) before concluding it isn't working; **no movement after two full cycles with fidelity confirmed** → raise a Child Find/formal-evaluation conversation with the family and the RTI/MTSS team.

## Retention/redshirting conversation structure

1. Open with the specific data: which construct(s) are below expectation, with the actual numbers, not a general impression.
2. Separate the academic-construct gap from the developmental/maturity read explicitly — name which one is driving the conversation.
3. State the research on retention outcomes plainly, before the family commits emotionally to "another year would help."
4. Propose the specific, targeted alternative (which intervention, what minutes/week, what recheck date) as the concrete counter-offer to "wait and see."
5. If the family still wants to pursue retention or redshirting after hearing the research, document the conversation and loop in the principal/counselor — this becomes a family-and-school joint decision, not a unilateral teacher call in either direction.
