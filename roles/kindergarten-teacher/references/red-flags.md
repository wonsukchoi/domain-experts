# Kindergarten Teacher — Red Flags

### Letter-sound fluency at or above benchmark, phoneme segmentation clearly below it
- **Usually means:** A genuine phonemic-awareness gap masked by strong print knowledge — the child recognizes letters and sounds but can't yet manipulate spoken sounds, which is the stronger predictor of first-grade reading difficulty.
- **First question:** "Can he segment a spoken word into its sounds with no letters in front of him at all?"
- **Data to pull:** A short oral segmentation/blending probe with no print present, plus the original screener's sub-scores broken out separately.

### A single below-benchmark screening score, no second data point yet
- **Usually means:** Overcorrection risk — one low score can reflect an off day, a rushed administration, or ordinary variability at this age, not a confirmed gap.
- **First question:** "Has this shown up on a second task tapping the same skill, or is this the only data point?"
- **Data to pull:** A short re-screen or a different task on the same construct within 4-6 weeks before starting an intervention plan.

### Behavior escalation specifically at transitions (line-up, center-to-carpet, dismissal prep)
- **Usually means:** A predictability/regulation gap, not defiance — the child doesn't yet have enough warning or a practiced routine for the change.
- **First question:** "Does this happen at every transition or specific ones, and was a warning given beforehand?"
- **Data to pull:** A one-week log of transition times, whether a visual/verbal warning preceded each, and the outcome.

### Same behavior showing up unpredictably, including low-demand free-choice time
- **Usually means:** A developmental, sensory, or medical driver rather than a classroom-management issue — behavioral scaffolds alone are unlikely to resolve it.
- **First question:** "Does this happen even during choice time or read-aloud, with no clear trigger?"
- **Data to pull:** An antecedent-behavior-consequence log across at least 5 instances spanning different times of day.

### Parent or grandparent raises retention or redshirting before any screening data has been discussed
- **Usually means:** The family is reasoning from a general impression (often comparing to an older sibling or a same-age peer) rather than construct-specific data — the conversation needs the numbers before it needs a decision.
- **First question:** "What specifically are you seeing that makes you think a year would help?"
- **Data to pull:** The child's current domain-by-domain screening data, to ground the conversation before either side commits to a position.

### Letter or number reversals (b/d, was/saw, 6/9) persisting past mid-first-grade
- **Usually means:** Within kindergarten itself, reversals are typical and not concerning on their own; persistence well past the typical window, or co-occurrence with a broader decoding difficulty, shifts it toward a pattern worth tracking.
- **First question:** "Is this happening in isolation, or alongside other decoding difficulty?"
- **Data to pull:** A dated log of reversal instances across several weeks, cross-referenced against the child's overall letter-sound and segmentation scores.

### A child avoids a specific fine-motor task repeatedly (pencil grip, scissors, name-writing)
- **Usually means:** The task is outside the child's current zone of proximal development presented without scaffolding, not a motivation problem.
- **First question:** "Does the child refuse outright, or attempt it and disengage after a specific point?"
- **Data to pull:** Two observations noting grip, duration of engagement, and the specific point of disengagement.

### Whole-group instruction blocks consistently running past 15-20 minutes
- **Usually means:** A pacing mismatch with the class's attention span, more often than a single distractible child — check the schedule design before flagging individual behavior.
- **First question:** "Is this one child losing focus, or does engagement drop for most of the group at the same point in the block?"
- **Data to pull:** A quick engagement scan (proportion of the class still tracking) at the 10-, 15-, and 20-minute marks of the block.

### A below-benchmark screening score tied to a child with an active accommodation or in-progress evaluation
- **Usually means:** The score needs to be checked against whether any existing support was actually in place during the screening, before it's folded into a new intervention or referral decision.
- **First question:** "Was any existing accommodation or support actually delivered during this specific assessment?"
- **Data to pull:** The accommodation-delivery log for that testing session, cross-referenced against the score.

### A Tier 2 intervention shows no movement after one full cycle
- **Usually means:** The intervention itself needs adjusting (frequency, group size, specific activity) before concluding the child needs a formal evaluation — one unsuccessful cycle is evidence about the intervention, not yet about the child.
- **First question:** "Was the intervention delivered with fidelity — right frequency, right group size, right activity — for the full cycle?"
- **Data to pull:** The intervention log (dates, minutes delivered, activity used) alongside the recheck probe score.

### Every below-benchmark screening score in a class gets referred to Child Find in the same month
- **Usually means:** Overcorrection — treating a screening threshold as an automatic referral trigger rather than running the Tier 2 process first, which will resolve most of the flagged scores without a formal evaluation.
- **First question:** "Has each of these gone through a full intervention cycle with a documented recheck, or are these first-flag referrals?"
- **Data to pull:** The intervention log and recheck data for each flagged child before any referral goes forward.
