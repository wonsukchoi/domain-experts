# Playbook

Filled templates and step sequences, not descriptions of them. Defaults, not laws — deviate consciously and note why in the record you keep.

## 1. Tenure/reappointment dossier — evidence by subfield currency

Confirm the department's actual written tenure document before building the file — the split below is a common stated pattern, not a universal rule.

| Subfield cluster | Primary currency | Typical article count at tenure | Evidence to include |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ethnography, historical-comparative, cultural sociology | Single-authored monograph, disciplinary-series press | 1–2 articles alongside the book | Signed press contract with series name and confirmed pub date; 2–3 named external comparators from the same subfield; pre-publication reader reports if available |
| Demography, social psychology, formal/computational sociology | Peer-reviewed articles in top generalist or specialty journals | 4–6 articles, first- or sole-authored on most | Publication log with venue, submission/acceptance dates; citation count with a subfield comparator, not a raw number; grant activity |
| Mixed-methods subfields (medical sociology, education) | Either, department-specified — confirm which | Varies — pull comparators before assuming | Whichever evidence type the confirmed comparators used |

## 2. IRB tiering flow for fieldwork and interview research

1. Does the study involve interaction with, or identifiable data about, living individuals? No → not human-subjects research; document the determination in writing anyway.
2. Yes, and is it a one-time anonymous survey or interview with no sustained access to a community or institution, minimal-risk topic? → file for **exempt** determination, 45 CFR 46.104, category 2.
3. Yes, and does it involve sustained fieldwork, participant observation, or named-institution access — even with pseudonyms in the eventual write-up? → file for **expedited** review at minimum; state explicitly in the protocol what contextual detail (neighborhood, institution type, time period) could re-identify a subject despite a pseudonym.
4. Vulnerable population (minors, incarcerated individuals, undocumented status disclosed) or greater-than-minimal risk (legal exposure from disclosed behavior, as in *On the Run*'s subject matter) → **full board** review; budget 4–8 weeks minimum before recruitment.
5. Set the continuing-review reminder before first contact with a subject or site — data collected after a lapsed protocol is typically unusable without a reportable deviation.

## 3. Stratification-topic classification (before teaching any unit likely to draw a complaint)

- **Settled empirical claim** (e.g., a racial wage gap of a specific documented magnitude exists and its measured mechanisms) → teach as settled, cite the empirical literature, do not manufacture a "second side" of a measurement.
- **Open normative/policy question** (e.g., which specific remedy a given disparity calls for) → assign readings representing the strongest form of at least two defensible positions; require one assignment (memo, debate) constructing the strongest case for a position the student doesn't hold.
- **Mixed** (the common case: a documented disparity embedded in a live policy debate) → teach the documented component as settled, then mark the transition explicitly ("the gap is not in dispute; what causes it and what to do about it is").

Documentation to keep contemporaneously: the reading list with source balance noted for the open component, and the assignment prompt showing the strongest-opposing-case requirement.

## 4. Qualitative sample-size planning (Guest, Bunce & Johnson; Small)

| Population homogeneity | Planning default | Adjust when |
|---|---|---|
| Relatively homogeneous (single site, similar role/experience) | ~12 interviews as the point new codes typically stop emerging | Saturation checked at 8, 10, 12 — stop when 2 consecutive interviews add no new code |
| Heterogeneous across 2+ relevant dimensions (site, role, and demographic) | 12 per meaningful subgroup, not 12 total | Budget accordingly before fieldwork starts, not after running short |
| Case-study / small-N comparative design | Case count set by theoretical logic (most-similar or most-different systems), not a fixed number | Follow Small (2009): justify case count by what comparison the argument needs, state it in the methods section |

## 5. Journal and press submission triage

| Outcome | Typical timeline | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Desk reject, generalist journal (ASR/AJS), generic note | Days to 3 weeks | Base-rate event (~90% overall rejection rate) — retarget a specialty journal matched to subfield within 2–4 weeks |
| Desk reject, editor names a specific framing issue | Days to 3 weeks | Fix the named issue only, submit elsewhere — don't resubmit to the same editor without the change |
| Reject after review | 3–6 months | Read for a design flaw (identification strategy, missing triangulation in fieldwork) vs. a presentation flaw; a design flaw needs new work before resubmission anywhere |
| R&R | 3–6 months to receive | Treat as the real submission; point-by-point response memo mandatory for every comment, including ones contested |
| Book: reader reports request major revision | 2–4 months to receive | Address every reader point in the revised-manuscript cover letter to the editor; a press rarely rejects after commissioning reader reports unless the response ignores them |

## 6. Grade dispute — steps in order

1. Student submits the dispute in writing, citing the specific rubric criterion believed misapplied.
2. Re-grade against the rubric's descriptor language only, ideally blind to the original score.
3. If the re-grade matches, the grade stands — respond in writing quoting the descriptor language for each criterion.
4. If a criterion was misapplied, correct only that criterion's score; re-grade the same criterion class-wide only if the misapplication was systematic.
5. Escalation past the instructor is decided on the rubric plus the two written responses already on record — no new argument introduced later outweighs what's already there.
