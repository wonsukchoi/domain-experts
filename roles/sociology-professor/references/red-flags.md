# Red Flags

Smell tests across teaching, research, and service. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Personnel committee applies a flat 4–5 article benchmark to a monograph-track file

- **Usually means:** the committee is scoring an ethnography/historical-comparative/cultural-sociology file against a quantitative subfield's currency — a category error, not a rigor finding.
- **First question:** "What do 2–3 recent tenure cases in this exact subfield at peer institutions actually show for article count and book status?"
- **Data to pull:** named comparator cases (article count, book contract/publication status, press tier) matched on subfield and career stage.

### Fieldwork or interview project filed as IRB-exempt because "there's no survey instrument"

- **Usually means:** the protocol assumed anonymous-survey-level identifiability for a project with sustained access to a named community or institution — the actual risk profile of immersive fieldwork.
- **First question:** "Could this specific site or informant be re-identified from the contextual detail the finished work will need to be credible, even with a pseudonym?"
- **Data to pull:** the IRB protocol's stated risk category, the draft manuscript's level of place/institution detail, comparison against the *On the Run*/Lubet exchange as the standing cautionary case.

### 4 or more articles published from a single ethnographic or interview dataset without disclosure

- **Usually means:** "salami-slicing" a single fieldwork dataset to inflate apparent productivity — read skeptically in tenure letters and hiring committees in book-culture subfields.
- **First question:** "How many distinct data collections underlie these publications, and is that stated in the dossier?"
- **Data to pull:** each article's data section, dates and sites of data collection, overlap across papers.

### Course evaluation response rate under ~50% on a term with an outlier score

- **Usually means:** the respondents are a self-selected subgroup, not a sample of the class — a swing is often nonresponse bias, not a class-wide reaction. Mengel, Sauermann & Zölitz (2019) additionally find perceived-gender effects on teaching ratings independent of actual performance, which compounds low-n terms.
- **First question:** "What's the response rate, and does the drop concentrate in one item or spread across all of them?"
- **Data to pull:** response rate, item-level breakdown vs. department mean, free-text comments and what fraction cluster on the same low-scoring item.

### Syllabus unit on a stratification topic with zero source representing the open normative question's second position

- **Usually means:** the settled-finding/open-question split wasn't applied — the specific gap that turns a classroom complaint into a sustained grievance.
- **First question:** "For the normative half of this unit, what's the second defensible position, and where's its reading?"
- **Data to pull:** the reading list for that unit, sorted by which claim (empirical or normative) each source addresses.

### A single ASR/AJS desk rejection treated as a stop/restart decision on the project

- **Usually means:** the rejection is being read as a quality verdict rather than a base-rate event at journals rejecting roughly 90% of submissions.
- **First question:** "Was this a generic desk reject, or did the editor name a specific, fixable framing problem?"
- **Data to pull:** the rejection letter's language, the journal's published rejection rate, a list of 2–3 subfield-matched alternative venues.

### Pre-tenure dossier leads with op-eds or media commentary ahead of peer-reviewed output

- **Usually means:** public-sociology output (Burawoy's fourth currency) is being positioned as a substitute for the professional record rather than a supplement to it.
- **First question:** "How many peer-reviewed pieces are in the file, and is the public-facing work listed as primary or supporting evidence?"
- **Data to pull:** the dossier's evidence ordering, publication log separated from public-facing output log.

### IRB continuing-review date passed with fieldwork still active

- **Usually means:** the protocol lapsed; data collected after the lapse is typically unusable without a reportable deviation.
- **First question:** "What was the approved end date on the current protocol, and has any contact occurred with a subject or site after it?"
- **Data to pull:** IRB approval letter with dates, fieldwork log or recruitment log with dates.

### Qualitative interview study reports saturation after fewer than 6 interviews with no heterogeneity justification

- **Usually means:** saturation is being claimed prematurely, or the population is more homogeneous than the research question assumes — either way the sample-size logic needs to be stated, not asserted.
- **First question:** "What's the justification for stopping at this count — a stated saturation check, or just running out of time?"
- **Data to pull:** interview log with dates, a coding memo showing when new codes stopped emerging.

### Grad TA grading a large gateway section without a norming session

- **Usually means:** inter-rater inconsistency large enough to generate grade disputes that are really a grading-process problem, not a specific paper's problem — a particular risk in a 100+ seat Intro to Sociology section with multiple TAs.
- **First question:** "Did the TAs score the same sample papers together before grading independently, and how much did their scores vary?"
- **Data to pull:** norming-session sample-paper scores by TA, or absence of a norming session at all.
