# Red flags — what a CSM notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### Product usage (active users, key-feature engagement) down 25%+ over a rolling 4-8 week window
- **Usually means:** a leading indicator of renewal risk that's visible months before a churn conversation would otherwise surface it.
- **First question:** "Is this a champion/stakeholder change, a broken workflow, or a genuine fit issue — and which one dictates the response?"
- **Data to pull:** usage trend by user and by feature over the window, timing overlap with any known stakeholder or org change at the account.

### A known internal champion who hasn't logged in or engaged in 30+ days
- **Usually means:** the champion may have moved roles, left the company, or disengaged — a five-alarm signal for renewal risk if the relationship was single-threaded.
- **First question:** "Do we have an active relationship with anyone else at this account, or does this account run through one person only?"
- **Data to pull:** stakeholder engagement map for the account, last-login/activity data for the champion specifically.

### A single-threaded account (one engaged stakeholder) above the book's average ARR
- **Usually means:** a high-value account carries outsized risk from one person's departure or disengagement, with no fallback relationship in place.
- **First question:** "Who else at this account would we talk to tomorrow if this person left today?"
- **Data to pull:** number of active stakeholder relationships per account, cross-referenced against account ARR tier.

### A QBR (quarterly business review) agenda built around new feature announcements rather than the customer's own outcome metrics
- **Usually means:** the review isn't reinforcing the value case that actually protects the renewal — it's a product update, not a value story tied to what the customer bought.
- **First question:** "Does this QBR restate the customer's own business outcome in their terms, or is it a feature tour?"
- **Data to pull:** the QBR deck/agenda, the account's original stated business case and current outcome metrics against it.

### An account past its expected time-to-first-value milestone with no flagged escalation
- **Usually means:** the customer likely hasn't reached the outcome that justified the purchase, and without an escalation this often goes unnoticed until much later, closer to renewal, when the intervention window is smaller.
- **First question:** "What was the expected time-to-first-value for this account, and where are they against it right now?"
- **Data to pull:** onboarding milestone plan and actual progress dates, escalation log if the milestone was missed.

### Disproportionate CSM time/effort logged against a low-value account relative to its tier
- **Usually means:** effort may be following whichever customer is loudest rather than a deliberate health-times-value triage, at the expense of higher-value accounts.
- **First question:** "Does this account's ARR and health tier justify this level of attention relative to the rest of the book?"
- **Data to pull:** logged activity/time by account, cross-referenced against the book's health-x-value segmentation.

### A health score built once and never re-validated against actual subsequent churn outcomes
- **Usually means:** the scoring model may no longer predict real risk accurately — a health score that doesn't correlate with actual churn is worse than no score, since it creates false confidence.
- **First question:** "When was this health-scoring model last checked against actual churned vs. retained accounts, and did it predict the outcome correctly?"
- **Data to pull:** health score history for churned accounts in the trailing 12 months, whether the model flagged them as at-risk before they churned.

### A renewal conversation opening with "please don't leave" framing rather than a value recap
- **Usually means:** the account's value case wasn't reinforced proactively earlier in the relationship, leaving the CSM negotiating from a weak, reactive position at the worst possible time.
- **First question:** "What outcome data do we have to open this conversation with, and why wasn't it presented before the renewal window opened?"
- **Data to pull:** the account's outcome/ROI data, the timing and content of the most recent QBR or check-in before the renewal conversation.
