# Playbook

Filled templates for the recurring artifacts of the role: the family food profile, the weekly batch-cook schedule, the multi-diet menu grid, and the grocery reconciliation table.

## Family food profile (living document)

Kept in a shared drive folder the estate manager can access; handed to any successor cook on day one.

| Field | Example entry |
|---|---|
| Member | [Child, age 9] |
| Allergies (confirmed, source, date) | Tree nuts — anaphylaxis, confirmed by [pediatric allergist], 3/2026 |
| Medical dietary restriction | Celiac disease — full gluten elimination, confirmed by [GI], 3/2026 |
| Preference (not medical) | Dislikes cooked spinach; will eat raw |
| Meals served (rolling 90 days) | 2026-06-02 GF chicken piccata; 2026-06-09 nut-free pad see ew; ... |
| Portion size at last review | 1.5x adult portion (growth spurt noted by parent, 5/2026) |
| Last reviewed | 2026-07-01 |

Rebuild trigger: any new diagnosis, any principal-flagged preference shift, or the quarterly review date — whichever comes first.

## Weekly batch-cook schedule (stable-calendar week)

One fixed prep day (example: Sunday) producing 4–5 dinners, portioned and frozen; fresh cooking fills the remaining 2–3 nights.

| Day | Task | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Sun (prep day) | Roast 6 lb chicken thighs (family-wide, nut-free/GF zone) | 8 portions, vacuum-sealed, 2/bag, labeled with cook date |
| Sun (prep day) | Cook 2 lb GF pasta + sauce (child's track, separate pot/colander) | 4 portions |
| Sun (prep day) | Batch-roast vegetables, 2 trays (shared-safe ingredients only) | 6 portions |
| Mon | Reheat: chicken + roasted veg | Family dinner |
| Tue | Reheat: GF pasta (child) + fresh-cooked chicken for rest of family | Split dinner |
| Wed | Fresh-cook (fish, quick turnaround) | Family dinner |
| Thu | Reheat: remaining chicken + veg | Family dinner |
| Fri–Sun | Fresh-cook or dine-out per family calendar | — |

Travel/event-week fallback: drop batching, cook fresh daily or send a portable dietary kit (nut-free/GF snacks, a labeled cooler) if the family travels without the cook.

## Multi-diet menu grid (one dinner, four dietary tracks)

Built so one protein and one starch base serves everyone with track-specific substitutions, rather than four unrelated meals.

| Component | Base (parents, older sibling) | Child (celiac + tree-nut) | Grandparent (visit weeks, low-sodium) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein | Herb-roasted chicken thigh, kosher salt rub | Same chicken, cooked in dedicated nut-free pan, unsalted rub applied before splitting off shared batch | Same chicken, unsalted, herb-only |
| Starch | Regular egg noodles | GF rice noodles, separate pot/colander | Regular egg noodles, no salt added at table |
| Vegetable | Roasted broccoli, olive oil, salt | Same broccoli, salted portion plated before child's unsalted portion is pulled | Broccoli, no added salt, lemon instead |
| Sauce | Soy-ginger glaze (contains sesame, no nuts) | Same glaze, confirmed nut-free at label level, made in dedicated station | Low-sodium tamari swap |

Sequencing rule: cook the most-restricted track first (or in the most controlled station) and portion it out before adding salt, nuts, or shared-equipment steps to the rest — never portion the restricted diner's food *from* a shared batch after the fact.
