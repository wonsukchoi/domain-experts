# Masonry Helper — Red Flags

### Mortar sitting on the board past 2.5 hours from gauging without being placed
- **Usually means:** batch was sized to the mixer's capacity instead of the mason's course rate, or the crew went short-handed mid-shift and pace dropped.
- **First question:** what time was this batch gauged, and has it already been retempered once?
- **Data to pull:** the day's batch-mix log (mix time per batch), not a guess from memory.

### Wall bracing loose, missing, or removed on a green (uncured) lift above the day's course height
- **Usually means:** equipment or foot traffic caught the bracing, or it was pulled to get material through and never reset.
- **First question:** how long has the wall been unbraced, and was any wind load or vibration present in that window?
- **Data to pull:** the bracing log/photo from this morning's setup and the current wind forecast.

### Dry-cutting a masonry saw without water or a vacuum shroud, even briefly
- **Usually means:** the water line wasn't hooked up, or the cut was treated as "too quick to bother."
- **First question:** why isn't the water or dust collection connected right now?
- **Data to pull:** the saw's Table 1 control requirement for this specific task from the site's silica exposure control plan.

### Sand visibly wet or saturated (rain, dew, fresh delivery) with mix water unadjusted
- **Usually means:** the batch is about to come off the mixer too wet, producing weak, sagging mortar.
- **First question:** has anyone run a trowel-cut slump check on this batch yet?
- **Data to pull:** today's mix-water log against yesterday's, and the weather log for the last 12 hours.

### Efflorescence appearing on the face within days of laying
- **Usually means:** high water-cement ratio, unwashed sand, or water intrusion behind the wall — in roughly that order of likelihood.
- **First question:** was the sand from a new pallet, and has the wall been rained on uncovered since laying?
- **Data to pull:** the sand supplier's washed-sand certification and this week's weather/cover log.

### Mortar joints tooled before the mortar is thumbprint-hard
- **Usually means:** the crew is rushing to keep pace, tooling on a fixed schedule rather than a feel check.
- **First question:** did anyone thumbprint-test this section before tooling, or was it timed off the last section?
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a feel check, not a document; the fix is a process reminder, not a report.

### Scaffold duty rating unposted while material is being stacked on the planks
- **Usually means:** the erecting crew didn't tag it, or the tag fell off and nobody flagged it before loading resumed.
- **First question:** who erected this scaffold, and can they confirm the rating before anything more goes on it?
- **Data to pull:** the scaffold inspection tag/log for today.

### A batch retempered more than once
- **Usually means:** the batch should have been discarded at the first retemper and the crew is trying to stretch material instead.
- **First question:** what time was this batch first gauged?
- **Data to pull:** the batch-mix log — if it's already past 150 minutes, the answer is discard, not a second retemper.

### Ambient temperature trending below 40°F with no cold-weather protection in place
- **Usually means:** the forecast wasn't checked against the mix plan, or protection was skipped to save setup time.
- **First question:** is the mix water heated, and are today's completed courses going to be covered before end of shift?
- **Data to pull:** today's forecast low and the ACI 530.1/TMS 602 cold-weather threshold for the mortar type in use.

### CMU or brick units arriving visibly cracked, chipped, or spalled beyond the face-shell
- **Usually means:** rough handling in transit or a bad batch from the supplier — in that order.
- **First question:** what fraction of this pallet shows the same defect?
- **Data to pull:** the delivery ticket and supplier lot number, to flag a pattern rather than a one-off unit.

### Helper buttering more than 2–3 units ahead of the mason's working edge
- **Usually means:** trying to look productive or misjudging the mason's actual pace.
- **First question:** how many units ahead is the buttering right now, and is the lead mortar starting to skin?
- **Data to pull:** none — visual check of the skin/tack on the leading units is the test.

### Rebar or ties missing or mispositioned in reinforced CMU cells before a grout pour is scheduled
- **Usually means:** a step got skipped in the rush to close cells before the pour truck arrives.
- **First question:** does the cell layout match the reinforcement schedule on the drawings, cell by cell?
- **Data to pull:** the structural drawing's reinforcement schedule and today's grout pour ticket/schedule.
