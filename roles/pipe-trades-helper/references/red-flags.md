# Pipe Trades Helper — Red Flags

### Trench 5 ft deep or greater with no shoring, shielding, or sloped walls
- **Usually means:** the crew is treating the protective system as optional for a "quick" task, or the competent person hasn't inspected yet today.
- **First question:** has the competent person classified the soil and inspected the trench today?
- **Data to pull:** today's competent-person inspection log/tag and the soil classification on file.

### Grade-rod deviation beyond the job's stated tolerance (commonly ±0.02 ft / ~1/4 in) at any station
- **Usually means:** the trench bottom was over- or under-dug relative to the cut sheet at that station.
- **First question:** what's the deviation, and has bedding been placed yet at this station?
- **Data to pull:** the grade-check log for this run and the cut sheet's design invert at this station.

### Spoil pile stacked at or over the trench edge
- **Usually means:** convenience — saving a wheelbarrow trip or loader pass — at the cost of surcharge load on the trench wall.
- **First question:** how far is the spoil pile from the edge right now?
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a tape-measure check against the 2 ft OSHA setback, not a document.

### Utility marked in the area but not confirmed by potholing before mechanical excavation
- **Usually means:** the crew is trusting the paint/flag mark's precision more than it deserves, or is behind schedule and skipping the hand-dig step.
- **First question:** has this utility been physically confirmed within the tolerance zone yet?
- **Data to pull:** the locate ticket and any potholing log for this excavation.

### Locate markout older than its stated validity window
- **Usually means:** the schedule slipped past the original dig date and nobody called for a re-mark.
- **First question:** what date was this locate placed, and what's this state's validity window?
- **Data to pull:** the original locate ticket date/number.

### Pipe laid directly on native trench bottom with no bedding placed
- **Usually means:** bedding material wasn't staged in time, or the step got skipped to keep pace with the crew.
- **First question:** is bedding material on site and ready, or does the crew need to wait for a delivery?
- **Data to pull:** today's material delivery log against the bedding spec for this pipe class.

### Backfill lift placed noticeably thicker than the spec's loose-lift depth (commonly 6–8 in) before compaction
- **Usually means:** rushing to close the trench faster, trading compaction quality for speed.
- **First question:** how thick was the lift just placed, and was it compacted before the next lift went on?
- **Data to pull:** the compaction log/lift record for this run, if the spec requires one.

### Rock or oversized stone visible in the pipe zone (bedding, haunching, or initial backfill)
- **Usually means:** native spoil was reused as backfill without screening, or a rock wasn't cleared from the trench bottom before bedding.
- **First question:** where did this material come from — screened bedding stock or native spoil?
- **Data to pull:** the bedding material spec (max stone size) for this pipe class.

### Threaded joint with visibly short thread engagement or an inconsistent turn count from the cut list
- **Usually means:** a threading-machine die issue or a rushed cut that didn't match the fitter's called-for spec.
- **First question:** how many turns past hand-tight did this joint take compared to the rest of the run?
- **Data to pull:** the cut list's called thread length/turns for this joint size.

### Copper joint fluxed without visible bright-metal cleaning on the tube or fitting
- **Usually means:** the prep step (emery cloth to a shine) was skipped or rushed before flux went on.
- **First question:** was this joint cleaned and fluxed in the same pass, or fluxed over old cleaning from earlier in the day?
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a visual check on the joint before it's assembled, not a document.

### No ladder or ramp within 25 ft of lateral travel in a trench 4 ft deep or greater
- **Usually means:** egress was set up for the trench's original footprint and not moved as the excavation extended.
- **First question:** how far is the nearest exit from where people are currently working?
- **Data to pull:** none — a tape-measure or pace-off check against the 25 ft rule.

### Trench reopened or re-entered after a rain event without a re-inspection
- **Usually means:** the crew assumed yesterday's classification and protective system still hold without re-checking.
- **First question:** has the competent person re-inspected since the rain?
- **Data to pull:** today's inspection log timestamp against the weather log.
