# Pipe Trades Helper Playbook

Filled templates for trench protection, bedding, and grade checks. Populate with the day's real numbers — don't work from the blank column headers.

## OSHA soil classification and slope ratio (1926 Subpart P, Appendix B, simple slope option, ≤20 ft depth)

| Soil type | Description | Max allowable slope | Angle from horizontal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type A | Cohesive, unconfined compressive strength ≥1.5 tsf, no fissures, not previously disturbed | 3/4:1 | 53° |
| Type B | Cohesive, unconfined compressive strength 0.5–1.5 tsf, or previously disturbed Type A | 1:1 | 45° |
| Type C | Granular (gravel, sand, loamy sand), submerged soil, or soil with seeping water | 1.5:1 | 34° |
| Unclassified | Not yet classified by the competent person | Treat as Type C until classified | 34° |

**Depth trigger:** any trench 5 ft deep or greater requires a protective system (sloping to the table above, benching, shoring, or a shield/box) unless the excavation is entirely in stable rock, confirmed by the competent person. Below 20 ft, a registered professional engineer must design the protective system — this table doesn't apply.

**Trench box / shield:** rated to a specific depth and soil class per the manufacturer's tabulated data sheet carried with the box — confirm the sheet matches today's depth and the competent person's soil classification before use; a box rated for Type B soil to a given depth is not automatically adequate for Type C at the same depth.

## Excavation daily checklist

1. Locate markout current (not expired) and permit on site.
2. Competent person inspects and classifies soil at shift start, and again after rain, vibration, or any change in trench conditions.
3. Protective system (slope, box, or shoring) matches today's classification and depth before entry.
4. Spoil pile kept back ≥2 ft from the trench edge (OSHA 1926.651(j)(2)).
5. Ladder, ramp, or stairway placed within 25 ft of lateral travel for any trench ≥4 ft deep.
6. Atmospheric testing performed where oxygen deficiency or a hazardous atmosphere could exist (near known gas lines, contaminated soil, low-lying stagnant trenches).

## Grade-check template

| Station | Design invert (ft) | Instrument HI (ft) | Rod reading (ft) | Shot elevation (ft) | Deviation (ft) | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0+00 | 92.50 | 98.75 | 6.25 | 92.50 | 0.00 | On grade |
| 1+00 | 91.50 | 98.75 | 7.30 | 91.45 | −0.05 | Shim bedding +0.6 in |
| 2+00 | 90.50 | 98.75 | 8.25 | 90.50 | 0.00 | On grade |

**Formula:** shot elevation = instrument HI − rod reading. Deviation = shot elevation − design invert. Flag and correct any deviation beyond the job's stated tolerance (commonly ±0.02 ft / ~1/4 in on gravity sewer) before pipe goes in and backfill covers it.

**Design invert from slope:** design invert at station = upstream invert − (station distance in ft × slope as a decimal). Example: 92.50 − (100 × 0.01) = 91.50.

## Bedding class reference (ASTM D2321 / AWWA C600 baseline)

| Zone | Material | Minimum depth | Compaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation (below bedding, only if trench bottom is unstable or rock) | Crushed stone or select granular fill | 4–6 in below planned bedding | Compacted, not just placed |
| Bedding (under pipe barrel) | Crushed stone or coarse sand, per spec class | 4 in (Class B typical minimum) | Shaped to fit barrel, hand-tamped |
| Haunching (springline to mid-pipe) | Same as bedding material | To pipe springline | Hand- or mechanically compacted in lifts — this zone provides the pipe's side support |
| Initial backfill | Bedding-grade material, free of stones >1.5 in (or per spec) | To 12 in over pipe crown | Compacted in lifts ≤6–8 in loose depth |
| Final backfill | Native or specified fill | Remainder of trench | Compacted per pavement/surface spec |

**Grade-correction shim:** when the trench bottom is over-dug relative to design invert, add the deficit as extra bedding thickness at that station, tapering back to standard bedding depth over 10 ft either side — never leave an abrupt step in bedding thickness, it reads as a joint-deflection point later.

## Pipe-end prep checklist

**Threading (NPT):** cut to called-for length from the cut list, thread at the standard 3/4 in per foot taper, hand-tight plus the crew's stated turn count past hand-tight (commonly 2–3 turns for a properly cut thread) as the working check — final tightness call belongs to the journeyman assembling the joint.

**Copper solder prep:** cut square with a tubing cutter, ream the burr from the ID, clean both the tube OD and fitting ID to bright metal with emery/sand cloth (visible shine, not just wiped), apply flux immediately after cleaning to limit oxidation before the joint is made.

## Locate / permit tracking

| Item | Typical validity | Action at expiry |
|---|---|---|
| Utility locate markout | Commonly 10–20 business days depending on state | Call for a re-mark before excavating past the expired zone — treat an expired locate as no locate |
| Excavation permit | Per jurisdiction/project | Confirm still active before starting or resuming work |

**Potholing:** hand-dig or vacuum-excavate within the tolerance zone around any marked utility (commonly 18–24 in either side of the marked line, confirm the state's specific tolerance) before running mechanical excavation through that zone.
