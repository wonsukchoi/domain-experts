# Red Flags

Smell tests for courses, capstone teams, assessment cycles, and grant proposals. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the check to run.

## D/F/W rate up more than 5 points over the 3–5 year rolling baseline

- **Usually means:** an instructor or format mix-shift in one section, not a weaker incoming cohort — cohort-quality changes are rarely this large or this fast.
- **First question:** "Is the jump concentrated in one section?"
- **Data to pull:** section-level D/F/W this term vs the rolling baseline; if available, concept-inventory or common-exam scores by section.

## Concept-inventory normalized gain under 0.3 in a course billed as active-learning

- **Usually means:** the "active learning" on the syllabus is surface-level (think-pair-share with no accountability) or the class time is still majority instructor talk.
- **First question:** "What fraction of class time is actually student-generated work versus lecture, measured, not estimated?"
- **Data to pull:** gain by section; a classroom observation protocol tally if one exists; compare against the published traditional-lecture (~0.23) and interactive-engagement (~0.48) benchmarks.

## The same ABET outcome scores below threshold two cycles running on the same course

- **Usually means:** the prior cycle's documented action either wasn't implemented or wasn't the right lever.
- **First question:** "What did the prior self-study say we'd change, and can I point to where it actually changed?"
- **Data to pull:** the continuous-improvement log entry from the last cycle, syllabus version diff between cycles.

## A capstone sponsor requests a scope change mid-semester

- **Usually means:** the sponsor is treating the course as free contract R&D, or the original problem statement was underspecified from the start.
- **First question:** "Does this change affect the agreed milestone or deliverable date?"
- **Data to pull:** the signed project agreement/statement of work, the milestone tracker.

## One capstone team member logging under a third of the team's per-person average hours

- **Usually means:** free-rider dynamics under a flat, undifferentiated team grade.
- **First question:** "What does the peer-evaluation instrument say, independent of the hours log?"
- **Data to pull:** individual time logs, peer-evaluation scores at the most recent design review.

## Student evaluation scores drop right after a switch to active learning

- **Usually means:** students are conflating productive struggle with poor teaching — a well-documented short-term resistance pattern, not necessarily a real quality drop.
- **First question:** "Did exam or concept-inventory performance move in the same direction as the evaluation scores, or the opposite direction?"
- **Data to pull:** evaluation-score trend alongside concept-inventory or common-exam trend over the same terms.

## Program's FE exam pass rate trails the national discipline average by more than 10 points

- **Usually means:** a specific topic area isn't reinforced enough relative to the NCEES exam specification, not a general rigor problem.
- **First question:** "Which FE exam subtopics show the lowest per-item pass rate for our test-takers?"
- **Data to pull:** the NCEES program diagnostic report, the curriculum map against the current FE exam specification.

## A capstone project reuses last year's problem statement with a new team

- **Usually means:** scope was recycled to save advisor prep time, which under-delivers on the "culminating major design experience" requirement since less of the design work is genuinely novel.
- **First question:** "What design decisions does this team actually have to make that last year's team didn't?"
- **Data to pull:** a diff of this year's problem statement against last year's, the design-review rubric scores for originality/scope.

## A grant proposal is budgeted at a planning-track scale with a department-restructuring ambition

- **Usually means:** the ask wasn't matched to the mechanism — a common, avoidable cause of an unfunded proposal.
- **First question:** "Which track's stated scope actually matches what this project is trying to do?"
- **Data to pull:** the solicitation's track descriptions, the budget justification.

## A lab incident or near-miss is reported

- **Usually means:** a gap in safety-training verification or equipment maintenance — "the student didn't follow instructions" is a symptom, not the cause, until proven otherwise.
- **First question:** "Was the pre-lab safety checklist actually verified for this student, not just distributed to the class?"
- **Data to pull:** the lab safety sign-off log, the equipment maintenance/calibration record.

## A tenure or promotion dossier leans on the numeric student-evaluation average as the primary teaching-effectiveness evidence

- **Usually means:** the case is built on the weakest available signal — well-powered studies put the evaluation/learning correlation near zero.
- **First question:** "What concept-inventory, peer-observation, or documented-curricular-change evidence exists instead?"
- **Data to pull:** peer classroom-observation reports, any concept-inventory or common-exam trend data tied to the candidate's sections.

## An assessment cycle's "action taken" field repeats the prior cycle's wording almost verbatim

- **Usually means:** compliance theater — the data gets filed, not acted on.
- **First question:** "Can I point to a specific syllabus line, assignment, or class session that changed because of this data?"
- **Data to pull:** the syllabus version history, curriculum-committee meeting minutes from the relevant term.

## Overall course grade average is stable while the concept-inventory gain is falling

- **Usually means:** grade inflation or partial-credit drift is masking a real conceptual decline — the exam and rubric are getting easier to pass at the same rate understanding is dropping.
- **First question:** "Has the exam or rubric changed in the same window the gain declined?"
- **Data to pull:** exam version history, rubric version history, item-difficulty statistics if the exam bank is tracked.
