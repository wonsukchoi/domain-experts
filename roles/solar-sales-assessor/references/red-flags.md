# Red flags

Smell tests an experienced rep or a reviewing manager catches before a deal closes, or before a bad one gets signed.

### "100% savings guaranteed" pitched with no rate schedule shown

- **Usually means:** the number was built off a national average or a full-retail net-metering assumption that doesn't match this customer's actual tariff — the classic setup for the post-install complaint (and the pattern behind several FTC and state AG enforcement actions against door-to-door solar sellers).
- **First question:** "Can you show me the exact utility rate schedule and export program this quote is built on?"
- **Data to pull:** the customer's actual rate schedule name and tier/TOU structure, the export compensation program (NEM vs. net billing), and whether the escalation assumption is written on the proposal itself.

### System sized to more than 100% of usage under a devalued export tariff, no battery attached

- **Usually means:** the rep sized to a round offset number instead of to the export tariff's real payout, producing a worse payback than a smaller, battery-attached design would.
- **First question:** "What share of this production gets self-consumed in real time versus exported, and what's the export credited at?"
- **Data to pull:** modeled self-consumption ratio, the utility's specific avoided-cost/export rate, and a side-by-side payback comparison against a right-sized alternative.

### Roof age and material not recorded before the proposal was built

- **Usually means:** the system will be quoted, sold, and possibly installed before anyone checks whether the roof has the remaining service life to outlast the panel warranty.
- **First question:** "What's the roof material, installation year, and rated service life — and did anyone walk it?"
- **Data to pull:** roof age, material, manufacturer warranty term, and a visual or aerial-report condition assessment.

### Quote presented only as a monthly payment ("just $129/mo")

- **Usually means:** a dealer fee is baked into the loan APR and is invisible at the payment level — the cash-price-equivalent is materially higher than what a cash buyer would pay for the identical system.
- **First question:** "What's the cash price of this exact system, and what's the dealer fee on this loan?"
- **Data to pull:** loan principal, APR, term, and the lender's disclosed dealer fee (or back it out by comparing to the cash price of an identical system).

### Shading estimate from a satellite tool only, on a lot with visible nearby trees or structures

- **Usually means:** the production number hasn't accounted for real obstruction and will run high, especially if any obstruction sits within ~30–45° of solar noon azimuth.
- **First question:** "Was an instrumented shading report run on this roof, or only a satellite model?"
- **Data to pull:** the shading report (fisheye, LiDAR, or on-site tool output) and the specific derate percentage it produced versus the unshaded baseline.

### Electrical panel capacity not checked before sizing the system

- **Usually means:** the site may need a panel upgrade the proposal doesn't price, turning into a change order after signing.
- **First question:** "What's the panel's current calculated load versus its rated capacity?"
- **Data to pull:** panel amperage rating, existing calculated load (per NEC 220 or the AHJ's method), and remaining headroom.

### Lease or PPA pitched as "the same as owning"

- **Usually means:** the customer isn't being told the third party keeps the tax credit, the payment or per-kWh rate escalates annually (commonly ~2.9%/yr), and the system isn't an asset on their property at resale unless the lease is transferred.
- **First question:** "Who owns this system, and what does the payment or rate look like in year 10?"
- **Data to pull:** the escalator percentage, the full-term payment/rate schedule, and the transfer-on-sale terms in the lease/PPA contract.

### Utility rate escalation assumed above roughly 4%/yr with no citation

- **Usually means:** the savings projection is being inflated by the escalation assumption rather than by the system's actual production — historical US retail electricity CAGR has run closer to 2–3%/yr (EIA).
- **First question:** "Where does the escalation percentage on this proposal come from?"
- **Data to pull:** the source cited for the escalation rate and a re-run of the savings table at 2.5–3.5%/yr for comparison.

### No interconnection or Permission-to-Operate timeline disclosed

- **Usually means:** the customer expects savings to start at install completion and doesn't know the system can sit uninspected or un-energized for weeks to months depending on the utility's interconnection queue.
- **First question:** "What's the expected time from install to Permission to Operate for this utility and interconnection standard?"
- **Data to pull:** the applicable interconnection standard (e.g., Rule 21), the utility's current average processing time, and any known local backlog.

### High-pressure same-day / "today only" pricing

- **Usually means:** the pricing pressure is substituting for the customer having time to compare a cash-price-equivalent across offers — a tactic named specifically in multiple state AG consumer-protection settlements against solar door-to-door sellers.
- **First question:** "What changes about this price if the customer takes 48 hours to compare it against another quote?"
- **Data to pull:** whether the same system/price is available at a later date; if not, get the discount structure justified in writing.

### Rep's certification/license number missing from the proposal

- **Usually means:** either an administrative gap or, less often, someone selling outside their credentialed scope — worth checking before signature, not after.
- **First question:** "What's the NABCEP or state contractor/salesperson license number on file for this rep?"
- **Data to pull:** the certification or license number and its current status against the issuing body's registry.
