# Court, Municipal, and License Clerk — Red Flags

### Filer asks "should I file this" or "what should I say happened"
- **Usually means:** the filer is confused about the legal substance of their case, not just the procedural form.
- **First question:** "Are you asking what the form requires, or what to argue — because I can only help with the first."
- **Data to pull:** the self-help-center referral list for this case type.

### Submitted fee is short by an amount that doesn't match any single line item
- **Usually means:** the filer or the clerk misapplied the fee schedule for this specific case type/county rather than simple arithmetic error.
- **First question:** which fee-schedule version applies to this case type and county — has it changed recently?
- **Data to pull:** current fee-schedule effective-date table.

### Records request touches a case type that's sometimes sealed but the system shows no flag
- **Usually means:** either the case genuinely isn't sealed, or the seal was entered under a different case number/consolidated case and didn't propagate.
- **First question:** is this case linked to or consolidated with another case number that might carry the seal?
- **Data to pull:** case-linkage/consolidation history in the CMS.

### Fee-waiver request with income just above the threshold but categorical benefits enrollment unverified
- **Usually means:** the filer may still qualify via the categorical path even though the income path fails.
- **First question:** are you enrolled in any public-benefits program, even if you didn't list it on the form?
- **Data to pull:** the jurisdiction's current qualifying-program list.

### Docket-entry date doesn't match the actual filing-received date
- **Usually means:** a backlog delay between receipt and entry, or a same-day entry error.
- **First question:** does the deadline calculation for this case depend on the entry date or the receipt date — check the governing rule.
- **Data to pull:** date-stamped receipt log vs. docket-entry timestamp.

### License/permit renewal submitted with prior credentials attached but no current-date proof
- **Usually means:** the applicant assumes prior good standing carries forward automatically.
- **First question:** is this renewal within or past the stated grace period?
- **Data to pull:** the credential's expiration date against today's date and the grace-period rule.

### A filing is rejected twice for two different reasons on two separate visits
- **Usually means:** the completeness checklist wasn't run in full on the first pass.
- **First question:** was the full checklist for this transaction type applied at intake, or just a partial review?
- **Data to pull:** the standard checklist for this transaction type, re-run from scratch.

### A supervisor override request cites "this filer's situation is unusual" without citing a specific rule exception
- **Usually means:** someone is asking for discretionary leniency the checklist doesn't have authority to grant.
- **First question:** which specific rule or statute provides the exception being requested?
- **Data to pull:** the local rule or statute cited, verified against its actual text.

### Fee collected exceeds the itemized total by a round amount (e.g., exactly $10 or $20 over)
- **Usually means:** an extra service was assumed to apply (e.g., record search) that the filer didn't actually request.
- **First question:** re-verify which boxes were checked on the intake form against what was charged.
- **Data to pull:** the original intake form's checked-box list.
