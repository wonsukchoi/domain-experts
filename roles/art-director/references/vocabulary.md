# Art Director — Vocabulary

### Concept vs. execution
Concept is the underlying strategic idea a piece is built on; execution is the craft of building it well. A feedback note belongs to one or the other, never both at once.
**In use:** "That's a concept note, not an execution note — we're not polishing the layout, we're changing what the ad says."
**Common misuse:** Generalists send both types of feedback in one undifferentiated round, leaving the team unsure whether to rebuild or polish.

### Creative brief
The strategic document — audience, core message, mandatories, budget, timeline — that all creative work is judged against.
**In use:** "This concept is strong craft, but it doesn't serve the audience line in the brief — that's the gap we need to fix, not the visuals."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a wishlist of everything the client wants, rather than a set of constraints that force tradeoffs.

### Divergent / convergent process
Divergent: generating genuinely different strategic directions. Convergent: narrowing to one, backed by rationale. A real creative process does both, in that order.
**In use:** "We're still in divergence — don't refine concept A yet, we need B and C explored first."
**Common misuse:** Skipping divergence and presenting one idea refined three visual ways as if it were three choices.

### Day rate
A vendor's per-day cost, covering labor and equipment for that day — not automatically inclusive of usage or licensing rights.
**In use:** "The photographer's day rate is $12,000; usage for digital and print through year-end is a separate $6,000 line."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to include unlimited future usage of the resulting images, which is usually a separate negotiated fee.

### Usage rights / licensing
The specific channels, duration, and geography a client is permitted to use a piece of creative work in, as defined in the vendor or talent contract.
**In use:** "This license covers digital and print through Q4 — if we want to run it in-store next year, that's a new usage negotiation."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be perpetual and unlimited-channel by default; it's almost always scoped.

### Contact sheet
The full set of frames captured during a shoot, reviewed in totality before any selects are made.
**In use:** "Pull the contact sheet before we pick — frame 3 isn't the only usable option from that setup."
**Common misuse:** Skipping the full review and picking selects from only the first handful of frames a photographer surfaces.

### Selects
The specific frames chosen from a shoot for retouching and use.
**In use:** "Our selects from setup 2 are frames 7, 12, and 19 — the rest go to archive."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with "the best-looking photos" in isolation, rather than "the photos that best serve the brief's message."

### Mandatories
Non-negotiable elements a client, legal team, or brand system requires in every deliverable — logo placement, disclaimers, taglines.
**In use:** "The disclaimer is a mandatory — it can't be dropped for a cleaner layout, it has to be designed around."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a creative suggestion that can be traded away if it clutters the composition.

### Comp (comprehensive layout)
A polished mockup meant to represent the intended final look and feel — not yet a production-ready file.
**In use:** "This comp is for client approval; production won't rebuild it as final art until the concept is locked."
**Common misuse:** Sent to a print vendor or developer as if it were production-ready, causing rework when it isn't.

### Scope creep / change order
Scope creep is new work absorbed into an existing budget without a formal adjustment; a change order is the documented, budgeted version of the same request.
**In use:** "That's a new deliverable, not a revision — it needs a change order before the team starts on it."
**Common misuse:** New requests get quietly folded into the current round's budget, invisibly eroding margin until the overage shows up at project close.

### Talent release
Signed permission for using a person's likeness in specified channels and for a specified duration.
**In use:** "The release covers digital use through December — if we extend the campaign, we need a new signature."
**Common misuse:** Assumed a general release signed at the shoot covers any future use of the footage, in any channel, indefinitely.

### Style frame
A single representative frame used to lock visual tone — color, type, mood — before full production begins, common in video and motion work.
**In use:** "Get sign-off on the style frame before the animator builds the full sequence — that's the tone check, not the final shot."
**Common misuse:** Confused with an actual frame pulled from the finished edit, rather than a pre-production tone reference.

### Art buying
The function of sourcing, vetting, and negotiating with photographers, illustrators, and production vendors — distinct from the creative-direction judgment itself.
**In use:** "Art buying is negotiating the day rate and usage terms; art direction is deciding whether this photographer's style serves the concept."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with the director's own creative-direction role, as if choosing a vendor and directing the resulting work were the same skill.
