# Art Director — Red Flags

### Client picks a concept with "I just like it" and no reference to the brief
- **Usually means:** the concepts weren't pressure-tested against the brief before presenting, or the brief itself lacks a clear strategic hook for the client to reference.
- **First question:** which line in the brief does this concept serve that the others don't?
- **Data to pull:** the original creative brief and the concept-rationale deck.

### A deliverable reaches revision round 3 on the same piece
- **Usually means:** concept and execution feedback got conflated in round 1, or two different people on the client side disagree and each round reflects a different reviewer's taste.
- **First question:** were rounds 1 and 2 execution notes, or did the client change their mind about the concept itself?
- **Data to pull:** the revision-round feedback log with the concept/execution label on each note.

### Production spend exceeds 30% of total budget before 30% of deliverables are complete
- **Usually means:** the exploration phase overran its cap, or scope grew without a corresponding budget conversation with the client.
- **First question:** what changed between the approved scope and the current deliverable count?
- **Data to pull:** the budget tracker against the deliverable checklist.

### A vendor day-rate quote has no usage/licensing line
- **Usually means:** the quote covers the shoot day only, not the rights to use the resulting images across the intended channels and duration — a gap that surfaces as a surprise invoice later.
- **First question:** what usage term and which channels does this day rate actually cover?
- **Data to pull:** the vendor contract's usage clause.

### Fewer than 2 genuinely distinct concept directions are presented
- **Usually means:** the exploration phase was rushed, or the team defaulted to refining one idea instead of diverging.
- **First question:** what's the second, strategically distinct angle that wasn't explored?
- **Data to pull:** the concept-to-brief mapping showing which brief line each direction serves.

### Talent or location release paperwork is incomplete at shoot wrap
- **Usually means:** usage rights for the intended campaign channels and duration aren't actually secured, which blocks legal use of the footage later.
- **First question:** is the release signed for the specific channels and duration in the current media plan?
- **Data to pull:** signed release forms per talent/location.

### A senior stakeholder's feedback contradicts an earlier sign-off from someone else on the same round
- **Usually means:** the approval chain wasn't clarified before the round started, and a full cycle gets spent reconciling internal disagreement instead of moving the work forward.
- **First question:** who has final sign-off authority on this deliverable?
- **Data to pull:** the approval-chain line in the project brief or SOW.

### Retouching hours logged exceed 1.5x the original estimate
- **Usually means:** either the shoot didn't capture enough usable selects (variance underestimated at planning), or scope crept mid-retouch — additional versions requested without a change order.
- **First question:** is this the same deliverable count as the original estimate, or did scope grow?
- **Data to pull:** the shot list against the delivered-asset count.

### The brand's style guide doesn't cover the application currently being produced
- **Usually means:** the guideline was built for a narrower set of applications than the campaign now needs (e.g., no motion/video spec when the campaign has grown to include video).
- **First question:** does an equivalent application exist in the guideline to extrapolate from, or is this a genuine new gap?
- **Data to pull:** the guideline's table of contents against the current deliverable list.

### A client-approved concept tests poorly in an informal audience review
- **Usually means:** the concept was chosen for internal or client taste rather than actual audience fit, or the informal review sample doesn't match the brief's stated target audience.
- **First question:** does the review sample match the audience definition in the brief?
- **Data to pull:** the brief's audience definition against the review panel's demographics.
