# Red flags — what an underwriter checks reflexively

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one can be innocent; two or three together on the same account is a pattern worth a referral even below the mandatory threshold.

### 3-year loss ratio above 70% driven by a single occurrence over $100K
- **Usually means:** either a one-time large loss that caps out and trends away (fixable), or a repeatable physical/operational hazard dressed up as bad luck (not fixable without a condition).
- **First question:** "Was the cause of loss corrected, or just paid?"
- **Data to pull:** loss run with cause-of-loss narrative, engineering/loss-control survey notes on whether the hazard was remediated, photos or inspection report dated after the loss.

### Modeled PML on a single risk within 10% of the treaty's automatic per-risk capacity
- **Usually means:** the account is one adverse model run (updated hazard curve, revised construction data) away from requiring facultative placement or exceeding net retention outright.
- **First question:** "What does the PML look like on the current model version, not last year's?"
- **Data to pull:** current-vintage cat model output (not a carried-forward number), treaty slip's automatic-capacity language, construction/COPE data confirming the model's inputs are current.

### Exposure base reported flat or declining while the insured's other financials show growth
- **Usually means:** revenue or payroll is being under-reported to hold the premium down, or the account's operations have shifted into a different, unreported class of business.
- **First question:** "Can we get the audited financials or payroll tax filings that back this number?"
- **Data to pull:** premium audit history, prior-year audit variance, any new locations/products not reflected in the exposure base.

### SIR or high-deductible request arriving without a collateral conversation
- **Usually means:** the broker is pricing the retention change on premium credit alone; the insured hasn't budgeted for the letter of credit or trust fund the carrier will require.
- **First question:** "Has the insured priced the collateral cost, not just the premium credit?"
- **Data to pull:** carrier's collateral formula for the requested retention level, insured's current credit facility headroom, comparable deductible-only alternative and its LER.

### Schedule credit exceeding 15-20% with no documented basis refreshed in the last renewal cycle
- **Usually means:** a credit that was justified at bind (strong safety program, low turnover) has been carried forward on inertia rather than re-verified, understating true rate need.
- **First question:** "What's the current evidence for this credit — when was it last field-verified?"
- **Data to pull:** original schedule-rating worksheet and justification, most recent loss-control survey, OSHA/EMR trend if applicable.

### Broker marketing the same account to multiple carriers at renewal with a compressed decision window
- **Usually means:** either genuine competitive shopping (fine) or the account is being shopped because the incumbent flagged a hazard or rate action the broker is trying to route around before it's disclosed.
- **First question:** "What changed this renewal that triggered a first-time marketing effort?"
- **Data to pull:** prior renewal history with this carrier, any loss-control recommendations issued and their compliance status, timing of the marketing relative to any recent large loss.

### Claims-made liability policy with a retroactive date that doesn't match the insured's actual history
- **Usually means:** a gap year, a prior carrier's non-renewal, or an unreported claim sitting outside the new retro date — coverage the insured thinks they have isn't there.
- **First question:** "Where was this account before the current retro date, and why does it start there?"
- **Data to pull:** prior carrier's declarations pages, any prior notice-of-circumstance filings, gap analysis between retro date and actual operating history.

### Named-insured or ownership change at renewal with loss history "starting fresh"
- **Usually means:** an entity restructuring (new LLC, M&A) intended, deliberately or not, to reset a loss history the underwriter would otherwise price against.
- **First question:** "Is this the same operation under a new name, or an actual change in what's being insured?"
- **Data to pull:** corporate structure filings, prior entity's loss runs under any name, continuity of operations/management/location.

### Deductible or SIR increase requested in the same renewal as a large open claim
- **Usually means:** the insured is trying to move future frequency into a retention layer while the current large loss still sits with the carrier — the timing, not the request itself, is the tell.
- **First question:** "Would this retention change have been requested absent the open claim?"
- **Data to pull:** claim status and reserve on the open loss, retention request history (new ask or recurring), broker's stated rationale.

### Cat-exposed property with construction/occupancy data unverified for more than one renewal cycle
- **Usually means:** the PML the carrier is pricing off is stale — a roof replacement, an occupancy change, or a protection-class change could move the modeled PML materially in either direction.
- **First question:** "When was COPE data last field-verified, not just broker-attested?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent engineering survey date, any permits or occupancy changes on file, insurer-to-value ratio trend.
