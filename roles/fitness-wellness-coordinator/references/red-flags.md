# Red Flags

### AED response time drilled at >3 minutes from collapse to first shock
- **Usually means:** the written emergency-response plan exists on paper but staff have never run a timed drill, or the AED is stored somewhere staff can't reach fast under stress (locked office, upstairs closet).
- **First question:** "When did we last run a timed drill, and what was the clock?"
- **Data to pull:** drill logs with timestamps; AED placement map versus floor plan and staffing positions at time of drill.

### No written emergency-response policy on file, only an AED on the wall
- **Usually means:** the facility bought equipment to check a compliance box without building the policy the AHA/ACSM joint position actually requires — staff roles, call sequence, and drill cadence are undefined.
- **First question:** "If someone collapses on the floor right now, who does what, in what order?"
- **Data to pull:** the written policy document itself (not the AED purchase invoice); most recent staff drill sign-off sheet.

### PAR-Q+ or ACSM screening pass rate above ~95% for a general adult population
- **Usually means:** staff are steering participants away from "yes" answers, skipping follow-up questions, or not administering the full instrument — Whitfield's NHANES analysis puts the expected physician-referral rate at 54.2% before any exercise and 2.6% before vigorous exercise specifically.
- **First question:** "Walk me through how the front desk actually administers this form."
- **Data to pull:** raw screening intake forms for the last 90 days; referral log cross-checked against intake volume.

### Biometric screen shows BP ≥130/85, waist ≥40in (men)/35in (women), triglycerides ≥150 mg/dL, HDL <40 mg/dL, or fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL, and the participant stayed in general coaching
- **Usually means:** a coach folded a flagged result into ongoing wellness coaching instead of referring out, either from not recognizing the cut point or from not wanting to lose the client relationship.
- **First question:** "Where's the referral record for this participant, and if there isn't one, why not?"
- **Data to pull:** biometric screening results log matched against the referral-out tracker for the same participant IDs.

### Participation target set above 50% with no personalized-outreach plan behind the gap
- **Usually means:** leadership anchored on an aspirational number (70–90%) without checking it against the fact that most employer programs land at 40–50% participation and only ~16% of employees report high engagement.
- **First question:** "What's the current enrollment number, and what specific outreach closes the gap to the target?"
- **Data to pull:** trailing 12-month enrollment and engagement trend; any segmented outreach plan tied to the target.

### Wellness incentive dollar amount set as a flat number without checking it against total plan cost
- **Usually means:** the incentive was priced off a benchmark or a leadership ask rather than the HIPAA/ACA ceiling of 30% of total cost of coverage (50% for tobacco-cessation), so it's likely non-compliant the moment total plan cost is known.
- **First question:** "What's our average total cost of coverage, and does this incentive stay under 30% of it?"
- **Data to pull:** current total-cost-of-coverage figure per employee; the proposed incentive memo with its dollar amount.

### Same liability waiver template used across facilities in multiple states
- **Usually means:** legal assumed enforceability travels with the document, missing that New York and Virginia treat fitness waivers as unenforceable as a matter of public policy regardless of wording.
- **First question:** "Has this specific waiver been checked against this state's case law, or just copied from another location?"
- **Data to pull:** waiver template version and effective date per location; most recent legal review date per state.

### A trainer or coach had a participant redo or continue an exercise after the participant reported symptoms (nausea, dizziness, chest tightness) mid-session
- **Usually means:** the exact fact pattern from *Allen v. LA Fitness* (N.J. App. Div. 2011) — the moment symptoms are reported and the activity continues anyway, ordinary negligence risk becomes gross-negligence risk that the waiver does not cover.
- **First question:** "What did the participant report, and what did staff do in the next 60 seconds?"
- **Data to pull:** incident report (or absence of one) for the session; staff account documented same-day, not reconstructed later.

### Instructor-to-participant ratio set without reference to any named standard
- **Usually means:** class size was set by room capacity or revenue target alone, with no ratio standard applied because none exists for that modality — a guess is being presented as a safety decision.
- **First question:** "What ratio standard did we check against before setting this class size, and if none exists, what's the safety margin we built in?"
- **Data to pull:** class roster counts versus room/equipment capacity; any incident history at current ratios.

### HERO scorecard or CDC Worksite Health ScoreCard results presented without saying which one was used and why
- **Usually means:** the audit answered a convenient question rather than the one leadership actually asked — HERO measures program leadership/strategy maturity, CDC ScoreCard measures policy and environmental supports, and they are not interchangeable.
- **First question:** "What decision is this audit supposed to inform, and does that map to HERO or to the CDC ScoreCard?"
- **Data to pull:** the audit instrument and version used; the original question or decision the audit was commissioned to answer.

### A coach's conversation notes mention diagnosis, medication changes, or a treatment plan
- **Usually means:** a coaching relationship has drifted past the NBHWC scope-of-practice boundary into territory that requires referral to a licensed provider, often because the coach didn't recognize the line in the moment.
- **First question:** "At what point in this conversation did the topic shift from behavior change to diagnosis or treatment, and was it flagged for referral?"
- **Data to pull:** session notes for the coaching relationship; referral log for the same participant.

### NBHWC-certified coach roster shows recertification hours below 36 over the current 3-year cycle
- **Usually means:** a coach's certification has lapsed or is about to, meaning they're operating (or about to operate) outside the credential the program relies on to defend its scope-of-practice boundary.
- **First question:** "Which coaches are behind on continuing-education hours, and how far into the 3-year cycle are we?"
- **Data to pull:** NBHWC certification and CE-hours tracker per coach; cycle start/end dates.
