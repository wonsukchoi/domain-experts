# Red flags

### OCT signal strength below vendor floor (Cirrus <6/10, Spectralis "poor" Q-band)
- **Usually means:** media opacity (cataract, vitreous hemorrhage), poor patient fixation, or dry/misaligned corneal surface — rarely a true anatomic finding.
- **First question:** has this patient's lens or vitreous clarity changed since the last visit that scored higher?
- **Data to pull:** prior visit's signal strength trend and lens status from the chart.

### Axial length differs between fellow eyes by >0.3 mm, no trauma/surgery history
- **Usually means:** contact-technique corneal compression artifact on one or both eyes, not real anisometropia.
- **First question:** which biometry technique (contact, immersion, optical) produced each eye's reading?
- **Data to pull:** raw biometry technique field and prior visit's axial lengths for both eyes.

### Patient reports "iodine or shellfish allergy" with ICG angiography ordered
- **Usually means:** a genuine relative contraindication to ICG specifically (sodium iodide component) — not automatically to fluorescein.
- **First question:** is fluorescein alone sufficient for this clinical question, or is ICG's choroidal-flow information actually needed?
- **Data to pull:** the specific order (FFA only, ICGA only, or both) and any documented prior dye reaction.

### Sudden bright hyperfluorescent spot at the IV site mid-angiogram
- **Usually means:** dye extravasation rather than a vascular finding.
- **First question:** is there localized pain or swelling at the injection site right now?
- **Data to pull:** injection-site exam, volume of dye actually delivered vs. ordered dose.

### Humphrey visual field with fixation losses ≥20% or false-pos/neg ≥33%
- **Usually means:** poor test reliability — the defect pattern shown may not represent the patient's true field.
- **First question:** was this the patient's first field, or has reliability degraded from a previously reliable baseline?
- **Data to pull:** reliability indices for this visit and the prior 2–3 visits for trend.

### Software progression flag (e.g., GPA "possible progression") on a field or OCT series
- **Usually means:** a real change, OR an unreliable visit driving a false flag — cannot be assumed which without checking.
- **First question:** did every visit in the flagged series meet its own reliability threshold?
- **Data to pull:** reliability index for each visit contributing to the flagged trend.

### Consent form laterality doesn't match the OR schedule or surgeon's verbal statement
- **Usually means:** a scheduling entry error, a stale consent, or (rarely) a genuine wrong-site setup in progress.
- **First question:** which document was updated most recently, and by whom?
- **Data to pull:** signed consent form, OR schedule entry, and the clinic note that generated the order.

### Trainee (COA/COT) files a study with no flags across an unusually long stretch of shifts
- **Usually means:** either genuinely clean work, or the trainee isn't recognizing what should be flagged.
- **First question:** when you ask them to explain why a specific borderline study wasn't flagged, can they articulate the threshold?
- **Data to pull:** a sample of their filed studies' reliability indices against the practice's stated thresholds.

### Patient with documented prior mild dye reaction, no premedication ordered for today's repeat study
- **Usually means:** the prior reaction wasn't carried forward into today's pre-test workflow.
- **First question:** has the ordering physician seen today's history against the prior reaction note?
- **Data to pull:** prior angiography report's reaction documentation and today's pre-test allergy screen.

### Wide-field image shows a peripheral finding absent from the posterior-pole-only note
- **Usually means:** the technical note reverted to describing only what a standard camera would show, losing the value of the wider field.
- **First question:** was the full wide-field image actually reviewed frame-by-frame, or only the default posterior crop?
- **Data to pull:** full wide-field capture vs. the note's described findings.

### Contact-technique axial length used directly in an IOL power calculation without a technique flag
- **Usually means:** the calculation is silently carrying a systematic under-measurement (softer/indentable globe most at risk).
- **First question:** was optical or immersion biometry attempted and unavailable, or simply not tried?
- **Data to pull:** biometry technique field on the reading feeding the IOL calculation.
