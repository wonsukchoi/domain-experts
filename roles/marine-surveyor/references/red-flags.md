# Red flags

### UTG reading below the class-rule minimum at a single point, while the plate's average diminution passes
- **Usually means:** localized pitting rather than general wastage — a mechanism the average obscures because it's driven by a small area, not the whole member.
- **First question:** how many of an extended gauging grid's supplementary readings around that point also fall below the minimum, and over what area?
- **Data to pull:** the raw point-by-point UTG dataset (not just the reported average) and the approved midship section drawing's rule-minimum thickness for that specific structural member.

### Special Survey shown as "in progress" or "extension granted" rather than "completed" on the class status report
- **Usually means:** the vessel's structural condition has not been independently re-verified this survey cycle, and any hull findings on file predate the current condition by up to the survey window's length.
- **First question:** what is the extension's expiry date, and has any structural gauging at all been completed under this cycle yet?
- **Data to pull:** the class society's survey status report (not the certificate face) and the extension approval letter if one exists.

### Certificate dates all current, but the class status report lists one or more open recommendations or conditions of class
- **Usually means:** a deficiency was identified and given a compliance deadline rather than requiring immediate correction — the certificate doesn't reflect it because recommendations don't suspend the certificate until the deadline lapses.
- **First question:** what is the compliance deadline on each open item, and does it fall before or after the transaction/policy date in question?
- **Data to pull:** the class society's outstanding-items or recommendations list, cross-referenced against the certificate expiry dates.

### Draft/freeboard reading shows the load line disc submerged past the assigned Summer mark
- **Usually means:** the vessel is loaded past its statutory limit for the current zone and season — a Load Line Convention non-conformity independent of hull structural condition.
- **First question:** what zone and season applies at the vessel's current position and date, and which load line mark governs?
- **Data to pull:** the vessel's Load Line Certificate (assigned freeboard and marks) and the current draft survey/loading computer printout.

### A structural repair area is sized directly from the single lowest UTG reading, with no extended gauging grid on file
- **Usually means:** the repair scope was estimated before the actual extent of the corrosion or pitting was established, risking either an undersized insert plate or an unnecessarily large one.
- **First question:** how many supplementary readings were taken around the low point, and what area do they define?
- **Data to pull:** the full gauging report for that location, not just the single reading cited in the recommendation.

### A pre-purchase or P&I condition survey report reads like a full class special survey checklist regardless of the commissioning party's actual question
- **Usually means:** the survey was scoped generically instead of to the client's decision, either wasting budget on data the client didn't need or, worse, missing the specific risk (insurability, valuation, causation) the client actually commissioned the survey to answer.
- **First question:** what decision is the commissioning party making with this report, and does every section serve that decision?
- **Data to pull:** the original survey instruction/commissioning letter and the report's table of contents against it.

### Machinery or safety-equipment finding described only in narrative terms with no measured value or test result attached
- **Usually means:** the item was inspected visually only, without the instrumented test (insulation resistance, pressure test, load test) the finding actually requires to be defensible.
- **First question:** what instrumented test, if any, backs this specific finding, and was it performed or only visually assessed?
- **Data to pull:** the test/measurement log for that piece of equipment, if one exists, alongside the survey report's narrative section.

### A hull or cargo damage survey report states a cause without a documented sequence of physical evidence supporting it
- **Usually means:** the cause was inferred from the damage's appearance alone rather than traced through fracture/deformation evidence, which is exactly the gap a counterparty's own surveyor will attack in a disputed claim.
- **First question:** what specific physical evidence (fracture pattern, corrosion product, deformation direction) supports this cause over the next most likely alternative?
- **Data to pull:** the survey's photographic log and any metallurgical or laboratory analysis ordered for the damaged component.
