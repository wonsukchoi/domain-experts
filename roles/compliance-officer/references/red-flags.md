# Red flags — what a compliance officer notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one can be innocent; two or three together on the same account, unit, or exam cycle is a pattern worth opening a case over.

### Cash deposits repeatedly sized $9,000–$9,900, spread across branches or days
- **Usually means:** deliberate structuring to stay under the $10,000 CTR threshold (31 U.S.C. § 5324), sometimes coached by a third party; second most likely is coincidental round-trip cash business (a legitimate cash-intensive merchant) that needs a documented rationale on file, not just a benign guess.
- **First question:** "Pull the prior 12 months for this account — is this the first time this pattern appears, or has it been running quietly below the alert threshold longer than the system caught?"
- **Data to pull:** 12-month transaction history, branch/channel of each deposit, customer's stated business type and expected activity from onboarding, any prior SARs on record.

### Whistleblower hotline report rate under ~1% of employees annually
- **Usually means:** either an unusually clean culture (rare) or employees don't trust the hotline's confidentiality/non-retaliation protections and are staying silent — industry benchmark data (Ethics & Compliance Initiative) runs closer to 1.4–2% annually for organizations with a functioning program.
- **First question:** "When we last surveyed employees on whether they'd feel safe reporting anonymously, what did they say — and have we ever surveyed that at all?"
- **Data to pull:** hotline volume trend by year and by business unit, exit-interview mentions of unreported concerns, anonymous-vs-named report ratio, retaliation-complaint log.

### A single business unit or relationship manager producing a disproportionate share of policy exception approvals
- **Usually means:** either that unit has a genuinely atypical risk profile that policy hasn't been updated to reflect, or someone is routing around a control they find inconvenient by requesting exceptions instead of complying.
- **First question:** "Show me the approval rationale for the last 10 exceptions from this person — are the justifications distinct, or copy-pasted?"
- **Data to pull:** exception log by approver and requester, approval turnaround time (rubber-stamped approvals under 5 minutes are a tell), comparison to peer units' exception rates.

### Same finding recurring across two or more consecutive exam or audit cycles
- **Usually means:** the remediation plan fixed the symptom, not the control gap, or was never independently retested before being marked closed.
- **First question:** "Who signed off that this was fixed last time, and did anyone outside that person retest it before closure?"
- **Data to pull:** prior finding's closure memo, retest evidence (or absence of it), root-cause statement from the prior cycle vs. this one — if they're worded identically, the root cause was never actually found.
- (See `references/playbook.md` §5 for the tracker structure.)

### OFAC/sanctions screening override rate rising without a documented rationale per override
- **Usually means:** screening thresholds are too sensitive and staff are clearing false positives without proper review, or — the more dangerous read — a specific relationship manager is systematically overriding true matches to avoid losing a client relationship.
- **First question:** "Pull every override from this desk in the last quarter — is there a written rationale for each, or a pattern of blank justification fields?"
- **Data to pull:** override log with rationale field completion rate, overrides by approver, correlation between override rate and any single relationship manager or branch.

### Training completion rate at or near 100% with completion time under half the module's median
- **Usually means:** click-through completion without engagement — staff are closing the module without absorbing the content, which will surface later as a "we were trained on this" defense that doesn't hold up under an exam interview.
- **First question:** "Spot-check five people who completed it in under 10 minutes — can they answer two questions about the content without looking it up?"
- **Data to pull:** per-user completion time distribution, module quiz scores if any, exam-interview transcripts from the last cycle referencing this training topic.
- (See `references/playbook.md` §6.)

### Backlog of committed remediation items past their committed date, especially any rated MRA/MRIA-equivalent
- **Usually means:** either the remediation was under-resourced relative to its stated priority, or the committed date was set optimistically to close the exam conversation rather than reflect real capacity.
- **First question:** "Of the past-due items, which are past due because the fix is hard versus because nobody was assigned to own it?"
- **Data to pull:** finding tracker with committed vs. actual dates, owner assignment history, any re-committed (slipped) dates on the same finding.

### Compliance function's reporting line moved under, or shared with, the business unit it monitors
- **Usually means:** an organizational restructuring that looks efficient on a org chart and quietly kills the independence a regulator relies on when assessing program credibility.
- **First question:** "Who can override or delay a compliance finding before it reaches the board or audit committee under the new structure?"
- **Data to pull:** current and prior org chart, escalation policy language before and after the change, any findings that were reworded or downgraded in the period following the reporting-line change.

### Gift/entertainment or government-contact log missing or unmaintained for a unit with regulator or foreign-official contact
- **Usually means:** either the log genuinely wasn't needed (no contact occurred, verify this) or FCPA/anti-bribery exposure exists with no contemporaneous record to defend it if questioned later.
- **First question:** "Has this unit had any contact with a government official or regulator in the period the log is missing, per calendars or expense reports?"
- **Data to pull:** expense reports cross-referenced against travel/calendar data for the unit, prior-period log entries for comparison, any related third-party/agent engagements in the same geography.

### A department's first-pass SAR/mandatory-filing draft consistently arrives with fewer than 5 days left before the statutory deadline
- **Usually means:** the case-intake-to-investigation handoff is too slow structurally, not that this particular case was unusually complex — recurring near-miss timing is a process problem, not a case problem.
- **First question:** "Pull the last 6 filings — is day-20 status (see workflow in playbook.md §3) consistently behind schedule, or was this one case an outlier?"
- **Data to pull:** filing workflow timestamps for the last two quarters, case analyst caseload at time of each near-miss, any staffing gap during the period.
