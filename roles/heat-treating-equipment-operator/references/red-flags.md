# Red flags

### Carburizing/case-hardening time scaled linearly with a target case depth change rather than via the diffusion relationship
- **Usually means:** the resulting case depth will fall meaningfully short of target.
- **First question:** what diffusion relationship and constant has actually been verified for this furnace/alloy/temperature combination?
- **Data to pull:** calculated time versus target depth using the verified diffusion relationship, versus the time actually used.

### Quenchant/agitation selected by default habit rather than matched to the specific alloy's documented hardenability requirement
- **Usually means:** either insufficient cooling rate to achieve target hardness, or excessive cooling rate risking cracking/distortion for this specific alloy.
- **First question:** what does the alloy's documented hardenability/quench requirement actually call for versus what's being used?
- **Data to pull:** quenchant/agitation used compared against the alloy's documented hardenability/quench requirement.

### A hardened part released without a documented tempering step, or with tempering under-specified relative to toughness requirements
- **Usually means:** the part may be too brittle for its intended service use despite having achieved target hardness.
- **First question:** what tempering parameters were used versus what the application's target hardness/toughness balance requires?
- **Data to pull:** tempering parameters used (temperature, time) compared against the target hardness/toughness balance required for this application.

### A heat-treated part released based on the process recipe having been followed rather than actual hardness/case-depth testing
- **Usually means:** furnace atmosphere variation, quench delay, or part geometry effects that produced an out-of-spec result could go undetected despite correct nominal parameters.
- **First question:** what does the actual hardness/case-depth test result show for this specific part or batch?
- **Data to pull:** actual hardness/case-depth test result compared against specification, for this specific part/batch.

### An asymmetric or mixed-thickness part heat treated without a distortion-control fixturing/orientation plan
- **Usually means:** avoidable warping discovered only after the part comes out of quench, rather than anticipated and controlled for in process planning.
- **First question:** what fixturing/orientation plan was actually used for this part's geometry?
- **Data to pull:** part geometry/thickness variation compared against the fixturing/quench method plan used.

### Quench delay not tracked or controlled
- **Usually means:** inconsistent quench start temperature, risking partial transformation before quenching begins and an inconsistent resulting microstructure or hardness.
- **First question:** what was the actual quench delay for this part versus the process's maximum allowable delay for this alloy?
- **Data to pull:** actual quench delay time compared against the process's maximum allowable delay for this alloy.

### A recurring hardness or case-depth out-of-spec pattern for a specific furnace/alloy combination appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — furnace calibration, quenchant condition, fixturing — rather than isolated one-off part variation.
- **First question:** does the out-of-spec occurrence log correlate with a specific process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** out-of-spec occurrence log compared against process parameter logs (furnace temp calibration, quenchant condition/age, fixturing method) over the same period.
