# Dietitian / Nutritionist — Playbook

## Caloric-needs calculation (Mifflin-St Jeor)

**Men:** BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) − (5 × age yrs) + 5
**Women:** BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) − (5 × age yrs) − 5

Total need = BMR × activity/stress factor.

| Stress/activity state | Factor |
|---|---|
| Confined to bed, minimal stress | 1.0-1.1 |
| Ambulatory, minor illness | 1.2-1.3 |
| Post-surgical recovery | 1.2-1.4 |
| Sepsis / major trauma | 1.4-1.6 |
| Severe burns | 1.6-2.0 |

Use **actual body weight** unless BMI ≥30, in which case use **adjusted body weight** = ideal body weight + 0.25 × (actual − ideal), to avoid overestimating needs from excess adipose tissue.

## Refeeding-syndrome risk stratification (NICE/ASPEN criteria)

**High risk — any ONE of:**
- BMI <16 kg/m²
- Unintentional weight loss >15% in 3-6 months
- Little/no nutritional intake for >10 days
- Low potassium, phosphate, or magnesium before feeding

**High risk — any TWO of:**
- BMI <18.5 kg/m²
- Unintentional weight loss >10% in 3-6 months
- Little/no nutritional intake for >5 days
- History of alcohol misuse or drugs including insulin, chemotherapy, antacids, diuretics

If high-risk: start at **10 kcal/kg/day**, advance over 3-5 days toward goal, checking electrolytes daily for the first 3-4 days, thiamine supplementation before/with feed initiation.
If extreme risk (BMI <14 or negligible intake >15 days): start at **5 kcal/kg/day**.
If not at risk: may start at or near goal rate, still monitoring first-week labs.

## Feeding-route decision sequence

1. Can the patient meet needs orally, with or without oral nutrition supplements? → Oral route, reassess intake at 3-5 days.
2. If oral is inadequate or unsafe (aspiration risk, altered consciousness) and the GI tract is functional → Enteral (tube feeding): NG/OG for short-term (<4-6 weeks), PEG/PEJ for longer-term.
3. If the GI tract is nonfunctional (bowel obstruction, ischemia, severe ileus, high-output fistula) or enteral access can't be safely obtained → Parenteral (IV) nutrition, central line preferred for full TPN, peripheral for short-term/lower-osmolality support.
4. Re-evaluate route at each reassessment — a patient started on parenteral nutrition should be transitioned to enteral or oral as soon as the gut becomes usable.

## Worked calculation: renal (dialysis) patient macronutrient targets

62 kg patient on hemodialysis 3x/week.

| Nutrient | Target | Calculation |
|---|---|---|
| Protein | 1.2 g/kg/day (higher than general population to offset dialysate losses) | 62 × 1.2 = 74.4 g/day |
| Potassium | 2,000-3,000 mg/day restricted | Individualized to labs |
| Phosphorus | 800-1,000 mg/day restricted | Individualized to labs, binder-dependent |
| Fluid | 1,000 mL + urine output/day | Individualized to residual renal function |
| Calories | 30-35 kcal/kg/day (not restricted like protein) | 62 × 32.5 = 2,015 kcal/day |

Note the counterintuitive pairing: protein is *increased* relative to a non-dialysis renal-restriction diet (which would restrict protein), because dialysis itself removes amino acids/protein — a common generalist error is applying pre-dialysis CKD protein restriction to a dialysis patient.
