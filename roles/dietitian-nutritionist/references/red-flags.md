# Dietitian / Nutritionist — Red Flags

### Intake <5 days (or <10 days for high-risk populations) before nutrition support starts
- **Usually means:** refeeding-syndrome risk is present or approaching; starting at goal calorie rate risks precipitating a phosphate/potassium/magnesium crash.
- **First question:** does this patient meet any NICE/ASPEN high-risk criterion (BMI, weight-loss %, intake duration, pre-existing electrolyte depletion)?
- **Data to pull:** documented oral-intake history, weight trend over 3-6 months, baseline electrolyte panel.

### BMI <16 kg/m² or unintentional weight loss >15% in 3-6 months
- **Usually means:** single-criterion high refeeding-syndrome risk — start at 10 kcal/kg/day regardless of other factors.
- **First question:** has the initial calorie order already been written at a full-goal rate?
- **Data to pull:** current and pre-illness weight, BMI calculation, feeding order as written.

### Serum phosphate drops >0.5 mg/dL within 24-72 hours of starting nutrition support
- **Usually means:** refeeding syndrome is beginning even if the patient was screened as lower-risk — screening tools aren't perfectly sensitive.
- **First question:** is potassium and magnesium also trending down (confirms a refeeding pattern rather than an unrelated lab fluctuation)?
- **Data to pull:** serial electrolyte panel, current feeding rate, time since feed initiation.

### Gastric residual volume >500 mL or new abdominal distension on tube feeds
- **Usually means:** feeding intolerance — the rate or formula may need adjustment, or a mechanical/motility issue (ileus, obstruction) needs ruling out.
- **First question:** is this a rate-related intolerance (reduce rate/hold and reassess) or a mechanical issue (imaging, physician evaluation)?
- **Data to pull:** residual volume trend, distension/pain exam findings, bowel-sound status.

### Prealbumin or albumin trending down despite an "on-target" calorie count
- **Usually means:** either the calculated need is underestimating actual requirement (unaccounted stress/injury factor), inadequate protein specifically (not just calories), or an inflammatory state is suppressing visceral protein synthesis independent of nutrition adequacy.
- **First question:** is CRP also elevated (suggests inflammation confounds the marker, not a pure nutrition-adequacy signal)?
- **Data to pull:** protein intake vs. target, CRP/inflammatory markers, calorie-count accuracy (actual delivered vs. ordered).

### MST/MUST screening positive but nutrition-focused physical exam looks unremarkable
- **Usually means:** early-stage risk not yet visible on exam, or a screening false-positive from an unrelated factor (e.g. recent GI illness causing transient reduced intake).
- **First question:** what's the trajectory — is intake recovering on its own, or continuing to decline?
- **Data to pull:** screening-tool component scores, recent weight trend, documented intake over the past week.

### Enteral tube-feeding order doesn't specify an advancement schedule or hold parameters
- **Usually means:** the order was written without refeeding-risk stratification, or the risk assessment wasn't communicated to the ordering team.
- **First question:** was a validated malnutrition/refeeding-risk screen completed before the order was written?
- **Data to pull:** admission nutrition screen (if any), current order, patient's intake/weight history.

### Renal or hepatic patient placed on standard (non-disease-specific) enteral formula
- **Usually means:** a general formula was selected without checking electrolyte/protein/fluid-restriction needs specific to the diagnosis.
- **First question:** does the current formula's electrolyte/protein profile match this patient's lab-driven restrictions?
- **Data to pull:** formula nutrition facts (K/phos/protein/fluid content per volume), most recent relevant labs.

### Parenteral nutrition ordered without a documented reason the enteral route was ruled out
- **Usually means:** enteral access wasn't attempted, or the "if the gut works, use it" principle was skipped for convenience/speed.
- **First question:** is there a genuine contraindication (obstruction, ischemia, high-output fistula, hemodynamic instability) or just a workflow shortcut?
- **Data to pull:** GI function/imaging findings, documented reason for parenteral route, enteral-access attempt notes if any.
