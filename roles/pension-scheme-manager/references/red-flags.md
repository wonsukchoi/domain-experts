# Red flags

### Funded status swings >5 percentage points quarter-over-quarter, driven primarily by discount rate change
- **Usually means:** the move is largely discount-rate noise rather than a genuine change in asset performance or liability structure, and reacting to it as if it were a fundamental shift risks a strategy change based on a temporary market condition.
- **First question:** "how much of this move is discount rate versus actual asset return — have we decomposed it?"
- **Data to pull:** funded-status attribution report splitting discount rate impact from asset return impact for the period.

### Plan duration mismatch between liabilities and the matching-bond allocation exceeds 2–3 years with no stated rationale
- **Usually means:** the bond sleeve was built for yield or convenience rather than deliberately matched to liability duration, leaving unhedged interest rate risk nobody explicitly chose to carry.
- **First question:** "was this duration gap a deliberate risk decision, or did it happen by default?"
- **Data to pull:** current liability duration versus bond sleeve duration, and the investment policy statement's stated rationale (or absence of one).

### Sponsor credit rating downgrade with no corresponding review of the plan's de-risking pace
- **Usually means:** plan strategy and sponsor financial health are being managed as separate workstreams instead of the linked risk they actually are.
- **First question:** "when the sponsor was downgraded, did we revisit the plan's risk allocation, and if not, why not?"
- **Data to pull:** sponsor credit rating history alongside the plan's allocation change history, checked for correlation or its absence.

### An external manager consistently beats its benchmark on absolute return while surplus volatility increases
- **Usually means:** the manager is taking on risk that isn't compensated in liability-relative terms, even though it looks good on a standard performance report.
- **First question:** "is this manager's outperformance coming with a funded-status cost we're not measuring?"
- **Data to pull:** manager performance against both its stated benchmark and a liability-relative surplus volatility metric.

### Regulatory underfunding premium is growing faster than the plan's asset base, with no contribution acceleration considered
- **Usually means:** the compounding cost of staying underfunded hasn't been modeled against the cost of an accelerated contribution, so the plan is paying an avoidable penalty by default.
- **First question:** "what's the projected premium cost over the next 3 years if we stay at current funding, versus the cost of closing the gap now?"
- **Data to pull:** the variable-rate premium trajectory under current funding versus an accelerated-contribution scenario.

### Mortality or longevity assumptions haven't been updated in longer than the standard actuarial review cycle
- **Usually means:** the plan's liability estimate is drifting away from actual participant experience, typically understating liabilities as longevity improves and assumptions lag.
- **First question:** "when were mortality assumptions last checked against our actual plan experience?"
- **Data to pull:** the actuarial valuation's assumption update history and current experience study results if one exists.

### Trustee committee proposes a major allocation shift based on a single quarter's funded-status move
- **Usually means:** short-term results are being treated as a trend rather than noise, given how long-duration the underlying liability actually is.
- **First question:** "if we look at the last 8 quarters instead of just this one, does the same conclusion hold?"
- **Data to pull:** funded-status trend over at least 2 years, not just the most recent measurement.

### DC plan default investment option is significantly mismatched to participant demographic profile, with low opt-out engagement
- **Usually means:** the default was set once and never revisited as the participant population aged or changed, and because most participants never touch the default, the mismatch persists silently.
- **First question:** "when was the default option last reviewed against current participant age/risk profile, and what's our opt-out rate telling us?"
- **Data to pull:** default fund glide path or allocation versus participant age distribution, plus opt-out rate by cohort.

### Contribution holidays taken during strong markets without stress-testing the downside funded-status scenario
- **Usually means:** the decision to skip contributions was made against the current favorable funded status without checking how quickly that cushion could disappear.
- **First question:** "what does funded status look like under a 20% equity drawdown plus a 100bp rate decline, given we're skipping this contribution?"
- **Data to pull:** a combined downside stress scenario applied to current funded status with and without the skipped contribution.

### Investment policy statement hasn't been reviewed despite a material change in sponsor financial condition
- **Usually means:** governance documents are on a calendar-based review cycle that isn't responsive to real events that should trigger an earlier look.
- **First question:** "what's changed about the sponsor since the IPS was last reviewed, and does the current policy still reflect the right risk tolerance?"
- **Data to pull:** the IPS's last review date compared against the timeline of any sponsor credit or financial events since.
