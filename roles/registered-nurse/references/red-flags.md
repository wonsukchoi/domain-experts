# Red flags

### NEWS2 aggregate ≥7 (or MEWS ≥5)
- **Usually means:** Acute decompensation already underway — sepsis, PE, hemorrhage, or a cardiac event — not an early warning anymore but a current one.
- **First question:** What specifically changed in the last hour, and how fast?
- **Data to pull:** Full vital-sign trend over the preceding 4 hours, last labs, and cumulative I&O.

### Any single NEWS2 parameter scoring 3 (RR ≤8 or ≥25, SpO2 ≤91% RA, SBP ≤90, HR ≤40 or ≥131, new confusion)
- **Usually means:** A single organ system is failing even if the aggregate score looks tolerable, because other parameters are still normal and diluting the total.
- **First question:** Does this one parameter alone meet urgent-review criteria, independent of the aggregate?
- **Data to pull:** That parameter's raw trend over the shift, not just the current score.

### Urine output <0.5 mL/kg/hr sustained over 2 consecutive hours
- **Usually means:** Early hypoperfusion or acute kidney injury, frequently preceding a measurable blood-pressure drop by an hour or more.
- **First question:** What is the cumulative fluid balance over the last 8 hours?
- **Data to pull:** I&O flow sheet, most recent creatinine, current weight vs. admission weight.

### New-onset confusion or altered level of consciousness in a previously oriented patient
- **Usually means:** Hypoxia, hypoglycemia, sepsis, stroke, or an opioid/benzodiazepine effect.
- **First question:** What is the finger-stick glucose and oxygen saturation right now?
- **Data to pull:** Last glucose reading, last narcotic/sedative dose and time, pupil and grip check.

### New calf pain/swelling or acute dyspnea/tachycardia within 72h of major orthopedic or abdominal surgery
- **Usually means:** DVT progressing to pulmonary embolism.
- **First question:** Is the patient still receiving VTE prophylaxis on schedule, and was a dose missed or delayed?
- **Data to pull:** Anticoagulant MAR, SpO2 trend, status of any ordered CT or D-dimer.

### Temp ≥38.3°C together with HR >90 and RR >20, trending together rather than isolated
- **Usually means:** Early sepsis.
- **First question:** What is the likely source — line, lung, urine, or wound?
- **Data to pull:** Lactate order status, blood cultures drawn, last WBC.

### Pain not improved 45–60 minutes after a full analgesic dose
- **Usually means:** Undertreated pain, but can also mask a new surgical complication (compartment syndrome, anastomotic leak) presenting as "just pain."
- **First question:** Is the pain the same location and quality as before, or has it changed?
- **Data to pull:** Pain location/quality trend, and for orthopedic patients, the last neurovascular check.

### A delegated vital sign or glucose reported "normal" by a tech, while the patient looks clinically worse
- **Usually means:** Measurement error, wrong patient or site, or a genuinely evolving process the delegate isn't trained to recognize.
- **First question:** Did I verify this myself before acting on it?
- **Data to pull:** Repeat the measurement personally before documenting or escalating.

### More than 2 of a 5–6 patient assignment requiring off-schedule reassessment within the same hour
- **Usually means:** The assignment is unsafe for current acuity — this is a staffing signal, not a coincidence of a bad hour.
- **First question:** Does the charge nurse need to rebalance the assignment or send help now?
- **Data to pull:** Current acuity levels against the unit's staffing grid.

### A scheduled high-alert medication (anticoagulant, insulin, IV antibiotic, potassium) running more than 30 minutes late
- **Usually means:** Task overload, with "right time" the right silently sacrificed first.
- **First question:** Is this specific delay clinically consequential for this specific drug?
- **Data to pull:** MAR late-dose reason code, time of last dose given.

### Family or a visitor reporting "something is different" about a patient who charts as unchanged
- **Usually means:** Subtle deterioration cues that often precede a measurable vital-sign change.
- **First question:** What specifically looks different to you right now?
- **Data to pull:** A full reassessment now, not deferred to the next scheduled round.

### Oxygen requirement escalating within one shift to maintain the same SpO2 (e.g., room air → 2L → 4L)
- **Usually means:** A worsening pulmonary process, not that the "right flow rate" was simply found.
- **First question:** What is driving the increased oxygen demand?
- **Data to pull:** Status of any ordered ABG or repeat chest X-ray, and direct observation of work of breathing.
