# Securities Sales Agent — Red Flags

### Account switched between similar products more than twice in 12 months
- **Usually means:** churning to generate repeat commissions, or a legitimately evolving client objective that hasn't been documented — the pattern alone doesn't distinguish the two.
- **First question:** "What specifically changed in the client's objective or circumstances between each switch?"
- **Data to pull:** transaction history, dated suitability files for each switch, commission earned per transaction.

### Recommended share class fails the client's stated holding-period breakeven test
- **Usually means:** the comparison was run at a generic horizon instead of the client's actual stated timeline, or the recommendation followed the compensation rather than the cost math.
- **First question:** "At the client's actual stated horizon, which share class is lower-cost?"
- **Data to pull:** the share-class cost comparator output and the client's documented time horizon.

### Annuity recommended without a rider-cost breakdown in the file
- **Usually means:** the guarantee was sold as a bundled "good deal" without pricing what it specifically insures against.
- **First question:** "What is the total annual cost of every rider, and what specific risk does each one cover?"
- **Data to pull:** the annuity's prospectus fee table, itemized by base contract and each rider.

### New account concentration >25% in a single product family with no documented diversification rationale
- **Usually means:** either a legitimate concentrated conviction (rare, and should be documented as such) or a lack of a real allocation process.
- **First question:** "What is the specific reason this client's allocation departs from the firm's model?"
- **Data to pull:** the client's risk-tolerance questionnaire and the firm's model allocation for a comparable client profile.

### Recommendation compensation and client-cost direction diverge with no documented resolution
- **Usually means:** a Reg BI Care Obligation gap — disclosure alone was treated as sufficient.
- **First question:** "Does the file explicitly document why the higher-paying option was (or wasn't) also the lower-cost option, and how that conflict was resolved?"
- **Data to pull:** the compensation schedule for both the recommended and alternative products, side by side.

### Close rate on qualified meetings below 15% for two consecutive quarters
- **Usually means:** either a qualification-screen problem (unqualified prospects reaching first meetings) or a pitch/product-fit problem — the funnel data usually distinguishes which.
- **First question:** "Is the drop happening at qualification, first meeting, or close?"
- **Data to pull:** the funnel tracker broken out by stage and source.

### B-share (CDSC) product recommended to a client with a liquidity need inside the surrender schedule
- **Usually means:** the liquidity check was skipped, or the CDSC schedule wasn't checked against the client's stated timeline.
- **First question:** "Does the client have any documented need for these funds before the CDSC schedule expires?"
- **Data to pull:** the CDSC schedule and the client's stated liquidity needs from intake.

### Suitability file completed after the transaction date rather than before or concurrent
- **Usually means:** the file was reconstructed defensively rather than used as the actual decision process — a strong signal in any regulatory review.
- **First question:** "What is the file's timestamp relative to the transaction date?"
- **Data to pull:** file creation/modification metadata against the trade confirmation date.

### Client's stated risk tolerance and the product's risk profile diverge by more than one tier
- **Usually means:** either the risk-tolerance intake was stale, or the recommendation didn't match the documented profile.
- **First question:** "When was the risk-tolerance questionnaire last updated, and does this recommendation match it?"
- **Data to pull:** the dated risk-tolerance questionnaire and the product's risk classification.
