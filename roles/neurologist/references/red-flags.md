### Thunderclap headache reaching maximal intensity in under 1 minute
- **Usually means:** subarachnoid hemorrhage until proven otherwise; second most likely: reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome.
- **First question:** what was the exact time from first sensation to peak intensity?
- **Data to pull:** non-contrast CT head within 6 hours of onset; if onset was >6 hours ago or CT is negative, LP with opening pressure and xanthochromia.

### NIHSS improving by more than 4 points between triage and treatment decision
- **Usually means:** a transient or rapidly resolving deficit (possible TIA); second: a genuine stroke with early spontaneous reperfusion.
- **First question:** is the deficit, right now, still disabling to this specific patient's function?
- **Data to pull:** a repeat NIHSS at the bedside immediately before the treatment decision, not the triage score.

### Bilateral, symmetric, ascending weakness with areflexia developing over days
- **Usually means:** Guillain-Barré syndrome / acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy; second: another acute inflammatory neuropathy variant.
- **First question:** any recent GI or respiratory illness, and is there any respiratory muscle involvement right now?
- **Data to pull:** bedside FVC/NIF, LP for albuminocytologic dissociation, nerve conduction studies.

### New focal deficit plus fever in a patient who is immunosuppressed or post-neurosurgical
- **Usually means:** brain abscess or other CNS infection; second: subdural empyema.
- **First question:** any recent sinusitis, dental infection, or IV drug use?
- **Data to pull:** contrast-enhanced MRI brain, blood cultures; LP only after imaging has excluded a mass lesion with shift.

### Progressive gait disturbance, cognitive decline, and urinary incontinence over months
- **Usually means:** normal pressure hydrocephalus; second: mixed vascular/neurodegenerative dementia.
- **First question:** which of the three came first, and is the classic triad actually present, not just cognitive decline alone?
- **Data to pull:** MRI brain with ventricular size (Evans' index), large-volume LP with pre/post gait timing.

### Sudden severe unilateral weakness that fully resolves within an hour
- **Usually means:** TIA; second: hypoglycemia or migraine-with-aura mimic.
- **First question:** what was the fingerstick glucose at symptom onset?
- **Data to pull:** ABCD2/ABCD3-I score (for documentation, not triage gating), carotid vascular imaging, echocardiogram, glucose.

### First seizure in a patient over 60 with no prior seizure history
- **Usually means:** a structural lesion (tumor, chronic stroke, subdural); second: a metabolic derangement.
- **First question:** did any witness describe focal onset features before generalization?
- **Data to pull:** contrast-enhanced MRI brain, metabolic panel, EEG.

### Diplopia and ptosis that worsen with sustained gaze or fatigue through the day
- **Usually means:** myasthenia gravis; second: a partial third-nerve palsy.
- **First question:** does it fluctuate through the day or improve with rest?
- **Data to pull:** AChR/MuSK antibodies, ice-pack test, repetitive nerve stimulation or single-fiber EMG.

### A positive functional sign (e.g., Hoover's sign) found alongside an objective sensory level
- **Usually means:** coexisting functional overlay *and* organic pathology; second: purely organic disease with an inconsistent exam under stress or pain.
- **First question:** has the organic workup this exam finding would normally trigger actually been completed?
- **Data to pull:** MRI of the full spine at the level suggested by the sensory level — do not stop at the functional finding.

### Status epilepticus not terminated after two appropriately dosed benzodiazepine doses
- **Usually means:** need to move immediately to the second-line IV agent per the timed ladder; second: psychogenic non-epileptic seizure mimicking refractory status.
- **First question:** what time did the seizure activity actually begin (the 5-minute rule), not what time did the patient arrive?
- **Data to pull:** continuous EEG, second-line agent selection per comorbidity (avoid valproate in hepatic disease/pregnancy).

### Painless vision loss with optic neuritis in a patient over 50
- **Usually means:** not a typical MS-associated optic neuritis — consider NMOSD or ischemic optic neuropathy instead.
- **First question:** is there pain with eye movement, and is a relative afferent pupillary defect present?
- **Data to pull:** MRI orbits with contrast, aquaporin-4 and MOG antibody testing.

### Foot drop with normal reflexes and sensory loss confined to the dorsum of the foot
- **Usually means:** peroneal neuropathy at the fibular head; second: an L5 radiculopathy.
- **First question:** any recent prolonged leg-crossing, kneeling, or rapid weight loss?
- **Data to pull:** EMG/NCS distinguishing a focal peroneal conduction block from an L5 root distribution.
