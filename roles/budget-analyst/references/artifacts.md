# Budget analyst artifacts — templates with real structure

Working templates an agent can fill in. Example figures throughout continue the City of ~120,000, Parks & Recreation FY27 scenario from `SKILL.md`'s worked example, general fund basis.

## 1. Decision package (new/changed program request)

One package per new program, position group, or material program change — never bundled with baseline continuation. Ranked in priority order against every other unit's packages (zero-based budgeting convention: rank 1-of-N, not just submit-and-hope).

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Package title | New Community Center — Operations (Year 1, partial year) |
| Requesting unit | Parks & Recreation |
| Priority rank (dept's own ranking, 1 = highest) | 1 of 4 packages submitted |
| Type | New program (facility opening) |
| Confirmed start date, source | Jan 1, 2027 — per Public Works construction schedule dated Sep 2026 (not the department's internal target date) |
| Requested cost, full-year basis | $492,000 ($312,000 personnel / $180,000 non-personnel) |
| Phased cost, actual basis | $324,000 ($234,000 personnel from Oct 1 hire date / $90,000 occupancy costs from Jan 1) |
| FTEs requested | 4.0 (2x Recreation Coordinator @ $71,000 loaded, 2x Facility Attendant @ $85,000 loaded — avg $78,000) |
| Workload/volume driver | Projected 450 memberships Year 1 (comparable center opened FY24 hit 380 Year 1, 520 Year 2) |
| Consequence if not funded | Building sits unstaffed at opening; no service-level alternative — this package is not a discretionary tier |
| Analyst finding | Approve, phased from confirmed dates (see SKILL.md worked example step 4) |

## 2. Budget-to-actual variance report

Run quarterly at minimum; year-end column added after close. All figures in $000s. Variance % = (Actual - Budget) / Budget; negative = favorable (under) for expenditure lines.

| Line | FY26 Adopted | FY26 Actual | Variance $ | Variance % | Flag | Driver |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Personnel — Recreation | 1,850 | 1,842 | (8) | -0.4% | — | Normal |
| Personnel — Maintenance | 1,120 | 1,048 | (72) | -6.4% | Review | Position #4471 vacant 7.5 of 12 mo. — 2nd year running (structural) |
| Contracted services | 210 | 224 | +14 | +6.7% | — | On-call landscaping overage, one quarter — timing |
| Utilities | 285 | 279 | (6) | -2.1% | — | Normal |
| Supplies & materials | 95 | 88 | (7) | -7.4% | Review | Delayed equipment order, will hit Q1 FY27 — timing, not structural |
| Capital outlay (minor equipment) | 40 | 29 | (11) | -27.5% | Review | Vendor backorder — will carry to FY27, not a true saving |
| **Total** | **3,600** | **3,510** | **(90)** | **-2.5%** | | |

**Review threshold applied:** any line beyond ±10% of budget, or beyond ±5% with no obvious one-time cause, gets a "Review" flag and requires a one-paragraph driver memo from the unit before the next quarter's report is finalized. Two consecutive Review flags on the same line with a "structural" driver triggers a baseline true-up recommendation the following budget cycle (see Personnel — Maintenance above, feeding directly into the SKILL.md worked example).

## 3. Position control roster (excerpt)

Tracks every authorized position against actual fill status — the source document for catching a chronically vacant line before it's rolled forward into next year's baseline unexamined.

| Position # | Title | Authorized FTE | Status | Months vacant, FY25 | Months vacant, FY26 | Loaded annual cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4468 | Recreation Supervisor | 1.0 | Filled | 0 | 0 | $91,000 |
| 4471 | Maintenance Tech II | 1.0 | Vacant | 8 | 7.5 | $72,000 |
| 4475 | Recreation Coordinator | 1.0 | Filled | 1 (backfill gap) | 0 | $71,000 |
| 4480 | Facility Attendant (part-time) | 0.5 | Filled | 0 | 0 | $38,000 |

**Rule applied:** any full-time position vacant ≥ 6 months in each of the last two fiscal years is flagged for the analyst's next-cycle recommendation — delete, downgrade, or convert to contract — rather than automatically re-budgeted at full cost (this is position #4471 in the SKILL.md worked example).

## 4. Annual budget calendar (fiscal year July 1 - June 30)

| When | What | Owner | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Oct (9 mo. before FY start) | Budget office issues call letter: target growth ceiling, COLA %, decision-package instructions | Budget Office | Call letter + templates |
| Nov | Department submissions due: baseline continuation + ranked decision packages | Departments | Submitted requests |
| Dec | Analyst review round: variance analysis, phase-in checks, baseline true-ups | Budget Analyst | Analyst recommendation memos (one per department) |
| Jan | Executive/City Manager balancing session — reconcile total requests to projected revenue | Budget Office + Exec | Balanced proposed budget |
| Feb | Proposed budget published; public review period opens | Budget Office | Published proposed budget document |
| Mar | Council/board budget work sessions, department by department | Council + Departments | Council-directed adjustments |
| Apr-May | Public hearing(s); formal adoption | Council | Adopted budget (legally binding appropriation) |
| Jun | Load adopted budget into financial system, effective July 1 | Finance/IT | Budget of record |
| Oct, Jan, Apr (quarterly, in-year) | Quarterly variance reports and reforecast; any amendment requiring a category shift goes back to Council | Budget Analyst | Variance report + amendment requests as needed |

**Process rule:** any in-year spending shift across appropriation categories (e.g., personnel to capital) requires a formal budget amendment adopted by the same body that adopted the original budget — an analyst-level memo can recommend it but cannot authorize it.
