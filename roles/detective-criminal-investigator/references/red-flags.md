# Detective / Criminal Investigator — Red Flags

### Confession contains no detail only the true perpetrator would know
- **Usually means:** interrogator inadvertently or deliberately disclosed case facts before or during the interview (contamination), or the confession is coerced/false.
- **First question:** what specific, non-public detail did the suspect provide unprompted, and can it be independently verified?
- **Data to pull:** full unedited interrogation recording; log of what case information was disclosed to the suspect before and during the interview.

### Two independent timestamp sources disagree by more than a few minutes, uncorrected
- **Usually means:** one or both clocks (DVR, device, system log) are unsynchronized or drifted from real time.
- **First question:** has either timestamp been checked against a network-synced or GPS reference?
- **Data to pull:** clock-drift verification against a third independent reference (POS system, carrier records, GPS-tagged event).

### Case theory closed after a single circumstantial lead with no documented disproof attempt
- **Usually means:** tunnel vision / confirmation bias.
- **First question:** what alternate suspects or explanations were considered, and how were they ruled out?
- **Data to pull:** case file's investigative-steps log and alternate-suspect notes.

### Eyewitness identification administered by someone who knew who the suspect was
- **Usually means:** risk of unconscious cueing inflating the witness's identification confidence.
- **First question:** was the lineup double-blind — did the administrator not know which position held the suspect?
- **Data to pull:** lineup administration log and protocol used.

### Probable-cause affidavit omits a fact known to the affiant that cuts against guilt
- **Usually means:** exposure to a *Franks v. Delaware* challenge — the warrant could be invalidated for material omission, not just false statement.
- **First question:** what did the affiant know that isn't in the affidavit, and why was it left out?
- **Data to pull:** full case file cross-referenced line-by-line against the affidavit's content.

### CSLI cited as placing a suspect at a specific address rather than a coverage area
- **Usually means:** overstating the precision of historical cell-site location information — a tower/sector typically covers a radius of fractions of a mile to several miles.
- **First question:** what is the documented coverage radius for the cited tower/sector at the relevant time?
- **Data to pull:** carrier's coverage-map or propagation data for that tower and time window.

### Witness statement materially changes between the initial report and the formal interview
- **Usually means:** memory contamination from media exposure, conversation with other witnesses, or leading questions — or the initial statement was incomplete under acute stress.
- **First question:** what happened between the two statements — media coverage seen, conversations with other witnesses, prior questioning?
- **Data to pull:** timeline of the witness's contact and information exposure between the two statements.

### Alibi timing gap treated as "tight but possible" without a distance/speed/time calculation
- **Usually means:** the alibi's physical feasibility hasn't actually been tested — it's an impression, not a finding.
- **First question:** has the required average speed for the claimed route and elapsed time actually been computed?
- **Data to pull:** mapped route distance, corrected (drift-verified) timestamps, plausible speed range for that route and mode of travel.

### Interrogation used minimization/maximization tactics on a juvenile or low-IQ suspect with no appropriate adult or counsel present
- **Usually means:** elevated false-confession risk given the interviewee's vulnerability.
- **First question:** was an appropriate adult, guardian, or counsel present, and is the full interview recorded?
- **Data to pull:** full interrogation recording; documentation of the suspect's age and any known vulnerability.

### Physical evidence appears in the case's evidence log days after the date it was allegedly collected
- **Usually means:** an undocumented custody gap, or genuinely late-discovered evidence requiring an explanation on the record.
- **First question:** when and by whom was this item actually collected, versus when it was logged?
- **Data to pull:** original scene photos/notes and evidence-log timestamps for the item in question.
