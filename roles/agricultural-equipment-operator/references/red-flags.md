# Red flags

Smell tests a veteran operator catches early in a season or a single field pass. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before changing anything.

### Drop-pan loss reading above ~2 bu/acre (soybeans or corn) over pre-harvest baseline

**Usually means:** ground speed is past the header's effective capacity for current yield/residue, or reel index/header height is set for a different field's conditions. Less often, concave clearance or rotor speed is causing separator loss rather than header loss.

**First question:** "Is this header loss or separator loss — did we pan-test behind the header alone, or behind the whole machine?"

**Data to pull:** header-only vs. whole-machine drop-pan readings side by side; current ground speed and reel index ratio; yield-monitor bushels/acre for that pass (higher yield raises the material load the machine has to process at the same speed).

### Planter spacing CV drifting above ~10–12% mid-field with no monitor alarm

**Usually means:** downforce isn't tracking a soil-texture or residue change within the field, or vacuum/disk speed was set for the field entrance and hasn't been rechecked. Monitors flag skips and doubles more reliably than CV drift, so this can go unnoticed without an active check.

**First question:** "Pull the last 10 acres of spacing CV off the monitor log — is it trending, or was it always this high?"

**Data to pull:** spacing CV by GPS zone for the field; soil type map overlay; downforce log for the same zones; seed lot size/shape spec (a seed-size change mid-bag is a common, overlooked cause).

### Blended acres/day is on pace, but the field-capacity worksheet says it shouldn't be

**Usually means:** either field efficiency is running higher than assumed (worth banking as real, not luck) or the operator is trading loss/quality for speed to hit an informal daily-acres target. These look identical from the acres/day number alone.

**First question:** "What's the drop-pan or singulation reading from today's pace, not just the acres logged?"

**Data to pull:** today's loss/singulation check against the season's running average; actual ground speed vs. the speed used in the capacity worksheet; whether field efficiency assumptions have been updated from early-season data.

### Harvest order set by "whichever field is closest" rather than a risk table

**Usually means:** no one has run the sequencing-by-risk exercise, so scheduling is defaulting to travel convenience while a higher-shatter or higher-lodging field waits unnecessarily.

**First question:** "Which field in the rotation has the highest days-past-maturity or worst stalk/pod condition right now?"

**Data to pull:** days-past-maturity by field; latest scouting notes on stalk quality or pod condition; any elevator/contract delivery deadlines that should also weight the order.

### A field-capacity shortfall against the forecast is being closed by adding hours, not capacity or sequencing

**Usually means:** the plan is being patched with fatigue rather than a real fix — extended hours help marginally but rarely close a double-digit-day gap, and operator fatigue itself becomes a safety and loss-rate risk past a certain point.

**First question:** "How many field-days does the current plan actually need versus how many the forecast gives us — and is a second machine or a priority cut on the table?"

**Data to pull:** field-capacity worksheet totals vs. the current extended-hours forecast; operator hours logged over the last week; loss/quality trend over the same period (fatigue often shows up there before it shows up as an incident).

### PTO shield missing, removed, or propped open "for now"

**Usually means:** a shield was pulled for a repair or adjustment and not replaced, or was judged to interfere with a specific job. This is one of the highest-severity, fastest-onset hazards in the operation and has no safe interim state.

**First question:** "Why is this shield off right now, and what's stopping us from replacing it before the next start-up?"

**Data to pull:** maintenance log for when the shield was last removed; whether the machine has run since without it; PTO speed rating for the implement in use.

### Grain-bin entry happening without a logged permit or observer

**Usually means:** the entry is being treated as routine because "it's just a quick look" or "the augers are already off." This is the exact pattern behind most documented entrapment cases — normalized shortcutting of a rare-but-catastrophic task.

**First question:** "Is the observer stationed outside right now, and is the permit filled out for this specific entry?"

**Data to pull:** entry permit log; lockout/tagout status of feeding and unloading equipment; whether the grain surface has been probed for bridging or crusting before anyone steps on it.

### Combine ground speed increased mid-harvest without a corresponding loss re-check

**Usually means:** speed was bumped to make up time against the schedule, and the loss-vs-speed curve (which is convex, not linear) is being trusted rather than re-measured at the new speed.

**First question:** "When was the last drop-pan test run at this new speed?"

**Data to pull:** timestamp and speed of the last loss check vs. current operating speed; yield-monitor bushels/acre trend (rising yield lowers the safe speed for the same loss target).

### Moisture readings scattered across a wide range within one field on the same pass

**Usually means:** uneven dry-down from topography, hybrid variability, or a lodged/downed section drying differently — harvesting to a single average moisture target across that field risks over-drying part of it and under-drying (storage risk) the rest.

**First question:** "Are we seeing this spread across the whole field or concentrated in specific zones — low ground, a different hybrid block, a lodged section?"

**Data to pull:** moisture readings by GPS zone or by truckload with field location noted; topography/drainage map; hybrid or variety boundary map for that field.
