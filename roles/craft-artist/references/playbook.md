# Craft Business Playbook

Filled templates and worksheets, not descriptions of them. Numbers are plausible examples for a mid-career solo maker; substitute your own cost basis and hourly rate.

## 1. Pricing worksheet (per SKU)

| Field | Example — ceramic mug | Example — silver pendant |
|---|---|---|
| Materials cost | Clay $1.10 + glaze $0.60 + kiln/fuel share $0.90 = **$2.60** | Silver 18g @ $0.85/g = $15.30 + cabochon $8.00 + findings $4.00 = **$27.30** |
| Labor hours | 0.75 hr (throwing, trimming, glazing amortized) | 1.5 hr |
| Hourly rate | $22/hr | $28/hr |
| Labor cost | $16.50 | $42.00 |
| **Total cost** | **$19.10** | **$69.30** |
| Wholesale (cost × 2) | $38.20 → round to **$38** | $138.60 → round to **$139** |
| Retail (wholesale × 2) | **$76** | **$278** |
| Classification | Production line | Production line (OOAK variants priced above formula) |

Rounding convention: round wholesale to the nearest whole dollar, retail to the nearest price ending in a common charm point ($X8, $X5, or $X9) so the number reads as intentional, not calculated live at the register.

**OOAK/gallery-tier override.** For a one-of-a-kind piece, run the same cost worksheet, then price at cost-formula-plus-a-scarcity-premium rather than at formula. A rule of thumb: if a maker's production-line pieces at a given show consistently sell out and a comparable OOAK piece doesn't move in 2–3 shows, the OOAK premium was set too high for that venue — reprice or move it to a higher-tier gallery instead of discounting to production-line levels.

## 2. Line sheet template

```
[Studio Name] — Wholesale Line Sheet — effective [date]
Terms: Net 30 on approved accounts; net 30% deposit, balance on delivery for new accounts.
Minimum opening order: $500. Minimum reorder: $250.
Lead time: 3–4 weeks for in-stock designs; 6–8 weeks for custom colorways.

| SKU     | Description                     | Wholesale | SRP  | Min Qty |
|---------|----------------------------------|-----------|------|---------|
| PND-001 | Sterling pendant, blue cabochon  | $139      | $278 | 3       |
| PND-002 | Sterling pendant, garnet         | $143      | $286 | 3       |
| MUG-010 | Stoneware mug, 12oz, celadon     | $38       | $76  | 6       |
```

Repricing trigger row: when the underlying commodity input moves >10% from the last line-sheet date, reissue the sheet with a new effective date rather than editing wholesale accounts' existing orders retroactively — honor quoted prices on orders placed before the trigger date.

## 3. Consignment agreement — the terms that matter, filled

```
Consignor: [Maker]            Consignee: [Gallery/Shop]
Piece(s): per attached inventory list, priced individually
Commission split: 60% consignor / 40% consignee (negotiate; 50/50 common for
  galleries, 60/40 or better common for shops without curatorial services)
Settlement: sold-item proceeds remitted within 30 days of sale (cap requested
  at 45 days; decline anything past 90 days without a strong traffic case)
Floor price: consignee may not discount below [90% of listed retail] without
  consignor's written consent
Liability: consignee insures consigned inventory at listed retail value;
  consignor retains title until sale
Term: 90 days, auto-renewing unless either party gives 30 days' notice;
  unsold inventory returned at consignor's expense on non-renewal
```

## 4. Jury submission checklist (adapt per show's stated specs)

1. Confirm current image requirements for the specific show — resolution/pixel dimensions, file format, and count (typically 4–5 piece images plus one booth/display shot) change year to year; use the current call for entries, not last year's.
2. Select images that read as one coherent body of work — same lighting setup, same background treatment, consistent color grading — over the individually flashiest pieces shot inconsistently.
3. Confirm the work shown matches the work that will be sold; jurors and show staff cross-check booths against jury images, and a mismatch risks disqualification.
4. Draft the artist statement to process and materials, third person optional, under the show's stated word limit — no biography unless requested.
5. Note the jury fee and booth fee separately in the studio's show-budget tracker (jury fee is sunk whether or not accepted; budget the booth fee only after acceptance).
6. After results: if rejected, request feedback if the show offers it: jurors commonly cite image quality, inconsistent body of work, or category fit as the top three reasons, distinct from a verdict on craftsmanship.

## 5. Show ROI worksheet

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Booth fee (10×10, weekend show) | $650 |
| Jury/application fee (non-refundable, already sunk) | $50 |
| Travel + lodging | $380 |
| Booth materials/display amortized share | $70 |
| **Total show cost** | **$1,150** |
| Break-even units at $278 retail (production pendant) | ~5 units |
| Historical average units sold at this show | 14 units |
| **Revenue** | **$3,892** |
| **Net before materials/labor already costed** | **$2,742** |

Rule of thumb applied here: total show cost ($1,150) is roughly 30% of typical show revenue ($3,892) — above the 20–25% marketing-spend threshold, which flags this specific show for review even though it was net-positive; check whether a comparable show at lower cost (regional vs. national circuit) would return similar revenue before re-booking automatically.

## 6. Repricing trigger table

| Material | Baseline reference | Reprice trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sterling silver | Spot price at last costing | >10% move from baseline | Recost affected SKUs, reissue line sheet with new effective date |
| Exotic hardwood (e.g., ebony) | Supplier list price at last order | >10% move or supplier surcharge notice | Recost, and confirm supplier's stated cause (tariff, species restriction) before absorbing vs. passing through |
| Natural fiber (e.g., silk, alpaca) | Seasonal supplier catalog price | New catalog cycle or >10% move | Recost at next production run, not mid-run |
