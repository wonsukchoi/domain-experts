# Red Flags

Smell tests for geothermal loop and heat pump installs. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Short-cycling or poor dehumidification on a "properly sized" loop

- **Usually means:** equipment tonnage oversized against the Manual J load, not a loop problem — extra bore length doesn't fix an oversized unit.
- **First question:** "What's the calculated load in tons versus the installed nameplate tonnage?"
- **Data to pull:** original Manual J report, equipment nameplate/spec sheet.

### EWT more than roughly 10–15°F outside the design band on a design-condition day

- **Usually means:** undersized bore length, an over-optimistic ground conductivity assumption, or thermal interference from adjacent boreholes spaced too tight.
- **First question:** "What k-value and ft/ton benchmark was this loop actually sized against?"
- **Data to pull:** original bore-sizing worksheet or TRT report, borehole spacing on the site plan.

### Flow rate under ~2.25 GPM per ton at the flow center

- **Usually means:** incomplete purge leaving an air pocket, or an undersized/failing circulator — cheaper and more likely than a loop-sizing problem.
- **First question:** "Was the purge log signed off at target velocity, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** purge log, flow meter reading at the flow center.

### Pressure drop on the hydrostatic test before backfill

- **Usually means:** a leak at a fusion joint, a field-damaged pipe section, or a fitting that shouldn't be below grade at all.
- **First question:** "Which joints were fused last, and is there any record of a field cut or repair on this run?"
- **Data to pull:** pressure-test record, fusion joint log with technician and timestamp per joint.

### Grout ticket volume under calculated annular volume

- **Usually means:** a void or short pour in the grout column — the borehole may not be fully sealed and the loop may be thermally short-circuiting to bypass air-filled sections.
- **First question:** "How many yards were delivered versus the calculated annulus volume for this depth and bore diameter?"
- **Data to pull:** grout delivery ticket, bore log with depth and diameter.

### EWT drifting worse year over year across a multi-bore field

- **Usually means:** boreholes spaced too tight, drawing down (or heating up) the same ground volume faster than it can recover between seasons.
- **First question:** "What's the actual on-center spacing versus what was specified, and has anything been added to the field since install?"
- **Data to pull:** as-built site plan with spacing, multi-year EWT trend log if available.

### Delta-T across the heat pump water side well above the design value with adequate flow

- **Usually means:** the loop or ground side is under-delivering relative to what the equipment is asking for — check bore length and k-value assumption before the refrigerant circuit.
- **First question:** "Is flow at spec, and if so, what was this delta-T designed to be at this load?"
- **Data to pull:** commissioning sheet with design delta-T, current flow meter and temperature readings.

### Delta-T well below design with adequate flow and normal EWT

- **Usually means:** the loop side is fine — look at refrigerant charge, compressor performance, or airflow before touching the ground loop.
- **First question:** "Has the refrigerant side been checked yet, or has every callback assumed it's the loop?"
- **Data to pull:** refrigerant charge/superheat-subcooling log (EPA 608-certified work).

### Homeowner reports "geothermal only struggles in extreme cold or heat"

- **Usually means:** the system is running at or near its design boundary — either the heating/cooling load was underestimated, or auxiliary/backup heat is undersized or not engaging as designed, not a general "geo doesn't work" issue.
- **First question:** "What's the outdoor temperature and EWT at the moment of the complaint, versus design conditions?"
- **Data to pull:** thermostat/EWT log during the complaint window, original load calc and balance-point assumption.

### Open-loop discharge volume or temperature near a permit limit

- **Usually means:** the system is drifting toward a regulatory violation on injection/discharge permitting that closed-loop rules don't apply to.
- **First question:** "What does the local water-use or injection-well permit actually cap, and how close is current operation to that limit?"
- **Data to pull:** permit documentation, discharge flow/temperature log.

### No documented k-value or benchmark source on the bore-sizing worksheet

- **Usually means:** the loop was sized off a generic rule of thumb without checking it against the actual formation — fine on some jobs, but undocumented, so nobody can tell later whether it was a reasoned assumption or a guess.
- **First question:** "What formation and k-value was this length actually calculated against, and is that written down anywhere?"
- **Data to pull:** bore-sizing worksheet, any local geological/well-log data for the area.
