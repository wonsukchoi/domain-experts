# Playbook

Filled templates a park naturalist actually uses, not descriptions of them. Adapt the numbers to the site; keep the structure.

## 1. Theme-development worksheet

Run before writing any program outline — five minutes on paper, not in your head.

```
RESOURCE FACT SET (everything true about this stop today):
  - Great Horned Owl resident pair, active on this property since [year]
  - Asymmetrical ear placement lets them triangulate prey location by sound alone
  - Silent flight from comb-like leading-edge feathers
  - Talon grip strength ~28 psi (enough to sever a rabbit's spine)
  - Nocturnal; roosts in conifer stands by day

AUDIENCE (who's actually booked):
  - Drop-in families, ages ~6-12 skewing young, evening program (post-dinner,
    lower attention span than a morning school group)

ONE THEME (say it out loud — if it takes a breath and a half, it's two themes):
  - "Owls hunt by sound, not sight, and that's why the woods have to go quiet
    for this to work."

WHICH FACTS SERVE THE THEME (keep):
  - Ear placement, silent flight  →  directly explain "hunts by sound"

WHICH FACTS DON'T (cut from the program, fine for a handout or Q&A):
  - Talon psi, roost habitat  →  interesting, doesn't serve THIS theme

TECHNIQUE (match to audience, not to what's easiest to prep):
  - Young evening family group → participatory/sensory (blindfolded
    sound-localization exercise), not lecture-with-handout (unreadable at dusk
    anyway)
```

**Rule:** if more than half the fact set survives the "serves the theme" filter, the theme is too broad — narrow it until it's doing real cutting.

## 2. Program timing block (45-minute guided walk, filled)

Every block should reconcile to the total slot time; if it doesn't, something got added without something else being cut.

| Segment | Minutes | Running total | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trailhead intro + theme statement + group-size check | 5 | 5 | State theme once, explicitly; verify group ≤ cap before moving |
| Participatory/sensory activity tied to theme | 10 | 15 | The TORE "enjoyable" + "relevant" core — do this early, not as a finale |
| Walk to primary interpretive stop | 10 | 25 | Movement segment; short theme call-backs only, no new content |
| Primary stop — theme payoff (direct observation, demonstration, or silence) | 10 | 35 | Where the theme is *experienced*, not just stated |
| Debrief + close, at the stop (not back at a kiosk) | 10 | 45 | Recap theme in one sentence; no new facts introduced here |

**Common failure this catches:** programs that run long because "one more fact" gets added at each stop — this table forces the tradeoff to be visible before the walk, not discovered mid-program.

## 3. Wildlife and invasive-species encounter triage (during a program)

```
WILDLIFE SIGHTING MID-PROGRAM:
  1. Species-general default buffer: 25 yards (most wildlife),
     100 yards (large predators: bear, mountain lion).
  2. Check for a posted seasonal closure or agency-specific buffer for THIS
     species (nesting raptors commonly carry buffers in the hundreds of
     meters, set by the state wildlife agency — not a judgment call).
  3. If the group's current position violates the buffer: move the group
     back FIRST, narrate second. Never let "this is a great teaching
     moment" delay the buffer correction.
  4. Log the observation (species, count, GPS/landmark, time) to iNaturalist
     or the site's internal wildlife log after the program — same day,
     not "when I get a chance."

SUSPECTED INVASIVE SPECIES SIGHTING (mid-program or on a monitoring walk):
  1. Confidence check: "reasonably confident" ID (matches 3+ diagnostic
     features you can name) vs. "possible" (one feature, could be a
     look-alike native).
  2. Reasonably confident + high-priority regional species (check your
     state's Early Detection watch list) → photograph, GPS point, report to
     EDDMapS same day; do not attempt removal without a resource-management
     protocol for that species (some require bagging, some require leaving
     in place for crew removal to avoid spreading seed/spores).
  3. Possible but unconfirmed → photograph and GPS point, hold the report,
     get a second ID (staff botanist/biologist or iNaturalist community ID)
     before submitting — a wrong EDDMapS report costs a partner biologist a
     verification trip.
  4. Either way: do not stop the program to conduct a full survey — note it,
     finish the program, follow up same day.
```

## 4. Group-size and staffing table

| Program type | Default cap | Staff required | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard guided walk | 15–20 | 1 naturalist | Above this, questions stop and the group strings out past voice range |
| Staffed special event / large program | 25–30 | 1 naturalist + 1 trained second adult | Second adult manages group spacing/safety, not content delivery |
| School group field trip | 25–30 per naturalist | 1 naturalist per group, split larger classes | Split at booking, not day-of — day-of splits mean an unprepared second leader |
| Sensitive-resource route (active nest, restoration plot) | 10–12 | 1 naturalist | Smaller cap independent of the standard table — set by the resource constraint, not program preference |

**Rule:** if a booking exceeds the cap for the route, split into two dated programs at the booking stage. Stretching the cap to avoid a scheduling conversation is the single most common way a program quietly damages the resource it's about.

## 5. Junior Ranger badge conversation (structure, not script)

```
1. Verify the booklet is actually complete (not just "signed") — spot-check
   2-3 activities, don't just check for ink in every box.
2. Ask ONE open question tied to something they wrote, not a quiz:
   "You drew the owl here — what made you pick that one?"
3. Listen to the answer fully before responding; this is the actual
   interpretive moment, not the badge itself.
4. Administer the pledge (site-specific wording) and hand over the badge.
5. If the booklet is incomplete: offer to finish 1-2 quick activities
   together on the spot rather than declining the badge outright — the
   goal is the engagement the booklet produced, not gatekeeping the badge.
```
