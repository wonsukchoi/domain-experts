# Moving Crew Supervisor — Red Flags

### Actual (reweighed) net weight more than 10% over a non-binding estimate
- **Usually means:** the original survey/walkthrough missed a closet, garage, attic, or storage unit; second most likely — items were added by the customer after the BOL was signed without an additional-service order.
- **First question:** is there a signed additional-service order covering the difference, or is this entirely unlisted household weight?
- **Data to pull:** original cube sheet, signed additional-service orders on file, both weight tickets (tare and gross, both ends).

### Customer or dispatch pushing to collect full actual charges at delivery on a non-binding shipment
- **Usually means:** the driver or crew lead hasn't run the 110% ceiling calculation, or is under pressure to close the job's cash out fully today.
- **First question:** what does 110% of the *original estimate's total* come to, and is actual billed above or below that number?
- **Data to pull:** signed BOL with estimate type and total, tariff sheet used for the linehaul rate.

### Sustained loading rate under ~250–300 lbs per crew-member-hour with no stairs or long carry
- **Usually means:** staging or access bottleneck (truck parked far from the entrance, narrow hallway, elevator wait); second most likely — crew is short a member relative to the job size booked.
- **First question:** where physically is the truck relative to the door, and is anyone standing idle waiting on a carry path?
- **Data to pull:** original survey's stated distance/access notes vs. what the crew is actually dealing with today.

### An item over ~75 lbs with unknown weight, poor grip, or a stair/long carry attempted by one person
- **Usually means:** the crew hasn't scored it against a lift threshold and is estimating safety from habit or from how past similar items went.
- **First question:** has anyone actually assessed grip, distance, and path, or is this "we've moved dressers before"?
- **Data to pull:** item weight if known from inventory; NIOSH lift-screening worksheet if not.

### No signed additional-service order for an item that's clearly not on the original cube sheet
- **Usually means:** the crew loaded it under verbal agreement to keep the job moving, deferring the paperwork conversation to delivery.
- **First question:** who authorized loading it, and is there any record — even a note — of what was agreed?
- **Data to pull:** cube sheet, BOL timestamp, crew lead's job notes for the day.

### High-value item (declared or apparent) with no High-Value Inventory form or condition tag
- **Usually means:** the pre-load walkthrough was rushed, or the crew didn't flag it as high-value in the moment.
- **First question:** did the customer declare a value for this item at booking, and is it tagged in the inventory sheet?
- **Data to pull:** HVI form, inventory sheet item numbers, origin condition photos if any exist.

### Damage claim filed with no origin condition photo or tag for the disputed item
- **Usually means:** the item wasn't flagged during the pre-load walkthrough, making causation genuinely unresolved rather than provable either way.
- **First question:** does any origin documentation — tag, photo, inventory note — exist for this specific item?
- **Data to pull:** origin inventory sheet, condition tags, any pre-move photos on file.

### Crew or driver refusing to unload until a payment above the 110% ceiling is received
- **Usually means:** the ceiling wasn't calculated, or was calculated and is being ignored under pressure to collect the full actual bill.
- **First question:** has the 110% cap been computed against the signed non-binding estimate, and does today's demand exceed it?
- **Data to pull:** signed BOL and estimate total, ceiling calculation, any prior correspondence about the shipment's cost.

### Repeated over-estimate variance (>15%) tied to the same salesperson's original surveys
- **Usually means:** that estimator is systematically under-surveying (rushing walkthroughs, missing storage areas) rather than each job being an isolated miss.
- **First question:** how many of this estimator's last 10 non-binding jobs came in over 110% of estimate at delivery?
- **Data to pull:** branch job-close reconciliation log across recent jobs by estimator.

### Customer declines to witness the reweigh, or the crew doesn't offer it
- **Usually means:** either genuine customer disinterest, or the crew treating the offer as optional paperwork rather than a right — the second is the pattern worth catching.
- **First question:** was the customer actually told they could witness both weighings, and when?
- **Data to pull:** weight ticket timestamps against the customer's presence at the scale, crew lead's job log.

### A same-day cash "rush fee" request tied to skipping a team lift or condition documentation
- **Usually means:** schedule or tip pressure pushing the crew to shortcut a safety or documentation step to save time.
- **First question:** what specific step is being skipped to make the faster time, and does it involve an item over the lift threshold or an undocumented high-value piece?
- **Data to pull:** job time log, lift-screening worksheet (if any), HVI form status.
