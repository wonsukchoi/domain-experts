# Architect — Red Flags

### Fee quoted as bare $/SF with no systems or finish assumption named
- **Usually means:** the estimator is anchoring to a past project's blended number, then second, the client hasn't scoped systems/finish level yet and the number is a placeholder.
- **First question:** "What structural system, envelope type, and finish tier is this $/SF assuming?"
- **Data to pull:** the comparable project's actual cost breakdown by CSI division, not just its total.

### Combined "code review" memo that doesn't separately cite a zoning ordinance section and a building code section
- **Usually means:** only one of the two gates was actually checked, most often zoning skipped in favor of building-code-only review.
- **First question:** "Which zoning ordinance section sets FAR/setback/height for this parcel, cited separately from the IBC citation?"
- **Data to pull:** the municipal zoning ordinance text for the specific parcel's use district.

### Occupant load calculated from "how crowded the room looks" rather than IBC Table 1004.5
- **Usually means:** egress width and required exits are being sized off intuition, not the governing occupant-load factor for the space's actual use classification.
- **First question:** "What use classification and SF/occupant factor did this occupant load use?"
- **Data to pull:** IBC Table 1004.5 factor for the specific occupancy classification.

### Value-engineering request targets a code-minimum system (egress width, fire rating, accessible route)
- **Usually means:** the reduction is being framed as a cost saving but removes the only margin the system has, since code minimums already carry zero slack.
- **First question:** "Does this reduction bring any dimension below its code-required minimum, or only below a design margin above code?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific code section and required value being reduced toward.

### Long-lead procurement item (curtain wall, elevators, specialty steel) not yet released at 60% CDs
- **Usually means:** the schedule risk from lead time hasn't been separated from design completion — waiting for 100% CDs to release long-lead items routinely adds months.
- **First question:** "What is this item's fabrication lead time, and does the design have enough certainty at 60% CDs to release it now?"
- **Data to pull:** the supplier's quoted lead time and the current project schedule's substantial-completion date.

### Verbal field instruction changing scope or a code-compliance detail during construction
- **Usually means:** an RFI response bypassed the documented process, leaving no paper trail if the change is later disputed on cost or code grounds.
- **First question:** "Is there a written, drawing-referenced RFI response for this field change?"
- **Data to pull:** the RFI log entry and the referenced sheet/detail number.

### FAR or allowable-area calculation using gross building area with no exclusions applied
- **Usually means:** mechanical space, parking, or other zoning-exempt area categories weren't excluded, understating actual available program area or overstating a zoning violation.
- **First question:** "Does the local ordinance exclude mechanical, parking, or common area from FAR calculation, and was that exclusion applied?"
- **Data to pull:** the ordinance's definition of "gross floor area" for FAR purposes.

### Construction cost estimate reconciles to budget within <2% with no named contingency line
- **Usually means:** the estimate is tuned to hit the number rather than independently derived, or a contingency was folded into a soft line item instead of stated.
- **First question:** "What is the stated design/construction contingency percentage, and is it a separate line?"
- **Data to pull:** the cost estimate's line-item breakdown and stated contingency percentage (typically 5-10% at DD, 3-5% at CD).

### Change order cost impact stated without a separate schedule-impact finding
- **Usually means:** float analysis wasn't run — a cost-neutral-looking change could still be on the critical path and delay substantial completion.
- **First question:** "Is this change order's activity on the critical path, and how much float does it have?"
- **Data to pull:** the current schedule's critical-path analysis and float for the affected activity.

### Consultant coordination review scheduled only at end-of-phase submittal, not at interim milestones
- **Usually means:** cross-discipline clashes (structural beam through MEP duct routing, etc.) will surface at permit review or in the field instead of during design.
- **First question:** "When was the last cross-discipline model/drawing overlay review, and what did it find?"
- **Data to pull:** the coordination review log and date of last overlay check.
