# Speech-Language Pathologist — Playbook

## Dysphagia screening-to-diet-recommendation worksheet

| Trial | Consistency | Volume | Wet vocal quality? | Cough response | Overt aspiration signs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Thin liquid | 5 mL | Yes | Weak/delayed | Coughing observed |
| 2 | Thin liquid | 10 mL | Yes | Weak/delayed | Coughing observed |
| 3 | Nectar-thick liquid | 5 mL | No | Strong, on cue | None |
| 4 | Puree | 5 mL bolus | No | N/A | None |

**Risk-factor checklist (each present item raises silent-aspiration concern):**
- [ ] Neurologic diagnosis with known dysphagia association (stroke territory, ALS, Parkinson's, TBI)
- [ ] Documented cough/choking episode with meals in the last 7 days
- [ ] Reduced cough strength or delayed cough response on exam
- [ ] Wet or gurgly vocal quality after any trial
- [ ] Prior aspiration pneumonia or unexplained recurrent pneumonia

**Decision rule (stated clinical heuristic — confirm against institutional protocol):** 0-1 risk factors and no overt signs on any consistency → bedside clearance for the least-restrictive safe consistency identified, reassess per unit protocol. 2+ risk factors, or any wet vocal quality/overt sign on a consistency intended for the diet order → restrict that consistency and refer for instrumental study (VFSS or FEES) before finalizing.

## Aphasia-classification worked table

| Feature | Broca's (non-fluent) | Wernicke's (fluent) | Conduction | Global |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fluency | Non-fluent, effortful | Fluent, normal rate | Fluent | Non-fluent |
| Auditory comprehension | Relatively preserved | Impaired | Relatively preserved | Impaired |
| Repetition | Impaired | Impaired | Severely impaired (disproportionate to other deficits) | Impaired |
| Typical lesion site | Left frontal (Broca's area) | Left posterior temporal | Arcuate fasciculus / left parietal | Large left perisylvian |

**Scoring note:** repetition is the differentiator between conduction aphasia and the fluent/non-fluent types above it — a workup that skips repetition testing cannot distinguish conduction aphasia from a milder Wernicke's or Broca's presentation and will misclassify type, which then misguides treatment focus (conduction aphasia treatment emphasizes phonological/repetition tasks distinct from Wernicke's comprehension-focused treatment).

**Severity banding (standardized battery, e.g., WAB Aphasia Quotient, stated convention — confirm against instrument manual):** AQ 76-93.8 mild, AQ 51-75.9 moderate, AQ 26-50.9 severe, AQ below 26 very severe. Severity band informs treatment intensity and whether AAC-supported communication should be introduced alongside verbal therapy from the outset (typically indicated at severe/very severe bands with limited functional verbal output).

## AAC-candidacy checklist

- [ ] Current functional communication is insufficient for basic needs/wants across at least one daily setting (documented, not assumed)
- [ ] Motor access evaluated — direct selection (finger, stylus) vs. switch-scanning, based on fine-motor and range-of-motion assessment
- [ ] Cognitive-linguistic readiness assessed — symbol/icon comprehension, sequencing ability appropriate to the device's symbol density
- [ ] Communication partners (family, care team, or classroom staff) willing and available to support device use — an unsupported device becomes an unused device
- [ ] Trial period planned with a specific, measurable communication goal (e.g., "request 3 preferred items independently using the device across 5 opportunities") rather than an open-ended "see how it goes" trial

**Fallback order when full high-tech AAC isn't immediately funded/available:** (1) low-tech communication board with core vocabulary as an immediate bridge, (2) mid-tech dedicated device on loan/trial from a manufacturer or lending library, (3) high-tech device with eye-gaze or switch access, funded through insurance/Medicaid AAC benefit — start the least-restrictive option that meets the current functional need rather than waiting for the highest-tech option to be approved.
