# Red flags

### Two or more posts/program areas below required ratio on the same shift
- **Usually means:** call-outs weren't reconciled against per-area minimums before opening — a property-wide headcount looked adequate while individual areas weren't checked.
- **First question:** "Did we do a roll call against the certification list per area, or just a total headcount?"
- **Data to pull:** today's per-area roster vs. required minimum, timestamped against opening time.

### Same worker generates 2+ guest complaints within a rolling 2-week window
- **Usually means:** a skill or attitude gap that's been absorbed as one-off incidents instead of tracked as a pattern.
- **First question:** "Were the prior complaints logged, and did anyone connect them before now?"
- **Data to pull:** the worker's complaint/coaching log for the trailing 30 days.

### New hire signed off independent after fewer shadow shifts than the venue's SOP minimum
- **Usually means:** a staffing gap was covered by shortcutting training rather than working the contingency chain.
- **First question:** "What was the staffing state on the day this sign-off happened?"
- **Data to pull:** the shadow-shift log for that worker and the shift roster for the sign-off date.

### A certification (lifeguard, ride operator, ACCT level) expiring within 7 days with no renewal scheduled
- **Usually means:** the certification tracker isn't being checked proactively — expirations are being discovered at roll call, not ahead of it.
- **First question:** "When was the certification tracker last reconciled against the roster?"
- **Data to pull:** certification-expiration report sorted by date, cross-checked against next 14 days of scheduled shifts.

### Call-out rate above 15% of a scheduled shift with no documented contingency action
- **Usually means:** either the float/on-call chain wasn't activated, or it doesn't exist for that program area.
- **First question:** "Which step of the contingency chain was used, and if none, why not?"
- **Data to pull:** shift handoff note for that date; float-pool activation log.

### Same post or attraction generating 2+ near-miss reports within a month
- **Usually means:** a training gap, an equipment issue not yet flagged to maintenance, or a procedure that's being worked around under pressure.
- **First question:** "Is this the same worker, the same time of day, or the same post regardless of who's on it?"
- **Data to pull:** near-miss reports for that post over the trailing 90 days, sorted by worker and time.

### Pre-shift briefing under 5 minutes on a day with a material change (weather, staffing, VIP group)
- **Usually means:** the briefing is being run as a formality/roll call rather than addressing the day's actual risk.
- **First question:** "What in today's briefing was different from yesterday's?"
- **Data to pull:** briefing log or notes for the date in question.

### Overtime on a shift running 20%+ above the trailing 4-week average with no matching attendance spike
- **Usually means:** the float/on-call chain is being used as a permanent patch for a scheduling gap rather than an occasional contingency.
- **First question:** "How many of the last 4 weeks needed float coverage on this same post?"
- **Data to pull:** overtime-by-shift report cross-referenced with the float-activation log for the same period.

### Vigilance-rotation log shows a gap greater than the posted interval at a high-risk post
- **Usually means:** the rotation schedule exists on paper but isn't being enforced in practice, often because the shift felt "quiet."
- **First question:** "Who was assigned to trigger rotations on this shift, and was the log filled in real time or after the fact?"
- **Data to pull:** the rotation log timestamps for the shift in question.

### A Tier-3 (safety-relevant) guest complaint resolved with only a shift-log mention, no incident report
- **Usually means:** the complaint was triaged as a service issue rather than checked against the near-miss criteria.
- **First question:** "Did the complaint describe a bypassed safety step or an injury, even a minor one?"
- **Data to pull:** the original complaint text and the shift log entry, compared against the Tier 3 criteria.

### A program area consolidated "temporarily" for 3+ consecutive shifts
- **Usually means:** step 2 of the contingency chain has quietly become the permanent staffing plan instead of a same-day fix, and the venue manager hasn't been told the underlying schedule is broken.
- **First question:** "Has this consolidation been reported up as a recurring gap, or handled fresh each shift?"
- **Data to pull:** shift handoff notes for the affected program area over the trailing 2 weeks.
