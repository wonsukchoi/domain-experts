# Playbook

Filled templates and thresholds for the recurring operational calls this role makes. Populate with venue-specific numbers where noted; the structures and default thresholds below are the starting point, not a description of one.

## 1. Contingency chain (call-out response)

Work these in order. Do not skip a step to save time — each step exists because the prior one has a cost or a limit.

| Step | Action | When to move to next step |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Activate on-call/float staff cross-trained for the short post or age band | Float pool exhausted, or floats already committed elsewhere this shift |
| 2 | Consolidate the lowest-priority program area for the day (cancel an elective, merge two under-filled sub-groups) and reassign its staff to the ratio-critical post | Consolidation would itself drop a *different* post below ratio |
| 3 | Pull a qualified staffer from a non-guest-facing task (admin, maintenance liaison) if they hold the required certification for the post | No certified staff available outside guest-facing posts |
| 4 | Escalate to the venue manager: request additional staff, or approve a capacity cap / program-area closure for the day | Always the last resort — closing capacity has revenue and guest-experience cost the venue manager, not the supervisor, is accountable for |

**Never:** open or continue running a post below its required ratio while working the chain. If step 1-3 haven't resolved it by opening time, the post stays closed or capped, not "open and thin."

## 2. Ratio requirements by context (fill in venue-specific standard; example values below)

| Context | Standard | Example ratio | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day camp, ages 7-8 | ACA accreditation standard | 1 staff : 6 campers | American Camp Association |
| Day camp, ages 9-14 | ACA accreditation standard | 1 staff : 8 campers | American Camp Association |
| Resident camp, ages 7-8 | ACA accreditation standard | 1 staff : 6 campers | American Camp Association |
| Pool, guarded zone | Facility aquatic safety program | Per zone-of-responsibility sightline, not a headcount ratio | Jeff Ellis & Associates / facility program |
| Ride/attraction crew | Manufacturer + ASTM F770 SOP | Named minimum crew per station (varies by ride) | Manufacturer operating manual, ASTM F770 |
| Challenge course / zip line | ANSI/ACCT standard | Named minimum supervision per participant group and course type | ACCT International |

Do not average across program areas to claim compliance — each row is checked independently.

## 3. Shadow-shift minimums before independent sign-off (example thresholds — set per venue SOP)

| Post type | Minimum supervised shifts before solo | Sign-off requires |
|---|---|---|
| Camp counselor, indoor/low-risk activity | 2 full shifts | Lead counselor confirms briefing execution and roster handling |
| Camp counselor, waterfront-adjacent | 3 full shifts | Above, plus water-safety orientation sign-off |
| Lifeguard | Per facility's certifying body (commonly a probationary in-service period plus a scanning audit) | Certifying supervisor confirms scanning technique and zone coverage independently observed |
| Ride/attraction operator | Manufacturer/venue-specified number of supervised cycles plus a written test | Ride-ops supervisor sign-off logged against the specific ride, not "operator" generally |
| Challenge course facilitator | Per ACCT practitioner level required for the course category | Course manager sign-off against ACCT level, logged with date and course type |

A transfer from another venue at an equivalent certified standard can substitute for shadow shifts if the certification is current and verified — document the verification, don't just take the worker's word.

## 4. Pre-shift briefing checklist (fill each line daily, not from memory)

1. **What changed since the last shift:** weather forecast/current conditions, any closures or capacity caps, any incidents or near-misses logged.
2. **Today's staffing state:** who's present vs. scheduled, any float/consolidation already in effect, and why.
3. **Bookings/groups:** VIP, large group, or school bookings requiring adjusted staffing or attention.
4. **Post assignments and any changes from the standard rotation.**
5. **Vigilance-rotation schedule for the shift** (interval and who covers whom).
6. **One reminder tied to a recent incident or near-miss anywhere in the venue**, not a generic safety platitude.

A briefing that only covers items 4-5 and skips 1-3 is a roll call, not a briefing — it misses the day's actual risk.

## 5. Guest complaint triage

| Tier | Example | Action | Documentation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Service | Slow service, minor rudeness, first occurrence | On-the-spot recovery (apology, small comp, immediate fix) | None required beyond a shift-log mention |
| 2 — Pattern | Same worker, 2nd+ complaint in a rolling 2-week window | Recovery, plus private coaching conversation with the worker | Coaching note in the worker's file, dated |
| 3 — Safety-relevant | Any complaint describing a near-miss, a bypassed safety step, or an injury, regardless of severity | Immediate recovery, plus incident/near-miss report, plus escalation to venue manager same day | Formal incident report, filed same shift — never held for end-of-week |

When in doubt between Tier 2 and Tier 3, default to Tier 3 — the cost of an unnecessary incident report is minutes; the cost of a missed safety pattern is the next incident.
