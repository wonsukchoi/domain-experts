# Carpenter Helper — Red Flags

### Guardrail missing any of the three required members within 6 ft of an edge
- **Usually means:** another trade or a material delivery knocked a rail loose; second most likely is a rail that was never fully installed after an earlier inspection.
- **First question:** "Has anyone worked past this section since the guardrail was last checked complete?"
- **Data to pull:** the site's daily fall-protection/guardrail inspection log, if one is kept, or a photo timestamp of the last confirmed-complete state.

### Layout mark disagrees with the framing plan by more than about 1/2"
- **Usually means:** a transcription error from plan to plate; second most likely is a plan revision that didn't make it to the field set.
- **First question:** "Is this the current plan revision, and does the carpenter's mark match a different (older) version?"
- **Data to pull:** the plan set's revision date/number stamped on the sheet in hand.

### Header or engineered-lumber size on site doesn't match the plan call-out
- **Usually means:** a supply substitution made without an engineering sign-off; second most likely is the wrong bundle pulled from the yard.
- **First question:** "Did the substitution go through the engineer of record, or did the yard just send what was in stock?"
- **Data to pull:** the structural plan's beam/header schedule and the delivery ticket for the lumber on site.

### Nail gun left on contact-trip during close-in or toe-nailing work
- **Usually means:** the setting wasn't switched back after ground-level sheathing; second most likely is a helper unaware the two modes exist on that gun.
- **First question:** "What was this gun doing five minutes ago, and did the task change since?"
- **Data to pull:** none — visual/physical check of the trigger selector before the next shot.

### Visible crown greater than about 1/4" over 8 ft on more than 1 in 10 studs in a bundle
- **Usually means:** a bad batch from the yard; second most likely is lumber that sat improperly stickered/stacked on site.
- **First question:** "Is this bundle from the same delivery as the rest of the wall, or a different one?"
- **Data to pull:** the delivery ticket/bundle tag to check the lumber's source and grade stamp.

### Diagonal measurements on an assembled panel differ by more than 1/8" on a wall up to ~16 ft
- **Usually means:** a stud cut long/short somewhere in the run; second most likely is the plates weren't held flush during layout transfer.
- **First question:** "Which corner moved when we checked the second diagonal?"
- **Data to pull:** re-measure both diagonals from the same reference corner to isolate which stud or plate is off.

### Concrete formwork stripped or shored based on elapsed days alone, with no strength confirmation
- **Usually means:** the crew is working off a rule-of-thumb cure time instead of the mix design's actual strength-gain data; second most likely is schedule pressure overriding the plan.
- **First question:** "What does the engineer's stripping-time note or the concrete supplier's strength data say for this mix, not the calendar?"
- **Data to pull:** the concrete mix design/strength-gain curve or the engineer's formwork-removal sign-off.

### A finding in an existing wall/floor cavity that isn't on the as-built drawings (old wiring, undocumented framing, water damage)
- **Usually means:** a prior renovation that wasn't documented; second most likely is deferred damage the seller/owner didn't disclose.
- **First question:** "Does this change the load path or the scope, and who needs to see it before we build over it?"
- **Data to pull:** the as-built or permit drawing set for that wall/floor section.

### Carpenter idle waiting on cut material more than once in the same day
- **Usually means:** the helper is cutting in plan order instead of build order; second most likely is a take-off gap discovered mid-wall.
- **First question:** "What's the next three pieces the carpenter needs, in the order they're needed?"
- **Data to pull:** the cut list against the actual build sequence, re-ordered if needed.

### A 4x8 sheet good being carried solo up a ladder, stair, or extended reach
- **Usually means:** time pressure or a missing second set of hands; second most likely is underestimating a wet/pressure-treated sheet's added weight.
- **First question:** "Is there a panel jack or a second person available before this carry?"
- **Data to pull:** none — stop and get help or equipment before proceeding.

### Fastener box/coil runs out mid-wall with no backup staged
- **Usually means:** the take-off didn't account for actual per-connection fastener counts; second most likely is a partial box from a prior job that wasn't flagged.
- **First question:** "How much of this wall is left, and is there another box on the truck or in the trailer?"
- **Data to pull:** the fastener line on the material take-off versus what's actually been consumed.

### Housekeeping debris (cut-offs, loose fasteners) accumulating in the path of a wall about to be stood
- **Usually means:** cleanup was deferred to end-of-day instead of continuous; second most likely is the crew is behind schedule and skipping it under pressure.
- **First question:** "Is this the path the wall panel travels to get tilted up, or the walkway to the saw?"
- **Data to pull:** none — clear the path before the tilt-up proceeds, not after.
