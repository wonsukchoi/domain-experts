# Carpenter Helper — Playbook

Filled templates for a live framing day. Numbers are worked examples, not universal constants — always re-derive from the actual plan.

## 1. Material take-off table (filled example: three 12-ft, 8-ft-tall walls, 16" o.c.)

| Item | Calculation | Quantity | Order as |
|---|---|---|---|
| Studs | (144 in / 16 in) + 1 = 10 per wall × 3 walls | 30 | 92-5/8" precut studs |
| Top plates (doubled) | 12 ft × 2 × 3 walls | 72 linear ft | six 12-ft 2x4 |
| Bottom plates | 12 ft × 3 walls | 36 linear ft | three 12-ft 2x4 |
| Wall sheathing | 12 ft × 8 ft × 3 walls = 288 sq ft ÷ 32 sq ft/sheet | 9.0 sheets | 10 sheets (10% waste) |
| Framing nails (16d, plates/studs) | approx. 4 per stud-to-plate connection × 30 studs × 2 ends | 240 nails min. | 1 box (typically 1,000-2,000 ct coil) |
| Sheathing nails (8d, 6"/12" pattern) | perimeter + field nailing, approx. 60/sheet × 10 sheets | 600 nails | 1 coil (2,000-3,000 ct) |

## 2. Waste-factor defaults

| Material | Default waste allowance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Dimensional lumber (studs, plates) | 10% | Higher (15%) if crown-culling more than 1 in 10 sticks |
| Sheet goods (OSB, plywood) | 10% | Round up, never down — a short sheet stops the wall |
| Trim/finish lumber | 15-20% | Miter waste and grain-match culling higher than rough framing |
| Fasteners | Order by box/coil, not exact count | A partial coil mid-wall costs more in downtime than the extra box |

## 3. Wall-panel build sequence (per wall, flat-build-and-tilt)

1. Snap plate lines on subfloor per the framing plan (chalk line).
2. Lay out top and bottom plates side by side, transfer stud marks with a speed square across both plates simultaneously (guarantees matching layout top and bottom).
3. Mark king-stud and jack-stud positions at each rough opening per plan call-out — do not infer header size from a similar-looking opening elsewhere on the job.
4. Cut studs to precut/marked length, cull for crown >1/4" over 8 ft, orient crown consistently per carpenter's direction.
5. Assemble flat: nail studs to top and bottom plates at marked positions, headers and cripples per opening detail.
6. Square the panel: 3-4-5 check for a fast read, then diagonal (corner-to-corner) measurement — panel is square when both diagonals match within 1/8".
7. Sheathe (if flat-sheathing before standing) or leave open for stand-then-sheathe per the day's sequencing call.
8. Stand and brace — minimum two temporary braces per wall section until permanent bracing/adjacent walls are in place.

## 4. Nail-gun trigger-mode decision table

| Task | Default trigger mode | Switch to sequential when |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern-nailing sheathing, flat on ground/deck, no one within swing radius | Contact-trip (bump) | Another worker or the helper's own hand/knee enters the swing radius |
| Toe-nailing studs to plates | Sequential-trip | Always — close-in, off-angle work |
| Nailing near an opening, corner, or edge | Sequential-trip | Always |
| Overhead or ladder work | Sequential-trip | Always — recoil control matters more than speed |

## 5. Pre-task fall-protection check (elevation change trigger)

Run this check at **every** elevation change, not once per shift:

1. What is the working height right now (slab / subfloor / wall-top / roof)?
2. Is the exposure within 6 ft of an edge or opening?
3. If yes: is the guardrail present with all three required members (top rail 42 in ± 3 in, midrail at the midpoint, toeboard where falling-object exposure exists) — or is a tied-off PFAS in use?
4. If the guardrail is incomplete and no PFAS is available: stop within 6 ft of that section, flag the carpenter, do not proceed on the assumption the gap is minor.

## 6. Squaring reference (3-4-5 vs diagonal)

- **3-4-5 rule**: from a corner, mark 3 ft along one plate and 4 ft along the perpendicular plate; the diagonal between those two marks should measure exactly 5 ft when square. Fast, catches gross error, done before final fastening.
- **Diagonal check**: measure both full corner-to-corner diagonals of the assembled panel; equal diagonals (within 1/8" on a wall up to about 16 ft) confirm square. This is the check the carpenter signs off on before sheathing — the 3-4-5 rule is a shortcut, not a substitute for it.
