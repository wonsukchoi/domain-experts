# Red flags

### A delay between furnace removal and forging with no re-verification of billet temperature
- **Usually means:** actual billet temperature may have dropped below the specified window even though the furnace setpoint was correct.
- **First question:** how long was the delay, and has billet temperature been re-checked with a pyrometer before forging?
- **Data to pull:** elapsed delay time, current pyrometer reading, material's specified temperature window.

### A localized underfill in a specific die feature (a rib, corner, or deep recess)
- **Usually means:** press/hammer energy or force distribution issue at that specific location, not necessarily a bulk temperature problem.
- **First question:** is the underfill isolated to a specific die feature, or does it appear across the whole part?
- **Data to pull:** underfill location and pattern, press energy/force setting, billet temperature at time of forging.

### Cracking appearing at a specific stress concentration point on the forged part
- **Usually means:** insufficient plasticity (billet too cold) or excessive force applied to a feature the material couldn't accommodate at its actual temperature.
- **First question:** was billet temperature verified at the point of forging, and does the crack location correspond to a known high-stress feature?
- **Data to pull:** billet temperature record, crack location relative to part geometry.

### A finish machining plan proposed for a forged part without checking grain flow exposure
- **Usually means:** the machining operation may cut across grain flow at a load-bearing surface, creating a structural weak point invisible in a dimensional check.
- **First question:** does the proposed machining expose grain flow ends at a critical, load-bearing surface?
- **Data to pull:** the part's grain flow pattern (from forging design or macro-etch reference), the machining plan's cut locations.

### Flash line thickness drifting from spec across a production run
- **Usually means:** die wear developing at the parting line, potentially before it's visually obvious on the die.
- **First question:** does flash line thickness show a trend over recent parts, or is it a one-off variation?
- **Data to pull:** flash line thickness measurements across recent parts, die usage count since last inspection/rework.

### Excessive scale or surface decarburization on a forged part despite peak temperature within spec
- **Usually means:** time-at-temperature (not just peak temperature) exceeded what's appropriate, likely from a processing delay.
- **First question:** how long was this billet held at or near forging temperature before being struck?
- **Data to pull:** time-at-temperature record, furnace dwell time and any delays before forging.

### A die returning to production after rework or repair without part-dimension verification
- **Usually means:** an assumption that the rework restored the die to its original specification without confirming via actual part output.
- **First question:** has a first-part inspection been performed since the rework, or is the die assumed restored based on the repair itself?
- **Data to pull:** first-part inspection results since rework, die rework/repair record.

### A pyrometer or temperature measurement device not recently calibrated
- **Usually means:** temperature readings being relied on for forge/no-forge decisions may not be accurate.
- **First question:** when was this device last calibrated/verified?
- **Data to pull:** calibration log for the temperature measurement device.

### A part passing dimensional inspection but showing reduced mechanical properties in later testing
- **Usually means:** possible grain flow disruption from machining, or a temperature-related material property issue not caught by dimensional checks alone.
- **First question:** does the machining plan for this part preserve grain flow at the tested/failed location?
- **Data to pull:** mechanical test results and location, machining plan and grain flow pattern for that location.

### A billet processed from a different heat/lot without re-verifying its specific temperature response characteristics
- **Usually means:** material composition variation between heats/lots can shift the actual optimal forging temperature or plasticity behavior slightly.
- **First question:** does this heat/lot's material certification match what the standard process was validated for?
- **Data to pull:** material heat/lot certification, standard process's validated material specification.
