## Heat-loss reference table (illustrative — always verify with actual pyrometer readings for the specific billet)

| Billet size/shape category | Estimated heat loss rate (ambient shop air) |
|---|---|
| Small (under 2 lb) | ~25-30°F/min |
| Medium (2-10 lb) | ~15-20°F/min |
| Large (over 10 lb) | ~8-12°F/min |

**Rule:** these figures are illustrative and vary by billet geometry, surface area-to-volume ratio, and shop ambient conditions — always verify actual temperature with a pyrometer rather than calculating an estimate and assuming it's accurate. Use the table only to judge whether a given delay is likely significant enough to warrant a direct check.

## Grain-flow machining evaluation checklist

1. Identify the part's critical load-bearing surfaces and directions from the design specification.
2. Reference the part's known or expected grain flow pattern (from forging design intent or a macro-etch reference sample).
3. For any proposed finish machining operation, check whether the cut would remove material in a way that exposes grain flow ends at a critical load-bearing surface.
4. If grain flow exposure is a concern, flag to design/engineering for review before finalizing the machining plan — this is a structural decision, not purely a dimensional one.
5. Document the grain-flow evaluation and any design/engineering sign-off in the part's process record, especially for safety-critical or high-load components.
