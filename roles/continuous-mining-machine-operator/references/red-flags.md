# Continuous Mining Machine Operator — Red Flags

### Face methane reading at or above 1.0% CH4
- **Usually means:** insufficient face ventilation for current advance, or cutting has intersected a geologic gas pocket/feeder
- **First question:** has power been de-energized per 30 CFR §75.323 (all circuits except the methane monitor) and has an air reading been taken at the face right now?
- **Data to pull:** current and trailing-15-minute methane trend, ventilation survey for this heading, examiner's last on-shift reading

### On-board methane monitor reading flat or zero while a handheld/bag sample shows detectable gas
- **Usually means:** monitor miscalibration, or the sensor is mounted in a spot the current air split or roof geometry doesn't sweep
- **First question:** when was this monitor last calibrated, and where is it mounted relative to the cutting head and the return?
- **Data to pull:** calibration log and due date, monitor mounting spec, side-by-side handheld reading

### Cumulative unbolted advance at or approaching the roof control plan's depth limit (commonly 20 ft, up to 40 ft with an approved mounted temporary roof support system)
- **Usually means:** cutting is outpacing the bolt crew, or the plan's numeric limit is being treated as a guideline rather than a stop condition
- **First question:** what's the current unbolted footage measured from the last row of permanent support, against this section's specific plan limit?
- **Data to pull:** roof control plan depth limit for this section, current advance log, bolt-crew position and status

### Roof sounds drummy (hollow/dull) rather than solid (ringing) when sounded with a bar over newly exposed area
- **Usually means:** a separation or void has opened above the immediate roof, independent of how the roof looks visually
- **First question:** has this specific location been flagged and has cutting/bolting stopped here pending assessment?
- **Data to pull:** roof-sounding log for this entry, prior roof-fall or cutter history in this panel

### Operator or other personnel positioned inside the red zone (ranger arms, cutting head, conveyor boom) during tramming or cutting
- **Usually means:** remote/walk-along positioning discipline has lapsed, often to get a better sightline on the face
- **First question:** what specific task required entry into the zone, and could it have been done with the machine stopped and de-energized instead?
- **Data to pull:** proximity detection system alarm/violation log, task log for the period in question

### Machine running with a known permissibility defect (cracked casting, missing bolt, damaged cable) in a heading with methane potential
- **Usually means:** production pressure is overriding the shutdown requirement in 30 CFR §75.503
- **First question:** has the defect been logged in the exam book, and is methane currently present in this heading?
- **Data to pull:** on-shift examination book entry, permissibility defect record, current face methane reading

### Rock dust incombustible-content sample below the plan's floor (commonly 80%, higher in sections with elevated methane liberation, per 30 CFR §75.403)
- **Usually means:** dusting frequency hasn't kept pace with cutting or haulage traffic re-exposing coal dust on ribs and roof
- **First question:** when was this specific area last rock dusted, and does the sample reflect current conditions or a stale application?
- **Data to pull:** incombustible-content sample results and location, rock-dusting log, ventilation plan's minimum for this section

### Shuttle cars queuing or coal backing up at the miner's discharge conveyor
- **Usually means:** haulage cycle time (car count, tram distance, dump-point congestion) exceeds the miner's cutting/loading rate, not a cutting-rate problem
- **First question:** is this a one-shift anomaly, or a standing mismatch between fleet size and this section's current tram distance?
- **Data to pull:** car count and rated capacity, current tram distance to feeder-breaker, cycle-time log

### CPDM reading trending toward or above 1.5 mg/m3 full-shift TWA before the shift is over
- **Usually means:** scrubber airflow, water spray function, or face ventilation has degraded mid-shift
- **First question:** is the scrubber and water spray system confirmed functioning right now, at the current cutting position?
- **Data to pull:** live CPDM reading, scrubber airflow/water-spray inspection log, face air-quantity reading

### Fresh slips, cutters, or visible rib/roof cracking near the working face
- **Usually means:** localized ground-stress concentration, often near a pillar corner, intersection, or proximity to old workings/faulting
- **First question:** has this condition been reported to the section's roof-control function, and does the roof control plan already address this specific geology?
- **Data to pull:** geologic hazard map for the panel, prior roof-fall history nearby, current on-shift examination entry

### Ventilation air quantity at the last open crosscut below the plan's stated minimum
- **Usually means:** a stopping, curtain, or regulator has failed, or an unplanned short-circuit has opened between entries
- **First question:** has an air reading been taken at the current face position (not just the last open crosscut) since the possible short-circuit?
- **Data to pull:** current air-quantity survey, ventilation plan's stated minimum for this section, stopping/curtain inspection log

### Section production trending materially below plan (roughly >15%) for more than one consecutive shift without a named cause
- **Usually means:** one of haulage cycle time, roof-bolting lag, or gas-related stoppages hasn't been isolated and is being logged as general downtime
- **First question:** has the shortfall been broken down by cause (haulage vs. bolting vs. gas stoppage vs. other downtime), or logged as a general "ran slow"?
- **Data to pull:** shift production log against plan, downtime log by category, haulage cycle-time log
