# Continuous Mining Machine Operator — Vocabulary

### Red zone
The dynamic area around a continuous miner's ranger arms, cutting head, and conveyor boom where pinning, crushing, or striking hazards exist during tramming or cutting motion.
**In use:** "Stay out of the red zone on the boom side — I'm about to swing the head."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as a fixed physical boundary painted on the floor rather than a zone that moves with the machine's current motion and configuration.

### Sump / sumping
Driving the cutting head straight into the coal face to the plan's target depth before any lateral movement.
**In use:** "We're still sumping — hold the car until the shear starts."
**Common misuse:** Using "sump" and "shear" interchangeably; they're the two distinct phases of one cutting cycle, not synonyms.

### Shear (shearing cut)
The lateral sweep of the cutting head across the face after sumping, cutting the full entry width to the plan's target depth.
**In use:** "Shear's done — full 20-ft width, ready to trail forward for the next sump."
**Common misuse:** Confusing this with a longwall shearer, a different machine entirely that shears coal along a retreating longwall panel, not a room-and-pillar entry.

### Permissibility
The regulatory state in which every ignition-capable component on the machine (motors, switches, connectors) remains fully enclosed and sealed against flame propagation, governed by 30 CFR §75.503.
**In use:** "That enclosure's cracked — machine's non-permissible until it's replaced, not just noted for the next PM."
**Common misuse:** Treating permissibility as a maintenance quality spectrum ("mostly fine") instead of the binary go/no-go condition it actually is.

### Continuous Personal Dust Monitor (CPDM)
A worn device giving a real-time, running full-shift time-weighted-average respirable dust exposure reading, required for designated occupations including continuous miner operators.
**In use:** "CPDM's reading 1.3 at hour five — that's trending toward the 1.5 limit, check the scrubber now, not at end of shift."
**Common misuse:** Confusing a CPDM's continuous personal reading with a designated-occupation area sample, which is a separate, less time-resolved measurement.

### Incombustible content
The percentage of total dust (rock dust plus coal dust combined) in mine air and on surfaces that will not propagate a flame, with a regulatory minimum under 30 CFR §75.403.
**In use:** "Sample came back at 74% — that's under our 80% floor, we need to re-dust before this area sees more production."
**Common misuse:** Equating "we rock dusted recently" with meeting the standard, when the actual requirement is the measured combined-dust percentage, not the act of applying rock dust.

### Drummy roof
Roof that produces a hollow or dull tone rather than a solid ringing tone when struck with a sounding bar, indicating a separation or void above the immediate roof line.
**In use:** "Sounded drummy about six feet in from the last row — flag that section before the bolt crew moves under it."
**Common misuse:** Relying on a visual inspection alone and skipping the sounding check, since drummy roof frequently looks identical to solid roof.

### Proximity detection system (PDS)
A magnetic-field-based system, mandated by 30 CFR §75.1732 for continuous mining machines in most room-and-pillar applications, that tracks personnel position relative to the machine and slows or stops machine motion when someone enters a defined danger zone.
**In use:** "PDS cut the tram function — somebody's inside the warning zone, find them before resetting."
**Common misuse:** Treating the PDS as a substitute for positioning discipline ("it'll stop the machine if I'm too close") rather than as a backstop behind deliberate red-zone avoidance.

### Feeder-breaker
A stationary crusher and conveyor transfer point at the end of a section haulage road where shuttle cars dump their load, breaking oversize coal before it reaches the main belt.
**In use:** "Feeder-breaker's backed up — hold the next car until it clears."
**Common misuse:** Confusing the feeder-breaker with the section conveyor belt itself; the feeder-breaker is the transfer/crushing point that feeds the belt, not the belt.

### Continuous haulage
A bridge-conveyor system that replaces shuttle or ram cars for moving cut coal from the miner to the section belt, used in some sections in place of wheeled haulage.
**In use:** "This section's on continuous haulage, so the bottleneck math is a belt-capacity question, not a car-count one."
**Common misuse:** Assuming continuous haulage is universal; many sections, especially in variable seam conditions, still run shuttle or ram cars.

### Roof control plan
A site-specific, MSHA-approved document (per 30 CFR §§75.220–75.223) setting the bolt pattern, spacing, and unbolted cut-depth limit for a given mine and seam condition.
**In use:** "Our plan caps unbolted advance at 20 ft — that's this mine's number, not a general figure to assume elsewhere."
**Common misuse:** Treating roof control practice as generic industry guidance rather than an enforceable, mine-specific document that varies significantly between operations.

### Cutter (rib cutter / roof cutter)
A crack or fracture indicating localized stress concentration, often appearing near a pillar corner, intersection, or area of elevated overburden loading — distinct from a cutting bit.
**In use:** "There's a fresh cutter at the intersection corner — that's a stress indicator, get it looked at before we cut closer."
**Common misuse:** Confusing "cutter" (a ground-stress crack) with the tungsten carbide cutting bits mounted on the drum — a false-friend pair of terms that sound related but describe unrelated things.

### Ram car
A shuttle-style haulage vehicle that discharges its load via a hydraulic ram or chain conveyor rather than a moving conveyor bed, often used in low-seam or specific mining conditions.
**In use:** "Low seam here, so we're running ram cars instead of standard shuttle cars."
**Common misuse:** Treating "ram car" and "shuttle car" as fully interchangeable terms; they discharge differently, which changes dump-point cycle time in a haulage bottleneck calculation.

### Scrubber (flooded-bed scrubber)
A dust-capture system mounted on the continuous miner that draws air through a water spray bed at the cutting head, with its own airflow and water-pressure minimums separate from section ventilation.
**In use:** "Scrubber airflow's down — that's a dust problem even if the section's air quantity reading is fine."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a good section ventilation air-quantity reading means dust control at the cutting head is also fine; the scrubber is a separate system with its own minimum spec.

### Advance
The distance cut into the face during one cutting cycle (sump plus shear) before the area must be bolted, bounded by the roof control plan's depth limit.
**In use:** "We're at 18 ft of advance on this cut — two feet left before we hit the plan limit and have to bolt."
**Common misuse:** Tracking advance loosely by eye instead of measuring it against the plan's specific numeric limit for the section.

### Pillar
The block of coal left standing between adjacent entries to support the roof, sized by the mine's pillar design as part of the roof control plan.
**In use:** "That intersection's cutting into the pillar corner more than the plan calls for — check the design before advancing further."
**Common misuse:** Applying retreat-mining (pillar recovery) hazard assumptions to development-phase pillar cutting; the two phases carry materially different risk profiles and plan requirements.
