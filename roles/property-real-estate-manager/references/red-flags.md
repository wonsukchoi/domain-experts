# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced property/real estate/community association manager notices instantly. Load this when triaging a maintenance, reserve, or tenant question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### A maintenance item is being deferred purely to protect the current budget cycle
- **Usually means:** the deferral's actual cost (accelerated degradation, emergency-pricing risk) hasn't been quantified against the near-term savings
- **First question:** "What's this component's degradation curve, and what does deferring it cost in accelerated replacement risk vs. what it saves this cycle?"
- **Data to pull:** the maintenance item's age/condition data and any available cost comparison between planned and reactive/emergency handling

### Reserve funding is set below the reserve study's recommended schedule with no explicit board disclosure
- **Usually means:** underfunding is happening by default (to avoid an assessment increase) rather than as a deliberate, disclosed fiduciary decision
- **First question:** "Is the board/ownership aware, explicitly, that current funding is below the study's recommendation, and has that tradeoff been documented?"
- **Data to pull:** the reserve funding schedule tracker comparing current funding to the study's recommended annual amount

### A unit has been vacant for an extended period at an asking rent above comparable market data
- **Usually means:** the vacancy cost of holding the higher asking rent likely exceeds the value of the rent premium being protected
- **First question:** "What's the actual vacancy cost so far, and does it already exceed what a rent reduction to market level would cost over the likely remaining lease term?"
- **Data to pull:** rent/vacancy-cost worksheet with current vacancy duration and comparable market time-to-lease data

### A tenant action (especially eviction) is being expedited by skipping a required notice or documentation step
- **Usually means:** legal exposure is being created to save time, and the cost of a procedural misstep usually exceeds the time saved
- **First question:** "Has every legally required step (notice period, documentation, fair housing compliance) been completed and documented for this action?"
- **Data to pull:** the tenant action's compliance checklist and documentation trail

### A vendor is selected for a life-safety or structural system based primarily on lowest bid
- **Usually means:** licensing, insurance, and track record may not have received the elevated diligence this system category warrants
- **First question:** "Has this vendor been checked against the elevated diligence tier for this system category, not just standard vendor comparison?"
- **Data to pull:** the vendor qualification checklist for this specific system category

### A reserve study hasn't been updated in more than the standard review cycle
- **Usually means:** component costs and useful-life estimates in the current study may no longer reflect actual market conditions
- **First question:** "When was the reserve study last updated, and does the funding schedule still reflect current replacement costs?"
- **Data to pull:** the reserve study's date and the current market cost estimates for major components vs. what the study assumed

### A tenant dispute is being resolved informally/verbally without documentation
- **Usually means:** if the dispute escalates later, there's no contemporaneous record of what was agreed or communicated
- **First question:** "Is there a documented record of this resolution, and does it need to be for this type of issue?"
- **Data to pull:** the property's dispute/communication log for this tenant
