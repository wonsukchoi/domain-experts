# Red flags — what a production manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### Actual field decline rate running meaningfully above (commonly 2x+) the reservoir model's projected decline
- **Usually means:** the field may already be past sustainable yield — current extraction practice could be actively causing the accelerated decline, not just failing to prevent it.
- **First question:** "Is current extraction rate a plausible contributor to this gap between modeled and actual decline, and what happens if we push output higher anyway?"
- **Data to pull:** decline curve data (actual vs. modeled) over the trailing 12-24 months, current extraction rate relative to historical levels.

### A corporate or budget target for increased output with no check against current decline trend or reservoir-wide pressure data
- **Usually means:** the target may be set without accounting for whether the field can actually sustain higher output without accelerating decline further.
- **First question:** "Does this target account for the field's current decline trajectory, or was it set independent of that data?"
- **Data to pull:** the target-setting rationale, current decline curve and pressure trend data for the field.

### A well or field production decision evaluated in isolation without checking effect on reservoir-wide pressure
- **Usually means:** a locally optimal decision for one well may degrade shared reservoir pressure and neighboring well output, a cost that won't show up in that well's own numbers.
- **First question:** "What's this well's effect on reservoir-wide pressure, not just its own output number?"
- **Data to pull:** reservoir-wide pressure monitoring data, the specific well's draw relative to its neighbors.

### A significant new-well or workover capital commitment based on a reservoir model with limited production history (commonly under 5 years) presented with high confidence
- **Usually means:** the model's uncertainty may not be adequately reflected in the capital decision, risking a large stranded-capital loss if the subsurface behavior deviates from the model.
- **First question:** "How many years of production history does this reservoir model rest on, and does the capital commitment's risk framing reflect that uncertainty?"
- **Data to pull:** the field's production history length, the model's stated confidence range or uncertainty bounds, comparable field outcomes where models proved wrong.

### Reinjection practice unchanged for years with no active review against current reservoir behavior
- **Usually means:** reinjection may be operating as a set-once compliance parameter rather than an active reservoir-management lever, missing opportunities to improve pressure maintenance or risking a problem (thermal breakthrough, seismicity) that's developed since it was last reviewed.
- **First question:** "When was reinjection location/rate/temperature last actively reviewed against current reservoir data, not just checked for compliance?"
- **Data to pull:** reinjection parameters and review history, current thermal breakthrough or pressure-maintenance indicators.

### Seismic activity near a reinjection zone increasing without triggering a defined response protocol
- **Usually means:** induced seismicity monitoring may be treated as passive data collection rather than an active operational trigger with real community and safety stakes.
- **First question:** "Does this seismic activity level trigger our defined response protocol, and has that protocol actually been activated?"
- **Data to pull:** seismic monitoring data and trend, the defined traffic-light response protocol and its trigger thresholds.

### An output target being met by increasing extraction rate on the same wells already showing the steepest decline
- **Usually means:** the response is treating the symptom (short-term output) while likely worsening the underlying cause (reservoir stress on already-declining wells).
- **First question:** "Are the wells being pushed harder the same ones already showing above-average decline, and if so, why?"
- **Data to pull:** per-well decline rates, extraction rate changes by well over the recent period.
