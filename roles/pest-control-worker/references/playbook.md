# Pest control treatment and documentation playbook

Filled worksheets for the four recurring pest-control decisions: method selection at barrier gaps, dilution/rate math, WDO report field entries, and warranty-type selection.

## 1. Trench-vs-rod method-switch worksheet

Use whenever the treatable perimeter includes both open soil and a slab/patio/walkway obstruction. The finished rate is identical across methods — only delivery changes.

| Section | Linear ft | Depth (ft) | Method | Rate basis | Finished dilution needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Exposed soil, north/east sides | 125 | 4 | Trench-and-treat | 4 gal / 10 lf / ft depth | 125 × 4 × 0.4 = 200 gal |
| Patio slab, south side | 40 | 4 | Rod injection through slab | Same: 4 gal / 10 lf / ft depth | 40 × 4 × 0.4 = 64 gal |
| **Total barrier** | **165** | — | — | — | **264 gal** |

Rule: no linear foot of the perimeter is ever left off this table. If a section can't be trenched, it gets its own row with an alternate method — it does not disappear from the total.

## 2. Dilution / concentrate calculator

```
oz concentrate per gallon finished dilution = (target finished % ÷ concentrate % AI) × 128 oz/gal

Total concentrate (oz) = oz/gal × total finished-dilution gallons
Total concentrate (gal) = total concentrate (oz) ÷ 128
```

| Target finished % | Concentrate % AI | oz concentrate / gal | For 264 gal total | Concentrate needed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.06% (label minimum, low pressure) | 9.1% | 0.84 oz/gal | 264 gal | 222 oz ≈ 1.73 gal |
| 0.08% (moderate/confirmed activity) | 9.1% | 1.13 oz/gal | 264 gal | 298 oz ≈ 2.33 gal |
| 0.125% (label maximum, high pressure/prior failure) | 9.1% | 1.76 oz/gal | 264 gal | 464 oz ≈ 3.63 gal |

Rule: never dilute above the label maximum to "make sure it works" — if 0.125% at the documented job size still isn't holding, the fix is re-inspecting for a missed conducive condition or barrier gap, not exceeding the label.

## 3. WDO / NPMA-33 field-entry template

Fill one block per structural area inspected. Never write a finding for an area not physically accessed.

```
Structural area: <e.g., crawlspace, subarea below bath, garage sill plate>
Accessible: <yes / no — if no, state what blocked access: insulation, stored items, no crawl access>
Finding: <no visible evidence / visible evidence of live infestation / visible evidence of previous treatment / visible damage>
Organism identified (if any): <species — e.g., subterranean termite, drywood termite, wood-boring beetle>
Conducive conditions noted: <wood-to-soil contact, moisture intrusion, cellulose debris, inadequate ventilation>
Damage assessed by PCO: <observed only — no structural opinion rendered> or <referred to structural evaluator, name/date>
Treatment history found: <yes/no — company, date, product if determinable from tags/stickers>
```

Rule: "visible evidence of previous treatment" and "visible damage" are separate checkboxes on the real form — a section can carry both, one, or neither. Never collapse them into a single "looks fine" line.

## 4. Warranty-type decision table

| Situation | Default warranty | Why |
|---|---|---|
| First liquid/bait treatment, no prior damage found | Retreatment guarantee, 1-year renewable | Covers re-service if activity returns; cheapest to underwrite, most common default |
| Customer specifically requests structural-damage coverage | Repair guarantee, priced and underwritten separately | Repair guarantee is a cost commitment on future structural loss, not a checkbox add-on — requires its own pricing and often its own inspection standard (e.g., no pre-existing damage beyond a stated threshold) |
| Renewal after a lapsed warranty period | New inspection required before renewal quote | A lapsed warranty means no continuous coverage record — treat as new risk, not an automatic renewal |
| Reinfestation found during warranty period | Retreat under existing guarantee; do not upsell repair guarantee at the same visit | Mixing a warranty claim visit with a new-product sale creates a conflict-of-interest appearance in the file |

## 5. Bed bug / German cockroach follow-up cadence

```
Visit 1 (initial): inspect, identify, treat (bed bug: crack-and-crevice + steam/heat as applicable;
  German cockroach: gel bait placement + monitor sticky traps, not broadcast spray)
Visit 2 (~2 weeks later): re-inspect monitors, retreat active harborage only
Visit 3 (~2 weeks after visit 2): confirm zero activity on monitors before closing job
```

Rule: closing a bed bug job after one visit is a scheduling shortcut, not a treatment decision — egg incubation routinely outlasts the residual of the first application.
