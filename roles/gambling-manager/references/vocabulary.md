# Working vocabulary

Terms of art practitioners use precisely, that generalists misuse. Load this when writing or reviewing content that needs to sound like an actual gaming manager, not a generalist describing one.

### Hold percentage
The actual percentage of money wagered that the house retains over a period, distinct from theoretical hold (the mathematical house edge for the game/rules variant).
**In use:** "Theoretical on this table is 1.2%, but actual hold this month is running 1.6% — that's within normal variance for the volume, not a signal."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a fixed, expected outcome for any given night — hold is a long-run average; short-run actual hold swings widely due to variance and isn't itself evidence of anything unusual until it's statistically extreme for the sample size.

### Theo (theoretical loss)
The expected amount a player will lose based on average bet, pace of play, hours played, and the game's house edge — used to price comps and credit, independent of actual session outcome.
**In use:** "He won $5,000 tonight, but his theo for the session was only $300 — comp him based on the $300, not the win."
**Common misuse:** Confused with actual win/loss — a player who wins big still has a calculable theo based on their play pattern, and that's the number that should drive the comp decision, not the session result.

### Advantage play
Legal techniques that shift edge toward the player using only public information and no equipment manipulation or collusion — card counting, shuffle tracking, hole-carding (when the exposure is a dealer procedure flaw, not player-caused tampering).
**In use:** "That's advantage play, not cheating — no marked cards, no collusion, just a sharp bet spread on true count. Response is a shuffle change or bet-spread limit, not an accusation."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with cheating — advantage play is legal in most jurisdictions (though a house can still refuse service/adjust rules); treating it as a security or law-enforcement matter creates real legal and reputational exposure.

### Cheating (game integrity violation)
Techniques requiring equipment manipulation, marked cards, collusion between players or with staff, or physical tampering (past-posting, capping bets after the outcome is known).
**In use:** "That's past-posting — he capped his bet after the card was already exposed. That's cheating, escalate to security and pull the surveillance timestamp."
**Common misuse:** Used loosely for any skilled or unusually successful play — the defining feature is the method (tampering, collusion, marked equipment), not the outcome (winning a lot).

### CTR (Currency Transaction Report)
A mandatory US federal filing (Bank Secrecy Act / FinCEN) triggered when a patron's aggregated cash transactions in a single gaming day meet or exceed a set threshold (currently $10,000, verify current figure).
**In use:** "Aggregate cash-in today is $10,400 for this patron across two cage windows — that's a CTR, file it."
**Common misuse:** Treated as discretionary based on whether the patron "seems legitimate" — it's a mechanical threshold trigger with no discretion once the aggregate is met.

### Structuring
Intentionally breaking up transactions to stay under a reporting threshold — itself a suspicious-activity indicator and potentially a separate federal offense, regardless of the legitimacy of the underlying funds.
**In use:** "Three transactions of $9,000 each across three cage windows in one day — that's a structuring pattern, file a SAR even though none of them individually hit the CTR threshold."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to only matter if the CTR was actually avoided — the pattern of apparent threshold-avoidance is itself the flag, independent of whether a report was technically triggered.

### Bet spread
The ratio between a player's maximum and minimum bet during a session, used as one (not sole) indicator of possible advantage play when correlated with game conditions.
**In use:** "8:1 spread correlated with true count — worth a surveillance review, though spread alone isn't proof of counting."
**Common misuse:** Treated as proof of advantage play by itself — spread has to be evaluated together with correlation to actual favorable conditions (count, shoe composition), not as a standalone red flag.

### Surveillance (eye-in-the-sky)
The independent camera/monitoring function reviewing floor activity, distinct from floor management's own observation — its independence is what makes it useful for both game protection and dispute resolution.
**In use:** "Pull the eye-in-the-sky footage before making a call on this — floor recollection alone isn't enough for a game-integrity decision."
**Common misuse:** Treated as redundant with floor supervisors' own observation — surveillance's value comes specifically from being a separate, recorded, independently-reviewable record, not from duplicating what the floor already saw.
