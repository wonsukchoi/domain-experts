# Red flags

Smell tests an operator catches before a capacity, stability, following-sea, or briefing situation turns into an incident. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before committing to a departure decision.

### Passenger headcount at or above the COI's max-persons number

- **Usually means:** the booking was sold on deck space or vibe rather than reconciled against the certificated stability-test condition.
- **First question:** "What does the COI actually say — not the builder's weight plate, the COI?"
- **Data to pull:** posted Certificate of Inspection, current manifest/headcount, crew count included in that total.

### Operator or dispatch citing the hull's structural weight-capacity plate to justify a bigger group

- **Usually means:** the flotation/structural number is being substituted for the COI's persons ceiling, which tests a different thing (heel and stability at a specific loading condition, not total buoyancy).
- **First question:** "Is that the COI's persons number, or the builder's max-weight plate?"
- **Data to pull:** COI max-persons figure, builder capacity plate, current fuel/gear weight aboard.

### Vessel modified (added seating, added deck fixtures, added upper-deck weight) since the COI was last issued

- **Usually means:** the vessel's tested loading condition no longer matches its actual configuration, and the certificated persons number may no longer be valid.
- **First question:** "Has this modification been reviewed against the current stability letter, or just installed?"
- **Data to pull:** COI issuance date, modification/work order records, any post-modification stability review.

### Route includes a following or quartering sea with forecast wave period implying a wavelength near or beyond the hull's waterline length

- **Usually means:** conditions for surf-riding and broach risk are developing — a small planing hull can be lifted and steered by a single wave in a way a large vessel isn't.
- **First question:** "What's the wave period telling us about wavelength versus our hull length, not just the forecast wave height?"
- **Data to pull:** marine forecast wave period and height, hull waterline length, planned heading relative to the sea.

### Trip plan runs the boat dead before a breaking following sea or straight into a bar/inlet crossing

- **Usually means:** the operator is prioritizing the shortest course over the steerage-preserving quartering approach.
- **First question:** "Are we quartering this sea, or running straight down it?"
- **Data to pull:** planned heading relative to sea direction, bar/inlet condition report if available, alternative route or timing options.

### OUPV ("six-pack") license holder operating with more than 6 passengers for hire

- **Usually means:** the vessel or trip has crossed into inspected small-passenger-vessel territory (46 CFR Subchapter T) without the required COI or license tier.
- **First question:** "How many passengers for hire are actually aboard, and does this license and this vessel cover that number?"
- **Data to pull:** operator's license class and endorsements, vessel's inspection status, actual passenger-for-hire count.

### Pre-departure passenger safety briefing skipped or abbreviated for a "regular" group

- **Usually means:** familiarity is being substituted for the actual life-safety system on a vessel with no separate muster stations or lifeboats.
- **First question:** "Was the full briefing — PFD location, muster point, MOB procedure — actually given today, or assumed from last time?"
- **Data to pull:** trip manifest/log entry for the briefing, time it was given relative to departure.

### No float plan or shore check-in scheduled for a route without VTS or reliable cell coverage

- **Usually means:** the trip has no external trigger for someone to notice it's overdue.
- **First question:** "Who's expecting a check-in call from us, and by when?"
- **Data to pull:** filed float plan, designated shore contact, scheduled check-in time.

### VHF channel 16 not actively monitored while underway

- **Usually means:** the vessel is relying on visual lookout alone for hailing/distress traffic on a route where no VTS is watching either.
- **First question:** "Is 16 actually up and monitored right now, or just powered on?"
- **Data to pull:** radio log/monitoring confirmation, last time a working channel check was done.

### Man-overboard response plan assumes more than about 10 minutes of active swimming ability in cold water

- **Usually means:** the response plan is budgeted against the 1-hour hypothermia stage instead of the 10-minute functional-movement cutoff of the 1-10-1 principle.
- **First question:** "Are we planning recovery for the 10-minute window, or hoping they can hold on for an hour?"
- **Data to pull:** water temperature, elapsed time since the person went in, recovery method actually staged aboard (ladder, sling, crew positions).

### Fuel or gear added before a trip without re-checking total persons load against the COI's tested condition

- **Usually means:** the vessel's actual load is drifting away from the certificated condition one addition at a time, unnoticed because no single addition looks significant.
- **First question:** "What's the combined persons-plus-gear-plus-fuel load right now versus what the COI was tested at?"
- **Data to pull:** current fuel log, gear manifest, COI tested persons load for comparison.

### PFD count or sizing doesn't match actual POB (child sizes needed but only adult sizes aboard)

- **Usually means:** PFD inventory was set for a typical trip rather than checked against today's actual passenger mix.
- **First question:** "Do we have the right sizes for who's actually boarding today, not just the right count?"
- **Data to pull:** PFD inventory by size, passenger manifest including ages/sizes where known.

### Vessel trimmed noticeably by the stern with a following sea in the forecast

- **Usually means:** aft-loaded weight (fuel, gear, passenger seating arrangement) is reducing stern freeboard right when following-sea broach risk depends on how much stern lift a wave can produce.
- **First question:** "What's driving the stern-down trim, and can it be redistributed before we run this sea?"
- **Data to pull:** current trim observation, fuel/gear/passenger distribution, options to redistribute weight forward or amidships.
