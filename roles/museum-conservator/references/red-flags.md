# Museum Conservator — Red Flags

### Insect trap count exceeds 3x the trailing 12-month average for that location
- **Usually means:** An active infestation has established near that trap (larvae feeding inside an object or nearby organic material), most likely a textile or natural-history specimen; second most likely is a seasonal transient influx from an exterior source (loading dock, propped door) that isn't yet inside a case.
- **First question:** Is the count concentrated near a specific object or shelving unit, or spread evenly across the room?
- **Data to pull:** The IPM trap log for that location over the trailing 12 months, and the last condition report for any object stored within 2m of the flagged trap.

### RH excursion beyond the set parameter for more than 24 hours
- **Usually means:** HVAC failure or a seasonal changeover the building's system can't keep pace with; second most likely is a door or loading dock left open during installation/deinstallation.
- **First question:** Is this an isolated reading (logger malfunction) or confirmed by a second logger or manual psychrometer reading in the same space?
- **Data to pull:** Data-logger history for the space (not just the single reading) and the facility's HVAC service ticket log for the same window.

### New flaking, cupping, or lifting paint/media not present in the last condition report
- **Usually means:** An RH or temperature cycling event (a new source of vibration, a changed HVAC schedule, a recent move) stressed the paint/substrate interface; second most likely is inherent vice in the original materials reaching a threshold independent of environment.
- **First question:** When was the object last moved, and has the environmental log for its location shown any excursions since the last report?
- **Data to pull:** Environmental log for the object's location since the last condition report; move/loan history for the object.

### Object returns from loan with condition changes not noted on the outgoing condition report
- **Usually means:** Damage occurred in transit or at the borrowing venue and wasn't caught at incoming inspection there, or handling during installation/deinstallation caused it; less likely, the outgoing report was incomplete.
- **First question:** Does the borrowing venue's facility report and courier report note any incident during the loan period?
- **Data to pull:** Outgoing and incoming condition reports side by side, courier report, and the borrowing venue's incident log if one exists.

### A treatment proposal has no named alternative considered
- **Usually means:** The proposal was written after the treatment was already decided (or already started), turning the approval step into a formality rather than a real risk/benefit decision.
- **First question:** What treatment would be used if the first-choice material or method weren't available — has that actually been thought through?
- **Data to pull:** The treatment proposal draft and the conservator's treatment notes/bench log for the object.

### Condition report cites "consistent with prior repair" but no treatment report exists in the object's file
- **Usually means:** A repair was made before current documentation standards were in place, or a past intervention was never written up; either way, the object's history has a gap.
- **First question:** Does any accession file, insurance appraisal, or exhibition history note a prior conservator or restorer working on this object?
- **Data to pull:** Full accession file, any prior insurance/appraisal documentation, exhibition loan history.

### Object scheduled for display has no lux-hour budget tracked for the year
- **Usually means:** Light-sensitive material is being displayed on the exhibition calendar's schedule rather than a conservation exposure budget — common when exhibition planning and conservation review happen on separate timelines.
- **First question:** What is this object's material sensitivity class, and how many lux-hours has it already accumulated on display this year?
- **Data to pull:** Light-level logger data for the case/gallery and the object's display history for the current calendar year.

### Composite object's treatment plan is based on only one material component's requirements
- **Usually means:** The most visible material (e.g., a wood substrate) drove the RH/handling plan while a more sensitive layer (paint, adhesive, textile facing) wasn't separately assessed.
- **First question:** Has each material class in this object been assessed against its own tolerance, or was one number applied across the whole object?
- **Data to pull:** Material identification notes for each layer/component and each material's known RH/temperature tolerance range.

### Mold observed on an object or in a storage area
- **Usually means:** A recent or ongoing moisture event (leak, RH spike, condensation from a cold surface) — mold does not appear without a moisture source, so the environmental cause is still active until found and fixed.
- **First question:** Is the mold currently active (moist, growing) or dormant/dead (dry, powdery, no longer spreading)?
- **Data to pull:** Environmental log for the space over the prior 30 days and a facility inspection for water intrusion (roof, pipe, HVAC condensate line).

### A treatment is proposed as "reversible" using a material older than ~15 years in the conservation literature
- **Usually means:** The reversibility claim was accurate when the material was introduced but hasn't been re-verified against how that material actually behaves after decades of aging — some early "reversible" adhesives and consolidants cross-link or become insoluble over time.
- **First question:** Has this specific material's long-term aging behavior been checked against current (not original) conservation literature?
- **Data to pull:** Current AIC or conservation-science literature on the specific product/material's aging behavior, not just its original technical data sheet.
