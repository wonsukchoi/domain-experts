# Government Property Inspector and Investigator — Playbook

## Physical inventory reconciliation and extrapolation (filled example)

| Step | Value |
|---|---|
| Recorded population | 500 units @ $1,200/unit = $600,000 |
| Sample size | 50 units (10%) |
| Sample discrepancies found | 3 units |
| Sample discrepancy rate | 3/50 = 6% |
| Initial extrapolated exposure | 6% × 500 = 30 units × $1,200 = $36,000 |
| Root-cause resolution | 1 of 3 discrepancies = records error (properly disposed, not recorded) |
| Corrected sample discrepancy rate | 2/50 = 4% |
| **Corrected extrapolated exposure** | 4% × 500 = 20 units × $1,200 = **$24,000** |
| **Decision** | Exposure still material — recommend full (100%) inventory |

**Use:** Never stop at the raw sample discrepancy rate — always investigate root cause on each discrepancy first (records error vs. real loss), then recalculate the extrapolation before deciding whether the exposure is significant enough to warrant expanded inventory.

## Loss reporting timeliness check (filled example)

| Item | Last confirmed inventory | Loss discovered/discoverable | Report filed | Required reporting window | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unit 2 | 6 months ago | 6 months ago (at last inventory) | Not yet filed | Contract-specified (commonly days from discovery) | **Violation — report is 6 months late relative to required window** |
| Unit 3 | 6 months ago | 6 months ago (at last inventory) | Not yet filed | Contract-specified | **Violation — same as above** |

**Use:** Measure the reporting-timeliness violation from the actual (or last reasonably possible) discovery date, not from when the report was eventually filed — the gap itself is the finding, separate from the underlying loss.

## Property management system outcome-area evaluation table (illustrative)

| Outcome area | Requirement | Evaluated independently? |
|---|---|---|
| Records | Accurate, timely, reconciled to physical inventory | Yes — check reconciliation rate and update timeliness |
| Physical inventory results | Periodic inventory performed, discrepancies investigated | Yes — check inventory cycle compliance and discrepancy resolution |
| Utilization | Property is being used for its intended contract purpose | Yes — check for idle/unused property without disposition action |
| Maintenance | Property is maintained per applicable standards | Yes — check maintenance records against schedule |
| Subcontractor control | Subcontractor-held property is tracked by the prime | Yes — check subcontractor holdings appear in prime's records |
| Disposition | Screening and authorization process followed before disposal | Yes — check disposal records against screening documentation |

**Use:** Score and report each outcome area independently — a contractor passing 5 of 6 areas still has a standalone corrective action required for the failing area, regardless of overall system impression.

## Findings memo — filled example

> **Property Inspection Findings — Contract [ref]**
> Sample inventory: 50 of 500 accountable units checked; 3 discrepancies found (6% sample rate).
> Root cause: 1 unit — records error (properly disposed, record not updated). 2 units — genuine unreported loss.
> Corrected discrepancy rate: 4% (2/50), extrapolated exposure: **$24,000** across the full population.
> **Finding 1 (High):** Undisposed loss of 2 units, discoverable at last inventory (6 months prior) but never reported — exceeds required reporting window. Immediate loss report and investigation required.
> **Finding 2 (Medium):** Records accuracy failure — 1 unit's disposition was not reflected in property records.
> **Recommendation:** Full (100%) physical inventory required given the extrapolated systemic exposure; sample result alone is insufficient to close this finding.
