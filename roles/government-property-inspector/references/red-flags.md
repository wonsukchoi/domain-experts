### A physical inventory sample discrepancy rate exceeds a few percent
- **Usually means:** A systemic records or control issue rather than isolated one-off errors — worth extrapolating to the full population before concluding the exposure is minor.
- **First question:** What is the extrapolated exposure across the full property population at this discrepancy rate, and does it warrant an expanded or full inventory?
- **Data to pull:** Sample size and discrepancy count, full population count and unit value.

### A discrepant item has no disposition record and no loss/damage/destruction report
- **Usually means:** A genuine unreported loss, as opposed to a records-update failure for a properly disposed item.
- **First question:** Is there any record (screening, scrap authorization, transfer document) showing this item left inventory through an approved process?
- **Data to pull:** Disposition/screening records, transfer authorization records.

### A loss/damage/destruction report was filed well after the item's last documented inventory check
- **Usually means:** A reporting-timeliness violation separate from the loss itself — the contract's required reporting window is typically measured from actual discovery, and a long gap since the last inventory raises the question of when discovery actually occurred.
- **First question:** When was this loss actually discovered, and how does that date compare to the contract's required reporting window?
- **Data to pull:** Last inventory date, loss report filing date, contract's specified reporting timeframe.

### Idle or unused government property is found at a contractor site with no active disposition process
- **Usually means:** Either neglect of the disposition/screening requirement, or an early-stage situation where the process hasn't yet been initiated — needs to be distinguished before treating it as a violation.
- **First question:** Has a disposition/screening request been initiated for this property, and if not, how long has it been idle?
- **Data to pull:** Property utilization records, disposition/screening request log.

### Government-furnished property is being used on a contract other than the one it was furnished under
- **Usually means:** Unauthorized use, which is a more serious finding than an administrative records issue and may warrant a fraud referral depending on scope and intent.
- **First question:** Was there any specific authorization (contract modification, agency approval) permitting cross-contract use of this property?
- **Data to pull:** Original GFP authorization/contract reference, actual usage records showing which contract the property was used on.

### A contractor's property management system shows strong performance in most outcome areas but a clear failure in one specific area
- **Usually means:** An overall favorable impression can mask a genuine, standalone failure that needs its own corrective action, independent of the system's general strength.
- **First question:** Does this specific outcome area (e.g., subcontractor control, disposition) meet its own defined requirement, regardless of how the system performs overall?
- **Data to pull:** Outcome-area-specific audit results, not just an aggregate system score.

### A subcontractor holding government property has not been included in the prime contractor's property control system
- **Usually means:** A subcontractor control gap — the prime contractor remains responsible for property in a subcontractor's possession, and excluding it from the tracking system is itself a finding.
- **First question:** Is this subcontractor's property holdings reflected in the prime contractor's property records and inventory process?
- **Data to pull:** Prime contractor's property record scope, subcontractor agreement's property flow-down terms.

### A records reconciliation discrepancy is dismissed as "immaterial" without investigating root cause
- **Usually means:** Skipping root-cause investigation on the assumption that a small discrepancy doesn't matter, when even a small sample-level discrepancy can extrapolate to meaningful exposure at scale.
- **First question:** What is this discrepancy's extrapolated value across the full property population, and has root cause actually been determined?
- **Data to pull:** Extrapolated exposure calculation, root-cause investigation notes (or absence thereof).
