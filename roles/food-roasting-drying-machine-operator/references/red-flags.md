# Red flags

### Total process time projected by linearly extrapolating an early-stage moisture loss rate
- **Usually means:** the batch will be underprocessed, since moisture loss rate declines significantly as the process continues.
- **First question:** what does this product's actual validated moisture-loss curve indicate for total time versus the linear extrapolation?
- **Data to pull:** early-stage moisture loss rate compared against the product's documented full moisture-loss curve and validated total process time.

### Batch released based on correct surface color without verifying internal moisture/doneness directly
- **Usually means:** the internal center may lag behind the surface, especially on larger particle sizes or batches.
- **First question:** what does a direct moisture or internal doneness measurement show, not just the surface color reading?
- **Data to pull:** internal moisture/doneness measurement compared against specification, alongside the surface color result.

### Process controlled only to a final target temperature/time rather than monitoring the full temperature/time profile
- **Usually means:** a process deviation mid-cycle could produce a different internal result at the same nominal endpoint.
- **First question:** does the actual temperature-vs-time profile match the validated path, not just the final reading?
- **Data to pull:** actual temperature/time profile log compared against the validated profile for this product.

### A characteristic process transition point assumed to occur at a fixed elapsed time rather than actively monitored
- **Usually means:** the actual timing may have shifted with this batch's size, moisture, or heat application rate, risking under- or over-processing if the assumed time doesn't match reality.
- **First question:** was the transition point's actual occurrence time observed and logged for this batch?
- **Data to pull:** observed transition point timing compared against the assumed/prior-batch timing.

### A validated process scaled to a larger batch size using the same time/temperature settings without direct outcome verification
- **Usually means:** changed heat/airflow dynamics at the new load may produce a different result than the validated smaller batch.
- **First question:** has actual product outcome been verified via direct testing at this new batch size?
- **Data to pull:** product test results at the new batch size compared against the previously validated smaller-batch results.

### A recurring underprocessing or quality complaint pattern for a specific product appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — moisture curve assumption, profile deviation, batch scaling — rather than isolated one-off run variation.
- **First question:** does the complaint/defect log correlate with a specific process parameter or batch size trend?
- **Data to pull:** complaint/defect occurrence log compared against process parameter logs (profile, batch size, transition timing) over the same period.

### Moisture content verified only at the end of the process, with no intermediate check during a long processing cycle
- **Usually means:** a deviation from the expected declining-rate curve wouldn't be caught until the batch is already fully processed and potentially off-spec.
- **First question:** were intermediate moisture checks taken during the run to confirm the curve is tracking as expected?
- **Data to pull:** intermediate moisture measurement log compared against the expected declining-rate curve at those checkpoints.
