# Vocabulary

### Falling rate period (drying)
The phase of a drying or roasting process, following the initial constant-rate period, during which moisture loss rate progressively declines as remaining moisture becomes more tightly bound.
**In use:** "We're well into the falling rate period now — don't expect the same points-per-minute loss we saw at the start."
**Common misuse:** Assuming the moisture loss rate observed early in a process (the constant-rate period) continues at the same pace throughout, rather than recognizing the falling rate period that follows.

### Constant rate period (drying)
The initial phase of a drying process when abundant surface/free moisture is removed at a roughly steady rate, before the falling rate period begins.
**In use:** "That 0.5 point-per-minute figure is a constant-rate-period number — it won't hold once we're into the falling rate phase."
**Common misuse:** Treating the constant-rate period's loss rate as representative of the entire process, rather than as the fast, early phase that inevitably slows.

### Color measurement (proxy indicator)
An instrument or visual assessment of a product's surface color, commonly used as a proxy for roast/dry progress but distinct from direct internal verification.
**In use:** "Agtron score's on target, but that's surface color — still need the internal moisture check before release."
**Common misuse:** Treating surface color measurement as sufficient confirmation of internal doneness, when color and internal transformation are governed by different rates.

### Temperature/time profile
The full path of temperature (and often airflow) over the duration of a process, as distinct from just the peak temperature or total elapsed time.
**In use:** "Two batches hit the same peak temp, but the profile leading up to it was different — that's why the internal results differ."
**Common misuse:** Verifying a process only by its final endpoint (peak temperature reached, or total time elapsed) rather than monitoring the actual profile path for deviations.

### Transition point (process marker)
A characteristic, observable physical or chemical marker during a roasting/drying process indicating a specific stage of progress, whose actual timing must be monitored rather than assumed fixed.
**In use:** "Transition point hit later than last batch — that tells us this load's moisture or size is different, not that the process is broken."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a characteristic transition point will occur at the same elapsed time as a prior batch, rather than actively monitoring for its actual occurrence each run.

### Batch loading density
The amount of product loaded per unit area or volume in a roasting/drying unit, which affects airflow penetration and heat distribution through the batch.
**In use:** "Loading density's higher this run — verify the profile still delivers even heat/airflow through the full batch depth."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a process validated at one loading density automatically produces the same result at a higher density, without reassessing airflow and heat distribution.

### Internal doneness / internal moisture
The actual state of transformation or moisture content at a product's internal center, as opposed to its surface, which is what final specifications are typically based on.
**In use:** "Surface looks perfectly roasted, but check internal moisture before calling this batch complete."
**Common misuse:** Assuming surface appearance represents the whole product's state, when internal transformation commonly lags behind surface development.

### Moisture analyzer (loss-on-drying / near-infrared)
An instrument used to directly measure a product's actual moisture content, as opposed to inferring it from process time or temperature.
**In use:** "Ran the moisture analyzer at the 30-minute mark — confirmed we're still above target, not done yet."
**Common misuse:** Relying on elapsed process time as a proxy for moisture content rather than taking a direct instrument measurement.

### Scale-up (roasting/drying)
The process of moving a validated roasting/drying process from a smaller batch size to a larger one, which does not automatically preserve the same process outcome.
**In use:** "This profile's validated at 50kg — re-verify actual moisture and doneness before running it at 200kg."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a validated process's time/temperature settings transfer directly to a larger batch size without re-verification of the actual outcome.

### Process validation (batch record)
Documentation establishing that a specific process profile reliably produces a product meeting specification, based on actual testing rather than assumption.
**In use:** "This 45-minute figure comes from validated moisture-curve testing, not a guess extrapolated from the first ten minutes."
**Common misuse:** Treating an untested extrapolation or assumption as equivalent to actual process validation based on direct product testing.
