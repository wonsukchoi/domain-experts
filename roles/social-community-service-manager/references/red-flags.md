# Red flags — what a program director notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one can be innocent; two or three together on the same program is a pattern worth escalating before the next audit or funder site visit finds it first.

### Restricted-fund balance negative for 30+ days
- **Usually means:** the program spent against a grant faster than it was drawn down or received, most often because a reimbursement request was late or a drawdown was miscalculated against the wrong period.
- **First question:** "Which specific draw is outstanding, and when was it submitted?"
- **Data to pull:** grant-by-grant fund balance report, drawdown submission log with dates, the line of credit balance and available capacity.

### Caseload at 1.4x the program's staffing-model ratio for a full quarter
- **Usually means:** vacancies aren't being backfilled fast enough for current turnover, or the staffing model was never rebuilt after the last round of attrition.
- **First question:** "Is this a vacancy problem or a caseload-target problem — was the target ever realistic against actual filled headcount?"
- **Data to pull:** vacancy-adjusted FTE by program over the trailing 12 months, current caseload by case manager, time-to-fill by role.

### Case-management turnover above 50% annualized in a single program
- **Usually means:** burnout from sustained above-ratio caseload, a pay gap against comparable local roles, or a specific supervisor/team dynamic — turnover concentrated in one program rather than agency-wide points away from a compensation-only explanation.
- **First question:** "Is turnover concentrated under one supervisor or spread evenly across the program?"
- **Data to pull:** exit interview themes by program and supervisor, caseload history for departed staff in their final quarter, comparable-role pay data.

### A reimbursement-basis drawdown more than 45 days past its billing period with no submission
- **Usually means:** finance staff capacity hasn't kept pace with the number of active grants, or a documentation gap (missing timesheets, unreconciled expenses) is blocking submission.
- **First question:** "What specifically is blocking this draw from going out today?"
- **Data to pull:** drawdown submission calendar vs. actuals for the last 4 quarters, outstanding documentation checklist for the blocked draw.

### Recovered indirect dollars flat while total organizational overhead cost is rising
- **Usually means:** new restricted grants were added without a matching uncapped/unrestricted source, so the indirect-recovery gap (see `playbook.md` §1) is widening and being absorbed by underfunding a real overhead function.
- **First question:** "What is the ratio of uncapped flexible dollars to the total indirect gap this year, versus last year?"
- **Data to pull:** indirect-cost reconciliation by funding source, overhead department budgets (finance, HR, IT, facilities) vs. actual spend for 3 years.

### A funder outcome metric trending toward its contractual floor (e.g., HUD APR exits-to-permanent-housing approaching the 80% threshold from above)
- **Usually means:** either a genuinely harder intake cohort this reporting period, or informal pressure building to decline harder referrals to protect the number.
- **First question:** "Has the acuity mix of referrals accepted this period changed, and can we show that with intake data?"
- **Data to pull:** referral acuity scores by quarter, intake acceptance/decline log with stated reasons, the CQI early-warning metric (e.g., re-engagement speed) for the same period.

### Board finance/program committee has not reviewed restricted-vs-unrestricted fund balances in the last quarter
- **Usually means:** the review cadence has slipped from quarterly to annual (often during a leadership transition or a busy grant-renewal season), which means a restricted-fund deficit is caught at audit instead of in time to correct it.
- **First question:** "When did the committee last see the fund-balance-by-restriction report, and who owns getting it back on the calendar?"
- **Data to pull:** committee meeting minutes and materials for the last 4 quarters, current fund balance by restriction status.

### A single-audit (2 CFR 200 Subpart F) management letter comment on cost allocation or effort reporting
- **Usually means:** shared staff time (a position split across two or more grants) isn't being supported by contemporaneous effort documentation, which is the single most common Uniform Guidance finding for multi-grant nonprofits.
- **First question:** "Which shared positions does this finding cover, and is the underlying timesheet practice fixed or still in place?"
- **Data to pull:** the cost allocation plan, sampled effort-reporting timesheets for flagged positions, the prior-year finding and corrective action plan if this is a repeat.

### A new grant opportunity offered outside any current program's client population or theory of change
- **Usually means:** the funder is pattern-matching the organization's brand/reputation to a need that doesn't match actual capacity — accepting it as-is risks either mission drift or an underfunded new program line.
- **First question:** "If we said yes, whose current job changes to build this, and what stops being done?"
- **Data to pull:** the true fully-loaded cost build (staffing, systems, supervision) against the grant's actual budget ceiling, referral-pipeline evidence for the new population.

### Match or cost-share obligation on a grant not separately tracked from general operations
- **Usually means:** the required in-kind or cash match (common on federal and some state/county awards) is being assumed to happen automatically rather than tracked, risking recapture of grant funds if the match isn't documented and met.
- **First question:** "Where specifically is the match being generated, and is it being logged as it happens or reconstructed at reporting time?"
- **Data to pull:** the grant agreement's match requirement and current match-tracking log, in-kind donation/volunteer-hour documentation if match is non-cash.
