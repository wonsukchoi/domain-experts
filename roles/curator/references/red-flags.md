# Red flags

### A proposed gift's display or naming condition runs longer than 5 years
- **Usually means:** the donor (or their estate/family) is optimizing for permanence and recognition, not for the collection's long-term flexibility; second most likely, a well-meaning development-office promise made before curatorial review.
- **First question:** what happens to this condition if the object stops being central to the collection's argument in year 8?
- **Data to pull:** the exact condition language in the draft deed of gift; comparable conditions accepted (or declined) in the last 5 years and how they played out.

### Acquisition case leads with appraised value and doesn't mention the collecting plan
- **Usually means:** the recommendation is chasing the size of the gift, not its fit; second, the collecting plan itself may be outdated or unwritten.
- **First question:** which specific gap or strength in the collecting plan does this object address?
- **Data to pull:** the current written collecting plan section for this department; a list of comparable objects already held.

### European object's ownership record shows an unexplained gap or transfer between 1933 and 1945
- **Usually means:** incomplete historical recordkeeping (the majority case); second, and the reason the check exists at all, unlawful appropriation during the Nazi era.
- **First question:** has this object been submitted to AAM's Nazi-Era Provenance Internet Portal, and is that submission current?
- **Data to pull:** full ownership chain documentation; portal submission record and date; any outstanding claim correspondence.

### An object may be a Native American human remain, funerary, sacred, or patrimony object and is on view or accessible without documented Tribal consultation
- **Usually means:** the item predates current NAGPRA consultation practice and was never re-reviewed; second, a new acquisition processed without the required screening step.
- **First question:** has free, prior, and informed consent been obtained and documented from the affiliated Tribe or Native Hawaiian Organization?
- **Data to pull:** consultation and consent records; current display/access status; date of last NAGPRA-related review of this accession.

### Deaccessioning proceeds are proposed for anything other than conservation, storage materials, or direct collections care
- **Usually means:** budget pressure elsewhere in the institution is being routed through a restricted fund; second, unfamiliarity with the current "direct care" definition among newer staff or leadership.
- **First question:** does this expenditure meet the AAMD/AAM direct-care definition, in writing, before the money moves?
- **Data to pull:** the deaccessioning policy's direct-care clause; the proposed budget line and its justification.

### A deaccessioned object goes straight to private sale with no documented attempt at transfer or public auction
- **Usually means:** speed or a specific buyer relationship is driving the decision; second, an assumption that private sale nets more without checking.
- **First question:** why weren't transfer to another public institution or public auction pursued first?
- **Data to pull:** the disposal preference-order documentation; any outreach record to peer institutions or auction houses.

### An object hasn't left storage — no exhibition, loan, or research request — in 10+ years
- **Usually means:** it no longer fits the collecting plan or was accessioned on appraised value rather than fit; second, it's a legitimate reference/study object that isn't meant to be displayed.
- **First question:** would this object clear today's acquisition review if it were offered now?
- **Data to pull:** loan/exhibition/request history from the collection-management system; comparison against current collecting plan.

### Condition report notes active deterioration (flaking paint, active pest activity, corrosion) discovered during a routine survey
- **Usually means:** environmental control failure in that storage area; second, an inherent material-stability issue reaching end of life.
- **First question:** does this object need to be isolated today, regardless of where it sits in the conservation queue?
- **Data to pull:** environmental monitoring logs for the storage location; conservator's isolation and treatment recommendation.

### A loan request arrives with no facility report attached
- **Usually means:** the borrowing institution assumes reputation substitutes for documentation; second, a rushed timeline that skipped the standard intake step.
- **First question:** can the loan agreement be signed without the facility report, or does approval wait?
- **Data to pull:** the borrowing institution's most recent facility report on file, if any; insurance certificate status.

### A general-audience object label draft runs past roughly 150 words
- **Usually means:** the label is doing the job of the catalogue essay or wall panel instead of its own job; second, multiple ideas were merged into one label instead of split across label and panel.
- **First question:** what's the one idea a visitor should leave with, and does the draft lead with it?
- **Data to pull:** the label draft; the exhibition's central-argument panel, to check for duplicated content.

### A single donor is pushing to bypass or accelerate committee review
- **Usually means:** time pressure tied to a tax year or estate deadline; second, an attempt to avoid scrutiny of an attached condition the donor knows would be contested.
- **First question:** what specifically would committee review catch that the accelerated path skips?
- **Data to pull:** the draft deed of gift's condition language; committee meeting calendar and whether an interim/email vote process exists for genuine deadline cases.
