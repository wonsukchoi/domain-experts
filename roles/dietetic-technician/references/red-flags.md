# Red Flags

Smell tests for screening data, tray lines, and food-safety logs. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Meal intake under 50% for 3 consecutive days
- **Usually means:** either a genuine decline (illness, depression, medication side effect) or an unrecognized texture/tolerance problem with the current diet order — rarely "just picky."
- **First question:** "Is this concentrated at one texture/consistency level, or across everything the resident is offered?"
- **Data to pull:** 3-day meal-by-meal intake log plus same-window nursing/CNA notes for coughing, pocketing, or refusal.

### Weight loss ≥5% in 30 days (or ≥7.5% in 90 days, ≥10% in 180 days)
- **Usually means:** crosses the Academy/ASPEN "significant loss" threshold — could be inadequate intake, a new illness, a medication interaction, or a scale/measurement error.
- **First question:** "Was this weight taken on the same scale, same time of day, same clothing/equipment as the prior weight?"
- **Data to pull:** weight history trend (not just two points), current intake log, active medication list for appetite-suppressing or GI-affecting drugs.

### Tray ticket diet order doesn't match the chart's active allergy/intolerance flag
- **Usually means:** the flag was added after the standing tray ticket was generated, or the ticket was never regenerated after a diet-order change.
- **First question:** "When was this ticket last regenerated relative to the allergy flag's entry date?"
- **Data to pull:** ticket generation timestamp vs. allergy/intolerance entry timestamp in the chart.

### Legacy diet-order terminology on a new order ("ADA diet," "no added salt cardiac diet," "NDD Level 2")
- **Usually means:** the prescriber is using outdated shorthand, or the facility's diet manual hasn't been fully converted to current terminology (consistent-carbohydrate naming, IDDSI levels).
- **First question:** "What does the current facility diet manual say this term maps to, and does that match what's actually being plated?"
- **Data to pull:** current diet manual entry, and a spot-check of the last week's tickets under the same order text.

### Hot-held item reads below 135°F on a routine temperature check
- **Usually means:** holding equipment malfunction, or the item sat too long between cooking and being placed in the hot-holding unit.
- **First question:** "How long has it been below 135°F, and can it still make the one-time reheat-to-165°F window?"
- **Data to pull:** temperature log with timestamps, holding-unit maintenance record.

### A resident coughs or pockets food specifically at the current texture/liquid level
- **Usually means:** the texture order no longer matches the resident's actual swallow function — a clinical signal, not a food-preparation quality issue.
- **First question:** "Is this new, or has it been happening across multiple meals/shifts?"
- **Data to pull:** nursing notes across the last several shifts, most recent swallow evaluation date.

### Intake and weight data collected by inconsistent methods across shifts
- **Usually means:** the percent-intake numbers being trended aren't actually comparable to each other — one aide's "50%" is another's "75%."
- **First question:** "Is everyone using the facility's visual reference guide, or estimating from memory?"
- **Data to pull:** the facility's intake-documentation training material and a sample of entries from different staff on the same tray to check for consistency.

### A DTR/NDTR's chart note reads like a nutrition diagnosis rather than an observation
- **Usually means:** scope creep — the technician is drafting language that belongs in the RDN's PES statement, even if the underlying observation is accurate.
- **First question:** "Is this a data point (intake %, weight, screening score) or a diagnostic conclusion (a stated nutrition problem with an etiology)?"
- **Data to pull:** the note in question, compared against the facility's documented NDTR scope-of-practice policy.

### Same diet order reordered unchanged after three consecutive care-plan reviews despite a documented complication
- **Usually means:** the order is on autopilot — nobody re-evaluated it against the resident's current status at the last review.
- **First question:** "What changed clinically since this order was last actively reviewed, and did the review actually reconsider it or just renew it?"
- **Data to pull:** order history with review dates, and the most recent care-plan meeting notes.
