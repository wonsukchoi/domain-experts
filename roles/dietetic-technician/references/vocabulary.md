# Vocabulary

Terms of art an NDTR uses precisely that generalists blur together. Format per term: definition, a sentence a practitioner would actually say, the common misuse.

### NDTR vs. DTR
- **Definition:** Nutrition and Dietetics Technician, Registered (NDTR) is CDR's current credential title, adopted circa 2013; "DTR" (Dietetic Technician, Registered) is the legacy name for the same credential.
- **In use:** "The chart still says DTR because it hasn't been updated since the title change — same credential, same scope."
- **Common misuse:** treating the name change as if it signaled a scope change. It didn't; the underlying supervised-practice and exam requirements are continuous.

### Nutrition diagnosis (PES statement)
- **Definition:** the RDN's formal problem-etiology-signs/symptoms statement within the Nutrition Care Process — a clinical judgment, not a data observation.
- **In use:** "I logged the intake drop and the weight loss; the PES statement is the RDN's to write once she's reviewed it."
- **Common misuse:** an NDTR writing diagnostic-sounding language ("inadequate intake related to depression") in a chart note — that sentence belongs to the RDN even when the observation behind it is accurate.

### Consistent carbohydrate diet
- **Definition:** current standard terminology for what used to be called an "ADA diet" or "diabetic diet" — the same total carbohydrate grams at each meal, day to day, rather than a sugar-restricted diet.
- **In use:** "The order says diabetic diet, but the manual's current entry is consistent carbohydrate at 60g breakfast, 75g lunch, 75g dinner."
- **Common misuse:** treating it as synonymous with "low sugar" or "low carb" — it's about consistency of carbohydrate load meal to meal, not restriction of total amount.

### IDDSI level
- **Definition:** a numbered texture (food, levels 3–7) or liquid (0–4) classification from the International Dysphagia Diet Standardisation Initiative, replacing the older National Dysphagia Diet levels and "nectar/honey-thick" terminology.
- **In use:** "Order reads IDDSI liquid level 2 — that's what used to be called nectar-thick, not the old honey-thick level."
- **Common misuse:** mixing legacy terms ("nectar thick") and IDDSI numbers on the same ticket as if they're interchangeable labels rather than a full standard the facility should have fully converted to.

### Significant vs. severe weight loss
- **Definition:** Academy/ASPEN-defined percentage-loss thresholds by time window (e.g., 5% in 1 month = significant, >5% = severe) — a graded clinical signal, not a single cutoff.
- **In use:** "5.6% in 30 days is significant, not yet severe — that's still a same-shift flag, just not a crisis-level one."
- **Common misuse:** treating any weight loss as equally alarming, or conversely dismissing loss under an arbitrary round number (5 lb, 10 lb) instead of computing the percentage against the correct time window.

### Nutrition Care Process (ADIME)
- **Definition:** the Academy's standardized model — Assessment, Diagnosis, Intervention, Monitoring & Evaluation — that structures how dietetics professionals document care.
- **In use:** "I own most of the Assessment data and all of the Monitoring & Evaluation; Diagnosis is the RDN's step."
- **Common misuse:** using "ADIME" as a generic label for "the notes I wrote" without distinguishing which step a given entry actually belongs to — the letters map to specific scope boundaries, not just chart sections.

### Diet manual
- **Definition:** the facility's controlled reference (often built on the Academy's Nutrition Care Manual) that defines exactly what each named therapeutic diet means in that facility — gram targets, food lists, texture mapping.
- **In use:** "The order just says 'renal diet' — check what the current manual entry actually specifies before the ticket goes out."
- **Common misuse:** assuming a diet name means the same thing across facilities, or relying on memory of a previous job's manual instead of the current one in force.

### Malnutrition Screening Tool (MST) score
- **Definition:** a validated 2-question screening instrument (recent weight loss + appetite/intake) yielding a score used to flag nutritional risk, distinct from a full nutrition assessment.
- **In use:** "MST came back at 3 — that's above the risk threshold, routing to the RDN inside the 24-hour window."
- **Common misuse:** treating the screening score itself as a diagnosis or a severity measure — it's a triage trigger, not a clinical conclusion.

### Danger zone (food safety)
- **Definition:** the FDA Food Code's 41°F–135°F temperature range in which bacterial growth accelerates; the working constraint behind cold-holding, hot-holding, and cooling rules.
- **In use:** "It sat in the danger zone for under 2 hours cumulative, so we're within the cooling window — no discard needed yet."
- **Common misuse:** using an outdated 40°F–140°F range from a prior Food Code edition, or averaging time spent above and below the boundaries instead of tracking cumulative time in range.

### Certified Dietary Manager (CDM) / Certified Food Protection Professional (CFPP)
- **Definition:** ANFP credentials for foodservice management, distinct from the NDTR clinical credential — some staff hold both, some hold only one depending on their role emphasis.
- **In use:** "She's CDM-credentialed for the foodservice side; the clinical screening work goes through the NDTRs."
- **Common misuse:** assuming CDM and NDTR are the same credential or interchangeable — they're issued by different bodies (ANFP vs. CDR) and cover different scopes.

### Texture-modified diet vs. therapeutic diet
- **Definition:** a texture-modified diet changes food consistency (for chewing/swallowing safety); a therapeutic diet changes nutrient content (sodium, potassium, carbohydrate) for a medical condition — a resident can be on one, the other, or both simultaneously.
- **In use:** "He's mechanical soft and consistent carbohydrate — texture for the dysphagia, therapeutic for the diabetes; changing one doesn't touch the other."
- **Common misuse:** treating "special diet" as one category, which leads to changing the wrong axis (adjusting texture when the actual complaint is about sodium, or vice versa).
