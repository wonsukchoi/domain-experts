# Red Flags

Smell tests for a booth cashier's shift, bank, and paperwork. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question a veteran asks, the specific record to pull.

### Drawer balances exactly, but a hand pay over $2,000 has no W-2G attached

- **Usually means:** the cashier paid fast to keep the line moving and treated the tax form as optional paperwork rather than a condition of the payout — the drawer count doesn't check for this at all.
- **First question:** "Where's the W-2G and the two IDs for the machine 214 hand pay?"
- **Data to pull:** the jackpot/hand-pay log against the W-2G form batch for the shift; any hand pay without a matching form gets written up regardless of count accuracy.

### Same patron, multiple cash-outs each just under the MTL threshold, same gaming day

- **Usually means:** either a coincidence of a high-volume player, or deliberate structuring to stay under the reporting line — indistinguishable from the booth alone.
- **First question:** "Has this patron transacted at another window today, and what's the running total?"
- **Data to pull:** the shared MTL/ACL log and cage transaction history for that patron ID across all windows for the gaming day.

### Fill slip missing a second signature

- **Usually means:** the fill was rushed to reopen a machine with a line forming, and the countersignature got skipped under time pressure.
- **First question:** "Who counted this fill with you, and why isn't their signature on it?"
- **Data to pull:** the triplicate fill slip's three copies (machine drop box, booth paperwork, cage/count team) — if any copy is missing the second signature, the fill is unverifiable.

### TITO ticket amount hand-keyed instead of scanned

- **Usually means:** the validation unit failed, or someone hit a torn/altered ticket and typed the printed number instead of escalating — a manual override bypasses the barcode check that exists specifically to catch altered tickets.
- **First question:** "Why didn't this ticket scan, and who authorized the manual entry?"
- **Data to pull:** the SDS validation log for that ticket ID; a ticket with no matching validation record but a paid amount is the exact signature of ticket fraud.

### Bank shortage recurs on the same booth or same shift pattern, even if small each time

- **Usually means:** a single small, ongoing skim is far more likely than repeated random counting error — random errors don't cluster by booth or shift.
- **First question:** "Pull the last eight closes for this booth — is the variance always the same sign?"
- **Data to pull:** shift-by-shift variance history for the booth, cross-referenced against who worked it.

### Counterfeit pen/UV light sits unused during a rush period

- **Usually means:** speed pressure overrode the intake check exactly when a counterfeiter would target the booth — busy windows with a visible line are where counterfeit bills get passed on purpose.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the last ten $50s and $100s you took in the last hour — were any checked?"
- **Data to pull:** surveillance footage timestamped to the transaction; camera angle on the counter, not just the cashier's face.

### Bank or coin cart left unattended, even briefly

- **Usually means:** the cashier stepped away to help a guest, restock, or answer a page without locking down first — breaks chain of custody the moment any variance needs explaining.
- **First question:** "Was the bank locked or the cart secured every time you left the station?"
- **Data to pull:** surveillance coverage of the booth/cart location for any gap in cashier presence without a corresponding lock-down action.

### Hand pay over the supervisor-verification threshold paid by the cashier alone

- **Usually means:** either the cashier didn't know the threshold applied to this game type, or paged a supervisor who never actually arrived and payment happened anyway under guest pressure.
- **First question:** "Whose signature is on this hand pay above the supervisor line, and were they physically present?"
- **Data to pull:** the hand-pay slip's countersignature against supervisor time-clock/badge-access records for that window.

### A "short pay" is corrected by adding cash without a reprinted slip

- **Usually means:** the machine paid less than the displayed win (a genuine short pay, common with low hopper levels) and the difference was made up in cash without documenting the discrepancy — creates an unexplained cash-out with no paper trail.
- **First question:** "What did the machine's meter say it paid versus what the patron actually received?"
- **Data to pull:** the machine's electronic meter log for the jackpot event against the short-pay correction slip; no slip means no defensible record of the true payout amount.

### New cashier's closing counts are consistently faster than experienced peers'

- **Usually means:** either genuine skill, or a count that isn't actually independent — glancing at the register total and writing it down instead of physically counting.
- **First question:** "Recount this drawer with me right now, out loud."
- **Data to pull:** nothing beats a live recount; a register-total match with a suspiciously fast physical count is worth spot-checking periodically regardless of tenure.
