# Dermatologist — Red Flags

### Pigmented lesion changing shape or color over weeks, not months
- **Usually means:** nodular melanoma, which often lacks classic ABCDE surface features and instead grows vertically fast — the "amelanotic, elevated, firm, growing" pattern rather than the flat, multicolor pattern most self-exam education targets.
- **First question:** is there elevation, bleeding, or a pink/skin-colored (amelanotic) component that a pigment-focused exam would miss?
- **Data to pull:** dermoscopy images and, if available, prior patient photos to confirm the growth timeline.

### Dermoscopy shows a blue-white veil
- **Usually means:** melanoma — blue-white veil is one of the single strongest individual dermoscopic predictors, though it also appears in blue nevi and some Spitz lesions.
- **First question:** has a full-thickness (punch or excisional) biopsy been planned rather than a shave?
- **Data to pull:** archived dermoscopy image for pattern re-review before biopsy.

### Breslow depth 0.8–1.0mm with no ulceration reported
- **Usually means:** the case falls in the current NCCN discuss-and-offer SLNB band, not the "automatically thin and done" category from the older 1.0mm threshold.
- **First question:** has SLNB been discussed and documented as offered, accepted, or declined?
- **Data to pull:** full pathology report — mitotic rate and ulceration status specifically, not just the depth number.

### Psoriasis BSA under 5% but involving face, genitals, palms, soles, or scalp
- **Usually means:** severity is being underestimated by body-surface area alone; special-site disease carries disproportionate quality-of-life impact regardless of BSA.
- **First question:** does a DLQI (Dermatology Life Quality Index) score or IGA match the "mild" BSA read?
- **Data to pull:** DLQI score and site-specific photos.

### Rapidly enlarging, painful, ulcerated nodule at a surgical or trauma site
- **Usually means:** pyoderma gangrenosum, frequently misread as infection — debridement typically worsens it via pathergy rather than helping.
- **First question:** has this been debrided already, and is there a pathergy history?
- **Data to pull:** tissue culture to rule out infection before any further debridement is considered.

### Isotretinoin patient missing the 7-day pregnancy-test window before pickup
- **Usually means:** iPLEDGE REMS non-compliance, which locks the prescription regardless of clinical risk assessment.
- **First question:** was the pregnancy test completed inside the required 7-day pre-pickup window?
- **Data to pull:** iPLEDGE portal compliance and authorization status.

### New widespread rash with mucosal involvement and fever, 1–8 weeks after a new medication
- **Usually means:** Stevens-Johnson syndrome/TEN, especially with anticonvulsants, allopurinol, or sulfa drugs.
- **First question:** what medication started in the preceding 1–8 weeks, and what percent of BSA shows detachment?
- **Data to pull:** SCORTEN score and the full medication timeline.

### Non-healing sore or "pimple" on sun-exposed skin persisting past 4 weeks
- **Usually means:** basal cell carcinoma (the classic "rodent ulcer" pattern), not a slow-healing pimple or scab.
- **First question:** is there a pearly, rolled border with visible telangiectasia?
- **Data to pull:** dermoscopy and biopsy.

### Pigmented nail streak with Hutchinson sign (pigment extending onto the proximal or lateral nailfold)
- **Usually means:** subungual melanoma until proven otherwise.
- **First question:** what is the band width, and is it a single nail or multiple?
- **Data to pull:** nail matrix biopsy.

### Positive Nikolsky sign with flaccid bullae
- **Usually means:** pemphigus vulgaris, though Nikolsky sign is not specific to it — SJS/TEN and staph scalded skin syndrome can also show epidermal separation.
- **First question:** was perilesional (not lesional) skin sent for direct immunofluorescence?
- **Data to pull:** DIF result and serum ELISA for desmoglein 1/3.

### Mood changes or new depressive symptoms reported by an isotretinoin patient
- **Usually means:** needs urgent psychiatric evaluation and documentation regardless of how the drug-relationship debate is ultimately settled.
- **First question:** is there a personal or family history of depression, and has a validated screening tool been used?
- **Data to pull:** PHQ-9 score trend across visits.

### Chronic hand dermatitis in a healthcare or food-service worker not responding to topical steroids
- **Usually means:** allergic or irritant contact dermatitis from an occupational exposure, not steroid-refractory eczema needing a stronger steroid.
- **First question:** what products or materials contact the hands at work, and has patch testing been done?
- **Data to pull:** patch test panel results (e.g., North American Contact Dermatitis Group series).
