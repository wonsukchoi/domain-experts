# Dermatologist — Vocabulary

### Breslow depth
The measured thickness of a melanoma in millimeters, from the granular layer to the deepest invasive cell — the primary prognostic and treatment-determining number in melanoma.
**In use:** "Depth is 0.9mm, non-ulcerated — that puts us in the discuss-SLNB band even without ulceration."
**Common misuse:** conflated with Clark level (the anatomic skin layer reached). Clark level has been superseded by Breslow depth as the primary staging input; citing Clark level as if it still carries equal weight is outdated.

### Fitzpatrick skin type
A six-category classification (I–VI) of how skin responds to UV exposure — burn versus tan tendency — not a classification of skin color or ethnicity.
**In use:** "She's a Fitzpatrick III, tans after initial burning, which affects phototherapy dosing, not her melanoma risk category."
**Common misuse:** used as reassurance that darker Fitzpatrick types carry lower melanoma risk overall; melanoma still occurs in darker skin, disproportionately as acral lentiginous disease on palms, soles, and nail units — sites a sun-exposure-focused exam can miss.

### PASI (Psoriasis Area Severity Index)
A calculated composite score combining erythema, induration, and scaling severity across four body regions, weighted by area involved in each.
**In use:** "PASI came down from 14 to 3 at week 12 — that's a PASI75+ response."
**Common misuse:** used as a loose synonym for "bad psoriasis" without actually calculating the composite, or treated as interchangeable with raw BSA, which measures area only and ignores lesion severity.

### Nikolsky sign
Epidermal separation and blister extension produced by lateral pressure on perilesional skin.
**In use:** "Nikolsky was positive at the edge of the bullae — get DIF before starting empiric treatment."
**Common misuse:** treated as diagnostic specifically for pemphigus vulgaris; it's also positive in SJS/TEN and staphylococcal scalded skin syndrome, so it narrows the differential rather than confirming one diagnosis.

### Dermoscopy (dermatoscopy)
A structured, magnified pattern-recognition examination of pigmented and non-pigmented lesions using defined criteria (7-point or 3-point checklists), not simply a closer look.
**In use:** "Dermoscopy scored 3 on the 7-point checklist — atypical network plus irregular streaks — that crosses the excision threshold."
**Common misuse:** described as "just magnification," skipping the scored-criteria step that gives it diagnostic value over naked-eye exam.

### Mohs micrographic surgery
A staged excision technique with real-time complete margin mapping, used for tissue-sparing removal in high-recurrence-risk sites or histologies.
**In use:** "Recurrent BCC on the nasal ala — that's an AUC-appropriate Mohs referral, not a standard excision."
**Common misuse:** treated as a universally "more thorough" option for any skin cancer regardless of site or risk; AUC criteria specifically scope it to high-risk anatomic zones and histologic subtypes, and using it for a low-risk trunk BCC is overuse, not extra safety.

### Patch testing
An epicutaneous test for delayed-type (type IV) hypersensitivity, read at 48 and 96 hours, used to identify allergic contact dermatitis triggers.
**In use:** "Chronic hand dermatitis not responding to steroids — patch test against the NACDG series before assuming it's just eczema."
**Common misuse:** confused with prick/IgE allergy testing, which tests a different immune mechanism and timeline entirely; ordering the wrong test misses the diagnosis.

### Direct immunofluorescence (DIF)
A biopsy-based test detecting antibody or complement deposition in skin, used to diagnose autoimmune bullous and connective tissue disease.
**In use:** "Send perilesional skin for DIF before starting steroids — lesional tissue won't show the deposition pattern reliably."
**Common misuse:** biopsying lesional skin instead of perilesional skin for most bullous disease, which invalidates or weakens the result.

### Actinic keratosis
A precancerous keratinocyte lesion from cumulative UV damage, existing within a broader area of subclinical sun-damaged skin (field cancerization).
**In use:** "Cryo will clear the visible lesion, but with this much field damage she needs 5-FU or imiquimod for the field, not just spot treatment."
**Common misuse:** treated as a single discrete lesion to freeze and forget, ignoring the field-cancerization concept that explains why new lesions keep appearing nearby after lesion-directed treatment alone.

### Koebner phenomenon
The appearance of new disease-specific lesions at a site of skin trauma, seen in a specific, named set of conditions (psoriasis, lichen planus, vitiligo, and a few others).
**In use:** "New psoriatic plaques along the scratch lines — that's Koebnerization, not a secondary infection."
**Common misuse:** applied loosely to any rash that shows up after trauma, when it's a specific phenomenon limited to a defined list of dermatoses.

### Ugly duckling sign
A comparative pattern-recognition heuristic: the lesion that looks different from a given patient's other nevi, not the objectively worst-looking lesion in isolation.
**In use:** "Every other nevus on his back is a soft brown macule — this one dark, irregular papule is the ugly duckling."
**Common misuse:** applied as an absolute aesthetic judgment rather than a within-patient comparison, missing that a uniformly atypical-looking nevus can be that patient's normal baseline.

### SCORTEN
A validated seven-variable mortality-risk score for Stevens-Johnson syndrome/toxic epidermal necrolysis, calculated at admission and day 3.
**In use:** "SCORTEN of 3 on admission — that's roughly a 35% predicted mortality, this needs a burn unit transfer today."
**Common misuse:** severity eyeballed from BSA detachment alone instead of calculating the actual seven-variable score, which also weighs age, malignancy, heart rate, and lab values that gestalt misses.

### Comedone
A follicular plug — open (blackhead) or closed (whitehead) — that is the primary, non-inflammatory acne lesion.
**In use:** "This is comedonal acne, not inflammatory — start with a topical retinoid alone before adding an antibiotic."
**Common misuse:** used generically for any acne bump, blurring the open/closed comedone distinction that helps determine first-line therapy and distinguishes acne vulgaris from other follicular disorders.

### iPLEDGE / REMS
The FDA Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy program mandating pregnancy testing and contraception documentation for isotretinoin prescribing.
**In use:** "She's outside the 7-day pregnancy-test window — the prescription is locked until a new test clears, no exceptions."
**Common misuse:** treated as a flexible guideline the physician can waive based on personal risk assessment of a specific patient; it is a procedural requirement, not a clinical judgment call.
