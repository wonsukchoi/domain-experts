# Playbook

Filled operational sequences a line cook actually runs — starting points to adapt to a specific kitchen's layout and pace, not scripts to follow blind.

## 1. Pre-shift line check and par sheet

Run 30–45 minutes before doors open. The par sheet is the chef's, not the cook's to set — the cook's job is holding to it and flagging when reality diverges.

**Filled example — sauté station, Saturday dinner, baseline 165 covers:**

| Item | Par (portions) | Backup-prep trigger | Prep lead time | FIFO check |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Chicken breast, trimmed/brined | 50 | 80% depleted (10 left) | 12 min (trim + brine + portion) | Yesterday's brine discarded, none carried over |
| Salmon, portioned | 35 | 75% depleted (9 left) | 6 min (portion + skin score) | Today's delivery only — salmon doesn't carry |
| Wild mushroom sauté mix | 8 qt | 70% depleted (2.4 qt left) | 15 min (clean + sauté + cool) | Oldest batch dated, pulled first |
| Beurre blanc | 3 qt | 60% depleted (1.2 qt left) | 20 min (breaks if rushed) | Made fresh, 4-hour hold max |

Line check confirms: every walk-in container labeled with contents and prep date, probe thermometer calibrated (ice-point check: reads 32°F ±1°F), station knives sharp, pans and tools staged left-to-right in fire order.

## 2. Course-fire timing (fine/casual dining, multi-course)

Targets a station paces against — not hard deadlines, but the numbers a cook checks a ticket's print time against.

| Course | Target: fire-to-window | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cold apps / salads | 6–8 min | Garde manger; no cook time, mostly assembly — a miss here is a staffing or ticket-reading problem, not a cooking one. |
| Hot apps | 8–10 min | Fry and sauté overlap here; batch by "all day" count, don't fire one basket per ticket. |
| Entrées, standard proteins | 12–15 min | Fired only once the table's apps are ~80% cleared, unless the ticket is marked "on the fly." |
| Entrées, long-cook items (braises, bone-in, well-done thick cuts) | 18–22 min | Fire these first when a large party's ticket has mixed cook times, working backward from one shared plate-up moment. |

`[heuristic — needs practitioner check]`: these bands vary by concept (fast-casual runs 30–40% faster; tasting-menu fine dining runs on a different clock entirely, paced by the dining room, not the ticket printer).

## 3. Calling the pass — exact script

**Ticket in:**
> Expo: "Fire two chicken, one salmon, ordering one filet."
> Sauté: "Heard — two chicken, one salmon."
> Grill: "Heard — one filet, ordering."

**Mid-service status, unprompted:**
> Sauté: "Behind on the grill side — six back, need a hand for ten."

**Ready to plate:**
> Sauté: "Chicken up, two."
> Expo: "Corner — chicken two, hot."

**86 call (see section 4):**
> Sauté: "86 the salmon — kitchen's out."
> Expo: "86 salmon, heard — floor's been told."

The discipline: every call gets a response repeating the content back. A call that goes unanswered is treated as unheard and repeated, not assumed received.

## 4. 86 protocol

1. **The instant a station knows an item can't be fulfilled** for the rest of service (not "probably enough for one more") — call "86 [item]" to expo immediately. No pause to confirm with the chef first; that happens after, if at all.
2. **Expo repeats the call back** and updates the physical 86 board or POS block within the same minute — a sold 86'd item after this point is an expo failure, not a floor failure.
3. **Station proposes the substitute** if one exists ("86 salmon, swordfish special covers it") — the floor needs an alternative to offer, not just a subtraction.
4. **Any ticket already fired for the 86'd item before the call is honored** — the 86 is forward-looking only; a ticket mid-cook doesn't get pulled.
5. **Post-shift, the 86 gets logged** with the cover count and time it hit, feeding back into the next par-sheet revision (see the worked example in `SKILL.md`).

## 5. Temp log — filled example, dinner shift

| Time | Item | Reading | Spec | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4:45pm (pre-shift) | Walk-in cooler | 38°F | ≤41°F | Pass |
| 4:45pm | Reach-in, sauté station | 40°F | ≤41°F | Pass, flagged for recheck at 7pm (running warm) |
| 6:00pm | Chicken breast, cooked | 168°F | ≥165°F | Pass |
| 7:00pm | Reach-in, sauté station | 43°F | ≤41°F | Fail — pulled all product, moved to walk-in, called maintenance, logged time out of spec (55 min, under the 4-hour cumulative limit) |
| 9:15pm | Hot-held beurre blanc | 138°F | ≥135°F | Pass |

A single out-of-spec reading triggers action before the next ticket, not a note for the morning meeting — the log documents the catch, it isn't the catch.

## 6. Shift-end reset

1. Break down station to bare pans; product either portioned and labeled for tomorrow's par or discarded per FIFO age limits (cooked proteins held no more than 3 days, sauces per house policy — typically 2–4 days refrigerated).
2. Walk-in restock counted against tomorrow's par sheet, shortfall or surplus noted for the sous chef, not silently carried.
3. Line check reversed: knives cleaned and stoned, station wiped and sanitized, reach-in temps logged one final time.
4. 86 log and any behind-the-pace incidents handed to the closing sous for the pre-shift meeting the next day — the point is the next cook on this station inherits the information, not a clean slate.
