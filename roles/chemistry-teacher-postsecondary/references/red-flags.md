# Red flags

### A reported yield above ~85% with no purity or corroboration check documented
- **Usually means:** incomplete drying, residual solvent, or unpurified byproduct is inflating the measured mass rather than genuine additional product.
- **First question:** was an independent check (melting point, titration, TLC) run to corroborate the claimed yield?
- **Data to pull:** the lab report's stated drying time/method, any corroborating measurement on file.

### A lab report's numeric result deviates from the typical range with no error analysis section
- **Usually means:** either a real, undocumented procedural error occurred, or the student doesn't yet know how to attribute and quantify error sources.
- **First question:** does the report identify a plausible cause for the deviation, and is the magnitude consistent with that cause?
- **Data to pull:** the procedure log for that student/group, any noted incident (spill, equipment issue) during the session.

### A problem-set answer marked wrong with generic feedback ("check your work") regardless of where the error occurred
- **Usually means:** the grader hasn't distinguished a conceptual setup error from a computational slip, so the feedback won't target the actual gap.
- **First question:** was the initial setup (stoichiometry, mechanism, limiting reagent identification) correct before the arithmetic was applied?
- **Data to pull:** the student's full work, not just the final numeric answer.

### A new demo or lab procedure scheduled to run live with no dry-run record for this specific room
- **Usually means:** ventilation, equipment scale, or reagent grade in this room may differ from the source material's assumptions, risking a safety or execution failure.
- **First question:** has this exact procedure been tested under this room's actual conditions, by this instructor or a verified prior term?
- **Data to pull:** the pre-run safety checklist, any prior term's run log for this procedure in this room.

### A grading curve where results obtained through undisclosed shortcuts outscore results with documented legitimate error
- **Usually means:** the rubric is weighting numeric outcome over methodology, incentivizing students to optimize the number rather than the technique.
- **First question:** does the rubric score methodology and error documentation as explicit line items, or only the final result?
- **Data to pull:** the rubric's point allocation across methodology, error analysis, and numeric outcome.

### A student granted open-ended procedural design freedom with no documented hazard review specific to their proposed procedure
- **Usually means:** the specific reagents, quantities, or steps the student intends to use haven't been checked against this room's safety constraints.
- **First question:** has this specific proposed procedure — not just the general lab topic — been reviewed and signed off?
- **Data to pull:** the hazard review sign-off record for that student's specific procedure.

### A lecture presenting a simplified model (Bohr model, octet rule, single-step mechanism) with no stated limit or exception
- **Usually means:** students may treat the simplification as complete, causing confusion or resistance when a more accurate model is introduced later.
- **First question:** did the lecture or notes state where this simplified model stops holding?
- **Data to pull:** the lecture slides/notes for that topic, any later-course feedback about student confusion on the same concept.

### Multiple lab reports in the same section reporting near-identical anomalous data points
- **Usually means:** possible data sharing, copying, or fabrication rather than independently obtained results — though shared equipment error is a second possibility.
- **First question:** were these groups working with shared equipment or reagents that could independently explain the match, or is the match otherwise unexplained?
- **Data to pull:** the lab section's equipment/reagent assignment log, raw data sheets from each group involved.
