# Payroll and Timekeeping Clerk — Red Flags

### Overtime paid at base rate in a workweek with a non-discretionary bonus
- **Usually means:** the payroll system or clerk applied a flat 1.5× base-rate overtime calculation without recalculating the regular rate of pay to include the bonus.
- **First question:** was any non-discretionary bonus, shift differential, or commission paid for a workweek that also contains overtime hours?
- **Data to pull:** the pay-period earnings statement broken out by pay component, and the workweek's total hours worked.

### Garnishment withholding exceeds 25% of disposable earnings with no support/tax-levy order present
- **Usually means:** the withholding was calculated against gross pay instead of disposable earnings, or a stacking error combined caps incorrectly.
- **First question:** what is this employee's disposable earnings for the period, and is this the only active garnishment order?
- **Data to pull:** the garnishment order itself, the disposable-earnings calculation worksheet, and any other active orders for the same employee.

### Timesheet exception flagged after the payroll run has already transmitted
- **Usually means:** the pre-lock exception review was skipped or rushed under deadline pressure.
- **First question:** is this an underpayment (may require an immediate off-cycle correction under state law) or an overpayment (usually can offset next cycle)?
- **Data to pull:** the state's final-pay/wage-correction-timing statute and the original timesheet-approval record.

### Employee's final paycheck issued on the normal next-cycle schedule after a termination
- **Usually means:** the clerk applied the standard pay-cycle timing without checking the applicable state's final-pay-on-termination statute, which in many states requires payment within 24 hours to a few days regardless of the normal cycle.
- **First question:** what does this specific state require for final pay after a termination (voluntary vs. involuntary can differ)?
- **Data to pull:** the state labor-department final-pay-timing table and the termination date/type.

### Multiple garnishment orders processed in the order they arrived rather than legal priority
- **Usually means:** the clerk queued orders by received-date instead of checking priority rules (support and tax levies outrank consumer-debt garnishments).
- **First question:** what type is each active order, and does the combined withholding respect the individual employee's aggregate CCPA cap?
- **Data to pull:** all active garnishment orders for the employee and their respective priority classifications.

### A bonus classified as "discretionary" has an announced formula or schedule
- **Usually means:** the bonus is actually non-discretionary under FLSA (the test is whether the employer retains genuine discretion over amount and timing, not the label used internally) and should be included in the regular rate.
- **First question:** does the bonus plan document specify a formula, threshold, or schedule known to employees in advance?
- **Data to pull:** the bonus plan document or policy describing how the amount is determined.

### Payroll register total doesn't match the general-ledger payroll expense account
- **Usually means:** a manual off-cycle correction wasn't posted to the GL, or a mid-period rate change wasn't reflected in both systems.
- **First question:** which specific pay component (wages, bonus, garnishment, tax) accounts for the variance?
- **Data to pull:** the payroll register detail and the GL payroll-expense sub-ledger for the same period.

### An hourly employee's time-clock record and manager-reported hours disagree by more than a rounding increment
- **Usually means:** either a missed punch, a system outage, or a manager approving hours without checking the actual clock record.
- **First question:** is there a documented system outage or malfunction for the disputed shift?
- **Data to pull:** the time-and-attendance system's raw punch log and any outage/incident log for that date.

### A newly eligible employee's first paycheck shows no tax withholding
- **Usually means:** a W-4 or state withholding form wasn't processed before the first payroll run, defaulting the system to no withholding or an incorrect default.
- **First question:** was a completed withholding form received and entered before the pay period closed?
- **Data to pull:** the employee's onboarding file and the payroll system's withholding-election record.

### A single employee shows overtime hours in every pay period for a full year
- **Usually means:** either a structural understaffing issue being masked by routine overtime, or a misclassification issue where the role should be salaried-exempt (or vice versa) and the current classification is wrong.
- **First question:** is this employee correctly classified as non-exempt under FLSA, and is the recurring overtime a scheduling/staffing signal worth escalating?
- **Data to pull:** the employee's job description against FLSA exemption tests, and a year-to-date overtime-hours trend.
