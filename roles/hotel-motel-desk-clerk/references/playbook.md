# Hotel/Motel Desk Clerk — Playbook

## Walk-priority list (filled example)

Applied in order when an oversell shortfall is confirmed and more than one guest must be considered for a walk:

| Priority | Criterion | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (protect first) | Elite loyalty tier (top 2 tiers) | Never walk without director-level sign-off |
| 2 (protect next) | Longest length of stay (LOS) remaining | A 5-night guest outranks a 1-night guest |
| 3 (protect next) | Group/event block attendee | Walking one guest from a group risks the whole block's future bookings |
| 4 (walk-eligible) | Single-night stay, booked same-day, no loyalty tier | Lowest relationship cost to walk |
| 5 (walk-eligible) | Single-night stay, OTA channel, no loyalty tier | Similar profile to #4; OTA channel has no direct-relationship cost |

Documented medical, family-emergency, or disability-related distress overrides this order regardless of tier.

## Compensation-authority matrix (filled example)

| Situation | Clerk authority (no escalation) | Escalate to shift supervisor | Escalate to GM |
|---|---|---|---|
| Walk compensation | Up to $150 total per walked guest (rate delta + dining credit + transport) | $150-$400 | Over $400, or any walk involving elite-tier guest |
| Late-checkout request | Free up to 2pm if next-day occupancy allows | Past 2pm or when next-day occupancy is tight | — |
| Room-quality complaint (noise, maintenance) | One complimentary breakfast or $25 credit | Room move + additional credit | Rate adjustment over one night's value |
| Rate-discrepancy dispute | Honor PMS-confirmed rate on the spot | Rate not resolvable from PMS/channel record | Any rate exception exceeding 20% of booked rate |

## Rate-discrepancy resolution sequence

1. Pull the PMS confirmation record for the reservation — rate, rate code, and booking channel.
2. Cross-check the rate code against the channel: OTA promo codes, corporate codes, and package rates each map through a distinct channel; a mismatch is almost always a mapping error at this step, not guest error.
3. If the PMS rate matches a valid rate code but differs from what the guest expected, show the guest the confirmation record — this resolves most disputes without further escalation.
4. If the PMS shows a channel-mapping error (e.g., a promo code that didn't apply, or a rate loaded to the wrong date range), honor the guest's original confirmed rate and log the mapping error for revenue-management review.
5. If the discrepancy exceeds the clerk's rate-exception authority (see matrix above), escalate with the PMS record and channel confirmation in hand — not just a verbal description of the dispute.

## Room-assignment constraint check (filled example)

Before cutting a key, confirm all three clear:

| Constraint | Check | Example status |
|---|---|---|
| Housekeeping status | Room marked "inspected," not just "clean" | Room 412: clean 2:15pm, inspected 2:40pm — assignable after 2:40pm |
| Guest request | Requested feature available in inventory | High floor requested; rooms 8-12 floors available at check-in |
| Arrival timing | Guest arrival vs. standard check-in time | Early arrival (11am) against 3pm standard check-in — hold for luggage storage or offer first-available room, not a forced early assignment |

If housekeeping status isn't yet "inspected," disclose a specific wait window ("your room will be ready by 3:15, may I hold your bags?") rather than assigning early or leaving the guest without a timeframe.
