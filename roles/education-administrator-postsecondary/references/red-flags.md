# Red flags — what a postsecondary administrator notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A program's financial viability argument based on major/enrollment count alone, with no total credit-hour or contribution-margin analysis
- **Usually means:** the real financial picture may be different — many departments' actual revenue comes substantially from non-major enrollment (gen-ed, service courses), which major count alone doesn't capture.
- **First question:** "What's the department's total credit-hour production and contribution margin, not just its major count?"
- **Data to pull:** total course enrollments (majors and non-majors) by section, tuition-per-credit-hour rate, direct department cost.

### A significant academic or curriculum decision announced without documented engagement through shared governance channels
- **Usually means:** a legitimacy and trust problem is being created regardless of the decision's underlying merit — faculty consent on these matters is a structural requirement, not a courtesy.
- **First question:** "Which governance body reviewed this, and what was the actual outcome of that review?"
- **Data to pull:** governance meeting minutes/votes related to the decision, timeline between governance engagement and the decision's announcement.

### A program continuing at low enrollment with no documented, explicit rationale (strategic value, pipeline role, mission fit) for why it's being subsidized
- **Usually means:** the program may be continuing by inertia or avoidance of a hard conversation rather than a deliberate decision, and its actual mission value hasn't been articulated or tested.
- **First question:** "What's the specific, documented strategic rationale for continuing to subsidize this program, and when was it last reviewed?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent program review documentation, any stated rationale for continued investment despite low enrollment.

### A structural or budget change affecting tenured faculty lines proceeding on a timeline typical of at-will staff reorganization
- **Usually means:** the process may not be respecting the different legal and governance protections tenure carries, risking both legal exposure and lasting trust damage.
- **First question:** "Does this change's process and timeline reflect the governance requirements for tenured-line decisions specifically, not a generic staffing reorganization timeline?"
- **Data to pull:** the institution's tenure/governance policy for structural changes, the actual process and timeline being used for this decision.

### A program in good accreditation standing being cited as evidence of strong educational quality with no separate quality assessment
- **Usually means:** compliance (the floor) is being conflated with quality (a separate, higher bar) — the two require different evidence.
- **First question:** "Beyond accreditation status, what does actual outcome or quality data show about this program?"
- **Data to pull:** accreditation status/report, separate program quality or outcome assessment data (completion rates, learning outcome assessments, post-graduation outcomes).

### A new or continuing program's financial model built on optimistic enrollment projections with no lower-enrollment stress test
- **Usually means:** the program's financial sustainability hasn't been checked against a realistic downside scenario, risking a later financial crisis that a conservative initial model would have surfaced.
- **First question:** "What does this program's financial model look like at 70-80% of the projected enrollment, not just the base case?"
- **Data to pull:** the program's enrollment projections and financial model, comparable historical program enrollment trends for stress-test calibration.

### Faculty development/support budget flat or declining while academic quality or faculty retention metrics are also declining
- **Usually means:** faculty support may be treated as a discretionary line item rather than a recognized lever on quality and retention, similar to the K-12 teacher-conditions dynamic.
- **First question:** "Does the faculty development budget trend track with, or move opposite to, our retention and quality trends?"
- **Data to pull:** faculty development budget trend, faculty retention rate and any available quality-outcome trend over the same period.

### A department review triggered only reactively during a budget crisis, with no standing review criteria defined in advance
- **Usually means:** decisions about program continuation are being made under acute financial pressure rather than against criteria the department and administration agreed to ahead of time, which tends to produce rushed, trust-damaging outcomes.
- **First question:** "What specific, pre-agreed criteria would trigger a review of this program, and were they in place before this crisis started?"
- **Data to pull:** any documented review-trigger criteria for the program/department, timeline of when the current review was initiated relative to the criteria (if any existed).
