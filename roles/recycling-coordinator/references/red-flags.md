# Red flags

Smell tests worth catching before they show up as a surprise budget shortfall or a rejected load at the scale house.

### Contamination rate above ~18-20% on the latest MRF audit, with tonnage reported as flat or growing
- **Usually means:** the program is reporting a success metric (tonnage) while sliding into a cost-negative contamination band; second most likely, a recent operational lapse (staffing, route-audit gap) rather than a behavior shift.
- **First question:** "What changed operationally in the last two audit periods — staffing, route coverage, cart-tagging enforcement?"
- **Data to pull:** MRF contamination audit history by period, route-level cart-tagging/enforcement staffing log, the contract's contamination-band pricing schedule.

### Program budget modeled at current commodity spot price with no stressed-price scenario
- **Usually means:** the budget assumes the current index price is durable, when recovered-material commodity indices have moved by more than half within 18-24 months in past cycles.
- **First question:** "What does this budget look like if the index drops 40% from here — has anyone modeled that?"
- **Data to pull:** the contract's index-reference terms, historical index price series for the referenced commodity, any price floor/collar language currently in the contract.

### Recent single-stream conversion with no corresponding increase in education/enforcement budget
- **Usually means:** the participation/tonnage gain was budgeted for, but the contamination cost that predictably comes with it wasn't.
- **First question:** "When we modeled this conversion, what contamination rate did we assume, and did the education budget scale with that assumption?"
- **Data to pull:** the original conversion business case/budget memo, contamination trend since conversion, education/outreach spend before and after.

### Diversion rate reported as flat or improving while rejected-load tonnage is rising
- **Usually means:** collected tonnage is being counted as "diverted" before rejection, masking a worsening contamination-driven landfill outcome inside a headline number that looks fine.
- **First question:** "Is diversion rate calculated net of MRF-rejected tonnage, or gross of it?"
- **Data to pull:** the diversion-rate calculation methodology in use, MRF-rejected-load tonnage by period, net-vs-gross diversion figures side by side.

### City-wide education campaign proposed in response to a contamination spike with no route-level breakdown first
- **Usually means:** treating a possibly operational or localized cause as a behavioral one, which is both the most expensive fix and the least likely to be the right one without evidence.
- **First question:** "Which routes or neighborhoods is the spike concentrated in, per the MRF's load-level data?"
- **Data to pull:** MRF load-tracking data by route if available, prior contamination-by-route breakdowns, staffing/enforcement changes by route or district.

### Hauler/MRF contract renewal under negotiation with no defined audit methodology or cadence in the draft terms
- **Usually means:** the rebate and fee structure is being negotiated in detail while the number those terms are priced against remains unverifiable or fully controlled by the counterparty.
- **First question:** "Who conducts the contamination audit, how often, and can we independently verify or dispute it?"
- **Data to pull:** current and draft contract language on audit methodology, any past disputes over an audit result, comparable contracts in peer jurisdictions for audit-term benchmarking.

### Education campaign completed with no pre/post measurement on the targeted subset
- **Usually means:** the campaign's cost is booked but its effect is unknown until the next full citywide audit, by which point a real localized improvement can be diluted into a "no measurable impact" reading.
- **First question:** "What was contamination on the targeted routes specifically, before and after, measured independently of the citywide average?"
- **Data to pull:** pilot-area cart-content sample or targeted audit data, citywide audit for the same period, campaign cost and household count targeted.

### Single-load rejection at the scale house accepted without a dispute or re-weigh request
- **Usually means:** the coordinator is treating the MRF's spot-check result as final without exercising a contractual dispute right, if one exists.
- **First question:** "Does the contract give us a re-weigh or dispute window for this load, and did we use it?"
- **Data to pull:** the contract's dispute/re-weigh clause, the specific load's audit documentation, a log of prior rejected loads and whether disputes were filed.

### Commodity index referenced in the contract has moved more than ~30% in either direction with no corresponding budget or council communication
- **Usually means:** the program's financial exposure to the index hasn't been translated into a budget update or a heads-up to elected officials before it shows up as a surprise line item.
- **First question:** "When the index moved, did we update the program's budget forecast, or is this the first time the swing is being surfaced?"
- **Data to pull:** commodity index history for the referenced material, last budget forecast revision date, any council or department communication tied to the price movement.
