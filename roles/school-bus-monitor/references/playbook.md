# Playbook

Filled sequences and templates for the checks a school bus monitor runs every route. Numbers below reflect Head Start §1303.71/.72, IDEA-driven IHCP practice, and SAE J2249/WC19/WC18 wheelchair-securement conventions — an individual district's own procedures control where they differ.

## 1. Pre-route checklist

Run before the bus pulls out, every shift:

| Item | Check | Pass criterion | If failed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manifest vs. roster | Compare today's manifest to the transportation department's current roster | No unflagged additions/removals since yesterday | Confirm with dispatch before departure — do not guess |
| IHCP/behavior-plan binder | Present, current, matches flagged students on manifest | Every flagged student has a plan on file | Hold departure, call dispatch for a copy |
| Wheelchair lift and tiedown anchors | Cycle lift, inspect all four tiedown straps and both belt anchors | Lift cycles fully; straps/anchors show no fraying, cracking, or missing hardware | Log defect, hold bus for the wheelchair-position students until repaired or a substitute vehicle is dispatched |
| Seat belt cutter, first-aid kit | Visual/functional check | Present, in date, accessible | Log defect, request replacement before departure |
| Radio | Test call to dispatch | Confirmed two-way contact | Do not run the route without a working radio |

## 2. Boarding sequence (per stop)

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Confirm the boarding student against the manifest by name, not by face recognition alone |
| 2 | Assist boarding; for a wheelchair user, run the full four-point tiedown sequence (below) before anything else |
| 3 | Secure seatbelt/harness/positioning vest per the student's plan |
| 4 | Update running headcount |
| 5 | Signal "clear" to the driver only after every boarding student at that stop is fully secured — never signal clear mid-securement |

## 3. Four-point wheelchair tiedown sequence (SAE J2249 / WC19 / WC18)

Run in full for every wheelchair-using student, every trip, regardless of length:

1. Position the wheelchair in the designated station, facing forward, brakes locked.
2. Attach the front two tiedown straps to the chair's designated anchor points (WC19-compliant chairs have four accessible attachment points built for this) at roughly a 45° angle to the floor.
3. Attach the rear two tiedown straps at a similar angle, then tension all four straps evenly — no single strap should be visibly slack relative to the others.
4. Fit the pelvic belt low across the pelvis (not the abdomen), anchored to the vehicle structure — not to the wheelchair frame — at an angle commonly cited in WC19/UMTRI belt-positioning guidance as roughly 45–75° from horizontal.
5. Fit the shoulder belt, also vehicle-anchored, snug across the chest and shoulder, clear of the neck.
6. Recheck all four tiedown points and both belt anchors for tension before signaling the driver clear.

A tiedown "adjusted" mid-route instead of fully redone is not equivalent to step 6 — if a strap loosens en route, stop and re-run the full check, not a single-point tug.

## 4. Three-count headcount protocol

| Checkpoint | Count method | Action if mismatch |
|---|---|---|
| At boarding, each stop | Manifest name check, not a face scan | Re-verify before signaling driver clear |
| Mid-route (after the last scheduled pickup) | Seat-by-seat walk of the aisle, count against manifest total | Full re-check before continuing; radio dispatch if unresolved |
| At unloading, each stop | Confirm departing student against manifest and release protocol | Hold student on bus and radio dispatch if the required receiving adult isn't present |
| End of route | Seat-by-seat walk-through before exiting the vehicle | Log discrepancy immediately; do not leave the vehicle unattended until reconciled |

## 5. Medical/behavior event response template

1. Identify the flagged student's plan (IHCP or behavior intervention plan) — follow its specific steps and thresholds first.
2. Time the event from onset using a phone stopwatch or watch, not a subjective read of severity.
3. At the plan's defined pre-alert point, radio dispatch a status call (no request yet, just situational awareness).
4. At the plan's defined action threshold, execute the specified action (call 911, administer a specified device, etc.) immediately — do not wait to see if the event resolves past the threshold.
5. Log onset time, pre-alert time, threshold time, resolution time, and total duration in the incident report.
6. Reconcile the headcount before the route continues.

## 6. Evacuation drill-card format

One card per route, updated whenever the roster changes:

| Role | Assigned to | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| Front-door clearance | Driver | Clears ambulatory students through the front exit |
| Rear-door clearance | Monitor | Clears ambulatory students through the rear exit |
| Non-ambulatory assist — Student A | Monitor (primary), driver (backup) | Physically assists the named student per their individual evacuation plan |
| Rally-point headcount | Monitor | Counts evacuated students against the manifest at the designated rally point; reports discrepancy to driver/dispatch immediately |

Drills are rehearsed at the state-mandated cadence — commonly twice per school year, once in the first weeks of the term and once in the second semester — and the card is reissued any time a flagged student is added to or removed from the route.

## 7. Radio call templates

**Hazard callout (to driver):**
> "[Location relative to bus, e.g. 'your two o'clock, curb side'] — [specific hazard]."

**Headcount mismatch (to dispatch):**
> "Dispatch, Monitor on Bus [#], Route [#]. Headcount mismatch at [stop] — manifest shows [N], count shows [N-x]. Re-checking now, will confirm before continuing."

**Medical event (to dispatch):**
> "Dispatch, Monitor on Bus [#], Route [#]. Medical event — student [initials], [event type], onset [time]. Following care plan. [Status/next step]."
