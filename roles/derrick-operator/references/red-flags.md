# Red flags — Derrick Operator

### Trip-tank fill under calculated displacement by >15% across two or more consecutive stands
- **Usually means:** swab-induced influx from pulling pipe faster than the annulus can equalize; second most likely is a partially depleted or tight zone giving a real volumetric effect rather than an influx.
- **First question:** is the deficit a constant percentage stand over stand, or did one stand self-correct the next?
- **Data to pull:** trip-tank log for the last 10 stands, trip speed (stands/hour), and the pore-pressure/depletion notes for the interval being pulled through.

### Trip-tank gain or pit gain >5–10 bbl with no logged surface addition
- **Usually means:** kick in progress; second most likely is a surface transfer or mixing operation that wasn't logged.
- **First question:** was anything added to or transferred out of the active system in the last 15 minutes, and is it logged?
- **Data to pull:** pit volume totalizer (PVT) trend, mixing/transfer log, standpipe pressure if pumps are running.

### Connection or trip gas holding or climbing rather than peaking and falling within a stand or two
- **Usually means:** the well is unloading formation gas faster than background trip gas explains; second most likely is a genuinely gassy but stable interval that hasn't been re-baselined.
- **First question:** is this reading decaying like the last five connections, or is it the third climbing reading in a row?
- **Data to pull:** gas trend chart against depth and against the last 5–10 connections, current mud weight vs. pore-pressure program.

### Escape line (Geronimo/Gemini) not visibly rigged and tested at the current board position
- **Usually means:** it was tested at shift start but not re-verified after the board moved with the pipe being racked; second most likely is a genuinely missed pre-tour check.
- **First question:** when was this device last function-tested from this exact board height, by whom?
- **Data to pull:** pre-tour safety checklist sign-off, escape-line inspection tag/date.

### Mud weight reading more than ~0.2 ppg over the program value after a weight-up
- **Usually means:** barite added beyond the calculated sack count, often from mixing "extra to be safe"; second most likely is an inaccurate starting-volume figure that threw off the calculation.
- **First question:** how many sacks went in against how many the calculation called for, and was system volume re-measured before mixing?
- **Data to pull:** mud balance readings before/after, hopper sack count, active-system volume log.

### Funnel viscosity swinging more than ~5 sec/qt between checks with no chemical treatment logged
- **Usually means:** solids control (shaker screens, desander) not keeping pace with cuttings load; second most likely is an untreated influx of formation fluid changing rheology.
- **First question:** are the shaker screens blinded or torn, and has returns volume matched pumped volume?
- **Data to pull:** shaker screen mesh/condition log, flow-in vs. flow-out comparison, last chemical treatment record.

### Racked stand count within ~10% of the derrick's rated setback capacity
- **Usually means:** the trip is being deferred past the point the equipment rating supports; second most likely is a miscount on the trip sheet.
- **First question:** what is the rated setback for this rig, and has anyone cross-checked the trip-sheet tally against a physical count?
- **Data to pull:** rig equipment documentation (rated setback capacity), current trip-sheet stand tally.

### Shaker screen discarding drilling fluid at a visibly higher rate than normal with no cuttings-size change logged
- **Usually means:** screen mesh too coarse for current cuttings or a torn screen passing fluid through; second most likely is a genuine increase in penetration rate outpacing the current mesh.
- **First question:** has the ROP changed enough to explain this, or does the screen need inspection?
- **Data to pull:** screen mesh size and install date, ROP log, pit-level trend during the interval.

### Standpipe pressure step-change at a connection with no pump-rate change
- **Usually means:** a washout or plugged nozzle changing system pressure; second most likely is an early kick showing up as an unexpected pressure signature before volume confirms it.
- **First question:** did anything change at surface (pump rate, liner size) to explain this, or is the well the variable?
- **Data to pull:** standpipe pressure trend across the last several connections, pump strokes/rate log.
