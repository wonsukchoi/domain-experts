# Red Flags

Smell tests across teaching, research, and the job market. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### A comparative-religion reading list at 0% insider/primary sources for one tradition while another sits above 60%

- **Usually means:** the syllabus fails Moore's internal-diversity standard for one or more traditions — the course teaches those traditions only through an outside scholarly lens while letting another speak in its own voice.
- **First question:** "For the under-represented tradition, is there any assigned text a practitioner or scripture translation, or only outside analysis?"
- **Data to pull:** the reading-list audit table (readings per tradition, insider vs. outsider count).

### Lecture slides for one unit running 3x or more above the course's own baseline prescriptive-language rate

- **Usually means:** register drift from descriptive/comparative teaching toward advocacy or devotional framing on that specific unit — the actual mechanism behind most fairness complaints that hold up.
- **First question:** "Does this unit's slide language say what adherents believe, or does it assert what's true?"
- **Data to pull:** a sampled slide-register count (prescriptive vs. descriptive phrasing) for the flagged unit against the other units in the same course.

### Evaluation response rate under ~50% on a term with an outlier "fairness" item score

- **Usually means:** the respondents are a self-selected subgroup — a swing on this specific item can be real or can be nonresponse bias, and the survey number alone can't distinguish the two.
- **First question:** "What's the response rate, and does the material itself (reading list, slides) independently corroborate the item score, or only the survey?"
- **Data to pull:** response rate, item-level score vs. department mean, and an independent audit of the named course material.

### A philosophy grade dispute where the student's objection targets the conclusion, not the reconstruction or validity criteria

- **Usually means:** the student is disputing agreement with the paper's thesis, not a rubric-scored element — a real rubric failure and a values disagreement look similar from the outside but require opposite responses.
- **First question:** "Which specific rubric row — reconstruction, validity, soundness, objection-engagement — is being disputed?"
- **Data to pull:** the graded rubric with per-row scores, and the specific sentence in the paper the dispute is about.

### An ethics-unit syllabus that assigns arguments for only one side of a named contested question

- **Usually means:** the philosophy convention of presenting competing arguments and evaluating validity has collapsed into one-sided advocacy — a distinct failure mode from the religion-course register problem, but drawing a structurally similar complaint.
- **First question:** "What's the strongest published argument for the position not assigned, and where is its reading?"
- **Data to pull:** the unit's reading list sorted by position argued.

### A tenure-track job candidate five-plus years post-PhD still applying broadly with no updated placement-rate check

- **Usually means:** sunk-cost persistence against a base rate that hasn't been checked recently — APDA and PhilJobs data updates yearly and the discipline-wide rate can move.
- **First question:** "When was the current placement-rate data for this subfield last checked, and does the search strategy reflect it?"
- **Data to pull:** the current year's APDA placement report for the relevant subfield, and the candidate's application log (venues applied, outcomes, cycle count).

### A manuscript at a generalist flagship journal with no editor communication 12+ months past submission

- **Usually means:** normal for that tier given typical review times, but worth a status check rather than an assumption either way.
- **First question:** "Has it passed the journal's own stated or historically typical decision time, and by how much?"
- **Data to pull:** submission date, the venue's typical decision window, a dated status query sent to the editor.

### A hiring or curriculum discussion citing a PhD program's Philosophical Gourmet Report ranking alone as a placement or quality proxy

- **Usually means:** the ranking measures faculty reputation among survey respondents, not placement outcomes or teaching quality, and using it as a stand-in for either substitutes a convenient number for the one that actually matters.
- **First question:** "What does the candidate's or program's actual placement record show, independent of the ranking?"
- **Data to pull:** APDA placement data for the specific program and cohort years in question.

### An adjunct or visiting instructor carrying five or more distinct preps across two or more campuses in one term

- **Usually means:** the workload structurally forecloses research or job-search time this term regardless of the instructor's ability or effort.
- **First question:** "Can any of these sections be consolidated into repeated preps at the same total pay?"
- **Data to pull:** the term's course schedule by prep and campus, and the effective hourly rate per prep at current pay.

### A citation count or publication list presented in a hiring file without noting venue tier (generalist flagship vs. specialist)

- **Usually means:** the raw count is being used to imply a quality judgment that depends heavily on which tier of venue it came from and how long that tier's review process runs.
- **First question:** "For each listed publication, which tier of venue, and what's the base rejection rate at that tier?"
- **Data to pull:** the publication list annotated by venue tier, cross-checked against the journal-submission triage table.

### A student complaint conflating a professor's classroom description of a religious practice with personal advocacy for or against it

- **Usually means:** either a genuine register slip (see the slide-register flag above) or a complainant reading a descriptive statement as prescriptive — both possible, and the response differs.
- **First question:** "Was the statement in question descriptive ('adherents hold') or prescriptive ('this is true/false') in the actual lecture record?"
- **Data to pull:** the lecture notes or recording for the specific session named.
