# Red flags

### Wax/design pattern built to the die's exact measured dimension without alloy-specific casting shrinkage compensation applied
- **Usually means:** the finished restoration's margin will be measurably undersized, risking a clinically unacceptable gap.
- **First question:** what shrinkage rate was actually applied for this specific alloy?
- **Data to pull:** the wax/design spec compared against the alloy's documented shrinkage data.

### Incoming impression or digital scan shows a void, distortion, or pulled margin at the prep line, and fabrication proceeds anyway
- **Usually means:** building a precise restoration on top of an inaccurate capture, producing a precisely-wrong result.
- **First question:** was a re-impression requested, or did the case proceed on the flawed capture?
- **Data to pull:** impression/scan inspection notes compared against whether a re-impression was requested.

### Finished casting's margin gap, measured under magnification, exceeds the clinically accepted threshold
- **Usually means:** a cement-exposure/leakage pathway risking recurrent decay, regardless of restoration quality elsewhere.
- **First question:** what does the actual measured margin gap show against the lab's stated tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** the measured margin gap compared against the lab's stated acceptable tolerance.

### Occlusal surface built or sculpted with no verification against the articulator-mounted bite relationship
- **Usually means:** a high spot that will disrupt the patient's bite, discoverable only at try-in.
- **First question:** were articulating paper contact marks actually checked during the build process?
- **Data to pull:** articulating paper contact marks compared against the design/build process used.

### Shade match finalized based on hue alone without checking value against the target
- **Usually means:** a value mismatch that will be more visually noticeable to the patient than any hue difference.
- **First question:** what does the value/hue/chroma breakdown of the match actually show against the target shade?
- **Data to pull:** a value/hue/chroma breakdown compared against the target shade.

### Model or die shows a defect (chip, pour distortion) and design work proceeds on the flawed model without flagging it
- **Usually means:** the same precisely-wrong-result risk as fabricating on a bad impression, just surfacing one step later in the process.
- **First question:** was the model inspected before design began, and was a repour considered?
- **Data to pull:** model/die inspection notes compared against whether the model was repoured.

### A recurring case type (e.g. molar crowns from a specific referring dentist) shows a pattern of margin-fit or occlusion remakes
- **Usually means:** a systemic issue in impression technique, bite registration, or case communication from that source, not isolated lab errors.
- **First question:** does the remake log show a concentration tied to one referring dentist or case type?
- **Data to pull:** the remake log broken out by referring dentist and case type over a defined period.
