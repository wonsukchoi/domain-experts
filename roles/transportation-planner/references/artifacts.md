# Artifacts

Filled worksheets, not descriptions of worksheets. Populate the structure with real numbers for the plan or study at hand.

## 1. Fiscal-constraint worksheet (MTP/TIP)

Columns, filled with plausible example figures for a 20-year MTP:

| Project | Current-yr cost | Programmed year | Years out | Inflation factor (3.5%) | YOE cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arterial widening | $180.00M | 2028 | 2 | 1.0712 | $192.82M |
| BRT corridor | $95.00M | 2031 | 5 | 1.1877 | $112.83M |
| Bridge replacement | $60.00M | 2027 | 1 | 1.0350 | $62.10M |
| Interchange reconstruction | $220.00M | 2035 | 9 | 1.3629 | $299.84M |
| Multimodal corridor | $57.00M | 2040 | 14 | 1.6187 | $92.27M |
| **Total** | $612.00M | | | | **$759.86M** |

**Revenue forecast (YOE):** $850.00M
**Headroom:** $850.00M − $759.86M = **$90.14M**

Rule: never sum the current-year-cost column against the revenue forecast. If a project's programmed year is uncertain, use the latest plausible year in its funding-phase range (design/ROW/construction), not the earliest — a plan that assumes best-case delivery timing systematically understates YOE cost.

## 2. Environmental-justice benefits-burdens worksheet

| Project | YOE cost | % within/serving EJ tracts | EJ-credited $ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Arterial widening | $192.82M | 0% | $0.00M |
| BRT corridor | $112.83M | 40% | $45.13M |
| Bridge replacement | $62.10M | 0% | $0.00M |
| Interchange reconstruction | $299.84M | 0% | $0.00M |
| Multimodal corridor | $92.27M | 100% | $92.27M |
| **Total** | $759.86M | | **$137.40M** |

EJ investment share = $137.40M / $759.86M = **18.08%**
EJ population share (regional) = **28.00%**
Finding: investment share (18.08%) < population share (28.00%) → disproportionately low benefit. Document either added EJ-serving investment or a specific programmatic justification before the list goes to public comment. The same worksheet catches the opposite failure mode — a burden concentration — by adding a "displacement/negative externality" column scored the same way against population share.

## 4. Purpose-and-need statement structure (corridor study feeding future NEPA)

1. **Existing deficiency, stated with a number.** "The US-40/Main St intersection operates at LOS F (v/c = 1.14) during PM peak, and the corridor's 2020–2024 crash history shows 14 crashes at this location against a statewide critical crash rate of 8.2 for this facility type."
2. **Forecast condition without action.** "Regional travel demand model forecasts 2045 PM peak volume of 3,200 vph through this segment, a 22% increase over 2024 counts, worsening the LOS F condition."
3. **Purpose, stated broadly enough to admit multiple alternatives.** "Improve safety and operational performance at the US-40/Main St intersection and along the adjacent 1.5-mile corridor through 2045." (Not: "construct a grade-separated interchange at US-40/Main St" — that phrasing forecloses signal, roundabout, and access-management alternatives before they're studied.)
4. **Range of alternatives the purpose statement must plausibly support:** no-build, signal optimization/ITS, roundabout conversion, grade separation, transit/TDM demand-management package. If the purpose-and-need as drafted can't plausibly support at least three of these being carried into detailed analysis, redraft it broader before it goes out for scoping comment.
