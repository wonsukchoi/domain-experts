# Red Flags

### MTP/TIP project list summed in current-year dollars against a YOE revenue forecast
- **Usually means:** staff skipped the inflation step, or applied it to revenue but not costs, overstating available headroom.
- **First question:** "What inflation rate and compounding period was applied to each project's programmed-year cost?"
- **Data to pull:** the fiscal-constraint worksheet with per-project inflation factors and programmed years, not just the summary total.

### Travel demand model output quoted in a public document with no validation statistics attached
- **Usually means:** the model run was never checked against a screenline of actual traffic counts, or the check failed and got dropped from the write-up.
- **First question:** "What was the screenline volume deviation and the %RMSE against counts for this scenario?"
- **Data to pull:** the model validation memo or calibration report for the current model version.

### EJ analysis consists of a demographic overlay map with no benefit or burden percentage computed
- **Usually means:** the analysis stopped at "here's where EJ populations are" instead of "here's their share of investment or burden versus their population share."
- **First question:** "What percentage of plan investment is credited to EJ tracts, and how does that compare to their population share?"
- **Data to pull:** the benefits-burdens worksheet with per-project EJ-credit percentages.

### A project's rank changed between draft and final list with no rubric-score change documented
- **Usually means:** a board member's preference or a jurisdiction's political pressure overrode the adopted scoring criteria without a recorded justification.
- **First question:** "What in the adopted scoring criteria changed, or is this an undocumented board override?"
- **Data to pull:** the scoring rubric versions (draft vs. final) and the board meeting minutes where the change was discussed.

### A regionally significant project added to the TIP in a nonattainment area with no new conformity determination
- **Usually means:** staff treated the amendment as administrative when it actually required a new or updated regional emissions analysis.
- **First question:** "Is this project regionally significant, and when was the last conformity determination run?"
- **Data to pull:** the project's regional-significance determination and the date/scope of the most recent conformity analysis.

### Corridor study purpose-and-need statement names a specific solution (interchange, bypass) rather than a deficiency
- **Usually means:** the study was scoped to justify a predetermined outcome rather than to support a genuine alternatives analysis.
- **First question:** "Does this purpose-and-need statement plausibly support at least three distinct alternative types?"
- **Data to pull:** the draft purpose-and-need language and the list of alternatives it's meant to screen.

### Plan expands capacity on a corridor while a PM2/PM3 performance target for that same system is being missed
- **Usually means:** fix-it-first sequencing was skipped, or a capacity project got prioritized ahead of a condition target for political rather than technical reasons.
- **First question:** "What is this facility's current pavement/bridge condition score, and is the region on track to meet its adopted PM2 target?"
- **Data to pull:** the latest performance-measure report (23 CFR 490) and the region's target-progress tracking.

### A transit capital project's cost-effectiveness index has not been computed before significant design work began
- **Usually means:** staff assumed FTA Capital Investment Grant eligibility without running the screening math, risking a late-stage funding-rating surprise.
- **First question:** "What's the current cost-effectiveness index, and where does it fall against the current rating thresholds?"
- **Data to pull:** the CIG cost-effectiveness worksheet and current FTA rating guidance.

### Public comment summary shows near-unanimous opposition but the project list is unchanged with no written rationale
- **Usually means:** either the comment wasn't substantively weighed, or there's a documented reason the plan didn't change that simply wasn't written down — both are defensibility problems.
- **First question:** "What's the documented staff response to the comment, and does the adoption record show it was considered?"
- **Data to pull:** the public-involvement summary report and the staff response memo required under the Public Participation Plan.

### Revenue forecast for a 20-year MTP hasn't been updated since the last full plan cycle (4+ years)
- **Usually means:** the fiscal-constraint math is being run against a stale forecast that no longer reflects current federal apportionment levels or state revenue conditions.
- **First question:** "When was this revenue forecast last reconciled against actual apportionments and state revenue projections?"
- **Data to pull:** the revenue forecast methodology memo and its last revision date.
