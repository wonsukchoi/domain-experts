# Gambling Surveillance Officer and Gambling Investigator — Red Flags

### Payout on a hand exceeds the confirmed pre-deal wager, with no dealer correction call logged
- **Usually means:** a chip was added to the spot after the outcome was known (past-posting), not a dealer counting error.
- **First question:** "What was the wager at the frozen frame taken at 'no more bets,' and does it match what was paid?"
- **Data to pull:** frozen frame at the betting cutoff call, frame-by-frame review of the payout moment across at least two camera angles.

### Same dealer/pit combination shows a repeated short-pay or missed-collection pattern across more than one shift
- **Usually means:** either a training gap that hasn't been corrected, or an insider scheme with a repeat confederate — repetition is what shifts the read from error to collusion.
- **First question:** "Pull this dealer's last 10 shifts — does the pattern correlate with the same player or seat number each time?"
- **Data to pull:** dealer shift-assignment history cross-referenced against player-tracking data for the flagged seat.

### A player's bet-spread ratio is unusually wide and tracks the shoe's remaining composition
- **Usually means:** legal advantage play (card counting or shuffle tracking), not cheating — the technique uses only public information.
- **First question:** "Does the bet size correlate with count-favorable shoe positions across a large enough hand sample to be statistically meaningful, or is this a lucky high-roller session?"
- **Data to pull:** hand-by-hand bet-size log against reconstructed running count or shoe composition.

### A fill or credit slip is processed without a floor supervisor's countersignature visible on camera at the time it's walked
- **Usually means:** a control-procedure bypass, which is a finding independent of whether the dollar amount later reconciles.
- **First question:** "Was this slip countersigned before or after the chips moved, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** fill/credit slip paperwork timestamp against the camera timestamp of the physical chip transfer.

### Cash purchases at the cage are split across multiple transactions or windows to stay under a reporting threshold
- **Usually means:** structuring — an attempt to avoid a Currency Transaction Report by keeping each transaction under $10,000.
- **First question:** "Aggregated across today's gaming day and all cage stations, does this patron's total cross $10,000?"
- **Data to pull:** cage transaction log for the patron across all windows and times for the current gaming day.

### A camera blind spot or obstruction is discovered at a high-limit or previously-disputed table
- **Usually means:** equipment drift, an obstruction (new signage, a plant, a promotional display) placed without a coverage review, or in rare cases deliberate tampering.
- **First question:** "When did this blind spot first appear, and what changed in that area since the last coverage audit?"
- **Data to pull:** camera coverage matrix for that table and a timestamped comparison against the last verified full-coverage check.

### Footage tied to an open dispute or a reportable-threshold incident is nearing the standard retention purge window without an extended-retention flag
- **Usually means:** the reviewing officer hasn't yet formalized the flag, often because the report itself isn't finished — but the footage doesn't wait for the paperwork.
- **First question:** "Is this file flagged for extended retention right now, independent of whether the report is done?"
- **Data to pull:** the file's current retention-flag status and days remaining before the standard purge cycle.

### Chips or cash move between the cage and a pit without a second person present ("walked" unaccompanied)
- **Usually means:** a control bypass that removes the multi-person check meant to prevent unrecorded skimming during transport.
- **First question:** "Who authorized a single-person transport, and was it logged as an exception?"
- **Data to pull:** transport log and camera coverage of the cage-to-pit corridor for the relevant time window.

### A patron's face plausibly matches an entry in the known-cheat or flagged-advantage-player database
- **Usually means:** either a genuine repeat individual or a false positive from an imperfect facial-recognition match — confirmation, not confrontation, is the next step.
- **First question:** "What's the match confidence, and can it be confirmed quietly via ID at the cage or player's-club sign-in?"
- **Data to pull:** match-confidence score, prior incident file for the flagged identity, and current visit's sign-in ID.

### A dealer's hand or body consistently obscures the base of the deck or shoe during the deal
- **Usually means:** could be incidental positioning, but is also the setup for a false shuffle or a second-dealing technique — pattern across multiple deals is the discriminator.
- **First question:** "Does this obstruction happen on specific hands only, or every hand regardless of bet size?"
- **Data to pull:** multiple full-shoe reviews from a camera angle that isn't the one being obscured.

### Ops reports a hold-percentage variance on a table, but the surveillance log has no corresponding review entry for that shift
- **Usually means:** a gap between what operations is tracking and what surveillance actually reviewed — the two departments' monitoring isn't as connected as assumed.
- **First question:** "Was this table's shift actually reviewed by surveillance, or only flagged after the fact by the ops variance report?"
- **Data to pull:** surveillance shift-review log for that table/date against the operations variance report timestamp.
