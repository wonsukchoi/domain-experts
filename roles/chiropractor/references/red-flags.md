# Chiropractor — Red Flags

### New neurological deficit (weakness, numbness, dysarthria, visual change) during or immediately after cervical manipulation
- **Usually means:** Vertebral or carotid artery dissection precipitated or worsened by the thrust; less commonly, a transient nerve-root irritation.
- **First question:** Is the deficit focal and progressive, or diffuse and already improving?
- **Data to pull:** Time of onset relative to the manipulation, symptom progression over the next 15–30 minutes, prior vascular risk factors.

### Neck pain with headache, dizziness, diplopia, or dysarthria (the "5 Ds and 3 Ns" pattern)
- **Usually means:** Possible vertebrobasilar insufficiency or dissection rather than mechanical neck pain — the presentations can look identical in the first visit.
- **First question:** Did the headache or neurological symptom start before or after the neck pain?
- **Data to pull:** Onset sequence, symptom character, any recent neck trauma or sudden movement (e.g., chiropractic manipulation elsewhere, sports injury, whiplash).

### Progressive bilateral leg weakness, saddle anesthesia, or new bowel/bladder dysfunction
- **Usually means:** Cauda equina syndrome — a surgical emergency, absolute contraindication to manipulation.
- **First question:** Is there saddle-area numbness or a change in bladder control, specifically?
- **Data to pull:** Onset timeline (hours vs. weeks), bilateral vs. unilateral distribution, post-void residual if available.

### Unexplained weight loss, night pain unrelieved by position change, or history of cancer
- **Usually means:** Possible spinal malignancy or metastatic disease.
- **First question:** Does the pain wake the patient at night regardless of position?
- **Data to pull:** Weight-loss timeline and amount, cancer history, age (risk rises >50).

### Trauma history with point tenderness, especially in a patient >50 or with known osteoporosis
- **Usually means:** Possible vertebral fracture.
- **First question:** What was the mechanism and force of the trauma?
- **Data to pull:** Imaging if available, bone-density history, medication list (steroids, bisphosphonates).

### Fever with new spinal pain, especially with recent infection or IV drug use history
- **Usually means:** Possible discitis or vertebral osteomyelitis.
- **First question:** Is there a recent infection, dental procedure, or IV drug use history?
- **Data to pull:** Temperature, recent infection/procedure timeline, inflammatory markers if available.

### Positive cervical instability test (e.g., Sharp-Purser) or history of rheumatoid arthritis / Down syndrome
- **Usually means:** Atlantoaxial instability — absolute contraindication to cervical manipulation.
- **First question:** Is there a known connective-tissue or inflammatory condition affecting the cervical spine?
- **Data to pull:** Instability test results, imaging if available, relevant medical history.

### No meaningful change after a full defined trial (6–12 visits, condition-dependent)
- **Usually means:** Working diagnosis may be wrong, or the condition isn't manipulation-responsive.
- **First question:** Has the objective measure moved at all, even if slowly?
- **Data to pull:** Serial objective measurements across the trial, not just visit-to-visit self-report.

### Progressive worsening of objective findings despite ongoing treatment
- **Usually means:** Current approach isn't working or an undetected red flag is present.
- **First question:** Is the trend consistent across visits or a single-visit outlier?
- **Data to pull:** Serial ROM/orthopedic-test results plotted over the course of care.

### Patient on anticoagulant therapy presenting for cervical manipulation
- **Usually means:** Elevated bleeding/vascular-injury risk with HVLA technique specifically.
- **First question:** What anticoagulant, at what dose, and how recently was it taken?
- **Data to pull:** Medication list, INR if on warfarin, indication for anticoagulation.
