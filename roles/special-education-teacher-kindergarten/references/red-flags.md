# Red flags

### An IFSP developmental goal copied into the IEP without rewriting
- **Usually means:** the goal isn't measurable against a kindergarten standard and won't hold up as an enforceable IEP target.
- **First question:** can this goal be restated as an observable classroom task with a baseline number and a target number?
- **Data to pull:** the IFSP transition summary, the current IEP draft's goal language.

### A behavior goal written for a child whose skill level was never directly probed 1:1
- **Usually means:** the team may be treating an unconfirmed skill deficit as a performance deficit, targeting the wrong intervention.
- **First question:** has the child's actual skill level been checked 1:1, with no audience, before the behavior goal was written?
- **Data to pull:** whole-group observation data, any existing 1:1 probe results.

### A whole-group accuracy score more than 30 percentage points below a 1:1 probe score
- **Usually means:** a performance/participation problem is masking as a skill deficit, or vice versa.
- **First question:** which specific condition (group size, novelty, adult proximity) is driving the gap?
- **Data to pull:** both scores, session-by-session, with setting notes for each.

### A self-contained or resource-room placement recommended at initial kindergarten eligibility without a general-education-with-support trial
- **Usually means:** placement inertia risk — first placements tend to persist through elementary school even after need decreases.
- **First question:** what specific data shows the child cannot access core instruction in general education even with accommodations and small-group support?
- **Data to pull:** accommodation trial log, any general-education access data collected before the recommendation.

### Present-levels data drawn from a single evaluation session with no classroom-observation corroboration
- **Usually means:** the score may understate the child's actual skill due to fatigue, novelty, or examiner rapport on a single test day.
- **First question:** is there a second data point (2+ weeks of teacher observation, parent report) confirming this score?
- **Data to pull:** the evaluation report date and conditions, classroom observation log.

### An inclusion-model lesson plan with no accommodated/modified/parallel breakdown by subject block
- **Usually means:** the general-education teacher is left to improvise access decisions in real time, producing inconsistent instruction.
- **First question:** for this subject block, is the child following the same objective with support, a modified objective, or a fully parallel activity?
- **Data to pull:** the co-planning document, the general-education teacher's lesson plan.

### An RTI/MTSS referral for evaluation with no documented intervention-fidelity data
- **Usually means:** the team cannot tell whether the child failed to respond to intervention or the intervention was never delivered as designed.
- **First question:** was the Tier 2/3 intervention delivered with fidelity, and is there session-level documentation?
- **Data to pull:** intervention fidelity checklist, session logs, progress-monitoring graph.

### A kindergarten IEP goal set using the eligibility category label rather than the child's specific present-levels profile
- **Usually means:** the goal may be generic and not tied to an actual, measured deficit for this child.
- **First question:** does this goal trace to a specific present-levels data point, or was it copied from a category-typical template?
- **Data to pull:** the present-levels section, the goal's stated baseline.

### A placement or goal decision finalized before the family was walked through the IFSP-to-IEP format change
- **Usually means:** the family may not understand why the measurement approach and meeting cadence changed, causing confusion or distrust later.
- **First question:** has the transition from developmental-milestone language to measurable academic/functional goals been explained to the family directly?
- **Data to pull:** the parent-communication log, transition-conference notes.
