# Secretary/Administrative Assistant (General) — Red Flags

### Two requests for the same time block or deadline, accepted without either requester told
- **Usually means:** the collision was resolved silently by picking whichever request felt easier or came from the more senior/insistent manager, not by an explicit rule.
- **First question:** "Does either requester know their request is competing with another one for the same window?"
- **Data to pull:** the queue entries for both requests, their stated deadlines, and whether a collision-notice message was ever sent.

### Weekly committed hours exceed available hours before the week starts
- **Usually means:** requests were accepted on a first-ask basis without checking capacity, and something will slip — the only open question is which task and how visibly.
- **First question:** "Which of the committed tasks has the softest deadline, and has that manager been told it might move?"
- **Data to pull:** the capacity-tracking worksheet's total-if-accepted column for the current week.

### A commitment exists only as a remembered hallway conversation, not a queue entry
- **Usually means:** the task will be forgotten or under-prioritized the moment something else takes attention, since nothing forces it back into view.
- **First question:** "Is this logged anywhere both the requester and I can see it?"
- **Data to pull:** the shared queue — if the task isn't there, it isn't tracked, regardless of whether it was verbally agreed to.

### A manager's request is marked urgent and this manager marks most requests urgent
- **Usually means:** either genuine urgency this time, or a pattern of over-marking that's degraded the label's meaning — the two require different responses.
- **First question:** "What happens if this specific task slips by one day?"
- **Data to pull:** this manager's last 5-10 requests and whether their stated urgency matched the actual consequence of a delay.

### Formatting or tone from one manager's material is being applied as the department default
- **Usually means:** a document is about to go out in the wrong voice for its actual audience or owner, risking it being sent back or, worse, sent out under the wrong name's style.
- **First question:** "Whose document is this, and did I check their specific preferences or just reuse the last template I had open?"
- **Data to pull:** the requesting manager's prior documents of the same type.

### A recurring scheduling conflict keeps getting resolved case-by-case instead of by a standing rule
- **Usually means:** the absence of a tie-break rule is costing repeated negotiation time and risks looking arbitrary to whichever party loses most often.
- **First question:** "Has this exact conflict happened before, and if so, why hasn't a standing rule been proposed yet?"
- **Data to pull:** the queue's history for the same requesters/time-slot pattern over the last month.

### A deferred task has no new target date attached
- **Usually means:** it's been dropped in practice even if nobody said so out loud — an unscheduled deferral is functionally a silent cancellation.
- **First question:** "When is this actually coming back, and does the requester know that date?"
- **Data to pull:** the queue entry's status field and whether a follow-up date exists.

### Every minor scheduling overlap is being escalated to all affected managers
- **Usually means:** the overcorrection after a prior conflict handled badly — now nothing gets resolved independently, even conflicts a standing rule already covers.
- **First question:** "Does an existing tie-break rule already resolve this without needing everyone's input?"
- **Data to pull:** the team's documented tie-break rules, checked before escalating.
