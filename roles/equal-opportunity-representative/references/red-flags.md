### Four-fifths rule is triggered but the underlying sample size is very small (a handful of hires)
- **Usually means:** The ratio may be an artifact of small numbers rather than a real pattern — a single hire/non-hire can swing the ratio dramatically at small sample sizes.
- **First question:** How many total selections does this ratio represent, and would a statistical significance test even be meaningful at this sample size?
- **Data to pull:** Total applicant and selection counts by group for the specific job group/cycle.

### Four-fifths rule and statistical significance test disagree
- **Usually means:** The finding is genuinely ambiguous and needs further investigation (stage-level breakdown), not a declaration that adverse impact is confirmed or cleared based on picking one test.
- **First question:** Does breaking the pipeline down by stage (application, interview, offer) isolate where the disparity actually originates?
- **Data to pull:** Stage-level selection rates by group.

### An adverse-impact finding is reported only at the aggregate hiring-rate level
- **Usually means:** The finding doesn't yet indicate what to fix — the disparity could originate at any stage of the process.
- **First question:** Has the pipeline been broken down by stage to isolate which specific step produces the disparity?
- **Data to pull:** Stage-by-stage applicant flow data.

### A placement goal was set based on a general diversity target rather than availability-analysis data
- **Usually means:** The goal may not be defensible or practically useful, since it wasn't derived from the actual qualified labor-market and internal-pool availability for the specific job group.
- **First question:** What external labor-market and internal promotable-pool data was used to derive this specific goal?
- **Data to pull:** Availability analysis data sources (census/labor-force data, internal promotable-pool records) for the job group.

### A hiring decision references a placement goal as a reason for selecting a specific candidate
- **Usually means:** The goal is being treated as a quota applied to an individual decision, which creates reverse-discrimination risk rather than the intended good-faith-effort framing.
- **First question:** Was this specific hiring decision based on individual qualifications, with the goal informing only broader recruitment/outreach efforts?
- **Data to pull:** Hiring decision documentation, recruitment/outreach effort records tied to the goal.

### A raw, unadjusted compensation gap between groups is reported without a controlled regression analysis
- **Usually means:** The gap hasn't yet been tested against legitimate, job-related factors (tenure, performance, education, level) that could explain some or all of it.
- **First question:** Has a regression analysis controlling for these factors been run, and does a statistically significant gap remain after controlling for them?
- **Data to pull:** Compensation data with tenure, performance, education, and job-level fields for the population being analyzed.

### A compensation-equity regression finds a significant aggregate gap with no analysis of which specific roles or individuals drive it
- **Usually means:** The aggregate finding doesn't yet point to an actionable remediation — averaging across many roles can mask where the actual disparity concentrates.
- **First question:** Which specific roles, levels, or individuals contribute most to the aggregate unexplained gap?
- **Data to pull:** Regression residuals broken down by role/level/individual.

### An OFCCP audit request is being answered with individual data requests handled piecemeal rather than a complete AAP submission
- **Usually means:** Incomplete responses tend to generate follow-up requests and extend the audit — a complete, organized submission against the standard AAP checklist is more efficient and defensible.
- **First question:** Has the response been checked against the complete list of standard AAP components before submission?
- **Data to pull:** OFCCP's specific audit request/scheduling letter, internal AAP component checklist.
