# Artifacts

Filled templates for the three documents a property developer produces most: the residual land value calculation, the capital stack summary, and an option-structured LOI.

## Residual land value calculation (filled example)

```markdown
# Residual Land Value — 45-unit townhome development, Site B

Projected sellout value: 45 units × $625,000 avg (comp-based)     = $28,125,000
Less hard costs: $195,000/unit × 45                                = $8,775,000
Less soft costs (16% of hard costs)                                 = $1,404,000
Less financing carry (18-mo construction, 70% LTC, 8.0% rate)       = $1,180,000
Less required developer profit (12% of gross sellout, this
  product type/market carries lower entitlement risk than infill
  multifamily, hence lower required margin)                         = $3,375,000
----------------------------------------------------------------
Maximum supportable land value                                     = $13,391,000

Seller ask: $12,900,000 — BELOW residual, deal supports the ask with room.
Decision: proceed to LOI at asking price; no option structure needed,
site is already zoned for this use (no entitlement contingency required).
```

## Capital stack summary (filled example)

```markdown
# Capital Stack — 120-unit multifamily, Site A

Total project cost: $43,700,000
  Land (per residual calc)             $5,900,000    13.5%
  Hard costs                           $29,400,000   67.3%
  Soft costs                           $5,290,000    12.1%
  Financing carry (construction)       $3,110,000     7.1%

Sources:
  Senior construction loan (65% LTC)   $28,405,000   65.0%
    Rate: 8.5% floating, DSCR covenant: 1.25x at stabilization,
    LTC ceiling: 65% — breach triggers lender consent requirement
    for any change order over $250K.
  Preferred equity (12% pref, 20% GP promote above 15% IRR hurdle)
                                        $10,925,000   25.0%
  GP/sponsor equity                     $4,370,000   10.0%

Coverage check: projected stabilized NOI $3,890,000 / annual debt
service $3,105,000 = DSCR 1.25x — exactly at covenant floor, no buffer.
FLAG: any construction cost overrun or lease-up delay pushes DSCR
below covenant. Recommend negotiating a 1.30x target or building a
$400K contingency reserve outside the construction loan draw.
```

## Option-structured LOI (filled example, entitlement-contingent deal)

```markdown
# Letter of Intent — Site C, 8.4 acres

Buyer proposes the following non-binding terms:

Purchase price: $5,900,000 (based on residual land value at 120-unit
  entitled density; see attached calculation)
Structure: Option agreement, not fee-simple purchase at signing.
Option deposit: $500,000, non-refundable, credited to purchase price
  at closing if buyer proceeds.
Due diligence / entitlement period: 18 months from execution, covering
  (a) environmental Phase I/II, (b) rezoning application to 120-unit
  density, (c) utility capacity confirmation.
Closing condition: purchase closes only upon final, non-appealable
  entitlement approval at or above 100 units. Below 100 units, buyer
  may terminate and forfeit deposit, or renegotiate price against a
  recalculated residual land value at the approved density.
Extension: buyer may extend the entitlement period by 6 months upon
  payment of an additional $150,000 non-refundable deposit, applicable
  toward purchase price.
Broker commission: per separate agreement, payable at closing only.

This LOI is non-binding except for the option deposit and confidentiality
terms, which become binding upon execution of a definitive option agreement.
```
