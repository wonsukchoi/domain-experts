# Red flags

### Land price at or above "as-entitled" comp value before entitlement is secured
- **Usually means:** the deal is pricing the land as if the rezoning or variance is already approved, leaving no margin for the real chance of denial or a reduced approved density.
- **First question:** "what does this site support at its *current* zoning, and what's that residual value?"
- **Data to pull:** current zoning designation, residual land value at current (not requested) density.

### Construction bids come in >10–15% over budgeted hard costs
- **Usually means:** either the original budget was built on stale unit-cost data, or scope crept between design development and bid without a corresponding budget update.
- **First question:** "which trade packages drove the overage, and were they re-bid or just accepted?"
- **Data to pull:** trade-by-trade bid comparison against the original budget line items.

### Absorption rate assumption exceeds the submarket's trailing-12-month historical average
- **Usually means:** the pro forma was built to make the deal pencil rather than to reflect actual market pace, often under pressure to hit a target IRR.
- **First question:** "show me the comp set's actual monthly absorption for the last 12 months — where does our assumption sit against it?"
- **Data to pull:** submarket absorption data from a market study or MLS/comparable-project sales pace.

### GC not signed to a GMP contract before major capital commitment or groundbreaking
- **Usually means:** the deal proceeded on a cost-plus or unsigned basis under schedule pressure, leaving construction cost overrun risk sitting entirely with the developer's equity.
- **First question:** "what contract type governs cost overrun risk on this job, and who signed it?"
- **Data to pull:** the executed GC contract, specifically its pricing structure clause.

### Projected DSCR sits at or below the lender's covenant minimum with no buffer
- **Usually means:** the deal was underwritten to just clear the covenant at the base case, with zero room for lease-up delay or a rent assumption miss.
- **First question:** "what does DSCR look like at 90% of projected NOI instead of 100%?"
- **Data to pull:** the DSCR sensitivity table across a range of NOI outcomes, not just the base case.

### Entitlement timeline has slipped >3 months with no updated carry-cost recalculation
- **Usually means:** the team is tracking the entitlement process but not translating delay into its dollar cost on the loan balance, so the financial impact stays invisible until it's large.
- **First question:** "what's the updated carry cost given the actual timeline so far, not the original schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** current loan balance × rate ÷ 12 × months of slippage, compared against original carry cost line item.

### Exit cap rate assumption sits below current market comps for stabilized assets in the submarket
- **Usually means:** the pro forma is using an aspirational or stale cap rate to inflate projected exit value rather than current comparable sales.
- **First question:** "what are the three most recent comparable stabilized-asset sales trading at, and how does our exit assumption compare?"
- **Data to pull:** recent comparable sales cap rates from broker data or public records.

### Change orders during construction exceed the contingency budget (typically 5–10% of hard costs) before 50% completion
- **Usually means:** the contingency is burning at a rate that won't survive the remaining scope, often signaling design documents weren't complete enough at bid.
- **First question:** "at this burn rate, what's the projected contingency shortfall at completion?"
- **Data to pull:** cumulative change order log against remaining contingency balance and percentage of completion.

### Pre-leasing or pre-sales pace is significantly behind pro forma at the midpoint of the marketing period
- **Usually means:** either pricing is off-market, the marketing campaign underperformed, or the submarket's actual demand doesn't match the underwriting assumption.
- **First question:** "is this a pricing problem, a marketing problem, or a demand problem — and how do we know which?"
- **Data to pull:** unit-by-unit pricing against comparable active listings, plus lead-to-lease conversion data.

### Mezzanine or preferred equity layered in without modeling its compounding effect on total cost of capital
- **Usually means:** the additional capital solved an immediate funding gap without anyone recalculating the blended cost of capital and its effect on required returns for the equity below it.
- **First question:** "what's the new blended cost of capital with this layer added, and does the deal still clear its return hurdle?"
- **Data to pull:** updated capital stack with blended weighted-average cost of capital recalculated.
