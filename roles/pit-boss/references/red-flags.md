# Red flags

Smell tests a pit boss catches from the shift log or the floor, before the shift-manager review finds them. Format: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

## 1. Fill-to-credit ratio ≥ 3:1 on a table in a single session, no surveillance note

**Usually means:** either genuine bad variance on a hot table, or a procedural gap — a dealer not flagging a pattern surveillance should have already reviewed. The process gap is the finding even when the dollar swing turns out to sit inside a normal variance band.

**First question:** "Has surveillance been asked to review this table in the last 45 minutes — yes or no?"

**Data to pull:** fill/credit slips for the session with timestamps, the table's average-bet and hands-per-hour to compute theoretical win, and the surveillance log for any prior note on this table or dealer.

## 2. Dealer's hands-per-hour drops >20% below rolling average

**Usually means:** fatigue past the rotation window, or the dealer is behind on a verification step (count, hole-card check, payout confirmation) they're not skipping but slowing down for — rarely simple laziness.

**First question:** "When was this dealer's last break, and how long have they been on this table?"

**Data to pull:** rotation schedule vs. actual relief times, hand-count log for the session, any recent procedure corrections logged for this dealer.

## 3. Same two (or more) players repeatedly seated together at high-limit tables

**Usually means:** could be nothing — friends who play together — but is also the signature of team play (signaling, bankroll pooling) or a dealer-patron collusion scheme if one of the "players" is a former employee or has an unusual relationship to staff.

**First question:** "Pull the last 90 days of ratings — do these two show up together at a rate that looks coincidental?"

**Data to pull:** player-tracking co-occurrence report, win/loss pattern for the pairing vs. each player's individual history, surveillance review of hand signals or betting synchrony.

## 4. Repeated buy-ins just under $10,000, same patron, across a shift or property visits

**Usually means:** structuring — deliberately staying under the CTR aggregation threshold — or, less often, an innocent pattern of round-number buy-ins that happens to cluster near the line.

**First question:** "What does the MTL show for this patron across today's transactions, aggregated?"

**Data to pull:** MTL entries for the patron for the gaming day, prior CTRs filed on this patron, cage records of any structuring pattern across visits.

## 5. Fill or credit slip missing an independent countersignature

**Usually means:** either a rushed shift where a control step got skipped under table-open pressure, or — the reason the control exists — an attempt to move chips without independent verification.

**First question:** "Who prepared this slip, who signed it, and were those two different people at two different stations?"

**Data to pull:** the slip itself, the cage log for the corresponding chip issue/receipt, whether this is a first occurrence or a pattern for this shift/dealer.

## 6. Player rated far above their observed buy-in size

**Usually means:** the rating was taken off buy-in amount instead of average bet × duration × house edge — a common shortcut under time pressure that both over-comps low-volume high-buy-in players and under-comps grinders.

**First question:** "What's the theoretical win calculation behind this rating, not the buy-in?"

**Data to pull:** average-bet log for the session, session duration, hands-per-hour for the game, recomputed theo.

## 7. A hot or cold table triggers an immediate dealer pull or patron accusation

**Usually means:** a single session's variance, mistaken for a signal — blackjack and baccarat sessions can run several standard deviations off theoretical win from pure chance, especially over a few hundred hands.

**First question:** "What's this table's or dealer's result over the last 4–6 shifts, not just tonight?"

**Data to pull:** table win/hold history across recent shifts, theoretical win computation for tonight's session, whether the swing exceeds roughly 2 standard deviations for the hand count involved.

## 8. Surveillance flag noted but no documented pit follow-up

**Usually means:** the flag was seen and judged low-priority in the moment, and never got closed out — which looks identical, in a later review, to the flag being ignored entirely.

**First question:** "What was done in response to this flag, and where's it logged?"

**Data to pull:** the original surveillance note, the shift log for the corresponding time window, any escalation record to the shift manager.

## 9. Marker or credit-line increase approved without a host or credit-manager review

**Usually means:** a pit-level shortcut taken to keep a player at the table, bypassing the credit check the property's marker policy requires — the exposure lands on the casino if the marker isn't collectible.

**First question:** "Whose authority approved this line increase, and is it on file?"

**Data to pull:** the patron's existing approved credit line, the marker request and approval chain, credit department's most recent review date for this patron.

## 10. Table inventory discrepancy at open or close exceeding tolerance

**Usually means:** most often a miscount during a rushed handoff; less often, chips missing between the last verified count and now.

**First question:** "Was the outgoing and incoming dealer both present and counting independently at handoff?"

**Data to pull:** the open/close count sheets for the last three shifts on this table, whether the discrepancy recurs on the same table or dealer pairing, drop-box seal numbers for the period.

## 11. Continuous deal time on a high-limit table exceeds the property's rotation policy

**Usually means:** short-staffing pressure kept a dealer on past the fatigue-risk window, trading a filled seat now for a higher error rate later in the session.

**First question:** "Why did this dealer run past the 20-minute high-limit window — no relief available, or the rotation wasn't followed?"

**Data to pull:** rotation schedule vs. actual relief log, staffing roster for the shift, any procedure errors logged for this dealer in the extended window.
