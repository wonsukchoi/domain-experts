# Fine Artist Playbook

Filled examples for the recurring transactions of a studio practice: pricing, consignment, licensing, and grant/exhibition submissions.

## Pricing a new series

Anchor table — pulled before setting any new price:

| Series | Size | Last price | Sell-through | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| "Coastal Studies" | 24x30 | $5,200 | 6/8 (75%) | hold or +5-10% |
| "Coastal Studies II" | 24x30 | $5,750 | 9/10 (90%) | +10-20% next series |
| "Interior Fragments" | 30x40 | $8,000 | 12/14 (86%) | +10-20% next series |

Rule applied: sell-through ≥75% with no single-collector concentration (no buyer taking >20% of the show) → increase 10-20% on the next comparable body. Sell-through 50-74% → hold price, address via marketing or venue, not price. Sell-through <50% → do not increase; consider a smaller format, a different price tier, or a different venue before touching the flagship price.

Price ladder discipline: never publish two different prices for objectively comparable work in the same period (same series, same size, same year) across venues — a collector who finds a lower price at a fair than at the gallery stops trusting either number.

## Consignment agreement — filled template

```
CONSIGNMENT AGREEMENT

Artist: [Name]                          Gallery: [Gallery LLC]
Effective date: [date]                  Term: 12 months, auto-renews unless
                                         either party gives 60 days' notice

Works consigned (attach schedule):
  1. "Untitled (Coastal VII)" — oil on canvas, 24x30in, 2026 — Retail: $5,750
  2. "Interior Fragment 3"    — oil on canvas, 30x40in, 2026 — Retail: $9,200
  ...

Commission split: 50% Gallery / 50% Artist on all sales at listed retail price.
  Discounts >10% off retail require Artist's written approval before sale.

Exclusivity: Gallery has exclusive representation for [paintings and works
  on paper only] within [metro area / region]. Artist retains the right to
  place sculptural and edition work, and to sell directly from the studio,
  outside this territory.

Insurance: Gallery insures consigned work at full retail value while in
  Gallery's possession; Artist to receive proof of coverage before delivery.

Trust status: Consigned work remains Artist's property held in trust by
  Gallery per [State] Arts and Cultural Affairs Law consignment statute;
  not subject to claims of Gallery's creditors.

Payment: Gallery remits Artist's share within 30 days of sale, with a
  sale report itemizing buyer, price, date, and any discount applied.

Return: Unsold work returned to Artist within 30 days of term end or
  written request, at Gallery's cost for shipping/insurance in transit.

Copyright: Sale of a work transfers title to the physical object only.
  Artist retains all copyright, reproduction, and licensing rights per
  17 U.S.C. §202 unless assigned separately in writing.

Signatures: ______________________          ______________________
            Artist                          Gallery
```

## Licensing / reproduction request — decision table

| Ask | Default term | Default fee basis |
|---|---|---|
| One-time editorial reproduction (magazine, book plate) | Non-exclusive, one-time use, credit required | Flat fee, ~$150-$800 depending on circulation |
| Merchandise (print, textile, homeware) | Non-exclusive, 2-3 year term, specific SKU list | Royalty (5-10% of wholesale) or flat buyout priced at 3-5x an estimated annual royalty |
| Commercial/advertising use | Exclusive within category, defined territory, 1-2 year term | Flat fee scaled to media spend/reach — do not price like editorial |
| Perpetual worldwide exclusive buyout | Only if fee reflects foreclosing all future licensing of that image | Fee = estimated lifetime royalty value, not a "nice round number" |

## Nonprofit exhibition/loan fee request — filled example

Institution operating budget: $2.1M/year (mid-size regional nonprofit).
Request: solo exhibition, 6 weeks, 18 works, artist talk included.

```
Exhibition fee request:

Based on comparable nonprofit exhibition fee benchmarks (W.A.G.E.
Certification methodology, scaled to organizational operating budget),
requesting:
  - Exhibition fee: $3,000
  - Artist talk/lecture: $500
  - Shipping and insurance: institution to cover, itemized separately
  - Total: $3,500 plus expenses

If budget constraints apply, open to a reduced fee in exchange for a
documented publication (catalog essay, press) with agreed print run
and distribution, valued against the fee gap.
```

## Grant / exhibition submission — artist statement opening (filled)

Weak (generic, derivable from a prompt): "My work explores themes of memory and place through abstraction."

Strong (concrete, non-derivable): "I build up and sand back twelve to fifteen layers of oil and cold wax per painting, a process that takes 3-4 weeks per piece and leaves visible strata of color where sanding exposes earlier layers — the finished surface is a record of decisions made and reversed, not a single gesture."

Rule: first sentence names the material process and a concrete number (layers, weeks, dimensions) before any thematic language. Jurors reading 200 statements skim past adjectives; a specific process claim is the one thing they remember.

Reconcile this sheet against every gallery sale report received; a gallery reporting a sale not matched on this sheet, or failing to report a sale a collector mentions directly, is the first sign of a consignment problem (see `references/red-flags.md`).
