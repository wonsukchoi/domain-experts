# Red flags

Smell tests a practitioner catches on the first pass through a pen, pasture, or pond. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before deciding a response.

### Dawn DO reading below 2 mg/L
- **Usually means:** overnight algae respiration combined with a recent cloudy day or algae die-off has consumed available oxygen faster than it's replenished; second likeliest cause is a stocking density increase without matching aeration capacity.
- **First question:** "What's the current biomass estimate for this pond, and what's the installed aeration horsepower against it?"
- **Data to pull:** dawn/dusk DO log for the last 7 days, cloud-cover and wind notes, stocking and last-known-weight records, aerator run-time log.

### Herd-wide BCS drop of more than 1 point over 60 days
- **Usually means:** pasture forage quality decline (drought, overgrazing) or a rising parasite load across the group; less likely, a ration formulation change that wasn't flagged.
- **First question:** "Has anyone pulled a fecal egg count or a forage/dry-matter test in the last month?"
- **Data to pull:** BCS trend log by animal, forage/dry-matter analysis, fecal egg count results, ration formulation history.

### Feed conversion ratio rising more than 10–15% with stable feed inputs
- **Usually means:** subclinical illness or, in aquaculture, a water-quality decline that hasn't yet produced visible mortality; second likeliest cause, an unlogged change in stocking or a new lot introduced without adjusting feed rate.
- **First question:** "Has water quality, temperature, or group composition changed in the window this FCR trend covers?"
- **Data to pull:** 30-day rolling FCR trend, DO/water-quality log (aquaculture) or water-trough and mineral-access check (livestock), recent introduction/movement records.

### New animal introduced without the standard 21–30 day quarantine
- **Usually means:** labor shortage or time pressure led someone to skip the isolation step; less often, a mistaken belief that a health certificate alone substitutes for the observation period.
- **First question:** "Where is this animal's isolation pen, and what's on its health certificate?"
- **Data to pull:** quarantine log, transport and health certificate, vaccination record, date of introduction to the main group.

### Repeated balking at the same point in a chute or alleyway
- **Usually means:** a shadow, reflection, drain grate, or texture/color change at that spot in the animal's visual field, not temperament (Grandin's flight-zone diagnosis); second likeliest cause, prior negative experience (e.g., prod use) associated with that location.
- **First question:** "Has anyone walked that spot at the animal's eye level to see what it sees?"
- **Data to pull:** facility layout diagram, time-of-day lighting notes, any video of the balk point, handling-incident log for that location.

### Fish gasping or piping at the surface at dawn
- **Usually means:** an active or imminent dissolved-oxygen crash, most likely in warm months following an algae bloom or a string of hot, still nights.
- **First question:** "What's the DO reading right now, not the last logged one?"
- **Data to pull:** immediate DO reading, water temperature, time of day, aerator run status, weather over the last 48 hours.

### Mortality clustered in one section of a pen or pond rather than spread evenly
- **Usually means:** a localized cause — feed spoilage in one delivery zone, a dead-algae mat, a contaminated inflow point — rather than a systemic disease spreading population-wide.
- **First question:** "Can we map exactly where each mortality was found?"
- **Data to pull:** mortality log with location, feed lot numbers by delivery zone, water-quality readings by section, inflow/drainage points.

### Lameness scores rising across multiple animals in the same pen
- **Usually means:** a recent ration change with excess grain/starch (subacute ruminal acidosis → laminitis) or prolonged wet housing conditions; less commonly, an infectious hoof condition.
- **First question:** "What's changed in the ration or the housing surface in the last two to three weeks?"
- **Data to pull:** ration formulation history and date of any change, hoof-trim and lameness-score records, housing moisture/bedding logs.

### Appetite looks normal but an animal is isolating from the group or standing apart
- **Usually means:** early-stage respiratory or digestive illness — appetite is frequently the last symptom to change, and isolation/posture change is an earlier behavioral signal.
- **First question:** "What's its temperature right now?"
- **Data to pull:** temperature log, recent stress events (weaning, transport, ration change), intake records for the pen.

### Vaccination log shows gaps at the point new animals joined the herd
- **Usually means:** the biosecurity protocol was followed on paper but not on the actual calendar date, usually under labor or time pressure at introduction.
- **First question:** "Pull the full vaccination schedule against the actual administration dates — where's the gap?"
- **Data to pull:** vaccination protocol document, administration log with dates, introduction date for each animal in question.

### Stocking density above the published guideline with no aeration capacity added since
- **Usually means:** production pressure to add stock outpaced infrastructure investment, often incrementally over several stocking cycles rather than in one deliberate decision.
- **First question:** "What's the installed aeration horsepower per 1,000 lb of biomass at expected harvest weight, not stocking weight?"
- **Data to pull:** stocking records over time, aerator inventory and horsepower, projected harvest-weight biomass estimate.
