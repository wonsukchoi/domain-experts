# Religious Education Director — playbook

Filled examples an agent can populate or execute directly. Numbers are plausible worked examples, not universal constants — recompute against the specific congregation's data.

## 1. Child-safety policy (filled template)

**Two-adult rule.** No adult is ever alone with a minor who is not their own child. Minimum: two screened adults in every classroom, or one adult plus a visible window/open door and a second adult checking in at a set interval (every 15 minutes) for spaces too small for two.

**Six-month rule.** A new volunteer may assist in a classroom under direct supervision immediately after screening, but may not be left in sole responsibility for a room (even alongside a second unscreened adult) until: (a) six months of regular attendance at the congregation, (b) background check cleared, (c) safety training module completed and logged.

**Background-check cadence:**

| Volunteer category | Initial check | Re-check interval |
|---|---|---|
| Classroom teacher/aide, direct minor contact | Before first unsupervised session | Every 3 years |
| Transportation (driving minors) | Before first drive, includes MVR check | Every 3 years |
| Overnight/off-site trip chaperone | Before the specific trip, even if current | Every 3 years, plus per-trip refresh if >18 months since last check |
| Office/admin, no direct minor contact | At hire | Every 5 years |

**Incident-reporting chain:** volunteer → DRE (same day, verbal + written log) → clergy/head of staff (within 24 hours) → denominational/diocesan safe-environment office and, where mandated-reporting thresholds are met, the state child-protective-services hotline (same day, before internal escalation completes if there is any doubt about timing — the legal reporting clock does not wait for the internal chain).

**Annual review checklist:** policy re-read and re-signed by every active volunteer at program-year start; incident log reviewed for patterns; background-check expirations pulled and cleared before the new program year opens, not mid-year when a lapse is discovered.

## 2. Volunteer-capacity worksheet (filled example)

Congregation: 220 enrolled, pre-K–8th grade, single Sunday session.

| Grade band | Enrolled | Ratio target | Adults needed | Adults committed | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Preschool (Pre-K–K) | 45 | 1:6 | 8 | 6 | −2 |
| Elementary (1st–4th) | 95 | 1:10 | 10 | 12 | +2 |
| Bridge (5th–6th) | 45 | 1:12 | 4 | 4 | 0 |
| Middle/High (7th–8th) | 35 | 1:15 | 3 | 2 | −1 |
| **Total** | **220** | — | **25** | **24** | **−1** |

Rule applied: scope follows capacity. With a net −1 adult (and a −2 gap specifically in preschool, the highest-ratio-sensitivity band), the correct move is not "run understaffed and hope" — it's to either recruit 2 preschool aides before the program year opens or combine the two youngest preschool sub-groups into one room at the 1:6 ratio with 4 adults covering 24 children each Sunday on a rotation, holding the program to what's actually staffed.

## 3. Grade-transition retention table (filled example, matches SKILL.md worked example)

| Transition | Cohort year N (count) | Cohort year N+1 (count) | Retention % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-K → K | 22 → 20 | 91% |
| 2nd → 3rd | 24 → 22 | 92% |
| 4th → 5th | 26 → 23 | 88% |
| **5th → 6th (bridge, before program)** | **40 → 20** | **50%** |
| **5th → 6th (bridge, after program)** | **45 → 28** | **62%** |
| 8th → 9th (into youth group) | 20 → 17 | 85% |

Reading the table: any transition running more than roughly double the program's average non-outlier drop (here, ~8–12%) is a target for redesign, not more general recruiting. The 5th→6th seam at 38–50% loss before intervention was nearly 4x the program baseline — that gap size is what justified a dedicated bridge-class role rather than a general "retention initiative."

## 4. Curriculum doctrinal-fit check (filled example)

Before adopting a publisher's curriculum for a doctrinally load-bearing unit (confirmation, baptism prep, communion prep):

1. Pull the publisher's theological statement or statement of faith.
2. Line it up against the congregation's/denomination's own doctrinal statement on the specific unit's topic (e.g., nature of the sacraments, view of scripture, atonement theory).
3. Flag any point of divergence explicitly — don't assume "mainstream Christian publisher" means aligned on every point.
4. Where divergence exists: supplement with a denominational insert lesson rather than adopting the unit as-is, or select a different publisher for that unit specifically (mixing publishers by unit is normal practice, not a red flag).
5. Log the decision and reasoning so the next DRE isn't re-litigating the same check from scratch.

Example flag: a widely-used nondenominational confirmation curriculum teaches believer's baptism by immersion as normative; a congregation that practices infant baptism needs either a denominational insert lesson on infant baptism theology before that unit, or a different curriculum for that unit — not a full-program replacement.
