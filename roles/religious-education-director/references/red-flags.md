# Religious Education Director — red flags

### A volunteer requests to drive, host, or supervise a specific minor one-on-one outside program hours
- **Usually means:** an innocent mentoring impulse from a well-meaning volunteer, less often the specific isolation pattern grooming relies on — the request itself can't distinguish the two.
- **First question:** "Can we set this up as a two-adult activity, or route it through a supervised program instead?"
- **Data to pull:** the volunteer's background-check and tenure record, and whether this is a first such request or a pattern with the same minor or across multiple minors.

### A new volunteer is filling a classroom gap before six months' tenure or a cleared background check
- **Usually means:** roster panic — a Sunday is about to be understaffed and someone said yes.
- **First question:** "Is there a second screened adult who can be in the room with them today?"
- **Data to pull:** the volunteer roster's screening-status column; if it's not tracked in a way that answers this in under a minute, the tracking system itself is the finding.

### Grade-to-grade retention at one transition running 2x+ the program's average drop
- **Usually means:** a specific structural mismatch at that seam (venue change, loss of familiar teachers, competing activity schedule) rather than general disengagement.
- **First question:** "What changes for a student specifically at this transition — room, teacher, time, curriculum — that doesn't change at any other transition?"
- **Data to pull:** the grade-by-grade retention table for the last 3 program years, and exit conversations (or their absence) with families who didn't continue.

### A curriculum's theological statement wasn't checked before adoption and a parent or clergy member flags a lesson mid-year
- **Usually means:** the adoption process skipped the doctrinal-fit check, not that the publisher is disreputable — most publishers are internally consistent, just not aligned with every tradition.
- **First question:** "Which specific point in this lesson conflicts with our stated doctrine, and is it isolated to this unit or throughout the curriculum?"
- **Data to pull:** the publisher's statement of faith/theological position document and the congregation's own doctrinal statement on the specific topic raised.

### Volunteer teacher turnover above roughly 25% year over year in a specific grade band
- **Usually means:** insufficient training or coaching support for that band, a mismatched curriculum for the age group, or an unaddressed classroom-management problem, more often than "hard to recruit for that age."
- **First question:** "What training and check-in did teachers in that band actually receive this year, compared to the bands with low turnover?"
- **Data to pull:** training-completion log by teacher, exit-interview notes (or their absence) from departed volunteers.

### A budget request for the education line is denied or deferred two cycles running
- **Usually means:** the request was argued in activity counts (sessions run, events held) rather than numbers the finance committee weighs (cost per participant, retention change, safety-compliance risk).
- **First question:** "What number in this request would change a finance committee member's vote, and is it in the first sentence?"
- **Data to pull:** the prior two budget requests as submitted, and the finance committee's stated priorities or rubric if one exists.

### Background-check expirations are discovered mid-program-year rather than caught at year-start review
- **Usually means:** the annual review checklist step was skipped or the tracking spreadsheet isn't actually being maintained.
- **First question:** "When was the volunteer roster's background-check column last audited against actual expiration dates, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** the roster's last-audited date and the background-check vendor's expiration report.

### Enrollment in a grade band exceeds the staffed ratio (adults committed < adults needed) after the program year has already started
- **Usually means:** the capacity worksheet wasn't rerun against actual September enrollment, or was rerun but the gap wasn't acted on before the year opened.
- **First question:** "What's the current adult-to-student ratio in that room right now, today, not at last spring's planning meeting?"
- **Data to pull:** current enrollment by grade band versus the volunteer-capacity worksheet from planning.

### A single long-tenured volunteer is exempted from the two-adult or six-month rule "because everyone trusts them"
- **Usually means:** the rule's authority is being eroded by the specific case it's least designed to catch — trusted-insider risk, not stranger risk, is what these controls exist for.
- **First question:** "If this exception is granted, what's the written justification on file, and would it hold up if reviewed by the denominational safe-environment office?"
- **Data to pull:** the written policy's exception clause (most well-drafted policies have none), and the volunteer's actual screening record.

### A youth-program participant discloses information suggesting risk of self-harm or abuse to a volunteer teacher rather than clergy
- **Usually means:** the disclosure went to whoever felt safe and available, not necessarily to the right first point of contact — the volunteer likely isn't trained for triage.
- **First question:** "Has the volunteer been connected with the DRE or clergy today, before the conversation is relied on from memory alone?"
- **Data to pull:** the congregation's mandated-reporting and referral protocol, and whether the volunteer has completed the training module covering it.
