# Religious Education Director — vocabulary

### Formation
The ongoing process of shaping a person's faith, character, and practice over years, as distinct from a single class or curriculum unit.
**In use:** "This curriculum is fine for content, but formation happens more in the relationship with the same small-group leader across three years than in any one lesson."
**Common misuse:** Used as a synonym for "curriculum" or "class," flattening a multi-year relational process into a single semester's material.

### Scope and sequence
A publisher's or denomination's map of what topics are taught in what order across grade levels, designed to avoid repetition and gaps.
**In use:** "Before we adopt this for 3rd grade, check the scope and sequence — does it assume the sacraments unit we actually teach in 4th grade?"
**Common misuse:** Treated as interchangeable with "curriculum," when it's specifically the topic-and-order map a curriculum is built on — two curricula can share a scope and sequence and still teach very differently.

### Catechesis
Structured instruction in the core teachings of a faith tradition, most precisely used in Catholic and some liturgical Protestant contexts for baptism, confirmation, and communion preparation.
**In use:** "Our catechists need the diocesan certification track completed within two years of starting, not just a background check."
**Common misuse:** Used generically for any religious teaching; in traditions with formal catechetical structures it specifically denotes doctrinally normative instruction tied to sacramental preparation, not enrichment classes.

### Safe environment / safe sanctuaries
A congregation's or denomination's formal child-protection policy framework: screening, training, the two-adult rule, and reporting procedures.
**In use:** "That volunteer can't be scheduled solo until they've completed safe-environment training — no exceptions for tenure."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a one-time training event completed once and filed, rather than a policy with ongoing re-screening and annual review requirements.

### Bridge class / bridge program
A class or program format specifically designed around a known attrition transition (commonly 5th–6th grade or 8th–9th), rather than a standard grade-level class.
**In use:** "The bridge class isn't just 5th and 6th combined for convenience — it's built around the specific reason kids drop off there."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to mean any combined-grade class; a combined class run for scheduling convenience without addressing the transition-specific attrition cause isn't functioning as a bridge program even if it's labeled one.

### Mandated reporter
A legal designation requiring specific professions — often including clergy and religious-education staff depending on state statute — to report suspected child abuse or neglect to authorities within a defined window, regardless of how the disclosure was framed.
**In use:** "As a mandated reporter I have to report this today, not after I've had a chance to talk with the family."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be overridden by clergy-penitent privilege in every case; most state statutes carve out abuse disclosures from that privilege regardless of the confessional framing — the scope varies by state and needs checking, not assuming.

### Confirmation track / confirmation prep
The multi-session (often multi-year) instructional sequence preparing an adolescent for the rite of confirmation, distinct from regular weekly formation classes.
**In use:** "Confirmation track attendance is tracked separately from Sunday school — a student can miss regular class and still be on track if confirmation sessions are current."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with general Sunday school attendance, when many traditions treat it as a separate, often mandatory-minimum-sessions requirement with its own completion criteria.

### Andragogy
The theory and practice of teaching adults, distinguished from pedagogy (teaching children) by its emphasis on self-direction, prior experience, and problem-centered learning.
**In use:** "This adult confirmation class needs andragogy, not a scaled-down version of the middle-school curriculum."
**Common misuse:** Applied to teen classes on the assumption that "almost adult" means the same teaching approach applies; adolescent formation is driven more by identity development and peer dynamics than by the self-direction andragogy assumes.

### Developmental assets
A framework (Search Institute) identifying specific internal and external factors — supportive relationships, boundaries, constructive use of time — correlated with positive youth outcomes, used as a diagnostic lens for program design.
**In use:** "Low scores on the 'other adult relationships' asset in our youth survey line up with the 8th-to-9th drop-off we're seeing."
**Common misuse:** Cited as a general youth-ministry buzzword without reference to the actual asset categories or survey data, turning a measurable framework into an unmeasured slogan.

### Cross-generational relationship
A sustained relationship between a young person and an adult outside their immediate family within the congregation, distinct from a peer friendship or a formal teacher-student assignment.
**In use:** "Sticky Faith research is the reason we require every confirmand to be paired with an adult mentor outside their own family, not just a same-age small group."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be satisfied by any adult supervision (a teacher taking attendance), when the research specifically concerns sustained, named relational investment rather than incidental contact.

### Diocesan safe-environment office
The regional or denominational-level administrative body that sets and audits child-protection policy across multiple congregations, distinct from a single congregation's own policy.
**In use:** "This incident gets reported to the diocesan safe-environment office within 24 hours, in addition to whatever the state hotline requires."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be optional or advisory; in many denominational structures, reporting to this office is a binding policy requirement independent of, and in addition to, state mandated-reporting law.

### Program year
The congregation's formation-program calendar, typically running September/fall to May/spring and distinct from the fiscal or calendar year the budget runs on.
**In use:** "The curriculum budget resets on the fiscal year, but enrollment and staffing planning run on the program year — those two clocks don't line up and that's where budget requests go stale."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be identical to the fiscal year, causing budget requests to be built against the wrong enrollment data.
