# First-Line Supervisor of Office and Administrative Support — Red Flags

### Schedule shows minimum coverage met on every daily/weekly total, but no one has checked shift-change or lunch windows
- **Usually means:** the schedule was built by summing hours, not by checking headcount-present at each hour.
- **First question:** "What's the headcount present at 12:00, 12:30, and the first hour after a callout — not the daily total?"
- **Data to pull:** an hour-by-hour coverage grid for the days in question, not just the daily roster.

### A team member's job title is "supervisor" but they spend most of the week doing the same tasks as their team
- **Usually means:** the FLSA executive-exemption test may not be met — this could be a misclassified non-exempt employee owed overtime.
- **First question:** "What percentage of a typical week is spent on management duties (scheduling, discipline, hiring input) versus the same clerical work as the team?"
- **Data to pull:** a time-allocation estimate over a representative 2-week period, and the job description against 29 CFR §541.100's duties test.

### Two employees had the same infraction, but only one has a written record
- **Usually means:** inconsistent documentation practice — often unconscious favoritism, sometimes a genuinely different situation that was never written down.
- **First question:** "Was there a documented difference between the two situations, or did one just not get written up?"
- **Data to pull:** both employees' documented history for the same infraction type over the last 12 months.

### A backlog has persisted through two or more scheduling adjustments
- **Usually means:** a capacity shortfall or a process bottleneck, not a scheduling problem that another shuffle will fix.
- **First question:** "What specifically changed in the last two adjustments, and did the backlog trend change at all?"
- **Data to pull:** backlog size by review period, alongside what staffing change (if any) was made each period.

### A new cross-trainee is being counted as full capacity in this week's schedule
- **Usually means:** the schedule doesn't account for the trainer's time being consumed, creating a hidden coverage gap.
- **First question:** "Who is training this person, and how much of their normal capacity is going to that instead of their usual work?"
- **Data to pull:** the training plan's stated duration and the trainer's adjusted task load for that period.

### One task type has only one primary and one backup on the cross-training matrix
- **Usually means:** a single point of failure — if both are out simultaneously, nothing covers that task.
- **First question:** "If both the primary and backup for this task were out on the same day, what's the plan?"
- **Data to pull:** the current cross-training matrix, sorted by number of trained people per task type.

### An employee's coaching conversation happened in front of the team or was bundled with unrelated positive feedback
- **Usually means:** the documentation, if any exists, will be harder to defend as a clear, specific, professional record.
- **First question:** "Was this addressed privately and specifically, or folded into a general check-in?"
- **Data to pull:** the coaching log entry — does it read as a specific, dated, private conversation, or a vague summary?

### Voluntary overtime was skipped in favor of mandatory overtime or agency coverage without a stated reason
- **Usually means:** either a genuine urgency justified skipping the preference order, or the backfill tier order wasn't actually followed.
- **First question:** "Was voluntary OT offered and declined, or not offered at all?"
- **Data to pull:** the backfill decision log for that shift, including who was asked and in what order.

### A performance issue was written up more than a few days after it happened
- **Usually means:** the documentation will rely on memory rather than contemporaneous specifics, and is more vulnerable to challenge.
- **First question:** "What's the gap between when this happened and when it was written down, and why?"
- **Data to pull:** the incident date versus the documentation date on the record.
