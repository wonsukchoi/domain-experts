# First-Line Supervisor of Office and Administrative Support — Playbook

## Coverage grid (minimum-2 rule, 4-person team, Tuesday)

| Time | Present (normal) | Present (2 callouts) | Meets minimum-2? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9:00–11:59am | 4 | 2 | Yes |
| 12:00–12:29pm (Patel lunch) | 4→3 | 2→1 | **No — gap** |
| 12:30–12:59pm (Cole lunch) | 3→3 (staggered) | 1→1 | **No — gap** |
| 1:00–5:00pm | 4 | 2 | Yes |

Gap total: 1 hour at 1-person coverage against a 2-person minimum. Fix: borrow cross-trained coverage for the 12:00–1:00pm window rather than accept the gap or cancel both lunches.

## Callout-backfill preference order

1. **Voluntary overtime** — ask remaining team members first; costs money, no cross-team dependency.
2. **Cross-trained backup from an adjacent team** — costs a favor/reciprocal coverage commitment, no overtime cost.
3. **Defer the lowest-priority task** — costs a delay on something that can tolerate one, only after confirming what the lowest-priority item actually is (not a guess).
4. **Escalate for temporary/agency coverage** — costs approval time and onboarding overhead; last resort, use when the gap will recur beyond one shift.

Skipping a tier without a stated reason (e.g., "no one available for OT, adjacent team fully staffed already") is a scheduling shortcut that erodes trust when someone notices later.

## Progressive-discipline tier table

| Tier | Trigger | Documentation required | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Verbal coaching | First occurrence of a minor issue (lateness, missed minor deadline) | Dated log entry, specifics, employee's stated reason if given | None beyond the conversation; noted for pattern-tracking |
| 2 — Written warning | Second occurrence within 90 days, or a more serious first occurrence | Signed written warning, specific incident, prior Tier-1 referenced if applicable | Formal record; performance-improvement expectation stated with a date |
| 3 — Final written warning | Third occurrence within 90 days, or failure to meet Tier-2 improvement expectation | Signed final warning; explicit statement that recurrence leads to termination | HR notified; timeline for the next review set |
| 4 — Termination | Recurrence after Tier 3, or a single severe-enough incident (per HR policy) | Full documented history reviewed with HR before action | Termination processed per HR procedure |

Applying a tier to one employee that skips a step applied to another for the same infraction pattern is the single most common documentation failure this table exists to prevent.

## Backlog-escalation trigger

| Review period | Backlog trend | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Backlog up 20% vs. baseline | Note it; check if it's a one-off (e.g., a staff absence) |
| Week 2 | Still up 15%+ after a schedule adjustment | Try one more targeted fix (reallocate a specific task, add planned OT) |
| Week 3 | Still not clearing after two adjustments | Escalate as a capacity problem — state the gap in hours/units and the ask (headcount, process change) |

Two failed adjustments is the trigger, not a fixed calendar date — a backlog that clears itself after one adjustment was a blip, not a capacity problem.
