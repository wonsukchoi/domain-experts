# Adapted Physical Education Artifacts

Filled templates for assessment selection, LRE placement, and present-level/goal drafting. Populate the bracketed fields; the surrounding structure is meant to be used as-is.

## 1. Assessment battery selection by mobility profile

| Presenting profile | Motor test | Fitness test | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Independent gait, no documented balance limitation | TGMD-3 (locomotor + ball skills) | PACER | Standard protocol; norm-referenced standard scores apply |
| GMFCS II or documented mild gait deviation, no assistive device | TGMD-3 | BPFT alternate: TAMT (Target Aerobic Movement Test) or single-stage step test | PACER contraindicated if stumbling documented on >1 of 10 shuttle lengths |
| GMFCS III+ or assistive-device/wheelchair user | PDMS-2, or TGMD-3 with adaptive-administration notes flagged in the report | BPFT wheelchair aerobic protocol | Coordinate positioning with PT before testing; do not score an untested skill as 0 |
| Autism spectrum, no motor diagnosis, participation-avoidance pattern reported | TGMD-3 + Ecological Task Analysis in the actual game context | BPFT standard protocol, monitor for sensory-driven refusal | Distinguish a true skill deficit from sensory/behavioral refusal before scoring a declined trial as a failed criterion |

## 2. LRE / service-delivery continuum (Sherrill/Winnick cascade)

| Step | Placement | Entry criterion | Typical direct minutes | Review cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | General PE, no support | Motor/fitness scores within 1 SD of mean; no functional participation barrier | 0 direct | Annual |
| 2 | General PE + modification card (equipment/rules/group size), no specialist minutes | 1–1.5 SD below mean on isolated skills; general PE teacher can execute modifications independently | 0 direct, consult only (e.g., 1x/quarter) | Semester |
| 3 | General PE + direct APE push-in/pull-in support | >1.5 SD below mean or component-level failure on specific skills that general PE differentiation can't reasonably address | 1–2x/week, 20–30 min | Quarterly |
| 4 | Part-time separate APE, general PE for remaining sessions | Multiple domains (motor + fitness + functional) below criterion; push-in support tried and insufficient per data | 2–3x/week, 30 min, separate small group | Quarterly |
| 5 | Full-time separate APE | General PE participation, even with maximum support, does not meet the student's safety or access needs per documented trial | Daily, individualized | Quarterly, with annual placement re-justification |

**Rule:** moving to a more restrictive step (4 or 5) requires a documented trial at the less restrictive step first, with data showing it was insufficient — never default to the slot with schedule room.

## 3. Present-level and goal-writing template

```
Assessment: [test name + edition], administered [date]
Raw score: [X / max]
Standard score / percentile OR HFZ classification: [value]
Item-level breakdown: [skill: score, skill: score, ... — flag the 1-2 skills furthest below the others]
Functional statement: [what the failing components look like in the actual PE/game setting,
  and what alternative the student has already self-selected, if any]

Annual Goal: By [date], given [condition — protocol, setting], [student] will
  [specific skill/component] with [criterion — X of Y performance criteria across
  X of Y trials], as measured by [who] via [method/frequency],
  up from a baseline of [X of Y].

Service Recommendation: [current minutes] -> [recommended minutes],
  delivered as [direct / push-in / pull-in / consult], with
  [modification card / paraprofessional training protocol] provided to
  [general PE teacher / paraprofessional].
```


## 4. Fallback order when direct minutes are cut or the specialist is short-staffed

Preference order — never silently absorb a minutes cut without amending the IEP:

1. Reduce consult *frequency* first (e.g., biweekly instead of weekly), but keep the specialist-authored modification card current.
2. Group students with similar component-level deficits into one direct session rather than dropping direct minutes for any single student.
3. Train a paraprofessional against a written, skill-specific protocol (not general supervision), with specialist spot-checks at least biweekly and documented in the service log.
4. Only as a last resort, shift to consult-only temporarily — and flag it explicitly to the IEP team as a compensatory-services question, with the gap and makeup plan documented in writing, not absorbed quietly.
