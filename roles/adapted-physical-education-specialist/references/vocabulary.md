# Vocabulary

### Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
The legal requirement to educate a student with disabilities alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate. In PE, it is a continuum of placement steps, not a binary in/out decision.
**In use:** "LRE for PE isn't 'general PE or nothing' — it's the least restrictive point on the cascade the current data actually supports."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a single yes/no placement decision made once, instead of a step re-justified from fresh data every year.

### Specially Designed Instruction (SDI)
IDEA's term for adapting content, methodology, or delivery to meet a student's disability-related needs, tied to an IEP goal and delivered by qualified staff.
**In use:** "Modifying the tag rules for one student isn't SDI unless it traces back to a written goal and someone qualified is delivering it."
**Common misuse:** Any informal, undocumented modification a general PE teacher improvises is labeled SDI, satisfying nothing on paper.

### Present Levels of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance (PLAAFP / PLOP)
The IEP section stating current standardized and functional data that the annual goal must logically follow from.
**In use:** "The hop/leap goal has to trace directly back to the item-level breakdown in the PLAAFP, not just the summary score."
**Common misuse:** Written as a narrative description of the student with no scored data point the goal can actually be measured against.

### Adapted Physical Education (APE) vs. "adaptive PE"
APE is a federally recognized, credentialed discipline of specially designed instruction; "adaptive PE" is informal shorthand staff often use for any modified activity.
**In use:** "He needs APE, not just an adaptive version of the same lesson plan taught a little slower."
**Common misuse:** The two terms are used interchangeably, implying that any teacher's on-the-fly tweak satisfies the legal requirement.

### GMFCS (Gross Motor Function Classification System)
A 5-level classification of functional mobility in cerebral palsy, used to set realistic motor expectations by level, not severity in general.
**In use:** "At GMFCS II, expect independent gait but real limits on running, jumping, and uneven terrain — don't benchmark him against a same-age peer's fitness norms."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a general severity or cognitive-ability proxy rather than a specific, mobility-function-only descriptor.

### Ecological Task Analysis (ETA)
Assessing a motor skill within its actual game or environmental context, systematically varying one task variable (equipment, space, rules) at a time to find where performance breaks down.
**In use:** "Before writing him off on catching, run an ETA — vary the ball size and distance and see exactly where it falls apart."
**Common misuse:** Used as a synonym for general "observation," skipping the systematic variable manipulation that makes it diagnostic rather than anecdotal.

### Healthy Fitness Zone (HFZ)
A health-referenced, criterion-based band on a fitness test indicating the score associated with favorable health outcomes — not a percentile rank against peers.
**In use:** "He's in the HFZ on the TAMT, so the issue isn't aerobic capacity — don't write a conditioning goal off this result."
**Common misuse:** An HFZ pass or fail is read like a percentile against typical peers, which it is explicitly not designed to be.

### Criterion-referenced vs. norm-referenced assessment
Criterion-referenced compares a score to a fixed standard (e.g., the HFZ); norm-referenced compares it to a reference population's distribution (e.g., TGMD-3 standard scores).
**In use:** "The BPFT alternate items are criterion-referenced — don't try to convert an HFZ pass into a percentile, they're different scales entirely."
**Common misuse:** A criterion-referenced pass/fail is reported as though it were equivalent to "average" on a norm curve.

### Peer-mediated instruction
Trained, structured use of non-disabled classmates to deliver practice opportunities under a specialist's written plan, including a data-collection role — distinct from informal buddy pairing.
**In use:** "Peer-mediated instruction needs a training script and a data sheet for the peer to fill out — an untrained buddy is just supervision."
**Common misuse:** Any informal peer pairing during class is called peer-mediated instruction even without training or a data-collection component.

### Regression / Extended School Year (ESY) in PE
Documented loss of a previously mastered motor skill over a break, severe enough that the recoupment time would be unreasonable, triggering ESY consideration.
**In use:** "His hop regressed from 3/4 to 1/4 criteria over summer with no recoupment by week 6 — that's an ESY case, not just rustiness."
**Common misuse:** Any post-break performance dip is labeled "regression" without comparing the actual recoupment time against the IDEA standard.

### Task analysis
Breaking a motor skill into its sequential component parts or scored criteria (e.g., takeoff, flight, landing) to identify exactly which sub-step is failing.
**In use:** "Task-analyze the hop into takeoff, flight, and landing before writing a goal on 'hopping' as one undifferentiated skill."
**Common misuse:** Used loosely as a synonym for "breaking down the lesson plan," rather than the specific skill-component diagnostic step it names.

### Related service vs. specially designed physical education
PE is unique under IDEA as a required curricular area for every eligible student, general or adapted — unlike related services (OT, speech), which the IEP team adds or drops based on need.
**In use:** "We can't drop PE from his IEP the way we dropped OT — PE has to be addressed one way or another for every eligible student."
**Common misuse:** PE is treated procedurally like an optional related service the team can decline if the student "doesn't need" it.

### CAPE (Certified Adapted Physical Educator)
The national credential issued through the APENS body-of-knowledge exam, distinct from a general state PE teaching license.
**In use:** "She holds a CAPE, so her assessment recommendations carry more weight in a due-process hearing than a general PE teacher's opinion would."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be automatically included in a general physical education teaching license, when it requires a separate national exam.
