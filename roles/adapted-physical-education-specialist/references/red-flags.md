# Red Flags

### PE goal reads "will participate in adapted PE" with no scored criterion
- **Usually means:** a shared goal bank was copy-pasted, or the writer never translated present-level assessment data into a measurable target.
- **First question:** "What specific test or protocol will we probe against to know if this goal is met?"
- **Data to pull:** the present-level statement's raw/standard scores and the goal's stated evaluation method.

### PACER administered to a student with an assistive device or a documented gait deviation
- **Usually means:** the default fitness battery was given without checking the test's gait assumption; the resulting score reflects an invalid administration, not true aerobic capacity.
- **First question:** "Can this student sustain independent running gait for the PACER's full shuttle distance?"
- **Data to pull:** GMFCS/mobility notes and the BPFT alternate-item decision tree.

### Identical locomotor/goal wording appears verbatim across 3+ students' IEPs
- **Usually means:** a shared goal bank was used without individualizing to each student's component-level deficits.
- **First question:** "What did this specific student's item-level TGMD-3/PDMS-2 breakdown show?"
- **Data to pull:** each student's raw item-level scores, not just the summary standard score.

### Motor or fitness reassessment not run in 3+ years for an elementary-age student
- **Usually means:** present levels are stale relative to typical developmental change at this age, creating a FAPE compliance gap.
- **First question:** "When was the last standardized re-administration, and does plausible growth since then change the present level?"
- **Data to pull:** the assessment history log and the age-based norm table for the test in use.

### APE minutes on the IEP don't match the service delivery log
- **Usually means:** scheduling conflicts, caseload overload, or a paraprofessional substituting for direct specialist time without documentation.
- **First question:** "Who delivered the last four sessions — the specialist, or an untrained aide?"
- **Data to pull:** the service delivery log compared line-by-line against the IEP minutes page.

### Behavior escalations in PE cluster on the exact skills where motor scores show the largest deficit
- **Usually means:** avoidance behavior driven by a real motor/balance limitation, not defiance — a behavior goal alone won't resolve it.
- **First question:** "Does the incident log correlate with single-limb-stance or specific-skill stations?"
- **Data to pull:** the behavior incident log cross-referenced against the motor-test item breakdown.

### Goal criterion set at 100% / "always" mastery
- **Usually means:** an unrealistic target given the documented baseline growth rate — it will read as goal failure at annual review regardless of real progress.
- **First question:** "What was the rate of change across the last two data points, and does the timeline support 100%?"
- **Data to pull:** the progress-monitoring probe history.

### Caseload exceeds roughly 50-60 students across 2+ buildings
- **Usually means:** direct-service minutes are being diluted into consult-only for students who actually need direct specially designed instruction.
- **First question:** "Which students on this caseload show recent regression or a placement more restrictive than current data supports?"
- **Data to pull:** the caseload roster cross-tabbed against each student's most recent probe trend.

### Fitness test reported as a raw score only, with no standard score, percentile, or HFZ classification
- **Usually means:** the test was administered but never scored to a norm or criterion reference — the present level can't legally support a goal yet.
- **First question:** "What is the Healthy Fitness Zone classification for this raw score at this student's age and sex?"
- **Data to pull:** the BPFT scoring tables for the relevant age/sex band.

### Student moved to full-time separate APE without a documented trial of general PE with modifications first
- **Usually means:** LRE creep — defaulting to the most restrictive setting instead of justifying it against a less restrictive trial.
- **First question:** "What modification was tried in general PE, for how long, and what data showed it insufficient?"
- **Data to pull:** the modification/accommodation log and any interim progress data collected during the general PE trial.

### Parent reports the child "hates PE," alongside declining out-of-school physical activity self-report
- **Usually means:** disengagement from physical activity broadly, not just an in-class complaint — a placement or motivational review is due.
- **First question:** "Is the same avoidance pattern showing up outside school, or only in this specific class context?"
- **Data to pull:** a physical-activity self-report or interest inventory, compared against the in-class participation log.

### A skill scored as 0/8 on every criterion with no note distinguishing "attempted and failed" from "refused the trial"
- **Usually means:** a behavioral refusal is being recorded as a motor-skill absence, inflating the apparent deficit and misdirecting the goal.
- **First question:** "Did the student attempt the trial and fail the criteria, or decline to attempt it at all?"
- **Data to pull:** the examiner's trial-by-trial administration notes, not just the summary score sheet.
