# Animal Caretaker — Playbook

## Capacity-for-care shift math (filled example)

| Input | Value |
|---|---|
| Kennel design capacity | 30 |
| Today's census | 42 |
| Caretakers on shift | 2 |
| Effective minutes/caretaker (8-hr shift − 30-min break) | 450 |
| Team capacity (minutes) | 900 |
| Standard minutes per dog, full protocol | 25 |
| Full-protocol capacity (dogs) | 900 ÷ 25 = 36 |
| Shortfall | 42 − 36 = 6 |

Triage order: isolation/medical holds and new intakes (first 72 hours) get full protocol first, no exceptions. Remaining budget covers as many general-population dogs as it can; the leftover count gets abbreviated care (spot-clean + feed/water, no full health/FAS recheck), pulled only from stable animals 30+ days in with FAS ≤1 on their last three checks. Reconcile the math before ending the shift: isolation/medical + full-protocol general population + abbreviated should equal today's census exactly.

Run this calculation at the start of every shift where census is within 10% of design capacity or higher — don't wait until the shortfall is visible in the task list.

## Intake and isolation protocol (filled example)

| Intake type | Vaccination proof | Housing on arrival | Observation window before general population |
|---|---|---|---|
| Owner surrender, records provided | Bordetella/DHPP ≥14 days prior | General population, standard intake exam | 72 hours |
| Owner surrender, no records | Unknown | Isolation ward | Minimum 10–14 days or until cleared by vet, whichever is longer |
| Transfer from another shelter with health certificate | Verified on certificate | General population if certificate <7 days old | 72 hours |
| Stray/found, no history | Unknown | Isolation ward | Minimum 10–14 days, longer if symptomatic |
| Cruelty-case group intake | Unknown, treat as unvaccinated | Isolation ward, cohort-separated by presenting symptoms | Case-by-case with vet sign-off; do not merge cohorts |

Symptomatic animals (cough, nasal/ocular discharge, diarrhea, lethargy) restart the observation clock from the last symptomatic day, not from intake date. Two or more animals in the same housing area developing matching symptoms within a several-day window is treated as a cohort outbreak, not independent cases — isolate the whole cohort, not just the first animal that showed symptoms.

## Daily care sequence and task-time budget

| Task | Target time/animal | Sequence position |
|---|---|---|
| Isolation/medical ward pass (dedicated PPE) | 25–30 min | Last, or with a foot-bath/glove change before returning to general population |
| General population spot-clean + feed/water | 10 min | — |
| General population full protocol (clean + feed/water + health/FAS observation) | 25 min | First for new intakes, before long-stay stable animals |
| Weigh-in and BCS check | 3–5 min | Weekly minimum, more often for medical holds |
| Grooming/mat check | Varies — see below | As scheduled or flagged |

Sequence the whole shift isolation-last (or isolation-first-with-full-PPE-changeover) — never interleaved with general population contact.

## FAS score tracking log (filled example)

| Date | Animal | FAS (0–5) | Appetite | Output | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 (intake) | Kennel 22, "Remy" | 3 | Ate half ration | Normal | New intake, hiding at back of kennel |
| Day 2 | Kennel 22, "Remy" | 3 | Ate full ration | Normal | Still avoids eye contact, no improvement |
| Day 3 | Kennel 22, "Remy" | 2 | Ate full ration | Normal | Approached front of kennel for first time |
| Day 4 | Kennel 22, "Remy" | 2 | Ate full ration | Normal | Stable — held for 2 consecutive checks, no escalation needed |

A score that holds flat for 2+ days after intake (not decompressing) is a flag on its own even without a rise — compare against the animal's own intake baseline, not just day-over-day.

## Matting severity grading (filled example)

| Grade | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Light | Loose tangles, coat moves freely underneath | Brush/comb out, routine session |
| 2 — Moderate | Mats present but skin visible when parted, some resistance | Dematting comb/spray, longer session, breaks if animal stresses |
| 3 — Heavy | Mats close to skin, limited coat movement, discomfort on light pull | Trial dematting on a small test area; move to shave-down if no progress in ~10 minutes or animal shows pain response |
| 4 — Pelted | Solid mat mass, skin taut underneath, restricted limb/tail movement | Clipper shave-down with a guarded blade; do not attempt brush-out — skin-tear and pain risk outweighs coat preservation |

Grade 4 gets a full skin check after the shave-down (hot spots, sores, and irritation are commonly hidden underneath) and a note in the animal's record so the next groom starts from an accurate baseline instead of re-discovering the same history.
