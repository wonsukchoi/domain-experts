# Animal Caretaker — Red Flags

### No food, water, or output across a 24-hour window or two consecutive scheduled checks
- **Usually means:** Illness (GI obstruction, infection, dental pain) or, in a very recent intake, unresolved stress past the normal decompression window.
- **First question:** "When was the last confirmed food/water intake and the last confirmed output, and how many scheduled checks has that spanned?"
- **Data to pull:** Feeding/output log for the last 48 hours, intake date (to rule in/out normal decompression), any medication changes.

### FAS score rises 2+ points across two consecutive observations
- **Usually means:** A handling, housing, or noise change the animal hasn't adapted to; less commonly, a new pain source presenting as stress/guarding behavior.
- **First question:** "What changed in this animal's environment or handling routine in the last 24–48 hours?"
- **Data to pull:** FAS log trend against intake baseline, housing location/neighbor changes, handler roster for recent contacts.

### Matting graded 4 (pelted to skin) discovered at a routine check, not flagged earlier
- **Usually means:** Grooming checks were skipped or too infrequent for the coat type, or the animal has been avoiding handling long enough that the mat progressed unnoticed.
- **First question:** "When was this coat last checked, and was matting present but under-graded at that check?"
- **Data to pull:** Last grooming/handling log entry, coat-check frequency for this animal's coat type.

### Two or more animals in the same housing area with matching new symptoms within a several-day window
- **Usually means:** A cohort outbreak (respiratory or GI) rather than independent, unrelated cases.
- **First question:** "Which animals share airspace, handlers, or equipment with the first symptomatic animal, and when did each one's symptoms start?"
- **Data to pull:** Housing map for the area, symptom-onset dates for each animal, shared-equipment/handler log.

### New intake enters general population with no verified vaccination record
- **Usually means:** An intake protocol step was skipped under time pressure, or an emergency safety-housing case was moved to general population without the exception being documented.
- **First question:** "Was this a documented safety-housing exception, or did the standard 10–14 day isolation get skipped?"
- **Data to pull:** Intake record, vaccination documentation (if any), isolation-ward log for that date.

### Weight loss trend of roughly 10%+ body weight over two weeks
- **Usually means:** Inadequate ration for the animal's activity/condition, an untreated GI issue, or dental pain limiting intake.
- **First question:** "Has the feeding ration changed, or has appetite/stool quality changed over the same period?"
- **Data to pull:** Weekly weigh-in log, BCS trend, feeding log, stool-quality notes.

### Diarrhea lasting more than 48 hours, or any blood in stool
- **Usually means:** Parasite load, dietary intolerance/change, or an infectious process that needs isolation regardless of cause while it's worked up.
- **First question:** "Has this animal been moved to isolation yet, and has stool been flagged for the vet?"
- **Data to pull:** Stool log (frequency, consistency, blood/mucus), diet or environment change timeline, isolation status.

### Repeated self-mutilation or excessive licking focused on one spot
- **Usually means:** A developing hot spot, an untreated wound, or a stress-driven behavior substituting for an unmet need (understimulation, pain elsewhere).
- **First question:** "Is there a visible skin lesion at that spot, and has this animal's enrichment/exercise routine changed recently?"
- **Data to pull:** Skin check notes, enrichment/exercise log, FAS trend for the same period.

### Bite or scratch during routine handling with no documented antecedent
- **Usually means:** A missed warning signal (stiffening, lip lick, ears back) rather than an unprovoked event, or a pain source making normally-tolerated handling suddenly aversive.
- **First question:** "Walk through the handling sequence in the seconds right before contact — what was being done, and was there any warning sign?"
- **Data to pull:** Incident report, handling log for that animal, any recent vet visit or injury.

### Census exceeding full-protocol capacity for more than two consecutive days
- **Usually means:** Intake rate is outpacing live-release rate, and the day-to-day triage the team is running is masking a population-management problem rather than a staffing-schedule problem.
- **First question:** "What's the intake-vs-release rate over the last week, not just today's census?"
- **Data to pull:** Daily census log, intake/release counts, length-of-stay trend for the current population.

### Skin tenting or other dehydration signs on a physical check
- **Usually means:** Inadequate water access (blocked bowl, malfunctioning waterer) or an illness driving fluid loss faster than intake.
- **First question:** "Is the water source confirmed functional and accessible right now, and has intake been logged normally?"
- **Data to pull:** Water-access check, intake log, any GI symptoms in the same period.
