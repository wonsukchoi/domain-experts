# Red Flags

Smell tests for the sterile field, the count, and instrument handling. Format per flag: most likely cause, the first question a veteran asks, and the specific check to run.

### Any count discrepancy, at any checkpoint

- **Usually means:** most often a genuine tally error caught by an independent recount — but a discrepancy that survives one recount raises the odds of an actual retained item by more than 100× (AORN).
- **First question:** "Did we recount independently, or did we just recite the same number together?"
- **Data to pull:** the count sheet from baseline forward, and a quadrant-by-quadrant field search log; if unresolved, the intraoperative X-ray order.

### Sterile drape or wrapper strike-through (visible fluid soak-through)

- **Usually means:** the barrier has been breached at that point — irrigation, blood, or prep pooling has wicked through to the non-sterile side, or vice versa.
- **First question:** "Is the wet area still in contact with anything sterile right now?"
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a visual, immediate-action item: re-drape or re-cover before continuing, don't wait for a convenient pause.

### Specimen leaves the field unlabeled or with an ambiguous description

- **Usually means:** the labeling step got deferred past the handoff moment — the point where mislabeling errors concentrate (surgical specimen ID error rate ~4.3 per 1,000 specimens, Makary et al. 2007).
- **First question:** "Who named this specimen out loud, and who confirmed the label against it, before it left the field?"
- **Data to pull:** the specimen log timestamp vs. the time the specimen actually left the patient.

### Instrument tray short or over against its count sheet at opening

- **Usually means:** a missing piece from a prior case's incomplete reassembly, or a sterile processing count-sheet mismatch — either way, the tray's baseline count is not what the sheet claims.
- **First question:** "Does the physical tray match the count sheet piece by piece, right now, before this becomes part of today's baseline?"
- **Data to pull:** the tray's count sheet and sterile processing's last reprocessing log for that set.

### Alcohol-based prep visibly pooled or dry time not confirmed before cautery activation

- **Usually means:** the team is treating dry time as a formality rather than a gate — flash-fire testing found ignition in up to 38% of pooled-alcohol trials even after a full 3-minute dry time.
- **First question:** "Has someone actually confirmed dry and no pooling, or are we assuming it from the clock?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the record — this is a real-time stop-and-check before the first cautery activation.

### IUSS requested for a case where the real issue is inventory, not an emergency

- **Usually means:** the set is under-stocked for the schedule and IUSS is being used to compress turnaround instead of fixing supply — trading sterility assurance margin for schedule speed.
- **First question:** "Is this a dropped/contaminated single instrument needed in minutes, or are we short a full set for a scheduled case?"
- **Data to pull:** IUSS log frequency by instrument/set over the past month — a set flashed routinely, not occasionally, is an inventory problem wearing a sterilization solution's clothes.

### Sharps passed hand-to-hand instead of through the neutral zone

- **Usually means:** the neutral zone wasn't established at setup, or time pressure caused the team to revert to direct passing.
- **First question:** "Where's the neutral zone for this case, and was it stated out loud at setup?"
- **Data to pull:** none — correct in real time; if it recurs across a shift, it's a training/setup-habit issue worth flagging to the educator or manager.

### Glove perforation indicator (colored inner glove) showing through the outer glove

- **Usually means:** an outer glove breach occurred, possibly during a sharp exchange or bony work — the indicator color is designed to make this visible fast.
- **First question:** "When did this most likely happen, and has anything sterile been touched since?"
- **Data to pull:** none — regown/reglove immediately; log the break if facility policy requires an exposure report.

### Extra supplies opened beyond the preference card, repeatedly, on the same procedure type

- **Usually means:** either the card is stale (surgeon's actual practice has shifted and nobody updated it) or the team has drifted into "just in case" opening that inflates both waste and count burden.
- **First question:** "Is this item actually used in most of these cases, or is it precautionary?"
- **Data to pull:** the preference card's last revision date and a tally of how often the extra item was actually used vs. opened over the last 10–20 cases.

### Visible smoke plume with no evacuation device engaged

- **Usually means:** the smoke evacuator wasn't turned on, isn't positioned close enough to the plume source, or the team has normalized "a little smoke" as acceptable.
- **First question:** "Is the evacuator on and within 2 inches of the plume source right now?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the moment — correct immediately; if it's a pattern, it's a facility compliance question given 18+ states now mandate surgical smoke evacuation by law.
