# Special Education Teacher — Playbook

## PLAAFP statement (filled example)

**Area of need:** Reading — oral reading fluency and comprehension.

**Current performance:** On curriculum-based measurement probes administered [date range], [Student] read grade-4 unpracticed passages at a median rate of 60 words correct per minute (wcpm) with 91% accuracy (3 probes: 58, 61, 60 wcpm). District end-of-year benchmark is 105 wcpm; fall 25th-percentile norm is 85 wcpm. On the [named comprehension assessment], [Student] scored in the 9th percentile for comprehension of grade-level text, with strongest performance on literal recall (75% correct) and weakest on inferential items (30% correct).

**Impact on involvement/progress in general curriculum:** Below-benchmark fluency and inferential-comprehension gaps limit independent access to grade-level texts across content areas (science, social studies), requiring read-aloud or text-to-speech support to access grade-level content in those subjects.

**Strengths:** Strong literal recall, on-task behavior during small-group intervention, responds well to explicit phonics instruction (documented growth from 42 to 60 wcpm over prior 12 weeks under Tier 3 intervention).

## Goal-writing worksheet

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Baseline | 60 wcpm median (3 probes, [dates]) |
| Documented growth rate under current intervention | +1.2 wcpm/week |
| Instructional weeks remaining in goal period | 36 |
| Growth-rate-only projection | 60 + (1.2 × 36) = 103.2 wcpm |
| Target growth rate (with intervention-intensity increase) | +1.4 wcpm/week |
| Target | 60 + (1.4 × 36) = 110.4 → **110 wcpm** |
| Measurement tool | Weekly CBM, 1-minute grade-level passage |
| Mastery criterion | 4 of 5 consecutive data points at or above trajectory line |
| Review checkpoint | 9 weeks — reconvene if fewer than 2 consecutive probes show growth |

Do not set the target directly to the district benchmark without this calculation — a target that isn't tied to a documented growth rate, adjusted for a stated intervention change, is either indefensible as unrealistic or indistinguishable from "no goal at all."

## Prior Written Notice (PWN) — filled excerpt

**Date:** [date]
**Action proposed:** Increase reading intervention frequency from 3x/week (30 min) to daily (30 min), effective [date].
**Description of action and why:** Progress-monitoring data over the prior 12 weeks shows growth of 1.2 wcpm/week under the current 3x/week schedule. To reach the proposed annual target of 110 wcpm, a growth rate of 1.4 wcpm/week is needed. The team is increasing intervention intensity rather than lowering the goal.
**Other options considered and why rejected:** Maintaining the current schedule and lowering the target to the growth-rate-only projection (103.2 wcpm) was considered and rejected because it falls short of the district benchmark and does not reflect the team's assessment of the student's capacity given strong response to prior intervention.
**Evaluation procedures/data used:** CBM progress-monitoring logs, [date range]; [named comprehension assessment] results, [date].
**Parent's procedural safeguards:** Copy of Procedural Safeguards Notice enclosed. Parent may request an IEP team meeting, an independent educational evaluation, or file a state complaint or due-process complaint if they disagree with this action.

## Manifestation determination review (MDR) checklist

Trigger: proposed disciplinary removal exceeds 10 cumulative school days in the school year AND constitutes a change in placement.

1. Convene the IEP team (including someone with knowledge of the student, the disability, and the IEP) within 10 school days of the decision to change placement.
2. Review the behavior incident, the current IEP, any evaluation/diagnostic results, and teacher observations.
3. Answer two questions in writing: (a) Was the conduct caused by, or did it have a direct and substantial relationship to, the disability? (b) Was the conduct the direct result of the district's failure to implement the IEP?
4. If either answer is "yes" — conduct is a manifestation. Return student to placement (unless weapons/drugs/serious-bodily-injury exception applies, which allows up to 45 school days in an interim alternative setting regardless of manifestation finding), conduct an FBA and implement/modify a BIP.
5. If both answers are "no" — conduct is not a manifestation. Standard disciplinary procedures apply, but the district must continue to provide FAPE (services enabling progress toward IEP goals) during any removal.
6. Document the finding and rationale in writing regardless of outcome — the reasoning, not just the checkbox answer, is what a hearing officer reviews.
