# Special Education Teacher — Vocabulary

### PLAAFP (Present Levels of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance)
The IEP section stating where the student currently performs, in measurable terms, in each area of need.
**In use:** "The PLAAFP has to have an actual number in it, not 'student struggles with reading.'"
**Common misuse:** Written as a narrative summary of the student's history instead of current, dated, measurable data — a PLAAFP with no number can't anchor a defensible goal.

### Accommodation
A change to how a student accesses instruction or demonstrates knowledge that does not alter the standard being measured.
**In use:** "Extended time is an accommodation — she's still taking the same test, same standard, just with more time."
**Common misuse:** Treated as interchangeable with modification; the distinction matters because modified-standard work can't be represented on a transcript as meeting the general-ed standard.

### Modification
A change to the content or performance standard itself, not just the conditions of access.
**In use:** "A shortened spelling list with fewer words is a modification — we lowered what's being measured, not just how."
**Common misuse:** Applied by default when a student struggles, before accommodations have been tried and shown insufficient.

### LRE (Least Restrictive Environment)
The IDEA requirement that students with disabilities be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, with removal to a more restrictive setting justified only when supplementary aids and services in the general-ed setting are insufficient.
**In use:** "We can't move her to the resource room without documenting what we tried in the general-ed classroom first."
**Common misuse:** Treated as "the setting where the student seems most comfortable" rather than the presumption-of-inclusion legal standard it actually is.

### FAPE (Free Appropriate Public Education)
The IDEA entitlement to special education and related services designed to meet the student's unique needs and provide meaningful educational benefit, at no cost to the family.
**In use:** "Even during a disciplinary removal, the district still has to provide FAPE — services enabling progress toward IEP goals."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to mean "the best possible education" rather than the legal standard of meaningful (not maximal) benefit.

### Manifestation Determination Review (MDR)
The required team meeting, triggered when a disciplinary removal exceeds 10 cumulative school days and constitutes a change in placement, to determine whether the behavior was caused by or directly related to the student's disability.
**In use:** "We're at day 9 of removal this year — the next incident triggers an MDR."
**Common misuse:** Confused with a general behavior-review meeting; the MDR has a specific two-question legal test and specific consequences depending on the answer.

### CBM (Curriculum-Based Measurement)
Brief, standardized, repeatable probes (e.g., one-minute oral reading fluency passages) used to track progress toward an IEP goal over time.
**In use:** "We're doing weekly CBM probes so we have five data points before the nine-week review."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a one-time assessment rather than a repeated-measures tool whose value comes from the trend line, not a single score.

### Present-levels-to-goal trajectory line
The projected line from a student's baseline to their annual goal, against which individual progress-monitoring data points are compared to judge whether progress is on track.
**In use:** "Two points below the trajectory line is our trigger to review the intervention, not wait for the annual review."
**Common misuse:** Ignored in favor of eyeballing "is the student getting better," which misses a student who's improving too slowly to reach the goal on time.

### IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)
The federal statute establishing the right to FAPE, the IEP process, procedural safeguards, and LRE for students with qualifying disabilities.
**In use:** "IDEA requires reevaluation at least every three years unless the parent and district agree it's not needed."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with Section 504, a different federal law with a broader disability definition but no requirement for an individualized, measurable-goal-driven IEP.

### Section 504 plan
A plan under the Rehabilitation Act providing accommodations to students with a disability that substantially limits a major life activity, without the IEP's individualized-goal and specialized-instruction requirements.
**In use:** "He doesn't need specialized instruction, so a 504 plan with accommodations is the right fit, not an IEP."
**Common misuse:** Used as a lighter-weight substitute for an IEP when a student actually needs specialized instruction, not just accommodations — the two serve different needs.

### Prior Written Notice (PWN)
The formal, legally required written notice a district must provide to parents any time it proposes or refuses to initiate or change the identification, evaluation, placement, or provision of FAPE.
**In use:** "We're increasing intervention frequency, so a PWN goes home explaining the change and why."
**Common misuse:** Skipped for "minor" changes; any proposed or refused change to identification, evaluation, placement, or FAPE requires one regardless of how small it seems.

### Response to Intervention (RTI)
A tiered system of increasingly intensive, data-tracked academic interventions used both to support struggling students and, when documented properly, to help identify a specific learning disability.
**In use:** "His RTI data across three tiers shows he didn't respond even to daily 1:1 intervention — that's part of the eligibility picture."
**Common misuse:** Treated as automatically diagnostic of a learning disability when tier fidelity wasn't actually documented, or used to delay a needed evaluation rather than to inform one.

### Procedural safeguards
The set of legal rights and protections IDEA guarantees to parents and students, including notice, consent, access to records, and dispute-resolution options (mediation, state complaint, due process).
**In use:** "Copy of the Procedural Safeguards Notice goes home with every PWN and at least once a year."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a one-time paperwork requirement at enrollment rather than an ongoing entitlement that must be reissued at specific triggers (initial referral, each PWN, annual review, request).

### Compensatory education
A remedy, typically ordered through due process or agreed to by the district, providing additional services to make up for a documented denial of FAPE.
**In use:** "The hearing officer awarded 40 hours of compensatory tutoring for the period services weren't delivered."
**Common misuse:** Confused with extended school year (ESY) services, which address regression risk over a scheduled break rather than remedy a past denial of FAPE.

### Extended School Year (ESY)
Services provided beyond the standard school year for students whose IEP team determines, based on data, that they would experience significant regression during a break that could not be recouped in a reasonable time afterward.
**In use:** "His regression-recoupment data from last summer supports an ESY recommendation this year."
**Common misuse:** Treated as automatically available to any student with an IEP, rather than a determination requiring specific regression/recoupment evidence.
