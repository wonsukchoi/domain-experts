# Special Education Teacher — Red Flags

### Progress-monitoring data more than 6 weeks stale going into any IEP-related decision
- **Usually means:** intervention was delivered but not tracked, or the assigned interventionist stopped administering probes without escalating.
- **First question:** "When was the last probe actually administered, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** progress-monitoring log with dates and administrator initials, not just the graph.

### Two consecutive progress-monitoring points below the goal trajectory line
- **Usually means:** intervention-fidelity breakdown (wrong dosage, wrong group size, substitute covering the group) or the goal itself was miscalibrated.
- **First question:** "Was the intervention delivered as written — same frequency, duration, group size — for both of these data points?"
- **Data to pull:** intervention log (fidelity checklist) cross-referenced against the two flagged probe dates.

### IEP goal has no baseline number stated
- **Usually means:** the goal was copied from a template or a prior year's IEP without updating present-levels data.
- **First question:** "What was this student's actual score on the last assessment in this area?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent PLAAFP source data (CBM, work samples, or formal assessment).

### Accommodation list has grown past what a general-ed teacher can realistically deliver to one student in a class of 25+
- **Usually means:** accommodations were added reactively over multiple meetings without ever being pruned or consolidated.
- **First question:** "Which of these accommodations has actually been used in the last month?"
- **Data to pull:** general-ed teacher's implementation log or informal check-in notes.

### Evaluation or re-evaluation timeline within 10 school days of the statutory deadline with no scheduled meeting
- **Usually means:** scheduling logistics slipped, or required evaluators haven't completed their assessments.
- **First question:** "Which piece of the evaluation is still outstanding, and who owns it?"
- **Data to pull:** evaluation tracking sheet showing consent date, each evaluator's completion date, and the statutory deadline.

### Behavior incident approaching the 10th cumulative day of removal in the school year
- **Usually means:** disciplinary removals have been tracked informally (day-by-day) rather than cumulatively, risking a missed manifestation-determination trigger.
- **First question:** "What is this student's cumulative removal-day count for the school year, counted across all incidents?"
- **Data to pull:** district discipline log filtered to this student, year-to-date.

### Parent request for a service or placement change with no written team response within a few days
- **Usually means:** the request is sitting informally instead of triggering a documented team decision (a meeting, a PWN of refusal, or an agreement) — silence itself can become a procedural violation.
- **First question:** "Has this request been logged, and does it need a formal IEP team response?"
- **Data to pull:** parent communication log and the district's procedural-safeguards response-time policy.

### A modification (not accommodation) appears on a general-education-diploma-track student's IEP without documented justification
- **Usually means:** the team conflated "student is struggling" with "student needs the standard lowered," without first exhausting accommodations.
- **First question:** "What accommodations were tried, and what data shows they were insufficient before moving to a modification?"
- **Data to pull:** accommodation trial log and any assessment data comparing accommodated vs. unaccommodated performance.

### LRE placement more restrictive than the prior year with no documentation of supplementary aids/services tried in the less restrictive setting
- **Usually means:** the team moved to a more restrictive placement based on a general impression of struggle rather than a documented trial of supports.
- **First question:** "What supports were tried in the current setting, and what data shows they weren't enough?"
- **Data to pull:** the prior IEP's service page and any documented behavior/academic data from the current setting.

### Student's growth rate under the current intervention is flat or negative for a full grading period
- **Usually means:** either the intervention isn't matched to the specific skill deficit, or a co-occurring factor (attendance, vision/hearing, emotional regulation) hasn't been ruled out.
- **First question:** "Has anything outside the academic intervention itself changed — attendance, health, home situation?"
- **Data to pull:** attendance record and any recent health/vision/hearing screening results alongside the progress-monitoring graph.
