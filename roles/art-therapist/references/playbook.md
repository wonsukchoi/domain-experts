# Assessment, media-staging, and consent playbook

Filled worksheets for the three recurring art therapy tasks: standardized directive scoring, media-control staging, and artwork consent/disposition.

## 1. PPAT / FEATS assessment worksheet

```
Client: <initials, age>
Directive: Person Picking an Apple from a Tree (PPAT)
Media: <specify — same media must be used at re-baseline for comparability>
Paper size: <e.g. 18x24>
Session #: <baseline or re-assessment number>

FEATS scores (0-5 scale, score each independently before viewing prior scores):
  Space Used:              <0-5>
  Energy:                  <0-5>
  Line Quality:             <0-5>
  Problem-Solving Approach: <0-5>   (does the figure engage a strategy - ladder,
                                      reaching, tool - or stand passively unable
                                      to reach the apple?)
  Realism:                  <0-5>
  Total (of scored items):  <sum>

Corroboration check (required before any formulation is written):
  Client's own narrative about the image: <verbatim or close paraphrase>
  Collateral/history consistent with the score pattern: <yes/no, specifics>
  This is a hypothesis pending corroboration, not a stand-alone diagnostic claim.
```

Rule: score before comparing to any prior session's numbers, and never finalize a formulation from the score alone — the corroboration check is not optional, particularly when the referral question touches safety or abuse.

## 2. Media-control staging plan

```
Client: <initials>
Presenting regulation capacity at intake: <acute/unknown/established>

Control continuum (resistive -> fluid), current stage marked:
  1. Pencil / graphite
  2. Collage (pre-cut or torn paper)
  3. Oil pastel / colored pencil
  4. Chalk pastel
  5. Tempera paint
  6. Wet/fluid paint, clay, finger paint

Advancement rule: hold at current stage until client shows the regulation
marker below in 2 CONSECUTIVE sessions, then advance one stage:
  Regulation marker: <e.g. cleans up materials unprompted AND shows no
    visible distress at session end>

Session log:
  Session #  Stage used  Marker met? (Y/N)  Notes
  1          <n>          <Y/N>              <>
  2          <n>          <Y/N>              <>
  ...

Do not advance on a fixed session count alone - advancement is gated on the
marker, independent of how many sessions have elapsed.
```

Rule: if a client regresses (marker absent for 2 consecutive sessions after having met it), step back one stage rather than holding at the current level and hoping it resolves.

## 3. Directive / open-studio session balance

```
Client: <initials>
Presenting theme relevant to structure choice: <e.g. loss-of-control after
  divorce/abuse - favor more open-studio balance; need for comparable
  assessment data - favor more directive weeks>

12-week plan (example split, adjust ratio to presenting theme):
  Week 1: Directive (baseline PPAT)
  Week 2: Open studio
  Week 3: Directive (treatment-focused prompt)
  Week 4: Open studio
  Week 5: Directive
  Week 6: Open studio
  Week 7: Directive
  Week 8: Open studio
  Week 9: Directive
  Week 10: Open studio
  Week 11: Directive
  Week 12: Directive (re-baseline PPAT, same media as week 1)
```

Rule: the re-baseline directive at termination must use the identical directive and media as the initial baseline, or the comparison isn't valid — swapping media between baseline and re-assessment invalidates the FEATS comparison.

## 4. Artwork consent and disposition worksheet

```
Client: <initials/name>, minor: <Y/N, guardian on record: <name>>

Consent captured at intake covers (check each explicitly, not as a blanket
statement):
  [ ] Ownership of artwork produced in session
  [ ] Storage/retention as part of the clinical record
  [ ] Disposition options at termination (retained in file / returned to
      client / destroyed) and who decides
  [ ] Exhibition consent (separate signature required per specific use -
      general treatment consent does NOT cover this)
  [ ] Publication/presentation consent (separate signature - image can be
      identifying even fully de-identified of text)
  [ ] Photography of in-progress or completed artwork for the record

Retention period applied: <state-specific - commonly matches other clinical
  documentation retention: e.g. 7 years post-treatment for an adult, or until
  age of majority + statute of limitations for a minor>

Disposition decision at termination: <retained / returned / destroyed>
  Documented in chart: <Y/N, date>
```

Rule: any use beyond the treatment itself (exhibition, publication, case presentation) requires its own signed release captured at the time of that specific use — never inferred from the general intake consent, and never skipped because the piece looks "already anonymous."
