# Red flags — signals an art therapist notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can warrant a documented note; more than one on the same case warrants an immediate treatment-plan or consent review.

### A single image is interpreted as a stand-alone diagnostic claim ("this drawing shows abuse") with no corroborating narrative or validated score
- **Usually means:** over-literal symbol reading, carrying real risk of a false-positive clinical claim — especially damaging when the claim touches abuse allegations that could enter a legal process.
- **First question:** "What does the client's own narrative say about this image, and has it been scored with a validated instrument, or is this impression alone?"
- **Data to pull:** session process notes, any FEATS/DDS scoring on file, transcript or paraphrase of the client's narrative.

### Fluid media (wet paint, clay) is introduced in session 1 or 2 with a client whose regulation capacity is unknown or already acutely dysregulated
- **Usually means:** risk of an affect flood the session's structure can't contain, since no baseline of the client's self-regulation has been established with more resistive media first.
- **First question:** "What's this client's demonstrated capacity to end a fluid-media session regulated, and has that been tested with resistive media first?"
- **Data to pull:** media-control staging log (`references/playbook.md` §2), presenting symptoms and regulation history.

### Client artwork is exhibited, published, or shown in a case presentation with no release specific to that use on file
- **Usually means:** the general treatment consent doesn't cover this — image-based material can be identifying even fully stripped of names or text, and a use-specific signature is required.
- **First question:** "Is there a signed release specific to this exact use, separate from the intake treatment consent?"
- **Data to pull:** artwork consent and disposition worksheet (`references/playbook.md` §4), intake consent scope.

### A standardized directive's FEATS score changes by less than 2 points total across 10+ sessions with no adjustment to the treatment plan
- **Usually means:** the current media, directive, or session frequency isn't producing measurable change, and continuing unchanged is more likely habit than clinical decision.
- **First question:** "What's changed in the directive, media stage, or session frequency since the score last moved?"
- **Data to pull:** FEATS score trend across sessions, treatment-plan revision history.

### A parent involved in active custody litigation requests a minor client's artwork or session records
- **Usually means:** the art record may become litigation evidence; release depends on jurisdiction's minor-record statute and the custody order, not on routine parental-access assumptions.
- **First question:** "Is this a routine parental-access request or a custody-related evidentiary request, and what does the custody order or consent say about record access?"
- **Data to pull:** custody/guardianship documentation, state minor-record-release statute, original consent's scope.

### A group art therapy session runs with no stated clinical directive and no verbal or observational processing of what was made
- **Usually means:** the modality has drifted from a facilitated clinical intervention into supervised craft time, losing both the therapeutic mechanism and the documentation basis for billing it as treatment.
- **First question:** "What was this session's stated clinical goal, and how was the art-making processed — verbally or through documented observation?"
- **Data to pull:** group session notes, the group's stated treatment-plan goals.

### Client artwork depicts an explicit self-harm or suicide method with no same-session verbal safety assessment documented
- **Usually means:** the image alone isn't a complete risk assessment — this content requires an explicit verbal safety check the same session, not deferral to the next visit.
- **First question:** "Was a direct verbal safety assessment conducted this same session, independent of the image content alone?"
- **Data to pull:** same-day session note, safety-assessment or screening documentation.

### Artwork is shredded, discarded, or returned with no documented decision or consent covering that disposition
- **Usually means:** the retention period for clinical records may not have been honored, and the client's or guardian's consent regarding disposition wasn't checked before acting.
- **First question:** "What does the intake consent specify about disposition, and is this decision documented in the chart?"
- **Data to pull:** artwork consent and disposition worksheet, chart entry for the disposal or return.

### The media or directive selected tracks the therapist's own preference more than the client's referral question or regulation stage
- **Usually means:** media/directive choice has drifted from a clinical decision into habit, undermining the rationale that distinguishes art therapy from a generic craft activity.
- **First question:** "What in this client's presentation or treatment goal specifically indicates this media or directive, versus what's simply familiar or available to the therapist?"
- **Data to pull:** treatment plan's stated rationale for the modality, media-control staging log.

### A referral asks the art therapist to determine, from a drawing alone, whether a child was abused
- **Usually means:** this exceeds what any single image or even a validated instrument can establish on its own — art can corroborate a finding but doesn't establish an abuse determination unaided.
- **First question:** "Is this request within art therapy's actual evidentiary role, or does it need referral to a forensic interview specialist alongside this work?"
- **Data to pull:** the referral's exact wording, any existing forensic-interview or child-protective-services involvement.
