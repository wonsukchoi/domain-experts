# Red flags

### An SLD evaluation referral opened after fewer than 6-8 weeks of a documented Tier 2 intervention
- **Usually means:** the low score reflects normal early-instruction variability, not yet enough data to judge response to intervention.
- **First question:** how many weeks of intervention and how many progress-monitoring data points exist so far?
- **Data to pull:** the intervention start date, the progress-monitoring log, session count.

### A progress-monitoring trend flat or declining across 8+ data points with no adjustment made to the intervention
- **Usually means:** the current intervention isn't working at the intensity or type currently delivered.
- **First question:** has the rate of improvement been calculated and compared against the rate required to close the gap?
- **Data to pull:** the full progress-monitoring data series, calculated rate of improvement (slope).

### An eligibility decision built on a single ability-achievement discrepancy score with no rate-of-improvement data
- **Usually means:** the team is relying on the older discrepancy-only model instead of pairing it with actual response-to-instruction evidence.
- **First question:** what does the student's rate of improvement over the intervention period show, alongside the discrepancy score?
- **Data to pull:** progress-monitoring data series, the ability-achievement discrepancy calculation.

### Pull-out minutes for a subject exceeding that subject's core-instruction minutes in the general-education classroom
- **Usually means:** the student is more isolated from grade-level content and peers than the deficit severity justifies.
- **First question:** what specific data supports this dosage versus a lower one?
- **Data to pull:** the master schedule, core-instruction minutes by subject, the IEP service-minutes page.

### A student showing no response after two full intervention tiers, still scheduled for inclusion-only support with no pull-out time
- **Usually means:** the service-minutes schedule hasn't been updated to reflect the student's actual response data.
- **First question:** when was the service-minutes schedule last revised against progress-monitoring data?
- **Data to pull:** service-minutes history, most recent progress-monitoring review date.

### Progress-monitoring probes administered by different staff, at inconsistent intervals, without a fidelity checklist
- **Usually means:** the resulting data may not be comparable across sessions, undermining any rate-of-improvement calculation.
- **First question:** was a fidelity checklist used, and were probes administered on a consistent schedule?
- **Data to pull:** the fidelity checklist log, the probe administration schedule and administrator record.

### A referral for evaluation with no documented check of attendance, mobility, or English-language-exposure history
- **Usually means:** an instructional-gap or exposure explanation hasn't been ruled out before treating the score as a disability indicator.
- **First question:** has the student's attendance, enrollment history, and language-exposure background been reviewed?
- **Data to pull:** attendance record, enrollment/transfer history, home-language survey.

### IEP reading or math goals written from generic grade-level norms rather than the student's own baseline and expected growth rate
- **Usually means:** the goal may be unrealistic or insufficiently ambitious for this specific student, undermining progress-monitoring decisions later.
- **First question:** does the goal's target and timeline derive from this student's baseline and an appropriate rate of improvement, or from a generic norm table?
- **Data to pull:** the IEP goal page, the student's baseline data and calculated target rate of improvement.

### A Tier 2 or Tier 3 intervention log showing under 80% session attendance or fidelity proceeding to an eligibility review as if it were full-dose data
- **Usually means:** the "non-response" being reported may reflect under-dosing, not a genuine lack of response to the intervention.
- **First question:** what was the actual session-attendance and fidelity rate across the intervention period?
- **Data to pull:** the session log, fidelity observation records.
