# Playbook

Filled worksheets for the three recurring judgment calls: is this an instructional gap or a disability signal, is the intervention actually working, and how many pull-out minutes does this student need right now.

## 1. Instructional-gap-vs-disability screen (complete before opening any intervention)

Run this before starting a Tier 2 intervention, not after. If any "gap" column is checked, address it first and re-screen in 2-4 weeks before starting formal intervention tracking.

| Factor | Threshold that flags an exposure/gap explanation | Maya (worked example) |
|---|---|---|
| Attendance | <90% year-to-date | 96% — not flagged |
| School mobility | 2+ school changes in current or prior year | 0 changes — not flagged |
| English language exposure | Home language survey indicates <3 years of English instruction | English-only home — not flagged |
| Prior grade retention/gaps | Missed a full unit of core instruction (long absence, remote-only period) | None recorded — not flagged |
| Universal screening trend | Single low score with no prior-year screening history | 2 prior years of screening data available, consistently low — flagged for tracking, not exposure |

Decision: no gap factors flagged → proceed directly to Tier 2 intervention with progress monitoring.

## 2. Tier 2/3 intervention progress-monitoring worksheet

Fill in weekly. Columns 5 and 6 are the ones that actually drive the continue/intensify/refer decision — level alone does not.

| Week | Date | ORF score (wcpm) | Sessions delivered / planned (fidelity) | Cumulative ROI (wcpm/wk) | Gap to spring benchmark (90) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 10/06 | 45 | 4/4 | — | 45 |
| 2 | 10/13 | 46 | 4/4 | 1.0 | 44 |
| 3 | 10/20 | 48 | 4/4 | 1.5 | 42 |
| 4 | 10/27 | 47 | 3/4 | 0.7 | 43 |
| 5 | 11/03 | 50 | 4/4 | 1.25 | 40 |
| 6 | 11/10 | 49 | 4/4 | 0.8 | 41 |
| 7 | 11/17 | 51 | 4/4 | 1.0 | 39 |
| 8 | 11/24 | 52 | 4/4 | 0.9 | 38 |
| 9 | 12/01 | 53 | 4/4 | 0.9 | 37 |
| 10 | 12/08 | 54 | 4/4 | 1.0 | 36 |

ROI formula: `(current score − baseline score) / weeks elapsed`. Required ROI formula: `(next benchmark − current score) / instructional weeks remaining`.

At week 10: ROI = 1.0 wcpm/week; required ROI = (90 − 54) / 20 = 1.8 wcpm/week (56% of target). Fidelity across all 10 weeks ≥90% (39/40 sessions delivered as planned). Decision rule below fires "intensify."

## 3. Dual-discrepancy decision table

Apply at every progress-monitoring review (typically every 8-10 weeks).

| Level gap (vs. benchmark) | Rate gap (ROI vs. required ROI) | Fidelity verified? | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| <10% below | Any | — | Fade support; monitor monthly |
| 10-25% below | ROI ≥ required | Yes | Continue current intervention unchanged |
| 10-25% below | ROI < required | No | Fix fidelity first; re-monitor 4 weeks before any other change |
| 10-25% below | ROI < required | Yes | Intensify dosage or change intervention type; re-monitor 8 weeks |
| >25% below | ROI < required | Yes | Intensify dosage now; if no improvement after intensification, open comprehensive SLD evaluation |
| >25% below | Flat or declining ROI across 8+ points | Yes | Open comprehensive SLD evaluation concurrently with continued intervention |

Maya: 40% level gap, ROI 56% of required, fidelity verified → row 5: intensify to Tier 3 now; escalate to evaluation only if intensification also fails to move the rate.

## 4. Pull-out vs. inclusion minutes worksheet

Start every student at the minimum validated dosage for their tier; only add minutes when data justifies it, and revisit at every progress-monitoring review — this is a schedule to be revised, not set once.

| Tier | Typical minimum dosage | When to increase | When to decrease |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 2 (targeted small group) | 20-30 min/day, 3-4x/week, in addition to core instruction | ROI <75% of required rate after 8-10 weeks with verified fidelity | ROI ≥ required rate for 2 consecutive review periods |
| Tier 3 (intensive, individualized or 1:2) | 30-45 min/day, 5x/week | ROI <75% of required rate after 8 weeks at Tier 3 with verified fidelity | Level gap closes to <15% and ROI ≥ required rate |
| IEP-specialized instruction (post-eligibility) | Set from the student's documented deficit severity and most recent ROI data, never a template default | Progress-monitoring data at annual/triennial review shows inadequate rate | Progress-monitoring data shows sustained closing of the gap |

Cap check: pull-out minutes for a subject should not exceed that subject's core-instruction minutes in the general-education classroom (e.g., if core reading block is 90 min/day, pull-out reading services should not reach or exceed 90 min/day) — if a proposed schedule does, treat it as a scheduling problem requiring co-teaching or push-in redesign, not an acceptable trade-off.
