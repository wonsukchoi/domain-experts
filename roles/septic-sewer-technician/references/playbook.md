# Playbook

Filled walkthroughs for the three places this trade gets guessed instead of measured: septic pump-timing, jetter nozzle/pressure selection, and pump-interval planning. Numbers are worked examples to adapt, not universal constants — always check the tank manufacturer's baffle spec, the jetter manufacturer's nozzle chart, and the local health department's pumping code.

## 1. Sludge judge readings and the pump-now decision

**Thresholds (NAWT/EPA-based field practice):** pump when the bottom of the scum layer is within **3 in.** of the bottom of the outlet tee, OR the top of the sludge layer is within **12 in.** of the outlet invert — whichever triggers first.

**Worked cases:**

| Case | Outlet invert height | Scum bottom | Distance to invert | Sludge top | Distance to invert | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 40 in. | 38 in. | 2 in. | 26 in. | 14 in. | Pump (scum threshold) |
| B | 40 in. | 34 in. | 6 in. | 30 in. | 10 in. | Pump (sludge threshold) |
| C | 40 in. | 30 in. | 10 in. | 20 in. | 20 in. | Monitor, recheck in 12 mo. |
| D | 38 in. | 36 in. | 2 in. | 29 in. | 9 in. | Pump (both thresholds triggered) |

**Reading procedure:** lower the sludge judge slowly through the scum layer until resistance drops (liquid), continue to the point resistance increases again (sludge), withdraw slowly and read the visible sludge line on the marked tube against the known outlet invert height (measured once per tank from the riser or lid opening down to the outlet pipe).

**Baffle/riser check (tank empty only):** inspect outlet tee for cracks or a detached baffle whenever the tank is pumped — a broken baffle lets scum bypass the calculated safety margin the sludge-judge threshold assumes, so a tank reading "fine" with a broken baffle is not actually fine. Effluent filter, if present, gets pulled and hosed off at every pump-out regardless of the sludge/scum reading.

## 2. Jetter nozzle and pressure selection

**Nozzle sequence for an unidentified mainline blockage:**
1. **Penetrating nozzle** (forward-jetting, minimal rear thrust) at reduced pressure (~1,500–2,000 PSI) to punch through the blockage without building back-pressure against the operator.
2. **Flushing nozzle** (rear-facing jets, no forward jet) at full working pressure once the line is open, to scour the pipe wall back to the cleanout.
3. **Root nozzle (chain flail / root saw)** only after camera confirmation of root mass — never as the first tool on an unknown stoppage.

**Pressure/flow by line size (typical trailer/truck-mounted jetter capability — confirm against unit spec plate):**

| Line size | Recommended PSI | Recommended GPM | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5–2 in. (lateral, fixture branch) | 1,500–2,000 | 4 | Cart jetter adequate |
| 3–4 in. (building lateral) | 2,000–3,000 | 8–12 | Cart or small trailer unit |
| 6 in. (main lateral) | 3,000–3,500 | 15–18 | Trailer/truck unit required |
| 8 in.+ (municipal/commercial main) | 3,500–4,000 | 18–25 | Truck-mounted; cart jetter will not reach flushing velocity |

**Worked case:** an 8 in. commercial main jetted with a cart unit rated 1,800 PSI / 4 GPM clears a visible path through the blockage (penetrating nozzle succeeds) but leaves grease film on 80% of the pipe wall, because 4 GPM can't generate flushing velocity in an 8 in. pipe — the call re-backs up within two weeks. Correct dispatch: truck-mounted unit at 3,500 PSI / 20 GPM for the flushing pass.

## 3. Pump-interval planning (household size vs. tank size)

**General EPA guidance:** inspect every 3 years; pump "as needed," typically every 3–5 years for an average household — actual interval is tank-size and household-size dependent, confirmed by the sludge-judge reading at each service, not assumed from the calendar.

**Illustrative interval table (adjust to local code and actual water use):**

| Tank size | 2-person household | 4-person household | 6-person household |
|---|---|---|---|
| 750 gal | ~4–5 yr | ~2–3 yr | ~1–2 yr |
| 1,000 gal | ~5–6 yr | ~3 yr | ~2 yr |
| 1,250 gal | ~6+ yr | ~4 yr | ~2–3 yr |

**Worked case:** a 4-person household on a 1,000-gal tank due for a "3-year" pump per the table is opened and sludge-judge reads well clear of both thresholds (Case C above) — the family's water-use habits (front-load washer, low-flow fixtures) pushed the actual interval past the table default. Correct action: note the measured reading on the service record, recommend a recheck in 12 months rather than a default 3-year callback, and pump only if the next reading crosses a threshold.
