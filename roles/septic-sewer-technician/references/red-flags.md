# Red flags

### Scum layer bottom within 3 in. of the outlet tee, or sludge top within 12 in. of the outlet invert
- **Usually means:** tank is at or past pumping threshold; second cause is a partially broken baffle letting scum sit closer to the outlet than an intact one would.
- **First question:** when was the outlet tee/baffle last inspected with the tank empty?
- **Data to pull:** current sludge judge reading against the recorded outlet invert height; last service record.

### All fixtures in the house slow simultaneously, not one fixture
- **Usually means:** tank-level or main-lateral restriction (scum/sludge near outlet, or a blockage past the tank); rarely a single branch clog.
- **First question:** has any one fixture been isolated as the sole slow drain, or are all of them affected together?
- **Data to pull:** sludge judge reading; camera pass on the main lateral if the tank reads clear.

### Surfacing effluent, soggy ground, or a green-grass stripe over the drainfield in dry weather
- **Usually means:** drainfield hydraulic overload or biomat clogging; second cause is a broken distribution line under that section of field.
- **First question:** was the tank pumped recently, and did the symptom persist afterward?
- **Data to pull:** distribution-box level check; field probe readings across all lines, not just the wet spot.

### Same cleanout backs up again within 6–12 months of the last mechanical clearing
- **Usually means:** root intrusion at a joint or crack, or a pipe defect (offset, belly) that a snake clears symptomatically but doesn't fix; second cause is grease buildup from an unaddressed kitchen source.
- **First question:** has this line ever been camera-inspected, or only ever snaked?
- **Data to pull:** CCTV footage with PACP-style defect log; pipe material and install date if available.

### H2S reading over 10 ppm at a tank, wet well, or manhole opening
- **Usually means:** normal anaerobic tank atmosphere at the low end; at higher readings (approaching the 100 ppm range), an accumulation risk serious enough that entry is unsafe without forced ventilation and full confined-space protocol.
- **First question:** is forced-air ventilation running before any entry is considered?
- **Data to pull:** continuous 4-gas meter log (H2S, O2, CO, LEL) for the duration entry is being considered.

### "Full tank" complaint on an ATU or pump-to-grade system under 3 years old
- **Usually means:** clogged effluent filter, not an undersized or genuinely full tank; second cause is a stuck float switch on the pump chamber.
- **First question:** when was the effluent filter last pulled and rinsed?
- **Data to pull:** filter inspection; pump-chamber float and alarm panel status.

### CCTV shows standing water covering more than roughly a third of the pipe diameter along a level run
- **Usually means:** a bellied (sagged) section holding water between cleanings; second cause is a settled trench over an old repair.
- **First question:** does the sag recur at the same station on repeat camera passes?
- **Data to pull:** footage counter/station reading at the sag; pipe material and depth of cover if on record.

### Grease-trap high-water alarm cycling more than once a week
- **Usually means:** trap undersized for current kitchen volume, or interceptor line partially blocked downstream; second cause is a skipped cleaning interval.
- **First question:** what's the trap's rated capacity against current meal-service volume?
- **Data to pull:** last three cleaning dates and volumes pulled; kitchen covers-per-day if available.

### Distribution box found unlevel by visual or level-tool check
- **Usually means:** one drainfield line is receiving disproportionate flow and biomat-clogging faster than the others; second cause is box settling after original installation.
- **First question:** which line sits lowest, and has that line's field area shown symptoms first?
- **Data to pull:** level reading at each outlet port; field probe results per line.

### Cart jetter used on a line 6 in. or larger and the blockage returns within two weeks
- **Usually means:** GPM was insufficient to reach flushing velocity for that pipe diameter, so the wall film wasn't removed even though the path was cleared; second cause is a persistent root or grease source upstream.
- **First question:** what GPM was the unit rated for against this line's diameter?
- **Data to pull:** service record's unit spec; camera pass to check wall condition, not just flow path.

### Root mass visible in camera footage at a joint, no visible crack or offset
- **Usually means:** a hairline joint separation not yet visible on camera, letting roots seek moisture through the gap; second cause is a poorly sealed repair coupling.
- **First question:** is the joint on original pipe or at a documented repair point?
- **Data to pull:** camera footage station number at the root mass; as-built or repair records for that segment if available.
