# Forensic Science Technician — Red Flags

### Evidence item count at lab intake doesn't match the scene collection log
- **Usually means:** a clerical logging/case-number transposition error at intake, or a genuine custody gap (loss, misfiling, theft).
- **First question:** what does the property-room sign-in sheet and transport manifest show for this specific item number?
- **Data to pull:** scene collection log vs. LIMS intake log vs. property-room custody sheet, cross-referenced by item number.

### Presumptive test reported as a finding without a noted confirmatory step
- **Usually means:** field-note terminology drift, or a confirmatory test was genuinely skipped before the report was written.
- **First question:** is there a bench-note entry for the confirmatory test (e.g., ABAcard, GC-MS), and if not, why not?
- **Data to pull:** analyst's bench worksheet for the confirmatory-test result and date.

### Pattern-match conclusion uses absolute language ("100% match," "to the exclusion of all others")
- **Usually means:** examiner language overstating certainty beyond the discipline's validated statistical basis (post-PCAST 2016).
- **First question:** what does the lab's SOP specify as the approved conclusion language (ACE-V terminology) for this discipline?
- **Data to pull:** examiner's technical review worksheet and the lab's written conclusion-language SOP.

### Analyst had access to suspect confession or criminal history before completing a pattern comparison
- **Usually means:** no documented linear-sequential-unmasking (LSU) protocol is in place, risking contextual/confirmation bias.
- **First question:** was the technical comparison completed and signed off before any non-forensic case context reached the analyst?
- **Data to pull:** case assignment log showing what information reached the analyst and when, relative to the comparison sign-off timestamp.

### Evidence seal shows tape that isn't initialed/dated at every opening
- **Usually means:** a custody transfer happened without a corresponding documented log entry.
- **First question:** who opened this seal, when, and where's the matching custody-log line?
- **Data to pull:** physical evidence bag/tape plus the custody log, cross-checked entry by entry.

### DNA mixture interpretation reported without stating contributor count or stochastic threshold
- **Usually means:** a low-template or complex mixture is being pushed past what standard interpretation can reliably resolve.
- **First question:** was probabilistic genotyping software (e.g., STRmix) used, and what analytical/stochastic threshold was applied?
- **Data to pull:** interpretation worksheet showing peak heights against the stated stochastic threshold.

### Time-sensitive evidence (e.g., sexual assault kit) sits in queue past its triage-tier turnaround target
- **Usually means:** a lab capacity issue, or the item wasn't flagged into the correct triage tier at submission.
- **First question:** was this case flagged as tier-1/expedite at intake, and if not, why?
- **Data to pull:** LIMS priority-tier field and submission-to-queue timestamp.

### Bloodstain area-of-origin conclusion rests on a single measured stain
- **Usually means:** an oversimplified single-stain angle extrapolated into an origin point, instead of a multi-stain area-of-convergence reconstruction.
- **First question:** how many independent stains were measured, and do their back-projected trajectories actually intersect?
- **Data to pull:** stain-measurement worksheet (width/length/calculated angle per stain) and the convergence diagram.

### Trace evidence report uses "consistent with" in the summary without documenting the underlying characteristics compared
- **Usually means:** class characteristics (fiber type, hair morphology) are being implied as individualizing without the microscopy basis stated.
- **First question:** which specific characteristics were compared, and is this evidence type capable of individualization at all?
- **Data to pull:** comparison microscopy bench notes.

### DNA extraction batch has no negative/blank control result on file
- **Usually means:** an unintentional lab-process omission that could invalidate results from that batch if contamination can't be ruled out.
- **First question:** where's the negative-control result for this specific extraction batch?
- **Data to pull:** extraction batch log including all control results, not just case samples.
