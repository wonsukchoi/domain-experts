# Faculty Playbook

Operational templates with concrete steps, dates, and thresholds. These are defaults, not laws — deviate consciously and log why in the annual-review file.

## 1. Annual effort-allocation table

Built each August, revised each January. Percentages are of total professional effort (a 40-hour nominal week across teaching, research, and service — not a legal timesheet).

| Career stage | Teaching | Research | Service | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-tenure, years 1–3 | 35% | 55% | 10% | Service capped hard; say no to anything not required by the department bylaws. |
| Pre-tenure, years 4–6 (dossier prep) | 30% | 55% | 15% | Teaching portfolio and publication record should already be assembled, not built retroactively. |
| Newly tenured, years 1–2 post-tenure | 30% | 45% | 25% | The "service cliff" — committees start asking; cap it before it becomes 40%+. |
| Senior/full, steady state | 35–40% | 30–35% | 25–30% | Mentoring junior faculty counts as service; don't let it silently become unpaid teaching. |

Reconciling check: allocations across the three legs should sum to 100% of professional effort at each stage; if a stage's row doesn't sum to 100, an activity is being double-counted or dropped.

## 2. NSF proposal timeline (single cycle, GEO directorate)

| Month (relative to submission) | Milestone |
|---|---|
| M-3 | Draft narrative complete; internal co-PI review. |
| M-2 | Data Management Plan and Broader Impacts section drafted as separate, reviewable documents — not appended paragraphs. |
| M-1 | Sponsored-programs office budget/compliance review (2–4 weeks minimum lead time; do not submit day-of). |
| M0 | Submission at target date. |
| M0 + ~2 | Panel assembled, reviews assigned. |
| M0 + ~4–5 | Panel meeting / review completion. |
| M0 + ~6 | Funding decision (NSF's stated internal target). |
| If declined | Reviewer comments available immediately; revise and resubmit at the next target date — do not wait a full year "to strengthen it" unless the comments require new preliminary data that takes that long to generate. |

Success-rate planning number: **~30–35%** for NSF's Geosciences directorate (above the NSF-wide ~25–28%). Plan staffing as if any single submission is more likely to be declined than funded; a research program with only one submission "in flight" at a time has no margin.

## 3. Tenure/promotion dossier structure

```
1. CV (full, dated)
2. Teaching statement (2–3 pages)
   - Course list with enrollment and evaluation-score trend, not single-semester snapshots
   - One redesigned course, with before/after assessment data if available
3. Research statement (3–5 pages)
   - Funding history table: award, amount, direct/indirect split, dates, role (PI/co-PI)
   - Publication list, annotated with venue and, where relevant, citation trajectory
4. Service statement (1–2 pages)
   - Departmental, university, and disciplinary-society service, dated
5. External letters (5–8 solicited; department chooses ~half, candidate suggests ~half)
   - Solicited from full professors at peer or aspirant institutions, arm's-length from direct collaborators
6. Chair's letter and committee vote
```

Weighting rule of thumb: committees write to the strongest leg and interrogate the weakest; a dossier with two strong legs and one adequate leg clears more often than one with three merely-adequate legs.

## 4. Field-course safety plan (JHA-based)

Complete **before** the enrollment cap or budget is finalized, not after.

```
1. Trip parameters: duration, remoteness (miles from definitive medical care), group size, ratio of instructors to students.
2. Job Hazard Analysis (JHA): list each planned activity (outcrop scramble, boat transect, night sky observation, etc.) with
   - hazard (fall, hypothermia, vehicle, wildlife, remoteness)
   - likelihood (low/med/high) x severity (low/med/high)
   - mitigation (PPE, buddy system, weather abort criteria, named turn-back trigger)
3. Emergency communication protocol: named check-in schedule, satellite/radio backup if cell coverage is absent, nearest medical facility identified in advance, one named on-site incident commander.
4. Pre-trip medical disclosure form, reviewed by instructor (not just filed) for any condition relevant to the specific hazards in step 2.
5. Departmental/university EH&S sign-off, logged with a date — "we've always just gone" is not a sign-off.
```

## 5. Course redesign from a concept inventory

For any intro topic, before writing learning objectives:

1. Pull the published concept-inventory items for the topic (e.g., Geoscience Concept Inventory, climate-literacy instruments).
2. Identify the 3–4 items with the highest documented pre-test miss rate in the published validation studies for that topic.
3. Administer the instrument pre-course (diagnostic only, first two weeks).
4. Write learning objectives that name the misconception directly ("distinguish weather variability from climate trend using a specific dataset," not "understand the difference between weather and climate").
5. Administer the same instrument post-course; compare item-level pre/post, not just total score — a flat item that stayed wrong despite a rising total score means that specific misconception was never actually confronted.
6. Carry forward only the items still flat to the next redesign cycle; retire items that moved.
