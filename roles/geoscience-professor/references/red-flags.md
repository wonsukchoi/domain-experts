# Red Flags

Smell tests across enrollment, funding, tenure dossiers, field safety, and teaching data. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the check to run.

## Gen-ed intro section enrollment down >15% year over year

- **Usually means:** a competing gen-ed option is cannibalizing seats, a scheduling conflict was created against a required major course, or the catalog's degree requirement changed. Less often, it's a real reputational drop from a prior semester's teaching problem.
- **First question:** "Did the gen-ed requirement list or a competing course's schedule slot change this year?"
- **Data to pull:** registrar cross-listing report, competing-course enrollment for the same term, and the gen-ed requirement catalog diff year over year.

## Funded grad student with <6 months of committed support and no proposal in the review pipeline

- **Usually means:** the PI let a resubmission window slip, often because teaching or service crowded out proposal-writing time in the preceding semester.
- **First question:** "When was the last proposal submitted on this line of funding, and what's the panel decision date?"
- **Data to pull:** award notice end date, sponsored-programs submission log, most recent panel summary/decline letter.

## Course evaluation mean drops more than 1.0 point (5-point scale) after a mid-semester syllabus or instructor change

- **Usually means:** the change itself, not underlying teaching quality, is driving the response — students penalize disruption independent of content quality.
- **First question:** "What specifically changed, and on what date relative to the evaluation window?"
- **Data to pull:** syllabus version history with dates, evaluation open/close dates, and open-ended comment themes filtered to post-change respondents only.

## Tenure or annual-review file with uniformly "satisfactory" ratings across all three legs, never "excellent" on any

- **Usually means:** no leg has a standout data point a committee can point to; the case reads as competent but unmemorable.
- **First question:** "Which single leg could plausibly reach 'excellent' with 6 months of focused effort before the dossier is due?"
- **Data to pull:** trend across the last 3 years of annual-review letters, not just the current year's.

## Field-trip waiver or medical-disclosure forms signed the morning of departure

- **Usually means:** the safety plan is being treated as paperwork to collect, not a plan that was actually reviewed against that trip's specific hazards.
- **First question:** "Has a Job Hazard Analysis been completed and signed off by EH&S, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** departmental EH&S sign-off log with a date that precedes the trip by at least the department's minimum lead time.

## A concept-inventory item's pre/post miss rate is unchanged despite the topic being "covered" in lecture

- **Usually means:** the misconception was layered over with correct information rather than directly confronted; students retain the original model alongside the new facts and default to it under exam pressure.
- **First question:** "Which specific item stayed flat, and did the learning objective for that topic name the misconception explicitly?"
- **Data to pull:** item-level pre/post response matrix (not the aggregate score) for the flat item.

## A single-season or single-site field result pitched to a journalist as a definitive trend

- **Usually means:** the finding is being overclaimed relative to its actual statistical power and scope, usually under pressure for visibility ahead of a grant renewal or tenure review.
- **First question:** "Does the underlying paper's own discussion section support the word 'trend,' or does it hedge with 'consistent with' or 'suggests'?"
- **Data to pull:** the paper's limitations/uncertainty section, and the press release or interview quote side by side.

## h-index or raw citation count cited as the sole justification in a hiring or tenure recommendation letter

- **Usually means:** the writer is substituting a metric for having read the candidate's actual papers.
- **First question:** "What do the top 3 cited papers say the field cites them for — method, dataset, or result?"
- **Data to pull:** the papers themselves, plus self-citation share (a high self-citation share inflates the raw count without external validation).

## Same 2 grad students co-authoring every paper from a lab by year 4, with no solo first-author paper from either

- **Usually means:** the advisor isn't structuring projects to let students individuate a research identity before the academic job market — a real liability for their placement, and a signal read negatively by external tenure letter-writers about the advisor's mentoring record.
- **First question:** "Does either student have a project defined narrowly enough to lead and publish alone?"
- **Data to pull:** authorship-order history across the lab's publication list for the last 4 years.

## NSF resubmission with only reviewer-requested wording edits and no new preliminary data

- **Usually means:** the underlying scientific concern the panel raised wasn't actually addressed, just the phrasing that surfaced it.
- **First question:** "For each panel weakness listed, what changed in the science, not the sentence?"
- **Data to pull:** side-by-side diff of the original and resubmitted narrative against the original panel summary.
