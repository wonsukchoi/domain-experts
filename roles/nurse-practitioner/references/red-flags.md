# Nurse Practitioner — Red Flags

### Chest pain with any cardiac risk factor (age >40, diabetes, smoking, family history) regardless of exam reassurance
- **Usually means:** Low-to-moderate pretest probability of ACS that exam alone can't rule out; second most likely is musculoskeletal or GI pain presenting atypically.
- **First question:** What is the calculated risk score (HEART, TIMI) and is there an EKG already on file?
- **Data to pull:** EKG, troponin if available, HEART score components.

### Fever >38.5°C in an immunocompromised patient (chemotherapy, transplant, high-dose steroids, uncontrolled HIV)
- **Usually means:** Neutropenic fever or serious infection until proven otherwise; second most likely is a benign viral illness, but the downside of missing the former is severe.
- **First question:** What is the most recent absolute neutrophil count, and when was it drawn?
- **Data to pull:** CBC with differential, blood cultures, immediate-care-level triage.

### New focal neurological deficit of any duration
- **Usually means:** Stroke or TIA until ruled out; second most likely is a migraine aura or Bell's palsy, but time-sensitive differentials override waiting for the benign explanation to prove itself.
- **First question:** What was the exact time of onset (for tPA window eligibility)?
- **Data to pull:** Immediate stroke-protocol imaging via ED referral — do not attempt outpatient workup.

### Diabetic foot ulcer present more than 2 weeks without documented improvement
- **Usually means:** Underlying osteomyelitis or inadequate perfusion; second most likely is simply inadequate offloading.
- **First question:** Is there palpable pulse distal to the wound, and has an X-ray ruled out bone involvement?
- **Data to pull:** Ankle-brachial index, X-ray, wound culture if signs of infection.

### Prescription request for a controlled substance from a new or infrequent patient with a compelling but unverifiable story
- **Usually means:** Could be legitimate; second most likely is diversion or doctor-shopping — the distribution is wide enough that pattern-checking is mandatory, not optional.
- **First question:** What does the state prescription drug monitoring program (PDMP) show for this patient?
- **Data to pull:** PDMP query result before writing the prescription.

### Practice-authority action being considered (prescribing a controlled substance, ordering advanced imaging, admitting a patient) in a reduced or restricted-practice state
- **Usually means:** This specific action may require a collaborating physician's co-signature regardless of clinical confidence.
- **First question:** Does the current collaborative-practice agreement cover this specific action?
- **Data to pull:** State practice-authority category and the specific collaborative agreement's scope.

### Patient returns with the same complaint after a prior visit's treatment plan, worse or unchanged
- **Usually means:** Working diagnosis may be wrong, not just "needs more time"; second most likely is genuine slow-resolving illness.
- **First question:** Does the working diagnosis still explain all the findings, or has something changed?
- **Data to pull:** Re-examine and reconsider the original differential rather than simply extending or escalating the same treatment.

### Medication list from patient self-report conflicts with pharmacy-fill history
- **Usually means:** Under-reported OTC/supplement use or a prescription from another provider not disclosed; second most likely is a filled-but-not-taken prescription.
- **First question:** Ask the patient directly about the specific discrepancy rather than silently updating the list.
- **Data to pull:** Pharmacy-fill or e-prescribing record, cross-checked against verbal history.

### Pediatric fever in an infant under 3 months of age
- **Usually means:** Requires different, more conservative protocol than fever in an older child — serious bacterial infection risk is substantially higher and less clinically apparent.
- **First question:** What is the exact age in days/weeks, and does the standard age-based fever protocol for this age band apply?
- **Data to pull:** Full septic workup per age-based protocol; this presentation is generally outside routine outpatient NP management without same-day ED/pediatric referral.
