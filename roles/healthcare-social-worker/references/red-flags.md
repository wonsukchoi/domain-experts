# Healthcare Social Worker — Red Flags

### LACE score ≥10 with a standard (non-escalated) discharge plan on the chart
- **Usually means:** the risk score was computed but never translated into plan intensity, or was computed too late (discharge day) to act on.
- **First question:** "When was the LACE score calculated, and did the discharge plan change after it was known?"
- **Data to pull:** admission-day LACE calculation timestamp vs. discharge-plan documentation timestamp.

### Discharge plan depends on a family caregiver whose capacity was never directly verified
- **Usually means:** the plan relied on the patient's report of caregiver willingness rather than a direct capacity check.
- **First question:** "Did anyone speak with the caregiver directly about specific hours and physical tasks?"
- **Data to pull:** caregiver contact log / documented conversation with the caregiver, not just the patient.

### 2+ ED visits or admissions in 6 months with no psychosocial barrier screen on the chart
- **Usually means:** recurrent utilization is being treated as a medical-only problem, missing a likely non-medical driver.
- **First question:** "Has anyone screened for medication cost, transportation, or caregiver capacity this admission?"
- **Data to pull:** utilization history (ED/admission count, 6-month lookback) and barrier-screen documentation.

### SNF placement recommended with "generalized weakness" as the sole documented justification
- **Usually means:** the recommendation will likely stall in utilization review for lacking a specific, numbered functional deficit.
- **First question:** "What specific ADL deficit, with what assist level, is documented?"
- **Data to pull:** PT/OT functional assessment with numbered assist levels (e.g., "max assist x2 for transfers").

### High-risk patient discharged with only a faxed referral packet, no warm handoff
- **Usually means:** the handoff was treated as complete once the fax was sent, leaving a window where the receiving party hasn't yet acted.
- **First question:** "Did anyone speak directly with the receiving clinician before the patient left?"
- **Data to pull:** handoff log — call timestamp, who was spoken with, what was confirmed.

### Follow-up appointment documented as "recommended" rather than "scheduled" for a high-risk patient
- **Usually means:** the discharge instructions list a follow-up need without a confirmed date/time, shifting the burden to the patient to self-schedule.
- **First question:** "Is there a confirmed date, time, and transportation plan for this appointment?"
- **Data to pull:** appointment confirmation record, not just the discharge-instruction text.

### Every patient over a certain age is recommended for SNF regardless of functional assessment
- **Usually means:** defensive/reflexive placement pattern rather than assessed need — a common overcorrection failure mode.
- **First question:** "What specific functional deficit does this patient have that a home + home-health plan couldn't address?"
- **Data to pull:** comparison of SNF-recommendation rate against documented ADL-deficit severity across recent cases.

### Medication reconciliation call scheduled but no confirmation the patient can afford the medications
- **Usually means:** affordability wasn't screened, and a "successful" reconciliation call may reveal the patient simply won't fill the prescription.
- **First question:** "Has pharmacy cost been discussed with this patient before discharge?"
- **Data to pull:** financial-counselor referral or medication-assistance-program enrollment status.

### Teach-back on new medication regimen was skipped or patient "said they understood"
- **Usually means:** health literacy wasn't actually verified — passive confirmation ("do you understand?") has a much higher false-positive rate than teach-back.
- **First question:** "Can you show me the teach-back documentation — what did the patient say when asked to explain it back?"
- **Data to pull:** teach-back documentation, not a yes/no literacy checkbox.

### Discharge plan unchanged after a documented psychosocial barrier was identified
- **Usually means:** the barrier was screened and noted but never actually addressed in the plan — screening without action.
- **First question:** "What specific action was taken in response to this documented barrier?"
- **Data to pull:** barrier-screen finding cross-referenced against the discharge-plan action items.
