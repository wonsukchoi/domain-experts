# Red flags

Smell tests a floor manager or senior associate catches before a full week goes by. Format: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull.

### Conversion rate down 3+ points week-over-week with traffic flat (within ~2%)
- **Usually means:** a floor-coverage or service gap concentrated in specific hours, not a traffic or product problem — check AUR first (Section vocabulary.md) to rule out pricing.
- **First question:** "Which hour block did conversion actually drop in, and who was scheduled during it?"
- **Data to pull:** hourly conversion by associate, fitting-room queue times for the affected window, schedule for that week vs. the comparison week.

### UPT falling while ATV holds steady
- **Usually means:** add-on/cross-sell isn't happening — customers are buying one item at a higher price point, not multiple items, so the average looks fine while basket size erodes.
- **First question:** "Are we suggesting a second item before the fitting room, or only at the register?"
- **Data to pull:** UPT by associate, mystery-shopper add-on-attempt score, pairing-list usage if tracked in POS notes.

### AUR (ATV ÷ UPT) falling while UPT holds steady
- **Usually means:** discounting or override creep, not a selling-technique problem — margin leak dressed up as a sales number that still looks okay.
- **First question:** "Show me discount/override frequency by associate and register for this period."
- **Data to pull:** exception report discount-override rate, markdown cadence calendar (to separate planned markdowns from ad hoc overrides), margin by category.

### A single register's void or no-sale rate is 2x+ the store average
- **Usually means:** most often legitimate price-check patterns on a high-traffic register; second most likely is drawer-open cover for an unrung item ("sweethearting") when no-sales track closely with voids.
- **First question:** "Do the no-sales cluster around specific transaction times we can check against camera coverage?"
- **Data to pull:** time-stamped void/no-sale detail for that register, camera footage for the flagged windows, comparison against the associate's own baseline from prior weeks.

### No-receipt returns spike on one SKU or one customer (3+ in 90 days)
- **Usually means:** wardrobing (wear-and-return) if the SKU is occasion-wear with a short window between purchase and return; price arbitrage (bought on markdown elsewhere or a prior visit, returned at full ticket) if the return value consistently exceeds the lowest recent selling price.
- **First question:** "Does the customer record show a pattern, or is this the first flagged return from this person?"
- **Data to pull:** customer return history via loyalty/card lookup, days-between-purchase-and-return distribution for that SKU, comparison of refund amount requested vs. lowest verified recent selling price.

### Shrink in one department is more than ~2x its share of store sales
- **Usually means:** most likely a receiving-count or planogram-location error that shows as a phantom sale/loss on the report; second most likely internal or external theft concentrated in that department.
- **First question:** "Have receiving counts for that department been reconciled against the PO in the last cycle?"
- **Data to pull:** receiving reconciliation log, planogram compliance audit for the department, cycle-count variance by SKU within the department, not just the department total.

### Mystery shopper score drops on greeting/opening specifically, other categories flat
- **Usually means:** a specific associate or shift reverting to a transactional opener ("can I help you?") under time pressure, most commonly during understaffed peak windows.
- **First question:** "Was the shopper visit during a peak or off-peak hour, and who was on the floor?"
- **Data to pull:** the shopper's timestamp and narrative, floor schedule for that time, comparable scores from the same associate on non-peak visits.

### Same-day return processed at a different register than the sale
- **Usually means:** most often benign (customer returned at a more convenient register); worth checking when paired with a high override rate, since it can mask a fraudulent void-and-refund pattern that a single-register exception report wouldn't catch.
- **First question:** "Does the refunding associate's override rate look normal independent of this transaction?"
- **Data to pull:** cross-register transaction log for the customer/card, refunding associate's exception report, whether the item was rescanned and matched to the original sale record.

### Sell-through rate under ~40% two weeks after a planned markdown cadence trigger
- **Usually means:** an inventory or merchandising problem, not a selling-technique problem — coaching the floor on a SKU that's simply overbought or poorly placed won't move the number.
- **First question:** "Is this SKU in its planogram-assigned location, and at what markdown tier?"
- **Data to pull:** sell-through by SKU vs. the store's standard markdown cadence, planogram placement audit, comparable SKU sell-through in a different location if available.
