# Red Flags

Smell tests across accreditation outcomes, lab safety, and faculty qualifications. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Credentialing-exam pass rate below ~70% for a single cohort

- **Usually means:** either a real curriculum gap or a faculty-qualification/course-coverage gap tied to one specific required course and semester, not a program-wide decline.
- **First question:** "Which required course maps to the exam's weakest subscore domain, and who was the instructor of record that semester?"
- **Data to pull:** the credentialing exam board's subscore breakdown report; the department's teaching-assignment history by course and semester.

### Practicum or internship site with no named on-site supervisor of record

- **Usually means:** an informal placement that won't count toward the program's accredited outcome hours no matter how strong the opportunity looks.
- **First question:** "Is there a signed affiliation agreement naming a qualified supervisor, and is their credential on file?"
- **Data to pull:** the affiliation-agreement file; the named supervisor's credential/license documentation.

### Lab course requiring a fingerstick or blood draw with no exposure control plan on file

- **Usually means:** OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard compliance (29 CFR 1910.1030) was never built for that specific course.
- **First question:** "Who is the designated owner of this lab's exposure control plan, and when was it last reviewed?"
- **Data to pull:** the written exposure control plan; the sharps-disposal and training log for that course.

### Faculty teaching a required lab course outside their credentialed or terminal-degree specialty

- **Usually means:** a staffing patch — leave coverage or an adjunct hire — that becomes a citable faculty-qualification finding at the next accreditation site visit if it isn't documented as temporary.
- **First question:** "Is this a one-semester documented exception with a remediation plan, or has it become the standing assignment?"
- **Data to pull:** faculty credential file mapped to the specific course; department teaching-assignment history across at least three terms.

### Skills check-off pass rates differing by more than ~15 percentage points across lab sections for the identical skill

- **Usually means:** no inter-rater calibration session was run before live grading, and different graders are applying the checklist inconsistently.
- **First question:** "Did every grader score the same sample demonstration before live grading, and how much did their scores vary?"
- **Data to pull:** calibration-session scores if they exist; section-by-section pass rates for the same skill and term.

### Self-study retention or placement rate calculated from total enrollment rather than the accreditor's defined completer-cohort denominator

- **Usually means:** the number was built off whatever denominator made the program look strongest, not the one the accreditor's annual report actually requires.
- **First question:** "What exact cohort definition does the accreditor's outcomes form specify for this metric?"
- **Data to pull:** the CoAES/CAAHEP annual report's cohort-definition instructions; the department's raw enrollment and completion roster for that cohort.

### Practicum hours log with only the student's own signature

- **Usually means:** the hours can't be independently verified and won't count toward the program's accredited outcomes at self-study, regardless of how detailed the entries look.
- **First question:** "Whose signature is on this log besides the student's own?"
- **Data to pull:** the hours log; the site supervisor's contact information and countersignature cadence on file.

### A nutrition or training claim in course material sourced only from a manufacturer or influencer, not a peer-reviewed source

- **Usually means:** a settled-vs-open classification error — a marketing claim is being taught as though it were a legitimate open scientific debate.
- **First question:** "What's the actual peer-reviewed evidence base for this specific claim, independent of the source's popularity?"
- **Data to pull:** the citation actually backing the claim on the lecture slide or in the assigned reading.

### A single gateway course with a fail rate over ~30% for three consecutive terms

- **Usually means:** a curriculum-sequencing or prerequisite-preparation problem, not a declining student-quality problem.
- **First question:** "What changed in this course or its prerequisite in the term the fail rate first jumped?"
- **Data to pull:** grade distribution by term for the course; grade correlation with the prerequisite course's grade for the same students.

### Fitness-assessment intake forms missing a documented screening-algorithm outcome before the test date

- **Usually means:** the test was run on verbal assurance rather than the documented ACSM screening decision, which is the record a liability review actually checks.
- **First question:** "Where is the completed screening form for this specific participant, dated before the test?"
- **Data to pull:** the intake/screening form; the informed-consent form for the specific test administered.
