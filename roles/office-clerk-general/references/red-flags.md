# Office Clerk, General — Red Flags

### A task list where every item is labeled "urgent" with no stated deadlines
- **Usually means:** Nobody has separated hard from soft deadlines yet, or requesters have learned that "urgent" is the only label that gets attention.
- **First question:** "What actually happens if this waits until tomorrow?"
- **Data to pull:** The original request (email, ticket, verbal ask) for any stated or implied deadline.

### Same task-category deferred two or more reporting periods in a row
- **Usually means:** A genuine capacity shortfall, not a personal productivity problem — either volume has grown or the task legitimately needs a specialist.
- **First question:** "Has this category's volume actually increased, or is something else eating the time?"
- **Data to pull:** Deferral log or handoff notes from the past 2-3 periods for that task-category.

### A same-day hard-deadline item arrives after most of the day's soft tasks are already underway
- **Usually means:** The schedule wasn't built with capacity reserved for likely late-arriving hard items — this is common for anything client-facing with unpredictable timing.
- **First question:** "Does this client/task-type have a known pattern of late-day urgent requests?"
- **Data to pull:** History of when similar hard-deadline items have arrived in past weeks.

### End-of-day handoff notes that say "did what I could" without naming what was deferred
- **Usually means:** No real triage happened — tasks were worked until time ran out rather than deliberately prioritized, and nobody downstream knows what's actually outstanding.
- **First question:** "Which specific items didn't get done, and were they Hard or Soft?"
- **Data to pull:** The day's original task list against what's actually marked complete.

### A covered-for specialist's queue (file clerk, data-entry keyer) sits untouched an entire absence
- **Usually means:** Coverage triage defaulted to "my own tasks only," leaving anything blocking someone else's work stalled with no visibility.
- **First question:** "Is anything in that queue blocking another person's deadline?"
- **Data to pull:** The absent specialist's open-item list, sorted by whether anyone else is waiting on it.

### Small quick tasks consistently finished while one or two larger soft-deadline items never get started
- **Usually means:** Clearing the list for the feeling of progress, rather than freeing the most real capacity — the opposite of the largest-low-consequence-first deferral rule.
- **First question:** "Would deferring one large item have freed more time than doing five small ones?"
- **Data to pull:** Time estimates for the day's completed items vs. the deferred ones.

### A recurring monthly/quarterly task (supply order, filing audit) is chronically late
- **Usually means:** It's being treated as low-priority every single day because no single day's deadline feels close, until it's suddenly overdue.
- **First question:** "What's the actual due date, and how many days of buffer are left?"
- **Data to pull:** The task's real recurrence schedule and last completion date.

### A generalist clerk reports being "behind" for months without a specific capacity ask ever reaching a supervisor
- **Usually means:** The escalation step in the decision framework isn't happening — deferral patterns are being absorbed silently instead of flagged.
- **First question:** "When was this pattern last mentioned to a supervisor, in those terms?"
- **Data to pull:** Any prior handoff notes or communications that named the pattern explicitly.
