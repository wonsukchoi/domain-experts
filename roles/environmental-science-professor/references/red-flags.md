# Red Flags

Smell tests for a field-based environmental science program. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Field trip roster exceeds the 1:12 instructor-to-student ratio set in the safety plan

- **Usually means:** enrollment grew after the safety plan was filed, or a co-taught section merged rosters without re-checking supervision capacity.
- **First question:** "How many supervising adults are confirmed for this specific field day, not the semester average?"
- **Data to pull:** the filed safety plan's stated ratio against the current-day roster and confirmed TA/co-instructor headcount.

### Two consecutive field seasons collecting under 60% of the proposed sample sites

- **Usually means:** either the original sampling design was overscoped for the available field-season window, or a recurring access/permit/equipment failure is eating field days that should be diagnosed once, not re-discovered each season.
- **First question:** "What specifically consumed the missing field days — access, weather, equipment, or staffing — and did it repeat from last season?"
- **Data to pull:** the field log (dates, sites attempted, sites completed, reason for any miss) for both seasons side by side.

### A federal proposal declined twice in the same program with the same core panel critique unaddressed

- **Usually means:** the resubmission addressed reviewer tone but not the substantive gap the panel actually named — a common failure is revising the writing while leaving the experimental design or preliminary-data gap untouched.
- **First question:** "Point to the specific paragraph in the resubmission that answers the panel's stated weakness — not where it was 'considered.'"
- **Data to pull:** both panel summaries side by side against the resubmitted narrative's relevant section.

### A mixed-methods project has an active data-collection instrument with a human component and no IRB protocol on file

- **Usually means:** the project scope drifted after the original (environmental-sampling-only) IRB determination — a survey, interview, or resident-observation element was added later without re-triggering review.
- **First question:** "Walk through every data-collection instrument in the current protocol — which ones touch a human subject directly?"
- **Data to pull:** the current data-collection instruments and the IRB determination letter's stated scope, compared line by line.

### Intro-level gen-ed environmental science course evaluations run more than roughly 0.8 points below the department's major-course average

- **Usually means:** likely a cohort-composition effect (non-majors fulfilling a distribution requirement rate differently than majors) rather than a content or delivery problem — but only a genuine finding if it holds across multiple sections and instructors, not one section.
- **First question:** "Does the gap hold across every section of this specific gen-ed course, or just this instructor's section?"
- **Data to pull:** section-level evaluation means for the gen-ed course across all instructors for the past 2–3 terms, next to the major-course average.

### Grant indirect-cost (F&A) recovery posted to the department doesn't match the award's stated F&A rate

- **Usually means:** a budget-modification or no-cost-extension changed the MTDC base without the department's post-award office updating the recovery calculation, or a subaward's F&A was miscalculated against the prime rate instead of its own negotiated rate.
- **First question:** "Pull the award notice's stated F&A rate and MTDC base — does the posted recovery match that calculation for this reporting period?"
- **Data to pull:** the award notice, the current budget modification (if any), and the department's F&A recovery ledger for the period in question.

### A field weather/safety go/no-go decision was made verbally with no written log entry

- **Usually means:** the decision itself may have been correct, but there's no record to defend it if a student is injured or a parent/administrator later disputes the call — this is the single most common gap a post-incident liability review finds.
- **First question:** "Where's the written log entry — forecast pulled, threshold compared, decision, who made it — for this specific field day?"
- **Data to pull:** the safety plan's required log template against the actual entries for the trip in question.

### A student research proposal (independent study, honors thesis) touches a collection permit or land-access agreement that isn't yet filed

- **Usually means:** the student and advisor built the project timeline around the science question without checking the compliance lead time (state collecting permits commonly run 60–90 days), and the field season start date is now closer than the permit turnaround allows.
- **First question:** "What's the permit or access agreement's current status, and does its expected approval date actually precede the planned first field day?"
- **Data to pull:** the permit application submission date and the agency's stated typical processing window.

### A politically salient content complaint (climate, environmental justice) is filed citing "bias" with no specific claim identified

- **Usually means:** either a genuine advocacy-without-disclosed-premises problem in the material, or a complaint about the topic's existence rather than its treatment — these require different responses and the file has to distinguish them before responding.
- **First question:** "Which specific claim in the lecture or reading is disputed, and on what evidentiary basis — not 'the topic' in general?"
- **Data to pull:** the lecture slides or assigned reading's sourced claims and their confidence-bound language, next to the complaint's specific language.

### Field equipment calibration log shows a gap over 90 days before a data-critical deployment

- **Usually means:** an instrument (multiparameter sonde, met station sensor) drifted out of spec silently — the readings look plausible but aren't trustworthy, and this is usually caught only in post-hoc QA, after a season's data is already collected.
- **First question:** "When was this specific instrument last calibrated against a known standard, and is that within the manufacturer's stated interval?"
- **Data to pull:** the equipment's calibration log against its manufacturer-stated recalibration interval.

### A tenure or third-year review compares an interdisciplinary field-based CV directly against a single-discipline department benchmark

- **Usually means:** the review committee defaulted to the most familiar comparison set (often bench-lab publication norms) instead of building the applicable one, systematically under- or over-crediting the candidate.
- **First question:** "What comparison set was this benchmark built from, and does it include any other field-based cases from this department?"
- **Data to pull:** prior tenure case files for field-based (not bench-lab) candidates in the department, with their output counts at the same review stage.
