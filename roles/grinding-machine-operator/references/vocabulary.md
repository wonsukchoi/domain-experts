# Vocabulary

### Surface finish (Ra)
A numeric measurement of surface roughness (roughness average), used to specify and verify how smooth a machined or ground surface is.
**In use:** "Spec calls for Ra 16 μin — check it on the profilometer, don't just run your thumb over it."
**Common misuse:** Judging surface finish by visual or tactile inspection alone rather than measuring it against the specified Ra value.

### Grinding burn
Localized thermal damage to a workpiece's surface from excessive heat during grinding, altering the material's microstructure and hardness without necessarily being visible.
**In use:** "Ra reads fine, but this wheel's overdue for dressing — run a nital etch before clearing these parts for burn."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a part that measures within dimensional/finish spec has no thermal damage, when burn is a separate, often invisible metallurgical defect requiring its own specific inspection.

### Wheel dressing
The process of truing and sharpening a grinding wheel's cutting surface to restore its cutting efficiency and dimensional accuracy.
**In use:** "Dress the wheel now — it's at 85 parts, past the recommended interval for this material."
**Common misuse:** Continuing to run a wheel based on how recent parts have measured, rather than tracking dressing interval against actual part count or wheel condition.

### Nital etch
A chemical etching test applied to ground steel surfaces to reveal grinding burn or other thermal damage that isn't visible to the naked eye.
**In use:** "Nital etch showed a dark band at the edge — that's burn, even though the part measured in-spec on the profilometer."
**Common misuse:** Relying on visual inspection or Ra measurement alone to clear a part for burn, when only a nital etch or equivalent method can actually detect this specific defect.

### Stock removal rate
The amount of material removed per pass or per unit time during grinding, which must be matched to the wheel/coolant system's actual heat-dissipation capacity.
**In use:** "Pushing removal rate up to hit the shift target, but check the coolant flow can actually keep up with that before committing."
**Common misuse:** Increasing stock removal rate to meet a production target based solely on machine capability, without verifying the wheel/coolant combination can dissipate the resulting heat without causing burn.

### Lapping
A fine abrasive finishing process using a lapping plate and loose or bonded abrasive to achieve very high flatness or surface finish, typically as a final step after grinding.
**In use:** "This needs lapping for flatness, not just polishing — polishing alone won't correct the residual waviness."
**Common misuse:** Treating lapping as interchangeable with polishing, when lapping specifically targets flatness and dimensional accuracy while polishing primarily targets surface appearance and reflectivity.

### Grit progression
The sequence of abrasive grit sizes, coarse to fine, used in lapping or polishing to progressively remove scratches from the previous step.
**In use:** "Don't jump from 400 grit straight to 1200 — that skips a step and leaves 400-grit scratches the 1200 can't fully clear."
**Common misuse:** Skipping a grit step in the progression to save time, leaving residual scratches from a coarser step that the next finer step can't fully remove within a reasonable processing time.

### Coolant (grinding)
Fluid applied during grinding to reduce heat and flush away debris, directly affecting both surface finish and burn risk.
**In use:** "Check coolant flow before this run — it's a burn-sensitive material at an aggressive removal rate."
**Common misuse:** Treating coolant flow/condition as a fixed, "set and forget" system rather than something to verify before a run, especially on burn-sensitive materials or at higher stock removal rates.

### Loading (wheel)
The buildup of workpiece material or debris embedded in a grinding wheel's abrasive surface, reducing its cutting efficiency and increasing heat generation.
**In use:** "This wheel's loaded, not just dull — that buildup needs dressing to clear, not just more time cutting."
**Common misuse:** Failing to distinguish loading from simple wheel wear or dulling, when both reduce cutting efficiency similarly but loading specifically involves material buildup that dressing addresses differently than wear alone.

### Profilometer
An instrument that measures surface roughness (Ra and related parameters) by tracing a stylus or optical sensor across a surface.
**In use:** "Profilometer's the actual verification step — a general sense of smoothness isn't a substitute for the reading."
**Common misuse:** Relying on a general sense of surface smoothness rather than an actual profilometer reading to verify a finish spec is met.
