# Red flags

### Wheel operated well past its manufacturer-suggested redressing interval while Ra readings still measure within spec
- **Usually means:** burn risk from wheel dulling/loading that Ra measurement alone won't detect.
- **First question:** how many parts has this wheel processed since its last dressing, versus the suggested interval?
- **Data to pull:** part count since dress compared against manufacturer redressing guidance, plus burn check status.

### Surface finish accepted based on visual or tactile judgment rather than a measured Ra value against the spec
- **Usually means:** the finish may be out of spec despite looking or feeling acceptable.
- **First question:** what does the profilometer actually read against the specified target?
- **Data to pull:** profilometer Ra measurement compared against the specified target.

### Stock removal rate increased to hit a production target without verifying the wheel/coolant combination's heat dissipation capacity at that rate
- **Usually means:** elevated burn risk even if the machine can physically sustain that removal rate.
- **First question:** what's the documented safe stock removal rate for this wheel/coolant/material combination?
- **Data to pull:** current stock removal rate compared against the documented safe rate.

### A lapping/polishing job skips an abrasive grit step in the progression to save time
- **Usually means:** residual scratches from the coarser step remain, invisible without magnification, that the finer step alone can't fully remove in reasonable time.
- **First question:** does the grit progression sequence actually used match the standard full progression for this finish spec?
- **Data to pull:** grit progression sequence used compared against the standard progression for this finish spec.

### Burn-sensitive material ground with no nital etch or hardness spot-check performed at any point
- **Usually means:** thermal damage could be present and go completely undetected.
- **First question:** does this material/process combination require burn checking, and was it performed?
- **Data to pull:** inspection method log compared against whether this material/process requires burn checking.

### A specific machine/wheel combination shows a recurring pattern of burn or out-of-spec finish across multiple jobs without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — dressing interval, coolant flow, stock removal rate — rather than isolated one-off part variation.
- **First question:** does the defect occurrence log correlate with a specific process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** defect occurrence log by machine/wheel compared against process parameter logs over the same period.

### Coolant flow/condition not verified before a grinding run, especially on a burn-sensitive material or aggressive stock removal rate
- **Usually means:** reduced heat dissipation capacity that increases burn risk independent of wheel condition.
- **First question:** was coolant flow rate and condition actually checked against the process's documented requirement?
- **Data to pull:** coolant flow rate/condition check compared against the process's documented requirement.
