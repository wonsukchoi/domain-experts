# Playbook

Filled reference tables and decision ladders for the four recurring high-stakes situations: febrile infant workup, newborn hyperbilirubinemia, the well-visit due-list, and vaccine hesitancy.

## Febrile infant workup by age band (Pantell et al., 2021)

Index event: a single documented temperature ≥38.0°C (100.4°F), rectal, in a well-appearing infant. Appearance does not change the age-band requirement.

| Age band | Required workup | Disposition rule |
|---|---|---|
| 8–21 days | Urinalysis + urine culture, blood culture, CBC, CSF studies (LP mandatory) | Admit, empiric antibiotics — regardless of any lab result. No low-risk exception exists at this age. |
| 22–28 days | Urinalysis + urine culture, blood culture, CBC + inflammatory marker (procalcitonin preferred; CRP + ANC if unavailable) | LP strongly recommended but may be deferred *only* if all low-risk criteria are met (see below) and close inpatient observation without antibiotics is feasible. Default to LP if any doubt. |
| 29–60 days | Same labs as above | If ALL low-risk criteria met: LP and empiric antibiotics may be withheld; outpatient management permitted with reliable caregiver + guaranteed 24h follow-up. If ANY criterion fails: LP + empiric antibiotics + admit. |

**Low-risk criteria (all five must be met to step down from full workup, 22–60-day band):**

| Criterion | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Urinalysis | Negative (no leukocyte esterase, no nitrite, <5 WBC/hpf) |
| Absolute neutrophil count (ANC) | ≤4,000/µL |
| CRP | ≤20 mg/L |
| Procalcitonin | ≤0.5 ng/mL |
| Exam | No focal bacterial source, non-toxic appearance |

A single value above threshold moves the infant back to the full-workup, admit, empiric-antibiotics path — the criteria are conjunctive, not averaged.

## Newborn hyperbilirubinemia — phototherapy thresholds (AAP 2022, Bhutani nomogram logic)

Total serum bilirubin (TSB, mg/dL) thresholds for starting phototherapy, by age in hours and risk category (lower-risk = ≥38 weeks gestation, no risk factors; higher-risk = 35–37 weeks and/or isoimmune hemolytic disease, G6PD, sepsis):

| Age (hours) | Lower-risk threshold | Higher-risk threshold |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | ~10 mg/dL | ~8 mg/dL |
| 48 | ~13 mg/dL | ~11 mg/dL |
| 72 | ~15 mg/dL | ~13.5 mg/dL |
| 96+ | ~18 mg/dL | ~16 mg/dL |

Reading rule: plot TSB against exact age in hours (not "day of life") and the applicable risk curve — the same 15 mg/dL value is a phototherapy threshold at 72 hours and comfortably below threshold at 96+ hours. Never compare a raw TSB number across infants without both age-in-hours and risk category attached.

## Vaccine hesitancy — fallback ladder (Opel et al., 2013)

Positions in order of preference; move to the next only if the current one fails:

1. **Presumptive announcement.** "Today he's due for DTaP, Hib, and PCV13." No pause for open debate on routine visits — presumptive framing alone resolves the majority of hesitancy that isn't firmly held.
2. **Motivational interviewing on the specific concern.** If resistance follows step 1, ask what specifically worries them (a named ingredient, a specific adverse-event story, a specific vaccine vs. all vaccines) — treat the concern as specific, not generic "anti-vax."
3. **Selective sequencing, not selective skipping.** If a caregiver will accept some vaccines now, prioritize by time-sensitivity and outbreak risk (pertussis-containing, Hib, PCV in infancy) over vaccines with longer safe deferral windows — never invent a non-standard "alternative schedule" wholesale; negotiate order, not omission, and re-offer the deferred ones at every subsequent visit.
4. **Documented declination.** If refusal persists after repeated offers, use a formal AAP-style refusal-to-vaccinate form, signed by the caregiver, and continue offering at every future visit — the relationship and future access matter more than winning this visit.
