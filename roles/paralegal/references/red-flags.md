# Red flags — what a paralegal notices instantly

Smell tests across discovery, calendaring, and billing work. Format per flag: signal with a threshold where one exists, what it usually means, the first question a veteran asks, and what to pull.

### A privilege log entry uses the same description language across dozens of entries
- **Usually means:** the log was drafted by rote at the end of the production deadline rather than reviewed document-by-document or by genuine category — the requesting party will notice the templated language before you do.
- **First question:** "Does this description actually describe what this specific document shows, or could it describe half the production?"
- **Data to pull:** the underlying documents behind the 5–10 most repetitive-sounding entries; compare descriptions against actual content.

### A privileged-communication entry has every recipient as a non-attorney business person
- **Usually means:** an attorney was cc'd elsewhere in the custodian's mailbox but not on this specific thread, and the privilege designation was applied by custodian rather than by thread.
- **First question:** "Is there an attorney anywhere in this specific thread, not just in this custodian's general correspondence?"
- **Data to pull:** the full email header/thread for the flagged entry, not just the metadata row in the log.

### A rule- or statute-driven deadline exists in only one calendar system
- **Usually means:** single-point-of-failure docketing — if that system has an outage, sync error, or the entry is deleted, nothing catches it.
- **First question:** "Where is the second, independent entry for this deadline, and who owns it?"
- **Data to pull:** the case-management system's docket export and the paralegal's/attorney's personal tickler for the same date range; confirm they match.

### A deadline is calendared for the exact statutory date with no earlier internal target
- **Usually means:** whoever docketed it treated the statutory deadline as the working filing target instead of a hard boundary.
- **First question:** "What's the internal hard-stop date, several business days before this one?"
- **Data to pull:** the docket entry's associated internal-target field; if blank, that is the finding.

### First-pass review QC sample shows an error rate above ~2%
- **Usually means:** either reviewer training gaps on that batch's document type, or the coding protocol was ambiguous for a recurring document pattern in that custodian's collection.
- **First question:** "Is the error concentrated in one reviewer or one document type, or spread evenly across the batch?"
- **Data to pull:** the QC sample's error log broken out by reviewer and by document type, not just the aggregate rate.

### A reviewer's throughput is roughly double the batch's average documents/hour
- **Usually means:** click-through coding without substantive review — the output looks complete but wasn't actually assessed.
- **First question:** "What's this reviewer's sample error rate, independent of their speed?"
- **Data to pull:** that reviewer's individual QC sample results, pulled separately from the batch aggregate.

### Bates numbering has a gap or an overlapping range across two productions
- **Usually means:** a document was pulled after numbering but before production (privilege claim, inadvertent duplicate), or two productions were numbered from the same starting point by mistake.
- **First question:** "Is the missing/overlapping range accounted for on a privilege log or a supplemental-production log?"
- **Data to pull:** the production log and the Bates range ledger side by side for the affected volumes.

### A discovery response is drafted and ready to serve with no attorney review of the privilege designations
- **Usually means:** time pressure led to skipping the sign-off step, not that the designations don't need review — every privilege call is the attorney's to make, not the paralegal's, however confident the read.
- **First question:** "Has the supervising attorney actually reviewed the withheld/redacted list, or just been told it's ready?"
- **Data to pull:** the sign-off log or email confirmation tied to this specific response, not a general engagement on the matter.

### A time entry describes the work in fewer than roughly ten words with no task specificity
- **Usually means:** the entry will be one of the first struck in a billing audit or fee petition, regardless of whether the work was legitimate — vague entries read as padding even when they aren't.
- **First question:** "Could someone outside this matter tell what was actually done from this entry alone?"
- **Data to pull:** the timekeeper's entries for that day compared against the task list or calendar for corroboration.

### A cite-check finds the same pin-cite error repeated across multiple briefs
- **Usually means:** a wrong pin cite from an early brief was copied forward into later filings without anyone re-checking the reporter.
- **First question:** "Which brief introduced this citation first, and was it ever checked against the actual reporter text?"
- **Data to pull:** the citation's appearance history across the matter's filings and the original reporter page.

### A TAR/predictive-coding validation report has no seed-set composition disclosed
- **Usually means:** the review is fast but not documented well enough to survive a challenge to methodology if opposing counsel moves on it.
- **First question:** "Can we show the seed-set's custodian and date-range diversity if asked, or only the final recall/precision numbers?"
- **Data to pull:** the seed-set selection log and the elusion-test sample results, not just the summary statistics.

### An exhibit on the trial exhibit list has an unresolved objection noted with no resolution plan attached
- **Usually means:** the objection was logged when raised but never routed back to the attorney for a stipulation, foundation witness, or motion in limine.
- **First question:** "What closes this objection before the pretrial conference — stipulation, witness testimony, or a motion?"
- **Data to pull:** the exhibit index's objection column and the pretrial-conference deadline for motions in limine.
