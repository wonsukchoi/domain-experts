# Genetic Counselor — Red Flags

### Posterior risk reported without showing the prior it derived from
- **Usually means:** the calculation was done informally or from memory rather than worked through explicitly, or the worksheet was discarded before documentation.
- **First question:** "What was the pedigree-based prior before we applied the test result or family evidence?"
- **Data to pull:** the Bayesian worksheet (prior, conditional, joint, posterior per hypothesis) for this consultand.

### Negative panel result reported as "negative" with no residual-risk figure
- **Usually means:** the counselor or report is treating panel sensitivity as 100%, or the report template doesn't force a residual-risk field.
- **First question:** "What's this panel's detection rate for the syndrome we're evaluating, and did we apply it to the pretest risk?"
- **Data to pull:** the lab's stated analytical/clinical sensitivity for the relevant gene panel and syndrome.

### Consultand's stated understanding doesn't match the number just given
- **Usually means:** the framing (percentage vs. natural frequency) didn't match the consultand's numeracy, or the session moved too fast from calculation to conclusion.
- **First question:** "Can you tell me in your own words what this number means for your decision?"
- **Data to pull:** teach-back response; re-explain using natural-frequency framing if the teach-back reveals a misunderstanding.

### A VUS is being treated as actionable evidence in a clinical recommendation
- **Usually means:** the ordering clinician or family is reading "variant found" as equivalent to "variant explains the condition," collapsing the classification tiers.
- **First question:** "Is a management decision (surgery, cascade testing, reproductive planning) resting on this VUS, and does the geneticist's report explicitly say it's not yet actionable?"
- **Data to pull:** the variant classification report's evidence-code summary and explicit actionability statement.

### Cascade-testing plan for at-risk relatives was never discussed
- **Usually means:** the session focused entirely on the consultand's own result and ran out of time, or the family-implications conversation was deferred and never revisited.
- **First question:** "Who else in the family does this result affect, and has the consultand thought about who to tell and when?"
- **Data to pull:** the pedigree, marked for which relatives are first/second-degree and therefore at meaningfully elevated risk.

### Consultand asked "what would you do" and got either a directive answer or a non-answer
- **Usually means:** the counselor either overstepped non-directive practice by steering toward a preferred outcome, or under-delivered by refusing to engage with the tradeoffs at all.
- **First question:** "Did we name the specific tradeoffs (what changes with each option) without picking one for them?"
- **Data to pull:** session notes on what was said in response to a direct request for the counselor's opinion.

### De novo variant is being counted as fully confirmed without parentage testing
- **Usually means:** duo sequencing (only one parent tested) or unconfirmed biological parentage is being treated as equivalent to trio-confirmed de novo status.
- **First question:** "Was parentage confirmed, and was this trio or duo sequencing?"
- **Data to pull:** the sequencing report's parentage-confirmation statement and trio/duo designation.

### Testing recommendation made without a GINA/insurance-discrimination conversation
- **Usually means:** the counseling session covered the medical risk calculation but skipped the legal-protection context that affects whether the consultand wants to test at all.
- **First question:** "Did we discuss what GINA does and doesn't cover (health insurance yes, life/disability/long-term-care insurance no) before recommending testing?"
- **Data to pull:** informed-consent documentation for the GINA-scope discussion.

### A minor is being tested for an adult-onset condition with no near-term clinical actionability
- **Usually means:** parental anxiety or convenience is driving a testing decision that professional guidelines (ACMG, NSGC) generally recommend deferring until the minor can consent, absent a childhood-actionable intervention.
- **First question:** "Is there a childhood-onset surveillance or treatment action this result would change, or does management wait until adulthood regardless?"
- **Data to pull:** the condition's age-of-onset and standard-of-care surveillance/intervention timeline.
