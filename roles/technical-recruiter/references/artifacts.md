## 2. Funnel comparison model (filled — see Worked example in SKILL.md for full narrative)

| | Unscoped requisition | Scoped requisition |
|---|---|---|
| Candidates sourced | 45 | 30 |
| Phone screened | 18 | 14 |
| Advanced to onsite | 9 | 6 |
| Late (onsite-stage) rejections | 5 (56%) | 0 |
| Interviewer hours spent | 25 (5 rejected × 5 interviewers × 1 hr) | 30 (6 onsite × 5 interviewers × 1 hr) |
| Interviewer cost (@ $120/hr) | $3,000 | $3,600 |
| Hires produced | 0 | 1 |
| Time elapsed | 6 weeks, no hire | 3 weeks to acceptance after scoping |

**Reading it:** the scoped process cost slightly more in interviewer hours but produced an actual hire — the unscoped process's $3,000 was a pure sunk cost with nothing to show for it.

## 3. Structured scorecard (filled example)

| Competency | Interviewer | Question asked | Score (1-4) | Independent notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Distributed systems debugging | Interviewer A | "Tell me about a time you diagnosed a cross-service failure." | 4 | Clear methodology, identified root cause via distributed tracing |
| System design | Interviewer B | "Design a rate limiter for a multi-region API." | 3 | Solid design, missed one edge case on clock skew |
| Collaboration | Interviewer C | "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision." | 4 | Specific example, showed both conviction and flexibility |

**Rule applied:** each interviewer owns a distinct competency and submits scores independently before the group debrief — no interviewer duplicates another's area, and no one sees another's score before submitting their own.

## 4. Offer close plan (filled example)

| Candidate priority (stated during process) | How the close addresses it |
|---|---|
| Growth into a tech lead path within 18 months | Included a specific written growth conversation with the hiring manager as part of the offer discussion, not just a verbal mention |
| Remote flexibility 2 days/week | Confirmed explicitly in the offer letter, not left as an assumed norm |
| Compensation competitive with one other offer in hand | Benchmarked and matched within a defensible band, communicated with the reasoning behind the number, not just the number itself |

**Rule applied:** the close addresses the candidate's actual stated priorities, gathered throughout the process — not assembled for the first time at the offer stage.
