# Red flags — what a recruiter notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A requisition that's just a copy of a generic job description template with no team-specific detail
- **Usually means:** sourcing against this will likely produce a funnel that gets rejected late for reasons that were never made explicit, since the actual bar hasn't been defined.
- **First question:** "What has this team specifically struggled with that this hire needs to solve, and what does success look like at 6 months?"
- **Data to pull:** the requisition as written, any prior hiring manager conversation establishing team-specific need.

### A high late-stage (onsite) rejection rate with no pattern analysis of why
- **Usually means:** candidates are advancing past screening only to fail on a criterion that wasn't screened for early — often a sign the requisition or phone-screen bar doesn't match what interviewers are actually evaluating.
- **First question:** "What's the common reason behind recent onsite rejections, and is it something that could have been screened for earlier?"
- **Data to pull:** rejection reasons for recent onsite candidates, comparison against the phone-screen criteria used.

### Interview loop questions and scoring criteria that vary noticeably interviewer to interviewer for the same role
- **Usually means:** unstructured interviewing correlates poorly with actual job performance — the loop's value depends on consistency across candidates.
- **First question:** "Are all interviewers for this role using the same core questions and a shared rubric, or improvising their own?"
- **Data to pull:** interview question banks/scorecards used per interviewer for this role.

### Group debrief happening before each interviewer submits an independent score
- **Usually means:** anchoring — the first strong opinion in the room, often the most senior person's, can disproportionately shape the outcome before others form their own independent view.
- **First question:** "Did each interviewer submit a scorecard independently before the group discussion happened?"
- **Data to pull:** scorecard submission timestamps relative to debrief meeting time.

### A candidate given no status update for 2+ weeks during an active process
- **Usually means:** ghosting damages employer brand regardless of the eventual decision, and a candidate treated this way becomes a detractor in their professional network.
- **First question:** "When was this candidate last given a status update, and is one overdue?"
- **Data to pull:** candidate communication log and timestamps, current pipeline stage duration.

### A time-to-fill metric being hit by thinning out the evaluation process rather than by fixing sourcing or scoping efficiency
- **Usually means:** speed is being purchased at the cost of rigor, which risks a bad hire — a cost typically far larger than the time saved.
- **First question:** "What specifically was cut to hit this timeline, and was it evaluation rigor or process inefficiency?"
- **Data to pull:** the process steps for this specific requisition versus the standard loop, what was skipped or shortened.

### Interview scores with no tracked correlation to actual on-the-job performance after hire
- **Usually means:** the interview process's predictive validity is unverified — it may be screening for the wrong things without anyone knowing.
- **First question:** "Has interview score been checked against actual performance review outcomes for people hired through this process?"
- **Data to pull:** interview scorecard data for past hires, corresponding performance review outcomes.

### An offer negotiation focused only on compensation with no discovery of what else the candidate actually values
- **Usually means:** the close may miss a lever that actually matters more to this specific candidate (growth path, team, flexibility, mission) than the number itself.
- **First question:** "What has this candidate said matters to them beyond compensation, and has that been addressed in the close?"
- **Data to pull:** candidate conversation notes on stated priorities/concerns throughout the process, not just at the offer stage.
