# Red flags

### Employer/alumni survey agreement on a Core Competency item drops below the program's self-stated benchmark (e.g., <75% vs. a stated ≥75% target)
- **Usually means:** one or two required courses mapped to that competency aren't actually assessing it with an authentic artifact, or one section/instructor is dragging the average down; second most likely: the survey instrument or respondent sample changed and isn't comparable to the prior review cycle.
- **First question:** which specific required course(s) map to this competency, and what does section-disaggregated data on the graded artifact show?
- **Data to pull:** the competency crosswalk, section-level rubric/grade data for the mapped course(s), and the survey instrument used in the prior comprehensive review for comparability.

### Full-time-faculty headcount below what the last COA site-visit report treated as adequate, with a vacant search line unfilled for more than one hiring cycle
- **Usually means:** administration reallocated the line during a budget freeze, betting adjuncts can cover delivery; second most likely: the tuition-revenue model no longer supports the line and no one has told the program formally.
- **First question:** is there a signed commitment from the dean/provost to refill the line before the next self-study, or is the curriculum being planned around a hire that isn't guaranteed?
- **Data to pull:** current faculty roster with FT/adjunct split, ALISE Statistical Report peer-program comparison, the prior site-visit report's faculty-sufficiency findings.

### A required course's pass or competency rate diverges more than ~20 points between sections taught by different instructors
- **Usually means:** the assessment method differs between sections (quiz vs. authentic capstone), not student ability; second most likely: one instructor, often adjunct, hasn't received or adopted the shared rubric.
- **First question:** are both sections grading the identical artifact against the identical rubric, or only nominally "the same course"?
- **Data to pull:** side-by-side syllabi, the rubric documents in use, and raw score distributions rather than pass/fail alone.

### A pre-tenure faculty member's committee/service load exceeds roughly 30–40% of reported effort for two years running
- **Usually means:** the department is understaffed for its accreditation and committee obligations and defaults to whoever says yes, disproportionately junior or women faculty; second most likely: the faculty member is substituting service for a research program that has stalled.
- **First question:** which of these service roles is explicitly documented as counted in the tenure letter, versus assumed "good citizenship" that isn't formally weighed?
- **Data to pull:** the annual activity report's time allocation, a department-wide service-assignment log, and the chair's letter language on service from the most recent tenure case.

### Doctoral cohort admits fall to single digits, two admission cycles running
- **Usually means:** RA/TA funding lines were cut or redirected elsewhere in the college; second most likely: the field-wide applicant pool has genuinely shrunk (a documented multi-year trend across LIS PhD programs) and recruiting hasn't adjusted.
- **First question:** is this a funding constraint internal to the program or an applicant-pipeline constraint shared across peer programs?
- **Data to pull:** the ALISE Statistical Report's doctoral-enrollment trend line across peer programs, and internal funding-offer data for the last three admit cycles.

### A cataloging/metadata course syllabus hasn't been substantively revised since before the 2013 RDA transition, or never mentions BIBFRAME
- **Usually means:** the instructor, often a long-serving adjunct, is teaching legacy AACR2/MARC content because it's what they were trained on, not because it reflects current practice; second most likely: no one on the curriculum committee owns "standards currency" as a specific, dated review item.
- **First question:** when was this syllabus last substantively revised, and by whom?
- **Data to pull:** the syllabus revision history/dates, and curriculum-committee minutes on standards-currency review.

### A practicum/field-placement site has hosted students for three or more consecutive terms with no documented faculty check-in
- **Usually means:** the placement has become a passive pipeline the program stopped actively supervising, which risks the accreditation documentation requirement for practicum oversight; second most likely: the relationship has gone informal/personal rather than institutional, creating single-point-of-failure risk if that supervisor leaves.
- **First question:** who last had a documented conversation with the site supervisor about learning objectives, and when?
- **Data to pull:** the practicum placement log with last-contact dates, and the learning-objective/evaluation forms on file for that placement.

### An online-format section's sole assessment is an auto-graded quiz while the in-person section of the same course uses an authentic project
- **Usually means:** the online section was built primarily for scale/cost reasons and its assessment design never received parity treatment; second most likely: no one has checked whether graduates of the online section perform equivalently once in the field.
- **First question:** does the competency map for this course actually get assessed in the online section, or only nominally covered?
- **Data to pull:** online vs. in-person syllabi and grading rubrics, and any available alumni-outcome comparison by delivery modality.

### An applied/practice-based scholarship record is entering a P&T dossier with no translation into standard research-committee vocabulary
- **Usually means:** the faculty member assumes the professional contribution is self-evidently valuable to a general-university committee; second most likely: the department chair hasn't coached the framing before external letters are solicited.
- **First question:** has anyone outside LIS on the committee actually read this section and understood the stated contribution?
- **Data to pull:** the draft dossier narrative, comparable successful dossiers from peers in other practice-oriented sub-fields, and the external-reviewer selection list.
