# Playbook

Filled templates for the recurring processes of a postsecondary LIS faculty job: curriculum-to-competency mapping, accreditation-response sequencing, pre-tenure time allocation, and adjunct calibration.

## 1. ALA Core Competence crosswalk (filled example)

Every required course must map to at least one competence statement and name the graded artifact that proves it — not just "covers the topic."

| Core Competence (ALA 2009, abbreviated) | Required course | Assessed artifact | Last verified |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Foundations (values, ethics, history of the field) | Foundations of LIS | Reflective essay, rubric-scored | 2024-08 |
| 3. Organization of recorded knowledge and information | Organizing Information | Original MARC/RDA record set + BIBFRAME crosswalk | 2025-01 (revised, see worked example) |
| 4. Technological knowledge and skills | Info Systems & Technology | Database/schema design project | 2023-08 |
| 5. Reference and user services | Reference & Information Services | Recorded reference-interview + transaction log critique | 2024-08 |
| 7. Research methods | Research Methods in LIS | IRB-ready proposal draft | 2024-01 |
| 9. Administration and management | (elective only — flagged) | none currently required | — flag for curriculum committee |

**Rule of thumb:** a competence row with no required-course mapping is either (a) genuinely elective-appropriate and should be documented as such in the self-study, or (b) a coverage gap the next curriculum-committee cycle needs to close — decide which explicitly; don't leave it silently blank.

## 2. Accreditation-response sequence, by time-to-review

COA operates on a seven-year comprehensive-review cycle with an interim/mid-cycle report roughly at year three or four. Response posture changes with the calendar:

| Time to next comprehensive review | Posture | Action |
|---|---|---|
| >4 years out | Pilot quietly | Test the fix in 1–2 sections, collect a cohort of data before touching committee bandwidth. |
| 2–4 years out (mid-cycle / interim report window) | Document formally, pilot in parallel | File the interim report with the finding, the dated fix, and a remeasurement plan (see worked example memo) — reviewers want evidence of response, not a finished result yet. |
| <12 months out (self-study drafting) | Full committee vote + written policy | No more piloting — the fix needs a curriculum-committee-approved policy on record before the site-visit team reads the self-study. |
| Site visit scheduled (<3 months) | Freeze scope, document only | Do not introduce new changes this window; a reviewer who sees a policy still in flux during the visit itself reads it as unstable, not responsive. |

## 3. Section/instructor disaggregation diagnostic (template)

Use whenever a course-level number (pass rate, satisfaction score, employer rating) is reported as a single figure and a decision is riding on it.

| Section | Instructor type | Enrolled | Assessment method | Pass/competency rate | Delta vs. best section |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | Adjunct | 24 | Legacy multiple-choice midterm | 58% | –33 pts |
| B | Full-time | 22 | Rubric-anchored capstone | 91% | baseline |
| C | Full-time | 20 | Rubric-anchored capstone | 90% | –1 pt |

**Decision rule:** if the spread between the best and worst section exceeds ~20 points, check assessment-method parity before concluding anything about instructor quality or student ability — a method mismatch (quiz vs. authentic artifact) is the far more common cause than either.

## 4. Pre-tenure time-allocation guide (6-year clock)

A starting allocation, adjusted by year and by what the department chair has confirmed counts toward the tenure letter:

| Year | Research | Teaching prep | Service | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | 50% | 40% | 10% | Decline all but one committee; the one you accept should be confirmed in writing as tenure-relevant. |
| 3–4 (mid-tenure review) | 45% | 35% | 20% | Some service growth is expected by the mid-tenure letter; keep it to roles with visible deliverables (a completed self-study section, a redesigned required course). |
| 5–6 (dossier prep) | 55% | 30% | 15% | Pull back service sharply; the dossier is judged on the research record accumulated, not on service momentum. |

**Trigger to renegotiate:** if committee/service load crosses ~30–40% of reported effort for two years running pre-tenure, that's the threshold to raise explicitly with the chair before the mid-tenure review, not after — see `red-flags.md`.

## 5. Adjunct-instructor calibration packet (contents, in handoff order)

1. Current rubric-anchored assessment materials (assignment prompt, grading rubric, one annotated exemplar at each grade band).
2. The competence-crosswalk row this course maps to, so the adjunct sees why the artifact is graded the way it is, not just how.
3. A scheduled one-hour co-grading session with a full-time instructor teaching the same course this term (calibration, not evaluation — framed to the adjunct as support).
4. A single point of contact (not the whole curriculum committee) for mid-term questions.
5. A dated confirmation that the packet was delivered and received — this is the paper trail an accreditation self-study needs if the same gap resurfaces.

**Test:** could an adjunct instructor, receiving only this packet with no other conversation, deliver an assessment equivalent to the full-time sections? If not, the packet is incomplete, not the instructor.
