# Red flags

### Dough feels noticeably stiffer or wetter than expected at the stated hydration
- **Usually means:** flour absorption differs from what the formula assumed, often due to a protein or moisture content difference between flour lots.
- **First question:** is this the same flour lot or brand the formula was originally developed with?
- **Data to pull:** the flour's spec sheet (protein %, moisture content) compared against prior batches.

### Bulk fermentation runs significantly faster or slower than the formula's stated time
- **Usually means:** the actual dough temperature differs from what the formula assumed.
- **First question:** what does the dough thermometer actually read right now versus the formula's assumed temperature?
- **Data to pull:** dough thermometer reading and the kitchen's ambient temperature log.

### Windowpane test fails (dough tears instead of stretching thin) well past the formula's stated mixing time
- **Usually means:** under-development from insufficient mixing energy — a mismatch between mixer speed and batch size — not simply that more time will fix it on its own.
- **First question:** does the mixer's speed setting match its rated capacity for this batch size?
- **Data to pull:** mixer speed setting and batch volume against the mixer's rated capacity.

### Loaf shows poor oven spring despite what seemed like an adequate bulk ferment
- **Usually means:** the shaped, final-proofed dough was over-proofed — gas capacity already spent — rather than an oven problem.
- **First question:** what did the poke test show right before the loaf went into the oven?
- **Data to pull:** the logged proof time and poke-test result recorded before baking.

### Crumb is dense and tight with unevenly sized holes
- **Usually means:** under-mixing (a weak gluten network), not under-proofing.
- **First question:** was the windowpane test actually checked and passed before ending the mix?
- **Data to pull:** mixing time and method used versus the formula, plus any recorded windowpane test result.

### Starter or leaven's rise-to-double time has slowed noticeably from its normal pattern
- **Usually means:** the feeding ratio, interval, or ambient temperature has drifted — not that the starter is simply weakening on its own.
- **First question:** what feeding ratio and interval has actually been used over the past several days?
- **Data to pull:** the starter feeding log (ratio, interval, ambient temperature).

### Batch-to-batch hydration or handling is inconsistent despite claiming to follow "the same recipe"
- **Usually means:** the formula was scaled by volume or eyeballed rather than recalculated in baker's percentages.
- **First question:** what were the actual measured ingredient weights used in the inconsistent batches?
- **Data to pull:** the recorded ingredient weights for each batch being compared.
