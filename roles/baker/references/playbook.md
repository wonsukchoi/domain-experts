# Playbook

## Baker's percentage scaling worksheet (filled example)

Base formula: flour 100% (1,000g), water 68% (680g), salt 2% (20g), instant yeast 1% (10g). Total percentage sum: 171%. Total dough weight: 1,710g.

Target: 1,140g total dough (2 loaves at 570g each).

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Total percentage sum | 100 + 68 + 2 + 1 | 171% |
| Flour weight for target batch | 1,140 ÷ 1.71 | 666.7g |
| Water | 666.7 × 0.68 | 453.3g |
| Salt | 666.7 × 0.02 | 13.3g |
| Instant yeast | 666.7 × 0.01 | 6.7g |
| Check: sum of all ingredients | 666.7 + 453.3 + 13.3 + 6.7 | 1,140.0g ✓ |

Result preserves the base formula's exact 68% hydration and 2%/1% salt/yeast ratios at any target batch size — recalculate this way for any yield change, never scale by eye.

## Dough-temperature / fermentation-time adjustment (filled example)

Formula assumes a dough temperature of 78°F with a bulk fermentation time of 3 hours to reach the target volume increase.

| Actual dough temperature | Approximate fermentation rate vs. formula assumption | Adjusted bulk time estimate |
|---|---|---|
| 78°F (matches formula) | 1.0× (baseline) | 3 hours |
| 82°F (4°F warmer) | ~1.3× faster | ~2 hr 15 min — check volume, don't run the full 3 hours |
| 74°F (4°F cooler) | ~0.75× slower | ~4 hours — check volume before assuming it's simply "not working" |

Rule applied: roughly every 4°F of dough temperature deviation from the formula's assumption shifts fermentation rate meaningfully — always confirm against actual dough volume/behavior rather than committing to the adjusted clock estimate blindly.

## Proofing decision log (filled example)

| Batch | Formula proof time | Actual kitchen temp | Poke test result at formula's stated time | Action taken |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 90 min | 78°F (matches assumption) | Springs back slowly, small dent remains — properly proofed | Baked at 90 min as the formula states |
| B | 90 min | 82°F (warmer than assumption) | Barely springs back, indentation stays — already overproofed by 90 min | Should have checked at ~75 min instead; next batch at this temperature, checked earlier and baked on the earlier reading |
| C | 90 min | 70°F (cooler than assumption) | Springs back quickly and fully — still underproofed at 90 min | Extended to ~120 min, rechecked poke test until it showed the slow, partial springback of batch A, then baked |
