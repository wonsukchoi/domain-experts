# Red flags

Smell tests a brake/signal/switch operator or reviewer catches before a cut is left unattended, a switch move is authorized, or a derail is trusted. Each entry: what it usually means, the first question a veteran asks, and the data to pull before proceeding.

### Hand-brake count applied matches the crew's habitual number rather than the grade/tonnage table for this specific track

**Usually means:** the count was carried over from a different siding or a previous shift instead of computed against today's actual grade and tonnage.
**First question:** "What does the securement table call for at this segment's grade and tonnage, and does the applied count match it?"
**Data to pull:** the track profile/employee timetable grade for this segment, today's trailing tonnage and car count, and the carrier's securement table.

### A cut is treated as secure because it "hasn't moved yet" while air brakes are still charged

**Usually means:** the air brake system is being counted on to make up a hand-brake shortfall, which 49 CFR 218.99 does not permit.
**First question:** "Has the effectiveness test been run with the air fully released, or has this only been watched with air still on?"
**Data to pull:** the effectiveness-test log (time, air-release confirmation, observed result) for this specific cut.

### An effectiveness test is skipped or shortened because the crew is behind schedule

**Usually means:** time pressure is being allowed to substitute for the one check that verifies the securement against its actual failure condition.
**First question:** "Was air fully released and the cut observed for the full specified interval, or was this cut short?"
**Data to pull:** the timestamped test log versus the carrier's specified wait interval for this grade band.

### A switch reads correctly on the target/lamp but hasn't had a physical point check

**Usually means:** verification stopped at a visual read instead of confirming the points are actually seated against the stock rail.
**First question:** "Has anyone physically checked the point-to-stock-rail fit, or is this reading based on the target alone?"
**Data to pull:** the point-gap check record (if any) and the track's class-specific gap tolerance under 49 CFR 213.135.

### A switch stand's lock is engaged but the connecting rod/point position wasn't checked first

**Usually means:** the lock is securing whatever position the points happen to be in, seated or not — locking doesn't fix an unseated point.
**First question:** "Was the point fit confirmed before or after the lock was applied?"
**Data to pull:** the sequence of the last verification (point check, then lock, or lock alone) and any obstruction (ice, debris) noted at the point.

### A derail's position is assumed from "how it's usually left" on this spur rather than directly checked

**Usually means:** the derail's actual state hasn't been confirmed before a move that depends on it.
**First question:** "Has this specific derail been checked today, or is its position being assumed from habit?"
**Data to pull:** the derail-check log for this shift and the carrier's standing instruction for that spur's default state.

### A derail is left in the off/cleared position after a move instead of restored immediately

**Usually means:** the crew intends to restore it "later" or at end of shift, leaving the main unprotected in the meantime.
**First question:** "Is this derail currently protecting the main, and if not, when is it being restored?"
**Data to pull:** the timestamp of the last move over this derail and its current logged position.

### A blind shoving move continues after radio or hand-signal contact with the point is lost

**Usually means:** the crew is finishing the move on the last known car count instead of stopping at the point contact was lost.
**First question:** "At what point was contact lost, and did the move stop there or continue on assumption?"
**Data to pull:** the radio traffic log or the point-protection crew member's statement on when contact broke.

### A securement left by a prior crew is trusted on the tag's count without re-testing

**Usually means:** conditions since the securement was applied (temperature drop, added tonnage, brake-shoe wear) haven't been accounted for.
**First question:** "Has anything changed on this cut or this track since the tag was written, and has the securement been re-tested since?"
**Data to pull:** the original securement log's timestamp and conditions, and today's conditions for the same cut.

### A cut's computed hand-brake requirement exceeds the number of cars with functional hand brakes on hand

**Usually means:** the requirement is at risk of being rounded down to whatever's practically available instead of resolved by adding cars or setting out defective ones.
**First question:** "Does the cut actually have enough functional hand brakes to meet the computed count, and if not, what's the plan — not just the workaround?"
**Data to pull:** the bad-order/defect status of hand brakes on cars in this specific cut.
