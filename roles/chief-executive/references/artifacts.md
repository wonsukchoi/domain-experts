## 2. Board update — hardest-truth-first structure (filled example)

> **Headline:** Core product roadmap is 2 quarters behind target; two enterprise deals ($1.2M ARR combined) are paused pending the missing features.
> **What we're doing about it:** Redirected the requested new-product-line headcount toward the core product's 5-engineer shortfall instead of approving it as submitted; targeting the delayed features shipped within 6 weeks.
> **Runway:** 11.6 months at current burn under this plan (would have been 10.6 if the original 8-hire request were approved in full).
> **New opportunity status:** Approved a 2-person pilot to validate the new product line without diverting from the core-product fix; will bring a go/no-go recommendation next quarter based on pilot data.
> **Ask of the board:** None this quarter beyond visibility; flagging that a fundraising conversation should start no later than month 9 of runway if the core-product recovery doesn't improve retention/expansion metrics by then.

## 3. Company-wide strategy communication (filled example, "why" included)

> **What:** For the next two quarters, engineering capacity is prioritized on core product roadmap recovery over new product exploration.
> **Why:** The core product is 2 quarters behind its own committed roadmap, and that's already cost us two paused enterprise deals worth $1.2M in combined ARR. A new product line is a real opportunity, but pursuing it at full scale right now would mean running two under-resourced priorities at once — we're choosing to fix the more concrete, already-quantified problem first.
> **What this means concretely:** the new-product-line request was scoped from 8 hires to a 2-person pilot; the redirected budget closes our known engineering gap on core.
> **What doesn't change:** support for the new product idea — we're testing it at a smaller scale in parallel, not shelving it.

## 4. Strategic-bet review template (filled example)

| Bet | Original success criteria | Review date | Actual result at review | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New product line pilot | 2-person team validates demand signal (e.g., 10+ qualified customer conversations, 1+ signed pilot customer) within one quarter | End of Q3 | 14 customer conversations, 1 signed pilot customer, but discovered a longer sales cycle (avg. 11 weeks) than assumed | Continue at current 2-person scale; do not scale to full 8-hire ask until sales-cycle assumption is re-tested with 2 more pilot customers |

**Rule applied:** a bet gets a decision (continue as-is, scale, or kill) at its planned review point based on evidence gathered since the last review — not an automatic continuation because reversing course would be uncomfortable to admit.
