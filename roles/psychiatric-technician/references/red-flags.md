# Red Flags

Smell tests for milieu practice, documentation, and restraint/seclusion use. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the check to run.

### Q15 checks charted for a time window staff can't account for (bathroom break, meal, handoff overlap)

- **Usually means:** the check didn't happen and was charted retrospectively from memory — the single most common finding in restraint-related sentinel event reviews.
- **First question:** "Walk me through exactly where you were standing when you observed this patient at that timestamp."
- **Data to pull:** unit camera footage (if available) or door-badge/key-fob logs for the timestamp in question, cross-referenced against the observation log.

### Restraint or seclusion episode running past the age-based order window without a new order or face-to-face

- **Usually means:** the clock wasn't tracked at initiation, or the ordering physician wasn't reachable and nobody escalated the gap.
- **First question:** "What time did this episode start, and when was the last face-to-face evaluation and order renewal?"
- **Data to pull:** the restraint/seclusion flowsheet initiation timestamp against the order renewal timestamp; facility incident log for any prior gaps this month.

### Fever >100.4°F, muscle rigidity, and autonomic instability (BP/HR swings, diaphoresis) within days of starting or increasing an antipsychotic

- **Usually means:** possible neuroleptic malignant syndrome (NMS) — rare, but has real mortality risk if treated as anxiety or "just agitated."
- **First question:** "When was the last antipsychotic dose or dose change, and has anyone taken a full vital-sign set in the last hour?"
- **Data to pull:** STAT vitals, medication administration record for the past 7 days, CK level if already ordered.

### Same patient in restraint or seclusion 3+ times within a 7-day span

- **Usually means:** either the underlying trigger (med regimen, roommate conflict, unaddressed trauma history) isn't being addressed between episodes, or the unit's de-escalation response is being skipped in favor of a faster physical one.
- **First question:** "What changed in the care plan after the first episode this week?"
- **Data to pull:** treatment team notes and care-plan updates dated between each restraint episode.

### New agitation or restlessness appearing 24–72 hours after an antipsychotic dose increase

- **Usually means:** drug-induced akathisia being misread as worsening psychosis, which risks the naive response (more antipsychotic) making it worse.
- **First question:** "Ask the patient directly — is this a subjective 'can't sit still' feeling, or are they responding to paranoid content/voices?"
- **Data to pull:** medication administration record timeline against symptom-onset timestamp; AIMS or a standardized akathisia screen if available.

### Brøset (or equivalent) score trending upward across 2+ consecutive shifts with no documented intervention

- **Usually means:** the scoring is being done as a compliance exercise, not acted on — the escalation-response ladder isn't actually being applied.
- **First question:** "At the score that crossed the threshold, what specifically changed about this patient's observation or engagement?"
- **Data to pull:** the scored checklist entries across shifts, matched against the observation-tier log for the same period.

### 1:1 observation order in place but staff assignment sheet shows the assigned tech covering 2+ other patients

- **Usually means:** staffing shortfall is silently downgrading a clinically-ordered observation level without anyone documenting the deviation or escalating it.
- **First question:** "Who is physically assigned to this patient right now, and what else are they covering?"
- **Data to pull:** the shift assignment sheet against the active observation orders for the unit.

### PRN medication given at or within 1 dose of the 24-hour maximum without RN/MD notification

- **Usually means:** the PRN is being used as a routine scheduled dose rather than an as-needed intervention, and the prescriber has lost visibility into how often it's actually being used.
- **First question:** "How many times has this PRN been given in the last 24 hours, and does the prescriber know the pattern?"
- **Data to pull:** medication administration record totals for the rolling 24-hour window, cross-checked against the standing order's stated max.

### Post-restraint debrief documented as a single line ("pt calm, no further issues") with no mention of what preceded the event

- **Usually means:** the debrief is being treated as a compliance box rather than the mechanism that prevents the next episode.
- **First question:** "What was the earliest point in this episode where a different response might have changed the outcome?"
- **Data to pull:** the incident timeline against the debrief note — do the two documents actually reference the same sequence of events?

### Elopement-risk patient found near an unlocked or propped-open unit door, or off the unit unescorted

- **Usually means:** a door-security or escort-protocol lapse, often during a shift change or a high-traffic period (meals, visiting hours, transport).
- **First question:** "What was the last time this patient was on eyes-on observation before being found here?"
- **Data to pull:** door-alarm/badge logs for the time window, staffing assignment at the time, elopement-risk flag status in the chart.

### Vital signs on a restraint/seclusion flowsheet that are identical across multiple consecutive entries

- **Usually means:** the vitals weren't actually retaken — they were copied forward from the prior entry.
- **First question:** "Can you show me the equipment you used to take this reading, and at what time?"
- **Data to pull:** device timestamp logs if the monitoring equipment records them; compare against the flowsheet timestamps.
