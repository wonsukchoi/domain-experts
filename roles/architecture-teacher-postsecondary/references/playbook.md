# Playbook — Filled Templates With Example Content

Real work products, filled in with plausible example numbers. Copy the structure, replace the content. The running example continues Student 11's Branchview Library Addition from SKILL.md's worked example, with two additional students from the same 16-person studio for contrast.

---

## 1. Weighted grading rubric — final review

Standard split for a design-studio final review: concept/iteration reasoning weighted higher than formal resolution, so a strong process with a rough finish doesn't automatically score below a polished finish with a thin process.

| Component | Weight | Student 11 (Branchview) | Student 4 (same studio) | Student 9 (same studio) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Concept/iteration reasoning | 40 pts | 36 — 6 documented iterations, each tied to a daylighting study | 22 — 2 iterations logged, final form unexplained relative to desk-crit record | 39 — 8 iterations, each with a stated design question answered |
| Constraint/code response | 35 pts | 18 — single-exit common path 20 ft over the 75 ft limit, hard-stop redesign required | 33 — code-compliant, minor accessible-route note | 12 — occupant load undercounted; actual load requires 2 exits, only 1 provided, plus a structural bay that doesn't align with the foundation plan |
| Formal resolution (execution) | 25 pts | 23 — clean drafting, clear material callouts | 24 — polished rendering, strong graphic execution | 19 — rougher model, but legible |
| **Total** | **100 pts** | **77** | **79** | **70** |

Reconciled: Student 4's polished formal execution (24/25) sits close to Student 11's (23/25), but Student 4's total lands within 2 points of Student 11's despite a materially weaker iteration record (22 vs. 36) — because the constraint component (code-compliant, 33/35) carries more weight than the 2-point formal gap. Student 9's low constraint score (12/35, two separate code/structural failures) pulls the total well below a strong iteration record alone would suggest — the rubric doesn't let concept strength offset an uncorrected hard-stop.

## 2. Egress / code pre-check worksheet (fill before final review, not during)

```
Project: Branchview Library Addition — Student 11
Occupancy classification: Group A-3 (library reading room), unsprinklered

Step 1 — Occupant load
  Net floor area (mezzanine): 1,400 sf
  Occupant load factor (library reading room): 50 net sf/occupant
  Occupant load = 1,400 / 50 = 28 occupants

Step 2 — Number of exits required
  Occupant load 28 is under the 49-occupant threshold that would
  automatically require 2 exits regardless of travel distance.
  → Single exit permissible ONLY if common path of travel is within limit.

Step 3 — Common path of travel
  Measured on plan (furthest occupiable point to the point where two
  independent paths become available): 95 ft
  Maximum permitted, single exit, unsprinklered Group A: 75 ft
  95 ft - 75 ft = 20 ft OVER → FAILS. Second means of egress required,
  or redesign to bring the common path under 75 ft.

Step 4 — Accessible route
  Ground floor: accessible route confirmed, ramp slope checked at 1:14
  (under the 1:12 maximum) — PASS.
  Mezzanine: served only by stair — no elevator or ramp shown. Flag for
  next review: either the mezzanine needs an accessible route, or the
  program has to confirm the mezzanine holds no occupiable program
  space requiring one.

Result: 1 hard-stop item (common path), 1 open flag (mezzanine access).
Both go in the critique note as required items, not aesthetic notes.
```

## 3. Precedent currency-check worksheet

Used before finalizing a studio brief's precedent list, or before a student cites a precedent as justification for a design move.

```
Precedent cited: "Riverside Branch Library" (hypothetical), completed 2011
Cited for: daylighting strategy via clerestory glazing

Currency check:
[x] Construction/material practice — clerestory glazing spec from 2011
    predates the energy-code glazing U-value tightening; current code
    would require a different glazing assembly to hit the same target.
    NOTE THIS to the student — the strategy transfers, the spec doesn't.
[x] Code edition — precedent designed under an earlier code cycle;
    confirm current adopted edition's egress and energy provisions
    before treating any dimension in the precedent as a benchmark.
[ ] Typological relevance — still directly relevant (branch library
    program hasn't shifted materially); no flag needed here.

Verdict: usable as a formal/conceptual precedent, NOT as a technical
spec source without updating the glazing assembly to current code.
Brief language updated to say so explicitly, rather than presenting
the precedent's construction detail as still current.
```

## 4. Desk-crit iteration log (excerpt, Student 11, weeks 3–8)

```
Week 3: Initial massing — single flat roof, reading room oriented
        south. Daylighting concern raised: south glare on reading
        surfaces, no shading strategy shown.
Week 4: Added a fixed horizontal brise-soleil. Improves glare but
        adds cost the budget line doesn't support — instructor flags
        as a program/budget conflict, not a design flaw.
Week 5: Pivoted to a folded roof plane admitting north light instead
        of south — this is the pivot that shows up in the final design.
        Reasoning documented: north light avoids direct glare without
        a shading device, trades some south-facing view for daylight
        quality. Iteration explicitly tied to the week 3 concern.
Week 6: Roof form refined structurally — bay spacing checked against
        a 24 ft clear span the studio's structural consultant confirmed
        as reasonable for the proposed glulam system.
Week 7: Mezzanine added to the program; egress not yet re-checked
        against the added occupant load — FLAGGED at desk crit, but
        not resolved before final review (this is the gap that
        surfaces as the common-path failure at final review).
Week 8: Final drafting and rendering pass; egress flag from week 7
        was not returned to.
```

The log is what makes the final grade defensible on appeal: the iteration credit (36/40) traces to weeks 3–6, and the constraint deduction traces to a flag raised at week 7 that went unresolved — not a surprise sprung at final review.

## 5. Split-jury reconciliation note

Used when outside jurors disagree sharply on the same project.

```
Project: Student 9, final review
Juror A score: 62/100 — cites unresolved structural bay + occupant
  load undercount (matches the studio's own constraint pre-check).
Juror B score: 84/100 — cites strong model craft and "confident"
  formal language, does not mention structural/occupant-load issues.

Reconciliation: Juror A's score traces to the brief's stated fixed
requirements (code, structural). Juror B's score traces primarily to
formal/craft criteria the brief treated as open, not fixed. These are
not the same axis and should not be averaged as if they were.
Recorded grade uses the constraint pre-check (matches Juror A) as the
binding component, with Juror B's formal-craft comments folded into
the formal-resolution sub-score only.
```
