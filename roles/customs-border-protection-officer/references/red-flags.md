# Customs and Border Protection Officer — Red Flags

### Declared value within 10% (and under) of a reporting or duty-exemption threshold
- **Usually means:** Deliberate under-declaration to avoid formal-entry review or a simplification-lane requirement; second most likely is a legitimately low-margin shipment that happens to land near a round number.
- **First question:** Does this importer/traveler have other entries showing the same near-threshold pattern?
- **Data to pull:** 90-day entry/crossing history for this importer/traveler ID, sorted by declared value against the relevant threshold.

### Goods description generic or vague relative to declared quantity or value
- **Usually means:** Either genuine shorthand from a busy shipper, or deliberate vagueness to obscure contents; the two are indistinguishable from the manifest alone.
- **First question:** Does the description match the HTS classification code filed for this entry?
- **Data to pull:** Manifest line-item detail and the filed classification code; compare to typical description specificity for that HTS chapter.

### Traveler's account of purpose-of-travel or relationship-to-goods changes between two differently-phrased questions
- **Usually means:** Rehearsed but incomplete cover story; second most likely is a genuinely nervous traveler being imprecise about incidental details.
- **First question:** Is the inconsistency about a core fact (why they're traveling, who packed the bag) or an incidental one (exact dates, minor details)?
- **Data to pull:** None beyond the interview itself — this is a behavioral indicator, escalate to instrument-based verification (X-ray, document check) rather than deciding on the interview alone.

### Import/crossing pattern shows 3+ instances clustering just under the same threshold across a review window
- **Usually means:** Structuring — deliberately splitting or scaling transactions to stay under a threshold that triggers additional scrutiny.
- **First question:** Are these transactions using different carriers, routes, or filers to avoid the pattern being visible in any single system view?
- **Data to pull:** Full transaction history for the importer/traveler ID across all available carriers/filers, not just the current shipment's carrier.

### X-ray or imaging shows density/mass inconsistent with the declared contents
- **Usually means:** Concealed contraband, undeclared goods, or a straightforward misdescription; rarely a false positive once a trained operator confirms the anomaly.
- **First question:** Does the anomaly's shape/location match a known concealment method (false compartment, body cavity, product substitution)?
- **Data to pull:** Prior imaging results for similar concealment methods at this port, if a database exists, to compare signature.

### Watchlist or database hit on identity, entity, or prior-crossing history
- **Usually means:** Ranges from an exact-match legitimate hit to a name-similarity false positive; severity determines the tier.
- **First question:** Is this a biometric/exact-identifier match or a name/demographic-similarity match?
- **Data to pull:** Full watchlist record detail (not just the alert flag) and biometric confirmation if available before treating as a confirmed hit.

### Documents pass visual inspection but fail forensic/UV authentication check
- **Usually means:** Counterfeit or altered travel/entry document; near-certain positive once forensic check confirms, unlike behavioral indicators.
- **First question:** Which specific security feature failed, and is that consistent with a known counterfeit-document pattern?
- **Data to pull:** Document forensics unit's current list of known counterfeit signatures for this document type/issuing country.

### Cash or monetary instruments carried at or just under the currency-reporting threshold
- **Usually means:** Deliberate structuring to avoid the reporting requirement; second most likely is genuine unfamiliarity with the reporting rule among infrequent international travelers.
- **First question:** Can the traveler explain the source and intended use of the funds coherently and consistently?
- **Data to pull:** Currency-reporting form if filed, and cross-check against any prior crossings by the same traveler with similar amounts.

### Importer/filer has a prior penalty or seizure history not disclosed or flagged at primary
- **Usually means:** Either a data-linkage gap (systems not cross-referencing) or the importer is relying on primary officers not catching the history; both warrant secondary.
- **First question:** Was the prior penalty/seizure for a related violation type (valuation, classification, contraband) or unrelated (e.g., a paperwork timing issue)?
- **Data to pull:** Full penalty/seizure case history under this importer's filer ID, including resolved/closed cases.

### High-value shipment routed through a lower-scrutiny lane or port typically used for low-value/low-risk traffic
- **Usually means:** Deliberate lane-shopping to reduce inspection probability; second most likely is a genuine logistics/routing decision unrelated to risk avoidance.
- **First question:** Is this importer's typical routing pattern consistent with this shipment, or is this an outlier route for them specifically?
- **Data to pull:** Importer's historical routing/port-of-entry pattern over the past 6–12 months.
