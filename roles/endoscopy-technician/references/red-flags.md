# Red Flags

Smell tests for reprocessing, equipment, and specimen handling. Format per flag: most likely cause, the first question a veteran asks, and the specific data to pull.

### MEC test strip reads below threshold, or borderline, on a reusable disinfectant/sterilant

- **Usually means:** the basin is at or past its labeled reuse-life expiration, or was under-activated/diluted at the start — either way, every load run since the last confirmed-passing test is now unverified, not just the current one.
- **First question:** "How many loads, and which scope IDs, have run on this basin since the last passing test?"
- **Data to pull:** the AER or manual cycle log for the basin, cross-referenced against the scope traceability system.

### Leak test fails (bubbles, pressure drop, or a "fail" readout)

- **Usually means:** a channel or sheath breach — could be from normal wear, a bite-block failure, or rough handling during the case.
- **First question:** "Did this scope touch the disinfectant basin before or after the leak test?"
- **Data to pull:** the leak-test record (automated printout or manual log) and the scope's prior repair history for recurrence.

### Point-of-use-to-cleaning delay exceeds the manufacturer's validated window (commonly 60 minutes)

- **Usually means:** case volume or turnover pressure pushed the scope past the window the standard cleaning cycle was validated against.
- **First question:** "What time did POU treatment finish, and what time did manual cleaning actually start?"
- **Data to pull:** the POU completion timestamp vs. the manual-cleaning start timestamp on the traceability log.

### Borescope inspection finds channel damage or retained debris after a repeat manual clean

- **Usually means:** normal wear from repeated manual cleaning and reprocessing cycles, or (less often) an already-known leak or rough handling event that was never logged.
- **First question:** "Does this breach the lumen or hold debris after a repeat clean, or is it surface-only?"
- **Data to pull:** the borescope image/video log and the scope's cumulative reprocessing-cycle count.

### AER cycle aborts mid-run (power loss, door seal fault, chemical delivery error)

- **Usually means:** an equipment fault on the reprocessor itself, not a problem with the scope — but the scope's disinfection status is now unverified regardless of cause.
- **First question:** "Are we restarting this scope's cycle from the beginning, or is someone tempted to resume from where it stopped?"
- **Data to pull:** the AER's fault log/error code and the scope's cycle history for the day.

### Scope's hang time in the drying cabinet exceeds the facility's validated storage-life limit (commonly 7–14 days)

- **Usually means:** low turnover on a less-used scope model, or a scheduling gap, rather than any defect in the scope itself.
- **First question:** "When was this scope last reprocessed, and is it past our storage-life limit right now?"
- **Data to pull:** the storage/hang-time log and facility policy's specific limit for that scope class.

### Repeated repair visits for the same scope within a short window (e.g., 3+ repairs in 90 days)

- **Usually means:** either a genuine recurring device defect or a handling/technique issue upstream in the room or during manual cleaning that keeps re-damaging the scope.
- **First question:** "Is this the same failure mode each time, or different ones?"
- **Data to pull:** the repair vendor's service history and the borescope findings log across those visits.

### Patient SpO2 drops below 90% during moderate sedation

- **Usually means:** sedation-related respiratory depression or airway obstruction, more likely with combined opioid/benzodiazepine dosing or an unrecognized difficult airway.
- **First question:** "Is supplemental oxygen already running, and at what rate?"
- **Data to pull:** the sedation/monitoring record (SpO2, BP, HR trend) and total sedation medication doses given.

### Biopsy or specimen container without an immediate, site-specific label

- **Usually means:** labeling got deferred past the handoff moment on a multi-specimen case, where several biopsies from different sites are easy to conflate under time pressure.
- **First question:** "Which anatomic site does this specimen match in the procedure note, and who confirmed it before the next one was taken?"
- **Data to pull:** the specimen log timestamps against the procedure note's biopsy sequence.

### Irrigation water bottle not changed or reprocessed per the facility's defined interval (commonly every case or every shift, per policy)

- **Usually means:** the bottle itself has become a contamination source independent of the scope's own reprocessing status — irrigation water bottles are a documented, separate infection-control point.
- **First question:** "When was this bottle last changed, and is that within policy?"
- **Data to pull:** the water-bottle changeout log for the room/shift.

### CO2 insufflator canister low or wrong gas source connected

- **Usually means:** a routine supply-management miss, but it directly affects the procedure (patient comfort, insufflation adequacy) and isn't a "notice it later" item.
- **First question:** "Is the correct gas connected and at adequate supply for this case's expected length?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the moment — verify and correct before the patient is positioned, not mid-procedure.
