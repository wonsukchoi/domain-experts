# Red Flags

Smell tests across teaching, research, and service. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Evaluation response rate under ~50% on a term with an outlier score

- **Usually means:** the respondents are a self-selected subgroup, not a sample of the class — a strong or weak swing is often nonresponse bias, not a class-wide reaction.
- **First question:** "What's the response rate, and does the drop concentrate in one item or spread across all of them?"
- **Data to pull:** response rate, item-level breakdown vs. department mean, free-text comment count and what fraction cluster on the same low-scoring item.

### Grade distribution mean more than ~0.3 GPA points above the department's course-level norm

- **Usually means:** grading standard has drifted from the rubric, or the rubric itself has crept looser term over term (Rojstaczer & Healy document a multi-decade national grade-inflation trend, roughly 2.5 to 3.1 average GPA from the 1960s to the 2000s, driven partly by rubric drift, not just ability changes).
- **First question:** "Would a paper scoring this grade last year score the same grade against the same rubric today?"
- **Data to pull:** grade distribution histogram for the current term vs. the same course's prior three terms; a re-grade of five prior-term papers against the current rubric.

### Syllabus reading list with zero opposing source on a classified-open question

- **Usually means:** the "best practice" balance standard wasn't applied, which is the specific exposure that turns a classroom complaint into a sustained grievance.
- **First question:** "For this specific open question, what's the second defensible position, and where's its reading?"
- **Data to pull:** the reading list for that unit, sorted by which side of the open question each source represents.

### A bias-response or Title IX-adjacent complaint naming a specific class session

- **Usually means:** either a genuine even-handedness gap, or a settled-empirical-question unit was mistaken for an open-normative one by the complainant — both are possible and the response differs.
- **First question:** "Was the specific claim in that session settled empirical content, open normative content, or a personal aside outside either?"
- **Data to pull:** the lecture slides/notes for that session, the syllabus classification of the topic, any contemporaneous student questions logged.

### Manuscript under review with no editor communication past the journal's stated timeline

- **Usually means:** it's stuck with a slow or non-responsive reviewer, not necessarily a bad sign about the paper.
- **First question:** "Has it passed the journal's own stated median time-to-decision, and by how much?"
- **Data to pull:** submission date, journal's published median decision time, a polite status query sent and dated.

### Pre-tenure faculty member on 3+ committees in a single year

- **Usually means:** the writing block is being displaced by visible, low-payoff work — the single most common route to a weak third-year review despite strong teaching.
- **First question:** "Which of these were chair-assigned versus self-selected, and how many hours per week does the total consume?"
- **Data to pull:** committee log with hours, calendar audit of a representative two-week span.

### Citation count cited alone, without a disciplinary comparator, in a tenure or hiring file

- **Usually means:** the number is being used to imply a quality judgment it can't support on its own — subfields within political science have very different baseline citation rates and time-to-citation curves.
- **First question:** "What's the citation count for a comparable cohort in the same subfield at the same career stage?"
- **Data to pull:** citation counts for 3–5 recent tenure cases in the same subfield, matched on years since PhD.

### IRB continuing-review date passed with data collection ongoing

- **Usually means:** the protocol lapsed, and data collected after the lapse is typically unusable without a reportable deviation.
- **First question:** "What was the approved end date on the current protocol, and has any participant been recruited after it?"
- **Data to pull:** IRB approval letter with dates, recruitment log with dates.

### A "controversial topic" gen-ed section repeatedly staffed by the newest adjunct or visiting faculty

- **Usually means:** the department is quietly offloading complaint risk onto the faculty member with the least job security and the least standing to push back on an unfair complaint.
- **First question:** "Who taught this specific section for the last three terms, and what was their appointment type each time?"
- **Data to pull:** section staffing history by instructor appointment type over 3+ terms.

### Grad TA grading without a norming session on a shared rubric

- **Usually means:** inter-rater inconsistency large enough to generate grade disputes that are really a grading-process problem, not a specific paper's problem.
- **First question:** "Did the TAs score the same sample papers before grading independently, and how much did their scores vary?"
- **Data to pull:** norming-session sample-paper scores by TA, or absence of a norming session at all.
