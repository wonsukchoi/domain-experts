# Red flags

Smell tests a genetics-literate breeder catches before a mating is finalized or a season's records are filed. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull.

### EPD accuracy below 0.30 on a heavily-marketed young sire
- **Usually means:** the catalog number is a parent average or a genomic-only estimate wearing a headline number, with little to no progeny data behind it yet.
- **First question:** "How many progeny, across how many herds, back this accuracy value — and is it genomic-only or blended with progeny data?"
- **Data to pull:** the EPD run's accuracy-source flag (parent average / genomic-enhanced / progeny-proven) and the underlying progeny/genotype count from the breed association's evaluation report.

### Predicted inbreeding coefficient above 12.5% on a proposed mating
- **Usually means:** the dam is a daughter or granddaughter of the candidate sire's close relative (full brother, sire himself, or common grandsire), often from earlier AI-sire use that wasn't cross-checked against the current catalog pick.
- **First question:** "Pull the dam's 4-generation pedigree — what's her relationship to this sire through every common ancestor?"
- **Data to pull:** full pedigree/numerator relationship matrix (mate-allocation software output) for the sire against every eligible dam, not just the ones flagged by memory.

### First-service FTAI conception rate below 45% on a batch
- **Usually means:** protocol compliance failure (an injection or CIDR removal outside its labeled window) or a body-condition/nutrition issue at synchronization start, not sire or semen quality.
- **First question:** "Was every injection and the fixed-time AI given inside its labeled window, timestamped?"
- **Data to pull:** the treatment log with actual times against the protocol's target windows; body condition scores taken at day 0 of the protocol.

### A GE-EPD's accuracy value quoted as if it equals a progeny-proven sire's
- **Usually means:** someone is reading the accuracy number without checking the accuracy-source flag, treating a genomic boost as equivalent to real calves on the ground.
- **First question:** "Is this accuracy genomic-only, or does it include progeny records — and how many?"
- **Data to pull:** the evaluation's accuracy-source breakdown; if genomic-only, compare against the breed association's published genomic-accuracy-equivalent (typically 10–15 progeny reports' worth).

### Herd-average marbling or growth EPD climbing while birth weight or calving-ease EPD moves the wrong direction
- **Usually means:** an unmanaged correlated-trait antagonism — selection pressure on the carcass/growth side is dragging maternal or calving traits along with it.
- **First question:** "What's the herd's multi-trait EPD trend over the last three sire generations, not just this year's average?"
- **Data to pull:** a multi-trait trend report (all relevant EPDs, not the one being marketed) across the last three sire-selection cycles.

### Registry rejects a submitted pedigree or DNA-parentage test
- **Usually means:** a cleanup or backup bull was present during the AI breeding window and sired the calf instead, or a recording error occurred at breeding or calving.
- **First question:** "Was a cleanup bull turned out with this cow group during or shortly after the AI window?"
- **Data to pull:** the DNA parent-verification report, the AI/breeding log, and the cleanup-bull turnout dates for that pasture group.

### Replacement heifer retention running above ~25–30% of the calf crop for multiple years with no stated reason
- **Usually means:** selection intensity on the female side has eroded — heifers are being kept to fill numbers rather than ranked and culled against an index, diluting genetic progress.
- **First question:** "What's the target herd size and cull rate this retention number is solving for?"
- **Data to pull:** replacement-heifer count against the herd's stated cull rate and target herd size for the last 3 years; index ranking of retained heifers versus the ones marketed instead.

### Cost per AI pregnancy exceeding natural-service cost per pregnancy by a wide margin with no index-value justification documented
- **Usually means:** the AI premium was paid out of habit or catalog excitement rather than a calculated index-value gap over the herd's current herd sire.
- **First question:** "What's the $-index gap between this AI sire and our current herd-sire average, times the expected pregnancies — does it clear the incremental AI spend?"
- **Data to pull:** cost-per-pregnancy calculation for the batch (semen, synchronization, labor ÷ expected conceptions) against the sire's index premium per calf.

### Heavy reliance on a small handful of popular AI sires herd-wide or breed-wide
- **Usually means:** "popular sire syndrome" — short-term genetic gain at the cost of narrowing the effective population size, a breed-level risk that compounds across many buyers making the same catalog-leader choice.
- **First question:** "What share of this herd's current cow inventory traces to this sire or his close relatives within two generations?"
- **Data to pull:** sire-usage history across the herd's last 3–4 breeding seasons; if available, the breed association's published effective-population-size trend.

### Synchronization protocol showing a wide spread of standing heat instead of a tight window around the expected fixed-time AI point
- **Usually means:** CIDR retention failure (a device that fell out early) or a mistimed PGF2α/GnRH injection, not an issue with the cows' underlying fertility.
- **First question:** "Did every CIDR stay in for the full 7 days, and was the day-7 prostaglandin injection given within an hour of schedule across the group?"
- **Data to pull:** CIDR retention checks at removal, injection timestamps against protocol targets, and the spread of standing-heat onset times relative to the fixed-time AI slot.
