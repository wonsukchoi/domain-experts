# Driver/Sales Worker — Red Flags

### Stale rate on an account exceeding roughly 1.5x its own trailing 8-week average
- **Usually means:** standing order/par level wasn't resized after a facing cut, seasonal demand shift, or store remodel, less commonly an actual demand decline
- **First question:** did shelf capacity or facing count change on this account in the last order cycle, and was the standing order recalculated against it?
- **Data to pull:** account's trailing 8-week stale-rate trend, facing count history, last three order-quantity entries

### Share of category-$ exceeding share of shelf by more than roughly 5–8 points
- **Usually means:** the account hasn't reset the planogram to reflect actual sell-through, or the rep hasn't brought scan data to the last facing conversation
- **First question:** when was the category last reset, and has the rep presented current share-of-shelf vs. share-of-$ data since then?
- **Data to pull:** current planogram facing count, trailing scan-data category report, date of last reset

### Facing count reduced without a corresponding order/par adjustment
- **Usually means:** the rep updated the shelf plan but left the standing order at the old par level, or vice versa
- **First question:** does the current order quantity match the shelf's physical capacity at the new facing count?
- **Data to pull:** current planogram facing count, last order quantity, case-pack size for the SKU

### Revenue-per-stop below roughly 3x the fully loaded cost-per-stop for two consecutive periodic reviews
- **Usually means:** the account has shrunk below the point that justifies its current visit frequency, or the route was built around a since-departed high-volume neighbor account
- **First question:** has this account's order size trended down over the last several cycles, or was it always marginal?
- **Data to pull:** route settlement report showing per-stop revenue trend, route time-and-mileage log for the stop

### Same SKU generating repeat return/credit disputes at one account
- **Usually means:** a placement or rotation issue (wrong shelf position, poor date-code visibility) rather than a product-quality problem
- **First question:** where on the shelf is this SKU actually positioned, and is the store's own stocking staff rotating stock front-to-back correctly?
- **Data to pull:** shelf photo or planogram position, code-date rotation log, prior credit history for this SKU at this account

### A competitor SKU newly occupying a facing that used to be the rep's
- **Usually means:** a planogram reset happened without the rep's data-backed pushback, or a chronic stockout on the rep's SKU gave the buyer a reason to reallocate
- **First question:** was there a stockout or fill-rate problem on this SKU before the facing was reallocated?
- **Data to pull:** order/fill-rate history for the SKU over the prior quarter, reset date and who was present for it

### Account requesting off-cycle or emergency drops more than once in a month
- **Usually means:** the standing order or visit frequency is miscalibrated to actual demand, less commonly a one-off event (local promotion, competitor stockout driving traffic)
- **First question:** is this a recurring pattern at this account or a specific, explainable event?
- **Data to pull:** order-vs-actual-sell-through history, frequency log of off-cycle requests over the last quarter

### Order quantity unchanged for six or more consecutive visits despite a facing or seasonal change
- **Usually means:** the rep is carrying forward a par level by habit rather than recalculating it each cycle
- **First question:** what was the basis for the current order quantity — this visit's sell-through check, or the number from last cycle?
- **Data to pull:** order-quantity history, sell-through trend, facing-count history over the same window

### Chronic stockout (out-of-stock at more than roughly 10% of visits) on a top-quartile-velocity SKU
- **Usually means:** the order is being sized to average demand rather than to the shelf's actual absorption capacity or a documented demand spike (local event, promotion)
- **First question:** is the stockout tied to a specific recurring day/time in the cycle, and does the order need a mid-cycle top-off rather than a one-time increase?
- **Data to pull:** out-of-stock log by visit, sell-through-by-day pattern if available, planogram capacity for the facing

### Fill-rate (delivered vs. ordered) below roughly 95% on a repeat basis for one account
- **Usually means:** a supply-side shortage upstream, not a rep-side ordering error — but it reads to the account like the rep's problem regardless
- **First question:** is the shortfall happening at the warehouse/load-out level or in what the rep is actually ordering?
- **Data to pull:** warehouse allocation/short-ship report, rep's order submission for the affected cycle

### Route settlement showing declining case volume across multiple accounts with no single explained cause
- **Usually means:** a structural shift (chain-wide reset, a new competitor route, a store closure pattern) rather than several unrelated account-level problems
- **First question:** do the declining accounts share a common chain, buyer, or geography?
- **Data to pull:** route settlement trend by account over the last quarter, chain/buyer grouping across affected accounts

### Rep consistently running significantly over or under scheduled route time for the same stop sequence
- **Usually means:** stop count or service-time-per-stop no longer matches the route's original design (new accounts added without resequencing, or an account's order-writing/facing-negotiation time has grown)
- **First question:** has a stop been added to or dropped from this sequence since the route was last resequenced?
- **Data to pull:** planned vs. actual time-per-stop log, route stop list history
