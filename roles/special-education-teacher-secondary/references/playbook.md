# Playbook

## Transition-goal drafting worksheet

Fill before finalizing any transition IEP annual review.

| Field | Vague draft (reject) | Measurable rewrite (use) |
|---|---|---|
| Employment goal | "Student will explore career options." | "By [date], student will complete a 40-hour unpaid work experience at [named site], rated proficient on 4/5 workplace-readiness competencies on [named rubric]." |
| Education/training goal | "Student will consider college." | "By [date], student will complete an application and a documented campus/admissions-requirements visit to [named institution/program]." |
| Independent living goal (if applicable) | "Student will increase independence." | "By [date], student will independently complete [named task, e.g., public-transit route to work site] on 4 of 5 trials without prompt." |
| Services tied to goal | (none named) | List each: course, agency referral (e.g., Vocational Rehabilitation), community experience, named provider, hours/week. |
| Evidence source | (none) | Cite the specific vocational assessment, interview, or work-sample data less than 2 years old that the goal is built from. |
| Student's own words (quote directly) | (paraphrased by team) | Direct quote from student interview, dated. |

**Rule:** if a goal can't be checked off as done/not-done by an outside reader with no context, it is not measurable — rewrite before the meeting, not during it.

## Diploma-track consequence table

Populate per state framework before presenting options to a family. Values below are illustrative structure, not a specific state's current rules — always verify against the state education agency's current framework.

| Diploma option | Four-year college eligibility | Two-year/community college eligibility | Trade licensure eligibility | Typical GPA/credit requirement |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard diploma | Yes, if course rigor requirements met | Yes | Yes, in most trades | Full state credit count (e.g., 24 credits) at passing grade |
| Modified/alternate diploma | Rarely — most 4-year institutions do not accept | Varies by program — often excluded from selective programs (nursing, vet-tech) | Often excluded — many licensing boards require standard diploma or equivalency | Reduced credit count, modified content standards |
| Certificate of completion | No | No | No, typically requires separate GED/equivalency first | Attendance-based, no standard credit requirement |

**Rule:** before recommending a track, name the specific program or goal the family has stated (e.g., "vet-tech program requires standard diploma") and check it against this table's actual gate — never present the track choice as a general risk-reduction default without naming what it forecloses.

## Instructional-hours allocation worksheet

Use when academic remediation and vocational/transition goals compete for the same weekly schedule, starting from the total periods/week available for IEP-supported instruction.

| Goal category | Requested hours/week (naive) | Adjusted hours/week (after tradeoff) | Justification for adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Academic remediation (e.g., reading intervention) | 2 | 3 | Directly gates required credit (e.g., English course pass rate) tied to diploma track. |
| Vocational/work-experience goal | 2 | 1 + summer block | Goal-relevant but not gating a graduation requirement this semester; summer block preserves total exposure. |
| Total weekly allocation | 4 | 4 | Total budget held constant — tradeoff is reallocation, not addition. |

**Rule:** always show the naive (unconstrained) request and the adjusted allocation side by side in the IEP file, with a one-line justification for which goal drew more hours — an IEP that lists both goals at full naive hours without reconciling the total is not an honest plan.

## Accommodation-to-testing-agency crosswalk

Complete at least 8 weeks before the earliest planned standardized/college-entrance test date.

| IEP classroom accommodation | College Board SSD equivalent request | ACT equivalent request | Documentation needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.5x extended time | Extended time (1.5x) | Extended time (time-and-a-half) | Current IEP + evaluation data showing pattern of need, not just diagnosis |
| Small-group/reduced-distraction setting | Small group testing | Test alone/small group | IEP or 504 documenting current use in classroom testing |
| Read-aloud for math (not reading comprehension sections) | Reader (human or text-to-speech) for eligible sections only | Reader accommodation | Documentation that the accommodation is content-restricted where required by the agency's own rules |
| Use of calculator beyond standard sections | Calculator accommodation | Calculator accommodation | Confirm agency's own approved-calculator list, which may differ from classroom-approved list |

**Rule:** submit the agency request the moment eligibility is confirmed, not when the student registers for the test — approval windows commonly run 7+ weeks, and a late request is the most common cause of a denied or delayed accommodation on test day.
