# Red flags

### A transition goal with no named setting, skill, or measurement window ("student will explore careers")
- **Usually means:** the goal was written to fill the compliance field, not built from actual assessment data or the student's stated preference.
- **First question:** can this goal be checked off as done/not-done by someone with no context on the case?
- **Data to pull:** the vocational assessment or student interview the goal claims to be based on, and its date.

### A diploma-track recommendation made without a documented conversation of its specific downstream consequence
- **Usually means:** the family is being steered toward a default administrative choice rather than an informed decision about a largely irreversible track.
- **First question:** has the family been shown, in writing, what this specific track does to their stated postsecondary goal (college eligibility, licensure)?
- **Data to pull:** the diploma-track consequence documentation and the family's signed acknowledgment.

### Both an academic remediation goal and a vocational goal listed at full, unconstrained hours in the same IEP
- **Usually means:** the plan hasn't actually reconciled a fixed instructional-hours budget — one or both goals will quietly get less time than written.
- **First question:** does the total hours committed across all goals match the actual available instructional periods per week?
- **Data to pull:** the student's master schedule and the hours-allocation worksheet.

### A vocational or transition assessment more than 2 years old for a student within 2 years of exit
- **Usually means:** the transition plan may be built on stale interest or labor-market data that no longer reflects the student's actual goal.
- **First question:** when was the most recent transition assessment, and does its content still match what the student says they want now?
- **Data to pull:** the assessment date and the most recent student interview.

### A standardized-test accommodation request submitted less than 7 weeks before the test date
- **Usually means:** the agency (College Board SSD, ACT) approval window has likely been missed, risking denial or delay on test day.
- **First question:** has the accommodation request already been submitted to the testing agency, separate from the IEP itself?
- **Data to pull:** the SSD/ACT request submission date and confirmation status.

### A classroom accommodation assumed to transfer automatically to a college-entrance exam without agency confirmation
- **Usually means:** the accommodation may be denied on test day because IEP accommodations do not automatically carry over to third-party testing agencies.
- **First question:** has this specific accommodation been separately approved by the testing agency, or only documented in the IEP?
- **Data to pull:** the testing agency's approval letter or portal status.

### A student near or past age of majority whose stated goal differs from the team's recommendation, with no documented rationale for the override
- **Usually means:** the team may be substituting its own judgment for a student who now holds meaningful legal standing over the decision, without a defensible basis for doing so.
- **First question:** is the student's own stated goal documented in the IEP, and if the team recommended differently, why?
- **Data to pull:** the student interview record and the IEP's stated rationale section.

### English/math credit progress behind the pace needed to meet a graduation requirement with fewer than 3 semesters remaining
- **Usually means:** the standard-diploma path may be at real risk without an immediate, targeted intervention change — not just continued generic remediation.
- **First question:** at current credit-completion pace, will the student reach the required credit count and GPA before the projected exit date?
- **Data to pull:** current transcript, credits completed vs. required, and GPA in required courses.

### A transition IEP with no named outside-agency referral (Vocational Rehabilitation, workforce board) despite a stated employment or training goal
- **Usually means:** the plan may be relying entirely on school-based services that end at graduation, with no bridge to adult-service funding or support.
- **First question:** has a referral to the relevant outside adult-service agency been made and documented, with enough lead time before exit?
- **Data to pull:** the referral date and the outside agency's response/eligibility status.
