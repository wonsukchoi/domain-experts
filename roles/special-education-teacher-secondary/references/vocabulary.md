# Vocabulary

### Measurable postsecondary goal
A transition-IEP statement of what a student will do after exit in employment, education/training, and (if applicable) independent living, written so a specific outcome can be checked as met or not met.
**In use:** "That goal isn't measurable yet — 'explore careers' doesn't tell me what we're checking for in June."
**Common misuse:** Writing the goal as a category of interest rather than a checkable target — a goal that can't be verified as done or not done by an outside reader fails compliance review and gives the student no actual target to work toward.

### Transition services
The coordinated set of activities, courses, community experiences, and agency linkages designed to move a student from school to a specific named postsecondary outcome, required in the IEP once a student reaches the state's transition-planning age (often 16, sometimes younger by state rule).
**In use:** "The transition services section needs to name the actual work-experience placement, not just say 'vocational support.'"
**Common misuse:** Treating transition services as a single generic line item ("career counseling") rather than a specific, individualized list of services tied to the student's own named goal.

### Diploma track (standard / modified-alternate / certificate of completion)
The category of exit credential a student is working toward, which determines eligibility for four-year college admission, many two-year program admissions, and various forms of trade licensure, largely independent of the student's actual demonstrated skill.
**In use:** "Before we recommend the alternate track, we need to show the family exactly what it closes off for the vet-tech program."
**Common misuse:** Treating the diploma-track decision as a routine administrative default based on current grades alone, without walking the family through the specific, often irreversible eligibility consequence tied to their stated goal.

### Age of majority
The age (18 in most states) at which educational decision-making rights transfer from parent/guardian to the student, absent a guardianship or other legal arrangement — a shift that affects who consents to the IEP and who the team is now legally required to center.
**In use:** "She turns 18 in March — we need the transfer-of-rights notice on file before then."
**Common misuse:** Continuing to route all decisions through the parent by habit after a student has reached age of majority, without documenting the legal transfer or the student's own stated position.

### Present Levels of Academic Achievement and Functional Performance (PLAAFP)
The IEP section documenting a student's current, data-based performance across academic and functional domains, which every goal in the IEP is required to trace back to.
**In use:** "This goal doesn't connect to anything in the PLAAFP — where's the data showing she's at this level?"
**Common misuse:** Treating PLAAFP as a formality to fill in after the goals are already written, rather than the evidentiary basis the goals are supposed to be derived from — a goal untethered from PLAAFP data is the first thing flagged in a due-process review.

### Services for Students with Disabilities (SSD) / testing agency accommodation approval
The College Board's (and analogously ACT's) separate review and approval process for accommodations on their exams, distinct from and not automatically granted by an IEP or 504 plan.
**In use:** "Her extended time is solid in the IEP, but SSD hasn't approved it yet for the SAT — that's a separate submission."
**Common misuse:** Assuming an IEP accommodation transfers automatically to the SAT or ACT — the testing agency has its own documentation standard, timeline (often 7+ weeks), and can deny a request the school considers well-established.

### Least restrictive environment (LRE) at the secondary level
The IDEA requirement that a student be educated with non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, applied at the secondary level to course placement, credit-bearing general-education access, and work-based learning settings.
**In use:** "We should look at whether this work placement can happen alongside typically-developing peers before defaulting to a segregated site."
**Common misuse:** Treating LRE as only a K-8 concept once a student reaches high school, missing that it still governs decisions like segregated vocational placements versus integrated community work sites.

### Indicator 13 (transition compliance checklist)
The federal IDEA reporting indicator (from the National Technical Assistance Center on Transition) states use to audit whether a transition IEP contains all legally required elements — measurable goal, age-appropriate assessment basis, and named transition services.
**In use:** "Before we finalize, run it against the Indicator 13 checklist so we're not missing an element on state review."
**Common misuse:** Treating Indicator 13 compliance as equivalent to a good transition plan — a plan can technically contain every required element while still using a vague, low-value goal statement that would not survive an actual outcome review.

### Certificate of completion
A non-diploma exit document awarded to a student who completes their IEP-defined program but does not meet standard diploma requirements, distinct from a modified/alternate diploma in that it typically carries no recognized credential value for postsecondary admission or licensure.
**In use:** "A certificate of completion isn't the same as even the modified diploma — she'd need a GED afterward for most programs to even consider her."
**Common misuse:** Treating a certificate of completion as a diploma-equivalent "safety option," when in most states and for most postsecondary/licensure purposes it functions closer to no credential at all.
