# Microbiologist — Vocabulary

### Presumptive identification
An identification based on preliminary characteristics (colony morphology, Gram stain, a rapid biochemical spot test) that narrows but does not confirm species identity.
**In use:** "We have a presumptive coagulase-negative *Staphylococcus* — MALDI-TOF is running now to confirm."
**Common misuse:** treated as a final identification and acted on (batch disposition, clinical treatment) before confirmatory testing returns.

### Confirmatory identification
An identification method validated to resolve species-level (or strain-level) identity with documented accuracy — MALDI-TOF against a validated library, or sequence-based methods.
**In use:** "The presumptive call was confirmed by 16S sequencing at 99.9% identity."
**Common misuse:** assumed to always be sequencing — MALDI-TOF against a validated, current library is confirmatory for the large majority of routine clinical/environmental organisms; sequencing is reserved for ambiguous or high-consequence cases, not a default step.

### Pulsotype
The distinct banding pattern an isolate produces on pulsed-field gel electrophoresis (PFGE), used to distinguish or match strains within a species.
**In use:** "The product isolate and the gowning-room isolate are indistinguishable pulsotypes — that's the strain-match evidence."
**Common misuse:** conflated with species ID — two isolates can be the same species with completely different, unrelated pulsotypes.

### Generation time (doubling time)
The time required for a microbial population to double in number under a given set of conditions.
**In use:** "Generation time under lab conditions was 80 minutes — that bounds how far back we can plausibly place the contamination event."
**Common misuse:** applied across mismatched conditions — a generation time measured in rich lab broth at 37°C does not directly transfer to a field environment at ambient temperature with limited nutrients; it's a starting estimate, not an exact field prediction.

### Breakpoint
The zone-diameter or minimum inhibitory concentration (MIC) value CLSI or EUCAST has validated as the cutoff between susceptible, intermediate, and resistant categories, for a specific organism-drug combination.
**In use:** "There's no CLSI breakpoint for that organism-drug pair — we report 'no interpretive criteria available,' not a guessed category."
**Common misuse:** applied to an organism-drug combination the breakpoint table wasn't validated for, producing a susceptibility call with no defined meaning.

### Alert level / action level
Environmental-monitoring thresholds — alert level signals a shift worth watching; action level triggers a mandatory investigation and, often, a batch-disposition hold.
**In use:** "That location hit action level, not just alert — we're opening an investigation, not just noting it in the trend log."
**Common misuse:** the two levels are used interchangeably, or a single alert-level hit is escalated with the same urgency as an action-level breach.

### Attributable source (vs. leading hypothesis)
A source conclusion is "attributable" or "confirmed" only when strain-level evidence supports it; without that, the strongest defensible claim is "leading hypothesis, species-match only."
**In use:** "This is a leading hypothesis pending PFGE results — we haven't confirmed common source yet."
**Common misuse:** a species-level match alone gets reported to leadership as a confirmed source, which overstates the evidence and can misdirect the corrective action.

### Selective medium
Growth medium formulated to favor recovery of certain organism categories while inhibiting others (e.g., MacConkey agar selects for Gram-negatives).
**In use:** "No growth on MacConkey doesn't clear the sample — that medium wouldn't recover a Gram-positive contaminant anyway."
**Common misuse:** a negative result on selective media is reported as "no contamination found" without qualifying what organism categories that medium was capable of detecting.

### CFU (colony-forming unit)
A measure of viable, culturable microbial count — one CFU represents one colony-producing cell or cell cluster on a plate, not necessarily one cell.
**In use:** "Environmental sample came back at 2 CFU on the Grade B surface — within the alert range but not action level."
**Common misuse:** treated as a precise cell count; CFU undercounts clustered or non-culturable-but-viable cells and is a culture-method-dependent estimate, not an absolute population count.

### Biotype
A subspecies-level classification based on biochemical/metabolic characteristics, coarser than strain typing but finer than species ID.
**In use:** "Matching biotype plus antibiogram gives weak supporting evidence, but it's not a substitute for PFGE if the finding is going in a regulatory report."
**Common misuse:** treated as equivalent evidentiary strength to genetic strain typing when it's a lower-resolution proxy, sufficient only for low-consequence trending, not source confirmation.

### Antibiogram
The pattern of susceptibility/resistance results across a panel of antimicrobials for a given isolate, sometimes used as a rough strain-comparison tool.
**In use:** "The antibiograms match, which is supporting evidence, but we still want PFGE before calling this a confirmed source."
**Common misuse:** used alone as proof of common source — isolates can share an antibiogram by chance or by common resistance mechanisms unrelated to shared ancestry.

### Bioburden
The total viable microbial population on or in a raw material, component, or product prior to a sterilization or reduction step.
**In use:** "Component bioburden history is the first thing to pull if no environmental match turns up — the contamination may not be environmental at all."
**Common misuse:** assumed to be zero for "clean" incoming materials without an actual bioburden testing program to support that assumption.

### Purity check (culture)
Confirmation that an isolate being identified or tested is a single organism, not a mixed culture producing a composite or ambiguous result.
**In use:** "Low MALDI-TOF score — first question is a purity check before we blame the library."
**Common misuse:** skipped when a result looks "close enough," when a low-confidence score is frequently explained by an unrecognized mixed culture rather than a library gap.
