# Working Vocabulary — Terms Generalists Misuse

Format per term: definition, a sentence the way a practitioner actually uses it, and the common misuse to avoid.

---

**Land-grant mission (the triad)**
The three-part public mandate — resident instruction (teaching), agricultural experiment station research, and Cooperative Extension — that land-grant universities were created to deliver under the Morrill, Hatch, and Smith-Lever Acts.
*Usage:* "My appointment letter is 40/40/20 across the triad; the dossier has to show evidence in all three, not just research."
*Misuse:* treating extension as generic "community service" rather than a statutorily funded third of the job with its own deliverables and its own line in the merit review.

**Hatch Act formula funds**
Federal funds distributed to each state's agricultural experiment station by a legislated formula (not competitive peer review), supporting baseline agricultural research capacity, often used for pilot/seed work.
*Usage:* "I used $15k of Hatch seed money to generate the preliminary data before writing the AFRI proposal."
*Misuse:* confusing Hatch funds with competitive federal grants — they're allocated by formula to the station, then internally awarded, and carry lighter reporting requirements than an extramural award.

**Smith-Lever / Cooperative Extension**
The federal-state-county partnership (Smith-Lever Act, 1914) that funds Extension agents and programming connecting land-grant research to practitioners — farmers, ranchers, and communities.
*Usage:* "The field day is a Smith-Lever-funded deliverable, not optional outreach — it's what justifies the research funding to the legislature."
*Misuse:* treating extension programming as a favor to the county agent rather than a funded, expected, and reviewed part of the job.

**AFRI (Agriculture and Food Research Initiative)**
USDA NIFA's flagship competitive extramural grant program for agricultural research, the largest source of competitive federal ag-research funding, organized into program areas (e.g., Physiology of Agricultural Plants) each with its own review panel and funding rate.
*Usage:* "This falls under AFRI Foundational, not AFRI Applied — different review criteria, different expected preliminary-data depth."
*Misuse:* treating "AFRI" as a single undifferentiated pot rather than dozens of program areas with different funding rates and different reviewer expectations for preliminary data.

**Indirect costs / F&A rate**
Facilities and administrative costs (overhead) the university adds on top of direct project costs, at a rate individually negotiated with the federal government — not adjustable by the PI in a proposal budget.
*Usage:* "At our 52% F&A rate, a $495k direct-cost budget carries about $258k in indirect — that's not padding, it's the negotiated rate."
*Misuse:* treating the indirect rate as something a PI can shave to fit a budget ceiling; the fix for an over-ceiling budget is cutting direct costs, not the indirect rate.

**Preliminary (pilot) data**
Early-stage data included in a grant proposal to demonstrate feasibility and effect size before full funding — its statistical power, not just its existence, is what reviewers score.
*Usage:* "Three reps at 28% CV reads as underpowered pilot data to this panel; we need eight reps before resubmitting."
*Misuse:* treating "we have preliminary data" as sufficient regardless of replicate count or variance — reviewers score the power behind the number, not the number's mere presence.

**Multistate regional project**
A Hatch-funded, formally chartered collaboration (e.g., an S-, W-, or NC-numbered project) among multiple state agricultural experiment stations working a shared problem over a 5-year renewable cycle, with guaranteed continuation funding.
*Usage:* "The multistate project renewal is administrative — the real work is the shared trial data due each October."
*Misuse:* assuming multistate projects require the same competitive proposal-writing effort as AFRI; renewal is largely a progress narrative against an already-approved project.

**Coefficient of variation (CV)**
A standardized measure of variability (standard deviation ÷ mean) used to judge whether a field trial's replicate count is adequate for the noise level observed.
*Usage:* "CV on yield came in at 28% with three reps — that's why the panel is going to flag power, not novelty."
*Misuse:* citing CV without stating the replicate count behind it — the same CV reads very differently at n=3 versus n=8.

**Appointment percentage (effort split)**
The research/teaching/extension percentage stated in a faculty member's official appointment letter, which is the literal basis for tenure and merit review, not just a rough description.
*Usage:* "I'm 40/40/20 — the P&T committee will expect roughly proportional evidence across the dossier's three sections."
*Misuse:* assuming the split is symbolic and that excelling in one pillar compensates for a near-empty section in another; committees score the letter's stated split.

**SET (Student Evaluation of Teaching)**
End-of-semester student ratings, a reliable signal of course logistics and communication, a weaker signal of content rigor or actual learning gains.
*Usage:* "The SET drop tracks with the semester grading turnaround slipping to three weeks — check logistics before rewriting the syllabus."
*Misuse:* treating SET scores as a direct, sufficient measure of teaching quality in either direction — ignoring a real multi-semester trend, or over-optimizing for scores via grade inflation.

**IACUC / IRB / biosafety protocol**
Institutional committees (Animal Care and Use, human subjects, and biosafety respectively) that must approve and periodically renew any research involving live animals, human subjects, or regulated biological materials before data collection.
*Usage:* "The protocol renewal is due before the grant's own reporting deadline — they're on separate calendars, track both."
*Misuse:* assuming a grant's active-award status implies protocol coverage; the two run on independent clocks, and a lapsed protocol invalidates data collected after expiration regardless of funding status.

**Individual Development Plan (IDP)**
A written, milestone-based plan created with a graduate student (often after a missed-deliverable pattern) documenting root cause, interim checkpoints, and an escalation trigger.
*Usage:* "We reset the IDP after the missed December deliverable — turned out to be an irrigation failure, not a motivation problem."
*Misuse:* skipping the root-cause check and writing an IDP as a disciplinary document by default, which misses infrastructure or access failures that are the actual cause more often than not.

**Faculty Activity Report (FAR)**
The annual reporting instrument (time allocation, publications, grants, teaching, extension deliverables) that merit raises and workload audits actually run on.
*Usage:* "Two years of FAR data show research time sitting at 25% against a 40% appointment — that's the gap to fix before the dossier, not after."
*Misuse:* treating the FAR as year-end paperwork rather than a running check against the appointment percentages that can catch drift early enough to correct it.
