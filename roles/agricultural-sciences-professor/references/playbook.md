## 1. AFRI-style proposal budget table (4-year Foundational award)

Ceiling for the program area: $650,000 direct + indirect over 4 years. Indirect (F&A) rate: 52% of modified total direct costs (MTDC) — the institution's federally negotiated rate, not negotiable in the proposal.

First draft, full-time postdoc + full equipment purchase:

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| PI summer salary (1 mo) | $9,800 | $10,100 | $10,400 | $10,700 | $41,000 |
| Postdoc salary + fringe (1.0 FTE) | $58,000 | $59,700 | $61,500 | $63,300 | $242,500 |
| Grad RA stipend + tuition | $34,000 | $35,000 | $36,000 | $37,000 | $142,000 |
| Field/lab supplies | $12,000 | $10,000 | $10,000 | $8,000 | $40,000 |
| Equipment (soil moisture probes) | $18,000 | — | — | — | $18,000 |
| Travel (2 conferences/yr) | $3,000 | $3,000 | $3,000 | $3,000 | $12,000 |
| **MTDC subtotal** | $134,800 | $117,800 | $120,900 | $122,000 | $495,500 |
| Indirect (52% of MTDC) | $70,096 | $61,256 | $62,868 | $63,440 | $257,660 |
| **Total** | $204,896 | $179,056 | $183,768 | $185,440 | **$753,160** |

Exceeds the $650,000 ceiling by $103,160. The fix is cutting direct costs — dropping the equipment purchase (buy access to a shared departmental probe instead) and trimming the postdoc to 0.75 FTE — not shaving the indirect rate, which the institution won't let a PI negotiate:

## 2. Graduate student Individual Development Plan (IDP) — year 1 reset

Used when a student misses two consecutive committee deliverables (SKILL.md heuristic).

```
Student: J. Okoye, PhD year 2, sorghum drought-stress project
Trigger: missed the December data-summary deliverable and the February
committee check-in; advisor observed no new data collected in 10 weeks.

Root-cause check (before assuming motivation):
[x] Data access — confirmed: field site irrigation controller failed for
    6 weeks, unreported to advisor. THIS is the root cause, not motivation.
[ ] Equipment failure unrelated to above
[ ] Mentoring cadence — meetings had dropped from biweekly to ad hoc

Interim milestones (next 8 weeks):
- Week 1: irrigation controller repaired (facilities ticket #4471, filed)
- Week 2: resume biweekly 30-min check-ins, Tuesdays 10am, standing calendar hold
- Week 4: replant lost plots; damage assessment memo to committee
- Week 8: revised data-collection timeline circulated to full committee,
  accounting for the 10-week gap — qualifying exam date pushed 1 semester,
  documented in writing with committee sign-off, not assumed

Escalation trigger: if week-4 replant milestone is missed with no
infrastructure-related cause, move to a documented performance conversation
with the graduate program director present.
```

## 4. Extension field day — agenda template

```
FIELD DAY: Drought-Tolerant Sorghum Variety Trial
Date: August 14, 2026, 8:00am–12:00pm · Location: County Ag Research Farm
Expected attendance: 60–80 (based on last 3 years' county-agent RSVPs)

8:00  Registration, coffee, handout: 1-page variety comparison (yield,
      days-to-maturity, water use — numbers, not a methods section)
8:30  Welcome + trial overview (10 min max — growers came for the plots,
      not a lecture)
8:45  Plot walk, station 1: irrigated vs. rainfed comparison
      — LEAD with the recommendation ("Variety X held 85% of irrigated
      yield under 40% less water this year") THEN the caveat
      ("one site-year; confirm against your own soil type before switching
      acreage")
9:30  Plot walk, station 2: planting date x variety interaction
10:15 County agent Q&A (agent fields the practical/economic questions —
      PI fields the physiology questions; division agreed in advance)
11:00 Wrap-up: where to find the full data (Extension bulletin, published
      after season-end analysis — not yet, don't overpromise a date)
11:15 Informal — this is where the real feedback happens; budget the time

Post-event: attendance count + 3 written questions collected → logged in
annual Faculty Activity Report as an extension deliverable.
```

## 5. Backward-design course table (excerpt — Soil Fertility, 400-level)

| Week | Learning objective | Assessment | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Explain cation exchange capacity's effect on nutrient availability | Problem set: compute CEC from lab data | Prereq check — students without soil-chem background flagged here, not week 6 |
| 3–4 | Diagnose a nutrient deficiency from a leaf-tissue report | Case study, real (anonymized) grower sample | Graded rubric distributed with the assignment, not after |
| 5–6 | Design a fertilizer recommendation for a given soil test | Group project, draft due wk 6 | Draft-then-revise catches logistics-driven confusion before it shows up in SET |
| 7 | Midterm | Exam | Grades posted within 5 business days — turnaround is the #1 SET-driver, not difficulty |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |
| 15 | Present final fertilizer plan to a mock grower panel | Oral presentation + written plan | Mirrors the extension field-day format deliberately — same "recommendation first" discipline |

The discipline: every assessment is named before the semester starts and matches a single objective — a common failure is assessments that test whatever was covered that week, disconnected from a stated objective, which is what generates "the exam didn't match what we studied" complaints regardless of actual difficulty.
