# Supervision, sanctions, and reporting playbook

Filled worksheets for the three recurring probation officer tasks: graduated sanctions matrix, criminogenic-needs case plan, and presentence investigation report structure.

## 1. Graduated sanctions matrix

| Violation type | First occurrence, isolated | Repeated despite intervention | New offense or safety risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Missed check-in | Reschedule, note reason, monitor | Increase check-in frequency for a defined period | Consider revocation petition if pattern indicates absconding risk |
| Positive drug test (non-violent offense history) | Increase treatment session frequency, tie to criminogenic need domain | Reassess treatment intensity/modality; consider brief structured sanction (e.g. short jail sanction per jurisdiction's sanction grid) | Consider revocation if combined with other risk indicators |
| Curfew violation | Verbal warning, document reason | Electronic monitoring or curfew adjustment with justification | Consider revocation if tied to a new offense pattern |
| Failure to attend required treatment | Case conference to identify barrier (transportation, scheduling, motivation), adjust plan | Escalate treatment intensity or change modality | Consider revocation if treatment refusal correlates with escalating risk indicators |
| New arrest (any offense) | N/A - always assess independently | N/A | Immediate risk reassessment; revocation petition consideration per jurisdiction policy |

Rule: the matrix's middle and right columns require documented escalation reasoning — a first-occurrence technical violation should almost never jump straight to the right column without an intervening graduated step, unless a specific safety indicator is present.

## 2. Criminogenic-needs case plan template

```
Risk assessment date: <date> | Overall risk level: <low/moderate/high>

Top criminogenic needs identified (rank by assessed severity):
  1. <domain, e.g. "antisocial associates"> - <specific finding>
  2. <domain, e.g. "substance abuse"> - <specific finding>
  3. <domain> - <specific finding>

Case plan interventions (each tied to a specific need above):
  Need 1 intervention: <specific referral/action>
  Need 2 intervention: <specific referral/action>
  Need 3 intervention: <specific referral/action>

Non-criminogenic needs noted but not prioritized for limited resources:
  <e.g. general self-esteem, life satisfaction - noted, not a primary case plan target>

Reassessment date: <scheduled periodic review, not just at intake>
```

Rule: every intervention in the case plan should map to a specific identified criminogenic need — a case plan full of generic goals ("be a better person," "follow all rules") without a criminogenic-need anchor isn't a plan, it's a wish list.

## 3. Presentence investigation report (PSI) structure

```
1. Offense summary: <factual account, sourced to police report/court record>
2. Defendant's criminal history: <sourced to official record, not memory or assumption>
3. Defendant's personal/social history: <employment, family, education - sourced to
   verification where possible, noted as self-report where not independently verified>
4. Victim impact (if applicable): <sourced to victim statement>
5. Risk-needs assessment summary: <actuarial score and criminogenic needs identified>
6. Mitigating factors: <explicitly included, not omitted>
7. Aggravating factors: <explicitly included, not overstated>
8. Sentencing recommendation (if required by jurisdiction): <specific, tied to the above,
   not a generic recommendation disconnected from the documented findings>
```

Rule: every factual claim needs a source notation (police report, court record, self-report, third-party verification) — an unsourced characterization in a PSI is exactly the kind of error that's hard to correct after a judge has relied on it at sentencing.

## 4. Violation classification decision tree

```
Is this a new arrest/new law violation?
├── Yes -> Immediate risk reassessment; follow jurisdiction's revocation policy
└── No (technical violation) -> Is there a safety risk indicator (violence, absconding,
      weapon, victim contact violation)?
    ├── Yes -> Escalate immediately regardless of "first occurrence" status
    └── No -> Is this an isolated first occurrence, or a repeated pattern despite
          prior intervention?
        ├── Isolated -> Apply least restrictive graduated sanction, document reasoning
        └── Repeated despite intervention -> Escalate sanction severity per matrix,
              consider revocation only if intervention has been genuinely attempted
              and failed
```
