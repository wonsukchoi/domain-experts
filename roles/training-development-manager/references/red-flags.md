# Red flags — what a training manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A training program requested for a performance gap with no diagnosis of whether it's actually a skill, will, or systems issue
- **Usually means:** training may be the default response reached for regardless of actual cause, and if the real cause is motivation, incentives, or a broken process, training won't fix it no matter how well it's designed.
- **First question:** "What data shows this is actually a skill gap, as opposed to a systems or incentive issue?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific performance metric in question and its trend, any process or incentive changes that coincide with the gap's onset.

### A performance metric sensitive to skill (e.g., close rate) that hasn't actually declined, while a different metric (e.g., volume, throughput) has
- **Usually means:** the problem likely isn't a skill gap at all — a stable skill-sensitive metric alongside a declining volume/systems-sensitive one points to a process or capacity cause, not a capability one.
- **First question:** "Which specific metric moved, and is it the one training would actually be expected to affect?"
- **Data to pull:** trend data for both skill-sensitive and volume/systems-sensitive metrics over the relevant period.

### A training program evaluated only by post-session satisfaction scores, with no behavior-change or business-outcome measure defined
- **Usually means:** satisfaction is a weak, easily-gamed proxy for actual impact — a well-reviewed session can still fail to change what people do differently at work.
- **First question:** "What specific behavior change or business outcome was this program meant to produce, and has that actually been measured?"
- **Data to pull:** the program's stated success criteria, any behavior or outcome data collected beyond the satisfaction survey.

### A single training session delivered with no built-in follow-up, reinforcement, or application opportunity
- **Usually means:** learning without reinforcement decays quickly (the forgetting curve), and a well-designed session alone is unlikely to produce durable behavior change.
- **First question:** "What reinforcement or application touchpoints are built into this program after the initial session?"
- **Data to pull:** the program design document, any scheduled follow-up or manager-reinforcement component.

### A full formal training program being built for a narrow, infrequent task
- **Usually means:** a job aid, checklist, or brief coaching conversation might address the need more efficiently than a formal program, which is a common and costly overinvestment for this type of gap.
- **First question:** "How frequent and how durable is this specific need, and does it actually warrant a full program versus a lighter-weight job aid?"
- **Data to pull:** frequency and recurrence of the task/need, comparable lighter-weight interventions considered.

### A training request proceeding without checking whether post-training workplace conditions (manager expectations, process support) actually support the new behavior
- **Usually means:** even well-designed training may not transfer to changed behavior if the workplace afterward doesn't expect, support, or create opportunity to apply it.
- **First question:** "Will managers and processes actually expect and support this new behavior once training ends, or does that need to change too?"
- **Data to pull:** manager awareness/buy-in for the program's goals, any process changes needed to support the new behavior.

### Passive, lecture-style content proposed for a skill that requires active, varied on-the-job application
- **Usually means:** information-transfer-style delivery often produces weak retention and transfer for skills that need practiced application, regardless of content accuracy.
- **First question:** "Does this delivery format include active practice, or is it primarily one-way information transfer?"
- **Data to pull:** the program's instructional design/format, whether practice or application exercises are included.

### A training program's ROI or impact claimed with no post-hire (or post-training) outcome data connecting it to an actual business result
- **Usually means:** the program's actual effectiveness is unverified — Kirkpatrick's higher levels (behavior, results) haven't actually been measured, only the easiest lower levels (reaction, learning).
- **First question:** "What behavior-change or business-result data exists connecting this program to an actual outcome, not just knowledge-check scores?"
- **Data to pull:** the program's evaluation data across all four Kirkpatrick levels, not just reaction/learning.
