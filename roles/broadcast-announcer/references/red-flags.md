# Red Flags

### Talk break running >15 seconds past its scheduled post, 3+ times in a week
- **Usually means:** backtiming discipline has slipped (host is estimating rather than counting down against the post); second most likely: the hot clock's talk allotment for that slot no longer matches the actual content load (a segment grew without the clock being adjusted).
- **First question:** "What's actually in the break that keeps pushing past the post?"
- **Data to pull:** automation as-run log, actual break durations versus the scheduled hot clock for the affected slot.

### A specific daypart's average-quarter-hour (AQH) drops >10% month over month while cume in that daypart holds steady
- **Usually means:** the content in that hour isn't holding attention minute to minute (pacing, too much flexible talk, a stale bit) rather than a promotion or awareness problem — cume holding steady means people are still sampling the station, they're just leaving during the hour.
- **First question:** "Which specific break or segment in the hour shows the tune-out on the minute-by-minute curve?"
- **Data to pull:** Nielsen PPM minute-by-minute tuning data for the affected daypart, segment by segment.

### More than 2 indecency/profanity complaints logged against one host in a quarter
- **Usually means:** content is drifting outside the format's standards generally, or one specific recurring bit is the actual source; second most likely: a single ad-libbed moment that got repeated in cume-driving clips or social reposts.
- **First question:** "Were the complaints during safe-harbor hours (10pm–6am) or daytime, and did any air via delayed repeat into daytime?"
- **Data to pull:** the station's compliance/complaint log cross-referenced against the program log (as-run log) timestamps.

### Audible vocal strain or hoarseness after 4+ consecutive on-air hours, recurring weekly
- **Usually means:** vocal overuse from pushing volume or pitch rather than breath support or mic technique; second most likely: mic placement too far from the mouth, forcing the host to compensate by straining.
- **First question:** "Has this host had any formal mic technique or voice coaching, and how far are they sitting from the capsule?"
- **Data to pull:** shift-length schedule log; any ENT or vocal-health referral history if one exists.

### A voice-tracked break references weather, traffic, or an event in near-identical wording across 2+ markets on the same day
- **Usually means:** the host is producing one generic track and letting automation stitch it everywhere rather than localizing per market, risking listener detection of a "fake local" host and program-director scrutiny at renewal time.
- **First question:** "How much lead time did the talent actually have to localize each market's version of this break?"
- **Data to pull:** voice-tracking system logs (WideOrbit/Zetta) showing per-market variant timestamps and content differences.

### A scheduled network or satellite join missed or cut the host off mid-sentence more than once in a month
- **Usually means:** break backtiming isn't accounting for the automation's hard forced switch — too much flexible content is being stacked against a fixed post with no buffer.
- **First question:** "What was the actual elapsed time from break start to the join on the instance it happened?"
- **Data to pull:** automation as-run log timestamp compared to the scheduled satellite/network join time.

### Legal station ID missed (not aired at or near the top of the hour) more than once in a rating period
- **Usually means:** a sequencing error in the log or automation, or a host skipping it under time pressure believing it's recoverable — it isn't, it's an FCC-relevant compliance record.
- **First question:** "Was this a system/log error, or a live decision to skip it under time pressure?"
- **Data to pull:** the FCC-required program log / as-run log for the affected hour.

### Contest-rules or sponsor-disclosure copy paraphrased instead of read verbatim, caught in a compliance or legal review
- **Usually means:** the host is treating legally required copy (odds disclosure, no-purchase-necessary language, sponsor attribution) as ordinary ad-lib material instead of a fixed, must-read block.
- **First question:** "Was the deviation caught before or after the spot aired?"
- **Data to pull:** the continuity/traffic log's scheduled copy compared against the as-aired audio or transcript.

### Same bit or segment format run unchanged for 6+ months with declining PPM engagement in that quarter-hour
- **Usually means:** format fatigue — regular listeners now predict the bit and tune past it rather than staying through it.
- **First question:** "When was this segment last refreshed, and against what data was that decision made?"
- **Data to pull:** PPM minute-by-minute tuning trend for that segment's time slot across the period in question.
