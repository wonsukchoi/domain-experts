# Red flags

### Take-up speed set from a prior product's setting without recalculating for the current throughput rate
- **Usually means:** actual denier will differ from target if throughput rate has changed.
- **First question:** what is the current actual throughput rate versus what the take-up speed setting was calculated for?
- **Data to pull:** current throughput rate compared against take-up speed and the calculated target denier.

### Draw ratio increased to maximize fiber strength without regard to the documented breakage risk threshold
- **Usually means:** increased fiber breakage during drawing without a proportional strength benefit.
- **First question:** what does the current draw ratio show against the documented optimal/maximum range for this polymer/process?
- **Data to pull:** current draw ratio compared against the documented optimal/maximum range for this polymer/process.

### Fiber property verification performed only on aggregate yarn denier, without individual filament checks despite downstream defect reports
- **Usually means:** a localized weak filament from a degraded spinneret hole could be masked by the aggregate average.
- **First question:** what do individual filament measurements show for the specific position/hole in question?
- **Data to pull:** individual filament measurements compared against aggregate yarn denier, for the specific position/spinneret hole in question.

### Fiber breakage responded to by broadly reducing speed/draw ratio across the line rather than diagnosing the specific cause
- **Usually means:** the actual cause — a specific degraded spinneret hole, localized temperature variation — goes unaddressed while production rate is unnecessarily sacrificed.
- **First question:** does the breakage occurrence correlate with a specific position or process parameter?
- **Data to pull:** breakage occurrence location/pattern compared against process parameter logs (spinneret position, temperature, draw ratio) for that specific position.

### Quench conditions changed without reassessing whether the current draw ratio still produces target mechanical properties
- **Usually means:** final fiber properties may shift even at an unchanged draw ratio, since quench affects the structure available for drawing to act on.
- **First question:** what do current quench conditions and actual property test results show against documented requirements?
- **Data to pull:** current quench conditions compared against documented requirements, and actual property test results against specification.

### A recurring denier or property out-of-spec pattern for a specific position/spinneret appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — throughput rate change, spinneret condition, draw ratio, quench — rather than isolated one-off variation.
- **First question:** does the out-of-spec occurrence log correlate with a specific position or process parameter?
- **Data to pull:** out-of-spec occurrence log by position compared against process parameter logs over the same period.

### A polymer or process change introduced without recalculating take-up speed/draw ratio for the new throughput characteristics
- **Usually means:** parameters validated for the prior polymer/configuration may not produce the target denier/properties on the new one.
- **First question:** what does the new configuration's actual throughput show against the take-up speed/draw ratio settings in use?
- **Data to pull:** new polymer/configuration's actual throughput characteristics compared against the take-up speed/draw ratio settings in use.
