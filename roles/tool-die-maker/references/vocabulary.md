# Vocabulary

### Die clearance
The gap between a punch and its die opening in a stamping die, usually expressed as a percentage of material thickness per side.
**In use:** "Clearance is 8% per side on this die — that's why the edge is clean with minimal burr on 0.060" stock."
**Common misuse:** Treating clearance as a single universal number regardless of material type, thickness, or hardness, rather than a value chosen specifically for the material at hand.

### Springback
The elastic recovery a formed metal part undergoes after the bending force is released, causing the final angle to differ from the tool's forming angle.
**In use:** "Punch is cut at 87.5° to compensate for springback — the part recovers to 90° once it's released."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a die machined to the print's exact angle will produce a part at that same angle, ignoring that metal always recovers some amount after any bend.

### Shrinkage rate (mold)
The percentage a plastic resin's dimensions reduce as it cools and solidifies from melt to solid inside a mold cavity.
**In use:** "This resin shrinks 2.2% along flow and 1.8% across it — the cavity's cut oversized to compensate in both directions."
**Common misuse:** Using one generic "plastic shrink" figure for every resin, when shrink rates vary significantly by material and can differ directionally (anisotropic) along versus across the flow path.

### Progressive die
A die with multiple sequential stations, each performing one operation on a continuous strip as it advances through the press.
**In use:** "Station 3 pierces the hole, station 4 forms the bend — that's the progressive sequence for this part."
**Common misuse:** Designing or evaluating each station in isolation, without checking whether the sequence leaves enough carrier web intact for the strip to survive to the next station.

### Carrier web
The strip of material connecting formed or stamped parts to the rest of the coil strip as it advances through a progressive die's stations.
**In use:** "Web's down to 0.030" at station 5 — that's below minimum, it'll tear before it reaches the next station."
**Common misuse:** Treating web width as a minor layout detail rather than the structural element that keeps the strip intact between stations.

### Shut height
The closed height of a stamping die, measured from the bottom of the ram to the top of the bolster, which must match the press it's intended to run in.
**In use:** "This die's shut height is 14 inches — check it against the press's shut height range before scheduling the job."
**Common misuse:** Confusing it with the die's general overall dimensions, when shut height specifically has to fall within the target press's compatible range for the die to close and function correctly.

### EDM (Electrical Discharge Machining)
A process using electrical sparks to erode hardened tool steel into precise shapes, used where conventional cutting tools can't machine the hardened material.
**In use:** "That cavity's already through-hardened — it's going to EDM, not the mill."
**Common misuse:** Assuming EDM is reserved for exotic or unusual geometry, when routinely it's simply required because the material is already too hard for conventional cutting tools to machine.

### Lapping / hand fitting
A manual finishing process using abrasive compound to achieve precise mating fit between tool components beyond what machining alone can hold.
**In use:** "These two die plates need lapping to seat flush — the mill can't hold that flatness alone."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as a cosmetic polishing step, when it's frequently the step that actually achieves the final functional tolerance a machine alone couldn't reach.

### Neutral axis (bending)
The theoretical line within a bent material that neither stretches nor compresses, used to calculate the developed (flat) blank length before forming.
**In use:** "Blank length is calculated from the neutral axis position, not the outside radius — that's what the K-factor accounts for."
**Common misuse:** Calculating blank length directly from a part's outside or inside dimensions, instead of from the neutral axis position, which shifts depending on bend radius and material thickness.

### First-off / first-article sample
The first part or parts produced from a newly built or modified tool, measured against print before production is released.
**In use:** "First-off came back within tolerance on every dimension — released for production."
**Common misuse:** Skipping first-off measurement on a "proven" die design, when tool wear, heat-treat distortion, or a new material lot can each independently shift actual results away from the assumed compensation values.
