# Playbook

## Temperature/humidity flash-time adjustment (filled example)

Product standard: 30 min flash-off at 70°F/50% RH. Shop conditions: 60°F/65% RH.

| Factor | Standard condition | Shop condition | Effect on flash time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 70°F | 60°F (10°F lower) | Slower evaporation — longer flash time needed |
| Relative humidity | 50% | 65% (15 points higher) | Slower evaporation — longer flash time needed |
| Combined adjustment (per product TDS cold/humid guidance) | 30 min baseline | — | Extend to 45–60 min |

**Comparison across shop conditions (same 30-min baseline):**

| Shop temp/humidity | Deviation from standard | Adjusted flash time |
|---|---|---|
| 70°F/50% RH (matches standard) | None | 30 min (no adjustment) |
| 65°F/55% RH (mild deviation) | Small | ~35–40 min |
| 60°F/65% RH (moderate deviation) | Larger | ~45–60 min |
| 55°F/75% RH (significant deviation) | Large | 60+ min, verify via test panel before committing to schedule |

**Rule applied:** As shop conditions drift further from standard, the adjustment needed grows — always verify against the product's actual TDS guidance for these condition ranges rather than assuming a small deviation needs no adjustment.

## Blotch-risk wood species reference (filled example)

| Wood species | Blotch risk | Recommended pre-treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Pine | High | Wash coat or gel stain recommended |
| Cherry | High | Wash coat or gel stain recommended |
| Birch | High | Wash coat or gel stain recommended |
| Oak | Low | Direct stain generally acceptable (open, uniform grain) |
| Walnut | Low | Direct stain generally acceptable |
| Maple (blotchy grain areas) | Moderate to high | Wash coat recommended, especially on curly/figured maple |

**Applied check:** Treating maple as uniformly low-risk (like oak or walnut) ignores that figured or curly maple grain patterns specifically increase blotch risk — species alone isn't sufficient, the specific grain character of the actual board matters too.
