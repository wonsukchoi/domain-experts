# Red flags

### Stain applied directly to a blotch-prone wood species without a wash coat/conditioner
- **Usually means:** uneven color absorption across the piece, most visible on areas of different grain density.
- **First question:** is this wood species/area known to be blotch-prone, and was a wash coat actually used?
- **Data to pull:** wood species/area compared against wash coat/conditioner use logged for this piece.

### Topcoat flash-off/cure time between coats compressed below the product's specification or actual shop-condition requirement
- **Usually means:** trapped solvent, poor intercoat adhesion, or a soft finish.
- **First question:** what was the actual time between coats versus the product's specified minimum, adjusted for current temperature/humidity?
- **Data to pull:** actual time between coats compared against the product's specified minimum, adjusted for current shop temperature/humidity.

### A sanding grit step skipped or under-completed before advancing to the next grit
- **Usually means:** scratches from the coarser grit will show through the clear finish once cured, even if it looks fine while wet.
- **First question:** does the sanding sequence actually completed match the standard grit progression for this wood/finish type?
- **Data to pull:** sanding sequence actually completed compared against the standard grit progression for this wood/finish type.

### Finish rubbed out/sheen-leveled based only on surface-dry feel, before verified full cure
- **Usually means:** risk of swirl marks, haze, or uneven sheen from abrading a still-soft finish film.
- **First question:** what is the actual cure time elapsed versus the product's stated full cure time under current shop conditions?
- **Data to pull:** actual cure time elapsed compared against the product's stated full cure time under current shop conditions.

### A finish schedule applied without adjustment despite shop temperature/humidity differing meaningfully from the product's stated standard conditions
- **Usually means:** actual cure/flash behavior differs from what the standard schedule assumes, risking defects the schedule wasn't designed to prevent under these conditions.
- **First question:** how far do actual shop conditions differ from the product's stated standard, and was schedule timing adjusted?
- **Data to pull:** actual shop temperature/humidity compared against the product's stated standard conditions, and whether schedule timing was adjusted.

### A recurring blotching, adhesion, or haze defect pattern for a specific wood/finish combination appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — missing wash coat, compressed flash time, humidity — rather than isolated one-off piece variation.
- **First question:** does the defect log correlate with a specific process parameter or wood/finish combination?
- **Data to pull:** defect occurrence log by wood/finish combination compared against process parameter logs (wash coat use, coat timing, shop conditions) over the same period.

### A new finish product or wood species introduced without checking its specific technical data sheet requirements
- **Usually means:** assuming a different product behaves like the prior one, risking a schedule mismatch.
- **First question:** what does the new product's technical data sheet actually specify versus the schedule being used?
- **Data to pull:** new product's technical data sheet requirements compared against the schedule actually used.
