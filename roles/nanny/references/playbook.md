# Nanny — Playbook

## Work agreement — core clauses, filled example

A one-family, live-out arrangement, drafted from the INA Nanny Work Agreement structure:

| Clause | Filled example |
|---|---|
| Schedule | Mon-Fri, 8:30am-5:30pm (9 hrs/day, 45 hrs/week) |
| Base rate | $24/hr |
| Overtime | 1.5x ($36/hr) for hours worked beyond 40/week, per FLSA domestic-service rule |
| Guaranteed hours | 40 hrs/week paid even if family cancels with <48 hrs notice (vacation, illness, closed office) |
| Additional-child differential | +$3/hr per child beyond the two named in this agreement |
| PTO | 10 paid days/year, plus the 7 federal holidays the family observes, accrued at ~0.83 days/month |
| Driving authorization | Authorized to drive [child names] in the family's [vehicle], insured under [policy #]; nanny's own vehicle not authorized for child transport without a separate rider |
| Medical authorization | Standing authorization for [named OTC medications, doses, max frequency] attached as Exhibit A; anything outside Exhibit A requires same-day written parental sign-off |
| Discipline approach | No physical discipline; redirection and time-in per the family's stated approach, documented in Exhibit B |
| Screen time | Per AAP age-band guidance (see table below) unless parents specify otherwise in writing |
| Notice | 2 weeks' paid notice from either party, absent safety cause |
| Review cadence | Scope, pay, and hours revisited quarterly (Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct) |

## Nanny-tax computation — filled example

Family paid a nanny $1,050/week ($54,600/year) in a state with a 1.0% state unemployment tax rate on the first $9,000 of wages (SUTA figures vary by state — verify against the current state rate).

| Item | Basis | Rate | Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| Federal filing threshold | Cash wages paid in the year | IRS Schedule H threshold (recent years ~$2,600-$2,800; verify current year) | Exceeded — Schedule H required |
| Social Security + Medicare (FICA), employer share | $54,600 | 7.65% | $4,176.90 |
| Social Security + Medicare (FICA), employee share (withheld from pay) | $54,600 | 7.65% | $4,176.90 |
| FUTA (federal unemployment) | First $7,000 of wages | 0.6% (after standard state credit) | $42.00 |
| SUTA (state unemployment, example state) | First $9,000 of wages | 1.0% | $90.00 |
| **Total employer cost beyond gross wages** | | | **≈ $4,308.90** |
| **Net to nanny after employee-share FICA withholding** (before any federal/state income tax withholding, which is optional but common) | | | **$50,423.10** |

The employer's true annual cost of a $54,600 nanny is closer to $58,910 once FICA/FUTA/SUTA are added — a number worth stating plainly during rate negotiation, since families often anchor on gross pay alone.

## Screen-time reference (AAP age bands)

| Age | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Under 18-24 months | Avoid screen media other than live video chatting with family |
| 18-24 months | If introduced at all, high-quality programming only, watched together, not solo |
| 2-5 years | Limit to about 1 hour/day of high-quality programming |
| 6+ years | Consistent limits that don't crowd out sleep, physical activity, and other behaviors essential to health |

Deviations from the family's written screen-time rule (a sick day, a long car ride) are logged in the handoff note, not treated as the new default.

## Car-seat stage reference (NHTSA)

| Stage | Typical transition point |
|---|---|
| Rear-facing | As long as possible, up to the specific seat's rear-facing height/weight limit — commonly well past a child's 2nd birthday |
| Forward-facing harness | After outgrowing rear-facing limits, until the seat's forward-facing height/weight limit (commonly in the 40-65 lb range) |
| Booster | After outgrowing the harness, until the vehicle seat belt fits properly without the booster — commonly around 4'9" tall, often ages 8-12 |
| Seat belt only, back seat | Once the booster is no longer needed; back seat recommended through age 12 |

Always verify against the specific seat model's manual — limits vary by manufacturer and are not a single nationwide number.

## Handoff log — filled example entries

> **Wed, 11:10am** — Gave 1 dose children's ibuprofen per Exhibit A (low-grade fever, 100.1°F at 10:45am) — within standing authorization, dose/time logged, texted parent at 11:12am for visibility.

> **Thu, 5:50pm** — [Child] fell off the porch step, no head contact, scraped knee only. Cleaned and bandaged. Not a head/height/skin-break-from-height incident requiring a formal report under our agreement, but noting here since it happened on my watch.

> **Fri, 6:00pm — end of week** — Hours this week: 57 (see pay reconciliation attached). Additional-child coverage Wed-Fri per text thread. Agreement review is due next Monday per the quarterly cadence — flagging now so it's not a surprise.
