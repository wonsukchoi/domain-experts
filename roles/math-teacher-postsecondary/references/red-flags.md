# Red flags

### Gateway-course DFW rate >30% for two consecutive terms with placement cut score unchanged
- **Usually means:** course pedagogy or assessment structure (lecture-only delivery, single high-stakes final) is the bottleneck, not the incoming cohort.
- **First question:** did the placement cut score, prerequisite chain, or course pacing actually change, or is everything else held constant?
- **Data to pull:** three-term DFW trend, placement-score-to-grade correlation, and a breakdown of DFW causes (failed final vs. withdrew early vs. cumulative low grades).

### Grade spread on the same proof question >1 point out of 8 across TAs on a common exam
- **Usually means:** rubric-interpretation drift between graders, not a real skill difference between sections.
- **First question:** was a blind-norming sample run before grading started, and if so, on how many exams per grader?
- **Data to pull:** per-TA average and distribution on that question, plus a fresh blind re-grade of a 5-exam sample per TA.

### Homework completion rate above 90% but exam performance on matched skills below 60%
- **Usually means:** homework is being completed with unrestricted collaboration or answer-lookup, so it's measuring completion, not individual mastery.
- **First question:** is the homework platform's answer-checking used as a submit-and-iterate loop with no cap on attempts, letting students converge on the answer without doing the reasoning?
- **Data to pull:** homework attempt-count distribution per problem, and a comparison of homework-only vs. proctored performance on structurally identical problems.

### Withdrawal or DFW concentrated in one section or one TA's recitation group, others in the same course stable
- **Usually means:** a section-specific instructional or grading-consistency problem, not a course-wide issue.
- **First question:** is the gap present on the common final (shared across sections) or only on section-specific quizzes and participation grades?
- **Data to pull:** section-by-section performance on the common final specifically, isolated from any section-graded components.

### Course evaluation scores drop sharply the term active learning replaces lecture
- **Usually means:** either the active-learning implementation lacked a graded accountability structure (unstructured group time reads as "wasted time" to students), or the format change wasn't explained/scaffolded before rollout.
- **First question:** was in-class work graded and tied to a visible deliverable each session, or was it open-ended discussion with no checkpoint?
- **Data to pull:** the specific evaluation comments (not just the score), cross-referenced against whether exam performance moved in the same direction as the evaluation score.

### Placement test cut score predicts final course grade worse than the previous placement instrument did
- **Usually means:** the diagnostic's item content has drifted from what the redesigned course actually requires, or the cut score was set once and never revalidated against outcomes.
- **First question:** when was the cut score last validated against actual pass/fail outcomes at this institution, versus adopted from vendor-recommended defaults?
- **Data to pull:** a scatter of placement score against final course grade for the last two cohorts, with the current cut score marked.

### Students consistently drop the same logical step across multiple, structurally different proof problems
- **Usually means:** a specific conceptual misconception (e.g., confusing the order of quantifiers, treating a converse as equivalent to the original statement) rather than scattered carelessness.
- **First question:** is the same error type showing up across problems that share no surface features, meaning it's conceptual rather than problem-specific?
- **Data to pull:** an error-type tally across the last two exams' proof questions, categorized by logical-step type, not just marked right/wrong.

### TA grading turnaround routinely exceeds one week on weekly problem sets
- **Usually means:** feedback arrives after the next topic is already taught, breaking the formative loop the problem set was supposed to provide.
- **First question:** is the delay grading-volume-driven (too many exams per grader) or rubric-ambiguity-driven (graders stalling on edge cases)?
- **Data to pull:** grading turnaround time by TA and by assignment, and whether office-hour questions repeat content from problem sets not yet returned.

### A CAS-enabled section outperforms a non-CAS section by a wide margin on an identical common final
- **Usually means:** the final is measuring tool fluency or arithmetic execution rather than the conceptual understanding it was designed to test.
- **First question:** which specific questions drive the gap — are they modeling/interpretation questions (where CAS use is legitimate) or manipulation questions (where it isn't)?
- **Data to pull:** per-question score gap between CAS and non-CAS sections, not just the aggregate final score gap.
