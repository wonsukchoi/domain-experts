# Red flags

### A riving knife, blade guard, or anti-kickback device removed or bypassed for any reason
- **Usually means:** a real kickback risk is being accepted for a perceived convenience, regardless of how routine the cut seems.
- **First question:** is there any legitimate reason this safeguard shouldn't be in place for this specific cut, or is it being bypassed for convenience?
- **Data to pull:** the machine's safety procedure for this cut type, current safeguard status.

### A cut planned through a visible defect (knot, check, crack) on a structural part
- **Usually means:** yield is being prioritized over structural integrity at a load-bearing location.
- **First question:** does the final part's design tolerate a defect at this specific location, or does it require full-length structural continuity?
- **Data to pull:** the part's structural requirement, defect location relative to the planned cut.

### A rip blade used for crosscutting, or a crosscut blade used for ripping
- **Usually means:** a blade mismatch that both degrades cut quality and increases binding/kickback risk.
- **First question:** does the blade currently mounted match the cut type actually being performed?
- **Data to pull:** blade type/tooth configuration, cut type being performed.

### A band saw precision cut started without verifying blade tension and tracking
- **Usually means:** the blade may drift during the cut even with a correct fence setting, producing a non-straight part.
- **First question:** has blade tension and tracking been checked directly, or assumed adequate because the saw is running?
- **Data to pull:** blade tension gauge reading, tracking adjustment status.

### A blade used well past its usage-based service interval
- **Usually means:** both kickback risk and cut quality have likely degraded, even if cuts don't yet look visibly rough.
- **First question:** what is the blade's actual usage count/hours versus its specified service interval?
- **Data to pull:** blade usage log, service interval specification.

### A kickback event or near-miss not formally reported
- **Usually means:** the event was resolved without injury and treated as not worth documenting, losing the pattern-detection value of near-miss reporting.
- **First question:** does the near-miss/incident reporting system show an entry for this event?
- **Data to pull:** near-miss reporting log, verbal accounts if no formal report exists.

### A cut plan optimized purely for yield without checking whether the resulting piece meets a defect-free length requirement
- **Usually means:** the plan may assume yield math alone determines feasibility, without checking whether any valid clear span actually exists.
- **First question:** does at least one clear span on the material actually meet the required defect-free length?
- **Data to pull:** material defect map/locations, required clear-span length for the part.

### A workpiece feeding technique allowing the material to twist or pinch during a cut
- **Usually means:** an elevated kickback risk condition, independent of blade sharpness or type.
- **First question:** is the material being fed with consistent support and pressure preventing twist, or is technique inconsistent?
- **Data to pull:** feeding technique observation, any recent binding/kickback near-misses.

### A cut quality issue (drift, rough edge) diagnosed without checking blade type, sharpness, and alignment/tension separately
- **Usually means:** the actual root cause among several possible distinct causes hasn't been isolated.
- **First question:** has each potential cause (blade type match, sharpness, tension/alignment) been checked individually?
- **Data to pull:** blade type, usage hours, tension/alignment status.

### A new wood species or board dimension run without re-evaluating blade selection or feed technique
- **Usually means:** settings validated for a different material may not be appropriate for the new one.
- **First question:** does current blade selection and feed technique match what's appropriate for this specific material?
- **Data to pull:** current material characteristics, blade/technique currently in use.
