# Playbook

## Defect-avoidance cut planning worksheet

Fill for any board/panel with a visible defect and a length-critical part requirement.

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Board | #B-2291, 48" hardwood |
| Defect | Knot, 8"-10" zone |
| Required part | 40" clear-span structural leg blank |
| Available clear span 1 | 0"-8" (8") |
| Available clear span 2 | 10"-48" (38") |
| Does any span meet requirement? | No — both fall short of 40" |
| Decision | Divert board to non-structural use (38" apron rail); pull additional board for required leg blank |

**Rule:** always check whether an actual clear span meets the requirement — total board length and defect location together determine feasibility, not total length alone.

## Blade selection reference table (illustrative — always use the specific blade manufacturer's guidance)

| Cut type | Tooth count (typical) | Tooth geometry | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rip cut | Fewer (e.g., 24-30 teeth) | Flat-top or shallow angle, larger gullets | Fast material removal along grain |
| Crosscut | More (e.g., 60-80 teeth) | Alternating bevel, smaller gullets | Clean shearing across grain fibers |
| Combination | Moderate (e.g., 40-50 teeth) | Mixed geometry | General use, compromise between rip and crosscut performance |

**Rule:** dedicated rip and crosscut blades outperform a combination blade for their specific cut type — use the dedicated blade when cut volume and quality requirements justify a blade change.

## Kickback-prevention safety checklist

1. Confirm riving knife/splitter is installed and properly aligned with the blade before any rip cut.
2. Confirm blade guard is in place and functional.
3. Confirm anti-kickback pawls (where equipped) are present and functioning.
4. Never remove or bypass any of the above for any cut, regardless of perceived ease or necessity.
5. Confirm blade is sharp and appropriate for the cut type before starting.
6. Use consistent, controlled feed pressure throughout the cut — avoid any technique allowing the workpiece to twist or wander.
7. If a bind or unusual resistance is felt during a cut, stop feeding immediately rather than forcing through it.
8. Report any kickback event or near-miss immediately and in full detail, regardless of whether injury occurred.
