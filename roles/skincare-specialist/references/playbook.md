# Skincare Specialist — Playbook

## Fitzpatrick-to-peel-strength matrix

| Fitzpatrick type | Typical response | Superficial peel (e.g. 10-20% glycolic/salicylic) | Medium peel (e.g. 30-40% glycolic, TCA) |
|---|---|---|---|
| I-II | Rarely tans, burns easily | Standard strength | Standard strength, watch for prolonged erythema |
| III-IV | Tans moderately, occasional burn | Standard strength | Reduce one strength tier from standard; extend interval between treatments |
| V-VI | Rarely burns, tans deeply | Standard strength, patch test recommended | Reduce two strength tiers; strongly consider superficial-only; PIH risk elevated regardless of technique |

PIH = post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. The matrix shifts by barrier status (see below) independent of Fitzpatrick type — a compromised barrier downgrades strength regardless of type.

## Active-ingredient interaction reference

| At-home active | In-office treatment | Interaction | Recommended spacing |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prescription retinoid (tretinoin, adapalene, tazarotene) | Chemical peel, microdermabrasion | Thinned stratum corneum increases penetration depth and irritation risk | Pause 5-7 days before/after |
| OTC retinol | Chemical peel | Milder version of above, still elevates risk | Pause 2-3 days before/after |
| Isotretinoin (current or within 6 months) | Any peel, waxing, laser | Impaired healing, elevated scarring/PIH risk | No treatment; refer to prescribing physician for clearance timeline |
| AHA/BHA at-home exfoliant | Chemical peel | Compounds exfoliation depth beyond intended | Pause 2-3 days before in-office treatment |
| Benzoyl peroxide | Chemical peel | Can cause uneven peel penetration on treated areas | Note treated areas; avoid peel directly over recently treated breakouts |
| Photosensitizing medication (doxycycline, some diuretics) | Any UV-adjacent treatment, sun exposure post-peel | Elevated burn/PIH risk with sun exposure during healing | Confirm SPF compliance is realistic before proceeding; consider deferring in high-UV season |

## Rebook-interval defaults by strength (adjust per observed reaction)

| Treatment strength | Standard interval | Shorten if | Extend if |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superficial (enzyme, light AHA) | 2-3 weeks | — | Client reports prolonged sensitivity |
| Medium (30%+ glycolic, light TCA) | 3-4 weeks | Never — under-treating risk from shortening is low | Any visible PIH or prolonged erythema at follow-up |
| Deep (physician-administered only) | Outside esthetician scope | — | — |
