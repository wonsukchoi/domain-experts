# Skincare Specialist — Red Flags

### Lesion meeting any ABCDE sign (asymmetry, border irregularity, color variation, diameter >6mm, evolving)
- **Usually means:** Could be a benign nevus that simply looks atypical, or an early melanoma — an esthetician cannot distinguish the two visually.
- **First question:** "How long has this been there, and has it changed recently?"
- **Data to pull:** Photo documentation with date, referral note to dermatologist — do not treat, extract, or peel over it.

### Current or within-6-months isotretinoin (Accutane) use
- **Usually means:** Skin is thinned and healing capacity is impaired; standard exfoliation/peel/wax protocols carry elevated scarring and reaction risk.
- **First question:** "When did you stop, and did your prescribing doctor give you a clearance date for facials or peels?"
- **Data to pull:** Medication history on intake form, physician clearance if within 6 months.

### Active cold sore or open lesion in the treatment area
- **Usually means:** Risk of spreading active HSV infection to other skin areas or to the esthetician, or of introducing product/bacteria into broken skin.
- **First question:** "Is this active right now, or has it fully healed?"
- **Data to pull:** Visual confirmation of healing status; reschedule if active.

### Prescription retinoid use within 5-7 days of a scheduled peel
- **Usually means:** Stratum corneum is thinned; a standard-strength peel will penetrate deeper and more unevenly than intended.
- **First question:** "When did you last apply it, and how many nights a week are you using it?"
- **Data to pull:** Product name/strength and last-use date from intake.

### Client reports "no reactions ever" as justification for skipping a patch test on a new product/strength
- **Usually means:** Past tolerance doesn't predict future tolerance — skin state, medications, and sun exposure change between visits.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed since your last visit — new medications, sun exposure, other treatments?"
- **Data to pull:** Updated intake at every visit, not just the first one.

### Visible residual erythema or barrier compromise at the start of a scheduled treatment
- **Usually means:** The skin is already in a reactive state from a prior treatment, sun exposure, or an unreported irritant — layering another active treatment on top compounds the reaction.
- **First question:** "Have you had any sun exposure, new products, or treatments in the last week?"
- **Data to pull:** Visual comparison to the client's baseline/prior-visit photos if available.

### Client on a photosensitizing medication scheduled for an outdoor event shortly after treatment
- **Usually means:** Elevated burn and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation risk if sun exposure isn't strictly managed during the healing window.
- **First question:** "What's your sun-exposure plan for the next week?"
- **Data to pull:** Medication list, planned activities before booking a peel or aggressive exfoliation.

### Request to combine multiple at-home actives (retinoid + AHA/BHA) into the same nightly routine
- **Usually means:** Client is optimizing for speed of results without understanding compounded irritation risk.
- **First question:** "Are you currently using these together, or asking whether you should start?"
- **Data to pull:** Current at-home routine in detail — product names, frequency, order of application.

### Pregnancy or breastfeeding not disclosed until mid-consultation
- **Usually means:** Certain actives (retinoids, some acids, hydroquinone) have pregnancy-safety considerations the client may not know apply to topical/professional treatments, not just oral medications.
- **First question:** "Are you currently pregnant or breastfeeding?" (ask directly on every new-client intake, don't wait for volunteering)
- **Data to pull:** Confirm which planned products/treatments have a pregnancy caution and substitute accordingly.
