# Red flags

Signals that call for a stop-and-verify response rather than "keep an eye on it and continue."

### Trench 5 feet or deeper with no protective system visible

- **Usually means:** the competent-person inspection either hasn't happened yet today or documented an exception that isn't posted at the site — the second most likely cause is a crew that started digging before classification was done.
- **First question:** "Has the competent person inspected and classified this trench today, and where's the log entry?"
- **Data to pull:** today's excavation inspection log, soil classification on file, protective-system type specified for this depth/classification.

### Soil classified Type A or B from an old geotechnical report, but visible seepage or nearby vibration source present today

- **Usually means:** the crew is relying on a stale lab classification instead of reclassifying on-site — the report's conditions no longer match ground conditions.
- **First question:** "Is water actively entering the trench wall or base right now, and is there a vibration source (traffic, equipment, pile driving) within range?"
- **Data to pull:** today's on-site classification note, distance and type of nearby vibration source, timestamp of last rainfall or groundwater change.

### Spoil pile, equipment, or staged material within 2 feet of a trench edge

- **Usually means:** surcharge load added right where the wall is weakest, usually from staging by delivery convenience rather than by the setback rule.
- **First question:** "How far is the nearest spoil or staged load from the trench edge, measured, not eyeballed?"
- **Data to pull:** measured setback distance, weight/type of material staged, whether it sits over the sloped or shored side.

### Sloping doesn't reach the required offset before hitting a utility, structure, or property line

- **Usually means:** the site footprint can't support a full slope for the classified soil type at that depth — sloping is being attempted anyway instead of switching to shoring or shielding.
- **First question:** "What's the required offset for this depth and classification, and what's actually available before we hit the obstruction?"
- **Data to pull:** slope-ratio table value for the classification, measured distance to the nearest utility/structure/line, utility locate markings.

### Excavation reaching or exceeding 20 feet deep

- **Usually means:** the job has moved past the standard sloping/shoring tables into engineered-design territory, and work is continuing without a registered professional engineer's sign-off.
- **First question:** "Is there a stamped protective-system design on file for this depth, and does it match what's actually installed?"
- **Data to pull:** engineer-stamped drawing, depth as measured today (not as planned), protective system as built versus as designed.

### No designated signal person before a lift, or more than one person giving hand signals

- **Usually means:** the crew is treating signaling as informal, which is exactly how an operator ends up taking a signal from the wrong person or acting on a gesture nobody intended as a command.
- **First question:** "Who is the one person authorized to signal this operator right now, and does the operator know who that is?"
- **Data to pull:** signal-person designation for the shift, operator confirmation, whether the hand-signal set and radio channel were agreed before the first lift.

### Operator proceeds on a radio instruction without a matching hand signal

- **Usually means:** radio is being treated as the primary channel instead of the backup, which fails exactly when interference or a dead battery would otherwise have stopped the lift.
- **First question:** "Did the hand signal match what came over the radio, or did the load move on voice instruction alone?"
- **Data to pull:** radio log/callsign confirmation, signal person's account of the hand signal given, whether the two channels were ever cross-checked before this lift.

### Crane swing radius or load path overlaps an occupied trench, active excavation, or another crew's work area

- **Usually means:** two trades are each managing their own hazard zone without anyone checking whether the zones physically overlap in time or space.
- **First question:** "Is anyone inside the trench or work area while the boom is swinging over it right now?"
- **Data to pull:** marked exclusion zone boundaries, current crew locations, whether the two operations were sequenced or are running concurrently.

### Worker below or inside an excavation while material or a load passes overhead

- **Usually means:** staging or lift sequencing didn't account for who's currently below the load path — often because the crew below started work before the lift was scheduled or announced.
- **First question:** "Is everyone clear of the load path before this lift starts, confirmed by the signal person, not assumed?"
- **Data to pull:** signal person's pre-lift area clearance check, crew locations at time of lift, whether an all-clear was explicitly given.

### Marked utility line falls inside the required sloping offset

- **Usually means:** the standard slope for this soil type and depth would undermine or expose the utility before reaching the required angle — sloping isn't a viable option on that side regardless of how it's been done on similar trenches nearby.
- **First question:** "What's the measured distance to the marked utility, and does it clear the required offset for today's classification?"
- **Data to pull:** utility locate markings and depth, slope-ratio table offset for current classification/depth, utility company notification record if within the marked buffer.

### Same trench reopened a second time this week for a "quick fix" without re-classification

- **Usually means:** the crew is treating a prior day's classification as still valid instead of reclassifying current conditions — weather, adjacent work, or wall condition may have changed since the original inspection.
- **First question:** "Has this trench been re-inspected since it was last backfilled or left open, or are we working off the earlier log entry?"
- **Data to pull:** date of last competent-person inspection, any rainfall or nearby work since then, current wall condition versus the earlier entry.
