# Animal Trainer — Playbook

## Shaping-plan criteria ladder (filled example: "settle on mat" behavior)

| Step | Criterion | Reinforcement schedule | Advance when |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Any paw contact with mat | Continuous (every rep) | 80%+ over 2 sessions |
| 2 | Two paws on mat | Continuous | 80%+ over 2 sessions |
| 3 | Four paws on mat, standing | Continuous | 80%+ over 2 sessions |
| 4 | Sit on mat | Continuous | 80%+ over 3 sessions |
| 5 | Down on mat, held 3 sec | Continuous | 80%+ over 3 sessions |
| 6 | Down on mat, held 30 sec | Fixed-ratio 2 (every 2nd rep) | 80%+ over 3 sessions |
| 7 | Down on mat with mild distraction (owner moves) | Variable-ratio ~3 | 80%+ over 3 sessions |

Drop back one step (not to step 1) on three consecutive failures at the current step. Never skip a step to save time — the ladder exists because each step is the minimum viable increment; skipping produces exactly the "three consecutive failures" outcome this table is built to avoid.

| 2026-03-02 PM | Doorbell-sit | 10 | 9 | 90% | Fatigue (stopped at trial 8, 1 extra easy rep to close) | Latency increasing after trial 6 — likely satiation, not failure |

The "ended on" column matters as much as the rate: a session ended on fatigue with a closing win is fine; a session ended on the clock running out mid-failure is a flag to shorten the next session.

## Reinforcement-schedule thinning sequence

1. Continuous (100% of correct reps reinforced) — new-behavior acquisition phase.
2. Fixed-ratio 2 (every 2nd correct rep) — once acquisition criterion is met.
3. Variable-ratio 3 (average every 3rd rep, randomized) — once fixed-ratio-2 criterion is met; this is the schedule most resistant to extinction, and the target end-state for a maintained behavior.
4. Intermittent/social reinforcement only (praise, no food) — final maintenance stage for well-established behaviors, months into training, not attempted in the first weeks.

Never thin more than one stage at a time, and never thin a safety-relevant behavior (recall, "leave it" around hazards) past variable-ratio without an explicit case-by-case decision — some safety behaviors stay on continuous reinforcement indefinitely by design.

## Function-based intervention selection (filled decision table)

| Observed function | First-line intervention | If insufficient after 2 weeks |
|---|---|---|
| Attention-seeking (jumping, mouthing) | Reinforce incompatible alternative (sit for greeting); extinguish original via no-attention-for-behavior | Add antecedent management (leash/tether at door) during training window |
| Escape/avoidance (fear-based reactivity) | Desensitization + counter-conditioning, graduated exposure ladder | Refer to veterinary behaviorist for possible medication support alongside training |
| Access to resource (resource guarding) | Trade-up protocol (higher-value item offered for release), never forced removal | Management (feed separately, no toy-sharing) while trade-up protocol continues; refer if bite risk present |
| Automatic/self-reinforcing (excessive licking, spinning) | Rule out medical cause first; environmental enrichment | Veterinary referral — behavior may be neurological or compulsive, not purely learned |
