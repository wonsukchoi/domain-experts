# Athletic training playbook

Filled sequences and thresholds, not descriptions of them. Load this when building an EAP, running a concussion-to-return-to-sport sequence, or setting functional return-to-play gates.

## Emergency Action Plan (EAP) — venue template

Every venue gets its own filled version of this, posted at the athletic training room and rehearsed at least once per season (preseason, before the heat/contact risk window opens).

| Field | Filled example (home football stadium) |
|---|---|
| Primary responder | Head AT on-site; assistant AT covers sideline phone |
| EMS access point | Gate 4, northwest corner — cones placed pregame to keep it clear |
| Estimated EMS response time | 6–8 min from call (confirmed with local dispatch preseason) |
| Emergency equipment location | AED and cold-immersion tub at midfield medical station; spine board in AT room, 40 yd from field entrance |
| Chain of command if head AT is the one down | Assistant AT calls EMS, team physician (if present) takes clinical lead |
| Communication | Sideline AT carries radio + cell; venue has no cell dead zones confirmed preseason |
| Hospital of transport | Regional Medical Center ED, 9 min transport — confirmed capable of treating EHS (ice tubs on-site) |
| Rehearsal log | Walked EMS access + equipment retrieval, dated, signed by all staff, before week 1 of practice |

**Threshold for calling this "done":** every AT and student worker on staff can, without looking anything up, state the EMS access point, the equipment location, and who calls 911 if the primary responder is the one incapacitated. If any staff member can't answer cold, the rehearsal wasn't sufficient — repeat it.

## Exertional heat stroke — field protocol

1. **Recognize:** collapse or altered mental status during/after exertion in heat. CNS dysfunction (confusion, combativeness, irritability, loss of consciousness) is the trigger, not skin temperature or how hot it feels outside.
2. **Confirm with rectal temperature only.** Target action threshold: rectal temp ≥105°F with CNS dysfunction = presumptive EHS, cold-water immersion starts immediately, no waiting for a second reading.
3. **Cool first, transport second:** full-body cold-water immersion (ice bath, water temp 35–59°F) on-site; call EMS simultaneously but do not transport until cooling target is reached.
4. **Cooling target and expected rate:** stop immersion at ~102°F rectal (not 98.6°F — overshooting into hypothermia is its own risk). Expected cooling rate in properly iced water: 0.15–0.20°F/min. If measured rate is under 0.10°F/min, add more ice or check that torso/core (not just extremities) is immersed.
5. **Document temp at 5–10 min intervals** through immersion; this trend is what a receiving ED and any later review will ask for first.
6. **Handoff to EMS** with the full temp trend and time-to-cooling-initiation — receiving physicians treat EHS differently based on how fast cooling started.

## Concussion: sideline-to-return-to-sport sequence

**Sideline (minute 0–15):**
1. Remove from play immediately on any suspected concussion — no exceptions for score, position, or "he seems fine."
2. SCAT6 (or age-appropriate child SCAT) administered by a trained evaluator — symptom checklist, cognitive screen, balance (mBESS), and neuro exam.
3. Compare to preseason baseline SCAT/ImPACT score where available; without a baseline, use normative cutoffs but weight clinical judgment more heavily.
4. No same-day return regardless of score. Athlete removed for the remainder of the session/game.

**Days 1–3 (symptom monitoring):**
- Daily symptom checklist (headache, dizziness, fogginess, light/noise sensitivity, sleep disruption) — trend matters more than any single day's score.
- Relative rest (not strict darkness/isolation) for 24–48 hours, then gradual return to light cognitive activity as tolerated.

**Graduated Return to Sport (GRTS) — 6 stages, minimum 24 hours each:**

| Stage | Activity | Advance if... | Drop back one stage if... |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Symptom-limited daily activity | Symptoms at or below baseline for 24h | Any symptom increase |
| 2 | Light aerobic exercise (walk, stationary bike, <70% max HR) | No symptom increase during/after | Symptom increase during activity |
| 3 | Sport-specific exercise (running drills, no head impact) | No symptom increase | Symptom increase |
| 4 | Non-contact training drills (resistance training, harder cognitive load) | No symptom increase | Symptom increase |
| 5 | Full-contact practice, after medical clearance | Tolerates full practice load | Symptom increase |
| 6 | Return to competition | — | — |

**Never compress this to skip stages** even under a playoff-game deadline — the 24-hour floor per stage exists because symptom recurrence after exertion can take several hours to appear, not just minutes.

## Ankle sprain — functional return-to-play gate

1. **Rule out fracture first** via Ottawa Ankle Rules: bony tenderness at posterior 6 cm of either malleolus tip, or inability to bear weight for 4 steps immediately and in the exam room → X-ray before proceeding.
2. **Grade for communication, not for timeline:** Grade I (mild, minimal instability), II (moderate, some laxity), III (complete tear, gross instability) — document the grade, then move to functional testing regardless of grade.
3. **Functional testing gate for return** (all must pass, not just one):
   - Single-leg hop for distance: injured-side distance within 10% of uninjured side.
   - Single-leg hop for time (e.g., 6-meter timed hop): within 10% of uninjured side.
   - Star excursion / Y-balance reach: within ~4 cm of uninjured side in the anterior direction.
   - Pain-free single-leg heel raises: at least 20 reps, matching uninjured side within 20%.
4. **If any one metric fails,** continue rehab and retest in 3–5 days rather than clearing on the others alone — asymmetry in even one plane predicts re-injury.

## Preseason baseline testing — what to actually bank

- SCAT6/ImPACT baseline (concussion reference point).
- Isokinetic or handheld dynamometer strength for prior-injury joints (e.g., both knees if any ACL history in the athlete's record).
- Single-leg hop and Y-balance baseline for athletes with any prior lower-extremity surgery.
- Resting heart rate and, where feasible, sickle cell trait status (consent-based) — changes the exertional-collapse differential from "cramping" to "possible exertional sickling," which needs a different immediate response (oxygen, rapid cooling, urgent transport — not stretching the "cramp").
