# Red flags

### Collapse during/after exertion in heat with confusion or combativeness (not just fatigue)
- **Usually means:** Exertional heat stroke (CNS dysfunction + hyperthermia); second: exertional sickling in an athlete with sickle cell trait.
- **First question:** "What's the rectal temperature — not oral, not tympanic?"
- **Data to pull:** Rectal temp reading and time obtained; WBGT reading for the session; sickle cell trait status if on file.

### Headache or dizziness worsening in the hours after a head impact rather than improving
- **Usually means:** Standard post-concussive symptom trajectory is flat-to-improving; a *worsening* trend, especially with vomiting, one-sided weakness, or increasing drowsiness, points toward intracranial bleed (epidural/subdural).
- **First question:** "Is this better, worse, or the same as an hour ago?"
- **Data to pull:** Serial symptom checklist scores with timestamps; pupil check; any focal neuro deficit.

### Pain out of proportion to exam findings, especially with a tense compartment or pain on passive stretch
- **Usually means:** Compartment syndrome or occult fracture; second most likely: vascular injury.
- **First question:** "Does passive stretch of the muscle group reproduce pain far beyond what the exam findings explain?"
- **Data to pull:** Compartment pressure if available, distal pulses, sensation, and time since injury.

### Inability to bear weight for 4 steps, or bony tenderness at the malleolus tip, after an ankle injury
- **Usually means:** Fracture per Ottawa Ankle Rules — second: severe (Grade III) ligament tear with associated bony contusion.
- **First question:** "Can you take four steps right now, weight-bearing, in this room?"
- **Data to pull:** Ottawa Ankle Rules checklist result; imaging order status.

### Neck pain with midline cervical tenderness after an axial-load mechanism (spearing, pile-up, diving)
- **Usually means:** Cervical spine injury until cleared — second: cervical strain without instability.
- **First question:** "Is there any numbness, tingling, or weakness in the arms or legs right now?"
- **Data to pull:** Midline palpation result, distal neuro exam (strength/sensation all four limbs), mechanism description from witnesses.

### Chest pain, syncope, or palpitations during or right after exertion in an athlete under 35
- **Usually means:** Cardiac etiology (commotio cordis if recent chest impact, or an underlying structural condition like hypertrophic cardiomyopathy) — not "just winded."
- **First question:** "Did this start during exertion, and is there any family history of sudden cardiac death under 50?"
- **Data to pull:** AED/rhythm strip if applied, family cardiac history from intake form, timing relative to exertion or chest impact.

### Recurrent "ankle sprains" in the same athlete without a full functional-testing clearance between episodes
- **Usually means:** Chronic ankle instability from an under-rehabbed prior sprain — second: unaddressed peroneal muscle weakness.
- **First question:** "Did the last sprain actually pass hop-test and balance criteria, or did it get cleared on time-off-the-calendar alone?"
- **Data to pull:** Prior injury file, whether functional test scores exist for the last episode, current single-leg balance and hop symmetry.

### Adolescent athlete with localized pain at a growth plate (tibial tubercle, calcaneus, medial epicondyle)
- **Usually means:** Traction apophysitis (Osgood-Schlatter, Sever's) or, less commonly, a growth-plate (physeal) fracture — treat differently from an adult soft-tissue strain at the same location.
- **First question:** "Is this athlete still growing, and is the pain specifically at the growth plate versus the muscle belly?"
- **Data to pull:** Skeletal maturity/age, point of maximal tenderness, imaging if bony involvement suspected.

### Rapid, intentional weight loss before a certification weigh-in (wrestling, combat sports)
- **Usually means:** Dehydration-based weight cutting — raises exertional heat illness and acute kidney injury risk independent of any other exposure.
- **First question:** "How much weight, over how many hours, and by what method (fluid restriction, sauna, diuretics)?"
- **Data to pull:** Weight trend over the cut window, urine specific gravity if tested, method used.

### Female athlete with a stress fracture, menstrual irregularity, and low energy intake pattern
- **Usually means:** Female Athlete Triad / RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport) — the stress fracture is often the presenting symptom, not the whole problem.
- **First question:** "When was the last normal menstrual cycle, and has training volume or eating pattern changed recently?"
- **Data to pull:** Menstrual history, bone density scan if repeat stress fractures, nutrition intake screen.

### A "cleared to return" note with no documented functional test results attached
- **Usually means:** The clearance decision was made on time elapsed or subjective report rather than objective criteria — a liability gap, not just a paperwork gap.
- **First question:** "What specific test scores justify clearing this athlete today versus yesterday?"
- **Data to pull:** The functional test battery results (hop symmetry, balance, strength ratio) with numeric values and dates.
