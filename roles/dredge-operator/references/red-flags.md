# Red flags

Smell tests a dredge operator or project engineer catches before or during a job, with what the signal usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before anyone proceeds.

## 1. Bulk sediment chemistry exceeds the reference envelope but bioassay hasn't been scheduled before mobilization

**Usually means:** the testing timeline was compressed against the mobilization schedule, or the exceedance was treated as marginal and deprioritized rather than escalated to Tier 2 testing.

**First question:** "Has the 10-day bioassay been ordered for this testing unit, and what's the expected turnaround relative to when the dredge reaches it?"

**Data to pull:** bulk chemistry results vs. reference sediment values; testing-unit boundaries on the project map; lab turnaround estimate vs. planned dredge schedule for that reach.

## 2. Hydraulic cutterhead scheduled to dredge a testing unit whose classification isn't finalized

**Usually means:** the plant assignment was made on production-schedule logic before the disposal-classification result was in hand, assuming it would come back clean.

**First question:** "What is this unit's current classification, and does the plant assignment change if it comes back contaminated?"

**Data to pull:** testing-unit classification status; dredge-type-selection matrix (`playbook.md` §2); mobilization schedule for the plant currently assigned.

## 3. Turbidity reading within ~80% of the permit's numeric limit with no containment response staged

**Usually means:** the team is treating the compliance threshold as a hard stop rather than a trend to act on before it's crossed.

**First question:** "What's the current reading versus the compliance limit, and is containment already staged to deploy, or does it still need to be brought out?"

**Data to pull:** current and background NTU readings; compliance limit per the permit's turbidity criteria; silt-curtain location and readiness (`playbook.md` §5).

## 4. Post-dredge as-built survey shows controlling depth below project depth in any cell

**Usually means:** the dredge template depth was undershot in that cell, or the cross-section's shallowest point wasn't the one actually surveyed/dredged to template.

**First question:** "What is the exact controlling-depth reading in that cell, and how far short of project depth is it?"

**Data to pull:** as-built hydrographic survey by cell; project depth and contracted overdepth; dredge production log for that cell.

## 5. Slurry density gauge reads below ~10% solids for a sustained period

**Usually means:** the cutter or draghead isn't engaging material efficiently, excess water is being drawn in, or the pump is running against a target that no longer matches the sediment actually being encountered.

**First question:** "Is this a sediment-encounter problem, a cutter/draghead positioning problem, or a pump-setting problem?"

**Data to pull:** density/velocity gauge log over the shift; production rate (cy/hr) vs. planned; cutter or draghead position relative to the dredge template.

## 6. Pipeline velocity trending toward the critical (minimum transport) velocity for the grain size in the line

**Usually means:** solids content is being pushed up toward or past the efficient band, or pump head has degraded, either of which risks settling and a plug.

**First question:** "What's the current velocity against the critical velocity for this grain size and pipe diameter, and is solids content or pump condition driving the trend?"

**Data to pull:** real-time velocity and density gauge readings; grain-size data for the current reach; critical-velocity reference for the pipe diameter in use (`playbook.md` §4).

## 7. Contaminated material dredged hydraulically arrives at the CDF as a larger volume than the CDF cell was sized for

**Usually means:** the dilution added by hydraulic transport was not accounted for when the CDF's capacity was originally sized against the in-situ contaminated volume estimate.

**First question:** "What in-situ volume was this cell sized against, and what's the actual slurry volume being delivered at current solids content?"

**Data to pull:** CDF cell design capacity and retention-time assumptions; in-situ contaminated volume from the testing-unit estimate; actual delivered slurry volume and solids content.

## 8. Advance maintenance or overdepth yardage logged as pay quantity beyond the contracted allowance

**Usually means:** dredge depth is being tracked against a template that exceeds what the contract actually allows as paid overdepth, or a positioning/survey error is inflating the removed-volume calculation.

**First question:** "What's the contracted paid-overdepth allowance, and does the logged pay quantity for this reach stay inside it?"

**Data to pull:** contract's paid-overdepth clause; survey-based pay-quantity calculation for the reach; advance maintenance allowance separately from paid overdepth.

## 9. Repeated pipeline plug or clog events on the same reach without a change to solids target or velocity

**Usually means:** the density target set for this sediment isn't holding critical velocity in practice, and the response has been to clear the plug rather than adjust the operating parameter causing it.

**First question:** "What was the density/velocity reading immediately before each plug event, and has the target actually been adjusted since the first one?"

**Data to pull:** density/velocity gauge history around each plug event; maintenance/downtime log; whether the solids target was revised after the first occurrence.

## 10. Silt curtain not repositioned as the dredge advances into a new reach adjacent to sensitive habitat

**Usually means:** containment was staged for the original work area and treated as a one-time setup rather than something that moves with the active dredging location.

**First question:** "Where is the dredge working right now relative to where the curtain is actually deployed?"

**Data to pull:** current dredge/discharge position; silt-curtain deployment location and date last moved; habitat-sensitivity map for the reach being entered.
