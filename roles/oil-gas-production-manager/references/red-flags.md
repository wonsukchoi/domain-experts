# Red flags

### Production shortfall is diagnosed straight to reservoir decline without first ruling out measurement/allocation error
- **Usually means:** the diagnostic process skipped the cheapest check (meter calibration, allocation logic) and jumped to the most expensive explanation, risking capital spent on the wrong fix.
- **First question:** "has the metering and allocation chain been audited before we start attributing this to the reservoir?"
- **Data to pull:** meter calibration history and truck ticket reconciliation against SCADA-reported volumes for the shortfall period.

### A well integrity anomaly continues to produce without investigation while awaiting the "next scheduled" review
- **Usually means:** production continuity is being prioritized over an unconfirmed safety signal, exactly the pattern behind major industry incident case studies.
- **First question:** "why is this well still producing before the anomaly has been ruled out?"
- **Data to pull:** the well's pressure/integrity monitoring log against the facility's stated integrity investigation policy and timeline.

### Stop-work or stand-down calls decline in frequency while production targets are under pressure
- **Usually means:** stand-down authority is being quietly discouraged rather than reinforced, which is a leading indicator of normalized deviance building toward a larger incident.
- **First question:** "is the drop in stand-downs because conditions genuinely improved, or because people have stopped calling them?"
- **Data to pull:** stand-down/near-miss report frequency trend against production-target pressure timeline (e.g., proximity to quarter-end).

### Workover or intervention capital is allocated by well visibility/urgency rather than an NPV-per-dollar ranking
- **Usually means:** capital is following whichever well is generating the most operational noise or executive attention, not the wells with the strongest actual economic case.
- **First question:** "was this intervention ranked against alternatives by NPV per dollar, or approved because it was the most discussed well?"
- **Data to pull:** the intervention approval history against a reconstructed NPV/$ ranking of all candidate wells that cycle.

### Water cut trend is rising with no updated long-term field economics or handling cost model
- **Usually means:** the field's economics are being evaluated on current lifting cost alone, missing that produced water handling cost can independently drive a field to uneconomic status.
- **First question:** "when was the field's economic model last updated to reflect current water cut trajectory?"
- **Data to pull:** water cut trend over the last several years against the field's produced water handling cost per barrel.

### Shut-in decisions are made purely on commodity price without a formation damage or restart risk assessment
- **Usually means:** shut-in is being treated as a free, reversible decision, when restart can carry real, sometimes permanent reservoir productivity cost.
- **First question:** "has reservoir engineering assessed the formation damage risk for this specific well before shutting it in?"
- **Data to pull:** the shut-in decision memo and whether it references a formation damage/restart risk assessment.

### Royalty or reserves reporting reconciliation lags behind actual allocated volumes beyond the required regulatory cadence
- **Usually means:** reporting has fallen behind operational reality, risking a compliance or audit finding that compounds the longer it's unaddressed.
- **First question:** "how far behind is the reconciliation, and what's driving the lag?"
- **Data to pull:** the reporting reconciliation timeline against the regulatory cadence requirement.

### Repeated near-miss reports on the same equipment or procedure with no corrective action closed out
- **Usually means:** near-miss reporting is functioning as documentation but not as the operational health signal it's meant to be — the same risk is recurring because the underlying cause was never fixed.
- **First question:** "how many times has this specific equipment or procedure appeared in near-miss reports, and what happened after each one?"
- **Data to pull:** near-miss report log filtered by equipment/procedure, with corrective action status for each.

### Artificial lift equipment is run past manufacturer-recommended service interval to avoid production downtime
- **Usually means:** short-term production continuity is being prioritized over equipment reliability, likely producing a larger unplanned failure and longer downtime later.
- **First question:** "how far past the recommended service interval is this equipment, and what's the plan to bring it current?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment service history against manufacturer-recommended intervals for the affected units.

### Production forecast is built on a flat historical average rather than a decline curve baseline
- **Usually means:** the forecast doesn't reflect the field's actual expected trajectory, which will produce a false "shortfall" or false "on-plan" reading independent of real performance.
- **First question:** "is this forecast decline-curve-based, or is it just last year's average projected forward?"
- **Data to pull:** the forecast methodology documentation and a comparison against a proper decline curve fit for the same wells.
