# Red flags

Smell tests for an intake complaint, a unit coming out of seasonal storage, or a repeat visit.

### Heavy blue-white smoke and an oily-fouled spark plug on a 2-stroke, no mechanical noise
- **Usually means:** oil-mix ratio too rich, often from doubling a container's rated quantity against the actual fuel volume mixed, less commonly aged fuel/oil separation in a pre-mixed can.
- **First question:** "What oil and how much gas did you mix, and in what container?"
- **Data to pull:** customer's actual mix math (oil oz ÷ fuel oz mixed), spark plug deposit condition, spark arrestor screen carbon loading.

### Two-stroke engine seizes or won't turn over by hand shortly after a refuel, with no prior symptoms
- **Usually means:** a lean mix (too little oil, or straight gas with no oil at all) scored the cylinder wall or piston.
- **First question:** "Did you mix this fuel yourself, and do you recall the ratio, or was it leftover fuel from a different tool?"
- **Data to pull:** cylinder bore/piston visual inspection through the exhaust port, a compression test if the engine turns at all, source and age of the fuel used.

### No-start or rough idle after 3+ months of off-season storage, fuel not drained or stabilized
- **Usually means:** ethanol-blend fuel degraded and varnished the carburetor's fixed idle jet or metering diaphragm.
- **First question:** "Was fuel stabilizer added before storage, and was the carb run dry or left full?"
- **Data to pull:** fuel color/smell, idle jet inspection under magnification, diaphragm condition if diaphragm-type carb.

### Float-type carb floods at idle or won't hold a set idle speed
- **Usually means:** float level set too high, or a worn/leaking float needle and seat; less commonly a foam float that has absorbed fuel and sunk.
- **First question:** "Does fuel weep from the carb vent or muffler at idle?"
- **Data to pull:** float level measurement against spec, needle/seat wear inspection, float weight/fuel-absorption check.

### Post-2011 engine loses power under load or runs noticeably hotter than before, carb recently serviced with no improvement
- **Usually means:** the spark arrestor screen or catalytic muffler is carbon-blocked — an emissions-era failure point absent on pre-2000s equipment.
- **First question:** "Has the spark arrestor screen or muffler been checked, separate from the carb?"
- **Data to pull:** screen/muffler visual inspection for carbon blockage, exhaust back-pressure check if the shop has the tooling.

### Spark plug gap measured tighter than spec with oily, wet fouling
- **Usually means:** a rich mix ratio or excess oil is depositing carbon that's closing the gap, not a defective plug.
- **First question:** "When was the plug last replaced or gapped, and what mix ratio are you running?"
- **Data to pull:** gap measurement against spec (commonly 0.020–0.030 in), deposit color/type on the plug.

### Trimmer or blower runs fine at idle but bogs or dies only under throttle
- **Usually means:** a diaphragm carb metering lever out of height spec, a stiff or torn fuel-pump diaphragm limiting delivery under demand, or a partially clogged in-tank fuel filter.
- **First question:** "Does it recover if you prime it again mid-bog, or does it die completely?"
- **Data to pull:** metering lever height against spec, diaphragm condition, in-tank fuel filter condition.

### Same customer returns within one season with the same smoking or fouling complaint after a carb service
- **Usually means:** the root cause (mix-ratio math, fuel choice) was never addressed at the customer-behavior level — only the carb itself was serviced.
- **First question:** "What fuel and oil are you using now, and how are you measuring it?"
- **Data to pull:** service history for the unit, the customer's current mixing method, current spark plug condition compared against the first visit.

### Riding or walk-behind mower blade shows chip damage, imbalance, or a bent spindle, surfaced during an unrelated "won't start" intake
- **Usually means:** an impact (rock, curb, root) the customer didn't mention or didn't connect to the stated complaint; unaddressed vibration transmits through the crank and risks bearing damage.
- **First question:** "Has this mower hit anything recently — a rock, a curb, a root?"
- **Data to pull:** blade balance check, spindle/crankshaft runout, deck inspection for impact marks.

### Shop parts shelf shows carb kits and belts out of stock in March, or shear pins and scraper bars out of stock in November
- **Usually means:** ordering was reactive to the current bench's volume instead of anticipatory against the coming season's forecast — an inventory-planning failure, not a supplier shortage.
- **First question:** "When did we place the last seasonal parts order, and against what forecast?"
- **Data to pull:** prior-season sell-through by part number, current on-hand quantity against typical seasonal peak usage.
