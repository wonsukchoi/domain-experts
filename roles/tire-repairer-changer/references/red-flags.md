# Red flags

Smell tests a tire repairer/changer catches in the first minute of an inspection or a completed job review. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before deciding.

### Puncture within 1 in of the tread edge

**Usually means:** injury is in or crossing into the shoulder, not the crown — the repairable zone ends at the shoulder, not at "still looks like tread."

**First question:** "Where exactly does the injury sit relative to the shoulder groove, measured, not eyeballed?"

**Data to pull:** the tire demounted and the injury measured from the shoulder rib, not estimated with the tire still mounted.

### Puncture diameter over 1/4 in (6mm) in the tread

**Usually means:** the injury exceeds the repairable-injury size limit even though it's in the correct zone — a common customer assumption is "small enough" when it isn't.

**First question:** "Has this actually been measured against a 1/4 in gauge, or estimated by eye?"

**Data to pull:** direct measurement with a repair-unit sizing gauge; note whether the object still lodged in the tire is larger than the hole it made (nails often are).

### Customer requests "just a plug" on a tire that's going back into daily use

**Usually means:** either the customer doesn't know a plug-only fix isn't a standard repair, or a previous shop sold it to them as one and it's already failed once.

**First question:** "Is this a get-home fix with a mileage limit, or does this tire need to be good for the next two years?"

**Data to pull:** whether the tire has already been demounted and inspected; if not, nothing about repairability is known yet regardless of what's requested.

### TPMS light stays on after tire replacement or rotation

**Usually means:** the relearn procedure was skipped, interrupted, or run before tire pressures were set to placard spec — more often this than a failed sensor.

**First question:** "Was the relearn run after pressures were set, or before?"

**Data to pull:** system type (direct vs. indirect), the specific relearn procedure used, and whether it was completed start to finish without interruption.

### Customer asks to downgrade load range or speed rating to save cost

**Usually means:** price is being compared without reference to the vehicle's actual GAWR or towing/loading use — the cheaper tire may still clear the minimum on paper but mismatch the other three tires' construction and inflation spec.

**First question:** "What's this vehicle's placard GAWR, and what's it typically loaded to — empty commuter or towing/hauling regularly?"

**Data to pull:** door placard load range, speed rating, and GAWR; T&RA load table for the proposed downgrade tire at its rated inflation.

### Run-flat tire with a documented low-pressure or warning-light driving event

**Usually means:** the sidewall has already carried the vehicle's weight without air support for some distance — structural overload that predates whatever puncture is being evaluated.

**First question:** "How far was it driven with the pressure warning on or the tire visibly low?"

**Data to pull:** manufacturer's specific run-flat repair policy for this tire line; mileage logged since the warning, if the customer or vehicle data can supply it.

### Wheel comeback with a loose or clunking sound within the first week

**Usually means:** torque was applied in a single pass or a circular pattern instead of staged star-pattern passes, or the re-torque check at 25–50 miles was never scheduled or performed.

**First question:** "Was this wheel re-torqued after the initial install, and on what pattern was it originally torqued?"

**Data to pull:** the job's original torque log if kept; lug stud and nut condition for stretch or elongation; whether the customer was told about the re-torque interval.

### Tire DOT date code over 6 years old, tread still legal

**Usually means:** age-related degradation (dry rot, belt separation) is a real risk independent of tread depth, and a customer focused only on tread wear hasn't been told.

**First question:** "Has anyone shown you the manufacture date on this tire, separate from the tread depth?"

**Data to pull:** decoded DOT date code; visible sidewall cracking or dry rot; any manufacturer-specific age-based replacement guidance for that tire line.

### Mixed load ranges or speed ratings already on the vehicle at intake

**Usually means:** a prior repair or replacement was done piecemeal without checking axle-match, often from a different shop under cost pressure.

**First question:** "Were all four tires replaced at once, or one at a time as they failed?"

**Data to pull:** load range, speed rating, and size stamped on all four tires currently mounted, compared against the placard spec.

### Repeat air-loss complaint on a previously "repaired" tire

**Usually means:** the original fix was a plug-only or patch-only repair rather than a full combination repair, and it's failing along the path the inspection would have caught the first time.

**First question:** "Was this tire demounted for the original repair, or was it fixed with the tire still on the rim?"

**Data to pull:** any record of the original repair method; current demount and full re-inspection rather than a second surface-level patch.

### Torque spec applied without a factory reference for that specific wheel

**Usually means:** aftermarket wheels or a less common trim level may carry a different torque spec than the shop's default assumption, and using a generic number risks under- or over-torquing.

**First question:** "Is this the factory wheel, or an aftermarket wheel with its own manufacturer spec?"

**Data to pull:** wheel manufacturer's torque spec if aftermarket; vehicle-specific factory torque spec if OE.
