# Red flags

### A subsequent power increase is planned without accounting for the ongoing xenon transient from a preceding power reduction
- **Usually means:** available reactivity to complete the increase may be smaller than expected due to continued xenon buildup.
- **First question:** where in the xenon transient timeline (rising, near peak, decaying) does the planned evolution actually fall?
- **Data to pull:** time since the power change compared against this reactor's typical xenon transient timing curve.

### Shutdown margin verified by observing rod insertion positions rather than by the calculated worst-case (single stuck-rod) margin
- **Usually means:** actual available margin under the analyzed worst-case condition isn't confirmed, only a general impression of "rods look inserted."
- **First question:** what does the calculated shutdown margin value actually show, not just rod position observation?
- **Data to pull:** calculated shutdown margin value compared against rod position observation alone.

### A rod withdrawal/insertion move's reactivity effect is assumed equal to a prior move of the same physical distance at a different position in the rod's travel range
- **Usually means:** an unexpectedly larger or smaller reactivity change than anticipated, since rod worth is position-dependent.
- **First question:** what does the rod worth curve show at the current position versus the position of the prior comparison move?
- **Data to pull:** rod worth curve at the current position compared against the position of the prior comparison move.

### Actual reactivity/power response after a planned evolution differs meaningfully from the predicted response, and the difference isn't investigated
- **Usually means:** an unaccounted process variable or condition is affecting the reactor differently than modeled.
- **First question:** was the deviation investigated before proceeding to the next step?
- **Data to pull:** predicted versus actual reactivity/power response, and whether the deviation was investigated before the next step.

### An operating procedure's step sequence is reordered or a step is skipped based on individual judgment that it "doesn't matter" in this specific case
- **Usually means:** the plant may be placed in a configuration outside what's been formally analyzed as safe, even if the individual step seems physically redundant.
- **First question:** does the sequence as executed match the sequence as approved?
- **Data to pull:** procedure step sequence as executed compared against the approved sequence.

### A reactivity-affecting evolution proceeds without a documented pre-evolution prediction of expected response
- **Usually means:** deviations can only be caught reactively, after the fact, rather than proactively against an expected value.
- **First question:** was a predicted reactivity/power response actually documented before the evolution began?
- **Data to pull:** whether a predicted response was documented before the evolution began.

### Xenon condition (rising, peaked, decaying) not explicitly assessed or logged before planning the next reactivity-affecting evolution
- **Usually means:** a subsequent evolution could be planned into an unfavorable point in the xenon transient without anyone having explicitly checked.
- **First question:** does the xenon condition assessment actually cover the timing of the next planned evolution?
- **Data to pull:** xenon condition assessment/log compared against the timing of the next planned evolution.
