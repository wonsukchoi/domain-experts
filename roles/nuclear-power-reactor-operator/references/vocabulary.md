# Vocabulary

### Reactivity
A measure of how far a reactor is from criticality and, more operationally, the rate at which reactor power will change.
**In use:** "Power's stable, but we're still adding positive reactivity right now — that's what the trend will show in a few minutes."
**Common misuse:** Treating reactivity as synonymous with power level, when a reactor at a stable power level can still have reactivity being added or removed by an ongoing transient.

### Xenon-135 transient
The time-delayed buildup and decay of the neutron-absorbing fission product xenon-135 following a reactor power change, which continues affecting reactivity for hours after the change itself.
**In use:** "We're four hours into the xenon transient from that power reduction — still rising, nowhere near the peak yet."
**Common misuse:** Assuming reactivity effects of a power change are complete once immediate feedback effects (temperature, void) settle, ignoring the separate, delayed xenon effect that continues developing afterward.

### Shutdown margin
The calculated amount of negative reactivity available to shut down the reactor from its most reactive credible condition, assuming the single most reactive control rod fails to insert.
**In use:** "Shutdown margin calculation accounts for the stuck-rod case — that's what actually clears us for this evolution, not just how the rods look right now."
**Common misuse:** Verifying shutdown margin by visually confirming rod positions look adequately inserted, rather than by the actual worst-case-failure calculation.

### Control rod worth
The amount of reactivity change produced by moving a specific control rod or bank, which varies non-linearly with the rod's position in its travel range.
**In use:** "That six-inch withdrawal near mid-range is worth a lot more reactivity than the same six inches would be near full-out."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a given physical distance of rod movement produces the same reactivity change regardless of where in the rod's travel range the movement occurs.

### Rod worth curve
A plotted or tabulated relationship showing how a control rod's reactivity worth changes across its full range of travel, typically peaking near mid-range.
**In use:** "Check the rod worth curve before planning this withdrawal — we're moving through the steepest part of the range."
**Common misuse:** Treating rod worth as a single constant value rather than consulting the position-dependent curve for the specific evolution being planned.

### Procedural compliance
Following an approved operating procedure's exact specified sequence and steps, based on the principle that procedures encode analyzed safety margins for specific scenarios.
**In use:** "That step's staying in the sequence exactly as written — its purpose isn't always obvious from the physics alone."
**Common misuse:** Reordering or skipping a procedure step based on individual judgment that it seems redundant or unnecessary in the moment, when the step's actual purpose may not be obvious from general physics reasoning alone.

### Iodine-135 / xenon-135 precursor relationship
The fission product decay chain where iodine-135, itself a fission product, decays into xenon-135, meaning xenon concentration keeps rising from iodine decay even as direct xenon production from fission changes with power level.
**In use:** "Xenon's still climbing because the iodine inventory built up during full-power operation is still decaying into it."
**Common misuse:** Assuming xenon concentration tracks power level directly and immediately, rather than understanding the delayed buildup driven by the iodine precursor inventory.

### pcm (percent milli-rho)
A common unit for expressing small reactivity changes in reactor operations.
**In use:** "That rod move was worth about 40 pcm — small, but worth logging against the prediction."
**Common misuse:** Describing reactivity changes only in qualitative terms ("a little," "significant") rather than in the specific quantitative units operating procedures and predictions are actually expressed in.

### Reactivity prediction (pre-evolution)
The practice of calculating or estimating the expected reactivity/power response of a planned evolution before executing it, to compare against actual response.
**In use:** "Predicted response was logged before the withdrawal — actual came in close, no investigation needed."
**Common misuse:** Executing a reactivity-affecting evolution and only reacting to power/reactivity changes as they're observed, without a documented prediction to catch deviations against.

### Single-failure criterion (shutdown margin context)
The safety analysis principle that a system must be able to perform its safety function even assuming the single worst credible failure occurs, applied to shutdown margin as the assumption that the most reactive control rod fails to insert.
**In use:** "Margin's calculated against the worst-case stuck rod, per the single-failure criterion — that's the number that clears us, not 'all rods inserting fine.'"
**Common misuse:** Calculating or assuming adequate margin based on "all rods inserting normally" rather than the required worst-case single-failure assumption.
