# Red flags

Smell tests to catch before they become a roadside citation, an in-transit loss, or an injury report.

### 1. Trailer tandem or drive-tandem axle weight over 34,000 lb on a certified scale, with gross combination weight under 80,000 lb

**Usually means:** freight was loaded and balanced against delivery sequence or dock convenience without an axle-distribution check, not that the load is actually overweight overall.

**First question:** "What was the load plan's fore-aft weight split before this went on the scale?"

**Data to pull:** axle-scale ticket by axle group, load plan/manifest with piece positions, delivery-sequence requirements for that run.

### 2. Dock leveler crossed by powered equipment with no audible or visual lock confirmation logged

**Usually means:** the crew is trusting the leveler's apparent stability under foot traffic rather than confirming the mechanical or hydraulic lock actually engaged.

**First question:** "Who confirmed the lock state before the first forklift or pallet jack crossed, and how?"

**Data to pull:** leveler maintenance/inspection log, crew's pre-crossing checklist sign-off if one exists, leveler's rated capacity versus equipment gross weight.

### 3. Irregular or unpalletized freight loaded onto a partial (LTL) trailer with visible void space and no blocking or bracing

**Usually means:** the crew relied on tight stacking or shrink-wrap to hold the load, which passes a stationary inspection but not real braking deceleration.

**First question:** "What's securing this piece against forward movement specifically, not just what's touching it?"

**Data to pull:** load photos or diagram at dispatch, tiedown/dunnage count against the piece's weight and length.

### 4. A single high-capacity tiedown used where the minimum tiedown-count rule requires two

**Usually means:** the aggregate working-load-limit math was checked and passed, but the separate minimum-count-by-article-length rule wasn't checked at all.

**First question:** "Does this article's length require two tiedowns regardless of what one strap's WLL covers?"

**Data to pull:** 49 CFR 393.110 count table for the article's length, tiedown count actually applied.

### 5. A drum, roll, or slick-sided skid lifted or positioned using the same technique as a cased good with cutout handholds

**Usually means:** the crew is assuming good coupling (CM near 1.0) on freight that doesn't have handholds, understating the actual NIOSH lifting index for that piece.

**First question:** "Does this piece have real handholds, or is that assumption carrying over from carton-handling habit?"

**Data to pull:** piece specifications/photos, injury or near-miss log for that freight type.

### 6. A dock plate or leveler bridging a gap wider than its rated span, or showing visible deck cracking or delamination

**Usually means:** the gap or the equipment condition has drifted past what the leveler was rated for, and the crew is bridging it with dunnage or judgment instead of escalating.

**First question:** "What's the leveler's rated span and condition, measured against today's actual gap?"

**Data to pull:** leveler spec sheet, most recent inspection report, photo of current gap and deck condition.

### 7. Load-shift claims recurring on the same lane or route across multiple loads

**Usually means:** a systemic blocking/bracing gap for a specific freight type or trailer configuration on that lane, not a one-off loading error.

**First question:** "What freight type and trailer configuration show up across these claims, and is the securement method the same each time?"

**Data to pull:** claims history by lane and freight type, securement method logged per load on that lane.

### 8. Wheel chocks or trailer restraint (ICC bar / dock lock) not engaged before forklift or pallet jack traffic enters the trailer

**Usually means:** the crew is treating the leveler crossing as the only safety step, skipping the separate trailer-restraint check.

**First question:** "Was the trailer restrained independent of the leveler being locked?"

**Data to pull:** dock's restraint interlock log if the system has one, crew's pre-loading checklist.

### 9. Freight consistently loaded nose-heavy or tail-heavy on multi-stop LTL runs with no axle-balance step in the process

**Usually means:** delivery-sequence (last-on-first-off) convenience is fully driving load layout with no counterbalancing axle check built into the process.

**First question:** "At what point in loading this run does anyone check axle balance, versus just following the stop order?"

**Data to pull:** standard loading procedure for multi-stop runs, sample load plans from recent citations or near-misses.

### 10. Tandem-slide adjustment made to correct axle weight, with no confirmation the slide pins are fully seated

**Usually means:** the axle math was corrected on paper (or on a scale) but the physical slide wasn't verified pinned before the tractor moved.

**First question:** "Who physically checked the slide pins after that adjustment, and how?"

**Data to pull:** pre-trip inspection record, slide-adjustment log if the yard keeps one.

### 11. A piece's weight or dimensions on the manifest don't match what's physically on the dock

**Usually means:** a manifest data-entry error, a substituted piece, or freight that arrived misdescribed from the shipper — any of which breaks both the axle-distribution plan and the securement sizing built on the wrong number.

**First question:** "Was this piece weighed or measured at the dock, or is the manifest number taken on faith?"

**Data to pull:** dock scale reading for the piece, shipper's bill of lading, manifest as received.

### 12. New or seasonal loaders hitting load-time targets with no axle or securement rework flagged by inspection

**Usually means:** inspection sampling on newer crew members hasn't caught up to the load volume they're now moving, not that they're getting axle math and securement right from day one.

**First question:** "Is this loader's load-plan spot-check rate the same as a tenured loader's, or has it lapsed early?"

**Data to pull:** inspection/spot-check sampling rate by loader tenure, citation or claims history tied to that loader's loads.
