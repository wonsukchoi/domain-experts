# Red flags

### Time from adhesive application to final clamping exceeds the specified open time, even though pot life wasn't exceeded
- **Usually means:** reduced bond strength from surface skinning or partial cure before clamping — a failure mode distinct from a pot-life violation.
- **First question:** what was the actual elapsed time from application to clamping versus the specified open time?
- **Data to pull:** application timestamp compared against final clamp timestamp and the open time specification.

### Bond line thickness controlled only by clamping pressure or squeeze-out amount rather than calibrated spacers or fixture stops
- **Usually means:** BLT may be outside the adhesive's specified optimal range, either starving the joint or over-thickening it.
- **First question:** what does the actual measured BLT show versus the adhesive's specified range?
- **Data to pull:** actual measured BLT compared against the adhesive's specified range, and the method used to control it.

### Bonding surface accepted as prepared based on visual cleanliness alone, with no surface energy or water-break test performed
- **Usually means:** invisible contamination — oils, mold release agent residue — could prevent proper adhesion.
- **First question:** what does the surface prep verification test actually show for this specific surface?
- **Data to pull:** surface prep verification test result compared against the specification.

### Cured/bonded assembly released for use based on chamber/oven setpoint and elapsed time rather than verified actual part temperature
- **Usually means:** the part itself may not have actually reached full cure even if the equipment showed correct settings.
- **First question:** does the thermocouple/data logger record show the actual part reached and held cure temperature for the specified duration?
- **Data to pull:** thermocouple/data logger record of actual part temperature compared against the chamber setpoint and specified cure profile.

### A gap or discontinuity in squeeze-out along a joint's length is noted but not investigated as a possible coverage void
- **Usually means:** an actual adhesive coverage gap at that location, not just cosmetic squeeze-out variation.
- **First question:** was any follow-up inspection (e.g., ultrasonic) performed at the specific gap location?
- **Data to pull:** squeeze-out continuity inspection notes by joint location, and whether follow-up inspection was performed.

### A recurring bond strength or adhesion failure pattern appears across multiple parts/joints without being traced to a specific process variable
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — BLT, open time, surface prep, cure profile — rather than isolated one-off part variation.
- **First question:** does the failure occurrence log correlate with a specific process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** failure occurrence log compared against process parameter logs (BLT, timeline, cure profile) over the same period.

### Adhesive batch/lot changed mid-production without re-verifying open time, pot life, or cure schedule parameters for the new batch
- **Usually means:** the new batch's actual working properties may differ from what the process was set up for.
- **First question:** were parameters actually re-verified for this new batch before production continued?
- **Data to pull:** adhesive batch/lot ID compared against whether parameters were re-verified for the new batch.
