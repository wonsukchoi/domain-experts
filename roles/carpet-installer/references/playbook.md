# Carpet Installer Playbook

Filled protocols and tables for the jobs referenced in SKILL.md. Numbers are the stated defaults from CRI 104/105 and ASTM test methods; a specific adhesive or goods manufacturer's spec sheet always overrides these when it states its own tolerance.

## Moisture-test protocol (glue-down over concrete)

**Test count by area (ASTM F1869 calcium chloride):**

| Area | Minimum test locations |
|---|---|
| First 1,000 sq ft | 3 |
| Each additional 1,000 sq ft (or fraction) | +1 |
| Example: 1,800 sq ft | 3 + 1 = 4 |
| Example: 3,200 sq ft | 3 + 3 = 6 |

- Condition the space to normal service temperature/humidity for 48 hours before testing; test at 65-85°F air temp, 40-60% RH.
- Dome sits for 60-72 hours per kit instructions; result reported in lbs MVER/1,000 ft²/24hr.
- Run ASTM F2170 RH probes alongside CaCl tests when the adhesive spec cites an RH limit — drill to 40% of slab depth (or per manufacturer), let probes equilibrate a minimum of 24 hours before reading.

**Adhesive selection by reading (typical thresholds — verify against the specific product sheet before ordering):**

| MVER (lbs/1,000ft²/24hr) | RH (%) | Adhesive class |
|---|---|---|
| ≤ 3 | ≤ 75% | Standard pressure-sensitive adhesive (PSA) |
| 3-8 | 75-85% | Moisture-tolerant urethane adhesive |
| > 8 | > 85% | Two-part epoxy moisture vapor barrier, then standard or urethane adhesive over it |

- Readings that land right at a threshold boundary (e.g., RH at 84-86%) get treated as the higher tier — the cost of over-mitigating by one tier is far smaller than a callback under warranty.
- Document every reading with location, date, and technician on the change order or submittal — this is the only record if a warranty claim comes later.

## Seam-layout decision sequence

1. Sketch the room, mark all doorways, windows, and the main light source (largest window or highest-lumen fixture).
2. Identify which walls exceed the goods roll width (12 ft standard broadloom) — those walls force at least one seam.
3. Draw candidate seam lines parallel to the main light source, positioned in the lowest-traffic third of the room.
4. Reject any candidate line that crosses a doorway threshold, sits under direct overhead lighting perpendicular to the seam, or falls inside the primary walking path between two doors.
5. Cross-check the chosen layout against the pattern repeat (for patterned goods) — shift the seam along the same wall if needed to land on a repeat boundary rather than mid-pattern.
6. Confirm all rolls for the job share the same dye lot / run number before cutting to this layout.

**Example layout — 16 ft × 20 ft great room, 12 ft roll, door at the 16 ft wall's midpoint, window bank on the opposite 20 ft wall:**

- Run goods the 20 ft length, breadths parallel to the window wall.
- Breadth 1 (12 ft): covers from the window wall to 12 ft in.
- Breadth 2 (4 ft): covers the remaining 4 ft, seam falls 12 ft from the window wall — inside the room's back third, parallel to window light, clear of the doorway's direct sightline.
- Cushion seam offset 90° from this carpet seam line (running the other direction) per CRI 105's right-angle-or-6"-offset rule.

## Power-stretch sequence (stretch-in installation)

1. Install tackless strip 3/8"-1/2" off every wall, gripper pins angled toward the wall.
2. Rough-cut and seam the carpet with roughly 2-3" of excess at each wall for trimming after stretch.
3. Hook the carpet onto the tackless strip along the longest wall first (the anchor wall) by hand or knee kicker — positioning only, no stretch tension yet.
4. Set the power stretcher's tail block against the anchor wall; extend tubes to reach the opposite wall (add extension tubes in ~4'8" increments for rooms wider than the base tool's reach).
5. Stretch and hook in a fan pattern from the center of the opposite wall outward to each corner, at roughly a 15° angle off perpendicular to the wall, achieving CRI's target of 1-1.5% total elongation — carpet should require firm hand pressure to hook, not go on slack.
6. Move to the remaining two walls and repeat the stretch-and-hook sequence; use the knee kicker only for final hooking in tight corners and closets the power stretcher can't reach.
7. Trim excess at each wall with a wall trimmer, tuck the edge cleanly behind the tackless strip.

## Stair installation method selection

| Method | Use when | Labor per step (typical) |
|---|---|---|
| Waterfall | Cut-pile/plush goods, standard riser depth, budget-conscious job | ~15-20 min |
| French cap (mitered) | Client wants a tailored/finished look, patterned goods, or non-standard riser | ~30-40 min |

- Waterfall: carpet runs continuously over the nose and down the riser without being tucked at the back of the tread — faster, forgives riser-depth variance.
- French cap: carpet is cut and mitered at the back of each tread, tucked and tacked individually — precise but roughly double the labor.
