## 1. Spotting session notes (filled example — TV drama, episode 4)

Taken live during the spotting session, timecoded against the picture lock cut. This is the document the composer leaves with.

| # | In (TC) | Out (TC) | Scene | Function | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4M1 | 00:01:12:00 | 00:02:40:10 | Cold open, kitchen | Underscore, tension build | Needs to land hard on the door slam at 02:38:00 |
| 4M2 | 00:07:20:00 | 00:07:55:00 | Flashback transition | Theme (protagonist motif), sparse | Recurring theme cue — flag for cue-sheet theme category |
| 4M3 | 00:14:05:00 | 00:14:05:00 | Radio in car | Source (diegetic) | Needle-drop or original source cue — licensed separately from underscore |
| 4M4 | 00:22:10:00 | 00:24:50:15 | Confrontation | Underscore, driving to silence | Cut music completely at 24:45:00 — silence is the choice |
| 4M5 | 00:41:30:00 | 00:43:12:00 | Closing montage | Theme, full statement | Season-arc payoff cue — reserve the fullest orchestration for this one |

**Rule:** every cue gets a function label (underscore / theme / source) at the spotting session itself, not left for the cue-sheet pass later — this is where theme and source get missed.

## 3. Rehearsal-minute budgeting table (concert programming)

Filled example — 63-minute subscription program, 4 rehearsal services of 2.5 hours (150 min) each:

| Work | Runtime | Familiarity multiplier | Raw rehearsal-minute need |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overture (standard rep) | 10 min | 0.75x | 7.5 min |
| Beethoven Symphony No. 7 | 35 min | 1x (known idiom) | 35 min |
| New commission (premiere, complex meter) | 18 min | 4x | 72 min |
| **Total need** | 63 min | | **114.5 min** |

Net usable minutes per service: 150 minutes minus a mandated 20-minute break per 80 minutes and ~10 minutes of stage/tuning setup ≈ 110 net minutes; four services ≈ 440 net minutes.

**Sequencing rule:** schedule the premiere across the first two services (while players are freshest and problems can still surface with two services left to fix them), not compressed into the last service before dress. Multiplier values above are a stated heuristic — no published industry standard — recalibrate per ensemble and conductor after a season or two of your own data.

## 5. Season/concert programming feasibility check

Before a program is locked, run down this list — publishers can't disclose rental rates, so treat every number as "confirm with quote," not as fixed:

1. **Rental window:** standard hire period is ~10 weeks pre-performance; request materials earlier and expect an administrative fee (illustrative figure from one major publisher: ~$20/part plus shipping) — confirm current terms with the specific publisher's Hire Fee Calculator.
2. **Orchestration size vs. hall/budget:** stacking two large-orchestration rental works on one program compounds hire fees and stage-plot logistics (risers, extra chairs, cartage) — check both against the venue and budget before announcing the program, not after.
3. **New/co-commissioned work:** confirm parts delivery date against the rehearsal-minute table above; a commission delivered inside the 2–4 week prep window (see SKILL.md heuristic) is a rehearsal-time risk, not just a paperwork one.
4. **Perusal vs. hire:** perusal scores are for evaluation only — never assume they're performance-ready; order hire materials as soon as the piece is confirmed, not the week of first rehearsal.
