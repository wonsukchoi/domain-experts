# Red flags — signals a wind energy development manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can be manageable; two or more on the same project stage is a pattern worth an immediate stage-gate review before further spend.

### Detailed engineering or full permitting spend authorized before the interconnection system impact study returns
- **Usually means:** the project is being advanced on the optimistic assumption that interconnection costs will be manageable, when the study result is frequently the single biggest kill-risk — spending here before that result is in is the most common way a developer wastes real capital on an unviable project.
- **First question:** "Has the system impact study returned, and does the project's economics still clear the bar at the studied cost allocation?"
- **Data to pull:** SIS/facilities study status and results, current project economics model, cost-allocation stress test (see `references/playbook.md` §2).

### Land option term shorter than the realistic cumulative timeline for remaining interconnection and permitting gates
- **Usually means:** the developer will face a forced decision — rush a gate under expiration pressure or lose site control — that a longer negotiated term or extension right would have avoided.
- **First question:** "What's the option's remaining term against the worst-case timeline for interconnection restudy plus permitting plus financial close?"
- **Data to pull:** option agreement term and extension rights, current stage-gate timeline estimate, land option term matrix (see `references/playbook.md` §3).

### Environmental/species-habitat screening not conducted until after site layout and interconnection point are finalized
- **Usually means:** a species-habitat conflict discovered at this stage forces an expensive redesign that also risks reopening the interconnection cost allocation, rather than being caught while the layout was still flexible.
- **First question:** "Was a desktop environmental/species screening run before the site layout was locked, or only as part of the formal permit application?"
- **Data to pull:** environmental screening report and its date, site layout finalization date, interconnection application date.

### Interconnection cost allocation accepted as final without checking for a cluster cost-share or restudy opportunity
- **Usually means:** the project may be absorbing the full cost of a network upgrade that could be shared across a queue cluster, or that a competing project's withdrawal could reduce — an unchecked assumption that costs the project real economics.
- **First question:** "Has a restudy or cost-share appeal been requested, or is the current allocation being treated as final without challenge?"
- **Data to pull:** interconnection queue cluster composition, any recent withdrawals in the same cluster, restudy request status.

### PPA price fixed and executed before tax-credit safe-harbor status is confirmed
- **Usually means:** the offtake price was negotiated on an assumption about tax-credit value that may not hold if the safe-harbor deadline is later missed, changing project economics after the contract is already locked.
- **First question:** "Is the safe-harbor documentation (5% cost test or physical-work test evidence) complete and dated before this deadline, or still pending?"
- **Data to pull:** safe-harbor deadline tracker (see `references/playbook.md` §4), procurement contracts and invoices supporting the cost test, PPA execution date relative to safe-harbor confirmation.

### Local government or community engagement not initiated until the formal permit application is filed
- **Usually means:** opposition is likely to surface for the first time at a public hearing, after significant capital is already committed, rather than earlier when the site plan was still flexible enough to address concerns.
- **First question:** "What's the stakeholder engagement log show for touchpoints before the application date, if any?"
- **Data to pull:** stakeholder engagement log, permit application timeline, any recorded community concerns from prior similar projects in the same jurisdiction.

### Wind resource assessment data covering less than the bankability-standard measurement period
- **Usually means:** lenders and tax equity investors typically require a multi-year (or long-term-correlated) wind data record to underwrite the project; a short measurement period risks a financing delay discovered late in the process.
- **First question:** "How many years of on-site measurement data exist, and has it been correlated to a long-term reference dataset?"
- **Data to pull:** met tower/lidar measurement period and data quality report, long-term correlation methodology and reference station used.

### A queue cluster restudy or withdrawal event occurring with no process to monitor or respond to it
- **Usually means:** a cost-allocation-reducing opportunity (or a cost-increasing one, if another project's withdrawal shifts more upgrade cost onto this project) could pass unnoticed without an active monitoring process.
- **First question:** "Who is tracking queue cluster composition changes, and how often is that checked against this project's cost allocation?"
- **Data to pull:** RTO/ISO queue status reports for the cluster, monitoring cadence and responsible party, any recent cluster composition changes.

### Safe-harbor documentation incomplete within 90 days of the applicable beginning-of-construction deadline
- **Usually means:** the procurement schedule hasn't been aligned to the tax-credit deadline as a hard constraint, risking a missed safe-harbor that changes project economics by the full credit value.
- **First question:** "What specifically is missing from the 5% cost test or physical-work test documentation, and can it be completed before the deadline?"
- **Data to pull:** safe-harbor deadline tracker, procurement contract and invoice status, physical-work evidence if pursuing that test instead.
