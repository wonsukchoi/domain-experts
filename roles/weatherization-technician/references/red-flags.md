# Weatherization Technician Red Flags

### Worst-case CAZ depressurization test skipped or missing from the job file after air sealing
- **Usually means:** the technician retested the blower door but not combustion safety after a pressure-changing measure; second most likely: the pre-work test was assumed to still hold.
- **First question:** was CAZ depressurization, draft, and spillage retested after the specific measure that changed the house's air-leakage rate, or only at intake?
- **Data to pull:** the combustion-safety test log, dated against the air-sealing work order line item.

### Post-work CFM50 reduction ≥20% from baseline with no ASHRAE 62.2 worksheet on file
- **Usually means:** the ventilation-assessment trigger was hit but the mechanical-ventilation calculation was never run; second: it was run informally and not documented.
- **First question:** what was the baseline-to-final CFM50 reduction, and where is the 62.2 required-CFM calculation for this job?
- **Data to pull:** pre- and post-work blower-door reports, the job's ventilation worksheet if one exists.

### Measures installed out of the audit tool's SIR-ranked order with no documented reason
- **Usually means:** the crew sequenced work to the client's preference or truck inventory instead of the priority list; second: a change order was made verbally and never logged.
- **First question:** does the as-installed measure order match the as-audited SIR ranking, and if not, what's the documented reason?
- **Data to pull:** the audit tool's output priority list versus the invoice or completion report's installed-measure order.

### A deferral-trigger condition (active mold, untested vermiculite, active vermin, structural hazard) noted at intake but work proceeded anyway
- **Usually means:** the technician judged the condition minor without documented testing or the program's mitigation path; second: schedule or client pressure overrode the deferral policy.
- **First question:** was the condition tested/remediated and documented, or does the intake note show it unresolved when work began?
- **Data to pull:** the intake walkthrough checklist, any lab test result or remediation invoice, the program's deferral policy for that hazard category.

### Ambient or flue-gas CO reading above the test protocol's action level at final walkthrough
- **Usually means:** an appliance combustion-safety issue wasn't fully resolved despite a passing draft/spillage result; second: an unrelated appliance (range, space heater) is contributing CO the CAZ test on the primary appliance didn't catch.
- **First question:** which appliance is the source, and does its individual test (draft, spillage, flue CO) actually pass at the stated thresholds?
- **Data to pull:** the combustion analyzer readout with appliance-by-appliance breakdown, not just a single ambient number.

### Job cost exceeds the program's per-unit budget cap with no change-order documentation
- **Usually means:** health-and-safety spending wasn't priced against the cap before SIR-ranked measures were funded, forcing an overrun; second: scope crept mid-job without re-running the audit.
- **First question:** was the mandatory H&S spend accounted for before the SIR-ranked measures were funded, and is there a signed change order for the overage?
- **Data to pull:** the audit tool's budget summary, the final invoice, any change-order paperwork.

### Insulation installed over a shared-flue water heater without checking the flue after a furnace replacement (orphaned water heater)
- **Usually means:** the furnace was replaced with high-efficiency, sealed-combustion equipment in the same scope, leaving an atmospheric water heater on a flue now oversized for its reduced load, degrading draft.
- **First question:** is the water heater still sharing a flue sized for the original furnace's larger load, and has draft been retested since the furnace was replaced?
- **Data to pull:** the furnace replacement work order, a current draft test at the water heater, the flue's diameter versus the manufacturer's sizing table for the remaining connected load alone.

### Final job file missing the baseline-to-final blower-door comparison or the combustion-safety pass results
- **Usually means:** documentation was done for the SIR-driving measures but the safety-testing paperwork wasn't carried through to the completion report.
- **First question:** does the completion report include both baseline and final numbers for blower door and combustion safety, or only the final?
- **Data to pull:** the program's required documentation checklist against what's actually in the submitted job file.

### Client reports a carbon monoxide alarm or combustion-odor complaint within 30 days of job completion
- **Usually means:** a combustion-safety mitigation that passed the on-site test didn't hold under real operating conditions (seasonal exhaust-fan use, a door left closed that wasn't during testing); second: an appliance issue unrelated to the weatherization work.
- **First question:** what changed between the passing on-site test and the complaint — different exhaust-fan usage, a door/window configuration, a different appliance now in use?
- **Data to pull:** the original CAZ test conditions and results, a re-test under the reported conditions, any fire-department or utility CO-call report.

### Storm windows, replacement windows, or another low-SIR measure installed despite SIR < 1.0 on the audit
- **Usually means:** the client requested it directly and the crew installed it as a courtesy or side job billed through the program invoice anyway; second: the audit was re-run with inputs adjusted until the measure cleared 1.0.
- **First question:** does the invoiced measure's SIR on file match what the audit tool actually computed, or was it altered to justify the install?
- **Data to pull:** the original, unedited audit tool output file, timestamped before the invoice.
