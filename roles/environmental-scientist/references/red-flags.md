# Environmental Scientist — Red Flags

### Non-detect result with a detection limit at or above the regulatory action level
- **Usually means:** the lab method wasn't sensitive enough for this site's action level, or the wrong method was requested — not that the site is clean.
- **First question:** "What's the detection limit relative to the action level, and does the lab have a lower-limit method available?"
- **Data to pull:** the lab report's method detection limit (MDL) and reporting limit (RL) columns, compared line-by-line against the jurisdiction's action-level table.

### Phase I records review relying only on a single regulatory database (EDR) report with no Sanborn/aerial cross-check
- **Usually means:** historical uses predating standardized environmental recordkeeping (pre-1980s) will be missed — database coverage is thin before that era.
- **First question:** "What's the earliest year this database has reliable coverage for this jurisdiction?"
- **Data to pull:** Sanborn fire insurance maps, historical aerial photo series, city directory listings for the parcel.

### REC determination made without a site reconnaissance visit
- **Usually means:** a desktop-only Phase I, which ASTM E1527 doesn't consider adequate on its own — visual evidence (staining, stressed vegetation, odors) can surface RECs no record shows.
- **First question:** "Was a site walk conducted, and by whom, on what date?"
- **Data to pull:** site reconnaissance field notes and photo log.

### Phase II sampling density that doesn't match the CSM's identified source areas
- **Usually means:** borings were placed on a generic grid instead of targeted at the documented source (e.g., the former UST location, the lift pit) — a common cost-cutting shortcut that produces false negatives.
- **First question:** "Where exactly is each boring relative to the documented source location, and why?"
- **Data to pull:** the boring location map overlaid on the historical source-location sketch.

### Groundwater pathway assumed "not complete" without a groundwater sample
- **Usually means:** the assessment inferred no pathway from depth-to-water estimates alone, without confirming actual groundwater quality — depth-to-water doesn't rule out vapor intrusion or lateral migration to a downgradient well.
- **First question:** "Has groundwater actually been sampled, or is this an inference from geology alone?"
- **Data to pull:** groundwater monitoring well logs or grab-sample lab results, if any exist.

### A fate-and-transport model result quoted as a specific future concentration
- **Usually means:** the model's screening-level output (an order-of-magnitude estimate under stated assumptions) is being over-interpreted as a precise prediction — often in a report meant for a non-technical audience.
- **First question:** "What retardation factor, degradation rate, and source-persistence assumptions went into this model, and how sensitive is the output to each?"
- **Data to pull:** the model's input-parameter sheet and a sensitivity run with +/-50% on the degradation rate.

### NEPA significance determination based on the action's absolute footprint rather than the delta from baseline
- **Usually means:** the analysis compared the proposed action against zero impact instead of against existing baseline conditions and cumulative effects from other pending actions in the area.
- **First question:** "What are the other reasonably foreseeable actions in the cumulative effects study area, and were they included?"
- **Data to pull:** the cumulative impacts table and the list of other NEPA actions pending in the same geographic scope.

### A CREC (Controlled REC) carried forward from a prior Phase I without re-verifying the institutional/engineering controls are still in place
- **Usually means:** deed restrictions, vapor barriers, or cap-and-cover systems noted years ago may have lapsed, been removed during later construction, or never been recorded properly.
- **First question:** "When was the institutional control last field-verified, and is it recorded against the current deed?"
- **Data to pull:** current title report and a site visit confirming the physical control (cap, barrier, fencing) is intact.

### Ecological risk screening using a generic (not site-specific) receptor list
- **Usually means:** the screening used a default regional species list instead of confirming which receptors (threatened/endangered species, sensitive habitats) actually occur at or near the site.
- **First question:** "Has a current species-occurrence database (e.g., state natural heritage program) been checked for this specific parcel?"
- **Data to pull:** the state/federal threatened-and-endangered-species database query results for the site's coordinates, dated within the last 12 months.
