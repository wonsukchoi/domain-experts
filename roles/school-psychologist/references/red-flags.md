# School Psychologist — Red Flags

### Intervention fidelity below 80% during the RTI window being used for eligibility
- **Usually means:** Data reflects an implementation problem, not the student's true response to intervention — a common ground to challenge an eligibility denial or approval.
- **First question:** What percentage of prescribed sessions were delivered, and by whom (licensed interventionist vs. rotating aide)?
- **Data to pull:** Fidelity checklist logs, session-delivery records, interventionist qualifications.

### Growth-rate comparison made only against the student's own baseline, not a peer/typical-growth norm
- **Usually means:** The team has level data but not rate data — dual discrepancy can't be established, weakening an SLD finding.
- **First question:** What is the grade-level or local peer growth-rate norm for this skill and time of year?
- **Data to pull:** District or national growth-rate norms (e.g., Hasbrouck & Tindal), peer comparison-group data.

### Eligibility report lists testing results with no explicit exclusionary-factor section
- **Usually means:** The team hasn't documented ruling out instruction, cultural/economic disadvantage, or sensory/motor causes — a due-process vulnerability even if the underlying determination is correct.
- **First question:** Where in the report is each exclusionary factor addressed with specific evidence, not just a checkbox?
- **Data to pull:** Vision/hearing screening results, attendance records, home-language survey, discipline/counseling history.

### FBA built entirely from interview/rating-scale data, no direct ABC observation
- **Usually means:** The hypothesized function may be wrong — indirect data alone misidentifies function often enough that a BIP built on it fails.
- **First question:** How many direct observations were conducted, and did the behavior occur during them?
- **Data to pull:** ABC observation logs across at least 3 occurrences.

### Behavior Intervention Plan adds consequences for the behavior without a replacement behavior serving the same function
- **Usually means:** The plan will suppress one behavior topography and see a new one emerge serving the same function.
- **First question:** What acceptable behavior does this plan teach the student to use instead, to get the same outcome (escape, attention, tangible, sensory)?
- **Data to pull:** BIP replacement-behavior section, teacher fidelity data on whether the replacement is being reinforced consistently.

### Full evaluation requested after a single low grade or one incident, with no documented General Education Intervention/RTI attempt
- **Usually means:** Premature request — IDEA generally expects evidence that general-education interventions were tried first (unless a specific state uses discrepancy-only model or a safety concern applies).
- **First question:** What Tier 1/2 intervention was attempted, for how long, and what data exists?
- **Data to pull:** RTI intervention log, prior grades/attendance trend, any prior teacher concern documentation.

### Student's ability-achievement discrepancy is close to but under the state's stated threshold
- **Usually means:** A borderline case where the team should weigh convergent evidence (classroom performance, RTI data, teacher/parent report) rather than treating the threshold as a hard cutoff that ends the analysis.
- **First question:** What does the student's functional classroom performance and RTI trend show, independent of the single discrepancy number?
- **Data to pull:** Classroom work samples, RTI progress-monitoring graphs, teacher narrative.

### Crisis-response triage relies on students self-referring to the counseling office
- **Usually means:** Withdrawn or avoidant students — often the ones at higher risk — go unidentified because they don't walk in.
- **First question:** Has a structured screener been administered to the exposed group, not just an open walk-in system?
- **Data to pull:** Screener completion rate, exposure-group roster cross-referenced against who has been seen.

### Manifestation determination review (MDR) not convened before a disciplinary placement change exceeding 10 cumulative days for a student with an active IEP
- **Usually means:** A procedural violation is underway regardless of whether the underlying behavior was actually manifestation-related.
- **First question:** Has the 10-cumulative-day threshold been tracked across the school year, not just the current incident?
- **Data to pull:** Discipline log with cumulative removed-days count for the student.

### Parent disputes an eligibility denial citing outside private evaluation with different conclusions
- **Usually means:** Either a genuine methodological difference (different instruments, different exclusionary-factor weighting) or an outside evaluator without access to school-based RTI/observational data reaching a conclusion the school team can't replicate.
- **First question:** What data did the outside evaluation have access to that the school team didn't, or vice versa?
- **Data to pull:** Outside evaluation report, comparison of instruments/data sources used by each team.
