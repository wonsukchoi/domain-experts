# School Psychologist — Playbook

## Dual-discrepancy worksheet (filled)

| Metric | Value | Benchmark | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Student current level (wcpm) | 44 | Peer median 70 | -26 wcpm (level discrepancy) |
| Student growth rate (wcpm/week) | 0.4 | Typical rate 1.1 | 36% of typical (rate discrepancy) |
| Intervention fidelity | 9/10 sessions (90%) | >=80% threshold | Fidelity confirmed |
| Attendance during intervention | 96% | — | Not an instruction-access issue |

Decision rule (stated heuristic, confirm against state code): dual discrepancy present when level gap exceeds ~1 SD below peer benchmark **and** rate is below ~50% of typical growth, with fidelity >=80% confirmed. One discrepancy alone (level or rate, not both) is a flag for continued monitoring, not automatic evaluation referral.

## Exclusionary-factor checklist (filled example)

| Factor | Status | Evidence | Ruled out as primary cause? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vision/hearing impairment | Screened, normal limits | Nurse screening, [date] | Yes |
| Motor impairment | No referral history | Teacher/parent report | Yes |
| Intellectual disability | No prior identification | No cognitive eval on file | Pending full eval |
| Emotional disturbance | No behavioral referrals | Discipline/counseling records reviewed | Yes |
| Cultural/economic disadvantage | Title I school, but peers show typical growth | Comparison group same demographic | Yes — peer comparison controls for this |
| Limited English proficiency | English is home language | Home language survey on file | Yes |
| Lack of appropriate instruction | 90% fidelity, licensed interventionist | Fidelity checklist, session log | Yes |

Do not simply state "no exclusionary factors present" — cite the specific evidence for each row. A hearing officer reviewing the file wants to see what was checked, not just the conclusion.

## FBA data-collection form (worked ABC table)

Behavior: student leaves seat and walks to classroom door during independent math work, 3+ times/period.

| Date | Antecedent | Behavior | Consequence | Hypothesized function |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3/2 | Independent worksheet assigned, 2-digit multiplication | Left seat, walked to door | Teacher redirected 1:1, missed 4 min of work | Escape from task perceived as difficult |
| 3/4 | Independent worksheet, same skill | Left seat, stood at door | Peer laughed, teacher redirected | Escape + possible peer attention (secondary) |
| 3/6 | Independent worksheet, same skill | Left seat, asked to "go to nurse" (no symptoms reported) | Sent to nurse, missed remainder of period | Escape from task confirmed across 3 occurrences |

Function determination: escape/avoidance of a task above the student's current independent skill level (2-digit multiplication; diagnostic probe shows student is at 60% accuracy on 1-digit multiplication, below mastery). Consequence pattern (redirection or removal) reinforces escape each time regardless of the specific behavior topography.

BIP replacement-behavior logic: since the function is escape from a too-difficult task, the plan must (a) adjust task difficulty to the student's actual skill level or provide a scaffold, and (b) teach a replacement behavior that also achieves escape/break access appropriately (e.g., a "break card" the student can use to request a 2-minute break) — a BIP that only adds consequences for leaving the seat, without addressing the escape function, predictably fails.

## Crisis-triage screener (filled example, post-incident)

| Student | Direct exposure (witnessed/involved) | Screener score (0-3 scale, higher = more reaction indicators) | Triage tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Direct witness | 3 (sleep disruption, avoidance, intrusive thoughts) | Tier 1 — individual follow-up within 24h |
| B | Indirect (heard about it) | 1 (mild worry, otherwise functioning) | Tier 3 — classroom-level psychoeducation sufficient |
| C | Direct witness, prior trauma history on file | 2 (avoidance, no sleep/intrusive-thought report yet) | Tier 1 — individual follow-up within 24h, prioritized given history |

Triage rule: direct exposure plus screener score >=2, or any prior trauma history plus direct exposure regardless of initial screener score, routes to Tier 1 same-day/next-day individual contact. Indirect exposure with low screener score routes to classroom-level psychoeducation and a 1-week re-check, not individual pull-out — individual attention to every indirectly-exposed student both overwhelms capacity and can convey the event is more dangerous than it was.
