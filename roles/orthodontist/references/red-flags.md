# Orthodontist — Red Flags

### Arch-length discrepancy >10mm after leeway space is subtracted
- **Usually means:** True severe crowding requiring extraction-based mechanics; non-extraction expansion at this magnitude reliably relapses.
- **First question:** Has leeway space already been subtracted, or is this the raw pre-leeway number?
- **Data to pull:** Bolton analysis worksheet, panoramic radiograph confirming primary tooth status.

### Skeletal Class II with CVM stage 4 or later at first orthodontic visit
- **Usually means:** The growth-modification window has closed or is closing; functional-appliance treatment will underperform.
- **First question:** Is camouflage (dental compensation) mechanically sufficient, or does the skeletal discrepancy require a surgical referral?
- **Data to pull:** Lateral cephalometric radiograph with CVM staging, ANB angle.

### Overjet >6mm in a growing patient with a history of trauma or high-activity lifestyle
- **Usually means:** Elevated risk of incisor trauma while untreated; case favors earlier Phase I intervention.
- **First question:** Can the family commit to a two-phase treatment timeline, or is single-phase at full permanent dentition the realistic path?
- **Data to pull:** Overjet measurement from lateral ceph or clinical exam, trauma history.

### Progress photos show molar mesial drift >2mm on the anchorage side mid-treatment
- **Usually means:** Anchorage loss — the "anchor" teeth are moving instead of holding position.
- **First question:** Was an anchorage plan (TAD, headgear, extraction pattern) specified at treatment planning, or was molar anchorage assumed without reinforcement?
- **Data to pull:** Progress models or intraoral scans compared to initial records.

### Patient reports retainer not worn for >2 weeks post-debond
- **Usually means:** Active relapse is likely already underway — periodontal ligament remodeling begins within days of reduced retention.
- **First question:** Can the retainer still be seated passively, or has enough movement occurred that it now binds?
- **Data to pull:** Retainer fit-check at chairside; if it doesn't seat, take new models before reordering.

### Bonded lingual retainer shows one segment debonded at a recall visit
- **Usually means:** The unsplinted tooth or teeth can shift within weeks even though the rest of the retainer is intact.
- **First question:** How long has the segment been loose, and is there already measurable movement?
- **Data to pull:** Direct visual/tactile check of retainer bond at each tooth, compare position to last visit's photo.

### Treatment plan calls for extraction based on a visual crowding impression, no documented space analysis
- **Usually means:** The extraction decision is not yet supported by measurement — risk of removing sound teeth unnecessarily.
- **First question:** Has a Bolton or equivalent tooth-size arch-length analysis been run and documented?
- **Data to pull:** Model analysis worksheet; if absent, this is not a finalized treatment plan yet.

### Patient with a Class III skeletal pattern presenting after growth is complete
- **Usually means:** Camouflage orthodontics has limited ability to correct true skeletal Class III; surgical referral is often indicated.
- **First question:** Is the discrepancy dental, skeletal, or both — and by how much (cephalometric ANB, Wits appraisal)?
- **Data to pull:** Full cephalometric analysis, growth-completion confirmation (CVM stage or hand-wrist film history).

### Clear-aligner case shows <75% predicted tooth movement achieved at a mid-treatment refinement check
- **Usually means:** Tracking failure — either compliance (wear time) or a movement type aligners handle poorly (e.g., rotation of round teeth, extrusion) was underestimated.
- **First question:** Is this a compliance issue or a biomechanics-limitation issue with the movement type prescribed?
- **Data to pull:** Wear-time compliance data if available (tray-fit tracking), comparison of predicted vs. actual tooth position on scan overlay.
