# Playbook

## Accommodation-fidelity checklist (6-period schedule)

Fill for any student when a grade or behavior concern surfaces, or on a routine quarterly check.

| Period | Class | Accommodation(s) required | Verification method used | Verified compliant? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ELA | Extended time 1.5x; speech-to-text | Direct observation (2 sessions) | Y | Both in use during in-class essay |
| 2 | Math | Extended time 1.5x; speech-to-text | Work-sample comparison | Partial | Speech-to-text blocked during "mental math," not IEP-exempted |
| 3 | Science | Extended time 1.5x; speech-to-text | Teacher self-report only | N | Teacher limits extended time to "major tests" only |
| 4 | Social Studies | Extended time 1.5x; speech-to-text | Direct observation (1 session) | Y | Confirmed on quiz |
| 5 | PE | N/A | N/A | N/A | No academic accommodation applies |
| 6 | Elective | N/A | N/A | N/A | No academic accommodation applies |

**Rule:** teacher self-report alone counts as "unverified" until cross-checked once per quarter with direct observation or a work sample — self-report-only rows get flagged for follow-up within 2 weeks, not treated as compliant.

## Self-advocacy skill-building progression

Use to place a student at the correct rung and plan the next step; do not skip rungs.

| Rung | Student behavior | Case manager support level | Example scripted language | Advance when... |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Names own accommodations when asked directly by case manager | Full prompting | "My IEP says I get extended time and speech-to-text." | Can state accurately, unprompted by content, 4/5 trials |
| 2 | States accommodation to a familiar teacher with a visual cue card | Cue card + case manager present | "Can I use extended time on this quiz? It's in my IEP." | States without reading the card, 3/4 trials |
| 3 | States accommodation to a familiar teacher, no cue card, case manager not present | Fading — check-in after, not during | Same phrasing, self-initiated | Confirmed via teacher report across 2 separate classes |
| 4 | States accommodation to a new/substitute teacher, unprompted | Independent | Same phrasing, in an unfamiliar setting | Confirmed by unprompted report from a teacher never coached in advance |

**Rule:** the goal's target rung and the fading schedule get named explicitly in the IEP (e.g., "advance from Rung 2 to Rung 3 by end of Q2") — an unranked "will advocate for needs" goal cannot be progress-monitored.

## 8th-grade course-sequencing data review

Complete before any track-placement recommendation affecting high-school eligibility (e.g., Algebra-in-8th, honors, diploma track).

| Data point | Q1 | Q2 | Accommodation fidelity confirmed this quarter? | Standardized assessment (most recent) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Course grade | 71% | 68% | N — teacher self-report only, no observation logged | Math benchmark: 62nd percentile |
| Homework completion rate | 74% | 70% | — | — |
| Behavior incidents (this class only) | 1 | 2 | — | — |

**Decision rule:**
- If fidelity is unconfirmed for either quarter → verify fidelity first; do not use this data to justify a track-down recommendation yet.
- If fidelity is confirmed for 2+ quarters and grades remain below 70% and standardized assessment is below the 40th percentile → track-down recommendation is data-supported.
- If fidelity is confirmed but standardized assessment is at or above the 50th percentile despite low grades → investigate a fidelity or engagement gap rather than a skill gap; keep the higher-rigor option open.
- Never base the recommendation on a single quarter, regardless of how low the grade is.
