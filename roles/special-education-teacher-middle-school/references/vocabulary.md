# Vocabulary

### Accommodation fidelity
The degree to which an IEP accommodation is actually implemented as written in a specific classroom, verified by observation or work sample rather than assumed from a signed acknowledgment form.
**In use:** "Fidelity in math and science was the gap — both teachers signed the form, but neither one was actually giving extended time on quizzes."
**Common misuse:** Treating a teacher's signature on the IEP acknowledgment form as evidence the accommodation is happening — the signature confirms the teacher received the plan, not that it's being applied in the specific classroom moment it's meant for.

### Self-advocacy goal
An IEP goal targeting the student's own ability to identify and request a specific accommodation from a specific person, with scripted language and a fading-support plan, distinct from a general independence or behavior goal.
**In use:** "The goal isn't 'will advocate for needs' — it's 'will state to a new teacher, unprompted, that she has extended time on tests,' with the case manager fading out cueing over the semester."
**Common misuse:** Writing the goal as an abstract trait ("will advocate for self") rather than a specific, practiced classroom behavior — a goal without the exact language and the exact person to say it to doesn't transfer to an actual new-teacher moment.

### FBA (Functional Behavior Assessment)
A structured process for identifying the environmental antecedents and consequences maintaining a specific behavior, used to design an intervention targeting the actual function rather than the visible behavior.
**In use:** "Before we write a BIP, we need the FBA to show whether this is escape-motivated or attention-motivated — those get different interventions."
**Common misuse:** Requesting or completing an FBA after a single dramatic incident rather than an established pattern — a functional assessment built on one data point risks misidentifying the function and producing an intervention that targets the wrong trigger.

### BIP (Behavior Intervention Plan)
A plan, built from FBA findings, specifying proactive strategies, replacement behaviors, and responses tied to the behavior's identified function.
**In use:** "The BIP's replacement behavior is asking for a break card, since the FBA showed the outbursts were escape-motivated during writing tasks."
**Common misuse:** Writing a BIP with only reactive consequences and no proactive strategy or replacement behavior — a plan built solely around what happens after the behavior does nothing to reduce the antecedent conditions that produced it.

### Present levels of academic and functional performance (PLAAFP)
The IEP section describing the student's current, data-based skill level in each area of need, the baseline against which all goals and progress are measured.
**In use:** "The PLAAFP needs updating — it still cites last year's reading fluency score, not this fall's benchmark."
**Common misuse:** Leaving PLAAFP language generic or outdated across annual reviews — goals written against a stale or vague present-level statement can't be meaningfully progress-monitored.

### Course/track sequencing
The sequence of course levels (e.g., standard, honors, Algebra-in-8th) a student is placed into across middle school, which constrains the high-school course options available afterward.
**In use:** "This isn't just a 7th-grade math placement — it's whether she's on the Algebra-in-8th track that keeps calculus open senior year."
**Common misuse:** Treating a single semester's difficulty in one course as sufficient basis for a track-down recommendation — a placement decision this consequential needs multiple quarters of accommodation-verified data, not one hard unit.

### ABC data (Antecedent-Behavior-Consequence)
A structured log recording what happened immediately before a behavior, the behavior itself, and what happened immediately after, used to identify the behavior's function before intervening.
**In use:** "The ABC log shows the outbursts cluster right after independent-writing prompts — that's pointing to an escape function, not attention-seeking."
**Common misuse:** Logging only the behavior and consequence (what disrupted the classroom) without the antecedent — without the antecedent, the log can't establish what's triggering the behavior, only that it happened.

### Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)
The IDEA requirement that a student be educated alongside non-disabled peers to the maximum extent appropriate, with removal to a more restrictive setting justified by documented need, not convenience.
**In use:** "Before we move him to the resource room for math, we need documentation showing general-ed with accommodations and fidelity checks wasn't sufficient — LRE requires that step."
**Common misuse:** Treating a more restrictive placement as a reasonable default response to a struggling student — LRE requires evidence that a less restrictive setting, with accommodations actually verified, was tried and insufficient first.

### Progress monitoring
The ongoing, scheduled collection of data against a specific IEP goal's target, distinct from report-card grades, used to determine whether the current plan is working.
**In use:** "Report card grades went up, but the progress-monitoring data on the writing goal is flat — that's the number the annual review should weigh more heavily."
**Common misuse:** Substituting general classroom grades for goal-specific progress-monitoring data — grades reflect many factors (participation, other assignments) and can move independently of whether the specific IEP goal is actually being met.
