# Red flags

### A grade drop of 10+ points in exactly one class while other classes hold steady
- **Usually means:** accommodation non-implementation in that specific classroom, less often a genuine subject-specific skill gap.
- **First question:** has the accommodation been directly observed or work-sampled in that classroom, or only confirmed by the teacher's signature on the IEP acknowledgment form?
- **Data to pull:** per-class grade breakdown over the last 2 quarters, most recent direct-observation or work-sample log for that classroom.

### Six teacher self-reports on accommodation implementation that are unanimously "yes, fully implemented"
- **Usually means:** teachers are reporting intent or the signed form, not verified daily practice — self-report compliance runs well above what direct observation typically finds.
- **First question:** has any period been directly observed or work-sample-verified this quarter, or is "yes" based on the acknowledgment form alone?
- **Data to pull:** direct observation log dates, work samples showing the accommodation in use versus not.

### A new behavior incident pattern appearing within 2 weeks of a schedule, teacher, or classroom change
- **Usually means:** an environmental or accommodation-fit trigger tied to the specific change, not a new or worsening disability-related condition.
- **First question:** what changed in the schedule/environment in the same window, and does the incident pattern track a specific class period or transition?
- **Data to pull:** ABC (antecedent-behavior-consequence) log, schedule-change date, incident log by period.

### A recommendation to move a student to a lower academic track based on one semester of difficulty
- **Usually means:** the recommendation reflects classroom-management convenience or a single hard unit, not a documented, accommodation-supported multi-quarter data pattern.
- **First question:** has accommodation fidelity been verified in that class before the placement recommendation, and does the data span 2+ quarters?
- **Data to pull:** grades and accommodation-fidelity checks for the prior 2 quarters in the class driving the recommendation.

### A student who cannot name their own accommodations when asked directly
- **Usually means:** the self-advocacy goal is missing, undertaught, or has never moved past an abstract "will advocate for needs" statement.
- **First question:** is there a current self-advocacy goal with specific scripted language, and when was it last practiced?
- **Data to pull:** IEP goal page, self-advocacy practice log, most recent progress note on the goal.

### An FBA (functional behavior assessment) request made before 2 weeks of ABC data exists
- **Usually means:** the team is reacting to a single dramatic incident rather than an established pattern, risking a plan built on one data point.
- **First question:** how many dated, antecedent-inclusive data points exist, and does the behavior recur across settings or is it isolated to one incident?
- **Data to pull:** ABC data log with dates, count of settings/periods where the behavior occurred.

### A student's course placement for 8th grade set without reviewing standardized assessment data alongside classroom grades
- **Usually means:** the placement decision is based on a partial picture — classroom grades alone can reflect implementation gaps rather than actual skill level.
- **First question:** does the placement decision draw on standardized assessment scores in addition to grades and accommodation-supported work samples?
- **Data to pull:** most recent standardized assessment scores, accommodation-supported work samples from the relevant subject.

### A behavior addressed with a consequence-based response before the functional cause is identified
- **Usually means:** the intervention targets the visible behavior rather than the underlying skill deficit, sensory trigger, or accommodation gap causing it.
- **First question:** has the ABC data been reviewed to identify a functional cause, or was a consequence applied based on the incident alone?
- **Data to pull:** ABC data for the incident, any accommodation-fidelity check for the period/setting where it occurred.

### A student moving between 5-6 general-education teachers with no documented accommodation-fidelity check this quarter
- **Usually means:** the case manager is relying on the annual IEP meeting and signed acknowledgments as the only verification point all year.
- **First question:** when was the last direct classroom check for this student, and how many of the 5-6 periods has it covered?
- **Data to pull:** accommodation-fidelity checklist coverage log across all periods for the current quarter.
