# Red flags

### Diagonal or joint-line cracking reappearing within one heating season of a repair
- **Usually means:** the underlying framing movement or fastener issue (truss uplift, undriven screw, missing back-blocking) was never corrected — the compound was re-mudded over the same defect.
- **First question:** was the crack cut out to the framing and the fastener/backing checked, or just re-taped over the existing crack?
- **Data to pull:** repair work order and photos from the original repair, framing type (truss vs. stick-built) for truss uplift pattern.

### Photographing (joint shadow visible under raking or window light) on a Level 4 wall with semi-gloss or gloss paint
- **Usually means:** the finish level specified doesn't match the paint sheen — Level 4 joints are exposed by any sheen above flat/eggshell.
- **First question:** what level of finish did the drywall spec call, and did the paint sheen get selected after the drywall was already priced and finished?
- **Data to pull:** the drywall spec's level-of-finish callout versus the paint schedule's sheen selection.

### Mesh tape joint separating or lifting at the seam
- **Usually means:** mesh was bedded in lightweight or all-purpose (drying-type) compound instead of setting-type — no bond substrate for the compound to key into.
- **First question:** what compound was used to bed the tape on this joint, and is it documented anywhere (batch/product on site)?
- **Data to pull:** material delivery log or empty bags/buckets from the finish date if still on site.

### Fastener pop-through (dimple or ring visible through paint) more than 6 months after finish, clustered along one wall
- **Usually means:** framing movement (moisture cycling in studs, truss uplift) working fasteners loose, not a finishing defect — re-skimming without re-setting or adding fasteners nearby will recur.
- **First question:** is the pattern isolated to fasteners along one framing member, or scattered randomly across the wall?
- **Data to pull:** framing moisture content at time of hang if recorded, HVAC commissioning date relative to the finish date (uncontrolled humidity swings before HVAC startup are a common cause).

### Soft, bubbling, or discolored compound at a joint weeks after finishing
- **Usually means:** a second coat of drying-type compound was applied over a first coat that wasn't fully dry, trapping moisture that's now degrading the paper tape or compound underneath.
- **First question:** how much time elapsed between coats on this joint relative to the compound's rated dry time for the ambient conditions at the time?
- **Data to pull:** the crew's daily coat log (if kept) or the compound product used and the HVAC/temperature log for that floor during finishing.

### Visible ridge or shadow specifically at every butt joint, but tapered joints look fine
- **Usually means:** butt joints were feathered the same width as tapered joints instead of wider, or weren't back-blocked, leaving the tape thickness with nowhere to hide.
- **First question:** were butt joints identified and flagged for extra feather width and back-blocking before finishing began, or finished with the same technique as tapered edges?
- **Data to pull:** the hang layout/plan showing where butt joints fall, compared against the finish walkthrough notes.

### Spray texture patch visibly different pattern size or sheen from surrounding field
- **Usually means:** hopper settings (orifice, pressure, distance) or flatten timing weren't matched to the original texture before spraying the repair — matched by texture name only, not by test patch.
- **First question:** was a test patch sprayed and compared under the room's actual light before the repair was sprayed, or was the hopper set from memory/label?
- **Data to pull:** none typically exists — this is a walkthrough/visual call; ask the crew directly whether a test patch was cut.

### Level of finish delivered doesn't match the written spec, discovered at designer walkthrough
- **Usually means:** the level was never confirmed against the scope document before mudding started, or a verbal change wasn't documented.
- **First question:** what document specified the level of finish for this area, and who confirmed it with the crew before the tape coat began?
- **Data to pull:** the drywall scope/spec sheet and any change-order or RFI trail for that area.

### Compound shrinkage cracks (fine web pattern) across a full skim coat (Level 5) within days of application
- **Usually means:** compound applied too thick in a single pass, or a fast-set product used for a skim coat where a thinner, low-shrinkage topping compound was called for.
- **First question:** what compound and approximate coat thickness was used for the skim, and was it applied in one heavy pass or two thinner passes?
- **Data to pull:** product data sheet for the compound used, comparing its recommended maximum coat thickness to what was applied.
