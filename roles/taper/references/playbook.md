# Playbook

Filled reference tables for the four decisions that carry real callback risk: level-of-finish specification, compound/tape selection, coat sequencing and dry time, and texture matching. Numbers below reflect GA-214, ASTM C840, and common manufacturer (USG, National Gypsum, CertainTeed) technical literature — always verify the current published data sheet for the specific compound and climate on a live job; working times and dry times shift with humidity, temperature, and airflow.

## Level of finish quick reference (GA-214)

| Level | Joints/fasteners | Full face | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 0 | No taping or finishing | None | Temporary construction, service corridors behind ceilings |
| Level 1 | Tape embedded in compound, no additional coat | None | Above ceilings, plenum returns, areas concealed permanently |
| Level 2 | Tape coat + one additional coat over joints/fasteners | None | Garages, storage areas, water-resistant backer behind tile |
| Level 3 | Tape coat + two additional coats over joints/fasteners | None | Heavy/medium-texture finishes, or where wall covering will hide minor imperfection |
| Level 4 | Tape coat + two additional coats over joints/fasteners | None | Standard flat and eggshell paint — the default absent other spec |
| Level 5 | Tape coat + two additional coats over joints/fasteners | Skim coat over entire surface | Semi-gloss/gloss paint, severe raking light (critical-lighting rooms), unbacked lightweight board |

**Rule:** the level applies per area, not per job — mixed specs (Level 5 corridor, Level 4 storage) are normal on commercial work and must be priced and sequenced separately, since a Level 5 skim coat adds a full pass over the entire face, not just wider joints.

## Compound and tape selection

| Situation | Compound | Tape | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard interior joint, normal schedule | All-purpose (drying-type) | Paper | Strong bond, shrinks least of the drying-type family, standard cost |
| Same-day fill/finish coat needed | Setting-type (Durabond/Easy Sand 45 or 90) | Paper | Chemical set independent of humidity; 45–90 min working time lets crew fill and finish same visit |
| Patch/repair, fast turnaround | Setting-type 20 or 45 | Paper or self-adhesive mesh | Fast set for small-area repairs; mesh acceptable here only because it's bedded in setting compound |
| Butt joints, corners needing max crack resistance | All-purpose or setting-type | Paper (never mesh on butt joints) | Paper conforms to the joint and resists the ridging/cracking that concentrates at non-tapered edges |
| Skim coat (Level 5) | Topping or all-purpose, thinned for spreadability | N/A (full-face, no tape) | Smooth, low-shrinkage finish coat over the entire surface |
| Cold (<50°F) or high-humidity space | Setting-type only | Paper | Drying-type compound will not cure reliably; setting-type cures by chemical reaction regardless of ambient drying conditions |

**Never:** bed fiberglass mesh tape in lightweight or all-purpose compound — mesh has no fiber for drying-type compound to key into, and the joint separates under normal seasonal movement.

## Coat sequencing and dry time (drying-type system, 70°F / 50% RH baseline)

| Coat | Purpose | Width past tape edge | Minimum dry time before next coat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tape/embed coat | Bed the tape, remove air bubbles | Tape width only | 24 hrs (drying-type) / set time only (setting-type: 20–90 min) |
| Fill coat | Build the joint, cover fastener heads | 6"–8" | 24 hrs |
| Finish coat | Feather to invisible edge | 12"–16" | 24 hrs before sanding |
| Skim coat (Level 5 only) | Uniform film over entire face | Full surface | 24 hrs before final sand |

**Adjustments:** each 10°F below 70°F or each 10% RH above 50% roughly doubles dry time for drying-type compound; below 50°F, drying-type may not fully cure regardless of wait — switch to setting-type or add temporary heat. Setting-type dry times are fixed by the product's rated working time (the number in the product name) and are not meaningfully slowed by cool, dry conditions the way they are by extreme cold (<40°F) or standing water in the mix.

## Texture matching fallback order

1. Identify the existing texture family (orange peel, knockdown, splatter, hand-troweled skip) and cut a test patch on scrap board or an inconspicuous area — never match directly on the repair.
2. Match hopper orifice size and air pressure to produce comparable droplet size before matching distance/angle — orifice and pressure drive droplet size more than spray distance.
3. For knockdown, match the flatten timing (minutes after spray before knocking down with a trowel) — too early collapses the texture flat, too late won't knock down at all.
4. Feather the texture edge into the existing field at least 12" past the patched drywall repair — a hard edge in texture is as visible under raking light as an unfeathered joint.
5. Prime the patch before final texture comparison under the room's actual light — texture reads differently on raw compound than on primed, painted board.
