# Red Flags

Signals a service is heading toward a physical, logistical, or custody failure. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Fewer than six bearers offered for a metal or hardwood casket hand-carry

- **Usually means:** the family under-recruited relative to actual loaded weight (commonly 360–430 lb), or nobody translated "we have four sons" into a per-bearer load number for them.
- **First question:** "What's the loaded weight, and what's that divided by the bearers we actually have?"
- **Data to pull:** casket material/weight from the order, decedent's recorded weight, and the terrain for the carry route (stairs, gravel, turf) to decide church-truck vs. hand-carry split.

### A cremains container or urn without a legible ID disc/tag at handoff

- **Usually means:** either the crematory's tag came loose in transit, or — rarer but the reason the check exists — a case mix-up upstream.
- **First question:** "Does the case number and name on this tag match the case folder, right now, before this goes any further?"
- **Data to pull:** case folder, crematory paperwork, and if the tag is genuinely illegible or missing, the crematory's case log for that batch before proceeding.

### Personal effects (jewelry, glasses, a specific item requested "returned with the body") not itemized on a signed receipt

- **Usually means:** the item was handled informally — pocketed, set aside "to deal with later," or handed over verbally — which is exactly how items go missing and become a family dispute weeks later.
- **First question:** "Is this on a receipt with a signature, or did someone just say it's handled?"
- **Data to pull:** the Personal Effects Receipt for that case; if none exists, create one before the item moves again.

### A family volunteer bearer placed on a load-bearing corner without a staff bearer paired

- **Usually means:** staffing was done by headcount, not by position — corners require steering and weight judgment that an untrained bearer hasn't practiced.
- **First question:** "Which two people are on the front corners, and are they staff?"
- **Data to pull:** the bearer assignment for that specific carry, not just the total count.

### Procession departs without confirming that state's right-of-way rule

- **Usually means:** the escort driver is operating on assumption or habit from a different state or a previous job, not the current jurisdiction's statute.
- **First question:** "What does this state's law actually say the escort vehicle can do at a controlled intersection?"
- **Data to pull:** the current state statute (procession right-of-way section), not memory of "how we always do it."

### A dressing/casketing task assigned with no mention of the Exposure Control Plan or PPE

- **Usually means:** the task is being treated as routine housekeeping rather than a regulated bloodborne-pathogen exposure task.
- **First question:** "What does the funeral home's written Exposure Control Plan say for this task, and is the required PPE actually available right now?"
- **Data to pull:** the facility's OSHA 1910.1030 Exposure Control Plan and current PPE inventory.

### Viewing room setup finished with no walkthrough from the entry point

- **Usually means:** items were checked individually against a list but never seen as a room, so a misspelled name on signage or a misrouted flower arrangement survives item-by-item checking.
- **First question:** "Has anyone walked in through the actual doors the family will use and looked at the room as they'll see it?"
- **Data to pull:** the service sheet diagram compared against the room as currently set, from the entry point inward.

### A piece of equipment (church truck, lowering device) not function-checked before the service that needs it

- **Usually means:** it was assumed fine because it worked last time, and equipment faults surface exactly when there's no slack to absorb them — mid-committal, in front of the family.
- **First question:** "Has this been physically operated today, not just visually inspected?"
- **Data to pull:** the equipment maintenance log's last-checked date and any open fault notes.

### A director decision being made by the attendant in the moment (seating change, content adjustment, family request granted on the spot)

- **Usually means:** the attendant filled a gap because the director wasn't immediately reachable, but the decision wasn't actually theirs to make.
- **First question:** "Is this logistics, or is this a decision that changes what the family experiences?"
- **Data to pull:** the service sheet — if the request isn't on it, it's a director call, not an in-the-moment attendant call.
