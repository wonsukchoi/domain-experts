# Funeral Attendant Playbook

Filled checklists and templates. Defaults, not laws — deviate when the situation calls for it and note why in the day's log.

## 1. Pre-service walkthrough checklist (30–60 min before doors)

Run from the family's expected entry point inward, not item-by-item from a random start.

| Check | Standard | Example finding |
|---|---|---|
| Entry/lobby | Register book open to first page, pen present, coat rack clear | OK |
| Signage | Directional sign to correct parlor, name spelled per service sheet | Name spelled "Whitfeld" — corrected to "Whitfield" before doors |
| Seating | Chair count ≥ service-sheet headcount + 10% | Sheet says 80; 84 chairs set — OK |
| Casket/urn position | Matches service-sheet diagram (head toward or away from entry per family instruction) | OK |
| Flowers | Family-ordered pieces placed per card; no duplicate or misrouted arrangement from another service that day | One arrangement addressed to a different family — pulled and rerouted before doors |
| Sound/AV | Playlist loaded and tested at service volume, not default volume | Tested — OK |
| Temperature/lighting | Room at comfortable temp, dimmer set per service type (viewing vs. funeral) | OK |
| Restroom/water station | Stocked, signage visible | OK |
| Equipment staged | Church truck, lowering device (if graveside same day), extra chairs in wings | Lowering device function-checked, hand crank moves freely — OK |

A finding that requires more than a two-minute fix gets flagged to the director immediately, not silently absorbed.

## 2. Bearer-staffing table

Loaded weight = casket weight + estimated decedent weight. Use the higher of family-stated or observed decedent weight when they differ.

| Casket material | Typical empty weight | Loaded weight (avg. adult ~180 lb decedent) | Min. bearers (hand-carry) | Per-bearer load at min. |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cardboard/fiberboard | 70–80 lb | ~250–260 lb | 4 | ~63–65 lb |
| Wood (pine) | ~150 lb | ~330 lb | 6 | ~55 lb |
| Wood (hardwood, e.g. mahogany) | up to 250 lb | ~430 lb | 6 | ~72 lb |
| Metal | 180–200 lb | ~360–380 lb | 6 | ~60–63 lb |

Rules of thumb:
- **Floor of six bearers for any metal or hardwood casket hand-carry**, regardless of how few volunteered — fill the gap with staff rather than reduce the count.
- **Every load above ~75 lb per bearer gets a church truck for any distance beyond a doorway threshold or a few steps** — stairs, gravel-to-turf transitions, and tight turns are the exceptions that force hand-carry, not routine flat-ground movement.
- **Untrained family bearers go on non-steering, non-corner positions** when mixed with staff bearers; staff take the front (steering) corners.
- **Recompute, don't estimate, when the decedent is notably above or below average** — a decedent significantly over 250 lb pushes loaded weight past 450–500 lb even in a lighter casket, which may require eight bearers or a mechanized carry the whole distance.

## 3. Funeral procession staging order

1. Escort/lead vehicle (funeral home staff or licensed escort service) — confirms state right-of-way rule for that jurisdiction before departure and briefs the hearse driver on the planned route and any known detours.
2. Hearse (funeral coach).
3. Family limousine(s), immediate family first.
4. Flower car, if separate from the hearse.
5. Remaining procession vehicles, headlights on and hazards or procession flags per local convention.

Right-of-way check before departure (varies by state — confirm current statute, do not assume uniformity):
- Some states grant the procession right-of-way at intersections outright, disregarding signals.
- Most states require only that the lead/escort vehicle legally entered the intersection (e.g., on green) before the rest of the procession may continue through on a changed signal, provided each following driver exercises reasonable care.
- A non-procession vehicle may not join, pass through, or break the procession under most state statutes; the escort vehicle's role includes holding cross-traffic at intersections where local law allows it.

## 4. Chain-of-custody log — remains, personal effects, cremains

Filled example entry format:

```
Case #: 4417
Decedent: Robert Hale
Item: Cremated remains, returned from [Crematory Name]
Transfer 1: Crematory → Attendant (K. Osei), [date/time]. ID disc verified against case folder: MATCH.
Transfer 2: Attendant → Urn (family-selected, mid-tier bronze), [date/time]. Weight recorded: 6.1 lb.
Transfer 3: Urn → Family (S. Hale, daughter), [date/time]. Signature obtained: [signature].
Personal effects: Wedding ring, watch — returned with cremains, itemized on Personal Effects Receipt #2291, signed by S. Hale.
Discrepancies: None.
```

Every transfer line requires a name, a timestamp, and either "MATCH" or a documented discrepancy escalated to the director before the item moves further — never a blank line implying it was fine.

## 5. Post-service reset checklist

1. Walk every space used (parlor, chapel, restroom, lobby) for left-behind personal items, cards, or jewelry before closing.
2. Return church truck, lowering device, and any staged equipment to storage; note any equipment fault (sticking wheel, slow hydraulic drop) for maintenance, not just for the next user to discover.
3. Log vehicle mileage and fuel level; flag anything under a quarter tank for the next scheduled service rather than the next driver finding out at departure.
4. File the day's chain-of-custody log and any director-flagged items before leaving.
