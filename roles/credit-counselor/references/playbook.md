# Credit Counselor — Playbook

## Debt-to-income and disposable income worksheet (filled example)

| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Net monthly income | $4,200 |
| Essential expenses (housing, utilities, food, transportation, insurance, other required obligations) | $2,800 |
| **Disposable income** | $4,200 − $2,800 = **$1,400** |
| Current debt minimum payments (total) | $750 |
| **DTI (minimums ÷ net income)** | $750 ÷ $4,200 = **17.9%** |

**Use:** Never stop at DTI alone — it can look manageable while the underlying debt is barely being paid down at high interest rates. Always follow with a true payoff trajectory check.

## DMP amortization calculation (filled example)

| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Total debt balance | $25,000 |
| Negotiated blended rate | 9% APR |
| Plan term | 60 months |
| Monthly rate (r) | 0.09 ÷ 12 = 0.0075 |
| (1+r)^60 | ≈ 1.5657 |
| Monthly payment: P × r(1+r)ⁿ ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ−1] | 25,000 × 0.0075 × 1.5657 ÷ 0.5657 ≈ **$519** |
| Total paid over 60 months | $519 × 60 = $31,140 |
| **Total interest paid** | $31,140 − $25,000 = **$6,140** |

**Feasibility check:** $519/month DMP payment vs. $1,400 disposable income → fits comfortably, $881/month remaining.

## Payoff-order method selection guide

| Client pattern | Recommended method | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Has abandoned debt/savings plans before, needs early motivation | Snowball (smallest balance first) | Quick wins build momentum and follow-through |
| Methodical, has completed past financial goals, cost-focused | Avalanche (highest rate first) | Minimizes total interest paid |
| Uncertain / no strong history either way | Default to avalanche, but check in early and switch to snowball if engagement drops | Avalanche is mathematically superior when either method would be sustained |

## Debt management recommendation memo — filled example

> **Debt Management Recommendation — Client [ref]**
> Disposable income: $1,400/month. Current minimum payments: $750/month (DTI 17.9%), but at 22-26% APR, minimums are barely retiring principal — current trajectory would take decades to pay off.
> Proposed DMP: $25,000 total balance, negotiated blended rate of 9% APR, 60-month term → **$519/month payment**.
> **Recommendation: Enroll in DMP.** Payment fits comfortably within disposable income ($881/month remaining), retires the full balance in 5 years, and reduces total interest to $6,140 versus a dramatically higher cost under the current minimum-payment trajectory.
