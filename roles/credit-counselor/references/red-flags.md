### A client's debt-to-income ratio looks manageable but their debts carry high interest rates (20%+ APR) at minimum payments
- **Usually means:** Minimum payments may be barely covering interest, meaning the debt would take decades to pay off (or never, in a negative-amortization scenario) despite a DTI ratio that looks fine on the surface.
- **First question:** At current minimum payments and interest rates, what is the actual projected payoff timeline and total interest cost?
- **Data to pull:** Full debt list with balances, rates, and minimum payments; amortization projection at current terms.

### A DMP is proposed using an assumed generic interest rate reduction rather than this agency's actual creditor concession terms
- **Usually means:** The proposed plan may not reflect what these specific creditors will actually agree to through this agency's relationship.
- **First question:** What are the current, verified concession terms this agency has negotiated with each specific creditor on this client's list?
- **Data to pull:** Agency's current creditor concession rate schedule, client's specific creditor list.

### A DMP payment, once calculated, exceeds the client's actual disposable income
- **Usually means:** The plan isn't sustainable as proposed and should be reconsidered rather than enrolled as-is — either the term needs adjustment, or bankruptcy/settlement should be evaluated instead.
- **First question:** What is the client's actual verified disposable income (income minus essential expenses), and does the proposed DMP payment fit within it with reasonable margin?
- **Data to pull:** Client's income and essential expense documentation, calculated DMP payment.

### A client is pursuing debt settlement without discussion of the tax consequences
- **Usually means:** Forgiven debt through settlement is generally taxable as cancellation-of-debt income, and a client may not be aware of the tax bill that can follow a settlement.
- **First question:** Has the client been informed that the forgiven amount will likely generate a Form 1099-C and taxable income?
- **Data to pull:** Estimated settlement amount and forgiven balance, client's approximate tax bracket.

### A bankruptcy counseling session is being conducted by an agency not confirmed on the applicable court district's approved provider list
- **Usually means:** The counseling may not satisfy the federal legal requirement, regardless of the session's actual quality or thoroughness.
- **First question:** Is this specific agency confirmed as an approved provider for the client's applicable bankruptcy court district?
- **Data to pull:** U.S. Trustee Program's approved provider list for the relevant district.

### A client has a documented history of abandoning prior debt payoff plans, but is being recommended the avalanche (highest-interest-first) method
- **Usually means:** The mathematically optimal method may not be the one most likely to actually be completed, given this client's behavioral pattern.
- **First question:** What has this client's follow-through looked like on prior debt or savings plans, and would an early-momentum method (snowball) be more realistic?
- **Data to pull:** Client's stated history with prior financial plans or goals.

### A client's total debt and income situation suggests a DMP payment, even after concessions, would still take an unusually long time (well beyond 5 years) to pay off
- **Usually means:** A DMP may not be the right tool for this level of debt relative to income, and bankruptcy or settlement should be evaluated instead of extending or stretching the DMP term.
- **First question:** What is the calculated DMP payoff timeline at a sustainable payment level, and does it fall within a reasonable term (commonly up to 5 years)?
- **Data to pull:** Total debt balance, negotiated rate, sustainable payment amount, resulting amortization term.

### A client is recommended a single option (DMP, settlement, or bankruptcy) without a documented comparison across all three
- **Usually means:** The recommendation may not reflect a full assessment of the client's specific numbers and circumstances across the available paths.
- **First question:** Has each option been evaluated against this client's specific debt, income, and asset situation, with the comparison documented?
- **Data to pull:** Client's full financial picture, comparison analysis across DMP, settlement, and bankruptcy.
