## Oversize-repair decision table

| Deviation over max allowable diameter | Disposition |
|---|---|
| 0.0000"-0.0015" | Check drawing repair note for an approved direct oversize-bolt substitution; if none listed, route to MRB. |
| >0.0015" | Do not select an oversize bolt in the field. Write NCR, route to MRB — likely disposition is an oversize bushing repair per an approved repair drawing, restoring standard fastener fit with specified interference. |
| Any deviation on a matched-drilled or fatigue-critical primary structure location | Always route to MRB regardless of magnitude — these locations carry tighter disposition requirements than general structure. |

## FOD / close-out checklist

Complete before any structure is closed (skin riveted over substructure, access panel sealed, fairing installed):

1. Visual sweep of the cavity for tools, hardware, drill shavings, and shop materials (rags, tape backing).
2. Reconcile the tool accountability count issued for the operation against the count returned — any discrepancy is a stop-work event until resolved.
3. Reconcile hardware issued (fasteners, washers, collars) against hardware installed plus hardware returned to bins — any unaccounted quantity is a stop-work event.
4. Confirm all required in-process inspection buy-offs for this structure are signed and timestamped *before* the close-out operation, not after.
5. Log the close-out inspection on the traveler with inspector stamp and timestamp.
6. If any item from steps 1-3 cannot be resolved, do not close the structure — escalate per the FOD program's search/disposition procedure before proceeding.
