# Red flags

### Piece formed to the exact target finished dimension without compensating for total shrinkage
- **Usually means:** the fired piece will come out significantly undersized by roughly the shrinkage percentage.
- **First question:** what wet-form dimension was actually used versus the shrinkage-compensated calculation?
- **Data to pull:** wet-form dimension used compared against target fired dimension and this clay body's documented total shrinkage rate.

### Glaze selected for a clay body based on color/finish alone without verifying documented glaze fit
- **Usually means:** risk of crazing or shivering after firing, discoverable only after the glaze firing is complete.
- **First question:** does documented fit-test data exist for this specific body/glaze combination?
- **Data to pull:** glaze/body combination used compared against documented fit-test results for that specific pairing.

### Firing schedule applies a uniform ramp rate through known critical temperature ranges rather than a slower rate specifically through those ranges
- **Usually means:** cracking risk from thermal stress even at correct peak temperature.
- **First question:** what was the actual ramp rate through the critical range versus the recommended slower rate?
- **Data to pull:** actual ramp rate through the critical range compared against the recommended rate for this body/ware thickness.

### Bisque and glaze firing combined, or bisque firing rushed/skipped to save kiln time
- **Usually means:** incorrect body porosity for glaze application, affecting glaze behavior in the subsequent firing.
- **First question:** does the firing schedule actually follow the standard bisque-then-glaze sequence for this body/glaze system?
- **Data to pull:** firing schedule/sequence used compared against the standard sequence for this body/glaze system.

### Firing verified by kiln controller temperature readout alone, without pyrometric cone confirmation
- **Usually means:** the ware may be under- or over-fired despite the controller showing correct peak temperature, if ramp rate or hold time differed from assumptions.
- **First question:** what does the cone deformation result show versus the kiln controller temperature log?
- **Data to pull:** cone deformation result compared against kiln controller temperature log for the same firing.

### A recurring crazing, shivering, or cracking defect pattern for a specific body/glaze/firing combination appears without a traced cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — shrinkage compensation, glaze fit, ramp rate, firing sequence — rather than isolated one-off piece variation.
- **First question:** does the defect occurrence log correlate with a specific combination or process parameter?
- **Data to pull:** defect occurrence log by body/glaze/firing combination compared against process parameter logs over the same period.

### A new clay body or glaze introduced to production without first verifying shrinkage rate and glaze fit
- **Usually means:** process parameters validated for a different body/glaze may not apply, producing dimensional or fit defects.
- **First question:** was the new material's documented shrinkage/fit data actually verified before full production use?
- **Data to pull:** new material's documented shrinkage/fit data compared against whether it was verified before full production use.
