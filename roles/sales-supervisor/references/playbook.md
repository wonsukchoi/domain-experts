# Playbook

Filled operational templates a non-retail sales supervisor executes directly — not descriptions of what they'd contain.

## Ramp-quota schedule (filled example, 90-day ramp, full quota $180,000/month)

| Month | % of full quota | Target | Actual (example rep) | Attainment vs. ramp target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 33% | $59,400 | $52,000 | 87.5% |
| 2 | 67% | $120,600 | $75,000 | 62.2% |
| 3 | 100% | $180,000 | — (not yet closed) | — |
| 4+ | 100% | $180,000 | graded on flat quota going forward | — |

Rule: a rep inside months 1–3 is graded against the ramp target in this table, never the flat $180,000 figure. A rep who inherits an existing book or active territory on day one skips the ramp and starts at 100%.

## Discount/credit-approval matrix (filled example, wholesale distributor)

| Discount or term request | Who can approve | Required check | Escalates to |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard price list, no exception | Rep, no approval needed | — | — |
| Up to 5% off list, existing account in good standing | Sales supervisor | Account not on credit hold; last invoice paid within terms | — |
| 5–12% off list, or extending net-30 to net-60 | Sales supervisor + credit/finance sign-off | Current DSO on the account; AR aging report pulled same day | Finance, same day |
| Over 12% off list, or any term extension past net-60 | District/regional sales manager | Written business case; competitive quote if cited | District manager |
| Any exception on an account with a credit hold, regardless of discount size | Finance/credit only — supervisor cannot approve | Full AR aging and credit-hold reason | Finance, before any commitment is made to the customer |

## Territory / account rebalancing worksheet (filled example, 6-rep team)

| Rep | Active accounts | Trailing-12-mo. revenue | Avg. revenue/account | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 34 | $1,890,000 | $55,588 | 2.0x tenured-team-average book — rebalance candidate |
| B | 41 | $1,240,000 | $30,244 | Within normal range |
| C | 38 | $980,000 | $25,789 | Within normal range |
| D | 36 | $1,010,000 | $28,056 | Within normal range |
| E | 29 | $760,000 | $26,207 | Within normal range |
| F (ramping) | 12 | $180,000 | $15,000 | New book, still building |
| **Tenured-team avg. (B–E, excl. A the flag candidate and F the ramping rep)** | **36.0** | **$3,990,000 / 4 reps** | **$27,708** | |

Rule: a rep at or above roughly 2x the tenured-team-average revenue/account (computed excluding the flagged rep and any still-ramping rep, so the outlier doesn't inflate its own baseline) gets a rebalancing review before any coaching plan — moving 6–10 of Rep A's smaller accounts to Rep E (whose book is thinnest and lowest-revenue among tenured reps) is evaluated before assuming either rep has a skill gap.

## Weekly floor report template (filled — see SKILL.md worked example for the fully populated version)

```
Week-ending floor report — [Team name]
Team attainment: [X%] ([$actual] / [$ramp-adjusted quota]) — [flat-quota number if materially different, and why]
Compliance flag(s), resolved or open: [abandonment rate / draw-balance / classification issue, with the specific number and the fix applied or pending]
Root cause by rep below target: [funnel stage that broke — dials, connects, quotes, close rate — and the specific fix]
Ramping reps: [ramp-adjusted attainment, no PIP inside the ramp window unless activity itself is below norm]
No action: [reps on or above target, with the specific reason no lopsided-book flag applies]
Escalating: [anything past supervisor authority, named specifically — account, dollar amount, requested approval]
```

## Coaching ride-along scorecard (filled example)

| Call segment | What's being checked | This call | Fix assigned |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening (first 30 sec) | States name, company, reason for call before pitching | Skipped reason for call, went straight to pitch | Script the first line, re-check next call |
| Discovery | Asks at least 2 open questions before quoting price | Asked 1, then quoted | Add discovery-question prompt to CRM call screen |
| Objection handling | Restates the objection before responding | Responded immediately without restating | Role-play 3 common objections next 1:1 |
| Close/next step | States a specific next step with a date, not "I'll follow up" | Said "I'll follow up," no date set | Require a dated next-step field before call is logged closed |

## Draw-against-commission reconciliation (filled example)

Rep on a $3,000/month recoverable draw against a 6% commission plan, terminated mid-month with $9,400 in unearned draw accumulated over the prior quarter (draw paid out consistently exceeded commissions earned).

| Line | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Draw paid, trailing quarter | $9,000 | 3 months × $3,000 |
| Commissions earned, trailing quarter | $6,850 | Against $114,167 in bookings at 6% |
| Unearned draw balance at termination | $2,150 | $9,000 − $6,850 |
| Deducted from final paycheck | **$0** | State wage law prohibits deducting an unearned draw balance from final pay as a wage-kickback risk regardless of plan language calling it "recoverable" |
| Treatment | Written off as a compensation cost | Logged to finance as a plan-design flag for the comp-plan owner — a 4th consecutive month of draw exceeding earned commission is the trigger to review this rep's quota or plan structure, not to collect the shortfall from them |
