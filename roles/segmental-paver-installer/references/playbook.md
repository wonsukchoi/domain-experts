# Segmental Paver Installation Playbook

Filled templates for take-off, base design, and installation sequence. Populate with the job's real numbers — don't work from the blank column headers.

## Base thickness by subgrade CBR and load (ICPI Tech Spec 4 guidance)

| Application | Subgrade CBR | Compacted base depth | Paver thickness |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pedestrian (patio, walkway) | ≥ 4% (sand/gravel, well-drained) | 4" (100mm) | 60mm |
| Pedestrian (patio, walkway) | < 4% (clay, poorly drained) | 6" (150mm) + geotextile | 60mm |
| Residential driveway, light vehicular | ≥ 4% | 6" (150mm) | 80mm |
| Residential driveway, light vehicular | < 4% | 8" (200mm) + geotextile | 80mm |
| Commercial/collector street, occasional heavy trucks | ≥ 4% | 10-12" (250-300mm), engineered per project geotech | 80mm |

CBR under 3% or any visible pumping/soft spot during proof-roll: stop and call for a geotech evaluation before proceeding on driveway or commercial work — the table above assumes a floor of CBR 3%, not weaker.

## Take-off formulas

**Base aggregate:** area (sq ft) x depth (ft) / 27 = compacted cu yd. Compacted density for dense-graded aggregate ≈ 1.4 tons/cu yd — multiply for tonnage to order.

**Bedding sand:** area (sq ft) x (1/12 ft) / 27 = loose cu yd. Sand density ≈ 1.35 tons/cu yd.

**Pavers:** area (sq ft) x coverage rate (units/sq ft) = net units. Add waste: 5% for a straight-run running bond with no border, 10% for a soldier-course border or herringbone with corners, 15%+ for circular patios or heavy custom cutting. Round up to whole units, then to whole pallets for ordering.

## Joint (polymeric) sand coverage

| Joint width | Coverage per 50-lb bag |
|---|---|
| 2-3mm (tight, 80mm vehicular pavers) | 80-100 sq ft |
| 3-5mm (standard, 60mm pedestrian pavers) | 60-80 sq ft |
| 5mm+ (wide-joint/permeable systems) | 40-60 sq ft |

Wider joints and permeable/open-graded joint aggregate use a different product entirely (No. 8 or No. 89 chip, not polymeric sand) — don't substitute polymeric sand into a permeable-pavement joint spec.

## Lift-compaction sequence (base)

1. Place base material in loose lifts no deeper than 4-6" (a thicker loose lift won't compact through its full depth regardless of pass count).
2. Compact each lift to 98% Standard Proctor density (ASTM D698) before placing the next lift — use a plate or vibratory roller sized to the area (walk-behind plate for residential, ride-on vibratory roller for commercial runs).
3. Proof-roll the finished base with a loaded wheelbarrow or small vehicle — no visible rutting, pumping, or deflection before moving to bedding sand.
4. Re-check final grade and slope against the design line (laser level or string line) before screeding bedding sand — correcting slope in the bedding layer instead of the base wastes sand and produces an uneven bed depth.

## Laying and jointing sequence

1. Set screed rails at the design bedding depth (1" loose), screed sand in one pass per section, do not walk on screeded sand before laying.
2. Lay border/edge course first, then work the field pattern into it; check joint width every 4-5 rows against the 2-5mm tolerance.
3. Cut border and closure units with a wet-cut or vacuum-shrouded saw; no cut piece under 1/3 of a full unit — resize the layout instead.
4. Compact dry (plate with protective pad, 2 passes) before jointing sand goes down.
5. Sweep polymeric sand into joints, vibrate/compact again to settle it fully into the joint depth, remove excess from the surface before any moisture contact.
6. Lightly mist per the joint-sand manufacturer's activation instructions (product-specific — confirm dry vs. wet-activated formulation before applying water).
7. Install edge restraint, spiked every 12" on straight runs and 18" on curves, before final cleanup.

## Slope and drainage check

Minimum 1.5% (3/16" per foot) slope away from any structure; 1% (1/8" per foot) is an absolute floor only on short runs with a confirmed downstream outlet. Compute total fall = slope (decimal) x run length (ft); verify against the actual elevation difference with a level, not by eye.

Example: 40 ft run at 1.5% = 0.015 x 40 = 0.6 ft = 7.2" of fall required end to end.
