# Red Flags

### Base proof-roll shows visible rutting or pumping under a loaded wheelbarrow
- **Usually means:** subgrade CBR is weaker than assumed (clay pocket, spring seep), or a base lift was placed thicker than 6" loose and never compacted through its full depth.
- **First question:** was this lift compacted individually, or placed on top of an already-questionable layer without a density check?
- **Data to pull:** lift log (depth and compaction pass count per lift), any subgrade soil test or geotech note from bid.

### Joint width measuring under 2mm or over 5mm on a spot check
- **Usually means:** paver spacing lugs are undersized/oversized for the pattern, or units are creeping during laying because the bedding sand wasn't screeded to a uniform depth.
- **First question:** is this isolated to one section (fixable now) or consistent across the field (layout error, stop and re-plan)?
- **Data to pull:** joint-width measurements across a 10-unit sample in the affected area vs. an unaffected area.

### Standing water on the surface more than 15 minutes after a moderate rain
- **Usually means:** a low spot from an uncorrected base grade, or slope was built below the 1.5% minimum and rounds down to flat in practice.
- **First question:** does the surface elevation survey (or a simple level check) show positive fall across the full run, or is there a reverse-graded section?
- **Data to pull:** as-built elevation shots at 10-ft intervals along the drainage path, compared to the design grade sheet.

### Paver edges spreading or a border line visibly bowing outward, 6-18 months post-install
- **Usually means:** missing or under-spiked edge restraint, most often at a curve or a vehicle-turning radius where lateral load is highest.
- **First question:** is restraint present and spiked at the correct interval (12" straight / 18" curve), or was it value-engineered out or spiked too sparsely?
- **Data to pull:** photos of the restraint line before backfill (if taken), spike spacing measurement where restraint is still accessible.

### Sinking or settling section, localized, appearing weeks to months after install
- **Usually means:** a utility trench or soft spot under that section wasn't fully compacted before base placement, or water is migrating under the base from a nearby downspout/grade break.
- **First question:** does the low spot align with a known trench crossing, or with a drainage path from an adjacent structure?
- **Data to pull:** utility locate records for the area, any as-built showing trench crossings under the paved section.

### Polymeric sand haze or white residue left on the paver surface after cure
- **Usually means:** excess sand wasn't fully removed/blown off before activation, or the surface was misted with more water than the product spec calls for.
- **First question:** was the manufacturer's specific water-activation volume and method followed, or was a generic "light mist" applied without checking the bag instructions?
- **Data to pull:** product data sheet's activation procedure, actual application log (surface-clean method, water volume/method used).

### Efflorescence (white, powdery deposit) on paver faces within the first year
- **Usually means:** moisture wicking soluble salts up through the base/bedding system — common with certain aggregate sources or where base drainage is blocked, generally cosmetic and not structural.
- **First question:** is it isolated to a section with poor drainage (against a wall, low spot) or spread evenly across the whole field (aggregate source issue)?
- **Data to pull:** base aggregate source/spec sheet, drainage path check for the affected section.

### Cracked or chipped paver units clustered near the border/cut line
- **Usually means:** cut units were sized under 1/3 of a full unit and lack enough interlock to resist edge loading, or the wet saw wasn't producing a clean, square cut.
- **First question:** what fraction of a full unit are the cracked pieces, and were they all cut with the same saw/blade condition?
- **Data to pull:** measurements of the cracked units against the 1/3-unit minimum, cut-list layout for that border run.

### Client reports "hollow" or "rocking" feel underfoot on specific units
- **Usually means:** a void under that unit from an inconsistent bedding-sand screed depth or a base low spot that wasn't caught during the proof-roll.
- **First question:** is it one isolated unit (spot-fix: lift, re-bed, reset) or a pattern across a section (base issue, bigger fix)?
- **Data to pull:** the affected unit's location relative to the base lift log and any documented soft spots from install.
