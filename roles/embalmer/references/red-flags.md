# Embalmer — Red Flags

### Two or more decomposition indicators present regardless of stated time-since-death
- **Usually means:** ambient temperature during the discovery/transport interval was higher than assumed, or cause of death (sepsis, prolonged illness) accelerated tissue breakdown independent of elapsed time.
- **First question:** "What was the actual storage/transport temperature and duration before this case reached me, not just the reported time of death?"
- **Data to pull:** transport/refrigeration log, cause-of-death documentation, intake assessment notes.

### Arterial injection meets resistance or fluid isn't distributing evenly
- **Usually means:** a vascular blockage (clot, prior medical intervention, calcification) or an incorrect injection site for this case's vasculature.
- **First question:** "Is the resistance localized to one region, or is drainage backing up at the injection site itself?"
- **Data to pull:** injection-site selection rationale, any known medical history (surgeries, vascular disease) from case intake.

### Tissue firms unevenly — some areas over-hardened, others still soft after standard injection time
- **Usually means:** uneven fluid distribution, often from a single-point injection on a case that needed multi-site treatment (autopsy, edema, obesity).
- **First question:** "Does this case's condition (autopsy status, edema, body composition) match the injection technique I used, or did I default to standard single-point on a non-standard case?"
- **Data to pull:** case-condition intake notes, injection-site log.

### Strong odor persists after cavity treatment and arterial injection
- **Usually means:** cavity aspiration was incomplete, or decomposition had progressed further than the initial assessment indicated.
- **First question:** "Was cavity aspiration thorough, or did I treat this as a standard case before confirming the decomposition stage?"
- **Data to pull:** cavity-treatment record, decomposition-indicator checklist from intake.

### Family's requested viewing timeline is shorter than the case's recommended hold period
- **Usually means:** the case's condition (edema, decomposition stage, cause of death) requires more preservation time than a standard case, and that hasn't been communicated to the funeral director yet.
- **First question:** "Has the funeral director been told this case needs more time before the family finds out at the viewing?"
- **Data to pull:** case-condition assessment, viewing-readiness recommendation, funeral director communication log.

### Skin discoloration or trauma area doesn't match the family-supplied reference photograph after restorative work
- **Usually means:** color-matching was done against the facility's standard cosmetic set rather than a natural-tone comparison, or the restorative build-up was completed in too few layers to get an accurate form match.
- **First question:** "Did I check color and form against the reference photo after each layer, or only at the end?"
- **Data to pull:** before/after photographs, reference photograph used.

### Formaldehyde exposure monitoring shows readings approaching the OSHA PEL or STEL on a high-case-volume day
- **Usually means:** ventilation isn't keeping pace with case volume, or case scheduling is bunching high-formaldehyde-index cases (decomposition, larger dilution volumes) into the same shift without spacing.
- **First question:** "Is this a ventilation problem, a case-scheduling problem, or both?"
- **Data to pull:** exposure-monitoring log, case schedule for the shift, ventilation-system maintenance record.

### A case arrives with incomplete or contradictory intake information (unclear cause of death, unknown time since death)
- **Usually means:** information gaps at intake, not necessarily an unusual case — but proceeding on assumptions instead of confirming can lead to a mismatched dilution ratio or missed decomposition indicators.
- **First question:** "What can I confirm through direct physical assessment right now, independent of what the intake paperwork says?"
- **Data to pull:** intake documentation, direct physical assessment against the decomposition-indicator checklist.
