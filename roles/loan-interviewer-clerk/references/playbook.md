# Loan interviewer/clerk playbook

## Document-checklist matrix by income type

| Income type | Required documents | Date-window rule |
|---|---|---|
| W-2 employee | Most recent 30 days of paystubs (YTD totals shown), most recent 2 years' W-2s, verbal or written VOE within 10 business days of closing | Paystubs must show YTD as of within 30 days of application |
| Self-employed (sole prop/1099) | 2 years personal tax returns (all schedules), YTD profit-and-loss statement, business license or CPA letter confirming 2+ years in business | P&L must be dated within 60 days of closing |
| Self-employed (S-corp/partnership) | 2 years personal AND business tax returns, K-1s, YTD P&L, business bank statements (2–3 months) | Same as above |
| Retirement/Social Security | Award letter or 1099 showing gross benefit, 2 most recent monthly bank statements showing deposit | Award letter must not project an end date within 3 years of closing (or income can't be used) |
| Rental income | Lease agreements, 2 years Schedule E (if previously reported), most recent mortgage statement on the rental property | Lease must be current, not expired |

## Conditions-tracking table (example, mid-file)

| Condition # | Description | PTA/PTF | Owner | Status | Follow-up date |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2-yr self-employment income documentation | PTA | Processor | Cleared | — |
| 2 | Source $9,200 deposit, business checking | PTA | Borrower | Cleared 9/14 | — |
| 3 | Updated homeowners-insurance binder | PTF | Borrower | Open | 9/20 (rate lock exp. 9/28) |
| 4 | Resolve name discrepancy (application vs. title report) | PTA | Loan officer | Escalated | 9/14 |
| 5 | Final VOE within 10 days of closing | PTF | Processor | Not due yet | Schedule for 9/25 |

A file is not ready to submit to underwriting with any PTA condition still open — PTF conditions can trail into the closing window but must all clear before the closing disclosure is finalized.

## Deposit-sourcing worksheet (example)

| Deposit date | Amount | Account | Pattern check | Threshold (25% of $5,375 = $1,344) | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 9/2 | $1,150 | Business checking | Matches typical weekly client deposit range ($800–$2,400) | Under threshold | No action — consistent with income pattern |
| 9/12 | $9,200 | Business checking | Outside typical deposit range and size | Over threshold | Sourcing letter + supporting documentation requested |
| 9/18 | $2,000 | Personal savings | Matches recurring transfer from business account (verified against business-account withdrawal same date) | Over threshold but internally consistent | Documented as inter-account transfer, no external sourcing needed — flag as internal transfer, not new income |

The third row is the common exception: a deposit that crosses the dollar threshold but is a transfer between the borrower's own accounts, not new unexplained money, only needs the transfer traced — not a full external-sourcing letter.

## Conditions-needed letter (filled template)

> Dear [Borrower],
>
> Your loan file has the following open item(s) before we can move to closing:
>
> 1. **Homeowners insurance binder** — please provide a current insurance binder or policy declarations page showing coverage effective on or before your closing date, with the lender listed as mortgagee.
>
> Please provide this by [date — set to allow buffer before rate-lock expiration]. If you have questions about what's required, contact [processor name/phone] directly.
>
> This request does not indicate any issue with your application — it's a standard step before closing.
