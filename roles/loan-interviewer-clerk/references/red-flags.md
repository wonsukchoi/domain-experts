# Red flags — loan interviewer/clerk

### Deposit exceeding ~25% of qualifying monthly income with no clear source
- **Usually means:** Undisclosed loan/liability, gift without documentation, or (less commonly) an income source not yet captured in the application.
- **First question:** Does the deposit pattern match the borrower's known recurring income, or is it a one-off?
- **Data to pull:** Full bank-statement history for the account (not just the statement page showing the deposit), payer identification if traceable.

### Name, address, or SSN mismatch across submitted documents
- **Usually means:** Maiden/legal name change not yet reflected everywhere, a data-entry error, or (rarely) identity inconsistency requiring fraud review.
- **First question:** Is there a legal document (marriage certificate, court order) explaining the name difference?
- **Data to pull:** Government-issued ID, credit report name history.

### Self-employed borrower's YTD P&L income materially exceeds the 2-year tax-return average
- **Usually means:** A genuinely improving business, seasonal timing quirk, or an inflated/unaudited P&L the borrower hopes will be used instead of the lower tax-return figure.
- **First question:** Is the increase consistent with the two-year trend, or a sudden jump with no explanation?
- **Data to pull:** Prior-year P&L for the same period (year-over-year comparison), business bank statements.

### VOE returns information inconsistent with the application (title, pay rate, start date)
- **Usually means:** The application was filled out with outdated or estimated information, or (less commonly) employment misrepresentation.
- **First question:** Does the corrected information change the qualifying income calculation?
- **Data to pull:** Most recent paystub matching the VOE-confirmed pay rate.

### Borrower unresponsive to a document request within the standard follow-up window
- **Usually means:** Borrower disorganization, difficulty obtaining the specific document (e.g., needs to request from an employer/CPA), or cold feet on the transaction.
- **First question:** Has the borrower acknowledged the request, or gone completely silent?
- **Data to pull:** Communication log (calls, emails) showing outreach attempts and dates.

### Document present but outside the required date window
- **Usually means:** Processor error in requesting the wrong period, or the borrower submitted an old copy they had on hand instead of a current one.
- **First question:** How far outside the window is it, and does the file's overall timeline still allow time to get a current copy?
- **Data to pull:** Investor guideline's specific date-window requirement for that document type.

### Employment gap greater than 30 days in the most recent 2 years, undocumented
- **Usually means:** Between jobs, medical leave, education, or caregiving — all explainable, but each has different documentation requirements (some gaps need only a letter, others need proof of alternate income during the gap).
- **First question:** Is there a written explanation in the file, or just a verbal note from a phone call?
- **Data to pull:** Letter of explanation, and if the gap involved any income (unemployment, disability), documentation of that income.

### Condition cleared with a document that doesn't actually match the condition's stated requirement
- **Usually means:** A processor under time pressure marked a condition complete because a document arrived, without verifying it satisfies the specific requirement.
- **First question:** Re-read the exact condition language — does the submitted document address it word-for-word, not just topically?
- **Data to pull:** The original underwriting condition text, compared line-by-line against the document received.

### Rate-lock expiration inside 10 business days with open PTF conditions
- **Usually means:** Processing delay, borrower slow-walking a document, or a condition that's proving harder to clear than expected (e.g., an insurance binder from a hard-to-reach agent).
- **First question:** Which specific condition is blocking, and who owns clearing it?
- **Data to pull:** Conditions-tracking table with owner and due-date columns, rate-lock expiration date.
