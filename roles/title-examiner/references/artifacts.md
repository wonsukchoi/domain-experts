# Title commitment, defect classification, and chain-of-title artifacts

Filled templates for the three recurring title-examiner deliverables: title commitment structure, defect classification worksheet, and chain-of-title tracing log.

## 1. Title commitment structure (ALTA-style)

```
SCHEDULE A
1. Effective date: <date>
2. Policy to be issued: <owner's policy / loan policy, amount>
3. Title to the estate is vested in: <current owner name, exactly as it appears on the vesting deed>
4. Title is estate in fee simple / leasehold / other: <specify>
5. Legal description: <attached as Exhibit A>

SCHEDULE B - Section 1 (Requirements, must be satisfied before policy issues)
1. Payment of the full consideration to the seller/borrower.
2. Recording of the deed/mortgage in proper form.
3. <Any curative document required — e.g., "Corrective deed correcting the legal
   description error in the 2019 deed, Book 412 Page 88.">
4. <Name-identity affidavit if a same-name match requires resolution.>

SCHEDULE B - Section 2 (Exceptions, standard and special)
1. Standard exceptions (taxes not yet due, rights of parties in possession, etc.)
2. Easement recorded [date], Book [X] Page [Y], for utility access.
3. <Any defect resolved by insuring over rather than curing, stated as a specific exception.>
```

## 2. Defect classification worksheet

| Defect found | Classification | Basis | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Judgment against similarly-named individual, 8 years old | Non-attaching (after verification) | 4 corroborating non-matches (address, occupation, suffix, DOB) | Name-identity affidavit |
| Prior mortgage with no recorded satisfaction | Curable | Paid-off but never released | Obtain and record satisfaction/release from prior lender |
| Legal description discrepancy between two deeds in chain (10 years apart) | Curable | Scrivener's error, corroborated by survey match | Corrective deed from prior grantor if available, or quiet title if grantor unavailable |
| Unrecorded easement discovered via current survey | Insurable-with-exception | Doesn't affect marketability, only use of a specific portion | Schedule B exception listing the easement, no cure needed |
| Break in chain — deed from a grantor who never appears as a grantee | Fatal (until resolved) | True gap in chain of title | Requires quiet title action or corrective conveyance before insurable |

Rule: never default every defect to "fatal, must cure" — classify based on the specific facts and underwriting standards, since over-classifying wastes time and can needlessly kill a transaction that a lighter resolution would have closed.

## 3. Chain-of-title tracing log

```
Root of title: <deed/instrument establishing ownership at the start of the required search period>
  Date: <date> | Recorded: Book <X> Page <Y> | Grantor: <name> | Grantee: <name>

Link 1: <next transfer>
  Date: <date> | Recorded: Book <X> Page <Y> | Grantor: <matches prior grantee?> | Grantee: <name>

Link 2: ... (continue through every transfer)

Current owner: <name, exactly as on vesting deed>
  Date acquired: <date> | Recorded: Book <X> Page <Y>

Search period required: <state's marketable title act period>
Search period actually covered: <root date to present, confirm it exceeds the requirement>
Gaps identified: <none, or specify with resolution plan>
```

Rule: every link's grantor must match the prior link's grantee exactly (name, or with a documented name-change basis — marriage, court order) — an unexplained mismatch is a gap in the chain, not a minor discrepancy to note and move past.

## 4. Recording statute type quick-reference

| Statute type | Priority rule |
|---|---|
| Race | First to record wins, regardless of notice of a prior unrecorded interest |
| Notice | A subsequent bona fide purchaser without notice of a prior unrecorded interest wins, regardless of recording order |
| Race-notice | A subsequent bona fide purchaser without notice AND who records first wins — both conditions required |

Confirm which type governs the jurisdiction before analyzing any priority dispute between a recorded and unrecorded (or competing recorded) interest — the same fact pattern produces different outcomes under each type.
