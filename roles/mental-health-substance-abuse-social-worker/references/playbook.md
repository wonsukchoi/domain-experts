# Mental Health and Substance Abuse Social Worker — Playbook

## Co-occurring severity quadrant

Score mental-health severity (PHQ-9, or clinician-rated equivalent) and substance-use severity (AUDIT for alcohol, DAST-10 for drugs) independently, then cross-reference:

| | SA low (AUDIT <20 / DAST-10 <6) | SA high (AUDIT ≥20 / DAST-10 ≥6) |
|---|---|---|
| **MH low** (PHQ-9 <20) | **Quadrant I** — primary care / brief intervention, either system can manage | **Quadrant III** — SUD system leads, MH-informed consultation available |
| **MH high** (PHQ-9 ≥20) | **Quadrant II** — MH system leads, SUD-informed consultation available | **Quadrant IV** — integrated, dual-diagnosis-capable program required; single-domain referral is a documented mismatch |

**Rule:** a Quadrant IV client referred to a single-domain program (MH-only or SUD-only) needs the mismatch documented explicitly in the chart, with the barrier named (no local integrated program, insurance network gap, waitlist), not silently accepted as "the plan."

## ASAM Criteria — six dimensions, filled example

Used to justify a level-of-care placement. Rate each dimension 0 (no risk) to 4 (severe/imminent danger); no single dimension mechanically sums to a level — the pattern across dimensions drives the recommendation, and the highest-severity dimension often sets the floor.

| Dimension | Example finding | Rating |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Acute intoxication/withdrawal potential | CIWA-Ar = 3 at 96 hrs post-last-drink; no withdrawal management needed | 0 |
| 2. Biomedical conditions/complications | No active medical conditions requiring concurrent management | 0 |
| 3. Emotional/behavioral/cognitive conditions | PHQ-9 = 22 (severe), recent suicide attempt 72 hrs prior | 3 |
| 4. Readiness to change | Contemplation; no independent SUD-reduction plan expressed | 2 |
| 5. Relapse/continued use potential | Daily use pattern 1 year, no prior treatment episode, no coping skills established | 3 |
| 6. Recovery/living environment | Stable housing with sister; sister is supportive but untrained in relapse triggers | 1 |

**Level-of-care ladder (ASAM), low to high:** 0.5 Early Intervention → 1 Outpatient → 2.1 Intensive Outpatient (IOP) → 2.5 Partial Hospitalization (PHP) → 3.1 Clinically Managed Low-Intensity Residential → 3.5 Clinically Managed High-Intensity Residential → 3.7 Medically Monitored Intensive Inpatient → 4 Medically Managed Intensive Inpatient.

**Read for the example above:** Dimension 3 and 5 severity (both 3), with Dimension 1 clear and Dimension 6 marginal, points to Level 2.5 (PHP with integrated psychiatric and SUD services) rather than residential (no medical/safety indication for 24-hour containment) or standard outpatient (severity too high for once-weekly contact).

## Civil commitment documentation checklist

Complete before any commitment petition or hold recommendation. Most US state statutes use a version of this tripartite standard — verify the exact codified language and hold duration in the specific jurisdiction.

| Standard | Evidence required | Do NOT rely on |
|---|---|---|
| Danger to self | Specific act, statement of intent, or plan within a recent, defined window (e.g., "attempted overdose 72 hours ago," not "has a history of depression") | General diagnosis, past history alone, current calm presentation |
| Danger to others | Specific threat, plan, or act directed at an identifiable person or class of persons, recent | Vague hostility, diagnosis-based assumption |
| Grave disability | Documented, current inability to provide food, clothing, or shelter *because of* a mental disorder — not situational poverty or homelessness alone | "Seems unable to cope," housing status without the causal link to the disorder |

**Documentation template:**
```
CRITERION MET: [danger to self / danger to others / grave disability]
SPECIFIC EVIDENCE: [dated behavior/statement — quote if possible]
TIMEFRAME: [when the evidence occurred relative to this assessment]
LESS-RESTRICTIVE ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED: [safety plan, voluntary admission,
  crisis respite — and why each was insufficient given the evidence above]
RECOMMENDATION: [petition for involuntary hold / voluntary admission accepted /
  outpatient safety plan with specified follow-up]
```
The "less-restrictive alternatives considered" line is the field most often missing — and the one a reviewing judge or hearing officer asks for first.

## 42 CFR Part 2 disclosure decision

| Situation | Requires Part 2-specific consent? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sharing SUD treatment info with the client's outpatient psychiatrist | Yes | Name the recipient, the specific information, and the purpose; a blanket "treatment purposes" release does not qualify. |
| Sharing with another program under the same umbrella agency (QSOA in place) | Consent may not be required | Only if a written Qualified Service Organization Agreement exists between the entities. |
| Court subpoena for SUD records | Ordinary subpoena: no. Court order meeting Part 2's stricter criteria (Subpart E, with notice and opportunity to object): yes, disclosure permitted per order's scope | A routine subpoena alone is insufficient under Part 2, unlike most other health records. |
| Medical emergency (client unconscious, unknown overdose history) | No — Part 2's bona fide medical emergency exception applies | Document the specific emergency and limit disclosure to what the emergency requires. |
| Sharing with the client's employer or a family member "just to keep them informed" | Yes, and default to declining absent a signed, purpose-specific consent from the client | This is the disclosure category Part 2 exists specifically to prevent. |

## Case-management intensity matching

| Client profile | Recommended intensity | Typical staff:client ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Stable, ≤1 hospitalization/year, housed, engaged in treatment | Standard case management | 1:25–40 |
| ≥2 psychiatric hospitalizations in 12 months, or housing instability, meets SPMI criteria | Assertive Community Treatment (ACT) | ~1:10 |
| High-acuity but ACT-ineligible (e.g., primary SUD without qualifying SPMI diagnosis) | Intensive case management / SUD-specific care coordination | 1:15–20 |

**Rule:** re-evaluate intensity at every hospitalization, not on a fixed annual review cycle — hospitalization count is the strongest available predictor of whether current intensity is adequate.

## Warm handoff script (dual-diagnosis discharge or transfer)

1. Call the receiving program's intake coordinator directly — a referral fax or portal submission alone does not constitute a handoff.
2. State the co-occurring quadrant, the specific risk flags (recent attempt, withdrawal status, C-SSRS finding), and the ASAM level recommended.
3. Confirm Part 2 consent is executed and scoped correctly for the information being shared in this call and in the written transfer.
4. Confirm the receiving program has a bed/appointment slot and the client's insurance/Medicaid is verified active.
5. Document: who was spoken with, when, what was confirmed, and the Part 2 consent reference.
