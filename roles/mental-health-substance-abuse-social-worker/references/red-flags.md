# Mental Health and Substance Abuse Social Worker — Red Flags

### Client screens severe on both PHQ-9 (≥20) and AUDIT (≥20) but chart shows two separate, unlinked referrals

- **Usually means:** severity was scored but never translated into a Quadrant IV integrated-placement decision — the referrals were generated by domain (whoever screened first) rather than by the severity pattern.
- **First question:** "Did anyone plot both scores onto the co-occurring quadrant before choosing where to refer?"
- **Data to pull:** PHQ-9 and AUDIT/DAST-10 scores and dates; referral log showing whether the two referrals are to a single integrated program or two unconnected agencies.

### Commitment recommended on "chronic ideation" with no dated, specific behavioral evidence in the last 72 hours

- **Usually means:** the recommendation is running on clinical impression or defensive caution rather than the statutory danger/grave-disability standard, and will likely be rejected or challenged at hearing.
- **First question:** "What specific, dated behavior or statement in the recent window meets the standard — not the diagnosis history?"
- **Data to pull:** the commitment documentation template's evidence and timeframe fields; whether less-restrictive alternatives were documented as considered.

### SUD treatment information faxed or emailed under a routine HIPAA authorization

- **Usually means:** the sender treated Part 2 and HIPAA as interchangeable, which is the most common Part 2 compliance failure and exposes both the agency and the client.
- **First question:** "Was there a Part 2-specific consent naming this recipient, this information, and this purpose — or just a general HIPAA release?"
- **Data to pull:** the consent form on file; whether a Qualified Service Organization Agreement exists if no separate consent was obtained.

### Client with ≥2 psychiatric hospitalizations in 12 months still on standard case management (1:25–40 ratio)

- **Usually means:** intensity was set once at intake and never re-evaluated against the hospitalization count that should trigger an ACT-level review.
- **First question:** "When was case-management intensity last reassessed, and against what trigger?"
- **Data to pull:** 12-month hospitalization count; date of last intensity review; ACT program eligibility criteria and waitlist status.

### Discharge/transition plan lists agency names and phone numbers with no confirmed appointment or enrollment

- **Usually means:** the plan was built as a referral list rather than a verified-access plan, and will likely fail at the first unconfirmed step after the case closes.
- **First question:** "Which of these referrals has a booked appointment or confirmed enrollment, and which are just contact information?"
- **Data to pull:** appointment confirmation records; insurance/Medicaid verification status; date of last contact with the receiving program.

### Client placed in an action-stage curriculum (relapse-prevention group, structured SUD program) on day one of engagement

- **Usually means:** stage of change was never assessed, or the client's stated willingness was mistaken for actual readiness.
- **First question:** "What evidence, beyond what the client said, shows action-stage behavior — an intake attended, a taper started, a sponsor secured?"
- **Data to pull:** stage-of-change documentation; attendance/engagement history in any prior treatment episode.

### Withdrawal risk (CIWA-Ar/COWS) not reassessed before a placement decision made days after admission

- **Usually means:** the initial score is being treated as permanent rather than time-bound, and the placement decision may be based on stale medical information.
- **First question:** "When was withdrawal risk last scored, relative to today's placement decision?"
- **Data to pull:** CIWA-Ar/COWS scoring timestamps; time since last use; any medical clearance note.

### Grave-disability justification cites homelessness or poverty without the causal link to a mental disorder

- **Usually means:** the assessment is conflating situational hardship with the statutory standard, which requires the inability to meet basic needs to result *from* the disorder.
- **First question:** "What evidence connects the inability to secure food, clothing, or shelter specifically to the mental disorder, rather than to external circumstances?"
- **Data to pull:** functional-status documentation; history of ability to self-manage basic needs prior to symptom onset or exacerbation.

### Family member's or sponsor's stated willingness to support the client accepted without independent verification

- **Usually means:** the plan is relying on a report of support rather than a confirmed, specific commitment — the same failure pattern as an unverified medical caregiver, applied here to sober-support or housing arrangements.
- **First question:** "Has anyone spoken directly with the support person about what, specifically, they're able to provide and for how long?"
- **Data to pull:** documented contact with the support person; specificity of the commitment (housing duration, supervision, transportation).

### Case closed immediately after a Quadrant IV referral is made, with no follow-up to confirm the client attended intake

- **Usually means:** the case-closure standard is "referral made" rather than "connection confirmed," which is especially costly for high-severity clients with the highest drop-out risk between referral and first appointment.
- **First question:** "Is there a documented follow-up contact confirming the client attended the first appointment?"
- **Data to pull:** case-closure checklist; date of referral vs. date of confirmed first contact with the receiving program.
