### A worker was recruited and traveled before receiving a written wage/job disclosure
- **Usually means:** A disclosure-timing violation — MSPA requires the written disclosure at the time of recruitment, before travel, not as a follow-up after arrival.
- **First question:** When was the written disclosure delivered relative to when the worker traveled or began work?
- **Data to pull:** Signed/acknowledged disclosure form with date, worker's travel/arrival date.

### A piece-rate worker's effective hourly rate (earnings ÷ hours) falls below the applicable minimum wage for a pay period
- **Usually means:** A true-up payment is owed for that period — the piece-rate total alone does not satisfy the wage floor requirement.
- **First question:** Has the true-up been calculated and paid for every pay period, not just averaged across the season?
- **Data to pull:** Hours worked and piece-rate earnings by pay period, applicable minimum wage/AEWR.

### Housing is being provided to workers without a documented pre-occupancy inspection against the applicable labor camp standard
- **Usually means:** A compliance gap that creates liability regardless of the housing's actual condition — the inspection requirement is independent of whether conditions turn out to be adequate.
- **First question:** Has this specific housing unit been inspected against the temporary labor camp standard before workers occupied it?
- **Data to pull:** Housing inspection record, occupancy start date.

### Workers are being transported in a vehicle without verified insurance, safety inspection, or appropriately licensed driver
- **Usually means:** One of the highest-liability, highest-fatality-risk compliance gaps in agricultural labor — enforcement agencies treat this as a priority area.
- **First question:** Has this specific vehicle's insurance, inspection status, and driver's license class been verified before this trip?
- **Data to pull:** Vehicle insurance and inspection records, driver's license class and current status.

### A grower client's contract labels the farm labor contractor as sole employer, but the grower directly supervises day-to-day work
- **Usually means:** The grower may be a joint employer under the economic-realities test regardless of contract language, exposing both parties to shared liability.
- **First question:** Who actually controls day-to-day work conditions, scheduling, and supervision — the contractor, the grower, or both?
- **Data to pull:** Actual supervision practices, equipment ownership, hiring/firing authority documentation.

### An H-2A contract period is ending with actual workdays significantly below what was promised in the job order
- **Usually means:** The three-quarter guarantee obligation may be triggered, requiring payment for the shortfall between 75% of the contract period's workdays and actual workdays provided.
- **First question:** What percentage of the contract period's total workdays has actually been worked, and does it fall below 75%?
- **Data to pull:** Job order's stated contract period and workday schedule, actual workday log.

### A recruitment or supervision task was subcontracted to another farm labor contractor without checking their license status
- **Usually means:** Using an unlicensed subcontractor creates liability that flows back to the contractor who engaged them.
- **First question:** Is the subcontractor's federal MSPA registration and applicable state license current as of this engagement?
- **Data to pull:** Subcontractor's license/registration records, engagement date.

### Wage disclosure was delivered verbally with no written or signed record
- **Usually means:** A documentation gap that will be hard to defend in an enforcement review, even if the verbal disclosure was accurate and timely.
- **First question:** Is there a signed or otherwise documented acknowledgment of the written disclosure, separate from any verbal explanation given?
- **Data to pull:** Any written disclosure record, worker acknowledgment/signature.
