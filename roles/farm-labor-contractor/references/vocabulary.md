### MSPA (Migrant and Seasonal Agricultural Worker Protection Act)
The federal law establishing registration, wage disclosure, housing, and transportation safety requirements for farm labor contractors.
**In use:** "MSPA registration has to be current before we start recruiting for this season."
**Common misuse:** Assuming state-level FLC licensing alone satisfies federal MSPA registration requirements — the two are separate obligations.

### Wage and job disclosure
The written statement of terms (wage rate, pay basis, work location, housing, transportation) that MSPA requires be given to a worker at the time of recruitment, before travel.
**In use:** "The disclosure needs to go out in Spanish and English before anyone commits to travel."
**Common misuse:** Delivering the disclosure verbally, or in writing only after the worker has already arrived, which doesn't satisfy the timing and form requirement.

### Piece-rate pay
A wage structure paying workers per unit of output (e.g., per pound, per bin, per row) rather than a straight hourly rate.
**In use:** "Piece rate here is $0.45 per pound picked."
**Common misuse:** Treating piece-rate earnings as automatically compliant without checking the effective hourly rate against the applicable minimum wage each pay period.

### True-up (minimum wage true-up)
The additional payment required when a piece-rate worker's earnings for hours actually worked fall below the applicable minimum wage or AEWR — bringing total pay up to the wage floor.
**In use:** "Effective rate came in under minimum wage, so a $25.50 true-up is owed for the day."
**Common misuse:** Averaging piece-rate earnings across a whole season or multiple days instead of calculating and truing up each pay period individually, per applicable wage-and-hour rules.

### Adverse Effect Wage Rate (AEWR)
The minimum wage rate the U.S. Department of Labor sets for H-2A agricultural workers, intended to prevent depressing wages for similarly employed U.S. workers.
**In use:** "AEWR for this state and job classification is $16.25/hour — that's the floor for H-2A workers here."
**Common misuse:** Applying the general state minimum wage to H-2A workers instead of the (often higher) AEWR specific to their job classification and location.

### Three-quarter guarantee
The H-2A program requirement that an employer guarantee work for at least three-quarters of the workdays in the contract period, or pay for the shortfall as if worked.
**In use:** "The season ended early due to weather — we owe a three-quarter guarantee shortfall payment for the missed workdays."
**Common misuse:** Treating a shortened season as simply reducing total pay owed, without calculating the specific three-quarter guarantee shortfall obligation.

### Joint employer
A legal status where two or more entities (e.g., a grower and a farm labor contractor) are both held responsible for wage, housing, or safety compliance for the same workers, based on shared control over work conditions.
**In use:** "Given how closely the grower supervises the crew day to day, they may be a joint employer here regardless of what the contract says."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a contract's labeling of "sole employer" determines legal liability, when courts and agencies apply an economic-realities test based on actual control.

### Economic realities test
The multi-factor legal test used to determine joint-employer status, examining who controls hiring/firing, supervision, work conditions, equipment, and pay — not just contract language.
**In use:** "Under the economic realities test, the grower's direct supervision of daily tasks weighs toward joint-employer status."
**Common misuse:** Treating any single factor (like who issues the paycheck) as determinative, rather than weighing the full set of control factors.

### Temporary labor camp standard
OSHA (or equivalent state) regulations setting minimum requirements for housing provided to agricultural workers — water supply, sanitation, sleeping quarters, and related conditions.
**In use:** "Housing needs to pass inspection against the temporary labor camp standard before anyone moves in."
**Common misuse:** Treating housing as compliant based on general appearance rather than a documented inspection against the specific regulatory checklist.

### Farm labor contractor (FLC) license/registration
The federal (MSPA) and, in many states, additional state-level authorization required to recruit, transport, house, or supervise agricultural workers for a fee.
**In use:** "Before we recruit for this grower, confirm our FLC registration renewal went through."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a license once obtained remains valid indefinitely without checking renewal requirements and deadlines.
