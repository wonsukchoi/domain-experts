# Vocabulary

### f'c
The specified compressive strength of concrete at 28 days, expressed in psi, that the mix design and acceptance criteria are built around — a design target the code evaluates statistically, not a number every individual cylinder must clear.
**In use:** "Set B broke at 3,790 against an f'c of 4,000 — check the ACI criteria before you call that a fail."
**Common misuse:** treating any single break below f'c as an automatic nonconformance, ignoring the code's individual-result margin and running-average criteria.

### Strength test result
The average of two cylinders from the same sample, both broken at 28 days — the unit ACI 318's acceptance criteria are actually evaluated against, not a single cylinder's break.
**In use:** "That's not one low cylinder failing — the strength test result is the average of the pair, and it's 4,120."
**Common misuse:** calling a single cylinder's break "the test result," which conflates one data point with the averaged value the code requires.

### Relative compaction (% compaction)
Field dry density expressed as a percentage of the maximum dry density from a laboratory Proctor curve for the same soil — meaningless unless the specific Proctor method (Standard or Modified) is stated alongside it.
**In use:** "We're at 99% of Standard Proctor on lift 3 — confirm the spec isn't actually calling for Modified before we sign it off."
**Common misuse:** reporting a bare percentage without the Proctor method, making the number unverifiable and potentially overstating field quality if the wrong curve was used as the denominator.

### Optimum moisture content (OMC)
The moisture content at which a given soil reaches its maximum dry density for a stated compactive effort — the peak of the Proctor curve, not a fixed property of the soil independent of compaction method.
**In use:** "Density's fine but we're 3 points wet of OMC — that lift still gets flagged."
**Common misuse:** assuming a passing density result alone proves adequate compaction, without checking whether moisture sits inside the spec's OMC tolerance window.

### Composite sample
A concrete sample built from at least two portions taken from different points in the load (per ASTM C172), combined within a defined time window — distinct from a single grab sample, which represents only its own location in the batch.
**In use:** "Pull a composite, not a grab off the chute — one wheelbarrow doesn't tell you about the whole truck."
**Common misuse:** treating any scoop of fresh concrete as an adequate sample regardless of how or where it was taken.

### Air-entrained concrete
Concrete with a deliberately dosed admixture that creates microscopic, evenly distributed air voids to give the hardened concrete freeze-thaw durability — a strength trade-off accepted for exposure protection, not a defect to be minimized.
**In use:** "It's exterior and air-entrained, so don't flag the lower strength on its own — check it against the air-entrained mix design, not a non-air mix."
**Common misuse:** comparing an air-entrained mix's strength directly against a non-air-entrained mix design's expected numbers, or treating entrained air itself as a quality problem.

### Nuclear density gauge
A field instrument that estimates soil wet density and moisture content via gamma-ray backscatter and neutron moderation, correlated against — not a direct replacement for — a physical reference method like the sand cone.
**In use:** "Gauge and sand cone are 4 pcf apart on this lift — that's outside tolerance, get the gauge re-standardized."
**Common misuse:** treating the nuclear gauge reading as ground truth on its own, without periodic reference checks against sand-cone or drive-cylinder results.

### Waste factor
A percentage added to a theoretical (plan-derived) material quantity to account for normal field losses — spillage, over-excavation tolerance, formwork imperfection — used to set a reasonable band for reconciliation, not to excuse an unexplained overage.
**In use:** "We're at 3% over theoretical, inside the 8% waste factor for footings — no flag."
**Common misuse:** treating the waste factor as a blank check that absorbs any overage, instead of a defined band beyond which a variance needs investigation.

### Cylinder break (compression test)
The destructive compressive strength test (ASTM C39) performed on a field-molded cylinder at a specified age, most commonly 28 days, that produces the psi value compared against f'c.
**In use:** "7-day break is informational only — don't make an acceptance call until the 28-day pair comes back."
**Common misuse:** using an early-age (7-day) break as if it were an acceptance result, when only the 28-day strength test result carries acceptance weight under most specs.

### Pay application (pay app)
A contractor's periodic billing submission claiming payment for a percentage of work completed, including materials quantities installed — a document the technician's field-verified takeoff quantities are used to check, not generate.
**In use:** "Concrete quantity on this pay app doesn't match what the tickets show for that pour — flag it before it's certified."
**Common misuse:** assuming a pay application's stated quantities are self-verifying, without reconciling them against independent field data (batch tickets, field-measured dimensions).

### Standard vs. Modified Proctor
Two laboratory compaction test methods (ASTM D698 and D1557 respectively) that apply different compactive energy to the same soil sample, producing different maximum dry density and optimum moisture values for the identical material.
**In use:** "This is a pavement subgrade — the spec's going to call Modified, not Standard, confirm before you compute the percentage."
**Common misuse:** assuming "Proctor" refers to one universal test, when the two methods produce materially different max-density numbers for the same soil and must be matched to what the spec actually requires.

### RFI (Request for Information)
A formal, written question routed from the field or contractor to the design team when a field condition, an ambiguity in the drawings, or a conflict between plan sheets needs a documented answer before work proceeds.
**In use:** "Rebar spacing in the field doesn't match the sheet — that's an RFI to the structural engineer, not a field call."
**Common misuse:** treating a verbal field conversation with the design team as equivalent to a documented RFI response, leaving no record if the resolved condition is later questioned.

### Core test
A cylindrical sample drilled from in-place hardened concrete (ASTM C42) used to evaluate actual in-place strength when cast cylinder results fail ACI 318's acceptance criteria — a follow-up investigation method, not a routine acceptance test.
**In use:** "If this set fails both criteria, expect the engineer to call for cores before anything else happens on that pour."
**Common misuse:** assuming a failed cylinder break automatically condemns the in-place concrete, when core testing (with its own separate acceptance threshold) is the code-specified next step.
