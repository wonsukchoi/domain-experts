# Red flags

### Slump measured more than 1.5 in above the specified target at time of placement
- **Usually means:** water added at the plant or in transit beyond the mix design, or a batching error at the plant.
- **First question:** was any water added after the batch ticket was printed, and is it documented on the ticket?
- **Data to pull:** the batch ticket's water quantity versus mix design, and the truck's mixing/discharge time versus the specified maximum (typically 90 minutes or 300 drum revolutions).

### 28-day cylinder break below f'c but the exact margin wasn't checked against ACI 318 §26.12.3.1
- **Usually means:** the technician or reviewer applied "below f'c = failure" instead of the code's two-part criterion (500 psi / 10% individual floor, plus the 3-test running average).
- **First question:** does this result breach criterion (b) on its own, and what do the two adjacent consecutive strength test results do to the criterion (a) average?
- **Data to pull:** the individual cylinder breaks (not just the averaged strength test result) for this set and the two most recent consecutive sets on the same mix design.

### Percent compaction reported without stating Standard (D698) or Modified (D1557) Proctor
- **Usually means:** the number was copied from a lab report or prior project without confirming which curve the current spec calls for.
- **First question:** which Proctor method does this specific spec section (or the geotechnical report) require for this fill area?
- **Data to pull:** the geotechnical report's compaction spec section and the Proctor curve's method designation.

### Field density passes % compaction but moisture is outside OMC ± spec tolerance
- **Usually means:** the lift was compacted dry-of-optimum (won't reach design strength/permeability under load) or wet-of-optimum (pore pressure risk, rutting under equipment).
- **First question:** is the lift's moisture trending toward or away from optimum compared to the last accepted lift?
- **Data to pull:** the Proctor curve's OMC value and the field moisture readings for the last three lifts at this location.

### Nuclear gauge and sand-cone/drive-cylinder check disagree by more than ~3 pcf on the same lift
- **Usually means:** gauge miscalibration, a soil composition (organic content, unusual mineralogy) the gauge wasn't correlated against, or a probe seating error.
- **First question:** when was this gauge last standardized against its reference block, and has the soil type changed at this location?
- **Data to pull:** the gauge's daily standardization count log and the reference (sand-cone/drive-cylinder) test's raw field data.

### Delivered concrete volume exceeds the theoretical takeoff-plus-waste-factor by more than the stated allowance
- **Usually means:** over-excavation before the pour, formwork bulge or blowout, or loss of material into an unconsolidated subgrade.
- **First question:** was the actual excavation or formed dimension field-verified against the plan dimension before the pour started?
- **Data to pull:** the pre-pour field-measured dimensions versus the plan-set dimensions, and the batch tickets summed for the pour.

### Air content measured outside spec range on an exterior/freeze-thaw-exposed element
- **Usually means:** an air-entraining admixture dosing error at the plant, or excessive mixer agitation/pumping that knocked air out of the mix.
- **First question:** does the air content reading correlate with a specific truck, or is it consistent across the whole pour?
- **Data to pull:** the batch ticket's admixture dosage and the pump/placement method used for this specific truck.

### Concrete cylinders cured or transported outside ASTM C31 initial curing requirements (temperature/moisture)
- **Usually means:** field cure box wasn't insulated or shaded appropriately for ambient conditions, or cylinders sat exposed before transport to the lab.
- **First question:** what was the recorded initial-cure temperature range, and does it fall within the 60–80°F (or standard/high-early variant) ASTM C31 window?
- **Data to pull:** the field cure box's temperature log for the initial 24–48 hour period.

### Rebar size, spacing, or cover measured in the field doesn't match the structural drawing
- **Usually means:** a field substitution made by the crew without an RFI, a shop drawing revision that didn't reach the field set, or a placement error.
- **First question:** is this a documented, approved deviation (submittal or RFI response on file) or an undocumented field change?
- **Data to pull:** the current structural sheet revision and any RFI/submittal log entries for this element.

### A quantity variance is resolved by "eyeballing it as close enough" instead of computing the percentage against the waste factor
- **Usually means:** the reconciliation step was skipped under time or schedule pressure at the end of the pour.
- **First question:** what is the actual computed variance percentage, and does it exceed the stated waste-factor band for this element type?
- **Data to pull:** the plan-set dimensions, the applied waste factor, and the summed batch tickets for the specific pour.

### Test report gives a pass/fail conclusion without the underlying raw numbers
- **Usually means:** the report was written from memory or a summary sheet rather than the original field data sheet.
- **First question:** can this specific result be reconstructed and independently checked from the report alone?
- **Data to pull:** the original field data sheet or data-collector export behind the summarized report.

### Concrete placement temperature outside the 50–90°F ACI 305/306 window with no hot/cold-weather protocol noted
- **Usually means:** hot- or cold-weather concreting procedures (accelerators, retarders, insulation blankets, delivery timing) weren't specified or weren't followed.
- **First question:** does the mix design or placement plan include a hot/cold-weather addendum, and was it actually in effect for this pour?
- **Data to pull:** the ambient and concrete temperature log for the placement window and the project's hot/cold-weather concreting plan, if one exists.
