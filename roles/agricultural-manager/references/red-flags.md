# Red flags — what an experienced operator notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### Debt-to-asset ratio above ~50-60%
- **Usually means:** the operation is over-leveraged relative to the standard agricultural benchmark (a healthy range is commonly cited around 30-40%), leaving thin room to absorb a bad price or weather year.
- **First question:** "What's the plan if net farm income is negative for two consecutive years — does the debt service still get covered?"
- **Data to pull:** balance sheet, current debt schedule, trailing 3-year net farm income.

### Working capital under ~10% of gross revenue
- **Usually means:** the operation doesn't have enough liquid cushion to cover a bad marketing year or an input-cost spike without new borrowing.
- **First question:** "How many months of operating expenses does current working capital actually cover?"
- **Data to pull:** current assets vs. current liabilities, monthly operating expense run-rate.

### Break-even price per bushel/unit above the current futures/forward price for the marketing window
- **Usually means:** the operation is planning to sell at a loss unless price improves — a bet on price recovery rather than a priced-in plan.
- **First question:** "What's the actual break-even cost of production this year, and where does today's forward price sit relative to it?"
- **Data to pull:** enterprise budget cost per acre/unit, current futures and local basis, forward contracts already locked.

### Crop insurance coverage level below ~70% of APH (Actual Production History) on a significant share of acreage
- **Usually means:** a real yield-shortfall year would hit cash flow much harder than the standard risk-management posture assumes.
- **First question:** "Was this coverage level chosen deliberately against the operation's cushion, or is it just what's always been carried?"
- **Data to pull:** current policy coverage level and APH, financial cushion available to self-insure the gap.

### More than ~50% of acreage or herd sales concentrated with a single buyer/elevator/processor
- **Usually means:** counterparty concentration — a contract dispute, price change, or the buyer's own financial trouble can disrupt a large share of revenue at once.
- **First question:** "What happens to this year's marketing plan if this buyer can't take delivery or renegotiates terms mid-season?"
- **Data to pull:** sales/contract volume by buyer for the trailing 2-3 years.

### Soil organic matter or key nutrient (P, K) levels declining across consecutive soil tests without a corrective plan
- **Usually means:** current input and tillage practices are extracting more than they're replenishing — a long-term productivity cost being traded for near-term yield or cost savings.
- **First question:** "What changed in the input or tillage plan that would explain this trend, and is there a correction planned for next season?"
- **Data to pull:** soil test history (3+ years), fertilizer/lime application records, tillage practice log.

### Herd body condition score trending down or disease/mortality incidence rising above the operation's historical baseline
- **Usually means:** a nutrition, stocking-density, or health-management gap that will show up in reproduction rates or growth performance before it shows up in the financials.
- **First question:** "What changed in feed, stocking density, or health protocol in the period this trend started?"
- **Data to pull:** body condition scoring records, mortality/morbidity log, feed ration changes.

### No forward contracts or hedges locked heading into the marketing window on a significant share of expected production
- **Usually means:** full exposure to price risk on a crop the operation doesn't set the price for, often from waiting for a better price that may not materialize.
- **First question:** "What's the plan if price falls from here before this crop is marketed — is that outcome survivable?"
- **Data to pull:** current forward-contracted percentage of expected production, break-even price, current futures curve.

### Cash rent per acre exceeding the break-even that a conservative (not forecast) price/yield scenario supports
- **Usually means:** the rental rate was set against an optimistic price case, or competitive bidding pressure pushed it past what the ground can support at realistic prices.
- **First question:** "What price and yield does this rent actually require to break even, and how does that compare to a conservative scenario?"
- **Data to pull:** lease terms, enterprise budget under both forecast and conservative price/yield assumptions.

### A single input decision (seed variety, feed ration) chosen purely on maximum yield/gain with no marginal-return check against its incremental cost
- **Usually means:** the decision optimized for output rather than profitability — a common trap when comparing input options on yield alone.
- **First question:** "What's the marginal cost of this input choice versus the prior one, and does the yield/gain gain actually clear that cost?"
- **Data to pull:** input cost comparison across options, trial or historical yield/gain data for each.
