# Red flags

### A remedial alternative comparison table where a threshold criterion is averaged into a numeric score
- **Usually means:** the analyst treated all nine CERCLA criteria as equally weighted scoring inputs instead of using the two threshold criteria (protectiveness, ARAR compliance) as a pass/fail gate — this can rank a non-protective alternative as the top choice.
- **First question:** did every alternative that reached the balancing-criteria comparison actually pass both threshold criteria independently, or were they scored on a blended scale?
- **Data to pull:** the detailed-analysis table and its scoring methodology section; the conceptual site model showing pathway completeness for each alternative screened out.

### An MNA or natural-recovery recommendation supported by fewer than 2 years of monitoring data
- **Usually means:** the declining trend cited may be seasonal variation or a single anomalous sampling event, not an actual attenuation trend — agencies routinely reject MNA proposals built on this basis.
- **First question:** how many sampling rounds, over what time span, and is the trend statistically distinguishable from seasonal or analytical variability?
- **Data to pull:** full sampling history (not just the most recent 2-3 rounds) with sampling dates, and any seasonal correlation (water table elevation, precipitation) noted in the record.

### A Feasibility Study cost estimate presented without its AACE/ASTM E2516 accuracy class or range
- **Usually means:** the number will get quoted downstream as a fixed budget by a client, agency, or funding body that doesn't distinguish an FS-stage Class 4-5 estimate from a design-stage Class 2 estimate.
- **First question:** what class is this estimate, and is the -30%/+50% (or equivalent) range stated next to the headline number in every place it appears?
- **Data to pull:** the FS cost estimate backup documentation and the summary table/executive summary where the number is repeated.

### A remedy comparison that ranks alternatives by capital cost only, with O&M duration differing by more than 5 years across options
- **Usually means:** a low-capital/high-duration alternative (long-term monitoring, extended pump-and-treat) is being favored on a number that omits the majority of its true cost.
- **First question:** has each alternative's O&M stream been discounted to present value at the same rate and compared as a life-cycle total, not just a capital figure?
- **Data to pull:** the life-cycle NPV worksheet; confirm the discount rate used is the agency's stated rate (commonly tied to OMB Circular A-94), not an unstated default.

### A stream or wetland restoration design with no named reference reach or reference site
- **Usually means:** the design was built to a generic "natural-looking" template rather than a site-comparable ecological or geomorphic target, leaving no falsifiable basis for post-construction performance standards.
- **First question:** what reference reach or reference wetland was used, and is it geomorphically/hydrologically comparable (same valley type, similar watershed area, similar soils)?
- **Data to pull:** the reference-site survey data (channel dimensions, hydrology, vegetation) the design was calibrated against.

### An institutional control (deed restriction, land-use covenant) cited in a Record of Decision but not confirmed as recorded against the parcel
- **Usually means:** the control exists on paper in the planning document but was never actually implemented — the single most common cause of a Five-Year Review finding a remedy "not protective."
- **First question:** has the recorded instrument (not the ROD's description of it) been pulled from the county recorder or land registry?
- **Data to pull:** the recorded deed notice/easement, its recording date, and the current property owner of record.

### A compensatory mitigation credit calculation using a flat ratio (e.g., 1:1) across restoration, enhancement, and preservation acreage
- **Usually means:** the ledger conflated activities that create different amounts of new ecological function — preservation creates none and should carry a materially lower credit ratio than restoration.
- **First question:** does the mitigation banking instrument or ILF program agreement specify different ratios by activity type, and were they applied per-category rather than blended?
- **Data to pull:** the approved mitigation banking instrument or ILF agreement's credit release schedule and ratio table.

### A Five-Year Review protectiveness determination of "protective" with no site visit or land-use change check documented
- **Usually means:** the review was a desktop paperwork exercise rather than a verification that conditions on the ground still match the remedy's assumptions.
- **First question:** was a site visit conducted, and was current land use (new wells, construction, zoning) checked against the exposure assumptions in the original ROD?
- **Data to pull:** the Five-Year Review report's site inspection section and any well-permit or zoning records for the review period.

### A restoration or remedial project schedule with no post-construction monitoring period or performance-standard failure trigger stated
- **Usually means:** the plan treats construction completion as project completion, with no defined path for what happens if the site doesn't meet its performance standard.
- **First question:** what is the monitoring duration, sampling frequency, and the specific numeric or condition-based trigger that would require remedial/adaptive action?
- **Data to pull:** the monitoring and adaptive-management section of the restoration plan or remedial design.
