# Roofer Helper — Playbook

Filled templates for a live tear-off/reroof day. Numbers are worked examples, not universal constants — always re-derive from the actual roof, materials, and hauler terms.

## 1. Tear-off debris weight worksheet (filled example: 20.1 squares, 2 layers)

| Item | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Roof area | 1,800 sq ft footprint × 1.118 (6:12 slope multiplier) | 2,012 sq ft = 20.1 squares |
| Weight per layer | 250 lb/square (3-tab shingles) + 15 lb/square (felt) | 265 lb/square/layer |
| Total layers weight | 265 lb/square/layer × 2 layers | 530 lb/square |
| Total debris weight | 20.1 squares × 530 lb/square | 10,653 lb ≈ 5.33 tons |
| Container needed | Weight ÷ hauler's per-container tonnage rating (not yardage) | 20-yard @ 6-ton (12,000 lb) rating clears it in one haul |

**Reference weight ranges (adjust per shingle type):**

| Roofing material | Approx. weight/square/layer |
|---|---|
| 3-tab asphalt shingle | 220–300 lb |
| Laminated/architectural shingle | 350–450 lb |
| Wood shake | 200–300 lb |
| Felt underlayment (15 lb) | 15 lb |
| Felt underlayment (30 lb) | 30 lb |

## 2. Dumpster sizing by weight, not yardage

| Container size | Typical mixed-C&D weight cap | Typical shingle-debris cap (denser material) |
|---|---|---|
| 10-yard | 3–4 tons (6,000–8,000 lb) | 2 tons (4,000 lb) |
| 15-yard | 4–5 tons | 3 tons (6,000 lb) |
| 20-yard | 5–6 tons | 4–6 tons (8,000–12,000 lb) |
| 30-yard | 6–8 tons | not recommended for shingles — weight cap hit long before volume |

Rule of thumb: divide the computed total debris weight by the container's *shingle-specific* cap, not its general C&D cap — shingles run out of allowed weight well before the container looks full.

## 3. Bundle staging and roof-jack spacing

| Shingle type | Bundles/square | Weight/bundle | Max stack height on deck (sound decking) | Max stack height (deck condition unconfirmed / older framing) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3-tab asphalt | 3 | 60–65 lb | 4 bundles per stack | 2 bundles per stack |
| Laminated/architectural | 4–5 | 65–80 lb | 3 bundles per stack | 2 bundles per stack |

- Distribute stacks across at least two truss/rafter bays; never stack a full delivery over one span.
- Set stacks straddling the ridge when possible — the ridge is the stiffest line on the deck.
- Roof jack spacing: set at every other rafter (typically 24–32 in o.c. depending on framing), first row roughly 4–6 ft above the eave, subsequent rows at a comfortable working reach (4–5 ft) up-slope — verify against the roofer's plan for the specific pitch before placing.

## 4. Heat work/rest cycle (OSHA heat-index-tier guidance)

| Heat index | Risk tier | Work/rest guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Under 91°F | Lower | Water available, normal breaks |
| 91–103°F | Moderate | Water + shade break every 15–20 min of continuous sun exposure |
| 103–115°F | High | Water + shade break every 15 min minimum; monitor for early heat-illness signs |
| Over 115°F | Very high | Reschedule non-essential exposed work where possible; mandatory frequent breaks and buddy monitoring |

Roof-surface temperature in direct sun commonly runs 40–60°F above ambient air temperature — use a surface read on the actual roof, not just the forecast air temperature, when the roof material is dark-colored.

## 5. End-of-day/continuous nail-sweep protocol

1. Run the magnetic sweeper over the drop zone immediately after each major debris drop, not only at day's end.
2. Sweep the driveway, walkway, and any parked-vehicle area before crew or homeowner vehicles move.
3. Run a final full-property sweep (driveway, walkway, adjacent yard within debris-drop radius) at day's end regardless of whether tear-off finished.
4. Log the sweep as complete in the end-of-day note — an unlogged sweep is treated as not done for liability purposes.

## 6. Deck-sounding check (after each section's tear-off)

1. Walk the newly exposed deck section in a grid pattern, weight on the balls of the feet.
2. Listen and feel for hollow sound, springiness, or visible sag — compare to adjacent, known-sound decking.
3. Any soft/springy spot: mark it, do not walk it further, flag to the roofer before staging any material near it.
4. Confirmed soft decking gets replaced before dry-in proceeds over that section — never bridged with underlayment alone.
