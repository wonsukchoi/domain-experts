# Roofer Helper — Red Flags

### Roof deck sounds hollow, springy, or visibly sags when walked after tear-off
- **Usually means:** water intrusion has degraded the sheathing under the removed layer; second most likely is undersized or improperly spaced original decking.
- **First question:** "Does this spot compare differently to the deck 3 ft away in any direction?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the moment — mark the spot, stop staging material near it, and flag before any load is placed over it.

### Building predates 1980 and existing felt/mastic hasn't been tested for asbestos
- **Usually means:** original construction-era roofing material never sampled; second most likely is a prior re-roof that reused or left original underlayment in place.
- **First question:** "Is there any prior testing or abatement paperwork for this roof on file?"
- **Data to pull:** permit history or prior contractor's abatement/testing records for the property.

### Tear-off exposes brittle, black-mastic-backed felt that doesn't match the rest of the roof's layers
- **Usually means:** an older roofing cement or built-up layer consistent with pre-1980s practice; second most likely is a patch repair using older leftover material.
- **First question:** "Does the build date and this material's appearance line up with the presumed-ACM window?"
- **Data to pull:** the property's build date and any available material spec sheet if the layer is documented.

### Tear-off produces more layers than the job was scoped and disposal was priced for
- **Usually means:** a prior re-roof-over wasn't disclosed or wasn't visible from the ground inspection; second most likely is the original estimate assumed single-layer based on a partial tear-off sample.
- **First question:** "Does this change the disposal weight math or the asbestos-testing question, and who needs to know before we keep going?"
- **Data to pull:** the original scope/estimate's stated layer count versus what's actually being removed.

### Computed tear-off debris weight approaches or exceeds the ordered container's rated capacity
- **Usually means:** the container was sized by yardage/volume instead of weight; second most likely is a layer-count or lb/square estimate that was too low going in.
- **First question:** "What's the hauler's actual weight cap for shingle debris on this container, not its general C&D rating?"
- **Data to pull:** the hauler's contract terms for the container size ordered, and the tear-off weight worksheet.

### Bundle stack sitting on a single unsupported deck span or directly over a known-soft spot
- **Usually means:** staging for convenience near the access point rather than distributing load; second most likely is the deck's condition wasn't checked before staging.
- **First question:** "Is this stack spanning at least two structural bays, and has this section of deck been sounded?"
- **Data to pull:** none — relocate the stack before continuing, don't wait for a sign of deflection.

### Ladder angle or extension doesn't meet the 4:1 rule with 3 ft above the roof edge
- **Usually means:** the ladder was set quickly for a short task without re-measuring; second most likely is ground conditions (slope, soft soil) shifted the base after initial setup.
- **First question:** "Has anyone moved or leaned on this ladder since it was set?"
- **Data to pull:** none — re-set before the next climb, don't estimate "close enough."

### PFAS anchor point is a vent pipe, satellite mount, or other non-engineered fixture
- **Usually means:** the nearest solid-looking object was used under time pressure; second most likely is no engineered anchor was installed for this roof section.
- **First question:** "Is this anchor rated to 5,000 lb per attached worker or engineered with a 2x safety factor, or is it just solid-looking?"
- **Data to pull:** the anchor manufacturer spec or engineering sign-off, if one exists for this point.

### Heat index or roof-surface temperature crosses into OSHA's moderate-or-higher work/rest tier without an adjusted break schedule
- **Usually means:** the crew is running the same pace as a cooler morning without re-checking conditions midday; second most likely is no one has taken a surface-temperature or heat-index reading that shift.
- **First question:** "When was the last water/shade break, and what's the current heat index or roof-surface read?"
- **Data to pull:** local heat index (NWS or a job-site app) and, if available, a roof-surface temperature reading.

### Nail sweep skipped or deferred past a debris drop onto a driveway, walkway, or parking area
- **Usually means:** the crew is behind schedule and treating the sweep as end-of-day-only; second most likely is a homeowner or vehicle wasn't expected back before end of day.
- **First question:** "Has anyone driven or walked through this drop zone since the last sweep?"
- **Data to pull:** none — sweep before allowing vehicle or foot traffic through the zone, not after.

### Flashing at a chimney, vent, or valley being reused instead of replaced during tear-off
- **Usually means:** an attempt to save material cost or time on a component the original scope assumed would be replaced; second most likely is the flashing looked serviceable on visual inspection alone.
- **First question:** "Was this flashing scoped for replacement, and has it been checked for hidden corrosion or fastener pull-through, not just surface condition?"
- **Data to pull:** the job's original scope/estimate line for flashing replacement.
