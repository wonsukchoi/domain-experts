## 3. Differential diagnosis worksheet (filled — teaching case)

| Rank | Diagnosis | Supporting evidence | Disconfirming/missing evidence | Consequence if missed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (most probable) | Anxiety/panic attack | Situational trigger, mild vitals | Doesn't explain immobilization risk factor or tachycardia fully | Low — missed anxiety diagnosis is not catastrophic |
| 2 (can't-miss) | Pulmonary embolism | Immobilization, tachycardia, PE judged plausible | Awaiting CTPA result | High — missed PE can be fatal |
| 3 | Musculoskeletal chest pain | Chest discomfort | Doesn't explain shortness of breath | Low |

**Rule applied:** ranking includes both probability (row 1) and consequence-if-missed (row 2), rather than ordering purely by probability — the can't-miss diagnosis gets an active rule-out step regardless of its rank.

## 4. Diagnostic time-out template (filled, teaching case — non-improving patient)

> **Trigger:** Patient not responding as expected to initial treatment after 48 hours.
> **Time-out questions:**
> 1. What was the original differential, and has anything on it not been actively re-examined?
> 2. What findings don't fit the current leading diagnosis?
> 3. Has a can't-miss diagnosis been re-considered given the lack of expected response?
> **Outcome (example):** Re-review surfaced a finding initially attributed to the leading diagnosis that, on reconsideration, better fit a different explanation on the original differential — treatment plan revised accordingly, rather than escalating the original treatment further.
