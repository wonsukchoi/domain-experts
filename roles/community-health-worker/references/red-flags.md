# Community Health Worker — Red Flags

### Caseload no-show rate rises above baseline for more than one reporting cycle
- **Usually means:** an outreach-timing/method mismatch (calling only during work hours for clients with daytime jobs), or a genuine new barrier (lost housing, lost transportation) affecting multiple clients at once — rarely a sudden drop in motivation across an entire caseload.
- **First question:** "What time of day and by what method are we attempting contact, and does that match this subgroup's actual schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** outreach-attempt log broken out by time-of-day and method, cross-referenced against client employment/schedule flags from intake.

### A referral sits "made" with no attendance confirmation past 14 days
- **Usually means:** the referral was never actually scheduled (client couldn't get through, or the partner org had no capacity), not that the client skipped it.
- **First question:** "Did we ever get a confirmed appointment date from the client, or did we just hand over a phone number?"
- **Data to pull:** the referral-tracking log's "contact confirmed scheduled" column for that entry.

### A client discloses a new symptom or crisis outside CHW scope
- **Usually means:** trust has grown to the point the client is sharing more, which is a good sign relationally, but requires an immediate scope check, not an attempt to help beyond training.
- **First question:** "Is this within my defined scope of practice, or does this need an immediate clinical/safety referral?"
- **Data to pull:** the program's documented CHW scope-of-practice and escalation protocol.

### A partner organization's referral acceptance rate drops sharply for one CHW's referrals specifically
- **Usually means:** the referrals sent aren't matching the partner's actual eligibility criteria (referring clients who don't qualify), which burns the relationship for the whole program, not just this CHW.
- **First question:** "Are we checking eligibility criteria before referring, or referring first and letting the partner screen?"
- **Data to pull:** the last 10-15 referrals to that partner, matched against the partner's stated intake criteria.

### Teach-back consistently "passes" on the first attempt across an entire caseload
- **Usually means:** the teach-back isn't actually being done (a "does that make sense?" check is being logged as teach-back), not that comprehension is uniformly high.
- **First question:** "Can you show me exactly what you asked the client to say or do to demonstrate understanding?"
- **Data to pull:** documentation notes for the specific teach-back question/method used, not just a "teach-back completed" checkbox.

### A client's SDOH screening looks identical visit after visit with no domains resolving or changing
- **Usually means:** either the screening is being copy-pasted from the prior visit rather than re-administered, or referrals made aren't actually resolving the flagged barriers.
- **First question:** "When was this screening last actually re-administered, word for word, with the client — not copied from the chart?"
- **Data to pull:** screening administration dates and the referral outcomes tied to each flagged domain.

### A CHW's documented contact frequency with a client drops but the client's flagged-domain count stays high
- **Usually means:** the CHW-client relationship has frayed (often after an overpromised referral fell through) and the client is disengaging from the CHW specifically, not from care broadly.
- **First question:** "What was the last interaction, and did a referral or promise not come through as expected?"
- **Data to pull:** contact log entries and referral outcomes in the 30 days before contact frequency dropped.

### A high proportion of referrals across the caseload are to the same one or two resources
- **Usually means:** the CHW is defaulting to familiar/easy referrals rather than matching to the client's actual stated need, or the resource landscape genuinely lacks options — these require different fixes.
- **First question:** "Are we referring to this resource because it fits, or because it's the one we always think of first?"
- **Data to pull:** referral-domain distribution across the caseload compared against the full resource directory available.
