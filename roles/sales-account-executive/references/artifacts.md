## 1. MEDDIC qualification (filled example)

| Element | Status |
|---|---|
| Metrics | Reducing reconciliation cost from $340,000/year toward the $85,000/year solution cost |
| Economic buyer | Not yet engaged directly — identified as VP Finance, no call scheduled |

| Decision process | Champion → VP Finance approval → procurement → legal review (4-step process, confirmed with champion) |
| Identify pain | 3 FTEs on manual reconciliation, documented error-related rework cost |

**Gap flagged:** no economic buyer engagement yet — this is what's keeping the deal at best-case rather than commit, regardless of champion enthusiasm.

## 2. ROI/business case (filled — see Worked example in SKILL.md for full narrative)

| Item | Vendor-estimated (initial) | Finance-validated (revised) |
|---|---|---|
| Manual reconciliation labor cost (3 FTE) | $195,000/year | $195,000/year |
| Error-related rework cost | $145,000/year (vendor estimate) | $95,000/year (finance-validated against Q2-Q3 incident data) |
| **Total cost of status quo** | **$340,000/year** | **$290,000/year** |
| Proposed solution cost | $85,000/year | $85,000/year |
| **Payback period** | **~3 months** | **~3.5 months** |

**Rule applied:** the revised, lower number is more valuable to the deal than the higher vendor estimate, because it's finance-owned and defensible internally — that's what actually unblocks the champion, not a bigger but unvalidated number.

## 3. Mutual action plan (filled example)

| Step | Owner | Target date | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Finance validates rework cost estimate | Champion + Finance contact | Week 1 | Complete |
| Economic buyer call scheduled | Champion | Week 2 | Pending |
| Technical evaluation / security review | Buyer's IT team | Week 3 | Not started |
| Procurement/legal review | Buyer's procurement | Week 4 | Not started |
| Signature | Economic buyer | Week 5 | Not started |

**Rule applied:** each step has a named owner and date on the buyer's side, not just the seller's — a plan the buyer hasn't co-built and doesn't own isn't a real mutual action plan.
