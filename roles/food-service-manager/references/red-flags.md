# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced food service manager notices instantly. Load this when triaging a cost, safety, or operations question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### Food cost % running >2 points over target for 2+ consecutive weeks
- **Usually means:** portion drift or waste, in that order of likelihood, before supplier price or theft
- **First question:** "Have we spot-checked plated portions on the top 3 sellers against recipe spec this week?"
- **Data to pull:** theoretical vs. actual usage report, plate spot-checks, waste log

### A single item accounts for a disproportionate share of comps or voids
- **Usually means:** either a recurring kitchen execution issue (consistently sent back) or a specific staff member's comp-approval pattern needing review
- **First question:** "Is this comp pattern tied to one dish, one server, one shift, or spread evenly?"
- **Data to pull:** comp/void report broken out by item, server, and shift

### Walk-in or reach-in temperature log shows a reading outside range, logged but with no follow-up action noted
- **Usually means:** the logging is happening but the escalation/correction step isn't — a compliance-theater gap
- **First question:** "What specifically happened after this out-of-range reading was logged?"
- **Data to pull:** temperature log with corrective-action column, maintenance ticket history for that unit

### Labor cost % spikes on a day that wasn't specifically forecast as high-volume
- **Usually means:** schedule was built from last week's pattern rather than an actual forecast, or a manager over-scheduled defensively after a prior bad understaffed shift
- **First question:** "What was the sales forecast for this shift, and who built the schedule against it?"
- **Data to pull:** forecast vs. actual sales by day-part, scheduled vs. actual labor hours

### A menu item has high sales mix but margin data hasn't been checked in over 6 months
- **Usually means:** ingredient costs have likely drifted since the item was last costed, and the menu price hasn't been revisited to match
- **First question:** "When was this recipe's cost last recalculated against current invoice prices?"
- **Data to pull:** recipe cost card date-stamped vs. current ingredient invoice prices

### A supplier substitutes an ingredient without prior notice
- **Usually means:** either a supply shortage upstream or a cost-cutting substitution the supplier didn't flag — both need a decision, not silent acceptance
- **First question:** "Does this substitution change the recipe cost, the dish's quality, or anything on the allergen/ingredient disclosure?"
- **Data to pull:** substitution notice (if any) and a side-by-side spec comparison

### Health inspection finds a critical violation that was also flagged in a prior inspection
- **Usually means:** the prior corrective action wasn't actually structural — it fixed the instance, not the process that produced it
- **First question:** "What changed operationally after the last time this was flagged, and why didn't it hold?"
- **Data to pull:** prior inspection reports and the corrective action documentation from the last occurrence

### Inventory par levels haven't been adjusted despite a documented shift in sales mix
- **Usually means:** pars were set once and never revisited, now producing either stockouts (under-par for a growing item) or spoilage (over-par for a declining one)
- **First question:** "When was this item's par last recalculated, and against what usage data?"
- **Data to pull:** par-level tracker vs. actual 8-week trailing usage for the specific SKU
