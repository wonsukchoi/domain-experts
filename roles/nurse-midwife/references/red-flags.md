# Red flags

### Postpartum blood loss trending toward 500mL in the first hour after vaginal birth
- **Usually means:** uterine atony (most common cause), second most likely: retained placental fragments or a genital-tract laceration not yet found
- **First question:** is the fundus firm and midline, or boggy/displaced?
- **Data to pull:** quantitative blood loss log (weighed pads/drapes/collection drape), vital-sign trend, time and dose of last uterotonic given

### No cervical change over 4 hours with confirmed adequate contractions (MVU ≥200) at ≥6cm
- **Usually means:** genuine active-phase arrest warranting a cesarean discussion, second: contraction adequacy was assumed from palpation and hasn't actually been confirmed by IUPC
- **First question:** was adequacy confirmed objectively (IUPC/MVU), or only palpated?
- **Data to pull:** IUPC tracing/MVU trend over the window, serial cervical-exam timestamps, oxytocin dosing history

### Category II or III fetal heart tracing during active labor
- **Usually means:** fetal intolerance of labor (cord compression, uteroplacental insufficiency) most likely, second: transient/positional artifact
- **First question:** does repositioning, oxygen, and an IV fluid bolus resolve it within the next 10-15 minutes?
- **Data to pull:** continuous EFM strip, maternal vital signs, time and nature of the last exam or intervention

### GBS status unknown or culture more than 5 weeks old at labor onset
- **Usually means:** screening wasn't completed or the result is lost, second: patient presented preterm before the scheduled 36-37 week culture
- **First question:** are risk-based prophylaxis triggers present regardless (prior GBS-affected infant, GBS bacteriuria this pregnancy, preterm labor, ROM ≥18 hours, intrapartum fever)?
- **Data to pull:** prenatal record GBS culture date and result, current gestational age, time since membrane rupture

### Second stage exceeding 3 hours nulliparous (4 with epidural) or 2 hours multiparous (3 with epidural) without descent
- **Usually means:** fetal malposition (occiput posterior/transverse) most likely, second: inadequate expulsive effort or a true cephalopelvic disproportion
- **First question:** what is the current station and position, and has either changed in the last 30-60 minutes?
- **Data to pull:** serial exam findings (station, position, caput, molding), contraction pattern, documented pushing effort

### Perineal or vaginal laceration with any suspicion of sphincter involvement
- **Usually means:** a 3rd- or 4th-degree tear (obstetric anal sphincter injury) needing a careful sphincter exam, not a routine 2nd-degree repair
- **First question:** is there a palpable defect on rectal exam, and is the external anal sphincter intact?
- **Data to pull:** post-delivery laceration exam documentation, delivery details (operative vs. spontaneous, shoulder dystocia present)

### Fundal height or growth measurement off by more than 2-3cm from expected for gestational age
- **Usually means:** growth restriction (lag) or macrosomia/polyhydramnios (lead) most likely, second: inaccurate dating
- **First question:** does the fundal-height discrepancy correlate with an ultrasound estimated fetal weight?
- **Data to pull:** serial fundal-height measurements, dating ultrasound vs. current gestational age, most recent growth ultrasound

### Patient reports decreased fetal movement in the third trimester
- **Usually means:** a benign fetal sleep cycle or positional change (most common), second: evolving placental insufficiency
- **First question:** has the patient done a kick count in the last hour, and what was the result?
- **Data to pull:** NST or BPP result if performed, kick-count log, most recent growth and amniotic-fluid assessment

### Postpartum headache, visual changes, or epigastric pain in the first 72 hours
- **Usually means:** postpartum preeclampsia (most common — can present after normal antepartum blood pressures), second: spinal headache if regional anesthesia was used
- **First question:** what is the current blood pressure, and has it been trending upward?
- **Data to pull:** blood pressure trend over the last 24-48 hours, urine protein/labs if drawn, anesthesia record

### Newborn not transitioning normally in the first minutes (poor tone, weak or absent cry, poor color)
- **Usually means:** a normal transitional delay (most common, especially preterm or cesarean-born), second: true neonatal depression needing resuscitation
- **First question:** what is the 1-minute Apgar, and is the heart rate above 100?
- **Data to pull:** Apgar scores at 1 and 5 minutes, cord gas if drawn, resuscitation steps taken and the newborn's response

### Repeated requests to decline a recommended screening or intervention across multiple visits
- **Usually means:** an informed, values-based decline (most common), second: an unaddressed misunderstanding or fear about the recommendation that hasn't surfaced yet
- **First question:** has the specific risk/benefit conversation been documented at least once, with the patient's own stated reasoning?
- **Data to pull:** prior visit notes for informed-refusal documentation, patient's stated concerns

### A new finding arises mid-labor for a patient who screened low-risk at intake (e.g., breech presentation on a late exam, meconium-stained fluid)
- **Usually means:** the risk status has genuinely changed and needs the protocol-defined consult or transfer, not a case-by-case call in the moment
- **First question:** does this finding appear on the practice's written risk-classification/transfer list?
- **Data to pull:** the practice's current risk-classification protocol document, timing of the finding relative to admission and prior exams

### Oxytocin augmentation running for more than 6 hours without reaching adequate contractions (MVU <200)
- **Usually means:** an underlying uterine or fetal factor limiting response (rather than "just needs more time/dose"), second: dosing protocol not actually escalated per the titration schedule
- **First question:** has the oxytocin dose actually been titrated per protocol, or held flat?
- **Data to pull:** oxytocin dosing/titration log with timestamps, IUPC/MVU trend, maximum dose reached
