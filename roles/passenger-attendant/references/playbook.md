# Passenger Attendant Playbook

Filled worksheets and reference tables for the sequences and calculations the SKILL.md decision framework calls for. Numbers below are illustrative, self-consistent examples in the shape of typical commuter-rail/paratransit mobility-assistance operations — always substitute the operating carrier's or contractor's actual procedures and equipment.

## 1. WC19 four-point-plus-belt securement sequence and time worksheet

| Step | Action | Typical time | Detail |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Deploy ramp or bridge plate | 15–20 sec | Ramp adds an incline-alignment check; bridge plate at level-boarding stations skips this |
| 2 | Position mobility device in designated securement area | 10 sec | Confirm brakes/power-off on the device before strapping |
| 3 | Attach front straps, angled 30–45° from the floor to each front anchor point | 20 sec (10 sec/strap) | Each anchor point rated to withstand ~2,500 lbf per SAE J2249/WC19 |
| 4 | Attach rear straps, angled 30–60° from the floor to each rear anchor point | 20 sec (10 sec/strap) | Rear straps resist backward tip under braking |
| 5 | Fasten lap belt across the pelvis (not the abdomen) | 10 sec | A shoulder belt is added where the vehicle/device configuration provides one |
| 6 | Stow ramp or bridge plate | 10–15 sec | Confirm rider and device clear of the doorway before stow |
| **Total** | | **85–100 sec** | Detraining reverses steps 6→1 in roughly the same time |

**Rule this table demonstrates:** the securement itself (steps 2–5) runs roughly 60–70 seconds regardless of ramp vs. bridge-plate boarding mechanism — the boarding-mechanism choice only affects steps 1 and 6, so "level boarding" shortens the total but does not shorten the part of the job that actually protects the rider.

## 2. Extended-dwell radio-request worksheet

| Scheduled dwell | Estimated securement time | Deficit | Within 90-sec assistance allowance? | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 45 sec | 85 sec | 40 sec | Yes (85 ≤ 90) | Radio conductor for 85-sec hold before starting; no supervisor call needed |
| 30 sec | 95 sec | 65 sec | Yes (95 ≤ 90)... | See row below — 95 > 90, exceeds allowance |
| 30 sec | 95 sec | 65 sec | No (95 > 90) | Radio conductor AND request supervisor/dispatch authorization before starting |
| 60 sec | 75 sec | 15 sec | Yes (75 ≤ 90) | Radio conductor for 15-sec extension; routine, no supervisor call |
| 45 sec | 70 sec (bridge plate, single strap already attached from a prior stop's partial boarding — flagged, redo from step 3) | 25 sec | Yes | Radio conductor; also log the pre-existing partial strap as a handoff discrepancy before proceeding |

**Rule this table demonstrates:** the 90-second allowance is measured against total securement time, not against the deficit versus scheduled dwell — a securement that takes 95 seconds needs supervisor sign-off even if the scheduled dwell were unusually generous, because the allowance caps the assistance duration itself, not just the overage.

## 3. Non-clinical medical triage table

| Presentation | Immediate action within scope | Escalate to EMS/dispatch medical line? | Do NOT do |
|---|---|---|---|
| Minor cut, scrape, controlled bleeding | Clean, direct pressure, bandage from approved kit | No, unless bleeding doesn't stop within ~2 min of direct pressure | Apply any medication beyond kit-approved antiseptic/bandage |
| Simple faint, recovers fully within ~1 min, normal color/breathing after | Position of comfort, monitor, offer water once alert | Optional — call in if recurs or rider requests it | Assume a diagnosis ("just low blood sugar") |
| Chest pain, shortness of breath, or diaphoresis | Position of comfort, stay with rider, prep for handoff | Yes, immediately | Administer any medication, including the rider's own, without guidance |
| Suspected stroke signs (facial droop, arm weakness, slurred speech) | Note time symptoms started, keep rider still | Yes, immediately — time-critical | Wait to "see if it passes" |
| Seizure, ongoing beyond ~5 minutes or a second seizure without regaining consciousness | Protect from injury, clear surroundings, time the seizure | Yes, immediately (status epilepticus threshold) | Restrain the rider or place anything in their mouth |
| Unresponsive, no normal breathing | CPR + AED immediately, no delay for consult | Yes, in parallel with CPR/AED, not before | Wait for EMS guidance before starting CPR |
| Allergic reaction with breathing difficulty or swelling | Use rider's own prescribed epinephrine auto-injector if trained and available; do not delay for an unambiguous presentation | Yes, immediately | Administer epinephrine that isn't the rider's own prescribed device unless the employer's program specifically authorizes it |

**Rule this table demonstrates:** the only presentations where the attendant acts before calling EMS are the ones where waiting is itself the harm (cardiac/respiratory arrest, unambiguous anaphylaxis with the rider's own device, an active seizure needing injury protection) — everything else gets a call placed at the same time as, or before, any hands-on action.

## 4. Manifest reconciliation checklist (per stop)

| Check | When | Action if mismatch |
|---|---|---|
| Boarded count matches manifest's expected riders for this stop | Before ramp/bridge-plate stow | Hold stow, recheck platform, radio dispatch if a manifested rider did not appear |
| Detrained count matches manifest's expected riders for this stop | Before signaling ready-to-depart | Hold departure signal, recheck vehicle, radio dispatch before releasing |
| Service-level flag (curb-to-curb vs. door-to-door) matches the assistance actually provided | At each boarding/detraining | Log the mismatch immediately; do not silently upgrade or downgrade assistance to match the rider's in-the-moment request |
| Refused-securement-component entries logged | Immediately after any refusal | If unlogged, treat as an open item requiring completion before end of run |
| Running manifest total reconciled against the day's full assisted-rider count | At each stop, not only at run's end | Any discrepancy carried past one additional stop gets escalated to dispatch, not just noted for later |

**Rule this table demonstrates:** every reconciliation happens at the stop where the discrepancy could occur, not at the end of the run — a manifest count that's "off by one" and only discovered at the terminal has already traveled uncorrected through however many intermediate stops, which is exactly the gap an evacuation-accountability plan can't tolerate.
