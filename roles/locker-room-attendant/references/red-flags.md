# Red flags

Smell tests an attendant or shift lead catches before they become a claim, a slip-and-fall, or a staffing failure.

### Ticket-to-item count mismatch at shift close exceeding 1–2 items per 100 checked

**Usually means:** an item was released without matching the stub (rushed retrieval surge), or a ticket was double-issued. Occasionally theft, but the base rate is procedural error, not crime.

**First question:** "Walk me through the retrieval surge — was every item verified against its stub, or did the line get waved through toward the end?"

**Data to pull:** intake log timestamps vs. retrieval timestamps; which attendant was on the counter during the surge window; whether the missing item(s) cluster around one time block (points to a rushed period) or are scattered (points to a systemic skip of the verification step).

### Guest reports a high-value item missing with no HV-tag or disclosure on record

**Usually means:** the item was genuinely undisclosed at intake (liability capped under standing policy), or the disclosure happened verbally but wasn't logged — which is a process failure on the attendant's side, not the guest's.

**First question:** "Is there anything on the intake tag or log noting this item, or an HV-declined mark?"

**Data to pull:** the physical tag/log entry for that ticket number; whether the counter was staffed by someone trained on the HV protocol at intake time; any camera coverage of the intake moment.

### Towel/robe on-hand count below 60% of calculated par before the midpoint of the shift

**Usually means:** either the day's actual usage is running well above the trailing average (peak event, unplanned volume) or a laundry cycle ran late/was missed.

**First question:** "Is this a demand spike or a supply miss — has the laundry vendor confirmed today's pickups ran on schedule?"

**Data to pull:** today's visit count vs. the 30-day average used to set standing par; laundry vendor's pickup/delivery log for the day; time the shortfall was first noticed vs. when par was last confirmed full.

### Slip-and-fall reported near a locker room shower or entry, with no mopping log entry in the prior 2 hours

**Usually means:** the cleaning cadence lapsed, understaffing pulled the attendant off floor duty, or the incident happened right after cleaning and the floor wasn't fully dry/signed.

**First question:** "When was this area last mopped and signed off, and was wet-floor signage up at the time?"

**Data to pull:** the facilities mopping/inspection log; staffing schedule for that window; whether wet-floor signage was deployed and for how long.

### Same locker number reported "missing item" twice within one season

**Usually means:** a specific lock or locker has a mechanical fault (doesn't latch, key duplicated), or one attendant/patron pattern is worth investigating — not necessarily theft, sometimes a locker that simply doesn't close reliably.

**First question:** "Has maintenance checked whether that locker's latch and lock mechanism are functioning correctly?"

**Data to pull:** maintenance log for that locker number; whether the same member/patron used that locker both times; key/lock control log if it's a house-issued lock.

### Tip pool split disputed by more than one staff member in a shift

**Usually means:** the pooling formula wasn't communicated clearly, hours worked weren't tracked consistently (someone left early but was included at full share), or the count of collected cash wasn't verified by two people.

**First question:** "Was the pool amount counted and confirmed by two people before it was split, and were hours worked logged for everyone in the pool?"

**Data to pull:** the shift-close reconciliation sheet; clock-in/out times for each attendant; whether the split used a per-hour or flat-per-person formula and whether that was posted in advance.

### Coat check retrieval queue exceeding 15–20 minutes at a timed event's end

**Usually means:** understaffing at the predictable retrieval surge (a scheduling failure, since event end times are known in advance), or a ticket-matching bottleneck from an earlier process shortcut.

**First question:** "How many attendants were scheduled for the last 45 minutes of the event versus the intake peak?"

**Data to pull:** the staffing schedule against the event's published end time; average retrieval time per patron during the surge; whether any secondary retrieval point could have split the line.

### Unattended locker room propped open or unsupervised during a scheduled attended window

**Usually means:** staffing gap (break coverage not arranged) or a policy drift where "attended" locker rooms have quietly become self-service without updating the liability signage.

**First question:** "Was this window supposed to be staffed, and if so, who was assigned — and does the signage still say 'attended' when it now runs as self-service?"

**Data to pull:** the staffing schedule for the window; current posted signage language; any incident reports filed during unstaffed periods historically.

### Lost & found item held past the posted retention deadline with no disposition logged

**Usually means:** nobody owns the lost & found process end-to-end, so items accumulate past the 30/90-day policy window without donation or disposal being executed.

**First question:** "Who owns the review of the lost & found log against retention deadlines, and when was it last reviewed?"

**Data to pull:** the lost & found chain-of-custody log; date-found vs. today's date for all open items; the last date a disposition review was completed.
