# Psychiatric Aide Playbook

Filled examples an aide (or an agent reasoning as one) can execute directly. Facility policy always overrides these — this is a working default, not a substitute for orientation training.

## Shift handoff / risk-flag sheet

Read this before touching a rounds sheet — it's the difference between generic ADL coverage and coverage that actually accounts for who's at risk of what.

| Bed | Patient | Risk flags | Observation level | Notes for this shift |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | R. | Depression, new SI language | Q15 (upgraded 1745) | See 5:45 PM entry — report ANY further statement, don't wait |
| 7 | T. | Elopement risk, new admit (day 1) | Q15, escort off-unit only | Unfamiliar with layout, redirect if near exit doors |
| 9 | M. | Fall risk, post-fall 2 days ago | Standard Q30 + gait belt for all transfers | Uses walker, do not let ambulate alone |
| 12 | K. | 1:1 continuous, active SI | 1:1 — dedicated staff, arm's length | Do not leave unattended for any reason, including bathroom |
| 14 | — | Bed available | — | New admit expected ~1800 |

**Rule:** if a flag column is blank, it means "nothing flagged as of this handoff," not "cleared" — a patient can move onto this list mid-shift, and the sheet gets updated the moment that happens, not at the next formal handoff.

## Daily activity schedule (the milieu, structured)

| Time | Activity | Aide's role |
|---|---|---|
| 7:00 AM | Wake / AM ADLs (hygiene, dressing) | Assist per care plan, observe hygiene follow-through as baseline data |
| 8:00 AM | Breakfast | Serve, observe intake, note refusals with time and reason if offered |
| 9:30 AM | Community meeting | Facilitate attendance, do not lead clinical content — that's staff/therapist-led |
| 11:00 AM | Structured rec (cards, walk, art table) | Actively participate, don't supervise from the doorway — engagement is the point |
| 12:00 PM | Lunch | Same as breakfast |
| 2:00 PM | Free time / 1:1 time with staff | Rotate individual check-ins, especially with quieter patients who won't seek you out |
| 5:00 PM | Dinner | Same as breakfast |
| 6:30 PM | Evening rec (games, TV, group activity) | Same as 11:00 AM slot |
| 9:00 PM | PM ADLs, wind-down | Assist per care plan, last full observation window before overnight lower-staffing rounds |
| 10:00 PM+ | Overnight rounds | Q15/Q30 per acuity, minimal engagement — the goal shifts to sleep, not activity |

**Rule:** if a patient skips two consecutive scheduled activities they'd normally attend, that's a reportable pattern on its own, independent of anything said — treat "opted out twice in a row" as data, not as the patient just having an off day.

## Boundary-test response script

Common patient asks, in escalating order of what they test, with the default response:

1. **"Can I have an extra snack / stay up later?"** → Point to the posted schedule/rule. "That's not on today's schedule — I can ask the nurse if there's a reason to make an exception." Never improvise a yes or no that isn't backed by the posted rule or a nurse's OK.
2. **"You're the only one who understands me / who's nice to me."** → Warm but flat redirect: "I'm glad our conversations help — everyone on this team is here to help you the same way." Do not accept the "only one" framing even implicitly; log it if it recurs with the same patient.
3. **"Can I get your phone number / can we talk after I'm discharged?"** → Immediate, unambiguous no, no explanation needed beyond "that's not something staff can do." Report to charge nurse same-shift regardless of how the patient reacts.
4. **Gift or favor offered** (patient's family brings something specifically for one aide, a patient repeatedly seeks out one aide over others). → Politely decline or redirect to a group-appropriate use ("let's share that at snack time"), and note it at handoff — not because any single instance is alarming, but because a pattern across a patient's stay is the signal, and only handoff notes make the pattern visible.

## Restraint-response role card (aide's part only)

An aide never initiates or ends a restraint and never chooses the technique. When a Code/response team is called, an aide's role is pre-assigned; execute exactly this, nothing more:

- **If assigned "spotter/counter":** verbally count down the timeline out loud (e.g., "one minute to face-to-face deadline") so the team lead doesn't have to track it themselves — this is the single highest-value thing an aide contributes to a restraint event, because missed deadlines are a common compliance failure.
- **If assigned "limb/hold":** hold exactly the position trained, release the instant the team lead calls release, never add pressure or a hold not part of the trained technique.
- **If assigned "door/area clear":** keep other patients and the hallway clear of the response area, redirect other patients to a different part of the unit, do not narrate what's happening to onlooking patients beyond "staff are helping someone, please head to the day room."
- **If not assigned a role:** stay out of the response area entirely — a well-meaning extra body in an already-crowded response is a documented source of injury, not backup.

## Observation-to-report escalation ladder

| What you observed | Timing | Who / how |
|---|---|---|
| Routine ADL/vitals-adjacent note (ate well, slept, mood stable) | End-of-round charting | Standard rounds sheet |
| One notable change from baseline (skipped one meal, quieter than usual) | Next scheduled check-in, verbally at minimum | Charge nurse, verbal + charted |
| Two or more notable changes same shift, OR any statement with self-harm/harm-to-others content | Immediately, in person | RN directly, not through a note left for later |
| Active danger (patient striking out, attempting to leave, medical emergency) | Immediately, call for help | RN + Code/response team per facility protocol |
