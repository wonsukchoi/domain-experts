# Red flags

Signals a supervisor should catch inside a monthly report or a single walkthrough, before a healthy-looking average or a paperwork label papers over the actual problem.

### Booth-rent conversion proposed for a provider producing well above their chair's rent breakeven

- **Usually means:** the proposal is being framed as a payroll-tax or overhead savings without modeling the retained-commission and retail-margin loss on a high producer — the shop is about to give away far more revenue than it saves.
- **First question:** "At this provider's trailing production, what does the shop actually net under commission versus under the proposed rent — run both numbers side by side?"
- **Data to pull:** trailing 3-month production and retail sales by provider; current commission split; the breakeven table in `references/playbook.md`.

### A booth-rent or contractor-classified provider still on a shop-assigned schedule, required at staff meetings, or using shop-set pricing

- **Usually means:** the classification label changed but the actual working relationship didn't — a live misclassification exposure under the IRS behavioral-control test regardless of what the rent agreement states.
- **First question:** "Walk me through this provider's actual week — who sets their hours, their prices, and do they have to attend our meetings?"
- **Data to pull:** the classification checklist in `references/playbook.md`; the signed rent/contractor agreement compared against actual scheduling and meeting-attendance records.

### Provider rebooking rate under ~45% for two or more consecutive months

- **Usually means:** a checkout-conversation gap — the provider isn't consistently asking clients to book their next visit before they leave the chair — rather than a demand or marketing shortfall.
- **First question:** "Sit in on this provider's next three checkouts — are they asking for the rebooking, or assuming the client will call?"
- **Data to pull:** per-provider rebooking-rate report for the trailing 90 days; a sample of checkout interactions (in person or via booking-system notes).

### Shop-wide retail attach rate reported as healthy while individual providers show near-zero attach

- **Usually means:** a blended average is absorbing one or two strong sellers while several providers barely sell product at all — the shop-wide number hides exactly who needs coaching or an incentive fix.
- **First question:** "Break the attach number down by provider — who's actually driving it, and who's at zero?"
- **Data to pull:** per-provider retail-attach percentage for the trailing 90 days; each provider's retail commission rate relative to their service commission rate.

### Retail attach rate stuck below target despite repeated sales-technique coaching

- **Usually means:** the retail commission rate is materially lower than the service commission rate, so providers are rationally prioritizing service upsells over product sales regardless of coaching — an incentive-design problem, not a skill problem.
- **First question:** "What's the retail commission percentage compared to the service commission percentage — would a rational provider actually prioritize selling product here?"
- **Data to pull:** current comp-plan commission schedule for both service and retail; attach-rate trend before and after the last coaching push.

### License renewal date within 30 days with no renewal or continuing-education proof on file

- **Usually means:** the provider or the front office assumed the renewal would happen on its own — a strict-liability gap that becomes a violation the moment the date passes, independent of the provider's skill or client load.
- **First question:** "Has this provider actually submitted renewal, or are we assuming it based on the calendar?"
- **Data to pull:** state board license-lookup portal status; continuing-education completion certificates on file.

### A provider performing a chemical or skin-breaking service with no patch-test or exposure-control documentation on file

- **Usually means:** the intake step was skipped under time pressure — invisible until a reaction is reported, at which point the missing record becomes a liability problem instead of a paperwork gap.
- **First question:** "Pull this client's patch-test record — when was it done, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** client intake/patch-test log; provider's exposure-control and PPE compliance record.

### Appointment column showing full utilization while same-day call-outs are absorbed with no documented protocol

- **Usually means:** call-outs are being handled ad hoc — silently double-booking a remaining provider's column rather than following the reassignment protocol — which shows up later as rushed services or client complaints with no paper trail explaining why.
- **First question:** "Show me today's call-out and how it was resolved — rebooked, transferred, or absorbed into another provider's column?"
- **Data to pull:** daily call-out log; column utilization for the provider who absorbed the gap; any same-day complaint tied to that provider.

### One or two providers accounting for a disproportionate share of total shop revenue with no cross-training or succession plan

- **Usually means:** the shop's revenue is concentrated in relationships that leave with the provider if they exit — client relationships in this trade are portable in a way that most retail staff turnover isn't.
- **First question:** "If this provider left tomorrow, what percentage of their client base do we realistically expect to retain versus follow them?"
- **Data to pull:** revenue concentration by provider (trailing 12 months); client rebooking history tied specifically to that provider versus the shop generally.

### Sanitation log showing consistent "pass" entries with no variation across shifts or providers

- **Usually means:** the log is being filled out as a routine rather than reflecting actual per-client disinfection — a common pattern before a state-board inspection finds a gap the paperwork didn't show.
- **First question:** "Spot-check three stations right now against the log — do they match?"
- **Data to pull:** sanitation log for the past two weeks; unannounced spot-check results compared against logged entries.
