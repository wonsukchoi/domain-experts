### A buying decision uses an open-to-buy figure calculated at the start of the season with no recalculation against actual sales
- **Usually means:** The figure may no longer reflect actual current inventory position and could lead to overspending or understocking relative to real demand.
- **First question:** When was open-to-buy last recalculated against actual sales and current inventory position?
- **Data to pull:** Current actual sales, current on-hand and on-order inventory, most recent OTB recalculation date.

### A buy's performance is reported using margin percentage or initial markup alone
- **Usually means:** The report may be masking weak capital efficiency (GMROI) that only becomes visible once markdowns and average inventory investment are factored in.
- **First question:** What is the buy's realized GMROI, not just its margin percentage or initial markup?
- **Data to pull:** Realized gross margin dollars, average inventory cost held during the period.

### Sell-through at the first checkpoint (e.g., 4 weeks) is meaningfully below plan, with no markdown or reorder-pause action taken
- **Usually means:** Deferring action risks a much deeper, costlier markdown later, once the shortfall compounds through the rest of the season.
- **First question:** How far below plan is sell-through at this checkpoint, and has an early markdown or reorder pause been considered?
- **Data to pull:** Actual vs. planned sell-through curve for this SKU group, remaining unit count and current retail price.

### A vendor negotiation focuses only on unit cost, with no discussion of cash discount or dating terms
- **Usually means:** Real margin may be left on the table, since these terms materially affect the true cost of goods beyond the sticker unit price.
- **First question:** What cash discount percentage and dating terms are being offered or requested as part of this negotiation?
- **Data to pull:** Vendor's standard terms sheet, prior negotiated terms with comparable vendors.

### A vendor compliance violation (late shipment, mislabeled cartons) occurred with no chargeback enforced
- **Usually means:** Margin protections built into the vendor agreement are being quietly foregone, eroding the value of those negotiated terms over time.
- **First question:** Does the vendor agreement specify a chargeback for this type of violation, and has it been applied?
- **Data to pull:** Vendor compliance agreement terms, documentation of the specific violation.

### A fashion/trend item is being managed with an ongoing reorder assumption
- **Usually means:** Fashion/trend items typically don't have the sustained sell-through pattern that justifies reordering, and applying a core-item replenishment mindset risks ending the season with excess, hard-to-clear inventory.
- **First question:** Is this item classified as core/replenishable or fashion/trend, and does the reorder plan match that classification?
- **Data to pull:** Item's assortment classification, historical sell-through pattern for comparable fashion/trend items.

### A core/replenishable item is being managed with an aggressive markdown cadence typical of fashion/trend goods
- **Usually means:** Markdowns are being taken prematurely on an item that would otherwise sell through steadily via reorder, unnecessarily sacrificing margin.
- **First question:** Is this item's sell-through pattern consistent with a core/replenishable classification, and does the current markdown plan match that?
- **Data to pull:** Item's sell-through history, current markdown schedule.

### A department's realized maintained markup differs significantly from its initial markup, with no analysis of why
- **Usually means:** The gap between IMU and MMU reflects real markdown/shortage/discount activity worth understanding before planning next season's buy.
- **First question:** What specifically (markdowns, shortage, promotional discounts) drove the gap between initial and maintained markup?
- **Data to pull:** IMU at time of buy, realized MMU at season end, breakdown of markdown/shortage/discount activity.
