# Red Flags

Smell tests for transport, transfer, and turnover situations. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Stretcher/wheelchair moved with rails down or brakes unlocked

- **Usually means:** the safety check was skipped under time pressure, or the equipment was assumed "fine from last use" instead of re-checked.
- **First question:** "When was the brake/rail check done for this specific move?"
- **Data to pull:** incident/near-miss reports for the unit; transport log timestamps vs. staffing levels at that hour.

### Assist-level note unchanged for >24 hours despite a new sedating medication or procedure

- **Usually means:** the chart hasn't been reassessed since a status-changing event; the documented level is describing a different clinical moment.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed — medication, procedure, reported symptom — since this note was written?"
- **Data to pull:** MAR for sedating meds in the last 30–60 minutes; most recent vitals or nursing note.

### Patient weight within ~10% of the equipment's rated safe working load (SWL)

- **Usually means:** the wrong equipment capacity class was selected, often because the bed-scale weight didn't include the IV pole, oxygen tank, or belongings that travel with the patient.
- **First question:** "What's the equipment's rated SWL, the patient's actual weight, and what else is riding along?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment rating plate; bed-scale reading and time it was taken.

### Manual one-person lifts logged for a patient on a documented 2-person-assist or mechanical-lift care plan

- **Usually means:** time pressure or understaffing is overriding the safety policy, and it's become a workaround culture rather than an isolated lapse.
- **First question:** "What made it feel like there was no time to page a second person or fetch the lift?"
- **Data to pull:** staffing ratio and page-response times at the hour of the incident; frequency of this pattern across the unit.

### Injury/strain reports clustered on one unit or shift

- **Usually means:** missing or broken mechanical lift equipment, chronic understaffing for 2-person lifts, or an informal norm of skipping the lift to save time.
- **First question:** "Was a functioning mechanical lift available and accessible at the time?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment maintenance/downtime log; injury reports by unit and shift over the last quarter.

### Isolation transport with no documented receiving-department notification

- **Usually means:** a break in the precaution chain — the receiving unit finds out about the isolation status only when the patient arrives, if at all.
- **First question:** "Did the receiving department know the precaution type before the patient left the unit?"
- **Data to pull:** transport log notification field; infection-control rounding notes for cross-contamination events.

### Room turnover completed well under the disinfectant's labeled dwell time

- **Usually means:** the surface was wiped dry before the disinfectant had its required contact time — the room looks clean but wasn't actually disinfected.
- **First question:** "What's this product's labeled wet-contact time, and did the clock support it?"
- **Data to pull:** EVS/turnover timestamps against the disinfectant manufacturer's dwell-time spec.

### Patient identification mismatch caught during a procedure rather than before transport

- **Usually means:** the two-identifier check was skipped or done from memory/familiarity rather than against the wristband.
- **First question:** "Was the wristband checked, or was the patient identified by room number or recognition?"
- **Data to pull:** transport log identifier field; incident report timeline.

### Bariatric-capacity equipment consistently unavailable or slow to arrive when requested

- **Usually means:** an equipment-planning gap rather than a one-off — the unit doesn't stock or reserve enough bariatric-class equipment for its patient population.
- **First question:** "How many minutes elapsed between the request and the equipment's arrival?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment request/response time log over the last month; census of bariatric-weight patients on the unit.

### Morgue transport with an incomplete or single-signature chain-of-custody form

- **Usually means:** the documentation was rushed under time or emotional pressure, and the two-signature verification step was compressed into one.
- **First question:** "Was identity verified against the chart at both the unit handoff and the morgue handoff, with a signature at each?"
- **Data to pull:** chain-of-custody form; morgue intake log.

### A "reportable" observation during transport goes unmentioned in the handoff

- **Usually means:** the orderly waited to be certain the finding was significant before reporting it, or assumed someone else would notice.
- **First question:** "What did the patient look or sound like at the start of transport versus at handoff?"
- **Data to pull:** transport log free-text field; compare against the receiving nurse's admission/arrival note for the same timeframe.
