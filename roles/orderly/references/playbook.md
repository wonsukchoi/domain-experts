# Orderly Playbook

Filled step sequences for the three recurring tasks: patient transport, bed/room turnover, and isolation/morgue transport.

## Transport checklist (any patient, any destination)

| Step | Action | Fail-safe |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Confirm 2 identifiers (name + DOB) against wristband and order | Never substitute room number or "I recognize them" |
| 2 | Read current precaution level and assist level from chart, not memory | If last update is >24h old and any sedating med given since, treat assist level as unverified |
| 3 | Weigh/estimate patient, compare to equipment SWL | If within 10% of rated SWL, escalate to next capacity class |
| 4 | Lock brakes, raise/secure rails, fasten straps, attach gait belt if ambulatory-assist | Do this before the patient moves toward the equipment, not after |
| 5 | Clear route; for isolation, notify receiving department and use designated elevator/corridor | Notification happens before the patient leaves the room, not on arrival |
| 6 | Move patient using the assessed method (manual only within policy weight/assist limits) | If assessed method requires a second person and none is available, wait — do not downgrade to manual solo |
| 7 | Monitor continuously; stop and reassess on any reported symptom or visible change | Stopping is never the wrong call |
| 8 | Hand off to a named receiving staff member; state precautions and anything observed | Verbal + written; don't leave a patient with equipment and no verbal handoff |
| 9 | Log transport (time, method, equipment, any incident) | Log before starting the next task, not at end of shift |

## Equipment capacity reference (typical facility classes — verify against your facility's rated equipment)

| Equipment | Typical SWL | Escalate to bariatric class if |
|---|---|---|
| Standard wheelchair | 250 lb | Patient weight ≥ 225 lb, or patient + IV pole/O2/belongings pushes near limit |
| Standard transport stretcher | 300–350 lb | Patient weight ≥ 275 lb |
| Standard mechanical/Hoyer lift sling | 400–450 lb | Patient weight ≥ 350 lb, or sling size mismatch (any dimension too small) |
| Bariatric wheelchair | 500–700 lb | Only if facility's bariatric-bariatric (>700 lb) equipment exists and patient exceeds standard bariatric rating |

Numbers vary by manufacturer — confirm against the rating plate on the actual unit in use, not a remembered figure.

## Assist-level decision table

| Documented assist level | Time since last update | New sedating med or symptom since? | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Independent | <24h | No | Proceed standard |
| Independent | ≥24h or unknown | Yes | Reassess before transfer — treat as assist x1 minimum until confirmed |
| Assist x1 | <24h | No | Gait belt, 1-person assist, standard equipment |
| Assist x1 | Any | Yes (opioid/benzo <30 min, or reported dizziness) | Reassess orthostatics before sit-to-stand; upgrade to assist x2 pending recheck |
| Assist x2 / mechanical lift ordered | Any | N/A | Mechanical lift or 2-person team lift, no exceptions for time pressure |
| Total/dependent | Any | N/A | Mechanical lift required; manual transfer is a policy violation regardless of staffing |

## Bed / room turnover sequence

Standard turnover (no special precautions), target 20–30 minutes:

1. Remove and bag linens; strip bed to frame.
2. Discard single-use disposables (basins, tubing, unused supplies past open-date).
3. Wipe high-touch surfaces first, in this order: bed rails/controls → call button → overbed table → IV pole → bathroom grab bars/toilet handle → doorknob/light switch. Then remaining furniture surfaces. Floor last.
4. Apply facility-approved disinfectant; **do not wipe dry before the labeled wet-contact (dwell) time has elapsed** — typical quaternary ammonium products require 3–10 minutes; check the product label, not a remembered number.
5. Remake bed with fresh linens; restock room (gloves, gown, basin, tissues, call-button check).
6. Verify equipment in room (IV pump, bed alarm if applicable) is functional and reset.
7. Log turnover completion time; notify bed-management/charge nurse room is ready.

**High-consequence room turnover** (C. diff, other spore-forming or resistant organism, per infection-control policy):

- Same sequence, but disinfectant must be sporicidal (e.g., bleach-based, not standard quaternary ammonium) with its own — typically longer — dwell time.
- PPE (gown + gloves at minimum) worn for the entire turnover, doffed per sequence below before leaving the room.
- Curtains and privacy screens replaced, not just wiped.
- Do not compress the dwell time to hit a bed-turn target — a cosmetically clean surface that hasn't had contact time is not disinfected.

## Isolation transport sequence

1. Confirm precaution type from door card/chart: contact, droplet, or airborne (each has a different PPE and routing requirement — don't apply one routine to all three).
2. Don PPE in the standard order: hand hygiene → gown → mask/respirator (fit-checked if N95/PAPR for airborne) → goggles/face shield → gloves.
3. Notify the receiving department before leaving the current unit — precaution type, expected arrival time, any equipment needs (isolation cart, extra gown for receiving staff).
4. Use the facility's designated isolation transport route/elevator if one exists; avoid common waiting areas.
5. At the destination, assist with patient placement, then doff PPE in the standard order: gloves → hand hygiene → goggles/face shield → gown → hand hygiene → mask/respirator last (it's the piece designed to filter what you're breathing) → hand hygiene again.
6. Do not touch clean surfaces (elevator buttons, your own phone, the transport log pen) between doffing steps — treat your hands as contaminated until the final hand hygiene step is done.
7. Log transport with precaution type documented explicitly, not just "isolation."

## Morgue / postmortem transport

1. Verify patient identity against two sources (wristband + chart) before covering/tagging — this is the last identifier check that can happen; errors here cannot be corrected afterward.
2. Complete toe-tag with full identifying information (name, DOB, MRN, time of death) per facility protocol.
3. Transport via the facility's designated route, covered, at a time and via a path that avoids public and family areas where possible.
4. Sign and complete chain-of-custody documentation at both handoff points (unit → transport, transport → morgue) — two signatures, not one.
5. Report any discrepancy (tag illegible, identity uncertain, personal effects unaccounted for) immediately rather than proceeding and noting it later.
