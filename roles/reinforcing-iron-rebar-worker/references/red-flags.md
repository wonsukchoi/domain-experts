# Red flags

Smell tests a rebar foreman, QC inspector, or engineer catches in the first pass over a placing drawing, tied mat, or suspected placement defect.

### Tension lap splice tied shorter than the length shown on the bar-bending schedule

- **Usually means:** the crew eyeballed a "close enough" length, or a grade substitution occurred and nobody recomputed the required length for the actual bar in the mat.
- **First question:** what length was actually tied at this splice, and does the bar's grade mark match the grade the schedule's length was computed for?
- **Data to pull:** bar-bending schedule/placing drawing for that location, mill cert or grade mark record for the bars tied there, pre-pour walk-through log.

### More than 50% of bars in a section spliced at the same location, tied as a Class A splice

- **Usually means:** the crew defaulted to the shorter Class A multiplier without checking the percentage-spliced or 2× steel-area condition that Class A requires.
- **First question:** what percentage of bars are actually spliced within this lap length here, and is the area of steel provided at least double what's required?
- **Data to pull:** placing drawing splice layout, steel-area calculation for the section, splice-class note on the schedule.

### Bar grade mill mark doesn't match the grade called out on the placing drawing

- **Usually means:** a mixed-heat bundle was delivered and tied in before anyone checked the mark, on the assumption that the delivery ticket and the steel match.
- **First question:** how many bars in this mat carry the mismatched mark, and has the engineer of record been notified before any further tying?
- **Data to pull:** mill certs for the delivered lot, physical grade-mark spot check across the bundle, as-built deviation log if a substitution was approved.

### Chair or bolster height rounded up to the next available stock size on a top-mat cover check

- **Usually means:** the exact required height wasn't a stock size and the crew rounded toward convenience rather than toward more cover.
- **First question:** what's the actual resulting cover at this chair height, computed against the member thickness and bar diameter — not just "close to spec"?
- **Data to pull:** chair/bolster spec sheet, cover-meter spot check at that location, placing drawing cover call for that face.

### Epoxy-coated bar tied into a mat with visible bare-steel patches, no repair record

- **Usually means:** coating damage from shipping straps, bending, or dragging was treated as cosmetic and skipped rather than measured against the repair threshold.
- **First question:** what's the measured damaged area on this bar, cumulative and at its worst single-foot section, against the repair/rejection thresholds?
- **Data to pull:** pre-placement coating inspection log, patch-repair record if one exists, governing spec's exact damage thresholds.

### Chairs/bolsters spaced wider than roughly 4 ft o.c. under a heavy bottom mat or high-traffic pour path

- **Usually means:** standard spacing was used regardless of bar size or expected foot/hose traffic, inviting sag between supports during the pour.
- **First question:** what's the actual chair spacing here, and does it match the tighter spacing called for by this bar size and pour condition?
- **Data to pull:** bar-support layout/spec, pour sequence and pump-hose routing plan, post-pour cover-meter scan if available.

### Splice location moved in the field to clear an embed, sleeve, or congestion, with no engineer sign-off

- **Usually means:** the crew hit a real conflict and improvised a location shift rather than routing it through the engineer of record.
- **First question:** does the engineer of record's record show an approved relocation and length for this splice, or was it moved without one?
- **Data to pull:** RFI/field-issue log for that location, as-built markup, original vs. as-tied splice coordinates.

### Rebar dowels or stub-ups left protruding above formwork with no cap or bend-over protection

- **Usually means:** impalement protection was treated as optional once the pour sequence got busy, rather than a standing requirement for any exposed vertical bar.
- **First question:** are all protruding bars in the active work area currently capped, bent over, or otherwise guarded per OSHA 1926.701(b)?
- **Data to pull:** site safety walk log, cap/protection inventory for the area, incident/near-miss log if one exists.

### Cover-meter (pachometer) spot check skipped before a pour on an exposed or corrosion-critical element

- **Usually means:** the mat "looked tied correctly" and the crew treated the instrument check as optional rather than the last chance to catch a shifted bar or chair.
- **First question:** was a cover-meter check actually run on this element before the pour, and if not, why was it skipped?
- **Data to pull:** pre-pour inspection checklist, cover-meter reading log if one exists, element's exposure classification.

### Grade-substituted bar tied at the original grade's lap length instead of a recomputed one

- **Usually means:** the crew assumed a higher grade number only makes the connection stronger, missing that development length scales with yield strength and must be recomputed for the actual bar in place.
- **First question:** what grade is actually tied at this splice, and was the lap length recalculated for that grade rather than reused from the original schedule?
- **Data to pull:** grade-mark verification for the tied bars, recomputed lap-length calculation, engineer-of-record approval for the substitution.
