# Fashion Designer — Red Flags

### Marker efficiency under 80% on first pattern draft
- **Usually means:** pattern pieces have curved or asymmetric edges that don't nest tightly, or fabric width is mismatched to piece dimensions.
- **First question:** which specific pieces are driving the waste — can a curve be squared or a piece rotated without a fit change?
- **Data to pull:** the marker layout image with piece-by-piece waste highlighted, and the pattern's piece-count/shape list.

### Costing sheet built from a sample-quantity fabric quote, not a bulk quote
- **Usually means:** the designer priced the garment before requesting a bulk quote, and sample pricing (often 20-35% above bulk) is inflating the assumed COGS.
- **First question:** has a bulk quote at the planned order quantity actually been requested from the mill/vendor?
- **Data to pull:** both quotes side by side (sample-run price/yard vs. bulk price/yard at the planned unit count).

### Sample fails at three or more measurement points simultaneously
- **Usually means:** a grading-rule error at the base pattern, not random factory cutting variance (which typically produces one or two isolated misses).
- **First question:** do the failed points share a common construction seam or pattern piece?
- **Data to pull:** the full grade rule table for the failed size, compared point-by-point against the base pattern.

### Fabric cost exceeds 50% of target COGS
- **Usually means:** the fabric choice was locked before the price target was confirmed, or the target price itself was set without checking fabric-market reality for the desired hand/quality.
- **First question:** is fabric substitution possible without materially changing the design intent, or does the wholesale price need revisiting?
- **Data to pull:** the full COGS buildup with each line item's share of total cost.

### Grade rule only checked at the base size and one adjacent size
- **Usually means:** the extremes of the size run (smallest and largest) haven't been sanity-checked and may carry a compounding increment error invisible until tabulated end to end.
- **First question:** what does the full size curve look like from XS to XXL laid out side by side?
- **Data to pull:** the complete grade rule table across every size, not just the base and immediate neighbors.

### A style's projected sell-through is below the season's line-wide floor but gets kept anyway
- **Usually means:** the style is being kept on aesthetic or designer-attachment grounds rather than portfolio economics, consuming sampling budget and buyer attention that could go to a stronger performer.
- **First question:** what specifically justifies keeping this style below the sell-through floor — a strategic reason (e.g. hero image piece) or inertia?
- **Data to pull:** prior-season sell-through data for the closest comparable style.

### Tech pack measurement points have no stated tolerance
- **Usually means:** the pack was drafted quickly without factory input, leaving the factory to guess an acceptable tolerance or treat every measurement as exact.
- **First question:** has this tech pack been reviewed by the specific factory that will cut it, given tolerances vary by their equipment and process?
- **Data to pull:** the tech pack's measurement table checked for a tolerance column.

### More than 15% of a season's line consists of newly added styles with no prior-season sales data
- **Usually means:** the line has drifted from a data-informed portfolio toward designer-preference-driven expansion, raising sampling cost and buyer-decision fatigue without evidence of demand.
- **First question:** what's driving the new-style ratio this season versus prior seasons?
- **Data to pull:** season-over-season new-style count as a percentage of total SKUs.

### A colorway is being tracked as a separate style for costing purposes
- **Usually means:** development cost (patternmaking, grading, sample rounds) is being double-counted across colorways of the same construction, inflating the apparent cost of the line.
- **First question:** does this colorway differ in construction/pattern, or only in print/dye?
- **Data to pull:** the style-to-colorway mapping in the line plan.
