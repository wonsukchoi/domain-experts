# Obstetrician-Gynecologist — Red Flags

### Confirmed BP ≥160/110 twice within 15 minutes, any gestational age
- **Usually means:** Severe-range gestational hypertension or preeclampsia with severe features; second most likely is chronic hypertension decompensating in pregnancy.
- **First question:** Is this the second confirmatory reading, and has a severe-features lab panel (platelets, LFTs, creatinine) been sent yet?
- **Data to pull:** Repeat BP in 15 min, CBC with platelets, LFTs, creatinine, urine protein/creatinine ratio.

### RMI ≥200 on an adnexal mass work-up
- **Usually means:** Substantially elevated ovarian malignancy risk (~42× background); second most likely is a benign complex mass with an incidentally elevated CA-125 (endometrioma, fibroid degeneration, pelvic inflammatory disease).
- **First question:** Has the ultrasound score been recalculated correctly (0/1/3, not a linear feature count) before accepting the RMI total?
- **Data to pull:** Full transvaginal ultrasound report with feature checklist, CA-125, menopausal status.

### Postmenopausal bleeding, any endometrial thickness
- **Usually means:** Atrophic endometrium (most common); second most likely is endometrial hyperplasia or carcinoma — the 2024 ACOG update no longer treats a thin stripe alone as sufficient rule-out for most patients.
- **First question:** Is this a single episode with a fully visualized endometrium ≤4mm and no risk factors — the only scenario where ultrasound alone is still appropriate?
- **Data to pull:** Transvaginal ultrasound endometrial thickness, prior bleeding episodes, risk factors (obesity, tamoxifen, unopposed estrogen, Lynch syndrome).

### Category III fetal heart rate tracing
- **Usually means:** Evolving fetal acidemia requiring immediate intervention; second most likely is a transient event (maternal position, recent vaginal exam) that resolves with repositioning.
- **First question:** Has intrauterine resuscitation (position change, IV fluid bolus, oxygen, stop oxytocin) been attempted, and is it improving?
- **Data to pull:** Full strip review over the preceding 30 minutes, maternal vitals, time since last exam or intervention.

### Postpartum EBL trending toward 1000mL (vaginal) or continuing to rise post-cesarean
- **Usually means:** Uterine atony (most common cause); second most likely is retained placental tissue or a genital tract laceration.
- **First question:** Has blood loss been quantified (weighed), not visually estimated, and has the fundus been checked?
- **Data to pull:** Quantitative blood loss total, fundal tone exam, vitals trend, time since delivery.

### Severe, sudden-onset unilateral pelvic pain with a known adnexal mass
- **Usually means:** Ovarian torsion; second most likely is a ruptured hemorrhagic cyst.
- **First question:** Is there Doppler flow to the ovary on ultrasound, and does the clinical picture match torsion enough to proceed to surgery even if flow is present (Doppler can be falsely reassuring)?
- **Data to pull:** Pelvic ultrasound with Doppler, pain onset/character, prior known mass size on imaging.

### Positive pregnancy test with pain and no confirmed intrauterine pregnancy on ultrasound
- **Usually means:** Ectopic pregnancy until proven otherwise; second most likely is a very early intrauterine pregnancy below the discriminatory zone.
- **First question:** What is the quantitative hCG, and does it clear the discriminatory zone where an IUP should be visible?
- **Data to pull:** Serial quantitative hCG, transvaginal ultrasound findings, hemodynamic stability.

### Suspected placenta accreta spectrum risk factors (prior cesarean + anterior low-lying placenta/previa)
- **Usually means:** Elevated risk of abnormal placental invasion requiring a planned, multidisciplinary delivery; second most likely is a normal placenta with reassuring imaging that still warrants a documented risk discussion.
- **First question:** Has dedicated accreta-protocol imaging (ultrasound ± MRI) been obtained, and is a multidisciplinary delivery plan (blood bank, anesthesia, possible gyn-onc) in place?
- **Data to pull:** Imaging report specific to accreta features, prior cesarean count, placental location relative to prior scar.

### Abnormal uterine bleeding in a reproductive-age patient with anovulatory pattern and obesity/PCOS
- **Usually means:** Endometrial hyperplasia risk from unopposed estrogen exposure; second most likely is a structural cause (fibroid, polyp).
- **First question:** How long has the bleeding pattern been anovulatory, and is there a plan for endometrial protection (progestin) regardless of biopsy timing?
- **Data to pull:** Bleeding pattern history, pelvic ultrasound for structural lesions, endometrial biopsy if indicated by age/risk factors.

### Sudden severe headache or visual changes in a patient with any hypertensive disorder of pregnancy
- **Usually means:** Impending eclampsia or evolving severe preeclampsia; second most likely is a primary headache disorder, but this distinction is made after ruling out the emergent cause, not before.
- **First question:** Is magnesium sulfate seizure prophylaxis already running, and if not, why not?
- **Data to pull:** Current BP, reflexes/clonus exam, magnesium status, recent severe-features labs.

### Abnormal cervical cytology (HSIL or ASC-H) regardless of HPV result
- **Usually means:** Needs colposcopy regardless of HPV co-test result — high-grade cytology overrides a reassuring HPV result in triage algorithms.
- **First question:** Has colposcopy been scheduled, or is the plan incorrectly deferring to a "repeat in a year" pathway meant for lower-grade results?
- **Data to pull:** Full cytology and HPV result, prior screening history, colposcopy/biopsy results if already performed.

### Retained products of conception suspected after a delivery or miscarriage
- **Usually means:** Incomplete expulsion of placental tissue causing ongoing bleeding or infection risk; second most likely is normal involuting endometrium mistaken for retained tissue on ultrasound.
- **First question:** Does the ultrasound show a discrete echogenic mass with vascularity, or a nonspecific thickened stripe that could be normal postpartum change?
- **Data to pull:** Ultrasound with Doppler, quantitative hCG trend if post-miscarriage, bleeding/infection symptoms.
