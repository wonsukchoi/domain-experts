# Obstetrician-Gynecologist — Playbook

## Risk of Malignancy Index (RMI) worksheet — adnexal mass triage

| Component | Scoring | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Ultrasound features (multilocular cyst, solid areas, bilaterality, ascites, metastases) | 0 features = 0 · 1 feature = 1 · ≥2 features = 3 | U |
| Menopausal status | Premenopausal = 1 · Postmenopausal = 3 | M |
| CA-125 (U/mL) | Raw serum value | C |

**RMI = U × M × C**

Example: multilocular + solid areas + ascites (3 features → U=3), postmenopausal (M=3), CA-125 210 → RMI = 3 × 3 × 210 = **1,890**.

**Interpretation (Jacobs et al., 1990):** RMI <200 → benign-consistent, general OB-GYN surgical management reasonable. RMI ≥200 → ~42× background malignancy risk, refer to gynecologic oncology for primary staging surgery rather than local salpingo-oophorectomy.

## Carpenter-Coustan 100g 3-hour OGTT — GDM diagnosis

| Timepoint | Threshold (mg/dL) |
|---|---|
| Fasting | ≥95 |
| 1 hour | ≥180 |
| 2 hour | ≥155 |
| 3 hour | ≥140 |

**Rule:** ≥2 of 4 values meeting or exceeding threshold = GDM diagnosis. One elevated value alone is not diagnostic — repeat screening or manage as impaired glucose tolerance per practice protocol, not as GDM.

Example: fasting 92 (normal), 1h 188 (abnormal), 2h 160 (abnormal), 3h 130 (normal) → 2 of 4 abnormal → **GDM diagnosed**, start nutrition therapy and glucose monitoring.

## Bishop score — induction-method selection

| Factor | 0 pts | 1 pt | 2 pts | 3 pts |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dilation (cm) | 0 | 1-2 | 3-4 | ≥5 |
| Effacement (%) | 0-30 | 40-50 | 60-70 | ≥80 |
| Station | -3 | -2 | -1/0 | +1/+2 |
| Cervical consistency | Firm | Medium | Soft | — |
| Position | Posterior | Mid | Anterior | — |

**Rule:** total ≥8 → favorable cervix, oxytocin induction alone is reasonable. Total <6 → unfavorable, use a cervical-ripening agent (misoprostol or mechanical dilator) before or with oxytocin. Score 6-7 is a judgment zone — factor in parity and indication urgency.

Example: dilation 2cm (1) + effacement 50% (1) + station -1 (2) + consistency medium (1) + position anterior (2) = **7 — borderline**; for an otherwise low-risk multipara, oxytocin alone is reasonable; for a nullipara, add a ripening agent first.

## FIGO 2023 endometrial cancer staging — abbreviated

| Stage | Extent |
|---|---|
| IA | Confined to endometrium or superficial (<50%) myometrial invasion, low-grade histology |
| IB | Invasion ≥50% myometrium, low-grade, or aggressive histology confined to polyp/endometrium |
| II | Cervical stromal invasion, or specific aggressive histologies confined to uterine corpus |
| IIIA1 / IIIA2 | Adnexal involvement (IIIA1) or uterine serosa invasion (IIIA2) |
| IIIB1 / IIIB2 | Vaginal/parametrial involvement (IIIB1) or pelvic peritoneal involvement (IIIB2) |
| IIIC1 / IIIC2 | Pelvic (IIIC1) or para-aortic (IIIC2) lymph node involvement |
| IVA | Bladder or bowel mucosal invasion |
| IVB / IVC | Extrapelvic peritoneal metastasis (IVB) or distant metastasis (IVC) |

**Molecular modifier:** append "m" + subtype (POLEmut, MMRd, NSMP, p53abn) when known — POLEmut can downstage (e.g., IAmPOLEmut), p53abn can upstage a Stage I/II tumor. Molecular classification is now encouraged on every case, not reserved for ambiguous histology.

## Staged postpartum hemorrhage response (with TXA timing)

1. **Recognition:** quantitative (weighed/measured) blood loss, not visual estimate. Cumulative ≥500mL vaginal or ≥1000mL cesarean, or any amount with abnormal vitals, triggers the response.
2. **Immediate:** bimanual fundal massage, first-line uterotonic (oxytocin), IV access ×2, notify full team, start quantifying blood loss actively.
3. **Within 3 hours of onset:** administer tranexamic acid 1g IV — WOMAN trial (Lancet, 2017) shows a mortality benefit from bleeding specifically when given within this window; benefit is not established for later dosing.
4. **Escalating uterotonics:** methylergonovine (contraindicated in hypertension) → carboprost (caution in asthma) → misoprostol, per contraindication profile, not in a fixed order regardless of history.
5. **Mechanical:** uterine balloon tamponade or compression sutures (B-Lynch) if pharmacologic management fails.
6. **Massive transfusion trigger:** activate protocol at cumulative EBL ≥1500mL with continued bleeding, or earlier with hemodynamic instability — don't wait for a lab-confirmed coagulopathy to activate.
7. **Surgical last resort:** uterine artery ligation or embolization, then hysterectomy — sequenced by fertility-sparing preference where time allows, abandoned in favor of hysterectomy immediately if the patient is hemodynamically unstable.

## Cervical cancer screening interval table (USPSTF 2018)

| Age | Default interval |
|---|---|
| <21 | No screening |
| 21-29 | Cytology alone, every 3 years |
| 30-65 | Cotesting (cytology + HPV) every 5 years, OR primary HPV every 5 years, OR cytology alone every 3 years |
| >65 | Stop, if adequate prior negative screening and no high-grade history |
| Post-hysterectomy (cervix removed, benign indication) | Stop |
