# Vocabulary — terms a generalist misuses

### Adequacy (satisfactory / unsatisfactory)
A specimen-level judgment, made before any interpretive read, on whether cellularity, obscuring factors, and (for gyn specimens) transformation-zone sampling meet the reporting system's documented thresholds.
**In use:** "Estimated 8,100 squamous cells against a 5,000 minimum — satisfactory, TZ component present."
**Common misuse:** Treating adequacy as a formality that can be backfilled once an interpretation is already decided, rather than a gate checked and documented first — an unsatisfactory specimen can't be diagnosed around.

### ASC-US (Atypical Squamous Cells of Undetermined Significance)
A Bethesda category for squamous cells showing nuclear changes (enlargement, mild contour irregularity, mild hyperchromasia) that fall short of a definitive low-grade lesion but exceed normal variation.
**In use:** "One cluster at 2.5x normal nuclear size with mild contour irregularity — that's ASC-US, referred for reflex HPV and pathologist review."
**Common misuse:** Assuming the category requires a minimum percentage of the specimen to be abnormal; Bethesda criteria are per-cell/group, so a single qualifying cluster is sufficient regardless of how rare it is among the total cell count.

### ASC:SIL ratio
A laboratory-level quality metric comparing the count of ASC-US calls to the count of SIL (LSIL + HSIL) calls over a period, used to detect calibration drift toward over- or under-calling.
**In use:** "Ratio's at 3.47:1 this month, above our 3:1 ceiling — flagging a batch review, not touching today's case."
**Common misuse:** Using the ratio to justify recategorizing an individual case — the ratio is a retrospective team-level signal, not an input to any single slide's morphologic call.

### Rescreen (10% / targeted)
The CLIA-mandated re-examination of a sample of negative gynecologic cases (at least 10%, selected to increase the odds of catching a false negative) plus targeted rescreening of cases meeting specific high-risk criteria.
**In use:** "That case has a prior HSIL history — it goes to targeted rescreen in addition to the routine 10% sample."
**Common misuse:** Treating rescreening as a confirmatory glance at the original screener's result rather than a genuinely blind independent read; a non-blind rescreen cannot catch what the first read missed.

### Sign-out authority
The scope within which a cytotechnologist can finalize a report independently (typically NILM only) versus what must be referred to a pathologist for confirmation and sign-out (ASC-US and above).
**In use:** "This is ASC-US — outside my sign-out authority, routing to Dr. Lee before it releases."
**Common misuse:** Assuming an experienced screener's confidence substitutes for the defined scope boundary; the boundary is structural, not a function of individual skill level.

### Reflex testing (HPV)
A predefined lab rule that automatically triggers an additional test — most commonly HPV — when a Pap result meets specified Bethesda criteria (typically ASC-US).
**In use:** "ASC-US on a liquid-based specimen — reflex HPV should fire automatically per the lab's validated algorithm."
**Common misuse:** Assuming reflex testing happens for every abnormal category by default; the trigger criteria are specific to the lab's validated protocol and the specimen collection type (liquid-based versus conventional).

### Transformation zone (TZ) / endocervical component
The cell population (endocervical or squamous metaplastic cells) whose presence indicates the collection device sampled the zone where cervical dysplasia originates.
**In use:** "Fourteen-cell endocervical cluster identified — TZ component present, meets the ten-cell minimum."
**Common misuse:** Treating TZ component absence as an automatic unsatisfactory result; per Bethesda 2014 it is reported as a quality note on an otherwise satisfactory specimen, not a rejection criterion by itself.

### Workload limit (slide-equivalent)
The CLIA-defined ceiling (100 slides in 24 hours) on the volume of cytology material one person can screen, with non-gynecologic cases counted using a lab-validated weighting factor rather than 1:1 against gynecologic slides.
**In use:** "Ninety-two slide-equivalents logged already today — eight left before the cap, including non-GYN weighting."
**Common misuse:** Counting every case as exactly one slide regardless of type; non-GYN cases typically carry a higher weighting factor validated per lab, and ignoring it understates true workload against the legal ceiling.

### Rapid on-site evaluation (ROSE)
Real-time adequacy assessment of an FNA pass at the bedside, giving the proceduralist immediate feedback on whether the target lesion's diagnostic material has been obtained.
**In use:** "Follicular groups present but under the six-group threshold for this target — recommend one more pass."
**Common misuse:** Calling adequacy from general smear cellularity rather than confirming the specific cell type relevant to the clinical question — a smear can look cellular and still miss the actual target.

### Discordance
A disagreement between two independent reads of the same case — cytotechnologist versus pathologist, or original screen versus rescreen — tracked as a distinct QA category from a routine category change.
**In use:** "That's a discordance worth logging — screener called NILM, pathologist's review found a focal HSIL-criteria group."
**Common misuse:** Treating every category adjustment during the normal referral-and-confirm workflow (ASC-US referred, pathologist upgrades to LSIL) as a "discordance" in the QA sense — the term is reserved for disagreements between two independent, blinded reads of the same material.

### Risk of malignancy (ROM)
The published, tier-specific probability of malignancy associated with a non-gynecologic reporting category (most explicitly in TBSRTC for thyroid), used to set the clinical follow-up pathway.
**In use:** "Category III, AUS/FLUS — ROM roughly 10-30%, which is why this goes to repeat FNA or molecular testing rather than straight to surgery."
**Common misuse:** Treating the category label alone as the clinically actionable output, without the associated ROM percentage that actually drives the follow-up decision.
