# Red flags — cytology screening and quality signals

### ASC:SIL ratio above roughly 3:1 for the month
- **Usually means:** calibration drift toward over-calling ASC-US (a defensive habit), less often a genuine shift in the referral population's risk profile.
- **First question:** is the elevated ratio concentrated in one screener's cases, or spread evenly across the team?
- **Data to pull:** month-to-date LIS category counts broken out per screener, benchmarked against the lab's own trailing 12-month average.

### Frozen or liquid-based Pap adequacy called "satisfactory" without a documented cellularity count
- **Usually means:** the screener substituted a visual impression for the required 10-field count-and-extrapolate, especially on borderline slides.
- **First question:** was an actual field count performed and logged, or is "looked adequate" the only basis in the record?
- **Data to pull:** the case's adequacy worksheet; recount on the original slide if the number is missing.

### A screener's average time-per-slide drops below their own historical baseline by a wide margin (e.g., under 5 minutes on a routine liquid-based Pap where 6-8 minutes is typical)
- **Usually means:** workload pressure driving a faster, less systematic screening pattern — CAP interlaboratory data tie this pattern to reduced sensitivity before any other explanation.
- **First question:** has the screener's daily slide count or shift length recently increased?
- **Data to pull:** LIS timestamp log per slide compared against that screener's 90-day rolling average.

### 24-hour CLIA workload tally exceeds 100 slide-equivalents, or non-GYN weighting factors are applied inconsistently across cases
- **Usually means:** a tracking/logging gap rather than deliberate overwork — but it is a compliance violation regardless of intent.
- **First question:** does the lab's non-GYN weighting formula match what was actually used for these specific cases?
- **Data to pull:** the LIS workload module's raw per-slide log for the 24-hour window in question.

### Rescreen (10% or targeted) shows the reviewing screener saw the original result before completing the rescreen
- **Usually means:** a workflow gap that defeats the purpose of rescreening — a non-blind rescreen cannot catch a false negative the same way a blind one can.
- **First question:** does the LIS or manual routing process expose the original category to the rescreening screener before their independent read is entered?
- **Data to pull:** the rescreen queue's routing configuration and the timestamps of when each read was entered.

### A discordance between the cytotechnologist's screening category and the pathologist's final sign-out on the same case
- **Usually means:** a genuinely difficult morphologic call more often than a screener competence issue — but the direction of the discordance matters.
- **First question:** did the discordance move toward a more severe category (missed finding, higher-risk direction) or a less severe one (over-call)?
- **Data to pull:** the case's discordance log entry with both categories and the pathologist's stated rationale.

### Non-gynecologic ROSE repeatedly calls adequacy from general smear cellularity rather than confirming the specific target cell type
- **Usually means:** a shortcut under time pressure at the bedside rather than a knowledge gap — the general impression is faster to form than a target-specific check.
- **First question:** for the cases in question, was the target lesion's specific cell type (e.g., follicular epithelium for a thyroid nodule) explicitly confirmed present, or only "cellular material in general"?
- **Data to pull:** the ROSE adequacy note for each case, checked against the requested clinical question.

### A specimen's endocervical/transformation zone component is repeatedly reported "absent" for the same ordering clinician's patients
- **Usually means:** a collection-technique issue at the point of care (device, timing relative to menopause/hysterectomy status) rather than a screening error.
- **First question:** is the absence pattern specific to one collector, one device, or one patient population (e.g., postmenopausal)?
- **Data to pull:** the TZ-component-absent rate broken out by ordering clinician or collection site over a quarter.

### CAP PAP Program proficiency test result shows a missed high-grade or malignant slide
- **Usually means:** an individual competency gap on that specific morphologic pattern needing targeted remediation, not a global screening failure.
- **First question:** was the missed pattern one this screener has seen infrequently in actual case volume, or a common pattern they should recognize reliably?
- **Data to pull:** the screener's proficiency test history and their actual case-mix exposure to that specific finding over the past year.

### A single case is re-categorized more than once before final sign-out
- **Usually means:** genuine morphologic ambiguity warranting the extra look — but repeated recategorization without a documented reason each time is a record-keeping gap.
- **First question:** is each recategorization tied to a specific new observation or consult, or is the case just being re-read hoping for a different answer?
- **Data to pull:** the case's full audit trail of category changes with the stated basis for each.
