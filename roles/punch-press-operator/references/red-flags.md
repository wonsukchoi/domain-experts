# Red flags

### A safeguard (light curtain, guard, two-hand control) defeated, blocked, or bypassed for any reason
- **Usually means:** a shortcut is being taken for speed or convenience, creating catastrophic injury risk regardless of how routine the task seems.
- **First question:** is there any legitimate reason this safeguard should not be active right now, or is it being bypassed for convenience?
- **Data to pull:** the press's safe-access procedure for the task being performed, current safeguard status.

### Excessive burr or a torn/ragged cut edge appearing on parts
- **Usually means:** die clearance is set incorrectly for the current material, not necessarily tool wear.
- **First question:** does current die clearance match the specification for this material's type and thickness?
- **Data to pull:** the die's clearance chart, current material specification, actual clearance setting.

### A progressive die producing an intermittent, worsening misalignment between stations
- **Usually means:** feed pitch drift, not tooling wear at the station where the misalignment appears.
- **First question:** has feed pitch been directly measured, or is the diagnosis focused only on the station showing the visible symptom?
- **Data to pull:** current feed pitch measurement vs. spec, feed roller/system condition.

### A die protection sensor alarm bypassed, reset repeatedly, or disabled to keep production running
- **Usually means:** the underlying fault condition (misfeed, retained part, foreign material) is being ignored rather than investigated.
- **First question:** what is this specific alarm indicating, and has that condition actually been resolved?
- **Data to pull:** die protection sensor alarm log, fault condition history for this die.

### A press running a new material thickness or hardness on a die without re-verifying tonnage requirement
- **Usually means:** an assumption that the die's prior tonnage requirement still applies, without checking whether the material change shifted it.
- **First question:** has required tonnage been recalculated for this specific material, or is the press running on the prior job's tonnage assumption?
- **Data to pull:** required tonnage calculation for the current material, press's rated capacity, prior job's material specification for comparison.

### An operator reaching into or near the point of operation during normal press cycling
- **Usually means:** a jam clear, adjustment, or inspection is being done without following the designated safe-access procedure.
- **First question:** is the press in its safe-access mode (locked out or single-stroke with guard intact), or still in normal cycling mode?
- **Data to pull:** press mode/status at the time of the observed access, the facility's safe-access procedure for this task.

### A part showing an incomplete or partial shear that "looks" acceptable
- **Usually means:** possible undersized tonnage for the operation, producing a defect that isn't immediately obvious.
- **First question:** does the cut/form show full material separation/formation through the material's actual thickness, or a partial condition?
- **Data to pull:** cross-section or edge inspection of a sample part, tonnage verification for this operation.

### Die clearance left at the setting from a prior job without verification for the current job's material
- **Usually means:** a setup step was skipped, assuming clearance doesn't need to change between jobs.
- **First question:** does the current job's material match the prior job's material and thickness exactly, or has it changed?
- **Data to pull:** current and prior job's material specifications, current actual clearance setting.

### Feed pitch checked only at job start, with no periodic re-verification during a long production run
- **Usually means:** a gradual drift (from wear, mechanical looseness) developing mid-run wouldn't be caught until a downstream defect appears.
- **First question:** how long has this run been going since the last feed pitch verification?
- **Data to pull:** feed pitch check log/frequency for this run, current run duration.

### A near-miss involving the point of operation not formally reported
- **Usually means:** the event was resolved without injury and treated as not worth documenting, losing the pattern-detection value of near-miss reporting.
- **First question:** does the near-miss reporting system show an entry for this event?
- **Data to pull:** near-miss reporting log, verbal accounts if no formal report exists.
