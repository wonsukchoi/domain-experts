# General Dentist — Playbook

## Referral-threshold table

| Finding | In-house threshold | Refer when |
|---|---|---|
| Periodontal pocket depth | ≤5mm, first-time finding | >5mm, or non-surgical SRP already failed at reevaluation |
| Third-molar impaction | Vertical or mesioangular, adequate access | Horizontal/distoangular impaction, or root proximity to inferior alveolar nerve on panoramic |
| Endodontic case | Single, straight canal, standard anatomy | Calcified canal, curved/multiple canals, retreatment case, or open apex |
| Restorative structure loss | Composite up to ~50% crown loss | Full-coverage crown or larger past ~50%; consider prosthodontist for multi-unit/complex occlusal cases |
| Orthodontic malocclusion | Minor spacing/crowding, adult limited treatment | Skeletal discrepancy, complex bite correction — orthodontist |

## Radiographic diagnostic-yield chart

| Radiograph type | Best for | Limited for |
|---|---|---|
| Bitewing | Interproximal caries, alveolar crest bone level | Root anatomy, periapical pathology, full arch survey |
| Periapical | Root anatomy, periapical lesions, single-tooth pathology | Interproximal caries screening across multiple teeth |
| Panoramic | Third-molar position, jaw pathology, full-arch overview | Fine interproximal caries detection (lower resolution) |

## Case-sequencing decision sequence (filled example)

1. Chief complaint present? → Rule out emergent infection/irreversible pulpitis first.
2. Comprehensive exam findings sorted into urgency tiers (see table above).
3. Cross-check each finding against referral-threshold table — anything past threshold goes on the referral list, not the treatment plan.
4. Remaining findings phased: disease control → restorative → elective, batched into visits.
5. Cost presented per phase, informed consent obtained per phase before scheduling the next.
