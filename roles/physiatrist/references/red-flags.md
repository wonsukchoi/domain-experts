### New headache and blood pressure ≥20–30 mmHg above baseline in an SCI patient at or above T6
- **Usually means:** autonomic dysreflexia from a bladder or bowel stimulus; second most likely: a skin-level stimulus (pressure injury, ingrown toenail, tight clothing).
- **First question:** when was the catheter or bag last checked, and when was the last bowel movement?
- **Data to pull:** bladder scan or catheter patency check, digital rectal exam for impaction, skin check below the level of injury.

### FIM efficiency tracking more than 20–30% below the unit's benchmark by day 4
- **Usually means:** an undiagnosed reversible cause (pain, depression, sedating medication); second: the benchmark itself doesn't fit this patient's comorbidity profile.
- **First question:** has a depression screen (e.g., PHQ-9) been done since admission, and what's changed on the medication list?
- **Data to pull:** current medication list flagged for sedating agents, pain scores at therapy sessions, PHQ-9 or equivalent.

### Sudden increase in spasticity or a new spasm pattern in a patient with a stable prior tone exam
- **Usually means:** a new noxious stimulus below the level of injury/lesion (UTI, pressure injury, fracture, ingrown toenail); second: medication non-adherence (baclofen, tizanidine) or pump malfunction if on intrathecal therapy.
- **First question:** any new fever, skin breakdown, or missed medication/pump refill?
- **Data to pull:** urinalysis, skin exam, pump log/refill history if applicable, plain films if fracture is suspected.

### A joint that's warm, swollen, and increasingly resistant to passive range of motion in a patient with a recent SCI or TBI
- **Usually means:** heterotopic ossification; second: septic arthritis or DVT.
- **First question:** how many weeks post-injury, and is there a fever?
- **Data to pull:** alkaline phosphatase trend, triple-phase bone scan (more sensitive early) or plain films (later), joint aspiration if infection is in the differential.

### Unilateral leg swelling, warmth, or calf tenderness in an immobilized or SCI patient
- **Usually means:** deep vein thrombosis; second: cellulitis or heterotopic ossification.
- **First question:** what's the current chemoprophylaxis regimen and when was the last dose?
- **Data to pull:** compression ultrasound, D-dimer (limited value in this population — imaging preferred), current anticoagulation status.

### A stage 3 or unstageable pressure injury discovered at a routine skin check
- **Usually means:** a positioning/offloading/turning-schedule failure; second: nutritional deficit impairing tissue tolerance.
- **First question:** what was the most recent Braden score, and is the turning schedule actually being followed?
- **Data to pull:** Braden score trend, wound measurements and staging per NPUAP criteria, prealbumin/albumin, current support surface and turning documentation.

### A TBI patient's agitation escalating when the team increases orientation questions or task demands
- **Usually means:** the patient is at Rancho level III–IV (confused-agitated) and is being overstimulated, not under-stimulated; second: an untreated pain source driving the agitation.
- **First question:** what is the current Rancho level, and has it actually been reassessed this week?
- **Data to pull:** current Rancho Los Amigos level, pain assessment (may need a behavioral pain scale if the patient can't self-report), review of environmental stimulation.

### A first seizure occurring more than 7 days after a traumatic brain injury
- **Usually means:** late post-traumatic epilepsy, which carries a materially different recurrence risk and management path than an early seizure; second: a new structural lesion (delayed hemorrhage, hydrocephalus).
- **First question:** was this an early (≤7 days) or late (>7 days) post-traumatic seizure?
- **Data to pull:** repeat CT/MRI to rule out a new structural cause, EEG, review of seizure-prophylaxis history.

### A prosthetic-limb patient reporting a new area of skin breakdown at the socket interface
- **Usually means:** volume change in the residual limb (edema fluctuation or atrophy) causing socket misfit; second: a gait deviation transferring abnormal load to one area.
- **First question:** has the patient's weight or residual-limb volume changed recently, and how many prosthetic socks are they currently using?
- **Data to pull:** socket fit check with the prosthetist, residual-limb volume/circumference measurement, gait observation for compensatory patterns.

### An IRF patient plateauing at a FIM level below what's needed for the planned discharge disposition, with a discharge date approaching
- **Usually means:** either a reversible medical/psychological barrier hasn't been found yet, or the discharge disposition itself needs to change (e.g., home with more support, or a different level of care); second: the original functional ceiling projection was optimistic for this patient's comorbidity burden.
- **First question:** has every reversible cause on the standard plateau differential (pain, depression, medication, undiagnosed infection, sleep disruption) actually been checked, not just considered?
- **Data to pull:** current FIM trend by item (not just total), medication list, PHQ-9, sleep/pain logs, family/caregiver capacity reassessment.
