# Red flags — what a management analyst notices instantly

Format per flag: the signal (with a threshold) → what it usually means → the first question a veteran asks → the data to pull. Any one of these can be innocent; two or three together in the same engagement is a pattern.

### Manager span of control below 4 direct reports in standardized/transactional work
- **Usually means:** a coordination-only layer that should be delayered, or a manager promoted for tenure rather than a genuine increase in span-worthy complexity.
- **First question:** "What decision does this role make that the layer above or below can't make without them?"
- **Data to pull:** org chart with span by role, a week of the role's calendar/decision log, the layer's actual sign-off authority vs. its stated authority.

### A "reorganization" that changes titles and reporting lines but not headcount or span
- **Usually means:** the redesign avoided the hard conversation (who actually goes) and produced a chart that looks different with a P&L that doesn't.
- **First question:** "Show me the FTE count and average span before and after — not just the boxes."
- **Data to pull:** before/after headcount by cost center, before/after span-of-control by layer.

### Savings estimate presented as a single "efficiency" or "synergy" line with no FTE or spend category named
- **Usually means:** the number was backed into to hit a target, not built bottom-up — it will not survive the CFO's business-case review.
- **First question:** "Which specific role or invoice line does this dollar come off of?"
- **Data to pull:** the savings bridge by named cost center, cross-checked against the current-year budget for that center.

### Three or more sites running "the same" back-office process with materially different cycle times (e.g., 22 min vs. 6 min per transaction)
- **Usually means:** one site has an unused capability (automation, a licensed module) the others don't, or the process isn't actually standardized despite being called the same name everywhere.
- **First question:** "Walk me through this process at each site, step by step, with a stopwatch — where does the time difference actually happen?"
- **Data to pull:** SIPOC or value-stream map per site, transaction-level cycle-time data, system/license inventory by site.

### A RACI where more than one role is "Accountable" on the same decision, or none are
- **Usually means:** the redesign never actually resolved who decides — it will re-litigate the first time the decision comes up for real.
- **First question:** "If these two people disagree tomorrow, whose call is it?"
- **Data to pull:** draft RACI, the last 3 instances of this decision type and who actually made the call each time.

### Headcount reduction target hit by applying the same percentage cut to every function
- **Usually means:** nobody diagnosed which functions are genuinely redundant vs. already lean; the lean function gets damaged and the redundant one is undershot.
- **First question:** "What's the baseline FTE-to-workload ratio for each function, benchmarked against sites doing comparable volume?"
- **Data to pull:** FTE by function by site, transaction/output volume by function, external benchmark ratios where available.

### A shared-services consolidation proposed across sites whose underlying processes or systems are not actually the same
- **Usually means:** the consolidation will re-add headcount at the new location within a year once the "one process" turns out to be three, or service quality degrades for the site with the different process.
- **First question:** "Which of these three sites' processes would have to change for one center to run all of them — and has anyone signed up to make that change?"
- **Data to pull:** process maps per site, ERP/system inventory per site, any prior consolidation attempt and why it reverted.

### Run-rate savings quoted without a Year-1 (in-year) number next to it
- **Usually means:** the business case is hiding the ramp — notice periods, transition timelines, and phased rollout mean Year 1 is routinely 50-70% of run-rate, and a board that hears only run-rate gets blindsided by the first-year actuals.
- **First question:** "What percentage of this run-rate number actually lands in the P&L this fiscal year?"
- **Data to pull:** implementation timeline with go-live dates by workstream, notice-period requirements by jurisdiction/contract, phased savings schedule.

### Implementation cost (severance, systems, change management) missing or under 10% of the quoted run-rate savings
- **Usually means:** the one-time cost was estimated late or optimistically to make the payback look better than it is — real org-redesign implementation cost typically runs 30-60% of Year-1 run-rate savings.
- **First question:** "Walk me through severance by role, systems cost, and change-management cost line by line — what's not in this number yet?"
- **Data to pull:** severance policy and headcount-by-tenure mix, systems/ERP integration quotes, change-management/communications budget from prior engagements of comparable scope.

### A recommendation that touches a stated exclusion (union labor, safety-critical, regulatory floor) to close the last few percent of the target
- **Usually means:** the team is padding the number to please the sponsor rather than telling them the honest achievable total.
- **First question:** "Is this inside or outside the constraint the sponsor gave us in week one — and did we get it re-opened explicitly, or did we just quietly cross it?"
- **Data to pull:** the original engagement scope/constraints memo, sign-off trail for any scope change.
