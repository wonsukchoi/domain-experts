# Red Flags

Smell tests for TA-run sections and grading. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### Grade-appeal rate above ~15% on one assignment in one section, under ~5% elsewhere

- **Usually means:** that section's TA is grading the rubric as written while other sections are grading it more leniently — the "harsh" section is frequently the accurate one, not the outlier.
- **First question:** "Which specific rubric line do the appeals cluster on?"
- **Data to pull:** a blind cross-section sample (8-10 papers per section, IDs hidden) regraded independently against the written rubric criteria.

### Same 2-3 students account for most verbal responses across multiple weeks

- **Usually means:** the section is running on an open-floor call pattern that reselects for already-confident students, not that the rest of the section has nothing to say.
- **First question:** "Is anyone being cold-called, or is every answer volunteered?"
- **Data to pull:** the section's roster call-log (who's been called on this term) against the exit-ticket or homework performance of students who never speak.

### Office hours draw 0-1 students weekly in a class of 100+

- **Usually means:** self-selection — the students most likely to attend are the ones who need it least, and the students who need it are filtered out by stigma, scheduling, or not knowing what to ask.
- **First question:** "Are office hours framed as remedial help, or as a normal part of doing the class?"
- **Data to pull:** office-hours sign-up log against the grade distribution of students who've never attended.

### A student discloses harassment, assault, discrimination, or a safety concern

- **Usually means:** this is a Title IX/CARE-team reportable event, not a private confidence the TA gets to sit on.
- **First question:** "Have I reported this to the Title IX office today, separate from however I respond to the student personally?"
- **Data to pull:** the institution's Title IX reporting form/contact and the "responsible employee" policy for the TA's specific role.

### Logged hours exceed the contracted appointment by >20% for 2+ consecutive weeks

- **Usually means:** the workload (grading volume, section size, exam-writing support) was underscoped at the start of the term, and it will recur every term unless flagged.
- **First question:** "Is this a one-time spike (midterm week) or the new steady state?"
- **Data to pull:** a week-by-week hours log against the contracted cap, itemized by task (grading vs. section-leading vs. office hours vs. exam support).

### Two sections of the same course post final grade distributions differing by roughly half a letter grade or more, with no difference in incoming student profile

- **Usually means:** unnormed grading drift between TAs, not a real difference in section performance.
- **First question:** "Was there a norming session before the final exam/paper was graded, and did every TA attend it?"
- **Data to pull:** a blind cross-TA regrade sample on the same final assessment.

### An international or multilingual TA's section shows declining attendance week over week while parallel sections hold steady

- **Usually means:** a communication or comprehension gap in section delivery that students are voting on with their feet, not that the material itself is harder in that section.
- **First question:** "Has anyone sat in on this TA's section, and what specifically is different about the delivery?"
- **Data to pull:** attendance trend by section over the term; results of any oral-English certification (e.g., SPEAK/TSE-style test) on file for the TA.

### Every regrade request from a section is for exactly +1 or +2 points, never a full re-argue of the answer

- **Usually means:** students have identified a specific rubric line that's being scored inconsistently and are exploiting the margin, not that the section as a whole disputes its grades.
- **First question:** "Which single rubric criterion do the +1/+2 requests concentrate on?"
- **Data to pull:** regrade-request log filtered by which rubric line was contested.

### Adjacent seats submit near-identical wrong-answer patterns on a proctored exam

- **Usually means:** collaboration or a shared incorrect source (a bad study guide, a leaked draft) — order of likelihood depends on whether the error is a rare one (points to collaboration) or a common one (points to a shared bad source).
- **First question:** "Is this error rare enough that independent students landing on it by chance is implausible?"
- **Data to pull:** seating chart, the specific wrong answer text from each exam, and whether the same error appears in any circulating study materials.

### Supervising faculty gives verbal-only answers to grading or policy edge cases

- **Usually means:** the policy will be remembered differently by each TA who heard it, and will look arbitrary the first time two students compare notes across sections.
- **First question:** "Can I get this in a one-line email so there's a record every TA can point to?"
- **Data to pull:** the syllabus and any existing written policy doc, to check whether the edge case is actually already covered.
