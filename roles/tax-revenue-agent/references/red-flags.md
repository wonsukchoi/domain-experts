### Unreported income is close to but hasn't been checked against the 25%-of-gross-income threshold
- **Usually means:** The examiner may be applying the wrong statute of limitations period without realizing an extended 6-year window could apply (or, if incorrectly assumed, might be treating a case as open when it's actually time-barred).
- **First question:** What is the exact ratio of unreported income to gross income reported on the return, and does it cross the 25% threshold?
- **Data to pull:** Reconstructed unreported income figure, gross income as originally reported.

### A fraud penalty is being proposed based on evidence that would only satisfy a preponderance standard
- **Usually means:** The evidence may not meet the clear-and-convincing standard civil fraud requires, risking the penalty being overturned even if the underlying deficiency is valid.
- **First question:** Which specific badges of fraud are present, and would this evidence hold up under a clear-and-convincing standard, not just "more likely than not"?
- **Data to pull:** Documented badges of fraud (concealment, implausible explanations, destroyed records, pattern of underreporting).

### A deduction is disallowed entirely due to missing documentation, with no Cohan estimate considered
- **Usually means:** If credible evidence suggests the expense plausibly occurred, a full disallowance may not be legally supportable — an estimate may be required instead.
- **First question:** Is there partial documentation, industry-comparable data, or other credible evidence suggesting the expense occurred in some amount?
- **Data to pull:** Any partial receipts, industry benchmarks, or circumstantial evidence supporting a reasonable estimate.

### A Cohan estimate is based entirely on the taxpayer's proposed figure with no independent derivation
- **Usually means:** The estimate may not be defensible if it wasn't independently bounded using some objective basis (partial records, comparable data).
- **First question:** What independent basis (beyond the taxpayer's own assertion) supports the specific dollar amount being allowed?
- **Data to pull:** Any partial substantiation, comparable industry/occupation data used to bound the estimate.

### An installment agreement or offer in compromise is being evaluated using only the taxpayer's proposed payment amount
- **Usually means:** The proposal hasn't been tested against an independent reasonable collection potential (RCP) calculation, risking acceptance of a below-potential settlement.
- **First question:** Has RCP been independently calculated (assets plus future income capacity), and how does it compare to the proposed amount?
- **Data to pull:** Taxpayer's asset and income documentation, independent RCP calculation.

### A lien or levy is being issued without confirmation that all required notices were sent in sequence
- **Usually means:** The collection action may be procedurally defective and subject to reversal, regardless of whether the underlying tax debt is valid.
- **First question:** Have all required notices (including the Collection Due Process notice and its waiting period) been confirmed sent and elapsed in the correct order?
- **Data to pull:** Notice issuance log with dates, required waiting periods per notice type.

### Records are described as "poor" or "incomplete" and treated as evidence of fraud without further analysis
- **Usually means:** Poor recordkeeping alone doesn't establish fraud — it needs to be distinguished from negligence using the specific badges-of-fraud criteria before elevating to a fraud determination.
- **First question:** Beyond incomplete records, is there evidence of concealment, implausible explanations, or a deliberate pattern, or does this look like ordinary negligence?
- **Data to pull:** Full badges-of-fraud checklist assessment, not just the recordkeeping quality alone.

### A statute of limitations determination doesn't account for whether the return was ever filed
- **Usually means:** If no return was filed for the year in question, the statute of limitations doesn't begin to run at all — this is a distinct, unlimited-period scenario from the standard 3-year or extended 6-year cases.
- **First question:** Was a return actually filed for this tax year, or is this a non-filer situation?
- **Data to pull:** Filing history/records for the tax year in question.
