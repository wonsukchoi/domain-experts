## Headcount protocol

1. Assign each group member a visible number or pairing at the tour's start (verbal count for small adult groups; clicker counter for groups over ~15; buddy-pairing for children's/school groups).
2. Count at every scheduled transition point (leaving each stop) and at any unscheduled high-crowd-density moment.
3. Count twice if the number doesn't match the last confirmed count — a miscount is more common than a missing person, but both get treated as real until the recount confirms otherwise.
4. If a person is confirmed missing: stop the group at the last stop, do not continue moving, and initiate the venue's or route's missing-person protocol (radio to other guides, venue security, or the group's tour operator) rather than searching alone while the rest of the group is unsupervised.

## Contingency plans (filled example)

| Disruption | Trigger | Fallback | Communication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rain during Stop 5 (viewpoint) | Steady rain, no cover at viewpoint | Move to covered arcade below viewpoint; deliver same narrative content with the view described rather than shown | "The viewpoint's exposed to this rain, so we're moving under cover — I'll describe what we'd be seeing from up there." |
| Stop 3 venue (market) unexpectedly closed | Locked gate / closure sign on arrival | Skip to Stop 4, deliver Stop 3's key narrative content as a walking commentary en route | "The market's closed today — I'll cover what makes it worth knowing about as we walk toward the next stop instead." |
| Group pace 20%+ slower than planned by Stop 3 | Headcount-point time check shows cumulative deficit | Trim Stop 3 and Stop 5 narrative per the "protect the final stop" heuristic; re-check pace at Stop 4 | See SKILL.md worked example for the reconciliation math. |
| Severe weather (lightning, extreme heat advisory) | Official weather alert for the route's area | End tour early at the nearest safe indoor point; do not attempt to reach Stop 6 | "For everyone's safety we're ending the tour here rather than continuing to the final stop — here's what you're missing and how to see it on your own." |

## Contingency-announcement template (structure)

1. State what's happening (one sentence, factual, no apology-first framing).
2. State the new plan (where the group is going, what changes).
3. Offer what's preserved or how the loss is mitigated (a description in place of a visit, a self-guided alternative, protected time elsewhere).
4. Invite questions only after 1–3 are delivered — a question mid-announcement fragments the group's attention before they know the plan.
