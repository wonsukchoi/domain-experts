# Tour Guide — Red Flags

### Headcount mismatch by even one person, at any transition point
- **Usually means:** A group member fell behind, ducked into a shop, or is in a restroom — occasionally means someone is genuinely lost or separated.
- **First question:** "Who was standing next to [missing person] most recently, and where?"
- **Data to pull:** The last confirmed headcount and location; any group members who can place the missing person's last-seen point.

### Cumulative time deficit exceeding 15% of the tour's total planned length by the halfway transition point
- **Usually means:** Group pace is genuinely slower than planned, or an earlier stop ran significantly over without being caught and corrected.
- **First question:** "Is this a one-time overrun at a single stop, or a pace deficit that's been compounding since the start?"
- **Data to pull:** Stop-by-stop actual vs. planned duration from the route-timing sheet.

### A group member showing visible heat exhaustion, dehydration, or mobility distress
- **Usually means:** Pace or weather conditions have exceeded that individual's capacity — a safety issue, not a schedule issue.
- **First question:** "Can this person continue at a slower pace, or does the tour need to pause/end early for them?"
- **Data to pull:** Time since the last water/rest break; current weather conditions (heat index, direct sun exposure).

### Official weather alert (lightning, extreme heat, flash flood) issued for the route's area mid-tour
- **Usually means:** The pre-planned contingency (shelter, early ending) needs to be executed now, not evaluated.
- **First question:** "Is the nearest safe point the pre-planned fallback, or has the alert made that fallback unsafe too?"
- **Data to pull:** The alert's specific guidance (seek shelter vs. evacuate) and the route's nearest matching safe point.

### A venue or stop is closed or inaccessible on arrival with no advance notice
- **Usually means:** A scheduling change on the venue's side that wasn't communicated to the tour operator — happens more often at smaller or seasonal venues.
- **First question:** "Is there a same-day reopening possible, or does this route permanently need an alternate stop going forward?"
- **Data to pull:** Posted closure signage/reason if available; whether the tour operator has a standing alternate-stop arrangement.

### Group member repeatedly wandering from the group or ignoring stated boundaries
- **Usually means:** Low engagement, unfamiliarity with group-tour norms, or — in a mixed-age group — a child not being actively supervised by their accompanying adult.
- **First question:** "Is this person aware of the headcount/boundary system, and do they understand why it matters here specifically (traffic, crowd density, restricted area)?"
- **Data to pull:** Whether boundaries were stated clearly at tour start; specific hazard present at the current location (traffic, water, restricted zone).

### A guide finds themselves improvising the response to a disruption rather than executing a known plan
- **Usually means:** Either this disruption type wasn't anticipated in the pre-built contingency plan, or the guide didn't review the plan before this run of the route.
- **First question:** "Is there genuinely no applicable pre-built fallback, or is one available and just not being used?"
- **Data to pull:** The route's contingency-plan document, checked before defaulting to improvisation.

### Narrative content running significantly longer than planned at an early stop with no corresponding group engagement signal
- **Usually means:** The guide is filling time with prepared content rather than reading the group, risking a schedule deficit for no engagement benefit.
- **First question:** "Is the group asking questions or showing engagement, or is this overrun coming from the guide's own script?"
- **Data to pull:** Stop duration against planned time; observable engagement signals (questions asked, phone use, eye contact).

### A group's composition changes mid-tour (someone joins late, a member leaves early) without an updated headcount baseline
- **Usually means:** The headcount protocol's reference number is now stale and the next count will appear to mismatch even though nothing is actually wrong.
- **First question:** "Has the headcount baseline been explicitly updated to reflect the new group size?"
- **Data to pull:** Time and circumstance of the composition change; confirmation from the person who joined/left (not just an assumption).
