# Artifacts

Filled templates for the three documents a central bank governor produces most: the policy statement, the emergency lending facility term sheet, and the reaction-function communication.

## Policy statement (filled example, split decision)

```markdown
# Policy Statement — March meeting

**Rate decision:** The Committee raises the target range for the policy rate by
25 basis points, to 5.25%–5.50%. Recent indicators show inflation remaining
elevated at 4.2% year-over-year against the Committee's 2% objective, while
the labor market remains near maximum employment with unemployment at 3.8%.
The Committee remains highly attentive to inflation risks and will continue
to assess incoming data in determining the extent of further policy firming
that may be appropriate.

**Financial stability note:** The Committee is aware of recent liquidity
strain at select depository institutions. This rate decision is separate
from and unrelated to those events, which are being addressed through the
liquidity facility described below. The banking system remains sound and
resilient in aggregate.

**Reaction function:** Future rate decisions will depend on the totality of
incoming data, including labor market conditions, inflation pressures, and
inflation expectations, and on the balance of risks. The Committee does not
have a preset path.
```

## Emergency lending facility term sheet (filled example)

```markdown
# Term Sheet — Bank Liquidity Support Facility

**Purpose:** Provide liquidity to depository institutions facing deposit
outflows against sound, high-quality collateral, to prevent forced asset
sales that would realize losses beyond what solvent institutions can absorb.

**Eligible borrowers:** Federally insured depository institutions in good
regulatory standing (not currently subject to resolution proceedings).

**Eligible collateral:** U.S. Treasury securities, agency debt, and agency
mortgage-backed securities owned by the borrower as of [date].

**Valuation:** Collateral valued at par, not current market value — this is
the specific design choice that distinguishes a liquidity facility from a
market bailout: it addresses the mismatch between held-to-maturity book
value and forced-sale market value, without absorbing credit losses on
genuinely impaired assets.

**Advance term:** Up to 12 months, renewable at the Board's discretion.

**Rate:** 1-year overnight index swap rate + 10 basis points — a modest
premium over market funding cost, consistent with Bagehot's penalty-rate
principle, without being punitive enough to discourage legitimate use during
a systemic liquidity event.

**Explicitly excluded:** Institutions determined by the primary regulator to
be insolvent (liabilities exceeding assets at fair value) are not eligible;
those cases proceed through standard resolution authority, not this facility.
```

## Reaction-function communication (filled example, press conference excerpt)

```markdown
Q: "Does today's hike mean you're done raising rates?"

A: "We're not on a preset path. We'll be looking at the totality of incoming
data — inflation readings, labor market conditions, and how financial
conditions are evolving. If we see inflation continuing to move toward our
2% objective in a sustained way, and if the labor market shows signs of
better balance between supply and demand, that would inform a shift in our
approach. Absent that, we should assume policy remains restrictive for as
long as it takes to see that sustained progress. I'd rather be clear that
this is data-dependent than commit to a specific number of further moves,
because committing to a number now and having to walk it back later does
more damage to our credibility than staying data-dependent from the start."
```

Rule applied throughout: state the *condition* that would change the path, not
a date or a fixed number of future moves — conditionality is what lets guidance
flex with reality without becoming a broken promise.
