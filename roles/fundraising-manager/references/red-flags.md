# Red flags — what a fundraiser notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A grant evaluated by headline dollar amount with no check of the implied per-unit cost against actual program cost
- **Usually means:** the grant may fund less than it appears to once the organization's real cost-per-unit is applied to the funder's assumed scale — a hidden funding gap.
- **First question:** "What's our actual cost per unit (per client, per program), and does the grant's scale assumption match what that actually costs to deliver?"
- **Data to pull:** current program cost and unit count, grant's stated or implied target scale, resulting cost-per-unit comparison.

### An overhead/administrative cost reimbursement cap in a grant that's below the organization's actual overhead rate
- **Usually means:** accepting the grant creates an unfunded overhead gap that has to be covered by unrestricted funds, a real cost not visible in the grant's headline figure.
- **First question:** "What's our actual overhead rate on this program, and does the grant's admin cap cover it?"
- **Data to pull:** organization's actual overhead rate, grant's stated administrative cost cap or allowance.

### Total unrestricted-fund exposure from a new grant/gift exceeding roughly 50-60% of current uncommitted unrestricted fundraising capacity
- **Usually means:** accepting this commitment is a major strategic tradeoff against other organizational flexibility, not a straightforward "yes" to more funding.
- **First question:** "What percentage of our current unrestricted capacity would this commitment consume, and is that a deliberate tradeoff we're making?"
- **Data to pull:** estimated annual unrestricted-fund gap this grant/gift would create, current uncommitted unrestricted fundraising capacity.

### A major-gift solicitation planned with no documented cultivation history for that specific prospect
- **Usually means:** the ask is likely to read as transactional rather than relationship-based, reducing its likelihood of success and risking the relationship for future cultivation.
- **First question:** "What's the documented cultivation sequence (moves) for this prospect so far, and is this ask timed appropriately in that sequence?"
- **Data to pull:** donor CRM cultivation/moves history for the prospect, time elapsed since last meaningful engagement.

### A donor or cohort that hasn't received any stewardship/impact communication since their last gift, approaching the next solicitation cycle
- **Usually means:** the donor has no reason to give again if they haven't learned what their previous gift accomplished — a preventable lapse risk.
- **First question:** "When did this donor last receive a specific impact update, and is it scheduled before the next ask?"
- **Data to pull:** stewardship communication log by donor/cohort, timing relative to the next planned solicitation.

### Even cultivation time allocation across the donor base regardless of documented giving capacity
- **Usually means:** scarce relationship-building time may be spread thin across low-leverage segments instead of concentrated on the smaller number of donors who provide most revenue.
- **First question:** "Does our cultivation time allocation match our donor pyramid, or is it roughly even regardless of capacity?"
- **Data to pull:** cultivation time/contact logs by donor segment, donor capacity/giving-history segmentation.

### An impact claim in fundraising materials that isn't traceable to specific program outcome data
- **Usually means:** the claim may be an overstatement that risks real reputational damage if a donor or funder checks it against actual outcomes.
- **First question:** "What specific data supports this impact claim, and could it withstand a funder's direct follow-up question?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific claim's source data, program outcome measurement methodology.

### A fundraising campaign evaluated only on total dollars raised for the current period, with no retention or donor-lifetime-value data
- **Usually means:** a campaign that raises a lot in the short term but burns out or alienates donors may be a worse long-term outcome than a smaller, more sustainable campaign — and this wouldn't show up in a total-raised metric alone.
- **First question:** "What's this campaign's projected effect on donor retention for the next cycle, not just this period's total?"
- **Data to pull:** current campaign's donor retention/renewal rate projection or comparison to a prior comparable campaign's actual retention outcome.
