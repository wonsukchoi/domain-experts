# Clinical/Counseling Psychologist — Red Flags

### Full Scale IQ requested when index scores span more than 23 points (1.5 SD)
- **Usually means:** the referral source doesn't know index-score reporting conventions, or is using FSIQ as an eligibility gate without understanding what it obscures.
- **First question:** what decision does this number need to make — does it actually require one global score, or would the index profile answer it better?
- **Data to pull:** the four (or five) index scaled-score table with confidence intervals.

### Self-report validity scale elevated (e.g. MMPI-3 F-r T-score >=80)
- **Usually means:** symptom over-reporting/exaggeration, an external incentive (litigation, disability, custody), or genuine acute severe distress.
- **First question:** is there a secondary-gain context (legal claim, benefit application, custody dispute) that would motivate over-reporting?
- **Data to pull:** collateral records, referral source, and any Performance/Symptom Validity Test results from the same session.

### Diagnosis assigned from a single elevated scale with no second independent measure
- **Usually means:** anchoring on the first salient finding instead of running the convergence check.
- **First question:** is there a second, independently-collected measure (different method, not just a different scale on the same test) pointing the same direction?
- **Data to pull:** the cross-method concordance table for this construct across all administered instruments.

### Self-report and performance-based measures of the same construct diverge by more than 1 SD
- **Usually means:** limited self-insight, or a performance-validity problem on the objective measure.
- **First question:** did the performance-based measure pass its embedded or standalone validity check?
- **Data to pull:** PVT/SVT pass-fail result for that testing session.

### Two clinicians reach different diagnoses from the same raw data
- **Usually means:** the referral question was never made explicit, or one clinician weighted collateral/history differently than the other.
- **First question:** what specific data point did each clinician treat as decisive?
- **Data to pull:** the raw protocol and both clinicians' formulation notes side by side.

### Adult-onset inattention complaint with no childhood history of symptoms or accommodations
- **Usually means:** anxiety, depression, sleep disorder, or a medical cause is more likely than adult-onset ADHD, which DSM-5-TR requires childhood-onset evidence for.
- **First question:** is there a parent/teacher report or school record establishing (or ruling out) childhood-onset symptoms?
- **Data to pull:** developmental history interview, any available school records.

### Client explicitly requests the report support a pending legal or disability claim
- **Usually means:** elevated risk that self-report will be shaped by the claim's incentive structure, whether consciously or not.
- **First question:** was a standalone Performance/Symptom Validity Test administered, independent of the self-report instruments?
- **Data to pull:** PVT/SVT results, base rate of invalid profiles in this specific referral context (forensic/disability literature).

### Test administered in a language or cultural context the norming sample doesn't represent
- **Usually means:** scores may not be valid — norms assume a reference population the client doesn't belong to.
- **First question:** does the test publisher offer a normed, adapted version for this population, and was it used?
- **Data to pull:** the test's technical manual section on normative sample composition.

### Feedback session compressed to under 20 minutes or skipped in favor of a mailed report
- **Usually means:** the client will misunderstand or reject the diagnosis, reducing treatment adherence.
- **First question:** did the client have an opportunity to ask questions about what the diagnosis does and doesn't mean for them?
- **Data to pull:** the feedback session note documenting client questions and understanding check.

### Primary diagnosis doesn't account for every reported symptom cluster
- **Usually means:** an unaddressed comorbidity — common pairs (e.g. anxiety and ADHD) co-occur in roughly a quarter to a third of cases and get missed when the first fitting diagnosis stops the search.
- **First question:** which specific symptoms are left unexplained by the primary diagnosis?
- **Data to pull:** the full symptom-cluster checklist against the chosen diagnosis's criteria set.
