# Red flags

Smell tests a licensed land surveyor catches in the first pass over field evidence, a prior survey, or a title chain.

### Found monument is disturbed, off-vertical, or shows soil disturbance inconsistent with its stated age

- **Usually means:** the monument was moved, replaced, or damaged since original placement — grading, utility work, or a prior surveyor's "correction" pulling it toward the record distance.
- **First question:** does the physical condition (material, age, disturbance pattern) match what the record says was originally set here?
- **Data to pull:** original plat's monument call, prior corner/monument records for this point, adjoining surveys' calls for the same corner.

### Traverse angular misclosure passes, but one course sits alone on a short or obstructed sight line

- **Usually means:** the overall closure looks fine while a single weak observation is hiding inside a proportional adjustment.
- **First question:** what's the individual angle and distance quality at that specific station, independent of the closure check?
- **Data to pull:** raw field observations for that station, instrument setup notes, sight-distance and obstruction log.

### Linear precision ratio computes below the standard for the survey's stated classification

- **Usually means:** a course was measured under conditions (short sight, poor GNSS satellite geometry, disturbed monument) that degraded that one observation below the rest.
- **First question:** which single course, if re-observed, would most plausibly fix the misclosure?
- **Data to pull:** latitude/departure table per course, field conditions log, GNSS PDOP/fix-quality data if RTK was used.

### Record deed's courses and distances don't form a mathematically closed figure on their own

- **Usually means:** the original description itself has an error (a hiatus or gap in the chain of title), not a field measurement problem in the current survey.
- **First question:** does this gap/overlap also appear in the adjoining owner's deed, confirming it's a record defect rather than a transcription error?
- **Data to pull:** full deed chain for the parcel, adjoining owner's deed, any prior boundary-line agreement of record.

### Adjoining owner's deed overlaps the client's deed by a measurable distance

- **Usually means:** a genuine title conflict (double conveyance, scrivener's error, or an unresolved unwritten-rights claim), not something the surveyor can plat around.
- **First question:** does either deed reference a monument or occupation line that predates and controls over both descriptions?
- **Data to pull:** both full deed chains, any recorded boundary-line agreement, occupation evidence (fence, cultivation) along the disputed strip.

### Fence, hedge, or cultivation line diverges from the record boundary by more than roughly a foot, established well past the state's statutory period

- **Usually means:** an unwritten-rights condition (acquiescence, adverse possession, or agreement) may have shifted the effective boundary regardless of the record line.
- **First question:** how long has this occupation line existed, and is there any evidence of an agreement or dispute about it?
- **Data to pull:** aerial imagery history, prior surveys showing the same offset over time, neighbor testimony if available, local statute of limitations for adverse possession.

### PLSS corner restored using single proportion where the corner is actually interior, not on the outer boundary

- **Usually means:** the surveyor defaulted to the simpler single-proportion method without confirming the corner's position within the section grid.
- **First question:** is this corner interior (requiring double proportion using both intersecting lines) or genuinely on the outer township/section boundary?
- **Data to pull:** GLO original plat, section grid diagram, record distances on both the line and the intersecting line.

### Prior survey's stated closed-traverse area differs from the newly computed area by more than roughly 2%

- **Usually means:** a monument was moved between surveys, a course was mis-measured in one of the two surveys, or the record "more or less" acreage was never actually verified by closure.
- **First question:** which specific course or corner accounts for most of the area difference?
- **Data to pull:** both surveys' raw closure data, monument condition notes from each, dates of each survey relative to any known grading or construction.

### ALTA/NSPS Table A item certified without a named, dated source document

- **Usually means:** the item was filled from a cached lookup, a client-supplied sketch, or general knowledge rather than the specific current record required.
- **First question:** what document, dated when, actually supports this specific certification?
- **Data to pull:** current FEMA FIRM panel and effective date (flood), current utility locate ticket or as-built (utilities), current zoning letter (setbacks/classification).

### Client requests corners set before a title commitment or full deed research is complete

- **Usually means:** schedule pressure is being allowed to skip the research step that catches gaps, overlaps, and unwritten-rights issues before they're physically monumented.
- **First question:** has the adjoiner's deed and any title exceptions actually been reconciled against the client's deed yet?
- **Data to pull:** title commitment status, adjoiner deed research status, research checklist completion record.

### RTK GNSS fix accepted with estimated horizontal precision worse than roughly 0.1 ft, no re-observation

- **Usually means:** poor satellite geometry, multipath near a structure or tree line, or a degraded base-station correction was treated as acceptable because the receiver reported "fixed."
- **First question:** what was the actual reported precision and PDOP at the moment of observation, and does it meet the survey's required tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** raw GNSS observation log (precision estimate, PDOP, fix type), base station distance and correction age at time of shot.
