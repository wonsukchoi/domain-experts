# Casting Director Red Flags

### Fewer than ~15 legitimately castable submissions within 48 hours of posting a breakdown
- **Usually means:** the breakdown's ask is too narrow or contradictory (age range crossed with an unrealistic skill combination), or it went out restricted to agents/managers when the role needs a wider open-call net.
- **First question:** "Would I submit my own client against this exact text?"
- **Data to pull:** the breakdown text as posted, submission count by hour since posting, and a comparable breakdown from a similar role that drew a healthy pool.

### A hold placed with no expiration date, more than a week old
- **Usually means:** the actor's rep is keeping the option open at zero cost to them while shopping other offers — the hold isn't protecting your production, it's protecting their leverage.
- **First question:** "What's the actual date this expires, and who confirmed it?"
- **Data to pull:** the avail/hold/option tracker's date-placed column and the rep's last written confirmation.

### An actor's quote exceeds the episodic budget line by more than ~40%
- **Usually means:** the gap is structural, not a billing or backend negotiation away from closing — pursuing it consumes decision-window time that should go to backup contenders.
- **First question:** "Is there a version of this deal (fee, option term, backend) that closes inside this budget, or are we negotiating to lose gracefully?"
- **Data to pull:** the actor's quote history on comparable roles, the deal memo draft, and the season 1 through option-term cost table.

### Self-tape read is dramatically stronger (or weaker) than the same actor's in-person callback
- **Usually means:** tape production quality (lighting, coaching, unlimited takes) inflated the self-tape, or nerves/unfamiliar space deflated the in-person read — neither is the actor's actual range.
- **First question:** "What changed between the tape and the room — the material, the space, or the take count?"
- **Data to pull:** the submitted tape, the reader's notes from the in-person session, and whether the actor requested a callback re-tape.

### A producer or showrunner wants to test more than 4 finalists for one role
- **Usually means:** no one has actually made a recommendation yet — the decision has been outsourced upward instead of narrowed by the people who ran the reads.
- **First question:** "Of these, who is our actual first choice, and why hasn't that been said yet?"
- **Data to pull:** the chemistry-read scorecard rankings and each finalist's read notes.

### A rep pushes "favored nations" billing across roles of clearly different size or marquee value
- **Usually means:** the rep is trying to use ensemble-equity language to lock in parity their client hasn't earned on this project, or to prevent a bigger name from being cast alongside their client at a higher rate.
- **First question:** "Are these roles actually comparable in size and marquee value, or is this parity language doing work the deal terms should be doing?"
- **Data to pull:** the character breakdown's billing tier for each role and comparable ensemble deals on similar-budget projects.

### Option-year escalator terms left as "standard" or undefined at the deal-memo stage
- **Usually means:** the deal will get renegotiated from scratch at each pickup instead of following a pre-agreed schedule — expensive leverage loss once the show is a hit and the actor's rep knows it.
- **First question:** "What's the actual percentage and base for each option year, in writing, before this memo is signed?"
- **Data to pull:** the deal memo's options/exclusivity section and the studio's standard escalator schedule for comparable deals.

### A breakdown's lived-experience requirement (disability, language, cultural background) isn't verified before booking
- **Usually means:** the submission was taken at face value from a resume or self-tape framing without the CD confirming it in the room — a recurring misrepresentation pattern that advocacy organizations in the industry actively track.
- **First question:** "Where in the process did we actually confirm this, versus assume it from the submission?"
- **Data to pull:** the audition notes or callback recording where the requirement was addressed directly.

### Chemistry read skipped for a lead pairing under schedule pressure
- **Usually means:** the production calendar is driving a casting decision that should be driven by read data — the risk gets discovered on set, at a much higher cost to fix, instead of in the room.
- **First question:** "What would the chemistry read have told us that we're deciding to find out during the shoot instead?"
- **Data to pull:** the production start date, the finalists' avail windows, and whether a compressed same-day chemistry read is actually possible before deciding to skip it.

### Casting associate or CD is looped into network/studio approval later than the actor's hold expiration
- **Usually means:** the approval chain (network current programming, studio business affairs) isn't synced to the casting timeline, and a hold will lapse waiting on a sign-off that was never on the critical path.
- **First question:** "What's the approval turnaround time, and does it fit inside the hold we already have?"
- **Data to pull:** the studio/network approval SLA and the hold's expiration date side by side.

### An actor accepts a fee well below quote with no billing, backend, or perk trade in return
- **Usually means:** either genuine passion for the project (real, and worth noting) or a rep who hasn't actually negotiated — worth a second call before locking the deal, since an unchallenged lowball can signal a rep who isn't advocating and will resurface as a problem later in the relationship.
- **First question:** "Did we actually negotiate, or did they just take the first number?"
- **Data to pull:** the negotiation email/call log and the actor's quote on their two most recent comparable roles.
