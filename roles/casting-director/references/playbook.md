# Casting Director Playbook

Filled templates for the four recurring artifacts: the breakdown, the avail/hold/option tracker, the deal memo, and the chemistry-read scorecard.

## 1. Casting breakdown template (as posted to Breakdown Express)

```
PROJECT: "Northline" (basic cable drama, straight-to-series, 10 eps)
UNION STATUS: SAG-AFTRA signatory
ROLE: DANA REYES — SERIES REGULAR
BREAKDOWN TYPE: Agents/Managers only (not open call)

Female, 32–42, any ethnicity. A homicide detective eighteen months
sober, whose competence at work is the one place she still trusts
herself. Needs to play controlled exteriors over visible internal
cost — not a hard-edged cop archetype. Native or fluent Spanish a
plus, not required. Series regular, 10-ep order, standard
network option-year deal.

SIDES: Attached (Scenes 4, 11)
SUBMIT: Self-tape, sides as written, one take per scene, slate on camera.
DEADLINE: 72 hours from posting.
```

Note what the breakdown does *not* do: it doesn't ask for "a strong female lead" (derivable from the job) — it states the specific internal contradiction the actor has to play, because that's what a rep uses to decide whether their client is actually right for it, and what a reader uses to judge the tape against.

## 2. Avail / hold / option tracking sheet

Reviewed daily once a role is down to finalists. Columns are commitment level, not vibe.

| Actor | Rep | Status | Date placed | Expires | Backup rank | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A. Chen | CAA (J. Ruiz) | **Option** — deal closed | Day 14 | — | — | Signed deal memo, start paperwork in progress |
| M. Torres | Gersh (L. Kim) | Hold, no expiration set | Day 9 | **undefined — fix today** | 1 | Flag: undated holds don't block anything for the actor's side |
| R. Alvarez | Innovative (D. Ford) | Avail confirmed | Day 11 | Day 16 | 2 | Available through network test date |
| K. Osei | UTA (P. Singh) | Avail confirmed | Day 12 | Day 15 | 3 | Tightest window — decide by Day 15 or lose |

**Rule of thumb:** every hold gets an expiration date the day it's placed, not "whenever we decide." An undated hold is a favor the rep is doing you, not a commitment — treat it as expiring at the next natural deadline (their next audition, their next offer) and confirm in writing.

## 3. Deal memo structure (short-form, precedes long-form contract)

```
DEAL MEMO — "Northline," Role: Dana Reyes
Actor: A. Chen                    Rep: CAA, J. Ruiz

COMPENSATION
  Season 1 (10 eps, firm):        $50,000/episode
  Option Seasons 2–4:              5% escalator/season (per attached schedule)
  Backend:                         1.5 points, contingent on series pickup S2+

BILLING
  Position:                        2nd, after [male lead]
  Card:                            Separate card, main titles
  Size:                            Not less than 75% of title size

OPTIONS / EXCLUSIVITY
  Studio options:                  4 additional seasons, standard 90-day
                                    pickup notice before each season
  Exclusivity:                     First position for series regular work;
                                    guest-star availability elsewhere
                                    subject to studio approval

PERKS
  Housing:                         Per diem + housing if principal photography
                                    relocates >50 miles from actor's residence

SIGN-OFF NEEDED FROM: Studio BD, Network current programming, Actor's rep
```

The memo exists so money, billing, and options are agreed as one package before long-form paperwork — reopening any one line after the memo is signed reopens all of them in the rep's eyes.

## 4. Chemistry-read scorecard

Used for the final 2–3 contenders reading opposite the already-cast scene partner. Filled in immediately after each read, before discussing as a group (to avoid anchoring on whoever speaks first).

| Finalist | Read quality (solo) | Chemistry w/ [lead] | Specific note | Rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| M. Torres | Strong — control under pressure landed | Strong — matched lead's rhythm, didn't chase energy | Best network-notes score of the three | 1 |
| R. Alvarez | Strong | Adequate — technically solid, no spark | Would work as a procedural partner, not this pairing | 2 |
| K. Osei | Adequate | Weak — overplayed vulnerability opposite lead's restraint | Read better solo than paired | 3 |

**Rule of thumb:** rank immediately after the reads, in the room, before opening it up to producers' impressions — the scorecard is the data point the recommendation traces back to, not a summary written after the group has already talked itself into a favorite.
