# Red flags

### Joint appointment with no written MOU, more than one semester in
- **Usually means:** service and teaching credit will silently accumulate in both units while annual-review responsibility falls to neither, or gets duplicated by both.
- **First question:** which unit's chair signed last year's annual review letter — and did the other unit sign anything at all?
- **Data to pull:** the faculty member's committee-assignment list from both units' governance records, compared against the (absent) MOU's expected split.

### Course enrollment within 3 students of the registrar's cancellation minimum, two consecutive terms
- **Usually means:** the course is surviving on interest alone rather than a durable requirement pipeline (major, minor, or gen-ed cross-listing).
- **First question:** what percentage of this term's enrollment came from students fulfilling a requirement versus pure elective interest?
- **Data to pull:** registrar enrollment-by-reason report (requirement fulfillment vs. elective), cross-listing options not yet used.

### More than 4 standing committee assignments for a pre-tenure faculty member in a joint or program-only line
- **Usually means:** disproportionate diversity/DEI-adjacent service load, a documented pattern for junior faculty in interdisciplinary and ethnic/area-studies fields specifically.
- **First question:** how many of these assignments were requested versus assigned without negotiation, and does the written FTE-service expectation actually authorize this load?
- **Data to pull:** the joint-appointment MOU (or its absence) and a year-over-year committee-count comparison against peers at the same rank.

### Monograph manuscript under 70% complete by the midpoint of a 6-year tenure clock, no press contract
- **Usually means:** the acquisition timeline (12–18 months from complete draft to signed contract) will not close before the external-letter deadline unless the remaining chapters are prioritized now.
- **First question:** what is the actual chapter-by-chapter completion date the candidate is working toward, in writing?
- **Data to pull:** the candidate's own writing schedule and the press's typical review-to-decision timeline for comparable manuscripts in the field.

### Tenure or merit file citing "impact" or "community engagement" with no named anchor
- **Usually means:** the non-traditional output hasn't been translated into the binding unit's evaluation criteria — it will read as unevaluable, not as evidence, to a committee outside the field.
- **First question:** what specific line in the written T&P criteria is this output claimed to satisfy, and what's the checkable anchor (citation count, named partner institution, IRB protocol)?
- **Data to pull:** the written T&P criteria document and a side-by-side translation table for every non-traditional item on the CV.

### External review letters for a tenure case drawn entirely from one disciplinary tradition
- **Usually means:** the committee will lack the expertise to evaluate the interdisciplinary half of the work, and the case will be judged on only the legible half.
- **First question:** does the letter list include reviewers who can speak to both the home discipline's standards and the field-specific contribution?
- **Data to pull:** the proposed external reviewer list and each reviewer's disciplinary home and area-studies familiarity.

### A course or program becomes the subject of outside media or legislative inquiry
- **Usually means:** the field's structural political exposure has activated on this specific course — not a sign the course was poorly designed by default.
- **First question:** does the current syllabus's stated learning objectives and source list already exist as a standing document, or is one being written reactively right now?
- **Data to pull:** the syllabus, the catalog description, and the specific claim or excerpt driving the inquiry.

### Declared majors/minors declining for 3+ consecutive years while total enrollment (via gen-ed) holds steady
- **Usually means:** the program is functioning as a service unit for other departments' requirements rather than building its own pipeline — a structurally weaker position when budget cuts are proposed.
- **First question:** what fraction of gen-ed-driven enrollment converts to a declared minor within a year, and has that conversion rate itself changed?
- **Data to pull:** registrar declared-major/minor trend data and gen-ed enrollment source breakdown by term.

### Area-studies program covering an eligible world region with no Title VI or FLAS engagement
- **Usually means:** the program is leaving a specific, competitive federal funding and graduate-recruitment mechanism unclaimed, likely because no one has run the eligibility check.
- **First question:** has anyone confirmed current NRC/FLAS eligibility criteria against this program's course array and language offerings in the last funding cycle?
- **Data to pull:** the U.S. Department of Education's current Title VI competition notice and the program's course/language inventory.

### More than 60% of core curriculum sections taught by contingent or adjunct faculty
- **Usually means:** the program's intellectual continuity and its ability to mentor majors/minors toward research is structurally thin, regardless of how strong any individual course is.
- **First question:** how many of the contingent instructors have taught the same course more than twice, versus rotating each term?
- **Data to pull:** the course schedule with instructor type (tenure-line, contingent, adjunct) for the last 3 years.
