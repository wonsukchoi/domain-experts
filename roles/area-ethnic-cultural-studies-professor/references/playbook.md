# Playbook

Filled templates and step sequences for the four recurring processes: joint-appointment MOUs, tenure/merit dossier structuring, course viability audits, and Title VI/FLAS-adjacent grant narratives.

## 1. Joint-appointment MOU (filled template)

A joint or cross-appointment without this document is the single most common source of silent overload. Draft before the first annual review, not after a conflict.

```
JOINT APPOINTMENT MEMORANDUM OF UNDERSTANDING
Faculty member: [Name]
Units: Latin American & Caribbean Studies Program (0.5 FTE) / Anthropology Department (0.5 FTE)
Effective: [Date] | Review cycle: annually each spring, or on renegotiation request

1. TEACHING
   - 2 courses/year in each unit (4 total), standard 2-2 load equivalent split evenly.
   - Cross-listed courses count toward BOTH units' teaching totals (not double-counted
     against the faculty member — count once per unit of record).

2. SERVICE
   - Program: 1 standing committee, OR chair of 1 ad hoc committee, not both.
   - Department: 1 standing committee.
   - University-wide service (senate, task forces) counted against WHICHEVER unit
     nominated the faculty member, not split.
   - Total standing commitments target: 2 per year pre-tenure, 3 post-tenure.

3. ANNUAL REVIEW
   - Program director and department chair submit ONE joint letter, not two
     independent ones, co-signed after a joint meeting with the faculty member.
   - Merit-point weighting (research/teaching/service) uses [unit]'s written formula,
     specified here to avoid ambiguity: Anthropology's formula governs, since it
     holds the tenure line.

4. TENURE HOME
   - Tenure line resides in: Anthropology.
   - Program's T&P criteria apply as a SECONDARY standard the file must also satisfy;
     Anthropology's standard is binding.

5. RENEGOTIATION TRIGGER
   - Either chair or the faculty member may request renegotiation with 60 days' notice;
     default terms above remain in force until a revised MOU is signed.
```

## 2. Tenure/merit dossier — translation table

The single highest-leverage document in an interdisciplinary case: maps each non-traditional output to the specific line in the binding unit's written criteria it satisfies. Build this before assembling the CV narrative.

| Output | Binding criterion it satisfies | Translation stated in the file |
|---|---|---|
| Community oral-history archive (40 interviews, deposited at [Library]) | "Original research contribution to the field" | "Functions as this discipline's primary-source monograph research: peer-reviewed by [named oral history association] standards prior to deposit; cited in 3 subsequent published articles by other scholars." |
| Public-facing curriculum used by [district/organization] | "Evidence of research impact beyond the academy" | "Adopted by [named institution], reaching [N] students/year; evaluated per AHA's Guidelines for the Professional Evaluation of Digital Scholarship." |
| Co-authored article in a field-specific journal (not top-ranked in home discipline) | "Peer-reviewed publication" | "Journal is the flagship venue of [named professional association]; editorial board includes [N] scholars holding appointments in [home discipline] at R1 institutions — equivalent rigor, field-specific audience by design." |
| Participatory action research project with community partner organization | "Scholarly methodology" | "Methodology reviewed and approved by IRB under [protocol #]; findings presented at [named conference]; methodology documented in [named handbook/source]." |

Rule: every row needs a named, checkable anchor (association name, IRB protocol, citation count) — "impactful" and "well-received" without an anchor is exactly the generic language a tenure committee discounts.

## 3. Course viability + political-exposure audit

Run per course, per term, before the term is scheduled.

**Enrollment viability check:**
```
Course: LACS 240 — Caribbean Diaspora Literature
Registrar cancellation minimum: 12
Projected enrollment (majors + minors): 5
Cross-listing options checked:
  - English dept (satisfies "global literature" gen-ed): +4 projected
  - Gender & Sexuality Studies minor requirement: +3 projected
  Revised projection: 12 — at the line, not above it.
Action: cross-list with English this term; revisit GSS cross-listing for next
offering rather than stacking both changes at once (isolates which cross-listing
actually drove enrollment for future planning).
```

**Political-exposure check (run once per new/revised syllabus, not only reactively):**
```
1. Stated learning objectives: do they name a skill or knowledge outcome
   ("analyze primary-source accounts of..."), not a political conclusion
   ("understand why X policy was wrong")?
2. Source list: does it include primary documents and more than one
   interpretive tradition, or only secondary sources from one school of thought?
3. Assessment design: does grading rubric language tie to the stated objectives,
   defensible independent of any student's or outsider's political read?
4. Public-facing description (catalog copy): does it match the syllabus's actual
   content and framing, or oversell/undersell relative to what's taught?
If any answer is "no," revise before the term starts — not after a complaint.
```

## 4. Title VI / FLAS-adjacent grant narrative — structure

For area-studies programs (region-focused, not identity-focused) applying for or renewing National Resource Center status or seeking FLAS fellowship lines.

```
1. NEEDS STATEMENT: named regional/national workforce or language gap this center
   addresses (cite a named source — e.g., a State Department or ACTFL
   less-commonly-taught-language shortage report — not an assumed gap).
2. CURRICULUM ARRAY: current course count cross-listed across units, with a
   named target for the grant period (e.g., "22 courses currently; target 28
   by adding 2 language courses and 4 cross-listed electives").
3. LANGUAGE INSTRUCTION: current less-commonly-taught-language (LCTL) offerings
   and enrollment; a program showing only the region's dominant language
   (e.g., Spanish for a Latin America center) with zero LCTL enrollment is
   the single most common reviewer flag — address it directly if it applies.
4. FELLOWSHIP ALLOCATION PLAN: FLAS award targets by language, not just a
   total count — reviewers score language diversity of awardees, not raw
   fellowship count.
5. OUTREACH: K-12 or community partnership component, named and quantified
   (schools reached, teachers trained) — required component of NRC scoring,
   not optional.
6. SUSTAINABILITY: what the program funds after the grant cycle ends without
   federal support, stated explicitly.
```
