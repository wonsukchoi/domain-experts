# Concierge — Red Flags

### A guest's stated request has no clarified occasion attached
- **Usually means:** the ask-behind-the-ask hasn't been surfaced yet — the literal request may not match the actual goal, risking a technically-fulfilled but occasion-irrelevant result.
- **First question:** "What's the occasion?" or "What matters most about tonight?"
- **Data to pull:** guest-preference profile for prior stated occasions or preferences on file.

### Plan A has passed the internal deadline with no confirmation and outreach is still "in progress"
- **Usually means:** sunk-cost momentum on a channel that isn't going to close in time, eating into Plan B's runway.
- **First question:** "If I stop trying this channel right now, is there still enough time to execute Plan B properly?"
- **Data to pull:** time remaining until guest departure/deadline vs. Plan B's minimum lead time.

### A vendor relationship has been used more than twice in the same week
- **Usually means:** approaching the relationship's practical usage ceiling — continuing to draw on it risks depleting it before a higher-stakes request needs it.
- **First question:** "Is this request worth spending this specific relationship on right now?"
- **Data to pull:** vendor-relationship log's recent-favor history for that contact.

### A "yes" was secured more than a few hours before execution and hasn't been reconfirmed
- **Usually means:** staffing changes, double-bookings, or simple forgetting on the vendor's end can silently invalidate an earlier confirmation.
- **First question:** "Have I verified this directly with the vendor within the last hour, in writing if possible?"
- **Data to pull:** timestamp of the last direct confirmation vs. current time.

### A recommendation keeps steering toward one specific vendor regardless of guest preference
- **Usually means:** possible referral-commission bias distorting judgment rather than genuine fit.
- **First question:** "Would I recommend this vendor if there were no commission attached?"
- **Data to pull:** the vendor-relationship log's commission/referral-fee notes, if any.

### A guest is told "it's handled" before every logistics leg is confirmed
- **Usually means:** premature reassurance that risks a worse failure later — a guest who was told "yes" and then told "actually, no" an hour before execution is harder to recover than one told honestly it's still in progress.
- **First question:** "Is every box on the logistics-confirmation checklist actually checked?"
- **Data to pull:** the logistics-confirmation checklist for the specific request.

### A request has no viable Plan B identified before Plan A outreach even starts
- **Usually means:** if Plan A fails, there's no fallback ready, and the time spent discovering a Plan B after the fact eats into the guest's remaining window.
- **First question:** "What's the fallback if the first-choice option says no?"
- **Data to pull:** partner-venue list for the relevant category (dining, transportation, entertainment).

### A favor was called in from a vendor and never repaid or acknowledged
- **Usually means:** a one-sided draw on the relationship that will eventually make that contact unavailable when needed most.
- **First question:** "What was offered back after the last favor, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** vendor-relationship log's "last favor repaid" column.
