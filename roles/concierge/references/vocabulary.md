# Concierge — Vocabulary

### Holdback
A table, seats, or inventory a venue reserves off the public books specifically for a trusted relationship contact, not released through normal reservation channels.
**In use:** "The maître d' has a two-top holdback for exactly this kind of last-minute ask — that's what I called on."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be unlimited or guaranteed; a holdback is a relationship favor with a real ceiling, not a permanent backdoor.

### Ask behind the ask
The underlying occasion or goal driving a guest's literal request, which may differ from the stated request itself.
**In use:** "The ask behind the ask here is impressing a client, not that specific restaurant — a comparable venue with better privacy might actually serve them better."
**Common misuse:** Treated as license to substitute anything convenient; the ask behind the ask still constrains what counts as an acceptable Plan B — it's not a blank check to ignore the guest's stated preference entirely.

### Relationship capital
The accumulated goodwill and reciprocal-favor history with a specific vendor contact, which determines how much can be asked before the relationship is strained.
**In use:** "We've drawn on that relationship twice this week already — I don't want to spend more relationship capital there until we've repaid something."
**Common misuse:** Treated as infinite because it worked the last three times; capital depletes with one-sided draws and needs active reciprocity to replenish.

### Les Clefs d'Or
The international professional guild for hotel concierges (the name means "The Golden Keys," referencing the crossed-keys pin members wear), which maintains a referral network concierges use for requests outside their own market or personal relationships.
**In use:** "This is an out-of-town request — I'll reach out through Les Clefs d'Or to a member concierge in that city instead of cold-calling."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be a booking service; it's a peer network of individual concierges helping each other, not a centralized reservation system.

### Plan-A deadline
The internally-set cutoff time by which the primary approach to a request must be confirmed or explicitly abandoned in favor of a fallback.
**In use:** "Our Plan-A deadline for the restaurant is 6pm — if the maître d' hasn't called back by then, we pivot to the partner venue."
**Common misuse:** Treated as flexible if Plan A "seems close" — the entire point of setting the deadline in advance is to remove in-the-moment sunk-cost reasoning from the decision.

### Same-caliber alternative
A Plan B option matched in quality/prestige/fit to the original request's implied standard, not merely "something available."
**In use:** "The requested restaurant is booked solid, but our same-caliber alternative has an open table and fits the occasion just as well."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with "any open option" — a downgrade offered as if it were equivalent reads to the guest as a failure, not a fulfilled request.

### Referral commission
A fee or credit a vendor pays a concierge (or the property) for directing business their way, common in luxury hospitality but requiring disclosure discipline.
**In use:** "That spa has a referral arrangement with the hotel — I'll mention that if the guest asks how I chose it, but it's not why I'm recommending it."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be inherently improper; the commission itself is standard industry practice — the problem is letting it override genuine fit or concealing it when asked directly.

### Logistics-confirmation
The final verification pass on every leg of an arranged request (time, headcount, special conditions, payment) directly with each vendor, distinct from the initial "yes."
**In use:** "The restaurant said yes at 2pm, but logistics-confirmation happens forty-five minutes before the guest leaves, not before."
**Common misuse:** Skipped because an earlier "yes" feels sufficient; vendor circumstances (double-bookings, staffing) can silently invalidate an earlier confirmation.

### Guest-preference profile
The accumulated record of a guest's prior stated preferences, occasions, and requests, used to anticipate needs before they're stated.
**In use:** "Her profile shows she requested a quiet table for an anniversary last year — worth asking if tonight's the same occasion."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a rigid preference database rather than a starting hypothesis to confirm — preferences change, and assuming last year's request still applies without asking can misfire.

### Vendor-relationship log
The concierge's own tracked record of standing contacts, favor history, and relationship strength across restaurants, venues, and service providers.
**In use:** "Before I call in another favor at that theater, let me check the log — we're close to their weekly ceiling."
**Common misuse:** Kept informally in memory rather than actually recorded; relationship history that isn't tracked gets over-drawn without anyone noticing until the relationship is already strained.
