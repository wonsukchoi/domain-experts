# Concierge — Playbook

## Vendor-relationship log (filled example)

| Vendor | Contact | Relationship strength | Last favor called in | Last favor repaid | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Restaurant A (fine dining) | Maître d', Marco | Strong — 4 yrs, monthly referrals both ways | 3 weeks ago (holdback table) | Referred 2 corporate groups this quarter | Holdback available most nights except Fri/Sat |
| Restaurant B (partner, same-caliber backup) | GM, Priya | Strong — standing partner-property agreement | This week (Plan B seat) | Reciprocal referral agreement, no per-favor tracking needed | Default Plan B for anniversary/occasion requests |
| Downtown Theater box office | House manager, Alan | Moderate — 6 mos, 3 favors called in | 2 months ago (2 house seats) | Sent a positive mention to their marketing team | Only 2 house seats/week available; don't over-ask |
| Florist (24hr) | Owner, Dana | Strong — 5 yrs, high volume | Ongoing (weekly orders) | Standing account, paid promptly | 90-min delivery commitment for standing accounts |
| Car service | Dispatch, standing account | Transactional — no personal favor needed | N/A | N/A | Booked through account, not relationship-dependent |

**Reading the log:** a "strong" relationship with a recent unrepaid favor is a relationship to protect, not spend further this week. A "moderate" relationship near its usage ceiling (e.g., Alan's 2-seats/week) should not be asked again until the ceiling resets, even for an urgent request — burning it now means it's unavailable for a higher-stakes ask later.

## Parallel-channel outreach sequence (for a time-constrained multi-vendor request)

1. **T+0 (request received):** Confirm the ask behind the ask (occasion, headcount, any hard constraints — dietary, accessibility, timing).
2. **T+0 to T+5 min:** Identify every viable channel for each component (primary relationship contact, backup partner venue, cold-outreach fallback).
3. **T+5 min:** Launch primary-channel outreach for all components simultaneously — do not wait for restaurant confirmation before starting the car-service booking.
4. **Set an internal Plan-A deadline** at roughly 60% of the total available window (e.g., a 3.5-hour window gets a ~2-hour Plan-A cutoff) — if Plan A hasn't confirmed by then, pivot to Plan B regardless of how promising it still looks.
5. **At the Plan-A deadline:** confirmed components proceed; unconfirmed components pivot to backup channel immediately, no further delay.
6. **T-45 min before execution:** reconfirm every leg directly with each vendor — time, headcount, special conditions. A "yes" from earlier in the day is not assumed still valid without this step.
7. **Post-execution:** log the outcome (which channel worked, which didn't, any favor called in) in the vendor-relationship log.

## Logistics-confirmation checklist (per vendor, before considering a request "done")

- [ ] Time and date confirmed directly with the vendor (not just relayed secondhand)
- [ ] Headcount / quantity confirmed
- [ ] Any special condition disclosed and acknowledged (allergy, accessibility, dress code, access instructions)
- [ ] Payment or prepayment method confirmed if required
- [ ] A named contact at the vendor for day-of issues (not a general line)
- [ ] Timing cross-checked against every other component of the request (does the car pickup time actually clear the restaurant reservation with buffer?)

**Test:** if any box is unchecked, the request is "in progress," not "confirmed" — don't tell the guest it's handled until every box clears.
