# Red flags — signals a floor loss prevention specialist notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds, observed live on the floor. Any one alone can be innocent; two or more on the same subject within the same visit is worth opening surveillance over.

### Subject checks for cameras or staff two or more times before touching a specific item
- **Usually means:** the approach-avoidance phase of the standard shoplifting behavior sequence — precedes concealment, not proof of it yet.
- **First question:** "Is this subject now moving toward a lower-visibility aisle or blind spot after the camera-check?"
- **Data to pull:** none yet — this is a cue to begin personal continuous observation, not to pull records.

### A second person engages a floor associate in an unrelated, extended conversation while a first person moves toward an exit
- **Usually means:** a diversion team — classic two-person concealment cover, though occasionally an innocent coincidence of two separate shoppers.
- **First question:** "Are these two subjects arriving and moving together, and is the conversation topic (returns, price checks) unusually drawn out relative to a normal question?"
- **Data to pull:** live radio call to exit-camera operator to pick up both subjects before they separate.

### Self-checkout item weight-mismatch or skip-scan alert on an item over $20 before the payment screen clears
- **Usually means:** intentional scan avoidance (barcode swap, bagging without scanning) — high enough value and early enough stage to justify pre-payment intervention.
- **First question:** "Can I intervene before this subject taps payment, or has the screen already cleared?"
- **Data to pull:** SCO exception log with item value and stage-caught timestamp.

### Same payment card, loyalty ID, or subject flagged on the self-checkout exception log 3+ times in a month
- **Usually means:** a repeat pattern rather than isolated scanning mistakes — possible organized skip-scan or a specific individual exploiting SCO gaps.
- **First question:** "Pull the last 90 days of exceptions tied to this ID — is it the same lane, same item category, or spread randomly?"
- **Data to pull:** SCO exception history by payment/loyalty ID, cross-referenced by lane and item category.

### Subject enters a fitting room with more items than they exit with, without a corresponding purchase
- **Usually means:** concealment inside the fitting room (tags removed, item worn under clothing) or a wardrobing setup for later return fraud.
- **First question:** "Did the attendant count items in and out, and does the count actually reconcile?"
- **Data to pull:** fitting-room item-count log if the store uses one; otherwise personal observation is the only record — flag for camera coverage on future visits.

### An associate's register shows a $0 or low-value transaction immediately followed by a friend or family member leaving with a full bag
- **Usually means:** sweethearting — the associate under-ringing or voiding items for someone they know.
- **First question:** "Is this the same associate-and-visitor pairing showing up on prior shifts, and does the timing cluster around this associate's shifts specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** void/no-sale log by employee ID, cross-referenced with shift schedule.

### Same subject, vehicle, or group observed at this store and at least one other nearby store within the same window
- **Usually means:** organized retail crime rather than an isolated shoplifting attempt — coordinated across locations.
- **First question:** "Does the vehicle or subject description match anything in the regional ORC bulletin or recent multi-store alerts?"
- **Data to pull:** cross-store video/incident correlation, regional ORC intelligence-sharing bulletin.

### Subject displays a possible weapon indicator, or the group size is three or more, during an otherwise-clean observation
- **Usually means:** the case may be evidentiarily solid, but the risk profile no longer supports an in-person stop.
- **First question:** "Is there any indicator serious enough that this stop should become a remote-observation-and-police-call instead?"
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a real-time risk override, not a data question; default to disengage and call police.

### A stop is made and the subject becomes verbally aggressive or attempts to flee
- **Usually means:** the situation has moved past the point where recovering the item is worth continued engagement.
- **First question:** "Is anyone at risk of injury if this continues, and is the item's value worth that risk?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the moment — disengage per no-pursuit policy; document immediately after for the incident report.

### A companion's items are included in an apprehension without the specialist having personally observed that companion's concealment
- **Usually means:** the apprehension is about to be actioned beyond what the observation chain actually supports.
- **First question:** "Did I personally, continuously watch this specific subject conceal this specific item, or am I inferring it from being with the first subject?"
- **Data to pull:** the specialist's own observation log/timeline — if there's a gap, that portion goes documentation-only.
