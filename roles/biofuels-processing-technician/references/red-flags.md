# Red flags

### A biodiesel batch processed at a standard catalyst dose without testing feedstock FFA content
- **Usually means:** actual FFA content may differ significantly from what the standard dose assumes, risking incomplete conversion.
- **First question:** has this specific feedstock batch been titrated for FFA content, or is the standard dose applied regardless?
- **Data to pull:** FFA titration result if performed, standard dose's assumed FFA baseline.

### Transesterification completeness confirmed by visual inspection rather than a glyceride content test
- **Usually means:** the fuel may appear normal while actually failing the glyceride content specification.
- **First question:** was an actual glyceride content test (ASTM D6584 or equivalent) performed, or was the batch released based on visual/physical appearance?
- **Data to pull:** glyceride content test result if performed, the applicable specification limit.

### Fermentation temperature/pH checked only at the end of the process rather than monitored continuously
- **Usually means:** a mid-process excursion that stressed or killed yeast could go undetected until yield loss is discovered at completion.
- **First question:** was temperature/pH actively monitored throughout, or only checked at start and end?
- **Data to pull:** continuous monitoring data if available, any excursion events logged.

### A stuck or incomplete fermentation with no investigation into whether a temperature/pH excursion occurred
- **Usually means:** the root cause (a process condition that stressed the yeast population) hasn't been identified, risking recurrence.
- **First question:** does the monitoring data show any temperature/pH excursion during the batch, correlating with when fermentation appeared to stall?
- **Data to pull:** temperature/pH log for the batch, fermentation progress/yield data.

### A process recipe run identically across feedstock batches without checking for characteristic variation
- **Usually means:** yield or quality loss could result from feedstock variation the fixed recipe doesn't account for.
- **First question:** has this batch's feedstock characteristics (starch/sugar content, FFA) been tested and compared to what the recipe assumes?
- **Data to pull:** feedstock test result, recipe's assumed baseline characteristics.

### Byproduct quality (glycerin purity, distillers grains composition) not tested or monitored separately from primary fuel quality
- **Usually means:** a byproduct's market value or usability could be reduced by process conditions optimized only for the primary fuel product.
- **First question:** is byproduct quality being tested and tracked as its own consideration, or only the primary fuel product?
- **Data to pull:** byproduct quality test data if available, process conditions used.

### A finished biofuel batch showing a downstream quality complaint (filter clogging, injector deposits) traced back to the production batch
- **Usually means:** incomplete conversion (residual glycerides) likely wasn't caught before release.
- **First question:** does this batch's quality record show an actual glyceride content test result, or was it released without one?
- **Data to pull:** the specific batch's quality record, the complaint's characteristics.

### A catalyst dose adjustment made without a documented FFA basis for the specific adjustment amount
- **Usually means:** the adjustment may be an estimate rather than a calculated response to the actual measured feedstock characteristic.
- **First question:** does the adjusted dose trace to a specific FFA measurement and calculation, or was it an approximate increase?
- **Data to pull:** FFA measurement, dose calculation basis.

### A new feedstock source introduced without establishing its typical characteristic range before full-scale processing
- **Usually means:** process parameters may not be appropriately calibrated for this new source's actual characteristics.
- **First question:** has this new source's typical FFA or starch/sugar characteristics been established, or is it assumed similar to prior sources?
- **Data to pull:** new source's characterization data if available.

### A recurring yield or quality issue correlating with a specific feedstock supplier or lot
- **Usually means:** a systematic feedstock characteristic difference from that source, rather than isolated process variation.
- **First question:** does the issue pattern correlate with a specific supplier/source, or does it appear randomly across sources?
- **Data to pull:** yield/quality data by feedstock source, feedstock characteristics by source.
