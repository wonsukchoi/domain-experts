# Red flags — what a credit analyst notices in a financial package

Format per flag: signal with a threshold → what it usually means → the first question to ask → the data to pull. Any one is often innocent; two together on the same file usually isn't.

### Adjusted EBITDA addbacks exceeding 15–20% of reported EBITDA
- **Usually means:** the borrower's package is engineered to clear a covenant or a target leverage multiple; some addbacks (a "one-time" cost that recurs every year) won't survive scrutiny.
- **First question:** "Which addbacks have documentary evidence — an invoice, a termination letter, a settlement agreement — versus a management assertion?"
- **Data to pull:** addback support schedule, prior two years' addback lists (to check whether "one-time" items repeat), tax return reconciliation to the adjusted number.

### DSCR clears the base-case floor (e.g., 1.30x) but falls below 1.10x under a single named stress
- **Usually means:** the base case is real but thin — one customer loss, one rate move, or one commodity swing removes the entire cushion.
- **First question:** "What's the single largest concentration or cost exposure this borrower carries, and what does DSCR do if it moves against them?"
- **Data to pull:** customer/AR concentration schedule, floating-rate debt exposure, top three cost inputs and their historical volatility.

### Current ratio or quick ratio drifting >20% below the RMA industry median for two consecutive years
- **Usually means:** working capital is being stretched to fund growth, cover an operating loss, or fund a distribution — read the direction, not just the level.
- **First question:** "Is the decline funding growth (inventory/AR building ahead of revenue) or funding a cash shortfall?"
- **Data to pull:** RMA comparison for the borrower's NAICS code and size band, AR aging trend, inventory turns trend, distribution history.

### Single customer >20% of revenue or >25% of accounts receivable
- **Usually means:** the repayment source is really two obligors, and the second one (the customer) hasn't been underwritten at all.
- **First question:** "What's the contract term and payment history with that customer, and what happens to DSCR without them?"
- **Data to pull:** customer contract, payment history, AR aging by customer, recomputed stress-case coverage excluding that customer.

### Related-party transactions on the balance sheet or income statement (loans to officers, above-market rent to an affiliate-owned property)
- **Usually means:** economics are being shifted between the borrower and an owner-controlled entity — true cash flow available for debt service may differ from what's reported.
- **First question:** "Walk me through every related-party line — what would change if this were an arm's-length counterparty?"
- **Data to pull:** related-party note in the financial statements, lease/management agreements with affiliates, org chart of commonly owned entities.

### Guarantor's personal debt service not disclosed or not reconciled to the credit report
- **Usually means:** the business DSCR looks adequate but the actual backstop — the guarantor — is already stretched by other obligations the file doesn't show.
- **First question:** "What's the guarantor's personal debt-service coverage, combined with the business, on a global cash flow basis?"
- **Data to pull:** personal financial statement, personal tax returns, personal credit report, any other guaranteed debt outstanding.

### Trailing-12 financials that don't reconcile to the tax return or bank statement deposits within ~5%
- **Usually means:** either the internal statements are unreliable, revenue is being recognized inconsistently, or there's unreported/understated activity somewhere.
- **First question:** "Tie the trailing-12 revenue to bank deposits and to the most recent filed return — where's the gap?"
- **Data to pull:** 12 months of bank statements, most recent filed tax return, interim financial statements, bookkeeper/accountant contact for a walkthrough.

### Covenant headroom under 10% on any financial covenant at the most recent test date
- **Usually means:** the borrower is one soft quarter from a technical default, and a waiver requested after the breach costs more (fees, rate step-up, tightened terms) than one requested before it.
- **First question:** "What's the covenant projection for the next four quarters under a mild downside, and at what point do we need to talk to the borrower?"
- **Data to pull:** covenant compliance certificates for the last 4 quarters, forward covenant projection, credit agreement's cure/waiver provisions.

### Gross margin declining >300bps year over year with no stated pricing or input-cost explanation
- **Usually means:** unrecorded price concessions to retain a customer, cost creep not yet reflected in pricing, or a product mix shift toward lower-margin work — any of which understates the forward EBITDA run-rate.
- **First question:** "Bridge the margin change: how much is price, how much is input cost, how much is mix?"
- **Data to pull:** product/customer-level margin detail, input-cost indices relevant to the industry, pricing change history.

### Inventory or AR growing faster than revenue for two consecutive periods
- **Usually means:** slow-moving or obsolete inventory building up, or AR aging quietly (a channel-stuffing or collections problem) — both overstate the current ratio's real liquidity.
- **First question:** "What's the inventory turnover and DSO trend, not just the balance?"
- **Data to pull:** inventory aging/turns by category, AR aging trend, any inventory write-downs in the last two years.

### New secured debt (equipment financing, factoring, a new lien) appearing outside the credit file's known facilities
- **Usually means:** the borrower has obtained financing the bank didn't originate — collateral coverage assumed in this file may already be encumbered elsewhere.
- **First question:** "Run a new UCC search — what liens exist that weren't in the last file?"
- **Data to pull:** current UCC lien search, borrower disclosure of all outstanding facilities, intercreditor or subordination agreements if any exist.
