# DJ Playbook

Filled templates and structures for mobile-event and club bookings — populate with the actual gig's numbers, don't rewrite from scratch each time.

## 1. Client intake sheet (mobile/wedding — fill before quoting)

| Field | Example filled entry |
|---|---|
| Event date/venue | 6/13/2026, Oak Hollow Barn, 5:00–11:00 PM load-in to load-out |
| Guest count | 150 |
| Ceremony music needed? | No — ceremony has own musician |
| Must-play list | First dance: "At Last" (Etta James); Parent dances: 2 songs, names + timing; 1 surprise hype song, title withheld until reveal |
| Do-not-play list | No line-dance novelty tracks, no country |
| MC duties in scope? | Yes — grand entrance, toasts intro, cake cutting, bouquet/garter, last call |
| Power/tech at venue | 2x 20A circuits at DJ booth, no house PA — DJ brings full system |
| Noise ordinance/curfew | Outdoor amplified sound must end 10:00 PM; indoor until midnight |
| Overtime rate | $150/half-hour past contracted end time, billed to card on file |
| Deposit | 50% due at booking, non-refundable; balance due 14 days before event |

## 2. Wedding reception day-of timeline (fill with real times, hold a 10–15 min buffer)

| Time | Moment | Notes |

## 3. Club/open-format set architecture (3-hour opener → headliner handoff example)

| Segment | Time | BPM range | Key discipline | Energy target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Warm-up | Hour 1 | 110–118 | Strict Camelot ±1 | Establish groove, low-risk selections |
| Build | Hour 2, first half | 118–124 | Camelot ±1, occasional relative major/minor jump | Peak dancers on floor |
| Peak | Hour 2, second half | 124–128 | Deliberate clashes allowed for reset moments | Sustained high energy |
| Handoff | Hour 3 | 124–128, matched to headliner's opening BPM | Last track keyed to headliner's first track | Smooth transition, no energy drop |

## 4. Floor recovery decision table

| Relative drop from peak (within ~30 min) | Action |
|---|---|
| <20% | Normal fluctuation — hold current direction |
| 20–40% | Watch next 2 songs; consider a smaller BPM/genre nudge |
| >40% | Recovery pivot within next 2 songs: step BPM toward a familiar genre in ~10–12 BPM increments across 3 tracks, then re-enter open format |

## 5. Gear redundancy checklist (mobile, paid, non-reschedulable events)

- [ ] Two independent playback sources (e.g., laptop + backup laptop, or controller + paired USB drives on separate players)
- [ ] Two full music libraries, synced within 24 hours of the event
- [ ] Backup mixer or a controller capable of standalone playback if the primary mixer fails
- [ ] Two wireless mic units (handheld + lapel/headset) with charged spare batteries
- [ ] Backup amplifier/powered speaker if the primary PA channel fails
- [ ] Extension cords and a power strip with surge protection independent from venue-supplied power
- [ ] Certificate of insurance (COI) naming the venue as additional insured, printed and on hand

## 6. Contract terms reference (ADJA-style norms)

| Term | Typical value |
|---|---|
| Deposit | 50% at signing, non-refundable |
| Balance due | 14 days before event |
| Cancellation by client, >90 days out | Deposit forfeited, no further charge |
| Cancellation by client, <30 days out | Full contract amount due |
| Overtime | Billed in 30-minute increments at a pre-stated rate, DJ's discretion to decline if no advance agreement |
| General liability insurance | $1M/$2M aggregate — commonly required by venues before load-in is permitted |
