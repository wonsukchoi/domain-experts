# Red Flags

### Floor drops more than ~40% relative to its most recent peak within 30 minutes
- **Usually means:** the last few selections skewed too niche or too fast for this specific room; second most likely: a natural lull (speeches, dinner service, bar rush) that will self-correct without intervention.
- **First question:** did anything external pull people away (bar opened, speeches announced, food came out) or was it the last 3 tracks?
- **Data to pull:** the BPM/genre of the last 3–4 tracks played and the timestamp against the printed event timeline.

### Client's "surprise" or must-play request changes within 24 hours of the event
- **Usually means:** a last-minute family dynamic (a song tied to a person who can't attend, a new in-law request); second: the couple simply forgot to finalize it earlier.
- **First question:** is this replacing an existing must-play or adding to it, and does it conflict with the do-not-play list?
- **Data to pull:** the signed contract's must-play/do-not-play addendum and the date of last written confirmation.

### Venue or neighbor sound complaint during the set
- **Usually means:** the DJ is running above the venue's stated dB limit or past the noise-ordinance curfew; second: bass frequencies carrying through a shared wall the DJ wasn't told about.
- **First question:** what's the venue's contracted dB limit at the mix position, and is a limiter/SPL meter actually running?
- **Data to pull:** the venue contract's sound clause and current SPL meter reading if one is mounted.

### Primary playback device fails mid-set with no backup cued
- **Usually means:** redundancy checklist wasn't followed for this booking — most common single point of failure is one laptop with no second library loaded elsewhere.
- **First question:** is the backup rig physically present and powered right now, or does it need to be set up from scratch?
- **Data to pull:** the gear redundancy checklist completed (or not) before load-in.

### MC/timeline running more than 15 minutes behind the printed schedule
- **Usually means:** dinner service or a speech overran; second: a vendor (photographer, caterer) isn't ready for the next cued moment.
- **First question:** does the coordinator want to compress the buffer or push every remaining moment back uniformly?
- **Data to pull:** the day-of timeline sheet with actual vs. scheduled times logged.

### Requests cluster heavily around a genre the DJ has little material for
- **Usually means:** the crowd's actual demographic (age, regional taste) doesn't match the DJ's assumed library mix going into the booking.
- **First question:** how many distinct guests are making this request, versus one person repeating it?
- **Data to pull:** a quick tally of request source/frequency over the last 20–30 minutes.

### Opening set for a headliner gets little to no floor response
- **Usually means:** BPM/energy handoff is set too high or too low relative to what this crowd came for; second: the room simply hasn't filled up yet.
- **First question:** what's current room capacity as a percentage of expected peak attendance?
- **Data to pull:** door count or venue capacity tracking against the expected peak time.

### A do-not-play track gets requested and played by accident
- **Usually means:** the do-not-play list wasn't cross-checked against the request, or a second DJ/MC covering part of the set didn't have the list.
- **First question:** was this list shared with everyone touching the booth tonight, in writing?
- **Data to pull:** the signed do-not-play addendum and who had access to it during the set.

### Audible clipping or distortion at the mixer's master output
- **Usually means:** gain staged too hot at the channel or master fader rather than at the source trim; second: a mistagged/over-mastered file with true peak already clipping before the mixer.
- **First question:** where's the gain riding — channel trim, channel fader, or master — when the clip light triggers?
- **Data to pull:** mixer channel meter readings and the source file's peak level.

### No certificate of insurance requested by the venue before load-in
- **Usually means:** the venue either doesn't enforce it or the DJ hasn't proactively offered it — either way it's a liability gap if equipment or a guest is injured.
- **First question:** does this venue have a written vendor insurance requirement in its own rental contract?
- **Data to pull:** the venue's vendor policy document and the DJ's current COI.

### Two vendors' timelines conflict (e.g., photographer needs the couple during the scheduled first dance)
- **Usually means:** timelines were built independently without a shared master schedule.
- **First question:** who is the single point of coordination for this event, and have they signed off on the adjusted order?
- **Data to pull:** the coordinator's master timeline versus the DJ's own copy.

### Harmonic-mixing software repeatedly suggests the same 2–3 "compatible" tracks
- **Usually means:** the library's key-tagging is too shallow for this crowd's energy needs, or the DJ is over-relying on the tool instead of ear-judging an intentional break.
- **First question:** is a deliberate energy reset overdue regardless of what's "compatible"?
- **Data to pull:** the last 5–6 tracks' BPM/key sequence to check for stagnation.
