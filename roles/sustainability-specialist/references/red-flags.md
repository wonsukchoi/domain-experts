### A Scope 3 emissions estimate is reported with the same apparent precision as metered Scope 1/2 data
- **Usually means:** The estimate's actual data-quality tier (spend-based vs. activity-based vs. supplier-specific) isn't being disclosed, overstating confidence in a modeled figure.
- **First question:** What method was used to calculate this Scope 3 category, and has its data-quality tier been explicitly flagged in the report?
- **Data to pull:** Scope 3 calculation methodology documentation, underlying activity or spend data.

### Organizational boundary methodology differs between entities in the same consolidation
- **Usually means:** Boundary choice may have been selected per-entity to produce a more favorable overall result rather than applied consistently.
- **First question:** Is the same boundary approach (equity share, financial control, or operational control) documented and applied across every entity in scope?
- **Data to pull:** Boundary-setting documentation for each entity in the consolidation.

### A proposed emissions-reduction target is described as "ambitious" or "science-based" without a trajectory calculation shown
- **Usually means:** The target's percentage may sound significant without actually meeting the specific cumulative reduction required by the relevant science-based framework for this base year and timeline.
- **First question:** What is the required minimum cumulative reduction over this exact period per the applicable trajectory (e.g., SBTi's near-term rate), and does the proposed target meet or exceed it?
- **Data to pull:** Base year, target year, proposed reduction percentage, applicable trajectory's minimum annual reduction rate.

### A Scope 2 figure is reported using only one method (location-based or market-based) when the company holds renewable energy certificates or PPAs
- **Usually means:** Reporting only one method omits information decision-makers or reviewers may specifically need — both are typically expected under GHG Protocol guidance when RECs/PPAs exist.
- **First question:** Does the company hold renewable energy certificates or power purchase agreements, and has the market-based figure been calculated and reported alongside the location-based figure?
- **Data to pull:** REC/PPA holdings and coverage percentage, both location-based and market-based calculations.

### A materiality assessment reports only financial materiality when the applicable framework requires double materiality
- **Usually means:** The assessment may not satisfy a specific regulatory requirement (e.g., CSRD/ESRS), regardless of how thorough the financial-materiality analysis is.
- **First question:** Does the applicable reporting framework require double materiality, and has impact materiality (the company's effect on people/environment) been separately assessed?
- **Data to pull:** Applicable framework's materiality requirements, current materiality assessment scope.

### An emission factor used is a generic global average despite a more specific regional or current-year factor being available
- **Usually means:** The calculation may be less accurate than achievable, and less defensible under scrutiny, than if the more specific factor had been used.
- **First question:** Is a region- and year-specific emission factor available for this activity, and if so, why was a generic factor used instead?
- **Data to pull:** Emission factor source and vintage, availability of more specific regional/current-year alternatives.

### A Scope 3 category calculation uses spend data with no adjustment for known supplier-specific carbon intensity differences
- **Usually means:** A spend-based estimate treats all spending in a category as equally carbon-intensive, which can significantly misstate the footprint if some suppliers are known to be materially more or less carbon-intensive.
- **First question:** Are there known carbon-intensity differences among major suppliers in this category that a flat spend-based factor would miss?
- **Data to pull:** Supplier-specific emissions data where available, spend breakdown by supplier.

### A sustainability report doesn't disclose which emission factors, boundary approach, or materiality methodology were used
- **Usually means:** The report may not be verifiable or defensible under external assurance or regulatory review without this methodological transparency.
- **First question:** Does the report include a methodology section documenting boundary approach, emission factor sources, and materiality assessment method?
- **Data to pull:** Report's methodology/appendix section (or its absence).
