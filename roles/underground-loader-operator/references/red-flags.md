# Underground Loader (LHD) Operator — Red Flags

### Ground control log shows no scaling/support sign-off for a heading, but production is pushing to muck it now
- **Usually means:** schedule pressure is treating the sign-off as a formality rather than the ride-on/remote gate it is under 30 CFR §57.3401
- **First question:** is the sign-off actually missing, or just not yet entered — and either way, has anyone confirmed the back is supported by inspection, not assumption?
- **Data to pull:** ground control log entry for this heading and shift, scaling crew's last reported location and time

### Shot report's fired hole count doesn't match the drilled hole count
- **Usually means:** a hole misfired (bootleg) and remains undetonated somewhere in the round
- **First question:** has the heading been barricaded and the shift boss and a qualified person notified before any mucking starts?
- **Data to pull:** drill log, blaster's fired-hole tally, misfire log if one already exists for this heading

### Unusual object, wire, or chemical odor visible in the muck pile
- **Usually means:** possible undetonated explosive material or detonator remaining from the blast
- **First question:** has mucking stopped at that specific spot, and has it been reported rather than bucketed aside?
- **Data to pull:** shot report for the round, misfire procedure sign-off, prior misfire history for this panel

### Ore pass or drawpoint fill level approaching its stated capacity
- **Usually means:** downstream truck or hoist cycle time isn't keeping pace with LHD delivery rate
- **First question:** what's the truck/hoist cycle time right now against this pass's typical clearance rate, and is there an alternate pass available?
- **Data to pull:** pass fill-level log or visual check, truck/hoist cycle-time log, alternate pass availability

### LHD backup alarm or proximity/visibility aid intermittent or inoperative
- **Usually means:** a wiring or sensor fault, sometimes masked by ambient noise in the heading making the fault seem minor
- **First question:** has the machine been pulled from ride-on service in shared-traffic areas until the alarm is confirmed repaired?
- **Data to pull:** pre-shift equipment inspection record, maintenance log, alarm test result

### Tram route grade steeper than the mine's stated ramp design maximum (commonly around 1:7 to 1:8, roughly 12–15%)
- **Usually means:** a temporary route deviation (dump point relocation, development ramp not yet at final grade) hasn't been re-flagged for a payload/speed derate
- **First question:** has the operator been briefed on this specific segment's actual grade, or is the derate being left to individual judgment?
- **Data to pull:** as-built ramp survey, machine's rated braking capacity at full payload on grade, route change log

### Secondary breakage (oversize) required on more than roughly three boulders in a single round
- **Usually means:** drill pattern spacing or powder factor for this ground type isn't matching current geology
- **First question:** has this been logged as a drill-and-blast pattern issue, or is each boulder being treated as an isolated one-off?
- **Data to pull:** drill-and-blast pattern for this round, oversize count log by round, geology/ground-type log for the panel

### DPM personal or area sample trending toward or above 160 µg/m³ total carbon (8-hour TWA)
- **Usually means:** ventilation short-circuiting to this heading, a degraded exhaust filter, or extended idle time at a dead-end face
- **First question:** is the machine's exhaust filter within its service interval, and how much idle time (not tram/dig time) did this heading see this shift?
- **Data to pull:** DPM sample trend, filter service log, airflow reading at the face, idle-time log if the machine logs engine-hours by mode

### Persistent hydraulic or brake warning light, especially before a loaded downhill tram
- **Usually means:** a brake system or hydraulic fault that full payload on grade will expose, even if it "held fine" empty
- **First question:** has the machine been derated or pulled from loaded grade work until the fault is diagnosed?
- **Data to pull:** pre-shift inspection record, maintenance log, brake test result at rated payload if available

### Shift tonnage trending materially below plan (roughly >15%) for more than one consecutive shift without a named cause
- **Usually means:** one of cycle time, ore-pass/drawpoint capacity, or oversize frequency hasn't been isolated and is being logged as general downtime
- **First question:** has the shortfall been broken down by cause, or logged as a general "ran slow"?
- **Data to pull:** shift tonnage log against plan, downtime log by category, ore-pass fill-level log, oversize count log

### Operator riding a machine into a heading where the ground control log shows partial scaling only
- **Usually means:** production pressure or habit is overriding the ride-on/remote gate
- **First question:** what specific task required ride-on entry, and could it have been done remotely instead?
- **Data to pull:** ground control log for the heading, task log for the period, any prior similar entries for this operator or crew
