# Red flags

Signals that change today's visit from routine to same-day-escalation, with the question and data that resolve them.

### Chest pain with any cardiac risk factor plus an abnormal vital sign
- **Usually means:** acute coronary syndrome or unstable angina; second most likely is a benign musculoskeletal or GI cause presenting with coincidental vitals noise.
- **First question:** does this meet criteria for immediate ED referral (HEART score, or any hemodynamic instability) rather than office workup?
- **Data to pull:** vitals trend, EKG if available same-visit, prior cardiac history, risk-factor list.

### Sudden "worst headache of life" (thunderclap onset)
- **Usually means:** subarachnoid hemorrhage until ruled out; second most likely is a severe primary headache (migraine, cluster) — but thunderclap onset itself is the red flag, not the pain intensity alone.
- **First question:** is this the first time this exact pattern has occurred, and did it peak within seconds to minutes?
- **Data to pull:** onset timing, any neuro deficit on exam, need for emergent imaging (not office-based workup).

### Unintentional weight loss >5% of body weight over 6–12 months
- **Usually means:** occult malignancy or another serious systemic process; second most likely is depression, an endocrine cause (hyperthyroidism), or unrecognized GI disease.
- **First question:** is the loss truly unintentional and confirmed against a documented prior weight, not patient recall alone?
- **Data to pull:** documented weight trend in the chart, review of systems for GI/endocrine/psychiatric symptoms, basic labs (CBC, CMP, TSH) as a starting panel.

### eGFR decline sustained >5 mL/min/1.73m² per year
- **Usually means:** diabetic nephropathy progressing faster than expected, or a second renal insult (undiagnosed glomerular disease, obstructive process); second most likely is a lab artifact from acute illness or dehydration at the most recent draw.
- **First question:** is this a sustained trend across at least two prior values, or one anomalous draw?
- **Data to pull:** eGFR trend over 12–24 months, urine ACR, current nephrotoxic medication list (NSAIDs, contrast exposure).

### A1c not at goal despite three or more medication classes at adequate doses
- **Usually means:** adherence gap (cost, side effects, regimen complexity) far more often than true medication failure; second most likely is an unrecognized secondary cause or an inappropriately set goal for this patient.
- **First question:** what does pharmacy fill data show about actual pickup pattern, versus what the patient reports?
- **Data to pull:** pharmacy refill history, cost/insurance coverage of current regimen, injection technique if on insulin.

### Same patient presents 3+ times in 4 weeks for different stated complaints
- **Usually means:** an unaddressed psychosocial stressor or hidden agenda driving repeated visits more than three unrelated medical problems; second most likely is a genuinely evolving medical picture that needs a dedicated visit to piece together.
- **First question:** has anyone directly asked what's actually going on at home or at work this month?
- **Data to pull:** visit history pattern, any recent life-event documentation, PHQ-9/GAD-7 if not recently done.

### Severe hypertension (≥180/120) with any symptom
- **Usually means:** hypertensive emergency (with symptoms: headache, vision change, chest pain, confusion) requiring immediate ED referral, versus hypertensive urgency (asymptomatic) manageable with close outpatient follow-up.
- **First question:** are there any symptoms of end-organ involvement, or is this an asymptomatic elevated reading?
- **Data to pull:** repeat manual BP, symptom review, prior BP control history.

### PHQ-9 ≥ 20, or any positive answer on item 9 (suicidal ideation)
- **Usually means:** severe depression requiring same-day safety assessment; a positive item 9 requires direct, explicit follow-up regardless of total score.
- **First question:** does the patient have a specific plan, means, and intent — assessed directly, not inferred?
- **Data to pull:** full PHQ-9 responses, prior psychiatric history, current safety plan if one exists.

### Polypharmacy: 10+ active medications in a patient over 65
- **Usually means:** medication list accumulated across multiple prescribers without a recent consolidated review; second most likely is genuine complex multimorbidity requiring the full list.
- **First question:** for each drug, is there a documented current indication and no met stopping rule?
- **Data to pull:** full active medication list with start dates and prescribing source, recent fall or cognitive-change history.

### New focal neurologic deficit or saddle anesthesia with back pain
- **Usually means:** cauda equina syndrome (surgical emergency) with saddle anesthesia/bowel-bladder symptoms; a focal deficit alone may indicate a different but still urgent radiculopathy.
- **First question:** are there bowel or bladder symptoms, or saddle-distribution numbness, alongside the back pain?
- **Data to pull:** focused neuro exam (strength, reflexes, perianal sensation), need for emergent imaging versus routine outpatient workup.

### Medication refill gap of 30+ days on a chronic-disease drug
- **Usually means:** nonadherence (cost, side effect, or belief-driven) more often than a documented discontinuation; second most likely is a care-transition gap (recent hospitalization, insurance change).
- **First question:** was this an intentional stop discussed with a clinician, or a silent lapse?
- **Data to pull:** pharmacy fill history, most recent hospital discharge summary if applicable.

### Abnormal cancer-screening result (e.g., rising PSA, BI-RADS 4/5 mammogram) without a documented follow-up plan
- **Usually means:** a care-coordination gap — the result was received but the loop wasn't closed — far more often than the result being wrong; treat every abnormal screening result as requiring an explicit, dated next step.
- **First question:** who owns tracking this to completion, and by what date?
- **Data to pull:** result date, any scheduled follow-up imaging or biopsy, registry/tracking system entry.
