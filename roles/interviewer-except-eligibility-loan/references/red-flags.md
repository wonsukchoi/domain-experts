# Interviewer — Red Flags

### Interview duration under 50% of the pretested median for that questionnaire
- **Usually means:** Respondent satisficing (racing through without reading/considering questions), or an interviewer skipping required probes.
- **First question:** Which specific questions or sections did the timestamp show being rushed — was it uniform across the whole interview, or concentrated in one battery?
- **Data to pull:** Question-by-question timestamp log if the CATI/CAPI system captures it; compare against the pretested per-question median.

### Identical response value selected across an entire rating-scale battery ("straight-lining")
- **Usually means:** Respondent disengagement/satisficing, most common in long grid-format batteries late in the interview.
- **First question:** Did engagement (response latency, open-end answer quality) drop off starting at a specific point, suggesting fatigue, or was it present from the start, suggesting a low-effort respondent from the outset?
- **Data to pull:** The full battery's response pattern and the interview's overall completion time relative to expected.

### A quota cell more than 20% behind its pro-rated fill target with under 3 field days remaining
- **Usually means:** That demographic segment is genuinely low-incidence or low-cooperation in the sample frame being used, not a temporary lull.
- **First question:** Is the shortfall due to low incidence (few people in that cell exist in the calling list) or low cooperation (people in that cell are being reached but refusing)?
- **Data to pull:** Contact-rate and refusal-rate breakdown specifically for that cell, separate from the overall study rates.

### Open-ended response that doesn't logically match the closed-ended answer immediately preceding it
- **Usually means:** Either a genuine, interesting nuance in the respondent's actual view, or a data-entry/transcription error.
- **First question:** Does the open-end explanation, read on its own, make internal sense — or does it contradict the closed-ended selection in a way no plausible respondent reasoning explains?
- **Data to pull:** The verbatim open-end text alongside the closed-ended selection, reviewed together rather than each checked in isolation.

### A cluster of break-offs at the same question number across multiple respondents
- **Usually means:** The question itself is confusing, uncomfortable, or too long — a script/questionnaire-design issue, not an interviewer-execution issue.
- **First question:** How many of the total break-offs (as a percentage) cluster at this one question, compared to a roughly even distribution across all questions?
- **Data to pull:** The break-off-point tally across all incomplete interviews for the field period, not just the day's cases.

### An interviewer's overall completion rate is significantly higher than peers working the same sample
- **Usually means:** Either genuinely strong refusal-conversion skill, or script deviation (rewording refusal-prone questions, skipping validity checks) that's inflating completions at the cost of data quality.
- **First question:** Does that interviewer's completed-interview data show the same duration/straight-lining/validity profile as peers, or is it systematically different?
- **Data to pull:** Per-interviewer completion rate alongside per-interviewer validity-flag rate — a high completion rate paired with a low validity-flag rate on a difficult questionnaire is itself worth checking, not just celebrating.

### A respondent answers a screening question inconsistently with a later substantive answer
- **Usually means:** Either a screener misunderstanding (the screening question was ambiguous) or a respondent answering the screener in a way they believed would qualify them (for an incentive, or to avoid seeming to not know something).
- **First question:** Does the substantive answer directly contradict the screener, or is it merely surprising given the screener response — the first case disqualifies the interview, the second may not?
- **Data to pull:** The exact screener question wording and the respondent's verbatim answer to it, not just the coded qualify/disqualify result.

### Respondent requests to skip a specific question but the interview continues past it without a "refused" code
- **Usually means:** Interviewer error in either the skip logic or the disposition coding for that item.
- **First question:** Is the missing "refused" code a one-time data-entry slip, or does it recur across multiple interviews from the same interviewer, suggesting a training gap?
- **Data to pull:** That interviewer's rate of "refused"/"don't know" coding on that specific question compared to the study-wide rate.
