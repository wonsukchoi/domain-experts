# Real estate agent red flags

### Portal lead unanswered for more than 5 minutes after inquiry
- **Usually means:** the lead was routed to voicemail/email triage instead of live response, or the agent was mid-showing with no backup coverage.
- **First question:** "who owns first-response coverage when I'm unreachable — an ISA, a partner, or nobody?"
- **Data to pull:** CRM speed-to-lead report for the trailing 30 days.

### Listing at 45+ days on market against a local median DOM of 18 for that price tier
- **Usually means:** the list price is above what the CMA supports, or showing feedback points to condition/photo problems the agent hasn't acted on.
- **First question:** "what's the showing-to-offer ratio, and what's the recurring theme in showing feedback?"
- **Data to pull:** MLS showing-activity report and the logged feedback from the last 10 showings.

### Seller cites a Zestimate or comparable online estimate more than 10% above the CMA-adjusted range
- **Usually means:** anchoring on an automated valuation model without knowing its published error rate, or a stale off-market estimate.
- **First question:** "when was that number pulled, and is the home showing as on-market or off-market in that tool?"
- **Data to pull:** the CMA's adjusted comps table plus the AVM provider's published accuracy/error-rate disclosure.

### Buyer requests a private showing with no signed buyer-representation agreement on file
- **Usually means:** the agent skipped or forgot the post-2024-settlement compliance step, or the buyer assumes touring is still a free, no-commitment service.
- **First question:** "has the brokerage-approved buyer-representation agreement been reviewed and signed for this buyer?"
- **Data to pull:** brokerage compliance checklist and the signed-agreement log in the transaction platform.

### Buyer's financing letter says "pre-qualification" rather than "pre-approval," or is older than 60 days
- **Usually means:** the file reflects a soft credit pull rather than an underwritten loan, or the buyer's financial picture has changed since the letter was issued.
- **First question:** "has this loan actually been submitted to underwriting, or does the clock start from zero at contract acceptance?"
- **Data to pull:** the lender's letter date and a direct confirmation call to the loan officer.

### Cooperating (buyer's-side) commission offer sits meaningfully below the local market's typical co-op range
- **Usually means:** a strategic net-price play by the seller, or an oversight in how the listing agreement was structured post-settlement.
- **First question:** "was this a deliberate pricing decision the seller understood, or a default that was never revisited?"
- **Data to pull:** local MLS/market co-op compensation averages for comparable listings in the trailing quarter.

### Earnest money deposit under 1% of contract price in a competitive, multiple-offer segment
- **Usually means:** the buyer is undercapitalized relative to the offer price, or simply defaulted to a low round number without strategic intent.
- **First question:** "what's the buyer's liquidity and backup plan if the seller counters on deposit size?"
- **Data to pull:** proof-of-funds documentation and the buyer's stated cash-reserve capacity from the consultation.

### Three or more price reductions on the same listing within 60 days
- **Usually means:** the initial CMA was overridden by seller preference at listing, or the market genuinely shifted and the CMA needs re-running, not just the price.
- **First question:** "did the comps move, or did we ignore them the first time?"
- **Data to pull:** an updated CMA with current comps and the local absorption rate trend.

### GCI paid into the brokerage cap crosses the stated annual threshold mid-transaction
- **Usually means:** the agent's next-deal split flips from percentage-based to a flat transaction fee, and any client-facing net estimate given before the crossing is now wrong.
- **First question:** "has the cap ledger been updated with this closing before quoting take-home on the next one?"
- **Data to pull:** the brokerage's YTD cap-tracking ledger.

### A lead source's cost-per-closed-transaction exceeds roughly 40% of the average net commission on deals from that source
- **Usually means:** the channel's conversion rate has degraded, or the lead cost has risen without a corresponding quality improvement.
- **First question:** "what's this source's trailing-12-month lead-to-close conversion rate, not just this quarter's?"
- **Data to pull:** CRM source-attribution report with cost, lead count, and closed-deal count by channel.

### A buyer's neighborhood shortlist narrows sharply after one conversation, without the buyer stating explicit criteria for the narrowing
- **Usually means:** unconscious steering — comps or listings selected based on assumptions about the buyer rather than their stated preferences.
- **First question:** "what specific, buyer-stated criteria justify excluding the areas that were dropped?"
- **Data to pull:** the buyer consultation notes documenting stated criteria versus the actual properties shown.

### A client asks a question that requires a legal, tax, or appraisal opinion and the agent answers directly rather than routing it
- **Usually means:** discomfort with an "I don't know, let me find out" answer in the moment, not actual expertise to answer safely.
- **First question:** "is this inside license-law scope, or does it need the broker, an attorney, or a CPA?"
- **Data to pull:** the brokerage's escalation policy for out-of-scope questions.
