# Real estate agent vocabulary

### GCI (Gross Commission Income)
The total commission dollars a deal generates before any split with the brokerage. It is not the agent's take-home.
**In use:** "That listing was $12,097 GCI, but after the split and the cap status, my net was about $8,073."
**Common misuse:** Treated as personal income when quoting annual production — an agent's "GCI year" and their actual take-home year can differ by 30% or more depending on split and cap status.

### Cap
The dollar amount of brokerage-share commission an agent pays in a year before the split flips (often to 100% minus a flat transaction fee).
**In use:** "We're at $17,929 of the $23,000 cap — two more average deals and I'm capped for the rest of the year."
**Common misuse:** Ignored mid-year when quoting a client or a lender letter the agent's expected net — the split before and after the cap threshold is not the same number.

### CMA (Comparative Market Analysis)
A price-range estimate built from recent comparable sales, adjusted line-by-line for differences in size, condition, and features — not an appraisal and not a guess.
**In use:** "The CMA supports $430,000 to $433,000; I'm not going to list at $475,000 just because that's the Zestimate."
**Common misuse:** Confused with an appraisal (a licensed appraiser's legal valuation) or presented as a single number without showing the adjusted comps that produced it.

### Absorption rate
The pace at which active inventory in a price tier or area is selling, usually expressed as months of supply; the leading indicator of whether a market favors sellers or buyers.
**In use:** "Absorption rate in this tier is under two months — that's a seller's market, and it should shape how tight we set the escalation cap."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a citywide constant when it varies sharply by price tier and neighborhood within the same market.

### DOM (Days on Market)
The number of days a listing has been active without going under contract; resets on relisting depending on MLS rules.
**In use:** "We're at 21 DOM against a median of 18 for this tier — not alarming yet, but worth watching the next two weeks."
**Common misuse:** Compared against a citywide average instead of the median for the specific price tier and property type, which makes an on-pace listing look slow or a slow one look fine.

### Buyer-Representation Agreement (BRA)
The signed, written agreement establishing a buyer's agency relationship and compensation terms, required before private showings in MLSs implementing the August 2024 NAR settlement rules.
**In use:** "I can't schedule a private tour until the BRA is signed — that's not brokerage preference, it's the MLS rule now."
**Common misuse:** Treated as optional paperwork to "get to later" rather than a precondition for touring, which exposes both agent and buyer to compensation disputes.

### Co-op commission / buyer-broker compensation
The portion of commission a listing offers to a buyer's agent for bringing a buyer, now negotiated directly (often outside the MLS) rather than published as a standard MLS field post-2024-settlement.
**In use:** "We're offering 2.5% co-op on this listing — that's below this submarket's typical range, so let's expect fewer buyer's-agent showings."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to still be a fixed, MLS-published percentage — it is now a negotiated term the listing agent sets deal-by-deal.

### Steering
Directing a client toward or away from specific neighborhoods based on protected-class assumptions, whether through explicit statements or through which listings and comps get shown.
**In use:** "I only show properties matching the buyer's stated criteria — I don't narrow a search based on my own assumptions about who fits where."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to require an explicit discriminatory statement — pattern-based steering through comp and listing selection is the more common real-world exposure.

### Farming
A long-term lead-generation strategy of consistent, repeated marketing to a defined geographic area or demographic niche to become the recognized agent there.
**In use:** "I've farmed the Maple Heights zip code for three years — most of my listings there now come from referrals inside the farm, not cold leads."
**Common misuse:** Expected to produce leads within weeks — farming is a multi-year compounding strategy, not a short-term lead source.

### Sphere of Influence (SOI)
An agent's personal and professional network — past clients, friends, family, community contacts — cultivated as a repeat and referral lead source.
**In use:** "SOI referrals convert at a much higher rate than portal leads, but building a productive SOI takes years of consistent touches."
**Common misuse:** Left uncultivated after the first year or two in the business, treated as a "nice to have" rather than the highest-converting long-term channel.

### Portal lead
An inbound lead purchased or generated through a third-party listing site (Zillow, Realtor.com) rather than sourced from the agent's own network or marketing.
**In use:** "Portal leads cost real money per lead, so the follow-up cadence has to be disciplined enough to justify the spend."
**Common misuse:** Treated as equivalent in quality to a referral lead — portal leads typically convert at a much lower rate and require a longer, more structured follow-up sequence.

### Dual agency / Designated agency
Dual agency: one agent represents both buyer and seller in the same transaction (where state law allows). Designated agency: two agents at the same brokerage each represent one side.
**In use:** "This is a designated-agency deal — my colleague represents the buyer, I represent the seller, and neither of us can advise across the table."
**Common misuse:** Treated as neutral facilitation rather than what it actually is — a disclosed reduction in advocacy duty that both clients must consent to in writing.

### FSBO (For Sale By Owner)
A property being sold without a listing agent, often a lead-generation target for agents but requiring a different conversation than a standard listing pitch.
**In use:** "That FSBO has been sitting for six weeks — worth a value-add call on pricing before pitching representation outright."
**Common misuse:** Approached with a generic listing-appointment script rather than acknowledging the seller's stated reason for going without an agent first.

### Pre-approval vs. pre-qualification
Pre-approval: a lender has pulled credit and reviewed documentation, often with the file submitted to underwriting. Pre-qualification: a self-reported, unverified estimate of borrowing capacity.
**In use:** "I need an underwritten pre-approval, not a pre-qual letter, before we set a 21-day financing contingency deadline."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably when structuring offer deadlines — a pre-qualification letter carries materially more financing risk than an underwritten pre-approval.

### Contingency
A condition in a purchase contract that must be satisfied (or waived) for the deal to proceed — inspection, financing, appraisal, or sale-of-buyer's-home being the common types.
**In use:** "Waiving the appraisal contingency here only makes sense if the buyer's reserves can cover a real gap, not just the best-case scenario."
**Common misuse:** Treated as boilerplate paperwork rather than a specific, priced allocation of risk between the parties.
