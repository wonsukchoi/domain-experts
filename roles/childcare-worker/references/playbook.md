# Childcare Worker — Playbook

## Ratio-reconciliation table (filled example)

Mixed-age room, single worker, checking whether current headcount is in compliance. Rule: when ages are mixed in one group, the ratio is governed by the youngest age band present, applied to the whole group.

| Age band | Children present | State ratio (this band) | Staff required if this band governs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infant (0-12mo) | 2 | 1:4 | 1 (2÷4, rounded up) |
| Toddler (1-2yr) | 3 | 1:6 | 1 (3÷6, rounded up) |
| Preschool (3-5yr) | 4 | 1:10 | 1 (4÷10, rounded up) |
| **Governing band: Infant** | **9 total** | **1:4 applies to all 9** | **3 (9÷4, rounded up)** |

Reading the table: don't add the per-band staff-required column (1+1+1=3 is a coincidence here, not the method) — the correct number comes from applying the *youngest present band's ratio* to the *total headcount*, which is 9÷4 = 2.25, rounded up to 3.

## Transition re-count checklist

At every transition point, re-verify before proceeding to the next activity:

1. Count children physically in the room/area right now (not the sign-in sheet count).
2. Confirm the youngest age band still present — a departure can change which ratio governs.
3. Confirm staff physically present and positioned for sightline — not "on the schedule," but in the room.
4. If ratio is out of compliance for any reason (staff break, supply run, late arrival of a second worker), note the start time and get coverage before continuing normal activities — don't wait until the gap resolves itself.

## Incident-report template (filled example)

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Child | [initials, per privacy policy] |
| Date/time | 2026-03-14, 10:22am |
| Location | Outdoor play area, climbing structure |
| Mechanism | Fell approx. 2 ft from the second rung, landed on right side |
| Body area | Right forearm — no visible deformity, child moved it freely after ~1 min |
| Witnessed by | Worker A (direct), Worker B (heard, turned within 3 sec) |
| Immediate action | Comforted, checked for deformity/inability to move, applied ice pack, monitored 30 min |
| Head involved? | No |
| Parent notified | Called at 10:35am, reached voicemail; texted 10:36am; verbal handoff at pickup 3:15pm |
| Follow-up | None required — full range of motion, no swelling by pickup |

**Report-vs-mention rule applied here:** even though the child recovered within a minute and showed no lasting symptoms, this gets a written report because the mechanism (a fall from height with unknown landing until assessed) meets the report threshold — the decision is made at the moment of the incident, not revised downward because the outcome looked mild in hindsight.

## Behavior-pattern log (filled example)

| Date | Time | Trigger/context | Behavior | Environmental note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3/10 | 8:50am | Drop-off transition | Crying, refusing to separate | New worker present that day |
| 3/11 | 8:55am | Drop-off transition | Crying, clinging | Same as above |
| 3/12 | 4:45pm | End-of-day, low staff | Hitting another child | Room combined with another group, high noise |
| 3/13 | 8:52am | Drop-off transition | Brief protest, self-resolved | Familiar worker present |

Pattern read: the 3/10-3/11 drop-off distress correlates with an unfamiliar worker, not the transition itself (3/13 with a familiar worker was fine) — the fix is consistent staffing at drop-off, not a "clingy kid" label. The 3/12 incident is a different pattern (end-of-day, combined groups, high noise) — a separate environmental cause, not the same underlying issue as the drop-off pattern.
