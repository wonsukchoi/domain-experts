# Childcare Worker — Red Flags

### Ratio recount not done at a transition (any transition — indoor/outdoor, activity change, meal, nap)
- **Usually means:** Staff assumed the morning headcount still holds; a departure, late arrival, or staff break went unaccounted for.
- **First question:** "What's the headcount and youngest age band present right now, not at drop-off?"
- **Data to pull:** Sign-in/sign-out sheet timestamps, current staff schedule/break log.

### A worker is "in the room" but not positioned to see or reach every child
- **Usually means:** Paperwork, phone use, or conversation is competing with active supervision; the worker is present but not scanning.
- **First question:** "Can you see every child from where you're standing right now?"
- **Data to pull:** Room layout/sightline map, staff positioning during the last observed period.

### Any head injury, regardless of how quickly the child recovered
- **Usually means:** Nothing sinister necessarily — but head injuries require documentation and a monitoring window regardless of the child's apparent recovery speed.
- **First question:** "Did the head make contact, and was there any loss of consciousness or altered behavior, even briefly?"
- **Data to pull:** Incident report, monitoring-window notes (typically checked at set intervals for a defined period).

### A behavior incident is being handled the same way every time without checking for a pattern
- **Usually means:** The worker is treating each incident as isolated rather than looking for a shared trigger (time, transition, specific pairing of children, staffing change).
- **First question:** "Has this happened before, and if so, what was different or the same each time?"
- **Data to pull:** Behavior-pattern log across the past 1-2 weeks.

### A late-pickup situation is being handled by individual judgment rather than the written policy
- **Usually means:** A worker is making an exception based on knowing the family, not following the documented late-pickup/release protocol.
- **First question:** "What does the written policy say, and are we following it exactly?"
- **Data to pull:** Program's late-pickup policy, authorized-pickup list, prior late-pickup incidents for this family.

### A child is released to someone not on the authorized pickup list
- **Usually means:** Either a genuine emergency substitution the parent should have called ahead about, or a lapse in checking the list.
- **First question:** "Is this person on the authorized list, and if not, do we have verified parent authorization for today specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** Authorized-pickup list, any same-day parent communication authorizing a change.

### A mandated-reporting trigger (suspected abuse/neglect indicator) is being discussed informally instead of escalated
- **Usually means:** Uncertainty about whether an observation rises to the reporting threshold is causing hesitation or delay.
- **First question:** "Does this meet a mandated-reporting trigger under our state's definition, and have we escalated to a supervisor immediately?"
- **Data to pull:** State mandated-reporter training materials, program's escalation policy — this is not a wait-and-see judgment call.

### Staffing coverage for a break or errand is arranged verbally and not confirmed before the covering worker leaves
- **Usually means:** A gap in coverage is about to open because "I'll be quick" replaced an actual ratio check.
- **First question:** "Who is covering, and does the room stay in ratio the moment you leave?"
- **Data to pull:** Real-time headcount and ratio calculation before departure, not after a gap is noticed.
