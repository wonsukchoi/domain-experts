# Finish Trades Helper — Red Flags

### Cement plaster mud stiffening on the board before it's applied, more than ~60-90 min from mixing
- **Usually means:** the batch was sized for normal-condition working life on a hot, dry, or windy day, or the plasterer's pace dropped mid-shift.
- **First question:** what time was this batch mixed, and what's today's temp/RH/wind been running?
- **Data to pull:** the day's batch-mix log against today's weather log — not a guess from memory.

### Prep on a pre-1978 structure disturbing more painted surface than 6 sq ft interior / 20 sq ft exterior with no containment up
- **Usually means:** the area was eyeballed as "small" instead of measured, or nobody checked the building's age before starting.
- **First question:** has this structure's build year been confirmed, and has anyone measured the actual disturbed area?
- **Data to pull:** the permit or listing record for build year, and a tape measurement of the scrape/sand area.

### Painter's tape still on more than 24 hours after application, especially in direct sun
- **Usually means:** the crew moved to the next area and lost track of which sections were masked when.
- **First question:** when was this section taped, and has it been in direct sun since?
- **Data to pull:** the day's masking log or, if none exists, a visual check for adhesive yellowing/hardening at the edge.

### A wallcovering seam bubbling or lifting within hours of hanging
- **Usually means:** the strip was under-booked (hung before paste activated) or over-booked (paste skinned before hanging), in roughly that order of likelihood.
- **First question:** how long between pasting and hanging this strip, and what did the manufacturer's instruction sheet call for?
- **Data to pull:** the product's book-time spec against today's actual timing, if logged.

### Two or more paint cans of the "same" color from different batch/lot numbers loaded without boxing
- **Usually means:** the crew ran short mid-job and grabbed a second can without checking the lot number on the lid.
- **First question:** do the lot numbers on the cans in use actually match?
- **Data to pull:** the lot/batch numbers stamped on each can's lid.

### Substrate moisture reading above the coating manufacturer's ceiling (commonly ~15% MC for exterior wood/acrylic systems) before a painter starts
- **Usually means:** a hidden leak or recent rain, not surface dampness that will dry by application time.
- **First question:** where exactly did this reading come from, and has it been rechecked in a different spot?
- **Data to pull:** the moisture meter log across multiple points on the wall, not a single reading.

### Dry-cutting or grinding stucco lath, EIFS, or cementitious board without water or a vacuum shroud
- **Usually means:** the water line or dust collector wasn't hooked up, or the cut was treated as too quick to bother with controls.
- **First question:** why isn't dust control connected for this specific cut?
- **Data to pull:** the task's Table 1 control requirement from the site's silica exposure control plan.

### RRP containment area with no visible warning signage or HEPA vacuum on-site
- **Usually means:** containment was set up in a rush and the compliance steps (signage, verified equipment) got skipped, not just the plastic sheeting.
- **First question:** is the HEPA vacuum actually on-site and functioning, or was regular shop-vac substituted?
- **Data to pull:** the containment setup checklist for today's job, if one exists.

### A cement plaster batch retempered by adding water after it starts stiffening
- **Usually means:** the crew is trying to stretch material rather than accepting a discard, treating plaster like mortar.
- **First question:** has this batch already begun to stiffen, or is it still within its original working-life window?
- **Data to pull:** the batch-mix log's start time against current time.

### Wallpaper pattern not matching at a seam by more than a fraction of an inch
- **Usually means:** drops were cut from mismatched dye lots, or the wall's out-of-plumb condition wasn't accounted for in the hang sequence.
- **First question:** are both strips from the same roll/dye lot, and has the wall's plumb been checked?
- **Data to pull:** the roll numbers on the strips in question and a level reading at the seam.

### Spray equipment tip or strainer visibly clogged mid-job, producing an uneven fan pattern
- **Usually means:** paint wasn't strained before loading, or the boxed batch has debris from an unclean container.
- **First question:** was this paint strained before it went into the sprayer?
- **Data to pull:** none — a test spray on scrap material confirms the fan pattern before returning to the wall.

### Fresh scratch or brown coat losing moisture visibly faster than expected (chalky, dry-looking surface within 1-2 hours)
- **Usually means:** wind or low humidity is pulling water out of the coat faster than the cure schedule assumes.
- **First question:** what's today's RH and wind, and is fogging/covering already happening?
- **Data to pull:** today's weather log against the ASTM C926 normal-condition cure assumptions.
