# Mobile Heavy Equipment Mechanic — Red Flags

### Cylinder drifts more than roughly 1 in./60 sec under load with the valve held neutral
- **Usually means:** internal seal bypass in the cylinder, less commonly a leaking directional valve spool
- **First question:** does the drift persist when the cylinder is isolated from the valve by blocking its hose?
- **Data to pull:** cylinder drift/isolation test result, valve spool clearance inspection, cylinder disassembly seal condition

### Cycle time up more than roughly 30% from baseline with relief pressure testing in spec
- **Usually means:** pump volumetric efficiency loss (internal wear), less commonly a downstream flow restriction
- **First question:** what does case-drain flow read against rated pump flow?
- **Data to pull:** in-line flow test at rated rpm, case-drain flow measurement, cycle-time baseline log

### Case-drain flow exceeding roughly 10% of rated pump or motor flow
- **Usually means:** internal wear (piston, vane, or gear clearance) in that component
- **First question:** has case-drain flow been trended over the last several PM intervals, or is this a single reading?
- **Data to pull:** case-drain flow history, oil analysis (metal content) for that component

### Track chain pitch elongation at or beyond roughly 3%
- **Usually means:** chain nearing end of service life, risking sprocket tooth ("hook") damage if run further
- **First question:** what's the current sprocket tooth profile reading — is it tracking the chain wear or still within spec?
- **Data to pull:** chain pitch gauge measurement, sprocket profile gauge reading, undercarriage inspection history

### Sprocket tooth profile showing early "hook" wear pattern
- **Usually means:** the chain running against it has been past its own replacement point for some time
- **First question:** which wore first — is this a chain problem that migrated to the sprocket, or a sprocket-alone issue?
- **Data to pull:** sprocket tooth profile gauge, chain pitch measurement, prior undercarriage service records

### Roller or idler flange thickness below roughly 50% of original
- **Usually means:** approaching the replace-before-failure point; pushed further, side-load on turns raises derailment risk
- **First question:** what's the measured flange thickness against the OEM new/wear-limit spec?
- **Data to pull:** roller/idler wear gauge reading, percent-worn undercarriage report

### Hydraulic fluid sample reading worse than the ISO 4406 target code for the circuit's most sensitive component
- **Usually means:** a breach during a recent repair (hose swap, cylinder reseal, pump replacement) or a bypassed/failed filter
- **First question:** was this circuit opened for any repair since the last clean sample?
- **Data to pull:** particle-count sample, filter bypass-indicator history, repair log for the circuit

### Same hydraulic component (valve, cylinder, or pump) replaced twice within roughly 500 operating hours
- **Usually means:** an unaddressed contamination or misalignment root cause, not a defective part
- **First question:** was a fluid sample and filter check done at the time of the prior repair?
- **Data to pull:** prior repair order, current ISO cleanliness sample, mounting/alignment inspection

### Structural crack or weld failure visible on a load-bearing member (boom, arm, main frame) during a PM
- **Usually means:** cyclic fatigue loading, or an earlier field weld repair done without an engineered procedure
- **First question:** is this a load-bearing member, and has it been welded before without an OEM-approved repair procedure?
- **Data to pull:** crack length and location, prior weld repair records, OEM structural repair procedure

### Hydraulic oil temperature sustained above roughly 180°F (82°C) under normal load
- **Usually means:** fouled or restricted cooler core, fan drive/clutch not engaging, or a relief valve dumping continuously
- **First question:** is the cooler core clean, and is the fan clutch engaging correctly under load?
- **Data to pull:** hydraulic temperature trend, cooler differential pressure, relief-valve cracking-pressure test

### Field-service dispatch requested where mobile cost plus travel time clearly exceeds a tow-to-shop quote
- **Usually means:** default-to-dispatch habit rather than an actual cost comparison having been run
- **First question:** has anyone calculated the break-even for this specific distance, repair scope, and idle-crew rate?
- **Data to pull:** mobile dispatch rate and travel time, tow/lowboy quote, parts-in-stock status on the service truck vs. shop

### Undercarriage components replaced as full matched sets at well under roughly 40% measured wear
- **Usually means:** overcorrection after a prior premature-failure incident, not an actual wear-driven replacement
- **First question:** what's the current percent-worn measurement, and does it justify replacement now versus at the planned 50–80% band?
- **Data to pull:** percent-worn undercarriage report, replacement history and stated justification for the prior call
