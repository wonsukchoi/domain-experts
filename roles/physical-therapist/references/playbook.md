# Physical Therapist — Playbook

## Oswestry Disability Index (ODI) scoring worksheet

10 sections (pain intensity, personal care, lifting, walking, sitting, standing, sleeping, social life, traveling, and sex life), each scored 0 (no limitation) to 5 (maximum limitation).

| Section | Raw score (0–5) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Pain intensity | 3 | Moderate pain, varies through day |
| Personal care | 2 | Some difficulty, manages independently |
| Lifting | 4 | Can only lift very light weights |
| Walking | 2 | Pain prevents walking more than 1 mile |
| Sitting | 3 | Pain prevents sitting more than 1 hour |
| Standing | 3 | Pain prevents standing more than 1 hour |
| Sleeping | 2 | Sleep mildly disturbed |
| Social life | 2 | Normal social life, increased pain |
| Traveling | 3 | Extra pain, but manages journeys over 2 hours |
| Sex life | N/A | Excluded — not applicable, drop from denominator |

**Scoring:** sum applicable raw scores ÷ (5 × number of applicable sections) × 100.
Example above: (3+2+4+2+3+3+2+2+3) = 24 ÷ (5×9=45) × 100 = **53.3%** — severe disability band.

**Interpretation bands:** 0–20% minimal, 21–40% moderate, 41–60% severe, 61–80% crippled, 81–100% bed-bound or exaggerating.

**MCID:** commonly cited at approximately 10 points on the 0–100 scale — a repeat score must move by at least this much to be treated as real change, not measurement noise.

## Goniometry normative range-of-motion reference (adult, approximate)

| Joint / motion | Normal range |
|---|---|
| Shoulder flexion | 0–180° |
| Shoulder abduction | 0–180° |
| Elbow flexion | 0–145° |
| Hip flexion | 0–120° |
| Knee flexion | 0–135° |
| Knee extension | 0° (full) |
| Ankle dorsiflexion | 0–20° |
| Lumbar flexion | 0–60° |
| Cervical rotation | 0–80° each direction |

A finding within 10° of normative and pain-free is generally not a treatment target on its own; a finding more than 15–20° below normative, especially if unilateral, is evidence supporting a specific structural or mobility deficit.

## Progression / plateau decision sequence

1. Re-administer the same standardized outcome measure under comparable conditions (same time relative to activity, same version of the instrument) at each reassessment interval.
2. Compare the change to the instrument's published MCID.
3. Change ≥ MCID and moving toward the functional goal → continue plan, may progress exercise intensity/complexity.
4. Change < MCID for 1 assessment → continue plan unchanged, reassess at next interval (single low-change points can be noise).
5. Change < MCID for 2 consecutive assessments → stop and reassess: re-examine the working diagnosis, modify the intervention approach, check adherence/barriers, or refer back to the physician.
6. Functional goals met and sustained across 2 consecutive visits → initiate discharge planning regardless of pain-scale status, per the pain-lags-function principle.
