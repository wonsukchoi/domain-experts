# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced natural sciences manager notices instantly. Load this when triaging a research portfolio or results-integrity question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### A project continuation is argued primarily on prior investment or team expertise
- **Usually means:** sunk cost or emotional investment is substituting for a check against the pre-set go/no-go criterion
- **First question:** "What does the pre-set go/no-go criterion say about the current data, independent of how much has already been spent?"
- **Data to pull:** the original go/no-go criteria document and the current interim results against it

### A striking positive result is being used to justify a major resource decision before replication
- **Usually means:** the result is being treated as a finding when it's still a hypothesis at the single-experiment stage
- **First question:** "Has this been independently replicated, and if not, does the size of the decision justify waiting for replication first?"
- **Data to pull:** the replication tracking log for this specific result

### Internal reporting/recognition consistently highlights positive results and omits or buries null ones
- **Usually means:** a publication-bias-style dynamic is being created inside the organization, training the team to produce or overstate positive findings
- **First question:** "When was the last time a null result was presented with the same visibility as a positive one in a team review?"
- **Data to pull:** recent internal research review agendas/materials, checking the ratio and framing of positive vs. null results presented

### A legacy project continues to receive its historical budget allocation without a recent expected-information-value review
- **Usually means:** inertia, not current merit, is driving the allocation
- **First question:** "When was this project's expected information value last scored against current alternatives, not just against its own history?"
- **Data to pull:** the portfolio scoring worksheet's last-updated date for this project

### A research direction shift is presented as purely scientific when it coincides with a grant renewal or publication opportunity
- **Usually means:** fundability or publication strategy may be silently driving a decision framed as scientific priority
- **First question:** "If funding/publication considerations were removed from this decision, would the same direction still be the top scientific priority?"
- **Data to pull:** the stated rationale for the direction change and its timing relative to grant/publication deadlines

### A go/no-go decision is being made without a criterion that was set before the data came in
- **Usually means:** the decision is at high risk of being rationalized after the fact to match whatever outcome the team prefers
- **First question:** "Was a specific go/no-go threshold documented before this data was collected, or is one being defined now to match the result?"
- **Data to pull:** program planning documentation dated before the checkpoint in question

### A single lab's internal replication is being treated as equivalent to independent replication for a high-stakes claim
- **Usually means:** the verification bar may not match the stakes — same-team replication doesn't rule out shared systematic error or bias the way an independent lab's replication does
- **First question:** "Given what this result would drive if acted on, does internal replication meet the appropriate verification bar, or does this need an independent check?"
- **Data to pull:** the stakes assessment for the claim and the verification level actually performed
