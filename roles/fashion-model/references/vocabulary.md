# Vocabulary

### Comp card (composite card)
A small, curated print or digital spread — usually 4 to 6 images plus measurements — that functions as a model's working résumé, distinct from a single headshot.
**In use:** "Send the client the updated comp card, the beauty shot from March replaced the old one."
**Common misuse:** treating it as a portfolio (the full range of tear sheets and editorial work) rather than the tight, agency-curated pitch document it actually is.

### Usage (usage rights)
The specific territory, media channel, and time period a client is licensed to run a model's image for, as distinct from the fee for the shoot day itself.
**In use:** "The rate covers the shoot, but we still need to price the usage — national, digital only, twelve months."
**Common misuse:** treating "usage" and "day rate" as one number, so a broad usage grant gets priced as if it were the shoot day alone.

### Buyout
A flat fee that grants the client unlimited or perpetual use of the images, in any medium, with no expiration.
**In use:** "That's not a one-year license they're asking for, that's a buyout — price it at the buyout multiplier, not the annual one."
**Common misuse:** calling any flat-fee shoot a "buyout" even when the usage is actually time-limited and channel-scoped.

### Day rate
The base fee for a model's time on a specific shoot day, before usage, exclusivity, or any other add-on is priced.
**In use:** "Day rate's $2,500 — now let's talk usage before we quote a total."
**Common misuse:** treating the day rate as the full value of a booking and negotiating only that number while usage and exclusivity go unpriced.

### TFP (Time For Print / Trade For Print)
An unpaid, collaborative shoot — model, photographer, and often stylist or MUA all trading time for portfolio images, with no client or usage fee involved.
**In use:** "That test was TFP, everyone split the images, nobody got paid — it was for the book."
**Common misuse:** an agency or client labeling paid commercial work "TFP" or "test" to avoid paying a day rate for what is functionally a client-facing shoot.

### Exclusivity (category exclusivity)
A contractual restriction preventing a model from working for a competing brand in a defined product category for a set period, priced as its own line item.
**In use:** "They want six months of swimwear exclusivity — that's a separate fee, not a courtesy that comes with the booking."
**Common misuse:** treating exclusivity as an unpaid condition of taking the job rather than a time-boxed restriction with its own negotiated price and expiration date.

### Option (first option, second option)
A client's provisional hold on a model's date before the booking is confirmed; options are ranked (first option outranks second) and expire on a stated date if not converted to a confirmed booking.
**In use:** "We're on first option for the 14th — don't confirm anything else until they release or convert it."
**Common misuse:** treating an option as a confirmed job and turning down other work before the client has actually converted or released the hold.

### Mother agency
The agency that first signs and develops a model, then places them with market-specific agencies (New York, Paris, Milan, Tokyo) for local bookings, taking a percentage of the placement agency's commission rather than booking clients directly.
**In use:** "My mother agency in Sydney gets a split of whatever the Paris agency earns off me, they don't book Paris castings themselves."
**Common misuse:** conflating the mother agency with the placement agency, or assuming the mother agency's cut is an additional full commission charged directly to the model rather than a split of the placement agency's fee.

### Chargebacks
Costs an agency advances on a model's behalf (comp card printing, travel, lodging) and later deducts from the model's earnings.
**In use:** "The comp card reprint and the rush courier came out as chargebacks on this statement — check they match what we agreed to cap."
**Common misuse:** assuming a booking's gross fee equals take-home pay without checking for chargebacks deducted after the commission split.

### Sample size
The garment size a design house cuts for runway, fitting, and photo samples — not a universal size, and it shifts by market and brand.
**In use:** "Their sample size runs French 34, that's a US 2, not the US 4 the last house used."
**Common misuse:** treating "sample size" as a single fixed number across the industry rather than a brand- and market-specific cutting standard.

### Digital replica
An AI-generated or otherwise synthetic reproduction of a model's likeness, voice, or image, distinct from a photograph or video of the model actually shot on set.
**In use:** "That clause covers a digital replica, not just the shoot images — it needs its own consent and its own fee."
**Common misuse:** assuming a broad, standard usage clause automatically covers AI-generated or synthetic versions of the model's likeness without a separate consent and price.

### Go-see
An informal casting walk-through where a model shows their book and comp card to a client or casting director, typically without a formal audition or reading.
**In use:** "Three go-sees this afternoon, just bring the book, nobody's asking you to do anything on camera."
**Common misuse:** treating a go-see like a high-stakes formal interview requiring the same preparation as a callback or fitting.

### Editorial rate
The (often below-commercial) day rate paid for magazine editorial work, priced for prestige and portfolio value rather than commercial reach.
**In use:** "It's an editorial rate, not a commercial one — we're doing it for the tear sheet, not the paycheck."
**Common misuse:** comparing an editorial day rate directly against a commercial day rate as if they represent the same market value, rather than two different pricing logics.

### Direct booking
A client engaging a model directly rather than through the agency's normal invoicing and commission process.
**In use:** "That's a direct booking request — route it through the agency anyway, the exclusivity agreement doesn't have a carve-out for going around them."
**Common misuse:** treating any direct client contact as a legitimate booking path without checking whether it conflicts with an existing exclusive agency agreement.
