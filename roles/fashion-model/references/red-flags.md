# Red Flags

### Any request for payment before a confirmed client booking (registration fee, "modeling convention" ticket, portfolio-development fee)
- **Usually means:** the FTC's documented advance-fee scam pattern, or a pay-to-play "school" masquerading as a placement path.
- **First question:** what specific booking or client has already committed to using this model?
- **Data to pull:** the agency's named client roster and credit history; FTC modeling-scam consumer alerts.

### Contract silent on usage scope, territory, or duration for the images
- **Usually means:** the client intends a de facto broad or perpetual grant priced at day-rate cost, because the model didn't require the scope in writing.
- **First question:** what territory, what media channels, and for how long?
- **Data to pull:** the signed model release/usage clause; comparable past bookings' itemized usage lines.

### Chargeback statement with unitemized "development costs" exceeding a meaningful share of a booking's earnings
- **Usually means:** the agency is financing comp cards, travel, or lodging without a pre-agreed cap, or double-billing an expense already covered elsewhere.
- **First question:** what specific expense, on what date, approved by whom, at what capped amount?
- **Data to pull:** the itemized earnings statement; the original advance agreement.

### A digital-replica or AI-generation clause folded into a general usage grant
- **Usually means:** the client is attempting to acquire synthetic-likeness or AI-training rights without the separate consent and compensation the deal actually requires.
- **First question:** does this clause name "digital replica," "synthetic," or "AI-generated" explicitly, and is it priced as its own line?
- **Data to pull:** full contract text; NY Fashion Workers Act digital-replica consent requirements (if the booking touches that jurisdiction).

### Agency won't provide a written contract within the jurisdiction's required window (45 days under NY's Fashion Workers Act)
- **Usually means:** an unregistered or non-compliant management company operating outside the statutory floor.
- **First question:** is this agency registered as a management company with the relevant state labor department?
- **Data to pull:** the state's management-company registry; the agency's registration date.

### Model's net payout differs from the client's total invoice by more than roughly 35–40%
- **Usually means:** a stacked double commission (client-side service fee plus model-side commission) compounded by undisclosed chargebacks.
- **First question:** what is the exact commission structure on both the client side and the model side of this specific booking?
- **Data to pull:** the booking confirmation and commission ledger, compared against the agency's disclosed rate card.

### Category-exclusivity clause with no stated end date or product-category boundary
- **Usually means:** an open-ended non-compete that blocks future bookings indefinitely for a fee priced as a single job.
- **First question:** what specific category and territory, and what calendar date does this expire?
- **Data to pull:** the signed exclusivity rider.

### Model still doing exclusively unpaid test shoots well past the first 10–20 bookings of a career
- **Usually means:** the book isn't converting to paid castings, or the agency is relying on free labor instead of escalating to paid work.
- **First question:** how many paid bookings has this model had in the last 90 days?
- **Data to pull:** the booking history log; the agency's stated development plan for this model.

### Agency pressure on measurements or weight with no documented health policy behind it
- **Usually means:** an informal body-standard push outside any published charter, carrying real health risk with no medical involvement.
- **First question:** is this request grounded in a written policy (e.g., the LVMH/Kering charter or CFDA guidelines), or is it an unstated preference?
- **Data to pull:** the agency's written health policy, if one exists; comparison against the relevant industry charter's thresholds.

### Request to extend a call time or add a reshoot with no additional pay discussed
- **Usually means:** unpriced scope creep — the booking confirmation didn't account for overtime, and nobody is going back to fix that before it happens again.
- **First question:** what was the agreed call time in the booking confirmation, and is overtime priced in it?
- **Data to pull:** the booking confirmation's call-time line; the signed on-set time log.

### Casting or client requesting a meeting alone, off-site, outside standard casting hours
- **Usually means:** a documented safety pattern flagged repeatedly in Model Alliance survey data on harassment risk.
- **First question:** is this casting on the agency's official schedule, with a booker aware of the location and time?
- **Data to pull:** the agency's booking calendar; the original casting notice.
