# Playbook

Booking-structuring templates a working model or booker actually fills in, with rate/usage tables, a negotiation ladder, and clock-tracking for exclusivity and options. Adapt the numbers to the specific market and category; the structures don't change.

## 1. Usage multiplier table (heuristic, apply on top of day rate)

| Usage scope | Multiplier on day rate | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Editorial (single magazine issue, no ongoing use) | 1x (often below commercial day rate entirely) | Paid low for prestige/portfolio value — not comparable to commercial pricing (vocabulary: editorial rate). |
| Regional/local print or digital, 3 months | 1.5–2x | Small footprint; lower end of the range. |
| National print + digital, 1 year | 3x | Default assumption when a client says "standard usage" with no written scope. |
| National print + digital, multi-year (2–3 yr) | 5–6x | Confirm exact end date in writing; auto-renewal clauses need a cap. |
| Unlimited / perpetual buyout, any medium | 8–10x | Client's first buyout offer is anchored low; this is the counter-anchor, not the ask price. |
| Social-only paid boosting/amplification | +20–30% of base usage fee | Priced separately from organic usage; confirm ad spend budget/duration. |
| Category exclusivity, 6 months, one category | +25% of (day rate + usage) | Scale down for shorter windows or a narrower product line. |
| Digital replica / AI-generated likeness | Separate negotiated line, not a multiplier | Requires standalone written consent (NY Fashion Workers Act default); do not fold into a general usage clause. |

[heuristic — trade-consensus figures, needs practitioner check against a specific agency/market]

## 2. Negotiation fallback ladder (filled example)

Prepare every negotiation with a ranked concession order before the call.

```
BOOKING: National activewear digital campaign, 1-day shoot
ANCHOR ASK: $3,000 day rate + 1-yr national digital usage (3x = $9,000) + no exclusivity = $12,000
FALLBACK 1 (if usage term resisted): drop to 6-month national digital usage (2x = $6,000),
  total $9,000 — usage duration is the first thing to trade, not the day rate.
FALLBACK 2 (if total resisted further): hold day rate and usage at Fallback 1, add a
  3-month category exclusivity at +15% ($1,350) instead of a rate cut — trade scope
  for money, not money for nothing.
FALLBACK 3 (walk-away floor): day rate alone at $3,000 with usage capped at single-channel,
  90-day digital only — below this the shoot day isn't worth the exclusivity or scheduling
  cost against other bookings; decline or refer to another model on the roster.
NEVER TRADE: payment terms (50% on booking confirmation, balance within 30 days of shoot),
  written usage scope, digital-replica consent as a separate line.
```

**Ranking rule:** usage duration and exclusivity scope are the primary negotiation levers — trade those before touching the day rate, since the day rate is the easiest number for a client to benchmark against a rate card and the hardest to move.

## 3. Double-commission worked structure

For a booking with a **$12,500 gross fee** (day rate + usage + exclusivity, itemized):

1. Confirm both sides of the commission before quoting a net figure to the model: client-side agency service fee (commonly 20%, billed on top, doesn't touch the model's number) and model-side commission (commonly 20%, deducted from the model's gross).
2. Client total invoice: $12,500 × 1.20 = **$15,000**.
3. Model gross after commission: $12,500 × 0.80 = **$10,000**.
4. **Reconciliation check:** model's net as a share of client spend should be reported to the model explicitly (here, $10,000 / $15,000 ≈ 67% before chargebacks) — a model told only "20% commission" will expect 80% of client spend, not two-thirds.
5. Subtract any chargebacks (see §5) after computing the commission split, never before — chargebacks are deducted from the model's post-commission gross, not from the pre-commission booking fee.

## 4. Exclusivity & option clock tracker (template, filled)

One row per active clause. Review weekly; anything inside 14 days of a threshold gets flagged in bold.

| Client/Booking | Clause type | Start date | Scope | End date | Days remaining | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brand A swim campaign | Category exclusivity | Jun 1 | Swimwear, national | Dec 1 | 9 | **Flag — decline any swimwear casting before Dec 1** |
| Brand B beauty | First option (unconfirmed) | Jul 10 | Single shoot date, Aug 3 | Aug 3 (auto-expires if not confirmed) | 3 | **Flag — call booker for confirm/release** |
| Brand C activewear | Digital-replica consent | Jul 1 | 1-year AI-generated social assets | Jul 1 next year | 340 | OK |
| Brand D catalog | National usage (no exclusivity) | May 15 | Print + digital, 1 yr | May 15 next year | 300 | OK |

**Rule:** an option that isn't confirmed or released by its stated date is treated as expired — do not hold a date open past the option's own deadline waiting for a client decision.

## 5. Chargeback worksheet (before accepting any advance)

Run before agreeing to let an agency advance and later deduct any cost.

1. List the specific expense (comp card reprint, travel, lodging, courier) and its dollar cap — a chargeback agreement with no cap is not itemized, it's a blank check.
2. Confirm the cap in writing before the expense is incurred, not after the statement arrives.
3. Request the deduction appear as its own line on the next earnings statement, tied to the specific booking or development activity it funded.
4. Compare cumulative chargebacks against cumulative bookings quarterly — if chargebacks are trending toward or past total earnings, escalate to the agency in writing before taking further test shoots that could add to the balance.

## 6. Advance-fee scam pre-flight (before signing with any agency, scout, or "modeling search")

1. Confirm the agency's registration status if operating in a jurisdiction that requires it (e.g., New York management-company registration under the Fashion Workers Act).
2. Ask directly: what specific booking or client has already committed to using this model? A real placement path names a client; a scam path names a "convention" or "development program."
3. Any request for payment before a confirmed booking — registration fee, convention ticket, portfolio-development fee — is declined by default. No legitimate agency charges a model up front.
4. If a mother-agency split is proposed, get the split percentage and the identity of the placement agency in writing before signing anything.
