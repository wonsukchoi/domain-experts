# Red Flags

### Exposure index more than one step outside the vendor's target range (e.g., EI target 300-450, reading <250 or >550)
- **Usually means:** Anatomy of interest wasn't fully over the active AEC chamber, or field size/collimation was off; second most likely is a genuine technique-factor error (wrong kVp/mAs entered).
- **First question:** Was the AEC chamber selection correct for this projection and is the anatomy centered over it?
- **Data to pull:** Post-exposure positioning image against the collimated field, chamber-selection log for the exposure, and the technique factors actually used vs the chart.

### Repeat/reject rate on a single room trending above 8-10% over a rolling month
- **Usually means:** Detector calibration, grid or SID alignment drift, or a tube/collimator hardware issue; second most likely is a genuine positioning-skill gap concentrated in one technologist or exam type.
- **First question:** Is the spike concentrated in exposure-reason codes (points to equipment) or positioning-reason codes (points to technique/training)?
- **Data to pull:** Reason-code breakdown by technologist and by exam type, and date of last physicist QA/calibration check on that room.

### Gonadal or fetal shield visibly overlying the region of clinical interest on a pelvis/abdomen study
- **Usually means:** Reflexive shielding placement without checking the clinical indication against AAPM's 2019 guidance; second most likely is a shield placed correctly but slipped during positioning.
- **First question:** Does the clinical question (e.g., obstruction, free air, pelvic fracture) require visualization of the exact anatomy the shield is covering?
- **Data to pull:** The order's clinical indication and the pre-exposure positioning image showing shield placement relative to the field.

### Patient reports a prior contrast reaction and the order has no premedication or alternate-agent note
- **Usually means:** Screening question was asked but not acted on, or screening wasn't completed before scheduling; second most likely is the reaction history wasn't captured in the intake record the technologist is working from.
- **First question:** Has the radiologist been notified of the reaction history before contrast is drawn up?
- **Data to pull:** Allergy/reaction history in the chart, radiologist order or premedication protocol, and agent/lot used in the prior reaction if documented.

### AEC used on a pediatric or small-adult patient outside the chamber's calibrated part-thickness range
- **Usually means:** Technologist defaulted to standard adult protocol without switching to manual pediatric technique; second most likely is a caliper measurement wasn't taken and thickness was estimated.
- **First question:** What was the measured (not estimated) part thickness at the chamber, and does it fall within this AEC's calibrated range?
- **Data to pull:** Documented part-thickness measurement, technique factors selected, and resulting EI compared to the pediatric target range.

### MRI patient with an implant listed as "MRI safe" verbally but no documentation available at the console
- **Usually means:** Screening relied on patient recall rather than implant card/manufacturer documentation; second most likely is documentation exists but wasn't pulled before scheduling.
- **First question:** Is there a manufacturer implant card, operative note, or device registry entry confirming MR-conditional status and the specific field-strength/scan conditions it's rated for?
- **Data to pull:** Implant card or operative report, device make/model, and MR-conditional labeling conditions (field strength, gradient limits, positioning).

### Order's stated clinical indication doesn't match the requested view or projection
- **Usually means:** Exam was ordered from a generic order set without editing for the actual clinical question; second most likely is the correct protocol was intended but the order code defaulted to a different one.
- **First question:** Does the requested projection actually answer what the indication asks to rule out (e.g., upright/decubitus for free air vs supine-only)?
- **Data to pull:** Full clinical indication text (not just exam code) and, if ambiguous, a callback confirmation from the ordering provider.

### Occupational dosimeter badge reading trending upward over consecutive quarters approaching a fraction of the NCRP annual limit (50 mSv/yr)
- **Usually means:** A specific high-volume procedure (portable/fluoroscopy) without adequate shielding distance or apron use; second most likely is a badge-wearing or badge-storage error inflating the reading.
- **First question:** Which procedures or rooms correlate with the technologist's highest-dose shifts, and was proper shielding/distance protocol followed on those?
- **Data to pull:** Dosimetry report by quarter, procedure log for high-dose shifts, and apron/shielding equipment inspection records for those rooms.

### Portable/bedside exam image shows tube angulation or SID inconsistent with what the order requires for anatomic comparison to a prior study
- **Usually means:** Bedside constraints (lines, tubes, patient positioning) forced a technique deviation that wasn't matched to the prior study's geometry; second most likely is the deviation wasn't documented for the reading radiologist.
- **First question:** Was the deviation from standard/prior geometry documented so the radiologist can account for it when comparing serial images?
- **Data to pull:** Prior exam's technique/positioning notes and the current image's documented deviations.

### Grid lines or grid cutoff artifact visible on an image using a stationary grid
- **Usually means:** SID mismatch with the grid's focal range, or grid tilt/off-centering during a portable exam; second most likely is an expired or damaged grid.
- **First question:** Was the SID and central ray alignment checked against this grid's focal range before exposure?
- **Data to pull:** SID used, grid focal-range specification, and grid inspection/replacement log.
