# Red flags

### Same fault fixed a 3rd time on one visit
- **Usually means:** the root cause hasn't been diagnosed — the helper (or journeyworker) is patching a symptom; second most likely: the wrong part was pulled from the take-off.
- **First question:** "has this been touched already today, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** work order / ticket history for the unit or circuit.

### Asked to remove or bypass a lock or tag
- **Usually means:** impatience with wait time, or a miscommunication about who's authorized on that energy source; second most likely: the person asking assumes "helper" and "authorized employee" are the same thing.
- **First question:** "am I the authorized employee listed for this specific energy source?"
- **Data to pull:** the LOTO permit / authorized-employee list for that lockout point.

### Helper idle more than ~20 minutes while the journeyworker is actively working
- **Usually means:** a staging or sequencing failure, not a lack of available work.
- **First question:** "what does the journeyworker need in the next 30 minutes, and is it staged?"
- **Data to pull:** today's task sequence or pull list against actual progress.

### A lift estimated over ~50 lb solo, or requiring reach/twist beyond arm's length
- **Usually means:** back-injury risk that a "muscle it once" attempt won't reliably avoid.
- **First question:** "is there a two-person lift or mechanical assist available right now?"
- **Data to pull:** NIOSH Recommended Weight Limit for the specific lift geometry, or the shop's stated two-person threshold.

### Task looks "basically the same" as licensed work watched many times before
- **Usually means:** scope creep is about to happen, usually with good intentions.
- **First question:** "is this task on the state contractor board's licensed-trade scope list?"
- **Data to pull:** state scope-of-practice list for the trade in question.

### Breaker or GFCI trips a second time immediately on reset
- **Usually means:** a load fault, not a nuisance trip — forcing a third reset is a fire-risk decision.
- **First question:** "who authorized resetting it a second time?"
- **Data to pull:** trip/incident log for that circuit.

### Panel egress, fire-watch area, or scaffold access is cluttered mid-shift, even if clean at day's end
- **Usually means:** housekeeping is being treated as an end-of-day task instead of a continuous requirement.
- **First question:** "was this clear three hours ago, or only right now?"
- **Data to pull:** OSHA 1926.25 / 1910.303(g) clearance requirement for the space in question.

### OJT hour log entries are written from memory at week's end instead of same-day
- **Usually means:** inaccurate task-category hours, which risks an apprenticeship program audit finding.
- **First question:** "was this logged the day it happened, or reconstructed?"
- **Data to pull:** daily OJT log against the day's actual timesheet and task list.

### Journeyworker is blocked waiting on the helper's rough-in more than 2x the estimated time
- **Usually means:** a take-off or measurement error, or a pacing mismatch that needs re-sequencing, not just more effort.
- **First question:** "was the pull list verified against drawings before starting?"
- **Data to pull:** material take-off vs. as-installed measurements.

### A tool leaves the crib or truck without being logged
- **Usually means:** the next person who needs it will assume it's still available and lose time finding out otherwise.
- **First question:** "who has it right now, and is it logged?"
- **Data to pull:** tool crib checkout log.

### An unexpected field condition doesn't match the drawings (abandoned wiring, undocumented gas line, suspect insulation, unmarked load-bearing member)
- **Usually means:** the as-built drawing is wrong in more than this one spot.
- **First question:** "has this been touched, or fully isolated and left alone?"
- **Data to pull:** as-built drawings against the actual field condition, photographed before anything is disturbed.

### On-site apprentice-to-journeyworker ratio exceeds the program or license limit
- **Usually means:** insufficient supervision exists for any licensed-adjacent task currently assigned.
- **First question:** "how many journeyworkers are actually present on this site right now, not just scheduled?"
- **Data to pull:** state apprenticeship ratio rule and the actual site roster for the shift.

### A customer or homeowner asks the helper for a price, timeline, or scope decision
- **Usually means:** the customer doesn't know who on-site is authorized to answer that question.
- **First question:** "has this gone to the journeyworker or the office yet?"
- **Data to pull:** none — redirect immediately, then log that the question was asked and redirected.
