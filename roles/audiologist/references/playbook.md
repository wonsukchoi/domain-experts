# Audiologist — Playbook

## Audiogram interpretation worksheet (filled example)

| Frequency (Hz) | AC (dB HL) | BC (dB HL) | ABG (dB) | Interpretation at this frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 500 | 45 | 10 | 35 | Significant ABG — conductive component |
| 1000 | 50 | 15 | 35 | Significant ABG — conductive component |
| 2000 | 55 | 15 | 40 | Significant ABG — conductive component |
| 4000 | 60 | 20 | 40 | Significant ABG — conductive component |

**PTA (AC, 500/1000/2000/4000 Hz):** (45+50+55+60) / 4 = **52.5 dB HL — moderate**
**Average ABG:** (35+35+40+40) / 4 = **37.5 dB — well above the 10 dB significance threshold**
**BC status:** within/near normal limits (10-20 dB HL) at all four frequencies

**Classification rule applied:**
- BC normal/near-normal + ABG ≥10 dB at multiple frequencies → **conductive**
- BC elevated ≈ AC, minimal ABG → **sensorineural**
- BC elevated but less than AC, real ABG present → **mixed**

## Vestibular-symptom screening checklist

- **Onset and duration of episodes:** seconds (BPPV-consistent) vs. minutes-to-hours (Meniere's-consistent) vs. days-to-weeks continuous (vestibular neuritis/labyrinthitis-consistent).
- **Positional trigger:** symptoms provoked by specific head movements (rolling over in bed, looking up) suggest BPPV; screen with Dix-Hallpike if history is consistent.
- **Associated hearing symptoms:** fluctuating hearing loss + tinnitus + aural fullness with vertigo episodes is the classic Meniere's triad — audiogram during or near an episode is more diagnostic than one obtained weeks later.
- **Unilateral vs. bilateral, sudden vs. gradual hearing loss alongside vestibular symptoms:** raises suspicion for retrocochlear pathology (e.g., vestibular schwannoma) — escalate to imaging referral rather than treating as routine BPPV/Meniere's without ruling this out when the pattern doesn't fit cleanly.
- **Central vs. peripheral red flags:** double vision, dysarthria, limb weakness/numbness, or a headache out of proportion accompanying dizziness are central-system red flags requiring urgent medical referral, not a routine audiology vestibular workup.
