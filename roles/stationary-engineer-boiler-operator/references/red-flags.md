# Red flags

### Steam production rate increases, or makeup water quality worsens, without a corresponding review of blowdown rate
- **Usually means:** boiler water TDS will trend upward toward or past the maximum allowable limit even though the blowdown valve setting itself hasn't changed.
- **First question:** has boiler water TDS actually been re-verified by conductivity meter since the production or water-quality change?
- **Data to pull:** current TDS reading versus the limit, and blowdown rate versus current steam production/makeup water demand.

### Safety valve testing is deferred past its code-required interval because the boiler has operated without incident
- **Usually means:** the last line of overpressure protection hasn't actually been verified functional, regardless of operating history.
- **First question:** what is the safety valve's last test date versus the required interval?
- **Data to pull:** safety valve test date compared against the required interval.

### Combustion is tuned or adjusted based on visual flame appearance rather than flue gas O2/CO analysis
- **Usually means:** a suboptimal excess air ratio, too much or too little, that visual inspection alone can't detect.
- **First question:** what do the actual flue gas O2/CO readings show against the target excess air percentage?
- **Data to pull:** flue gas O2/CO readings compared against the target excess air percentage for this fuel/burner.

### Low-water fuel cutoff functional test is deferred or skipped, with only visual/power confirmation the device is installed
- **Usually means:** the device's actual trip function hasn't been verified — the specific protection against the most catastrophic boiler failure mode.
- **First question:** what is the cutoff's last functional test date and result versus the required interval?
- **Data to pull:** low-water cutoff functional test date/result compared against the required test interval.

### A recent safety valve test or water chemistry check is treated as evidence that other safety systems are also likely fine
- **Usually means:** a false sense of overall safety coverage, when each system actually protects against a distinct failure mode requiring independent verification.
- **First question:** what are the independent test/verification dates for each distinct safety system?
- **Data to pull:** test/verification dates for blowdown/chemistry, safety valve, and low-water cutoff, checked individually.

### Boiler log entries record general status rather than actual measured values
- **Usually means:** a later investigation into an incident or drift lacks the specific data needed to identify what changed.
- **First question:** does the log actually capture the required specific values (TDS, O2/CO, test results)?
- **Data to pull:** boiler log entry detail compared against the code/facility-required logging content.

### A recurring scale, carryover, or efficiency problem appears without being traced to a specific cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic operational issue — blowdown rate, combustion tuning, chemical dosing — rather than isolated one-off variation.
- **First question:** does the defect/efficiency trend correlate with a specific process parameter change over the same period?
- **Data to pull:** defect/efficiency trend compared against process parameter logs (blowdown rate, O2/CO readings, chemical dosing) over the same period.
