# Vocabulary

### Blowdown (boiler)
The process of draining a portion of boiler water, continuously or periodically, to remove accumulated dissolved solids that concentrate as steam is produced.
**In use:** "Blowdown rate needs to go up now that production's higher — chemistry alone won't hold TDS down without it."
**Common misuse:** Relying on chemical treatment dosing alone to control boiler water solids, when blowdown is what actually removes them rather than just adjusting their chemical form.

### Total dissolved solids (TDS)
The concentration of dissolved mineral or solid content in boiler water, which rises as steam (pure water) leaves the boiler and must be controlled below a maximum limit via blowdown.
**In use:** "TDS is trending toward the 3,500 ppm limit — increase blowdown before it gets there, not after."
**Common misuse:** Assuming TDS stays constant once a blowdown rate is set, without accounting for how changes in steam production rate or makeup water quality shift the required blowdown rate.

### MAWP (maximum allowable working pressure)
The maximum pressure a boiler or pressure vessel is certified to safely operate at, which safety valve set pressure must be at or below.
**In use:** "Safety valve's set right at MAWP — that's the ceiling, not a target we operate near."
**Common misuse:** Treating MAWP as a target operating pressure rather than an upper limit the safety system is specifically there to prevent exceeding.

### Safety valve (boiler)
A code-required device that automatically releases pressure if the boiler exceeds its set pressure, serving as the last line of overpressure protection.
**In use:** "Safety valve's due for its lift/reseat test this month — that's not something we can push to next quarter."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a safety valve is functional because it's installed and has never activated, rather than verifying via scheduled testing per code requirements.

### Excess air (combustion)
The amount of combustion air supplied beyond the theoretical minimum needed to fully burn the fuel, which must be tuned to an optimal percentage for efficiency.
**In use:** "Excess air's running high based on the O2 reading — that's wasted fuel heating air that does nothing for combustion."
**Common misuse:** Judging combustion adequacy by visual flame appearance rather than flue gas O2/CO analysis, which is what actually reveals the true excess air ratio.

### Flue gas analysis
Measuring the oxygen (O2) and carbon monoxide (CO) content of combustion exhaust gas to verify and tune combustion efficiency.
**In use:** "Flue gas analyzer's showing CO creeping up — that's incomplete combustion, not a flame that just looks a little different."
**Common misuse:** Relying on visual burner/flame inspection as a substitute for actual flue gas measurement when tuning or verifying combustion performance.

### Low-water fuel cutoff
A safety device that automatically shuts off fuel to the burner if boiler water level drops below a safe minimum, protecting against dry-firing.
**In use:** "Ran the slow-drain test on the low-water cutoff today — confirmed it trips and secures fuel at the correct level."
**Common misuse:** Confirming the device is installed and powered without performing its actual functional test to verify it trips at the correct water level.

### Carryover (boiler)
The undesirable transport of boiler water solids or moisture into the steam being delivered, often caused by excessive TDS or water level issues.
**In use:** "Carryover's showing up in the steam quality checks — check TDS and blowdown before assuming it's just a level control issue."
**Common misuse:** Attributing carryover to a single cause, like water level, without checking whether elevated TDS from inadequate blowdown is also contributing.

### Makeup water
Fresh water added to a boiler system to replace water lost as steam that isn't returned as condensate, which introduces new dissolved solids that blowdown must remove.
**In use:** "Makeup water demand's up 25% with the production increase — that's 25% more dissolved solids entering per unit time too."
**Common misuse:** Assuming makeup water quality and quantity are constant and don't need periodic re-verification against the blowdown rate currently in use.

### Code-required interval (boiler safety testing)
The specific, mandated testing frequency for safety devices — safety valves, low-water cutoffs — set by applicable codes or insurance requirements.
**In use:** "That interval's set by code, not by how the boiler's been running — it's due regardless of operating history."
**Common misuse:** Treating these intervals as recommended best practice that can be extended based on operational judgment, rather than as a compliance requirement independent of operating history.
