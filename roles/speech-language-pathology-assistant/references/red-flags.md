# Red Flags

Smell tests for data quality, supervision compliance, and scope drift. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the record to pull.

### Client hits ≥90% accuracy in a single session after weeks in the 50-70% range

- **Usually means:** day-to-day variability or a lucky trial set, not sudden mastery — a single high session is noise until it repeats.
- **First question:** "What cue level and trial count produced this session's number?"
- **Data to pull:** the last 3 sessions' trial-by-trial logs, broken out by cue level.

### Overall session accuracy reported without a cue level attached

- **Usually means:** the number is close to uninterpretable — 75% independent and 75% with maximal cueing represent very different points in skill acquisition.
- **First question:** "What cue level was used on each trial in this session?"
- **Data to pull:** the raw trial sheet, not just the end-of-session percentage.

### Assistant's active caseload pushes the supervising SLP's assistant count past the state ratio cap

- **Usually means:** scheduling drift as new referrals were added without anyone recomputing the ratio.
- **First question:** "Has anyone recomputed the current ratio since the last new client was assigned?"
- **Data to pull:** current roster with start dates for every SLPA that SLP supervises.

### Supervising SLP has no logged direct/on-site observation in over a month

- **Usually means:** supervision documentation is being backfilled rather than performed on schedule, exposing both parties to a licensure complaint.
- **First question:** "When was the last logged direct observation, and is one already scheduled?"
- **Data to pull:** the supervision log with observation dates and type (direct vs. indirect).

### A caregiver's "should we change the goal" question gets an answer beyond describing data

- **Usually means:** scope creep into clinical judgment the assistant isn't credentialed to make.
- **First question:** "Did the response include a recommendation, or only observed data?"
- **Data to pull:** the exact wording used in the response, ideally as sent (message/note).

### A feeding or swallowing task is run without the supervising SLP physically present

- **Usually means:** a violation of the dysphagia supervision requirement, which exists specifically because aspiration risk has no do-over.
- **First question:** "Is the supervising SLP present in the room right now, per the plan's documented requirement for this task?"
- **Data to pull:** the swallowing-specific supervision-level line in the current plan of care.

### Screening tool administered wasn't on the supervising SLP's designated list for this assistant

- **Usually means:** an out-of-scope assessment occurred, regardless of how standardized or simple the tool is.
- **First question:** "Who selected this specific tool for this assistant to administer, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** the SLP's written authorization or the plan-of-care line naming approved instruments.

### Progress note or data summary submitted without the supervising SLP's co-signature

- **Usually means:** a documentation-compliance gap that risks both a licensure finding and a billing/audit failure.
- **First question:** "Has this note actually been reviewed and co-signed, or just filed?"
- **Data to pull:** the signature/timestamp field on the note.

### Same generalization-probe result for 4+ consecutive probes despite rising drill accuracy

- **Usually means:** the skill is acquired in structured drill but isn't transferring to functional or conversational contexts — stuck in the acquisition phase.
- **First question:** "What's the current gap between drill accuracy and probe accuracy, and is it closing at all?"
- **Data to pull:** the side-by-side drill-vs-probe accuracy trend over the last month.

### A billing claim is submitted for a date of service delivered solely by the assistant with no supervision documentation attached

- **Usually means:** a reimbursement-compliance risk, since most payers (Medicare in particular) don't recognize assistant-delivered services the way they do for PT/OT under the CQ/CO modifier system.
- **First question:** "Under what billing code and supervision model was this date of service submitted?"
- **Data to pull:** the claim form and the corresponding supervision log entry for that date.

### Assistant introduces self to a new caregiver or on a note using SLP-implying language ("speech therapist")

- **Usually means:** a title-representation problem that misleads the family about who is providing clinical oversight, and can be an ethics/licensure violation.
- **First question:** "What exact title was used, in speech and in writing?"
- **Data to pull:** the intake note or introduction script used with that family.
