# Red flags

Smell tests a paperhanger catches during a walk-through, an order review, or a callback, before cutting the first strip. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull.

### Roll order calculated from the roll's printed square footage, not repeat-adjusted drop count

- **Usually means:** the order will fall short once repeat rounding is applied — a large repeat can consume a third or more of a roll's length as match waste that never shows on the label.
- **First question:** what's the pattern repeat, and has the drop length been rounded up to the next full repeat increment before dividing into the roll length?
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer spec sheet's stated repeat and match type, a recalculated drops-per-roll figure.

### Rolls in hand carry two different run/batch numbers for the same pattern

- **Usually means:** a reorder or a retailer substitution pulled stock from a different production run, which can shade visibly differently under raking light even though the pattern matches.
- **First question:** were all rolls ordered and shipped together, or was any portion a later reorder?
- **Data to pull:** run/batch number printed on each roll's label, order date and shipment records.

### Seam lifts or curls 2–8 weeks after hanging, isolated to one wall

- **Usually means:** that wall has a moisture source (exterior wall, bath/kitchen humidity, or a wall the covering's adhesive/product wasn't rated for) that a non-porous covering is now trapping against.
- **First question:** is this an exterior wall or a room with routine humidity load, and was a mildew-resistant, vinyl-rated adhesive used there?
- **Data to pull:** room's humidity/ventilation history, adhesive product data sheet, moisture reading on the affected wall if accessible.

### Seams look tight at hang but open up within days, adhesive visible dried outside the seam line

- **Usually means:** seams were rolled too early, before the adhesive passed its still-viscous stage, squeezing paste out of the seam rather than seating it.
- **First question:** how long after hanging were the seams rolled?
- **Data to pull:** crew's install log/timing if kept, adhesive product's stated set time.

### Pattern visibly rakes out of vertical starting two or three strips past a corner

- **Usually means:** a full-width strip was wrapped around an out-of-plumb corner instead of two-piece cut, carrying the corner's plumb error into every following strip.
- **First question:** was that corner checked for plumb before hanging, and was it wrapped or two-piece cut?
- **Data to pull:** plumb reading at the corner (if retaken), photos of the strip sequence from the corner outward.

### Bubbles that don't flatten with a smoother, appearing in the first hour of hanging

- **Usually means:** paper that needed booking (traditional paste-the-paper product) was hung before it finished expanding, or non-woven product was booked when it shouldn't have been and lost tack.
- **First question:** was this a paste-the-wall or paste-the-paper product, and was the booking/relax time followed?
- **Data to pull:** product type off the label (non-woven vs. traditional vs. vinyl), crew's booking timing.

### Wallcovering is going up over a wall that was primed with standard interior paint primer, not a wallcovering primer

- **Usually means:** the adhesive won't have the intended release layer, risking a bond direct to the drywall's paper face that tears the wall on future removal, and possibly poor adhesion now.
- **First question:** what primer is actually on this wall, and does it match the wallcovering manufacturer's substrate requirement?
- **Data to pull:** primer product used (paint can or spec sheet if available), wallcovering manufacturer's installation instructions substrate section.

### Existing wallcovering is being papered over rather than removed, with no strippability check

- **Usually means:** the old covering may not be a true liner, and moisture/paste from the new install can reactivate old adhesive or cause the old covering to detach from the wall carrying the new one with it.
- **First question:** is the existing covering a manufacturer-rated liner, or just old wallpaper being covered over to save prep time?
- **Data to pull:** a test patch (peel or scrape) on the existing covering, manufacturer documentation if the old product is identifiable.

### Bid line-items "hang" with no separate prep charge on a wall with visible texture or a prior covering

- **Usually means:** the estimator folded prep into the per-roll hang price rather than scoping it, which understates the job and either erodes margin or gets cut corners under time pressure.
- **First question:** what prep does this wall actually need (wash, skim, strip, prime) and is it priced as its own line?
- **Data to pull:** wall condition notes from the walk-through, prep-to-hang time ratio table for the wall's condition category.

### Outside corner was hung as a single wrapped strip rather than two-piece cut

- **Usually means:** the corner is exposed to abrasion and impact along the wrap edge — the first place a covering lifts in a hallway, stairwell, or high-traffic commercial corridor.
- **First question:** is this an outside corner, and was it two-piece cut regardless of how plumb it read?
- **Data to pull:** photos of the corner, traffic level of the space (residential hallway vs. commercial corridor).
