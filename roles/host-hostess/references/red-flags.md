# Red flags

Smell tests a veteran host catches from the stand within the first few minutes of a shift going wrong.

## 1. A wait-list party is more than 15% past their quoted time

**Usually means:** the quote was too optimistic for current house occupancy, or a table that should have turned by now is lingering over dessert or a check that hasn't been dropped. Second most likely: the party was quietly skipped in rotation because a louder or more visible party got seated instead.

**First question:** "Which table were we counting on to open for them, and what's actually happening at that table right now?"

**Data to pull:** the wait-list check-in time and original quote; current status of the specific table the quote assumed would turn; house occupancy percentage at the moment the quote was given versus now.

## 2. One section is 20+ points below its cover-share for two hours running

**Usually means:** rotation has been running on table-count turns instead of cover-share, and that section has smaller or slower-turning tables that keep getting skipped in practice even when nominally "in rotation." Second: the section's server is slow to bus/reset, so tables there look occupied longer than they are.

**First question:** "Pull the cover-share sheet — has this section actually been furthest-below at every assignment point, or did we route around it?"

**Data to pull:** hourly cover-share by section for the shift; table-turn time by section; whether any VIP or manager overrides skipped that section repeatedly.

## 3. Two 6+-top tickets fired inside the kitchen's stated spacing floor

**Usually means:** large-party seating was routed by "which section has a ready table" instead of checking the recent ticket log first — a rotation or convenience decision made without a pacing check.

**First question:** "What did the ticket log show before we walked that party to the table?"

**Data to pull:** kitchen ticket-fire timestamps for the last 30–45 minutes, filtered to 6+ covers; which host/seating decision preceded each; expo's stated backup, if any, in that window.

## 4. A held reservation table sits empty more than 20 minutes past grace period

**Usually means:** the host is avoiding the release decision — often because the guest is a regular or the last time a "no-show" turned out to be an 18-minute-late arrival and the host got an earful. Second: no phone number was on file and there was genuinely nothing to check before releasing.

**First question:** "Did we call the number on file at the grace-period mark, and what does the wait list look like right now that this table could have gone to?"

**Data to pull:** reservation phone-contact attempt log; wait-list elapsed times during the hold; the house's stated grace-period policy versus what actually happened.

## 5. The same two or three walk-in parties are the ones who get "bumped" every week

**Usually means:** a subjective house preference (regulars, appearance, perceived tip potential) is overriding quote-order and cover-share rotation without anyone naming it as policy — which is a fairness and reputational risk, not just an ops inefficiency.

**First question:** "Walk me through why this party went ahead of a party that checked in earlier with a similar size."

**Data to pull:** wait-list check-in order versus actual seating order over several shifts; any VIP-flag usage log; whether the override pattern correlates with anything the house wouldn't want written down.

## 6. Quoted waits have been padded upward for several consecutive shifts with no occupancy change

**Usually means:** overcorrection after one bad public wait-time miss — the host or team started padding every quote "to be safe," which now reads as either suspicious accuracy or an underused floor with empty tables sitting next to a 30-minute quote.

**First question:** "What's our actual quote-versus-actual gap been running the last week — are we consistently beating our own numbers by 10+ minutes?"

**Data to pull:** quoted-vs-actual wait time by shift for the last 1–2 weeks; house occupancy at each quote; any incident that preceded the padding pattern.

## 7. No-show rate above roughly 20% on a night with no card-on-file requirement

**Usually means:** the reservation system isn't requiring a deposit or card hold for parties above a certain size or on high-demand nights, so no-shows are running near the unmanaged industry baseline instead of the lower rate a hold produces.

**First question:** "What's tonight's no-show rate by party size, and are any of them above our deposit threshold?"

**Data to pull:** no-show count and rate by party size and by reservation source (phone vs. platform); current deposit/hold policy thresholds; comparison to weeks where card-on-file was enforced.

## 8. A server requests to "skip" their next rotation turn more than once in a shift

**Usually means:** that section is genuinely underwater (slow kitchen ticket, a difficult table, a training server) and needs the host to slow their rotation deliberately — not a server trying to avoid work. Rarely, it's the opposite: a server gaming the rotation to protect a big upcoming table or a regular they want.

**First question:** "What's actually happening in that section right now that's making the next table unmanageable?"

**Data to pull:** that section's current open-ticket count and ticket age; the server's covers-served-per-hour tonight versus their average; any manager notes on that server's current section.
