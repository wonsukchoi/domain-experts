# Vocabulary

### Splice (ply/belt)
The overlapping joint where the two ends of a ply or belt material meet during tire building, a specified-tolerance stress point.
**In use:** "Measured this splice at 9mm — that's under the 12mm minimum, not just a little short."
**Common misuse:** Treating any visible overlap as an adequate splice without measuring it against the specific tire design's tolerance range.

### Tack (rubber compound)
The stickiness of an uncured rubber component that allows it to adhere to adjacent components during building, which diminishes over time after calendering or mixing.
**In use:** "This stock's past its tack window — it'll look fine going on, but adhesion won't hold like it should."
**Common misuse:** Assuming stock rubber is usable indefinitely as long as it's physically present and looks unchanged, ignoring that tack specifically degrades within a defined window.

### Green tire
An uncured, fully assembled tire before it goes into the curing/vulcanizing press.
**In use:** "Catch it at the green tire stage — once this goes into the press, that splice defect is locked in."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a "finished-looking" green tire has no remaining defects to catch, when the final inspection stage exists precisely because build-stage defects are far easier to catch before curing than after.

### Bead
The reinforced, high-strength ring at each edge of a tire that seats against the wheel rim and holds the tire in place under inflation pressure.
**In use:** "Bead tension's off on this one — check it before we move to the next station, that's a rim-seating safety issue."
**Common misuse:** Assuming bead seating is a routine mounting step like the rest of the build, rather than a safety-critical step whose tension and position directly affect rim-seating integrity.

### Curing / vulcanization
The heat-and-pressure process that transforms a green (uncured) tire into its final, hardened structural form.
**In use:** "Curing won't fix that air pocket — it locks in whatever structure was already there going into the press."
**Common misuse:** Assuming curing can resolve or mask a build-stage defect — an air pocket, an under-lapped splice — when curing locks in whatever structural condition existed in the green tire rather than correcting it.

### Uniformity (tire)
A finished tire's consistency in radial and lateral force variation and balance around its circumference, traced back to build-stage precision.
**In use:** "This uniformity failure traces to a splice placement issue at build, not anything that happened in the press."
**Common misuse:** Treating uniformity issues as purely a curing-stage or finishing-stage problem, when component placement precision during building is a primary driver of uniformity outcomes.

### Belt (tire construction)
A layer of reinforcing material — often steel or fabric — positioned under the tread to stabilize the tire's crown area.
**In use:** "Belt splice tolerance is different from the ply splice spec — don't apply the same 15mm rule to both."
**Common misuse:** Confusing belts with plies, when belts and body plies serve different structural roles and are positioned and spliced according to different specifications.

### Calendering
The process of coating fabric or steel cord reinforcement with rubber compound to create ply/belt stock material.
**In use:** "Track the calendering timestamp on this roll — that's what the tack window is measured from."
**Common misuse:** Assuming calendered stock's properties, particularly tack, remain constant regardless of how long it's been since calendering, rather than tracking usability from that specific point.

### Traceability (build)
The ability to trace a specific finished tire back to the exact build station, shift, and component stock batch used to produce it.
**In use:** "Traceability shows all three defective tires came off station 4 on the same shift — that's not a coincidence."
**Common misuse:** Treating a defect found in a finished tire as an isolated incident without using traceability records to check whether the same station or batch produced other similarly-defective tires.

### Building drum
The rotating cylindrical machine on which a tire's components — plies, belts, bead, tread — are assembled into a green tire.
**In use:** "Bead position relative to the drum's centerline is what determines whether this tire runs uniform."
**Common misuse:** Assuming the drum's role is purely mechanical support, rather than recognizing that component tension and positioning relative to the drum directly determine the tire's eventual uniformity and structural integrity.
