# Insurance Sales Agent — Red Flags

### Client's stated coverage request is under 30% of the needs-analysis result
- **Usually means:** client is anchoring on a premium they've heard is "affordable" rather than an analyzed need, or hasn't been walked through a needs analysis before.
- **First question:** "What made you land on that number — is it a budget ceiling, or is that the coverage amount you think you need?"
- **Data to pull:** income, outstanding debt, dependent ages, existing coverage — rerun the full needs-analysis worksheet before quoting.

### Recommendation lands on the highest-commission product in the comparison set without a documented reason
- **Usually means:** commission structure is driving the pitch order rather than client fit — the single most common source of suitability complaints.
- **First question:** "If I strip out commission difference, does this product still win on coverage terms and price for this client's stated need?"
- **Data to pull:** commission schedule across the compared products; side-by-side coverage-terms table (not just premium).

### Client wants to replace an in-force policy and the breakeven period exceeds 10 years
- **Usually means:** the premium-savings pitch is ignoring surrender charges and forfeited cash value, which usually dominates any monthly savings.
- **First question:** "Have we run the full replacement-cost comparison — surrender charge, forfeited cash value, and the contestability-clock reset — against the premium savings?"
- **Data to pull:** in-force policy's surrender-charge schedule and current cash value; new policy's contestability terms.

### Elderly client (65+) being sold a final-expense or burial policy with a first-year commission exceeding 80% of first-year premium
- **Usually means:** this segment carries the highest regulatory scrutiny for suitability abuse — a high commission-to-coverage ratio is the pattern regulators flag first.
- **First question:** "Does this client have an existing burial/final-expense policy already, and does this one duplicate or improve on it?"
- **Data to pull:** existing coverage inventory, commission disclosure statement, state suitability-questionnaire responses.

### Application health-history answers contradict information the client mentioned verbally
- **Usually means:** client is minimizing a condition out of fear of a higher premium or decline — this is the leading cause of denied claims for material misrepresentation.
- **First question:** "You mentioned [condition] earlier — I want to make sure the application reflects that accurately, because a mismatch here can void a claim later."
- **Data to pull:** full medical history disclosure form, prior carrier records if a replacement is in progress.

### Quoted premium comparison across carriers doesn't list exclusions or required riders
- **Usually means:** the comparison was built on premium alone, which understates the true cost of the carrier with narrower coverage.
- **First question:** "Have we matched deductibles, exclusions, and required riders across all three quotes before ranking them?"
- **Data to pull:** full policy specimen or outline of coverage for each carrier being compared, not just the rate quote.

### Client's occupation or hobby (aviation, scuba, high-risk sport) isn't reflected in the quoted premium
- **Usually means:** the initial quote was run at a standard rate class before flagging an activity that will trigger a rider or rating adjustment — the bound premium will differ materially from the quote.
- **First question:** "Does the client have any hobbies or occupational exposures the initial quote didn't account for?"
- **Data to pull:** application's occupation and avocation disclosure section; carrier's rider/rating schedule for the flagged activity.

### Client asks to under-report income or omit a health condition "to keep the premium down"
- **Usually means:** client doesn't understand that misrepresentation voids claims — this is a compliance and ethics line, not a negotiation point.
- **First question:** "I want to walk through what happens if a claim is filed and the carrier finds this wasn't disclosed — can we talk through that before we submit?"
- **Data to pull:** none — this is a disclosure-obligation conversation, not a data-gathering step; document that the conversation happened.

### Premium quote was given before pulling a motor-vehicle report or scheduling a medical exam, and the client has treated it as final
- **Usually means:** expectation-setting gap — the client believes the number is locked when it's a pre-underwriting estimate.
- **First question:** "Did I clearly explain that this number can move once underwriting reviews your file?"
- **Data to pull:** none needed immediately; re-send the client a written note confirming the quote is pre-underwriting before proceeding.
