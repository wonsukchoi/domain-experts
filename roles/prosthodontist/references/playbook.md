# Prosthodontist — Playbook

## Bone quality/quantity assessment (filled example)

| Site | Height above nerve/sinus | Width | Misch density class | Minimum implant clearance | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| #19 (mand. molar) | 11.0mm | 7.2mm | D2 (dense-to-porous cortical, coarse trabecular) | 8mm length needs ≥10mm height; 4.3mm-diameter fixture needs ≥6.3mm width | Clear — standard protocol |
| #30 (mand. molar) | 8.0mm | 5.1mm | D3 (thin porous cortical, fine trabecular) | Same minimums as above | Fails width; D3 quality independently elevates risk at molar load — graft first |
| #8 (max. anterior) | 13.5mm | 6.8mm | D1 (dense cortical throughout) | 10mm length needs ≥12mm height; narrower fixture (3.5-4.0mm) needs ≥5.5mm width | Clear — excellent primary stability expected |

Rule of thumb for width clearance: implant diameter + 3.0mm (1.5mm buccal + 1.5mm lingual/palatal residual plate) is the floor, not the target — sites at exactly the floor number should be flagged for a second read of the CBCT slice, not treated as automatically clear.

## Material selection by tooth position and occlusal load

| Position | Bruxer / heavy occlusion | Normal occlusion, esthetic priority | Normal occlusion, load priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anterior (esthetic zone) | Layered zirconia (strength + acceptable esthetics) | Lithium disilicate (best esthetics, ~360-400 MPa flexural strength) | Layered zirconia |
| Premolar | Monolithic zirconia | Lithium disilicate | Monolithic zirconia |
| Molar | Monolithic zirconia (~900-1200 MPa) | Full-contour zirconia or PFM (esthetics rarely load-bearing here) | Monolithic zirconia |

Flexural-strength numbers above are representative literature ranges by material class, not a single manufacturer's spec sheet — verify against the specific ceramic system being prescribed.

## Kennedy classification — partial denture design worksheet

| Class | Edentulous space location | Default retainer design | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Bilateral free-end saddle (posterior to remaining teeth, both sides) | RPI or RPA clasp assembly | Free-end saddle rotates under function; rigid clasp transmits torque to abutment |
| II | Unilateral free-end saddle | RPI/RPA on the saddle side; rigid clasp acceptable on the tooth-bordered side | Same rotation concern limited to one side |
| III | Tooth-bordered space (teeth remain both mesial and distal) | Rigid direct retainer (circumferential clasp) acceptable | Space is tooth-supported, not tissue-supported — less rotational stress |
| IV | Single anterior edentulous space crossing the midline | Case-specific; often a fixed option is preferable to removable for esthetics | Anterior removable clasps are visible; patients frequently reject them on sight |

## Occlusal vertical dimension (OVD) trial protocol

1. Determine existing OVD (rest position minus interocclusal freeway space, normally 2-4mm) and proposed increase.
2. If proposed increase exceeds ~4-5mm at the incisal pin, fabricate a removable or fixed provisional at the new OVD.
3. Have the patient wear the provisional a minimum of 2-4 weeks, tracking muscle soreness, TMJ symptoms, and speech adaptation.
4. Only proceed to final fabrication once the patient reports the provisional as comfortable at rest and under function — do not shortcut this phase because the provisional "looks right" on day one.

## Implant primary-stability confirmation for immediate loading

| ISQ (resonance-frequency analysis) | Interpretation | Loading protocol |
|---|---|---|
| ≥ 70 | High primary stability | Immediate loading acceptable in most cases |
| 65–69 | Adequate stability | Immediate loading with caution; consider provisional splinting |
| 60–64 | Borderline | Delayed loading (8-12 weeks) recommended |
| < 60 | Low stability | Delayed loading (3-6 months); reassess before proceeding |

ISQ thresholds above are representative practice guidance drawn from resonance-frequency-analysis literature, not a single universal cutoff — device manufacturer guidance and specific implant system data should be checked case by case.
