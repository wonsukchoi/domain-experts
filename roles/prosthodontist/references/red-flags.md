# Prosthodontist — Red Flags

### Residual bone width under 6mm at a posterior implant site
- **Usually means:** insufficient buccal/lingual plate to place a standard-diameter fixture without risking dehiscence; second most likely — imaging slice was taken off-axis and needs a repeat CBCT read.
- **First question:** "Does the width measurement account for a 1.5mm safety margin on both sides of the planned fixture diameter, or is it raw ridge width?"
- **Data to pull:** CBCT cross-sectional slice at the exact planned implant long axis, not just the panoramic reconstruction.

### Misch D3-D4 bone density at a load-bearing molar site combined with a standard loading timeline
- **Usually means:** elevated early-failure risk per published implant-stability data; second most likely — the density read is being made from a panoramic film rather than CBCT, which underestimates trabecular quality.
- **First question:** "Was resonance-frequency analysis (ISQ) done at placement to confirm actual primary stability, or is loading timeline based on bone-density estimate alone?"
- **Data to pull:** ISQ value at placement; CBCT density (Hounsfield unit) reading at the specific site.

### Proposed OVD increase greater than 5mm at the incisal pin with no provisional-trial phase planned
- **Usually means:** the case is being planned to look complete on the articulator without testing patient tolerance; second most likely — the existing OVD was measured incorrectly (freeway space not accounted for).
- **First question:** "What is the planned provisional-adaptation period before final fabrication, and what symptoms would trigger revising the OVD downward?"
- **Data to pull:** Measured freeway space (rest position minus occlusal contact), planned OVD increase in millimeters.

### Cantilevered pontic longer than one tooth on an implant-supported fixed prosthesis
- **Usually means:** a named risk factor for screw loosening or porcelain fracture is being accepted as a routine design choice rather than an explicitly justified tradeoff; second most likely — an adjacent implant site was ruled out for cost reasons without documenting the added mechanical risk.
- **First question:** "Was an additional implant at the cantilever site considered and explicitly declined, or was the cantilever the default from the start?"
- **Data to pull:** Occlusal-load analysis or at minimum a documented note on the patient's parafunction/bruxism status.

### Monolithic zirconia specified for a heavy bruxer's opposing natural dentition without an occlusal guard plan
- **Usually means:** the restoration will survive but the opposing natural teeth may wear excessively against the harder ceramic; second most likely — bruxism severity wasn't screened before material selection.
- **First question:** "Has the patient been screened for bruxism (wear facets, masseter hypertrophy, reported jaw pain), and is a nightguard part of the post-delivery plan?"
- **Data to pull:** Clinical wear-facet assessment; patient-reported grinding/clenching history.

### Kennedy Class I or II partial denture design specifying a rigid direct clasp on the free-end saddle side
- **Usually means:** a design default was applied without accounting for the rotational torque a free-end saddle transmits under function; second most likely — the abutment tooth has unusually strong periodontal support that changes the calculus (rare but real).
- **First question:** "What is the periodontal status of the abutment tooth adjacent to the free-end saddle, and does it justify a rigid clasp despite the rotation risk?"
- **Data to pull:** Periodontal probing depths and mobility grade on the abutment tooth.

### Implant treatment plan quoting the same fixture cost across sites with materially different bone quality
- **Usually means:** the plan hasn't priced in grafting, a wider/narrower fixture, or an extended healing timeline that a compromised site actually requires; second most likely — the sites genuinely are equivalent and the flat quote is correct.
- **First question:** "Was each site's bone quality and quantity independently assessed before the cost estimate was generated, or is this a per-tooth flat rate?"
- **Data to pull:** Per-site CBCT measurements against the per-site quote breakdown.

### Full-mouth reconstruction case proceeding to final fabrication without a facebow record
- **Usually means:** the articulator mounting is using an averaged hinge axis rather than the patient's actual jaw-movement pattern, risking occlusal interferences discovered only after delivery; second most likely — a facebow was taken but the record wasn't verified against a check bite.
- **First question:** "Was a facebow transfer used for the articulator mounting, and was it verified with a protrusive or lateral check bite?"
- **Data to pull:** Facebow record and check-bite documentation in the case file.

### Patient reports persistent muscle soreness or TMJ discomfort more than 3 weeks into a provisional at increased OVD
- **Usually means:** the OVD increase exceeds this patient's adaptation capacity and needs to be reduced before final fabrication; second most likely — the provisional itself has an occlusal interference unrelated to the OVD increase.
- **First question:** "Is the discomfort present at rest, only under function, or both — and has the provisional's occlusion been checked for a localized interference separate from the overall OVD?"
- **Data to pull:** Occlusal contact pattern on the provisional (articulating paper marks); symptom timeline since delivery.
