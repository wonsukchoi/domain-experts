# Dispensing Optician — Red Flags

### Patient reports true diplopia (not blur) within the first week of a new pair, and lensmeter verification shows the Rx in spec
- **Usually means:** vertical or horizontal prism imbalance induced by centration/seg-height error, or an anisometropic Rx where the two lenses individually verify but combine to a symptomatic differential (see Prentice's Rule calc in `playbook.md`).
- **First question:** "Is it double vision, or does everything just look blurry/off?" — diplopia and blur point to different causes and different fixes.
- **Data to pull:** original fitting record (PD, seg height per eye), lensmeter reading of the dispensed pair at the actual reading position (not just at the optical center), and the Rx spherical-equivalent difference between eyes.

### Segment height on a progressive measured or ground below the design's stated minimum fitting height
- **Usually means:** frame B-measurement too small for the design chosen, or seg height marked from the frame's geometric midline instead of the measured pupil position.
- **First question:** "What's this design's minimum fitting height at this add power, and does the frame's B-measurement actually support it?"
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer's fitting-height spec sheet for the specific progressive design and add power ordered; the frame's B-measurement.

### Patient with sphere ≥ ±5.00D reports vision "not as sharp" after a cosmetic pantoscopic-tilt or vertex adjustment
- **Usually means:** the adjustment moved the lens's effective vertex distance or tilt enough to shift effective power at this magnitude, even though nothing about the adjustment looked wrong visually.
- **First question:** "Was this lens re-verified on the lensmeter after the adjustment, or only checked visually?"
- **Data to pull:** pre- and post-adjustment vertex distance measurement; lensmeter reading post-adjustment.

### Frame returns with a hinge or temple crack within days of an adjustment on a nylon/TR-90 or titanium frame
- **Usually means:** heat-gun technique applied to a material that needed cold-forming (titanium) or over-flexing without adequate heat (nylon/propionate).
- **First question:** "What adjustment technique and heat level was used on this specific frame material?"
- **Data to pull:** frame material spec (often on the temple or box), adjustment log if the practice keeps one.

### Two lenses each verify within ANSI Z80.1 tolerance individually, but the patient is symptomatic
- **Usually means:** in-tolerance errors on each lens are stacking in the same direction between the eyes — individually acceptable, combined outside comfortable range.
- **First question:** "What's the imbalance between the two lenses, not just each lens against the Rx?"
- **Data to pull:** paired lensmeter reading (both lenses read together at the same reference points), not sequential single-lens readings.

### Patient requesting "unbreakable" or impact-resistant lenses without a documented occupational or sport safety need
- **Usually means:** either a genuine impact-resistance want (answer: polycarbonate or Trivex lens material) being misheard as a frame-brand request, or an undocumented workplace requirement that should trigger an ANSI Z87.1 safety-eyewear conversation instead of a standard dispense.
- **First question:** "Is this for a job or activity with an eye-protection requirement, or general durability preference?"
- **Data to pull:** employer eye-safety policy or job description if occupational; otherwise proceed on lens-material grounds alone.

### Multifocal wearer complains of a narrow, "swimming," or distorted intermediate zone that wasn't present in their prior pair
- **Usually means:** seg height or pantoscopic tilt off from the design's fitting spec, or a design/corridor-length mismatch for this patient's add power and frame size — distinct from ordinary short-corridor progressive adaptation.
- **First question:** "Is this the same progressive design and corridor length as the prior pair, and was fitting height verified on-face rather than on the frame board?"
- **Data to pull:** current vs. prior lab order (design name, corridor length, seg height), fitting-height measurement taken at dispense.

### Rx received from the prescriber with no vertex distance noted, but sphere power ≥ ±6.00D
- **Usually means:** the refraction's assumed vertex wasn't recorded, or the ordering optician defaulted to standard 12mm without confirming against how the Rx was measured.
- **First question:** "Was vertex distance measured and matched between the phoropter refraction and this frame, or assumed?"
- **Data to pull:** prescriber's chart note if available; if not available, measure the new frame's vertex distance and flag the assumption in the fitting record.

### Patient returns more than three times for the same complaint with each verification passing
- **Usually means:** either an intermittent/postural factor not captured in a static verification (progressive habitual head tilt, monovision adaptation, binocular vision anomaly outside optician scope), or a verification method that isn't checking the actual symptomatic condition.
- **First question:** "What position or activity triggers the complaint, and are we verifying under that same condition?"
- **Data to pull:** patient's own description of when/where it happens; consider referral back to the prescriber for a binocular vision or refraction recheck if the third verification still passes clean.
