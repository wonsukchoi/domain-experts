# Red flags — fire behavior and crew-status warning signs

Smell tests with thresholds. Format per flag: signal → what it usually means → first question a veteran asks → data to pull. Any one of these can be transient; two together in the same 60–90 seconds is a pattern demanding a strategy re-check.

### Smoke shifting from light gray/brown to black with rising velocity inside 60–90 seconds
- **Usually means:** rapid pre-flashover heat buildup — the compartment is approaching its ignition point faster than the crew's mental clock assumes.
- **First question:** "What's the ceiling layer doing on the TIC — can we knock it down from the doorway before advancing another step?"
- **Data to pull:** thermal imaging reading of the ceiling layer, elapsed time since the color/velocity change was first called out.

### Turbulent, pressurized black smoke pushing from small gaps with high heat but little or no visible flame
- **Usually means:** backdraft conditions — an oxygen-starved, superheated compartment waiting on an air source.
- **First question:** "Has this opening been vented from a remote or protected point yet, or are we about to open it directly?"
- **Data to pull:** window/door condition (crazed or stained glass), any reported whistling/hissing sound, confirmed vent point relative to the suspected opening.

### Rollover ("angel fingers") visible at the ceiling during advance
- **Usually means:** flashover is starting in that compartment, not a cosmetic fire-behavior detail to push past.
- **First question:** "Do we hit the ceiling layer with a short burst right now before advancing further?"
- **Data to pull:** distance remaining to the seat of the fire, hoseline flow confirmed and charged.

### Smoke showing from the attic/roof with no confirmed attic access, on a structure built post-1990s
- **Usually means:** active fire in a lightweight engineered truss or I-joist space, with a collapse window measured in single-digit minutes rather than the 18+ minutes legacy dimensional lumber allows.
- **First question:** "Do we know this building's construction era and roof system, or are we assuming?"
- **Data to pull:** pre-plan/permit records if available, visible smoke from soffit/eave vents, time since fire was first reported.

### Interior crew's SCBA reaches the two-thirds consumption mark before the assigned task is complete
- **Usually means:** the air budget was set too optimistically for the exertion level, or the task scope grew after entry.
- **First question:** "Are we turning to exit now, or is there a specific, short, named reason to push past the planned trigger point?"
- **Data to pull:** reported psi or elapsed time from the crew, task completion status relative to the pre-entry plan.

### Interior crew reports zero visibility and rising heat with no hand contact on the hoseline or a wall
- **Usually means:** disorientation risk is now active, with an uncontrolled flow path likely nearby.
- **First question:** "Can you get a hand on the hose or follow a wall to a known exit right now?"
- **Data to pull:** crew's last reported division/location, hoseline lay direction from the pump panel.

### A ventilation opening (failed window, propped door) exists before the attack line is charged and in position
- **Usually means:** an uncoordinated flow path is now feeding the fire toward whatever exit exists — including toward a search team's own entry point.
- **First question:** "Who opened that, and is a charged line flowing on it yet?"
- **Data to pull:** radio timestamp of the opening versus the line-charged confirmation from the pump operator.

### A search or attack team misses its planned check-in interval by more than 60 seconds
- **Usually means:** lost radio contact, disorientation, or a mayday condition developing that hasn't been declared yet.
- **First question:** "Command to [unit], radio check — location and air status, over."
- **Data to pull:** last confirmed PAR, tag-board assignment, timestamp of last transmission.

### Two or more units operating in the same division without a stated, logged assignment
- **Usually means:** the incident is losing accountability faster than it's gaining resources — a classic precursor to an undiscovered mayday.
- **First question:** "Who assigned you to this division, and does command know you're here?"
- **Data to pull:** the assignment/tag board versus actual reported unit locations.

### Exterior fire visible from more than half the structure's exposed sides with no confirmed viable rescue
- **Usually means:** the building has already made the strategy decision — continuing offensive risks crews for a structure that's no longer savable.
- **First question:** "Is there a confirmed, viable rescue, or are we now protecting exposures and going defensive?"
- **Data to pull:** 360-degree walk-around report, any life-safety confirmation from occupants or family on scene.

### A mayday is declared and normal-volume tactical traffic continues on the same channel
- **Usually means:** command hasn't cleared the channel, which delays the only thing that matters in the first 60 seconds after a mayday.
- **First question:** "Command, confirm — are we clear for mayday traffic only?"
- **Data to pull:** radio log timestamp of the mayday declaration versus the next non-mayday transmission on that channel.
