# Gambling Dealer Playbook

Filled procedure sequences, not descriptions of procedures. Numbers are standard casino-floor practice; property-specific figures (fill thresholds, exact penetration) are flagged where they vary.

## 1. Blackjack round, deal to payout

Table: six-deck shoe (312 cards), posted 3:2, $25–$1,000, cut card set at 1.5 decks (78 cards) from the back — meaning play stops and reshuffle happens once 312 − 78 = 234 cards have been dealt.

1. Burn one card face-down after each shuffle/cut, before the first hand of the new shoe.
2. Deal two cards to each player position and two to the dealer (one up, one down), left to right, dealer last.
3. Call "no more bets" the instant the first card touches the table — any chip added or removed after this point on an active seat is a procedural violation regardless of intent.
4. Resolve player decisions (hit/stand/double/split/surrender) seat by seat, left to right, per the posted rules card — surrender and resplit-limit rules vary by property; the placard is the source of truth, not the last table worked.
5. Reveal the dealer's hole card only after all player decisions are complete; draw to the property's stand/hit rule (commonly stand on all 17s, or hit soft 17 — this is a placard fact, not memory).
6. Pay winning hands seat by seat: count the exact ratio out loud or under the pit's sightline (even money 1:1, blackjack 3:2 or 6:5 per the posted sign) before moving chips into the player's stack. Collect losing bets only after all payouts on that round are complete.
7. Sweep and re-square the layout; confirm the tray total mentally against the running count before the next round's bets are placed.

## 2. Chip-tray fill call

Trigger: tray drops below the property's fill threshold (commonly ~50% of the opening bank) at any point between hands.

Example: opening bank $3,000, threshold $1,500, current tray $1,440 (below threshold by $60).

1. Do not deal the next hand until the fill is requested — a fill mid-shoe is a controlled pause, an empty tray mid-hand is an uncontrolled stop.
2. Call the floor supervisor with the exact numbers: current tray total, threshold, and requested fill amount and denomination mix.
   > "Fill call, table 12 — tray at $1,440, threshold $1,500, requesting a $1,500 fill, $1,000 in black and $500 in green."
3. Floor supervisor and a cage runner bring the fill; count it into the tray in full view of the eye-in-the-sky camera, chip by chip, before signing.
4. Both the dealer and floor supervisor sign the fill slip (three-way: dealer, floor, cage) confirming the exact amount and denomination breakdown.
5. Resume dealing only after the slip is signed and the tray is squared — a verbal "looks right" without the signed slip is not a completed fill.

## 3. Suspected past-post or pinch

Trigger: a chip stack at a resolved position changes after the outcome is visible (a chip added to a winning bet, or removed from a losing one).

1. Do not touch the stack and do not accuse the player — freeze the hand as-is.
2. Call the floor supervisor by name/position, stating the seat and what was observed: "Floor, seat 4, stack changed after the deal — need a review."
3. Floor supervisor pulls the eye-in-the-sky footage for that hand before any ruling is made; the dealer's job ends at the freeze-and-call, not the ruling.
4. If confirmed, the hand is paid/settled per the footage-verified original bet, and the incident is logged with time, seat, and camera reference number.
5. If unconfirmed or ambiguous, the floor supervisor's ruling stands and is logged the same way — ambiguous incidents get the same paper trail as confirmed ones.

## 4. Marker (credit) request at the table

1. Player requests chips against their credit line at the table — the dealer does not extend chips against a verbal promise.
2. Dealer calls the floor: "Marker request, seat 2, requesting $500."
3. Floor supervisor or credit desk verifies the patron's approved limit and remaining headroom (cage/credit system lookup, not the dealer's or floor's memory).
4. If approved, a marker slip is signed by the patron and floor supervisor; chips are issued from the tray against the signed marker, not before.
5. If declined (limit exceeded or no credit application on file), the floor states the standing account term to the patron — the dealer does not debate the number at the table.

## 5. Bet-spread flag (suspected count tracking)

Observation: a player's bets jump from a flat $25 to $200+ (an 8x spread) immediately following a fresh shuffle, repeated across multiple shoes.

1. Continue dealing normally — no change in service, no confrontation.
2. Note the pattern quietly to the floor at a natural break (post-shuffle, seat change): "Seat 6, spread jumped to 8x right after the cut the last two shoes."
3. Floor supervisor/surveillance reviews the pattern against camera history; any resulting action (shuffle-point change, bet-spread cap enforcement, or no action) comes from the floor, not the dealer.
4. Log the observation regardless of outcome — a pattern noted once and not repeated is different information than one that recurs shift after shift.
