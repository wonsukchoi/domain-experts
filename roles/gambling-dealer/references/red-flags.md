# Red Flags — Gambling Dealer

### Chip stack at a resolved position changes after the outcome is visible
- **Usually means:** past-posting (chip added to a winning bet) or pinching (chip removed from a losing bet) — the classic exploit of the payout window; second most likely is an innocent player fidgeting with their own unresolved stack.
- **First question:** did the outcome-determining card/roll/spin already happen before the stack changed?
- **Data to pull:** eye-in-the-sky footage timestamped to the hand, seat number, and camera reference.

### Bet spread beyond roughly 1:8 (min to max) appearing right after a fresh shuffle
- **Usually means:** count tracking or shuffle tracking — the player is timing bet size to a perceived favorable shoe state; second most likely is a high-variance recreational player who genuinely presses winning streaks.
- **First question:** does the spread jump correlate with shuffle point specifically, or with the player's own win/loss streak?
- **Data to pull:** bet-size log across the last 3–5 shoes for that seat, cross-referenced to shuffle timestamps.

### Dealer payout doesn't match the posted ratio on the table's rules card
- **Usually means:** muscle-memory carryover from a different pit or game variant (3:2 vs 6:5 blackjack is the common case); second most likely is a genuine miscount under time pressure.
- **First question:** what ratio is actually posted on this table's placard right now?
- **Data to pull:** table rules card photo/reference and the specific hand's bet and payout amounts.

### Tray total drops below the property's fill threshold (commonly ~50% of opening bank)
- **Usually means:** a sustained run of player wins outpacing the bank; second most likely is an uncalled prior fill that should already have happened.
- **First question:** is the current total below threshold now, or was it already below threshold before this round started?
- **Data to pull:** tray count, opening bank figure, and the shift's fill-call log.

### Player requests chips or a marker with no signed slip or credit-desk confirmation
- **Usually means:** an attempt to get credit or chips issued outside the control process, whether from urgency or intent to bypass the limit check; second most likely is a patron genuinely unaware of the marker procedure.
- **First question:** has the floor/credit desk confirmed the patron's remaining headroom on file?
- **Data to pull:** patron's credit application and current outstanding-marker balance from the cage/credit system.

### Same seat disputes a ruling more than once in a shift
- **Usually means:** either a genuine repeated procedural ambiguity at that table (worth fixing) or a player probing for a dealer who'll rule in their favor without escalating; second most likely is simple bad luck read as unfairness.
- **First question:** were both disputes escalated to the floor and logged, or resolved at the table?
- **Data to pull:** shift incident log for that table and seat.

### Shuffle sequence or cut-card placement deviates from the posted procedure
- **Usually means:** dealer error under time pressure; second most likely (rare, serious) is an attempted false shuffle to manipulate card order.
- **First question:** did the deviation change penetration (cards dealt before reshuffle) from the property's specified depth?
- **Data to pull:** camera footage of the shuffle sequence and the property's specified penetration figure.

### Player consistently plays textbook-optimal strategy on every hand with no hesitation
- **Usually means:** a trained basic-strategy or card-counting player, which is legal but worth surveillance awareness if paired with a bet spread; second most likely is simply an experienced player.
- **First question:** does the perfect play pair with a bet-spread pattern, or is it flat-betting?
- **Data to pull:** hand-decision log cross-referenced with bet-size log for that seat.

### A player repeatedly asks how much they can cash out "without paperwork"
- **Usually means:** probing for the Title 31 CTR threshold to structure transactions — a cage/compliance issue, but the dealer is often the first person to hear it.
- **First question:** is this a one-off curious question or a repeated probe across visits?
- **Data to pull:** flag to the floor/compliance officer; not a dealer-level determination.

### Fill requested twice within one shift for the same table without an unusually large payout explaining it
- **Usually means:** either genuinely high table volume/volatility, or a tray-count discrepancy masking as a "fill need"; second most likely is a hot table with real player wins.
- **First question:** does the payout log for the period between fills account for the drawdown?
- **Data to pull:** payout log between the two fill timestamps, reconciled against the fill amounts.
