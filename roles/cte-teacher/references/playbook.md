# Playbook: Credential Gap Analysis, WBL Placement, Safety Incident, Articulation Dispute

Filled process for the four recurring executions of the role. Reference context: welding program of study, two-year sequence, 32 enrolled / 24 concentrator-exiters this reporting year.

## 1. Perkins V Credential-Attainment Gap Analysis (filled example)

**Step 1 — Establish the correct denominator.** Pull the concentrator-exit list for the reporting year from the SIS/CIP-code report, not the full course roster. This year: 24 exiters out of 32 enrolled (8 continuing juniors excluded).

**Step 2 — Pull exam results at the module level, not just pass/fail.** NOCTI Welding results for the 20 exiters who attempted: written/blueprint-reading module and GMAW performance/bend-test module scored separately.

| Outcome | Count |
|---|---|
| Passed both modules | 15 |
| Failed bend-test module only | 3 |
| Failed written module only | 2 |
| Did not attempt | 4 |
| **Total exiters** | **24** |

**Step 3 — Calculate the rate against the real target.** 15 ÷ 24 = 62.5%, against a 65% state-negotiated 5S1 target — a 2.5-point gap (1 additional pass closes it: 16 ÷ 24 = 66.7%).

**Step 4 — Cross-walk the failure cluster against the exam blueprint**, not the whole course. The 3 bend-test failures all cite the same NOCTI rubric line (visible crack on the guided bend), which traces to bead-consistency technique taught in weeks 5–6 of the sequence — a 2-session technique remediation, not a re-teach of the unit.

**Step 5 — Schedule remediation and retest scoped to the failing module only.**
- Bend-test retakes (3 students): two 45-minute shop sessions on bead-consistency drills, then retest the bend-test module only.
- Written-module retakes (2 students): a targeted review of the specific missed blueprint-reading objectives (identified from the item report), then retest the written module only.
- Non-attempters (4 students): schedule a makeup exam date before the Perkins fiscal-year cutoff (June 30).

**Step 6 — Recompute after retests, before the CAR submission deadline.** If 1 of 4 non-attempters and 1 of the 5 retake students pass, the rate becomes (15 + 1 + 1) ÷ 24 = 70.8%, above target.

## 2. Work-Based Learning Placement Packet (filled example)

Employer: a local auto body shop offering one WBL slot for a "shop helper/apprentice" role.

**Step 1 — Pull the student's certified skill list**, not the employer's job title. Candidate has signed competency checklists for: hand-tool safety, MIG welding (thin-gauge steel only, under supervision), basic vehicle lift operation. No certification yet for spray-booth/paint operations.

**Step 2 — Cross-walk the employer's actual task list against that skill list.** Employer's task list includes: frame-straightener operation (not certified), MIG tack-welding on body panels (certified), parts inventory/shop cleanup (no certification required), spray-booth prep (not certified).

**Step 3 — Scope the placement agreement to the certified subset only.** Training agreement explicitly excludes frame-straightener operation and spray-booth prep; includes MIG tack-welding (supervised) and shop-support tasks. Any task expansion requires a new signed addendum after the student completes that specific certification.

**Step 4 — Confirm the paperwork chain before day one**, in this order: (a) signed training agreement scoped per Step 3, (b) confirmation of the district's student-liability/insurance coverage for off-campus placements, (c) a named on-site supervisor and documented check-in schedule (weekly minimum), (d) parent/guardian signature.

**Step 5 — Log placement start date and scope**; any employer request to expand tasks routes back through Steps 1–3 before it's approved, not approved informally on-site.

## 3. Equipment Safety Incident / Near-Miss Response

**Trigger:** daily pre-use inspection on a floor-model band saw finds the blade guard doesn't fully seat.

**Step 1 — Pull the machine from rotation immediately** and tag it out (lockout/tagout per OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147) — do not let the current group "finish the cut first."

**Step 2 — Log the finding**: machine ID, date, specific defect, who found it, action taken (tagged out, maintenance requested).

**Step 3 — Reassign the day's task** to an alternate machine or a non-machine task for the affected group; do not skip the day's competency-building step, substitute the tool instead.

**Step 4 — Notify facilities/maintenance and the department chair same day**, with the log entry attached — not verbally, and not after the fact.

**Step 5 — Re-inspect before returning to rotation**; log the repair confirmation and the re-inspection date separately from the original defect entry, so the chain (defect → tag-out → repair → re-inspection) is fully documented.

## 4. Articulation Credit Dispute (filled example)

Partner community college's automotive program awards 3 transcripted credits for students who complete the high school's Auto Tech II course with an 80%+ final grade and a signed skills-verification form, submitted by the college's May 1 deadline.

**Dispute:** a student's final grade posted at 78%, one point under the college's threshold on a rounding basis (77.6% rounded to 78%); parent argues the student "clearly" qualifies.

**Resolution process:**
1. Check the articulation agreement's exact language, not the parent's framing — most agreements specify a minimum posted grade, not a rounding allowance; if the agreement says "80% or higher, as recorded," 78% doesn't qualify regardless of the underlying 77.6% or 78.4%.
2. Check whether the skills-verification form (a separate document from the grade) was itself fully completed and signed — a low grade with a completed, signature-verified skills form is a different case from a low grade with an incomplete one; some colleges accept the skills form as the binding artifact for borderline grades. Confirm which the specific partner college's agreement actually treats as binding before responding.
3. If the grade is genuinely outside the agreement's threshold and the skills-verification form isn't independently sufficient per that college's rules, communicate the specific clause and the deadline for any grade-recalculation request (e.g., missing extra-credit points, a scoring error) — separate from arguing the outcome should change.
4. If a genuine scoring error is found on recheck, correct it and resubmit before the college's May 1 cutoff; if no error is found, the credit doesn't transfer this cycle, and the student can pursue the college's own credit-by-exam or placement-test option as a separate path.

**Deliverable (quoted, sent to parent):** "Our articulation agreement with [College] requires a final grade of 80% or higher, as posted, for the 3-credit Auto Tech II transfer — [Student]'s posted grade is 78%. I rechecked the full gradebook for a scoring error and found none. Per the agreement, this cycle's credit doesn't transfer, but [College] offers a credit-by-exam option directly through their admissions office, which isn't tied to our internal grade. I can send [Student]'s skills-verification form along with that application if it's useful."
