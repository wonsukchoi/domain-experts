# Set and Exhibit Designer — Red Flags

### Per-element crew-hour estimate wasn't summed against the booked window before fabrication drawings were released
- **Usually means:** the load-in schedule was treated as a wrap-up task instead of a design constraint, second most likely a first-time designer on a touring show underestimating commissioning/calibration time.
- **First question:** "What's the total estimated crew-hours across every zone, and how does that compare to crew headcount times window hours?"
- **Data to pull:** the load-in schedule draft and the venue contract's booked window hours and confirmed crew headcount.

### Sightline check was only run from the model-review table or a single "good seat"
- **Usually means:** the worst-seat check was skipped or deferred to tech rehearsal/gallery walkthrough, when fixing it is expensive.
- **First question:** "Has this been checked from the worst seat in the house or the most oblique gallery angle, not just the best one?"
- **Data to pull:** the sightline worksheet with viewing positions listed, or a rendering from the extreme seat/angle.

### Fabrication material was selected on lowest unit cost with no run-length or travel-cycle count attached
- **Usually means:** a single-install cost lens was applied to a multi-city or long-run show, second most likely a budget-constrained decision made without pricing the maintenance/repair delta.
- **First question:** "How many load-in/strike cycles will this material see, and what's the repair cost if it doesn't survive them?"
- **Data to pull:** the tour schedule (number of venues) and the material spec sheet's durability rating.

### A rigging point is loaded above 60% of its rated capacity on a touring show
- **Usually means:** the point-load rating was pulled once at design time and not re-confirmed per venue, or the design didn't leave margin for venue-to-venue rating variance.
- **First question:** "What's the confirmed point-load rating at this specific venue, and what percentage of it does this element use?"
- **Data to pull:** the venue's current rigging-point rating documentation and the element's actual hung weight.

### Graphics or AV specs were finalized after the substrate was already fabricated
- **Usually means:** the design and production teams worked sequentially instead of in parallel, second most likely a late content change from the curator or director.
- **First question:** "Were the file-ready specs and mount points locked before or after fabrication started?"
- **Data to pull:** the fabrication start date versus the graphics/AV file-lock date.

### Budget was allocated evenly across elements regardless of structural complexity or visual prominence
- **Usually means:** the allocation was done by element count as a shortcut rather than by weighting the hero element(s) that carry the concept.
- **First question:** "Which element does the audience's eye land on first, and is its budget allocation proportional to that?"
- **Data to pull:** the per-element budget breakdown and a description of each element's structural/technical complexity.

### Dock-door, freight-elevator, or truck dimensions weren't checked against crate dimensions before crate build
- **Usually means:** the shipping constraint was assumed rather than confirmed with the specific venue, especially on a first-time tour stop.
- **First question:** "Has the venue confirmed dock door and freight elevator interior dimensions against our largest crate?"
- **Data to pull:** the venue's confirmed dock/elevator dimensions and the crate manifest.

### An element's fabrication cost exceeds its allocated budget line by more than 15% mid-build
- **Usually means:** the original estimate underpriced material or labor, second most likely scope crept after the budget was locked.
- **First question:** "What changed between the estimate and the current cost — material substitution, scope addition, or an estimating error?"
- **Data to pull:** the original cut-list estimate versus current fabrication-shop invoicing.

### The load-in contingency margin has been spent down to zero on the nominal plan rather than reserved
- **Usually means:** scope was added late without re-checking the crew-hour total, or the contingency was misread as available capacity instead of a buffer.
- **First question:** "What was the original contingency margin, and what's consumed it?"
- **Data to pull:** the original crew-hour total versus the current revised estimate.
