# Playbook

Filled protocols with real thresholds. These reflect AUA/EAU/NCCN-framework defaults — deviate consciously for local formulary, imaging turnaround, or patient-specific factors, and document why.

## 1. LUTS/BPH — medical-to-surgical ladder

### AUA Symptom Index (AUA-SI / IPSS) tiers

| Score | Tier | Default management |
|---|---|---|
| 0-7 | Mild | Watchful waiting, lifestyle (fluid timing, caffeine/alcohol reduction) |
| 8-19 | Moderate | Alpha-blocker (tamsulosin, alfuzosin) first line; add 5-alpha-reductase inhibitor (finasteride, dutasteride) if prostate >30-40cc |
| 20-35 | Severe | Combination therapy (alpha-blocker + 5-ARI) per MTOPS; surgical evaluation if inadequate response after 4-6 weeks at effective dose |

**Absolute surgical indications (bypass the AUA-SI ladder entirely, regardless of symptom score):** refractory urinary retention (failed voiding trial), recurrent UTI attributable to BPH, recurrent gross hematuria of prostatic origin, bladder stones, renal insufficiency attributable to bladder outlet obstruction.

**Surgical options by prostate size (guideline-typical thresholds, confirm against local practice):**
- <30cc: transurethral incision of the prostate (TUIP) or Rezum
- 30-80cc: TURP, laser enucleation (HoLEP), or Rezum
- >80-100cc: HoLEP or simple prostatectomy (open/robotic) — TURP syndrome risk rises with large-gland resection time

## 2. Urolithiasis — MET vs. surgical thresholds

| Stone size/location | Default | Spontaneous passage rate |
|---|---|---|
| Distal ureter, ≤5mm | Observation, MET (tamsulosin) 4-6 weeks | ~70-98% |
| Distal ureter, 5-10mm | MET trial 4-6 weeks, then ureteroscopy if failed | ~48-68% |
| Proximal ureter, any size >5mm | Lower passage rate — earlier ureteroscopy or SWL discussion | ~35-50% at 5-10mm |
| >10mm, any location | Ureteroscopy or SWL first-line, MET trial not indicated | <25% |
| Renal stone <20mm, favorable location | SWL or ureteroscopy | N/A — planned intervention |
| Renal stone ≥20mm or staghorn | Percutaneous nephrolithotomy (PCNL) | N/A — SWL/URS stone-free rates drop sharply above this size |

**Emergency override (skip MET/observation entirely):** fever + hydronephrosis/obstruction on imaging = infected obstructing stone → same-day decompression (ureteral stent or percutaneous nephrostomy tube), IV antibiotics, culture-directed therapy. Definitive stone treatment is deferred until the infection is controlled — treating the stone before decompressing an infected system risks urosepsis.

**Metabolic workup trigger:** second symptomatic stone episode, or any stone in a patient <50, or a first stone with a strong family history → 24-hour urine (volume, calcium, oxalate, citrate, uric acid, sodium), stone composition analysis if a stone is recovered. ~50% of first-time stone formers recur within 5-10 years without dietary/medical intervention (increased fluid intake to >2.5L urine output/day, dietary sodium/animal protein moderation, thiazide for hypercalciuria, citrate for hypocitraturia).

## 3. Microhematuria risk stratification (AUA/SUFU 2020)

| Risk tier | Criteria | Workup |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Women <50 / men <40, 3-10 RBC/HPF, no risk factors (smoking, gross hematuria history, chemical/pelvic radiation exposure) | Repeat UA in 6 months; no imaging/cystoscopy required |
| Intermediate | Age 40-59 (men) / 50-59 (women), 11-25 RBC/HPF, OR low-risk group with 1 additional risk factor | Cystoscopy + renal ultrasound |
| High | Age ≥60, >25 RBC/HPF, gross hematuria history, or ≥2 risk factors | Cystoscopy + CT urogram (or MR urogram if contrast contraindicated) |

Risk factors counted: current/former smoking, age, gross hematuria history, irritative voiding symptoms, prior pelvic radiation, prior cyclophosphamide/ifosfamide exposure, chronic indwelling catheter, occupational carcinogen exposure (dyes, rubber), analgesic abuse.

## 4. Prostate cancer — NCCN risk groups and active surveillance protocol

| Risk group | Criteria (must meet all listed) |
|---|---|
| Very low | GG1, PSA <10, cT1c, <3 cores positive, ≤50% involvement per core, PSA density <0.15 |
| Low | GG1, PSA <10, cT1-T2a |
| Favorable intermediate | GG1-2, 1 intermediate risk factor (PSA 10-20, cT2b-c, or GG2), <50% cores positive |
| Unfavorable intermediate | GG2-3, 2-3 intermediate risk factors, or ≥50% cores positive |
| High | GG4-5, PSA >20, or cT3a |

**Active surveillance protocol (very low/low, selected favorable intermediate):**
- PSA every 3-6 months
- DRE annually
- Confirmatory mpMRI + repeat biopsy at 12 months, then per MRI/PSA trend (typically q2-3yr biopsy thereafter, or biopsy triggered by PSA velocity/density change or new MRI lesion)
- Trigger for reclassification to treatment: Grade Group upgrade, increase in cores positive or % involvement, PSA density rising above 0.15, patient preference change

## 5. Renal mass — Bosniak classification and RENAL nephrometry

### Bosniak (cystic renal masses)

| Category | Malignancy risk | Management |
|---|---|---|
| I | ~0% | No follow-up |
| II | ~0% | No follow-up |
| IIF | ~5-10% | Surveillance imaging (6mo, 12mo, then annually to 5yr) |
| III | ~40-60% | Surgical exploration/resection (or biopsy if surveillance is being considered) |
| IV | ~85-100% | Resection |

### RENAL nephrometry score (solid masses, partial vs. radical nephrectomy candidacy)

Sum of 5 components (Radius, Exophytic/endophytic, Nearness to collecting system, Anterior/posterior, Location relative to polar lines), each scored 1-3 (location scored 1 or 2, no 3):

| Total score | Complexity | Partial nephrectomy feasibility |
|---|---|---|
| 4-6 | Low | Favorable for partial regardless of surgeon experience |
| 7-9 | Moderate | Partial feasible with experienced surgeon |
| 10-12 | High | Partial technically demanding; radical often preferred unless solitary kidney/bilateral disease |

## 6. Non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer — EORTC risk table and BCG protocol

| Risk group | Criteria | Default management |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Ta, low-grade, single, <3cm, no prior recurrence | TURBT + single immediate post-op intravesical chemo instillation (mitomycin C); surveillance cystoscopy |
| Intermediate | Ta, low-grade, multifocal or recurrent or >3cm | TURBT + induction intravesical chemo or BCG course |
| High | T1, high-grade, CIS, or any high-grade recurrent disease | TURBT (repeat resection at 4-6 weeks if T1 or incomplete initial resection) + BCG induction (6 weekly instillations) + maintenance BCG (3-week courses at 3, 6, 12, 18, 24, 30, 36 months) |

**BCG-unresponsive definition:** persistent/recurrent high-grade disease within 6 months of adequate BCG (induction + at least one maintenance cycle, or two induction courses). At that point: cystectomy discussion is the guideline default, not a repeat full BCG induction course — clinical trial enrollment (intravesical gemcitabine/docetaxel, systemic immunotherapy) is a documented alternative for cystectomy-ineligible or -refusing patients.

## 7. Acute scrotum — TWIST score and time-to-exploration

| TWIST component | Points |
|---|---|
| Testicular swelling | 2 |
| Hard testicle | 2 |
| Absent cremasteric reflex | 1 |
| Nausea/vomiting | 1 |
| High-riding testis | 1 |

**Score interpretation:** 0-2 = low risk (Doppler ultrasound reasonable before deciding); 3-4 = intermediate (ultrasound, but do not let it delay urology consult); 5-7 = high risk — proceed to scrotal exploration without waiting for imaging, especially if symptom onset is within the last 6-12 hours.

**Salvage rate by time from symptom onset:** >90% at <6 hours, ~50% at 12 hours, <10% beyond 24 hours (Cummings et al.). Exploration is bilateral: the affected side is detorsed and assessed for viability (orchiopexy if viable, orchiectomy if not), and the contralateral testis is fixed regardless, because the anatomic predisposition (bell-clapper deformity) is typically bilateral.
