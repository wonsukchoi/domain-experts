# Coroner — Playbook

## Reportable-death checklist (common statutory triggers — confirm against local statute), applied to the worked-example case

| Trigger | Applies here? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| No attending physician within statutory window (commonly 24-36 hours, varies by jurisdiction) | **Yes** — last physician visit was 8 months prior | Most common trigger for "routine" deaths at home; this alone establishes jurisdiction |
| Sudden or unexpected death (previously apparently healthy) | No — known coronary artery disease history | |
| Violent, suspicious, or traumatic death | No — no trauma found at scene | |
| Death in custody (jail, prison, law enforcement contact) | No | Often mandates independent/external examiner when it does apply |
| Death during or shortly after a medical/surgical procedure | No | Jurisdiction-specific time window |
| Death of a minor / child | No — decedent was 61 years old | Many jurisdictions mandate autopsy regardless of apparent cause when this applies |
| Death from suspected occupational exposure or industrial accident | No | |
| Unidentified decedent | No — identified by next of kin at scene | |

**Use:** Only one row needs to apply to establish jurisdiction — here, the "no attending physician" trigger alone was sufficient, and it also mandated autopsy under this jurisdiction's rule regardless of the otherwise unremarkable natural-cause presentation.

## Postmortem interval cross-check worksheet (filled example)

| Method | Finding | Estimated interval |
|---|---|---|
| Algor mortis (temperature) | Rectal temp 89.6°F, ambient 70°F; (98.4-89.6)/1.5 | ≈ 5.9 hours |
| Rigor mortis | Present jaw/neck only, not limbs | 2-6 hours (early stage) |
| Livor mortis | Present, dependent, still blanches under pressure | Under ~8-10 hours (not yet fixed) |
| **Cross-checked range** | All three consistent | **4-6 hours** |

**Use:** Report the overlapping range across methods, not any single method's point estimate. If one method falls well outside the other two, investigate environmental or body-condition factors before trusting the outlier.

## Cause vs. manner determination matrix (illustrative, not exhaustive)

| Cause of death | Possible manner (depends on circumstances, not cause alone) |
|---|---|
| Gunshot wound | Homicide, suicide, or accident — determined by trajectory, distance, scene evidence, history |
| Drug overdose | Accident, suicide, or undetermined — determined by intent evidence, prior history, scene findings |
| Blunt force trauma | Accident (fall), homicide (assault), or undetermined |
| Acute myocardial infarction | Natural (standard), unless circumstances suggest a triggering assault or stress event under investigation |
| Drowning | Accident, suicide, or homicide — determined by circumstances, water type/location, injury pattern |

**Use:** Never infer manner directly from cause — always evaluate manner against the specific circumstantial and physical evidence for that case.

## Autopsy/toxicology decision checklist

1. Does the jurisdiction's statute mandate autopsy for this death category regardless of apparent cause (e.g., custody deaths, minors)? If yes, order it — no further judgment call needed.
2. Is the apparent cause otherwise unexplained by known medical history? If yes, order autopsy.
3. Is there any visible trauma inconsistent with the reported circumstances? If yes, order autopsy.
4. Could a substance (medication, drug, alcohol) plausibly have contributed, even if cause seems clear? If yes, order toxicology regardless of autopsy decision.
5. If none of the above apply and history fully explains the apparent cause, autopsy may be waived per jurisdictional policy — document the reasoning.

## Interim vs. final death certificate — filled example

> **Interim entry (pending lab results):**
> Cause of death: Pending autopsy and toxicology
> Manner of death: Pending
> Basis for interim status: [ordered tests] pending as of [date]

> **Final entry (after results return):**
> Cause of death: Acute myocardial infarction due to atherosclerotic coronary artery disease
> Manner of death: Natural
> Basis: Autopsy confirmed [finding]; toxicology negative for contributory substances, resulted [date]

**Use:** Never skip the interim step when results are pending — an interim certificate that is later finalized reads as a documented, careful process; a final certificate that later gets amended reads as a documented reversal.
