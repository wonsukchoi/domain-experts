# Executive Administrative Assistant — Red Flags

### A requester marks three or more consecutive requests "urgent" in a two-week span
- **Usually means:** Either a genuine pattern of crunch on their side, or urgency-inflation to jump the scheduling queue.
- **First question:** "What's the actual deadline or consequence if this waits until [next available slot]?"
- **Data to pull:** The requester's request history — how many of their "urgent" flags in the last quarter turned out to have a hard deadline attached.

### A calendar has fewer than 15 minutes of buffer between three or more consecutive meetings
- **Usually means:** Back-to-back scheduling optimized for slot-filling rather than for the executive's actual cognitive load and prep time.
- **First question:** "Which of these can move, and which genuinely can't?"
- **Data to pull:** Meeting-length history for each recurring meeting — many run over their scheduled time, compounding the buffer problem.

### An expense line item exceeds policy cap by more than 20%
- **Usually means:** Either a legitimate exception (client entertainment, an emergency purchase) or a policy the traveler didn't check before booking.
- **First question:** "Is there a business reason documented for this specific line, or was it just booked without checking the cap?"
- **Data to pull:** The itemized receipt and any pre-trip approval correspondence.

### A request references a "standing approval" or "the executive already said yes" with no corroborating record
- **Usually means:** Either a real verbal approval that wasn't logged, or a requester testing whether the claim will be verified.
- **First question:** "Can you point me to the email or calendar note where that was confirmed?"
- **Data to pull:** Search the executive's sent mail and the delegated-authority log for the specific approval.

### Two internal parties both claim their meeting is the higher priority with no external constraint cited
- **Usually means:** A genuine priority conflict that the EA cannot resolve through the rubric alone.
- **First question:** "What happens if this specific meeting doesn't happen this week?"
- **Data to pull:** Each party's stated deadline or dependency, if any — the absence of one is itself the answer.

### An external party (recruiter, vendor, unfamiliar name) requests direct, unscreened access to the executive
- **Usually means:** A cold outreach attempting to bypass the standard intake process, occasionally a legitimate relationship the EA doesn't yet know about.
- **First question:** "Has [executive] mentioned this contact or organization before?"
- **Data to pull:** Prior correspondence history with the sender's name/domain.

### A travel itinerary has a connection window under 60 minutes for an international leg or under 45 for domestic
- **Usually means:** A booking optimized for price or total travel time rather than for missed-connection risk.
- **First question:** "Does this trip have a hard-start commitment on arrival, or is there slack?"
- **Data to pull:** The historical on-time performance for that specific route/carrier, if available.

### A delegated-authority decision required interpreting a rule rather than applying it directly
- **Usually means:** The standing authority boundary has a gap that hasn't been formalized yet.
- **First question:** "Is this close enough to an existing rule that I should just log it as precedent, or does it need explicit sign-off first?"
- **Data to pull:** The delegated-authority log's last-reviewed date — if it's been more than two quarters, the gap is likely structural, not one-off.

### The same type of scheduling conflict recurs three or more times in a month
- **Usually means:** A structural calendar problem (e.g. two standing meetings that should never have been scheduled in the same window) rather than a series of unrelated one-offs.
- **First question:** "Is there a standing conflict I should restructure rather than resolve ad hoc each time?"
- **Data to pull:** A month's worth of conflict-resolution notes, sorted by which two meeting types collided.
