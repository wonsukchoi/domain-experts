# Executive Administrative Assistant — Playbook

## Scheduling-priority rubric (filled example)

| Tier | Requester type | Default handling | Bypass condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Board members, major investors, the executive's own manager | Book within 24hr, confirm personally, never auto-decline | None — always escalate to executive if a true conflict exists |
| 2 | Direct reports (1:1s, team escalations) | Protect standing 1:1 slots; ad-hoc requests get same-week slot | Direct report flags "urgent, time-sensitive decision needed" |
| 3 | Cross-functional peers (other VPs, directors) | Book into open slots only; no displacing Tier 1/2 | Peer's request ties to a Tier-1-visible deadline (e.g. board deliverable) |
| 4 | External vendors, recruiters, unsolicited requests | Default decline or redirect to a delegate; batch into a weekly window if recurring | Executive has pre-approved the specific relationship |
| 5 | Internal all-hands, optional briefings | Add to calendar as tentative; drop first when conflicts arise | — |

Rule: a lower-tier request that arrives with a harder external constraint (a flight, a filing deadline, another person's limited availability) gets treated one tier higher for scheduling purposes only — not for gatekeeping purposes.

## Multi-timezone availability grid (worked example)

Scheduling a 45-minute call between an executive in New York (ET), a client in London (GMT), and a partner in Singapore (SGT):

| Local time | New York (ET) | London (GMT) | Singapore (SGT) | Feasible? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8:00am ET | 8:00am | 1:00pm | 9:00pm | No — 9pm Singapore, after typical business hours |
| 7:00am ET | 7:00am | 12:00pm | 8:00pm | Marginal — 8pm Singapore, executive's early-morning slot |
| 6:00pm ET (prior day for SGT) | 6:00pm | 11:00pm | 7:00am (+1 day) | No — 11pm London |

Resolution: no slot is comfortable for all three; the New York exec has the most schedule flexibility of the three parties (per prior confirmation), so the 7:00am ET slot is proposed, explicitly framed to the exec as "early but the only window that keeps London and Singapore in business hours."

## Expense-reconciliation worksheet (filled example)

Three-day client-site trip, submitted receipts vs. approved booking:

| Line item | Approved estimate | Actual receipt | Variance | Flag? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Airfare | $680 | $680 | $0 | No |
| Hotel (2 nights) | $420 | $468 | +$48 | Yes — over per-diem hotel cap by $24/night |
| Ground transport | $150 | $92 | -$58 | No — under budget, no action needed |
| Meals | $180 (3 days @ $60) | $245 | +$65 | Yes — exceeds daily meal cap on day 2 ($95 vs $60 cap) |
| **Total** | **$1,430** | **$1,485** | **+$55** | — |

Do not submit as a single reconciled total. Submit with the two flagged line items called out individually, each with a one-line reason (e.g. "day 2 dinner included client, itemized on receipt") so the approver is deciding on the exceptions, not re-deriving them from the total.
