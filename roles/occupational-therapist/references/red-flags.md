# Occupational Therapist — Red Flags

### ADL self-report significantly better than directly observed performance
- **Usually means:** Patient hasn't attempted the task in the actual required context yet (overestimating from memory of pre-injury ability), or has undisclosed compensatory strategies that won't transfer to the discharge environment.
- **First question:** "Show me, don't tell me — can you do that task right now, the way you'd do it at home?"
- **Data to pull:** Directly observed trial under conditions matching the discharge environment as closely as possible.

### Repeated safety-relevant errors on a sequencing task (2+ trials) despite normal conversational fluency
- **Usually means:** An executive-function, attention, or sequencing deficit that doesn't present in casual conversation — common after stroke, TBI, or with early dementia.
- **First question:** "Is this error random, or does it repeat the same way each time?"
- **Data to pull:** Formal standardized cognitive screen, not a bedside impression from conversation.

### Adaptive equipment or home modification recommended from verbal description only, no photo or visit
- **Usually means:** The spec (height, mounting surface, clearance) is a guess and has a meaningful chance of being wrong for the actual environment.
- **First question:** "Do I have a photo with a scale reference, or a confirmed measurement, for this?"
- **Data to pull:** Photo with scale reference, measured dimensions, or an in-person home visit before finalizing a permanent installation.

### Physical-impairment measures plateaued but ADL/functional performance still improving
- **Usually means:** The impairment score and the functional outcome are tracking different things — this is expected, not a treatment failure.
- **First question:** "Is the patient closer to the discharge-readiness target on the task-level measure, regardless of the impairment number?"
- **Data to pull:** Trend on the ADL/IADL scoring table over the last 3 sessions, not the impairment-level measure alone.

### Discharge planned to a multi-story home with an unassessed stairway
- **Usually means:** Stair safety is one of the highest-frequency fall-risk factors post-discharge and is frequently deprioritized relative to bathroom/bedroom assessment.
- **First question:** "Has the actual stairway — rail presence, step height, lighting — been assessed, or only asked about verbally?"
- **Data to pull:** Photo or direct observation of the stairway; a simulated stair trial with the patient's actual assistive device.

### Family or caregiver reports patient is "back to normal" but patient hasn't been observed alone
- **Usually means:** Family presence changes patient performance (cueing, physical support, covering for gaps) in ways that don't reflect solo function.
- **First question:** "Has this task been observed with no one else in the room providing cues or standby assistance?"
- **Data to pull:** An unassisted, unobserved-by-family trial, or as close an approximation as the setting allows.

### Return-to-work clearance requested without a job physical-demand-level description
- **Usually means:** Without the actual job requirements, any clearance is a guess dressed up as a professional opinion.
- **First question:** "Do I have the employer's written physical-demand description for this specific job, or am I inferring from the job title?"
- **Data to pull:** Employer's job description with lifting/standing/reaching requirements; a functional capacity evaluation matched line-by-line against it.

### Cognitive screen skipped because the referral is for a purely orthopedic/physical diagnosis
- **Usually means:** Comorbid cognitive impairment (pre-existing mild cognitive impairment, delirium from hospitalization, medication effects) is common in the same population referred for orthopedic rehab and is easy to miss when the referral diagnosis anchors attention on the physical injury alone.
- **First question:** "Is there anything in the chart, or in how the patient is managing the session itself, suggesting cognition should be screened even though the referral is orthopedic?"
- **Data to pull:** Chart review for delirium risk factors (recent hospitalization, polypharmacy, advanced age), brief cognitive screen if any inconsistency is observed.

### Equipment recommendation not re-verified after a reported change in the home environment
- **Usually means:** A prior equipment spec (grab bar height, ramp slope) may no longer be correct if the home setup changed (renovation, moved to a different room, different tub installed).
- **First question:** "When was this environment last confirmed, and has anything changed since?"
- **Data to pull:** Updated photo or visit confirming the current state of the specific location.
