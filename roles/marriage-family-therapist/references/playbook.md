# Systemic assessment, confidentiality, and safety playbook

Filled worksheets for the three recurring MFT tasks: genogram/systemic assessment, no-secrets policy structuring, and IPV screening/safety planning.

## 1. Genogram and systemic assessment worksheet

```
Identified patient: <who was presented as "the problem">
Presenting symptom(s): <specific, with timeline>

Family structure map:
  <parents/partners, children, key extended family - note roles, alliances, conflicts>

Timeline correlation check:
  Symptom onset date: <date/approximate>
  Other significant family events/patterns in the same window: <e.g. marital conflict
    escalation, a loss, a relocation, a birth>
  Correlation noted: <yes/no, specific dates>

Systemic hypothesis (e.g. triangulation, parentification, enmeshment, cutoff):
  <specific hypothesis, held provisionally, not stated as fact to the family>

Multigenerational pattern (if relevant):
  <recurring pattern across generations, e.g. conflict avoidance, triangulating a child>
```

Rule: never finalize a treatment plan based solely on the presenting narrative — complete the timeline correlation check and hold a systemic hypothesis before designing the intervention, even when the family's framing seems complete and confident.

## 2. No-secrets policy structuring (intake)

```
Policy stated at intake (example language):
"In this treatment, I don't keep secrets between partners/family members from each other.
If something is disclosed to me individually that's directly relevant to our work together,
I'll ask that it be shared with [the other partner/family member], and I'll support how
and when that happens - but I won't hold it as a secret going forward. If you're not
comfortable with that, let's discuss it before we start individual sessions."

Application when a secret IS disclosed:
1. Acknowledge the disclosure without judgment.
2. Remind the client of the policy stated at intake.
3. Discuss timing/manner of disclosure to the other party (therapist supports the process,
   doesn't necessarily mandate immediate disclosure in-session).
4. Document the policy application and the plan for disclosure.
5. If client refuses to disclose and it's material to treatment (e.g. an ongoing affair
   during couples work), consider whether continuing conjoint treatment is still viable
   or whether individual treatment is more appropriate for that person going forward.
```

Rule: the policy has to be stated BEFORE any individual/collateral session, not introduced reactively once a secret has already been disclosed — introducing it reactively looks like a betrayal of trust regardless of the actual content.

## 3. IPV/safety screening protocol

```
Screening conducted: individually, NOT in conjoint session
Screening questions (illustrative, not exhaustive):
  - Has your partner ever threatened to hurt you or someone you care about?
  - Do you feel free to disagree with your partner without fear of consequences?
  - Has your partner ever prevented you from seeing friends/family or controlled your
    finances/movements?
  - Have things ever gotten physical during a disagreement?

Findings: <specific, documented per individual>

Classification:
  High-conflict, mutual, no coercive control/fear -> conjoint couples therapy appropriate
  Coercive control, fear, or safety risk present -> conjoint couples therapy CONTRAINDICATED
    as primary treatment; individual safety-focused work and appropriate referral required

If contraindicated:
  - Do not proceed with standard conjoint sessions as primary treatment
  - Safety plan developed individually with the at-risk partner
  - Referral to domestic violence advocacy/specialized services
  - Mandated reporting assessed per jurisdiction if children are involved and at risk
```

Rule: this screening happens individually and before conjoint work proceeds as the primary modality — conjoint-only assessment structurally cannot catch a case where a partner can't safely disclose in front of the other.

## 4. Multipartiality tracking

```
Session: <date>
Approximate speaking/attention time by member: <rough tracking>
Validation offered to each member this session: <specific example per person>
Any perceived alignment imbalance noted: <yes/no, specific observation>
Correction made (if needed): <specific action taken to rebalance>
```

Rule: track this periodically, not just when a problem is already apparent — multipartiality drift is often gradual and easier to correct early than after a family member has already disengaged from treatment.
