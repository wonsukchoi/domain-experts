# Red flags — signals a marriage and family therapist notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can be manageable within the current treatment approach; two or more together warrants an immediate reassessment of the treatment structure.

### A family presents an identified patient's symptom with no systemic assessment conducted before treatment planning
- **Usually means:** the symptom may be functioning within a broader relational pattern (marital conflict, triangulation, parentification) that will remain untouched if treatment targets only the identified patient.
- **First question:** "What else was happening in the family system around the time this symptom started or worsened?"
- **Data to pull:** genogram and systemic assessment worksheet (see `references/playbook.md` §1), symptom timeline against other family events.

### An individual or collateral session is scheduled with no no-secrets policy established at intake
- **Usually means:** the therapist may be walking into a structural bind if a secret is disclosed, with no pre-agreed framework for handling it.
- **First question:** "Has the no-secrets policy been explicitly stated to this family/couple before any individual sessions begin?"
- **Data to pull:** intake documentation, session notes confirming the policy was communicated and understood.

### IPV or safety screening has only occurred in conjoint sessions, never individually
- **Usually means:** a partner experiencing abuse or coercive control may not be able to safely disclose in front of the other, meaning conjoint-only screening structurally misses exactly the cases most needing to be caught.
- **First question:** "Has each partner been screened individually, separate from the conjoint session structure?"
- **Data to pull:** IPV/safety screening protocol results (see `references/playbook.md` §3), session structure history.

### Conjoint couples therapy is continuing as primary treatment despite an identified pattern of coercive control or fear
- **Usually means:** standard conjoint work may be actively unsafe for the at-risk partner and clinically contraindicated as the primary modality in this situation.
- **First question:** "Does the screening indicate mutual high-conflict, or coercive control/fear — and does the current treatment plan match that classification?"
- **Data to pull:** IPV screening classification, current treatment modality and session structure.

### A secret was disclosed in an individual session and the therapist is deciding how to handle it in the moment, with no pre-established policy to apply
- **Usually means:** without a stated policy, any response (holding the secret, disclosing it, deflecting) carries a real risk of undermining either the couple's work or the individual's trust — this needed to be resolved before the disclosure occurred.
- **First question:** "Was a no-secrets policy stated at intake, and what does it specify for this exact situation?"
- **Data to pull:** intake policy documentation, the specific content and materiality of the disclosure.

### A therapist consistently validates one family member's perspective more than others across multiple sessions
- **Usually means:** multipartiality may be drifting toward a perceived alignment, risking an alliance rupture with the less-validated members that can become unrecoverable.
- **First question:** "Reviewing the last several sessions, is validation and attention actually balanced across family members, or has a pattern emerged?"
- **Data to pull:** multipartiality tracking notes (see `references/playbook.md` §4) across recent sessions.

### A family's presenting narrative places sole responsibility on one member with no exploration of the broader system's role
- **Usually means:** accepting this frame at face value risks reinforcing a scapegoating dynamic rather than addressing the actual systemic pattern that may be maintaining the presenting symptom.
- **First question:** "What would the systemic hypothesis look like if this member's symptom is understood as a signal rather than the sole problem?"
- **Data to pull:** genogram, timeline correlation between the presenting symptom and other family/relational events.

### A treatment plan for a child's behavioral symptom doesn't include any parental/family-system-focused component
- **Usually means:** the plan may be treating a symptom in isolation from the family context that's plausibly maintaining it, especially when the timeline suggests a systemic correlation.
- **First question:** "Has a systemic assessment ruled out (or in) a family-pattern contribution to this child's symptom before finalizing an individual-only plan?"
- **Data to pull:** genogram and systemic assessment worksheet, treatment plan's current scope.

### Mandated reporting obligations haven't been assessed despite a disclosure suggesting possible child abuse or neglect
- **Usually means:** a legal and ethical obligation may be triggered regardless of how the disclosure emerged (individual session, conjoint session, secret disclosure) and needs immediate, separate evaluation.
- **First question:** "Does this disclosure meet this jurisdiction's mandated reporting threshold, independent of the therapeutic confidentiality considerations otherwise in play?"
- **Data to pull:** specific disclosure content, jurisdiction's mandated reporting statute and threshold.
