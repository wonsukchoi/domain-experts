# Red Flags

### Guest fails the go/no-go gauge but insists they "rode it fine last week"
- **Usually means:** the guest's clothing, body position, or belongings have changed since then; second most likely — the restraint hardware itself has drifted or been swapped/adjusted since the last inspection.
- **First question:** has this seat's restraint been serviced, adjusted, or swapped since the last shift, and has the guest changed anything (bag, jacket, footwear)?
- **Data to pull:** the seat/restraint's maintenance and calibration log for the current week, plus the shift checkout sheet.

### Measured dispatch interval creeping below the O&M-manual floor
- **Usually means:** load-side staffing pressure or a queue complaint is pushing the loading attendant to rush the restraint check; second most likely — a new or undertrained attendant hasn't internalized the floor as fixed.
- **First question:** who set today's pace, and were they told to "speed it up"?
- **Data to pull:** timed dispatch intervals over the last 10 cycles, compared against the O&M manual's stated minimum.

### E-stop activated more than once in a shift on the same attraction
- **Usually means:** a genuine intermittent mechanical fault; second most likely — an operator error pattern (mistimed dispatch, sensor misread) rather than the equipment itself.
- **First question:** was each activation logged with cycle number and symptom, or were any "quietly reset"?
- **Data to pull:** the incident log entries for each activation this shift and the prior 7 days for the same attraction.

### Unusual sound, vibration, or hesitation noticed during the empty-car test cycle
- **Usually means:** a mechanical component (brake, wheel, drive chain) beginning to wear; second most likely — normal seasonal/temperature variation the attendant hasn't seen before.
- **First question:** is this new since yesterday's checkout, or has it been present and unreported for several shifts ("normalized")?
- **Data to pull:** the last 5 days of checkout sheets for the same attraction, looking for the same note repeated and dismissed.

### Sustained wind at an inflatable attraction approaching the manufacturer's placard rating
- **Usually means:** a genuine evacuation trigger approaching; second most likely — the attraction was never re-checked against the current placard after a manufacturer update or unit swap.
- **First question:** what does today's specific unit's placard say (not a remembered generic threshold), and what's the current sustained (not gust) wind reading?
- **Data to pull:** the placard/manual for the specific inflatable unit in service and the live wind reading from the park's weather station.

### Game booth payout rate significantly outside the target band for the shift
- **Usually means:** an attendant is running an off-procedure "easier" or "harder" version of the game, intentionally or through drift; second most likely — a genuine equipment issue (rigged ring toss bottles shifted, dart board tension changed).
- **First question:** is the booth running this season's approved procedure, verified against the posted card, not last season's habit?
- **Data to pull:** the shift's prize-out count against ticket/play count, compared to the target ratio for that specific game.

### Unaccompanied child at a height- or age-restricted attraction
- **Usually means:** a parent dropped the child at the entrance assuming attendant supervision covers it; second most likely — the child slipped the line unnoticed during a queue-management gap.
- **First question:** where is the accompanying adult right now, and does this attraction's posted rule require adult accompaniment or just meet the height bar alone?
- **Data to pull:** the attraction's posted age/height/accompaniment rule (rules vary by ride even within the same park).

### Restraint "click" heard but the manual pressure-check step was skipped
- **Usually means:** the attendant is relying on an audible cue as a substitute for the full documented check, usually under time pressure.
- **First question:** was the full manual check run, or did the audible click alone end the process?
- **Data to pull:** the O&M manual's exact restraint-check procedure for that specific ride model (audible cues are frequently necessary but not sufficient by design).

### Queue wait-time complaints rising with no corresponding change in measured dispatch interval
- **Usually means:** queue population has grown (a special event, a nearby ride down and redirecting guests) rather than a throughput problem; second most likely — a miscount in posted wait-time signage.
- **First question:** has the physical queue length actually grown, or does it just feel longer to guests today?
- **Data to pull:** queue counter/switchback occupancy readings compared to the same day last week.

### A "regular," employee, or VIP requests to skip the sizing gate or gauge check
- **Usually means:** a social-pressure request with no safety basis — familiarity is not fit data regardless of how many times someone has ridden before.
- **First question:** would this exception be defensible in the incident log if something went wrong on this exact ride, today?
- **Data to pull:** none needed — this is a policy question, not a data question; the answer is no, escalate to a supervisor if pressure continues.
