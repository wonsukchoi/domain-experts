# News Journalist — Playbook

## Source-tiering table

| Tier | Definition | Attribution in story | Corroboration bar |
|---|---|---|---|
| On the record | Named, quotable, attributed | Full name + title, direct quote | Standard (self-corroborating if verifiable claim) |
| On background | Content usable, source described but not named ("a senior official") | Descriptive attribution only | One additional independent source or document |
| Deep background | Content usable, no attribution at all — reporter states it as fact | None — reporter owns the assertion | Two independent sources or one source plus a document |
| Off the record | Not usable in the story at all — informational only, to guide further reporting | Not published | N/A — used to find on-record sources, not published directly |

Agree the tier explicitly before the source talks. Renegotiating a tier after the interview (source asking to go from on-record to anonymous after saying something they regret) is a case-by-case editorial call, not an automatic accommodation — the default is the tier agreed going in.

## Corroboration decision tree

1. **Is there a document or recording?** If yes, verify authenticity/provenance (metadata, chain of custody, cross-check against a known-authentic sample) before treating it as one corroborating source.
2. **Is there a second human source?** If yes, confirm independence: did they learn this from Source A, or independently? A source who says "I heard the same thing from [Source A]" is not independent.
3. **Is the claim verifiable against a public record** (court filing, procurement record, financial disclosure, property record)? Public records are the strongest corroboration because they don't depend on anyone's memory or motive.
4. **Neither document, second source, nor public record available?** Hold the claim. A single uncorroborated source, no matter how credible, does not clear the bar for a factual assertion — attribute it explicitly as a claim ("according to one person familiar with the matter") only if the story's news value justifies running it as an allegation, and only with the subject's response included.

## Corroboration-count worked table

| Claim | Source 1 | Source 2 | Document | Independent? | Publish as |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Contract awarded to relative's company | Anonymous tip (Source A) | — | Public bid-award record | Yes — document independent of tip | Fact (bid award is public record) |
| Award was "steered" | Anonymous tip (Source A) | — | — | No — single source, uncorroborated interpretation | Dropped — not published |
| Relationship disclosed, recusal occurred | — | Council member's on-record statement | Disclosure memo obtained via records request | Yes — document independent of the statement | Fact |

## Filled correction-notice template

> **Correction:** An earlier version of this story stated that Councilmember Dana Ruiz voted on the contract award to a company owned by her brother-in-law. Records show Ruiz recused herself from the vote. The story has been corrected, and we regret the error.
>
> *Posted [date], [time]. This correction has also been noted at the top of the original story.*

## Deadline-triage worksheet (breaking news, filled example)

| Fact | Status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Building fire reported at 4th & Main, 9:47pm | Confirmed — fire department dispatch log, photo from bystander | Publish |
| 3 injuries reported | Confirmed — fire department spokesperson on record | Publish, attributed to FD |
| Cause suspected electrical | Single source (unnamed firefighter on scene) | Hold — attribute as "under investigation," no cause stated until fire marshal confirms |
| Building owner has prior code violations | Confirmed — public code-enforcement database, 3 violations 2021-2023 | Publish in follow-up once story stabilizes, not in the breaking alert |

The breaking alert runs with only the first two rows. The suspected-cause claim and the code-violation history are true and newsworthy but not yet corroborated/contextualized to the bar needed for the initial fast-moving alert — they go in the follow-up story once verified and once there's room to give them proper context rather than implying a causal link the reporting doesn't yet support.
