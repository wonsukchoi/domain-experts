# News Journalist — Red Flags

### Single source, no document, story is time-critical
- **Usually means:** the corroboration hasn't been done yet, not that it can't be done — or the story genuinely can't clear the bar in the available time.
- **First question:** what specifically would count as a second independent source or document for this claim, and is there time to get it before the next competitive window closes?
- **Data to pull:** the source's own documentary evidence (emails, texts, files) if they have any; a public-records request filed in parallel even if it won't return before deadline.

### Two sources who both learned the claim from the same third party
- **Usually means:** this is one source counted twice, not independent corroboration.
- **First question:** where did each source first hear this — trace it back to the origin.
- **Data to pull:** nothing substitutes here; the origin source needs to talk on record or be corroborated separately.

### Source becomes newly available or newly talkative right before a competitor's known publication date
- **Usually means:** the source (or someone coordinating with them) is trying to get ahead of a story breaking elsewhere, which changes their incentive to shade facts favorably.
- **First question:** does this source have a reason to want this story out now specifically, and does that change what they're emphasizing or omitting?
- **Data to pull:** the source's public statements or affiliations from the same period, to check for a coordinated narrative.

### Compelling narrative that confirms the reporter's prior assumption about the story
- **Usually means:** confirmation bias is doing the verification work instead of actual corroboration — the fit itself is the red flag.
- **First question:** if this account were the opposite of what I expected, would I be applying the same scrutiny to it?
- **Data to pull:** actively seek a source or document that would contradict the account, not just ones that support it.

### Anonymous source requesting anonymity for a claim that doesn't obviously endanger them
- **Usually means:** the source may be avoiding accountability for their own claim rather than protecting themselves from retaliation.
- **First question:** what specifically are they afraid will happen if named, and does that risk match the sensitivity of what they're saying?
- **Data to pull:** none substitutes for pressing the source directly on the reason; document the stated reason for internal editorial review.

### Document obtained through an unverified chain of custody
- **Usually means:** could be authentic, altered, or fabricated — provenance unknown means authenticity unknown regardless of how convincing the content looks.
- **First question:** who provided this, how did they obtain it, and can any detail in it be independently checked against a known fact?
- **Data to pull:** metadata (creation/modification timestamps, authorship fields), and any independently verifiable detail in the document's content.

### Quote that seems unusually clean or on-message for how people actually talk
- **Usually means:** possible over-editing of a quote past the point of accuracy, or a source reciting a prepared talking point rather than speaking candidly.
- **First question:** does the recording/notes match this exact phrasing, or was it smoothed in transcription?
- **Data to pull:** the raw recording or contemporaneous notes, checked word-for-word against the published quote.

### Denial from the subject arrives after the story's publish deadline
- **Usually means:** either the outreach was too close to deadline to allow a real response, or the subject is stalling to avoid being quoted before publication.
- **First question:** was the outreach attempt documented with enough lead time that a reasonable response window existed?
- **Data to pull:** timestamped record of the outreach attempt (email sent time, call log) to assess whether the window was fair.

### A prior story on this beat needed a correction within the last month
- **Usually means:** either a systemic verification gap on this beat or a source relationship that's compromising standards, not necessarily this specific story's fault.
- **First question:** does this story rely on the same source or same category of evidence as the one that needed correcting?
- **Data to pull:** the corrections log for this reporter/beat over the trailing 90 days.

### Public-records request response takes longer than the story's news cycle
- **Usually means:** the documentary corroboration won't arrive in time for the initial story, forcing a choice between waiting or publishing with weaker sourcing.
- **First question:** is the story still accurate and fair without the pending document, or does the document change the story's central claim?
- **Data to pull:** an interim public statement from the records-holding agency on expected response timing, if available.
