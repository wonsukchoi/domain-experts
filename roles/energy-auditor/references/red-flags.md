### Worst-case CAZ depressurization reads below -5 Pa with confirmed spillage
- **Usually means:** An atmospherically vented appliance is backdrafting under realistic operating conditions (exhaust fans, dryer, closed interior doors running together), not just under artificial test conditions.
- **First question:** Has mitigation (make-up air, duct interlock) been attempted and retested, or is this the first reading?
- **Data to pull:** Idle and worst-case CAZ pressure readings, spillage test duration and result, appliance type and venting configuration.

### Blower-door CFM50 reading is tight (near or below the program's post-work target) but no room-level or duct diagnostic was run
- **Usually means:** The whole-house number looks good on paper but the air-sealing crew sealed whatever was visible rather than the largest actual leakage paths, or a duct-driven pressure imbalance is masquerading as an envelope problem.
- **First question:** Was a zonal pressure diagnostic or duct leakage test run before or after the air sealing, or only the whole-house blower-door number?
- **Data to pull:** Pre- and post-work CFM50, duct leakage-to-outside figures, any room-specific comfort complaints on file.

### Duct leakage to outside exceeds roughly 4 CFM25 per 100 ft² of conditioned floor area
- **Usually means:** Supply or return ducts run through unconditioned space (attic, crawlspace, garage) are leaking conditioned air directly to the outdoors, and may also be distorting combustion-appliance-zone pressures.
- **First question:** Is duct sealing sequenced before insulation and air-sealing work in this zone, or was it treated as a separate, lower-priority line item?
- **Data to pull:** Total CFM25 vs. leakage-to-outside CFM25, duct location and accessibility, CAZ pressure readings for zones sharing air handler closets with the ducts.

### A savings estimate is computed against the whole utility bill rather than an isolated end use
- **Usually means:** The estimate is counting energy the proposed measure never touches (e.g., water heating or plug load folded into a heating-savings percentage), overstating the savings figure.
- **First question:** What specific end use does this measure affect, and has that end use been isolated from the rest of the bill before the percentage or delta was applied?
- **Data to pull:** Utility billing broken into estimated end uses, equipment nameplate/age for anything sharing the same fuel or meter.

### A measure is ranked and funded by simple payback rather than SIR
- **Usually means:** A fast-payback, short-service-life measure is crowding out a slower-payback measure that creates more value over its actual life, or a measure that fails the program's 1.0 SIR cutoff is being funded anyway.
- **First question:** What is this measure's SIR at its actual service life and the program's discount rate, not just its payback in years?
- **Data to pull:** Measure cost, annual savings, service-life table (e.g., NIST/DOE lifetime assumptions), program discount rate.

### Post-work ACH50 target crosses below the natural-ventilation threshold implied by ASHRAE 62.2 for the home's floor area and occupancy
- **Usually means:** The air-sealing scope will tighten the house past the point where infiltration alone meets minimum ventilation needs, without a mechanical ventilation measure in the scope to compensate.
- **First question:** Does the scope of work include a mechanical ventilation measure, and if not, does the pre-work ACH50 already sit below the threshold (meaning tightening isn't what crossed it)?
- **Data to pull:** Pre- and post-work ACH50 targets, ASHRAE 62.2 minimum ventilation rate for the home's floor area and bedroom count, existing exhaust/ventilation equipment inventory.

### Infrared thermography image shows a clear temperature differential and is the sole basis for an insulation recommendation
- **Usually means:** The differential could be missing insulation, air leakage, or moisture intrusion — thermography shows a delta, not the cause.
- **First question:** Has this finding been confirmed with a blower-door reading or moisture meter, or is the IR image being treated as a complete diagnosis on its own?
- **Data to pull:** IR image with reference scale, blower-door zonal pressure data for the same area, moisture meter reading if staining or the differential's pattern suggests water intrusion.

### Ambient CO reads 36 ppm or higher during a routine visit unrelated to a combustion-safety complaint
- **Usually means:** An appliance is producing elevated CO under current conditions, which crosses from "investigate before closing" into "shutdown and remediate before any other work proceeds."
- **First question:** Is the reading confirmed with a second measurement, and has the specific appliance producing it been identified?
- **Data to pull:** Exact ppm reading (not rounded), appliance(s) operating at the time, CAZ pressure at time of reading.

### A measure's installed cost quote is round-numbered and matches a common contractor rule-of-thumb price per square foot rather than a scope-specific estimate
- **Usually means:** The cost side of the SIR calculation may be as unverified as an inflated savings claim — a generic $/ft² figure doesn't reflect this building's specific access, material quantity, or labor complexity.
- **First question:** Is this cost quote scope-specific (material quantity, labor hours for this building's access conditions) or a generic per-square-foot rule pulled from a price list?
- **Data to pull:** Itemized material and labor estimate, comparable recent job costs for similar scope and access conditions.
