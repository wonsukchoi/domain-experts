# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced clinical research coordinator notices instantly. Load this when triaging an enrollment, deviation, or data-integrity question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### A protocol deviation is being characterized as "too minor to report"
- **Usually means:** the coordinator is making a significance judgment that belongs to the sponsor/IRB reporting process, not to individual discretion in the moment
- **First question:** "Has this been logged and routed through the defined reporting channel regardless of how minor it seems?"
- **Data to pull:** the study's protocol deviation reporting requirements and timeline, and the deviation log for this and similar prior events

### Enrollment is behind target and the response is "increase outreach/referrals"
- **Usually means:** the actual bottleneck hasn't been identified — referral volume is often not the limiting stage
- **First question:** "What does the stage-by-stage funnel show — is referral volume actually behind assumption, or is the drop-off happening at screening, eligibility, or consent?"
- **Data to pull:** funnel worksheet broken down by stage and, if the bottleneck is at eligibility, by specific screen-fail reason

### A specific exclusion criterion accounts for a disproportionate share of screen-fails vs. protocol assumption
- **Usually means:** either the target population's actual characteristics differ from what was assumed in protocol design, or the criterion is being applied more conservatively at the margin than the protocol intends
- **First question:** "Is this criterion's screen-fail rate materially above what the protocol's eligibility-rate assumption implied, and if so, has this been raised with the medical monitor?"
- **Data to pull:** screen-fail reason breakdown vs. the protocol's stated eligibility-rate assumptions

### Participant retention/dropout is being addressed by proposing increased compensation
- **Usually means:** compensation is being assumed as the lever before checking whether scheduling burden or coordinator communication quality is the actual driver
- **First question:** "Have exit interviews or dropout reasons pointed to compensation specifically, or to scheduling/burden/communication issues?"
- **Data to pull:** dropout/withdrawal reason documentation for recent participant losses

### A data point in the study database can't be traced to a specific original source
- **Usually means:** an ALCOA (attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, accurate) gap exists regardless of whether the value itself looks plausible
- **First question:** "Where is the original source for this value, and who recorded it, when?"
- **Data to pull:** source document trail for the specific data point in question

### Consent hasn't been re-confirmed since a protocol amendment or new safety information was issued
- **Usually means:** the study is relying on original enrollment consent for information the participant hasn't actually seen or agreed to
- **First question:** "Has a re-confirmation conversation happened with active/continuing participants since this amendment or safety update?"
- **Data to pull:** consent re-confirmation checklist completion status against the amendment/safety-update date

### An eligibility determination has no documented reasoning from the time it was made
- **Usually means:** if this determination is later questioned (audit, query), there's no contemporaneous record of what was known and how the criteria were applied
- **First question:** "What was documented at the time of the eligibility determination, not reconstructed afterward?"
- **Data to pull:** the eligibility assessment documentation timestamp vs. the determination date
