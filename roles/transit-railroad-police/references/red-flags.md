### Person or vehicle reported on the roadbed with train movement inside the section
- **Usually means:** trespasser taking a shortcut, or an active crisis/self-harm situation — the two look identical from a witness report and only diverge once contact is made
- **First question:** has the emergency stop been declared and acknowledged by control center, or is someone still deciding whether it's "serious enough" to call in
- **Data to pull:** control center's hold/acknowledgment timestamp against the officer's initial radio call timestamp

### Officer or first responder about to step onto the roadbed without a read-back power confirmation
- **Usually means:** urgency is overriding the protection sequence — the single highest-frequency precursor to a struck-by or electrocution near-miss
- **First question:** what is the exact time and name/ID of the person who confirmed power isolation, and has it been read back
- **Data to pull:** control center radio log for the isolation confirmation transmission

### Three or more track-intrusion or trespass calls at the same segment within 90 days
- **Usually means:** a physical defect (fence gap, worn desire-line path, missing lighting) rather than a behavioral enforcement problem
- **First question:** has facilities/engineering been sent a CPTED referral naming the specific defect, or has the response only been more patrol hours
- **Data to pull:** 90-day incident log filtered by milepost/segment, cross-referenced with the last facilities inspection date for that segment

### Signal or track wire theft reports rising at a yard or right-of-way segment
- **Usually means:** organized scrap-metal theft ring, not opportunistic petty theft — and a live safety hazard, since missing signal wire can defeat a block-occupancy circuit
- **First question:** has the railroad's signal department been notified to verify circuit integrity before anyone treats this as a property-crime-only case
- **Data to pull:** signal department's circuit-test log for the affected block since the theft was discovered

### Fare-evasion arrest rate rising while summons/warning rate stays flat or falls
- **Usually means:** enforcement drifting toward arrest-first policing on a low-level offense, usually following informal pressure ("crack down") rather than a documented pattern-offender policy
- **First question:** what share of this period's fare-evasion arrests were pattern offenders (2+ documented prior contacts) or had an independent warrant, versus first-contact arrests
- **Data to pull:** fare-enforcement disposition report broken out by prior-contact count

### Unattended item report where officer opened, moved, or leaned over the item before establishing a perimeter
- **Usually means:** normalization of frequent false alarms eroding the standard protocol — the base rate of "it's nothing" makes officers skip the stand-off step
- **First question:** what was the actual distance and time between first sighting and perimeter establishment
- **Data to pull:** body-worn camera timestamp of first sighting versus perimeter-call timestamp

### K9 explosive alert with no device or residue found on three or more consecutive deployments
- **Usually means:** handler fatigue, a training-currency lapse, or a genuine false-positive rate increase — all three require the same first step
- **First question:** when was the dog's last recertification and what was its alert accuracy on the last proficiency evaluation
- **Data to pull:** K9 unit's certification and deployment log for the trailing quarter

### Assault-on-transit-worker reports increasing at a specific line or shift
- **Usually means:** either a genuine rise in rider aggression on that line, or under-reporting elsewhere making one line's honest reporting look like an outlier
- **First question:** is the increase specific to this line's actual incidents, or is it a reporting-completeness artifact from a recent supervisor push to log everything
- **Data to pull:** FTA 49 CFR 673 safety-event submissions for the agency across all lines for the same period, not just the flagged line

### A person photographing or measuring non-public infrastructure (substations, control points, bridge structural elements)
- **Usually means:** most often incidental (transit enthusiast, engineering student, contractor) but is the specific behavior pattern protective-intelligence training flags for closer, non-alarmist follow-up
- **First question:** can the person articulate a plausible reason and does it check out on the spot (employer badge, permit, visible tools consistent with the claim)
- **Data to pull:** any prior similar reports at the same asset in the last 12 months

### Third-rail contact injury or fatality where the power-down log shows no confirmed isolation entry
- **Usually means:** a protection-sequence failure — either isolation wasn't actually requested, wasn't confirmed, or was confirmed verbally but never logged
- **First question:** pull the exact radio transcript around the incident time before anyone characterizes it as unavoidable
- **Data to pull:** control center's full radio log and the officer's incident report, time-aligned minute by minute
