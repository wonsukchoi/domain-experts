# Playbook

Filled operational tools an usher/house-management team actually runs, not descriptions of them.

## 1. Pre-doors staffing & flow plan (filled example, 2,200-seat house)

Built by the house manager 60 minutes before doors, posted at the FOH desk.

| Door | Lanes | Rated throughput | Assigned staff | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (main) | 3 | 36/min | 3 ushers + 1 lead | Highest-volume door, opens first |
| 2 | 2 | 24/min | 2 ushers | |
| 3 | 2 | 24/min | 2 ushers | Scanner Lane 2 flagged — 2 jams in last 4 shows, watch it |
| 4 (accessible entrance) | 1 | 12/min | 1 usher + accessible-seating deputy | Deputy also owns dispersal map |
| B (reserve) | 1 | 12/min | 1 rover, held in reserve, not opened by default | Opens only on house-manager call |
| 5 (rush/standing-room) | 1 | 12/min | 1 usher + clicker counter | Counter tracks against the 100-person standing cap |

**Rule:** any door running a single working lane for more than 10 minutes during the ingress window gets a radio check from the house manager — don't wait for the queue to visibly back up before checking lane status.

## 2. Occupancy tracking worksheet (filled example)

Run at the FOH desk, updated every 10 minutes during ingress, locked at doors-close.

```
CERTIFIED OCCUPANCY: 2,300 (2,200 fixed seats + 100 standing @ 5 sq ft/person, 500 sq ft rail area)

Time    Seats scanned   Standing count   Running total   % of cap
6:45    340             8                348             15%
7:00    1,180           31               1,211           53%
7:15    1,890           58               1,948           85%
7:25    2,140           74               2,214           96%
7:33    2,194           74               2,268           99%  <- DOORS CLOSE

Standing-room hard stop: at 100, the Door 5 usher stops admitting standing-room
patrons regardless of remaining seated capacity — the two counts are tracked
and capped separately, not pooled.
```

**Rule:** if the running total crosses 90% of certified occupancy before doors are scheduled to close, the house manager gets a radio heads-up automatically — this is not a judgment call left to whichever door notices first.

## 3. Late-seating decision matrix (filled example)

Set jointly by house manager and stage manager before the run, posted at every door usher's station.

| Show moment | Hold policy | Line to the patron |
|---|---|---|
| Before house lights dim | Seat immediately | — |
| House lights dimmed, pre-show announcement running | Seat immediately, quietly | — |
| First 5 minutes of Act I (no scene break) | Hold at back/standing | "I can walk you in as soon as there's a break — should be just a few minutes." |
| Mid-scene, no break for 20+ min | Hold at back/standing, offer a program | "This scene runs about 20 more minutes — I'll come get you the moment there's a break." |
| Intermission | Seat immediately | — |
| Final 15 minutes of the show | Hold in standing position only, no walk-in | "We're close to the end — I can seat you as the house lights come up." |

**Rule:** the usher never estimates "should be just a few minutes" from memory — that number comes from the stage manager's break sheet, updated if the show is running long or short that night.

## 4. Incident escalation ladder (filled example)

| Level | Example | Who's called | Response target |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Minor | Seating dispute, lost item, patron complaint | Usher resolves directly against seating chart | Immediate |
| 2 — Moderate | Intoxicated/disruptive patron, minor injury (no mobility loss) | House manager + first-aid-trained staff | Within 2 min |
| 3 — Serious | Medical event with mobility/consciousness concern, altercation | House manager + venue security/EMS dispatch | Within 60 sec |
| 4 — Critical | Fire alarm, structural concern, active security threat | Full evacuation protocol, 911, house manager overrides all other cues | Immediate, all staff |

**Radio call format at Level 2+, every time:** location → what's visible → count of people involved/nearby → what's already been done. Example: *"Orchestra right, row K — patron unconscious, one companion present, no first-aid staff on scene yet, requesting EMS."*

## 5. Accessible-seating dispersal quick reference (filled example, applying 2010 ADA §221)

For a 2,200-seat house with 8 price tiers and 6 seating sections:

- Minimum wheelchair spaces + companion seats: sized to house capacity per the venue's original ADA compliance filing (not recalculated ad hoc) — for this house, 24 spaces.
- Those 24 spaces must be **dispersed across at least the required span of price tiers and sightlines**, not clustered in the cheapest or worst-sightline section. This house's map places them across 6 of 8 tiers, front orchestra through rear mezzanine.
- **Designated aisle seats:** 5% of all aisle seats (this house: 41 of 820 aisle seats) marked and held for mobility-limited patrons who don't need a full wheelchair space.
- **Swap rule:** an ADA space or companion seat can be temporarily reassigned only to another ADA-eligible patron, and only if a same-tier ADA space is opened elsewhere the same performance — never simply reallocated to general sale to relieve a full house. The accessible-seating deputy, not a floor usher, makes this call and logs it.
- **Audit trigger:** any night where more than 2 ADA swaps happen, the deputy flags it to the house manager — a pattern usually means the dispersal map itself needs revision, not that the swap rule needs bending.
