## Fee-schedule dispute resolution (filled example)

| Step | Action | Example outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pull the ILS transaction record for the item/patron | Due date recorded: June 3. Patron claims returned May 30. |
| 2 | Compare against the physical return-date stamp or in-transit scan log, if available | No return scan found before June 3 — item shows checked out through June 5, returned June 5 |
| 3 | Check for a standard first-time courtesy waiver in written policy | Patron has no prior fine history — courtesy waiver applies per policy §4.2 |
| 4 | Apply the waiver and note the policy citation in the record | Fine waived, noted "first-time courtesy waiver per §4.2," not "waived — seemed reasonable" |
| 5 | If no waiver applies and the patron still disputes, escalate to the supervising librarian with the transaction record attached | N/A — resolved at step 4 in this example |

The record always cites *why* an exception happened (policy section, or "escalated, no policy basis found") — never just that it did. That citation is what lets the next disputed case be checked for consistency.
