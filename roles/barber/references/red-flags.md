# Red flags

Smells a senior barber catches fast, with what to ask and what to check before deciding anything.

### Same client rebooking every 1–2 weeks asking to "fix" the same spot
**Usually means:** the blend zone or foundation guard progression was wrong last time, not that the client's hair grows unusually fast.
**First question:** "What guard progression and blend width did we use last visit, and does the shape match what they actually asked for?"
**Data to pull:** the client's last consultation card / ticket note and before-after photos if the shop keeps them.

### Fade line is still sharply visible at 10–12 days of growth
**Usually means:** the blend zone was cut too narrow for the client's hair density — a common miss when a photo reference was copied literally instead of adjusted for texture.
**First question:** "What blend width did we use, and what's this client's actual density and texture?"
**Data to pull:** guard progression notes from the ticket, client's hair type on file.

### Line-up leaves visible bumps or ingrown hairs 24–48 hours later on curly/coily hair
**Usually means:** razor or trimmer work against the grain on skin prone to pseudofolliculitis barbae (PFB), not a hygiene failure.
**First question:** "Does this client have a known razor-bump history on file?"
**Data to pull:** client card notes on skin sensitivity; consider clipper-only edging instead of razor on future visits.

### Clipper motor bogging down or overheating mid-shift
**Usually means:** blades are dull or overdue for oiling, not motor failure — a maintenance gap, not an equipment replacement need.
**First question:** "When was this blade last oiled or replaced?"
**Data to pull:** the shop's blade maintenance log, if one exists (if none exists, that's the actual gap).

### Disinfectant jar is visibly cloudy or discolored
**Usually means:** the solution is past its labeled contact-time efficacy — most EPA-registered wet sanitizers need daily change or immediate change once visibly soiled.
**First question:** "When was this solution last changed?"
**Data to pull:** solution-change log or date, if the shop tracks one; if not, that absence is itself the flag.

### A nick or skin contact happened but wasn't logged or flagged
**Usually means:** an informal, undocumented handling of a bloodborne-pathogen exposure — a licensing and liability risk regardless of how minor it seemed.
**First question:** "Was the blade discarded immediately and a fresh one used?"
**Data to pull:** the shop's incident/exposure log, if maintained; if the shop has none, that's a structural gap worth raising.

### One barber's rebooking rate sits well under the shop average
**Usually means:** technical inconsistency (different result each visit) or a weak consultation habit — rarely "bad luck" with client mix.
**First question:** "Pull the last 10 tickets — same style logic each time, or improvising per client without a clear read on why?"
**Data to pull:** rebooking-rate report from the booking system, broken out per barber.

### Chair time for a standard style runs well over the shop's average (e.g., 45 min for a basic fade vs. a 25-min average)
**Usually means:** checkpoints aren't being used during the cut, so mistakes get caught and corrected late and expensively instead of at each stage.
**First question:** "Walk me through your checkpoints during the cut — where do you check symmetry before the end?"
**Data to pull:** appointment-duration logs by service type, per barber.

### Booth rent is consistently above ~40% of a barber's gross weekly revenue
**Usually means:** the rent-to-revenue ratio has flipped from a manageable cost to a solvency risk, especially heading into a slow season.
**First question:** "What's the trailing 8-week average client count and ticket size?"
**Data to pull:** weekly revenue log against the fixed rent figure; compare to the commission breakeven threshold in `references/playbook.md`.

### A client shows a reference photo of visibly different hair texture/density than their own
**Usually means:** the request as photographed will not translate directly and needs technical adjustment before cutting.
**First question:** "Have you had this exact style before, and how did it hold up on your hair?"
**Data to pull:** none from a system — run fingers through the hair for density and texture before committing to any guard number.
