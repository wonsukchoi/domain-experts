# Playbook

Filled reference material a barber actually uses chair-side — adapt the numbers to the head in front of you, but start here rather than from scratch.

## 1. Clipper guard chart (standard, brand-variance noted)

| Guard # | Length (in) | Length (mm) | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 000/skin | ~1/16" or less | 1–1.5mm | skin-fade base, closest cut without a blade |
| 1 | 1/8" | 3mm | tight buzz, low fades |
| 1.5 | 3/16" | 4.5mm | blend step between 1 and 2 |
| 2 | 1/4" | 6mm | short sides, common fade start point |
| 3 | 3/8" | 10mm | short-medium, businesslike sides |
| 4 | 1/2" | 13mm | medium length, common taper top |
| 5 | 5/8" | 16mm | medium-long |
| 6 | 3/4" | 19mm | long clipper cut |
| 7–8 | 7/8"–1" | 22–25mm | longest guard lengths, rarely used on sides |

**Caveat:** these lengths are the widely-used Wahl/Andis convention, not a regulated standard — guards vary by up to ~1/16" between brands and between "taper" vs. "skin-safe" guard lines from the same brand. Confirm on a new brand before committing to a client's usual number.

## 2. Blend-zone width by density/texture (filled heuristic)

| Density / texture | Blend-zone width | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Fine/thin, straight or wavy | 1.25"–1.5" | Low density shows contrast lines fast; a narrow zone reads patchy within days as it grows. |
| Medium, straight/wavy | 0.75"–1" | Standard starting point for most requests. |
| Thick, straight/wavy | 0.5"–0.75" | Density masks the transition; can hold a tighter line longer. |
| Coarse/coily (type 3–4) | 0.5"–0.75", cut with growth direction | Curl pattern itself helps mask the transition; cutting against grain here causes visible tracking, not a cleaner line. |

Rule of thumb: every step down in density buys roughly +0.25"–0.5" of blend-zone width if the cut needs to hold past two weeks.

## 3. Skin/bald fade sequence (filled, numbered)

1. Establish the guideline: mark the desired top length with shears or a guard, comb-check for symmetry front-to-back.
2. Remove bulk with the guard progression top-down (e.g., #2 → #1.5 → #1), working in 1/4"-overlap passes.
3. Drop to skin at the lowest point of the blend zone with a bare or near-bare blade, angled clipper-over-comb or freehand.
4. Blend the zone from both directions — top-down with the lower guard, bottom-up freehand against the skin line — until no visible step remains under natural light.
5. Detail the hairline and sideburns with a trimmer or shavette; confirm symmetry both sides in the mirror, not just one.
6. Dry-check: hair looks different wet than dry, especially wavy/curly types — do the final blend pass dry or near-dry.

## 4. Consultation script (filled)

```
BARBER: "What are we doing today — same as usual or something different?"
CLIENT: [shows photo / describes]
BARBER: "Okay — so [restate: 'shorter on the sides, fade down to skin, keep
  length on top for a comb-over'] — that's what I'm seeing in the photo.
  Your hair's [finer/thicker/wavier] than his though, so I'm going to
  widen the blend a bit so it doesn't get patchy in a week — you'll still
  get the same look, it'll just hold longer. Sound good?"
CLIENT: [confirms or corrects]
BARBER: [notes any crown/cowlick/scar found during comb-through, states it
  out loud: "you've got a bit of a double crown back here, I'm going to
  leave a touch more length over it so it doesn't stick up."]
```

The two moves that matter: repeating the request back in the barber's own words (catches mismatches before the first cut), and naming any grain irregularity out loud before it becomes a mid-cut surprise.

## 5. Disinfection/sanitation checklist

- **Wet-sanitize** combs, clips, and non-porous tools in an EPA-registered disinfectant (e.g., Barbicide) at full immersion for the label's stated contact time (commonly 10+ minutes) between every client.
- **Change the solution daily at minimum**, or immediately if visibly cloudy, discolored, or contaminated with hair/debris — degraded solution doesn't reach labeled contact-time efficacy.
- **Single-use blades only** for anything touching skin (shavette, edge razor) — discard in a sharps container after one client, no exceptions for a "quick line-up."
- **Clipper blades**: oil every few heads per shift; alcohol-wipe or spray-disinfect the blade housing between clients even when guards are used (skin contact still occurs at the neckline and ears).
- **Nicks or skin contact with a used blade**: stop, discard the blade immediately, log it if the shop maintains an exposure record — treat it as a bloodborne-pathogen event, not an inconvenience.

## 6. Booth rent vs. commission — breakeven calculation (filled example)

Inputs: booth rent $250/week, average ticket $35, commission split 60% to barber / 40% to shop.

Set commission take-home equal to booth-rent take-home at client count *n*:

```
0.60 × (35 × n)  =  (35 × n) − 250
21n              =  35n − 250
250              =  14n
n                ≈  17.9   →  18 clients/week
```

**Below ~18 clients/week**, commission nets more (no fixed cost exposure in a slow week). **Above ~18/week**, booth rent nets more and the gap widens with volume — at 30 clients/week, booth rent keeps $800 (76%) vs. commission's $630 (60%) of the same $1,050 in revenue. New barbers building a book, or shops with seasonal swings, generally sit on the commission side of that line until volume is consistently well clear of it; established chairs with a steady 20+/week book generally move to booth rent.

## 7. Straight-razor / shavette shave sequence (filled)

1. Prep: hot towel 3–5 minutes to soften hair and open pores.
2. Lather with a brush, working against the grain of the beard to lift hair.
3. **First pass, with the grain** — the safest, closest-to-comfortable pass; relather.
4. **Second pass, across the grain** (90° to growth) for closer results on tolerant skin — skip this pass on a client with known razor-bump history.
5. **Third pass, against the grain** — only for a client who has tolerated the first two without irritation on past visits; this is where razor bumps and irritation originate on curl-prone skin, so it's opt-in, not default.
6. Cold towel and aftershave/balm to close pores; check for nicks before the client leaves the chair.
7. Blade is discarded (shavette) immediately after use — never reused, never held over for "just a touch-up" later in the day.
