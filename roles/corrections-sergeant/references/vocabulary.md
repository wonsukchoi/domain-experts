# Corrections Sergeant — Vocabulary

### Deliberate indifference
The legal standard from *Farmer v. Brennan* (1994) for supervisory/failure-to-protect liability: knowing of and disregarding an excessive risk to inmate health or safety, judged by what the supervisor actually knew and how they responded, not by the outcome alone.
**In use:** "We documented the response the same day the risk was reported — that's the difference between an incident and a deliberate-indifference finding."
**Common misuse:** treating the standard as requiring intent to harm; it requires actual knowledge of a risk plus a disregard of it, which a documented but inadequate response can still satisfy.

### Countersignature
A supervisor's signature on a subordinate's report certifying that it was reviewed against available evidence (camera footage, other officers' accounts) before being accepted into the official record — not an administrative formality.
**In use:** "I'm not countersigning this until the timestamp on Cam 14 lines up with what the narrative says happened."
**Common misuse:** signing as a routine step once the report is submitted, without actually checking it against available footage or other accounts.

### EIS (Early Warning/Intervention System)
A tracking system flagging officers who cross defined thresholds (use-of-force frequency, sustained complaints, attendance patterns) within a rolling window, requiring a documented supervisory review and response once the threshold is met.
**In use:** "That's his third use-of-force in under 90 days — EIS threshold's met, I need the review done before his next shift."
**Common misuse:** treating an EIS flag as a passive record-keeping event rather than a required-action trigger with a documented response.

### Mandate / mandatory overtime
An involuntary overtime assignment used to restore a post to its required minimum staffing level, typically assigned in reverse-seniority order off a tracked list with a protection window against repeat mandates.
**In use:** "Patel's inside the 30-day window, skip to the next name on the mandate list."
**Common misuse:** calling whichever officer answers fastest instead of working the documented seniority order — defensible mandating depends on the order being followed, not just the outcome of a post being filled.

### Incident command
The role a supervisor assumes during a multi-officer response — directing (calling the code, assigning roles, requesting backup/medical) rather than personally engaging in the physical response.
**In use:** "I'm running command on this one — Vance, sally port; Osei, escort to medical."
**Common misuse:** assuming "supervising" means physically joining the response; command and hands-on intervention are different roles, and doing both means neither is done well.

### Progressive discipline
A staged response to misconduct (verbal counseling, written reprimand, suspension, termination) applied consistently to the same misconduct category across officers, with each step documented.
**In use:** "Same category as last time — this is his second occurrence in six months, so we're at the written-reprimand step, not back to verbal."
**Common misuse:** skipping or accelerating steps based on personal rapport with the officer rather than the documented misconduct history — the disparity, not the discipline itself, is what a grievance or arbitration challenges.

### PREA compliance manager
The designated staff role that receives notification of PREA-relevant disclosures or observations and oversees the facility's prevention, detection, and response protocol under 28 CFR Part 115.
**In use:** "That's a PREA-relevant disclosure — separate them now, notify the compliance manager before anything else."
**Common misuse:** treating the sergeant's own read on the disclosure's credibility as the trigger for action, rather than the disclosure itself.

### Staffing floor
The minimum number of officers a post requires under the facility's board-approved staffing plan or governing consent decree, tied to housing type and census — a legal minimum, not a scheduling target.
**In use:** "We're one under floor on the C-unit housing floor — mandate list, now, not after something happens."
**Common misuse:** treating the floor as a soft target that can be missed occasionally without consequence, rather than a documented legal minimum that requires either a mandate or a logged compensating measure whenever unmet.

### Grievance exhaustion
The requirement, under the Prison Litigation Reform Act, that an inmate exhaust the facility's internal grievance process before filing suit — meaning the facility's own response timeliness and completeness directly affects whether a later claim can proceed.
**In use:** "The deadline lapsed with nothing filed — that's the kind of gap that gets an exhaustion defense thrown out."
**Common misuse:** assuming an informal verbal response to the inmate satisfies the grievance process; exhaustion turns on the documented, policy-compliant response, not an off-the-record conversation.

### Compensating measure
A documented adjustment (e.g., increased round frequency, joint patrol) used to offset a post running below its staffing floor when a mandate can't immediately restore full coverage.
**In use:** "Rover's uncovered tonight — compensating measure is a joint floor round at the top of every hour, logged."
**Common misuse:** treating an undocumented, ad hoc adjustment as sufficient — a compensating measure only functions as a defense if it's specific and logged, not just informally understood by the shift.

### Post-and-relief staffing method
An ACA-referenced staffing methodology that calculates required officer counts per post based on relief coverage, breaks, and shift-change overlap, rather than a flat headcount-to-inmate ratio.
**In use:** "The post-and-relief numbers already build in the break relief — that's why the raw headcount looks higher than the census ratio suggests."
**Common misuse:** comparing raw officer-to-inmate ratios directly against staffing-plan numbers without accounting for the relief factor built into the post-and-relief calculation.
