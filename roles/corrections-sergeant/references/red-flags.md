# Corrections Sergeant — Red Flags

### Same 2-3 officers account for more than 40% of mandatory-overtime fills over a rolling quarter
- **Usually means:** the mandate list isn't actually being worked in seniority order — a supervisor is defaulting to whoever answers the phone fastest rather than the documented next name.
- **First question:** pull the mandate list order and compare it against who was actually called each time — does the sequence match?
- **Data to pull:** mandate log with hire dates, last-mandated dates, and call-attempt timestamps.

### Use-of-force report countersigned within the same shift with no camera-review note
- **Usually means:** the report was accepted on the officer's narrative alone, without a check against available footage.
- **First question:** was camera coverage available for that location, and if so, was the timestamp checked against the narrative before signing?
- **Data to pull:** the countersigned report, camera log for that post/time, any prior reports from the same officer for comparison.

### Officer crosses the EIS threshold with no documented review on file
- **Usually means:** the pattern was seen and not acted on, or the EIS entry itself was never logged.
- **First question:** when the threshold was crossed, what specific review and coaching conversation happened, and is it documented?
- **Data to pull:** EIS record for the officer, supervisor log for the relevant date range.

### Grievance response deadline passed with no extension on file
- **Usually means:** the response was deprioritized under shift workload, or an informal verbal answer was treated as sufficient.
- **First question:** was an extension request filed before the deadline, and if not, why not?
- **Data to pull:** grievance intake log with received/response/deadline dates.

### Progressive discipline record shows a step skipped for one officer but applied in full for another on the same misconduct category
- **Usually means:** inconsistent application driven by personal rapport rather than a documented distinguishing fact.
- **First question:** what specific, written fact distinguishes the two cases enough to justify different treatment?
- **Data to pull:** both officers' personnel discipline files for the same misconduct category, side by side.

### Key or tool control count discrepancy at shift change not resolved before the outgoing shift leaves
- **Usually means:** the count was rushed at shift change, or an item is genuinely missing and the response is being deferred.
- **First question:** is this a counting error (recount before anyone leaves) or a genuine missing item (facility lockdown protocol applies)?
- **Data to pull:** shift-change count log, prior shift's closing count for comparison.

### PREA-relevant disclosure logged with no notification timestamp to the compliance manager
- **Usually means:** the disclosure was recorded to meet a compliance checkbox but the mandatory notification step was skipped or delayed.
- **First question:** what time was the compliance manager notified relative to when the disclosure was received?
- **Data to pull:** PREA log, notification records, housing assignment history for the reporting inmate.

### A post runs below its board-approved staffing floor for two or more consecutive shifts without a logged compensating measure
- **Usually means:** mandate authority isn't being used, or the compensating-measure requirement is being treated as optional.
- **First question:** was the mandate list worked for each of those shifts, and if not, what's on record explaining why not?
- **Data to pull:** staffing plan floor for the unit, roster logs for the affected shifts, mandate call logs.

### No documented incident commander named during a logged multi-officer response
- **Usually means:** the sergeant engaged hands-on instead of directing, or command wasn't formally assumed by anyone.
- **First question:** who called the code and assigned roles, and is that person named in the after-action log?
- **Data to pull:** incident log, radio traffic timestamps, after-action report.

### A probationary officer is working a post alone before field-training sign-off is on file
- **Usually means:** a staffing gap was filled with the fastest available body rather than checking training status first.
- **First question:** is the FTO sign-off dated before or after this shift's post assignment?
- **Data to pull:** training file, post assignment roster for the date in question.
