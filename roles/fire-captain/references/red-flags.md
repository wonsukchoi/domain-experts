# Red flags

### Two or more consecutive shifts below minimum staffing on the same unit
- **Usually means:** a chronic vacancy or injury-leave gap being absorbed as day-to-day callback rather than reflected in authorized strength; less often a short-term illness cluster.
- **First question:** how many FTE vacancies does today's roster reflect against authorized strength for this unit?
- **Data to pull:** 90-day staffing/callback log by unit, cross-referenced against authorized vs. filled positions.

### Overtime budget over ~90% consumed with more than 3 months of the fiscal year remaining
- **Usually means:** structural understaffing being paid for through overtime instead of budgeted positions; sometimes a seasonal spike (wildfire season, holiday call volume) that will self-correct.
- **First question:** is the overtime concentrated in a small number of names, or spread evenly across the roster?
- **Data to pull:** OT-by-employee report, year to date, sorted by total hours.

### A single member appears on the callback list more than roughly 8 times in a 28-day cycle
- **Usually means:** either the member is deliberately maximizing callback pay, or the callback rotation isn't being enforced evenly across the eligible list.
- **First question:** what are this member's total hours worked this FLSA 28-day cycle, and how close are they to the 212-hour §7(k) threshold?
- **Data to pull:** individual hours-worked report against the 212-hour cycle, plus the callback rotation log.

### Apparatus flagged past its NFPA 1911 annual test due date and still in front-line service
- **Usually means:** a parts or vendor testing backlog, or a decision to keep the unit running because it "seems fine."
- **First question:** which specific test is overdue — pump, aerial, brake — and has any interim inspection been documented?
- **Data to pull:** apparatus maintenance and testing certificate log.

### Turnout gear beyond 10 years from manufacture date still in active rotation
- **Usually means:** budget-deferred replacement; NFPA 1851 sets a hard service-life limit regardless of visible condition.
- **First question:** has an advanced inspection been completed in the last 12 months regardless of the gear's age?
- **Data to pull:** PPE inventory log with manufacture dates and advanced-inspection sign-offs.

### Live-fire training run with an instructor-to-student ratio worse than roughly 1:5
- **Usually means:** an understaffed training cadre pushing to meet a scheduling deadline rather than postponing the evolution.
- **First question:** who is documented as the safety officer for this specific evolution?
- **Data to pull:** the NFPA 1403 training-evolution documentation packet, including instructor roster.

### Near-miss reports drop sharply with no matching drop in call volume or risk exposure
- **Usually means:** a reporting-culture collapse, not that the shift got genuinely safer.
- **First question:** when did a near-miss report last lead to a visible, communicated fix?
- **Data to pull:** near-miss log trend by month, compared against call-volume and incident-type trends.

### One member has 3 or more documented SOP violations in a quarter involving PPE, SCBA, or seatbelt use
- **Usually means:** either a genuine individual safety-culture problem, or inconsistent enforcement that let a habit form unchallenged.
- **First question:** were the prior violations documented and addressed at the time they occurred?
- **Data to pull:** the individual's discipline/coaching file, in chronological order.

### ISO PPC-relevant compliance gaps (training hours, equipment test status) only surface at the annual audit
- **Usually means:** records aren't being reconciled in real time at station level.
- **First question:** who owns the monthly reconciliation between the logs and the ISO self-assessment worksheet?
- **Data to pull:** ISO self-assessment worksheet compared line-by-line against the actual training and testing logs.

### Response times to a specific first-due area trend upward over consecutive months
- **Usually means:** apparatus or unit availability degrading — repeated brown-outs or mutual-aid substitutions — rather than a change in traffic or geography.
- **First question:** how many of those responses were run by the first-due unit versus a substitute unit from aid?
- **Data to pull:** CAD run report broken out by responding unit and first-due area.

### A member reports fatigue symptoms or requests light duty after back-to-back callback shifts
- **Usually means:** the rest interval between shifts is being compressed below department or NFPA 1500-aligned guidance.
- **First question:** how many hours of rest did this member have before their most recent callback?
- **Data to pull:** individual shift roster with actual start/end timestamps for the preceding 7 days.

### Fire-prevention inspection referrals from the station drop to near zero for a quarter
- **Usually means:** station-level prevention/education duties are being deprioritized under call-volume pressure, or referrals are happening but not being logged.
- **First question:** is this a workload problem or a data-entry problem?
- **Data to pull:** monthly prevention-contact and referral log, cross-checked against the fire marshal's office intake.
