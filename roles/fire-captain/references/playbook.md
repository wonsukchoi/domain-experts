# Playbook

Filled worksheets a captain actually runs at station level. Adapt the numbers to the department; keep the structure and the trigger rules.

## 1. Shift-staffing fill worksheet

Run at the start of any shift where a unit is below SOP minimum staffing.

| Unit | SOP minimum | On hand at 0700 | Gap | Fill option chosen | Rest/hours check | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine 14 | 4 | 3 | 1 | Callback — Alvarez | 172 hrs this 28-day FLSA cycle; last worked 3 days ago | $1,152 (24 hr × $48/hr OT) |
| Ladder 14 (RIT-capable) | 3 | 2 | 1 | Callback — Diaz (Chen skipped) | Diaz: 140 hrs, last worked 6 days ago. Chen: 205 hrs + callback 36 hrs ago — fatigue flag, not used | $1,152 |

**Fill preference order, in this sequence, each tier only after the prior one is exhausted or unsuitable:**
1. On-duty reassignment or hold-over of a rested off-going member.
2. Callback list, screened against FLSA 212-hr/28-day threshold *and* actual rest interval (not hours alone — a member under 212 hrs who worked 36 hours ago is a worse fill than one at 200 hrs who rested 6 days).
3. Automatic or mutual aid to cover the response gap without pulling the home unit.
4. Brown-out — document the specific response-time or RIT-coverage impact before choosing this, not just the dollar savings.

**Rule:** a RIT-capable or otherwise rescue-critical unit never gets a partial fill — full relief or an aid substitution, not "close enough."

## 2. Overtime run-rate tracker

Reconciled monthly against the fiscal-year budget, not just year-over-year.

| Month | Monthly OT spend | Cumulative OT | Budget | % of budget used | % of fiscal year elapsed | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Month 6 | $17,900 | $108,400 | $180,000 | 60% | 50% | Within normal variance |
| Month 7 | $18,600 | $127,000 | $180,000 | 71% | 58% | Watch |
| Month 8 | $19,200 | $146,200 | $180,000 | 81% | 67% | Escalate — gap widening |
| Month 9 | $18,900 | $165,100 | $180,000 | 92% | 75% | Escalate to battalion chief |

**Trigger rule:** when % of budget used exceeds % of fiscal year elapsed by more than ~10–15 points for two consecutive months, escalate as a staffing-model question, not a spending-discipline question. Check whether the OT is concentrated in a small number of names (fatigue/burnout risk, individual coaching) or spread department-wide (vacancy/injury-leave coverage, structural fix).

## 3. Training and certification compliance tracker

Reviewed monthly per member, not only at annual audit.

| Member | Cert/requirement | Due date | Status | Restriction if lapsed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ortiz | EMT recert | 2026-08-15 | Current | — |
| Diaz | Live-fire refresher (NFPA 1403 annual) | 2026-06-30 | 12 days overdue | Restricted from interior live-fire evolutions until recertified; not restricted from response duty |
| Nguyen | SCBA fit test | 2026-09-01 | Current | — |
| Alvarez | Driver/operator (pump) recert | 2026-07-01 | Current, expires in 19 days | Flagged for scheduling before lapse |

**Rule:** a lapsed cert restricts the member from the specific task it governs, not from the shift roster generally, unless the governing standard requires full removal (e.g., an expired SCBA fit test removes someone from any IDLH-atmosphere assignment, not just training).

**Live-fire evolution staffing check (NFPA 1403):** instructor-to-student ratio must not exceed roughly 1:5. A scheduled burn with 3 qualified instructors caps enrollment at 15 students regardless of how many are on the waitlist; postpone rather than run over-ratio.

## 4. Apparatus and PPE readiness log

Checked before each shift for anything approaching a hard standard, not just a visible defect.

| Asset | Standard | Last test/manufacture date | Next due | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engine 14 pump | NFPA 1911 annual service test | 2025-09-10 | 2026-09-10 | Current |
| Ladder 14 aerial | NFPA 1911 annual test | 2025-11-02 | 2026-11-02 | Current |
| Diaz's turnout gear | NFPA 1851, 10-yr service life from manufacture | Manufactured 2016-03 | Retire by 2026-03 | 11 months remaining — flag for budget request now |
| Reyes's turnout gear | NFPA 1851 advanced inspection, annual | 2025-10-01 | 2026-10-01 | Current |

**Rule:** "ran fine last shift" is not a substitute for a due test. An asset past its test or service-life date is pulled from front-line service until it passes, or until a documented interim inspection under department SOP explicitly clears it — the clearance itself goes in the log with who signed it.

## 5. Fire-prevention referral and community-contact log

Tracked monthly at station level — feeds both the department's actual risk picture and its ISO PPC score.

| Month | Inspection referrals made | Code-education contacts | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| Apr | 6 | 40 | Normal |
| May | 5 | 35 | Normal |
| Jun | 1 | 8 | Investigate — workload spike, or referrals happening but not logged? |

**Rule:** a sharp drop with no corresponding drop in station call volume or community contact is a data-entry or workload-prioritization problem to run down immediately, not a metric to let ride until the annual ISO self-assessment surfaces it.
