# Operating Engineer — Red Flags

### Chart was checked at the pick-point radius but the load will swing to a wider radius before it's set
- **Usually means:** the crew read the chart once at the start and never re-checked capacity at the widest point in the lift path
- **First question:** what's the largest working radius the load passes through from pick to set, and what does the chart show there?
- **Data to pull:** load chart at the maximum radius in the planned path, rigging plan/lift path drawing

### Load weight compared to gross chart capacity with no rigging deduction shown
- **Usually means:** hook block, ball, slings, or spreader bar weight was never subtracted from the chart's gross number
- **First question:** what's the total weight hanging below the hook, and what's net capacity after subtracting it?
- **Data to pull:** rigging weight list, chart gross capacity at the working radius, net capacity calculation

### Crane set up with outriggers only partially extended, chart read as if fully extended
- **Usually means:** the on-outriggers chart is being applied to a configuration it doesn't cover
- **First question:** are the outriggers fully extended and the crane leveled, or is this actually a partial/on-rubber configuration?
- **Data to pull:** outrigger extension confirmation, level check, chart page matching the actual configuration

### Mechanized excavation reaching the painted locate line without a prior hand-dig/pothole
- **Usually means:** the locate mark is being treated as an exact line instead of the centerline of an approximate tolerance zone
- **First question:** has this zone been hand-exposed, and what's the stated tolerance band for this locate ticket?
- **Data to pull:** locate ticket, tolerance-zone specification from the responding utility owner, pothole log

### Locate ticket older than the jurisdiction's stated validity window, still being worked against
- **Usually means:** marks have likely faded, been disturbed, or been graded/paved over since the ticket was issued
- **First question:** when was this ticket issued, and has the job extended past its stated validity window?
- **Data to pull:** ticket issue date and validity window, current condition of marks on the ground

### Grade-control spot-check reads outside tolerance partway through a cut, work continues without re-checking the benchmark
- **Usually means:** a shifted or bumped benchmark producing a consistent wrong reading across the whole run, not a one-off error
- **First question:** has the benchmark itself been re-verified against a known control point since the deviation appeared?
- **Data to pull:** benchmark verification log, grade-control system calibration record, spot-check history across the run

### Ground personnel or foot traffic present within the machine's swing radius with no barricade or signal person assigned
- **Usually means:** the exclusion zone was never calculated for this configuration, or a horn/wave is being relied on instead of a physical or communicated boundary
- **First question:** what's the calculated exclusion zone (working radius plus counterweight swing plus buffer), and is a qualified signal person stationed?
- **Data to pull:** exclusion-zone calculation, signal person assignment and sightline confirmation, barricade placement

### Load is at or above roughly 85% of net chart capacity for what's described as a routine lift
- **Usually means:** the working margin below the chart's built-in safety factor has been used up, leaving little buffer for wind, pad settlement, or side-loading during the swing
- **First question:** is there a configuration change (closer crane position, different boom length, added counterweight) that would restore margin instead of proceeding at this radius?
- **Data to pull:** net capacity at the actual working radius, percentage of net capacity the load represents, alternative configuration options

### Wind conditions at or near the OEM's stated limit for the crane's current configuration, lift proceeding on schedule pressure
- **Usually means:** the stand-down decision is being weighed against the schedule instead of the OEM chart note for this specific boom length and load
- **First question:** what does the OEM chart or lift plan specify as the wind limit for this exact configuration, and where does current wind sit against it?
- **Data to pull:** OEM wind-limit note for the boom/load configuration, current wind reading, anemometer log if available

### Excavator or loader with a conventional (non-zero) tail swing operating close to a fixed object or trench edge
- **Usually means:** the exclusion-zone calculation only accounted for the bucket/boom side and missed the rear house swing
- **First question:** has the machine's specific tail-swing dimension been checked against clearance on the rear/side of the machine, not just the working side?
- **Data to pull:** machine spec sheet tail-swing dimension, measured clearance to the nearest fixed object

### Same locate discrepancy (depth, offset, or unmarked line) found during potholing and not reported back to the one-call center
- **Usually means:** the next crew or the next phase of work on the same site will hit the same unreported discrepancy
- **First question:** has this discrepancy been logged with the one-call center and the utility owner, not just noted on this crew's log?
- **Data to pull:** discrepancy report, original locate ticket, as-found utility depth/offset measurement
