# Red flags

### Praxis 5122 first-attempt pass rate below 80% for a cohort, or a drop of >15 points from the prior cohort
- **Usually means:** contact hours in one or two exam categories have drifted below that category's exam weighting; second most likely is a delivery-quality issue in a category already getting adequate hours.
- **First question:** which of the four Praxis categories are the misses clustered in, per the ETS category-level score report?
- **Data to pull:** ETS sub-score reports for the cohort; the curriculum-to-standard mapping matrix's contact-hour column for the flagged categories.

### Standalone FCS major enrollment down two or more consecutive years
- **Usually means:** a name/branding perception problem ("home economics" association) driving prospective-student avoidance; second most likely is a genuine placement or career-outcome weakness the program hasn't documented well enough to counter the perception.
- **First question:** what do exit surveys and admitted-but-didn't-enroll surveys say prospective students believed the major was, before deciding whether this is a marketing problem or an outcomes problem?
- **Data to pull:** 3-year enrollment trend, placement-rate data by track, admitted-student survey responses citing program name or reputation.

### One faculty member holding self-study responsibility for two or more accreditors (e.g., CAEP and ACEND) with no dedicated release time
- **Usually means:** the department's growth in specialty tracks has outpaced its administrative staffing; second most likely is that the tracks were merged into one department for budget reasons without an accreditation-workload review.
- **First question:** what is this faculty member's current course load relative to peers carrying only one accreditor's self-study?
- **Data to pull:** self-study calendar for every accreditor in the department; faculty course-load and service-credit records for the current and prior two years.

### Articulation agreement with a feeder high school not reviewed in more than 3 years
- **Usually means:** no single calendar date forces the review, so it lapses by default; second most likely is a district-side contact change that nobody on the postsecondary side was notified of.
- **First question:** does the current high-school syllabus for the articulated course still match what the postsecondary course description assumes?
- **Data to pull:** the original articulation agreement text, the current high-school course syllabus, and the transfer-credit records of the last 3 students who used the agreement.

### Curriculum-mapping matrix shows a content area under 10% of contact hours against a standard weighting near 25%
- **Usually means:** course assignments were made by faculty availability rather than by exam or standard weighting; second most likely is a legacy course structure that predates the current adopted standards revision.
- **First question:** when was the current course's contact-hour allocation last set, and against which version of the standards?
- **Data to pull:** current syllabus contact-hour breakdown; the adopted standards document's stated weighting or emphasis for that content area.

### Perkins V performance indicator (placement rate, nontraditional enrollment) below the state-negotiated target for two consecutive reporting years
- **Usually means:** the "concentrator" definition or program-of-study sequence has drifted from what the state's current Perkins plan requires; second most likely is a genuine placement problem in the local labor market for that CTE pathway.
- **First question:** does the current program-of-study sequence still match the state's most recently approved Perkins V plan for this CTE area?
- **Data to pull:** state Perkins V performance report for the program; concentrator counts by the federal definition, not raw enrollment.

### FCCLA chapter advised by the program's student teachers shows zero STAR Event participation for a full year
- **Usually means:** the methods course is treating STAR Events as optional enrichment rather than a required competency the student teacher is evaluated on; second most likely is a placement-site FCCLA chapter that was already inactive before the student teacher arrived.
- **First question:** did the student-teaching evaluation rubric include any FCCLA-advising competency at all this term?
- **Data to pull:** the student-teaching evaluation rubric; the placement site's FCCLA chapter activity log for the prior 2 years.

### A dietetics-track (or other specialty-track) student fails competency verification at internship placement after completing all listed coursework
- **Usually means:** the department's course-to-competency mapping for that specialty accreditor (e.g., ACEND) hasn't been updated since the accreditor's last standards revision; second most likely is an internship site applying a stricter local standard than the accreditor's minimum.
- **First question:** which specific competency did the internship site flag, and does the current course map show which course was supposed to cover it?
- **Data to pull:** the specialty accreditor's current competency list; the department's course-to-competency crosswalk; the internship site's verification report.

### Two or more accreditation self-study cycles land in the same academic year with separate, non-overlapping evidence files being built
- **Usually means:** nobody has plotted all the department's accreditor cycles on one shared calendar; second most likely is genuinely unrelated cycle timing that happened to collide.
- **First question:** how much of the evidence (syllabi, contact-hour data, faculty CVs) is duplicated across the two self-studies right now?
- **Data to pull:** both self-study evidence files' tables of contents, compared side by side.

### Administration proposes renaming or merging the FCS department with no FCS faculty input in the initial framing
- **Usually means:** a budget or enrollment-driven restructuring decision made from outcome data alone, without anyone framing the discipline-content case for the faculty; second most likely is a genuine, already-settled decision being communicated late rather than proposed.
- **First question:** what outcome data (enrollment, placement, licensure pass rates) is driving the administration's framing, and does the department have equally specific data to respond with?
- **Data to pull:** the administration's stated rationale document; the department's own 3-year enrollment, placement, and pass-rate trends.
