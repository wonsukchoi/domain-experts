# Vocabulary

### Home economics
The field's pre-1994 name, before the American Home Economics Association renamed itself the American Association of Family & Consumer Sciences.
**In use:** "The rename wasn't cosmetic — it tracked a real shift from home-management skills toward consumer science, family resource management, and human development research."
**Common misuse:** Generalists (and some administrators) treat "home economics" and "family and consumer sciences" as interchangeable labels for the same cooking-and-sewing curriculum, missing that the field's research base and course content changed substantially around the rename.

### Body of Knowledge (BOK)
AAFCS's disciplinary integration framework spanning human development, family/consumer resources, nutrition and wellness, housing, and textiles/apparel.
**In use:** "Our curriculum map assigns one course per BOK area, but the BOK is arguing for integration across areas, not a checklist of coverage."
**Common misuse:** Treating the BOK as a course-by-course checklist to satisfy once, rather than the field's stated case for why the areas belong together in one discipline.

### National Standards for Family and Consumer Sciences Education
NASAFACS's content-area framework for K-12 and CTE FCS curricula, most recently revised in 2018.
**In use:** "Before redesigning the methods course, pull the state's currently adopted revision of the National Standards — some states are still on an older version than 2018."
**Common misuse:** Assuming every state has adopted the same, most recent revision, or that a program's compliance with one state's version transfers automatically to another state's licensure requirements.

### Concentrator (Perkins V)
A federally defined status under the Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act: a student who has completed a specified credit threshold within an approved CTE program of study.
**In use:** "Our placement-rate report only counts concentrators, not every student who ever enrolled in an FCS elective."
**Common misuse:** Conflating "concentrator" with total program enrollment or with "major," which inflates or deflates the federally reportable placement and performance numbers.

### Articulation agreement
A formal, signed agreement specifying which high-school CTE course credits transfer into which postsecondary course, under what conditions.
**In use:** "The agreement hasn't been reviewed since the district changed its culinary arts syllabus three years ago — treat the transfer credit as unverified until we re-check it."
**Common misuse:** Assuming an articulation agreement is self-maintaining or automatically current, rather than a document that requires periodic re-verification against both sides' actual course content.

### FCCLA STAR Events
"Students Taking Action with Recognition" — Family, Career and Community Leaders of America's structured competitive-event program with official rubrics, at chapter, regional, state, and national levels.
**In use:** "A student teacher who has only watched a STAR Event, never judged one against the rubric, isn't ready to advise a chapter through it."
**Common misuse:** Treating STAR Events as an optional after-school enrichment activity rather than a rubric-driven competitive structure that a licensed FCS teacher is expected to run and that a methods course should train pre-service teachers to judge and coach.

### Praxis Family and Consumer Sciences (5122)
ETS's subject-specific licensure content exam for FCS teacher candidates, reported both as an overall pass/fail and as category-level sub-scores.
**In use:** "Pull the category-level sub-scores before assuming the whole cohort needs more test prep — the misses are probably clustered in one or two categories."
**Common misuse:** Tracking only the overall pass rate and discarding the category-level diagnostic data ETS already provides, which is the data that actually points to which course needs redesigning.

### CAEP (Council for Accreditation of Educator Preparation)
The national accreditor for educator-preparation programs broadly, covering FCS teacher-ed alongside every other teaching license area at an institution.
**In use:** "CAEP's Standard 4 is what requires us to report completer licensure pass rates every cycle, not just at self-study time."
**Common misuse:** Confusing CAEP (which accredits the whole educator-preparation unit) with CAFCS (which accredits the FCS-specific teacher-ed program structure within it) — they review different things on different but sometimes overlapping calendars.

### CAFCS (Council for Accreditation of Family and Consumer Sciences)
AAFCS's accreditation body specifically for FCS teacher-education programs and other FCS-related degree programs.
**In use:** "Our CAFCS self-study and CAEP self-study land the same year, so we're building one shared evidence file mapped to both standard sets."
**Common misuse:** Treating a CAFCS self-study as redundant with CAEP's, when the two accreditors' standard language and review focus differ even when the underlying course evidence overlaps.

### Program of study (Perkins V)
A federally defined, sequenced set of secondary-to-postsecondary CTE courses within a single career pathway, required for Perkins funding eligibility.
**In use:** "The state's program-of-study sequence for culinary arts changed with the last Perkins reauthorization — our articulation agreement needs to be checked against the current version, not the one it was signed under."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a program of study, once approved, stays valid indefinitely rather than being tied to a specific Perkins reauthorization and state plan that can change the sequence or credit thresholds.

### Human ecology / human ecosystems framework
The theoretical lens (individuals, families, and communities as interacting systems within physical, biological, and social environments) underlying much of the modern FCS discipline.
**In use:** "The human-ecosystems framework is why a nutrition course and a family-resource-management course sit in the same department — they're both about how individuals function within interacting systems."
**Common misuse:** Treating "human ecology" as a synonym for environmental science, rather than the discipline-specific framework linking individual, family, and community well-being.

### ACEND (Accreditation Council for Education in Nutrition and Dietetics)
The specialty accreditor for dietetics degree and internship programs, which may sit inside a broader FCS department alongside the teacher-ed track.
**In use:** "The dietetics track's competency crosswalk needs its own ACEND-specific review — it doesn't ride along automatically with the CAEP self-study."
**Common misuse:** Assuming one department-wide accreditation review covers every track's specialty accreditor, when ACEND (like CIDA for interior design) reviews its own track independently on its own cycle.

### Self-study
The formal evidence document an accredited program prepares ahead of a site visit, mapping current practice against the accreditor's standards.
**In use:** "We're drafting one shared self-study evidence file this cycle since CAEP and CAFCS both come due the same year."
**Common misuse:** Treating the self-study as a one-time compliance document rather than the accreditor's expected ongoing evidence trail, parts of which should be maintained continuously rather than assembled retroactively in the year it's due.
