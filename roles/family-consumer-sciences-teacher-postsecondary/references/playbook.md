# Playbook

Filled examples an agent can execute or populate directly. Numbers are plausible defaults, not universal constants — swap in the program's actual data before using.

## 1. Curriculum-to-standard mapping matrix (filled)

Use before any accreditation self-study or after any pass-rate drop. Columns: content area, exam/standard weighting, current contact hours, gap flag.

| NASAFACS / Praxis 5122 content area | Exam weight | Current contact hours (of 45) | Gap flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| I. Human Development & Family Studies | ~25% | 3 | **Under** — target ~11h |
| II. Nutrition, Food Science & Wellness | ~25% | 3 | **Under** — target ~11h |
| III. Textiles, Apparel, Housing & Design | ~25% | 12 | At target |
| IV. Consumer & Family Resources, Education & Careers | ~25% | 27 | **Over** — candidate for trimming to ~12h |

**Rule of thumb:** flag any area more than 5 percentage points off its exam/standard weight as a share of total contact hours. Rebalance by moving hours from the most-over area first, not by simply adding hours everywhere (credit-hour budgets are fixed).

## 2. Accreditation self-study master calendar (filled example)

For a department carrying teacher-ed plus one specialty track:

| Accreditor | Governs | Cycle length | Next self-study due | Site visit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CAEP / state licensure board | Teacher-ed track (methods, student teaching, Praxis) | 7 years | Yr 5 (self-study drafted) | Yr 7, spring |
| CAFCS | FCS teacher-ed program structure specifically | 7 years | Yr 5 | Yr 7, fall |
| ACEND (if dietetics track present) | Dietetics competencies, internship placement | 5 years (or on-demand for new programs) | Yr 3 | Yr 5 |
| CIDA (if interior design track present) | Interior design studio competencies | 6 years | Yr 4 | Yr 6 |

**Rule of thumb:** when two cycles fall in the same academic year (as CAEP/CAFCS commonly do, since CAFCS review is often bundled into the state's CAEP visit), draft one shared evidence file mapped to both standard sets rather than two separate binders — most contact-hour and syllabus evidence is identical, only the citation language differs.

## 3. Praxis 5122 diagnostic-to-remediation workflow (filled)

Step sequence with thresholds, for use the semester after any cohort's score reports return:

1. **Pull category-level score reports** for every candidate (ETS provides four category sub-scores per examinee, not just pass/fail).
2. **Compute per-category miss rate**: (candidates below category-level proficiency band) / (cohort size). Flag any category above 25% miss rate.
3. **Cross-reference flagged categories against the curriculum mapping matrix** (section 1). A flagged category with contact hours already at or above its exam weight points to a delivery-quality problem, not a coverage problem — pull student evaluations and syllabi before touching hours.
4. **A flagged category with contact hours below its exam weight** — the more common case — triggers a contact-hour reallocation for the next cohort, sized in section 1's terms.
5. **Add a week-8 practice-test checkpoint** in the redesigned course, keyed to the same four categories. Any candidate scoring more than one category standard-error band below the state cut enters a mandatory remediation block (recommended: 4 contact hours per flagged category) before the licensure exam window opens.
6. **Track the redesign's own before/after category pass rate** for one full cohort cycle before declaring the fix worked — a single cohort's swing can be noise; two consecutive improved cohorts is the signal.

## 4. FCCLA STAR Event pre-service training sequence (filled)

Methods-course unit that trains candidates to run competitive events they will advise as licensed teachers, not just attend as chaperones:

| Week | Activity | Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Read FCCLA STAR Events national handbook, current-year event list and rubrics | Event-selection memo: which 2 events this candidate would advise first-year |
| 2 | Judge a recorded/archived STAR Event round using the official rubric | Completed rubric with score justification per criterion |
| 3 | Draft a chapter-level event calendar (local qualifier → regional → state) with real deadline dates from the current handbook | Filled calendar with all deadline dates |
| 4 | Mock-advise: coach a peer "competitor" through one event's project requirements | Advising notes + one round of feedback given |

**Rule of thumb:** a candidate who has only watched STAR Events, never judged or advised one, is not ready to run a chapter — judging exposes the rubric's actual weighting in a way observation doesn't.

## 5. Articulation agreement review checklist (filled)

For any high-school CTE program of study feeding credit into the postsecondary FCS program:

- [ ] Agreement reviewed within the last 3 years (flag immediately if not — treat credit-transfer terms as unverified until reviewed)
- [ ] High-school course syllabi obtained and compared line-by-line against the postsecondary course they claim to satisfy
- [ ] Perkins V "program of study" sequence confirmed current (concentrator credit thresholds can change between Perkins reauthorizations)
- [ ] Transfer-credit cap confirmed (how many credits can transfer in, and whether that cap has changed since the last cohort used the agreement)
- [ ] Named contact at the high-school district confirmed still in the role that signed the original agreement

**Rule of thumb:** default to re-verifying a stale agreement before the first transfer student under it enrolls, not after — a credit dispute discovered mid-semester is far more disruptive to fix than one caught during summer advising.
