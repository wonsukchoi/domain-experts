# Red flags

### Daily source check reads outside the expected count-rate range (typically ±20% of the established mean) before a survey is performed
- **Usually means:** probe contamination, mechanical/electronic drift, or an undocumented source-decay correction issue — the instrument's readings for that day aren't trustworthy yet.
- **First question:** has the check source's decay-corrected expected count rate been recalculated recently, and does the failure track a specific probe or the whole instrument?
- **Data to pull:** source-check log trend over the last 10–20 checks, and the instrument's calibration due date.

### Corrected smear result at or within 10% of the removable contamination action level
- **Usually means:** the area is trending toward a contamination control problem even though the individual reading technically passes.
- **First question:** what did the last three smear results at this same location show — is this an isolated reading or a trend?
- **Data to pull:** location-specific smear survey history for the last several routine surveys.

### MDA for the count time/instrument combination in use is equal to or above the action level being checked against
- **Usually means:** a "not detected" result from this setup isn't usable evidence of compliance — the instrument or count time can't actually resolve a violation from a clean surface.
- **First question:** what count time or instrument change would bring MDA safely below the action level?
- **Data to pull:** calculated MDA for the current background, count time, and efficiency versus the applicable action level.

### CAM (continuous air monitor) alarm with no independently counted grab/filter sample to confirm it
- **Usually means:** either a real airborne radioactivity event or a false alarm from a known benign cause (e.g., natural radon progeny) — indistinguishable without the independent count.
- **First question:** has the filter been pulled and independently counted, and does its result match the CAM's alarm-level reading?
- **Data to pull:** CAM alarm log timestamp, filter/grab sample count result, and local dose-rate/particulate conditions at alarm time.

### Technician's period dose reading reaches roughly 80% of the site's ALARA administrative control level, well below the 5 rem/yr regulatory limit
- **Usually means:** continued unrestricted RCA access for this technician risks an exceedance of the site's own internal trigger, independent of the regulatory limit still being far away.
- **First question:** has RP supervisor review and task reassignment been considered before further controlled-area entry this period?
- **Data to pull:** individual dosimetry trend for the current dose period against the site ALARA administrative control level.

### Stay time for a job calculated from a single RWP-listed average dose rate rather than the peak dose rate on the current survey map
- **Usually means:** the calculated stay time under-budgets dose for any hot-spot portion of the job, risking a mid-task dose-budget exceedance.
- **First question:** does the current survey map show a uniform field, or are there localized higher-dose-rate spots the RWP average doesn't capture?
- **Data to pull:** current radiological survey map for the specific work location versus the RWP's listed dose-rate estimate.

### Gamma spectroscopy result shows an isotope not expected for the source/process being sampled
- **Usually means:** either a process upset introducing an unexpected nuclide, cross-contamination of the sample, or a spectroscopy calibration/identification error.
- **First question:** is the unexpected isotope consistent with a known process condition, or does it point to sample handling or instrument calibration?
- **Data to pull:** current spectroscopy calibration records and recent process/operations log for the sampled system.

### A calibration due date has passed on an instrument still in active use for surveys
- **Usually means:** results generated on that instrument since the due date are not valid survey data, regardless of how normal the readings look.
- **First question:** which surveys were performed on this instrument since the due date, and do they need to be redone on a currently calibrated instrument?
- **Data to pull:** instrument calibration records and the log of surveys performed on that instrument since the due date.

### Fixed contamination assumed cleared based solely on a passing removable (smear) survey result
- **Usually means:** the item's total contamination — including non-removable fixed activity — hasn't actually been assessed, only the fraction that transfers to a wipe.
- **First question:** does the release procedure for this item/area require a separate fixed-contamination or dose-rate assessment beyond the smear result?
- **Data to pull:** applicable release procedure's required survey types for this item or area classification.

### Removable contamination found outside the posted boundary of a radiologically controlled area
- **Usually means:** contamination spread beyond the controlled boundary — a control breakdown, not an isolated reading to log and move past.
- **First question:** what is the extent of the spread, and does the RCA boundary or posting need to be adjusted immediately?
- **Data to pull:** extent-of-contamination survey results and current RCA boundary/posting documentation.
