# Customs Broker — Playbook

## Landed duty calculation worksheet (filled example)

| Step | Component | Rate | Base | Amount |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Customs value (transaction value, FOB, freight/insurance excluded) | — | — | $50,000.00 |
| 2 | General duty rate (HTS 7318.15.80.30) | 6.2% | $50,000.00 | $3,100.00 |
| 3 | Section 301 additional duty (HTS 9903.88.03, List 3) | 25% | $50,000.00 | $12,500.00 |
| 4 | Merchandise Processing Fee (MPF) | 0.3464% | $50,000.00 | $173.20 (within $27.75-$538.40 range) |
| 5 | Harbor Maintenance Fee (HMF, ocean only) | 0.125% | $50,000.00 | $62.50 |
| | **Total duty and fees** | | | **$15,835.70** |

**Use:** Recalculate rows 2-3 whenever HTS classification or origin changes; recheck row 3 against the current Section 301/232 or AD/CVD list before every entry, since these rates and product scopes change by federal notice, not on a fixed schedule.

## Trade-agreement preference vs. general/Section 301 rate comparison

| Scenario | Duty basis | Requirement to claim |
|---|---|---|
| No preference claimed | General HTS rate (+ any Section 301/AD-CVD) | None — default treatment |
| USMCA preference claimed | Preferential rate (often 0%) if rule of origin met | Signed certification of origin on file; product meets regional value content or tariff-shift rule |
| Foreign-trade zone (FTZ) admission | Duty deferred until withdrawal from zone; can elect lower of raw-material or finished-good rate | FTZ operator agreement; accurate inventory control and recordkeeping system |
| Duty drawback (post-entry) | Refund of up to 99% of duties paid on re-exported or destroyed goods | Traceable linkage (specific lot or approved accounting method) from entry to qualifying export/destruction |

**Use:** Never assume the lowest-rate row applies by default — each requires its own affirmative documentation, checked before or very shortly after entry, not reconstructed after the fact during an audit.

## Filing timeline (ocean shipment, formal entry)

| Event | Deadline | Consequence of missing |
|---|---|---|
| Importer Security Filing (ISF, "10+2") | At least 24 hours before vessel loading at foreign port | Liquidated damages claim (commonly $5,000 per violation), possible cargo hold on arrival |
| Entry filing (goods arrival) | Concurrent with or shortly after arrival, per port practice | Cargo held at port, demurrage charges accrue |
| Entry summary (CBP Form 7501) and duty payment | Within the CBP-specified post-release window | Late-filing penalties, interest on unpaid duty |
| Liquidation | CBP-determined, typically within ~314 days of entry absent extension | Starts the 180-day protest clock |
| Protest deadline | 180 days from liquidation date | Loss of ability to dispute classification/valuation/duty on this entry |

**Use:** Track liquidation date per entry explicitly — it is not the entry date, and the 180-day protest window is measured from it, not from when the overpayment was discovered.

## Reasonable-care documentation checklist (per classification decision)

1. HTS heading and subheading selected, with GRI reasoning noted.
2. CROSS binding-ruling search performed and result recorded (ruling number if one applies, or confirmation none found).
3. Country-of-origin determination with supporting basis (manufacturing records, mill certificate, or substantial-transformation analysis).
4. Applicable additional-duty program check (Section 301/232, AD/CVD) performed as of the entry date, with source list/version noted.
5. Trade-agreement preference claim (if any) supported by a signed, on-file certification of origin.
6. Customs value basis documented, including any assists identified and added.

**Use:** This checklist is the record CBP expects to see reconstructed during an audit — treat each classification decision as if it will be reviewed two years later with no other memory of the transaction available.
