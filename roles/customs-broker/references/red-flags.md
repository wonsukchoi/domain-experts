### Classification chosen "by analogy" to a past shipment with no CROSS ruling check
- **Usually means:** The product or its specs changed slightly since the last shipment, or a binding ruling now exists that supersedes the prior informal classification.
- **First question:** Has this exact product (or a materially similar one) been checked against CBP's CROSS database recently?
- **Data to pull:** CROSS ruling search results, product spec sheet, prior entry's classification rationale.

### Commercial invoice value looks unusually low relative to the product's market price
- **Usually means:** Undervaluation to reduce duty, an unreported assist (buyer-supplied tooling/materials), or a related-party transfer price that doesn't reflect arm's-length transaction value.
- **First question:** Is the buyer and seller related, and if so, has the transaction value been tested against an arm's-length standard?
- **Data to pull:** Purchase order, any side agreements on tooling/materials supplied by the buyer, related-party disclosure.

### Country of origin is declared as the shipping country without a manufacturing/assembly trail
- **Usually means:** Goods transited or were lightly processed (repackaged, relabeled) in a third country without genuine substantial transformation — a common tariff-evasion pattern, especially where Section 301 or antidumping duties apply to the true origin.
- **First question:** What specific manufacturing or assembly operation occurred in the declared origin country, and does it rise to substantial transformation?
- **Data to pull:** Mill/manufacturing certificate, bill of materials, factory production records.

### A trade-agreement (e.g., USMCA) preference is claimed with no certification of origin on file
- **Usually means:** The preference was assumed based on the shipping route rather than verified against the agreement's specific rule-of-origin text and documentation requirement.
- **First question:** Is there a valid, signed certification of origin for this specific shipment, and does the product meet the agreement's regional value content or tariff-shift rule?
- **Data to pull:** Certification of origin, bill of materials with country-of-origin per component, regional value content calculation.

### A shipment is placed on hold or receives a CBP Request for Information (CBP Form 28)
- **Usually means:** A classification, valuation, or origin claim didn't match CBP's risk-targeting expectations for this product/importer/origin combination.
- **First question:** What exact question is CBP asking, and does the existing documentation answer it directly?
- **Data to pull:** The CBP Form 28 itself, all entry documents, any prior rulings relied on.

### Liquidation date is approaching or has passed without a protest filed on a known overpayment
- **Usually means:** An identified duty overpayment or misclassification wasn't escalated in time, or the 180-day protest deadline wasn't tracked against the actual liquidation date.
- **First question:** What is the exact liquidation date on this entry, and how many of the 180 days remain?
- **Data to pull:** CBP liquidation notice, entry summary date, protest filing log.

### Section 301/232 or antidumping/countervailing duty applicability wasn't checked for this HTS/origin pair
- **Usually means:** The classification and origin were confirmed, but the additional-duty program lists (which change more frequently than the base tariff schedule) weren't checked against the current entry date.
- **First question:** Has the current Section 301 exclusion/modification list and any active AD/CVD scope ruling been checked for this specific HTS code and origin, as of today's date?
- **Data to pull:** Current USTR Section 301 action list, Commerce Department AD/CVD scope ruling database.

### The importer's continuous bond amount looks insufficient for the shipment's declared value and duty exposure
- **Usually means:** Bond sufficiency wasn't recalculated after an increase in import volume, value, or duty rate (e.g., a new Section 301 tariff on existing volume).
- **First question:** When was the bond amount last recalculated against current annual duty, tax, and fee exposure?
- **Data to pull:** Current continuous bond amount and CBP's bond sufficiency worksheet.

### A duty-drawback or foreign-trade-zone claim has weak linking documentation between imported and exported/processed goods
- **Usually means:** The claim exists on paper but the required traceability (lot tracking, manufacturing records linking specific imported inputs to specific exports) isn't actually in place.
- **First question:** Can this specific drawback claim be traced, unit for unit or by an approved accounting method, back to the original import entry?
- **Data to pull:** Drawback claim file, inventory/lot tracking records, original import entry summary.
