# Red flags

### A helper making an independent quality-acceptance or machine-adjustment decision
- **Usually means:** a decision requiring skilled-worker context/training was made without that context, risking a wrong call.
- **First question:** was this decision within the helper's authorized scope, or should it have been flagged to the responsible skilled worker?
- **Data to pull:** the task scope definition for this role, the specific decision made and by whom.

### An unusual observation (noise, spill, irregularity) not reported until well after it was noticed
- **Usually means:** a problem may have continued or worsened during the delay, and downstream cost/risk grew unnecessarily.
- **First question:** when was the observation first noticed, and when was it actually reported?
- **Data to pull:** timestamp of observation, timestamp of report, any downstream consequence in the interim.

### An observation reported in filtered/minimized language ("probably nothing")
- **Usually means:** the actual severity may not register with the skilled worker receiving the report, delaying appropriate action.
- **First question:** was the observation relayed as-observed (what was seen/heard, how long, how many), or minimized in the telling?
- **Data to pull:** the actual report language used, the underlying observation's raw characteristics.

### A specific instruction improvised on rather than followed precisely
- **Usually means:** the deviation may have disrupted a downstream process requirement the helper didn't have visibility into.
- **First question:** was the instruction followed exactly, and if not, what specific deviation occurred and why?
- **Data to pull:** the original instruction, the actual action taken, any downstream effect.

### A helper continuing one assigned task while a more urgent need elsewhere went unaddressed
- **Usually means:** overall floor risk/output may have been worse than if attention had been reallocated to the more urgent need.
- **First question:** was the helper aware of the more urgent need, and if so, why wasn't attention reallocated?
- **Data to pull:** the competing needs and their relative urgency, the helper's actual task sequence during that period.

### A helper working in a multi-hazard area without demonstrated awareness of moving equipment/hazard zones
- **Usually means:** elevated risk of an incident, since the helper's mobility across the floor requires continuous hazard tracking a stationary worker wouldn't need to the same degree.
- **First question:** does the helper's movement pattern show consistent hazard-zone awareness, or were there lapses?
- **Data to pull:** the work area's hazard zone map, any near-miss or incident history involving helpers in this area.

### A helper's task scope expanding informally over time without a corresponding update to training/authorization
- **Usually means:** the helper may now be routinely performing tasks beyond what their training/authorization actually covers.
- **First question:** does the helper's current actual task set match their documented scope and training, or has it drifted?
- **Data to pull:** documented scope/authorization, actual tasks performed over a recent period.
