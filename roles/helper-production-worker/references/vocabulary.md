# Vocabulary

### Scope boundary
The defined set of tasks a helper is authorized to perform independently, distinct from tasks (adjustment, quality acceptance, safety-critical calls) requiring the skilled worker's judgment.
**In use:** "That's outside my scope boundary — flagging it to the operator rather than adjusting it myself."
**Common misuse:** Treating scope boundary as a rigid list memorized once rather than a live judgment applied per situation — ambiguous cases should default to asking, not to assuming inclusion or exclusion.

### Observation relay
The act of promptly and accurately reporting something noticed (a sound, irregularity, spill, near-miss) to the responsible skilled worker, without filtering it as unimportant or exaggerating it.
**In use:** "Observation relay on the grinding sound went straight to the operator within a minute of hearing it."
**Common misuse:** Editorializing an observation before relaying it ("probably nothing, but...") — the raw, as-observed detail (what, how long, how many) is what enables the skilled worker to judge severity themselves.

### Near-miss
An event where a hazard or defect nearly caused harm/damage but didn't, still worth reporting since it reveals a risk that could cause actual harm next time.
**In use:** "That was a near-miss with the pallet jack — reporting it even though nothing happened."
**Common misuse:** Not reporting a near-miss because "nothing actually happened" — the value of reporting is in preventing the next occurrence from having a worse outcome.

### Task precision (instruction-following)
Executing a skilled worker's specific instruction exactly as given, without improvised deviation, because the instruction is based on process context the helper may not fully see.
**In use:** "Held it at exactly the angle specified — didn't adjust based on how it looked to me, since the operator set that angle for a reason I don't have full visibility into."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a locally-reasonable improvised adjustment is harmless — a deviation can disrupt a downstream process requirement invisible from the helper's vantage point.

### Attention reallocation
Shifting focus to the most urgent real-time support need across a work area, rather than continuing a lower-priority assigned task while a more pressing one goes unaddressed.
**In use:** "Reallocated from stacking to the spill at station 4 since that was the more urgent need."
**Common misuse:** Rigidly finishing an assigned task regardless of a more urgent competing need — the assignment was made under prior conditions, not a fixed commitment regardless of what changes.

### Hazard zone awareness
Continuous tracking of moving equipment and dangerous areas while moving through a multi-operation work area, a heightened requirement for mobile support roles compared to a stationary operator.
**In use:** "Kept hazard zone awareness up while crossing behind the forklift path to reach station 6."
**Common misuse:** Treating hazard awareness as a one-time orientation/training checkbox rather than an ongoing state maintained throughout movement across the floor.

### Material staging
Positioning and organizing materials or parts in advance of when a skilled operator will need them, a core helper task distinct from the operator's own process execution.
**In use:** "Staged the next batch of blanks at the infeed so the operator doesn't have to pause the line."
**Common misuse:** Staging material without confirming the operator's actual sequence/timing needs — staging ahead of the wrong point in the process can create clutter or a bottleneck rather than smoothing the workflow.

### Shift handoff report
A summary of task completion status and any unresolved observations communicated from one shift's helper to the next or to a supervisor.
**In use:** "Shift handoff report noted the burr-defect parts set aside are still pending the day-shift operator's disposition call."
**Common misuse:** Omitting unresolved observations from a handoff because the reporting helper's own shift is ending — an unresolved item needs to transfer forward, not lapse with the shift change.
