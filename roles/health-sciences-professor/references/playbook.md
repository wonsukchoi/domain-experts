# Playbook

Filled sequences and templates for the recurring processes in running an accredited health-professions program's curriculum and clinical operation. Defaults, not laws — deviate consciously and document why.

## 1. Licensure/board pass-rate cohort tracker

Track first-time pass rate per cohort and the rolling aggregate the state board or accreditor actually uses for approval status — not just the single-year number.

| Cohort year | First-time takers | First-time passed | Pass rate | Rolling 2-yr aggregate | Status vs. 80% floor |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2022 | 58 | 52 | 89.7% | — | — |
| 2023 | 62 | 56 | 90.3% | (52+56)/(58+62)=90.0% | Above |
| 2024 | 60 | 44 | 73.3% | (56+44)/(62+60)=82.0% | Above, but trending down |
| 2025 (projected, same rate as 2024) | 60 | 44 (est.) | 73.3% (est.) | (44+44)/(60+60)=73.3% | **Below — projected warning** |

**Reading the table:** a single below-floor cohort rarely triggers action by itself; the rolling aggregate is what the board acts on. Project the aggregate forward one cohort using the worst plausible next-cohort rate before deciding "no action needed" — a program that only reacts once the aggregate has already crossed the floor has lost the one cohort's worth of lead time it needed to fix the curriculum in sequence.

## 2. Curriculum-to-accreditor-standard mapping matrix

Build before an accreditor citation response, not after — most citations are found in this mapping gap.

| Accreditor standard (exact clause) | Required content/competency | Course(s) currently covering it | Assessment evidence | Mapping status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| CCNE / AACN Essentials, Domain 2 (Person-Centered Care) | Cultural humility in care planning | NURS 310 Community Health, unit 3 | Case-study rubric, item 4 | Mapped |
| CCNE / AACN Essentials, Domain 8 (Informatics/Technology) | EHR documentation competency | Not currently a discrete unit — embedded informally across clinicals | Preceptor checklist, no rubric item | **Gap — documentation, not content** |
| State BON regulation, clinical hour minimum | 500 direct-care clinical hours pre-licensure | Clinical rotation schedule, all terms | Clinical hour logs, MOU-tracked | Mapped |

**Decision rule:** if the content is taught somewhere but has no assessment evidence tied to the standard's language, fix the mapping documentation and assessment tie, not the curriculum. Only add new coursework when the content genuinely isn't taught anywhere in the program.

## 3. Clinical placement / preceptor capacity planning sequence

1. **Inventory current-year contracted sites and preceptors by term**, not last year's list — MOUs lapse, hospital units close to students, preceptors change roles.
2. **Compute steady-state site need at the target cohort size** using the program's required student-to-preceptor ratio (state-board-set, commonly in the 1:6–1:10 range for acute-care pre-licensure clinicals; confirm the exact ratio against the current state regulation, it varies).
3. **Maintain backup capacity at roughly 1.2x steady-state need.** A program with exactly enough sites for the current cohort has no slack for a single lost affiliation; losing one mid-size site (typically 8–12 student slots) without backup capacity forces an admissions cut with only one semester's notice.
4. **Before admitting a cohort above the prior year's size, confirm signed capacity for that specific incoming cohort** — a verbal assurance from a clinical partner is not a contracted slot count.
5. **Log every site loss and its cause** (unit closure, preceptor turnover, competing program's placement) — a pattern across sites (e.g., three losses in one health system in one year) signals a relationship problem worth escalating above the program level, not a series of unrelated events.

## 4. Simulation substitution ceiling worksheet

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Total required clinical hours (program/state minimum) | 500 |
| Current simulation hours | 225 |
| Simulation as % of total | 225/500 = 45% |
| NCSBN-study substitution ceiling (stated heuristic) | ≤50% |
| Headroom before ceiling | 500 × 0.50 − 225 = 25 hours |
| Simulation standard compliance check | Confirm against INACSL Standards of Best Practice — fidelity level, debrief structure, facilitator training documented |

**Rule:** a shortfall in clinical placement hours can be covered by simulation only up to the remaining headroom row, and only if the simulation itself is logged against a recognized standard — unstructured "skills lab" time doesn't count as the studied substitution and doesn't close the gap on paper even if it fills a schedule.

## 5. Clinician-educator effort reconciliation table

For an annual review where actual time and the stated effort allocation may have drifted apart.

| Activity | Stated effort allocation (FTE) | Actual time logged (recent term, annualized) | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical practice (RVU-generating) | 60% | 55% | −5 pts |
| Precepting/teaching | 20% | 32% | **+12 pts** |
| Research/scholarship | 10% | 6% | −4 pts |
| Administrative/committee | 10% | 7% | −3 pts |

**Decision rule:** a persistent gap of roughly 10 points or more between stated and actual effort in either direction is worth a written renegotiation at the next review — not an informal adjustment — because the stated allocation, not the actual time, is what a promotion committee or RVU target compares against.
