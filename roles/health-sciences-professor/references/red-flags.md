# Red Flags

Smell tests for pass-rate, clinical-placement, and accreditation signals in a health-professions program. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the data to pull.

### First-time licensure/board pass rate drops more than 10 points in a single cohort

- **Usually means:** either a genuine content-area gap traceable to a curriculum change one to two years earlier, or a cohort-composition anomaly (unusually large transfer-student subset, a delayed testing window that let weaker students test alongside a later group).
- **First question:** "What does the item-level content-domain breakdown show, and which curriculum change touched that domain two years ago?"
- **Data to pull:** the cohort's Candidate Performance Report (or equivalent board-exam diagnostic) broken out by content domain, plus the curriculum map for the terms that cohort actually took.

### Rolling multi-year pass-rate aggregate is within 3–5 points of the state board's minimum floor

- **Usually means:** either a single bad cohort about to roll out of the window and self-correct, or an early trend that will cross the floor next cycle if the next cohort performs similarly.
- **First question:** "If the next cohort matches this year's rate, does the aggregate cross the floor — and can a curriculum fix still reach the cohort that hasn't finished its sequence yet?"
- **Data to pull:** the rolling-aggregate calculation projected forward one cohort at the current rate, cross-referenced against which cohort is still early enough in sequence to be affected by a curriculum change.

### Simulation hours approach or exceed 50% of total clinical hours

- **Usually means:** either a defensible, evidence-backed substitution level still within the studied ceiling, or a placement-capacity shortfall being quietly papered over with simulation past the point the substitution evidence supports.
- **First question:** "Is this simulation logged against a recognized standard (INACSL) with documented fidelity and debrief structure, or is it unstructured skills-lab time relabeled?"
- **Data to pull:** the simulation hours log with standard-compliance documentation, compared against the accreditor's or state board's stated ceiling (which may be lower than 50%).

### A clinical-affiliation site cancels or reduces capacity with no backup site identified

- **Usually means:** either a one-off site-level issue (unit closure, staffing crisis at the host institution) or an emerging pattern across a health system signaling a deteriorating institutional relationship.
- **First question:** "Is this the first site lost from this health system in the past two years, or the third?"
- **Data to pull:** the site-loss log across the past several years, grouped by parent institution, alongside the current backup-capacity ratio against steady-state need.

### An accreditor citation names content the curriculum committee believes is already taught

- **Usually means:** either a genuine coverage gap the committee has misjudged, or a documentation gap — the content exists somewhere in the program but isn't mapped to the standard's specific language with assessment evidence attached.
- **First question:** "Show me the exact course, unit, and assessment item that maps to this standard's language — not just 'we cover that in general.'"
- **Data to pull:** the curriculum-to-standard mapping matrix, specifically the assessment-evidence column for the cited standard.

### The same accreditation citation recurs across two consecutive self-study cycles

- **Usually means:** either the prior response fixed the documentation but not the underlying practice, or the fix was implemented but never re-verified before the next cycle's self-study was written.
- **First question:** "What changed in practice after the last citation, and where's the evidence it's still happening, not just that it happened once right after the last visit?"
- **Data to pull:** the prior citation response plan and the current-cycle evidence collected against the same standard, compared date-by-date.

### A clinician-educator's stated non-clinical FTE and logged teaching/precepting time diverge by 10+ points

- **Usually means:** either an outdated effort allocation that was never renegotiated after a service assignment grew, or informal absorption of extra teaching load without documentation.
- **First question:** "When was this effort split last formally renegotiated, and does the current annual review record reflect actual time or the original allocation?"
- **Data to pull:** the effort-reconciliation table (stated vs. logged time) and the date of the last written effort-allocation agreement.

### A predictive licensure exam (ATI/HESI/Kaplan) shows more than 20% of a cohort below the program's stated cut score close to graduation

- **Usually means:** either the cut score or remediation trigger isn't being enforced consistently, or a genuine cohort-wide content gap that mirrors what will show up on the actual board exam.
- **First question:** "How many students below the cut score have completed the mandated remediation plan, versus how many were cleared without it?"
- **Data to pull:** the predictive-exam score roster against the remediation-completion log, not just the aggregate pass/fail summary.

### Faculty vacancy rate roughly doubles, or adjunct/part-time share of clinical faculty crosses half the roster, within two years

- **Usually means:** either a broader market pattern (documented nursing/allied-health faculty shortage tied to noncompetitive academic salaries relative to clinical practice) or a program-specific retention problem.
- **First question:** "Are departing faculty leaving for other academic programs, or back into clinical practice — and what's the pay gap in each direction?"
- **Data to pull:** faculty exit-interview data and a salary comparison against both peer academic programs and local clinical-practice compensation.
