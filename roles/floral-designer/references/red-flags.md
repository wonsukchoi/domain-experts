# Floral Designer — Red Flags

### Client's requested stem list prices out above 15% over stated budget
- **Usually means:** the client's inspiration photos (often from a higher-budget shoot or a different season) don't match their actual spend target, or the guest count/table count grew after the budget was set.
- **First question:** "Which is fixed — the guest count, or the per-table look?"
- **Data to pull:** the original inspiration references against the priced-out stem list, and the current guest/table count.

### Requested variety has a wholesale confirmation window inside 10 days of the event
- **Usually means:** the variety is being sourced from a volatile market (imported, weather-dependent, or short domestic season) and the order hasn't been locked with a grower yet.
- **First question:** "Do we have a same-family backup already identified if this doesn't confirm?"
- **Data to pull:** the wholesaler's confirmed-availability date and the backup variety's own availability status.

### A single vendor/venue conflict puts floral delivery and another vendor's load-in in the same window
- **Usually means:** the venue coordinator built the day-of timeline without floral's setup time factored in, or floral's own estimate of setup time is optimistic.
- **First question:** "How much floor space and time does setup actually need, measured against last similar event, not estimated?"
- **Data to pull:** the venue's master timeline and the floral crew's setup-time log from a comparable past event.

### Perishable blooms (dahlia, ranunculus, garden rose, peony) built more than 24 hours ahead of the event
- **Usually means:** the build schedule was sequenced by convenience (staff availability) rather than by each flower's vase-life clock.
- **First question:** "What's this variety's realistic hold time at our cooler temperature, and does the build schedule respect it?"
- **Data to pull:** the processing log's hydration-start timestamp against the event start time.

### Ethylene-sensitive stems (carnation, delphinium, snapdragon) stored in the same cooler zone as ripening fruit or decaying botanicals
- **Usually means:** cooler space is tight and zoning discipline slipped, or a delivery came in and got shelved without a zone check.
- **First question:** "What's sharing cooler space with the ethylene-sensitive stems right now?"
- **Data to pull:** the cooler zone log or a walk-through inventory by shelf.

### A tropical stem (orchid, anthurium, ginger) has been stored below 50°F
- **Usually means:** it was shelved in the general cooler by habit instead of the warmer tropical zone, risking visible chilling-injury spotting within 24-48 hours.
- **First question:** "Was this batch zoned to the tropical shelf, or the general cooler?"
- **Data to pull:** the storage-temperature log for that batch's zone assignment.

### Client requests a design "just like this photo" with no named varieties
- **Usually means:** the photo is stylistically aspirational but botanically unspecific — the actual varieties in the photo may be out of season, region-unavailable, or simply unidentified by the client.
- **First question:** "Do we know what's actually in that photo, or are we matching color/form only?"
- **Data to pull:** a variety-identification pass on the reference photo before pricing against it.

### Final headcount or table count changes inside 2 weeks of the event
- **Usually means:** the floral order (centerpiece count, boutonniere/corsage count) is now mismatched to the actual event, and the wholesale order may already be placed at the old count.
- **First question:** "Has the wholesale order shipped yet, and can the count still change?"
- **Data to pull:** the wholesale order's confirmation/ship status.

### A same-family substitution is proposed across bloom form, not just color
- **Usually means:** the true match ran out at the wholesaler and a form-mismatched stand-in (e.g., a spray rose substituted for a garden rose) is being proposed as equivalent.
- **First question:** "Does this substitution match bloom form and scale, or only color?"
- **Data to pull:** a side-by-side comparison of bloom diameter and petal structure between original and substitute.

### Proposal was priced from flower cost alone, with labor and delivery/strike folded into "markup"
- **Usually means:** the pricing worksheet skipped separate labor/delivery line items, risking an undercharge once actual hours are logged.
- **First question:** "What are the design-labor hours and delivery/strike costs, priced as their own lines?"
- **Data to pull:** the costing worksheet's line-item breakdown, not just the bottom-line total.
