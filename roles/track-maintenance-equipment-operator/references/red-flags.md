# Red Flags

### Crosslevel difference ≥1.5 in within a 62-ft interval at ≥6 in elevation
- **Usually means:** a superelevated curve where ballast settlement or a recent lift has outpaced the crosslevel transition; second most likely is a low joint or tie that's lost tamping hold.
- **First question:** where exactly along the 62-ft window does the difference concentrate — one point (localized fix) or a gradual drift across the whole span (broader resurfacing job)?
- **Data to pull:** the geometry-car exception report's station log and the section's last two surfacing work orders.

### Mud or fines visible at tie ends after rain or a train pass
- **Usually means:** fouled ballast losing drainage; second most likely is a cracked or degraded subgrade pumping fines upward under load.
- **First question:** is this the first time this location has shown pumping, or is it on the fouling-history list already?
- **Data to pull:** prior tamping work orders for the location — repeated tamps with no undercutting scheduled is the tell.

### Anchor pattern visually "complete" but wrong for the territory
- **Usually means:** the every-other-tie tangent pattern applied by habit on a grade or high-tonnage segment that requires every-tie boxing.
- **First question:** what's this segment's grade and traffic classification per the territory anchor map, not what pattern looks normal on a walk-by?
- **Data to pull:** territory anchor-pattern classification and a 100-tie sample anchor count.

### Curve elevation trending toward the class cap after successive lifts
- **Usually means:** each lift corrected the immediate profile problem without checking cumulative elevation against the 7-in (Class 3–5) or 8-in (Class 1–2) cap.
- **First question:** what was the elevation before this lift, and what will it be after — not just whether this lift alone stays under the cap.
- **Data to pull:** elevation history for the curve across the last 3 surfacing passes.

### Tamper onboard computer reports a large correction with no known geometry-car exception behind it
- **Usually means:** the programmed reference (string line or prior survey) is stale or was set against already-shifted rail; second most likely is a data-entry error in the design string.
- **First question:** when was this reference line last field-verified against a physical chord/versine reading?
- **Data to pull:** the design string's creation/last-verified date and the most recent geometry-car pass for the section.

### Repeated callback to the same station within days to weeks of a surfacing pass
- **Usually means:** consolidation was skipped (no stabilizer pass, no temporary speed restriction to let train loading do it) or the tamp was rushed to close a work window.
- **First question:** did the section get a stabilizer pass or an equivalent loading period before being released at full speed?
- **Data to pull:** the work order's step log — specifically whether step 8 (stabilize) was completed or skipped.

### Ballast shoulder under ~6 in on tangent, or under ~12 in on a curve's high shoulder
- **Usually means:** ballast delivery fell short of the job, or a regulator pass was skipped to save time in a closing work window.
- **First question:** was the shoulder measured before or after the ballast regulator's last pass?
- **Data to pull:** ballast tonnage delivered vs. planned tonnage for the job.

### New tie shows rail seat abrasion or plate cutting within one season of installation
- **Usually means:** inadequate tie plate/fastener hold or a tamping pass that didn't achieve proper crib compaction under that tie.
- **First question:** was this tie part of a spot repair (isolated tie swap) or a full surfacing pass — spot repairs are more likely to miss full crib compaction around the new tie.
- **Data to pull:** the tie-replacement work order and the surfacing pass record (if any) that followed it.

### Gauge widening at tie plate locations concentrated in curve territory
- **Usually means:** plate cutting or spike/fastener wear letting the rail roll outward under lateral load, most common on the high rail of a curve.
- **First question:** is the widening confined to a run of consecutive ties (fastener/plate problem) or scattered (individual tie condition)?
- **Data to pull:** hand-gauge readings at the flagged location plus 10 ties each direction, compared to the last geometry-car pass.

### Track buckle risk indicators in CWR at high rail temperature following recent disturbance
- **Usually means:** ballast hasn't consolidated enough after recent tamping/lifting to hold the rail's neutral-temperature restraint, most common shortly after undercutting or major surfacing.
- **First question:** what speed restriction, if any, is currently in effect for this section, and does it account for the rail temperature forecast?
- **Data to pull:** the CWR plan's speed-restriction schedule for post-disturbance track and the current/forecast rail temperature.
