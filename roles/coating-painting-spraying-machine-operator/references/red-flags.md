# Red flags

### Coating thickness judged by visual coverage/appearance rather than measured wet or dry film thickness
- **Usually means:** the actual DFT may be significantly under or over target despite looking fully covered.
- **First question:** what does the wet film gauge or DFT gauge actually read versus the calculated target?
- **Data to pull:** measured WFT/DFT compared against the target calculated from percent volume solids.

### Wet film thickness applied at a value numerically equal to the target DFT, without dividing by percent volume solids
- **Usually means:** the resulting dry film thickness will be substantially under target, by roughly the same proportion as the volume solids percentage.
- **First question:** was WFT calculated as DFT ÷ %solids, or applied as if WFT and DFT were the same number?
- **Data to pull:** applied WFT compared against target DFT and the coating's percent volume solids.

### Flash-off time between coats shortened to increase throughput under production pressure
- **Usually means:** trapped solvent risking blistering, solvent popping, or intercoat adhesion failure that surfaces after cure, not at the time of the next coat.
- **First question:** what was the actual flash time observed versus the specified minimum for this coating and conditions?
- **Data to pull:** actual flash time observed compared against the specified minimum flash time for this coating/environmental conditions.

### Cure verified by oven setpoint and elapsed time rather than actual part temperature
- **Usually means:** the part may be under-cured despite the equipment showing correct settings.
- **First question:** does the thermocouple/data logger record show the actual part reached and held cure temperature?
- **Data to pull:** thermocouple/data logger record of actual part temperature compared against the chamber setpoint and specified cure profile.

### Coating applied over a surface without verifying preparation actually met specification
- **Usually means:** an adhesion failure that traces back to inadequate surface prep, not the coating application itself, regardless of how correctly the coating was applied.
- **First question:** what does the surface prep verification test (profile depth, cleanliness) actually show for this specific part?
- **Data to pull:** surface prep verification test result compared against specification, for this specific part.

### Spray application parameters selected/tuned without considering transfer efficiency's cost or compliance impact
- **Usually means:** unnecessarily high material waste and emissions per part coated, an avoidable cost and compliance issue.
- **First question:** what transfer efficiency is actually being achieved versus what's achievable for the equipment in use?
- **Data to pull:** transfer efficiency achieved for the current application method/parameters compared against the achievable rate for the equipment in use.

### A recurring coating defect pattern appears across multiple parts without being traced to a specific process cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — surface prep, flash time, WFT/DFT calculation, cure profile — rather than isolated one-off part variation.
- **First question:** does the defect occurrence log correlate with a specific process parameter trend?
- **Data to pull:** defect occurrence log compared against process parameter logs (WFT/DFT, flash time, cure profile, surface prep) over the same period.
