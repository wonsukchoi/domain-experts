### Traffic impact scoped against existing conditions, not by-right baseline
- **Usually means:** consultant or applicant overstated the impact (comparing to a vacant/underbuilt lot) to either inflate mitigation asks or, less often, understated it by comparing to a larger existing use than what's actually entitled.
- **First question:** what does the current zoning entitle the parcel to build today, without any discretionary approval?
- **Data to pull:** current zoning district and its by-right density/use table; the traffic study's stated baseline assumption.

### Fiscal-impact analysis presents revenue only, no service-cost line
- **Usually means:** the analysis was prepared or summarized by someone advocating for approval and stopped at the number that supports the conclusion.
- **First question:** what is the jurisdiction's per-capita or per-unit marginal service-cost estimate, and has it been applied here?
- **Data to pull:** the finance or budget office's most recent per-capita service-cost figure.

### Density-bonus public benefit described in the application narrative but no recorded instrument attached
- **Usually means:** the benefit is a stated intent, not an enforceable commitment — it can evaporate after approval with no recourse.
- **First question:** is there a recorded covenant, deed restriction, or development agreement securing this benefit?
- **Data to pull:** title report or recorded-document index for the parcel.

### v/c ratio increase cited as basis for mitigation without stating the adopted LOS threshold
- **Usually means:** the traffic memo is arguing from direction of change rather than the actual decision rule, which inflates the apparent severity.
- **First question:** what is the jurisdiction's adopted LOS threshold for this facility type, and does the projected v/c actually cross it?
- **Data to pull:** the transportation element's adopted LOS standards table.

### Variance request lacks a finding on whether the hardship is self-created
- **Usually means:** the applicant (or a prior owner) took an action — a lot split, a setback encroachment — that created the condition now being cited as hardship.
- **First question:** was this parcel configuration or condition created by an application, plat, or permit action within the last several years?
- **Data to pull:** the parcel's plat history and any prior variance or permit records.

### Comprehensive-plan consistency finding is a single sentence with no policy citation
- **Usually means:** the consistency check wasn't actually run against the plan text — it's a conclusion looking for support after the fact.
- **First question:** which specific land-use map designation and policy number does this parcel fall under?
- **Data to pull:** the adopted comprehensive plan's land-use map and the cited policy's exact text.

### Public comment volume is high and the staff recommendation changed between draft and final report with no new technical finding
- **Usually means:** the recommendation shifted for political rather than evidentiary reasons, which weakens the record on appeal.
- **First question:** what specific new fact, not just new opinion, entered the record between drafts?
- **Data to pull:** the hearing record and any supplemental technical memo dated between the draft and final report.

### Affordable-unit assessed value used the market-rate comparable in a fiscal-impact model
- **Usually means:** the model wasn't updated after the density-bonus negotiation, overstating projected tax revenue.
- **First question:** does the fiscal model use the actual restricted rent/value for the income-restricted units, or the market-rate comp for all units?
- **Data to pull:** the fiscal model's per-unit assessed-value assumptions, cross-checked against the affordability covenant's restriction level.

### Stormwater or infrastructure capacity sign-off is missing from the staff report despite a density increase
- **Usually means:** the application moved through land-use review faster than the engineering review, and staff is recommending approval ahead of a required technical clearance.
- **First question:** has public works/engineering issued written capacity confirmation for water, sewer, and stormwater at the proposed density?
- **Data to pull:** the engineering division's capacity-review memo or its absence from the file.
