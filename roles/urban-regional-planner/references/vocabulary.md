### By-right
The development a zoning district permits without discretionary approval.
**In use:** "The applicant doesn't need us — this density is by-right under the current R-3 zoning."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a synonym for "whatever the applicant is currently proposing," when it specifically means the maximum entitlement under existing zoning with no approval required.

### Comprehensive plan
The jurisdiction's long-range, adopted policy document guiding land use, typically with a future land-use map.
**In use:** "The rezoning conflicts with the Plan's Low-Density Residential designation on this parcel."
**Common misuse:** Confused with the zoning ordinance — the plan is policy guidance and a map designation; the ordinance is the enforceable regulatory text. They can diverge.

### Spot zoning
An unlawful rezoning of a single parcel in a way that is inconsistent with the surrounding area and the comprehensive plan, benefiting one owner without a public-interest justification.
**In use:** "Approving this without a plan amendment first risks a spot-zoning challenge."
**Common misuse:** Used loosely to describe any unpopular small rezoning; the legal test hinges specifically on plan inconsistency and lack of public benefit, not on parcel size or neighbor objection alone.

### Variance
Relief from a specific numeric zoning standard (setback, height, lot coverage) granted on a hardship finding unique to the parcel.
**In use:** "This isn't a variance situation — the constraint applies to every lot on the block, not just this one."
**Common misuse:** Requested for general disagreement with a zoning standard rather than a parcel-specific physical hardship; conflated with a conditional use permit, which is a different approval type for a different problem.

### Conditional use permit (CUP) / special exception
Discretionary approval for a use the zoning ordinance allows in a district only subject to specific findings and conditions.
**In use:** "A daycare is conditionally permitted in this district — we're reviewing it as a CUP, not a rezoning."
**Common misuse:** Treated as interchangeable with a variance; a CUP addresses use, a variance addresses a numeric standard.

### Level of service (LOS)
A graded (A–F) measure of traffic flow quality at an intersection or roadway segment, tied to a volume-to-capacity ratio.
**In use:** "The intersection stays at LOS C under the projected v/c — no mitigation is triggered."
**Common misuse:** Assumed that any LOS grade below A is a problem; each jurisdiction adopts its own acceptable LOS threshold (often C or D), and only crossing that adopted threshold triggers a mitigation obligation.

### Trip generation
The estimated number of vehicle trips a land use produces, typically per unit or per 1,000 square feet, from a standard reference (ITE Trip Generation Manual).
**In use:** "Net new trip generation is the proposed rate minus the by-right rate, not the proposed rate alone."
**Common misuse:** Cited as the gross proposed-use trip count rather than the net delta against the by-right baseline, overstating the impact.

### Density bonus
Additional development capacity (units, height, FAR) granted above the base zoning in exchange for a public benefit, commonly affordable housing.
**In use:** "The bonus is contingent on the affordability covenant being recorded before the final plat, not just proposed."
**Common misuse:** Credited toward approval based on a narrative commitment rather than a recorded, enforceable instrument.

### Inclusionary zoning
An ordinance requiring or incentivizing a percentage of units in new residential development to be income-restricted.
**In use:** "The 15% set-aside satisfies the inclusionary ordinance's minimum for this density tier."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to apply uniformly across jurisdictions at a fixed percentage; the required set-aside percentage and income tier vary by local ordinance.

### Nonconforming use
A use or structure that was lawful when established but no longer complies with current zoning, and is typically allowed to continue under a legal-nonconforming status.
**In use:** "Expanding the nonconforming use resets its protected status — that's the line we can't let them cross without a variance."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to be permanently protected regardless of changes; most ordinances terminate nonconforming status on abandonment (a defined vacancy period) or substantial expansion.

### Form-based code
A zoning approach regulating building form, scale, and street relationship rather than only use and density.
**In use:** "This corridor is under the form-based code — the review focuses on building frontage and massing, not a use table."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to have no use regulation at all; most form-based codes still regulate use, just as a secondary rather than primary control.

### Impact fee
A one-time charge on new development to fund the marginal infrastructure capacity (roads, parks, schools) the development requires.
**In use:** "The impact fee schedule assesses this project at $4,200 per unit for parks capacity."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with a general tax; impact fees are legally required to have a documented nexus and rough proportionality to the specific impact being charged for.

### Fiscal impact analysis
A projection of a development's net effect on the jurisdiction's revenue versus its marginal service cost.
**In use:** "The fiscal impact analysis nets to a $121,760 annual deficit once service cost is included, not a gain."
**Common misuse:** Presented as revenue-only, omitting the service-cost side, which produces a systematically optimistic picture of every development regardless of type.

### Plat
The recorded map dividing land into lots, blocks, streets, and easements, which becomes the legal description once approved and recorded.
**In use:** "The affordability covenant has to be recorded before final plat, or the density bonus isn't secured."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably with "site plan"; a plat is a legal subdivision document, a site plan is a design/development review document — they serve different approval processes.
