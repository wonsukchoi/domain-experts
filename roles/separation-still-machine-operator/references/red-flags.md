# Red flags

### Reflux ratio set at a high/conservative default rather than calculated as the minimum needed to achieve target purity
- **Usually means:** unnecessary energy cost and reduced product throughput without a corresponding purity benefit.
- **First question:** what does the actual minimum-required calculation call for versus what's currently set?
- **Data to pull:** current reflux ratio compared against the calculated minimum required reflux ratio for target purity.

### Column separation calculations performed using nameplate physical tray count rather than tray-efficiency-adjusted theoretical stages
- **Usually means:** separation capability is being overestimated, risking off-spec product at a reflux ratio that looks adequate on paper.
- **First question:** what do the effective theoretical stages actually come out to once tray efficiency is applied?
- **Data to pull:** physical tray count compared against documented tray efficiency and the effective theoretical stages actually used in calculations.

### Feed tray location not reassessed after a feed composition change
- **Usually means:** the column may be running at reduced separation efficiency, requiring more reflux than necessary or failing to hit the target purity.
- **First question:** does the current feed composition still match the column's internal composition profile at the current feed tray?
- **Data to pull:** current feed composition compared against the column's internal composition profile and the feed tray location in use.

### Filter/separator throughput increased without verifying residence time still meets the minimum required for the actual particle size
- **Usually means:** separation efficiency may be silently reduced even though overall flow rate targets are being met.
- **First question:** what does actual residence time show at the new throughput versus the minimum required for this particle size distribution?
- **Data to pull:** current residence time compared against the minimum required for this particle size distribution, at the new throughput rate.

### Product purity/composition not verified by actual sampling despite nominally correct process parameters
- **Usually means:** an unaccounted disturbance — feed change, fouling, column flooding/weeping — could be producing off-spec product undetected.
- **First question:** what does an actual product sample analysis show against specification?
- **Data to pull:** actual product sample analysis results compared against specification, for the current run.

### A recurring off-spec product or reduced-efficiency pattern appears without being traced to a specific cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — tray efficiency/fouling change, feed tray mismatch, reflux ratio, residence time — rather than isolated one-off run variation.
- **First question:** does the off-spec/efficiency trend correlate with a specific process parameter?
- **Data to pull:** off-spec/efficiency trend log compared against process parameter logs over the same period.

### Process parameters reused from a prior run without re-verifying they're adequate for a changed feed composition or product spec
- **Usually means:** the prior run's validated parameters may not apply to the current conditions.
- **First question:** do the current feed composition and product spec match what the reused parameters were originally validated for?
- **Data to pull:** current feed composition/spec compared against the conditions the reused parameters were originally validated for.
