# Vocabulary

### Reflux ratio
The ratio of liquid returned to a distillation column (reflux) versus liquid withdrawn as distillate product, a key variable trading product purity against throughput and energy cost.
**In use:** "Reflux ratio's calculated at the minimum needed to hit 98% purity — running it higher just burns more energy for no gain."
**Common misuse:** Defaulting to a high, conservative reflux ratio "for safety" rather than calculating the actual minimum needed to achieve the target purity specification.

### Theoretical stage (distillation)
An idealized separation unit representing complete vapor-liquid equilibrium, used as the basis for separation calculations, distinct from a physical tray which achieves less than ideal separation.
**In use:** "This column needs 14 theoretical stages to hit spec — that's not the same as saying it needs 14 physical trays."
**Common misuse:** Treating physical tray count as equivalent to theoretical stage count, ignoring that actual separation capability is reduced by tray efficiency.

### Tray (Murphree) efficiency
The percentage of the theoretical separation a physical tray actually achieves compared to an ideal equilibrium stage, typically well under 100%.
**In use:** "At 70% tray efficiency, this 20-tray column only delivers 14 effective theoretical stages."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a column's physical tray count directly determines its separation capability without applying the actual documented tray efficiency for that specific separation.

### Feed tray location
The specific tray in a distillation column where the feed stream is introduced, chosen to match the feed's composition to the column's internal composition profile at that point.
**In use:** "Feed composition shifted — check whether this is still the right feed tray or if the profile's moved."
**Common misuse:** Treating feed tray location as fixed and correct by default, without reassessing it when feed composition changes, which can waste separation capacity or require unnecessary additional reflux.

### McCabe-Thiele method (or equivalent)
A graphical or calculation-based method for determining the theoretical stages and reflux ratio required for a target distillation separation.
**In use:** "Ran the McCabe-Thiele calc with the corrected 14 effective stages — that's what the new reflux target's based on."
**Common misuse:** Skipping a proper stage/reflux calculation in favor of an assumed or habitually reused reflux ratio, without verifying it's actually adequate for the current separation's effective stages and target purity.

### Residence time (separation)
The time a material spends within a separator or filter, which must meet a minimum threshold relative to particle size/settling characteristics to achieve adequate separation efficiency.
**In use:** "Pushed throughput up 20%, but that drops residence time below what this particle size actually needs."
**Common misuse:** Increasing throughput to meet a production target without verifying residence time still meets the minimum required for the specific particle size distribution being processed.

### Flooding / weeping (distillation column)
Operational upset conditions in a column where excessive vapor flow (flooding) or insufficient vapor flow (weeping) disrupts normal tray operation and separation efficiency.
**In use:** "Check for weeping before assuming the reflux calculation is wrong — a tray upset can mimic an efficiency problem."
**Common misuse:** Assuming column upsets only manifest as obvious visible problems, rather than as a gradual separation efficiency decline that requires product analysis to detect.

### Composition profile (column)
The varying concentration of components at different points along a distillation column's height, which the feed tray location should match.
**In use:** "Composition profile's shifted since the reflux ratio changed — feed tray might not be optimal anymore."
**Common misuse:** Assuming the composition profile is static and doesn't need reassessment, when it shifts with changes in feed composition, reflux ratio, or other operating conditions.

### Separation efficiency (filtration/separation)
The percentage of the target phase or particulate actually removed by a filter or separator, governed by residence time and driving force relative to particle characteristics.
**In use:** "Throughput's up, but check actual separation efficiency — flow rate alone doesn't confirm the particles are still being removed."
**Common misuse:** Judging separation adequacy by overall throughput or flow rate targets alone, without calculating or verifying actual separation efficiency for the specific material being processed.

### Product analysis (composition verification)
Direct sampling and laboratory or online analysis of a separation process's product to verify actual composition against specification, distinct from inferring compliance from process parameters.
**In use:** "Parameters all look nominal, but pull a sample anyway — that's the only thing that actually confirms purity."
**Common misuse:** Relying on process parameters — reflux ratio, temperature, throughput — appearing nominally correct as sufficient evidence of product spec compliance, without actual compositional verification.
