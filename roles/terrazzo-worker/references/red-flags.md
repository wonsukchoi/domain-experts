# Red flags

### RH probe test (ASTM F2170) reads above 80% before an epoxy pour
- **Usually means:** slab is under-dried for the resin system specified — either no vapor retarder was installed below the slab, or too little time has elapsed since placement.
- **First question:** was a polyethylene vapor retarder installed directly under the slab, and how many days since placement?
- **Data to pull:** RH test report (probe count, depth, equilibration time), slab placement date, vapor-retarder spec sheet.

### Blistering or bubbling in an epoxy terrazzo topping within days to weeks of cure
- **Usually means:** trapped moisture vapor pushing the resin off the substrate, or an off-ratio resin mix that trapped air/solvent during cure.
- **First question:** what were the pre-install RH/MVER test results, and was a moisture-mitigation membrane specified?
- **Data to pull:** moisture test log, resin batch mix-ratio records, ambient temperature/humidity log for the pour day.

### Random cracking outside the divider-strip lines on cementitious terrazzo
- **Usually means:** panel spacing exceeded the 4 ft × 4 ft / ≤25 sf shrinkage-control rule, or strips weren't tied to the substrate's actual control-joint layout.
- **First question:** what was the as-built strip spacing versus the substrate joint map, panel by panel?
- **Data to pull:** strip layout drawing, substrate control-joint map, photos of the crack relative to the nearest strip.

### Chip pop-out or plucking during grinding
- **Usually means:** grinding started before the minimum cure window for the binder (cementitious under 3–4 days, epoxy before the resin's stated grind-ready time), or the chip grade was too large for the topping thickness.
- **First question:** how many days or hours elapsed between pour and first grind pass, and what grit did that first pass use?
- **Data to pull:** pour log with date/time stamps, grind log with grit sequence, chip-grade and topping-thickness spec.

### A visible scratch pattern or haze under raking light after "final polish"
- **Usually means:** a grit step in the progression was skipped, leaving the previous tier's scratch pattern intact under a thin polish layer.
- **First question:** what was the complete grit sequence run, in order, with no steps described as "combined"?
- **Data to pull:** grind log, a raking-light photo or in-person check of the affected section.

### Divider-strip corrosion or dark staining bleeding into the surrounding matrix
- **Usually means:** the wrong strip alloy was used for the exposure (e.g., uncoated ferrous strip in a wet or high-chloride area).
- **First question:** what alloy was specified versus what alloy is actually in the floor, and what is the area's exposure (wet, exterior, deicing chemicals)?
- **Data to pull:** strip spec/submittal sheet, area-use description, photos of the affected strip.

### Lippage greater than roughly 1/32 in across a divider strip or panel joint
- **Usually means:** uneven screeding/troweling at the strip during placement, or substrate settlement after the fact.
- **First question:** when was substrate flatness last verified, and what were the FF/FL numbers?
- **Data to pull:** substrate flatness survey, strip-setting photos from installation, current lippage measurement by location.

### Efflorescence or white haze on cementitious terrazzo shortly after grouting or sealing
- **Usually means:** soluble salts wicking to the surface because sealer or densifier was applied over residual moisture, or the section cured too fast in hot/dry conditions.
- **First question:** was the substrate/topping moisture-tested before sealer application, and within what window after grinding was it applied?
- **Data to pull:** cure log, sealer/densifier product data sheet (application-moisture requirements), moisture readings if taken.

### Hollow sound when tapped on a bonded terrazzo installation
- **Usually means:** the bonding coat failed or was skipped, or the substrate had contamination (curing-compound residue, laitance, dust) not removed before the bond coat went down.
- **First question:** was the substrate mechanically profiled (shot-blast or scarified) and tested clean before the bonding coat was applied?
- **Data to pull:** substrate prep log, concrete surface profile (CSP) rating, bonding-agent product data sheet.

### Visible color variation or blotching across a single continuous pour
- **Usually means:** cement from mixed batches/lots, or chip segregation during seeding rather than a pre-blended, homogeneous mix.
- **First question:** was all cement for this pour from a single lot, and were chips pre-blended before seeding or seeded straight from separate bags?
- **Data to pull:** material delivery tickets (lot numbers), batch mix log, seeding sequence notes.

### New terrazzo doesn't match an adjacent existing or historic floor in gloss or pattern
- **Usually means:** chip size, binder color, or final grit/polish level wasn't matched to the original before the full pour was committed.
- **First question:** was a ground-and-polished sample panel approved on-site against the existing floor before ordering the full chip lot?
- **Data to pull:** original floor spec if available, sample-panel approval record, side-by-side photos in the same lighting.

### Sealer or finish wearing unevenly in traffic lanes within a few months of turnover
- **Usually means:** sealer coverage rate was under-applied, or the wrong sealer type (topical vs. penetrating) was chosen for the actual traffic classification.
- **First question:** what sealer product and coverage rate was used, and what was the projected traffic classification for that area?
- **Data to pull:** sealer product data sheet and applied coverage log, foot-traffic/rolling-load estimate for the space.
