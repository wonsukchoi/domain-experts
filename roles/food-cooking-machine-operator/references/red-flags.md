# Red flags

### Lethality confirmed by surface temperature spot-check rather than coldest-point data logging
- **Usually means:** the product's actual coldest point may not have reached and held the required temperature, even with compliant surface readings.
- **First question:** was a probe placed at the actual coldest/slowest-heating point (center of the thickest piece), or only surface spots checked?
- **Data to pull:** the cook cycle's temperature data log if available, probe placement location.

### Frying oil condition assessed by elapsed time in service or visual appearance alone
- **Usually means:** actual degradation (free fatty acid level, polar compounds) hasn't been measured, and degradation rate varies with usage pattern.
- **First question:** has oil condition been tested against a specific degradation indicator, or judged by time/appearance?
- **Data to pull:** oil test results if performed, hours/batches since last oil change or test.

### Cook parameters held identical across batches with visibly different incoming ingredient characteristics
- **Usually means:** a process validated for one batch's characteristics may produce an over- or under-cooked result on a batch with different moisture/viscosity/size.
- **First question:** were incoming batch characteristics checked, and were parameters adjusted if they differed from the validated baseline?
- **Data to pull:** incoming batch characteristic data, cook parameters used.

### A batch released based on sensory quality (taste, appearance) without separate lethality verification
- **Usually means:** sensory acceptability doesn't confirm food-safety lethality was achieved — these are different measurements.
- **First question:** does a documented lethality verification (coldest-point time-temperature data) exist for this batch, separate from sensory assessment?
- **Data to pull:** lethality verification record, sensory quality assessment record.

### A temperature sensor not recently verified against a calibration schedule
- **Usually means:** the sensor could be displaying an inaccurate reading while appearing to show a compliant temperature.
- **First question:** when was this sensor last calibrated/verified against a known reference?
- **Data to pull:** sensor calibration log.

### A cook cycle's time-at-temperature calculated from cycle start rather than from when the coldest point actually reached target temperature
- **Usually means:** the hold-time requirement may not have been correctly measured, since the coldest point reaches target temperature later than cycle start.
- **First question:** is hold time measured from when the coldest point actually reached the target temperature, or from overall cycle elapsed time?
- **Data to pull:** the temperature-time log showing when the coldest point reached target vs. total cycle time.

### A product's thickest/largest piece not specifically identified for coldest-point probe placement
- **Usually means:** the probe may not be placed at the actual worst-case location, understating the true risk.
- **First question:** was the batch's largest/thickest piece specifically identified and probed, or was a typical/average piece used?
- **Data to pull:** probe placement rationale, batch piece size variation.

### An oil quality test result trending toward a degradation threshold without a scheduled oil change before reaching it
- **Usually means:** oil condition is approaching an unacceptable state, and reactive (rather than proactive) management risks producing product with degraded oil.
- **First question:** does the trend project reaching the degradation threshold before the next scheduled oil change/test?
- **Data to pull:** oil quality trend data, current oil change schedule.

### A recurring cook quality issue (inconsistent texture, under/overcooking) correlating with a specific ingredient supplier or lot
- **Usually means:** batch-to-batch ingredient variation may not be adequately accounted for in the cook process.
- **First question:** does the affected batch/lot show different characteristics (moisture, particle size) than the process was calibrated for?
- **Data to pull:** ingredient lot characteristics, cook process parameters used for that lot.

### A food safety lethality requirement not clearly documented or posted at the cooking station
- **Usually means:** operators may be relying on memory or assumption rather than the actual specified requirement.
- **First question:** is the specific time-temperature lethality requirement for this product clearly available at the point of use?
- **Data to pull:** station documentation, the product's actual specified requirement.
