# Red flags

### A visible symptom (loose sole, squeak) repaired without checking for an underlying structural cause
- **Usually means:** the repair addresses the symptom but not the root cause, risking failure again shortly after.
- **First question:** what is the item's actual construction method, and does that construction's typical failure mode point to something beyond the visible symptom?
- **Data to pull:** construction type identification, inspection of the relevant structural element (welt stitching, sole bonding foundation).

### A repair method applied without first identifying the item's actual construction type
- **Usually means:** the chosen method may be appropriate for a different construction than what this specific item actually has.
- **First question:** has the item been confirmed as cemented, welted, or stitched construction, or was a method chosen by default/habit?
- **Data to pull:** visual/physical construction identification (welt presence, stitch pattern, sole attachment method).

### Contact cement or adhesive applied to a welted shoe's sole separation without inspecting welt stitching
- **Usually means:** the actual failure may be in the stitching, and cement is masking rather than fixing the structural issue.
- **First question:** is the welt stitching in the affected area intact, or has it failed/rotted?
- **Data to pull:** direct inspection of welt stitching condition in the affected area.

### Leather stretched repeatedly or aggressively to fully resolve a fit complaint
- **Usually means:** risk of exceeding the leather's actual physical stretch limit, causing thinning or tearing.
- **First question:** does the amount of stretch being attempted fall within this leather type's known safe limit, or is it being pushed further to satisfy the complaint fully?
- **Data to pull:** leather type/thickness, amount of stretch already applied, visible signs of stress (thinning, surface texture change).

### An item accepted for repair without distinguishing cosmetic damage from structural damage
- **Usually means:** a repairability assessment may be based on surface appearance alone, missing a structural issue that affects the repair's viability or safety.
- **First question:** has the item been checked for structural integrity (sole/welt/stitching foundation), separate from surface/cosmetic condition?
- **Data to pull:** structural inspection findings, cosmetic damage assessment.

### A repair material selected for visual match without checking functional compatibility with the original construction
- **Usually means:** the material may look right but bond or wear differently than the surrounding original material.
- **First question:** is this specific adhesive/sole compound/thread type actually compatible with the item's original construction and materials, or was it chosen for appearance alone?
- **Data to pull:** original material/construction specification, chosen repair material's compatibility.

### A repaired item returning with the same complaint shortly after repair
- **Usually means:** the original repair may have addressed a symptom rather than the actual root cause.
- **First question:** was the underlying structural cause actually diagnosed and addressed in the original repair, or only the visible symptom?
- **Data to pull:** the original repair record, current inspection findings on the recurring issue.

### An item declared "not repairable" without specifying the actual structural reason
- **Usually means:** the assessment may not be clearly grounded in a specific, checkable structural finding.
- **First question:** what is the specific structural damage (location, extent) that makes this item uneconomical or unsafe to repair?
- **Data to pull:** the specific structural finding driving the non-repairable determination.

### A customer's stated complaint treated as a complete diagnosis without independent inspection
- **Usually means:** the customer's description is what they noticed, not necessarily the actual cause — treating it as sufficient diagnosis risks missing the real issue.
- **First question:** has the item been independently inspected to confirm or refine the customer's stated complaint, or is the repair based solely on their description?
- **Data to pull:** independent inspection findings vs. the customer's stated complaint.

### A repair proceeding without communicating findings to the customer when the actual issue differs from what they expected
- **Usually means:** the customer may be surprised by a different repair approach or cost than anticipated, without having been informed of the actual finding first.
- **First question:** was the customer informed of the actual diagnosis (e.g., failed welt stitching vs. their assumption of "loose sole") before the repair proceeded?
- **Data to pull:** customer communication record.
