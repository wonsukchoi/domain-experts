# Playbook

## Construction-type diagnostic table

| Feature to check | Cemented | Goodyear Welted | Blake Stitched |
|---|---|---|---|
| Visible stitching on sole edge | Usually none | Yes, via separate welt strip | Yes, direct line through sole |
| Sole attachment | Adhesive primarily | Welt stitching (structural) | Direct stitch through upper/insole/sole |
| Sole replacement approach | Full sole cementing, care re: upper damage risk | Remove/re-stitch at welt, minimal upper risk | Re-stitch through existing stitch line |
| Sole separation likely cause | Adhesive bond failure | Welt stitching failure (check first) | Stitch failure |

**Rule:** always identify construction type before selecting repair method — a method appropriate for one construction can be inappropriate or damaging for another.

## Repairability assessment worksheet

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Item | Men's Leather Dress Shoes, Goodyear Welted |
| Customer complaint | Sole loose at toe |
| Construction identified | Goodyear welted |
| Cosmetic damage found | Minor surface scuffing |
| Structural damage found | 2" welt stitching failed/rotted at toe |
| Root cause of complaint | Welt stitching failure (NOT primary adhesive issue) |
| Repair approach selected | Re-stitch 2" welt section, welt-appropriate thread/technique |
| Repair approach rejected | Contact cement (masks symptom, risks future repair difficulty) |
| Estimated remaining service life post-repair | Several years (proper fix) vs. weeks-months (cement-only) |

## Leather stretching limit guide (illustrative — always assess per specific leather type/thickness)

| Leather type/thickness | Typical safe stretch limit | Signs of exceeding limit |
|---|---|---|
| Full-grain, heavier weight | More forgiving, moderate stretch capacity | Visible thinning at stress points, surface texture change |
| Top-grain/finished, moderate weight | Moderate stretch capacity | Surface cracking, color change at stress points |
| Softer/thinner leather (suede, kid) | Limited stretch capacity | Tearing risk at relatively small stretch amounts |

**Rule:** always stop stretching before reaching visible signs of stress (thinning, texture change, cracking) — if the requested fit adjustment would require exceeding these signs, communicate the limit to the customer rather than pushing past it.
