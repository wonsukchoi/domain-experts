# Red flags — what a real estate broker checks before a file moves forward

Any one of these can be innocent. Two or more on the same file is a pattern worth surfacing to the client in writing.

### Listing sits at 1.5x+ the local median days-on-market for its price tier with zero price reduction proposed
- **Usually means:** the list price was set to the seller's wish rather than the CMA, or a competing new listing undercut it and nobody re-ran comps.
- **First question:** "When did we last pull fresh comps, and what's changed in the market since we listed?"
- **Data to pull:** current MLS active/pending/sold comps within the same radius, showing count and buyer feedback log from the last 30 days.

### Buyer waives the appraisal contingency without documented proof of funds covering the CMA-to-ask gap
- **Usually means:** the buyer's agent is coaching a generic competitive tactic without checking whether this specific buyer can absorb a shortfall — or the buyer is overconfident about the appraisal.
- **First question:** "If this appraises at the CMA midpoint instead of the contract price, where does the buyer get the difference?"
- **Data to pull:** buyer's bank statement or proof-of-funds letter, CMA range for the subject property.

### Escalation clause has an increment but no stated cap
- **Usually means:** the offer was drafted from a template without the cap field filled in, or the buyer's agent is intentionally leaving it open to see how high the seller pushes — both are a liability for the buyer.
- **First question:** "What's the absolute ceiling this buyer authorized in writing?"
- **Data to pull:** the buyer's pre-approval amount and the signed escalation addendum.

### Earnest money deposit under 1% of contract price in a competitive market
- **Usually means:** the buyer is testing the market with minimal commitment, or the buyer's agent used a boilerplate EMD figure from a slower market or a different price tier.
- **First question:** "Is this buyer prepared to be outcompeted on commitment signal alone?"
- **Data to pull:** local median EMD percentage for the price tier over the trailing quarter (available from MLS comp data or brokerage transaction logs).

### Pre-approval letter is dated more than 60 days old or says "pre-qualification" rather than "pre-approval"
- **Usually means:** the letter reflects a soft credit pull and stated income, not an underwritten review — the actual approval could come back lower or fall through entirely.
- **First question:** "Has underwriting actually reviewed this buyer's file, or is this a marketing letter from the lender?"
- **Data to pull:** updated pre-approval letter, lender contact for direct confirmation.

### Financing contingency deadline under 14 days on a non-cash offer with only a pre-qualification letter
- **Usually means:** the buyer's agent is manufacturing an aggressive-looking offer that manufactures fallout risk instead — lenders rarely clear full underwriting that fast without a fully-documented file already in process.
- **First question:** "Has this buyer's loan already been submitted to underwriting, or does the clock start at acceptance?"
- **Data to pull:** loan application/submission date, lender's stated processing timeline.

### CMA comps are all older than 6 months with no market-trend adjustment noted
- **Usually means:** the market moved (rate change, seasonal shift, new competing inventory) and the CMA is internally consistent but stale relative to current conditions.
- **First question:** "What's happened to days-on-market and sale-to-list ratio in the last 60 days versus the comp period?"
- **Data to pull:** trailing-60-day sold/pending data, average sale-to-list ratio trend.

### Seller net sheet not reconciling — commission, payoff, and prorations don't sum to the stated "net to seller" figure
- **Usually means:** a line item was estimated instead of pulled (mortgage payoff especially — interest accrual matters on the actual closing date), or a concession was verbally agreed and not entered.
- **First question:** "Which line is an estimate and which is confirmed with a document?"
- **Data to pull:** current mortgage payoff statement (not the last statement balance), signed concession terms if any.

### Dual or designated agency disclosure signed after an offer is already in negotiation, not before
- **Usually means:** the broker (or a colleague at the same brokerage) picked up both sides mid-transaction without pausing to re-paper the reduced-duty relationship, which is a compliance and E&O exposure regardless of intent.
- **First question:** "Did both parties consent to this in writing before any negotiating advice was given under the new arrangement?"
- **Data to pull:** signed agency disclosure form with the date, and the transaction log showing what advice (if any) was given before that date.

### Inspection reveals a material defect and the listing agent proposes "let's not put it in writing, just adjust the price"
- **Usually means:** someone is trying to avoid a disclosure obligation that follows the property regardless of how this deal resolves — the defect must be disclosed to any buyer, not negotiated around informally with this one.
- **First question:** "Is this defect required to be on the seller's disclosure statement regardless of what happens with this buyer?"
- **Data to pull:** state-required seller disclosure form, inspection report, any contractor estimate for the repair.

### Same buyer shown a narrower range of neighborhoods than their pre-approval and stated criteria would support
- **Usually means:** unconscious steering — the buyer's actual criteria (schools, commute, price) support a wider set of listings than what's being surfaced.
- **First question:** "Would this same search, run with these same filters, return this same result for any buyer regardless of who they are?"
- **Data to pull:** the saved search criteria and the full result set it should return, compared to what was actually shown.

### Title commitment shows an unresolved lien, judgment, or open permit right before contingency removal
- **Usually means:** the payoff/lien search wasn't run early enough in the transaction, leaving no runway to resolve it before the buyer's contingency deadlines expire.
- **First question:** "How long has this been sitting on the title report, and who clears it before closing?"
- **Data to pull:** title commitment, payoff/release documentation timeline from the lienholder.
