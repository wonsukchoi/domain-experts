# Archivist — Red Flags

### Backlog growing faster than processing throughput (intake linear ft/year > processing linear ft/year)
- **Usually means:** Item-level processing is being used as the default when series/box-level (MPLP) would clear the queue; second-likeliest is understaffing relative to accession volume.
- **First question:** What's the current average processing rate in hours per linear foot, and does it match the collection's actual use projection?
- **Data to pull:** Accession log (linear feet received per year) vs. processing log (linear feet completed per year) for the last 3 years.

### Collection offered with no deed of gift or legal transfer instrument
- **Usually means:** The donor hasn't formally relinquished ownership or copyright yet — accepting physical custody without the legal instrument creates ambiguous title.
- **First question:** Has the deed of gift been drafted and reviewed by counsel, and does it specify copyright disposition?
- **Data to pull:** Draft or signed deed of gift; institutional donation-acceptance policy.

### Item-level description requested for a collection with no demonstrated research demand
- **Usually means:** A donor or administrator is equating thoroughness with quality; second-likeliest is a prominent-name collection getting default VIP treatment regardless of actual use.
- **First question:** What's the projected or historical request volume for comparable collections from this era/creator?
- **Data to pull:** Reference request logs for similar collections; comparable collections' processing level and their subsequent use.

### Active mold, red rot, or vinegar syndrome detected in a box
- **Usually means:** Environmental control failure (humidity/temperature) in storage, or the material arrived already compromised.
- **First question:** Is the deterioration contained to this box, or has it spread to adjacent material on the shelf?
- **Data to pull:** Storage-area environmental logs (temperature/RH); condition-survey notes for adjacent boxes.

### Original order looks "disorganized" and someone proposes reorganizing by subject
- **Usually means:** A well-intentioned non-archivist (or a new hire without archival training) is about to destroy provenance evidence by imposing a library-style subject arrangement.
- **First question:** Is there a discernible order the creator actually used, even if it isn't obvious at first glance?
- **Data to pull:** The collection's existing folder/box structure and any accompanying correspondence describing how the creator used the records.

### Restricted material (HR, medical, legal, donor-privacy) found mixed into an otherwise open series
- **Usually means:** The collection wasn't appraised at the series level before arrangement began, or restricted material was misfiled by the creator before transfer.
- **First question:** How much restricted material is there, and can it be physically segregated without breaking the rest of the series' order?
- **Data to pull:** Institutional records-restriction policy; the specific folder(s) containing restricted content.

### Digitization requested with no preservation-master format or fixity plan specified
- **Usually means:** The request is being framed as "preservation" when it's actually an access project — a compressed derivative with no checksum will not survive as evidence of authenticity over decades.
- **First question:** What format is the preservation master, and is there a fixity-check (checksum) schedule after ingest?
- **Data to pull:** Digitization project specification; digital-preservation policy (if one exists).

### Two accessions with overlapping/duplicate-looking content proposed for merging
- **Usually means:** The same document exists in two different creators' files for different reasons (one sent it, one received it) — merging erases which copy came from which provenance.
- **First question:** Do the two accessions have different provenance (different creating individuals or offices)?
- **Data to pull:** Accession records for both collections, specifically the provenance/source field.

### Finding aid published with no restriction statement
- **Usually means:** Restricted material was identified during appraisal but the restriction wasn't carried through to the public-facing finding aid — a discoverability/access-control gap.
- **First question:** Does the finding aid's restriction note match what appraisal actually flagged?
- **Data to pull:** Appraisal notes vs. published finding-aid text, side by side.

### Deaccession under consideration with no documented rationale
- **Usually means:** Space pressure is driving a disposal decision without the same rigor the original appraisal required — deaccessioning is appraisal in reverse and needs the same evidentiary case.
- **First question:** What changed since the original appraisal that justifies removing this material now?
- **Data to pull:** Original accession/appraisal notes; institutional deaccession policy (many require donor notification or governing-board approval).
