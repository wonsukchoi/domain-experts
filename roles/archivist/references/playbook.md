# Archivist — Playbook

## Accession log entry (filled example)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Accession number | 2026.014 |
| Date received | 2026-03-02 |
| Source/donor | [Professor], Dept. of History (retiring faculty transfer) |
| Legal instrument | Deed of gift signed 2026-02-20; copyright assigned to university |
| Extent | 40 linear feet (18 record cartons, 4 flat boxes of photographs) |
| Condition at receipt | Good, except 1 carton with active mold — isolated on arrival |
| Restrictions | 2 lf departmental personnel records — restricted 25 years |
| Initial appraisal note | Correspondence/manuscripts: enduring research value (department history). Personnel records: retain per policy, restrict, box-level only. |

Accession record is written **before** any arrangement or description work starts — it is the custody record regardless of processing timeline.

## Processing-rate decision table

| Collection profile | Processing level | Stated rate (hrs/linear ft) | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| High-use, donor-prominent, litigation/FOIA-relevant | Item-level | 15–20 | Justify explicitly in accession note — this is the exception, not the default |
| Standard research-value collection | Series/box-level (MPLP) | 2–4 | Default for most accessions |
| Low-value, high-volume (routine transactional records) | Collection-level only, no box inventory | <1 | Bulk transfers with minimal expected research use |
| Restricted material found within an otherwise open collection | Box-level entry only, seal item | N/A — pulled, not processed further | Personnel, medical, legal, active-donor-privacy records |

Rate figures are stated heuristics reflecting commonly cited MPLP-era ranges (Greene & Meissner, 2005), not fixed constants — recalibrate against your own repository's completed-collection logs.

## DACS finding-aid skeleton (filled)

```
Title: [Professor] Papers, 1978-2025
Extent: 38 linear feet (16 record cartons, 4 flat boxes)
Repository: [University] Special Collections
Biographical note: [Professor] joined the Department of History in 1979,
  chaired the department 1994-2001, and retired in 2025. Research focus:
  20th-century labor history.
Scope and content note: Correspondence (1980-2025, 12 lf) with colleagues,
  publishers, and former students; course materials and lecture notes
  (1979-2010, 8 lf); manuscript drafts of three monographs with editorial
  correspondence (1985-2015, 10 lf); photographs from fieldwork and
  conferences (1980-2000, 4 flat boxes); administrative correspondence
  from department chairship (1994-2001, 8 lf).
Arrangement: Original order substantially preserved as maintained by the
  creator; five series established along existing functional divisions
  (Correspondence, Teaching, Manuscripts, Photographs, Administrative).
Restrictions: Series 6 (Personnel/Administrative HR records, 2 lf) closed
  until 2051 per university personnel-records policy. Remaining series
  open to research without restriction.
Preferred citation: [Professor] Papers, [Accession 2026.014], [University]
  Special Collections.
```

## Preservation-triage scoring table

| Item/series | Condition score (1=stable, 5=active decay) | Use frequency (requests/yr) | Priority score (condition x use) | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Photograph series, nitrate negatives | 5 | 3 | 15 | Cold storage transfer this quarter |
| Manuscript drafts, acidic paper | 3 | 8 | 24 | Rehouse in acid-free folders, top of queue |
| Correspondence, standard bond paper | 1 | 2 | 2 | Standard rehousing, no urgency |
| Administrative records, mold-affected carton | 5 | 0 | — | Isolate/treat regardless of use score — active mold is a containment issue, not a ranking exercise |

Priority score ranks routine triage; anything scoring 5 on condition (active, spreading deterioration) is pulled out of the ranked queue and handled immediately, independent of its use score.
