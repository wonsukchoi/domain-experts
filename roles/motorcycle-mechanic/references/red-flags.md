# Red flags

Smell tests for an intake inspection, a stored bike coming out of layup, or a comeback.

### Rear tread within 1/32 in of the wear bar (or wear bar flush)
- **Usually means:** the bike was ridden through most of its usable tread without a swap scheduled — often not visually obvious because crown wear from highway miles looks fine while the shoulders, where lean-angle grip lives, are worn further.
- **First question:** "Measure tread depth at the crown and both shoulders separately — is the wear even, or is it worse at the shoulders?"
- **Data to pull:** tread-depth gauge readings at three points, tire DOT date code, rider's typical use (commuter/upright vs. spirited/leaned).

### Chain slack over roughly 1.5× the platform's spec upper bound at its tightest point
- **Usually means:** chain adjustment was skipped for one or more service intervals, frequently accompanied by hooked sprocket-tooth wear that a chain-only replacement won't fix.
- **First question:** "When was this chain last adjusted and lubed, and does the sprocket show a hooked tooth profile?"
- **Data to pull:** slack measurement at multiple points around a full wheel rotation to confirm the tightest point, sprocket tooth-profile comparison, chain roller-to-roller wear-limit measurement.

### Tire DOT date code older than roughly 5–6 years regardless of remaining tread
- **Usually means:** sidewall rubber has hardened or begun cracking from UV/ozone exposure even where tread depth looks adequate, reducing grip specifically at lean.
- **First question:** "What's the DOT date code, and is there any cracking at the bead or in the tread grooves?"
- **Data to pull:** DOT week/year code, visual sidewall crack check, storage history (covered/uncovered, indoor/outdoor).

### Brake fluid not flushed in 2+ years, or visibly dark/cloudy in the reservoir sight glass
- **Usually means:** hygroscopic moisture absorption has lowered the fluid's boiling point — more consequential here than on a car because the front brake alone typically supplies most of a motorcycle's stopping force.
- **First question:** "When was this fluid last flushed, and how does the lever feel — firm or spongy?"
- **Data to pull:** last-flush date from service history, visual fluid color/clarity, lever-feel check.

### Carbureted bike returns from 60+ days of storage with a rough or no-idle complaint and no documented stabilizer added before layup
- **Usually means:** fuel varnish has clogged the pilot/idle jet — the smallest passage in the circuit — not a comprehensive fuel-system failure that a refill would fix.
- **First question:** "Was fuel stabilizer added before storage, and is the remaining fuel visibly discolored?"
- **Data to pull:** pilot jet inspection under magnification, float-bowl sediment/varnish presence, fuel color and smell.

### EFI bike throws an intermittent fault code with no reproducible symptom on a test ride
- **Usually means:** a connector or ground corrosion issue rather than an actual sensor failure, especially on bikes with wet or road-salt exposure history.
- **First question:** "Does the code return under a specific condition — cold start, vibration, a particular lean angle — or truly at random?"
- **Data to pull:** live-data stream captured at the moment the code sets (if the tool supports freeze capture), connector and ground continuity/corrosion check.

### Valve-clearance service overdue relative to the platform's interval, with no clearance-specific service line in the history
- **Usually means:** general "service" was done without an actual clearance check — a tight clearance from wear burns a valve progressively with far less warning than a loose one, which announces itself with noise first.
- **First question:** "When was clearance actually measured, not just when was the bike serviced — and what were the readings?"
- **Data to pull:** service history for a clearance-specific line item, cold-engine feeler-gauge readings against the platform's spec on the next check.

### Customer describes "feels vague" or "wanders a bit" at speed, with no chassis inspection yet performed
- **Usually means:** wheel-bearing play or steering-head bearing wear/notchiness — on a single-track vehicle this degrades the counter-steering feedback the rider needs to stay balanced, not just ride comfort the way it would on a car.
- **First question:** "Does the vagueness happen at a specific speed or lean angle, or is it constant?"
- **Data to pull:** wheel-bearing hand-rock play check, steering-head bearing drag/notch check with the front wheel off the ground.

### Battery not maintained on a tender through a multi-month storage period, then found weak or non-holding in spring
- **Usually means:** sulfation from resting at a low state of charge for an extended period, which degrades capacity even if the battery initially starts the bike.
- **First question:** "Was this battery kept on a float charger during storage, and what's its resting voltage now?"
- **Data to pull:** open-circuit voltage, load-test result, charge/discharge history if a smart tender logged it.

### Aftermarket tire size/profile mounted that fits the rim but wasn't checked against the bike's GVWR-derived load requirement
- **Usually means:** the tire was selected on rim-size compatibility alone, without verifying its load index against the bike plus rider plus cargo weight.
- **First question:** "What's this tire's load index, and what does the bike's GVWR actually require per tire?"
- **Data to pull:** tire sidewall load index and speed rating, bike's GVWR and curb weight from the owner's manual.

### Recommended-services line blends a chassis-safety finding with a routine advisory
- **Usually means:** a documentation shortcut, or a deliberate blur so the customer can't distinguish a ride/no-ride item from something merely worth watching.
- **First question:** "Which of these findings means the bike shouldn't be ridden until fixed, and which are advisories the customer can reasonably defer?"
- **Data to pull:** the RO's line-item breakdown, the measurement supporting each finding (slack in mm, tread depth, fluid age).
