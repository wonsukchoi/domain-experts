### Initial estimate lands within 10-15% of the state total-loss threshold
- **Usually means:** Real risk that hidden damage found during teardown will push the vehicle over the line — this isn't a settled repairable case yet.
- **First question:** Has the shop scheduled a teardown, and what's the history of supplement rates on similar damage profiles from this shop?
- **Data to pull:** Initial estimate total, applicable state TLF threshold and percentage, this shop's historical supplement-approval rate.

### A supplement request has no photo or teardown documentation for the specific added line item
- **Usually means:** Either the documentation simply wasn't attached yet, or the line item is being added without the evidence to support it.
- **First question:** Can the shop provide teardown photos or a written explanation tying this specific item to the discovered damage?
- **Data to pull:** Original estimate photos, requested teardown photos for the new line item.

### Labor hours on a specific operation diverge from book/reference time with no stated reason
- **Usually means:** A legitimate non-standard condition (corrosion, prior poor repair, rust-through) that wasn't documented, or an inflated hour request.
- **First question:** What specific vehicle condition justifies the deviation from book time on this operation?
- **Data to pull:** Estimating platform's book-time reference for the operation, shop's stated justification and supporting photos.

### ACV is based on a single valuation-guide number with no local comparable-sales check
- **Usually means:** The guide value may lag actual local market conditions, especially in high-demand, low-inventory, or unusual-trim situations.
- **First question:** Are there at least 2-3 actual local comparable listings (same year/trim/mileage range) to check the guide value against?
- **Data to pull:** Guide-book valuation, local marketplace comparable listings.

### Aftermarket or non-OEM parts are specified on a vehicle under approximately 3 years old, or on a structural/safety component
- **Usually means:** The parts-source decision may violate the specific state's disclosure or restriction rules for newer vehicles or safety-critical parts.
- **First question:** Does this state's parts-disclosure statute restrict or require disclosure of non-OEM parts for a vehicle this age or this part class?
- **Data to pull:** State parts-disclosure regulation, vehicle model year, part classification (cosmetic vs. structural/safety).

### Salvage value is estimated as a flat percentage of ACV rather than an actual bid or auction comparable
- **Usually means:** The insurer's net-cost calculation on a total loss may be materially wrong, since salvage value varies significantly by damage type, part demand, and regional salvage market.
- **First question:** Has an actual salvage auction bid or comparable been obtained for this specific damage profile and vehicle?
- **Data to pull:** Salvage auction comparable data, damage profile description.

### A body shop's cumulative supplement total approaches or exceeds the original estimate amount
- **Usually means:** Either genuinely extensive hidden damage (plausible after significant frame/structural impact), or a pattern of incremental additions that should have been caught in the initial estimate or an earlier supplement round.
- **First question:** What is this shop's typical supplement-to-original-estimate ratio on comparable damage severity, and does this case fall within or well outside that pattern?
- **Data to pull:** This shop's supplement history, full sequence of supplement requests with dates and documentation.

### Insured or shop requests a diminished-value payment alongside the repair estimate
- **Usually means:** A separate claim category (loss of resale value even after proper repair) that needs its own evaluation methodology, not an add-on to the repair estimate.
- **First question:** Does this state recognize first-party diminished-value claims, and what methodology applies?
- **Data to pull:** State diminished-value claim rules, pre- and post-repair comparable market value analysis.
