# Red flags

### A "significant" result at p between .04 and .05 with n<30 per group in your own or a student's data
- **Usually means:** an underpowered pilot inflating the true effect size (winner's curse), or undisclosed multiple comparisons across several DVs/subgroups
- **First question:** how many outcome variables or subgroup splits were examined before this particular result was the one reported?
- **Data to pull:** the full analysis log or lab notebook, every DV/subgroup that was tested, not just the reported one

### Student evaluation mean drops more than 0.5 points (on a 5-point scale) semester over semester with no syllabus change
- **Usually means:** a shift in the enrolled cohort (different major mix, more non-majors fulfilling a requirement) or a perceived rigor increase, not necessarily worse teaching
- **First question:** did the grade distribution or the drop/withdraw rate shift the same semester?
- **Data to pull:** grade distribution, drop/withdraw rate, and comparative SET data for other sections of the same course

### IRB protocol more than 12 months since the last continuing review with no renewal filed
- **Usually means:** the approval has lapsed and any ongoing data collection is technically unauthorized
- **First question:** what is the current approval's expiration date in the IRB portal?
- **Data to pull:** the protocol's status page and expiration date

### Grad student has not submitted a first-author manuscript by year 3 of a 5-year PhD
- **Usually means:** an over-scoped thesis project, or an advisor-side bottleneck on draft feedback turnaround
- **First question:** what is the status of the current draft, and when did the advisor last return comments on it?
- **Data to pull:** draft version history/timestamps and the advisor's comment-turnaround log

### Grant proposal returns a bottom-half percentile score or "not discussed" outcome
- **Usually means:** the aims read as incremental rather than novel, or the preliminary data didn't convincingly de-risk the approach
- **First question:** what did the summary statement say specifically about significance and approach, not just the overall score?
- **Data to pull:** the full summary statement and the percentile/impact score

### A course fills within hours of registration opening but carries a 30%+ D/F/W rate
- **Usually means:** a popular-but-punishing reputation, often from an assessment scheme that doesn't match the stated learning objectives or rubric weighting
- **First question:** does the actual grade distribution match what the syllabus rubric implies it should?
- **Data to pull:** grade distribution histogram against the syllabus rubric weights

### A manuscript has been "under review" at a single journal for more than 6 months with no editor update
- **Usually means:** a non-responsive reviewer or desk-level indecision on the editor's part
- **First question:** has a formal status inquiry been sent to the handling editor?
- **Data to pull:** submission-portal timestamp history and any editor correspondence

### Pre-tenure service load exceeds 3 standing committees
- **Usually means:** over-commitment that is quietly eroding protected research time without proportionally strengthening the dossier
- **First question:** which of these committees actually counts toward the P&T service section versus reads as invisible goodwill work?
- **Data to pull:** the committee-assignment letters and an honest hours-per-week log for each

### A replication attempt (yours or another lab's) fails to reproduce a published effect
- **Usually means:** the original finding was underpowered or a false positive, or a real moderator/boundary condition differs between the two studies
- **First question:** was the replication attempt itself adequately powered for the original claimed effect size?
- **Data to pull:** the replication's preregistration and its sample size relative to the original's effect size

### Authorship order or inclusion is contested after a project nears submission
- **Usually means:** authorship criteria were never explicitly agreed at project start
- **First question:** was there a documented authorship agreement (email, project charter) made when the project began?
- **Data to pull:** the earliest project-planning correspondence establishing roles and expected contributions

### Course-evaluation "easiness" and "overall quality" ratings correlate above r=.6 within a section
- **Usually means:** the SET score is tracking grading leniency more than learning, consistent with the weak SET-learning relationship in the literature
- **First question:** does that same correlation pattern hold across other instructors' sections in the department, or is it specific to this course?
- **Data to pull:** department-wide SET dataset with easiness and quality subscales broken out

### A 3rd-year review letter flags "insufficient publication trajectory"
- **Usually means:** a pipeline problem that originated 12-18 months earlier — a data-collection delay, an IRB holdup, or betting the whole review period on one large study instead of a program of smaller ones
- **First question:** what is currently sitting at each pipeline stage right now (collecting/analyzing/writing/under review)?
- **Data to pull:** the personal project-pipeline tracker across every active line of research
