# Red flags

Smell tests a coach or scout should catch before a grade, a lineup call, or a training decision gets locked in.

### Acute:chronic workload ratio above ~1.5 for any starter over a rolling 4-week window
- **Usually means:** a game went to overtime, a short week compressed practice into fewer high-intensity days, or a player returned from injury straight into full volume without a ramp.
- **First question:** "What was this player's rep count the last 4 weeks, week by week — not just this week's number?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly live-rep or GPS-distance totals for the last 4 weeks, chronic average, and the specific week driving the spike.

### A recruit's file is highlight-cut plays with no full-game charting behind it
- **Usually means:** the evaluator (or the recruit's own service) selected the best 8-10 plays of a season, which tells you the ceiling and nothing about the floor — miss rate, leverage, and technique under fatigue are invisible in a cut-up.
- **First question:** "Has anyone charted a full game, not the highlight reel?"
- **Data to pull:** at least two full, uncut games; missed-tackle or missed-assignment counts against total attempts, not just plays made.

### A prospect's testing numbers jumped sharply between two events with no logged training explanation
- **Usually means:** either real off-season development, a different testing surface/conditions, or (less often) a testing-day manipulation issue at a private combine — the three look identical without a training log.
- **First question:** "What changed in this player's training between the two testing dates, and who supervised each test?"
- **Data to pull:** training log or strength-program notes for the gap period; testing conditions (surface, wind, footwear) for both events; whether the second test was independently verified.

### Missed-tackle or missed-assignment rate above roughly 15% on a sample of 20+ charted attempts
- **Usually means:** a technique pattern, not bad luck — at 20+ attempts the rate is no longer noise, and technique flaws correct more slowly than measurable athleticism does.
- **First question:** "Is this leverage/angle technique, or is it an effort problem?" (the two have very different developmental trajectories)
- **Data to pull:** the charted attempts broken out by technique-cause vs. effort-cause; whether the flaw shows up against a specific formation/route concept or across the board.

### A roster's birth dates cluster heavily toward the older half of the selection-year cutoff
- **Usually means:** relative age effect — evaluators have been systematically rewarding early physical maturity and missing later-maturing, often more skilled, younger-in-cohort players.
- **First question:** "What's the birth-month distribution on this roster and on the players we passed on?"
- **Data to pull:** birth-quarter breakdown of the roster vs. the eligible player pool; production of passed-on players in subsequent seasons.

### Recruiting grades cluster tightly in the 55-65 band across an entire class
- **Usually means:** grade inflation under scholarship-number pressure, or an evaluator unwilling to commit to a spread — a file where nobody is below 50 or above 70 has stopped discriminating.
- **First question:** "Walk me through why this specific player is a 58 and not a 62 — what's the trait that separates them?"
- **Data to pull:** the full distribution of grades issued by that evaluator over the cycle; whether any grade changed after a scholarship-number conversation rather than after new film.

### A star player is taking 90%+ of practice reps in-season with no live-rep reduction plan
- **Usually means:** the coaching staff is optimizing this week's practice quality at the cost of season-long availability — the same overreach the ACWR threshold exists to catch, applied without the data because "he's fine."
- **First question:** "What's this player's rep count the last month, and when did we last plan a scout-team-rep swap for him?"
- **Data to pull:** rep-count log by player; comparison against the ACWR band; games missed by comparable players at this position leaguewide from cumulative overload.

### Return-to-play push before 24 symptom-free hours at the current concussion-protocol stage
- **Usually means:** competitive pressure (a playoff game, a rivalry game) is being weighed against a medical gate that isn't supposed to have a competitive input at all.
- **First question:** "Has the player been symptom-free for a full 24 hours at this stage, independent of the schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** the symptom log and timestamps for the current stage; who signed off on advancing the player and on what basis.

### The team's win total is rising while point differential is flat or negative
- **Usually means:** a favorable run of close games or schedule luck, not real improvement — win totals regress toward what point differential predicts, and a staff crediting itself for the former will misread its own personnel needs.
- **First question:** "What's our point differential trend, not just our record?"
- **Data to pull:** point differential by game, strength of opponent, and close-game (one-score) win rate versus the leaguewide average for that rate.

### A depth chart hasn't changed in 6+ weeks despite a clear matchup or performance shift
- **Usually means:** the staff has anchored on the August-camp order and is treating a reshuffle as an admission of an earlier mistake rather than a normal in-season adjustment.
- **First question:** "If we were setting this chart today with no history, who starts?"
- **Data to pull:** per-game performance grades by position over the last month; specific matchup data for the upcoming opponent.
