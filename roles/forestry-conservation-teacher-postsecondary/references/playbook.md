# Forestry and Conservation Science Teacher, Postsecondary — Playbook

## Field-lab safety plan (filled example — chainsaw felling demonstration)

| Element | Requirement | Source/standard |
|---|---|---|
| PPE | Chaps or cut-resistant leg protection, hard hat, eye protection, hearing protection, steel-toe boots | OSHA 29 CFR 1910.266(d) |
| Crew qualification | One qualified faller (faculty/staff with documented felling experience) per demonstration; students observe from outside the two-tree-length felling zone | Department field-safety plan, modeled on 1910.266(h) |
| Escape route | Briefed and physically walked before the first cut, minimum two routes at 45° angles opposite the felling direction | Standard forestry field-safety practice |
| Communication | Handheld radios or whistle signal confirmed working before the saw starts; one spotter with sightline to the faller at all times | Department field-safety plan |
| Weather hold | No felling in sustained winds above 20 mph or during active lightning within 10 miles | Department field-safety plan (site-specific threshold) |
| Documentation | Sign-in sheet with PPE check, pre-lab safety briefing acknowledgment signed by every student | EH&S office requirement |

## Mensuration practical exam (filled example — blind station rotation)

Six stations, 8 minutes each, unlabeled specimens or plots:

1. Identify 5 unlabeled tree specimens (bark, leaf/needle, twig) to species — 2 points each, 10 total.
2. Measure DBH and total height on 3 marked trees using a DBH tape and clinometer; score against instructor's blind pre-measurement, ±0.2 in DBH and ±2 ft height tolerance — 10 points.
3. Run one prism point (BAF 10) at a marked station, record trees "in," compute basal area/acre — 10 points, method (correct prism use, correct borderline-tree call) scored separately from the arithmetic.
4. Age a tree from an increment core using a hand lens and ring count — 5 points.
5. Identify a site index curve reading from a given height/age pair — 5 points.
6. Written short-answer: state the minimum number of points required for a reliable stand-level basal-area estimate on a 40-acre stand of moderate variability, and why one point is inadequate — 10 points, graded on the *why*, not just a memorized number.

Blind stations catch what a written exam alone doesn't: a student who can define "basal area factor" on paper but misreads a borderline tree in the field every time.

## McIntire-Stennis capacity report (filled example — annual REEport entry, partial)

| Line item | Amount | Capacity-building justification |
|---|---|---|
| Graduate research assistant stipend (0.5 FTE, 12 months) | $14,000 | Trains a student in remote-sensing forest-inventory methods named in the current Statement of Regional Research Priorities (forest health monitoring) |
| Field equipment (2 DBH tapes, 1 prism, 1 increment borer) | $1,800 | Builds departmental field-data-collection capacity used across 3 courses and 2 active research projects |
| Faculty summer salary (1 month) | $9,500 | Time to develop the remote-sensing inventory protocol that will be taught in FOR 340 starting next academic year |
| **Total McIntire-Stennis formula funds requested** | **$25,300** | — |

Every line ties to a named capacity outcome (a method taught, equipment usable beyond one project, a protocol that becomes course content) — a line item that reads like an ordinary single-project grant expense (e.g., "publication page charges" with no capacity tie) is the kind of entry an audit flags separately from any question about the research itself.

## Field-safety incident report (filled example, EH&S-facing)

1. **What happened, in one factual sentence**: "During a supervised chainsaw-felling demonstration on [date], a student inside the briefed escape route zone was struck by a falling branch (not the tree itself); minor laceration, treated on-site with the field first-aid kit, no hospital transport required."
2. **Which specific safety-plan step failed**: "Escape route was briefed but not re-walked after wind shifted the likely branch-fall direction 15 minutes before the cut."
3. **Immediate correction made same day**: "Felling paused; escape routes re-briefed and re-walked relative to current wind; lab resumed with a lower wind threshold (10 mph, down from 20) for the remainder of the term."
4. **Report routed to**: University EH&S office (same day, written) and department chair; incident logged against the course's field-safety plan for revision before the next offering.
