# Forestry and Conservation Science Teacher, Postsecondary — Red Flags

### A stand-level inventory report built from a single plot point
- **Usually means:** The student ran out of field time and extrapolated from one point, or collected multiple points but only wrote up the cleanest-looking one.
- **First question:** How many plot points does the raw field notebook show, and do they match the number the report claims to average?
- **Data to pull:** The original field notebook or data sheet, not the typed-up summary.

### Field-lab safety briefing with no written escape-route or PPE checklist signed that day
- **Usually means:** The lab is being run from institutional memory rather than a documented plan, which is exactly the gap an OSHA or EH&S review will ask about after an incident, not before.
- **First question:** Where is today's signed sign-in/PPE-check sheet for this specific lab session?
- **Data to pull:** The department's field-safety plan template and this session's completed sign-in sheet.

### A student or crew member in a wildland-fire lab with no current NWCG red card
- **Usually means:** Qualification tracking lapsed, or the lab's fire component was added to the syllabus after the safety plan was last reviewed.
- **First question:** Is there a current, dated Incident Qualification Card on file for every participant in this specific burn?
- **Data to pull:** The red-card roster cross-checked against the lab's actual participant list.

### SAF curriculum-mapping gap discovered during the self-study year itself
- **Usually means:** The catalog-to-standard mapping wasn't reviewed annually, so a curriculum drift (a required course dropped, a credit-hour count that quietly fell) went unnoticed until the deadline forced a look.
- **First question:** When was this specific standard element last checked against the current catalog, and by whom?
- **Data to pull:** The prior three years of curriculum-mapping tables, if they exist.

### A field-mensuration exam score that correlates with handwriting neatness more than measurement accuracy
- **Usually means:** The exam is graded on the final written number rather than blind-verified against the grader's own field measurement.
- **First question:** Was each station scored against an instructor pre-measurement taken independently, or against what "looks reasonable"?
- **Data to pull:** The instructor's blind pre-measurement key for each station.

### McIntire-Stennis funds spent entirely on a single narrow project with no capacity-building line
- **Usually means:** The funds were treated like an ordinary competitive research grant instead of the formula-funded capacity mechanism they are, which is a compliance exposure independent of whether the research itself is sound.
- **First question:** Which line items build reusable capacity (equipment, trained personnel, a protocol that becomes course content) versus consumed entirely by one project?
- **Data to pull:** The current Statement of Regional Research Priorities and the itemized budget against it.

### A dendrology or species-ID practical where the class-wide pass rate drops more than 20 points from the prior offering
- **Usually means:** Either the specimen set changed to something genuinely harder (a legitimate difficulty shift) or the specimens were mislabeled or degraded (pressed leaves that dried into an unrecognizable state).
- **First question:** Were this term's specimens verified against a reference key immediately before the exam?
- **Data to pull:** The specimen list with verification sign-off, compared across terms.

### A near-miss in a field lab that gets resolved informally with the student instead of filed with EH&S
- **Usually means:** The instructor is treating it as a one-off rather than evidence the safety plan has a specific gap that will recur.
- **First question:** What in the written safety plan would have prevented this, and has it been updated?
- **Data to pull:** The current safety plan version and the date it was last revised.

### A prescribed-burn or herbicide-application lab added to a syllabus without a corresponding line in the department's field-safety plan
- **Usually means:** The lab was added for pedagogical reasons (a new instructor, a new partnership with a land manager) without the safety-plan update that should accompany any new live-hazard activity.
- **First question:** Does the department's field-safety plan have a specific entry for this lab, or is it covered only by the general "field trips" boilerplate?
- **Data to pull:** The field-safety plan's table of contents against the current syllabus's lab list.

### An accreditation self-study communications-standard element supported only by a course description, with no rubric or sample student work attached
- **Usually means:** The course exists and is mapped correctly on paper, but the specific outcome (e.g., technical writing competency) was never formally assessed against a rubric, which a site-visit team will ask to see evidence of, not just a catalog entry.
- **First question:** Is there a scored rubric and a sample of actual student work for this standard element, or only a course title?
- **Data to pull:** The rubric and three sample graded student artifacts for that course.
