# Hoist and Winch Operator — Red Flags

### Load checked against the nameplate (bare-drum, layer-1) rating instead of the layer active at the critical point of the lift
- **Usually means:** the crew has not calculated which drum layer will be active at the top (or start) of the specific planned lift, and the actual derated capacity there may be below the load
- **First question:** how much rope will be wound on the drum at the critical point of this lift, and what's the capacity chart's figure for that specific layer?
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer layer-specific line-pull chart, planned lift height, drum width and rope diameter

### Anti-two-block device trips repeatedly during normal operation and gets reset without investigation
- **Usually means:** a reeving, geometry, or switch-adjustment problem is producing nuisance trips, and the crew is treating the symptom as an annoyance rather than a signal
- **First question:** what specific hook position or configuration triggers the trip, and has the switch/reeving been inspected since the pattern started?
- **Data to pull:** trip log with load and configuration at each event, device manufacturer's function-test procedure

### Anti-two-block device jumpered, disconnected, or otherwise bypassed to keep working
- **Usually means:** the device is being treated as an obstacle rather than the last independent layer preventing a two-block failure
- **First question:** who authorized the bypass, and has the equipment been stood down until the device is restored and function-tested?
- **Data to pull:** equipment tag-out/tag-in record, device wiring/status, incident or near-miss log

### Load left suspended on the brake for an extended period without a separate holding-capacity check
- **Usually means:** the crew is assuming lifting/lowering capacity implies indefinite holding capacity, without confirming the holding brake's rating or checking for creep
- **First question:** what's this brake's rated holding capacity against the actual suspended load, and has creep been checked over a stated dwell period?
- **Data to pull:** brake manufacturer holding-capacity rating, dwell-period observation log, load weight

### Measurable creep observed on a suspended load and the crew leaves it unattended anyway
- **Usually means:** the holding brake is not adequately rated or is worn beyond its holding margin for this load
- **First question:** has the load been lowered to a supported position or backed up mechanically, or is it still hanging on a brake that's already shown drift?
- **Data to pull:** creep observation record, brake inspection/maintenance history, load weight versus rated holding capacity

### Wire rope observed cross-winding, piling, or riding up over a prior wrap on the drum
- **Usually means:** fleet angle is out of tolerance or drum grooves are worn, and the layer-capacity chart no longer describes what's actually spooled
- **First question:** what's the measured fleet angle at this point in drum travel, and does it exceed tolerance for this drum type (smooth vs. grooved)?
- **Data to pull:** fleet-angle measurement, drum groove inspection record, rope condition at the affected wraps

### Load or periodic brake-certification test date has lapsed
- **Usually means:** the equipment is being operated on a certification that no longer reflects current condition, whether or not a prior lift went fine
- **First question:** what's the current test/certification date against the required interval, and has the equipment been stood down pending retest?
- **Data to pull:** load-test and brake-certification records, applicable standard's required interval

### Operator trained on one hoist/winch context applying that context's operating discipline to a different one
- **Usually means:** the operator is carrying over assumptions (inspection cadence, safety factor, duty cycle) from a mine hoist, marine winch, or construction hoist background into a context with a different governing standard
- **First question:** which standard governs this specific equipment and context, and does the operator's current practice match it?
- **Data to pull:** governing standard for the equipment's actual service, operator's certification/training scope, current operating procedure in use

### Rope safety factor or inspection criteria applied from a materials-only standard to a personnel-carrying hoist (or vice versa)
- **Usually means:** the crew has not distinguished that personnel-carrying hoist service (e.g., a mine man-hoist) commonly requires a materially higher rope safety factor and inspection frequency than materials-only service
- **First question:** is this hoist rated and inspected for the service it's actually being used for right now?
- **Data to pull:** hoist service rating/certification, current load type (personnel vs. materials), applicable safety-factor requirement

### Level-wind mechanism (where fitted) visibly out of sync with drum rotation
- **Usually means:** the level-wind is no longer tracking correctly across drum width, which will produce the same cross-winding a fleet-angle violation causes even if fleet angle itself measures within tolerance
- **First question:** is the level-wind mechanism mechanically synced to drum rotation right now, and has it been inspected recently?
- **Data to pull:** level-wind inspection/maintenance record, current spooling pattern on the drum
