### Rising EtCO2 (>10mmHg over 10 min despite stable ventilation) + tachycardia + masseter rigidity after succinylcholine
- **Usually means:** Malignant hyperthermia; second most likely is inadequate anesthetic depth with hypoventilation, but the masseter rigidity is the discriminator
- **First question:** "When was succinylcholine given, and is the jaw actually rigid on exam?"
- **Data to pull:** EtCO2 trend on the anesthesia record, time since succinylcholine, temperature trend, arterial blood gas if available

### SpO2 declining across ≥2 laryngoscopy attempts
- **Usually means:** Failing airway plan continuing past its decision point; second most likely is a mask seal or SGA seal failure not yet recognized
- **First question:** "Have we hit the attempt limit we set before induction — are we moving to the next plan now?"
- **Data to pull:** Number of attempts logged, time since last effective ventilation, SpO2 trend line

### Hypotension unresponsive to fluid bolus and first-line pressor after a neuraxial block
- **Usually means:** Block level higher than intended (high spinal) affecting sympathetic outflow; second most likely is occult hemorrhage unmasked by sympathectomy
- **First question:** "What's the sensory block level right now, and is it still rising?"
- **Data to pull:** Dermatomal block level on exam, vasopressor doses given so far, surgical field blood loss estimate

### Perioral numbness, metallic taste, or tinnitus during or shortly after local anesthetic injection
- **Usually means:** Early local anesthetic systemic toxicity (LAST); second most likely is a vasovagal reaction from the injection itself
- **First question:** "How much local anesthetic has gone in total, and is it still being injected?"
- **Data to pull:** Cumulative local anesthetic dose (mg and mg/kg), time course of symptom onset relative to each increment

### Sudden increase in peak airway pressure (>10cmH2O over baseline) without ventilator setting change
- **Usually means:** Bronchospasm or mainstem intubation (tube migration); second most likely is pneumothorax, especially after central line or regional block near the chest
- **First question:** "Are breath sounds equal, and where's the tube depth relative to the last check?"
- **Data to pull:** Tube depth marking, breath sounds bilaterally, capnograph waveform shape (shark-fin suggests bronchospasm)

### Train-of-four ratio <0.9 at planned extubation
- **Usually means:** Residual neuromuscular blockade; second most likely is inadequate reversal dosing for the blockade depth present
- **First question:** "What's the quantitative TOF ratio right now, not the qualitative twitch count?"
- **Data to pull:** Quantitative TOF monitor reading, time and dose of last neuromuscular blocker and reversal agent

### STOP-BANG ≥5 patient with a standing postoperative opioid PCA order
- **Usually means:** Respiratory-depression risk not reflected in the analgesia plan; second most likely is an order set applied without checking the score
- **First question:** "Does this patient need continuous pulse oximetry/capnography on the floor, and is the PCA basal rate off?"
- **Data to pull:** STOP-BANG score, PCA settings (basal rate, demand dose, lockout), monitoring level ordered

### Unexplained tachycardia and rising temperature hours after an otherwise unremarkable case
- **Usually means:** Delayed-onset or masked malignant hyperthermia presentation (rare but reported), or a separate cause (infection, transfusion reaction, thyroid storm) — differential, not a single answer
- **First question:** "What triggering agents were used, and how long ago?"
- **Data to pull:** Intraoperative agent record, PACU vital sign trend, temperature curve

### Increasing FiO2 requirement to maintain SpO2 intraoperatively
- **Usually means:** Developing shunt physiology — atelectasis, mainstem intubation, or pulmonary embolism; second most likely is a circuit leak
- **First question:** "Is this gradual (atelectasis) or sudden (embolism/mainstem), and did anything change in positioning or the airway?"
- **Data to pull:** FiO2 trend over time, EtCO2 trend (embolism typically drops it), recent position change or surgical manipulation

### Cumulative crystalloid >3–4L with rising peak airway pressures and falling urine output in a long case
- **Usually means:** Third-spacing/fluid overload approaching pulmonary edema; second most likely is intra-abdominal hypertension from surgical packing or insufflation
- **First question:** "What's the running fluid balance, and has the surgeon been asked about intra-abdominal pressure or packing?"
- **Data to pull:** Total fluids in/out, peak and plateau airway pressures, urine output per hour
