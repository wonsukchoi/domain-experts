# Anesthesia protocol playbook

Filled protocols, not descriptions of protocols. Doses assume adult patients unless noted; verify against current facility policy and package insert before use.

## Malignant hyperthermia (MH) dosing ladder

| Step | Action | Dose / detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Stop all triggers | Discontinue volatile agent and succinylcholine; switch circuit or machine if feasible |
| 2 | Hyperventilate | 100% O2, 2–3× normal minute ventilation |
| 3 | Dantrolene bolus | 2.5mg/kg IV push (conventional formulation: 20mg/vial, reconstitute each vial with 60mL sterile water — not bacteriostatic) |
| 4 | Reassess at 5 min | If EtCO2/HR still rising, redose 2.5mg/kg |
| 5 | Cumulative ceiling | Up to 10mg/kg total before reassessing diagnosis |
| 6 | Cool | Cold IV saline, surface cooling if temp >39°C; stop cooling at 38°C to avoid overshoot |
| 7 | Call for help | MHAUS hotline (US: 1-800-644-9737); treat hyperkalemia, myoglobinuria, arrhythmia per standard ACLS-adjacent protocol |

**Weight-based worked table (80kg patient):**

| Dose level | mg | Vials (20mg each) | Sterile water needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Initial (2.5mg/kg) | 200mg | 10 | 600mL |
| Second dose (cumulative 5mg/kg) | 400mg | 20 | 1,200mL |
| Ceiling (cumulative 10mg/kg) | 800mg | 40 | 2,400mL |

Concentrated formulation (Ryanodex, 20mg/5mL) reconstitutes the same 200mg dose in one vial with 5mL sterile water — use it if stocked; it removes the mixing-volume bottleneck above.

## ASA/DAS difficult airway escalation ladder

1. **Plan A — primary intubation attempt**, optimized: sniffing position, video laryngoscope first-line if difficulty predicted, ≤2 attempts by the most experienced provider present.
2. **Plan B — supraglottic airway (SGA)** if Plan A fails or after 2 failed attempts: place SGA, confirm ventilation, decide whether to proceed with SGA as the airway, attempt intubation through it (if aintree/fiberoptic-capable SGA), or wake the patient.
3. **Plan C — face-mask ventilation** if SGA fails: two-person technique, airway adjuncts (oral/nasal airway), call for help now if not already called.
4. **Plan D — emergency front-of-neck access (CICO)** if face-mask ventilation also fails and oxygenation cannot be achieved: cricothyrotomy, called and started within 60 seconds of declaring "can't intubate, can't oxygenate" — do not attempt a fourth laryngoscopy instead.

**Decision gate:** move to the next plan on a fixed attempt/time budget decided before induction (commonly ≤2 attempts or ~3 minutes of desaturation trend), not on a feeling that "one more try" will work.

## LAST (local anesthetic systemic toxicity) protocol

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Stop injecting local anesthetic at first sign (perioral numbness, metallic taste, tinnitus, or CNS/cardiac signs) |
| 2 | Call for lipid emulsion (20% Intralipid) and airway management equipment |
| 3 | Lipid bolus | 1.5mL/kg of 20% lipid emulsion IV over 1 minute (≈105mL for 70kg patient) |
| 4 | Lipid infusion | 0.25mL/kg/min, continue for at least 10 min after circulatory stability |
| 5 | Repeat bolus | Up to 2 more times if instability persists; may increase infusion to 0.5mL/kg/min if BP still low |
| 6 | Ceiling | ~12mL/kg total lipid emulsion in first 30 minutes |
| 7 | Seizure/arrhythmia management | Benzodiazepines for seizure (avoid propofol as primary agent if cardiovascularly unstable); standard ACLS with modifications — avoid vasopressin, calcium-channel blockers, beta-blockers; reduce epinephrine doses (<1mcg/kg boluses) |

## ASRA neuraxial/regional anticoagulation intervals (selected agents)

| Agent | Hold before neuraxial block | Restart after block/catheter removal |
|---|---|---|
| LMWH, prophylactic dose | 12h | 4h |
| LMWH, therapeutic dose | 24h | 4h (24h if traumatic) |
| Unfractionated heparin, IV therapeutic | 4–6h (normal aPTT) | 1h |
| Warfarin | INR ≤1.4 | Per surgical plan |
| Aspirin (monotherapy) | No hold required | — |
| Clopidogrel | 5–7 days | 6h after catheter removal |

## PONV prophylaxis stacking (Apfel-score-based)

| Apfel risk factors present | Baseline risk | Prophylaxis |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 | ~10–20% | None or single low-cost agent (dexamethasone 4mg) |
| 2 | ~40% | 2 agents, different classes (e.g., dexamethasone 4mg + ondansetron 4mg) |
| 3 | ~60% | 3 agents + consider TIVA with propofol instead of volatile |
| 4 | ~80% | 3–4 agents + TIVA + minimize opioids |

## NPO fasting intervals (ASA 2017)

| Ingested substance | Minimum fasting time |
|---|---|
| Clear liquids | 2h |
| Breast milk | 4h |
| Infant formula / non-human milk | 6h |
| Light meal (toast, clear liquids) | 6h |
| Fatty/fried meal or meat | 8h |
