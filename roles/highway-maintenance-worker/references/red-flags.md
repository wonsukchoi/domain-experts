### Patch fails within 2 weeks of a hot-mix application
- **Usually means:** compaction temperature was too low when placed (ambient dropped mid-shift), or the failure is subsurface/drainage-driven and the surface patch never addressed the cause.
- **First question:** what was the pavement temperature at placement, and has this exact station been patched before this season?
- **Data to pull:** the daily maintenance log entry for the original patch (temperature, material, crew) and the route's patch-history record for that station.

### Same pothole location patched 2+ times in one season
- **Usually means:** subgrade failure or a drainage defect (blocked culvert, failed underdrain) feeding water under the pavement, not a surface defect.
- **First question:** is there standing water or a visible drainage path feeding this station after rain?
- **Data to pull:** route drainage map and the most recent culvert/ditch inspection for the segment.

### Straight rock salt applied below ~15°F pavement temperature with no visible melt response
- **Usually means:** the chemical is past its effective floor — more NaCl will not fix it; the fleet needs to switch to a chloride blend or abrasives.
- **First question:** what does the pavement sensor (not the truck cab) read right now?
- **Data to pull:** pavement temperature sensor log and the chemical application record for the pass.

### Taper or device count short of the MUTCD formula for the posted speed
- **Usually means:** the crew estimated the closure by device count on the truck instead of calculating L = WS or WS²/60 for the actual posted speed.
- **First question:** what is the posted (not observed) speed, and what taper length does the formula give for it?
- **Data to pull:** the work zone traffic control plan for the route class and the device count actually deployed.

### Guardrail section reinstalled after a hit with a fixed object now closer than its rated dynamic deflection
- **Usually means:** the crew matched the pre-crash geometry without checking the offset against the system's tested rating — often because the fixed object (culvert extension, new sign foundation) postdates the original installation.
- **First question:** what is the current measured offset from the back of the rail to the nearest fixed object, and what deflection is this system rated for?
- **Data to pull:** as-built guardrail system spec or manufacturer rating, and a fresh field measurement.

### Guardrail end terminal missing, crushed flat, or clearly mismatched to the rail height
- **Usually means:** a prior repair used whatever terminal was in parts stock rather than the MASH-rated unit for that system, or a hit damaged the terminal without anyone flagging it for replacement.
- **First question:** does the terminal's part number match the currently installed rail system?
- **Data to pull:** terminal manufacturer spec sheet and the most recent post-crash inspection note.

### Cold-mix patch still in place with no hot-mix follow-up logged after one full season
- **Usually means:** the original follow-up work order was never created, or was created and never triggered on a qualifying dry/warm day.
- **First question:** does a follow-up work order exist for this station, and what was its trigger condition?
- **Data to pull:** the route's open work order queue filtered by "temporary repair" flag.

### Vehicle intrusion into an active taper or work space
- **Usually means:** taper/buffer length undersized for actual approach speed (which can exceed posted speed), device spacing too wide, or advance warning signage placed too close to the work space to give drivers reaction time.
- **First question:** what was the taper length and buffer space actually in place, and does it match the formula for this road's real approach speed?
- **Data to pull:** the traffic control plan used for the closure and any available speed data for the approach segment.

### Sign retroreflectivity visibly degraded at night or flagged by a routine sign inspection
- **Usually means:** sign sheeting has aged past its service life (commonly 10–15 years depending on sheeting type) or has UV/weather damage.
- **First question:** when was this sign installed or last replaced, and what sheeting type is specified for it?
- **Data to pull:** the sign inventory record for that station.

### Mowing or brush clearing leaves a sightline obstruction near an intersection or curve
- **Usually means:** the mowing pass followed a fixed schedule instead of checking the sight triangle at that specific location, or growth outpaced the scheduled interval.
- **First question:** is the obstruction inside the intersection sight triangle or the curve's stopping-sight-distance envelope?
- **Data to pull:** the roadway's sight-distance requirement for its design speed and the mowing schedule log for the segment.

### Culvert or ditch holding standing water more than 48 hours after a storm with no rain in the interim
- **Usually means:** partial or full blockage (debris, sediment, collapsed pipe) reducing the design flow cross-section.
- **First question:** when was this culvert last inspected or cleared, and is the blockage visible from either end?
- **Data to pull:** the culvert's design capacity and its inspection history.

### Crew working within a few feet of ≥50 mph traffic or after dark in Class 2 hi-vis apparel
- **Usually means:** the crew grabbed whatever vest was in the truck instead of matching apparel class to the exposure.
- **First question:** what is the posted speed and is this work happening after dark or in reduced visibility?
- **Data to pull:** the agency's hi-vis apparel policy for the exposure class in question.
