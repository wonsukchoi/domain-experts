# Red Flags — Commercial Pilot

### Computed takeoff/landing distance within 15% of runway available at today's actual weight and density altitude
- **Usually means:** performance was checked against a remembered or "typical" number instead of today's current AFM chart at actual DA and weight; second most likely: runway length was pulled from memory or an app cache rather than current NOTAMs (displaced threshold, work in progress).
- **First question:** "What DA and weight did you actually plug into the chart, and when did you last check the runway-length NOTAM?"
- **Data to pull:** current AFM performance chart, current altimeter/OAT, airport NOTAMs for runway length and displaced thresholds.

### TAF carries a TEMPO or PROB30/40 group bracketing the planned arrival window
- **Usually means:** the forecaster's bracketed conditions are being read as "unlikely, so ignore it" rather than the condition to plan around; second: the 1-2-3 alternate requirement wasn't triggered even though it should have been.
- **First question:** "If the TEMPO conditions are what's actually there at your ETA, do you still have a legal and genuinely usable alternate?"
- **Data to pull:** full TAF text with amendment time, current METAR trend, the alternate airport's own forecast.

### Fuel plan lands with exactly the regulatory reserve (45 min IFR / 30–45 min VFR) and nothing more
- **Usually means:** the reserve was treated as available fuel during planning rather than a floor; second: no contingency was built in for a hold, reroute, or missed approach.
- **First question:** "What's the plan if you get one hold or one missed approach — where does that fuel come from?"
- **Data to pull:** fuel-planning worksheet, winds-aloft forecast used, actual burn rate from this tail's last several legs.

### CG computed only at takeoff weight, never rechecked at estimated landing weight
- **Usually means:** fuel burn shifts CG over the flight (often forward as an aft-mounted fuel arm empties) and was never recomputed; second: a last-minute cargo/pax change was applied to total weight but not to the moment calculation.
- **First question:** "Where's the CG at your planned landing weight, not just at brake release?"
- **Data to pull:** W&B worksheet with both takeoff- and landing-weight CG entries, actual loaded manifest vs. planned manifest.

### A load change after W&B was finalized, with no recomputation logged
- **Usually means:** it was treated as "close enough" because total weight is still under gross; second: nobody reran the moment calculation because the scale ticket shows weight, not arm.
- **First question:** "Show me the recomputed CG after that last box went in, not before."
- **Data to pull:** final loaded manifest with actual arms, updated W&B form, load-sheet timestamp vs. door-close timestamp.

### Personal-minimum ceiling/visibility set no stricter than the legal VFR/IFR minimum
- **Usually means:** personal minimums were never actually written down, defaulting to "legal is my minimum"; second: they were set once at initial training and never tightened for a lapse in currency.
- **First question:** "What's your written personal minimum, and when did you last update it for your current currency?"
- **Data to pull:** the pilot's personal-minimums card, logged instrument/night currency, hours in type over the last 90 days.

### Departure or continuation decided under unspoken schedule pressure, with no divert trigger set before takeoff
- **Usually means:** plan continuation bias — the real decision was made when the trip was booked, not at the airplane; second: no one else in the loop heard the actual reasoning, only the go decision.
- **First question:** "What specific number or condition would have made this a no-go, and was it written down before engine start?"
- **Data to pull:** dispatch release or flight-following log, pre-takeoff briefing notes, weather/fuel state at the actual decision point vs. at planning time.

### Density altitude more than roughly 2,000 ft above field elevation, with performance checked against field-elevation numbers
- **Usually means:** a remembered or lower-elevation performance figure was used instead of recomputing DA for today's OAT and altimeter; second: no chart cross-check was applied before assuming "it always makes it out of here."
- **First question:** "What's today's actual density altitude, and what chart row did you read from?"
- **Data to pull:** current OAT and altimeter setting, DA computation, AFM performance chart at that DA and actual weight.

### Crosswind component at or above the aircraft's maximum demonstrated crosswind value
- **Usually means:** "demonstrated" is being treated as a hard certified limit rather than the highest value a test pilot happened to demonstrate — not itself a safety margin; second: gust spread wasn't applied to the steady-state component.
- **First question:** "Did you compute that off the gust peak or the steady wind, and what's your personal max regardless of the demonstrated figure?"
- **Data to pull:** current METAR wind including gust, runway heading, crosswind component chart, the pilot's personal crosswind minimum.

### Alternate meets the 1-2-3 rule trigger but its own forecast sits close to its own approach minimums
- **Usually means:** the 1-2-3 rule was treated as proof the alternate is adequate rather than proof one is required; second: the alternate's NOTAMs (approach out of service, runway closed) weren't rechecked.
- **First question:** "If you actually had to fly the approach at the alternate, what would ceiling/vis need to be, and what's forecast there at that hour?"
- **Data to pull:** alternate's TAF, alternate's lowest available approach minimums, alternate's current NOTAMs.

### Duty/flight time approaching Part 135 or personal fatigue limits on a schedule already delayed once
- **Usually means:** cumulative fatigue from an earlier delay gets discounted because "this leg itself is short"; second: nobody recomputed remaining duty-time margin after the delay.
- **First question:** "What's your duty time if this leg runs long, and where's the hard stop?"
- **Data to pull:** duty-time log, Part 135 OpSpecs/GOM duty limits, actual report time vs. current time.
