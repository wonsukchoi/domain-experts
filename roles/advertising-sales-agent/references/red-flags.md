# Advertising Sales Agent — Red Flags

### Format running above 80% sold-through but proposal quotes a discount anyway
- **Usually means:** habit-driven discounting, or the agent hasn't pulled current sellout data before quoting.
- **First question:** "What's this format's sellout percentage for the flight period right now?"
- **Data to pull:** current-period avails/sellout report by format.

### Budget split evenly across formats without checking avails
- **Usually means:** the proposal was built from the rate card alone, not from actual remaining inventory — likely to oversell scarce formats and undersell remnant.
- **First question:** "Does the requested impression volume for each format actually exist in current avails?"
- **Data to pull:** avails report cross-referenced against the proposed impression counts per format.

### Guarantee written as per-day or per-week minimum when client only needs a flight-total outcome
- **Usually means:** unnecessary make-good exposure if any single day/week underdelivers, even if the flight total would have been fine.
- **First question:** "Does the client actually need daily/weekly pacing, or just total delivery by the end of the flight?"
- **Data to pull:** the client's stated campaign timing requirements (e.g. a sale-event deadline vs. general awareness).

### A rate discount given without any stated justification (bundling, volume, off-peak) in the IO
- **Usually means:** the discount will surface in agency rate comparisons with no defensible reason attached, weakening the next negotiation.
- **First question:** "What's the stated reason for this rate, and is it documented in the insertion order?"
- **Data to pull:** insertion order / contract language for the discount rationale field.

### Pipeline forecast reported at raw deal value instead of probability-weighted value
- **Usually means:** early-stage inquiries and unsigned proposals are being counted the same as signed IOs, inflating forecast accuracy.
- **First question:** "What stage is each deal in, and what's the weighted total, not the raw total?"
- **Data to pull:** CRM pipeline report broken out by stage and weighted-forecast column.

### Make-good liability from a prior campaign not tracked or resolved before booking new inventory against the same format
- **Usually means:** the format is being oversold again — new commitments plus an unresolved make-good may exceed actual capacity.
- **First question:** "Is there an open make-good obligation against this format, and has it been fulfilled or scheduled?"
- **Data to pull:** make-good liability tracker for the format in question.

### Client anchors a premium-format price against a remnant-format CPM quoted earlier in the conversation
- **Usually means:** CPM was quoted across formats without noting the audience-quality/demand difference, inviting an apples-to-oranges comparison.
- **First question:** "Did the client see the sellout-rate context for why the premium format is priced differently?"
- **Data to pull:** the original proposal's per-format sellout-rate annotations, if included.

### A deal closes at a rate more than ~10-15% below card on a format that was above 70% sold-through at the time
- **Usually means:** the agent discounted out of habit or negotiating pressure rather than actual inventory pressure — likely leaving revenue on the table.
- **First question:** "What was this format's sellout percentage at the time this deal was quoted?"
- **Data to pull:** historical sellout snapshot for the format at the quote date, compared against the closed rate.

### Renewal proposal quotes the exact same rate as the prior period without checking current sellout
- **Usually means:** the rate is being carried forward on autopilot rather than re-priced against current demand, which may now be higher (underpricing) or lower (overpricing, risking the renewal).
- **First question:** "Has current sellout for this format changed since the last period's rate was set?"
- **Data to pull:** period-over-period sellout comparison for the renewing format.
