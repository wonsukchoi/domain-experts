# Advertising Sales Agent — Playbook

## Avails-based pricing worksheet (filled example)

Client: mid-size regional retailer. Requested budget: $25,000. Flight: 2 weeks.

| Format | Card CPM | Current sellout % | Avails (impressions) | Pricing default |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage takeover | $45 | 95% | 80,000 | Hold at card ± 5% |
| Video pre-roll | $32 | 88% | 150,000 | Hold at card ± 5-8% |
| Newsletter sponsorship | $2,200/wk flat | 70% | 2 slots/wk | Light discount (5-10%) if bundled |
| ROS display | $7 | 40% | 3,500,000 | Discount 15-25% to absorb remainder |

**Allocation order:** scarce formats first (cap at avails, price near card) → remnant format absorbs remaining budget last.

1. Homepage takeover: 60,000 impressions × $43 CPM = $2,580
2. Video pre-roll: 120,000 impressions × $30 CPM = $3,600
3. Newsletter: 2 weeks × $2,000/wk (9% off, bundled discount) = $4,000
4. Remaining budget: $25,000 − $2,580 − $3,600 − $4,000 = $14,820
5. ROS display: 14,820 ÷ 0.0058 (effective CPM $5.80, 17% off card) × 1,000 = 2,555,172 impressions

Total delivered: 60,000 + 120,000 + 2,555,172 = 2,735,172 impressions across formats + 2 newsletter sends. Total revenue: $25,000 (matches budget).

## Make-good liability tracker (filled example)

| Client | Guarantee (impressions) | Delivered to date | Shortfall | Make-good owed | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Riverside Motors | 5,650,000 (flight total) | 5,410,000 (flight ended) | 240,000 | 240,000 impressions, next available inventory | Open — must fulfill before new deal counted as clean revenue |
| Coastal Realty Group | 1,200,000 (flight total) | 1,235,000 | 0 (over-delivered) | None | Closed |
| Fresh Market Co-op | 3,000,000 (per-week: 750,000/wk × 4) | Wk1: 810,000, Wk2: 690,000, Wk3: 780,000, Wk4: TBD | Wk2 short by 60,000 | 60,000 impressions owed against Wk2 specifically (per-week guarantee, can't borrow from Wk1 surplus) | Open |

The Fresh Market row is the standard trap: a per-week guarantee doesn't let a surplus week offset a shortfall week — only a flight-total guarantee would have absorbed that Wk2 dip for free.
