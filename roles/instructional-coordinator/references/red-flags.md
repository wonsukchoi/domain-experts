# Instructional Coordinator — Red Flags

### Instructional-day allocation gap over 3 percentage points vs. test-blueprint weight for any domain
- **Usually means:** the pacing guide was inherited from a prior textbook adoption's chapter order rather than built from the current assessment blueprint, or a mid-year schedule disruption ate days from one domain without adjustment elsewhere.
- **First question:** when was the pacing guide last checked against the current (not a prior year's) technical manual weighting?
- **Data to pull:** current-year state assessment technical manual point-weighting table; the pacing guide's day-by-domain allocation.

### DOK-alignment rate under 60% among standards tagged DOK 3 in the blueprint
- **Usually means:** activities were selected for topical coverage, not cognitive-complexity match — a common byproduct of adopting a curriculum for its pacing convenience rather than auditing its task complexity.
- **First question:** for the DOK-3-tagged standards below the 60% threshold, what activity type is currently on file — worksheet, single-step problem, or genuine multi-step/non-routine task?
- **Data to pull:** item specifications by standard; the actual activity or task artifact filed against each standard in the curriculum map, not just the standard citation.

### PD hours logged with zero scheduled classroom-observation follow-up in the same semester
- **Usually means:** the PD was designed around a topic or compliance requirement rather than one observable practice, making a follow-up check structurally impossible to define.
- **First question:** what single classroom practice was this PD session supposed to change, and how would an observer recognize it in a walkthrough?
- **Data to pull:** the PD session's stated objective; the walkthrough/observation calendar for the following 4–6 weeks.

### Item-level, standard-tagged score report requested more than 60 days after results release
- **Usually means:** curriculum decisions are being made off the aggregate score alone, before the data that would actually localize the problem is even in hand.
- **First question:** has any curriculum change already been proposed based on the aggregate score, before the standard-level breakdown was reviewed?
- **Data to pull:** the assessment vendor's item-level, standard-tagged report; the timeline of any curriculum-revision proposals made before it arrived.

### A curriculum map marked "coverage complete" for a domain with no DOK crosswalk documentation on file
- **Usually means:** coverage was tracked as a checklist against topic titles, not cross-referenced against the item specifications' complexity requirements.
- **First question:** what artifact, beyond the standard's citation in a lesson plan header, demonstrates the complexity level actually taught?
- **Data to pull:** the curriculum map's supporting artifacts folder for the flagged domain.

### A domain-level score drop of more than roughly 10 points triggering a full-curriculum rewrite proposal
- **Usually means:** the response is being sized to the alarm the score drop caused, not to the scope of standards actually implicated by the item-level breakdown.
- **First question:** how many distinct standards, and at what DOK levels, does the item-level breakdown actually implicate?
- **Data to pull:** item-level standard-tag report for the affected domain; the proposed rewrite's scope compared against that list.

### Pacing guide unchanged for more than 3 consecutive years despite a state blueprint revision in that window
- **Usually means:** the pacing guide is being maintained as a static document rather than re-derived each time the thing it's supposed to track — the blueprint — changes.
- **First question:** what was the blueprint's point-weighting the last time the pacing guide was actually recalculated against it?
- **Data to pull:** the version history (or lack of one) for both the pacing guide and the state technical manual.

### A teacher's self-reported "standard covered" with no assessment, activity, or rubric artifact on file
- **Usually means:** coverage is being self-certified rather than documented, which makes any later DOK or alignment audit impossible to verify.
- **First question:** what specific artifact — a task, a rubric, a set of student work samples — supports the coverage claim?
- **Data to pull:** the curriculum map's artifact repository for that standard.

### A new curriculum adopted district-wide with no pilot cohort or pre-rollout DOK-alignment audit
- **Usually means:** the adoption decision was driven by publisher marketing materials or a sales-cycle deadline rather than a verified match to the district's own test blueprint.
- **First question:** was the proposed curriculum's DOK distribution checked against the state blueprint's DOK requirements before adoption, or only its topic-coverage claim?
- **Data to pull:** the publisher's alignment documentation; an independent DOK crosswalk against at least one full domain.

### PD attendance rate reported without a paired practice-implementation rate
- **Usually means:** the reporting metric available (attendance) is being substituted for the metric that actually matters (observed practice change), often because no walkthrough was scheduled to measure the latter.
- **First question:** what walkthrough or observation data exists for the weeks following the PD session?
- **Data to pull:** the walkthrough calendar and observation notes tied to the PD session's stated practice objective.
