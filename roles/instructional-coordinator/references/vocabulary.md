# Instructional Coordinator — Vocabulary

### Backward design
A curriculum-design sequence (Wiggins & McTighe) that starts from the desired assessment evidence, then works back to the learning activities that would produce it, rather than starting from activities.
**In use:** "We're not picking the reading list until we've written the DOK-3 assessment task it needs to prepare students for."
**Common misuse:** treated as a one-time template filled in after activities are already chosen, turning a design sequence into a compliance form.

### Depth of Knowledge (DOK)
Webb's four-level scale (recall, skill/concept, strategic thinking, extended thinking) describing the cognitive complexity a task requires, independent of how hard the content itself is.
**In use:** "The standard is on the pacing guide, but every activity filed against it tops out at DOK 2 — it's covered, not aligned."
**Common misuse:** conflated with difficulty or grade level — a DOK-1 task can use grade-level-appropriate vocabulary and still only require recall.

### Standards alignment (Webb's four criteria)
The combined test of categorical concurrence (same content category), DOK consistency (matching complexity), range of knowledge correspondence (breadth within the standard), and balance of representation (proportional emphasis) — all four are required for genuine alignment, not just one.
**In use:** "It passes categorical concurrence — same topic — but fails DOK consistency, so it's not aligned by the full criteria."
**Common misuse:** shortened to "it's aligned" after checking only that the standard is cited, without checking the other three criteria.

### Pacing guide
A document assigning instructional days or weeks to each standard or unit across a school year.
**In use:** "The pacing guide gives Geometry 15% of instructional days against an 18% test weight — that's the gap driving the review."
**Common misuse:** treated as fixed once published, rather than re-derived whenever the test blueprint's weighting changes.

### Curriculum map
A cross-referenced document linking standards, instructional activities, assessments, and pacing across a course or grade band.
**In use:** "Pull the curriculum map's artifact folder for 7.G.4 before crediting it as covered."
**Common misuse:** used interchangeably with "pacing guide," though a map documents what's taught and assessed, not just when.

### Item specification
The assessment vendor's or state's technical documentation defining, per standard, the DOK level, format, and point weight of test items.
**In use:** "The item specs tag 7.G.1 at DOK 3, format: constructed response, weight: 2 points."
**Common misuse:** assumed to match the standard's own text exactly, when the specification often narrows or emphasizes a specific aspect the raw standard doesn't make explicit.

### Vertical alignment
Consistency of standards progression and complexity expectations across consecutive grade levels within the same domain.
**In use:** "Before revising the grade 7 Geometry sequence, check vertical alignment against what grade 6 assumes as prerequisite and grade 8 builds toward."
**Common misuse:** checked only at the standard-citation level, without confirming the DOK progression actually escalates grade to grade.

### Horizontal alignment
Consistency of standards coverage and complexity across parallel sections or teachers within the same grade level.
**In use:** "Section C's DOK-alignment rate is 80%; sections A, B, and D are at 40% — that's a horizontal-alignment problem, not a curriculum problem."
**Common misuse:** overlooked when curriculum documents look identical across sections, because the gap lives in what's actually taught, not in the written plan.

### Scope and sequence
The ordered list of what content is taught and in what order across a course, distinct from the day-by-day pacing.
**In use:** "The scope and sequence puts Statistics last in the year — check whether that still matches the test-blueprint weight before assuming order doesn't matter."
**Common misuse:** treated as fixed by tradition ("we've always done Geometry in the spring") without revisiting whether the order still serves the current blueprint or student readiness data.

### Test blueprint
The document specifying which standards are tested, at what DOK level, and at what point-weight, for a given state or vendor assessment.
**In use:** "Pull this year's blueprint before assuming last year's weighting still holds — states revise these."
**Common misuse:** assumed static year over year without checking for a published revision.

### Coverage
The claim that a standard was addressed in instruction, typically measured by time or presence on a pacing guide.
**In use:** "Coverage isn't the question — DOK alignment is; a standard can be fully covered and still taught two complexity levels below how it's tested."
**Common misuse:** used as if it were equivalent to student readiness or mastery, rather than a time-allocation claim only.

### Fidelity of implementation
The degree to which a curriculum or instructional practice is actually delivered as designed, versus adapted or partially skipped in practice.
**In use:** "The curriculum map shows a DOK-3 task assigned to this standard; the walkthrough found a DOK-1 substitute in three of four sections — that's a fidelity gap."
**Common misuse:** assumed to be guaranteed once a curriculum is adopted, without walkthrough data to confirm what's actually happening in the classroom.

### Practice-implementation rate
The share of teachers observed actually using a targeted practice after PD, distinct from the share who attended the PD session.
**In use:** "Attendance was 4/4; practice-implementation rate at the week-3 walkthrough was 2/4 — that's the number that predicts whether scores move."
**Common misuse:** substituted with the attendance rate in reporting, since attendance is easier to collect and looks more favorable.

### Walkthrough / instructional rounds
A brief, structured classroom observation focused on a specific, predetermined practice, distinct from a full formal teacher evaluation.
**In use:** "Schedule the walkthrough for week 3, scoped to whether the DOK-3 task is in use — not a general evaluation visit."
**Common misuse:** run without a predetermined focus, producing broad impressions instead of a yes/no answer on the specific practice PD was meant to change.
