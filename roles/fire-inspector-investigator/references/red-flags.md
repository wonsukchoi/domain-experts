# Fire Inspector/Investigator — Red Flags

### Report concludes "incendiary" solely because no accidental cause was identified
- **Usually means:** Negative-corpus reasoning (NFPA 921 §18.6.5 prohibited methodology), or a genuinely incendiary fire whose ignition-source evidence just wasn't documented.
- **First question:** What specific ignition-source hypothesis was tested and what physical evidence supports it — not what was ruled out?
- **Data to pull:** The hypothesis-testing section of the report; if it doesn't name a specific ignition source with supporting evidence, the conclusion doesn't meet NFPA 921 standard.

### "Alligatoring," "crazed glass," or dramatic char patterns cited as sole accelerant evidence
- **Usually means:** Pre-2011 folklore-pattern reading, not fire-dynamics-informed analysis; both occur in ordinary flashover fires.
- **First question:** Was ILR lab testing (ASTM E1618) performed, and what was the result?
- **Data to pull:** Lab report; if absent or negative and the conclusion still asserts accelerant, the pattern evidence is being over-weighted.

### Multiple independent points of origin identified with no common ignition path explained
- **Usually means:** (1) a genuine multiple-origin incendiary fire, or (2) flashover creating a false appearance of separate origins.
- **First question:** Was full-room involvement (flashover) documented in the incident timeline before pattern analysis was performed?
- **Data to pull:** Fire department dispatch/incident timeline; ventilation conditions (open doors/windows) at time of origin analysis.

### Char depth or burn-pattern measurements taken after overhaul without correction
- **Usually means:** Hose-stream and overhaul disturbance altered the physical evidence being measured; conclusions built on it are unreliable.
- **First question:** Was pre-overhaul photography/video taken, and does it corroborate the post-overhaul measurements?
- **Data to pull:** First-arriving-crew body-cam or photo timestamps against overhaul-start time.

### Occupant-load calculation uses the wrong occupancy-classification factor
- **Usually means:** Space was reclassified (e.g., retail converted to event venue) without a corresponding code re-inspection, or the calculator defaulted to a familiar factor instead of checking the actual use.
- **First question:** What is the space's current, actual use — not its permitted-on-paper use?
- **Data to pull:** Certificate of occupancy; any special-event or temporary-use permits filed for the space.

### Egress-width calc omits door-hardware/stop encroachment
- **Usually means:** Nominal door-frame width was used instead of measured clear width, overstating available egress capacity.
- **First question:** Was clear width measured with the door in its normal open position, hardware included?
- **Data to pull:** Field measurement notes, not architectural drawings alone (as-built conditions frequently diverge from plans).

### Witness statements collected days after the event with no documentation of who else was present
- **Usually means:** Memory degradation and cross-contamination between witnesses (comparing notes before being interviewed) reduce statement reliability.
- **First question:** When was each statement taken relative to the event, and was the witness isolated from others beforehand?
- **Data to pull:** Interview timestamps; canvass log showing order of contact.

### Factual findings and opinion are blended in the same paragraph
- **Usually means:** The report wasn't structured to survive an admissibility challenge — opposing counsel can attack the entire section if fact and inference aren't separable.
- **First question:** Can every sentence in the findings section be traced to a direct observation, with all inference moved to the opinion section?
- **Data to pull:** Report structure itself — re-read for sentences that assert cause or intent inside the "factual findings" heading.

### Sprinkler status was assumed rather than confirmed before applying egress-width factors
- **Usually means:** Using the sprinklered reduced-factor table (0.15/0.2 in/person) on an unsprinklered building understates the required width and can mask a real violation, or vice versa.
- **First question:** Is there a current fire-protection-system inspection record confirming operational sprinkler coverage for this specific space?
- **Data to pull:** NFPA 25 inspection/testing records for the sprinkler system, not just its presence on an old drawing.
