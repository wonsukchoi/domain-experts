# Occupational Health and Safety Specialist — Red Flags

### Personal exposure sample at or above the action level (50% of PEL) with no re-monitoring scheduled
- **Usually means:** the hearing-conservation or exposure-monitoring program treats the action level as a one-time trigger instead of an ongoing watch condition.
- **First question:** when was this location or task last re-sampled, and was there any process/equipment change since?
- **Data to pull:** sampling history for the location/task, equipment-change log, current control inventory.

### TRIR trending up for 2+ consecutive quarters, even if still below industry benchmark
- **Usually means:** an emerging control failure not yet severe enough to trip a benchmark comparison — benchmarks lag real-time drift by design.
- **First question:** do the incidents share a body part, task, or shift pattern?
- **Data to pull:** OSHA 300 log filtered by body part/task/shift for the trend period.

### Near-miss report rate falling while recordable rate stays flat
- **Usually means:** reporting suppression (fear of light-duty reassignment, incentive-program penalties for reporting) rather than genuine hazard reduction.
- **First question:** did anything change in how near-misses are reported or incentivized recently?
- **Data to pull:** near-miss log trend, any recent changes to safety incentive program structure.

### A corrective action closed with no owner or date on file
- **Usually means:** the finding was acknowledged but never actually implemented — a common failure point between investigation and follow-through.
- **First question:** who verified this control is in place, and when?
- **Data to pull:** corrective-action tracking log, physical/procedural verification of the control.

### Root-cause investigation stops at "employee error" or "failure to follow procedure"
- **Usually means:** the investigation didn't ask why the procedure was hard to follow correctly — task design, time pressure, or equipment access gap.
- **First question:** was the correct action physically convenient to do in the time available?
- **Data to pull:** task timing/staffing data for the shift, JSA for the task, prior near-misses on the same task.

### A task performed "the same way for years without incident" involves an unverified energy-isolation assumption
- **Usually means:** the task has been relying on a usually-true-but-unverified condition (e.g., an upstream valve is "always" closed) instead of a positive lockout/tagout.
- **First question:** is there a written, task-specific LOTO procedure, or is isolation assumed from experience?
- **Data to pull:** LOTO procedure documentation for the specific task and equipment.

### PPE recommended as the primary control for a newly identified hazard with a known feasible engineering control
- **Usually means:** cost or schedule pressure is driving the control selection instead of the hierarchy.
- **First question:** was an engineering-control option priced and rejected, or never evaluated?
- **Data to pull:** the control-selection memo or lack thereof; vendor quotes if any exist.

### Audiometric Standard Threshold Shift (STS) flagged with no corresponding recent noise sampling
- **Usually means:** the exposure has likely existed above the PEL for longer than the sampling program has visibility into.
- **First question:** when was this station/task last sampled, and has any equipment changed since?
- **Data to pull:** audiometric trend for the individual, station sampling history, equipment-change log.

### Confined-space or hot-work permits completed as a formality (identical hazard list every time) regardless of actual conditions
- **Usually means:** the permit process has become paperwork rather than a live hazard assessment — atmospheric testing results may not reflect that day's actual conditions.
- **First question:** do the atmospheric test readings on file vary at all across permits, or are they identical every time?
- **Data to pull:** permit log with actual test readings, not just checkbox completion.

### A safety incentive program ties rewards to low recordable counts at the team or site level
- **Usually means:** a structural incentive to underreport, independent of actual safety performance.
- **First question:** does the incentive reward leading indicators (JSA completion, near-miss reporting) or only the lagging injury count?
- **Data to pull:** incentive program structure, near-miss reporting trend before/after program launch.
