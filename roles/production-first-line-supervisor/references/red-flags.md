# Red flags

### A defect or reject rate jumping several multiples above baseline
- **Usually means:** a developing process issue (tooling wear, material lot change, fixture problem) rather than normal variation.
- **First question:** does the current inspection method reliably catch defects at this new, elevated rate, or is escape risk increasing the longer it continues?
- **Data to pull:** reject rate trend over the shift, inspection station's known detection reliability, any recent process/material/tooling changes.

### A production decision that would delay addressing a quality or safety signal to protect the schedule
- **Usually means:** rate is being treated as an equal or higher priority than a signal that should be the binding constraint.
- **First question:** is the production shortfall from addressing this now actually more costly than the risk of letting it continue unaddressed?
- **Data to pull:** current production status vs. target, the specific quality/safety signal and its trend.

### An open issue not mentioned at shift handoff
- **Usually means:** the outgoing supervisor assumed it would be discovered rather than explicitly communicating it.
- **First question:** does the handoff log/report list every open item, or does it rely on verbal recall?
- **Data to pull:** the shift handoff log, any active maintenance tickets, quality holds, or personnel matters from the prior shift.

### A procedural shortcut that "worked" being repeated without correction
- **Usually means:** the shortcut hasn't caused a visible problem yet, but the compliance drift is accumulating.
- **First question:** does this shortcut carry a real safety or quality risk under different conditions, even if this instance was fine?
- **Data to pull:** the documented procedure being shortcut, frequency of the shortcut's use.

### An issue escalated that was actually within line-level authority to resolve
- **Usually means:** avoidance of a difficult but resolvable decision, or genuine uncertainty about authority boundaries.
- **First question:** does this issue require expertise or resources genuinely outside line-level control, or could it have been resolved directly?
- **Data to pull:** the facility's escalation authority matrix, the specific issue's actual requirements.

### An issue NOT escalated that required expertise or authority beyond line level
- **Usually means:** an attempt to resolve something at line level that actually needed maintenance, quality, or EHS involvement.
- **First question:** was this issue genuinely within line-level capability, or did it require specialized diagnosis/authority?
- **Data to pull:** the outcome of the attempted line-level resolution, whether it actually addressed root cause.

### A performance or safety-behavior issue deferred to "when things calm down"
- **Usually means:** immediate production pressure is being prioritized over a correction that will only get more costly the longer it's deferred.
- **First question:** has this specific issue actually been addressed, or has the deferral become the de facto response?
- **Data to pull:** any documentation of the issue and correction status, whether it has recurred since first observed.

### A safety near-miss not formally reported
- **Usually means:** the event was resolved without consequence and treated as not worth documenting, losing the pattern-detection value of near-miss reporting.
- **First question:** does the near-miss reporting system show an entry for this event, or was it only discussed verbally?
- **Data to pull:** near-miss reporting log, verbal accounts from witnesses if no formal report exists.

### A production shortfall with a vague or generic explanation at handoff ("had some issues today")
- **Usually means:** the actual causes weren't tracked specifically enough to communicate clearly, or weren't communicated fully.
- **First question:** can the shortfall be broken down into specific, attributable causes with time/quantity impact?
- **Data to pull:** production tracking data for the shift, any logged downtime/delay events.

### The same escalation or issue type recurring across multiple shifts without a documented root-cause resolution
- **Usually means:** each shift is treating the recurrence as a new isolated event rather than recognizing a pattern requiring a different level of response.
- **First question:** does the issue's history across shifts show a pattern, and has that pattern itself been escalated (not just each individual occurrence)?
- **Data to pull:** issue/escalation log across recent shifts for this same type of problem.
