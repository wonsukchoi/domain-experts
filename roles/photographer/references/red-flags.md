# Photographer — Red Flags

### Client requests a "buyout" without specifying scope
- **Usually means:** the client wants unlimited future use across all media and geography priced at the day-rate/standard-usage number, not a true buyout rate.
- **First question:** "Buyout of what — which media, which duration, worldwide or specific markets, forever or a term?"
- **Data to pull:** the requested use case against the standard usage-fee grid, to show the delta a real buyout should price.

### Histogram shows clipped highlights on a non-recoverable RAW frame
- **Usually means:** overexposure that pushed one or more color channels past the sensor's recoverable range — no post-processing pull-back will restore that detail.
- **First question:** "Which channel clipped — one color individually, or all three together?" (a single-channel clip is sometimes recoverable; a full clip is not.)
- **Data to pull:** per-channel histogram, not the combined luminance histogram.

### An irreplaceable moment has only one usable frame in the contact sheet
- **Usually means:** the shot list didn't flag that moment as high-risk, so no redundant angle or bracketed exposure was captured.
- **First question:** "If this single frame is unusable — blink, focus miss, blocked sightline — is there a backup?"
- **Data to pull:** frame count captured for that specific moment against the shot list's coverage-requirement column.

### Outdoor golden-hour shoot scheduled with no lighting backup plan
- **Usually means:** the schedule assumes clear weather with no contingency if the sky is overcast or the light window is missed.
- **First question:** "What's the fallback if golden hour is overcast or the schedule slips past it?"
- **Data to pull:** the shoot's backup-plan field in the pre-shoot brief; if blank, the risk hasn't been priced into the schedule.

### Key-to-fill lighting ratio doesn't match the stated brief
- **Usually means:** a ratio set by habit or personal style preference rather than the deliverable's actual mood requirement — an 8:1 dramatic ratio on a brief asking for "approachable and warm," for example.
- **First question:** "Does the brief call for dramatic or approachable, and does the measured ratio match that?"
- **Data to pull:** incident light meter readings, key-only vs key+fill.

### Model or property release not signed before the shoot
- **Usually means:** the images may be technically excellent but legally unusable for the intended commercial licensing, regardless of quality.
- **First question:** "Do we have a signed release for every identifiable person and any private property in frame?"
- **Data to pull:** release-form roster cross-checked against the shot list's talent/location entries.

### Client wants "unlimited digital use, just for social" with no duration or geography limit
- **Usually means:** the request is being priced like a narrow use case when social posting is often global, indefinite-duration, and high-reach — the opposite of narrow.
- **First question:** "How long will this stay live, and does the brand's audience reach beyond one region?"
- **Data to pull:** the brand's social following size and geographic distribution.

### ISO pushed well above the camera's clean-noise ceiling with no stated reason
- **Usually means:** either the available light was genuinely insufficient, or a tripod/strobe option was available and not used.
- **First question:** "Was a tripod or additional light source available on this setup, and why wasn't it used?"
- **Data to pull:** EXIF data (ISO, shutter, aperture) against a note on the available light source for that setup.

### Full retouching begins before the client has approved the frame selection
- **Usually means:** post-production time is being spent on frames that may not survive the client's selection round, which is wasted labor if the selection changes.
- **First question:** "Has the client signed off on the select, or is this a guess at what they'll choose?"
- **Data to pull:** timestamp of client selection approval against the timestamp full retouching began.

### Color grade shifts visibly across a single delivered set or story
- **Usually means:** each image was graded independently rather than synced from one reference frame, so skin tones or white balance drift image to image.
- **First question:** "Was a single reference frame used to sync the grade across the whole set?"
- **Data to pull:** side-by-side skin-tone or gray-card sampling across the delivered set.

### Usage fee quoted verbally before scope was confirmed in writing
- **Usually means:** the number given was a rough anchor, not a priced quote — and the client may treat the verbal number as final regardless of what usage scope actually gets agreed.
- **First question:** "Was that number scoped to a specific medium/duration/geography, or was it a placeholder?"
- **Data to pull:** any written follow-up (email, contract draft) confirming the scope the verbal number was based on.
