# Red flags

### Sharp, joint-specific pain present at the start of warm-up that hasn't eased after 10–15 minutes
- **Usually means:** An active injury (ligament, tendon, or joint) rather than ordinary muscular fatigue.
- **First question:** "Point to exactly where, and describe it — sharp or dull, and does it change with movement or stay constant?"
- **Data to pull:** PT triage note from that day; compare against the dancer's own pain log for the past two weeks.

### Week-over-week rehearsal load increase above ~30%
- **Usually means:** A return-to-dance ramp compressed too aggressively, or a new piece added on top of existing rep without dropping anything else.
- **First question:** "What got added or intensified this week, and what would need to come out to bring the increase back under 30%?"
- **Data to pull:** Session-load log (minutes × RPE) for the current and prior three weeks.

### Visible knee valgus or ankle rolling ("sickling") during grand plié or développé
- **Usually means:** Turnout being manufactured at the knee or ankle instead of the hip — the front-line-of-the-leg is being forced past the dancer's actual hip external rotation range.
- **First question:** "Where does the rotation stop if you find neutral pelvis first and don't force the foot line?"
- **Data to pull:** Video of the movement from front and side; hip external rotation range from the last PT or physio assessment.

### Missed or irregular menstrual cycles alongside a recent drop in body weight
- **Usually means:** Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S) — energy intake not covering training demand, with downstream effects on bone density and injury risk, not a body-type or aesthetic issue.
- **First question:** "Has this been raised with the company physician, and when was the last bone-density or bloodwork check?"
- **Data to pull:** Physician referral status; recent weight and training-load trend together, not either alone.

### Same-site strain recurring across two or more seasons
- **Usually means:** An underlying alignment or strength deficit that got treated as a one-off injury each time instead of a recurring pattern needing a standing conditioning fix.
- **First question:** "What changed in conditioning after the last occurrence, and did it actually address this joint?"
- **Data to pull:** PT injury history file across seasons, not just the current incident report.

### Marking every rehearsal of a role right up through the week before tech
- **Usually means:** The full-out version of the role has never actually been rehearsed at performance intensity — tech week becomes the first real test.
- **First question:** "When was the last time this was run full-out, start to finish?"
- **Data to pull:** Rehearsal log noting marked vs. full-out designation per run-through.

### A dancer consistently not called back for roles they're technically qualified for
- **Usually means:** A stylistic fit issue, an injury-history concern from the rehearsal director's side, or a casting-politics factor — rarely a pure technique gap at that level.
- **First question:** "Has direct feedback been requested and received from the artistic director on this specifically?"
- **Data to pull:** Audition or casting notes if the company keeps them; pattern across the last 3–4 casting cycles, not one instance.

### A partner reporting inconsistent counts or timing going into a lift
- **Usually means:** Under-rehearsed partnering communication, not a strength or trust problem — the two dancers have different internal counts for the same moment.
- **First question:** "Have you counted this out loud together outside of full-tempo run-throughs?"
- **Data to pull:** Whether a dedicated partnering rehearsal (not folded into a full run) has happened at all.

### Uneven pointe shoe wear pattern concentrated on one side or one part of the shoe
- **Usually means:** An alignment or weight-distribution asymmetry being compensated for through the shoe rather than corrected in technique.
- **First question:** "Is this new, or has it always worn this way?"
- **Data to pull:** Photos of shoe wear over successive pairs; any known leg-length or strength asymmetry on file with PT.

### A dancer reporting fatigue, low mood, and declining performance quality despite unchanged or reduced rehearsal hours
- **Usually means:** Overtraining syndrome or under-recovery (sleep, nutrition) rather than a motivation or attitude issue — the load number alone doesn't capture recovery debt.
- **First question:** "How has sleep and eating actually been this week, separate from the schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** Session-load trend alongside self-reported sleep and any recent illness.
