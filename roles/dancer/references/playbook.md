# Dancer playbook

Filled operational templates for the situations a dancer manages most often: audition structure, rehearsal-week load planning, return-to-dance ramps, and injury escalation.

## Audition day structure (professional company open call)

| Segment | Typical length | What happens | What's being watched |
|---|---|---|---|
| Check-in / number assignment | 15–30 min | Headshot + résumé collected, dancer pinned with a number | Punctuality, presentation basics |
| Barre or center warm-up | 15–20 min | Company-led warm-up, sometimes skipped for pre-warmed candidates | Alignment at rest, before adrenaline masks it |
| Combination 1 (center) | 20–30 min | Taught in groups of 8–12, run 2–3 times, danced in small groups | Musicality, speed of pickup, corrections retained on the second run |
| **First cut** | — | Numbers called or not called | — |
| Combination 2 (allegro / jumps) | 20–30 min | Faster, more athletically demanding combination | Stamina, elevation, whether technique holds under fatigue |
| **Second cut** | — | Field narrows further | — |
| Pointe work / partnering (if applicable) | 15–30 min | Adagio or partnering combination with provided or self-selected partner | Line under sustained balance, partnering communication |
| Callback / individual attention | Varies | Choreographer or AD gives individual notes, may ask questions | Coachability — how a single correction changes the next run |

**Fallback positions in preference order if not cast in the main company:** ask about second company / apprentice program openings → ask to be kept on file for mid-season injury replacements → request specific feedback on what would change the outcome next cycle. Never leave without asking at least one of the three — silence reads as disinterest, not humility.

## Rehearsal-week load plan (steady state, no injury)

Session load unit: rehearsal/class minutes × perceived exertion (0–10) = AU (arbitrary units), summed per day.

| Day | Company class | Rehearsal (marked) | Rehearsal (full-out) | Daily AU (approx.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | 90 min @ RPE 6 (540) | 60 min @ RPE 4 (240) | — | 780 |
| Tue | 90 min @ RPE 6 (540) | 30 min @ RPE 4 (120) | 60 min @ RPE 7 (420) | 1,080 |
| Wed | 90 min @ RPE 5 (450) | 90 min @ RPE 5 (450) | — | 900 |
| Thu | 90 min @ RPE 6 (540) | 30 min @ RPE 4 (120) | 60 min @ RPE 7 (420) | 1,080 |
| Fri | 90 min @ RPE 6 (540) | 60 min @ RPE 5 (300) | — | 840 |

Weekly total ≈ 4,680 AU → weekly average ≈ 825–940 AU/day-equivalent depending on how the week is normalized; this is the reference steady state a company physical therapist compares future weeks against, not an absolute target every dancer must hit identically.

## Return-to-dance ramp (post-injury, medically cleared)

Default ramp: cap week-over-week increase at 20–30% of the prior week's actual load, expressed as % of the dancer's own pre-injury baseline (see worked example in SKILL.md for a full reconciled case).

| Week | Target % of pre-injury baseline | Rehearsal content allowed | Partnering |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | ~50% | Company class, marked run-throughs only | None |
| 2 | ~65% | Full-out marking, no jump combinations at full height | Non-overhead lifts only |
| 3 | ~80–85% | Full rep in rehearsal | Full, if pain-free at 65% stage |
| 4 | 100% | Full rep including performance | Full |

**If the ramp requires reaching 100% inside 2 weeks instead of 4** (opening night constraint): hold the same per-week percentage caps and instead split performance load with a second cast for the first performance week — do not compress the ramp itself.

## Injury reporting escalation (in preference order)

1. **Self-manage** — RICE (rest, ice, compression, elevation) for a known, low-grade, muscular ache that resolves within the same rehearsal day.
2. **Same-day PT triage** — any pain that is joint-specific, sharp, or present at the start of warm-up and not resolving after 10–15 minutes.
3. **Physician referral** — any PT-triaged injury not improving within the PT's own expected timeline, or any suspected fracture, tear, or RED-S indicator (missed cycles, unexplained fatigue, stress-fracture history).
4. **Season-scope conversation with rehearsal director** — any injury requiring more than 2 weeks of modified duty; this is a casting-planning conversation, not just a medical one, and should happen before the two weeks elapse, not after.

## Cross-training allocation (weekly)

| Category | % of total weekly training time | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Company class + rehearsal | 70–80% | As above |
| Supplemental conditioning | 20–30% | Pilates (proprioception, deep stabilizers), targeted strength (hip external rotators, single-leg control), low-impact cardio for company members in jump-heavy rep |

Exceeding ~30% supplemental volume in a normal (non-rehab) week is a sign the plan is competing with rehearsal recovery rather than supporting it — trim conditioning volume before touching rehearsal time.
