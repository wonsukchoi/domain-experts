# Physical Therapist Assistant — Playbook

## Progression-within-parameters log (filled example)

Plan of care parameter: seated leg extension, progress 2-5 lb increments contingent on 3x10 reps completed at current resistance with pain ≤3/10, ceiling 15 lb before mandatory PT re-evaluation.

| Session | Resistance (lb) | Reps completed | Pain during set (0-10) | Progression decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | 10 | 3x10 | 2 | Hold | First session at this resistance; plan requires 3x10 tolerated before progressing |
| 8 | 10 | 3x10 | 2 | Hold, confirm | Second consecutive 3x10 at low pain — progression criteria met for next session |
| 9 | 10 → 13 | 3x10 | 2 | Progress +3 lb (mid-range step) | Criteria met at session 8; chose mid-range increment, not max, to leave one data point before ceiling |
| 10 | 13 | 2x10, 1x7 | 4 | Hold at 13 | Did not complete full 3x10; pain exceeded 3/10 on final set — plan's regression trigger, not a step back, just hold |
| 11 | 13 | 3x10 | 2 | Progress +2 lb → 15 (ceiling) | Criteria re-met; this step reaches plan ceiling, triggers mandatory PT re-evaluation before next session |

Rule embedded in the table: reaching the plan's stated ceiling is not itself a decision the PTA makes silently — it's the trigger that hands the case back to the PT, by design.

## Outcome-measure re-test consistency checklist

Before administering a scheduled re-test, confirm the conditions match the baseline as closely as practical:

- [ ] Same instrument/version as baseline (not a shorter or updated form)
- [ ] Same time relative to exercise (both pre-exercise, or both post — not one of each)
- [ ] Same time of day if the condition has known diurnal variation (e.g., morning stiffness in arthritic conditions)
- [ ] Patient not acutely fatigued from an unrelated activity immediately before testing
- [ ] Same scoring method applied (e.g., same denominator-exclusion rule if a section is marked not applicable)

If any box can't be checked, note the deviation in the report to the PT rather than presenting the score as directly comparable to baseline — an uncontrolled condition difference can manufacture or mask real change.

## Escalation-routing template (filled example)

```
To: [Supervising PT]
Re: [Patient initials/ID] — finding outside current plan of care

Date/session: Session 9, [date]
Current plan of care: Post-TKA rehab, week 3, quad strengthening progression

Planned session: Completed as scheduled — resistance progressed 10→13 lb per
plan parameters (2-5 lb increment range), 3x10 completed, pain 2/10, no
increase in swelling.

New finding (outside current plan scope): Patient reports intermittent
tingling, surgical-side foot, onset since last visit (session 8). Not
present at initial evaluation or documented in prior session notes.

PTA action taken: Completed today's planned exercise session as above.
Did not modify plan of care. Flagging for your review before next
scheduled visit — requesting guidance on whether this changes today's
plan for the remaining sessions this week.

Next scheduled session: [date]
```

The template's structure — planned-session-report first, new-finding-and-request second — keeps the routine documentation separate from the item that needs a PT decision, so the escalation doesn't get lost in a longer note.
