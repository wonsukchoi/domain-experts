# Physical Therapist Assistant — Red Flags

### New or changed pain in a location the current plan of care doesn't address
- **Usually means:** A new, unrelated issue (early signs of a different musculoskeletal problem, a medical complication) or a compensation pattern from favoring the treated area — either way, outside the PTA's scope to evaluate.
- **First question:** Is this pain in the area the plan of care targets, or somewhere new?
- **Data to pull:** Location, onset timing relative to last visit, whether it's present at rest or only during specific movements.

### Sensory change (numbness, tingling) not present at evaluation
- **Usually means:** Could be benign (post-surgical swelling pressing on a nerve, positioning during the session) or a sign of nerve compromise requiring prompt medical attention — the distinction is a PT/physician call, not a PTA one.
- **First question:** When did this start, and has it changed since it started?
- **Data to pull:** Exact location, whether it's constant or intermittent, any recent change in swelling or bracing at that site.

### Patient reports a fall or significant new trauma since the last visit
- **Usually means:** May have introduced a new injury on top of the condition being treated, or signals a fall-risk factor the plan of care needs to account for.
- **First question:** Where did they fall and did they hit or twist the area being treated?
- **Data to pull:** Date/mechanism of fall, any new pain or swelling since, whether they sought medical evaluation.

### Missed 2+ consecutive session goals despite no reported barrier
- **Usually means:** The plan's parameters may no longer fit the patient (too aggressive, wrong exercise selection) or an unreported issue (pain the patient isn't volunteering, adherence problem at home) is interfering.
- **First question:** Is anything making this exercise harder than it was two sessions ago?
- **Data to pull:** Session-by-session performance log, any home-program adherence gaps, pain trend across the missed sessions.

### Medication change since the last visit
- **Usually means:** A new or discontinued medication (especially pain medication, blood thinners, or anything affecting balance/alertness) can change what's safe to do in today's session.
- **First question:** What changed, and when did the change start?
- **Data to pull:** Medication name and change type (new/stopped/dose change), date of change, any new symptoms since.

### Patient or caregiver requests a change to the plan of care
- **Usually means:** A reasonable ask that is nonetheless outside PTA authority to grant on the spot — the plan was set by the PT and modifying it requires the PT's involvement.
- **First question:** What in today's plan isn't working for you?
- **Data to pull:** The specific request, the reason behind it, whether it's a one-time accommodation or an ongoing concern.

### Reaching the plan of care's stated progression ceiling
- **Usually means:** The patient has reached the upper bound the PT anticipated at evaluation — by design, this is a checkpoint, not a plateau or a failure.
- **First question:** Has the PT's re-evaluation been scheduled, or does this trigger one now?
- **Data to pull:** The plan's stated ceiling and re-evaluation trigger language, date of last PT evaluation.

### Two consecutive sessions of no-difficulty performance at the plan's minimum-progression rate
- **Usually means:** The plan's parameters may be under-dosed for this patient's actual capacity — worth flagging even though it's the opposite problem from missed goals.
- **First question:** Is the patient completing this with no effort, or with appropriate effort at an easy resistance?
- **Data to pull:** Reported exertion level, whether the patient volunteers that it feels "too easy."

### Supervision-level uncertainty (facility policy vs. state practice act conflict)
- **Usually means:** A facility requiring more oversight than the state minimum is enforceable; treating the state minimum as sufficient when the facility requires more is not.
- **First question:** What does this facility's policy require, separate from what the state allows?
- **Data to pull:** Facility supervision policy document, state practice act supervision-level requirement for this setting.
