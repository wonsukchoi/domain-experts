# Red Flags

### Aide asked more than once in a week to "just start" a task that isn't on their signed delegation list for that patient
- **Usually means:** schedule overload pushing scope creep onto the aide as an informal fix; second most likely, staff genuinely unaware the task was never delegated for this specific patient.
- **First question:** what does the signed delegation checklist say for this patient, and who signed it?
- **Data to pull:** delegation/competency checklist, the day's schedule/staffing ratio, front-desk or clinician request pattern over the prior 2–4 weeks.

### A visit note is signed by the OT/OTA describing a task the aide actually performed unsupervised
- **Usually means:** billing/compliance risk — an aide-performed service documented and billed as though the clinician delivered or directly supervised it in real time.
- **First question:** was the clinician physically present or immediately reachable per facility policy at the time the task happened?
- **Data to pull:** supervision log, clinician's schedule/sign-in for that time slot, timestamp on the note versus the clinician's other patient assignments.

### Manual transfer attempted solo when the patient's dependent weight exceeds ~35 lb, or the patient is combative/unpredictable
- **Usually means:** time pressure, or the mechanical lift wasn't available/charged near this bay.
- **First question:** was the powered lift accessible and charged, and was a second staff member paged before the transfer started?
- **Data to pull:** incident/near-miss log, lift-equipment maintenance and charge log, staffing roster at the time of the transfer.

### Shared modality equipment (hydrocollator packs, paraffin, gait belts, mats) turned over without meeting the posted dwell time
- **Usually means:** patient volume outpacing the turnover window built into the schedule.
- **First question:** what's the actual time gap between patients on this equipment today versus the required dwell time?
- **Data to pull:** cleaning/dwell-time log, same-day scheduling density for that equipment.

### Aide notes written in clinical or interpretive language ("ROM significantly limited," "patient appears deconditioned," "not tolerating treatment")
- **Usually means:** the aide was trained on clinician shorthand rather than the facility's aide-note format, or is drifting into clinical narrative under habit.
- **First question:** who trained this aide on documentation, and does the facility have a separate aide-note template?
- **Data to pull:** sample aide notes against the facility's aide-note template; training/onboarding record.

### Patient reports pain or distress during an aide-run activity and the activity continues to completion
- **Usually means:** the aide is unclear on the plan's stated precautions, or is prioritizing finishing the set over stopping.
- **First question:** what does the plan of care's precaution section say about this specific activity and this patient?
- **Data to pull:** plan of care precautions/contraindications section, incident report if one was filed.

### The same aide appears in multiple denied or recouped claims within one billing cycle
- **Usually means:** a systemic scope-creep pattern in how tasks are being delegated or documented, not an isolated slip.
- **First question:** what specific tasks and patients are common across the denied claims?
- **Data to pull:** payer denial/recoupment letters, delegation logs for the flagged patients, note timestamps versus clinician schedules.

### New aide performing a client-related task with no signed competency check-off on file
- **Usually means:** onboarding shortcut taken under staffing pressure.
- **First question:** has this exact task been observed and signed off by an OT or OTA for this aide?
- **Data to pull:** competency checklist file, onboarding training record.

### Critical supply (PPE, single-use treatment items, launderable gait belts) repeatedly at or below par level
- **Usually means:** reorder par level set too low for current patient volume, or supplier lead time exceeds the reorder trigger point.
- **First question:** what's the current par level, lead time, and how does it compare to actual weekly usage?
- **Data to pull:** inventory log, supplier lead-time record, weekly usage trend.

### Patient or family member asks the aide a clinical question (prognosis, discharge timing, "is this working") and gets a substantive answer instead of a redirect
- **Usually means:** aide trying to be helpful/reassuring without recognizing the answer belongs to the clinician.
- **First question:** was the question redirected to the supervising clinician, and if not, what was actually said?
- **Data to pull:** none formal — surface it in a documentation/communication review with the aide directly.

### Supervising OT/OTA unexpectedly unavailable and a delegated task proceeds anyway
- **Usually means:** the facility's supervision model (direct on-site vs. general/indirect) wasn't actually checked before proceeding.
- **First question:** what supervision level does this specific delegation require, and was it in place at the moment the task happened?
- **Data to pull:** facility supervision policy, clinician schedule/whereabouts log for that time slot.
