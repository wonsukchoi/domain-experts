# Playbook

Filled examples an aide can execute or populate directly — not descriptions of what a good version would contain.

## 2. Patient transport / transfer decision tree

1. **Check the plan's weight-bearing and assist-level fields first** (e.g., WBAT, PWB 50%, NWB, TTWB, min/mod/max assist, 1-person/2-person). Never infer assist level from how the patient looks today.
2. **Estimate dependent weight if a manual lift is being considered.** If the portion of body weight you'd actually be supporting exceeds roughly 35 lb, or the patient can't reliably follow instructions/cooperate, do not attempt a solo manual lift — get the mechanical lift or a second staff member. This is a hard stop, not a judgment call to make under time pressure.
3. **Gait belt for any ambulatory transfer or transport requiring standby-to-moderate assist**, applied per training — snug, over clothing, buckle checked before weight-bearing begins.
4. **Two-person or mechanical assist for any transfer flagged max-assist, dependent, bariatric, or combative/unpredictable**, no exceptions for "just this once, we're behind."
5. **If anything changes mid-transfer** (patient reports pain, knees buckle, patient becomes confused) — stop, lower to the nearest safe surface, do not try to complete the planned transfer, and notify the supervising clinician immediately.
6. **Log the transfer method actually used**, not the plan's default, if they diverged (e.g., plan said 1-person/gait belt but you used 2-person because the patient seemed unsteady that day) — the deviation itself is useful data for the clinician's next reassessment.

## 3. Shared-equipment cleaning log (dwell times, filled example)

| Equipment | Cleaning agent | Contact/dwell time | Frequency | Logged by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hydrocollator pack cover | EPA-registered intermediate-level disinfectant wipe | 3 min wet contact | Between every patient | Initial + time |
| Mat table surface | Facility-approved surface disinfectant | 2 min wet contact | Between every patient | Initial + time |
| Gait belt (shared stock) | Machine wash, or wipe-down if non-launderable | Per manufacturer label | Between patients / daily minimum | Initial + time |
| Paraffin unit rim/handles | Disinfectant wipe (not submerged) | 2 min wet contact | Between every patient | Initial + time |
| Wheelchair arms/wheels used for transport | Facility-approved surface disinfectant | 2 min wet contact | After each transport | Initial + time |

**Rule:** "looks clean" is not the same as dwell time elapsed. If patient volume means the dwell time can't be met before the next patient needs the equipment, that's a staffing/scheduling problem to flag up, not a reason to shorten the wipe-down.

## 4. Escalation script (when a delegated task hits something outside its parameters)

Use this structure verbally or as a chart flag, in order: **what you were doing → what happened, in the patient's words/observable facts → what you did in response (stopped, held, repositioned) → what you need from the clinician.**

Example: "I was applying the hot pack per Mr. G's protocol, 5 minutes in. He said his shoulder felt 'sharper than usual' and asked me to stop. I removed the pack immediately and had him rest sitting up. He hasn't had that complaint on this protocol before — can you take a look before we continue with the OTA session?"
