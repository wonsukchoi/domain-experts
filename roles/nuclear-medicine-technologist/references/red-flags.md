# Red flags

Smell tests a senior technologist catches before a study goes to reading, or before a patient walks out the door.

### Measured exposure rate at 1 m exceeds 7 mrem/hr at planned discharge for an I-131 therapy patient

- **Usually means:** administered activity or patient-specific clearance means the default-table release criterion isn't met — not automatically that the patient must be admitted.
- **First question:** "What's the effective half-life for this patient's clinical picture — ablation with no remnant, or an intact gland — and has the case-by-case calculation been run?"
- **Data to pull:** measured dose rate at 1 m, indication (ablation vs. Graves'), household occupancy factors, prior case-by-case calculations for similar patients.

### Dose-calibrator daily constancy check off by more than 10% from the expected decay-corrected value

- **Usually means:** calibrator drift, wrong isotope setting/energy calibration selected, or a reference-source handling error (contamination, wrong geometry).
- **First question:** "Has this been repeated with the same source, and with a second reference source?"
- **Data to pull:** constancy log trend over the last 30 days, last accuracy/linearity check dates, any recent service or relocation of the instrument.

### Injection-site probe shows counts materially above background after a Tc-99m myocardial, renal, or lymphoscintigraphy dose

- **Usually means:** extravasation or partial infiltration, not a genuine perfusion or clearance abnormality.
- **First question:** "Is the injection site in the field of view on every acquisition phase, and how does the residual count compare to a normal injection?"
- **Data to pull:** bilateral probe time-activity data, visual injection-site image, technologist's severity note on the worksheet.

### Blood glucose above 200 mg/dL at FDG PET check-in

- **Usually means:** uncontrolled diabetes or a missed/incorrect fasting instruction; will degrade tumor-to-background contrast regardless of how "close" the number looks.
- **First question:** "Can this be corrected and re-checked today, or does it need to be rescheduled?"
- **Data to pull:** current glucose reading, time since last meal, insulin/oral agent timing, department's correction protocol threshold.

### Gamma camera fails daily flood-field uniformity

- **Usually means:** collimator damage, PMT drift, or a genuine detector fault — rarely a one-off fluke.
- **First question:** "Has the test been repeated with a fresh flood source before assuming the camera itself failed?"
- **Data to pull:** uniformity integral/differential values against manufacturer spec, trend over the last 2 weeks, date of last service.

### Free pertechnetate or unbound radiotracer visible on labeled-cell or bone-agent images (thyroid, stomach, salivary gland uptake where it shouldn't be)

- **Usually means:** poor radiolabeling efficiency — reducing agent oxidized, kit past its use window, or technique error during preparation.
- **First question:** "What was the labeling efficiency on the QC strip for this kit, and when was it prepared relative to injection?"
- **Data to pull:** kit lot number and expiration, radiochemical purity QC result, time from preparation to injection.

### Discordant thyroid uptake between the 4-hour and 24-hour measurements (4-hour higher than 24-hour)

- **Usually means:** subacute thyroiditis, iodine contamination, or a rapid-turnover hyperthyroid state rather than classic Graves' disease — changes the treatment dose calculation.
- **First question:** "Is there a recent iodinated contrast study or dietary iodine load that could explain this?"
- **Data to pull:** both uptake values, recent contrast/imaging history, TSH/thyroid antibody results if available.

### Gallbladder nonvisualization by 60 minutes on a HIDA scan, no morphine given

- **Usually means:** cystic duct obstruction (acute cholecystitis) — but chronic cholecystitis, prolonged fasting, or TPN can also delay or block visualization.
- **First question:** "Should morphine augmentation be given per protocol before calling this obstructive, and has fasting/TPN status been checked?"
- **Data to pull:** fasting duration, TPN status, morphine-augmentation protocol threshold (typically administered if no gallbladder activity by 60 min), delayed imaging results if obtained.

### Administered activity or absorbed dose differs from the prescribed value by more than 20%, with an effective dose difference exceeding 0.05 Sv (5 rem)

- **Usually means:** a potential NRC/Agreement State reportable medical event (10 CFR 35.3045) — not a routine dosing variance.
- **First question:** "Has this been reported to the RSO immediately, before anything else happens with this patient?"
- **Data to pull:** ordered vs. measured (calibrator) activity, calculated effective dose difference, timestamp of discovery relative to administration.

### Persistent high count rate at the injection site during the flow phase of a dynamic renal or first-pass cardiac study

- **Usually means:** infiltration corrupting the time-activity curve used for quantitative results (differential renal function, ejection fraction).
- **First question:** "Does the flow curve look like a clean bolus, or is there a shoulder/tail consistent with a slow leak at the site?"
- **Data to pull:** raw time-activity curve from the injection site region of interest, comparison to a normal bolus curve shape, technologist's real-time observation note.

### PET/SPECT patient weight or BMI far outside the department's standard dose/acquisition-time chart

- **Usually means:** the standard weight-based dose or scan time will under- or over-shoot image quality for this patient specifically.
- **First question:** "Does this patient fall outside the dose chart's tested range, and does the protocol have a documented ceiling or extended-time adjustment for this?"
- **Data to pull:** patient weight/BMI, department's dose chart range and ceiling activity, prior comparable-weight studies' image quality notes.
