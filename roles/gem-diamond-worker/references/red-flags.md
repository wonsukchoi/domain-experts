# Red flags

### A cutting plan finalized before completing inclusion mapping
- **Usually means:** an inclusion could end up in a visible, value-reducing location that a pre-cut plan would have avoided.
- **First question:** has the rough stone been fully mapped for inclusions (location, size, type) before the cutting plan was set?
- **Data to pull:** inclusion mapping record, the finalized cutting plan.

### A cutting plan optimized purely for maximum carat weight without considering the specific inclusion pattern
- **Usually means:** the resulting stone may retain more visible flaws than necessary, reducing per-carat value despite higher weight.
- **First question:** was a deliberate yield-vs-clarity trade-off evaluated, or was maximum yield assumed as the default goal?
- **Data to pull:** the cutting plan's stated objective, inclusion locations relative to the planned orientation.

### Cutting begun without identifying the stone's crystal structure/grain direction
- **Usually means:** risk of fracture or a significantly more difficult cut if cutting against the grain.
- **First question:** has crystal structure been identified and factored into the cutting plan?
- **Data to pull:** crystal structure identification, planned cutting direction relative to grain.

### A clarity or color grade assigned based on general impression rather than standardized grading criteria
- **Usually means:** the grade may not reflect an objective, consistent standard, risking financial/reputational consequences from misgrading.
- **First question:** was the grading assessment performed against the specific standardized scale's criteria, or based on subjective impression?
- **Data to pull:** the grading methodology used, comparison to standard reference criteria/samples.

### A cutting plan finalized without the stone owner/client being informed of the yield-vs-clarity trade-off before an irreversible cut
- **Usually means:** the client may not have had the opportunity to weigh in on a decision with real, permanent financial consequences.
- **First question:** was the trade-off communicated and approved before cutting began, or was the decision made unilaterally?
- **Data to pull:** client communication record regarding the cutting plan.

### A finished stone's clarity grade significantly lower than what the rough material's characteristics suggested was achievable
- **Usually means:** the cutting plan may not have adequately accounted for inclusion location, resulting in an avoidable clarity reduction.
- **First question:** does the inclusion's final position in the cut trace back to a planning decision that could have positioned it more favorably?
- **Data to pull:** the original inclusion map, the finished stone's actual inclusion position and grade.

### A cut proceeding without re-verifying the plan after an unexpected finding during initial cutting (an unmapped inclusion, unexpected crystal structure)
- **Usually means:** the original plan may no longer be optimal given new information, but cutting continued without reassessment.
- **First question:** was the plan re-evaluated after the unexpected finding, or did cutting continue on the original plan regardless?
- **Data to pull:** the unexpected finding, whether the plan was adjusted in response.

### A multi-stage cutting process handed off between workers without documenting the established crystal structure orientation and inclusion map
- **Usually means:** the incoming worker may not have the information needed to continue the plan consistently.
- **First question:** does the handoff include the crystal structure orientation and inclusion map established so far?
- **Data to pull:** the handoff record, cutting plan documentation.

### A stone graded for sale without independent verification of the initial grading assessment
- **Usually means:** a single grader's assessment, without cross-check, carries more risk of error given the financial stakes involved.
- **First question:** does this stone's value/context warrant an independent verification of the grading, or was a single assessment considered sufficient?
- **Data to pull:** the stone's value tier, verification practice used.
