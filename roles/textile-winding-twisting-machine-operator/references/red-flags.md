# Red flags

### Twist direction on a singles yarn package trusted from its label without physical verification
- **Usually means:** a labeling or receiving error could go undetected until the ply fails a twist-liveliness check or fabric shows torque/puckering issues.
- **First question:** has twist direction been physically verified on this specific package, or assumed from its label?
- **Data to pull:** physical twist direction check result, package label.

### A plied yarn showing untwisting, kinking, or snarling when tension is released
- **Usually means:** an incorrect twist direction combination between the singles and the ply twist.
- **First question:** were the singles' actual twist directions and the ply twist direction verified as the correct combination before plying?
- **Data to pull:** twist direction verification records for both singles and ply twist, twist-liveliness test result.

### Tension checked on each parallel end individually against a general range, without comparing ends to each other
- **Usually means:** relative inconsistency between ends could exist even when each end's individual tension is technically within range.
- **First question:** has tension been compared across all parallel ends relative to each other, not just each against a standalone range?
- **Data to pull:** tension readings for all parallel ends, relative variance between them.

### A yarn package showing inconsistent density or an irregular traverse pattern
- **Usually means:** downstream unwinding at the next process stage may tangle, snag, or break.
- **First question:** does the package build show consistent density and pattern throughout, or noticeable variation?
- **Data to pull:** visual/physical inspection of package build, winding machine settings used.

### Draw ratio confirmed only as an average value without checking for variation along the strand's length
- **Usually means:** localized fluctuation (slubs, thin spots) could be present even with a correct overall average.
- **First question:** has yarn diameter/evenness been tested along the strand's length, or only the average draw ratio setting confirmed?
- **Data to pull:** evenness tester data if available, draw ratio setting and any tension/speed fluctuation log.

### A fabric-stage quality issue (puckering, torque, uneven appearance) with no investigation into the source yarn lot
- **Usually means:** a yarn-stage defect (twist mismatch, tension inconsistency, draw ratio variation) may be the actual root cause, appearing downstream as a fabric defect.
- **First question:** does the fabric defect pattern match a known yarn-stage defect signature, and can it be traced to a specific yarn lot?
- **Data to pull:** yarn lot traceability for the affected fabric, yarn lot's quality record if available.

### A yarn lot released to fabric production without documented twist, tension, and draw ratio verification
- **Usually means:** quality assurance relied on visual inspection alone rather than the specific verifications relevant to yarn-stage defects.
- **First question:** does the lot's quality record show twist direction verification, tension uniformity check, and draw ratio consistency data?
- **Data to pull:** the lot's quality record.

### Repeated tangling or breaks at the fabric-production stage traced to yarn from a specific winding/twisting machine
- **Usually means:** a systematic package build or process issue on that specific machine, rather than isolated yarn variation.
- **First question:** does the tangling/break pattern correlate with yarn from a specific machine or operator?
- **Data to pull:** yarn source traceability, machine-specific quality history.

### A new fiber type or yarn specification run without re-verifying twist/tension/draw ratio settings from a prior, different specification
- **Usually means:** settings validated for a different yarn type may not be appropriate for the new one.
- **First question:** were process settings re-verified for this specific new yarn specification, or carried over from a prior job?
- **Data to pull:** current yarn specification, process settings actually used.

### A minor yarn variation flagged and rejected despite falling within the specified normal tolerance
- **Usually means:** over-scrutiny may be rejecting acceptable yarn without a genuine quality basis.
- **First question:** does the flagged variation actually exceed the specified tolerance, or is it within normal acceptable range?
- **Data to pull:** the specific measured variation, the specification's stated tolerance.
