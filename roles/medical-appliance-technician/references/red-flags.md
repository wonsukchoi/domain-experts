# Red flags

### Positive model rectified with uniform wall clearance/thickness rather than a zone-specific pressure map
- **Usually means:** pain or pressure at bony prominences and/or inadequate load transfer at intended weight-bearing zones.
- **First question:** does the rectification plan specify different treatment for pressure-sensitive versus pressure-tolerant zones, or is it uniform?
- **Data to pull:** the rectification spec compared against the patient's documented anatomical pressure map.

### Device assembled and delivered based on bench alignment alone, with no dynamic (weight-bearing/gait) verification performed
- **Usually means:** gait-related issues — instability, abnormal wear pattern, discomfort during actual walking — go undetected until the patient reports problems later.
- **First question:** does the fitting record show a dynamic alignment observation and any resulting adjustment from bench alignment?
- **Data to pull:** the fitting record's dynamic alignment documentation.

### Material or rigidity grade selected without reference to the patient's documented weight or activity level
- **Usually means:** risk of premature device failure under real-world load that a lighter or less active patient's device wouldn't experience.
- **First question:** what material/rigidity spec was actually used versus what the patient's documented weight and activity level call for?
- **Data to pull:** the material/rigidity spec used compared against the patient's documented weight and activity level.

### A patient reports localized pain/pressure at a specific point, and the response is a generic uniform adjustment rather than a targeted check against the pressure map at that location
- **Usually means:** the actual cause — a rectification error at that specific zone — goes unaddressed while an unrelated adjustment is made.
- **First question:** what does the rectification map show specifically for the location the patient is reporting?
- **Data to pull:** the patient-reported pain location compared against the rectification map for that zone.

### A specific device type or patient population shows a recurring pattern of rectification-related complaints or remakes
- **Usually means:** a systemic rectification technique or model-capture issue, not isolated one-off patient variation.
- **First question:** does the complaint/remake log show a concentration by device type or technician?
- **Data to pull:** the complaint/remake log broken out by device type and technician over a defined period.

### Alignment angles used in assembly match "standard" bench alignment values with no documented adjustment for this specific patient's residual limb characteristics
- **Usually means:** the device may not account for this patient's specific biomechanics even if it matches a generic alignment reference.
- **First question:** what patient-specific dynamic adjustment, if any, was documented from the bench alignment starting point?
- **Data to pull:** bench alignment values used compared against any patient-specific dynamic adjustment documented.

### A device repair or adjustment request doesn't reference the original rectification map or alignment settings on file
- **Usually means:** the repair may not preserve the original fit-critical decisions, risking reintroducing a previously solved fit problem.
- **First question:** was the original fabrication documentation actually consulted before making this repair?
- **Data to pull:** the original fabrication documentation compared against what's referenced during the repair.
