# Playbook

## Rectification spec worksheet (filled example)

Transtibial socket, patient ID [XX].

| Anatomical landmark | Zone type | Rectification amount | Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fibular head | Pressure-sensitive | 4mm | Relief (plaster removed, socket wall stands further away) |
| Tibial crest | Pressure-sensitive | 3mm | Relief |
| Patellar tendon | Pressure-tolerant | 3mm | Build (plaster reduced, socket wall presses in more firmly) |
| Popliteal area | Pressure-tolerant (moderate) | 2mm | Build |
| Distal tibia/fibula | Pressure-sensitive | 5mm | Relief |

**Rule applied:** Every pressure-sensitive landmark gets relief; every pressure-tolerant, load-bearing zone gets build-up. No landmark is left at "zero" (uniform/unmodified) unless specifically documented as neutral for that patient's anatomy.

## Material/rigidity selection reference (filled example)

| Patient weight | Activity level | Recommended material/rigidity |
|---|---|---|
| Under 150 lb | Low (limited community ambulation) | Standard thermoplastic, moderate flexibility |
| 150–200 lb | Moderate (full community ambulation) | Laminate, standard rigidity |
| Over 200 lb | High (active, sports/heavy labor) | Reinforced laminate, high rigidity grade |
| Any weight | Very high impact activity (running, jumping) | Reinforced laminate with dynamic-response component matching |

**Applied check:** A 210 lb patient with a documented high activity level (return to manual labor) selected on standard thermoplastic (meant for a lighter, lower-activity patient) is a mismatch — flag for reselection to reinforced laminate before fabrication proceeds, not after a failure in the field.

## Bench-to-dynamic alignment adjustment log (filled example)

| Stage | Alignment setting | Observation | Adjustment made |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bench alignment | Standard reference angles (5° socket flexion, neutral ankle) | Static assembly, no patient loading | None — starting point |
| Dynamic alignment, initial walk | Same as bench | Lateral trunk lean during stance phase on prosthetic side | Socket adjusted 2° additional abduction |
| Dynamic alignment, re-check | 5° flexion, 2° added abduction | Lateral lean resolved, gait now symmetric | Final — documented for delivery record |

The bench setting alone would have been delivered with a visible gait abnormality; the dynamic re-check caught and corrected it before the patient left with the device.
