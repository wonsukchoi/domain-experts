# Red flags

### Measured printed dot at a control patch exceeds the plate's compensated target by more than a few percentage points
- **Usually means:** actual press dot gain differs from what was assumed at plate-making, not a general ink density problem.
- **First question:** what's the actual measured gain versus the plate's compensation curve?
- **Data to pull:** densitometer reading at control patches compared against the plate's dot percentage.

### Ink density adjusted mid-run to visually correct a color problem that's actually a dot-gain issue
- **Usually means:** the correction will flatten the tonal range rather than fix the specific midtone/gain problem.
- **First question:** do highlight and shadow control patches show the same shift as the midtone, or is the problem isolated to the midtone?
- **Data to pull:** density readings across highlight/midtone/shadow control patches before and after the adjustment.

### Registration measures off by more than the job's stated tolerance and is accepted as "close enough"
- **Usually means:** visible color fringing will likely appear in fine process-color work, even if it isn't obvious on the makeready proof alone.
- **First question:** what's the actual registration measurement versus the job's stated tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** registration measurement compared against the job's specified tolerance.

### Same press settings (ink, water, pressure) reused when a job moves from one substrate to a different one
- **Usually means:** dot gain and density will behave differently on the new substrate, producing an uncontrolled color shift.
- **First question:** what does each substrate's spec (coated/uncoated, absorbency) show compared to the settings actually used?
- **Data to pull:** substrate specification for both runs compared against the settings applied.

### Makeready proof approved once with no further density/registration spot-checks through the rest of the run
- **Usually means:** drift during the run — as the press reaches equilibrium or ink/water balance shifts — goes undetected until a large portion of the run is already off-spec.
- **First question:** what's the spot-check frequency actually being used versus this press's typical drift pattern?
- **Data to pull:** the spot-check log and frequency through the run.

### Visual color matching under press-room lighting is the sole method used to approve a run's color
- **Usually means:** color drift invisible under that specific lighting, or masked by operator fatigue over a long run, goes undetected.
- **First question:** what do density or spectrophotometer readings actually show for this run?
- **Data to pull:** density/spectro readings compared against the visual-only approval notes.

### Screen ruling (lpi) is mismatched to the press or plate's actual resolution capability for the job's required detail level
- **Usually means:** either wasted resolution the press can't hold, or a visible dot pattern in the finished print if the ruling is too coarse for the required detail.
- **First question:** does the press have a demonstrated track record of holding this lpi on this specific substrate?
- **Data to pull:** the job's specified lpi compared against the press's demonstrated capability for that substrate.
