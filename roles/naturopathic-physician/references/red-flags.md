# Naturopathic Physician — Red Flags

### Unintentional weight loss >10% of body weight over 6 months
- **Usually means:** occult malignancy or hyperthyroidism; second most likely is depression or an unrecognized eating disorder
- **First question:** "Has this been checked with imaging or labs already, and when?"
- **Data to pull:** CBC, CMP, TSH, and imaging history; conventional referral if none on file

### Rectal bleeding or black stool in a patient over 45, or any age with new anemia
- **Usually means:** colorectal pathology requiring endoscopic workup; second most likely is a benign source (hemorrhoid, fissure) that still needs to be confirmed, not assumed
- **First question:** "Has this been scoped, or is that the reason for today's visit?"
- **Data to pull:** CBC with ferritin, referral status to GI

### TSH >10 mIU/L, or a TSH trend that moved despite an unchanged levothyroxine dose
- **Usually means:** an absorption interaction (calcium, iron, coffee, high-fiber meal timing) or a missed dose pattern; second most likely is true progression of thyroid disease
- **First question:** "Has anything changed about how or when the medication is taken, including new supplements?"
- **Data to pull:** full medication/supplement timing log, repeat TSH after correcting any interaction

### Ferritin <15 ng/mL with concurrent GI symptoms
- **Usually means:** GI blood loss (ulcer, malignancy, inflammatory bowel disease); second most likely is dietary insufficiency alone
- **First question:** "Any GI symptoms, NSAID use, or family history of GI cancer?"
- **Data to pull:** stool occult blood, GI referral status, full iron panel

### INR outside therapeutic range in a patient on warfarin who started or stopped any botanical or supplement in the prior 2 weeks
- **Usually means:** a CYP-mediated or vitamin K-content interaction from the new/discontinued substance; second most likely is a dietary vitamin K change
- **First question:** "What exactly changed in the last two weeks — supplements, diet, or the medication itself?"
- **Data to pull:** current INR, full supplement list with start/stop dates, prescriber notification

### Postmenopausal vaginal bleeding
- **Usually means:** endometrial pathology requiring gynecologic workup; second most likely is atrophic bleeding, which still requires exclusion of the former
- **First question:** "Has this been evaluated by a gynecologist yet?"
- **Data to pull:** referral status; do not initiate a naturopathic hormonal-support plan first

### New palpable breast or testicular mass
- **Usually means:** requires same-visit referral for imaging/biopsy pathway regardless of naturopathic differential
- **First question:** "How long has it been present, and has it been imaged?"
- **Data to pull:** referral order placed same day, documented in chart

### Active suicidal ideation with a plan
- **Usually means:** acute psychiatric emergency, not a scope item for naturopathic management alone
- **First question:** "Do you have a plan, and access to means, right now?"
- **Data to pull:** crisis-line/ED referral documentation; do not proceed with the visit's original agenda until safety is addressed

### Fever in an infant under 3 months of age
- **Usually means:** requires emergency pediatric evaluation; sepsis risk in this age group is not stratifiable in an outpatient naturopathic visit
- **First question:** "Has this infant been seen in an ED or by a pediatrician today?"
- **Data to pull:** none — refer immediately, do not attempt naturopathic workup

### Persistent lymphadenopathy >2 cm lasting more than 4 weeks without a clear infectious cause
- **Usually means:** requires biopsy-pathway referral to rule out lymphoma or metastatic disease; second most likely is a resolved infection with slow node regression
- **First question:** "Is it still growing, and has an infectious cause already been ruled out?"
- **Data to pull:** referral status, prior CBC/infectious workup

### A patient requesting a "natural cancer treatment" in place of, rather than alongside, oncology care
- **Usually means:** the documented pattern behind the field's highest-profile adverse outcomes — delayed or foregone conventional treatment during a curable window
- **First question:** "Is this instead of your oncologist's plan, or in addition to it?"
- **Data to pull:** current oncology treatment plan and status; do not accept "instead of" framing without an explicit, documented discussion of the tradeoff

### A supplement request for a condition where the therapeutic order would normally place a pharmaceutical at step 5–6, when no natural agent has trial-level evidence for that specific indication
- **Usually means:** patient preference outrunning the evidence base, often driven by marketing rather than a naturopathic indication
- **First question:** "What specifically are you hoping this changes, and by when?"
- **Data to pull:** the substance's actual evidence grade in the interaction/evidence reference, not its marketing claim

### A state-license mismatch — a plan that requires ordering labs, prescribing, or performing a procedure the practitioner's actual state license doesn't grant
- **Usually means:** scope creep from training-based confidence rather than license-based authorization
- **First question:** "Does this specific state's naturopathic license actually include this action?"
- **Data to pull:** current state formulary/scope document, not memory of a prior state's rules
