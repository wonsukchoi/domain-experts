# Naturopathic Physician — Playbook

Filled templates for the intake-to-plan sequence. Populate with the case at hand; the structures below are the actual artifacts, not a description of what to include.

## 1. Intake red-flag screen (run before anything else)

| Domain | Screening question | If positive |
|---|---|---|
| Constitutional | Unintentional weight loss >10% body weight in 6 months? | Refer for conventional workup same visit |
| Cardiopulmonary | Chest pain, exertional dyspnea, syncope? | Refer/ED same visit, do not proceed with intake |
| GI | Rectal bleeding, black stool, or age >45 with new-onset anemia? | Refer for GI workup, do not treat anemia empirically first |
| Neuro | New headache pattern in a patient >50, or with fever/neck stiffness? | Refer/ED same visit |
| Gyn | Postmenopausal bleeding? | Refer for gynecologic workup, no naturopathic workup first |
| Psych | Active suicidal ideation with plan? | Refer/crisis line same visit, do not continue standard intake |

Only proceed to Step 2 once every row above is cleared "negative" or already has a documented conventional workup on file ruling it out.

## 2. Medication/supplement reconciliation and interaction check

Fill this table completely before proposing anything new.

| Substance (drug or supplement) | Dose/frequency | Prescriber/source | Checked against interaction reference? | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Levothyroxine | 100 mcg qAM | PCP | Y | Calcium/iron co-dose reduces absorption ~25–30% |
| Calcium carbonate | 600 mg qAM | Patient self-started | Y | Separate 4+ hrs from levothyroxine |
| Warfarin | 5 mg qday | Cardiologist | Y | Vitamin K–rich botanicals, fish oil >3g/day, and many herbs alter INR — recheck INR within 1–2 weeks of any new addition |
| St. John's Wort (proposed) | — | Patient request | Y | CYP3A4 induction — contraindicated with OCP, SSRI, warfarin, immunosuppressants; do not add |
| Kelp/iodine (proposed) | — | Patient request | Y | Contraindicated in confirmed Hashimoto's — do not add |

Rule: no new substance is proposed until its row is filled and flagged. An empty "Flag" cell is not the same as "no interaction" — it means "not yet checked."

## 3. Therapeutic order — case sequencing sheet

For each active problem, name the step being applied and why later steps aren't used yet.

| Step | Intervention type | Applied to this case? | Re-check interval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Establish conditions for health (diet, sleep, stress, movement) | Yes — sleep hygiene, iron-rich diet counseling | 4 weeks |
| 2 | Stimulate self-healing (vis medicatrix naturae) | Not primary lever here — deficiency-driven case | — |
| 3 | Support/restore weakened or damaged systems | Yes — iron repletion (ferrous bisglycinate 25 mg elemental, dosed 4+ hrs from levothyroxine) | 8 weeks (repeat ferritin) |
| 4 | Correct structural integrity | N/A this case | — |
| 5 | Specific natural substances for pathology | Declined — no natural substance indicated for absorption-interaction TSH rise | — |
| 6 | Specific pharmaceutical substances | Deferred to PCP — levothyroxine dose adjustment after recheck | 8 weeks |
| 7 | Suppress/control symptoms | Not needed — root causes addressed at steps 1/3 | — |

Skip only with a stated reason in the right-hand column; a blank row without a reason is a gap, not a decision.

## 4. Functional vs. reference range worksheet

| Marker | Lab reference range | Functional target used | Source for functional target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ferritin | 15–150 ng/mL (this lab) | <30 ng/mL = treat as deficient | Guyatt et al. 1992 (92% sens / 98% spec) |
| TSH | 0.4–4.5 mIU/L (this lab) | <3.0 mIU/L for this patient's prior symptom-free baseline | Patient-specific baseline, not a population claim |
| Iodine intake | RDA 150 mcg/day; adult UL 1,100 mcg/day | Avoid supplementation entirely in confirmed autoimmune thyroiditis | IOM Dietary Reference Intakes |

Never write a functional target into a chart without naming the source; an un-sourced "optimal" number is indistinguishable from a guess to the next reviewer (or to a co-managing physician).

## 5. Scope-of-practice check (run before finalizing any plan)

| Action | This state's license includes it? | If no |
|---|---|---|
| Order a CBC/CMP/ferritin/thyroid panel | Y (most licensed states) | N/A |
| Prescribe or adjust levothyroxine dose | N (this jurisdiction) | Co-manage: document explicitly deferring to prescriber |
| Prescribe a Schedule III–V controlled substance | Varies by state — check current formulary | If not granted, refer to prescriber; do not substitute a supplement for the drug's mechanism |
| Perform IV nutrient therapy | Varies — some states require a separate injection-therapy certification | If not certified/licensed for it, refer out |

## 6. Co-management letter skeleton

```
[Patient] presents with [finding + relevant trend/numbers].
[Named confounder, if any] could account for [X]; addressed by [specific action].
I am / am not adjusting [specific medication] — that decision stays with you / is mine to make.
Recommend recheck of [specific labs] in [interval].
[Explicit statement of what I am declining to recommend and why, if the patient requested it.]
```

Every co-management letter states, in plain language, which decisions the ND is making and which are explicitly left to the other prescriber — a letter that only describes findings without this line produces the "who owns the next decision" gap that causes dropped follow-up.
