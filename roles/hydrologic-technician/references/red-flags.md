# Red Flags

Field and record smell tests. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the check to run.

### Discharge measurement deviates from the current rating by more than 8% on a single visit

- **Usually means:** either a genuine control change (scour, aggradation, debris, vegetation) or a measurement made under poor conditions (rapidly changing stage, reduced vertical count, equipment problem) — the two look identical from the number alone.
- **First question:** "What quality rating (excellent/good/fair/poor) does this measurement itself earn given today's conditions, and does the deviation exceed that band?"
- **Data to pull:** field notes for meter spin-test results, stage-change rate during the measurement, and the two prior discharge measurements at this station.

### Two or more consecutive measurements deviate from the rating in the same direction

- **Usually means:** a real, progressive change at the control section (siltation, scour, new debris jam, vegetation growth) rather than measurement noise.
- **First question:** "Is the magnitude also growing visit over visit, or holding roughly steady?"
- **Data to pull:** the station's full measurement-vs-rating history for the current rating period; site photos from the last 2–3 visits to check the control physically.

### Meter or sensor fails its post-check but passed the pre-check

- **Usually means:** something happened during the measurement itself — debris fouling, a dropped meter, cable damage, biofouling on a sonde — not a pre-existing calibration problem.
- **First question:** "Was there anything unusual during the measurement (impact, heavy debris, extended immersion) that could explain drift mid-visit?"
- **Data to pull:** the measurement notes for anomalies logged in real time, and the specific post-check numeric result versus tolerance.

### A continuous stage record shows a step change with no matching field visit or known event

- **Usually means:** a datum shift — the reference point itself moved (staff gage bumped, sensor reinstalled at a different elevation, structural settling) rather than a hydrologic change.
- **First question:** "Does the step line up with any recorded maintenance, or with a documented high-flow/debris event near the gage?"
- **Data to pull:** maintenance log for the station, nearby precipitation/flow records for a plausible hydrologic cause, and a level survey against the station's reference marks if unexplained.

### A water-quality result is a clean round number with no qualifier

- **Usually means:** the value hit the method's reporting limit, or the wrong significant figures were carried through from the field data, not a suspiciously tidy true value.
- **First question:** "Is this value at or below the method reporting/detection limit for this analyte?"
- **Data to pull:** the lab's method detection limit and reporting limit for that analyte, and the raw field sonde/sample log.

### A discharge measurement's largest single vertical carries more than ~15–20% of total flow

- **Usually means:** the cross-section was under-subdivided, usually because of time pressure or a wide, deep channel measured with too few verticals.
- **First question:** "Could this panel be split into two verticals on the next visit without exceeding the practical measurement time?"
- **Data to pull:** the full verticals table with % of total Q per vertical, and the channel width/depth profile.

### An autosampler storm set returns a flat concentration series across the whole rise

- **Usually means:** the trigger fired too late — sampling started after the rising-limb pulse had already passed, or the intake was clogged/buried, not that the storm genuinely had no pulse.
- **First question:** "Did the first bottle fire before or after the stage started climbing?"
- **Data to pull:** the trigger log/timestamp against the stage hydrograph, and the intake condition noted at retrieval.

### A field-calibrated sonde reads a plausible but slightly-off value all deployment, discovered only at retrieval

- **Usually means:** calibration drift undetected because no mid-deployment check was run, or a slow biofouling accumulation on the sensor.
- **First question:** "What was the last successful field-check reading, and how long before retrieval was it?"
- **Data to pull:** deployment log with all field-check timestamps and results, and the post-retrieval calibration check magnitude/direction of drift.

### Rating shift applied but the next measurement doesn't confirm it

- **Usually means:** the shift was applied on an isolated point that should have been flagged for a follow-up instead, or the control changed again since the shift was applied.
- **First question:** "Was the shift applied after a single flagged deviation, or after a confirmed multi-visit trend?"
- **Data to pull:** the shift history for the rating period and the measurement that triggered the original shift.
