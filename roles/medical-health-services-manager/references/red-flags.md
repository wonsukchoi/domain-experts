# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced medical/health services manager notices instantly. Load this when triaging a staffing, compliance, or revenue-cycle question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### A staffing reduction proposal is justified by census alone, with no acuity data cited
- **Usually means:** the proposal may be based on an incomplete picture — flat or declining census can coexist with rising acuity, which changes the actual staffing need
- **First question:** "What's the acuity-adjusted workload trend for this unit, not just the census trend?"
- **Data to pull:** patient classification/acuity scoring data over the same period as the census trend

### Denial rate has risen and the explanation offered is "payers are getting stricter"
- **Usually means:** an internal, fixable process gap (documentation, coding, authorization) is the more likely and more common cause, and hasn't been checked first
- **First question:** "What's the denial breakdown by category this month vs. last, and which category grew the most?"
- **Data to pull:** denial root-cause tracker broken out by category over the trailing 2-3 months

### A quality/safety initiative is declined for lacking a clear budget-line ROI
- **Usually means:** the proposal wasn't evaluated against the specific adverse-event cost it would avoid — a real, quantifiable number that often exceeds the initiative's cost
- **First question:** "What's the current rate of the adverse event this initiative targets, and what's the calculated avoided cost at the current published or historical cost-per-event figure?"
- **Data to pull:** current adverse-event rate, patient-day volume, and a cost-per-event benchmark

### Compliance documentation is being assembled in the weeks immediately before a scheduled survey
- **Usually means:** day-to-day operations aren't maintaining continuous compliance, and the survey-prep effort itself is evidence of a gap between "compliant on paper for the survey" and "actually compliant"
- **First question:** "Is this documentation reflecting ongoing practice, or being created/backfilled specifically for the survey?"
- **Data to pull:** documentation timestamps relative to the actual dates of the practices being documented

### An error or near-miss report leads to individual disciplinary action as the first response
- **Usually means:** the review process is skipping systems/root-cause analysis in favor of individual blame, which will suppress future reporting
- **First question:** "Has a root-cause analysis been done to identify the systemic contributing factors, separate from any individual accountability question?"
- **Data to pull:** the incident review documentation and whether a systems-level analysis (not just an individual account) was conducted

### A safety-critical staffing ratio is being discussed as a "we'll figure out the budget impact later" decision
- **Usually means:** the floor/non-negotiable nature of the ratio is being treated as flexible under discussion, rather than being the fixed constraint the budget conversation should work within
- **First question:** "Is this ratio a regulatory/policy floor, and if so, why is it part of the budget discussion at all rather than a separately funded requirement?"
- **Data to pull:** the applicable state/accreditation staffing ratio requirement for this unit type

### Patient satisfaction scores are declining while clinical outcome metrics look stable
- **Usually means:** an operational/service issue (wait times, communication, discharge process) distinct from clinical quality — worth investigating separately rather than assuming it reflects clinical care quality
- **First question:** "What specifically do the patient satisfaction verbatims/subscores point to — is it clinical care, or the surrounding experience (wait, communication, environment)?"
- **Data to pull:** patient satisfaction survey subscores and verbatim comments, not just the composite score
