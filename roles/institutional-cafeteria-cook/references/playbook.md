# Playbook

Filled templates for the four documents an institutional kitchen actually runs on: the recipe-scaling factor table, the production record, the time-temperature log, and the substitution list — plus a per-meal cost reconciliation. Use these as the executable shape, not as illustrations.

## 1. Recipe-scaling factor table

The scale factor (SF) = desired yield ÷ recipe's written yield. Protein, starch, and vegetable weights scale at the raw SF. Seasoning, leavening, and acid need a correction past SF 4, because surface-area-to-volume ratio drops in large kettles and evaporation slows.

| Scale factor (SF) | Protein/starch/veg | Seasoning/leavening/acid | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2x | SF × quantity | SF × quantity | No adjustment needed |
| 2–4x | SF × quantity | SF × quantity, taste before hold | Spot-check, minor adjustment expected |
| 4–8x | SF × quantity | (SF × quantity) × 0.85, then taste-adjust | Start below linear, add up, don't start high |
| 8x+ | SF × quantity | (SF × quantity) × 0.80, then taste-adjust | Always taste-adjust before batch goes to hold; never serve the raw linear number |

**Worked conversion — sauce base, 50 → 480 portions (SF = 9.6):**

| Ingredient | Base (50 portions) | Linear × 9.6 | Corrected amount used |
|---|---|---|---|
| Heavy cream | 3 cups | 28.8 cups | 28.8 cups (dairy component — scales linear, it's not a seasoning) |
| Grated parmesan | 2 cups | 19.2 cups | 19.2 cups (linear — dairy solid, not seasoning) |
| Garlic powder | 1 Tbsp | 9.6 Tbsp | 7.7 Tbsp (× 0.80), tasted, held at 8 Tbsp |
| Salt | 2 tsp | 19.2 tsp | 15.4 tsp (× 0.80) starting point, tasted before hold |
| White pepper | 1 tsp | 9.6 tsp | 7.7 tsp (× 0.80) |

Note: only ingredients that concentrate flavor per unit mass (salt, ground spice, acid, leavening) get the correction factor. Dairy, protein, and starch that are structural to the dish, not seasoning, scale linearly even past SF 8.

## 3. Time-temperature log (critical limits + filled example)

**Critical limits (post these at every station):**

| Stage | Limit |
|---|---|
| Cold food receiving | ≤ 41°F |
| Frozen food receiving | Frozen solid, no evidence of thaw/refreeze |
| Cooking — poultry, stuffed items | ≥ 165°F, held 15 seconds |
| Cooking — ground meat, ground fish | ≥ 155°F, held 15 seconds |
| Cooking — whole-muscle beef/pork/fish, eggs for immediate service | ≥ 145°F, held 15 seconds |
| Reheating for hot-holding | ≥ 165°F within 2 hours of starting reheat |
| Hot-holding | ≥ 135°F, checked at least every 2 hours |
| Cold-holding | ≤ 41°F |
| Cooling, stage 1 | 135°F → 70°F within 2 hours |
| Cooling, stage 2 | 70°F → 41°F within an additional 4 hours (6 hours total) |
| Cumulative danger-zone time (41–135°F) | 0–2 hrs: safe to use/refrigerate. 2–4 hrs: use immediately, do not cool or re-hold. 4+ hrs: discard. |
| Site hold-quality ceiling | 4 hours from cook-end regardless of probe reading |

## 4. Substitution list (filled excerpt)

| Item # | Standard ingredient | Approved substitute | Trigger | Nutrient-equivalence note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | Heavy cream + parmesan (alfredo base) | Oat-milk roux + nutritional yeast | Documented dairy allergy | Meat/alt and grain oz-eq unaffected; fat/sodium re-checked against original recipe's certified values, within 5% |
| 22 | Whole wheat penne | Corn-based GF penne | Documented gluten/wheat allergy | Grain oz-eq crediting recalculated per GF product's manufacturer spec sheet — not assumed equal |
| 31 | Diced peanuts (salad topping) | Omitted, no substitute added | Documented peanut/tree-nut allergy | Loses no meal-pattern component; peanuts were a garnish, not a credited component |
| 40 | 1% milk | Lactose-free 1% milk | Documented lactose intolerance (not allergy — no cross-contact protocol needed) | Same milk-component credit, different SKU |

Any swap not on this list requires the dietitian's or nutrition director's written sign-off before that day's production — logged in the same production record, not verbally approved.

## 5. Per-meal cost reconciliation (school lunch example)

Federal free-lunch reimbursement rate (contiguous US, SY 2023–24 baseline): **$4.85/meal**. Site cost-allocation target: food ~40%, labor ~38%, overhead/admin ~22% of the reimbursement.

| Line | Target (% of $4.85) | Target $ | Actual (this week, 2,100 meals served) | Actual $/meal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Food cost | 40% | $1.94 | $4,494 total invoice ÷ 2,100 | $2.14 |
| Labor cost | 38% | $1.84 | $3,948 payroll ÷ 2,100 | $1.88 |
| Overhead/admin | 22% | $1.07 | (fixed allocation) | $1.05 |
| **Total** | 100% | $4.85 | | **$5.07** |

Reconciliation: food cost ran $0.20/meal over target (~10% over), or $420 total against the week's 2,100 meals ($4,494 actual − $4,074 target). A produce price spike on the broccoli line item accounts for a third of that: invoice shows $0.31/lb over the prior month's average, × 480 lb ordered that week = $149. The remaining $271 traces to ordinary price variance spread across the rest of the invoice, not a second concentrated spike. Labor and overhead were within 3% of target — the deviation is mostly one commodity line plus normal noise, not a systemic cost problem, and doesn't require a menu-cycle change, just a note to the purchasing log for that vendor.
