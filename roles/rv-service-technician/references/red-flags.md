# Red flags

Field smell tests a senior RV tech catches in the first ten minutes on site. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before gas, electrical, or structural work gets touched.

### 1. Manometer lock-up test shows any drop over the 3-minute hold at 11 in. W.C.

- **Usually means:** an LP-gas leak downstream of the regulator — most commonly a vibration-loosened flare fitting behind an appliance, a perished appliance connector hose, or a shutoff valve not fully seated.
- **First question:** "Which appliance was last serviced, connected, or disconnected?"
- **Data to pull:** soap-solution leak-search results by connection point, last regulator replacement date, appliance connector condition.

### 2. Moisture-meter reading above 20% at a wall, floor, or ceiling seam

- **Usually means:** active or recent water intrusion, most likely from a failed roof seal, window/vent gasket, or unsealed screw penetration above that location.
- **First question:** "When was the roof last inspected or resealed, and is there a maintenance record?"
- **Data to pull:** moisture readings at multiple points including an unaffected baseline location, roof seal inspection date, membrane type (EPDM vs. TPO) and unit age against expected service life.

### 3. Roof sealant inspection or recoat overdue by 12+ months

- **Usually means:** the highest-likelihood entry point for a leak that hasn't shown as an interior symptom yet — sealant crazes and chalks well before the membrane itself fails.
- **First question:** "Does the maintenance log show a seal inspection in the last 6–12 months, and what does the sealant look like at the vents, AC shroud, and edge trim?"
- **Data to pull:** sealant condition photos at every penetration, last recoat date, moisture-meter baseline readings at the most vulnerable seams.

### 4. Slide-out gap difference (top vs. bottom, or side to side) exceeding roughly ⅛–³⁄₁₆ in.

- **Usually means:** frame racking — commonly a tire-pressure imbalance or an unlevel park, occasionally a water-softened floor section — not a slide-mechanism fault.
- **First question:** "What's the tire pressure at each position against the placard spec, and is the unit level?"
- **Data to pull:** tire pressure by position, frame level readings, moisture-meter check of the floor near the slide opening if pressure and level are both in spec.

### 5. Converter/charger output stuck below ~13.2V DC under load, or holding at bulk voltage (14.4–14.8V) indefinitely

- **Usually means:** the charger isn't stepping down through its stages correctly, or the charge profile is mismatched to the installed battery chemistry (a lead-acid profile feeding a lithium/LiFePO4 bank).
- **First question:** "What battery chemistry is installed, and does the charger's profile setting match it?"
- **Data to pull:** converter model and profile setting, battery chemistry and age, voltage measured under a real load rather than open-circuit.

### 6. GFCI trips repeatedly only on shore power, not on generator or inverter

- **Usually means:** a moisture-compromised outlet or junction, or a genuine ground/neutral-bond wiring fault — not a "weak" GFCI breaker.
- **First question:** "Does it also trip on a different, known-good shore pedestal?"
- **Data to pull:** polarity and ground-bond test results at both the pedestal and the RV's shore inlet, moisture check at any outlet near a water source.

### 7. Sagging or soft floor felt underfoot near an entry step or slide opening

- **Usually means:** decking rot from long-term water intrusion, typically well advanced by the time it's felt underfoot rather than caught by a moisture meter earlier.
- **First question:** "Has this area ever had a documented roof or plumbing leak, even a minor one that was patched?"
- **Data to pull:** moisture readings radiating out from the soft spot, decking material and thickness spec, prior repair history for that section.

### 8. Customer-reported propane odor that a first soap test at the appliance knobs doesn't find

- **Usually means:** a leak at a less-accessible fitting (behind the appliance, under the unit) or one that only opens under road vibration or thermal cycling — not a false alarm.
- **First question:** "Does the odor correlate with the rig moving, a specific appliance running, or outdoor temperature?"
- **Data to pull:** full manometer lock-up test result (not a spot soap-test alone), leak search at every accessible fitting, appliance connector and quick-connect condition.

### 9. Water heater not bypassed before pumping RV antifreeze

- **Usually means:** the technician (or the customer, on a DIY unit arriving for a check) skipped the bypass step — antifreeze either wasted filling a tank being drained, or the tank left unprotected.
- **First question:** "Is the bypass kit installed, and were the valves actually turned before antifreeze was pumped?"
- **Data to pull:** bypass kit valve position, water heater drain-plug status, gallons of antifreeze used against the expected amount for a properly bypassed system.

### 10. Holding tank or low-point drain that hasn't fully stopped flowing, or shows ice residue before winterization is complete

- **Usually means:** a low spot or incomplete drain that will freeze and crack a fitting even after antifreeze is added upstream of it.
- **First question:** "Did water actually stop flowing from every low-point drain and both gray and black tank valves?"
- **Data to pull:** drain sequence log, ambient and interior temperature during the process, confirmation each tank valve reached full open.
