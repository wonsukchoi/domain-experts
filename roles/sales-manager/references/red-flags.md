# Red flags — what a sales manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A rep on pace to miss quota being put on a closing-skills PIP with no pipeline coverage check
- **Usually means:** the actual problem may be insufficient pipeline volume, not closing ability — a closing-skills intervention applied to a volume problem will fail regardless of execution quality.
- **First question:** "What's this rep's pipeline coverage ratio against quota, and how does it compare to the team standard?"
- **Data to pull:** current pipeline value and coverage ratio, team average coverage ratio, historical coverage trend for this rep.

### Quota assigned as an even split of a top-down number across reps with uneven territory potential
- **Usually means:** some reps are being set up to fail and others to coast, regardless of individual effort or skill, because the underlying territory potential wasn't accounted for.
- **First question:** "Does this rep's quota reflect their actual territory/account potential, or just an even division of the team target?"
- **Data to pull:** territory account count/potential by rep, quota assigned by rep, variance between the two.

### A forecast call where "at risk" or "best-case" designations are consistently punished or second-guessed by leadership
- **Usually means:** reps are likely being trained to only report good news, degrading the forecast into a performance rather than a genuinely useful planning signal.
- **First question:** "What happens to a rep who flags a deal as at-risk — problem-solving, or a negative reaction?"
- **Data to pull:** forecast call history and how at-risk designations were received, forecast accuracy trend over recent quarters.

### A coaching 1:1 that's a generic status check ("how's it going") rather than tied to a specific deal or skill gap
- **Usually means:** generic coaching doesn't change behavior — the highest-leverage coaching is deal-specific and skill-specific.
- **First question:** "What specific deal and what specific skill gap did this 1:1 actually address?"
- **Data to pull:** 1:1 notes/agenda, whether a named deal and stakeholder were discussed versus general performance talk.

### A new hire held to full-quota expectations from day one with no ramp-adjusted target
- **Usually means:** new reps ramp on a curve, and comparing them to full-quota performance immediately sets up an unfair, demoralizing comparison before they've had a fair chance.
- **First question:** "Does this rep's target reflect a ramp curve, or full quota from month one?"
- **Data to pull:** the rep's tenure, the quota/target structure applied to them versus the team's standard ramp model.

### A comp/recognition system that rewards closed revenue alone while leadership says it wants better discovery/qualification behavior
- **Usually means:** the team optimizes for what's measured — if the comp plan doesn't reward the stated priority, expect the comp plan to win regardless of what's said in meetings.
- **First question:** "Does the comp plan actually reward the behavior we say we want, or only the final outcome?"
- **Data to pull:** the comp plan structure, the specific behavior leadership has stated it wants to see more of.

### A sales manager who steps in to personally close a struggling rep's deals rather than coaching the rep to close them
- **Usually means:** the manager's impact is being capped at their own personal capacity instead of multiplied across the team, and the rep isn't developing the skill that was actually missing.
- **First question:** "Is the manager coaching this rep to close, or closing it for them?"
- **Data to pull:** deal ownership and activity log, whether the manager or the rep is driving the remaining steps.

### A missed number discovered at quarter-end with no earlier pipeline-coverage warning flagged
- **Usually means:** the leading indicator (pipeline coverage) was likely visible weeks or months earlier, and the miss was predictable before it became a lagging, too-late-to-fix result.
- **First question:** "What did pipeline coverage look like 6-8 weeks before this miss, and was it flagged at the time?"
- **Data to pull:** historical pipeline coverage trend leading up to the miss, any prior flagging or intervention attempted.
