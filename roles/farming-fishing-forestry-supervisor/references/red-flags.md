# Red flags

Smell tests a veteran crew supervisor or ag-compliance auditor catches in the first pass over a payroll or field log.

### Crew's blended average hourly earnings looks fine, but individual daily stubs show sub-floor days

- **Usually means:** a few fast pickers are pulling the average above the minimum-wage floor while slower or newer workers are running under it every day — blended numbers hide individual make-up-pay exposure the same way a blended CAC hides a decaying channel.
- **First question:** "Show me each worker's individual lugs-and-hours for the last 3 days, not the crew average."
- **Data to pull:** per-worker daily lug count, hours worked, and computed effective hourly rate; count of worker-days below the applicable minimum wage in the last pay period.

### Crew re-enters a field less than the label's REI after a spray

- **Usually means:** either the application log wasn't checked before the schedule was set, or schedule pressure overrode a known REI. Both are the same violation to a WPS inspector.
- **First question:** "Pull the application record for this block — what product, what REI, and when was it applied?"
- **Data to pull:** pesticide application log (product, EPA registration number, date/time applied, labeled REI), posted-notice records, crew entry/exit times for the block in question.

### H-2A payroll shows fewer guaranteed workdays paid than three-fourths of the contract period

- **Usually means:** a slow stretch (weather, low yield, early finish) quietly cut worker hours below the 3/4 guarantee without anyone flagging the shortfall against the contract, which is a wage claim waiting to be filed.
- **First question:** "How many workdays has each H-2A worker actually been offered against the contract period, and where does that sit relative to three-fourths?"
- **Data to pull:** H-2A work contract (total contract-period workdays), actual days offered/worked per worker, any voluntary absences (which don't count against the employer) versus employer-side schedule cuts.

### Same piece rate carried across multiple seasons with no re-test against pace

- **Usually means:** the rate was correct once, for a specific yield density and crew skill level, and nobody re-ran the wage-floor test after conditions changed (denser fruit set, an inexperienced crew rotation, a new variety).
- **First question:** "When was this rate last tested against actual picking pace, and what changed in the field since then?"
- **Data to pull:** rate-setting history/date, current-season average pace samples, minimum-wage rate for the current year.

### Overtime hours worked but the pay stub shows flat piece-rate pay with no separate premium line

- **Usually means:** the OT premium on piece-rate work (regular rate × 0.5 × OT hours) was never computed — a common payroll-system gap when the system is built for hourly, not piece, pay.
- **First question:** "Walk me through how the payroll system computes the regular rate and the OT premium for a piece-rate worker's 10-hour day."
- **Data to pull:** payroll calculation logic or manual worksheet for OT days, sample worker's daily hours vs. pay stub breakdown.

### Field sanitation units still at the prior sub-block after the crew moved

- **Usually means:** the crew relocation was decided (often for a weather push or a fresh block) without a parallel check on sanitation coverage, which is a 29 CFR 1928.110 violation the moment the new location is more than about a quarter mile or a five-minute walk from the units.
- **First question:** "Where are the crew working right now, and where are the toilet, handwash, and water stations relative to that location?"
- **Data to pull:** current field map with crew location and sanitation unit locations, date/time of last crew move, unit relocation log.

### Farm labor contractor invoice with no registration number or written disclosure

- **Usually means:** the contractor is operating outside MSPA registration requirements, which shifts liability for wage and safety violations back onto the farm operation that hired them.
- **First question:** "What's this contractor's Farm Labor Contractor Certificate of Registration number, and where's the written disclosure of wages and terms given to the workers?"
- **Data to pull:** FLC registration number and expiration, worker disclosure forms, contractor's own payroll records for the crew supplied.

### Housing headcount at or over the rated capacity for an H-2A crew

- **Usually means:** a late crew addition or a housing unit taken offline for repair got absorbed into existing units without re-checking capacity, which is both a labor-standards violation and a fire-safety exposure.
- **First question:** "What's the rated occupancy for each housing unit, and what's the current headcount in each?"
- **Data to pull:** state/DOL housing inspection certificate with rated capacity per unit, current occupancy roster.

### Harvest schedule holds through a red-flag weather forecast with no logged go/no-go call

- **Usually means:** nobody owned the decision — the crew kept working the original plan by default rather than because someone actively evaluated the threshold and decided to hold.
- **First question:** "When the forecast crossed the push threshold, who made the call to hold, and what was the reasoning?"
- **Data to pull:** weather forecast history for the decision window, pre-set go/no-go threshold documentation, any dated memo or log entry recording the decision.

### Mixed H-2A/domestic crew paid two different rates for identical work with no CBA on file

- **Usually means:** domestic workers are being paid state minimum wage while H-2A workers in the same job are paid the (higher) AEWR, or vice versa — both a "corresponding employment" violation under 20 CFR 655.120 unless a certified collective bargaining agreement justifies the difference.
- **First question:** "Is there a certified CBA that sets a different rate for domestic workers in this job classification — and if not, why are the rates different?"
- **Data to pull:** job classification and rate paid per worker by immigration/employment status, any CBA documentation, the current AEWR for the job classification.
