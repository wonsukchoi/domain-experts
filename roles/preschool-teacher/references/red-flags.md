# Preschool Teacher — Red Flags

### One screening domain below cutoff, all others typical
- **Usually means:** A real domain-specific delay (most likely) or a single bad testing day/context issue (second most likely — e.g., child was sick, screening happened right after a hard transition).
- **First question:** "Has this shown up in classroom observation too, or only on the screening tool?"
- **Data to pull:** Two weeks of anecdotal records specific to that domain; the screening administration notes (time of day, child's state).

### Behavior escalation clustered specifically at transitions
- **Usually means:** Predictability/regulation problem, not defiance — the child doesn't have enough warning or structure around the change.
- **First question:** "Does this happen at every transition, or specific ones (e.g., only outdoor-to-indoor)?"
- **Data to pull:** A one-week log of transition times and whether a warning was given before each.

### Same behavior showing up unpredictably, including low-demand times
- **Usually means:** Sensory processing issue or a medical/regulatory cause, not a classroom-management problem — behavioral fixes alone won't resolve it.
- **First question:** "Does this happen even during free play or quiet time, with no clear trigger?"
- **Data to pull:** Antecedent-behavior-consequence log across at least 5 instances spanning different times of day.

### Expressive vocabulary noticeably behind receptive understanding
- **Usually means:** The child understands more than they can produce — common and often resolves, but worth tracking if the gap is large or not narrowing over a semester.
- **First question:** "Can they follow multi-step verbal instructions even though they're not saying much?"
- **Data to pull:** A running list of words/phrases used over 2-4 weeks, compared against typical range for the age (roughly 200-1,000 words at 3-4 years).

### Ratio holds on paper but group is functionally merged (e.g., combined nap-time coverage)
- **Usually means:** A staffing gap elsewhere in the building is being solved by quietly merging groups, which can violate group-size limits even when the adult:child ratio still checks out.
- **First question:** "Is this a today-only coverage gap, or has it happened more than twice this month?"
- **Data to pull:** The sign-in sheet for both groups and the licensing group-size maximum for the combined age range.

### A child avoids or resists a specific fine-motor task repeatedly (scissors, pencil grip)
- **Usually means:** Task is outside the child's current zone of proximal development — being asked to do it unscaffolded, not a motivation problem.
- **First question:** "What does the child do instead when offered the task — refuse entirely, or attempt and get frustrated?"
- **Data to pull:** Two observations of the specific attempt (grip, duration of engagement before disengaging).

### Parent reports a concern the teacher hasn't independently observed
- **Usually means:** The behavior is context-specific (happens at home, not school) or is a normal developmental phase the parent is unfamiliar with — rarely a sign the teacher is missing something in the classroom.
- **First question:** "Can you describe exactly when and where you see this at home?"
- **Data to pull:** Nothing yet from the classroom — start a targeted classroom observation log before responding either way.

### Same child involved in 3+ minor incident reports within two weeks
- **Usually means:** An unmet regulation or sensory need driving repeated small incidents, or a supervision/environment gap in the classroom itself — check both before assuming it's the child.
- **First question:** "Are the incidents clustered around the same time of day or activity?"
- **Data to pull:** All incident reports for that child in the period, cross-referenced against the daily schedule block.

### A screening referral recommendation made from a single low score, no second observation
- **Usually means:** Overcorrection — treating one data point as sufficient evidence, which risks referring typical developmental variability as a confirmed delay.
- **First question:** "Has this domain been re-checked, or is this the only data point?"
- **Data to pull:** Screening history for that domain across prior intervals, if available.
