# Preschool Teacher — Playbook

## Daily routine block (half-day program, 8:00-12:00, 16 children, 2 adults)

| Time | Block | Ratio check | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8:00-8:20 | Arrival / free choice | 1:8 (both adults present) | Peak injury-risk window — highest child mobility, lowest structure |
| 8:20-8:35 | Circle time | 1:16 (one adult leads, one floats) | Floating adult redirects, doesn't co-lead — avoids two competing voices |
| 8:35-9:15 | Centers (art, blocks, sensory, dramatic play) | 1:8 per adult, split room | Each adult owns a zone, not a rotating float — consistent supervision beats coverage-by-wandering |
| 9:15-9:20 | Transition to snack | 2-min warning given at 9:13 | Transition-warning skipped is the single most common cause of the 9:15 escalation spike |
| 9:20-9:40 | Snack | 1:16, both adults seated at tables | Choking/allergy supervision — this block is never split |
| 9:40-10:10 | Outdoor gross motor | 1:16, perimeter positions | Two adults on opposite sides of the play area, not together — coverage, not company |
| 10:10-10:40 | Small-group instruction (rotates 4 children per group) | 1:4 for the active group, 1:12 float for the rest | This is where scaffolded, domain-targeted instruction happens — not during free play |
| 10:40-11:15 | Free choice / individual observation window | 1:8 | Anecdotal records written during this block, not at day's end from memory |
| 11:15-11:45 | Story + fine-motor table activity | 1:16 / 1:8 split | |
| 11:45-12:00 | Dismissal | 1:1 per family confirmed at pickup | Sign-out log checked against authorized-pickup list every time, no exceptions for "regulars" |

## Developmental screening cycle (ASQ-3, per child, 3x/year)

1. **Administer** the age-appropriate interval questionnaire (30-month, 36-month, 48-month, etc.) — parent completes at home or in a 15-minute in-person session if literacy/language is a barrier.
2. **Score by domain**, not summed: Communication, Gross Motor, Fine Motor, Problem Solving, Personal-Social, each 0-60 from six items scored 0/5/10.
3. **Compare each domain independently** against the published interval cutoff. Three zones: white (above cutoff, typical), grey (monitoring zone, 1-2 SD below), black (below cutoff, refer).
4. **Any domain in grey or black** → pull two weeks of anecdotal records for that specific domain before deciding.
5. **Second data point confirms the pattern** → recommend referral, with the specific domain and observed pattern, not a general concern.
6. **No second data point (isolated low score)** → re-screen that domain in 4-6 weeks rather than referring immediately.

## Parent conference structure (developmental concern)

1. Open with what's on-track, named specifically (not "she's doing great overall").
2. State the specific domain of concern and the screening result in plain terms (score + what it means, not just "below average").
3. Give the anecdotal pattern that confirms it's not a single bad day.
4. State clearly whether this is "worth watching" or "worth a referral now" — don't let tone carry that distinction.
5. Offer the concrete next step (referral form, re-screen date) before the parent has to ask what happens next.

## Behavior escalation triage (in order, cheapest fix first)

1. **Is it transition-specific?** → add/lengthen visual or verbal warning before the transition (5-min, 2-min, "last one").
2. **Is it request/communication-specific** (happens when child wants something and can't express it)? → offer a visual choice board or two-word model phrase before redirecting.
3. **Is it unpredictable across contexts** (happens during low-demand times too)? → this is the escalation trigger for a sensory or medical conversation with the family, not a classroom-management fix.
4. **Only after ruling out the above** → individual behavior plan with specific antecedent-behavior-consequence tracking over 2 weeks.
