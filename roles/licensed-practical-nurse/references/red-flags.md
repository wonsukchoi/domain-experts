# Red flags

### New or worsening confusion plus temp ≥38.3°C (101°F) within the same shift
- **Usually means:** UTI or pneumonia progressing toward sepsis in an elderly resident — the two most common sources of acute change in condition in long-term care.
- **First question:** What was the resident's documented baseline mental status this shift, and when did he/she last void?
- **Data to pull:** Vital-sign trend since the last documented baseline, urinalysis order status, respiratory assessment.

### Any single Stop & Watch item triggering, regardless of how many others are absent
- **Usually means:** An early, still-reversible change — the tool is designed to catch it before the aggregate picture looks obviously bad.
- **First question:** Which specific item triggered, and what did the resident look like at the last documented baseline?
- **Data to pull:** The Stop & Watch form and the baseline vitals/behavior it's compared against.

### A physician or facility asks you to perform a task not on your state's LPN/LVN authorized-task list
- **Usually means:** A covering clinician unfamiliar with this state's scope rules, or urgent need with no RN currently reachable.
- **First question:** Is an RN available right now, on-site or by telehealth, to take this task instead?
- **Data to pull:** The state Nurse Practice Act's task list and the facility's specific delegation policy for this task.

### Resident or family refuses a transfer that meets the facility's objective transfer criteria
- **Usually means:** A capacity, advance-directive, or POLST conflict rather than a simple refusal to cooperate.
- **First question:** Does this resident currently have decision-making capacity, and what does the POLST/advance directive actually say?
- **Data to pull:** POLST form, capacity documentation, healthcare proxy contact information.

### A verbal/telephone order from the on-call physician with no read-back documented
- **Usually means:** A rushed call under time pressure, most commonly late at night with no RN present to double-check the process.
- **First question:** Did I read the order back verbatim before ending the call, and is that documented on the telephone-order form?
- **Data to pull:** The telephone-order form, call time, and ordering physician's name.

### Unplanned weight loss ≥5% in 30 days or ≥10% in 180 days
- **Usually means:** Early dysphagia, depression, or an undiagnosed illness — a standard long-term-care nutrition threshold, not a normal aging variation.
- **First question:** Is intake actually declining, or is this a scale/measurement inconsistency?
- **Data to pull:** Weight trend, documented meal-intake percentages, dietary consult status.

### New or worsening pressure injury on a resident with a Braden score ≤12
- **Usually means:** The ordered turning/repositioning schedule isn't actually being delivered, or a moisture/friction issue was missed.
- **First question:** What turning frequency is this resident actually receiving versus what's ordered?
- **Data to pull:** Turning/repositioning log, Braden score history, current wound measurements.

### A resident with a dementia baseline goes from agitated to unusually calm or lethargic
- **Usually means:** Frequently mistaken for improvement, but often a sign of worsening — sedation, hypoglycemia, or early sepsis.
- **First question:** What changed in medications or vitals in the same window?
- **Data to pull:** Sedating medications given in the last 4 hours, current vital signs.

### A CNA reports a delegated vital or observation as normal on a resident already flagged by Stop & Watch
- **Usually means:** A measurement or reporting gap, or a still-evolving change the CNA isn't trained to recognize as significant.
- **First question:** Did I personally re-verify this before documenting or standing down?
- **Data to pull:** Repeat the measurement personally rather than accepting the delegated report as closing the loop.

### Responsible party not notified within the facility's required window after a significant change in condition
- **Usually means:** A documentation or communication gap, not necessarily a missed call — check both.
- **First question:** Has notification actually occurred, or only been planned?
- **Data to pull:** Notification log timestamp and the phone record.

### Administrator or DON asks you to hold off calling 911 or the on-call physician despite meeting the facility's own transfer criteria
- **Usually means:** Pressure related to avoidable-hospitalization/readmission metrics, not a clinical judgment.
- **First question:** Does this request conflict with the facility's own written change-in-condition protocol?
- **Data to pull:** The written protocol and the facility's chain-of-command policy for disagreements with a supervisor's directive.

### High-alert medication (opioid, anticoagulant, insulin) more than 30 minutes late with no documented reason
- **Usually means:** Task overload on the shift, with right-time silently sacrificed first among the medication rights.
- **First question:** Is this specific delay clinically consequential for this specific drug and this specific resident?
- **Data to pull:** MAR late-dose reason code and time of the last dose actually given.
