# Veterinarian — Red Flags

### Cat with any reported human OTC-medication exposure, regardless of stated dose
- **Usually means:** Owner underestimates the amount ingested, or the true onset was earlier than reported.
- **First question:** What exact product, concentration, and approximate time of ingestion — check the packaging if available, don't rely on the owner's memory of the dose.
- **Data to pull:** Methemoglobin level and PCV if acetaminophen suspected; liver enzymes; time-since-ingestion for decontamination window.

### Dose calculation that requires scaling more than 10x from a known-safe reference dose
- **Usually means:** Either a legitimate extra-label protocol, or a units/decimal error propagating from the reference dose.
- **First question:** Recompute independently from the patient's actual current weight rather than trusting the referring note's stated total dose.
- **Data to pull:** Original formulary entry, patient's current weight, and the specific mg/kg used at each step.

### Herding-breed or herding-breed-mix patient on an extra-label high-dose antiparasitic
- **Usually means:** MDR1 mutation risk at this specific dose range, even if the same drug class is safe at label doses.
- **First question:** Is genotype known, and does the requested dose fall in the low-risk (prevention) or high-risk (extra-label) range?
- **Data to pull:** MDR1 genotype test result if available; drug's known dose-toxicity relationship for MDR1-mutant dogs specifically.

### Owner reports symptom onset as "just started" but exam findings show chronic changes (muscle atrophy, callused pressure points, matted fur over a painful area)
- **Usually means:** True onset predates the reported timeline; owner noticed only when it crossed a visible threshold.
- **First question:** What is the earliest sign, however minor, the owner now recalls in retrospect once asked directly?
- **Data to pull:** Weight trend from prior visit records if available; radiographs to assess chronicity of any orthopedic finding.

### Production-animal client reports "a few sick animals" without a count against herd size
- **Usually means:** Anecdotal impression that may or may not exceed the operation's actual background disease rate.
- **First question:** What is the exact count of affected animals against total herd size, and what has this operation's historical rate been for this condition?
- **Data to pull:** Herd health records for the same condition over the past 1-2 years; current affected count.

### Euthanasia request for a patient with a non-terminal, treatable condition
- **Usually means:** Owner cost constraint, caregiver burnout, or a misunderstanding of prognosis — rarely that the animal's welfare genuinely requires it that day.
- **First question:** Walk through a structured quality-of-life conversation (appetite, mobility, hygiene, interest, more good days than bad) before scheduling.
- **Data to pull:** Prior treatment attempted, cost estimate for continued treatment, and a specific quality-of-life scoring tool if the owner is open to it.

### Food-producing animal prescribed a drug without checking the client's next sale/slaughter date
- **Usually means:** An otherwise-correct treatment choice that creates an illegal-residue risk if the withdrawal period exceeds the client's timeline.
- **First question:** When is this animal's next scheduled sale, milking pickup, or slaughter date?
- **Data to pull:** FARAD withdrawal-period lookup for the specific drug, species, and route of administration.

### Vaccination history reported as "up to date" without a specific date or record
- **Usually means:** Owner recall is unreliable; "up to date" often means "I think so" rather than a confirmed record.
- **First question:** Do you have the vaccination record, or was this at a clinic we can call to confirm?
- **Data to pull:** Prior clinic records if available; if unconfirmed, treat as unvaccinated for differential-ranking purposes on any vaccine-preventable-disease presentation.

### Repeat presentation for the same complaint after a declined diagnostic or treatment recommendation
- **Usually means:** The declined recommendation was likely the correct next step, and the underlying problem has progressed.
- **First question:** What was declined at the prior visit, and has the owner's cost/time constraint changed?
- **Data to pull:** Prior visit's documented recommendation and the owner's stated reason for declining.
