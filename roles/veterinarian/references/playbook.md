# Veterinarian — Playbook

## Weight-based dose calculation worksheet

| Step | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Patient weight | 28 kg (61.7 lb) | Use current weight, not last-visit weight — dose on stale weight for a growing/losing patient is a common error |
| 2. Labeled/target dose | 6 mcg/kg (prevention) or per formulary | Confirm units (mcg vs mg) before multiplying — a 1000x unit error is the single most common dosing mistake |
| 3. Total dose | weight (kg) × dose (mcg or mg/kg) | 28 × 6 = 168 mcg |
| 4. Cross-check vs toxic ceiling | Compare to species/breed-specific toxic threshold, not just "is this a normal dose" | For MDR1-risk breeds, check whether the dose falls in the low-risk or high-risk range specifically |
| 5. Concentration to volume | total dose ÷ drug concentration (mg/mL or mcg/mL) | e.g., 8.4 mg ÷ 10 mg/mL = 0.84 mL |
| 6. Round to dispensable increment | Round to a measurable volume/tablet fraction | Document if rounding changes effective mg/kg by more than 10% |

## Differential-ranking table (filled example: 3-year-old intact male Labrador, acute vomiting)

| Rank | Differential | Supports | Against | Next step if top of list |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Dietary indiscretion / gastroenteritis | Common in breed/age, no other signs, known trash access | — | Symptomatic care, recheck in 24-48h |
| 2 | Foreign body obstruction | Intact male Lab (known chewer breed pattern), vomiting | No abdominal pain on palpation yet | Abdominal radiographs if not improved in 24h or pain develops |
| 3 | Parvovirus | Vomiting | Fully vaccinated, no diarrhea, no lethargy | Parvo SNAP test only if vaccination history uncertain |
| 4 | Pancreatitis | — | No high-fat meal history, no abdominal pain | Lipase/amylase only if pain or lethargy develops |

## MDR1-risk drug decision sequence

1. Identify if the patient's breed carries meaningful MDR1-mutation prevalence (Collies, Australian Shepherds, Shelties, and related herding-breed crosses).
2. Identify which dose range the planned drug/dose falls into: prevention-level (low, generally safe regardless of genotype) vs extra-label/high-dose (genotype-dependent risk).
3. If low-dose/prevention-level: proceed without testing delay.
4. If high-dose/extra-label and genotype unknown: either test (3-5 day turnaround) or substitute a non-P-glycoprotein-substrate alternative (e.g., isoxazoline class) if clinically equivalent and time-sensitive.
5. Document the reasoning in the record regardless of which path is chosen — "genotype unknown, chose alternative drug to avoid delay" is a defensible record entry; silence on the tradeoff is not.
