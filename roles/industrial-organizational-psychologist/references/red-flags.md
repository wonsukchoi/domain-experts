# Industrial-Organizational Psychologist — Red Flags

### Selection-rate ratio below 0.80 with N large enough for a significance test
- **Usually means:** either a real, job-relevant screening effect that needs a validity defense, or an unvalidated tool with disparate impact and no job-relatedness evidence on file.
- **First question:** does a current job analysis and validity study exist for this exact position, or was the tool adopted off a vendor's generic claim?
- **Data to pull:** applicant-flow log by protected class, the tool's technical validation report, job-analysis date.

### Local validation sample under ~30 reported as a precise correlation
- **Usually means:** someone is reporting a coefficient with a confidence interval wide enough to include zero as if it were stable.
- **First question:** is there a defensible validity-generalization argument available instead of the local number?
- **Data to pull:** the raw sample size, the confidence interval around the reported r, the relevant meta-analytic VG table for this predictor/job family.

### Performance-rating distribution >85% in the top category, one rater or company-wide
- **Usually means:** leniency or halo bias compressing the variance any validity study depends on to detect a relationship.
- **First question:** is this one rater's pattern or a system-wide calibration problem?
- **Data to pull:** rating distribution by individual rater, rater-training history, forced-distribution policy status.

### Job analysis older than 3-5 years, or job has changed materially since it was run
- **Usually means:** the content-validity linkage between the assessment and the actual current job is stale and discoverable as a weak point.
- **First question:** what specifically changed in the role (tasks, tools, reporting structure) since the last job analysis?
- **Data to pull:** the job-analysis document date, any job-description revisions, task-inventory comparison old vs. current.

### Assessment vendor won't share the technical validation report
- **Usually means:** either the study doesn't meet Uniform Guidelines documentation standards, or it was never conducted for this job family, only claimed by analogy.
- **First question:** what specific jobs and samples does the vendor's validity evidence actually cover?
- **Data to pull:** vendor's technical manual, the SIC/job-family codes their validation sample covered, any independent third-party review.

### Interrater reliability (ICC) below .70 on a structured interview panel
- **Usually means:** "structured" in name only — panelists are scoring inconsistently, which caps the maximum validity the interview can show (reliability ceiling).
- **First question:** are panelists using the same behaviorally anchored rating scale, or improvising?
- **Data to pull:** pairwise or ICC reliability statistics across the last N interview panels.

### Cut score set at a round number with no standard-error-of-measurement rationale
- **Usually means:** a hard cutoff manufactured adverse impact at the margin that banding would have avoided without changing who's ultimately hired.
- **First question:** how many candidates fall within one SEM of the cutoff, and what's their demographic composition?
- **Data to pull:** score distribution near the cutoff, SEM of the instrument, near-miss candidate demographics.

### Validity-generalization citation for a job outside the meta-analysis's covered domain
- **Usually means:** VG evidence is being stretched past its transportability boundary to justify a convenient off-the-shelf tool.
- **First question:** what job families and content areas does the cited meta-analysis actually include?
- **Data to pull:** the meta-analysis's job-family coding, a side-by-side task comparison against the target job.

### Differential prediction not checked across subgroups before deployment
- **Usually means:** the tool may predict performance differently (different regression slope/intercept) for different groups, which a single overall validity coefficient hides.
- **First question:** has a moderated regression or subgroup regression comparison been run?
- **Data to pull:** subgroup-specific regression coefficients, sample sizes per subgroup, overall vs. subgroup R².

### Business necessity argument offered without considering less-discriminatory alternatives
- **Usually means:** the legal defense is incomplete — Griggs/Uniform Guidelines requires showing alternatives were considered, not just that the current tool is valid.
- **First question:** what alternative selection procedures with plausibly similar validity and lower impact were evaluated, and why were they rejected?
- **Data to pull:** the documented alternatives-considered analysis, or its absence.
