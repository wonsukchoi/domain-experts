# Red flags — orthoptic exam and therapy smell tests

Format per flag: signal with threshold, most likely causes, the first question a veteran asks, and what to pull to check.

### Leukocoria (white pupillary reflex) noted on red reflex or in a photograph
- **Usually means:** retinoblastoma, congenital cataract, or another visual-axis-obstructing pathology until proven otherwise — this is not a routine strabismus/amblyopia workup finding.
- **First question:** "Has this been seen by an ophthalmologist this week, not at the next routine follow-up?"
- **Data to pull:** red reflex exam (Bruckner test), dilated fundus exam status, any family photos showing the reflex over time.

### Acquired esotropia in a child over age 6 with headache, morning vomiting, or ataxia
- **Usually means:** a sixth-nerve palsy from raised intracranial pressure (posterior fossa mass, hydrocephalus) rather than a typical accommodative or age-appropriate comitant esotropia.
- **First question:** "Is the deviation comitant or does abduction show a specific limitation, and are there any of the associated neurologic symptoms?"
- **Data to pull:** versions/ductions grading (abduction deficit), neurologic review of systems, urgent neuro-imaging referral status.

### Amblyopia occlusion therapy plateaued with no objective compliance verification on file
- **Usually means:** the prescribed dose was never actually delivered (see the 75%-of-Rx verification threshold), not that the treatment tier has failed.
- **First question:** "What does an occlusion dose monitor or a cross-checked diary say the actual daily wear time was?"
- **Data to pull:** ODM data or diary cross-checked against caregiver report, current prescribed hours vs. measured hours.

### Same-session repeat cover-test measurements varying by more than 10 prism diopters on a deviation assumed comitant
- **Usually means:** a fixation, attention, or cooperation problem during measurement, or an underlying incomitance not yet identified — not a genuinely variable comitant angle.
- **First question:** "Was this remeasured under a different fixation target or attention state before the number was charted?"
- **Data to pull:** repeat measurements at distance and near, versions/ductions grading to check for incomitance.

### Visual acuity or angle measurement charted as "unable to test, assume normal"
- **Usually means:** a genuine data gap (age, cooperation, fatigue) has been silently converted into a false-negative result rather than flagged with the fallback method used.
- **First question:** "What objective fallback (Bruckner, Hirschberg/Krimsky, alternate-cover recovery speed) was attempted, and what's the confidence level on this result?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific fallback test result and a documented confidence flag, not a bare "WNL."

### Stereoacuity reported as gross/fly-only with no residual angle checked
- **Usually means:** suppression or a microtropia with a small residual angle that a routine alternate cover test alone may miss.
- **First question:** "Does the Worth 4-dot and a careful prism cover test rule out a small-angle monofixation syndrome?"
- **Data to pull:** Worth 4-dot result at distance and near, prism cover test result, stereoacuity in arcseconds if any fine testing was possible.

### Diplopia persisting beyond the expected adaptation window after strabismus surgery
- **Usually means:** an under- or over-corrected angle, a new incomitance from surgical effect on muscle mechanics, or an unmasked sensory anomaly that wasn't apparent preoperatively.
- **First question:** "What's the current angle compared to the planned surgical correction, and is it comitant or has a new pattern appeared?"
- **Data to pull:** postoperative angle measurement at distance and near, comparison to preoperative and planned-target angles, versions/ductions recheck.

### Reduced ocular motility with pain following facial or orbital trauma
- **Usually means:** an orbital floor fracture with extraocular muscle entrapment — a surgical emergency, not a routine motility-disorder workup.
- **First question:** "Is there restriction on forced duction testing consistent with mechanical entrapment, and has this been flagged for same-day imaging?"
- **Data to pull:** forced duction result if performed, orbital CT status, time since trauma.

### Convergence insufficiency symptoms treated as "just eye strain" without NPC/vergence measurement
- **Usually means:** a diagnosable and treatable condition (NPC break >6cm, recovery >10cm) is being managed with reassurance instead of the CITT-supported therapy sequence.
- **First question:** "What was the measured NPC break and recovery point, and has a validated symptom score been used?"
- **Data to pull:** NPC measurement, positive fusional vergence at near, symptom score (e.g., CISS).

### Angle of deviation measured without noting whether habitual correction was worn
- **Usually means:** the charted prism-diopter value doesn't correspond to the condition surgery or treatment will actually be delivered under.
- **First question:** "Was this measurement taken with or without the patient's current correction, and does that match the condition the surgeon needs?"
- **Data to pull:** measurement condition annotation (corrected/uncorrected, distance/near) alongside the angle value itself.

### Nystagmus documented with no null point or anomalous head posture assessment
- **Usually means:** a workup that hasn't identified the information most relevant to management (null-point-based prism or surgical planning) and may be missing a neuro-ophthalmologic cause.
- **First question:** "Has a null point or compensatory head posture been looked for and documented, and does the presentation warrant neuro-ophthalmology referral?"
- **Data to pull:** head posture observation, visual acuity in primary gaze vs. null-point gaze, referral status if onset was acquired rather than congenital.
