# Red Flags

### ETCO2 sustained above 45 mmHg for more than 2 minutes
- **Usually means:** Hypoventilation from a plane too deep or an obstructed/kinked airway; second most likely is exhausted CO2 absorbent in the circuit.
- **First question:** Is the patient actually hypoventilating (check chest excursion and circuit patency), or does the vaporizer setting need to come down?
- **Data to pull:** Capnograph waveform shape, circuit connections, CO2 absorbent color/change indicator, respiratory rate on the flowsheet.

### ETCO2 flat-line or "shark-fin" waveform
- **Usually means:** Circuit disconnect, esophageal intubation, or endotracheal tube kink/obstruction — not a monitor malfunction until those are ruled out.
- **First question:** Is the tube actually in the trachea and the circuit actually connected?
- **Data to pull:** Direct airway check, chest auscultation, SpO2 concurrently.

### SpO2 sustained below 95%
- **Usually means:** Hypoventilation, airway obstruction, or a probe placement/perfusion artifact — check the probe before treating it as true hypoxemia.
- **First question:** Is this a real desaturation (check pulse waveform quality) or a probe reading off a poorly perfused site?
- **Data to pull:** Probe site and waveform quality, concurrent ETCO2 and RR, mucous membrane color.

### Two or more of jaw tone / palpebral / HR / RR moving toward "lighter" together across sequential checks
- **Usually means:** Anesthetic plane genuinely lightening, most often from surgical stimulus outpacing the current vaporizer setting.
- **First question:** Are the reflex findings and the vitals moving the same direction together, or is only one parameter off?
- **Data to pull:** Anesthesia flowsheet across the last two to three checks, current vaporizer setting, timing relative to surgical stimulus.

### Calculated CRI rate under about 2 mL/hr on a standard syringe pump
- **Usually means:** The undiluted drug concentration is too high for the ordered dose to land in the pump's accurate flow range.
- **First question:** Should this drug be diluted to raise the rate into the pump's rated accuracy range before programming it?
- **Data to pull:** Pump manufacturer's minimum accurate flow rate, drug concentration on hand, ordered dose.

### Drug dose or CRI rate that "looks about right" without re-derivation from the vial concentration
- **Usually means:** The number was recalled from a similar patient rather than calculated from this patient's weight and this vial's concentration — the setup for a 10x decimal error.
- **First question:** Does re-deriving mL or mL/hr from the concentration printed on the vial in hand match the number about to be given?
- **Data to pull:** Vial/bag concentration, patient's current weight, the order as written.

### Probing depth exceeding 3 mm (dog) or 1 mm (cat) at any site
- **Usually means:** Subgingival attachment loss not visible from surface calculus — potential Stage 2–4 periodontal disease at that site.
- **First question:** Does this site need a dental radiograph before deciding whether it's scale-only or extraction?
- **Data to pull:** Full-mouth probing chart, dental radiograph if taken, AVDC stage assigned at prior visits if any.

### Radiographic image washed out or too dark twice on the same technique-chart setting
- **Usually means:** Patient's actual tissue thickness at the site doesn't match the chart's assumed thickness for that species/weight class; second most likely is a generator calibration drift.
- **First question:** What does the caliper say the thickness actually is at the collimated site, right now?
- **Data to pull:** Caliper measurement at the site, technique chart, generator service/calibration log.

### PCV below 20% or TP below 3.5 g/dL on pre-anesthetic screen
- **Usually means:** Anemia or hypoproteinemia that changes the anesthesia risk calculus — the veterinarian may want to delay, adjust protocol, or investigate before elective sedation.
- **First question:** Has the veterinarian seen this result before drugs are drawn up?
- **Data to pull:** PCV/TP result, patient history (chronic disease, recent bloodwork trend), ASA status on the chart.

### Doppler or oscillometric systolic pressure below 90 mmHg (dog) sustained under anesthesia
- **Usually means:** Anesthetic-associated hypotension from vasodilation or depth, or early hypovolemia.
- **First question:** Is the vaporizer setting appropriate for the current plane, and has a fluid bolus been considered?
- **Data to pull:** Concurrent HR/plane assessment, fluid rate running, blood loss estimate if surgical.

### Recovery extubation delayed more than about 20 minutes past the expected reflex-return window for the drug protocol used
- **Usually means:** Drug metabolism slower than expected (hypothermia, hepatic/renal status) or an unrecognized residual deep plane.
- **First question:** Is the patient normothermic, and is swallow/cough reflex actually present yet?
- **Data to pull:** Temperature trend during recovery, drugs and doses given, time since last dose.
