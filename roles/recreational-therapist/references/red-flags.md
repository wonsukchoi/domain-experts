# Red Flags

### RT chart notes read as activity descriptions (not goal language) in more than half a facility's audited charts
- **Usually means:** staff are documenting for the family/shift log, not for the interdisciplinary record — often a training gap after a new hire's onboarding skipped the goal-writing template.
- **First question:** "If I read this note with the goal section covered, could I still tell what deficit this session targeted?"
- **Data to pull:** last 10 RT progress notes across 3 different staff members; check each for a named linked discipline goal.

### RT minutes added to a schedule the same week a facility's therapy-hours total is short, with no goal citation in the note
- **Usually means:** RT is being used to pad an intensity requirement (e.g., IRF 3-hour rule) rather than to deliver a coordinated intervention — the single most common trigger for a payer or surveyor to strike an entire week's RT minutes.
- **First question:** "Which PT/OT/SLP goal does this specific session's content target, and is that goal named in the note?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly interdisciplinary minutes summary plus the RT note for the same period; confirm the coordinated-goal citation is present, not just co-located timing.

### Group attendance logged at 3+ consecutive sessions with no re-assessment score recorded
- **Usually means:** the team is tracking presence, not progress — the outcome measure picked at intake has quietly stopped being administered.
- **First question:** "What was this client's score on the intake instrument, and when was it last repeated?"
- **Data to pull:** the client's assessment history; compare dates against the facility's stated re-assessment interval.

### Client plateaus on the same outcome measure across two consecutive re-assessment periods
- **Usually means:** the intervention or goal difficulty hasn't changed even though the score has stopped moving — a design problem, not a motivation problem, and repeating the same activity with more encouragement won't move it.
- **First question:** "What has changed about the activity or the goal since the last unchanged score?"
- **Data to pull:** the task-grading log (which rung the client is working at) for the last 4 sessions.

### Behavioral/dementia client consistently disengages in groups above roughly 1:8 facilitator ratio
- **Usually means:** the facilitator has lost 1:1 redirection capacity, not that the activity itself is wrong for the population.
- **First question:** "What was the actual facilitator-to-client ratio in the room, and did it match what was planned?"
- **Data to pull:** group census log for the session versus the planned roster size.

### New referral order says only "recreational therapy evaluation" with no diagnosis or functional reason stated
- **Usually means:** the referral was generated by a template/order-set default rather than a clinician's specific concern — evaluating without a stated reason produces a generic assessment that won't support a defensible goal later.
- **First question:** "What specific functional, cognitive, or psychosocial concern prompted this order?"
- **Data to pull:** the referring clinician's progress note from the date of the order.

### Family reports the client "does nothing" at home within 2–4 weeks of a documented successful discharge
- **Usually means:** the leisure-education/community-reintegration handoff was skipped or was never made concrete (no named person, program, or equipment source) — in-facility gains that don't transfer are the single most common late failure of an otherwise well-run stay.
- **First question:** "Who specifically was named at discharge as continuing this activity, and did that handoff happen?"
- **Data to pull:** the discharge summary's handoff checklist section; confirm a named person/program, not "family to continue as able."

### FIM score plateaued for more than a week while RT documentation still says "participated with cues"
- **Usually means:** the RT goal was set to track FIM improvement generically instead of a specific RT-owned deficit, so RT has nothing distinct to show once the shared functional score stalls.
- **First question:** "What is the RT-specific goal here, separate from the FIM number PT/OT already track?"
- **Data to pull:** the RT goal statement; check whether it duplicates a PT/OT goal instead of naming an independent target.

### A weekly intensity-requirement summary shows a total just under the required threshold with no explanatory note
- **Usually means:** either a scheduling gap that will trigger a compliance flag, or a documentation gap where minutes were delivered but not logged.
- **First question:** "Is this a delivery gap or a documentation gap?"
- **Data to pull:** the raw session sign-in sheets for the week versus what was entered in the minutes summary.

### CTRS time split shows more than half spent on non-billable administrative or program-maintenance tasks versus direct client contact
- **Usually means:** understaffing is being absorbed silently as reduced client contact rather than escalated as a staffing problem.
- **First question:** "Is this a temporary coverage gap or the standing caseload structure?"
- **Data to pull:** a 2-week time-use log broken into direct contact, documentation, and program administration.
