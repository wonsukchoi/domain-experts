# Medical Assistant — Red Flags

### BP ≥160 systolic or ≥100 diastolic on intake
- **Usually means:** Uncontrolled hypertension, medication non-adherence (missed doses, ran out of refill), or white-coat effect — in that rough order of likelihood for an established hypertension patient.
- **First question:** "When did you last take your blood pressure medication?"
- **Data to pull:** Medication list/refill history, prior visit BP trend.

### O2 saturation <92% on room air
- **Usually means:** Acute respiratory issue, undiagnosed chronic condition flare, or a faulty pulse-ox reading (poor perfusion, nail polish, cold extremity).
- **First question:** "Are you having any trouble breathing right now?"
- **Data to pull:** Recheck on a different finger/site before treating as confirmed; prior O2 sat baseline if available.

### Standing order doesn't specify dose/route/site for this patient's situation
- **Usually means:** The order was written for the typical case and this patient is an edge case (pediatric, unusual body habitus, known allergy/contraindication).
- **First question:** "Does this order actually cover this specific situation, or am I inferring?"
- **Data to pull:** Full standing-order text, patient's allergy/contraindication list.

### Patient's stated symptom doesn't match the scheduled reason for visit
- **Usually means:** New/acute issue since scheduling, or the original reason was miscommunicated at booking.
- **First question:** "You're here for X today — is that still the main thing, or has something changed?"
- **Data to pull:** Original scheduling reason vs. patient's current statement, both documented.

### EKG shows an unexpected pattern the physician flags as "doesn't look right"
- **Usually means:** Lead misplacement (most common), patient movement/muscle artifact, or an actual cardiac finding — check the mechanical causes before assuming pathology.
- **First question:** "Can you verify lead placement and retake it?"
- **Data to pull:** Photo/note of lead placement used, patient position during recording.

### Injection site shows unexpected pain, numbness, or bruising during/after administration
- **Usually means:** Nerve proximity (site selection error), vessel puncture, or technique issue (wrong angle/depth).
- **First question:** "Is the numbness/pain still present, and where exactly?"
- **Data to pull:** Exact site used, needle gauge/length, patient's baseline coagulation status if on anticoagulants.

### A task request from a physician or patient falls outside the written standing order
- **Usually means:** Genuine oversight in the order, a one-off verbal request that hasn't been formalized, or (rarely) a request that shouldn't be happening at all.
- **First question:** "Can you confirm this in the standing order, or should I get a specific order for it?"
- **Data to pull:** Current standing-order document, verbal-order documentation policy.

### Patient reports a new medication or supplement not on the current med list
- **Usually means:** Prescribed elsewhere without this practice's knowledge, over-the-counter/supplement use the patient didn't think to mention, or a med-list documentation gap.
- **First question:** "What is it, how much, and how long have you been taking it?"
- **Data to pull:** Full name/dose/frequency, prescriber if known, interaction check against current med list before physician visit.

### Repeated no-shows or missed refills for a chronic-condition patient
- **Usually means:** Access barrier (cost, transportation), health literacy gap, or a symptom the patient hasn't disclosed (e.g., side effects causing self-discontinuation).
- **First question:** "Has anything made it hard to keep up with the medication or appointments?"
- **Data to pull:** No-show/refill history pattern, not just the most recent gap.
