# Playbook

Filled sequences and templates for the checks a school bus driver runs every route. Numbers below are FMCSA federal baselines and commonly-used state pupil-transportation code conventions — an individual district's or state's own procedures control where they differ.

## 1. Pre-trip inspection checklist (school-bus-specific items)

Run before pulling out of the yard, every shift:

| Item | Check | Pass criterion | If failed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stop arm | Full extend/retract cycle | Extends fully, red lights sync with extension | Hold bus, log defect, call for substitute |
| Amber/red light sequence | Cycle through amber-only, then red+arm | Amber activates independently before red+arm | Hold bus if red/arm can't be isolated from amber |
| Crossview mirrors (FMVSS 111) | Visual check from driver's seat, all zones | Danger zone in front and both sides visible with normal seat position | Adjust before departure; do not defer |
| Crossing-control arm | Full extend/retract with stop arm | Extends in sync with stop arm | Hold if it fails to extend — no substitute for the sightline it forces |
| Interior/child-check reminder system | Functional test (alarm or placard reminder) | Alarm sounds/reminder triggers at engine shutoff or door-open-last sequence | Log defect; do not treat as optional on routes without the alarm hardware |
| Emergency exits (rear door, roof hatches, window releases) | Functional check | All open/release without excess force | Hold bus |

## 2. Ten-step loading/unloading sequence (timed)

Run at every stop where a student boards or exits:

| Step | Action | Typical time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Activate amber warning lights at state-mandated distance before stop (commonly 100–300 ft depending on posted speed) | — (distance-based, not time-based) |
| 2 | Decelerate, scan mirrors | 3–5 sec |
| 3 | Come to a full stop | — |
| 4 | Extend stop arm, switch amber to red lights (only once fully stopped) | 1–2 sec |
| 5 | Open door | 1 sec |
| 6 | Run danger-zone mirror/visual scan (below) before releasing any student | 5 sec |
| 7 | Release student(s) already seated at the door; hold any student needing to cross | — |
| 8 | For a crossing student, signal to cross **in front of** the bus only, after confirming both directions clear | 3–5 sec |
| 9 | Confirm student(s) clear of the danger zone (on the curb/sidewalk, not lingering at the bumper) | 2–3 sec |
| 10 | Close door, retract stop arm/red lights, check mirrors again, proceed | 2–3 sec |

A student instructed to cross behind the bus is a procedural failure regardless of outcome — the driver has no sightline there under any mirror configuration.

## 3. Danger-zone mirror/visual scan sequence

Run before releasing any student from step 6 above, in this order:

1. Interior mirror — confirm no student out of seat or in the aisle before opening the door.
2. Left crossview mirror — the zone along the left side and toward the front-left danger zone.
3. Right crossview mirror — the zone along the right side and toward the front-right danger zone.
4. **Zone directly in front of the bus, through the windshield** — the wedge a seated driver's eye height over the hood cannot show in any mirror; requires an active head movement/lean, not a glance.
5. Curb-side door mirror/camera — confirm the immediate boarding area is clear.
6. Final sweep of the full danger zone (front, both sides, roughly 10 feet out) before any release signal.

The zone is roughly 10 feet around the entire bus, per NASDPTS's "Understanding the Danger Zone" guidance — a distance chosen because it approximates the area where a child under typical elementary-school height falls below the driver's forward and mirror sightlines simultaneously.

## 4. Railroad-crossing stop procedure (49 CFR §392.10)

Execute at every railroad-highway grade crossing with students aboard, regardless of visible signals or gates:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Stop between 15 and 50 feet before the nearest rail |
| 2 | Place transmission in a gear that will not require a shift while crossing |
| 3 | Open door and/or window to listen for an approaching train |
| 4 | Visually check both directions along the track |
| 5 | Proceed only when clear; do not shift gears while crossing the track(s) |

Exception: crossings with an official flagger or a functioning automatic gate/signal already actively stopping traffic and a designated "exempt crossing" sign, per the applicable state code — absent both conditions, the stop is mandatory even with no train visible.

## 5. Stop-arm violation report (radio/written template)

> "[Dispatch], Bus [#], Route [#]. Stop-arm violation at [location] — [vehicle description, color/make], [direction of travel], [approximate time]. [Any injury: yes/no]. Logging for the transportation office; will file written report at [depot]."

Never pursue, flash lights at, or verbally confront the violating vehicle. Complete the loading/unloading sequence for students already at the door first; log the violation only once the danger zone is clear.

## 6. Behavior-incident escalation ladder (no administrator aboard)

| Level | Behavior | Driver response |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Minor disruption | Talking out of seat, loud noise | Verbal redirect, name the student, state the expectation once |
| 2. Repeated/defiant | Ignoring redirect, out-of-seat while moving | Seat reassignment at next stop; log the incident on the driver report |
| 3. Conflict between students | Verbal altercation, shoving | Radio call to transportation supervisor while continuing to drive safely; separate seating at next stop |
| 4. Imminent danger | Physical fight, object thrown at driver, attempt to exit moving bus | Pull over safely if possible, radio dispatch/law enforcement immediately; physical intervention reserved for this level only |

A seating-chart change is the first-line tool at level 2, not a last resort — it's the only intervention available without stopping the bus or diverting attention from driving.
