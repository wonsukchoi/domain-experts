# Red flags

Smell tests a deck or engine-room rating (or the mate/engineer supervising one) catches before a mooring injury, a missed casualty precursor, or a compliance lapse happens. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before committing to an action.

### Personnel standing on the direct line of pull or within the surrounding arc of a mooring line under load

- **Usually means:** the snap-back zone hasn't been identified or cleared before tension came on, not that the position is actually safe because the line "looks fine."
- **First question:** "Where exactly is everyone standing relative to the line's path, and has that zone actually been called clear?"
- **Data to pull:** current tension reading against the line's stated MBL, fairlead-to-fitting span, positions of all hands on deck.

### Mooring line tension at or above roughly 50% of its stated minimum breaking load (MBL) with no easing planned

- **Usually means:** the load is reaching a working limit that calls for easing or additional tending, not a number to watch passively until it either holds or parts.
- **First question:** "What's the current tension as a percentage of MBL, and what's the plan if it climbs further?"
- **Data to pull:** line specification and MBL, current tension estimate, wind/current conditions driving the load.

### A riding turn (overlapping wraps) forming on a loaded winch drum

- **Usually means:** the line is about to jam or surge unpredictably, and reaching in to clear it while under tension is how hands get caught or crushed.
- **First question:** "Can this be eased and cleared from the slack side, or does tension need to come off first?"
- **Data to pull:** current drum load, whether the line can be surged, position of all hands relative to the drum.

### Gauge-round reading deviating more than roughly 5% from the mean of comparable units or from its own prior-watch baseline

- **Usually means:** an early-stage mechanical issue on that specific unit, not noise in the instrument — but the rating's job stops at reporting the number, not diagnosing the cause.
- **First question:** "What's the reading, what's the baseline it's being compared against, and has it been reported yet?"
- **Data to pull:** full round's readings for all comparable units, prior-watch log for the same round, timestamp of the report to the officer/engineer of the watch.

### Sounding or ullage result inconsistent with the last logged tank transfer by a meaningful margin

- **Usually means:** either an unlogged transfer, a leak, or a sounding error — not a discrepancy to average away or round to the expected number.
- **First question:** "What does the transfer log say this tank should read, and how far off is the actual sounding?"
- **Data to pull:** last transfer log entry, current sounding/ullage reading, tank capacity table.

### Gauge round or sounding log shows an entry that reads as estimated rather than actually walked

- **Usually means:** the round wasn't physically completed this watch, whether from time pressure or fatigue — the log is the only record anyone else has that the check happened at all.
- **First question:** "Was every gauge and tank actually read this round, or were any values carried forward?"
- **Data to pull:** round timestamps against expected walk duration, comparison of consecutive entries for suspiciously identical values.

### Paint holiday (bare-metal spot) found below the waterline or inside a ballast/void tank

- **Usually means:** active corrosion is already running at a faster rate than the same finding topside, because of continuous salt-water contact.
- **First question:** "Is this holiday in an immersed or intermittently wetted area, and how urgent does that make the repair relative to the current work order?"
- **Data to pull:** location and extent of the holiday, tank/immersion status, last coating survey for that compartment.

### Chip/prime/paint cycle compressed to same-day primer and topcoat against the data sheet's recoat window

- **Usually means:** the section will pass today's inspection and fail within months, because the coating didn't get its specified cure time.
- **First question:** "What does the data sheet say the recoat window is, and did this section actually get it?"
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer data sheet recoat window and ambient-temperature adjustment, actual elapsed time between coats as logged.

### Crew turnover exceeding roughly 25% since the last departure with no off-cycle abandon-ship/fire drill logged within 24 hours

- **Usually means:** the drill schedule is running on the routine monthly cycle only, missing the separate SOLAS trigger tied to crew change.
- **First question:** "What's the actual turnover percentage this port call, and has the 24-hour drill requirement been logged?"
- **Data to pull:** crew sign-on/sign-off list this port call, drill log timestamps, departure time.

### Lifeboat not lowered/launched within the SOLAS 3-month interval

- **Usually means:** the drill schedule has drifted past a hard maximum, not just a target date, and the lapse itself is a reportable deficiency.
- **First question:** "What was the date of the last actual launch, not just a lowering-to-embarkation-deck drill, and how many days remain against the 3-month limit?"
- **Data to pull:** lifeboat drill log with launch (not just lowering) dates, current date, days remaining against the interval.

### A rating adjusting, resetting, or repairing engine-room or deck equipment beyond assigned rounds/watch duty without an officer's or engineer's order

- **Usually means:** initiative has crossed into diagnostic or repair authority the rating isn't certificated for, removing the escalation check the watch system depends on.
- **First question:** "Was this adjustment ordered by the officer or engineer of the watch, or decided independently?"
- **Data to pull:** watch orders log, the specific equipment and adjustment made, whether it was reported before or after the fact.
