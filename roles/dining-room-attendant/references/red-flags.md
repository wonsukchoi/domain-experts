# Red flags

Smells a shift lead or manager should catch on a single walk-through, with the first question to ask and the data to pull before making a staffing or coaching decision.

### Table dead time (check-closed → next-seated) exceeds ~2× the target reset time

**Usually means:** attendant-to-table ratio is below the operation's peak par (roughly 1:12–15), so bussing/resetting is queuing rather than happening as checks close; second most likely is a reset sequence that's drifted into an unnecessarily formal template (linen changes, full silverware rolls) for parties that didn't need one.

**First question:** "What's the actual attendant-to-table ratio right now, and is the reset being done to the incoming party size or to a default template?"

**Data to pull:** POS table-status timestamps (check-closed to next-seated) for the shift; attendant headcount scheduled against table count for that time block; a timed sample of 5 resets against the target-time table.

### Sanitizer bucket untested for more than 2 hours, or reading below label ppm

**Usually means:** the running side-work item got silently cut under time pressure — usually the first invisible item to slip because nobody notices it until an inspection or an illness complaint.

**First question:** "When was this bucket last test-stripped, and what did it read?"

**Data to pull:** side-work checklist sign-off log for the running shift; test-strip readings if logged; a spot-check of every station's current bucket right now.

### A garnish, mixer, or ice bin hits zero during peak ("86 on limes" called mid-rush)

**Usually means:** bar-back restocking is reactive (waiting to be asked) instead of par-triggered; second most likely is the par sheet itself is undersized for that night's covers (e.g., a private event pushed volume above the standard Friday par).

**First question:** "What was the par for this item tonight, and when did it cross the half-par restock trigger?"

**Data to pull:** par sheet for the shift vs. actual covers served; time the item was last restocked vs. time it ran out; whether tonight's covers were forecast above standard par (event, holiday, promo).

### Closing checklist consistently incomplete on the same items, night after night

**Usually means:** understaffing that's being silently absorbed rather than escalated — check whether the incomplete items are cosmetic (acceptable short-term triage) or safety/stock items (not acceptable, regardless of staffing).

**First question:** "Which specific items are incomplete, and are they the triage-last cosmetic items or something that should never be cut?"

**Data to pull:** signed-off checklist boards for the last 2 weeks; which items recur as incomplete; staffing levels on those nights vs. par.

### Attendant-to-table ratio drifts below ~1:15 at peak without a corresponding staffing change

**Usually means:** the floor grew (new tables added, a patio opened) without side-work/bussing coverage scaling with it, or a call-out wasn't backfilled.

**First question:** "Has table count changed recently, or is this a scheduling gap from an uncovered shift?"

**Data to pull:** current table count vs. count when the staffing model was last set; schedule vs. actual clock-ins for the shift; dead-time trend over the same period.

### Wiping cloths found dry, in an apron pocket, or draped over a shoulder instead of in the sanitizer bucket

**Usually means:** a training gap, not a one-off lapse — this is the highest cross-contamination risk in the role because the same cloth touches every table it's carried to.

**First question:** "Where does this attendant store their cloth between tables, and who trained them on the sanitizer-bucket rule?"

**Data to pull:** most recent training checklist sign-off for that attendant; a walk of the floor to see how many stations show the same pattern (isolated vs. systemic).

### Bar backup stock (kegs, call-brand bottles) reads at zero on the par sheet mid-shift

**Usually means:** the par sheet's restock trigger (second-to-last unit) wasn't followed, or the storeroom/keg cooler wasn't stocked to par at the start of the week — check whether this is a bar-back execution gap or an ordering gap upstream.

**First question:** "Was this item below its restock trigger before service started, or did it deplete faster than par during the shift?"

**Data to pull:** opening inventory count vs. par sheet; depletion rate against a typical Friday; purchase order history for the item.

### An attendant is observed taking a guest's order, suggesting a menu item, or handling a complaint

**Usually means:** role creep, usually well-intentioned (trying to help a buried server) but it removes accountability — the guest's expectation is now set by someone with no check access or authority to resolve a problem.

**First question:** "Did a server ask them to do this, or did they self-initiate?"

**Data to pull:** none needed beyond direct observation; the fix is a training conversation, not a data pull — but repeat occurrences across shifts suggest a staffing gap on the server side that's pushing work downward.

### Bussing is loud or visually disruptive during service (tray-sprinting, tub-banging, crumbing mid-course at an adjacent table)

**Usually means:** the attendant is racing a backlog rather than working a sequence — fast-but-visible is a symptom of being behind, not of being efficient.

**First question:** "How many tables are currently backed up behind this one?"

**Data to pull:** current dirty-table count on the floor; attendant's position in the room-scan rotation; whether this is a one-off surge (a large party just left) or a sustained pattern.

### Walk-in wait time climbs while the host stand reports tables as "available soon" for an extended stretch

**Usually means:** tables are sitting in dead time (bussed-but-not-reset, or reset-but-not-flagged-ready) rather than actually turning — the host stand's optimism is based on visual impression, not the POS table-status flag.

**First question:** "Pull the POS table-status log — how long has each 'available soon' table actually been in that state?"

**Data to pull:** table-status change log for the last hour; count of tables stuck in a pending state vs. genuinely mid-reset; attendant coverage for that section right now.
