# Red Flags

### Device unused for its intended task more than 3 months after dispensing
- **Usually means:** magnification was under- or over-prescribed relative to the task (see the acuity-reserve calculation), illumination/contrast wasn't addressed alongside magnification, or psychosocial adjustment was never resolved and the client quietly disengaged.
- **First question:** "When did you last actually use it, and for what?"
- **Data to pull:** device follow-up/outcome log, original magnification calculation vs. task requirement, task-lighting notes from the fitting session.

### Chart acuity and reported functional performance disagree (e.g., 20/70 on the chart but can't recognize a face at 10 feet)
- **Usually means:** an undiagnosed or unmeasured contrast-sensitivity or glare deficit that the acuity chart doesn't capture, or the failing task happens in lighting/contrast conditions never replicated during the clinic exam.
- **First question:** "What's the lighting and background like in the situation where this happens?"
- **Data to pull:** Pelli-Robson or Mars contrast sensitivity result, glare test result, a lux reading from the actual environment.

### Client reschedules or stalls at the same training milestone three or more times
- **Usually means:** unresolved psychosocial adjustment (denial, fear, unprocessed grief) rather than a scheduling problem — pushing the skill again without addressing this repeats the stall.
- **First question:** "What happens in your head right before we'd start that lesson?"
- **Data to pull:** psychosocial intake notes, any existing referral to counseling or peer-support groups.

### Cane arc consistently narrower than shoulder width during observed travel
- **Usually means:** insufficient tactile coverage of the path ahead, a real fall/drop-off risk, not simply "needs more practice" — this is a technique defect, not a fluency gap.
- **First question:** observe directly; measure arc width against a marked reference during a timed trial.
- **Data to pull:** session observation log with arc-width and tip-placement notes across at least three trials.

### New referral's most recent ophthalmology visit is more than 12 months old and the diagnosis is progressive
- **Usually means:** the acuity/field data the training plan would be built on is stale; a route, device, or driving-eligibility plan based on it may already not match the client's current vision.
- **First question:** "When was your last eye exam, and did anything change since then?"
- **Data to pull:** current ophthalmology report, most recent visual field test date.

### Bioptic-driving candidate's telescope provides a field of view under 6° at the magnification they intend to drive with
- **Usually means:** the device is mismatched to the candidate's needs or the state's statutory minimum, not a training gap — no amount of practice widens an optical field of view.
- **First question:** "What field of view and power does this bioptic actually provide, checked against this state's statute?"
- **Data to pull:** the current state DMV bioptic-driving program requirements, the device manufacturer's spec sheet.

### Family reports "stopped going out alone" but no fall or incident triggered it
- **Usually means:** self-imposed activity restriction driven by fear or emerging depression, not a documented safety event — a common precursor to isolation that a standard ADL checklist won't surface on its own.
- **First question:** "What specific outings stopped, and when did that start?"
- **Data to pull:** an activity/participation log over the last 3–6 months, and consider a depression screen referral (e.g., PHQ-9) if isolation is new and unexplained.

### Client needs progressively brighter light for the same task over a period of weeks
- **Usually means:** the underlying condition is progressing faster than the current device/adaptation plan accounts for, not that the client simply needs encouragement to use more light.
- **First question:** "Has the amount of light you need for this task noticeably gone up in the last month?"
- **Data to pull:** illumination log compared across sessions, an updated ophthalmology report.

### Case file goal language is generic ("improve independence," "increase confidence") with no trial data after two or more sessions
- **Usually means:** the training plan itself may be undirected, and the documentation will not survive a funding-agency audit regardless of whether real progress is happening.
- **First question:** "What specific task, in what environment, succeeded in how many of how many trials?"
- **Data to pull:** the Individualized Written Rehabilitation Program (IWRP) goal sheet and session-by-session trial counts.

### Guide-dog candidate whose stated daily travel pace and environment (e.g., under 2 mph, low-traffic rural setting) doesn't match a working guide dog's typical fit criteria
- **Usually means:** the choice is being driven by preference or image rather than fit, which is a leading cause of early dog return to the training school.
- **First question:** "Walk me through a typical day's travel — distance, pace, and how much is on quiet vs. busy routes."
- **Data to pull:** the lifestyle/mobility-needs assessment, and the specific guide-dog school's published intake criteria.
