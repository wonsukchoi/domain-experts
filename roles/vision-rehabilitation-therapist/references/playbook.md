# Playbook

Filled templates and worked tables for the four recurring artifacts: low-vision device selection, O&M skill progression, bioptic-driving eligibility comparison, and pediatric ECC prioritization.

## 1. Magnification calculation (low-vision device selection)

Formula: **Required magnification = (current acuity denominator ÷ target acuity denominator) × reading-fluency reserve.**

Reserve factor: 1x for bare letter/threshold identification tasks (e.g., "can they see the doorbell button"); 2x for sustained fluent reading (labels, mail, short text); 3x for high-precision or safety-critical tasks (insulin dosing, medication label fine print).

| Client near acuity | Target task acuity | Base magnification | Task type → reserve | Required device power |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20/100 | 20/40 | 100/40 = 2.5x | Mail, casual reading → 2x | 5x |
| 20/200 | 20/40 | 200/40 = 5x | Prescription labels → 2x | 10x |
| 20/400 | 20/40 | 400/40 = 10x | Insulin dosing → 3x | 30x (electronic, not optical — see device table) |
| 20/70 | 20/40 | 70/40 = 1.75x | Large-print book → 1x | 1.75x (round up to nearest stocked power: 2x) |

**Device-power ceiling rule:** optical hand/stand magnifiers become impractical above ~12–16x (field of view collapses below a usable working distance); anything the table calls for above that goes to an electronic video magnifier/CCTV, which decouples magnification from field of view via a camera and screen.

| Task | Device class | Typical power range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signage, spotting (intermittent, brief use) | Bioptic telescope | 2x–4x, field of view 6°–16° | Mounted in superior lens position; used briefly, not continuously worn as primary correction. |
| Mail, labels, short text | Illuminated stand or hand magnifier | 4x–12x | Needs task lighting ≈300–600 lux; illumination substitutes for some magnification when contrast sensitivity is reduced. |
| Sustained reading, high magnification (>12x) | Portable or desktop video magnifier (CCTV) | 12x–60x+ | Controls contrast (reverse polarity) and lighting independently of magnification — required when Pelli-Robson score is materially below normal (≈1.65–2.00 log units). |

## 2. O&M skill-progression sequence (adventitiously blinded adult, no useful travel vision)

| Stage | Milestone | Advance when | Typical session count |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Indoor cane skills | Two-point touch technique, arc ≥ shoulder width, consistent tip placement outside each foot before it lands | 3/3 trials clean technique across two room layouts | 2–4 sessions |
| 2. Sighted-guide fade | Client initiates and releases sighted-guide grip appropriately; transitions to independent cane use indoors | Independent indoor travel with no guide contact, 3/3 trials | 2–3 sessions |
| 3. Quiet residential route | Independent travel on a known low-traffic route, curb and driveway detection | 3/3 independent trials, no missed drop-offs | 3–5 sessions |
| 4. Business-district route | Independent travel with unpredictable pedestrian/obstacle density | 3/3 independent trials across varied conditions (rain, crowd) | 3–6 sessions |
| 5. Signaled intersection crossing | Independently judges parallel-traffic surge as a crossing cue, or uses accessible pedestrian signal (APS) audio, completes crossing within the signal's timed interval | 3/3 independent, correctly-timed crossings at 2 different intersection types | 4–8 sessions |

**Route-selection default:** for a client with usable residual vision (not the "no useful travel vision" case above), start at stage 3 equivalent with an adapted mobility device (support cane, identification cane) rather than working through stages 1–2, which are built around clients with no visual feedback to cross-check technique against.

## 3. Bioptic-driving eligibility — state comparison (illustrative; verify current statute before advising)

| State | Unaided/base acuity requirement | Acuity through bioptic | Visual field requirement | Behind-the-wheel training |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Illinois | 20/100 or better in better eye (unaided) | 20/40 or better through telescope | 140° combined | Program-specific, evaluator-determined |
| Indiana | — | 20/40 or better through bioptic system | 120° combined (one or both eyes) | Program-specific, evaluator-determined |
| Pennsylvania | — | Per PennDOT bioptic program | 120° bilateral horizontal meridian | Minimum 20 hours with a PennDOT-approved CDI/CDRS low vision rehabilitation specialist |

As of the most recent multi-state reviews, roughly 45 states plus DC permit bioptic driving under a defined program; Arkansas, Iowa, Kansas, Nebraska, and Wyoming do not. **Never advise a client on driving eligibility from this table alone — confirm the current statute with the state DMV's bioptic/low-vision driving program directly**, since thresholds and program existence change.

## 4. Expanded Core Curriculum (ECC) — pediatric prioritization matrix

Nine domains; not all are equal priority for every case. Filled example for a 9-year-old with congenital low vision, mainstreamed, no additional disabilities:

| ECC domain | Priority this IEP cycle | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Orientation & mobility | High | Independent travel between classrooms not yet established |
| Sensory/visual efficiency skills | High | Functional vision assessment shows inconsistent use of residual vision for near tasks |
| Compensatory/functional academic skills | High | Needs large-print and assistive-tech accommodations formalized before next grade transition |
| Assistive technology | Medium | Screen-magnification software introduced but not yet fluent |
| Social interaction skills | Medium | Peer reports of isolation at unstructured times (recess) |
| Independent living skills | Low this cycle | Age-appropriate for stage; revisit at middle-school transition |
| Self-determination | Low this cycle | Will become high priority approaching IEP transition-planning age (14+) |
| Recreation & leisure | Low this cycle | Already engaged in an accessible after-school activity |
| Career education | Not applicable yet | Revisit at secondary level |

**Rule of thumb:** re-run this matrix at every IEP cycle and at every school transition (elementary → middle → high) — priority domains shift predictably with age even when the visual diagnosis is stable.
