# Red flags

### Wax pattern built to the design's nominal target dimension with no alloy-specific shrinkage allowance applied
- **Usually means:** the finished cast piece will come out undersized by roughly the alloy's shrink percentage.
- **First question:** what shrinkage rate was actually applied for this specific karat and alloy?
- **Data to pull:** the wax spec sheet compared against the alloy's standard shrink rate.

### Stone setting judged secure by visual appearance alone, with no loupe or tap-test verification of metal thickness
- **Usually means:** the setting may be under-metaled at the actual retention points despite looking tight.
- **First question:** does the prong or bezel metal thickness at the retention points actually meet the minimum standard for this setting style?
- **Data to pull:** loupe inspection notes and tap-test result compared against the minimum metal thickness standard.

### Metal weight in (stock, solder, findings) doesn't reconcile with finished piece weight plus recovered scrap, beyond the shop's normal tolerance
- **Usually means:** either a measurement/recording error, or actual unaccounted metal loss.
- **First question:** what does the job's full metal log show — weights in versus weights out — against the shop's stated reconciliation tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** the job's metal log compared against the shop's standard reconciliation tolerance.

### Ultrasonic cleaning or heat applied to a stone without checking its specific hardness, cleavage, or heat-sensitivity profile first
- **Usually means:** risk of irreversible stone damage — fracturing, color change — from a process that's safe for harder, more stable stones but not this one.
- **First question:** what is this specific stone's known sensitivity to heat or ultrasonic vibration?
- **Data to pull:** the gemstone's identification and its known handling-sensitivity reference.

### Karat purity assumed from a stamped mark with no verification (acid test or electronic test) on an unfamiliar, resized, or repaired piece
- **Usually means:** the actual alloy composition may not match the stamp, especially on older or altered pieces where solder or prior repairs can shift the true composition.
- **First question:** does the actual karat test result match the stamped mark?
- **Data to pull:** the karat test result compared against the stamped karat mark.

### Pricing quoted using the piece's total weight at a flat per-gram rate rather than actual pure-gold-content weight
- **Usually means:** over- or under-pricing, depending on which direction the flat-rate approximation errs relative to the piece's actual karat.
- **First question:** what does the karat fraction times total weight actually work out to at current market price?
- **Data to pull:** karat fraction, total weight, and current market price per pure gram.

### A repair or resize proceeds on a piece with a stone already showing a visible fracture or inclusion near the girdle, without flagging the elevated breakage risk
- **Usually means:** proceeding without disclosing a risk inherent to the stone's existing condition, distinct from the technique being used.
- **First question:** what did the pre-repair stone inspection actually note about this stone's condition?
- **Data to pull:** pre-repair stone inspection notes compared against the work actually performed.
