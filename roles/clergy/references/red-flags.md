# Clergy — Red Flags

### Disclosure includes a suicide plan, method, or timeline
- **Usually means:** Active suicidal ideation has moved from passive ("I wish I weren't here") to a specific plan — the single highest-acuity signal in pastoral care.
- **First question:** "Do you have a way you'd do this, and have you thought about when?"
- **Data to pull:** None to pull — act immediately: engage a crisis line (e.g., 988) or emergency services, and do not leave the person alone until handoff is complete.

### Disclosure describes physical injury to a minor or dependent adult from a caregiver
- **Usually means:** Abuse or neglect requiring a mandatory report — regardless of the confessional framing the disclosure was made in.
- **First question:** "Does my jurisdiction's clergy-privilege statute cover this specific type of disclosure, or does the reporting duty override it?"
- **Data to pull:** The current state/jurisdiction's clergy-privilege statute text and mandatory-reporting requirements, checked before responding, not assumed.

### A person asks you to promise confidentiality before telling you anything
- **Usually means:** They're testing the boundary, or they've been burned by a prior confidence being broken — but an unconditional promise you can't legally keep sets up a worse breach later.
- **First question:** Can I state the actual scope of what I can and can't keep confidential before they disclose, so the promise I make is one I can keep?
- **Data to pull:** None — this is a disclosure-boundary conversation to have before the substantive disclosure, not a data question.

### Symptoms of depression, mania, or psychosis persist beyond 2-4 weeks with functional impairment
- **Usually means:** Clinical, not situational, distress — pastoral support alone is not the appropriate primary intervention past this point.
- **First question:** Has this affected your ability to work, sleep, eat, or care for yourself, and for how long?
- **Data to pull:** Self-reported duration and functional-impairment specifics (missed work, appetite/sleep change) to inform the referral urgency.

### A congregational conflict has been escalating for more than one board cycle without resolution
- **Usually means:** The presenting complaint is a symptom of an unaddressed systemic anxiety source (e.g., a leadership change, a financial shortfall) rather than the real issue.
- **First question:** What changed in the congregation's life in the weeks before this conflict became visible?
- **Data to pull:** A timeline of recent staffing, financial, or leadership changes against the conflict's escalation timeline.

### An officiant is asked to perform a ceremony with legal effect on short notice (under 2 weeks)
- **Usually means:** Licensing or filing paperwork may not be confirmed, risking a ceremony that isn't legally valid.
- **First question:** Has the required license or paperwork already been filed, and can you show me confirmation?
- **Data to pull:** The jurisdiction's specific filing deadline and required documentation for the ceremony type.

### Volunteer program relies on the same 2-3 people covering shortfalls month after month
- **Usually means:** The program is scoped beyond what the committed volunteer base can sustain, and burnout — not a recruiting shortfall — is the near-term risk.
- **First question:** What's the volunteer-hours-needed vs. volunteer-hours-committed gap, expressed as a percentage?
- **Data to pull:** Monthly volunteer-hours needed at current program scope vs. hours actually logged, over the last three months.

### A person discloses substance use that includes signs of physical dependence (withdrawal symptoms, morning use)
- **Usually means:** Substance dependence requiring a referral to medical/addiction-treatment care, not solely pastoral support.
- **First question:** Have you tried to stop or cut back, and what happened physically when you did?
- **Data to pull:** None to pull in the moment — this is a referral-triage question, not a data-gathering one; refer to an addiction-medicine or treatment resource.

### A referral is made but no follow-up is scheduled
- **Usually means:** The handoff will read as a one-time deflection rather than continued care, and the person may not follow through on the referral.
- **First question:** Did I schedule a specific follow-up date, or did I just give a name and phone number?
- **Data to pull:** None — this is a process-completeness check on the referral itself.
