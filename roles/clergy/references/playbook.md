# Clergy — Playbook

## Pastoral-triage log (filled example)

Used at the point of a disclosure, to decide spiritual-care-sufficient vs. referral, and to document within the limits of privilege.

| Field | Entry |
|---|---|
| Date / setting | Home visit, pre-scheduled |
| Presenting concern (as stated) | "I haven't been able to get out of bed most days this month." |
| Safety screen | No stated self-harm ideation; screened directly — "Have you had thoughts of hurting yourself?" — answer: "No, just tired of everything." |
| Duration/severity of symptoms | ~5 weeks, worsening; sleep and appetite both disrupted per self-report |
| Triage decision | Refer — duration, functional impairment (missed work twice), and appetite/sleep disruption exceed what pastoral support alone addresses |
| Referral made | Warm handoff to congregation's affiliated counseling network; called intake with the person present, appointment set for this week |
| Confidentiality scope disclosed | Told the person directly: "What you've shared today isn't something I'm required to report, and it stays between us — but I want to be honest that if you ever tell me about a plan to hurt yourself or someone else, I have to act on that, not keep it private." |
| Documentation | Logged: date, that a referral was made and to whom, and the safety-screen answer. Did not log clinical detail beyond what's needed to track the referral. |
| Follow-up | Scheduled a check-in call for one week after the first counseling appointment. |

## Mandatory-reporting decision path (filled example)

| Step | Question | This case |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Was the disclosure about suspected abuse of a minor or dependent adult? | Yes — a 15-year-old described ongoing physical discipline from a parent that left bruising. |
| 2 | Does the jurisdiction's clergy-privilege statute exempt this disclosure type? | No — checked; this state's statute exempts confessional/penitential communication only, not a disclosure made in a youth-group counseling conversation. |
| 3 | Is there an immediate safety concern requiring emergency contact now? | No stated ongoing immediate danger, but a mandatory-reporting duty still applies regardless. |
| 4 | Report filed | Yes — filed with the state child-welfare hotline within 24 hours per statute, before informing the family. |
| 5 | Family informed | Not before the report, per legal counsel from the denomination's risk office — informing first can be read as tipping off and creates liability. |

## Congregational-conflict mapping (filled example)

Presenting complaint: a faction wants to change the Sunday service time; another wants to keep it. Surface issue looks like a scheduling preference.

| System-mapping question | Finding |
|---|---|
| Who is triangulated? | The music director is being pulled into both camps as an ally, and is avoiding both. |
| What changed recently that raised anxiety? | A well-liked staff member left three months ago; no formal announcement of why. |
| Is the stated issue (service time) actually driving the conflict? | No — individual conversations surfaced that the real concern is uncertainty about staff turnover and whether more changes are coming. |
| Mediation approach | Individual listening sessions first (per default heuristic), then a joint congregational meeting addressing the staffing uncertainty directly, separate from the service-time question, which was tabled for a later vote once anxiety had settled. |
