# Red flags

### A pour occurring after a delay, with readiness judged by temperature alone
- **Usually means:** melt treatment fade (if applicable) may have exceeded its window even though temperature still reads adequate.
- **First question:** if a treatment (inoculation, degassing) was applied, how much time has elapsed since, versus the specified fade window?
- **Data to pull:** time since treatment, treatment's specified fade window, current temperature reading.

### Any doubt about ladle, tool, or refractory dryness before contact with molten metal
- **Usually means:** a real steam explosion risk exists that must be resolved before proceeding, not assumed away.
- **First question:** has dryness been directly verified, or is there any uncertainty being overlooked under time pressure?
- **Data to pull:** drying procedure completion record, direct verification method used.

### A ladle used without a pre-use refractory inspection
- **Usually means:** wear, cracking, or buildup that developed since the last use hasn't been checked, risking contamination or handling failure.
- **First question:** was this specific ladle inspected before this use, or assumed adequate based on its last successful pour?
- **Data to pull:** refractory inspection log, ladle usage history.

### Pour rate not matched to the specific mold/gating system's design intent
- **Usually means:** a generic "steady and careful" pace may not match what this specific gating design was engineered for.
- **First question:** does the mold/gating design specify an intended fill rate, and does actual pour rate match it?
- **Data to pull:** gating design specification, actual pour rate/timing observed.

### A casting showing porosity or sand inclusion defects
- **Usually means:** pour rate too fast for the gating design, causing turbulence and gas entrapment or mold erosion.
- **First question:** does the defect pattern match a turbulence signature, and was pour rate consistent with the gating design's intent?
- **Data to pull:** defect location/pattern, pour rate data if recorded.

### A casting showing cold shut or misrun defects
- **Usually means:** pour temperature too low at the point of pour, or pour rate too slow, causing premature solidification before full mold fill.
- **First question:** was actual pour temperature verified at the point of pour, or only furnace/tap temperature?
- **Data to pull:** pour temperature record, elapsed time since furnace tap.

### A melt treatment's fade window exceeded, with no follow-up microstructure/quality verification performed
- **Usually means:** a metallurgical degradation risk exists that visual/dimensional inspection wouldn't catch.
- **First question:** has a metallographic or equivalent check been performed on castings from this specific pour?
- **Data to pull:** treatment timing record, any follow-up quality testing performed.

### A pour crew handoff not specifying current ladle/refractory condition or treatment timing constraints
- **Usually means:** the incoming crew lacks the information needed to make correct pour-readiness decisions for melt currently in process.
- **First question:** does the handoff specify ladle condition and any active treatment timing window, or just general status?
- **Data to pull:** the handoff record, current ladle/melt status.

### A casting failing mechanical property testing despite normal-looking pour and solidification
- **Usually means:** a possible treatment fade or temperature-related metallurgical issue not visible in the pour itself.
- **First question:** does the pour record show treatment timing and temperature within spec, or a deviation that wasn't followed up on?
- **Data to pull:** the specific casting's pour record, treatment timing and temperature data.

### A refractory or ladle repair performed without a subsequent pre-use verification before returning to production
- **Usually means:** an assumption that the repair restored full adequacy without confirming it.
- **First question:** was the repaired ladle/refractory inspected and verified before its next use, or returned to service based on the repair alone?
- **Data to pull:** repair record, post-repair inspection record.
