# Red Flags — Smell Tests a Working Poet/Lyricist Catches Instantly

Each entry is a trigger for a specific, minutes-long check — not an automatic verdict.

### Reading fee over $5 for a general (non-contest) submission
- **Usually means:** the fee itself, not the acceptance rate, is the venue's actual revenue model — legitimate magazines don't charge to read general submissions.
- **First question:** "Is this a contest with a disclosed, verifiable prize pool, or a flat fee to be read?"
- **Data to pull:** the venue's CLMP membership status, fee-to-prize ratio if it's a contest, and whether the fee applies every cycle indefinitely.

### Journal open for submissions with no issue published in 2+ years
- **Usually means:** the journal is dormant in practice and still collecting submissions (and possibly fees) with no active editorial pipeline behind them.
- **First question:** "When was the most recent *published* issue — not 'forthcoming' — actually released?"
- **Data to pull:** the journal's archive/back-issues page and its ISSN registry entry if one exists.

### Anthology invitation conditions inclusion on the poet pre-purchasing a copy or slot
- **Usually means:** a vanity or pay-to-play operation, not a vetted press — legitimate anthologies pay contributors or at minimum don't charge them to appear.
- **First question:** "Does inclusion depend on payment, in any form?"
- **Data to pull:** the invitation's actual terms and whether the press has other publications without a pay-to-appear structure.

### Contract asks for "all rights" or "exclusive in perpetuity" for a $0-$50 payment
- **Usually means:** a rights grab disproportionate to what's paid — most reputable magazines request First North American Serial Rights only, reverting after publication.
- **First question:** "Can this be renegotiated to first rights, reverting to me after publication?"
- **Data to pull:** the actual rights clause in the contributor agreement, not the venue's public submission guidelines (the two sometimes differ).

### Co-writer wants to "figure out the split later"
- **Usually means:** a dispute waiting to happen — splits negotiated after a song has commercial value are adversarial in a way they never are beforehand.
- **First question:** "Can we sign the split sheet today, before we leave the session?"
- **Data to pull:** a dated, signed split sheet; if none exists, session notes reconstructing who contributed what, as soon as possible.

### A single workshop reader's note contradicts the rest of the room
- **Usually means:** a taste or persona mismatch rather than a craft flaw — unless that reader names a specific mechanical failure (broken metaphor, tense slip, meter break), it's one data point.
- **First question:** "What specifically on the page produced that reaction — which word, which line?"
- **Data to pull:** the annotated draft with all readers' notes side by side, checking whether anyone else independently flagged the same location.

### Lyric still doesn't scan against a fixed melody after 3+ rewrites
- **Usually means:** the lyric was written independent of the melody's stress pattern instead of scanning it first — the constraint was applied backwards.
- **First question:** "Has this been sung against a click track or reference vocal, or only read silently?"
- **Data to pull:** a scratch-vocal recording of the current draft against the actual melody, not a syllable count on the page.

### Same unrevised batch resubmitted after 10+ rejections
- **Usually means:** the craft issue causing the rejections is real, not a run of ten independent editors missing something — the base rate of that many unrelated misses is low.
- **First question:** "Has this had a fresh outside read since the first rejection?"
- **Data to pull:** the submission log with the draft version submitted each time — if the version hasn't changed in ten submissions, that's the finding.

### Acceptance held while other simultaneous submissions stay unnotified
- **Usually means:** a misunderstanding of etiquette — the obligation to withdraw triggers on acceptance, not on the other venues' response timing, and skipping it is tracked informally across a small editorial community.
- **First question:** "Have all other pending markets been notified within 24-48 hours of this acceptance?"
- **Data to pull:** the submission tracker showing acceptance date versus withdrawal-email dates to every other pending market.
