# Red flags

Field smell tests — what a signal usually means, the one question a veteran asks before guessing, and the data to pull before naming a cause or recommending a fix.

### Circular/patch pattern with smoke-ring-gray margins, visible in early morning dew, expanding past ~15% of lawn area in a week
- **Usually means:** fungal disease (Brown Patch/Rhizoctonia, dollar spot) favored by extended overnight leaf wetness, not drought.
- **First question:** "What time does irrigation actually run, and what's the leaf-wetness duration from that start time to sun-dry?"
- **Data to pull:** irrigation controller schedule/start times, overnight low temperatures for the past week, measured patch coverage (paced-off sq ft vs. total lawn sq ft).

### Turf lifts like loose carpet with little root resistance, and a 1-sq-ft sample turns up 8-10+ grubs
- **Usually means:** white grub (Japanese beetle/June beetle larvae) root damage.
- **First question:** "How many grubs came up in that one square foot?"
- **Data to pull:** grub count from a folded-back sample, history of adult beetle activity on the property in the prior June-July, irrigation/moisture level (grub damage often masks as drought since roots can't take up water).

### Footprints or tire tracks stay visible (blades don't spring back), blue-gray cast to the turf
- **Usually means:** drought stress — but only confirm, don't assume, since disease and grub damage can mimic this from a distance.
- **First question:** "What's the soil moisture at 4-6 inches, not just the surface?"
- **Data to pull:** soil probe reading at depth, rainfall + irrigation total for the past 2 weeks, ET estimate for the period.

### Yellow-orange patches confined to full-sun areas on St. Augustine, zoysia, or Bermuda
- **Usually means:** chinch bug damage — insects concentrate in hot, dry, sunny turf and are frequently misdiagnosed as drought since the patches also look sun-stressed.
- **First question:** "Did you check for chinch bugs at the patch edge before recommending water?"
- **Data to pull:** flotation test count (coffee-can method: cut both ends, push into turf at patch edge, fill with water, count insects floating up within 5-10 min), patch location relative to shade/sun map of the property.

### Ragged, torn blade tips — white, gray, or brown discoloration visible from a few feet away
- **Usually means:** dull mower blade tearing rather than cutting, which increases disease entry points and gives the lawn a stressed, off-color look independent of any other problem.
- **First question:** "When was this blade last sharpened, and how many hours has it cut since?"
- **Data to pull:** blade sharpening log, mower hour meter, visual close-up of cut blade tips.

### Grass clumping, matting, and yellowing in the days after a wet-grass mow
- **Usually means:** clippings left too heavy from cutting wet, smothering the turf underneath and creating a moist surface layer that favors fungal growth.
- **First question:** "Was this property mowed wet, and were clippings bagged or left in windrows?"
- **Data to pull:** mowing date vs. last rain/irrigation, clipping distribution (bagged vs. mulched vs. clumped), current cutting height relative to species target.

### Fertilizer burn or growth-rate streaks matching the spreader's travel path
- **Usually means:** spreader miscalibration or overlap gaps — either doubled application on overlap lines or skipped strips between passes.
- **First question:** "What did the catch-pan calibration test actually show for this spreader's current setting, against the label's lb N/1,000 sq ft target?"
- **Data to pull:** spreader calibration test result, product N% and label rate, applied rate back-calculated from bags used ÷ sq ft covered.

### Bark damage or a girdling ring at the base of trees on a property mowed/trimmed weekly
- **Usually means:** repeated string-trimmer or mower-deck contact at the trunk base ("mower blight"), which compounds over seasons into vascular damage and eventual canopy dieback.
- **First question:** "Are mulch rings or trunk guards in place, and is the trimmer making contact with bark on this pass?"
- **Data to pull:** tree canopy health/dieback history, presence and radius of mulch rings, trimmer-line contact observed on the current visit.

### New irregular dead patches appearing within days of a chemical application
- **Usually means:** herbicide drift, an off-label tank-mix rate, or application during wind/heat conditions outside label parameters.
- **First question:** "What was the actual mix rate and wind speed at the time of application, against the label?"
- **Data to pull:** application log (product, rate, date, weather at time of application), label's specified rate and wind/temperature restrictions, applicator's license/certification on file.

### Debris ejection or a near-miss during mower operation
- **Usually means:** a missing or damaged discharge chute deflector, or a guard removed for convenience.
- **First question:** "Is the discharge deflector/guard present and functioning per the pre-op checklist?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment pre-operation inspection checklist, OSHA 1910.243 guarding compliance status, incident/near-miss report.

### Crew reports ringing ears or has to shout to be heard after a full day on a blower or trimmer
- **Usually means:** noise exposure above the ~85 dBA threshold without consistent hearing protection.
- **First question:** "What's this specific equipment's rated dB output at the operator's ear, and was hearing protection worn today?"
- **Data to pull:** equipment noise spec sheet, PPE compliance log, hours of continuous operation without rotation.

### Dry, donut-ring-shaped patch centered on a single irrigation head while the rest of the zone looks fine
- **Usually means:** a clogged, misaligned, or low-pressure head creating a coverage gap, not a zone-wide watering problem.
- **First question:** "Does this head's spray pattern actually reach its neighbors, or is there a dry gap between throw radii?"
- **Data to pull:** head-to-head coverage check (run the zone, watch the pattern), catch-cup distribution uniformity test if the gap isn't obvious by eye.
