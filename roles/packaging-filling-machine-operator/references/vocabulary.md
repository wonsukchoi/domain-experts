# Vocabulary

### Overfill margin
The amount by which a fill target is set above the labeled or declared weight, calculated to statistically ensure compliance with average-weight and individual-minimum regulatory requirements given the process's actual fill variability.
**In use:** "Overfill margin's 12g on this product — that's what keeps the individual-minimum non-compliance rate acceptably low given our SD."
**Common misuse:** Setting the fill target at exactly the label weight, or using an arbitrary flat overfill percentage, rather than calculating the margin from the process's actual measured standard deviation.

### Average weight rule
A regulatory requirement that the average net contents of a production lot must meet or exceed the labeled weight.
**In use:** "Lot average is 501g against a 500g label — that clears the average weight rule on its own."
**Common misuse:** Assuming compliance is achieved as long as the average happens to be at or above label weight, without also verifying individual-package minimums are satisfied.

### Individual minimum rule
A regulatory requirement setting the maximum allowable amount any single package can fall below the labeled weight, distinct from and in addition to the average weight rule.
**In use:** "Average's fine, but check the individual minimum too — that's a separate requirement this fill target might still violate."
**Common misuse:** Focusing solely on hitting the correct average fill weight while ignoring the separate individual-package tolerance requirement, which a low-variability-assumption fill target can still violate.

### Standard deviation (fill weight)
A statistical measure of how much individual fill weights vary around the process mean, which directly determines what overfill margin is needed to achieve a target compliance rate.
**In use:** "SD's up to 8g from last month's 5g — the overfill margin needs recalculating, not just carried forward."
**Common misuse:** Setting a fill target or overfill margin without referencing the process's actual current measured standard deviation, using an assumed or historical figure that may not reflect current process performance.

### Checkweigher
An in-line scale that weighs each filled package and automatically accepts or rejects it based on calculated tolerance thresholds.
**In use:** "Checkweigher reject threshold's set from the individual-minimum calc, not from what felt like a reasonable buffer."
**Common misuse:** Setting checkweigher tolerance thresholds by operator judgment or comfort rather than calculating them from the specific regulatory average-weight and individual-minimum requirements for the product.

### Headspace
The gap between a filled product's surface and the container's closure, which serves specific functional purposes depending on the product and process.
**In use:** "Headspace target's set for this retort process specifically — that's not just leftover unfilled space."
**Common misuse:** Treating headspace purely as wasted, unfilled space to be minimized, rather than a process-specific functional variable with its own correct target.

### Z-score (statistical)
The number of standard deviations a specific value, like a regulatory minimum weight, falls from the process mean, used to estimate the probability of individual units falling below that value.
**In use:** "Z-score comes out to -3.375 at this fill target — that's the number that actually tells us the non-compliance rate, not a general sense of margin."
**Common misuse:** Relying on a qualitative sense that a fill target has "enough margin" rather than calculating the actual z-score and corresponding non-compliance rate implied by the process's measured variability.

### Statistical process control (SPC)
The practice of monitoring a process's key statistics — mean, standard deviation — over time, rather than just pass/fail outcomes, to detect drift before it produces out-of-spec results.
**In use:** "SPC chart's showing the mean creeping down over the shift — catching that now, before it shows up as rejects."
**Common misuse:** Monitoring only individual reject counts or pass/fail outcomes rather than tracking the underlying mean/SD trend that would reveal drift before it becomes a compliance problem.

### Legal-for-trade
Packaged goods regulation requiring that declared net contents on a label be accurate within defined statistical tolerances, enforced by weights-and-measures authorities.
**In use:** "This isn't just a quality target — legal-for-trade rules make the fill weight a compliance requirement with defined statistics."
**Common misuse:** Treating fill weight accuracy as purely a quality or cost consideration rather than also a specific regulatory compliance requirement with defined statistical rules.

### Retort / thermal processing (packaging context)
A heat-sterilization process applied to sealed containers, during which contents and any headspace undergo thermal expansion that the container and seal must accommodate.
**In use:** "Headspace has to account for the expansion this product will see going through retort, not just how it looks at ambient temperature."
**Common misuse:** Setting headspace based only on fill-line/visual convention without accounting for the specific thermal expansion behavior this container/product will undergo during retort processing.
