# Red flags

### Fill target set at exactly the label/declared weight rather than label weight plus a statistically justified overfill margin
- **Usually means:** a meaningful fraction of individual packages will fall below the legal individual-minimum requirement due to normal process variability.
- **First question:** what does the process's actual measured standard deviation imply about the individual-package non-compliance rate at the current fill target?
- **Data to pull:** fill target versus label weight versus process SD versus calculated non-compliance rate.

### Checkweigher reject threshold set by operator comfort/habit rather than calculated from the applicable regulatory requirements
- **Usually means:** either passing non-compliant underweight product or unnecessarily rejecting compliant product.
- **First question:** does the current threshold match the calculated value based on the average-weight and individual-minimum requirements for this product?
- **Data to pull:** checkweigher threshold compared against the calculated value based on regulatory requirements for this product.

### Headspace minimized to maximize fill without regard to the process-specific functional headspace requirement
- **Usually means:** risk of container deformation, rupture, or vacuum/seal integrity failure during processing or storage.
- **First question:** what does the measured headspace show against the process-specific target for this container/product type?
- **Data to pull:** measured headspace compared against the process-specific target.

### Machine speed increased to raise throughput without re-verifying fill weight accuracy/consistency at the new speed
- **Usually means:** fill accuracy validated at the prior speed may not hold at the higher speed, especially for viscous or foam-prone products.
- **First question:** what does the fill weight mean/SD at the new speed show against the previously validated speed's statistics?
- **Data to pull:** fill weight mean/SD at the new speed compared against the previously validated speed's statistics.

### Overfill margin reduced to cut product cost without basing the reduction on the process's actual current measured standard deviation
- **Usually means:** the reduction may shift the fill distribution into non-compliance if actual variability is higher than assumed.
- **First question:** what does the current actual measured SD imply about the resulting non-compliance rate at the proposed new target?
- **Data to pull:** proposed new fill target versus current actual measured SD versus resulting calculated non-compliance rate.

### Fill weight monitored only by individual reject count rather than tracking the underlying mean and standard deviation trend
- **Usually means:** a gradual drift in process variability or mean could go undetected until it produces a spike in individual rejects, by which point compliance may already have been compromised for a period.
- **First question:** what does the SPC trend for mean and SD show over the run, not just the reject count?
- **Data to pull:** fill weight SPC trend (mean, SD over time) compared against reject count alone.

### A recurring fill-weight compliance concern or checkweigher reject pattern appears without being traced to a specific process cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — speed change, product viscosity/temperature variation, headspace setting — rather than isolated one-off variation.
- **First question:** does the reject/compliance trend correlate with a specific process parameter change over the same period?
- **Data to pull:** reject/compliance trend log compared against process parameter logs (speed, target, SD) over the same period.
