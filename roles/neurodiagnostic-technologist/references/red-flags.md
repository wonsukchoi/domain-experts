# Red flags

### Single channel showing high-frequency "spike-like" activity not present on neighboring electrodes
- **Usually means:** Electrode contact artifact (poor prep, drying paste, popped lead) — much more common than a true focal discharge confined to one lead.
- **First question:** What's the impedance on that electrode right now, and does the finding disappear on re-prep?
- **Data to pull:** Live impedance check, electrode re-derivation using a different reference to see if the finding persists.

### Rhythmic delta or theta pattern on cEEG that has not changed in frequency, location, or morphology across several minutes of review
- **Usually means:** Interictal periodic discharge (GPD/LPD) or encephalopathic background rather than an ongoing electrographic seizure.
- **First question:** Has this pattern evolved at all — frequency, spatial spread, or morphology — over the last 30-60 seconds of trace, not just the current screen?
- **Data to pull:** Trend/spectrogram view over the last 10-15 minutes; compare against the patient's clinical exam at the same timestamps.

### SSEP amplitude down >50% from case baseline with latency unchanged
- **Usually means:** Technical/anesthesia-related change (temperature drift, anesthetic bolus, blood pressure drop) rather than a mechanical surgical injury, since isolated amplitude loss without latency shift is a weaker combined signal.
- **First question:** What changed in the anesthesia record or patient temperature in the last 10-15 minutes, and does the timing align better with that or with the surgical step?
- **Data to pull:** Anesthesia flowsheet (agent/rate changes, MAP, temperature), surgical time-stamped step log.

### MEP response present but at markedly higher stimulation threshold than baseline
- **Usually means:** Early warning of a developing motor pathway issue, or an anesthesia depth change (higher MAC of volatile agent suppresses MEPs disproportionately).
- **First question:** Has the volatile anesthetic concentration or muscle relaxant level changed since baseline?
- **Data to pull:** Anesthesia record for volatile agent MAC and train-of-four/twitch monitor reading.

### Routine EEG report reads "normal" but the technical note shows hyperventilation or photic stimulation was skipped or incomplete
- **Usually means:** Patient refusal, time constraint, or contraindication — not confirmation that no epileptiform activity exists.
- **First question:** Was the omission documented as a limitation in the technical note, and does the ordering physician know the study is an incomplete negative?
- **Data to pull:** Technical/limitations section of the report; original order to see if the clinical question specifically depended on the missing activation procedure.

### Temporal-region rhythmic activity appearing only during a specific patient behavior (chewing, anxious movement, talking)
- **Usually means:** Glossokinetic, EMG (muscle), or movement artifact mimicking temporal lobe rhythmicity.
- **First question:** Does the pattern disappear when the behavior stops, and is there a corresponding EKG or EMG channel correlate?
- **Data to pull:** Time-locked video (if available), EKG channel, chin/temporalis EMG channel if recorded.

### Repeat-study or repeat-electrode rate on a given room or technologist trending upward
- **Usually means:** Equipment/impedance-meter calibration drift or a systemic prep issue, more often than an individual skill problem.
- **First question:** Is the increase concentrated on one room, one amplifier, or one shift, or is it spread evenly?
- **Data to pull:** QA log of repeat/incomplete studies by room, technologist, and equipment ID over the trailing month.

### Continuous EEG monitoring order left open beyond 48 hours with no seizures captured and no periodic patterns noted
- **Usually means:** Either the clinical concern has resolved and monitoring can stop, or a low-yield location/montage is missing the events (e.g., events are subtle, sub-scalp, or the montage doesn't cover the region of concern).
- **First question:** Has the ordering physician been given an explicit recommendation to continue or discontinue, with the specific findings (or lack of them) stated?
- **Data to pull:** Full monitoring summary with event count, any periodic patterns noted (even without frank seizures), and clinical correlation notes.

### IONM baseline established immediately after induction shows unstable amplitude across the first two averages
- **Usually means:** Anesthesia hasn't reached steady state yet, or electrode/needle placement isn't fully settled.
- **First question:** Has at least one additional averaging run been done to confirm stability before calling this the case's reference baseline?
- **Data to pull:** The raw averaged traces from the first 2-3 baseline runs side by side, not just the summary amplitude number.

### Free-run EMG shows a burst of activity during retraction that the surgeon doesn't ask about
- **Usually means:** Most retraction-related EMG bursts are brief, mechanical, and not independently alarm-worthy — but a sustained (not burst) discharge is a different and more concerning signal.
- **First question:** Was the discharge a brief burst (seconds) or a sustained neurotonic pattern, and did it repeat with subsequent retraction?
- **Data to pull:** The EMG channel trace duration and repeat pattern across the retraction sequence.
