# Red flags

### Solid wood panel wider than roughly 6–8 inches glued rigidly on all edges into a frame
- **Usually means:** no accommodation for wood movement, creating high risk of cracking as ambient humidity changes.
- **First question:** has expected seasonal movement actually been calculated for this panel's width and species?
- **Data to pull:** the species' movement figure compared against the panel's actual width.

### Incoming lumber's moisture content reading differs from the shop's equilibrium moisture content by more than roughly 2 percentage points
- **Usually means:** the wood hasn't acclimated and will keep moving after assembly regardless of how well it's built.
- **First question:** how long has this lumber actually been stored in the shop's environment?
- **Data to pull:** moisture meter reading compared against the shop's EMC log.

### Two pieces joined with grain running in different directions, assembled with a rigid, full-length glue joint
- **Usually means:** guaranteed eventual cross-grain cracking as the two pieces move differently over time.
- **First question:** what grain direction does each piece actually run, and how was the joint glued?
- **Data to pull:** a grain-direction diagram for the joint and the glue method actually used.

### A load-bearing joint (chair or table leg) selected primarily for speed of execution rather than established load and racking performance
- **Usually means:** reduced structural life under the connection's actual in-service stress.
- **First question:** what load path and stress will this specific connection actually see in use?
- **Data to pull:** the joint type used compared against the connection's expected service load.

### Finish applied without regard to the wood's expected movement, showing early cracking at panel edges or joints
- **Usually means:** the finish system itself, not the underlying joinery, is where wood-movement stress is showing up first.
- **First question:** does the finish's flexibility rating actually accommodate the panel's calculated movement allowance?
- **Data to pull:** the finish type/flexibility rating compared against the panel's movement allowance.

### Client reports gaps that appeared after delivery on solid wood furniture
- **Usually means:** the piece was built with lumber that hadn't fully acclimated, or without adequate movement allowance, rather than a defect in the joinery itself.
- **First question:** what was the wood's moisture content at the time of build versus the typical humidity of the delivery environment?
- **Data to pull:** build-time moisture content readings compared against the delivery environment's typical humidity range.

### Veneer substrate (plywood or MDF) selected without checking its movement characteristics against the veneer species' movement
- **Usually means:** differential movement between veneer and substrate risking delamination or surface checking over time.
- **First question:** does the substrate's movement spec actually align with the veneer species' movement coefficient?
- **Data to pull:** substrate movement specification compared against the veneer species' published movement coefficient.
