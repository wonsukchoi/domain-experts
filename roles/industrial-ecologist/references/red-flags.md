# Red flags

### Mass or energy balance gap exceeds ~5–10% of throughput
- **Usually means:** a meter is miscalibrated or a flow (fugitive emission, evaporative loss, unmetered discharge) was never instrumented; second most likely is a unit-conversion or double-counting error in the model.
- **First question:** "Which flows in this balance are metered continuously versus estimated from a single spot check or supplier invoice?"
- **Data to pull:** meter calibration logs, weighbridge tickets, and the raw metering data behind every line item in the balance, not just the rolled-up monthly total.

### "100% recyclable" claim with no stated actual recycling rate
- **Usually means:** the claim is about material properties (technically capable of being recycled), not about what actually happens to the product post-use — the two are routinely conflated in marketing copy.
- **First question:** "What percentage of this material actually gets collected and reprocessed in the markets where it's sold, and what's the source for that number?"
- **Data to pull:** regional collection/MRF (materials recovery facility) recovery-rate data for the specific material stream, not an industry-wide average for a broader category.

### Recycled-content percentage claimed without a chain-of-custody certificate
- **Usually means:** the recycled fraction was estimated or aspirational rather than mass-balance verified; sometimes it's a book-and-claim credit system rather than physical content.
- **First question:** "Is this a physically segregated recycled fraction, a mass-balance allocation, or a book-and-claim credit — and can I see the certificate?"
- **Data to pull:** the supplier's chain-of-custody documentation (e.g., ISCC, RecyClass, or GRS certificate) for the specific batch or period claimed.

### Comparative public claim published without a critical-review panel
- **Usually means:** the LCA was scoped and run for internal decision support and then repurposed for external marketing without the ISO 14044-required independent review for comparative assertions.
- **First question:** "Who outside this project reviewed the methodology and boundary choices before this claim went external?"
- **Data to pull:** the critical-review report and reviewer credentials/independence statement, if one exists.

### Symbiosis exchange running on an MOU with no contracted minimum volume
- **Usually means:** the exchange is a pilot that will quietly stop the first time either partner's production schedule, price, or ownership changes — the reported "savings" are then retroactively unreal.
- **First question:** "What happens to this exchange, contractually, if [partner]'s production drops 30% next quarter?"
- **Data to pull:** the executed contract or term sheet's minimum-offtake and price-adjustment clauses, not the press-release description of the partnership.

### Functional unit differs between the options being compared
- **Usually means:** someone is comparing per-kg-of-material footprints across options with different service lives, use-efficiencies, or delivered functions, making a worse option look better.
- **First question:** "What is the functional unit, exactly, and is it identical across every option in this comparison?"
- **Data to pull:** the goal-and-scope memo's stated functional unit for each option under comparison.

### Single aggregated sustainability score used for a decision
- **Usually means:** multiple impact categories (GWP, eutrophication, water use, resource depletion) were weighted and collapsed into one number, hiding a tradeoff where option A wins on carbon but loses badly on water.
- **First question:** "Can I see the category-by-category breakdown before the weighting was applied?"
- **Data to pull:** the disaggregated LCIA results by impact category, and the weighting scheme's source and justification.

### Allocation method changed between project phases without documentation
- **Usually means:** the model was adjusted (often from mass to economic allocation, or vice versa) to move a headline number, and the change wasn't logged.
- **First question:** "What allocation method was used in the original scoping memo, and does it match what's in the current model?"
- **Data to pull:** the version history of the LCA model file and the goal-and-scope memo's originally stated allocation method.

### "Net zero" claim leaning primarily on offset purchases rather than measured reduction
- **Usually means:** gross emissions are flat or rising and the net number is being carried by offset credits of uneven quality (some unretired, some from projects with weak additionality).
- **First question:** "What fraction of this net-zero number is gross reduction versus offset credit, and are the offsets retired and third-party verified?"
- **Data to pull:** the registry retirement records for every offset credit counted, and the year-over-year gross emissions trend independent of offsets.

### Byproduct-exchange savings claim with no counterfactual baseline
- **Usually means:** 100% of the environmental credit for a byproduct exchange is being claimed without checking whether the byproduct would have found another buyer, been recovered anyway, or been landfilled regardless.
- **First question:** "What would have happened to this material or energy stream in the absence of this specific exchange?"
- **Data to pull:** prior-year disposal or sale records for the byproduct stream before the exchange began.

### Data pedigree not disclosed for a headline environmental number
- **Usually means:** the underlying data is a mix of high-quality metered values and low-quality proxy/industry-average values, and the report doesn't distinguish which is which.
- **First question:** "Which inputs in this model are site-specific measured data, and which are industry-average proxies?"
- **Data to pull:** the data-pedigree matrix (or equivalent quality scoring) for the model's key input flows.
