# Respiratory Therapist — Red Flags

### SpO2 reading normal (>94%) but the patient is tachypneic, diaphoretic, or has a new mental-status change
- **Usually means:** Rising CO2 (hypercapnic respiratory failure) that pulse oximetry can't detect, since SpO2 measures oxygenation, not ventilation; less commonly, a probe/perfusion artifact masking a true desaturation.
- **First question:** "What's the trend on respiratory rate and mental status over the last hour, and when was the last ABG or venous CO2 drawn?"
- **Data to pull:** Most recent ABG or VBG with PaCO2/pH; respiratory rate trend over the shift; capnography waveform if in place.

### Plateau pressure sustained above 30 cmH2O with tidal volume already at 6 mL/kg predicted body weight
- **Usually means:** Worsening lung compliance (progressing ARDS, abdominal compartment pressure transmitted to the chest) or a Vt calculated from actual rather than predicted body weight.
- **First question:** "What tidal volume and predicted body weight are we actually using, and has an inspiratory hold been checked recently?"
- **Data to pull:** Height-derived PBW recalculation; static compliance (Vt ÷ driving pressure); abdominal exam/intra-abdominal pressure if available.

### Sudden rise in peak pressure with falling SpO2 and asymmetric breath sounds in an intubated patient
- **Usually means:** Mainstem intubation (tube migrated too deep), pneumothorax, or a large mucus plug obstructing one side.
- **First question:** "Did this happen suddenly or gradually, and are breath sounds equal on both sides right now?"
- **Data to pull:** Tube depth at the lip versus documented baseline depth; immediate auscultation both sides; portable chest x-ray or point-of-care ultrasound.

### Rising peak pressure with stable or unchanged plateau pressure, especially in obstructive lung disease, alongside new hypotension
- **Usually means:** Auto-PEEP (breath stacking / dynamic hyperinflation) reducing venous return, not primary cardiac dysfunction or hypovolemia — the ventilator's displayed PEEP will read the set value, not the trapped value.
- **First question:** "Can we run an expiratory-hold maneuver right now before we start fluids or pressors?"
- **Data to pull:** Expiratory-hold auto-PEEP measurement; I:E ratio and expiratory time currently set; trend of peak-minus-plateau pressure gap.

### Rapid shallow breathing index near the 105 threshold (95–115) in a patient who is elderly, cardiac, or has been ventilated more than 7 days
- **Usually means:** Marginal weaning readiness where the single-number threshold is less reliable — cardiac patients can fail SBTs from a cardiac, not respiratory, mechanism (SBT-induced cardiac stress).
- **First question:** "Is there a cardiac history, and should we be watching a hemodynamic trend during the trial, not just the respiratory numbers?"
- **Data to pull:** Cardiac history and recent echo/BNP if available; full vitals trend (not just RR/Vt) throughout the trial, not only the endpoint RSBI.

### Persistent hypoxemia (SpO2 < 88–90%) despite FiO2 already at 0.8–1.0
- **Usually means:** Severe shunt physiology (refractory ARDS), a new pneumothorax, or a large mucus plug/complete lobar collapse rather than a titration problem solvable by more FiO2.
- **First question:** "Has anyone auscultated and suctioned in the last hour, and is this a candidate for prone positioning or a recruitment maneuver?"
- **Data to pull:** Recent suction return/character; chest imaging; P/F ratio trend to confirm ARDS severity banding; prone-positioning candidacy per unit protocol.

### Post-extubation stridor or increased work of breathing within the first hour after extubation
- **Usually means:** Laryngeal or subglottic edema from cuff pressure or a traumatic/prolonged intubation, especially if the pre-extubation cuff leak test was borderline or skipped.
- **First question:** "Was a cuff leak test done before extubation, and what was the leak volume?"
- **Data to pull:** Cuff leak volume if recorded; stridor severity and trend; readiness of reintubation equipment and NIV/high-flow standby.

### Tidal volume or minute ventilation declining while respiratory rate climbs during a pressure-support wean or SBT
- **Usually means:** True weaning failure developing in real time — the rapid, shallow pattern the RSBI is designed to catch — rather than the patient simply needing more time.
- **First question:** "Is this trend still going in the wrong direction over the last 5 minutes, or has it stabilized?"
- **Data to pull:** Vt and RR sampled at 5-minute intervals during the trial, not just start/end values; decision to terminate the trial before hard failure criteria (RR>35, SpO2<90%) are hit if the trend is clearly worsening.

### COPD patient with known chronic CO2 retention becoming more somnolent after supplemental oxygen was increased
- **Usually means:** Oxygen-induced hypercapnia (worsened V/Q mismatch and Haldane effect, not simply "loss of hypoxic drive") pushing PaCO2 higher — a titration overshoot, not a reason to withhold oxygen from a truly hypoxic patient.
- **First question:** "What was the SpO2 target we were titrating to, and has an ABG been rechecked since the FiO2 change?"
- **Data to pull:** FiO2/flow change timeline versus mental-status change timeline; repeat ABG; current SpO2 versus the 88–92% target band for this patient.

### End-tidal CO2 (capnography) trending persistently below PaCO2 by a widening gap
- **Usually means:** Increasing physiologic dead space — classically pulmonary embolism, but also severe hypoperfusion or worsening auto-PEEP with air trapping.
- **First question:** "Has the EtCO2-to-PaCO2 gap been stable, and is there a clinical reason to suspect a new PE (recent immobility, unilateral leg swelling, sudden hypoxemia)?"
- **Data to pull:** Paired EtCO2 and ABG PaCO2 at the same timepoint; D-dimer or imaging workup status; hemodynamic trend.
