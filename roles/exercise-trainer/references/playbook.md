# Exercise Trainer — Playbook

## Movement assessment: overhead squat (filled example)

| Observation point | Client presentation | Likely limitation | Corrective substitution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feet | Turn out >15° | Ankle dorsiflexion restriction, or tight calves/soleus | Ankle mobility drill before squat; elevate heels on a plate short-term |
| Knees | Cave inward (valgus) | Weak glute medius, or poor hip external-rotation control | Banded lateral walks, glute-medius activation before loaded squat |
| Low back | Excessive arch (lumbar hyperextension) | Tight hip flexors, weak anterior core | Dead-bug or plank progression; hip-flexor stretch |
| Torso | Excessive forward lean | Restricted ankle dorsiflexion, weak upper back | Combine with ankle-mobility fix; thoracic extension drill |
| Arms | Fall forward from overhead | Restricted shoulder flexion/lat length | Lat stretch, shoulder-flexion mobility before overhead loading |

Record which compensations appear **consistently across at least 3 of 5 reps** — a single-rep compensation is often just an attention lapse, not a limitation. Re-run the same assessment at reassessment intervals (every 4–8 weeks) using the same rep count and observation points so results are comparable.

## 1RM estimation and load-progression table (Epley formula)

Formula: 1RM ≈ weight × (1 + reps / 30). Most accurate at ≤10 reps; treat estimates from higher-rep sets as rough.

| Set performed | Estimated 1RM | Week 1 working load (75%) | Week 2 (78%, +4%) | Week 3 (81%, +4%) | Week 4 (deload, 65%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 reps @ 185 lb | 216 lb | 162 lb | 168 lb | 175 lb | 140 lb |
| 8 reps @ 135 lb | 171 lb | 128 lb | 133 lb | 139 lb | 111 lb |

Deload week (roughly every 3–4 weeks of accumulation) drops volume or intensity by 30–40% to let recovery catch up before the next progression block — skipping the deload is the most common way a program's progression outruns adaptation.

## PAR-Q+ intake triage (filled example)

| Question flagged "yes" | Action |
|---|---|
| "Has a doctor said you have a heart condition?" | Physician clearance required before any moderate/vigorous intensity |
| "Do you feel pain in chest during physical activity?" | Physician clearance required, no exception |
| "Do you have a bone/joint problem that could be worsened by exercise?" | Physician or PT clearance for the specific affected movement pattern; light activity elsewhere may proceed |
| "Are you pregnant, or have you given birth in the last 6 months?" | Route to the PAR-Q+ pregnancy-specific follow-up form, not the general algorithm |
| No "yes" answers | Cleared for self-directed moderate-to-vigorous intensity per PAR-Q+ guidance |

A single "yes" anywhere routes to clearance regardless of how minor the client believes it to be — the form doesn't have a "probably fine" outcome.
