# Exercise Trainer — Red Flags

### Chest pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath disproportionate to effort during a session
- **Usually means:** A cardiovascular event risk (angina, arrhythmia) or, less urgently, exercise-induced asthma or dehydration.
- **First question:** "Is this pain/dizziness worse than what you'd expect from this effort level, and has it happened before?"
- **Data to pull:** PAR-Q+ intake answers, any prior cardiovascular flags, current session's intensity relative to the client's typical tolerance.

### Sharp, localized pain appearing mid-set (not the next day)
- **Usually means:** An acute strain, sprain, or joint irritation — not delayed-onset muscle soreness (DOMS), which appears 24–72 hours later and is diffuse, not sharp or localized.
- **First question:** "Point to exactly where it hurts, and is it sharp or a dull ache?"
- **Data to pull:** The specific exercise, load, and rep at which the pain appeared; whether the same load was completed pain-free on a prior set.

### A movement compensation appears consistently across reps but the client insists "it feels fine"
- **Usually means:** The client's proprioception hasn't caught up to the compensation — subjective comfort isn't evidence the pattern is safe under increasing load.
- **First question:** "Can you slow the rep down and describe what your knees/back are doing?"
- **Data to pull:** Video of the movement from the assessment angle used originally, compared side-by-side.

### Client requests a 1RM test to "prove" the estimated max is wrong
- **Usually means:** Ego-driven testing pressure, or genuine curiosity — either way, a maximal-effort test carries injury risk the estimate was chosen to avoid.
- **First question:** "What would change in your program if the number were 10lb higher or lower?"
- **Data to pull:** How the estimate was derived (rep count, RPE at that set) — if it came from a set well under 10 reps at a controlled RPE, it's a reasonably reliable estimate already.

### Load progression missed two sessions in a row
- **Usually means:** Accumulated fatigue, insufficient recovery (sleep, nutrition, stress), or the progression rate was too aggressive for this client.
- **First question:** "How has your sleep and stress been the last week?"
- **Data to pull:** Session log for the prior 2–3 weeks — look for a pattern of declining RPE-at-target-load or missed reps, not just the most recent session.

### Client with a flagged PAR-Q+ answer wants to start training before seeing a physician
- **Usually means:** Time pressure or minimizing the flagged condition — not a reason to bypass the referral gate.
- **First question:** "What did the flagged question actually say, and has anything changed since?"
- **Data to pull:** The exact PAR-Q+ question and answer on file; whether physician clearance has been obtained and documented.

### Group-fitness participant can't maintain form as class intensity increases
- **Usually means:** The class-wide intensity has exceeded this individual's current capacity — a group setting can mask an individual overreach that 1:1 training would catch immediately.
- **First question:** "Can you scale this movement down and still keep form?"
- **Data to pull:** Whether a regression option was offered and taken, and whether the same participant has shown this pattern in prior classes.

### Client reports feeling "great, no soreness at all" after a new, high-volume stimulus
- **Usually means:** Either genuinely low training stress (program too easy) or a delayed pain-signal presentation that hasn't shown up yet — not automatically a sign the session went well.
- **First question:** "How did the last few reps of each set feel compared to the first few?"
- **Data to pull:** RPE logged during the session vs. the target RPE for that block.

### A corrective exercise has been assigned for 8+ weeks with no reassessment
- **Usually means:** The corrective was never verified to be working — it may have become a habit rather than a targeted fix.
- **First question:** "When did we last re-run the movement screen on this pattern?"
- **Data to pull:** Reassessment dates and findings from the client's file.
