# Dental Assistant — Red Flags

### Biological indicator (spore test) returns positive
- **Usually means:** Sterilizer malfunction (temperature/pressure/time cycle failure), improper loading (overpacking blocking steam/gas penetration), or occasionally a test-strip handling/storage error.
- **First question:** What was the last passing test date, and were there any maintenance events between then and now?
- **Data to pull:** Sterilizer cycle log, maintenance/service log, and the full list of instrument loads run since the last passing indicator.

### Radiograph shows a vertical light or dark band across part of the image
- **Usually means:** Cone-cut from PID misalignment with the sensor/film — the beam wasn't fully covering the receptor.
- **First question:** Was the PID visually aligned directly over the sensor before exposure, or estimated by feel?
- **Data to pull:** Positioning-device setup notes; compare against the standard cone-cutting error pattern before retaking.

### Two-component material mixed but dentist not ready within stated working time
- **Usually means:** Procedure sequencing didn't account for the material's clock, or an unexpected delay (patient movement, adjacent-tooth issue) ate the window.
- **First question:** How much of the stated working time has elapsed since mixing started?
- **Data to pull:** Manufacturer data sheet's working-time figure for that specific product/lot.

### Patient's medical-history update includes a new anticoagulant or bisphosphonate since last visit
- **Usually means:** A previously planned procedure (extraction, deep scaling, surgical placement) may now carry elevated bleeding or osteonecrosis risk.
- **First question:** Has the dentist reviewed this update against today's planned procedure before starting?
- **Data to pull:** Updated medical-history form, medication start date, and any physician clearance on file.

### Instrument tray for today's procedure doesn't match the procedure actually scheduled
- **Usually means:** Schedule change wasn't communicated, or the tray was set up from habit rather than today's chart.
- **First question:** Does the chart's procedure code match what's on the tray?
- **Data to pull:** Day sheet/schedule against the tray setup list for that operatory.

### Sterilization pouch shows moisture, tears, or a chemical indicator that didn't change color
- **Usually means:** Compromised sterile barrier (pouch failure) or a cycle that didn't reach the parameters needed to trigger the indicator.
- **First question:** Is this an isolated pouch or does it affect the whole load?
- **Data to pull:** Load's cycle printout (temperature/pressure/time achieved) and whether other pouches in the same load show the same issue.

### Patient reports a new allergy or reaction not on file
- **Usually means:** A prior exposure (latex, a specific anesthetic, a metal) triggered a reaction the chart doesn't reflect yet.
- **First question:** Has this been added to the chart and flagged to the dentist before any material or anesthetic is used today?
- **Data to pull:** Updated allergy list; cross-check against every material planned for today's procedure.

### Suction or evacuation isn't keeping the field visible during a procedure
- **Usually means:** Clogged line, incorrect tip position, or equipment malfunction — not just "patient producing more saliva than usual."
- **First question:** Is the issue isolated to this operatory/line, or does it affect suction elsewhere in the office?
- **Data to pull:** Equipment maintenance log; whether the issue resolves with a tip/line swap.
