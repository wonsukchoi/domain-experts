# Red Flags — Airline Pilot

### ETOPS diversion-time margin under 10% at the route's critical point given today's MEL status
- **Usually means:** the rating was checked against the fleet's placarded maximum rather than today's MEL-downgraded rating; second most likely: the adequate-airport list wasn't rechecked for NOTAM closures at the diversion field.
- **First question:** "Which ETOPS rating applies to this tail right now, and what MEL items are open against the ETOPS-MEL supplement?"
- **Data to pull:** current MEL/CDL log, ETOPS-MEL supplement, dispatch release's critical-point calculation, adequate-airport NOTAMs.

### Dispatch release accepted with no captain query on a marginal weather, fuel, or MEL line
- **Usually means:** the release was treated as a formality to sign rather than a document to personally audit; second: schedule pressure discouraged raising a query before pushback.
- **First question:** "What specific line item did you check yourself, versus accept as filed?"
- **Data to pull:** dispatch release, captain's preflight sign-off log, OCC (operations control center) contact log.

### PM silent through more than one PF automation-mode change during a high-workload phase
- **Usually means:** monitoring duty has drifted into passive backup rather than active independent verification — the Asiana 214 pattern; second: an authority gradient is discouraging the PM (often the FO) from challenging the PF.
- **First question:** "What did the PM verbally cross-check on that mode change, and did PF read it back?"
- **Data to pull:** CVR/FDR (post-incident) or, proactively, LOSA/line-check observation notes, SOP callout compliance records.

### Non-essential conversation or activity logged below 10,000 ft with no safety-of-flight justification
- **Usually means:** sterile cockpit is being treated as a courtesy rather than a hard §121.542 rule; second: a high-terrain airport's AGL-based threshold wasn't applied instead of the standard MSL figure.
- **First question:** "Was that a safety item — and if not, why wasn't it deferred to level-off?"
- **Data to pull:** CVR (if available), company OpSpecs for that airport's sterile-cockpit altitude definition, SOP compliance audit.

### Flight-duty-period calculated off the scheduled report time rather than the actual one after a delay
- **Usually means:** the Part 117 FDP table was checked once at original dispatch planning and never rerun after an irregular-operations delay pushed the actual report time later or added a segment; second: cumulative duty from prior legs wasn't carried into the recheck.
- **First question:** "What's the FDP limit at your actual report time and segment count — not the original schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** Part 117 FDP table, crew-scheduling system duty log, actual vs. scheduled report/release times.

### Amended dispatch release accepted without an independent recompute of the changed line item
- **Usually means:** the amendment was trusted because it came from dispatch, without the captain's own cross-check of the number that changed; second: gate-time pressure discouraged a second look.
- **First question:** "What did you personally recompute on this amendment, versus take on the dispatcher's word?"
- **Data to pull:** original and amended release side by side, timestamp of amendment vs. scheduled departure.

### Critical-fuel-scenario figure unchanged after a route or ETOPS-rating change on the release
- **Usually means:** dispatch updated the track but the critical-fuel number was carried forward from the original plan rather than rerun for the new critical point; second: the flight-planning software auto-populated the old figure and nobody flagged it.
- **First question:** "Was critical fuel rerun for the new critical point, or carried over from the original release?"
- **Data to pull:** flight-planning system's critical-fuel output for both the original and amended release, with timestamps.

### PF and PM roles not restated aloud across a crew swap or an extended-duty leg
- **Usually means:** the assumption "captain is always PF" persisted past a fatigue- or workload-driven point where the roles should have swapped; second: no briefing captured who currently holds controls and radios.
- **First question:** "Who is PF and who is PM on this leg, and was that stated out loud at the briefing?"
- **Data to pull:** crew briefing notes, CVR (post-incident), company SOP on PF/PM assignment by leg.

### Operational-control disagreement between captain and dispatcher resolved verbally, with no amended release issued
- **Usually means:** one side deferred without a documented resolution, breaking the joint-operational-control record required by §121.533; second: dispatch gave verbal guidance that was never reflected in a written amendment.
- **First question:** "Where's the amended, signed release reflecting what was actually decided?"
- **Data to pull:** OCC contact/communication log, original vs. amended release, company operational-control manual.

### Type-rating recurrent or proficiency check due within days of a demanding route assignment
- **Usually means:** crew scheduling assigned the pairing without cross-checking currency against that specific route's requirements (new ETOPS segment, new destination category); second: the pilot didn't flag the conflict upward before accepting the pairing.
- **First question:** "Is your recurrent/proficiency check current for this route's specific requirements, not just current in general?"
- **Data to pull:** training records system, route-qualification requirements for that city pair.
