# Manicurist / Pedicurist — Red Flags

### Green-black discoloration under a lifted enhancement, with a faint odor
- **Usually means:** bacterial infection (commonly *Pseudomonas*) trapped between the natural nail and the product
- **First question:** "How long has this section felt loose, and has it smelled or looked different than the rest?"
- **Data to pull:** soak-off inspection of the exposed plate; date the lifting was first noticed

### New or changing dark longitudinal streak under the nail, widening or reaching the cuticle
- **Usually means:** melanonychia that needs a dermatology referral to rule out subungual melanoma — not a design element to match into nail art
- **First question:** "Has this streak always looked like this, or has it changed — gotten wider, darker, or moved closer to the cuticle?"
- **Data to pull:** comparison against the client's last visit's photo/notes if on file; whether it's isolated to one digit (higher concern) or present on several (more consistent with a benign cause)

### Enhancement resists a 15-minute acetone soak with no visible softening
- **Usually means:** MMA-based product or an over-cured top coat, not a soak-time problem
- **First question:** "Do you know what product or salon this set was originally applied with?"
- **Data to pull:** how the product behaves at the 20–25 minute mechanical-assist checkpoint; condition of the plate underneath once exposed

### Swelling, redness, or pus around the nail fold that looks worse than a hangnail
- **Usually means:** paronychia (bacterial or fungal infection of the nail fold)
- **First question:** "Has this area been sore, warm, or draining anything, not just red?"
- **Data to pull:** visual read of the fold under good light; whether it's isolated or the client has similar findings on other digits

### Nail plate separating from the bed with no reported trauma
- **Usually means:** onycholysis — possibly from infection, psoriasis, thyroid changes, or a prior chemical/mechanical injury, not a cosmetic product problem
- **First question:** "Did anything happen to this nail recently — a jam, a new medication, a skin condition flaring up?"
- **Data to pull:** how much of the plate is separated and whether the exposed nail bed looks pink and healthy or discolored

### Nail plate is thickened, yellow, and crumbly at the free edge
- **Usually means:** onychomycosis (fungal infection), not a cosmetic ridge or product residue
- **First question:** "Has this nail always looked this way, or has it changed over the past few months?"
- **Data to pull:** whether other nails show the same pattern; any prior fungal-infection diagnosis on file

### Client reports tingling or burning during a past product application, or a known reaction
- **Usually means:** allergic sensitization, commonly to an acrylate (MMA/methacrylate family)
- **First question:** "Was that with gel, acrylic, or dip powder — and did it happen the first time or after repeated services?"
- **Data to pull:** product family used at the reaction visit if on file; whether symptoms were limited to the nail fold or spread to the fingertip skin

### Implements pulled from disinfectant solution before the labeled contact time, under time pressure
- **Usually means:** a schedule squeeze is about to turn into an infection-control breach, not just a minor shortcut
- **First question:** "Is there a backup disinfected set staged, or does this client's start need to slip?"
- **Data to pull:** time the implements entered solution versus current time; labeled contact-time requirement on the disinfectant in use

### Diabetic or circulation-compromised pedicure client requesting callus blade or aggressive foot work
- **Usually means:** elevated risk that a minor nick becomes a slow-healing wound
- **First question:** "Has your doctor said anything about what's safe for callus removal on your feet?"
- **Data to pull:** any documented podiatrist clearance; visible skin integrity and circulation signs (color, warmth, sensation) before deciding on file-only versus blade

### Foot spa/whirlpool basin shows visible biofilm, or the between-client disinfection cycle was skipped for time
- **Usually means:** elevated risk of a *Mycobacterium* or other waterborne-organism outbreak, the documented cause of past nail-salon footbath outbreaks
- **First question:** "When was the last full basin disinfection cycle logged, not just the liner change?"
- **Data to pull:** basin-maintenance log; intake-screen cleaning date if the unit has one
