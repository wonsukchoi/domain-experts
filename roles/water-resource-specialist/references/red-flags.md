# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced water resource specialist notices instantly. Load this when triaging a supply, allocation, or conservation question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### A supply reliability plan is evaluated against average annual yield only
- **Usually means:** the plan hasn't been stress-tested against a drought scenario, and reliability will fail exactly when it's needed most
- **First question:** "What does this plan look like under the basin's documented drought-year or multi-year-drought scenario, not the average?"
- **Data to pull:** historical hydrologic sequence data including the worst documented drought years, not just the long-run average

### Water-rights curtailment priority hasn't been communicated to users before a shortage is declared
- **Usually means:** users will be surprised by a legally-determined curtailment order they didn't understand in advance, creating both a planning and trust failure
- **First question:** "Has the priority/curtailment schedule been distributed to all affected users, and when was it last updated?"
- **Data to pull:** the curtailment priority schedule and its last communication/distribution date

### A conservation program target exceeds realistic demand-reduction ranges without a supply-side plan for the remaining gap
- **Usually means:** the plan is relying on conservation to do more than demand-side measures typically can, without a backup plan for the shortfall
- **First question:** "What's the realistic evidence-based reduction range for this type of program, and what closes the gap if conservation falls short of the full target?"
- **Data to pull:** comparable conservation program performance data and the full water balance showing what conservation alone would need to achieve

### Ecological/environmental flow requirements are being discussed as the first thing to reduce under supply pressure
- **Usually means:** a legally-binding constraint is being treated as a discretionary allocation, risking both legal exposure and long-term ecosystem damage
- **First question:** "Is this flow requirement legally binding in this jurisdiction, and has legal/regulatory counsel confirmed it can be adjusted before any reduction is considered?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific regulatory or legal basis for the ecological flow requirement

### An allocation or management plan treats surface water and groundwater independently in a basin with known connectivity
- **Usually means:** pumping or diversion decisions in one system may be affecting availability in the other without being accounted for
- **First question:** "Has hydrologic connectivity between surface and groundwater been assessed for this specific basin, and does the plan account for it?"
- **Data to pull:** basin hydrogeology studies or modeling addressing surface-groundwater interaction

### A demand forecast used for supply-demand balance planning doesn't account for growth or climate trend
- **Usually means:** the plan is based on a snapshot of current demand that will be outdated by the time it's implemented
- **First question:** "What growth rate and climate trend assumptions are built into this demand forecast, and when were they last updated?"
- **Data to pull:** the demand forecast's underlying assumptions and their last revision date

### A drought response plan has no negotiated agreement with senior rights holders and relies solely on strict priority administration
- **Usually means:** a junior user (often municipal) could face severe curtailment in a real drought with no negotiated alternative in place
- **First question:** "What happens to junior users under strict priority administration in the documented worst-case drought scenario, and is there a voluntary sharing agreement in place or under negotiation?"
- **Data to pull:** the strict-priority allocation outcome under the drought scenario and the status of any drought-sharing agreements
