# Red Flags

Smell tests during intake, charging, and remains processing. Format per flag: what it usually means, the first question to ask, the record to pull.

### Intake weight doesn't match the funeral home's paperwork by more than a few pounds

- **Usually means:** a transcription error on the transfer form, or — rarer and more serious — the wrong body was pulled from the cooler.
- **First question:** does the ID disc on the body match the case folder's disc number, right now, before anything else happens?
- **Data to pull:** cooler intake manifest and the case's chain-of-custody log entry from the moment of receipt.

### Implanted-device field left blank on the authorization form

- **Usually means:** the intake interview didn't cover it — not that no device is present. Blank fields are especially common with elderly decedents and rushed intakes.
- **First question:** has staff physically palpated the chest and abdomen, independent of what the form says?
- **Data to pull:** the funeral home's embalming report (which often documents devices found during prep) alongside the authorization form.

### Cycle finishes noticeably faster than the weight-band estimate

- **Usually means:** the secondary chamber never held its required temperature before the primary shut down — an emissions problem, not an efficiency win.
- **First question:** did the secondary chamber temperature log show a sustained hold in the required range at any point during this cycle?
- **Data to pull:** continuous temperature trend log for both chambers, timestamped against the cycle's start and stop.

### Processed remains weight is more than ~20% lighter than recovered bone weight

- **Usually means:** incomplete recovery from the chamber (bone left behind, risk of trace commingling into the next case) or a cremulator/scale calibration issue — less commonly, a mismatch between the logged case and the material actually processed.
- **First question:** was the chamber fully swept and vacuumed after this case, and was the cremulator cleared after the prior one?
- **Data to pull:** chamber-clearing checklist entry and the prior case's cremulator-clear timestamp.

### Stack opacity or CO monitor alarms mid-cycle

- **Usually means:** a combustion-air imbalance or a startup transient — occasionally a true permit exceedance if it's sustained.
- **First question:** what is secondary chamber temperature doing right now, and is the alarm still active after five minutes?
- **Data to pull:** continuous emissions/opacity monitor trend and burner modulation log for the alarm window.

### More than one ID disc recovered from a single chamber load

- **Usually means:** an unauthorized multi-occupancy charge — a serious compliance failure, not a convenience shortcut.
- **First question:** is there a signed multi-occupancy authorization on file covering both case numbers?
- **Data to pull:** charge log for the cycle and the authorization file for every case number recovered.

### Legal hold status unclear but the case is already on the day's charge schedule

- **Usually means:** scheduling pressure is being allowed to substitute for confirmation that the waiting period or ME/coroner release has actually cleared.
- **First question:** has the hold actually cleared, with a document or system confirmation, or is it assumed clear because the case "has been here a while"?
- **Data to pull:** state death-certificate/permit tracking system entry for this case number.

### Refractory brick shows visible cracking or spalling well before the manufacturer's expected reline interval

- **Usually means:** chronic overheating from routinely undersized burner throttling on large-bodied charges, or thermal shock from opening the door before full cooldown.
- **First question:** what has average burner modulation looked like across the last 50 cycles, for cases in the 250 lb+ band specifically?
- **Data to pull:** maintenance log and cycle count since the last refractory reline.

### Heavy, oily black smoke visible from the stack during a cycle

- **Usually means:** incomplete combustion from prosthetic/silicone material or a non-compliant container, not a routine variation.
- **First question:** what container material and known implants or prosthetics were logged on this case's pre-charge inspection sheet?
- **Data to pull:** pre-charge inspection sheet and container-compliance record for the case in progress.
