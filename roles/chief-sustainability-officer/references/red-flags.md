# Red flags — what a CSO notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any single one can be innocent; two or three together are a pattern.

### A public claim using an unqualified absolute ("carbon neutral," "net zero," "sustainable") without a named scope and methodology attached
- **Usually means:** the claim is likely to imply more coverage (e.g., full footprint including Scope 3) than what's actually been measured or achieved.
- **First question:** "What specific scope does this claim cover, and can we name the methodology if asked?"
- **Data to pull:** the underlying emissions data and scope boundaries the claim is meant to represent.

### Offset purchases covering 100% of a claimed reduction with no additionality review of the offset provider
- **Usually means:** some or all of the credited tons may represent reductions that would have happened anyway, meaning the real offset value is lower than the purchased volume implies.
- **First question:** "What's the independently reviewed additionality rate for this specific offset project type and vintage?"
- **Data to pull:** third-party verification/rating for the offset project, project type's typical additionality range from independent reviews.

### Scope 3 emissions absent from a company's headline sustainability claim when Scope 3 is known or estimated to be the majority of total footprint
- **Usually means:** the more flattering, more controllable numbers (Scope 1/2) are being reported while the larger share of real impact goes unaddressed and unmentioned.
- **First question:** "What percentage of our total footprint does this claim actually cover, once Scope 3 is included?"
- **Data to pull:** Scope 1/2/3 breakdown (even if Scope 3 is estimated rather than precisely measured), the claim's stated or implied coverage.

### A sustainability commitment with a target date but no named accountable owner or budget line
- **Usually means:** the pledge is likely to be discovered as unfunded and untracked the first time someone asks for a progress update.
- **First question:** "Who owns hitting this target, and what's the current budget and headcount behind it?"
- **Data to pull:** the commitment's internal ownership and resourcing documentation, if any exists.

### An ESG/sustainability initiative justified only in values terms, with no cost-avoidance, risk-reduction, or revenue angle documented
- **Usually means:** the initiative is vulnerable to being cut first in the next budget-tightening cycle, regardless of its actual long-term value.
- **First question:** "What's the cost-avoidance or risk-reduction case for this, separate from the values case?"
- **Data to pull:** the initiative's business case documentation, or lack thereof.

### A materiality assessment that hasn't been refreshed in 2+ years, or was never run at all before setting current reporting priorities
- **Usually means:** current sustainability effort and reporting may be spread across generic checklist topics rather than concentrated on what's actually financially material to this specific business now.
- **First question:** "When was the last materiality assessment, and does today's reporting focus still match what it found?"
- **Data to pull:** the most recent materiality assessment date and findings, current reporting topic list.

### Regulatory disclosure prepared to meet only the current minimum requirement in a jurisdiction with a known tightening trajectory (e.g., phased-in climate disclosure rules)
- **Usually means:** the data and reporting infrastructure being built today likely won't scale to the next phase-in without a costly redo.
- **First question:** "What does the next phase of this requirement ask for, and does our current data infrastructure support it?"
- **Data to pull:** the regulation's published phase-in schedule, current data collection scope versus the next phase's requirements.

### Supplier engagement or Scope 3 reduction effort spread evenly across the entire supply base rather than concentrated on categories where the company has real purchasing leverage
- **Usually means:** effort is being diluted across suppliers the company has little influence over, rather than focused where engagement can actually move the number.
- **First question:** "Where are we a top-3 customer for a supplier by spend, and is our Scope 3 effort concentrated there?"
- **Data to pull:** supplier spend concentration, current supplier-engagement program's actual target list.
