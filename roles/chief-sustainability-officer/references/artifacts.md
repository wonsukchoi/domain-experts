## 2. Claims-review checklist (filled example)

| Check | Question | Answer for this claim |
|---|---|---|
| Scope named | Does the claim state which scope(s) it covers? | Revised claim: yes (Scope 1+2, Scope 3 explicitly excluded and flagged) |
| Methodology traceable | Can the underlying number be traced to a named accounting standard? | Yes — GHG Protocol, third-party reviewed offset additionality |
| Additionality checked | Has the offset/reduction been checked for real incrementality? | Yes — 60% additionality applied, not assumed 100% |
| Absolute language avoided | Does the claim avoid unqualified terms ("carbon neutral," "sustainable") without scope? | Yes — replaced with scoped, specific language |
| Owner assigned | Is there a named accountable owner for the underlying commitment? | Yes — sustainability team, quarterly tracking |
| Survives adversarial read | Would this claim hold up to a skeptical journalist or NGO fact-check? | Yes — all numbers traceable, gaps disclosed proactively |

## 3. Materiality assessment summary (filled example)

| Issue | Stakeholder significance | Financial materiality | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Supply chain labor practices | High (customer & investor scrutiny rising) | Medium-high (compliance and brand risk) | Top priority — new supplier code of conduct rollout |
| Water use in manufacturing | Medium | High (operating region facing water stress, permit risk) | Top priority — water reduction target set |
| Scope 1+2 emissions | Medium | Medium (moderate regulatory exposure, some cost-avoidance upside) | Ongoing — efficiency program continues |
| Office recycling programs | Low | Low | Deprioritized — not material to this business, minimal reporting |

**Rule applied:** priority and reporting depth follow the materiality assessment's actual findings, not a generic industry ESG checklist — low-materiality items get proportionally less effort and reporting space.

## 4. Regulatory phase-in tracker (filled example, CSRD)

| Reporting year | Requirement phase | Company's current readiness | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Year 1 | Scope 1+2 disclosure, limited assurance | Ready | None |
| Year 2 | Scope 3 disclosure added, limited assurance | Partial — supplier data collection at 40% coverage | Building supplier engagement program to reach 80%+ coverage before Year 2 filing |
| Year 3 | Reasonable assurance required (higher bar than limited) | Not yet started | Scoping external auditor engagement now, 18 months ahead of requirement |

**Rule applied:** build data infrastructure and assurance readiness against the next phase-in requirement, not just the current year's minimum — a scramble at each phase-in is a sign this tracker wasn't maintained.
