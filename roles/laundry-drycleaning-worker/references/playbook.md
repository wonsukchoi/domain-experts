## Fiber-sensitivity reference table

| Fiber | Sensitivity | Treatment consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Cotton | Tolerates most standard treatments, alkaline and heat generally safe | Standard concentration typically fine for 100% cotton |
| Silk | Alkali-sensitive, heat-sensitive | Dilute alkaline treatments; low-heat pressing only |
| Wool | Heat- and agitation-sensitive (felting risk) | Cool water, minimal agitation, low-heat finishing |
| Synthetic (polyester, nylon) | Generally solvent-tolerant, but some finishes/coatings are heat-sensitive | Check for coatings/laminates before high-heat pressing |
| Blends | Sensitivity determined by the MOST sensitive fiber present, not the dominant one | Always treat to the most sensitive component's tolerance |

## Intake documentation checklist

1. Record fiber content (from label, or fabric test if unlabeled).
2. Record care label instructions in full.
3. Inspect and document (with photos where practical) any existing damage, wear, fading, or loose components (buttons, trim, beading).
4. Identify and note every visible stain with a best-guess origin/chemistry category.
5. Flag any item requiring a spot/bleed test before full processing, and note the test result once performed.
6. Note any customer-provided information about the stain's origin or prior treatment attempts (a prior treatment attempt can change how a stain responds to standard chemistry).
7. Confirm documentation is complete and attached to the item's tag/record before processing begins.

## Heat-application readiness check

Before pressing or hot-drying any item, confirm:
1. Every identified stain has been treated and visually confirmed removed while the area was still wet (not just assumed removed after drying).
2. For any protein-stain risk, a UV or closer inspection has ruled out residual staining not visible under normal light.
3. No treatment chemistry from a prior step remains un-neutralized on the fabric.
4. Only once all three are confirmed does the item proceed to heat-based finishing.
