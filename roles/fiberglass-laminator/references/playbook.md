# Playbook

## Temperature-adjusted catalyst reference table (illustrative — always use the specific resin's manufacturer datasheet)

| Ambient temperature | Catalyst % (typical polyester/MEKP) | Approximate gel time |
|---|---|---|
| 60°F | 2.0% | ~30 min |
| 77°F (reference) | 1.5% | ~20 min |
| 85°F | 1.25% | ~15 min |
| 95°F | 1.0% | ~18-20 min (adjusted to restore working time) |
| 105°F+ | 0.75% or per manufacturer minimum | Consult manufacturer — may require additional cooling/scheduling measures |

**Rule:** never use a single fixed catalyst ratio regardless of ambient temperature. Check the current shop temperature against the manufacturer's specific table before every batch, especially on days with unusual weather.

## Trapped-air/dry-spot disposition table

| Defect | Depth/extent | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Surface-only cosmetic air bubble, no fiber involvement | Surface gel coat only | Cosmetic repair acceptable |
| Dry spot in outer ply only, structural plies underneath fully wetted | Single outer ply | Localized rework of that ply may be acceptable — evaluate against structural criticality of location |
| Dry spot/void spanning multiple plies or in a structurally critical zone | Subsurface, multiple plies | Scrap and re-lay affected section/part — surface patch cannot restore base laminate integrity |
| Void discovered post-cure via tap test/inspection with unclear extent | Unknown until further inspection | Non-destructive testing (ultrasonic, tap test mapping) required before disposition decision |

## Layup sequence checklist

1. Confirm ply schedule (count, orientation, fiber type/weight) against job specification before cutting material.
2. Check ambient temperature/humidity and set catalyst ratio per manufacturer's current table.
3. Size resin batches to match realistic application rate within the adjusted gel time — smaller batches in hot/humid conditions.
4. Apply resin to fully wet out each ply; roll from center outward, checking for trapped air before adding the next layer.
5. Verify ply orientation matches the schedule for each layer as it's placed, not just at final inspection.
6. Monitor exotherm on thick sections; if excessive heat is detected, pause and allow cooling per manufacturer guidance before continuing.
7. Allow full cure/post-cure time per specification before handling for structural use or placing into service.
8. Document batch ratios, ambient conditions, and any deviations before the part proceeds to inspection/release.
