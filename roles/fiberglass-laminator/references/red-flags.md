# Red flags

### Resin beginning to gel before a ply is fully wetted out and rolled
- **Usually means:** catalyst ratio wasn't adjusted for ambient temperature, or batch size was too large for the available working time.
- **First question:** was the catalyst ratio checked against the manufacturer's temperature-adjustment table for today's shop conditions?
- **Data to pull:** current ambient temperature/humidity, catalyst ratio used, manufacturer's temperature-adjusted ratio guidance.

### Visible dry spots or white/pale areas in a ply after rolling
- **Usually means:** incomplete resin saturation of the fiber, often from insufficient resin, premature gelling, or insufficient rolling pressure/time.
- **First question:** did the batch gel before this section was fully worked, or was insufficient resin applied to begin with?
- **Data to pull:** resin batch mix time and gel time observed, area and location of the dry spots.

### A laminate feeling noticeably heavier than expected for its ply count and glass weight
- **Usually means:** excess resin applied beyond what's needed to wet out the fiber — a resin-rich condition that weakens rather than strengthens the part.
- **First question:** does calculated glass content (dry glass weight / total laminate weight) fall within the target band?
- **Data to pull:** dry glass weight used, total cured laminate weight, target glass content range for this process.

### A ply oriented differently than the specified layup schedule, even if it appears to use material more efficiently
- **Usually means:** a deviation from the structural design intent, since orientation determines directional strength.
- **First question:** does the actual ply orientation match the specified schedule, verified against the job's layup drawing?
- **Data to pull:** the specified ply schedule, the as-built orientation for each ply.

### Excessive heat felt or measured during cure of a thick layup section
- **Usually means:** over-catalyzed resin or insufficient heat dissipation in a thick section, risking exotherm-driven cracking or warping.
- **First question:** was catalyst ratio correct for this thickness and ambient condition, and is a temperature probe monitoring the exotherm?
- **Data to pull:** catalyst ratio used, exotherm temperature reading if monitored, layup thickness at the affected location.

### A part handled or placed into service before its specified full cure/post-cure time has elapsed
- **Usually means:** schedule pressure treated tack-free/handleable as equivalent to fully cured/design-strength.
- **First question:** has the full cure or post-cure time per the resin's specification actually elapsed, or just enough time for the surface to feel solid?
- **Data to pull:** resin cure schedule specification, actual elapsed time since layup/last resin application.

### Trapped air or a void discovered after cure (via tap testing, visual inspection of an edge, or failure)
- **Usually means:** air wasn't fully worked out during layup before the resin gelled, and is now undetectable except through destructive or specialized non-visual inspection.
- **First question:** where in the layup sequence did this occur, and does the pattern suggest a specific ply or technique issue?
- **Data to pull:** layup log for ply sequence and timing, any known rushed sections during layup.

### A dry-spot or void repair attempted as a surface patch on a structural part
- **Usually means:** the underlying laminate defect (subsurface dry spot, void) isn't actually resolved by a surface-level patch.
- **First question:** does this defect affect the base laminate's structural continuity, or is it genuinely a surface-only cosmetic issue?
- **Data to pull:** the defect's depth/extent, the part's structural criticality at that location.

### A resin batch mixed larger than what can realistically be applied within its working time
- **Usually means:** batch sizing didn't account for current ambient conditions or the actual layup pace achievable.
- **First question:** does the batch size match the realistic application rate given current gel time, or was it sized for convenience/fewer mixing interruptions?
- **Data to pull:** batch size mixed, actual time to fully apply it, gel time under current conditions.

### A new resin/catalyst system or supplier used without checking its specific temperature-adjustment table
- **Usually means:** the ratio/working-time assumptions from a familiar prior system were carried over without verifying they apply to the new one.
- **First question:** has this specific resin system's datasheet been checked, or is the ratio being assumed from experience with a different product?
- **Data to pull:** the current resin system's technical datasheet, the ratio/schedule actually used.
