# Playbook

Filled worksheets an instructor actually runs — not descriptions of them. Adapt the numbers to the medium and market; keep the structure.

## 1. Break-even and pricing worksheet

Run this before the curriculum is finalized, whenever price, enrollment cap, or studio revenue-share changes.

**Inputs (example: 6-session wheel-throwing intro, 2 hrs/session):**

| Line | Value |
|---|---|
| Contact hours | 12 |
| Non-contact hours (glazing, kiln loads, prep) | 3 |
| Total hours committed | 15 |
| Tuition (list price) | $220/student |
| Studio revenue-share to instructor | 70% |
| Materials cost/student | $42.50 (25 lb clay @ $1.10/lb + $15 glaze/fire) |
| Target hourly wage | $50/hr |

**Formula:** Net = (tuition × instructor share × n) − (materials cost × n)

**Solve for minimum viable enrollment (n) at target wage:**

```
Net / 15 hrs ≥ $50
(0.70 × 220 − 42.50) × n ≥ 750
111.5n ≥ 750
n ≥ 6.73  →  minimum viable enrollment = 7
```

**Enrollment sensitivity table (fill this in for every course, not just when something feels off):**

| n | Gross | Studio cut (30%) | Materials | Net | $/hr (15 hrs) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | $880 | $264 | $170 | $446 | $29.73 |
| 5 | $1,100 | $330 | $212.50 | $557.50 | $37.17 |
| 7 | $1,540 | $462 | $297.50 | $780.50 | $52.03 |
| 10 (cap) | $2,200 | $660 | $425 | $1,115 | $74.33 |

**Rule:** if the studio's stated minimum-to-run is below your break-even n, don't accept it silently — combine sections, extend registration 48 hours with a referral incentive, or apply a published small-batch surcharge (see template below). Never discover this gap on the morning of session one.

## 2. Session-1 placement-and-win template

Ten minutes, before any content is taught.

1. **Placement task (2–3 min):** one small demonstrated action at the medium's actual skill, not a verbal self-report. *Example (pottery): center a ball of clay on the wheel for 30 seconds — wobble-free centering separates true beginners from repeat students in one attempt.* *Example (cooking): knife-cut a carrot into ¼" dice — grip and consistency show experience level immediately.*
2. **Group by result, not by registration category.** Two or three tiers max; over-segmenting a small room defeats the point.
3. **Deliver one guaranteed personal result before the session ends** — a small bowl pulled and trimmed enough to survive bisque, a knife cut clean enough to plate, a 16-bar phrase played from memory. Pick the smallest unit of the medium that still feels like a finished thing.
4. **Defer sequencing/theory** ("why centering works," scale theory, mother sauces) to session two, once the room has a reason to come back.
5. **Close with the concrete plan for next session** stated aloud — removes the ambiguity that lets a marginal student quietly not return.

## 3. Cancellation / no-show policy (publish at registration, not at the first incident)

```
MINIMUM ENROLLMENT: Sections run at [n] confirmed enrollments by [X days]
before start. Below minimum, we will offer: (a) combine with an adjacent
section at a compatible level, (b) a small-batch rate adjustment of
$[Y] to run as scheduled, or (c) full refund/credit if neither works
for you. You'll hear which by [X-1 days] before start.

NO-SHOW / MAKE-UP: One missed session may be made up in another live
section at the same level within [30] days, subject to space. No-shows
without 24-hour notice forfeit make-up eligibility for that session.

WEATHER / INSTRUCTOR ILLNESS: Session rescheduled to [standard makeup
slot]; if no slot works for a majority of the roster, prorated credit
issued for that session only.
```

**Small-batch surcharge language (drop into registration terms up front):** "Sections with fewer than [6] enrolled at the registration deadline carry a $[30] small-cohort materials/facility fee, reflected in your final balance — smaller sections mean more individual instruction time per person."

## 4. Mixed-level station structure (when lockstep instruction doesn't fit)

Use when the medium doesn't require single-file sequencing (contrast with a one-time safety demo, which does).

| Station | Who works here | What they're doing |
|---|---|---|
| A — Fundamentals | First-timers from placement task | Guided, instructor checks in every 5–8 min |
| B — Building | Returning students, shaky on one specific skill | Self-directed with a reference sheet; instructor spot-checks |
| C — Independent | Repeat students past the course content | Own project, instructor available on request only |

**Rotation rule:** don't rotate people between stations mid-session based on a single stumble — reassign at the start of the next session based on that session's actual output, not a live judgment call that can feel punitive.

## 5. No-show-rate pricing switch

| No-show rate (trailing 4 sessions) | Action |
|---|---|
| Under 10% | Drop-in pricing is fine as-is |
| 10–20% | Introduce a punch-card/block discount to reward pre-commitment, keep drop-in available |
| Over 20% | Move the section to prepaid-block-only; drop-in is creating the revenue variance, not absorbing it |
