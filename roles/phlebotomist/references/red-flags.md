# Phlebotomist — Red Flags

### Citrate tube fill below 90% of stated draw volume
- **Usually means:** discard tube was skipped on a winged-set first-tube draw, vein flow slowed/stopped mid-draw, or vacuum was compromised (tube punctured/expired).
- **First question:** was a discard tube drawn first, and was this tube first in the sequence on a butterfly set?
- **Data to pull:** measured fill volume against the tube's stated nominal draw volume; tube lot/expiration.

### Visible hemolysis in a serum or plasma tube after centrifugation
- **Usually means:** traumatic draw (too-small needle for flow rate, excessive suction, vein collapse), prolonged tourniquet time, or vigorous tube shaking instead of gentle inversion.
- **First question:** which analytes on this panel are hemolysis-sensitive (K+, LDH, AST) versus unaffected (most chemistry, glucose)?
- **Data to pull:** lab's hemolysis-index cutoff for the specific analyzer/analytes ordered.

### Patient can't verbally confirm identity and only one identifier source is available
- **Usually means:** unconscious, cognitively impaired, non-English-speaking without interpreter, or pediatric patient with no accompanying adult.
- **First question:** is there a second independent identifier (wristband + chart, or verbal confirmation from a second staff member) before proceeding?
- **Data to pull:** wristband ID, chart MRN, requisition — all three should match before the draw.

### Two unsuccessful venipuncture attempts on the same patient
- **Usually means:** difficult access (small/fragile/rolling veins, dehydration, prior scarring from repeated draws), not necessarily a technique failure.
- **First question:** has a different site or a butterfly set been tried, or were both attempts the same approach?
- **Data to pull:** site(s) attempted, technique used, any complicating factors (edema, prior IV lines, chemo port restrictions).

### Requisition tube-type doesn't match what's on hand, or a "STAT" order arrives with an incomplete tube set
- **Usually means:** requisition entry error, or a partial order was sent while the rest is still being prepared.
- **First question:** does the ordering system show the full test panel, and does each test map to the correct tube type?
- **Data to pull:** full requisition against the facility's test-to-tube-type reference table.

### A tube shows a clot after gentle inversion in an anticoagulated tube type (EDTA, citrate, heparin)
- **Usually means:** inversion was delayed too long after the draw, insufficient number of inversions, or additive-to-blood ratio issue from underfill.
- **First question:** how much time elapsed between draw and inversion, and how many inversions were performed?
- **Data to pull:** time-to-inversion if logged; fill volume (underfill compounds clotting risk in citrate tubes specifically).

### Blood culture bottles were not drawn first in a multi-tube sequence
- **Usually means:** requisition-order sequencing was followed instead of CLSI order-of-draw, or cultures were added to the order after other tubes were already drawn.
- **First question:** were cultures ordered from the start, or added mid-draw?
- **Data to pull:** draw-sequence documentation; if cultures were drawn after other tubes, flag for possible contamination risk assessment by the lab.

### A specimen sits unrefrigerated/unprocessed past its analyte-specific stability window
- **Usually means:** collection-to-processing delay from workflow bottleneck, batch transport timing, or a missed pickup.
- **First question:** what is this analyte's specific stability window at room temperature (varies widely — glucose degrades fast without a fluoride tube, most chemistry is stable for hours)?
- **Data to pull:** collection timestamp vs. accessioning timestamp vs. the test's published stability window.
