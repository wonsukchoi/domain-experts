# New Accounts Clerk — Red Flags

### Photo on the ID doesn't clearly match the person presenting it
- **Usually means:** benign — poor-quality photo, weight/hair change since issuance; less commonly, borrowed or fraudulent ID.
- **First question:** "Can you provide a second form of ID?"
- **Data to pull:** the ID itself (check for tampering, mismatched fonts/security features), a second document.

### Applicant is unusually reluctant to provide a Social Security number or ID number
- **Usually means:** privacy concern (address with a reasonable explanation) or, less commonly, an attempt to avoid CIP/OFAC screening entirely.
- **First question:** "This is required to open any account here — is there a specific concern I can address?"
- **Data to pull:** whether an ITIN applies instead of an SSN (non-resident applicants), bank's non-documentary alternatives.

### OFAC screening returns a possible match
- **Usually means:** common-name collision (majority of possible matches) or a data-entry variant; rarely, an actual match.
- **First question:** does a secondary identifier (DOB, address, nationality) clearly differ from the SDN entry?
- **Data to pull:** the SDN entry's listed identifiers side-by-side with the applicant's documentation; escalate regardless of your own read.

### Beneficial-ownership percentages don't sum to 100%
- **Usually means:** an owner was left off the certification, either by oversight or intentionally.
- **First question:** "Who holds the remaining [X]%?"
- **Data to pull:** operating agreement or cap table if available; do not certify until the gap is resolved.

### Multiple related individuals open separate accounts on the same day
- **Usually means:** legitimate family/business coordination — but also a structuring pattern (avoiding a reporting threshold by splitting funds across accounts).
- **First question:** is there a stated, coherent reason for opening multiple accounts simultaneously?
- **Data to pull:** funding source and amount for each account; flag to a supervisor if amounts cluster just under a reporting threshold.

### Applicant wants to add an authorized signer who isn't present and won't be verified
- **Usually means:** legitimate absent co-owner (spouse traveling, business partner in another location) — or an attempt to add access for someone who wouldn't pass verification themselves.
- **First question:** what's the bank's documented process for verifying an absent signer (notarized signature card, video verification, in-person visit later)?
- **Data to pull:** whichever absent-signer verification method the bank's procedure specifies — don't substitute a phone confirmation for it.

### Business entity's registered address is a mail drop or registered-agent address with no other verifiable business presence
- **Usually means:** normal for many small/home-based businesses or LLCs using a registered-agent service — but also a shell-company indicator.
- **First question:** does the business have any other verifiable presence (website, prior tax filings, physical inventory/equipment)?
- **Data to pull:** business registration/formation documents, EIN confirmation letter.

### Customer requests a product mismatched to their stated use and pushes back when a better fit is suggested
- **Usually means:** they have a reason you don't have visibility into (paying down a specific bill schedule, prior bad experience with a product type) — not necessarily an error.
- **First question:** is there a reason for this product beyond what's stated that changes the fit calculation?
- **Data to pull:** none required — document the recommendation was made and respect the customer's final choice.

### Signature card is set to "any one signer" on a large joint account without both parties present to confirm
- **Usually means:** the application defaulted to the common setting without anyone actually deciding it — the most frequent source of a later signer dispute.
- **First question:** "Do you both want either of you to be able to withdraw the full balance alone?"
- **Data to pull:** verbal confirmation from all named signers, or the bank's absent-signer verification process if one isn't present.
