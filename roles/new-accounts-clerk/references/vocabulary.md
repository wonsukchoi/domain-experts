# New Accounts Clerk — Vocabulary

### CIP (Customer Identification Program)
The BSA-mandated procedure (31 CFR 1020.220) requiring a bank to collect and verify a minimum set of identity elements before opening an account.
**In use:** "CIP requires the four elements — name, DOB, address, ID number — plus verification, not just collection."
**Common misuse:** treated as satisfied once the fields are filled in, without the verification step actually resolving to a reasonable degree of certainty.

### Documentary verification
Confirming identity via a physical, government-issued document (driver's license, passport).
**In use:** "She had a valid passport, so we verified documentarily — no need for a credit-bureau check."
**Common misuse:** accepted from an expired or clearly altered document because "it's still basically the same person."

### Non-documentary verification
Confirming identity through an independent source other than a physical ID — credit bureau, public database, or comparison against existing customer information.
**In use:** "This was a mail-in application, so we ran non-documentary verification through the credit bureau."
**Common misuse:** skipped for a mail-in or online application when neither documentary nor non-documentary verification actually occurred — the account gets opened unverified.

### Beneficial owner
An individual owning 25% or more of a legal-entity customer, required to be identified under the CDD Rule regardless of whether they're involved in day-to-day operations.
**In use:** "All three members clear the 25% beneficial-ownership threshold, so all three need to be identified and verified."
**Common misuse:** confused with "authorized signer" — a beneficial owner may never touch the account day-to-day, and an authorized signer may own none of the entity.

### Control person
The one individual, regardless of ownership percentage, who manages or directs the legal-entity customer — required to be identified on every entity account separate from the beneficial-ownership disclosure.
**In use:** "Owner C is both a 25% beneficial owner and the control person — both boxes get checked for her."
**Common misuse:** assumed to always be the largest owner — it's whoever has managerial control, which can be a minority owner or a non-owner employee.

### OFAC SDN List
The Treasury Department's Specially Designated Nationals list — individuals and entities a U.S. financial institution is prohibited from doing business with.
**In use:** "The name screened clean against the SDN list, so we proceeded to CIP verification."
**Common misuse:** treated as a one-time check at account opening only — ongoing accounts are typically re-screened against list updates, per bank policy.

### Possible match (OFAC)
A screening result that isn't a clear miss or a clear exact match — partial name overlap, proximate but not identical identifying details.
**In use:** "This came back as a possible match, not exact — holding activation and escalating to compliance."
**Common misuse:** dismissed at the clerk level based on a confident personal read ("obviously not the same person") rather than escalated, which is a strict-liability compliance violation regardless of how reasonable the dismissal felt.

### Signature card
The account record naming every authorized signer, their signature specimen, and the scope of their authority to act on the account.
**In use:** "The signature card is set to require two-of-three signers for withdrawals over $5,000, per the operating agreement."
**Common misuse:** treated as a formality filled out from a default rather than a deliberate authority-scope decision confirmed with all named signers.

### CDD Rule (Customer Due Diligence Rule)
FinCEN's 2018 rule (31 CFR 1010.230) requiring beneficial-ownership identification for legal-entity customers, layered on top of the base CIP requirement.
**In use:** "CDD requires the beneficial-ownership certification in addition to standard CIP for this LLC account."
**Common misuse:** conflated with CIP as if they're the same requirement — CIP applies to every account, CDD's beneficial-ownership prong applies specifically to legal-entity customers.

### Structuring
Deliberately splitting transactions or accounts to stay under a reporting threshold (e.g., the $10,000 CTR threshold), which is itself illegal regardless of whether the underlying funds are legitimate.
**In use:** "Two related applicants opening separate accounts with amounts just under the reporting threshold on the same day is a structuring red flag worth escalating."
**Common misuse:** assumed to only matter if the money is illicit — structuring is a standalone violation even when the funds themselves are entirely legal.

### Non-resident alien (NRA) account
An account opened for an individual who is not a U.S. citizen or resident, typically requiring an ITIN in place of an SSN and additional documentation.
**In use:** "He doesn't have an SSN, but the ITIN plus passport satisfies CIP for an NRA account."
**Common misuse:** treated as ineligible for account opening entirely when lacking an SSN, rather than routed to the bank's NRA/ITIN procedure.

### Registered agent address
An address used by a business entity to receive official/legal correspondence, which may not reflect where the business actually operates.
**In use:** "The registered-agent address alone isn't disqualifying, but it's worth checking for another verifiable business presence."
**Common misuse:** either automatically trusted as a legitimate business address or automatically treated as suspicious — it requires the same due-diligence follow-up either way.
