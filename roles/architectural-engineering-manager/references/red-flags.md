# Red flags & diagnostics

Signals an experienced AE manager notices instantly. Load this when triaging a project health or firm-health question — not for general reasoning (that's `SKILL.md`).

### Net multiplier below 2.7 on a project tracking to completion
- **Usually means:** scope creep absorbed unbilled, or original fee was quoted below break-even without a conscious loss-leader decision
- **First question:** "How many hours have we logged against this project that aren't in an approved change order?"
- **Data to pull:** project time-tracking report filtered to hours logged outside the original approved scope/phase budget

### Firm-wide utilization above 78% for two consecutive months
- **Usually means:** staff are overcommitted, review time is being compressed to hit deadlines, quality risk is rising even though "everyone's busy" looks healthy on a dashboard
- **First question:** "Which projects have had QA/QC review time cut or compressed in the last 60 days?"
- **Data to pull:** planned vs. actual review hours by project for the current quarter

### A signed project has no specific staff assigned two weeks before kickoff
- **Usually means:** the project was sold against projected capacity that hasn't materialized, or a staffing conflict with another project is being deferred rather than resolved
- **First question:** "Who specifically, by name, is doing the first deliverable, and what are they currently assigned to that this would displace?"
- **Data to pull:** current staffing/utilization tracker cross-referenced against the new project's start date

### Client requests a "quick" out-of-scope item more than twice on one project
- **Usually means:** the additional-services mechanism either wasn't in the contract or isn't being enforced, and the client has learned the firm will absorb small asks for free
- **First question:** "Was this logged as a change order, and if not, why not?"
- **Data to pull:** change-order log for the project vs. actual scope-adjacent work performed

### A fee was quoted lump-sum against a program described as "still being finalized"
- **Usually means:** the firm has accepted scope-definition risk it can't control, and any client-driven program change becomes unbilled hours by default
- **First question:** "What specifically is frozen at the fee-quote date, and what happens contractually if it changes?"
- **Data to pull:** the proposal's scope-basis paragraph and whether a program-freeze date is explicitly stated

### A life-safety or structural element's peer review gets rescheduled or shortened when a project is over budget
- **Usually means:** review rigor is being traded against fee pressure — the highest-liability-exposure item is being treated as a discretionary cost
- **First question:** "Is this review being shortened because the design is actually simpler than planned, or because the budget is tight?"
- **Data to pull:** original QA/QC plan's review-hour allocation for this specific system vs. current planned hours

### A design decision under dispute has no contemporaneous documentation
- **Usually means:** the decision may have been reasonable, but standard-of-care defense depends on showing what was known and considered at the time, not on reconstructing the reasoning after the fact
- **First question:** "What's in the project file — meeting notes, calc basis, code section referenced — dated at or before the decision?"
- **Data to pull:** project correspondence and calculation package timestamps around the decision date

### Professional liability insurance renewal reviewed as a line-item cost, not against claims/risk mix
- **Usually means:** coverage limits or exclusions may not match the firm's actual current project risk profile (e.g., more high-rise or life-safety work than when the policy was last actually reviewed)
- **First question:** "Has our project mix changed materially since the last time we reviewed coverage against actual risk, not just premium?"
- **Data to pull:** current backlog by project type/risk category vs. policy limits and any project-type exclusions

### A client pushes back hard on additional-services billing for a request they characterize as "basically the same thing"
- **Usually means:** the original scope boundary wasn't specific enough to be defensible, or wasn't communicated clearly at proposal stage
- **First question:** "Does the original scope-of-work section explicitly exclude this, or are we relying on an implicit understanding?"
- **Data to pull:** the specific scope-of-work clause being invoked, read as a client unfamiliar with the project would read it
