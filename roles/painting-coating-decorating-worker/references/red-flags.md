# Red flags

### Ambient temperature or humidity outside the coating material's validated application range
- **Usually means:** cure and application behavior may differ from what the standard technique/timing was validated for.
- **First question:** by how much do current conditions differ from the validated range, and does flash time need adjustment?
- **Data to pull:** current temperature/humidity reading, material's specified validated range.

### Film thickness checked at only one point on a part
- **Usually means:** uneven application from technique variation could be present elsewhere on the part and go undetected.
- **First question:** has thickness been checked at multiple representative points, or just one location?
- **Data to pull:** film thickness readings across multiple points if available.

### A recoat applied earlier than the specified flash time due to schedule pressure
- **Usually means:** solvent from the previous coat may not have had time to release, risking trapped-solvent defects (bubbling, poor cure).
- **First question:** how much time actually elapsed since the previous coat, versus the specified minimum?
- **Data to pull:** actual elapsed time, material's specified flash time window.

### A recoat applied well after the specified flash time window
- **Usually means:** the previous coat may have fully cured to a point where intercoat adhesion is compromised.
- **First question:** how much time elapsed since the previous coat, versus the specified maximum recoat window?
- **Data to pull:** actual elapsed time, material's specified maximum recoat window.

### Decorative/finish consistency judged only against the immediately preceding unit in a run
- **Usually means:** small deviations could be accumulating across the run without being caught against a fixed standard.
- **First question:** is a fixed reference sample or documented spec being used for comparison, or just the previous unit?
- **Data to pull:** the fixed reference sample/spec, current unit's comparison basis.

### A coating defect (run, sag, orange peel, bubbling) with no clear diagnosis before rework
- **Usually means:** the specific cause (technique, thickness, flash time, environmental condition) hasn't been identified, risking a repeat of the same defect.
- **First question:** does the defect pattern point to a specific one of these causes, or is rework proceeding without diagnosis?
- **Data to pull:** film thickness data, flash time observed, environmental conditions at time of application, spray technique notes.

### A spray technique applied identically to a prior job despite different ambient conditions
- **Usually means:** an assumption that technique alone determines outcome, without accounting for condition-driven differences in atomization/deposition behavior.
- **First question:** do current conditions match the conditions this technique was originally validated under?
- **Data to pull:** current vs. validated environmental conditions.

### A part released without documented film thickness or environmental condition data
- **Usually means:** quality verification relied on visual inspection alone rather than measured data.
- **First question:** does the job record show actual measured film thickness and conditions, or just a pass/fail note?
- **Data to pull:** the job's quality record for this part.

### A coating showing delayed adhesion failure (peeling, delamination) discovered after the part is in service
- **Usually means:** possible flash-time or environmental-condition issue at time of application that wasn't caught immediately.
- **First question:** does the original application record show flash time and conditions within spec for this part?
- **Data to pull:** the part's original application record if traceable.

### A worker reporting they judge film thickness "by feel/experience" without gauge verification
- **Usually means:** actual thickness compliance isn't being confirmed, relying on subjective judgment that can drift or vary by individual.
- **First question:** is a wet mil gauge actually being used, or is thickness estimated by visual/tactile impression?
- **Data to pull:** the worker's stated verification method, any gauge readings on record for recent work.
