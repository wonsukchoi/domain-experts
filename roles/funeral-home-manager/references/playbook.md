# Funeral home pricing, compliance, and break-even playbook

Filled templates for the four recurring funeral-home-manager decisions: GPL structure, cash-advance disclosure, break-even case-mix modeling, and the arrangement-conference checklist.

## 1. General Price List (GPL) structure

Required sections per 16 CFR 453 — every price itemized, never bundled as the only presented option:

| Section | Example line items | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Basic Services Fee | Non-declinable fee covering funeral director/staff time, overhead, permits, coordination | Must be disclosed as non-declinable and applies regardless of other selections |
| Transportation | Removal from place of death, hearse, family car | Itemized per use, not folded into a package price |
| Facilities/staff for viewing/service | Visitation use of facility, staff for ceremony | Priced per day/use |
| Merchandise | Casket, urn, outer burial container | Each priced individually; a printed casket price list is a separate required document |
| Cremation-specific | Crematory fee, alternative container | Priced whether or not a service is selected |
| Cash-advance items | Obituary notices, flowers, officiant honorarium, death certificates | See §2 — markup disclosure required if any exists |

Present the GPL as the first document in every arrangement conference, in-person or read aloud with mailed follow-up if requested by phone, before any package or bundled-price conversation begins.

## 2. Cash-advance markup disclosure log

| Item | Vendor cost | Amount billed to family | Markup % | Disclosed on GPL? | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Floral arrangement | $274 | $315 | 15% | No | Add disclosure line: "A service charge is added to the cost of cash advance items" per 16 CFR 453, or reduce to cost |
| Obituary notice (newspaper) | $180 | $180 | 0% | N/A | No action — pass-through at cost |
| Death certificates (10 copies) | $100 | $115 | 15% | No | Same disclosure fix as floral line |
| Officiant honorarium | $250 | $250 | 0% | N/A | No action — pass-through at cost |

Rule: any row with a markup and "No" in the disclosed column is a compliance action item, not a pricing preference — resolve before the next arrangement conference, not at the next GPL reprint cycle.

## 3. Break-even case-mix model

```
Fixed costs / year: $780,000
Case mix (current year):
  Traditional:            149 cases × $9,200  = $1,370,800
  Direct cremation:        130 cases × $2,100  =   $273,000
  Cremation-with-service:   31 cases × $4,800  =   $148,800
  Total revenue:                                 $1,792,600
Estimated COGS (casket wholesale, cash-advance pass-through, ~40% of revenue): $717,040
Net margin: $1,792,600 - $780,000 - $717,040 = $295,560
```

Recompute this model whenever cremation share shifts more than ~3 percentage points year over year. A rising cremation-direct share with flat total case volume erodes net margin faster than it appears in the top-line revenue number, because fixed costs don't scale down with the mix shift.

## 4. Arrangement conference checklist

- [ ] GPL presented (in person, mailed, or read aloud per phone-disclosure rule) before any merchandise/package discussion
- [ ] Funding source identified (at-need payment, insurance assignment, preneed trust) — before quoting a guaranteed price
- [ ] If preneed: guaranteed vs. non-guaranteed status confirmed against the actual funding vehicle and state law, stated plainly to the family
- [ ] Full service tier range presented (traditional, cremation-with-service, direct cremation) — not just the tier the family initially asked about
- [ ] Cash-advance items itemized with markup disclosed if one exists
- [ ] Statement of Funeral Goods and Services Selected completed and signed
- [ ] Licensed funeral director present/conducting per state requirement
- [ ] Aftercare follow-up scheduled separately from this conversation

## 5. Guaranteed vs. non-guaranteed preneed decision

| Funding vehicle | Guarantee appropriate? | Risk-bearing party if costs rise |
|---|---|---|
| Trust-funded, state permits guarantee, funeral home assumes risk | Yes, if home's reserve/trust performance supports it | Funeral home |
| Insurance-assigned, policy face value fixed at signing | Yes, if policy explicitly assigns full benefit to cover guaranteed services | Insurance carrier (funeral home absorbs gap if any) |
| Trust-funded, state prohibits guarantee on this contract type | No | Family (balance due at time of need) |
| Informal payment plan, no trust/insurance vehicle | No — do not represent as guaranteed under any circumstance | Family |

Never state or imply a guarantee to a family unless the funding vehicle and state law in the row above actually support it — this is a document-driven answer, not a sales judgment call.
