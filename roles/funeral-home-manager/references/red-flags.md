# Red flags — signals a funeral home manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can be a fixable gap; two or more together on the same case type or staff member is a pattern worth an immediate compliance or financial review.

### Arrangement conference documentation shows a bundled package price presented before the GPL
- **Usually means:** the arrangement counselor is defaulting to a sales-friendly bundled pitch under time pressure, which is a direct 16 CFR 453 violation regardless of whether the family was ultimately satisfied with the outcome.
- **First question:** "Pull the last 10 arrangement files for this counselor — is the GPL timestamped or logged as presented before any package discussion in every one?"
- **Data to pull:** arrangement conference logs/checklists, GPL disclosure timestamp if tracked, counselor-by-counselor comparison.

### A cash-advance item billed to families above the vendor's actual invoiced cost with no markup disclosure on the GPL
- **Usually means:** either an oversight in how the GPL was last updated, or a standing practice nobody flagged as a compliance issue — both are FTC exposure the moment a family or examiner compares the invoice to the vendor bill.
- **First question:** "For this vendor category, what's the actual invoice cost versus what's billed to families, and where does the GPL disclose the difference?"
- **Data to pull:** cash-advance disclosure log (see `references/playbook.md` §2), vendor invoices for the period, current GPL cash-advance section language.

### Direct-cremation selection rate rising faster than the audited disclosure rate of the cremation-with-service option
- **Usually means:** families are defaulting to the cheapest visible option because the mid-tier alternative isn't being consistently presented, not because they compared it and declined it.
- **First question:** "Sample the last 20 direct-cremation files — how many document the cremation-with-service option being offered before the family signed?"
- **Data to pull:** case files with service-tier documentation, counselor-by-counselor breakdown, revenue-per-case trend for direct cremation versus cremation-with-service.

### A preneed contract file with "guaranteed" language but a funding vehicle or state statute that doesn't support a guarantee
- **Usually means:** either a sales script error or a genuine misunderstanding by staff of which contracts can legally guarantee price — either way, the family is now expecting a price lock the home cannot legally or financially honor.
- **First question:** "What's the actual trust or insurance-assignment structure behind this contract, and does state law allow a guarantee on it?"
- **Data to pull:** preneed contract funding-vehicle documentation, state statute on guaranteed preneed contracts, sample of recent preneed contracts for the same pattern.

### A shift scheduled without a state-licensed funeral director/embalmer covering first-call response
- **Usually means:** a staffing gap that isn't just a service-quality issue — it's an operating-without-a-license exposure in most states if a removal or arrangement occurs during that window.
- **First question:** "Who was the licensed director on call during this shift, and is that documented in the schedule?"
- **Data to pull:** staffing schedule with license credentials listed, state board requirements for the jurisdiction, any first-call logs from unlicensed-coverage windows.

### Net margin declining while total case volume stays flat or grows
- **Usually means:** the case mix has shifted toward lower-revenue categories (cremation-direct) faster than the cost structure has adjusted — a break-even problem hiding behind a stable top-line case count.
- **First question:** "What's this year's case mix by category compared to three years ago, and has the break-even model been recalculated since then?"
- **Data to pull:** case mix by category and year, fixed-cost trend, break-even model (see `references/playbook.md` §3).

### Preneed trust or insurance reconciliation hasn't been performed in over a year
- **Usually means:** the funeral home may not actually know whether its guaranteed-price contracts are adequately funded against current merchandise and service costs, which surfaces as a funding shortfall exactly when a family needs the service delivered.
- **First question:** "When was the last trust/insurance reconciliation, and does it show the funded amount tracking current GPL prices for the same package?"
- **Data to pull:** trust account statements, insurance policy assignments, current GPL prices for the contracted package, last reconciliation date.

### A state board complaint or inquiry references a specific arrangement conference practice rather than a one-off incident
- **Usually means:** the practice in question (pricing sequence, markup disclosure, licensing coverage) may be systemic across multiple cases, not isolated to the complainant's case.
- **First question:** "Has this same practice appeared in any other case file in the last 12 months?"
- **Data to pull:** complaint details, comparable case files from the same counselor or time period, prior board correspondence if any.

### Staff turnover concentrated among licensed funeral directors rather than support staff
- **Usually means:** either compensation has fallen behind market for a licensed role, or scheduling burden (first-call coverage frequency) is driving burnout among the exact staff the business is legally dependent on.
- **First question:** "What's the on-call frequency per licensed director, and how does compensation compare to regional benchmarks?"
- **Data to pull:** on-call schedule and frequency per director, exit-interview themes, regional compensation benchmarking (NFDA or state association data).
