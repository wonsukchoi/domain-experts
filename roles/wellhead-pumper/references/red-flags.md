# Red flags

Smell tests a pumper catches in the first few minutes of a site round. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before deciding anything.

### Personal H2S monitor reads 10 ppm or higher near a wellhead or tank hatch with no wind

- **Usually means:** a fitting, thief hatch, or valve is venting sour gas at a rate above background, or a downwind low-lying pocket has collected gas with no dispersion.
- **First question:** "Is this a sustained reading or a transient spike, and where does it drop off as I move away?"
- **Data to pull:** the well's H2S history/classification (per API RP 55 site records), wind direction and speed at time of reading, whether the reading holds, rises, or clears as distance from the source changes.

### Perceived smell of H2S fading or disappearing during a known or suspected exposure

- **Usually means:** olfactory fatigue setting in as concentration climbs through the range (roughly 50–100 ppm) where the nerve begins under-reporting true concentration — not that the area is clearing.
- **First question:** "What does the instrument say right now, independent of what I can smell?"
- **Data to pull:** current monitor reading, monitor calibration/bump-test date, time since entering the area.

### Dynamometer card shows a near-vertical load drop mid-downstroke

- **Usually means:** fluid pound — the pump is running faster than the well can fill the barrel.
- **First question:** "What SPM is the pump running at, and what does the tank-gauge trend say the well can actually fill per stroke?"
- **Data to pull:** current SPM, recent fillage/production trend, prior card for this well (to confirm this is a change, not baseline).

### Dynamometer card bottom corner is rounded/sloped instead of sharp

- **Usually means:** gas interference — free gas in the barrel delaying pressure buildup — more often than pump wear.
- **First question:** "Is there a downhole gas separator on this string, and is it sized for current gas-oil ratio?"
- **Data to pull:** current GOR, gas separator specification and installation date, card area trend over recent visits.

### Tank or separator pressure sits within roughly 10% of its relief-valve set point

- **Usually means:** the vessel is operating at the edge of its design margin, not "something to watch on the next round."
- **First question:** "What changed upstream — choke setting, separator level control, gas rate — that pushed pressure this close to the set point?"
- **Data to pull:** relief-valve set point vs. MAWP from the vessel nameplate, current operating pressure trend over the last several readings, upstream choke/flow-control settings.

### Casing or tubing pressure creeping upward with no operational change behind it

- **Usually means:** a tubing leak, gas migration behind casing, or a developing communication path — not the well "finding a new level" on its own.
- **First question:** "When did this trend start, and did any setting, workover, or offset-well activity coincide with it?"
- **Data to pull:** pressure history over the last several weeks, any recent workover or stimulation on this or an offset well, annular fluid level if monitored.

### Disposal-well injection pressure at or above ~90% of max authorized surface pressure (MASP)

- **Usually means:** injection rate is pushing the formation toward its fracture gradient, risking an out-of-zone fracture and a reportable permit exceedance.
- **First question:** "What's the injection rate right now, and can it come down without shutting the well in?"
- **Data to pull:** current injection pressure and rate, permitted MASP, step-rate test history establishing the fracture gradient for this injection zone.

### Measured production more than ~15% below the decline-curve projection for two consecutive readings

- **Usually means:** a mechanical problem (fillage, leak, paraffin, gas interference) more often than a genuine reservoir-decline acceleration, especially if the deviation appeared abruptly rather than gradually.
- **First question:** "Did this show up as a step change or a gradual drift, and does the dynamometer card look any different from the last baseline card?"
- **Data to pull:** decline-curve parameters (qi, Di, b) and projected rate for this reading, current and prior dynamometer cards, SPM and any recent setting changes.

### Missed scheduled check-in beyond the grace window (commonly 30 minutes)

- **Usually means:** most often a dead radio/phone or a longer-than-planned task — but on a solo remote route it must be treated as a possible incapacitation until confirmed otherwise.
- **First question:** "What was the last confirmed location and task, and who's closest to dispatch there now?"
- **Data to pull:** last check-in time and location, route plan for the day, vehicle GPS/telemetry if equipped.

### BS&W (basic sediment and water) cut rising with no known workover or stimulation

- **Usually means:** water breakthrough at the perforations, a casing/tubing integrity issue, or an emulsion problem forming upstream in the separator.
- **First question:** "Is the water cut rising gradually across several readings or did it jump at one visit?"
- **Data to pull:** BS&W trend over recent thief samples, separator/treater temperature and chemical-injection rate, any recent offset-well activity.

### Frost or ice forming on a gas line in warm weather

- **Usually means:** rapid gas expansion through a leak, an undersized choke, or a partial restriction — the Joule-Thomson cooling effect made visible.
- **First question:** "Where exactly on the line is the frost, and is it at a fitting, a choke, or a straight run?"
- **Data to pull:** line pressure upstream and downstream of the frosted section, choke size if applicable, whether the frost location has changed since the last visit.
