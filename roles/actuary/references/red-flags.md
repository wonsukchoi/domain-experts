# Red flags — what an actuary notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Format per flag: signal → most likely cause(s) → first question → data to pull. Any one can be innocent; two or three together is a pattern.

### Age-to-age factor for the latest 1–2 diagonals running >10% above the 5-year average
- **Usually means:** a regime shift (social inflation, a venue change, a new plaintiff-bar tactic) is emerging in the line, or a claims-handling change (reserving philosophy, adjuster staffing) is inflating case reserves faster than actual loss cost is rising.
- **First question:** "Is this a claims-department process change or an external loss-cost trend — and does it show up in paid losses too, or only in case reserves?"
- **Data to pull:** paid-only triangle alongside reported triangle for the same accident years, claims department staffing/process-change log, closed-claim severity by settlement quarter.

### Claim counts below the 1,082-claim full-credibility standard for a segment carrying a large indicated rate change
- **Usually means:** the raw indication is mostly sampling noise; a large swing on thin data is being treated as signal.
- **First question:** "What's the credibility-weighted indication once blended with the complement, and does the story survive that blend?"
- **Data to pull:** claim count by segment, the complement of credibility being proposed (statewide/countrywide indication), and the segment's indication history for the last 3 filings (a segment that swings wildly filing to filing is a credibility problem, not a trend).

### IBNR-to-case-reserve ratio for a long-tail line dropping below ~20% for an immature accident year (under 36 months)
- **Usually means:** the reserve is being carried almost entirely as case reserves with little actuarial IBNR provision — either the case reserves are unusually strong (verify) or the actuarial indication hasn't been updated to reflect emergence patterns typical at this maturity.
- **First question:** "What was the IBNR-to-case ratio for the last 3 accident years at this same maturity, and why is this one different?"
- **Data to pull:** IBNR/case ratio by accident year at matched maturity, average case reserve per open claim by accident year (a rising average case reserve without a rising IBNR usually means the actuarial selection lagged a real trend).

### Combined ratio improving while paid-to-incurred ratio is declining for 2+ quarters
- **Usually means:** the "improvement" is a reserve-recognition artifact — losses are being case-reserved lower or IBNR is being released faster than paid claims are actually closing, not an underwriting or claims improvement.
- **First question:** "Bridge the combined-ratio improvement: how much is frequency/severity actually down, versus reserve releases?"
- **Data to pull:** paid loss ratio vs. incurred loss ratio trend by accident year, reserve release/strengthening by accident year for the last 8 quarters.

### A rate filing's permissible loss ratio implied by the requested change doesn't match the filed expense ratio
- **Usually means:** the expense provision used in the rate indication is stale (doesn't reflect current commission, overhead, or reinsurance cost) or the profit provision was set by convention rather than a cost-of-capital calculation.
- **First question:** "What expense ratio and profit provision are actually embedded in this permissible loss ratio, and when were they last recalculated from the current expense base?"
- **Data to pull:** current-year expense study by category, target return on capital used to set the profit provision, prior 3 filings' permissible loss ratios (a permissible LR that hasn't moved in 5 years despite expense-ratio drift is a red flag on its own).

### NAIC RBC ratio trending down toward 250–300% of Authorized Control Level with no capital plan in place
- **Usually means:** reserve strengthening, adverse underwriting results, or asset losses are eroding surplus faster than earnings are replenishing it, and the company is on a trajectory toward the 200% Company Action Level trigger within a few reporting periods.
- **First question:** "At the current trend, how many quarters until we cross 200%, and what's the plan before we get there?"
- **Data to pull:** RBC ratio trend by quarter for 2 years, surplus bridge (underwriting result, investment result, reserve development, capital actions), reinsurance program renewal terms (a shrinking program can itself move the ratio).

### A statutory reserve opinion's "risk of material adverse deviation" section is boilerplate-identical to the prior 3 years
- **Usually means:** the disclosure isn't being re-derived from the current year's actual risk factors (new line of business, a known emerging trend, reinsurance collectability) — it's copy-forward language that no longer reflects the book.
- **First question:** "What changed in the risk profile this year that should change this section's language?"
- **Data to pull:** prior 3 years' SAO risk-of-adverse-deviation sections side by side, list of new/discontinued lines and reinsurers this year, any known emerging trend flagged internally this year.

### Loss trend selection uses a single blended frequency-severity figure for a line where the two are known to move in opposite directions
- **Usually means:** a real severity acceleration is being masked by a frequency decline in the blended number, understating the true loss-cost trend for pricing purposes.
- **First question:** "What are frequency and severity trend running separately, and does the blended figure hide a divergence?"
- **Data to pull:** frequency and severity trend fit separately by accident half-year, at least 3 trend-period alternatives (e.g., 4-, 6-, 8-quarter fits) to check sensitivity.
