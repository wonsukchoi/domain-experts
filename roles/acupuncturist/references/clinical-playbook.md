# Clinical Playbook

Filled operational references: the intake screen, the four-examination capture table, the pattern-to-formula map, needling-depth thresholds at high-risk points, course-length defaults, and a SOAP template. Defaults, not laws — deviate consciously and document why.

## 1. Intake red-flag screen (run before any diagnostic conversation)

| Check | Question | If positive |
|---|---|---|
| Anticoagulant/antiplatelet | "Any blood thinners, high-dose fish oil, or herbs like danshen, ginkgo, dong quai?" | Reduce needle count, avoid high-risk-depth points, confirm recent INR/platelet status if on warfarin |
| Bleeding disorder | "Any history of easy bruising, hemophilia, or clotting disorder?" | Same as above, plus consider physician clearance |
| Pacemaker/ICD | "Do you have any implanted cardiac device?" | No electroacupuncture without documented cardiology clearance; manual technique only otherwise |
| Pregnancy | "Any chance you're pregnant, or what week are you at?" | Apply the traditional exclusion list (SP6, LI4, BL60, GB21, lower abdomen/sacrum) as a disclosed convention; document informed consent either way |
| Active infection/broken skin | "Any open wounds, active skin infection, or eczema at likely treatment sites?" | Select alternate points; do not needle through compromised skin |
| Structural/systemic red flags | Unexplained weight loss, night sweats, progressive neuro deficit, fever | Refer to physician before treating empirically |

## 2. Four Examinations — data capture

| Examination | What's captured | Example entry |
|---|---|---|
| Look | Tongue body color, coating, shape/cracks; complexion; posture | Tongue: pale body, thin white coat, no cracks |
| Listen/smell | Voice strength, breath sound, odor | Voice slightly weak, no notable odor |
| Ask | Sleep, appetite, digestion, temperature preference, pain quality/timing, stress, menstrual history if relevant | Headache worse late afternoon/with stress, better with rest; occasional dizziness |
| Palpate | Pulse (rate, depth, quality per position), channel/muscle palpation for Ashi points | Pulse: wiry-thin bilateral; neck/shoulder tension on palpation |

## 3. Pattern → point-formula map (representative, not exhaustive)

| Pattern | Key signs | Representative points | Adjunct |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liver Qi stagnation | Irritability, distending pain that moves, wiry pulse | LR3, LI4 ("Four Gates"), GB34 | Cupping over tense muscle bands |
| Liver Yang rising | Headache, dizziness, wiry-thin pulse, worse with stress | LR3, LR2, GB20 (avoid GB21 depth risk), KI3 | — |
| Blood deficiency | Pale tongue/complexion, dizziness, wiry-thin (not wiry-full) pulse, insomnia | SP6, ST36, BL17, BL20 | — |
| Spleen Qi deficiency | Fatigue, loose stools, poor appetite, pale tongue with scalloped edges | ST36, SP3, SP6, Ren12 | Moxa on Ren4/Ren12 |
| Kidney Yang deficiency | Cold extremities, low back weakness, frequent clear urination, deep-weak pulse | Ren4, BL23, KI3, GV4 | Moxa (direct or needle-handle) |
| Damp-Heat | Greasy yellow coating, slippery-rapid pulse, localized heat/inflammation | SP9, LI11, ST40 | Avoid moxa (contraindicated in heat patterns) |

## 4. Needling depth/angle thresholds at anatomically high-risk points

| Point | Risk | Depth/technique default |
|---|---|---|
| GB21 | Pneumothorax — ~30% of acupuncture pneumothorax cases trace here; pleural depth 14.6mm (women) to 17.4mm (men) mean, less in thin/low-BMI patients | Oblique/transverse insertion only, ≤12mm depth as a conservative default; substitute GB20 for channel access when in doubt |
| ST12 | Pneumothorax (supraclavicular fossa overlies lung apex) | Shallow oblique insertion only; avoid in thin patients without direct palpation confirmation of safe depth |
| BL13–BL15 (upper back Shu points) | Pneumothorax | Oblique insertion angled away from the spine and ribcage, shallow depth |
| Ren17/CV17 | Overlies sternum but adjacent soft tissue near mediastinum in some builds | Shallow perpendicular insertion, confirm bony landmark by palpation first |
| Any point over a pacemaker/ICD pocket | Device damage/interference | Avoid direct needling over the device site; no electroacupuncture without cardiology clearance |

## 5. Course-length and reassessment cadence defaults

| Condition category | Initial course commitment | Reassessment cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Acute musculoskeletal (recent onset, <6 weeks) | 3–4 visits | Every visit — fast-moving presentations should show early signal |
| Chronic musculoskeletal (pain type-tension headache, chronic low back pain) | 6–8 weekly visits | Every 3rd visit |
| Systemic/internal-medicine support (digestive, stress/mood) | 8–10 weekly visits | Every 3rd–4th visit |
| Fertility support | 3 monthly cycles minimum (10–12 visits) before evaluating | Every full cycle |

Judging non-response before the committed course is complete confuses a slow-responding correct pattern with a wrong one; going past the committed course without a documented reassessment is how "protocol acupuncture" (see red-flags.md) happens.

## 6. SOAP note template (filled example — chronic tension-type headache, visit 3)

> **S:** Pt reports headache frequency reduced from 3x/wk to 2x/wk over 3 visits; less neck tension; denies bleeding or bruising at needle sites (on ASA 81mg qd).
> **O:** Tongue: pale-pink, improved from pale; thin white coating unchanged. Pulse: wiry-thin, less wiry than baseline. Points needled: GB20 bilat, LR3 bilat, SP6 bilat, Ren4, ST36 bilat; superficial oblique technique throughout given antiplatelet therapy; GB21 avoided, GB20 substituted for channel access. Retention 20 min. No adverse events.
> **A:** TCM pattern shifting from Liver Yang rising (excess, branch) toward the underlying Blood deficiency (root) as the excess symptom resolves — consistent with expected course. Functional improvement: headache-free days increased from 4/7 to 5/7.
> **P:** Continue weekly x 5 more visits; formula shifted this visit toward Qi/Blood tonification (LI4 dropped, ST36/Ren4 added); reassess pattern and headache frequency at visit 6. Continuing coordination with PCP re: ASA; no medication changes recommended by this practitioner.

Every Assessment line names both the pattern-language finding *and* a quantified functional outcome — the pattern language alone doesn't survive a medical-necessity review; the functional trend line is what does.
