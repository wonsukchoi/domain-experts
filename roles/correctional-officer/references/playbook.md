# Correctional Officer — Playbook

| Factor | Scoring rule | This inmate | Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Current charge severity | Misdemeanor=0, felony property=2, felony person=4 | Felony assault (person crime) | 4 |
| Prior record | None=0, non-violent felony=1, violent felony=3 | One non-violent felony (3 yrs ago) | 1 |

| Gang/security-threat-group affiliation | None=0, documented=3 | None documented | 0 |

| **Total** | | | **6** |

Custody-level cutoffs (facility-specific, illustrative): 0-3 minimum, 4-7 medium, 8+ maximum. Score of 6 = medium custody by the instrument alone.

**Override layer** — applied separately from the score, never blended into it: any of the following triggers a special-management flag regardless of point total — direct threat to a named victim/witness, documented enemy in the facility, PREA vulnerability disclosure, active suicide-risk screen positive. Document the trigger, the source (who reported it, exact words if a verbal threat), and the specific housing/contact restriction applied. A flag changes housing and contact rules; it does not change the underlying classification score unless a supervisor formally reclassifies.

## Use-of-force continuum reference

| Tier | Description | When justified |
|---|---|---|
| Officer presence | Uniformed presence, positioning | Any interaction |
| Verbal commands | Direct, short commands | Non-compliance, no immediate physical threat |
| Soft-hands control | Escort holds, wrist/arm control | Passive resistance, non-compliance after verbal, or immediate threat requiring hands-on control |
| Hard-hands control | Strikes, takedowns | Active aggression, assaultive behavior |
| Less-lethal | OC spray, taser, impact weapon | Active aggression with continued resistance to hard-hands, or weapon-adjacent threat |
| Lethal | Firearm | Imminent threat of death or serious bodily injury to self or others |

**Entry point rule:** the tier selected is matched to the threat level presented *at first contact*, not started at the bottom of the table every time. An inmate who lunges with a raised fist at close range justifies entering at soft- or hard-hands directly, documented as such — the report should state explicitly why a lower tier was not attempted first (e.g., "no time/distance for verbal command to take effect before contact").

## Disciplinary-report evidence checklist ("some evidence" sufficiency)

Before submitting an incident report expected to support a disciplinary finding, confirm it contains:

- [ ] **Specific observed facts in time sequence** — not a summary conclusion first.
- [ ] **Distance/positioning detail** — how close, what direction of movement.
- [ ] **Exact words quoted** where a verbal threat or refusal is the basis for the finding — not paraphrased as "inmate was verbally aggressive."
- [ ] **Compliance/non-compliance explicitly stated** — what command was given, whether it was followed, how long the officer waited before escalating.
- [ ] **Witness names** (staff and, where relevant, other inmates) who can corroborate.
- [ ] **Force level applied and justification tied to the observed facts above**, not asserted independently.
- [ ] **Supervisor notification time** and whether supervisor was present.

A report missing three or more of these is at meaningful risk of reversal on appeal — "some evidence" is a low bar, but it is not a zero bar, and a pure conclusion with no supporting facts fails it.

## Suicide-risk rescreen trigger log (filled example)

| Date | Trigger event | Screen result | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Booking (intake screen) | Low risk | Standard housing |
| Day 4 | Adverse court outcome (bail denied) | Elevated — flat affect, statement "nothing matters now" | Moved to closer-observation housing, mental-health referral same day |
| Day 6 | Mental-health follow-up | De-escalated, no current ideation | Returned to standard housing, 72-hr informal check-in scheduled |
| Day 11 | Disciplinary segregation placement | Not yet rescreened | **Gap — rescreen due before placement takes effect, not after** |

The Day 11 gap is the pattern to catch: segregation placement is a listed transition-point trigger and should generate an automatic rescreen requirement before the move, not as an afterthought once the inmate is already isolated.
