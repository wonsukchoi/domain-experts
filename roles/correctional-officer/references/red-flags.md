# Correctional Officer — Red Flags

### Classification score and officer's subjective read disagree by 2+ custody tiers
- **Usually means:** the point instrument missed a factor the file doesn't capture (active threat statement, undocumented enemy conflict), or the officer's read is driven by personal friction rather than risk.
- **First question:** what specific fact, not on the scoring sheet, is driving the disagreement?
- **Data to pull:** intake interview notes, current-charge narrative, any prior facility records if a transfer.

### Incident report leads with a conclusion, not observed facts
- **Usually means:** the officer is summarizing from memory after the fact rather than documenting in sequence, or is aware the facts alone don't clearly support the force level used.
- **First question:** can you restate this incident as a strict timeline of what was seen/heard/done, with no adjectives?
- **Data to pull:** the raw incident report, any camera footage timestamp, witness statements.

### Contraband search recovery rate trending toward zero over consecutive cycles
- **Usually means:** the search schedule has become predictable and the population has adapted around it.
- **First question:** when was the search day/target-selection method last changed?
- **Data to pull:** search log with dates, cells targeted, and selection method (fixed roster vs. intelligence-driven vs. random).

### Suicide-risk screen only exists at intake, no record of rescreening at transition points
- **Usually means:** the facility's protocol treats screening as a one-time checkbox rather than a re-triggered process.
- **First question:** does the facility policy define transition-point rescreen triggers, and is compliance tracked?
- **Data to pull:** screening log against the inmate's event history (court dates, housing moves, disciplinary placements).

### Use-of-force report shows a full climb through every continuum tier before the tier actually applied
- **Usually means:** either the officer is over-documenting a slower escalation than actually occurred (covering), or genuinely under-responded to an immediate threat by wasting time on ineffective lower tiers.
- **First question:** what was the actual time elapsed between first contact and force application?
- **Data to pull:** camera footage timestamps against the written report's stated sequence.

### Classification override applied with no written justification
- **Usually means:** the override is being used to bypass the instrument for reasons that wouldn't hold up on review — personal bias, capacity pressure (bed availability), or genuine safety information that just wasn't documented.
- **First question:** what specific fact justifies deviating from the scored custody level?
- **Data to pull:** the classification file, override log, and whether a supervisor countersigned.

### Disciplinary hearing report has zero direct quotes when a verbal threat is the alleged violation
- **Usually means:** the officer paraphrased rather than recorded the actual statement, weakening the evidentiary basis.
- **First question:** can the officer recall and now document the closest-to-verbatim wording, with a note that it's reconstructed if not contemporaneous?
- **Data to pull:** original report, any audio/video capturing the statement.

### PREA-relevant safety disclosure logged with no follow-up disposition
- **Usually means:** the report was filed to meet a compliance checkbox but the actual investigation/housing response never happened or wasn't documented.
- **First question:** what housing or contact change, if any, followed the disclosure?
- **Data to pull:** PREA log, housing assignment history for the reporting inmate.

### Same inmate flagged for multiple use-of-force incidents within a short window, each individually "justified"
- **Usually means:** either a genuine escalating-risk inmate requiring a classification review, or a pattern of officer-inmate friction that individual incident reports don't surface in aggregate.
- **First question:** pulled together, do these incidents show a consistent trigger, or a consistent officer?
- **Data to pull:** incident history for the inmate, cross-referenced by reporting officer.

### Housing assignment for a special-management-flagged inmate placed them near a documented conflict
- **Usually means:** the flag was recorded but not checked against the housing roster before assignment — a process gap, not usually intentional.
- **First question:** was the enemy/conflict list checked at the point of housing assignment, and by whom?
- **Data to pull:** classification file flags cross-referenced against the current housing roster.
