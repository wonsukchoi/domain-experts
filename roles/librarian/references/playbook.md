Floor rule: any category whose raw circulation-share allocation would fall below the policy-set floor gets the floor instead; the floor amount comes off the top before the remaining budget is split by share among the other categories. Turnover below 2.0 for two consecutive years triggers a MUSTIE pass before the next budget cycle, regardless of allocation.

## Reference interview script (filled excerpt)

Patron: "I need a book about the Civil War."

Librarian (RUSA — approachability, then one open clarifying question, not a closed one): "Happy to help you find something. Is this for a school assignment, or more for your own reading?"

Patron: "School — I have a 5-page paper due Friday on a specific battle."

Librarian (now searches for battle-specific secondary sources with citable scholarly apparatus, not a general Civil War overview): "That changes what we're looking for — you'll want something focused and citable, not a general history. Do you already have a battle picked, or do you want help narrowing to one with enough written about it to fill 5 pages?"

The naive read of the original question — "Civil War, general nonfiction shelf" — would have sent the patron to browse the 973.7 section for 20 minutes and likely produce a paper built on a survey text with thin citation support. The clarifying question redirected to the reference collection's battle-specific monograph series in under two minutes.

## CREW shelf-life table (stated heuristic, by subject — confirm against local policy)

| Subject area | Typical shelf-life before re-review |
|---|---|
| Computer science / technology | 2–3 years |
| Law / tax guides | 1–3 years (annual for tax) |
| Medicine / health | 5 years |
| Travel guides | 2 years |
| History (general) | 10+ years unless superseded |
| Literature / classics | No fixed X-date — MUSTIE + core-list check only |

These figures are CREW-method conventions used widely in public-library practice; they are not a single cited constant and should be confirmed against the branch's own collection-development policy before being applied as a hard cutoff.
