# Librarian — Vocabulary

### MUSTIE
CREW method's discard-criteria mnemonic: Misleading, Ugly, Superseded, Trivial, Irrelevant, Elsewhere available.
**In use:** "This edition is Superseded — we've got the updated tax-law title, pull it."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a mandatory checklist applied mechanically, rather than a triage filter that still needs a core-list and consortium-holdings check before a final discard.

### Turnover rate
Circulation divided by holdings for a given period or category — the standard collection-health metric.
**In use:** "Cooking's turnover is 5.4 — it's earning its shelf space twice over."
**Common misuse:** Confused with raw circulation count, which doesn't account for how many copies (and how much budget and shelf space) are tied up producing that number.

### CREW method
Continuous Review, Evaluation, and Weeding — the Texas State Library's standard ongoing-deselection methodology, pairing MUSTIE with subject-specific shelf-life guidance.
**In use:** "We're on a five-year CREW cycle for the 600s."
**Common misuse:** Run as a one-time cleanup project rather than a continuous, scheduled process built into the collection-development calendar.

### Shelf-life / X-date
CREW's suggested years-since-publication threshold before a title needs re-review, varying widely by subject.
**In use:** "The X-date on that programming title was 3 years — it's due for review."
**Common misuse:** Applied as one uniform number across all subjects instead of the CREW subject-specific table (fast for computer science, slow to nonexistent for classics).

### Reference interview
The structured conversation that moves from a patron's stated question to their actual information need.
**In use:** "The reference interview surfaced that she needed peer-reviewed sources, not just 'articles.'"
**Common misuse:** Skipped in favor of taking the patron's first sentence literally and searching immediately.

### RUSA behaviors
The Reference and User Services Association's four observable reference-desk behaviors — approachability, interest, listening/inquiring, searching — used to diagnose a weak reference transaction.
**In use:** "The transaction failed on listening, not searching — she never asked a follow-up question."
**Common misuse:** Treated as a script to recite verbatim rather than a diagnostic lens for reviewing a transaction afterward.

### Authority control
The practice of maintaining one standardized form for each name or subject heading across the catalog, with variant forms pointing to it.
**In use:** "Run an authority-control pass before the vendor load — half these headings predate the last LCSH update."
**Common misuse:** Confused with basic spell-checking; it's about consistency of form across the whole catalog, not the correctness of any single record.

### MARC record
The machine-readable cataloging format encoding a bibliographic record's fields — title, author, subject, holdings link — for ILS interoperability.
**In use:** "The 856 field's broken — patrons can't reach the e-book link."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably with "the catalog record" in casual speech, when MARC specifically names the underlying data format, not the description content.

### RDA (Resource Description and Access)
The current cataloging content standard, replacing AACR2, governing how bibliographic description is structured.
**In use:** "That's an AACR2 legacy record — it'll need an RDA touch-up before it matches the rest."
**Common misuse:** Conflated with MARC, which is the format/carrier; RDA is the content rule set that fills the MARC fields.

### Hold ratio
Holds placed divided by copies owned — the standard trigger metric for duplicate-copy purchase decisions.
**In use:** "Hold ratio's at 0.4 on that title — we're buying two more copies."
**Common misuse:** Acted on immediately without checking whether the demand is a temporary spike (media tie-in) or sustained interest.

### Weeding
The deliberate removal of items from a circulating collection based on documented criteria, distinct from discarding lost or damaged items.
**In use:** "That's a weed, not a loss — it circulated fine but hasn't moved in four years and we own six regional copies."
**Common misuse:** Used interchangeably with damaged-item disposal, which is a different workflow with different documentation and doesn't require a MUSTIE or exception check.

### ACRL Framework
The Association of College & Research Libraries' six interconnected "frames" for information-literacy instruction (e.g., Authority Is Constructed and Contextual), replacing a skills-checklist teaching model.
**In use:** "We built the session around the 'Authority Is Constructed' frame instead of just demoing the database."
**Common misuse:** Taught as six independent topics to cover sequentially rather than interconnected lenses meant to be applied together to a single research task.

### Consortium / union catalog
A shared catalog across multiple libraries showing combined holdings, used to check whether a title is "held elsewhere" before weeding, or to route interlibrary-loan requests.
**In use:** "It's in the union catalog at three branches within 15 miles — safe to weed the local copy."
**Common misuse:** Assumed to reflect real-time item availability, when a consortium record can lag actual item status (lost, damaged, in transit) by days.

### Cost-per-circulation
Acquisitions cost divided by an item or category's lifetime circulation count — the metric used to justify budget decisions to a board or funder.
**In use:** "That reference set cost $340 and circulated four times — a cost-per-circ of $85 is why it's not getting renewed."
**Common misuse:** Applied to non-circulating reference-only materials, where the metric doesn't capture legitimate in-library use that never generates a checkout transaction.
