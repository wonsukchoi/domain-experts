# Librarian — Red Flags

### Turnover rate below 1.0 in a subject category for two consecutive fiscal years
- **Usually means:** the category is oversized relative to patron demand, or holdings are stale/superseded and overdue for a MUSTIE pass; second-most-likely: shelving or signage makes the section hard to browse, suppressing circulation independent of the titles themselves.
- **First question:** When was this category's last full weeding pass, and does its shelf placement (end-cap, signage, proximity to entrance) match its circulation history?
- **Data to pull:** 3-year circulation-by-title report for the category, plus a shelf walk-through note.

### Hold ratio above 0.25 (holds ÷ copies owned) sustained for 60+ days
- **Usually means:** a genuine demand spike — new release, media tie-in, book-club pick — that justifies a duplicate-copy purchase; second: a single popular title skewing an otherwise-quiet category's aggregate ratio.
- **First question:** Is the hold volume concentrated on one title or spread across the category?
- **Data to pull:** per-title hold counts and hold-placement dates from the ILS.

### More than 15% of a subject heading's records use inconsistent or legacy terms after an RDA/LCSH update
- **Usually means:** a bulk retrospective conversion was skipped or partially applied; second: original cataloging predates the standard and was never touched during a vendor record load.
- **First question:** Was this backlog flagged in the last authority-control vendor report, and is it growing or shrinking?
- **Data to pull:** authority-control vendor report; records-touched-since-date query.

### A single donor or grant restricts more than 20% of the acquisitions budget to one subject area
- **Usually means:** near-term collection balance will skew regardless of circulation data; second: the gift terms may not specify format or edition, leaving room to route funds toward higher-turnover formats within the restricted subject.
- **First question:** Does the gift agreement specify format, or only subject area?
- **Data to pull:** the signed gift or grant agreement and its renewal terms.

### Reference-question abandonment exceeds 10% of desk transactions in a service period
- **Usually means:** the opening approach or first question is too narrow or jargon-heavy, cutting the interview short before the real need surfaces; second: wait time at the desk exceeds patron tolerance before a librarian is free.
- **First question:** Are abandonments clustered at desk-open/close times (staffing gap) or spread evenly (interview-technique issue)?
- **Data to pull:** desk transaction log with timestamps and outcome codes.

### A core-list or classic title is flagged for weeding by circulation criteria alone
- **Usually means:** MUSTIE's "trivial" or low-circulation flag is firing on a title the collection-development policy explicitly protects — the raw data doesn't encode canon status.
- **First question:** Is this title on the branch's or system's core-list or classics appendix?
- **Data to pull:** the collection-development policy's core-list appendix.

### Consortium ILL requests for a locally owned title exceed 3 in a 90-day window
- **Usually means:** the local copy is lost, damaged, or unavailable in practice (perpetually checked out or on hold) despite showing as "owned" in the catalog.
- **First question:** What is the current status and last-scanned date of the local copy or copies?
- **Data to pull:** item-level status history from the ILS.

### Information-literacy post-assessment shows under 60% correct on source-evaluation questions
- **Usually means:** the session emphasized database mechanics over evaluation criteria, so patrons can run a search but not judge what they found.
- **First question:** Did the lesson plan include a dedicated evaluation-criteria segment, or was it a full-period database walkthrough?
- **Data to pull:** the session lesson plan and the post-assessment item-level results.

### Cataloging backlog exceeds 4 weeks of average weekly receiving volume
- **Usually means:** a staffing gap or a vendor record-load failure is silently accumulating; second: a format (e.g., non-English acquisitions) lacks vendor copy cataloging and requires original cataloging time the schedule didn't budget for.
- **First question:** Is the backlog concentrated in one format or language, or spread across the board?
- **Data to pull:** receiving log vs. cataloged-item log, dated.

### Discard rate in a single weeding pass exceeds 25% of a section's holdings
- **Usually means:** either a genuinely neglected section that's overdue (legitimate), or MUSTIE applied on circulation data alone without the consortium-holdings and core-list exception checks — over-weeding.
- **First question:** Was the consortium union catalog checked before each discard, or was MUSTIE applied on circulation numbers alone?
- **Data to pull:** the weeding worksheet showing exception-check columns filled in for each discarded item.
