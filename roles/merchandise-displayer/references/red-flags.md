# Merchandise Displayer — Red Flags

### Planogram compliance below 85% at audit
- **Usually means:** Fast-restock zones (endcaps, promo tables) drifted because staff restock from whatever's fastest to grab, or the planogram itself fights natural restocking flow.
- **First question:** Is the drift concentrated in specific zones/SKUs, or spread evenly across the store?
- **Data to pull:** Zone-by-zone compliance audit log from the last 3 audits, to see if drift is recurring in the same spots.

### Reset labor-hours planned from a single blended average across fixture types
- **Usually means:** The plan will understate hours needed whenever the fixture mix skews toward complex types (endcaps, windows, mannequin groupings).
- **First question:** What share of this reset's fixtures are non-standard, and what's their actual historical reset time?
- **Data to pull:** Fixture inventory list by type, prior reset time logs by fixture type.

### Sales lift reported as proof a new display concept worked
- **Usually means:** The lift may be driven by traffic volume, seasonality, or a concurrent promotion, not the display itself.
- **First question:** What was passing foot traffic during this window vs. the prior concept's window?
- **Data to pull:** Foot-traffic counter data and capture rate for both concepts over matched time windows.

### Reset scheduled with under ~15% person-hour slack against the closure window
- **Usually means:** No buffer for delivery delays, damaged fixtures, or signage errors — any disruption pushes the reset past the store's reopening time.
- **First question:** What happens operationally if the reset isn't finished by 6am?
- **Data to pull:** Historical reset overrun log — how often and by how much have past resets run over.

### Window/hero display test run for less than one full week
- **Usually means:** The result is likely dominated by a single unusual day (local event, weather, a one-off promotion) rather than the display's actual effect.
- **First question:** Did the test window include both weekday and weekend traffic patterns?
- **Data to pull:** Daily passing-traffic and entry counts for the test window, checked for one outlier day skewing the average.

### High-margin or slow-turning SKU placed outside the 57–63 in buy zone
- **Usually means:** Buy-zone space was allocated by what's newest or most visually appealing rather than by which SKU most needs the sales lift.
- **First question:** Which SKUs are currently in the buy zone, and are they the ones the buyer most needs to move?
- **Data to pull:** Current planogram buy-zone assignments cross-referenced against sell-through/margin data from the buyer.

### Cross-merchandised complementary item placed more than a few feet from the primary item
- **Usually means:** The adjacency was set for visual balance rather than shopper reach — attachment rate will underperform a true within-reach placement.
- **First question:** Can a shopper standing at the primary fixture physically reach the complementary item without walking?
- **Data to pull:** Fixture map distance between the primary and complementary placements.

### Full-store reset scheduled in response to one failed zone audit
- **Usually means:** Overcorrection — labor-hours are being spent re-setting zones that never drifted, when a targeted re-set of the failed zone would fix the actual problem.
- **First question:** Which specific zones failed the audit, and did the rest of the store pass?
- **Data to pull:** Full audit results broken out by zone, not just an overall store compliance score.

### Reset-time estimates never updated after actual reset completion
- **Usually means:** The per-fixture-type time library is stale, and future reset plans will keep inheriting the same estimation error.
- **First question:** When was this store's fixture-type time library last recalibrated from actual logged times?
- **Data to pull:** Actual time-per-fixture-type logs from the last 2–3 resets, compared against the estimates used to plan them.
