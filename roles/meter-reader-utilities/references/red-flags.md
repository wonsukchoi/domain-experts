# Meter Reader, Utilities — Red Flags

### Consumption drop >70% vs. seasonally-adjusted baseline on an occupied, non-vacant-flagged account
- **Usually means:** Meter bypass/tamper, or equipment failure; less often a genuine efficiency change (solar install, appliance replacement).
- **First question:** Is there a logged account change (solar, move-out, renovation) that would explain it?
- **Data to pull:** 12-month usage history, same-month-last-year comparison, vacancy-flag status.

### Register value lower than the previous cycle's recorded value (apparent negative consumption)
- **Usually means:** An unlogged meter exchange, a misread digit, or register rollover not accounted for.
- **First question:** Was there a meter change-out work order on this account since the last read?
- **Data to pull:** Meter-exchange history, register rollover threshold for this meter model.

### Broken or missing tamper seal
- **Usually means:** Potential unauthorized access to the meter internals.
- **First question:** Is there a corresponding bypass wire, reversed connection, or other physical tamper indicator visible?
- **Data to pull:** Photo documentation, prior seal-install date and serial number on file.

### Two or more consecutive estimated reads on the same account
- **Usually means:** Access has been denied or the route has been skipping the property.
- **First question:** What's documented in the prior visit notes about why access wasn't obtained?
- **Data to pull:** Access notes from the last two to three visits.

### Zero consumption for a full cycle on a non-vacant account
- **Usually means:** Meter stopped, bypass installed, or the account should be flagged vacant and isn't.
- **First question:** Is there a move-out or vacancy change on record that hasn't been flagged?
- **Data to pull:** Account status change log.

### Aggressive animal or blocked access noted on repeated visits
- **Usually means:** The route needs an alternate access arrangement, not repeated unsafe attempts.
- **First question:** Has the customer been contacted to secure the area or arrange supervised access?
- **Data to pull:** Prior visit safety notes, customer contact log.

### Reading exceeds physical capacity/rated load for the meter's class
- **Usually means:** Wrong CT ratio/multiplier applied, or the account is misclassified (e.g., residential meter on what's now a commercial load).
- **First question:** Does the meter's nameplate multiplier match what's recorded on the account?
- **Data to pull:** Meter install record and multiplier/CT ratio on file.

### Visible gas odor or exposed/arcing electrical wiring at the meter location
- **Usually means:** An active safety hazard, not a reading task.
- **First question:** Is anyone in immediate danger right now?
- **Data to pull:** None first — evacuate the immediate area and escalate per emergency protocol before doing anything else.

### Customer present and disputing the read on-site
- **Usually means:** The account needs a formal reread or investigation ticket, not an on-the-spot resolution.
- **First question:** Is there a documented prior dispute on this account?
- **Data to pull:** Dispute history and prior resolution notes.

### Register reads suspiciously round or unchanged across two or more cycles on an occupied account
- **Usually means:** A stuck register, or a read that was estimated in the office rather than taken in the field.
- **First question:** Does the route log's GPS/timestamp confirm a visit actually occurred?
- **Data to pull:** Route log timestamps for the visit in question.
