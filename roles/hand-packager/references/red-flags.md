# Red flags

Smell tests to catch before they become a weights-and-measures failure, a transit-damage claim, an allergen incident, or an injury report.

### 1. Checkweigher sample average trending toward the labeled weight over a shift, even while every individual bag still clears MAV

**Usually means:** a scoop or dispenser calibration drift is eroding the safety margin gradually — the average requirement will fail before any single unit does.

**First question:** "What did the last three skip-lot sample averages look like, in sequence, not just this one?"

**Data to pull:** checkweigher sample history for the shift, dispenser calibration log, last recalibration date/time.

### 2. Any individual package weight below the MAV floor, even when the lot average is comfortably above label

**Usually means:** a genuinely separate legal failure — the average-requirement check and the individual-package check are independent, and a good average doesn't excuse it.

**First question:** "Which specific units were below floor, and were they clustered in one sample or spread across the shift?"

**Data to pull:** individual-unit weights from the failing sample, dispenser settings at the time, prior samples from the same station.

### 3. Allergen changeover completed with no ATP swab reading or strip result logged

**Usually means:** the changeover was done on a visual "looks clean" basis rather than the validated procedure, which doesn't detect allergenic protein residue.

**First question:** "Where's the swab reading and the approver's signature for this changeover?"

**Data to pull:** changeover validation log, ATP/strip readings, SOP for the specific allergen transition performed.

### 4. Void-fill or cushioning volume per unit rising for a SKU with no fragility-class or shipping-lane change

**Usually means:** the void-fill choice has drifted off the material's cushion curve into an over-cushioned, cost-wasting range, or a packer is compensating for an unaddressed handling issue.

**First question:** "Has this SKU's fragility rating or drop-test spec changed, or is this just more material for the same product?"

**Data to pull:** current fragility/cushion-curve spec for the SKU, dimensional-weight cost trend, damage-claim rate for the same period.

### 5. Damage-claim rate rising on a SKU with no fragility-class or cushion spec change

**Usually means:** void-fill is under-delivering against the curve — either the material or the applied thickness has drifted below spec.

**First question:** "What's the applied cushion thickness on recent units of this SKU versus the spec sheet?"

**Data to pull:** damage-claim log by SKU and lane, void-fill application spec, recent QA spot-checks on packed cartons.

### 6. Skip-lot sampling interval being informally widened during high-volume periods

**Usually means:** sampling is being treated as a bottleneck to work around under rate pressure rather than the control that catches a compliance failure before it ships as a full day's lot.

**First question:** "Who authorized the wider interval, and is it documented anywhere?"

**Data to pull:** sampling interval log versus the facility SOP, correlation with rate-target periods.

### 7. Units/hour trending up while checkweigher or changeover-validation pass rate trends down for the same station

**Usually means:** a rate incentive or informal pace expectation is training a shortcut, and nobody has paired the two metrics to see it.

**First question:** "Is there a rate threshold this station is chasing right now, and when did the pass-rate dip start relative to it?"

**Data to pull:** paired rate-and-pass-rate trend by station, incentive plan terms, recent staffing changes.

### 8. A changeover completed faster than the facility's validated minimum changeover time for that allergen transition

**Usually means:** a step in the validated cleaning sequence was compressed or skipped to save time.

**First question:** "Walk me through the changeover step by step — which steps took less time than the validated procedure calls for?"

**Data to pull:** changeover timestamp log, validated procedure's minimum step durations, swab result for that specific changeover.

### 9. A packer reporting soreness or discomfort concentrated in one hand or wrist tied to a specific station or task

**Usually means:** a calculable Hand Activity Level / peak-force concern at that station, not an individual-conditioning issue.

**First question:** "What's the cycle time and the exertion/force level at that specific station?"

**Data to pull:** the station's HAL inputs (cycle time, force/exertion level, duty cycle), injury/first-aid log for that station.

### 10. A new or seasonal packer hitting full rate targets within days with no corresponding increase in QA sampling on their output

**Usually means:** QA isn't checking that packer's output as closely as tenured staff yet, not that the packer is unusually fast and accurate from day one.

**First question:** "Is this packer's QA sampling rate the same as everyone else's at this tenure, or has it lapsed to standard too early?"

**Data to pull:** QA sampling rate and results by packer tenure, ramp-up schedule for new hires.

### 11. Customer-reported shorted count on a multipack with no corresponding checkweigher failure on record

**Usually means:** the checkweigher is sampling weight but the specific failure mode was a missing unit inside an otherwise correct-weight pack (e.g., substituted heavier filler), which a weight-only check won't catch.

**First question:** "Does the checkweigher on this line verify count, or only aggregate weight?"

**Data to pull:** checkweigher configuration for this SKU, count-verification method (vision system, manual count, weight-only), customer complaint log by SKU.
