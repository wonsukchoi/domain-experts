# Insulation Installer Red Flags

### Blower-door result above the code ACH50 target after insulation is marked "complete"
- **Usually means:** air sealing was skipped or left incomplete at top plates and penetrations; second most likely: the attic hatch or rim joists were never sealed.
- **First question:** was air sealing performed and tested before insulation went in, or was only insulation installed?
- **Data to pull:** blower-door test report (pre- and post-work if both exist), the air-sealing line items on the invoice or scope of work.

### Batt insulation compressed below its designed thickness in the cavity
- **Usually means:** the wrong R-value batt was ordered for the cavity depth; second: the installer used whatever batt was on the truck.
- **First question:** what is the actual cavity depth, and does it match the batt's designed (uncompressed) thickness?
- **Data to pull:** batt product label R-value and thickness, a direct cavity-depth measurement.

### Soffit vents blocked by insulation with no baffles visible from inside the attic
- **Usually means:** the loose-fill or batt installer didn't check for or install baffles before covering the eave; second: baffles present but too few for the number of vented bays.
- **First question:** can you see daylight or feel airflow at the soffit from inside the attic at each rafter bay?
- **Data to pull:** baffle count versus vented rafter-bay count, attic interior photos at the eave line.

### Vermiculite attic insulation present with no documented test result
- **Usually means:** possible asbestos contamination — a substantial share of historically sold vermiculite insulation (particularly material traced to the Libby, MT source) tested positive.
- **First question:** has this specific material been lab-tested, or is it being treated as presumed asbestos-containing?
- **Data to pull:** any existing lab test result; absent one, treat per EPA guidance as presumed-ACM and stop disturbing it until tested.

### Knob-and-tube wiring found in a cavity slated for insulation
- **Usually means:** many code jurisdictions and insurers prohibit covering active K&T with insulation because heat can't dissipate normally, raising fire risk.
- **First question:** is the wiring still active, and does the local code permit insulation in contact with K&T in this jurisdiction?
- **Data to pull:** an electrical inspection report on the wiring's condition, the local code section addressing insulation over K&T.

### Exposed spray foam in a finished or habitable space with no gypsum or listed coating over it
- **Usually means:** the installer skipped the code-required thermal barrier; second: an ignition-barrier-rated foam was used but the listing wasn't documented for inspection.
- **First question:** is this specific foam product tested and listed for exposed application, or does it require a 1/2" gypsum thermal barrier?
- **Data to pull:** the foam manufacturer's ICC-ES/AC377 evaluation report, permit and inspection sign-off.

### Ice dams recurring on a roof with recently upgraded attic insulation
- **Usually means:** insulation was added without air sealing or without preserving the ventilation path, so warm air still reaches the roof deck from below.
- **First question:** was air sealing done as part of this job, and are the soffit-to-ridge vent paths still intact?
- **Data to pull:** attic inspection for bypass locations, baffle presence and condition at each vented bay.

### Musty odor or visible mold after an interior vapor-impermeable finish was added over insulation in a mixed- or hot-humid climate
- **Usually means:** an interior poly vapor barrier trapped inward, AC-driven vapor drive from outside the wall.
- **First question:** which direction does vapor drive in this climate, and does the installed assembly's vapor-retarder placement match it?
- **Data to pull:** climate zone, the wall assembly's layer order (interior to exterior), a moisture-meter reading inside the cavity.

### Dense-packed cellulose showing a visible void or settling gap at the top of a wall cavity within a year of install
- **Usually means:** the material was blown at a density below the ~3.5 lb/ft³ dense-pack minimum for that cavity.
- **First question:** what density was the material blown to, and was it checked against the machine's blow chart at install?
- **Data to pull:** the installer's density log or blow-chart setting for that job, a thermal-camera or core-sample check of the cavity.

### Invoiced R-value higher than a direct depth check in the attic supports
- **Usually means:** the depth wasn't remeasured after settling, or the invoice used the material's nominal (as-blown) figure instead of its settled R-value.
- **First question:** what depth was actually measured after installation, and does that match the product's settled-R coverage chart?
- **Data to pull:** the depth-check ruler-card reading, the product bag's FTC-required coverage chart.

### A combustion appliance (furnace, water heater) shows spillage or backdrafting shortly after air sealing and insulation work
- **Usually means:** tightening the envelope shifted the pressure balance around an atmospherically vented appliance that was previously getting makeup air through the leaks that just got sealed.
- **First question:** has a worst-case combustion-safety depressurization test been run since the air-sealing work?
- **Data to pull:** BPI combustion-safety test results, a CO reading at the appliance.

### Recessed light fixtures buried directly in insulation with no visible IC rating
- **Usually means:** the fixture isn't rated for direct insulation contact and needs clearance, or an IC-rated fixture should have been used instead.
- **First question:** is the fixture IC-rated, and if not, is the code-required clearance from insulation being maintained?
- **Data to pull:** the fixture's spec label or model number, the insulation coverage measured at that specific location.
