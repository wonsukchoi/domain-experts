# Home Health Aide — Red Flags

### Hypoglycemia-consistent symptoms within 30–90 minutes of a rapid-acting insulin dose
- **Usually means:** True hypoglycemia during the drug's peak-effect window, especially if a meal was delayed or skipped.
- **First question:** What time was the injection, and has the client eaten since?
- **Data to pull:** Time of last dose, time of symptom onset, last meal time and how much was eaten.

### Medication pillbox count doesn't match the expected number for the day
- **Usually means:** A missed dose (extra pills present) or possible over-taking/diversion (fewer pills than expected).
- **First question:** Is this the first discrepancy, or has the count been off on prior visits too?
- **Data to pull:** Plan-of-care dosing schedule; prior visit notes for the same medication.

### Unexplained bruising, especially bilateral or in a finger/grip-shaped pattern
- **Usually means:** A fall not disclosed, or (more concerning) physical mishandling/abuse — the pattern and location matter more than the presence of bruising alone.
- **First question:** Where exactly, and does the client's explanation match the pattern (a shin bruise from a bump reads differently than bilateral upper-arm bruising)?
- **Data to pull:** Location, size, color/age estimate, client's exact words, any fall history in recent visit notes.

### Unplanned weight loss the aide can observe (looser clothing, uneaten prepared meals across visits)
- **Usually means:** Undiagnosed illness, depression, swallowing difficulty, or inadequate food access/self-neglect.
- **First question:** Is food being prepared but not eaten, or is there not enough food in the home to prepare?
- **Data to pull:** Meal-prep and intake notes from the last several visits; refrigerator/pantry contents.

### Client asks the aide not to report an observed injury or condition to the agency
- **Usually means:** Embarrassment, fear of losing independence/placement, or — if paired with other signs — an abuse dynamic where the client fears the reporter.
- **First question:** Is this request paired with any other red flag (bruising, fear around a specific person, financial changes)?
- **Data to pull:** None before reporting — mandatory-reporter status is not waived by the client's request; report to the agency the same day regardless.

### Missing money, valuables, or a new person with unusual control over the client's finances
- **Usually means:** Financial exploitation, often by a caregiver, family member, or new acquaintance with access to accounts or the home.
- **First question:** Who has access to the client's home, mail, and accounts, and did anything change recently?
- **Data to pull:** Timeline of when items went missing relative to any new person's involvement.

### Home environment newly unsanitary, unheated/uncooled, or unsafe compared to prior visits
- **Usually means:** Client's functional decline outpacing support, a utility shutoff, or self-neglect/family neglect.
- **First question:** Is this a gradual decline across visits or a sudden change since the last visit?
- **Data to pull:** Prior home-safety-checklist notes for comparison; date of last utility bill payment if known.

### Client fails to answer the door or misses a visit with no explanation
- **Usually means:** A missed appointment, a fall/medical event inside the home, or a caregiver/family member blocking access.
- **First question:** Has this happened before, and is there a way to reach the client or an emergency contact right now?
- **Data to pull:** Emergency contact info, agency's no-answer/welfare-check protocol, last confirmed contact date.

### Family caregiver's description of the client's condition doesn't match the aide's direct observation
- **Usually means:** Caregiver burnout affecting reporting accuracy, or (less commonly but more seriously) an attempt to mask a change the aide would otherwise catch.
- **First question:** What specifically differs — severity, timing, or the symptom itself?
- **Data to pull:** Aide's own visit note from today versus family's verbal account, both documented separately.

### New confusion, disorientation, or slowed responses without an obvious cause
- **Usually means:** Infection (especially UTI in older adults), medication interaction, dehydration, or an evolving neurological event.
- **First question:** Is this the client's baseline, or a change from how they presented at the last visit?
- **Data to pull:** Baseline mental-status notes from recent visits; any new medication started.

### Client reports pain, numbness, or weakness on one side of the body, or sudden difficulty speaking
- **Usually means:** Possible stroke — this overrides the normal report-and-wait sequence.
- **First question:** What time did it start (thrombolytic treatment is time-sensitive)?
- **Data to pull:** None — call 911 immediately, then notify the agency; do not wait to finish other tasks first.

### A visit reveals no one has checked on the client since the last scheduled visit
- **Usually means:** An isolation gap where a decline could go unnoticed for days — itself a risk factor independent of the client's current status.
- **First question:** How many days since any contact, and does the client have any other regular visitors or check-ins?
- **Data to pull:** Visit schedule/frequency on the POC versus actual gap; whether a family/neighbor check-in was assumed but never confirmed.
