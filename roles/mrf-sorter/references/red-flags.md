# Red flags

Smell tests worth catching before they show up as a fire, an amputation injury, or a rejected bale.

### Battery or pressure vessel found swollen, leaking, smoking, or hot to the touch on the belt
- **Usually means:** a lithium cell in thermal runaway or approaching it, or a pressurized canister with a compromised seal — both are immediate line-stop events regardless of how the item entered the stream.
- **First question:** "Has the E-stop cord been pulled, and is the item isolated in the fire-safe container, not the routine battery bin?"
- **Data to pull:** the line-stop log entry, the item's isolation location, whether the supervisor was alerted before restart.

### Bale purity result drops a full price tier from its normal band, with no obvious single cause reported
- **Usually means:** a station sat uncovered during a break rotation, or belt speed was increased beyond the sorting team's rated pick rate — a structural or coverage cause, not usually a sudden drop in individual pick accuracy.
- **First question:** "Was every station covered for the full shift the sampled bale came from, per the rotation log?"
- **Data to pull:** the break/rotation coverage log for that shift, the QC audit timestamp, belt-speed setting history for the shift.

### Picking-station guard rail found propped open or with a section removed
- **Usually means:** a sorter or maintenance worker defeated the guard to reach a jam or a high-value item more easily — a compliant-on-paper guard that's routinely bypassed is not a controlled hazard.
- **First question:** "How long has the rail been in this state, and is it tied to a specific recurring task?"
- **Data to pull:** maintenance/inspection log for that station, any near-miss reports at that location, shift supervisor's walk-through notes.

### A sorter observed reaching across a guard rail or into a neighboring station's zone to intercept an item
- **Usually means:** a learned habit chasing recovery rate or a specific high-value material, not an isolated lapse — the behavior recurs until it's corrected at the individual level.
- **First question:** "Is this a one-time reach or a pattern for this sorter or this station?"
- **Data to pull:** direct observation log or video review if available, prior coaching notes for the station or individual.

### A hazard-bin tally (batteries, sharps) trending toward zero while the input stream composition hasn't changed
- **Usually means:** hazard items are being missed or misclassified as routine contamination rather than actually declining — a stream's hazardous-item rate rarely drops to near-zero on its own.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed in training, staffing, or line speed that would explain fewer hazard pulls, or is this a detection problem?"
- **Data to pull:** hazard-bin tally history by shift, staffing/training records, any recent line-speed change.

### E-stop pull cord tested and found non-functional or slow to respond
- **Usually means:** a maintenance gap on the single most-used hazard-response tool on the line — every minute this goes uncorrected, the line-stop default has no working mechanism behind it.
- **First question:** "When was this cord last function-tested, and is there a documented inspection interval?"
- **Data to pull:** E-stop cord maintenance/test log, incident reports (if any) involving delayed stop response.

### Medical sharps or needles found handled without gloves or a sharps container in use
- **Usually means:** PPE compliance has lapsed at that station, often because sharps are infrequent enough that the routine feels unnecessary until an injury occurs.
- **First question:** "Is a sharps container stocked and accessible at this station right now?"
- **Data to pull:** PPE/supply stocking log for the station, any needle-stick incident history.

### Recurring propane/aerosol canisters or unlabeled chemical containers from the same input source or route
- **Usually means:** an upstream contamination-source issue (a specific collection route or commercial account) rather than a random one-off — this is a signal worth escalating to the program/coordinator level, not just handling item-by-item at the station.
- **First question:** "Is the MRF's load-tracking data showing this item type concentrated in a specific route or account?"
- **Data to pull:** load-level tracking data by route if available, hazard-bin/line-stop log entries by shift and, where recorded, by incoming load.

### Purity or hazard-pull performance reviewed by individual sorter with no reference to station coverage or belt-speed context
- **Usually means:** a management or coaching process that will misattribute a structural cause (coverage gap, speed change) to an individual's skill, training the team to hide near-misses rather than report them.
- **First question:** "Does this performance review account for rotation coverage and belt speed during the sampled period?"
- **Data to pull:** the coaching/performance record in question, the corresponding rotation and belt-speed logs for the same period.
