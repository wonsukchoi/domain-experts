# Red flags

Smell tests a veteran captain or deckboss catches before a trip is staged or a settlement is signed. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before anyone commits.

### Visible ice accretion on rigging or deck gear with more sets planned

- **Usually means:** the vessel's center of gravity is already rising, and the crew is about to compound it with more catch weight on deck — the sequence behind a large share of vessel-disaster fatalities.
- **First question:** "When did we last chip ice, and how much has accumulated since?"
- **Data to pull:** air temp and spray conditions over the last 2-4 hours, time since last chip-ice stop, current deck load versus the stability letter's marks.

### Forecast crosses into a Gale Warning (34-47 kt) with the trip already planned or underway

- **Usually means:** the trip was planned against a forecast that has since deteriorated, or quota-deadline pressure is overriding the original go/no-go call.
- **First question:** "Does this forecast still sit inside the boat's tested stability envelope, or are we now rationalizing past it?"
- **Data to pull:** current NWS marine forecast and nearest buoy reading, vessel's stability-letter envelope, hours since the forecast was last checked.

### Crew-share settlement doesn't reconcile to the landed-weight and price numbers on the fish ticket

- **Usually means:** either a math error in the cost stack, an unrecorded cost being passed through informally, or a genuine shorting of the crew — all three look identical from the crew's side until the worksheet is shown.
- **First question:** "Walk me from gross landed value to my share number, line by line."
- **Data to pull:** fish ticket (landed weight, price/lb, grade), fuel/ice/bait receipts for the trip, IFQ cost-recovery fee calculation, the share-split agreement in writing.

### Bycatch of a capped or prohibited species climbing set-over-set

- **Usually means:** the boat has drifted onto grounds with a different species mix than the target tow was planned for, and continuing risks triggering a fishery-wide closure, not just a personal violation.
- **First question:** "What's the bycatch rate on the last two sets versus the first two, and how much of the cap is left?"
- **Data to pull:** per-set bycatch log, current fishery-wide or trip cap remaining as reported by the observer/monitoring program, tow location and depth versus prior sets.

### IFQ leased at a price close to the current ex-vessel price

- **Usually means:** the lease economics no longer clear a real margin once fuel, bait, and ice are added — the trip is being taken for the activity, not for the arithmetic.
- **First question:** "After harvest costs, what's actually left once the lease is paid — walk me through it."
- **Data to pull:** lease price per pound, current ex-vessel price, expected harvest cost per trip, break-even worksheet.

### EPIRB last tested more than a month ago, or registration lapsed

- **Usually means:** a compliance item has quietly slipped from "checked every trip" to "checked when someone remembers" — exactly the drift that turns a routine gear check into a missing safety device during an actual emergency.
- **First question:** "When was this EPIRB last tested, and is the 406 MHz registration current with NOAA?"
- **Data to pull:** EPIRB test log, NOAA registration confirmation, battery expiration date.

### Immersion suit count below crew count, or a suit not reachable within about 30 seconds

- **Usually means:** crew turnover or gear reorganization moved a suit out of its stowed position without anyone re-checking accessibility, or a suit failed inspection and wasn't replaced before the next trip.
- **First question:** "Can every person on this trip get to and don a suit in the time it'd take this boat to go down?"
- **Data to pull:** suit count versus crew manifest, most recent annual inspection date per suit, stowage location relative to likely egress points.

### "We've fished worse than this before" as the stated justification for a go

- **Usually means:** the go/no-go call has shifted from the vessel's documented stability envelope to informal precedent, which is exactly how a boat that handled marginal conditions once starts treating marginal conditions as normal.
- **First question:** "What does the stability letter actually say the safe envelope is, independent of what we've gotten away with?"
- **Data to pull:** stability letter figures, current forecast, deck load and icing status for this specific trip.

### Quota balance requires landing an unrealistic daily rate in the days remaining before closure

- **Usually means:** the season's calendar days and the boat's realistic fishable days have diverged, and the plan hasn't been updated to reflect it — the gap is what generates weather-defying trip pressure.
- **First question:** "At our actual CPUE, how many fishable days do we need to land what's left, and does the forecast give us that many?"
- **Data to pull:** IFQ balance, days remaining in season, forecast for the remaining window, recent CPUE by grounds.

### TED or BRD found tied shut, removed, or modified beyond its certified configuration

- **Usually means:** someone traded a catch increase in the low single digits for a serious enforcement and liability exposure — often justified informally as "it wasn't catching anything anyway."
- **First question:** "Who altered this gear, and was it certified in this configuration?"
- **Data to pull:** gear inspection log, certification paperwork for the TED/BRD model and bar spacing, catch comparison logs if the claim is that it's costing catch.

### Deck load or hold weight visibly above the marks noted on the stability letter

- **Usually means:** the boat is being loaded to the day's opportunity (a good set, a full hold in reach) rather than to its tested limit — the two are unrelated numbers that get conflated under time pressure.
- **First question:** "What does the stability letter say the limit is here, and where are we against it right now?"
- **Data to pull:** stability letter loading marks, current freeboard measurement, remaining sets planned before returning to port.
