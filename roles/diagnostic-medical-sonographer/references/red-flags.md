### Sonographic Murphy sign negative despite gallstones and wall thickening >3mm
- **Usually means:** Early cholecystitis where the wall/fluid criteria are ahead of the tenderness response, or recent analgesia/sedation blunting the pain response — less commonly, chronic cholecystitis without an acute component.
- **First question:** When was the last dose of opioid or antiemetic, and does the timing explain a blunted response?
- **Data to pull:** Medication administration record, repeat scan focused on pericholecystic fluid and wall layering.

### Appendix diameter 6-7mm, compressible, no periappendiceal fat changes
- **Usually means:** Technically difficult exam with incomplete gas displacement, or a normal-variant appendix, less commonly early appendicitis before secondary signs develop.
- **First question:** Was graded compression actually adequate, or was the view gas-limited?
- **Data to pull:** WBC/CRP trend, CT correlation if clinical suspicion remains, repeat scan with alternate patient position.

### Renal transplant resistive index (RI) >0.80
- **Usually means:** Acute tubular necrosis in the first 1-3 post-op days, acute rejection later in the post-op course, or venous outflow obstruction — the three have overlapping RI signatures.
- **First question:** How many days post-transplant is this, and what was the baseline RI on the prior study?
- **Data to pull:** Creatinine trend, prior duplex baseline, transplant surgical/nephrology notes.

### Color Doppler aliasing (wraparound) at a narrowed segment
- **Usually means:** Velocity exceeding the current Nyquist limit at the set scale/PRF — an artifact of settings, not automatically a true elevated-velocity stenosis.
- **First question:** Was scale/PRF increased and baseline shifted before the velocity was called elevated?
- **Data to pull:** Repeat spectral trace at corrected scale with angle ≤60°, compare to contralateral side if paired vessel.

### Free intraperitoneal fluid with a positive pregnancy test and an adnexal mass
- **Usually means:** Ruptured ectopic pregnancy until proven otherwise.
- **First question:** Is the patient hemodynamically stable right now, in this room?
- **Data to pull:** Current vitals, quantitative beta-hCG, immediate call to the interpreting physician — do not complete the remainder of a routine protocol first.

### Absent arterial flow on color/power Doppler in a painful testicle
- **Usually means:** Testicular torsion — a time-sensitive surgical emergency with a viability window measured in hours.
- **First question:** How many hours since symptom onset, and does the contralateral testicle show normal flow for comparison?
- **Data to pull:** Contralateral Doppler flow, immediate escalation call — do not wait to finish the scrotal protocol.

### Indeterminate calf-vein compression in a patient with Wells score ≥3
- **Usually means:** Guarding, tenderness, or body habitus limiting full compression assessment at the exact segment under the symptomatic site — not a true negative.
- **First question:** Which specific segment couldn't be compressed, and was a secondary maneuver (repositioning, alternate transducer) attempted?
- **Data to pull:** Wells score components, D-dimer result if drawn, plan for repeat duplex in 5-7 days.

### Biophysical profile score of 6/10
- **Usually means:** An equivocal result — could reflect a transient fetal sleep cycle or an evolving hypoxic process; the two look identical on a single observation.
- **First question:** Was the full 30-minute observation window completed before scoring, or was it truncated?
- **Data to pull:** Extend observation or repeat BPP, add a non-stress test if not already scored as a component.

### Carotid ICA peak systolic velocity elevated but plaque burden looks modest
- **Usually means:** Doppler angle error (>60°) inflating the velocity, or compensatory flow increase from contralateral occlusion.
- **First question:** What angle of insonation was used, and was it re-checked at ≤60°?
- **Data to pull:** Re-measurement at corrected angle, contralateral carotid duplex for comparison.

### Hepatic artery RI <0.5 or absent diastolic flow, post-liver-transplant
- **Usually means:** Hepatic artery stenosis or thrombosis — a surgical emergency, not a variant finding.
- **First question:** Is this a change from the immediate post-operative baseline duplex?
- **Data to pull:** Prior post-op duplex baseline, immediate call to the transplant surgical team.

### Apparent mass posterior to the diaphragm on a subcostal view
- **Usually means:** Mirror-image (reverberation) artifact from the diaphragm, not a true lesion.
- **First question:** Does the "mass" disappear or shift illogically when scanned from a different intercostal window?
- **Data to pull:** Rescan from an alternate window before charting the finding as real.

### IOTA Simple Rules mass with both benign (B) and malignant (M) features present
- **Usually means:** The mass genuinely doesn't fit the binary classification the rules are built for — this is an explicit "inconclusive" result, not a coin-flip toward benign.
- **First question:** Has a Level II/expert sonographer or gynecologic oncology reviewed the images, or was the Simple Rules verdict reported alone?
- **Data to pull:** Refer for expert second review or ADNEX model scoring rather than finalizing a Simple Rules call.

### Sonographer reporting sustained wrist/shoulder pain during or after a shift
- **Usually means:** Early-stage work-related musculoskeletal disorder from grip force and sustained deviation — the modal outcome, not an outlier.
- **First question:** Which exam types or postures correlate with the pain, and has technique been adjusted, or has volume simply not allowed for it?
- **Data to pull:** Exam-volume schedule, transducer/positioning technique review, occupational health referral before symptoms progress.
