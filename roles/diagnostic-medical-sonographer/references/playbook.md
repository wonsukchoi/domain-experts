# Protocol playbook

Filled worksheets and threshold tables for the highest-volume, highest-stakes protocols. Every table has real thresholds an agent can apply as-is; verify against current institutional policy before clinical use.

## DVT compression duplex sequence

Perform in this order; do not skip a segment to save time even if proximal segments are normal — distal DVT is a distinct entity with its own risk of propagation.

| Segment | Maneuver | Normal finding | Action if abnormal/limited |
|---|---|---|---|
| Common femoral vein | Compress in transverse, augment with distal calf squeeze | Full compression, phasic flow, augments | If noncompressible: positive for DVT, stop and call physician |
| Femoral (superficial femoral) vein | Compress every 2cm proximal to distal, thigh | Full compression at each interval | Any segment noncompressible = positive |
| Popliteal vein | Compress with patient prone or knee flexed | Full compression | Noncompressible = positive; note if isolated |
| Calf veins (posterior tibial, peroneal) | Compress with color/spectral confirmation | Full compression, augmentation | If guarding/gas prevents full compression: label segment "technically limited," do not call negative |

**Wells score quick reference** (each present item = +1 unless noted): active cancer; paralysis/paresis/recent cast; bedridden ≥3 days or major surgery within 12 weeks; localized deep-venous tenderness; entire leg swollen; calf swelling >3cm vs. asymptomatic leg (measured 10cm below tibial tuberosity); pitting edema confined to symptomatic leg; collateral non-varicose superficial veins; previously documented DVT; alternative diagnosis at least as likely (−2). Score ≥3 = high pretest probability; 1-2 = moderate; ≤0 = low.

**Indeterminate-segment rule:** technically limited segment + Wells ≥3 → recommend D-dimer (if not done) or repeat duplex in 5-7 days, not "negative." Technically limited segment + Wells ≤0 → reasonable to call negative with the limitation documented, since pretest probability is already low.

## RUQ / gallbladder — cholecystitis criteria

| Category | Finding | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Major | Sonographic Murphy sign | Maximal tenderness directly over visualized gallbladder with probe pressure |
| Major | Gallbladder wall thickening | >3mm |
| Major | Pericholecystic fluid | Any |
| Minor | Biliary dilatation (intra/extrahepatic) | Any |
| Minor | Gallbladder hydrops | Transverse diameter >5cm |

**Call rule:** 2 major, or 1 major + 2 minor = sonographic diagnosis of acute cholecystitis. Sonographic Murphy sign alone is negative in up to 70% of true cholecystitis cases — absence does not rule it out; report wall thickness and fluid regardless of Murphy result.

## Appendix — graded compression technique

1. Locate the cecum, follow the tenia coli to the appendiceal base.
2. Apply steady graded compression with the transducer to displace overlying bowel gas — this is displacement technique, not the venous "compressibility" test; do not confuse the two in documentation.
3. Measure outer-wall-to-outer-wall diameter in transverse, at the widest point, under compression.
4. Assess for compressibility, peristalsis, and periappendiceal fat echogenicity.

| Finding | Threshold | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Diameter | >6mm, noncompressible | Positive (Puylaert criteria) |
| Diameter | 6-7mm, compressible, no secondary signs | Equivocal — document explicitly, recommend clinical correlation/CT |
| Periappendiceal fat | Increased echogenicity/hyperemia | Supports inflammation even at borderline diameter |
| Appendicolith | Present | Supports diagnosis regardless of diameter |

## First-trimester dating / redating (ACOG-AIUM-SMFM Committee Opinion 700)

| Gestational age by LMP | Redate by CRL if discrepancy exceeds |
|---|---|
| <9w0d | 5 days |
| 9w0d-13w6d | 7 days |
| 14w0d-15w6d (BPD) | 7 days |
| 16w0d-21w6d | 10 days |
| 22w0d-27w6d | 14 days |
| ≥28w0d | 21 days |

CRL is reliable for dating only up to ~84mm (≈14 weeks); above that, switch to second-trimester biometry (BPD/HC/FL) for any subsequent redating decision.

## Doppler reference thresholds by organ

| Study | Metric | Abnormal threshold | Typical implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Renal transplant | Resistive Index (RI) | >0.80 | Rejection, ATN, or venous outflow obstruction — correlate with post-op day and creatinine trend |
| Native renal artery stenosis | Waveform | Tardus parvus (prolonged acceleration time, blunted systolic upstroke) distal to stenosis | Suggests proximal >60% stenosis |
| Hepatic artery, post-transplant | RI | <0.5, or absent diastolic flow | Hepatic artery stenosis/thrombosis — surgical emergency, escalate immediately |
| Carotid, ICA | Peak systolic velocity | >230 cm/s | ≥70% stenosis (NASCET-correlated); confirm Doppler angle ≤60° before reporting |
| Testicular torsion | Color/power Doppler flow | Absent arterial flow vs. contralateral | Torsion until proven otherwise — escalate before completing routine protocol |

## IOTA Simple Rules — adnexal mass triage

| Result | Call |
|---|---|
| Only B (benign) features present | Benign |
| Only M (malignant) features present | Malignant |
| Both B and M features present | Inconclusive — do not force a binary call; refer for expert (Level II) review or ADNEX model scoring |
| Neither B nor M features present | Inconclusive — same as above |

B features (examples): unilocular, solid component <7mm, acoustic shadows, smooth multilocular <10cm, no flow (color score 1). M features (examples): irregular solid tumor, ascites, ≥4 papillary structures, irregular multilocular solid ≥10cm, strong flow (color score 4).
