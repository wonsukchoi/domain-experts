# Ophthalmologist playbook

Filled protocols and decision tables for the four highest-volume clinical decisions: glaucoma medication escalation, anti-VEGF treat-and-extend, cataract preoperative planning, and retinal tear/detachment triage. Load this when a task needs the actual thresholds and sequencing, not just the reasoning.

## Glaucoma medication escalation ladder

Target IOP reduction from untreated baseline: **25-30%** for mild disease, **30-40%** for moderate, **≥40%** for severe/advanced field loss (AAO PPP POAG, 2020). Escalate only after confirming adherence and ruling out diurnal variation as the explanation for an apparently "at target" reading that still shows progression.

| Step | Agent/action | Expected IOP drop | Escalate when |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st line | Prostaglandin analog (latanoprost, bimatoprost, travoprost), once nightly | 25-33% | No response at 4-6 weeks, or progression on field/OCT despite target achieved |
| 2nd line (add-on) | Topical beta-blocker (timolol) or laser (SLT) | +15-20% additional | SLT preferred first add-on in most patients without contraindication — comparable IOP-lowering to a second drop, no adherence burden (LiGHT trial, 2019) |
| 3rd line (add-on) | Carbonic anhydrase inhibitor (dorzolamide/brinzolamide) or alpha-agonist (brimonidine) | +10-15% additional | Add-on chosen by side-effect profile (avoid alpha-agonist in patients on MAOIs; avoid CAI in sulfa allergy) |
| 4th line | Fixed-combination drops to reduce drop count/adherence burden | Same as components | Adherence confirmed poor with multiple bottles |
| Surgical | Trabeculectomy, tube shunt (Ahmed/Baerveldt), or MIGS (iStent, Hydrus) at cataract surgery | Variable, target-dependent | Maximum tolerated medical therapy + laser fails to halt progression, or medication burden/side effects are intolerable |

**Reassessment interval:** repeat IOP + gonioscopy at 4-6 weeks after any change; OCT-RNFL and 24-2 visual field every 6-12 months depending on stage, compared against the prior 2-3 fields for trend (a single field is noise — event-based and trend-based analysis both require a baseline of at least 3 reliable fields).

## Anti-VEGF treat-and-extend schedule (wet AMD / DME)

| Visit | Interval from prior | Action if OCT shows fluid | Action if OCT dry |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loading | q4 weeks x 3 injections | — | — |
| 4 | 4 weeks after 3rd load | Continue q4wk, consider agent switch if no anatomic response after 3 injections | Extend to 6 weeks |
| 5+ | Prior interval ± 2 weeks | Shorten interval by 2 weeks | Extend interval by 2 weeks, to a ceiling of 12-16 weeks |
| Recurrence at extended interval | — | Shorten back by 2 weeks from the last dry interval and hold there ≥2 cycles before re-extending | — |

**Agent-switch criterion:** persistent central subfield fluid or acuity decline after 3 consecutive injections of the same agent — switch class (e.g., ranibizumab/bevacizumab to aflibercept) rather than continuing the same agent past 3 non-responses (DRCR.net Protocol T subgroup data showed agent-dependent response differences concentrated in eyes with worse baseline acuity).

**Never** extend past the point the last two consecutive visits were both dry — one dry visit is not yet evidence the interval is earned.

## Cataract preoperative planning table

| Input | Threshold / rule | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| Biometry axial length | <22 mm or >26 mm | Use a formula validated for the extreme (Hoffer Q for short eyes, Barrett Universal II or Kane for long eyes) — SRK/T systematically errs outside this range |
| Formula default | Barrett Universal II (or Kane if available) | Current best general-purpose accuracy across axial length ranges (Melles et al. 2018 comparative accuracy study) |
| Corneal astigmatism | ≥0.75 D | Toric IOL or limbal relaxing incision candidacy discussion — untreated, residual astigmatism is the single largest driver of post-op spectacle dependence and patient dissatisfaction |
| History of prior refractive surgery (LASIK/PRK) | Any | Do not use standard formula on manufacturer-reported keratometry alone — use a post-refractive-specific method (ASCRS post-refractive IOL calculator, Barrett True-K) or formula error routinely exceeds 1.00 D |
| Posterior capsule rupture risk factors | Pseudoexfoliation, dense brunescent (4+) nucleus, poor pupil dilation (<5mm), prior vitrectomy, high myopia | Case-mix-adjust the consent conversation's stated complication rate upward from the population baseline (~1-2%) — do not quote the average risk to a case with 2+ risk factors present |
| Target refraction | Discuss explicitly, don't default silently to plano | Monovision, mini-monovision, or plano-both-eyes is a patient preference decision, not a surgeon default — document the conversation |

## Retinal tear / detachment urgency triage

| Finding | Urgency tier | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Symptomatic horseshoe tear, flat surrounding retina, macula attached | Same day | Laser retinopexy or cryotherapy same visit — untreated progression risk ~30-40%, treated <5% |
| Asymptomatic tear found incidentally | Days, not same-day | Laser within days; lower urgency because no active traction symptom, but still treat — incidental tears still carry a non-trivial detachment risk over time |
| Retinal detachment, macula attached ("macula-on") | Emergency — within ~24 hours | Same-day OR or same-day retina-surgeon evaluation; visual prognosis is time-dependent while the macula is still flat |
| Retinal detachment, macula detached ("macula-off") | Urgent — within days (commonly cited window ~7-10 days without meaningfully worsening final acuity) | Schedule surgery promptly but the same-day emergency framing no longer changes the visual outcome once the macula itself has detached |
| Symptomatic PVD, no tear found on first exam | Recheck in 4-6 weeks | Roughly half of tears associated with symptomatic PVD are missed on the first exam due to vitreous hemorrhage/media opacity — the scheduled recheck, not a one-time discharge, is what catches the second look |
