# Red flags

### Sudden painless monocular vision loss with a relative afferent pupillary defect
- **Usually means:** Central retinal artery occlusion (stroke-equivalent) or ischemic optic neuropathy; second most likely is a large vitreous hemorrhage from proliferative retinopathy.
- **First question:** "Exactly when did it start, and has any vision returned since?" — CRAO management and prognosis are time-anchored the same way stroke is.
- **Data to pull:** Dilated fundus exam for cherry-red spot/retinal whitening, same-day stroke-service referral, carotid duplex and cardiac workup (embolic source), ESR/CRP if giant cell arteritis is a concern in a patient over 50.

### New flashes and floaters with no tear found on a single dilated exam
- **Usually means:** Benign posterior vitreous detachment; second most likely is a tear obscured by vitreous hemorrhage or media opacity on this visit.
- **First question:** "Was the exam done with scleral depression to the ora serrata, and was the view actually clear?"
- **Data to pull:** Documented periphery coverage on the exam note; schedule and confirm a 4-6 week recheck rather than a one-time discharge.

### Posterior capsule rupture rate above ~2% (case-mix adjusted) for a given surgeon or center
- **Usually means:** Technique issue (capsulorhexis control, phaco chop technique) or a case-mix shift (more pseudoexfoliation, denser cataracts, less pupil dilation) not yet accounted for in the raw rate.
- **First question:** "Adjusted for case complexity, is this surgeon's or center's rate actually above benchmark, or is the case mix harder than average?"
- **Data to pull:** Surgeon-level complication log with case complexity flags (pseudoexfoliation, dense nucleus, small pupil, prior vitrectomy) over the last 100-200 cases.

### IOP "at target" in clinic but visual field or OCT-RNFL still progressing
- **Usually means:** Diurnal IOP peak occurring outside clinic hours, or medication non-adherence; second most likely is a true target-IOP miscalculation for that eye's individual damage threshold.
- **First question:** "Has this patient ever had a diurnal or home-tonometry curve, and when did they last take their drops before today's reading?"
- **Data to pull:** 24-hour or home tonometry curve, pharmacy refill history, prior 3+ visual fields for a real trend comparison (not just the two most recent).

### Anti-VEGF interval extended twice in a row without a confirmed dry OCT at both visits
- **Usually means:** Interval extension is running on a fixed schedule rather than that visit's actual fluid status.
- **First question:** "Was the OCT actually dry at both of the last two visits, or was one extension made 'to see how it goes'?"
- **Data to pull:** OCT central subfield thickness trend across the last 4 visits, not just the most recent reading.

### Red, painful eye treated with a topical antibiotic without a slit-lamp exam
- **Usually means:** Bacterial conjunctivitis was assumed from history alone; second most likely is anterior uveitis or microbial keratitis being missed, since neither responds to antibiotic drops and both need urgent same-visit escalation.
- **First question:** "Was there photophobia or decreased vision, and was a slit lamp actually used before the prescription was written?"
- **Data to pull:** Documented visual acuity, slit-lamp findings (cell/flare, corneal infiltrate, dendrite), and IOP from the visit in question.

### Post-refractive-surgery patient (LASIK/PRK history) getting cataract surgery with a standard IOL formula
- **Usually means:** The surgical team used the manufacturer's keratometry reading directly instead of a post-refractive-specific calculation method.
- **First question:** "Is there a documented pre-LASIK keratometry and refraction on file, and which post-refractive IOL method was actually used?"
- **Data to pull:** Historical refraction/keratometry records if available, and the specific formula (ASCRS post-refractive calculator, Barrett True-K) used for the current IOL power selection.

### Macula-on retinal detachment scheduled for repair more than ~24-48 hours out
- **Usually means:** Scheduling/logistics treated as equal priority to a macula-off case, or the urgency wasn't communicated clearly at referral.
- **First question:** "Is the macula confirmed attached on today's exam, and does the OR schedule reflect that as an emergency, not a routine urgent case?"
- **Data to pull:** Today's exam note confirming macula status, OR booking timestamp versus exam timestamp.

### Ocular hypertension started on medication without a documented OHTS-style risk calculation
- **Usually means:** IOP number alone (e.g., "22 is high") drove the treatment decision rather than an actual 5-year risk estimate.
- **First question:** "What was the calculated 5-year conversion risk, and what inputs (CCT, cup-to-disc ratio, PSD) went into it?"
- **Data to pull:** Central corneal thickness, vertical cup-to-disc ratio, pattern standard deviation, and the resulting risk percentage documented in the chart.
