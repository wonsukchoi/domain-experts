# Forester — Red Flags

### Cruise report states a volume figure with no confidence interval or sample size
- **Usually means:** a point estimate is being presented as measured fact, or the cruiser never computed sampling error.
- **First question:** what was the cruise's coefficient of variation and number of points?
- **Data to pull:** field tally sheets, cruise-design memo, compilation worksheet.

### Stand basal area exceeds the upper stocking guide line for its species and site index
- **Usually means:** the stand is past due for a thinning; continued growth is shifting from diameter increment to competition-driven mortality.
- **First question:** when was the last thinning, and is a final harvest already scheduled within 2-3 years?
- **Data to pull:** current stand exam data, stocking chart for the species.

### Rotation-age recommendation given with no stated discount rate
- **Usually means:** a biological (MAI-culmination) rotation age is being presented as if it were the financial optimum, or vice versa.
- **First question:** is this recommendation optimizing total wood volume or discounted value — and at what rate?
- **Data to pull:** LEV/rotation-age comparison worksheet.

### Clearcut prescribed for a shade-tolerant regeneration species with no salvage/sanitation justification
- **Usually means:** the system was chosen by convention or landowner preference rather than matched to the target species' regeneration requirement.
- **First question:** what is the target regeneration species and its shade tolerance class?
- **Data to pull:** species composition and regeneration survey data.

### Site index derived from a single sample tree
- **Usually means:** an unreliable growth-and-yield input if that tree was suppressed, forked, or previously topped rather than dominant/codominant.
- **First question:** how many site trees were measured, and were they dominant or codominant, undamaged stems?
- **Data to pull:** site-tree measurement sheet (species, age, height, crown class).

### Fixed BAF prism used across a stand with visibly varying density
- **Usually means:** over-sampling in sparse areas and under-sampling in dense areas, inflating total cruise error beyond the reported CV.
- **First question:** was the stand stratified by density or age class before cruising?
- **Data to pull:** stand map or recent aerial/LiDAR imagery.

### Post-harvest mill scale differs from the pre-harvest cruise estimate by more than roughly 10%
- **Usually means:** a systematic cruise bias — wrong volume table, mismatched log rule, or a miscounted BAF — not ordinary sampling noise.
- **First question:** which log rule and volume table did the cruise use, and does it match the mill's scale method?
- **Data to pull:** mill scale tickets, cruise compilation, volume-table citation.

### Regeneration survey at the 3-5 year post-harvest check shows stocking below the minimum stems-per-acre threshold
- **Usually means:** competing vegetation, inadequate seed source, or a site-prep/herbicide-release step that wasn't completed as prescribed.
- **First question:** was the prescribed site prep and release treatment completed on schedule?
- **Data to pull:** regeneration survey plot data, site-prep completion records.

### Harvest decision driven solely by a current stumpage price spike
- **Usually means:** the rotation-age economics (LEV at this age vs. waiting) were not consulted before deciding to cut.
- **First question:** what does the stand's current MAI/PAI trend and LEV comparison show for this age versus a later one?
- **Data to pull:** growth-and-yield projection, LEV worksheet.

### Thinning prescription specifies a tree count or percentage removed instead of a residual basal-area target
- **Usually means:** the mark is unenforceable in the field, since tree count alone doesn't control post-thin density on a stand with variable diameter distribution.
- **First question:** what residual basal area per acre is the marking crew supposed to leave?
- **Data to pull:** marked-tree tally with a residual basal-area calculation.
