### A high-spend keyword has informational search intent
- **Usually means:** Paid bidding on this term likely has low commercial value relative to its cost — organic content is probably the better lever.
- **First question:** What is this keyword's actual conversion rate and cost-per-acquisition compared to transactional-intent terms in the same campaign?
- **Data to pull:** Keyword-level conversion rate and CPA, search intent classification.

### A keyword's cost-per-click is high despite a reasonable bid
- **Usually means:** Quality Score is likely depressed by weak ad relevance or poor landing page experience, and the bid is compensating for a fixable structural issue rather than genuine competition.
- **First question:** Which Quality Score component (expected CTR, ad relevance, landing page experience) is scoring lowest for this keyword?
- **Data to pull:** Quality Score component breakdown, landing page load time and relevance to ad copy.

### A channel is being cut or scaled based only on last-click attribution
- **Usually means:** The decision may miss the channel's actual contribution as an upper-funnel assist that a data-driven or multi-touch model would reveal.
- **First question:** How does this channel's performance compare under last-click versus a data-driven/multi-touch attribution model?
- **Data to pull:** Channel performance under both attribution models, conversion path data showing where this channel typically appears.

### Brand paid search ROAS is reported without any incrementality testing
- **Usually means:** A significant share of the "attributed" conversions may be cannibalized organic clicks that would have converted anyway.
- **First question:** Has a geo holdout or similar incrementality test been run on this brand campaign, and if not, when was the last one conducted?
- **Data to pull:** Last incrementality test results (or their absence), current last-click ROAS figure.

### A budget increase is proposed based on average ROAS across all existing spend
- **Usually means:** The channel or keyword set may already be approaching saturation, where the next incremental dollar returns significantly less than the average dollar already spent.
- **First question:** What is the marginal ROAS at the current spend level, calculated from the most recent incremental spend increase, not the average across total spend?
- **Data to pull:** Spend-tier performance data showing ROAS at increasing spend levels (a saturation curve).

### A large share of a campaign's "conversions" come from branded search terms
- **Usually means:** These conversions are especially likely to be cannibalized organic traffic, since branded searchers are already highly likely to find and click the organic listing regardless of the ad.
- **First question:** What percentage of this campaign's attributed conversions come from branded vs. non-branded terms, and has incrementality been tested specifically on the branded segment?
- **Data to pull:** Conversion breakdown by branded vs. non-branded keyword, incrementality test results if available.

### Organic content targeting a transactional keyword reads like an informational blog post (or vice versa)
- **Usually means:** A content-type-to-intent mismatch that likely hurts both ranking potential and conversion rate for that page.
- **First question:** Does this page's format and content type match what searchers and search engines expect for this keyword's actual intent?
- **Data to pull:** Current SERP results for the keyword (format of top-ranking pages), keyword's classified intent.

### An incrementality test was run once, long ago, and hasn't been repeated
- **Usually means:** True incrementality can shift over time as organic ranking strength, brand awareness, or competitive landscape changes — a stale test result may no longer reflect current cannibalization levels.
- **First question:** When was the last incrementality test run, and has anything changed since then (organic ranking position, brand search volume, competitive activity) that would affect the result?
- **Data to pull:** Date of last incrementality test, current organic ranking position for relevant brand terms.
