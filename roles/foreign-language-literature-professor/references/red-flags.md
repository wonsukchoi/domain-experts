# Red flags

### Declared majors declining 3+ consecutive years while intro-sequence (101/102) enrollment holds steady
- **Usually means:** the program is functioning purely as a gen-ed service unit and losing its major-declaration pipeline specifically — a different problem than an overall enrollment crisis, and one a blended headcount number will hide.
- **First question:** what fraction of GER 102/201 completers go on to declare the major, and has that conversion rate itself dropped, or is the raw pool just smaller?
- **Data to pull:** registrar declared-major trend by cohort, cross-referenced against 102/201 completion counts for the same cohorts.

### Placement-band DFW rate above roughly 15% (department's typical acceptable ceiling) for two consecutive terms
- **Usually means:** the cut score for that specific band is miscalibrated, not that the students in it are underprepared generally.
- **First question:** is the elevated DFW concentrated in one placement band, or spread evenly — and does it correlate with heritage-learner status?
- **Data to pull:** DFW rate by placement band and by heritage/non-heritage status, most recent 2 terms.

### Heritage-background student placed by written test alone into a classroom-L2-calibrated section
- **Usually means:** the instrument is scoring oral and written skill on one axis when the student's actual profile is split across the two — the numeric result is unreliable for this population specifically.
- **First question:** was an interview override offered, and if not, why was this student's placement treated identically to a classroom L2 learner's?
- **Data to pull:** the placement instrument's stated design population and the department's override policy (if one exists).

### A program-review or budget report presents a single blended cost-recovery or SCH figure with no service-vs-major breakdown
- **Usually means:** the framing will read a healthy service function and a thin major as one number, making the whole program look weaker than either function actually is on its own terms.
- **First question:** what percentage of the reported SCH comes from courses required by a college-wide requirement, independent of major enrollment?
- **Data to pull:** the underlying course-by-course SCH report, split by service vs. major-track designation.

### More than 70% of introductory (101/102-level) sections taught by non-tenure-track instructors
- **Usually means:** the pipeline feeding the major and the certification track runs on faculty least positioned to build long-term relationships with students who might otherwise advance into upper-division study — a continuity risk more than a cost-efficiency win.
- **First question:** how many of the non-tenure-track instructors have taught the same course level more than twice consecutively, versus rotating each term?
- **Data to pull:** course schedule with instructor type (tenure-line, contingent, adjunct) for the last 3 years.

### A curriculum or staffing change touching upper-division literature/culture courses proposed without a licensure-pipeline check
- **Usually means:** the change is being evaluated purely on enrollment or budget grounds, with no verification of whether it breaks a state teacher-certification content requirement.
- **First question:** does the state's licensure regulation for this language name a specific upper-division literature/culture course count or content area that this change would reduce below the required threshold?
- **Data to pull:** the state licensure statute or regulation text and the current curriculum map showing which courses satisfy it.

### A tenure or merit file spanning language-pedagogy and literary scholarship, with an external-reviewer list drawn from only one sub-field
- **Usually means:** the committee will be unable to evaluate the sub-field with no matching reviewer, and that portion of the record will be scored as unevaluable rather than as evidence.
- **First question:** for each major output in the file, is there at least one letter-writer qualified to assess that specific type of work?
- **Data to pull:** the proposed reviewer list mapped against each publication's sub-field.

### A student's OPI or equivalent proficiency rating is unknown or untested within 2 semesters of a study-abroad or student-teaching gate
- **Usually means:** the program is relying on course GPA as a stand-in for proficiency, and the actual gate requirement may not be met when formally tested.
- **First question:** has this student had a formal or informal OPI-style check within the last academic year, and what rating resulted?
- **Data to pull:** the student's course grade history and most recent proficiency assessment (or absence of one).

### Local semester-over-semester enrollment decline reported without a national MLA benchmark comparison
- **Usually means:** a decline that's actually in line with (or below) the national trend for that language is being treated as a local failure requiring a local fix.
- **First question:** what was the most recent MLA-reported national enrollment change for this specific language, and how does the local number compare?
- **Data to pull:** the department's own enrollment trend and the most recent MLA "Enrollments in Languages Other Than English" survey figure for the language in question.

### A language department's core curriculum textbook or syllabus unchanged for 10+ years despite a World-Readiness Standards revision in the interim
- **Usually means:** the curriculum has drifted from the current national framework (the 5 Cs) without anyone auditing it against the revised standard.
- **First question:** when was the syllabus last mapped explicitly against the current World-Readiness Standards edition?
- **Data to pull:** the current syllabus and the most recent National Standards Collaborative Board framework document.
