# Playbook

Filled templates and step sequences for the four recurring processes: program-viability SCH analysis, placement-instrument validation, OPI benchmark tracking, and mixed pedagogy/literature tenure dossiers.

## 1. Program-viability SCH analysis (filled worksheet)

Run before responding to any program-review or budget-reallocation report that frames a language program by aggregate cost recovery.

```
Program: German Studies | Review cycle: [Year]

STEP 1 — List every course, section, enrollment, credits:
  GER 101 (x2 sections): 22, 22 students | 4 credits | SCH: 176
  GER 102 (x2 sections): 18, 18 students | 4 credits | SCH: 144
  GER 201 (x1 section):  14 students      | 4 credits | SCH: 56
  GER 350 (x1 section):  32 students      | 3 credits | SCH: 96
  GER 410 (x1 section):   5 students      | 3 credits | SCH: 15
  TOTAL SCH: 487

STEP 2 — Tag each course SERVICE (required regardless of major) or MAJOR-ONLY:
  SERVICE: 101, 102, 201, 350  → 176+144+56+96 = 472 SCH (96.9%)
  MAJOR-ONLY: 410              → 15 SCH (3.1%)

STEP 3 — Compute revenue and recovery rate at the college's stated RCM rate:
  Rate: $350/SCH | Revenue: 487 x 350 = $170,450
  Cost: $255,000 (2 TT @ $95,000 + 1 lecturer @ $65,000)
  Recovery rate: 170,450 / 255,000 = 66.8%

STEP 4 — Test the "cut the major" counterfactual explicitly:
  SCH removed if major eliminated: 15 (GER 410 only)
  Cost removed: $0 — the same 3 FTE still staff the service sequence
  New recovery rate if major cut: (487-15)x350 / 255,000 = 64.8%
  Conclusion: cutting the major LOWERS recovery rate slightly (fewer SCH,
  same cost) while losing the certification-pipeline credential. Cutting
  the major does not solve a service-side revenue problem.

STEP 5 — Identify the actual lever (service-side SCH growth) and quantify it:
  Cross-list GER 350 against 2 additional gen-ed categories.
  Projected gain: 10 students x 3 credits = 30 SCH
  New total: 517 SCH | New revenue: 517 x 350 = $180,950
  New recovery rate: 180,950 / 255,000 = 70.96%
```

## 2. Placement-instrument validation (filled audit)

Run at minimum every 3 years, or immediately if DFW (D/F/Withdraw) rates spike at any level.

```
Instrument: [Placement test name] | Last validated: [Date]

STEP 1 — Pull outcome data by placement level, most recent 2 terms:
  Placed into GER 102: n=36 | DFW rate: 11% | next-level (201) pass rate: 88%
  Placed into GER 201: n=14 | DFW rate: 29% | next-level (300) pass rate: 61%

STEP 2 — Compare against the department's acceptable-DFW threshold (commonly ~15%):
  GER 201 placement DFW at 29% is nearly double the 15% threshold — audit
  the cut score for the 201 placement band specifically, not the whole test.

STEP 3 — Heritage-learner override check:
  Of the 14 placed into GER 201, 4 were heritage-background students.
  Their DFW rate within that group: 50% (2 of 4) vs. 10% (1 of 10) for
  classroom L2 learners in the same placement band — the instrument is
  misplacing heritage learners specifically, not the band cut score broadly.

STEP 4 — Action:
  Add a mandatory interview override for any heritage-background student
  scoring within 1 band of a placement boundary, rather than adjusting
  the numeric cut score for all students.
```

## 3. OPI benchmark tracking (filled tracking sheet)

Run each term for majors within 2 semesters of graduation, or for any student entering a study-abroad or student-teaching pipeline.

```
Student | Target milestone | Required ACTFL level | Current OPI rating | Gap
A. Chen | Study abroad (partner requires Intermediate-Mid) | Intermediate-Mid | Intermediate-Low | 1 sublevel
B. Diaz | Student teaching (state requires Advanced-Low)   | Advanced-Low     | Intermediate-High | 1 sublevel
C. Okafor | Graduation only (no external gate)              | none required    | Advanced-Low      | none

ACTION per gap:
  A. Chen: enroll in conversation-intensive elective next term; re-test
     via informal OPI-style interview before abroad application deadline.
  B. Diaz: flag to licensure advisor now — a 1-sublevel gap at this stage
     typically needs 1 full semester of targeted speaking practice to close;
     confirm the state's testing window still allows a retake before the
     certification application deadline.
  C. Okafor: no action needed; document rating for the file.
```

## 4. Mixed pedagogy/literature tenure dossier — translation table

Build before assembling the CV narrative, whenever the candidate's scholarship spans SLA/applied linguistics and literary criticism.

| Output | Binding criterion it satisfies | Reviewer type needed | Translation stated in the file |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classroom-based study on task-based instruction outcomes (published in a language-pedagogy journal) | "Original research contribution to the field" | Applied linguist / SLA researcher | "Peer-reviewed empirical study using a pre/post OPI-rated design; journal is the flagship venue of [named applied-linguistics association]." |
| Literary-critical monograph chapter on 20th-century German prose | "Original research contribution to the field" | Literary scholar in the relevant national literature | Standard literary-criticism evaluation applies directly; no translation needed. |
| Curriculum redesign moving a two-tiered sequence to an integrated MLA-model sequence, documented with enrollment/retention data | "Evidence of teaching innovation with measurable outcome" | Department chair + 1 applied linguist | "Redesign followed the MLA (2007) integrated-curriculum model; retention from 102 to 201 rose from [X%] to [Y%] over 2 cohorts post-implementation." |
| Corpus-linguistics analysis of learner error patterns | "Original research contribution to the field" | Corpus/computational linguist | "Methodology and significance require a reviewer with corpus-linguistics background — a literary-criticism-only review committee should not be the sole evaluators of this piece." |

Rule: every row assigns a specific reviewer type, not just "external reviewers" generically — a tenure committee that solicits letters only from literary scholars for a mixed-scholarship file will produce a case with half its research unevaluated.
