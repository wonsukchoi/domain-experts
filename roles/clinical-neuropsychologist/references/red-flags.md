# Red flags

### Only one PVT administered, and it's borderline
- **Usually means:** insufficient validity assessment for the referral's stakes; second most likely is a rushed/insurance-limited battery that skipped a second effort measure.
- **First question:** what does a second, methodologically independent PVT (not another subscale of the same test) show?
- **Data to pull:** full PVT summary table for the evaluation; if only one exists, flag the report as unable to conclude validity either way rather than defaulting to "valid."

### TOMM Trial 2 below 45/50 (90%)
- **Usually means:** invalid effort; second most likely is a genuine, severe amnestic disorder (e.g., advanced dementia, anoxic injury) where even a two-choice recognition task is impaired.
- **First question:** is there a documented, independently corroborated severe memory disorder that would explain low recognition-memory performance even with full effort?
- **Data to pull:** Trial 1 vs. Trial 2 trend (should improve, not decline, with genuine severe amnesia), retention trial score, imaging/diagnosis supporting severe amnesia.

### Reliable Digit Span ≤7 on WAIS-IV Digit Span
- **Usually means:** invalid effort; second most likely is genuinely low premorbid intellectual functioning.
- **First question:** what is the estimated premorbid FSIQ from academic/occupational records?
- **Data to pull:** school transcripts, prior testing, occupational history, and results of the other administered PVTs.

### More than ~40% of scores in a 20+ measure battery fall below −1.5 SD with no PVT failure
- **Usually means:** expected base-rate scatter in a large battery, not diffuse impairment.
- **First question:** how many total measures were administered, and does the published base-rate table for that battery size predict this many low scores by chance?
- **Data to pull:** Iverson/Brooks-style base-rate table matched to the number of measures given; count of low scores in domains the referral did not flag.

### Serial testing shows a score increase at retest with no practice-effect correction
- **Usually means:** a normal practice effect being read as clinical improvement.
- **First question:** what is the published test-retest interval and expected practice-effect gain for this instrument at this retest interval?
- **Data to pull:** instrument's technical-manual test-retest data; run the Reliable Change Index calculation before writing "improved" or "declined."

### Verbal-memory deficit reported after epilepsy pre-surgical workup with no lateralization data on file
- **Usually means:** an incomplete pre-surgical workup — memory norms assume typical language/memory lateralization.
- **First question:** which hemisphere is language- and memory-dominant per Wada or fMRI?
- **Data to pull:** Wada procedure report or fMRI lateralization report; re-interpret verbal/nonverbal memory splits against the dominant hemisphere, not generic norms.

### Litigating mTBI claimant's reported symptom severity doesn't match the contemporaneous injury record
- **Usually means:** symptom exaggeration or invalid effort in a litigation context; second most likely is a genuine persistent post-concussive syndrome with anxiety/somatic amplification.
- **First question:** what does the ED or urgent-care record from the time of injury show (GCS, loss-of-consciousness duration, imaging)?
- **Data to pull:** ED/urgent-care chart, initial GCS and imaging report, any prior mental-health or substance-use history that predates the injury.

### Reported cognitive/functional complaint severity mismatches independent collateral report
- **Usually means:** overreporting, possibly effort-related; second most likely is a genuine deficit the patient can't self-monitor (anosognosia), which cuts the opposite direction diagnostically.
- **First question:** what does an independent informant (spouse, coworker) report about the same functional domain?
- **Data to pull:** collateral questionnaire or interview notes; compare item-by-item against the patient's self-report.

### Full Scale IQ reported as a single number despite index scores spanning more than 23 points (1.5 SD)
- **Usually means:** the evaluator defaulted to a global composite without checking index spread, obscuring a real strengths/weaknesses profile.
- **First question:** what is the actual highest-to-lowest index spread, and does the referral question require a single global number at all?
- **Data to pull:** full index score table with confidence intervals; report indices separately when spread exceeds threshold.

### Forensic report states an opinion "to a reasonable degree of neuropsychological certainty" without addressing alternative causes
- **Usually means:** the causation opinion is vulnerable to cross-examination for an incomplete differential.
- **First question:** was substance use, pre-existing learning disability, depression/anxiety, and prior head injury explicitly ruled out, not just unmentioned?
- **Data to pull:** substance-use screening results, prior psychiatric and educational records, and any pre-injury baseline testing if it exists.
