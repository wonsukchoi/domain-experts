# Red Flags

### Two or more similar incidents at the same location within 12–24 months
- **Usually means:** the current coverage/lighting/access-control model doesn't match the site's actual risk pattern; second most likely, an earlier incident was under-documented so the pattern wasn't visible until now.
- **First question:** "What did we change in coverage, lighting, or access control after the first incident?"
- **Data to pull:** incident log filtered by location and type, trailing 24 months; post order revision history for the same period.

### Sustained overtime above ~10% of scheduled hours for two consecutive pay periods at one site
- **Usually means:** a structural staffing gap the base schedule can't cover; second most likely, one or two officers absorbing most of the OT (burnout/quit risk) rather than an even spread.
- **First question:** "Is this the same 2–3 officers every period, or spread across the roster?"
- **Data to pull:** payroll OT report by officer and site, trailing 60 days; current open-requisition count for the site.

### Use-of-force report where applied force is two or more steps above documented resistance
- **Usually means:** officer over-response under stress or inadequate de-escalation training; second most likely, incomplete resistance documentation understating what actually happened.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the 30 seconds before force was applied — what did the subject do first?"
- **Data to pull:** incident report, any camera/body-cam footage for the window, officer's last de-escalation/use-of-force training date.

### Post order last revised more than 12 months ago at a site with any incident in that window
- **Usually means:** the post order wasn't updated after the incident review that should have triggered a change; second most likely, the review happened but the update was never pushed to the written order.
- **First question:** "What did the after-action review recommend, and where did that recommendation go?"
- **Data to pull:** post order revision log; incident after-action notes for the same period.

### Pass-down log entries that are blank, identical across shifts, or signed within seconds of shift start
- **Usually means:** officers are initialing without reading; second most likely, the prior shift genuinely had nothing to report, but that should be rare enough to be visible, not the default.
- **First question:** "What time did you arrive, and what time did you sign the log?"
- **Data to pull:** timestamped sign-in vs. sign-off records; log content variance across a sample of 10 consecutive shifts.

### Guard-tour/checkpoint system shows missed checkpoints above ~15% during a site's documented risk window
- **Usually means:** the roaming route or interval doesn't match the officer's actual coverage load (too many posts, too tight an interval); second most likely, the device or app has a reliability problem masking real compliance.
- **First question:** "Which checkpoints are being missed — always the same ones, or random?"
- **Data to pull:** checkpoint compliance report by location and hour, trailing 30 days.

### Armed post staffed by an officer with a lapsed firearms or use-of-force recertification
- **Usually means:** a training/credentialing tracking gap, not a scheduling oversight — the roster and the certification system aren't cross-checked before shift assignment.
- **First question:** "When did this lapse, and how many shifts has this officer worked armed since?"
- **Data to pull:** certification expiration report cross-referenced against the armed-post schedule for the same period.

### Client requests a post cut or downgrade shortly after an incident with "no injury"
- **Usually means:** the client is optimizing for the visible cost line without seeing the foreseeability exposure the incident just created; second most likely, budget pressure from elsewhere in the client's org is being routed through security spend because it's the easiest line to cut.
- **First question:** "How many similar incidents has this location had in the last two years?"
- **Data to pull:** prior-similar-incident count and dates; proposed gap window vs. incident timestamps.

### New-hire attrition above roughly 40% in the first 90 days at a site
- **Usually means:** the site's pay rate, shift pattern, or working conditions are out of line with what was represented at hire; second most likely, onboarding/training is inadequate and officers are being set up to fail post orders they were never actually taught.
- **First question:** "What do exit conversations at this site actually say, not what does the exit form say?"
- **Data to pull:** 90-day attrition by site, trailing 12 months; exit interview notes if any exist.

### Incident report filed more than 24 hours after the event
- **Usually means:** the officer or supervisor is reconstructing from memory rather than contemporaneous notes, which weakens the report's evidentiary value; second most likely, a reporting-workflow gap (no accessible form, no supervisor available to receive it).
- **First question:** "What were you doing in the 24 hours between the incident and filing this?"
- **Data to pull:** incident timestamp vs. report-filed timestamp across the last 90 days, by officer.

### A subcontracted or third-party post has no post order on file at the primary contractor
- **Usually means:** coverage was arranged informally (a client relationship, a quick fill) without the standard onboarding process, so there's no enforceable standard if something goes wrong on that post.
- **First question:** "Who wrote and approved the post order this subcontractor is working from?"
- **Data to pull:** post order file for the specific post; subcontract agreement's referenced scope of work.

### Client site has had a threat, restraining order, or workplace violence report on file with no documented response plan
- **Usually means:** the threat was logged as an HR/legal matter and never crossed over into the security operating plan for that site; second most likely, a plan exists but was never briefed to the officers who'd actually encounter the person.
- **First question:** "Does the officer on that post tonight know this person's description and what to do if they show up?"
- **Data to pull:** workplace violence prevention plan for the site; briefing acknowledgment log for the relevant threat.
