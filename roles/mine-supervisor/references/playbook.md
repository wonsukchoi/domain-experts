## Roof control plan deviation log (filled example)

| Date/shift | Location | Condition found | Plan section applied | Support delta | Time cost | Reported to |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7/17 Day | Entry 3, Sta. 55–75 | Drummy sound, 20-ft diagonal slip | §4 Adverse Roof Conditions | 4×4 → 4×2 spacing, +25 bolts, +10 straps | 50 min | Mine foreman's office, safety dept. |
| 7/12 Night | Entry 6, Sta. 10–15 | Water seepage softening rib | §5 Wet/Soft Ground | Added timber cribs at 5-ft centers | 20 min | Shift foreman log only (within routine authority) |
| 6/29 Day | Entry 2, Sta. 30 | Isolated crack, single point | §4 Adverse Roof Conditions | Localized 2-ft-radius supplemental bolts (+4) | 12 min | Shift foreman log only |

**Escalation rule applied:** any deviation whose required support falls outside a plan section's written contingency (e.g., a slip wider than the largest documented example in §4, or a condition spanning more than 30 ft) triggers withdrawal from the affected area and a call to the engineer of record — not an extrapolated field pattern.

## Gas action-level response table (as posted at the section)

| Reading (CH4, working face) | Mandatory action | Who executes |
|---|---|---|
| < 1.0% | Continue normal operation, log reading | Certified examiner |
| 1.0%–1.4% | De-energize electrical face equipment in the area | Section supervisor / equipment operator |
| ≥ 1.5% | Withdraw all personnel except those correcting the condition | Section supervisor |
| ≥ 2.0% (return air) | Immediate withdrawal of all persons except those correcting hazard, notify mine foreman's office | Section supervisor |

## Fallback positions when an escalation is delayed (in preference order)

1. **Withdraw the affected area only** — pull the crew back past the last confirmed-safe row of support and continue production in unaffected entries while awaiting engineering response.
2. **If the engineer of record is unreachable within a reasonable window and the condition isn't worsening**, hold the withdrawal in place, document the attempted contacts and timestamps, and escalate to the next level of safety management rather than making the design call independently.
3. **If the condition is actively worsening (increasing slip width, new cracking, gas trending up)**, withdraw the full section regardless of engineering contact status — a worsening condition removes the option to wait.
4. **Never resume production in the affected area on a supervisor's own extrapolation of an existing contingency** — extrapolation beyond the plan's documented scope is a plan-revision question, not a shift decision.
