# Red flags

### Pre-shift/on-shift exam book entries read identically across consecutive shifts
- **Usually means:** the examiner copied forward a prior entry instead of physically walking the route; second most likely, the section genuinely had an unusually stable few shifts, which is rare enough to verify rather than assume.
- **First question:** "Did you personally observe conditions at each listed location this shift, or is this carried forward?"
- **Data to pull:** examiner's book timestamps cross-checked against the section access/cap-lamp tracking log or mantrip dispatch record.

### Methane reading ≥1.0% CH4 at working-face equipment with no logged ventilation change
- **Usually means:** a damaged stopping, a curtain out of position, or an airflow split that no longer matches the approved ventilation plan; second most likely, a genuine gas emission increase from newly exposed strata.
- **First question:** "When was the last air-quantity survey at this split, and does today's reading match what that survey assumed?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly examination record and most recent air-quantity survey log (30 CFR §75.363/§75.364).

### Section tonnage >15% below shift target for three or more consecutive shifts with no logged downtime
- **Usually means:** undocumented stoppages (safety-related work-arounds happening off the record) or a haulage/bolting bottleneck being mis-attributed to the cutting machine.
- **First question:** "Pull cycle-time data — is the miner idle, or is the shuttle car, belt, or bolter the actual constraint?"
- **Data to pull:** dispatch/production tracking system's cycle-time log broken out by equipment.

### Bolts-installed-per-foot below the roof control plan's design density with advance rate unchanged
- **Usually means:** the bolting crew is skipping rows to keep pace with the cutting cycle — the single most common precursor to an unwarrantable-failure roof-control citation.
- **First question:** "Count bolts installed against footage advanced this shift — does the ratio match the plan's design spacing?"
- **Data to pull:** bolter operator's tally sheet against the approved roof control plan's row spacing.

### Near-miss/close-call report rate drops toward zero over several weeks on an unchanged crew and section
- **Usually means:** the crew has stopped reporting, not that conditions improved — a Heinrich's-triangle-style pyramid assumes a roughly constant reporting rate, and a falling one breaks that assumption before it breaks the incident rate.
- **First question:** ask the crew directly, off the record, whether anything's gone unreported recently.
- **Data to pull:** near-miss log trend plotted against the citation/injury trend for the same period — divergence is the tell.

### Supervisor repeatedly field-modifying an approved plan beyond its written contingency provisions
- **Usually means:** either the plan is stale for current conditions and needs a formal revision filed with the regulator, or the supervisor is exceeding certified authority on a design question that isn't theirs to make.
- **First question:** "Is this modification listed in the plan's contingency section, or is it new?"
- **Data to pull:** current MSHA-approved plan copy and its revision/approval date history.

### Fixed methane monitor calibration overdue against the plan's required interval (typically monthly)
- **Usually means:** sensor drift toward under-reading, which is the failure mode that matters — a stale calibration risks a false "clear" reading more than a false alarm.
- **First question:** "When was the last calibration certificate issued for this specific sensor?"
- **Data to pull:** calibration log/certificate for the sensor in question, not the section's monitoring system generally.

### Mill-feed grade reconciliation running below block-model estimate at matching tonnage
- **Usually means:** blast overbreak or loading discipline at the muck pile mixing waste into the ore load beyond the plan's designed dilution factor, not a resource-model error.
- **First question:** "Pull the blast design against the as-built overbreak survey for that stope or bench."
- **Data to pull:** grade-control sampling results and the overbreak survey report for the loading event in question.

### Multiple MSHA Part 50 reportable events clustering on one shift/crew pairing relative to others
- **Usually means:** a supervisor- or crew-specific pattern (rushing, fatigue, an inadequate pre-shift habit), not random variation.
- **First question:** "Is this the same supervisor/crew pairing each time these cluster?"
- **Data to pull:** Part 50 report log filtered and sorted by shift and crew assignment.

### Surface-pit berm height measured below the mid-axle height of the largest haul truck using that bench
- **Usually means:** berm grading and maintenance has fallen behind the production and haul-road wear pace.
- **First question:** "When was this specific berm last built or rebuilt to spec, and against which truck's axle height?"
- **Data to pull:** berm inspection log and the haul truck manufacturer's axle-height specification sheet.
