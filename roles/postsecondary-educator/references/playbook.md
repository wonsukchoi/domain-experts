# Postsecondary Educator — Playbook

## Syllabus grading-policy table (filled example)

| Component | Weight | Policy stated in syllabus |
|---|---|---|
| Homework (10 sets, lowest dropped) | 15% | Due 11:59pm; auto-accepted up to 24h late at -20%, not accepted after |
| Quizzes (weekly, lowest 2 dropped) | 10% | No makeups; missed quiz counts as a dropped one |
| Midterm 1 | 20% | No curve applied unless item analysis flags a specific question |
| Midterm 2 | 20% | Same as Midterm 1 |
| Final exam | 25% | Cumulative; no curve |
| Participation | 10% | Defined as: 2+ substantive discussion-board posts/week, scored 0/5/10 per week, averaged — not attendance |
| **Total** | **100%** | Extra credit: none offered individually; class-wide only if announced in a written syllabus addendum |

## Rubric (filled example — research paper, 100 pts, 4 criteria)

| Criterion | 0–9 (Beginning) | 10–17 (Developing) | 18–20 (Proficient) | 21–25 (Exemplary) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Thesis (25) | No identifiable position | States a position, no counterargument addressed | States a position, addresses one counterargument | Addresses and weighs multiple counterarguments |
| Evidence (25) | Unsupported claims | Sources present, weakly connected to claims | Sources directly support each claim | Sources support claims and are evaluated for reliability |
| Organization (25) | No discernible structure | Structure present but paragraphs don't build | Logical paragraph progression | Progression plus explicit transitions tying sections together |
| Mechanics (25) | Errors obstruct meaning | Frequent errors, meaning still clear | Occasional errors | Publication-ready |

Score by locating the paper in each row independently, then sum. Never start from a target total and distribute points backward to match it.

## Grade-appeal sequence (worked example)

1. **Log the claim.** Student states a specific ground (arithmetic error, criterion mismatch, procedural issue) — "I deserve a higher grade" alone is not yet an actionable claim; ask which criterion or which item.
2. **Blind regrade.** A second reader (or the original grader after a cooling period, without seeing the prior score) re-scores against the rubric anchors, criterion by criterion.
3. **Compare.** Original: Thesis 18, Evidence 20, Organization 22, Mechanics 18 = 78. Regrade: Thesis 20, Evidence 20, Organization 22, Mechanics 18 = 80.
4. **Resolve.** Difference traced to one criterion (Thesis: paper matched the 20/25 anchor, not the 18/25 anchor). Grade corrected 78 → 80. The other three criteria are reported as re-checked and unchanged, closing them to further negotiation on this appeal.
5. **Respond in writing**, criterion by criterion, quoting the anchor language that drove the correction — this is the artifact that closes the appeal and is what a chair would read if it escalates.

## Teaching/research/service FTE allocation (filled example, tenure-track, annual)

| Category | Contracted % | Actual hours/week (32-week term) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Teaching (2 courses/term, "2-2" load) | 40% | 16h | Prep, delivery, grading, office hours |
| Research | 40% | 16h | Blocked on calendar in two 8h blocks, protected before scheduling committee work |
| Service (1 standing committee + informal advising) | 20% | 8h | Capped at 1 standing committee per term absent a course release |
| **Total** | **100%** | **40h** | A second committee invitation this term requires either declining or requesting a course release — not absorbing it into the 40h |
