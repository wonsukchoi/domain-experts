# Postsecondary Educator — Red Flags

### Grade distribution bimodal with no curve applied
- **Usually means:** The exam tested two different skill sets unevenly, or sections/questions were graded by different people applying different standards.
- **First question:** Was this exam graded by more than one person, and did they norm on a sample together first?
- **Data to pull:** Item-level statistics broken out by grader or section.

### More than 15% of a class requesting a regrade on the same question
- **Usually means:** The question or its rubric/answer key is genuinely ambiguous, not that a sixth of the class misunderstood the material.
- **First question:** Does the answer key allow more than one defensible interpretation of the question as written?
- **Data to pull:** The question text and rubric/answer key side by side.

### A participation score below 50% with no documented log entries
- **Usually means:** Participation was never defined in numeric, event-based terms and is being assigned from memory at the end of the term.
- **First question:** What specific, dated events is this score based on?
- **Data to pull:** The attendance or discussion-post log for the term.

### Text-similarity score above 80% with no clear citation gap
- **Usually means:** Either a genuine integrity violation, or an assignment type (code, math derivations, fixed templates) known to generate high similarity scores from structural overlap, not copying.
- **First question:** Is this an assignment type known to trigger high similarity independent of misconduct?
- **Data to pull:** The similarity report's actual matched-source breakdown, not the headline percentage.

### Course-evaluation response rate below 40%
- **Usually means:** The results are not representative of the class and shouldn't be weighted heavily in a teaching review.
- **First question:** What was the response rate, and does it correlate with the grade distribution of respondents?
- **Data to pull:** Response rate alongside (if available) the grade distribution of students who responded.

### Two co-teaching graders' mean scores differ by more than one full letter grade on the same assignment
- **Usually means:** The graders are not applying the rubric consistently, not that one section is genuinely stronger.
- **First question:** Did the graders norm on a shared sample before grading independently?
- **Data to pull:** Five identical papers, blind-regraded by both graders, compared.

### Enrollment-cap increase request above 20% with no added grading support requested
- **Usually means:** The grading-hours math wasn't done before agreeing to the larger section.
- **First question:** What is the current grading-hours-per-student figure, and does it still fit contracted hours at the new size?
- **Data to pull:** Last term's actual logged grading hours.

### A grade-change request citing a verbal in-class statement with nothing in the written syllabus
- **Usually means:** Either a real gap between what was said and what was written down, or a misremembered comment — both require the same check.
- **First question:** Was a written syllabus addendum or announcement issued at the time the verbal statement was made?
- **Data to pull:** Syllabus version history and any LMS announcement or email from that session.

### Parallel sections of the same course differing by more than 10 percentage points in %A
- **Usually means:** Inconsistent rubric application across instructors, not a real ability gap between the sections' students.
- **First question:** Did instructors use a shared rubric and answer key, or independently authored ones?
- **Data to pull:** The rubric documents from each section, side by side.

### A faculty member carrying 3+ standing committees with no course release
- **Usually means:** Service commitments were accepted reactively (asked, said yes) rather than checked against the written workload agreement.
- **First question:** What does the workload agreement specify as the service expectation, in FTE terms?
- **Data to pull:** The signed annual workload/service agreement.
