# Human Services Assistant — Red Flags

### Application denials for "failure to provide verification" occur more than once in six months for the same client
- **Usually means:** the intake screening didn't flag the exact documents required for that specific program before submission, not that the client failed to comply.
- **First question:** "What documentation list did we give the client at intake, and did it match this program's actual requirement, not a generic checklist?"
- **Data to pull:** the intake checklist used, matched against the program's current documentation requirements and the denial notice's stated reason.

### A coordinated-entry prioritization score lands within 1-2 points of a tier threshold
- **Usually means:** the tool's scoring is genuinely close to the boundary, which is exactly where a validated instrument is least reliable — not that the client's need is settled either way.
- **First question:** "Has this score been reviewed in a case conference, or is it being used to auto-assign the tier?"
- **Data to pull:** the full scored instrument and any time-bound risk factors (like a hard housing deadline) that the score alone doesn't capture.

### A benefit-sanction rate for the caseload rises above the program's baseline for more than one reporting cycle
- **Usually means:** a documentation or outreach-timing gap the agency could have caught (missing verification, contact attempts only during work hours) is being reported upstream as client noncompliance.
- **First question:** "Of the sanctioned cases, how many had a documentation gap the agency should have flagged before the deadline?"
- **Data to pull:** sanction notices cross-referenced against the intake documentation checklist and outreach-attempt log for each case.

### A case note contains a diagnostic or clinical-sounding label instead of a behavioral fact
- **Usually means:** the assistant is documenting an impression rather than an observation, which is both outside scope and unusable in an appeal or audit.
- **First question:** "What did the client actually say or do that led to this note, word for word?"
- **Data to pull:** the note in question, compared against the agency's documentation-scope guidance.

### A partner organization's referral acceptance rate drops sharply for one assistant's referrals specifically
- **Usually means:** referrals are being sent before eligibility or capacity is confirmed with that specific partner, burning the relationship for the whole program, not just this assistant.
- **First question:** "Are we confirming the partner's current intake criteria and capacity before referring, or referring first and letting them screen?"
- **Data to pull:** the last 10-15 referrals to that partner, matched against the partner's stated current criteria and capacity.

### A referral is logged "made" with no enrollment confirmation past 14 days
- **Usually means:** the referral was never actually scheduled or accepted (client couldn't reach the partner, or the partner had no opening), not that the client disengaged.
- **First question:** "Do we have a confirmed enrollment date from the client or the partner, or did we just hand over contact information?"
- **Data to pull:** the referral log's confirmation field and the last outreach attempt date.

### An ABAWD client's documented countable work hours drop below 80/month
- **Usually means:** a work-requirement time limit is about to trigger (loss of SNAP after 3 months in a 36-month period without meeting the requirement, absent a waiver), not that eligibility is unaffected.
- **First question:** "Is this client in a waived area, and if not, how many of the three countable months have already elapsed?"
- **Data to pull:** the client's countable-month tracking record and the current area waiver status.

### The same client returns to request the same emergency assistance fund more than twice within a year
- **Usually means:** an underlying structural need (chronic income gap, unaffordable rent-to-income ratio) isn't being addressed by one-time emergency funds, not that the client is failing to plan.
- **First question:** "What's the client's rent-to-income ratio, and has anyone screened for a longer-term intervention beyond repeat one-time assistance?"
- **Data to pull:** the client's emergency-assistance request history and current income/rent figures.

### Group session disclosures of risk are never logged with a private follow-up note
- **Usually means:** either disclosures aren't happening (unlikely over time) or they're being handled informally without triggering the mandated-reporting protocol, which is a compliance gap, not a judgment call.
- **First question:** "Walk me through the last group disclosure and exactly what happened after the session ended."
- **Data to pull:** group session notes and the mandated-reporting log for the same date range.
