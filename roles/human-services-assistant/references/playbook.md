# Human Services Assistant — Playbook

## Benefits intake screening sequence (filled example)

| Step | Question / check | Client response | Flagged? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Program mandate | "What brought you in today?" | "I need a bus pass for job interviews" | Presenting request only — screen broader |
| Household composition | "Who lives with you, and who's on your household budget?" | 2 adults, 1 child, currently staying with an aunt | Housing instability |
| Income | "What's your household's gross monthly income, and from what source?" | $2,950/month, one part-time job | Near SNAP gross-income threshold — run full calculation |
| Categorical eligibility trigger | "Has anyone in the household received a TANF-funded service, including through this agency, in the current contact?" | Yes — today's intake/referral service is TANF-funded | BBCE applies — recheck gross test at 200% FPL, not 130% |
| Documentation on hand | "Do you have a pay stub or income verification with you today?" | Has one pay stub, no ID for the child | Missing document flag — note before submission, don't deny |
| Safety | "Is your current living situation safe and stable for the next 30 days?" | "For now, but my aunt's said we can't stay past the month" | Time-bound housing risk — add to shelter/RRH screening queue |

**Ranking pass (confirm with client, don't assume):** client names transportation as most urgent (job interviews this week), income/benefits second, housing third even though housing is the higher structural risk — address in stated order, but don't let the housing deadline (aunt's month-end limit) slip off the follow-up date.

## SNAP eligibility calculation (filled example, household of 3)

| Test | Threshold (2024 illustrative, 48 contiguous states) | Household figure | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Base gross income test | 130% FPL ≈ $2,839/month | $2,950 | Fails by $111 |
| BBCE gross income test (if triggered) | 200% FPL ≈ $4,368/month | $2,950 | Passes, $1,418 margin |
| Net income test | 100% FPL ≈ $2,184/month | $2,950 − $590 (20% earned-income deduction) − $198 (standard deduction) = $2,162 | Passes, $22 margin |

**Rule:** always check whether a BBCE-triggering service applies before reporting a gross-income denial — the base 130% test is not automatically the binding one. Verify current-year FPL figures and the state's specific BBCE policy before finalizing; the figures above are an illustrative calculation, not a lookup table.

## Coordinated-entry prioritization scoring (VI-SPDAT-style, filled example)

| Domain | Client response | Points |
|---|---|---|
| History of housing/homelessness | Doubled up, no prior shelter stay | 1 |
| Risks (i.e. legal, health, victimization) | None disclosed | 0 |
| Socialization and daily functioning | Employed part-time, manages own appointments | 0 |
| Wellness (physical/behavioral health) | No disclosed condition affecting housing stability | 0 |
| Family/household | Intact family unit, 1 minor child | 1 |
| **Total (illustrative single-family scale)** | | **2** |

**Threshold interpretation (program-specific, confirm locally):** 0–3 typically routes to prevention/diversion conversation rather than shelter or permanent supportive housing; 4–7 to rapid rehousing; 8+ to permanent supportive housing review. A score of 2 with a hard 30-day housing deadline is a boundary case for the "no intervention needed" tier — flag for supervisor case conference rather than closing the housing domain on score alone.

## Group facilitation session structure (filled example — life-skills psychoeducation group)

1. **Opening (5 min):** restate ground rules aloud every session (confidentiality limits, no cross-talk, what happens if someone discloses risk) — don't assume returning members remember them.
2. **Check-in (10 min):** one-sentence round from each member; redirect anything longer than a sentence to "hold that, we'll come back to it" to protect time for the full group.
3. **Content (20–25 min):** deliver the session's curriculum topic (e.g., budgeting basics, tenant rights); use concrete scenarios over lecture.
4. **Discussion (15 min):** open floor, facilitator manages airtime so no single member dominates past roughly two turns without another member speaking.
5. **Disclosure handling (as needed):** if a member discloses a safety risk, acknowledge briefly without processing it publicly ("thank you for sharing that — let's talk one-on-one right after group"), continue the session, then follow the mandated-reporting protocol immediately after.
6. **Close (5 min):** one takeaway per member, confirm next session date/topic.
7. **Post-session (same day):** log attendance, note any disclosures requiring escalation (with the private follow-up outcome), and flag any member who missed two consecutive sessions for outreach.

## Case-aide documentation — before/after example

**Impression-based note (out of scope, avoid):** "Client seemed unmotivated about the job search and possibly depressed."

**Factual, in-scope note:** "Client reported applying to 3 jobs this week, down from a stated goal of 5. Client reported feeling 'hopeless most days' and difficulty sleeping over the past two weeks. Referred to supervising social worker for behavioral-health screening per scope-of-practice protocol; client agreed to follow-up call within 48 hours."

**Why it matters:** the first note offers a diagnostic impression the assistant isn't authorized to make and gives an appeals reviewer or auditor nothing verifiable to act on. The second documents observable facts, routes the behavioral-health question to the person authorized to assess it, and creates a verifiable record of the escalation.
