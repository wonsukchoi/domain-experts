# Red flags

### Fabric yardage ordered from a simple area/piece-count calculation for a patterned fabric with a stated repeat
- **Usually means:** the order will fall short once pattern-matching waste across cut pieces is accounted for.
- **First question:** has the pattern repeat length actually been factored into the cut plan?
- **Data to pull:** the cut plan compared against the fabric's stated repeat specification.

### Foam selected by hand-feel or showroom sample alone with no density (lb/ft³) or ILD spec checked
- **Usually means:** the cushion may sag prematurely despite feeling right at the point of selection.
- **First question:** what does the foam's actual density and ILD spec sheet show?
- **Data to pull:** the foam specification sheet (density, ILD) compared against what was actually selected.

### Suspension system substituted from what was quoted or sampled to the customer, without disclosure
- **Usually means:** a sag-resistance and durability mismatch against the customer's actual expectation.
- **First question:** does the installed suspension type match what was originally quoted?
- **Data to pull:** the quoted suspension type compared against what's actually being installed.

### Nap-fabric panels cut without checking direction consistency against a reference swatch
- **Usually means:** a visible color or sheen mismatch across panels once installed, even from the same fabric roll, once viewed under changing light.
- **First question:** does the nap direction marked on each cut piece match the reference swatch?
- **Data to pull:** nap direction markings on each cut piece compared against the reference swatch.

### Tacking or stapling sequence starts at a corner or edge rather than the center of the panel
- **Usually means:** uneven fabric tension that can produce wrinkles or stress-point sag over time.
- **First question:** what sequence was actually used to tack this panel?
- **Data to pull:** the tacking sequence used compared against the standard center-out sequence for this panel type.

### Frame or suspension not inspected or repaired before new upholstery is applied over it
- **Usually means:** underlying structural issues (broken springs, cracked frame joints) get covered rather than fixed, and resurface quickly.
- **First question:** was the frame and suspension actually inspected before reupholstery began?
- **Data to pull:** frame/suspension inspection notes recorded before the job started.

### Finished piece shows a visible pattern mismatch across a seam
- **Usually means:** the cut plan didn't actually account for repeat alignment at that specific seam, not a fabric manufacturing defect.
- **First question:** what does the cut plan's repeat alignment marking show at that specific seam location?
- **Data to pull:** the cut plan's repeat alignment notes at the mismatched seam.
