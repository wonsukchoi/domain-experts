# Playbook

## Patterned-fabric yardage calculation (filled example)

Sofa reupholstery: 3 seat cushions, top + bottom panels each (6 panels total). Panel finished size: 24" × 26". Fabric repeat: 27" vertical. Bolt width: 54".

| Step | Calculation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Naive area calculation | 6 panels × 24" × 26" = 3,744 sq in | 3,744 sq in |
| Naive yardage (÷ 54" bolt width) | 3,744 / 54 = 69.33 linear inches | 1.925 yards |
| Repeat-driven length per panel | Panel (26") doesn't divide evenly into 27" repeat — each panel consumes one full repeat length | 27" per panel |
| Repeat-driven total length | 6 panels × 27" | 162" = 4.5 yards |
| Difference vs. naive estimate | 4.5 / 1.925 | 2.34× more fabric actually needed |
| Recommended order (with margin) | 4.5 yd + ~0.5 yd cutting/matching margin | 5 yards |

**Comparison — plain (non-patterned) fabric, same job:** Since there's no repeat to align, the naive area-based calculation (1.925 yd, rounded up with standard cutting margin to ~2.25 yd) is accurate — the repeat-driven method only applies when a stated pattern repeat exists.

## Foam density/ILD selection table (filled example)

| Application | Recommended density | Recommended ILD | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily-use sofa seat cushion | 1.8–2.0 lb/ft³ | 30–35 | Higher density for durability under frequent, heavy use |
| Occasional-use accent chair | 1.5–1.8 lb/ft³ | 25–30 | Slightly softer feel acceptable given lower use frequency |
| Firm-back support cushion | 2.0+ lb/ft³ | 40–45 | High density and high ILD both needed for structural back support |
| Decorative throw pillow (non-structural) | 1.2–1.5 lb/ft³ | 20–25 | Durability less critical; softness/appearance prioritized |

**Common mismatch to catch:** A showroom sample foam might feel similarly firm at 1.2 lb/ft³, 30 ILD as a 1.8 lb/ft³, 30 ILD option — same ILD, very different density and therefore very different expected lifespan under daily use. Always check both numbers, not just the felt firmness.

## Suspension substitution disclosure log (filled example)

| Job | Quoted/sampled suspension | Actually installed | Disclosed to customer? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Job 114 (sofa) | 8-way hand-tied | 8-way hand-tied | N/A — matches quote |
| Job 115 (loveseat) | 8-way hand-tied | Sinuous springs (material shortage) | **Must disclose** — meaningfully lower sag resistance than what was originally quoted |
| Job 116 (chair) | Drop-in coil unit | Drop-in coil unit | N/A — matches quote |

Job 115's substitution changes the expected durability of the piece; proceeding without disclosing it to the customer misrepresents what they're actually receiving relative to what they were quoted.
