# Bailiff — Red Flags

### In-custody defendant placed in visible restraints with no written judicial finding on file
- **Usually means:** the restraint decision defaulted to transport agency habit rather than a case-specific request to the court.
- **First question:** what specific escape or courtroom-safety factor, tied to this defendant, is on the record?
- **Data to pull:** the restraint memo (if any), the judge's on-record ruling, the defendant's custody incident history.

### Jury note log has a gap — a note referenced in trial minutes with no matching logged entry
- **Usually means:** a note was handled verbally or informally instead of through the sealed hand-delivery process.
- **First question:** who received the note from the jury room, and what happened between receipt and the judge seeing it?
- **Data to pull:** trial minute record, jury note log, courtroom clerk's docket entries for that time window.

### Screening checkpoint line extends past the controlled perimeter at session start
- **Usually means:** lane count wasn't re-run against actual attendance for a high-interest session (verdict, sentencing, high-profile testimony).
- **First question:** when was the throughput worksheet last recalculated, and against what attendance estimate?
- **Data to pull:** daily attendance log, lane staffing schedule, the last throughput calculation on file.

### Exhibit hand-off with no signature in the chain-of-custody log
- **Usually means:** the hand-off happened verbally during a fast-moving session and was never recorded, or was recorded after the fact from memory.
- **First question:** who physically held this exhibit between the last logged entry and now?
- **Data to pull:** chain-of-custody log, courtroom clerk's exhibit list, any video/audio covering the relevant time window.

### Courtroom officer headcount for a case with no documented threat factor exceeds the standard two-officer detail
- **Usually means:** staffing was set by roster availability rather than a stated, case-specific rationale — the exposure *Holbrook v. Flynn* flagged as the line between permissible and prejudicial.
- **First question:** what specific fact in this case justifies staffing above the standard detail?
- **Data to pull:** the security plan's stated rationale, defendant/case threat history, any prior incident reports.

### Disruptive-defendant incident report shows physical restraint applied before a documented verbal warning
- **Usually means:** the bailiff skipped the *Illinois v. Allen* warning-then-removal sequence, either from time pressure or a genuine immediate-safety situation not reflected in the writeup.
- **First question:** was there a specific, immediate physical threat that made a warning-first sequence unsafe, and is that documented?
- **Data to pull:** courtroom incident report, judge's on-record statements at the time, any courtroom audio/video.

### Sequestered jury's daily transport or lodging log missing a headcount at a transition point
- **Usually means:** roll call was treated as a formality on a routine day rather than a required check at every transition.
- **First question:** which transition is missing, and can it be reconstructed from another record (van manifest, hotel access log)?
- **Data to pull:** sequestration log, transport manifest, hotel access records for the missing window.

### Restraint status and disruption-response actions recorded together in a single incident narrative with no separation
- **Usually means:** the report writer conflated two independent decisions (why the restraint level is what it is, and why the disruption was handled the way it was) into one narrative.
- **First question:** can you state, separately, what the restraint status was before the incident and what specifically changed, if anything?
- **Data to pull:** pre-trial restraint memo, the incident report itself, any post-incident restraint-status update.

### Bailiff reports "confirming" or reacting to jury note content in front of the jury
- **Usually means:** a lapse in the no-reaction rule, often under the impression that a minor logistics note ("what time is lunch") doesn't carry the same restriction as a substantive one.
- **First question:** did any verbal or nonverbal reaction happen before the judge's response was delivered?
- **Data to pull:** jury note log, any observer accounts (clerk, courtroom deputy), courtroom video if available.

### Family/support-witness separation for a protective-order or cooperating-witness case decided the morning of trial
- **Usually means:** the separate entrance/seating plan wasn't built into the pre-trial security plan and is being improvised under time pressure.
- **First question:** was this flagged in the case file before today, and if so, why wasn't it in the security plan?
- **Data to pull:** case file protective-order/witness-safety flags, the security plan filed before trial.
