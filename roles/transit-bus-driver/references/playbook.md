# Playbook

Filled sequences and templates for the checks a transit/intercity bus driver runs every shift. Numbers below are typical operating benchmarks (FMCSA, DOT ADA rule, SAE J2249/WC19, and common agency practice) — an individual carrier's own procedures and CBA control where they differ.

## 1. Pre-trip / lift cycle-test checklist

Run before pulling out of the yard, every shift, on every accessible vehicle:

| Item | Check | Pass criterion | If failed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lift/ramp deploy | Full deploy cycle, no load | Deploys fully in ≤10 sec, no grinding/stall | Hold bus, log defect, call for substitute |
| Lift/ramp stow | Full stow cycle | Stows fully, interlock engages | Hold bus, log defect |
| Kneeling function | Kneel and return to ride height | Full range, no more than ~4 sec each way | Note for maintenance; hold only if it won't return to ride height |
| Securement anchors (x4 minimum stations) | Visual + tug test on each strap/anchor | No fraying, anchor doesn't shift under a firm pull | Tag station out of service, log which station |
| Mirrors | 8-point sequence (below) from driver's seat | All 8 zones visible with normal seat position | Adjust before departure; do not defer |
| Interior/exterior lighting, stop-request signage, PA/announcement system | Functional check | All functional | Log defect; PA/announcement failure is a hold on ADA-designated routes (visual + audio stop announcements required) |

A failed lift/ramp cycle test is a **hold**, logged on the defect card before the bus leaves the yard — not a note for the next scheduled inspection. Running an accessible route with a known-defective lift is an ADA out-of-service condition, not an operational judgment call.

## 2. Wheelchair securement sequence (timed)

| Step | Action | Typical time |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Deploy ramp/lift | 20 sec |
| 2 | Position wheelchair in securement station, brakes set | 15 sec |
| 3 | Attach 4-point tie-down: two front straps ~30–45° from floor, two rear straps ~30–60° from floor, each anchor rated ~2,500 lbf (SAE J2249/WC19) | 40 sec |
| 4 | Lap belt across pelvis (not abdomen) + separate shoulder belt anchored above the shoulder | 15 sec |
| 5 | Stow ramp/lift, confirm interlock | 10 sec |
| **Total** | | **~100 sec (1:40)** |

If a rider declines the lap/shoulder belt: log it as a documented refusal on the trip incident log (date, time, stop, rider description if repeat rider) — the 4-point tie-down alone is still applied; the belt refusal is the rider's choice to record, not the driver's step to skip.

## 3. 8-point mirror / blind-spot sequence

Run before releasing the brake at every stop, in this order:

1. Interior mirror (cabin/aisle status — standees, seated riders, any disturbance).
2. Left exterior (West Coast) mirror — full length of the bus.
3. Right exterior mirror — full length of the bus.
4. Left convex/crossover mirror — inside-of-turn blind zone.
5. Right convex/crossover mirror — curb-side door blind zone.
6. **Zone directly ahead of the bus, through the windshield, low** — the blind area a low-mounted bus front hides for the full width of the vehicle; a small child, wheelchair user, or dropped item can sit here undetected.
7. Door-side mirror/camera at point of closing (confirm clear before closing).
8. Final scan of the intended path of travel before releasing the brake.

Reduce target speed *before* a turn on a bus with a long rear overhang (typically ~7–8 ft behind the rear axle on a 40-ft coach) — the tail swings opposite the turn direction in the first second, then whips through the arc, putting an inside-of-turn pedestrian or cyclist at risk in a way a shorter vehicle doesn't.

## 4. De-escalation response ladder (CPI-style)

| Rider behavior level | Signs | Driver response |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Anxiety | Raised voice, pacing, muttering | Supportive: calm tone, name the concern back, offer a concrete option ("next stop is in two minutes") |
| 2. Defensive | Argumentative, refusing instruction, testing limits | Directive: short, clear, one-instruction statements ("please take a seat" — not a debate) |
| 3. Risk behavior | Threats, blocking the aisle, physical intimidation | Limit-setting + radio call: state the boundary once, radio dispatch/police immediately, do not escalate voice or approach |
| 4. Tension reduction | Behavior de-escalating on its own after intervention | Therapeutic rapport: neutral tone, no lecturing, resume normal operation |

Never meet a raised voice with a raised voice — move exactly one rung of response per rung of behavior. Call dispatch/police at the first credible threat (level 3), not after contact occurs.

## 5. Delay-code radio call (template)

> "[Dispatch], [Route] Run [#]. Currently [X] minutes down at [location], account of [one delay-reason code: mechanical / ADA securement / weather / disturbance / traffic incident]. [Recovery status: terminal recovery intact / need additional resource]. Will advise if that changes."

Keep to one delay-reason code per call; a compound cause gets a follow-up call once resolved, not a run-on explanation.

## 6. Duty-day worksheet (example, intercity motorcoach under FMCSA §395.5)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| On-duty window start | 05:00 |
| Driving segments (sum) | 9 hr 15 min (limit 10 hr) |
| On-duty, not driving (pre-trip, fueling, passenger stops) | 3 hr 20 min |
| Total on-duty time | 12 hr 35 min (limit 15 hr on-duty window) |
| Remaining driving time available | 45 min |
| Remaining on-duty window | 2 hr 25 min |
| 7-day rolling total | 58 hr 10 min (limit 60 hr/7 days) |

For local fixed-route transit operators not on FMCSA Part 395, replace this table with the agency's own fatigue-management/run-cutting limits under its FTA-required Public Transportation Agency Safety Plan (49 CFR Part 673) and the applicable union contract — the table's shape (on-duty window, driving cap, rolling total) is the same idea, but the actual numbers are agency-specific and control over the FMCSA figures above.
