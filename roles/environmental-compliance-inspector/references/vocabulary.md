### NPDES permit
National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System permit, issued under the Clean Water Act, setting numeric and narrative limits on what a facility may discharge to surface waters.
**In use:** "The facility's NPDES permit caps BOD at 30 mg/L monthly average."
**Common misuse:** Citing a discharge concentration as a violation without checking it against the specific permit's own limits, which vary facility to facility.

### Discharge monitoring report (DMR)
The periodic report a permitted facility submits to the regulator, self-reporting its actual discharge monitoring results against permit limits.
**In use:** "March's DMR shows a self-reported exceedance on BOD."
**Common misuse:** Treating a DMR exceedance as automatically worse than a facility with no DMR exceedances, without accounting for differences in monitoring rigor.

### Title V permit
An operating permit under the Clean Air Act for major sources of air pollution, consolidating all applicable air emissions requirements into a single enforceable document.
**In use:** "This facility's Title V permit sets the emissions limit we're checking today's stack test against."
**Common misuse:** Assuming Title V requirements are uniform across all facilities rather than checking the specific permit's negotiated conditions.

### RCRA
The Resource Conservation and Recovery Act, the federal framework governing hazardous waste generation, transport, and disposal (the "cradle to grave" system).
**In use:** "RCRA generator status determines which recordkeeping rules apply to this facility this month."
**Common misuse:** Treating RCRA compliance as a one-time facility certification rather than an ongoing, monthly-generation-based determination.

### Hazardous waste generator status (VSQG/SQG/LQG)
The RCRA classification of a facility based on the amount of hazardous waste it generates per month — very small quantity generator, small quantity generator, or large quantity generator — each with different recordkeeping and manifest obligations.
**In use:** "Their generation jumped this quarter — they've moved from SQG to LQG and now need to follow the stricter manifest and contingency-plan rules."
**Common misuse:** Applying a facility's historical or assumed generator category instead of checking actual generation records for the specific period being inspected.

### Manifest (hazardous waste)
The tracking document required to accompany hazardous waste from the point of generation through transport to final disposal, creating a documented chain of custody.
**In use:** "The manifest doesn't match the waste codes logged in the generation record — that's a discrepancy worth flagging."
**Common misuse:** Treating manifest paperwork as a formality rather than the actual legal record establishing proper waste handling.

### Notice of Violation (NOV)
A formal enforcement document citing a specific violation of a permit or regulation, typically requiring a response and compliance schedule within a stated deadline.
**In use:** "This exceedance is confirmed and verified — it gets an NOV with a 60-day compliance schedule, not an informal note."
**Common misuse:** Treating an NOV as an informal warning with no real deadline or enforcement consequence attached.

### Economic benefit (of noncompliance)
The financial advantage a facility gained by delaying or avoiding a required compliance action, calculated using time-value-of-money methods on the avoided or delayed cost.
**In use:** "Economic benefit here is the avoided cost of capital on the delayed upgrade, not the full $85,000 project cost."
**Common misuse:** Using the full capital or compliance cost as the economic benefit figure instead of the time-value-adjusted avoided cost specific to the delay period.

### Gravity (penalty component)
The portion of a penalty reflecting the severity, duration, and environmental sensitivity of a violation, separate from any economic benefit calculation.
**In use:** "Gravity component landed at $8,000 given the 16.7% exceedance and moderate-sensitivity receiving water."
**Common misuse:** Treating gravity as the entire penalty calculation without a separate economic-benefit component.

### Multimedia inspection
An inspection covering more than one environmental permit category (air, water, hazardous waste) at a single facility in one visit, used when a facility's process suggests cross-media compliance risk.
**In use:** "The scrubber's wastewater byproduct means this needs a multimedia inspection, not just an air-permit check."
**Common misuse:** Inspecting only the permit medium that triggered the visit, missing a violation created by how that medium's compliance strategy shifts burden to another.

### Mass loading
The total quantity of a pollutant discharged per unit time (commonly pounds per day), calculated from concentration and flow rate, as distinct from concentration alone.
**In use:** "Concentration was under some daily limits, but mass loading at this flow rate still exceeds the permit's lbs/day cap."
**Common misuse:** Comparing only concentration (mg/L) against a permit limit when the permit's actual binding limit is a mass-loading (lbs/day) cap that also depends on flow rate.

### Compliance schedule
The set of specific deadlines and required actions a facility must meet to return to compliance, typically attached to a Notice of Violation.
**In use:** "Compliance schedule gives them 60 days to complete the aeration upgrade and demonstrate results."
**Common misuse:** Treating the compliance schedule as a suggestion rather than an enforceable deadline with consequences for missing it.
