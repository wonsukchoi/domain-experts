# Environmental Compliance Inspector — Playbook

## Mass-loading calculation and permit comparison (filled example)

| Step | Value |
|---|---|
| Self-reported concentration (March DMR average) | 42 mg/L |
| Average discharge flow | 0.5 MGD |
| Mass loading = concentration × flow × 8.34 | 42 × 0.5 × 8.34 = 175.1 lbs/day |
| Permit limit | 150 lbs/day |
| **Result** | 175.1 lbs/day exceeds 150 lbs/day by 25.1 lbs/day (16.7% over) |

**Use:** Always convert concentration to mass loading when the permit's binding limit is expressed as lbs/day — a concentration that looks acceptable on its own can still violate the mass-loading cap at higher flow rates, and vice versa.

## Penalty calculation worksheet (filled example)

| Component | Calculation | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| Economic benefit — avoided cost of capital | $85,000 delayed upgrade × 8%/yr × 0.5 yr delay | $3,400 |
| Economic benefit — deferred O&M costs | Estimated | $2,000 |
| **Total economic benefit** | | **$5,400** |
| Gravity component | Per penalty matrix: 16.7% over limit, single-month duration, moderate-sensitivity receiving water | $8,000 |
| **Total penalty** | Economic benefit + gravity | **$13,400** |

**Use:** Calculate and present both components separately in the enforcement record — a penalty that can't show its economic-benefit and gravity components separately is harder to defend if challenged.

## Hazardous waste generator status thresholds (monthly generation)

| Category | Threshold (hazardous waste generated per calendar month) | Key obligations |
|---|---|---|
| Very Small Quantity Generator (VSQG) | Under 100 kg/month | Minimal recordkeeping; must still ensure proper disposal |
| Small Quantity Generator (SQG) | 100 kg to under 1,000 kg/month | Manifest system, 180-day (or 270-day with distance) accumulation limit, basic contingency plan |
| Large Quantity Generator (LQG) | 1,000 kg/month or more (or over 1 kg/month of acutely hazardous waste) | Full manifest system, 90-day accumulation limit, detailed contingency plan, biennial reporting |

**Use:** Check the current inspection period's actual generation records against these thresholds — a facility can move between categories month to month based on production volume, and the applicable obligations follow the current month's actual generation, not a prior classification.

## Multimedia inspection scoping checklist

1. Does the facility's process generate a byproduct stream (e.g., air pollution control scrubber wastewater, wastewater treatment sludge) that crosses into a different permit medium?
2. Are all of the facility's active permits (air, water, hazardous waste) current and available for review before the visit?
3. Has a prior single-medium inspection at this facility missed a finding later discovered under a different medium?
4. If any of the above apply, scope the inspection to cover all relevant media in a single visit rather than scheduling separate follow-up inspections.

## Notice of Violation vs. informal correction decision table

| Finding type | Response |
|---|---|
| Numeric permit-limit exceedance (verified) | Notice of Violation with compliance schedule |
| Missed required report or monitoring | Notice of Violation |
| Hazardous waste manifest violation | Notice of Violation |
| Administrative/paperwork issue with no release or exposure (e.g., outdated contact info on file) | Informal correction |

**Use:** Default to NOV whenever the finding involves an actual numeric exceedance, missed report, or manifest violation — reserve informal correction strictly for administrative issues with no environmental release or exposure component.

## Notice of Violation — filled example

> **Notice of Violation — NPDES Permit [ref]**
> Violation: BOD mass loading of 175.1 lbs/day (March monthly average) exceeds the permitted limit of 150 lbs/day by 25.1 lbs/day (16.7%).
> Verification: Sampling and laboratory chain-of-custody reviewed; no QA/QC discrepancy identified.
> Penalty: Economic benefit $5,400 + gravity component $8,000 = **$13,400 total penalty**.
> **Compliance schedule: Facility must complete aeration system upgrade and demonstrate return to permitted limits within 60 days of this notice.**
