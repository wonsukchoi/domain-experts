### A discharge or emissions sample exceeds the permit limit by a small margin (under ~10%)
- **Usually means:** Could be a genuine minor exceedance, or could be sampling/instrument variability — worth checking before treating as a confirmed violation.
- **First question:** Was the sampling method and lab QA/QC (calibration, chain of custody, holding time) verified for this specific sample?
- **Data to pull:** Lab chain-of-custody record, instrument calibration log, sampling method documentation.

### A facility's self-reported exceedance count is unusually high compared to similar facilities
- **Usually means:** More often reflects more rigorous/frequent monitoring than a similar facility with looser monitoring, not necessarily worse actual performance — needs direct comparison of monitoring frequency, not just exceedance counts.
- **First question:** What is this facility's monitoring frequency and method compared to peer facilities, and does that explain the higher reported count?
- **Data to pull:** Monitoring frequency requirements per permit, peer facility exceedance/monitoring comparison if available.

### Hazardous waste generator status hasn't been reassessed since a change in production volume
- **Usually means:** The facility may have shifted categories (e.g., from small quantity generator to large quantity generator) without updating its recordkeeping and manifest practices accordingly.
- **First question:** What is the facility's actual monthly hazardous waste generation for the current inspection period, and does it match the category currently being used for compliance?
- **Data to pull:** Current month's waste generation log, manifest records, prior generator status classification on file.

### A penalty calculation includes only a gravity component with no economic-benefit component
- **Usually means:** The facility retains the financial benefit of having delayed or avoided compliance, undermining the deterrent purpose of the penalty.
- **First question:** What cost did the facility avoid or delay by not complying on time, and has that been calculated separately from the severity-based gravity amount?
- **Data to pull:** Facility's capital/operating expenditure records related to the required compliance action, timeline of the delay.

### A facility with multiple permit types (air, water, waste) is inspected under only one medium
- **Usually means:** A cross-media compliance strategy — where fixing one permit's compliance shifts burden to another medium (e.g., an air scrubber creating a wastewater discharge) — could be missed entirely.
- **First question:** Does this facility's process generate a byproduct stream that crosses into a different permit medium than the one currently being inspected?
- **Data to pull:** Facility process flow diagram, all active permits (air, water, waste) for this facility.

### A Notice of Violation's compliance schedule deadline has passed with no facility response
- **Usually means:** Either the facility missed the deadline and the matter should escalate to formal enforcement, or a response was sent but not logged/tracked.
- **First question:** Has the facility submitted any compliance schedule update or extension request, logged or not?
- **Data to pull:** NOV issuance date and stated deadline, facility correspondence log.

### A facility requests treating a numeric permit exceedance as an informal correction rather than an NOV
- **Usually means:** A misunderstanding (or an attempt to minimize) about which findings qualify for informal correction versus formal notice — numeric exceedances and hazardous waste manifest violations are generally NOV-level by default.
- **First question:** Does this specific finding involve a numeric permit-limit exceedance, missed required report, or manifest violation, or is it purely an administrative/paperwork issue?
- **Data to pull:** The specific permit condition violated, classification criteria for NOV vs. informal correction.

### An economic-benefit calculation uses the full capital cost of a delayed upgrade rather than the time-value-adjusted avoided cost
- **Usually means:** The penalty may overstate the actual financial benefit the facility gained, since the facility didn't avoid the expense entirely, only delayed it.
- **First question:** What discount/interest rate and delay period were used in the economic-benefit calculation, and is the full capital cost or only the avoided cost of capital being penalized?
- **Data to pull:** Delay period, applicable interest/discount rate, capital cost of the required upgrade.
