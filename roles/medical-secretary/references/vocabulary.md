# Medical Secretary — Vocabulary

### Prior authorization
An insurer's pre-approval requirement before certain procedures, medications, or referrals are covered.
**In use:** "This MRI needs a prior auth before we can schedule it — insurer's SLA is 5 business days, but I'm planning for the worst case."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as a formality to submit "whenever," rather than a deadline-driven process that has to be timed backward from the appointment.

### Minimum necessary standard
The HIPAA Privacy Rule principle that only the minimum information needed to accomplish the purpose of a records request should be used or disclosed.
**In use:** "The authorization only covers the cardiology visits from this year — I'm not pulling the psych notes even though they're in the same chart."
**Common misuse:** Assuming that because a requestor is entitled to some records, they're entitled to the whole chart.

### Eligibility verification
Confirming a patient's insurance coverage is active and covers a specific service before the visit.
**In use:** "This was booked six weeks out, so I'm re-verifying eligibility 48 hours before instead of trusting the original check."
**Common misuse:** Treating a single verification at booking time as permanent, ignoring that coverage can lapse or change.

### Psychotherapy notes (HIPAA-defined)
A specific, separately protected category of mental-health provider notes under HIPAA, requiring distinct patient authorization even when other records are released.
**In use:** "General records are authorized, but psychotherapy notes need their own signed release — I'm holding those back."
**Common misuse:** Bundling psychotherapy notes into a general chart release because they're stored in the same system.

### Superbill
An itemized form listing services rendered, diagnosis codes, and procedure codes, used to generate a claim.
**In use:** "The superbill from today's visit doesn't match what was scheduled — flagging it before it goes to billing."
**Common misuse:** Assuming the superbill is just a receipt rather than the document billing accuracy depends on.

### CPT code
Current Procedural Terminology — a standardized code identifying a specific medical service or procedure, used for billing.
**In use:** "I'm not assigning the CPT code myself — that's the coder's call — but I flagged that the visit type doesn't match what was ordered."
**Common misuse:** A non-coder guessing at or assigning a CPT code to move a visit through scheduling, rather than escalating an ambiguity.

### ICD-10 code
International Classification of Diseases, 10th revision — the standardized diagnosis-code system used alongside CPT codes for billing and clinical documentation.
**In use:** "The referral says I10, but the patient's stated reason for visit doesn't match — confirming with the clinician before I book it as a follow-up."
**Common misuse:** Treating ICD-10 codes as scheduling-irrelevant, when a mismatch between diagnosis code and visit type routinely causes claim denials.

### Warm handoff
A direct, real-time transfer of information between care team members or across care settings, rather than a passive document transfer.
**In use:** "I called the imaging center directly to confirm they received the auth, instead of just faxing it and assuming it landed."
**Common misuse:** Treating a fax or portal upload as equivalent to confirmed receipt.

### Buffer slot
A scheduling block deliberately held open for same-day urgent add-ons rather than filled with routine bookings.
**In use:** "The buffer slot is already used for today — this next urgent request needs to bump a non-urgent same-day check-in to a phone follow-up instead."
**Common misuse:** Filling buffer slots with routine requests early in the day, leaving no capacity when a genuine urgent case arrives.

### Authorization scope
The specific date range, record types, and purpose defined in a signed HIPAA release-of-information authorization.
**In use:** "The scope says 2023 visits only — I'm not including this year's notes even though they'd be relevant."
**Common misuse:** Releasing records outside the authorized scope because they seem helpful or related.

### Turnaround time (TAT)
The insurer's stated processing time for a prior-authorization request, from submission to decision.
**In use:** "Their published TAT is 5 days, but I'm planning for the additional-info-request scenario, which adds another 5."
**Common misuse:** Treating the published TAT as the guaranteed worst-case timeline rather than the best-case one.

### Clearinghouse
A third-party intermediary that transmits and validates insurance-eligibility and claims data between a practice and payers.
**In use:** "The clearinghouse flagged this plan as inactive as of last month — re-verifying directly with the payer before we schedule."
**Common misuse:** Assuming clearinghouse data is always current, when there can be a lag between a plan change and the clearinghouse's records updating.
