# Playbook: Arrival, Gap-Filling, Escalation, and Handoff

Filled process for the four recurring executions of the role. Reference context throughout: a one-day secondary assignment, five periods, unless noted.

## 1. Arrival Verification Sequence (filled)

Run this before touching any lesson material, in order — skipping steps because "it's just one day" is exactly when the skipped step matters.

| Step | Action | What to check it against |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Report to the front office, not the classroom | Confirm the assigned room/period/subject matches what the office actually has posted for today — dispatch-platform assignments occasionally list a stale room or period |
| 2 | Collect the sub folder | Confirm it's this teacher's, this room's, current folder — some rooms are shared by two teachers on different periods |
| 3 | Read safety information first | Allergy/medical alert sheet, fire/lockdown evacuation route and rally point, any accommodation flags for each period — before reading a single lesson page |
| 4 | Cross-check the plan's stated date/day against today's actual calendar date and bell schedule | A plan labeled for the wrong day is a signal to confirm with the office before teaching from it, not a stale label to ignore |
| 5 | Locate the seating chart and roster | If neither is provided and the office can't produce one within ~10 minutes of the period starting, default to a self-made numbered seating record for that period, since assigning consequences later requires knowing who sat where |
| 6 | Note the building's specific sign-in/attendance procedure | Procedures differ by building even within the same district — don't assume the last building's routine applies here |

## 2. Filling a Lesson-Plan Gap (decision table)

Compute the gap in minutes first; don't estimate by feel.

| Gap size (minutes remaining after usable plan content) | Default action |
|---|---|
| 0–5 min | Let the period run out on the completed work; no filler needed |
| 5–15 min | Silent reading or a short review of material already covered that day (content-neutral, no new grading) |
| 15–30 min | Pre-vetted department/grade-level "sponge" packet (a standing independent-practice worksheet not tied to today's specific lesson) |
| 30+ min | Combine a sponge packet with silent reading in sequence, and flag the gap to the office — a gap this large usually means a missing plan, not a short one, and the office may have a fuller backup |
| Any size | Never: open/free time, a movie unrelated to the curriculum, or letting early finishers self-direct with no assigned task |

**Worked instance:** Period 3, 47-minute period, usable plan content covers only 15 minutes (a warm-up meant for a different day). Gap = 47 − 15 = 32 minutes → falls in the "30+" band: math department's 20-minute standing ratio-review worksheet (20 min) + silent reading for the remainder (32 − 20 = 12 min) = 32 minutes filled, 15 + 20 + 12 = 47 minutes accounted for the full period.

## 3. Compressed Escalation Ladder

Same least-to-most-restrictive logic as a regular teacher's ladder, but the substitute defaults to moving up a rung sooner, since there's no next-day relationship to repair a delayed response with.

| Rung | Response | Move to next rung when |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Proximity — move near the student while continuing instruction | Off-task behavior continues past ~30 seconds |
| 2 | Non-verbal cue (eye contact, a pre-posted signal if the room has one) | Behavior continues past the cue |
| 3 | Quiet, private redirect at the student's desk, naming the expected behavior | Redirect is ignored or repeats within the same period |
| 4 | Involve a paraprofessional or send for office support if one is available in the room or nearby | The pattern repeats after rung 3, or affects instruction for the rest of the class |
| 5 | Office referral, with the rung 1–4 history attached in writing | Any safety-related behavior, or repetition despite rungs 1–4 — for a substitute, this rung is reached one incident sooner than a regular teacher might reach it, precisely because there's no tomorrow in this room to resolve it differently |

Skip directly to rung 5 (or call the office immediately) only for a genuine safety issue — never for a first instance of talking, off-task work, or a claimed-exception dispute.

## 4. Handoff Note (filled template)

Write this in real time or from the incident log at day's end — not from memory the next morning, since there is no next morning in this room.

```
[Teacher name], Rm [room] — [Day], [date], Periods [range], Sub: [name]

Periods completed as planned: [list] — work collected in [location].

Periods with a deviation: [period] — [what didn't match: missing/mismatched plan,
insufficient material, etc.] — [what was substituted and why] — [any action needed
from the returning teacher, e.g. "quiz not given, may need rescheduling"].

Incidents: [period] — [student, behavior, rung reached, time, resolution] or "none."

Attendance: submitted per office procedure each period. Room left in arrival
condition: [yes/no, note exceptions].
```

**Filled instance** (matches the SKILL.md worked example) is the deliverable quoted there — use that as the reference for tone and specificity: names the exact deviation, the exact substitution, and asks a specific yes/no question rather than narrating the day in general terms.

## 5. Dispatch-Platform Job Acceptance Checklist

Before accepting a same-day or advance job on a platform (Aesop/Frontline Absence & Substitute Management, SmartFindExpress):

1. Check grade level and subject against what you're actually prepared to cover — accepting a subject you can't reasonably manage and cancelling later carries a penalty in most platforms' no-show/late-cancellation tracking.
2. If a plan is attached, skim it before accepting, not after — a job with no plan attached at all is a signal the assignment may be genuinely last-minute (higher chance of a Section-2-style gap on arrival).
3. Confirm the assignment length as coded (single day vs. multi-day) — a multi-day listing approaching the district's long-term threshold (commonly ~10 consecutive school days; verify the specific number) is a different pay/duty conversation before accepting, not after showing up on day 8.
4. Decline promptly rather than accepting-then-cancelling if the assignment doesn't fit — most platforms weight cancellation after acceptance more heavily against future job priority than a same-day decline.
