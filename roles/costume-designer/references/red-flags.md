# Costume Designer — Red Flags

### A flagged quick-change has no timed rehearsal logged before tech week
- **Usually means:** the build/rent decision for that garment was made on a paper estimate, and nobody has confirmed the actual dresser can execute the change in the available seconds.
- **First question:** has this specific change been run with the actual garment (or a construction-accurate sample) and the assigned dresser, stopwatch in hand?
- **Data to pull:** the change-timing log — every flagged change should have at least one logged run before the first tech rehearsal.

### A rental garment is the source for a change under ~20 seconds
- **Usually means:** the source decision was made on price without checking whether the rental house's no-alteration terms allow the quick-rig hardware (Velcro, magnets, cut seams) the change needs.
- **First question:** does the rental contract permit permanent modification, and has the actual sample been timed unmodified?
- **Data to pull:** the rental house agreement's alteration clause, and the timed-rehearsal result for that specific garment.

### Fabric or trim cost exceeds ~40% of a garment's allocated build budget
- **Usually means:** the fabric was chosen before the per-garment budget was confirmed, or the budget was set without checking current fabric-market pricing for the desired hand and weight.
- **First question:** is a substitute fabric viable without changing the design intent, or does the per-garment allocation need revisiting?
- **Data to pull:** the full costing sheet with each line item's share of the garment's total budget.

### Build hours were quoted from a sketch rather than a comparable prior garment
- **Usually means:** the estimate is a guess, not a shop-capacity claim, and is likely to be wrong in the direction of underestimating beading, boning, or tailoring-heavy construction.
- **First question:** what's the closest garment built in this shop in the last two seasons, and how many hours did it actually take?
- **Data to pull:** the shop's build-hour log for the nearest comparable piece.

### More than ~15% of remaining shop build hours are unallocated with under three weeks to tech
- **Usually means:** the build schedule has drifted behind the calendar, either from added scope or from underestimated hours on pieces already in progress.
- **First question:** which pieces on the rack are candidates to convert from build to rent or buy without compromising a hero look?
- **Data to pull:** the shop's hours-remaining-vs.-hours-needed tally, garment by garment.

### A swatch was approved under shop light only, with no tech-table or camera check scheduled
- **Usually means:** the color/texture approval is provisional, not final, and risks a mid-run re-costume if the fabric reads differently under the actual rig.
- **First question:** is a tech-table run (or camera test) scheduled before the fabric gets cut, and can cutting wait for it?
- **Data to pull:** the swatch approval log with light condition noted, and the tech schedule.

### The costume plot hasn't been updated since the last blocking change
- **Usually means:** stage management, the wardrobe supervisor, and the dressers are executing the show against stale information, which surfaces as a missed change on a night the designer isn't present.
- **First question:** when was blocking last changed for this section, and does the plot reflect it?
- **Data to pull:** the plot's last-revised date against stage management's blocking-note log.

### A multi-piece look (e.g. jacket + vest + trim) is cut from separate fabric bolts or dye lots
- **Usually means:** the pieces were ordered or dyed at different times without cross-checking lot numbers, risking a color mismatch invisible on individual swatch cards but visible under stage wash.
- **First question:** do all pieces in this look share a bolt/dye-lot number, and if not, was a side-by-side comparison done under performance lighting?
- **Data to pull:** the bolt/lot numbers logged against each piece in the look.

### An understudy or swing costume isn't built or fitted until inside the final two weeks before opening
- **Usually means:** the build schedule prioritized principal looks on the assumption the understudy track wouldn't be needed soon, leaving no buffer if it is.
- **First question:** what is the actual fitting and build status for every understudy/swing track, not just the principal cast?
- **Data to pull:** the fitting schedule cross-referenced against the full cast list, including covers.

### The contingency line is more than 60% spent with over a third of the tech/preview period still remaining
- **Usually means:** the contingency is being used to absorb scope additions rather than the fit and timing problems it exists for, and a genuine late fix may have no funding left.
- **First question:** what has the contingency actually been spent on so far, itemized?
- **Data to pull:** the contingency-line ledger against the remaining tech/preview calendar.
