# Red flags

Smell tests a supervisor should catch inside the first walkthrough or the first week of a report, before a quota number or a clean-looking average papers over the actual problem.

### Attendant sustaining >15–20% above the credit or square-footage standard for 2+ consecutive days

- **Usually means:** the section is understaffed and the shortfall is being absorbed by one person's pace instead of being redistributed — an early lead indicator of an inspection-fail or complaint spike 3–5 days out, not a productivity win.
- **First question:** "What did we cut from that section to hit the number — dwell time, a task, or a room?"
- **Data to pull:** per-attendant daily credit/square-footage log for the past 2 weeks; inspection scores for that attendant's sections over the same window; any guest-complaint or callback tied to those rooms.

### Inspection score climbing while guest complaints on cleanliness also climb

- **Usually means:** the inspection process is being gamed — inspectors checking their own team's work, a checklist that doesn't cover what guests actually notice (odor, hair, surface stickiness), or scores rounding up on borderline items.
- **First question:** "Who inspects these rooms, and do they ever inspect a section they didn't assign?"
- **Data to pull:** inspector-to-section assignment history; a sample of "passed" rooms re-inspected by someone outside the normal rotation; complaint verbatims mapped against inspection checklist categories to find the gap.

### Secondary chemical container without a GHS-compliant label

- **Usually means:** a spray bottle was decanted from bulk without relabeling — often "everyone knows what's in the blue bottle," which is not a defense in an OSHA HazCom (29 CFR 1910.1200) inspection.
- **First question:** "What's in this bottle, what's the dilution ratio, and where's the SDS for it?"
- **Data to pull:** current SDS binder/digital library completeness; last chemical-safety training date for the attendant using the container; a spot count of unlabeled or hand-labeled secondary containers across the property that shift.

### Same-day absenteeism above ~25% with no on-call activation logged

- **Usually means:** the shortfall was absorbed by silently cutting stayovers or dwell time rather than triggering the documented shortfall protocol — the gap shows up later as a quality or safety issue with no paper trail explaining why.
- **First question:** "Show me today's credit demand versus present capacity, and what was actually deferred to close the gap."
- **Data to pull:** daily attendance log vs. schedule; on-call activation log; list of any rooms deferred and the stated reason.

### Turnover in this crew running well above the property's other hourly departments

- **Usually means:** normal for this labor segment at a baseline level, but a spike specifically in housekeeping/janitorial (versus food & beverage or front desk) points to a schedule, workload, or supervisor-specific issue rather than a market-wide labor problem.
- **First question:** "Is this turnover in line with our historical housekeeping baseline, or is it a new spike relative to it — and relative to our other hourly departments this quarter?"
- **Data to pull:** trailing-12-month turnover by department; exit-interview themes specific to housekeeping/janitorial; recent schedule or workload changes (new property wing, reduced headcount, new productivity target).

### A disinfectant task completed faster than its labeled contact/dwell time

- **Usually means:** a quota-driven quick pass that defeats the chemistry — the surface looks clean and tests dirty, and it's invisible until a pathogen-specific complaint or an audit swab.
- **First question:** "What's the labeled contact time for this product, and how long did the attendant actually leave it wet on the surface?"
- **Data to pull:** SDS/label contact-time spec for the product in use; time-motion sample of the task as actually performed; any recent illness-cluster or complaint pattern tied to the affected area.

### "Available" room or floor count from the property system used to justify a same-day sale or release without a completed-clean confirmation

- **Usually means:** a system-level inventory flag is being treated as equivalent to a verified physical clean — common when front desk or facilities pulls the number under time pressure without checking with housekeeping directly.
- **First question:** "Has this specific room/floor cleared its full clean and compliance steps, or is this the system count?"
- **Data to pull:** housekeeping's own real-time board versus the property/facilities system's availability flag; timestamp of last confirmed clean completion for the specific unit in question.

### Missing or expired Hepatitis B vaccination offer record for staff assigned bloodborne-pathogen-exposure tasks

- **Usually means:** onboarding or reassignment didn't route the employee through the required OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard (29 CFR 1910.1030) offer within 10 working days of task assignment — a standing compliance gap that surfaces at the worst time, during an actual incident or an audit.
- **First question:** "Pull this employee's exposure-control file — when was the vaccination offer made, and is it signed (accepted or declined)?"
- **Data to pull:** exposure control plan roster; vaccination offer/declination records by hire or reassignment date; incident log for any exposure events in the same period.

### Bid or contract labor cost per square foot noticeably below the regional benchmark for the stated scope and frequency

- **Usually means:** the frequency, headcount, or supply allowance was quietly reduced to hit the price point, and the gap will surface later as service-level complaints rather than upfront.
- **First question:** "At this price and headcount, what's the implied labor hours per square foot per month — does it match the stated cleaning frequency?"
- **Data to pull:** contract scope of work and frequency schedule; back-calculated labor hours from the quoted price and local loaded labor rate; comparable regional cost-per-square-foot benchmarks (BSCAI-style).
