## Chain-of-custody log format

```
Log #: [sequential]
Item: [species, sex, identifying marks/tags]
Seized by: [officer], [date/time], [GPS coordinates]
Condition at seizure: [photographed / measured / sampled — note which]
Storage location: [facility, unit #]
Transfers: [date, from, to, reason] — one row per transfer, no gaps permitted
Final disposition: [destroyed / released to lab / retained as evidence / returned]
```

A gap in the transfer chain — any period where custody isn't documented — is grounds for a defense challenge to the evidence's integrity, independent of whether tampering actually occurred.

## Habitat/pollution-crime causal-chain checklist

1. Identify the specific discharge or action (date, source, quantity/duration).
2. Identify the specific downstream effect (fish kill count, habitat loss acreage, water-quality reading).
3. Establish the causal link between 1 and 2 — hydrology, timing, and dose-response all need to support the connection, not just proximity.
4. Identify the responsible actor with evidence tying them to the specific discharge, not general site ownership.
5. Document baseline conditions (pre-incident water quality, population data) if available, to support the "before/after" comparison.

A case built only on steps 1 and 2 without step 3's causal link is a condition report, not a prosecutable case.
