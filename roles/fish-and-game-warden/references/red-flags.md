# Fish and Game Warden — Red Flags

### Search or seizure occurred inside curtilage without a warrant or consent
- **Usually means:** an officer treated open-fields authority as extending closer to a dwelling than it legally does; second possibility — exigent circumstances genuinely applied and weren't documented.
- **First question:** how far was the search location from the dwelling, and was it fenced, posted, or otherwise marked as private?
- **Data to pull:** scene photos with distance/GPS to the nearest structure, any consent documentation, exigency notes if claimed.

### Harvest count exceeds license allocation by more than 1 animal
- **Usually means:** intentional over-harvest; second possibility — a hunting party's combined take was misattributed to one license.
- **First question:** whose tags were actually used, and does the party's total harvest reconcile against everyone's combined allocation?
- **Data to pull:** all party members' licenses and tags, individual harvest photos/timestamps if available.

### Trophy-class animal restitution calculated as flat per-animal fine only
- **Usually means:** the scoring step was skipped, understating the case's financial exposure — often because scoring requires a second visit or specialized measurement.
- **First question:** has the animal's antler/horn been field-scored against the state's trophy threshold yet?
- **Data to pull:** field-scoring measurement sheet, comparison against the statutory trophy threshold for that species.

### Carcass or tissue evidence held more than 24 hours before species-ID sampling
- **Usually means:** evidence-collection priority was misordered — paperwork or interviews were completed before securing degrading evidence.
- **First question:** what is the current visible condition of the tissue, and is species identification still possible?
- **Data to pull:** time-stamped photos from seizure to now, storage-temperature log.

### Habitat/pollution case has a condition finding but no dated, sourced discharge event
- **Usually means:** the investigation stopped at "the site shows harm" without establishing the specific causal chain required for prosecution.
- **First question:** what specific date, source, and quantity produced this effect — and what evidence ties it to that date?
- **Data to pull:** discharge permits/records for the site, water-quality or fish-kill timeline, any upstream monitoring data.

### Mistake-of-species defense raised on a species with clearly distinct field markings
- **Usually means:** likely a pretextual defense rather than a genuine identification error; second possibility — light/distance conditions genuinely supported confusion between similar species.
- **First question:** what was the shooting distance, light condition, and does the hunter's account match the physical evidence (entry angle, shot placement)?
- **Data to pull:** scene reconstruction notes, weather/light data for the time of the incident.

### Repeat license-holder shows escalating violation severity across seasons
- **Usually means:** progression from minor technical violations toward intentional poaching; second possibility — unrelated violations coincidentally clustering on one license.
- **First question:** pull the full violation history — is there a pattern in species, method, or location?
- **Data to pull:** statewide violation database record for the license holder across all seasons on file.

### Restitution and fine total significantly exceeds the animal's apparent market/meat value
- **Usually means:** this is expected and correct when a trophy-scoring schedule applies — not itself a red flag, but a common point of dispute requiring the schedule to be clearly documented for the case file.
- **First question:** is the full statutory calculation (base + trophy add-on, if applicable) documented and attached to the report?
- **Data to pull:** the completed restitution-schedule calculation with the specific statute section cited.

### Check-station stop extends into a vehicle search beyond the harvest compartment
- **Usually means:** the check exceeded the scope that administrative-inspection authority supports without independent probable cause developing.
- **First question:** what specific observation during the harvest check created probable cause to extend the search?
- **Data to pull:** officer's contemporaneous notes on what was seen before the extended search began.
