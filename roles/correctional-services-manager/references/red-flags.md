# Red flags

### Staffing ratio falls below the facility's documented critical minimum without an explicit risk-acceptance memo
- **Usually means:** a budget or scheduling decision was implemented administratively without escalating the safety tradeoff to the body accountable for accepting that risk.
- **First question:** "who signed off on operating below the documented minimum, and is there a written risk acceptance?"
- **Data to pull:** the facility's staffing schedule against its documented critical-minimum ratio, and the risk-acceptance memo trail if one exists.

### Use-of-force incident rate rising with no corresponding increase in after-action reviews conducted
- **Usually means:** incidents are being logged and closed as routine rather than each one triggering the systemic review that would surface recurring contributing factors.
- **First question:** "how many of this quarter's use-of-force incidents actually received a completed after-action review?"
- **Data to pull:** use-of-force incident count against after-action review completion count for the same period.

### Restrictive housing placements exceed the stated maximum duration without documented periodic review
- **Usually means:** restrictive housing has drifted from a last-resort, time-limited tool into a default population-management practice.
- **First question:** "how many current restrictive housing placements are past their scheduled review date?"
- **Data to pull:** restrictive housing census with placement date and last review date for each inmate currently housed.

### Population exceeds rated capacity for an extended period with no classification or transfer mitigation plan
- **Usually means:** overcrowding is being treated as a static condition to operate around rather than an active risk factor requiring a mitigation plan.
- **First question:** "what's the active plan to bring population back under rated capacity, and who owns it?"
- **Data to pull:** population trend against rated capacity over the last 2 quarters, plus any documented mitigation plan.

### Mandatory reporting or investigation timelines are missed, or an investigation backlog is growing
- **Usually means:** compliance processes are under-resourced relative to volume, or a specific bottleneck (staffing, records processing) is consistently causing delays.
- **First question:** "where specifically in the process is the backlog forming, and how long has it been growing?"
- **Data to pull:** compliance tracking system showing timeline adherence and backlog trend over recent months.

### Classification reviews aren't triggered by disqualifying events despite policy requiring it
- **Usually means:** the classification system is relying on the fixed periodic review cycle alone rather than the event-triggered reviews the policy actually requires.
- **First question:** "pull the last 10 disqualifying events — how many triggered a review within the policy timeframe?"
- **Data to pull:** the classification review trigger log against the actual events (assaults, disciplinary infractions, mental health flags) recorded in the same period.

### Programming or treatment budget is cut disproportionately relative to security budget under fiscal pressure, with no risk analysis
- **Usually means:** programming was treated as the default cut under budget pressure without weighing its documented effect on population stability and long-run incident rates.
- **First question:** "was the incident-rate and recidivism tradeoff of this cut analyzed and presented, or was it an automatic reduction?"
- **Data to pull:** budget change by category over recent cycles, alongside any documented risk analysis for the programming reduction.

### Incident reports show a recurring pattern in the same housing unit or shift with no systemic review triggered
- **Usually means:** incidents are being handled individually without anyone stepping back to notice the pattern pointing to a systemic cause — staffing, physical plant, or classification issue specific to that unit or shift.
- **First question:** "has anyone looked at these incidents together, not just one at a time?"
- **Data to pull:** incident log filtered and grouped by housing unit and shift over the last 2 quarters.

### Mental health crisis flags don't result in documented screening or housing adjustment within the policy timeframe
- **Usually means:** mental health flagging exists as a data point but isn't reliably triggering the required downstream action, often due to a staffing or process bottleneck.
- **First question:** "what's the actual completion rate for mental health screening within the policy window after a crisis flag?"
- **Data to pull:** mental health flag log against screening completion timestamps for the same period.

### Grievance backlog is growing with no tracking of whether unresolved grievances correlate with incident locations
- **Usually means:** grievances are being processed as an administrative queue rather than as an early-warning signal for conditions that could escalate into incidents.
- **First question:** "are unresolved grievances concentrated in any particular unit, and does that unit's incident rate track with it?"
- **Data to pull:** grievance backlog by housing unit cross-referenced against the same units' incident rates.
