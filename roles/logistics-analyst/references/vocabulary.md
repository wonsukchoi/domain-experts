### Total landed cost
The full cost of a shipment including base freight rate, fuel surcharge, accessorial fees, and expected costs of service failures (damage claims, late-delivery penalties) — the actual basis for comparing carriers or modes.
**In use:** "Carrier Y's quote is higher, but total landed cost is lower once we factor in Carrier X's accessorial history."
**Common misuse:** Comparing carriers on quoted base rate alone, treating it as equivalent to total cost.

### LTL (less-than-truckload)
A freight mode for shipments too large for parcel but too small to fill a full trailer, where multiple shippers' freight is consolidated on one trailer.
**In use:** "At 8,000 lbs on this lane, LTL is the right mode — well under FTL breakeven."
**Common misuse:** Defaulting to LTL or FTL by habit rather than checking the current weight/cube breakeven for the specific lane and contract rates.

### FTL (full truckload)
A freight mode where a shipment (or shipper) has exclusive use of an entire trailer, typically economical once weight or cube approaches a trailer's practical capacity.
**In use:** "This order fills about 90% of trailer cube — FTL beats LTL here."
**Common misuse:** Committing to FTL for a shipment or lane whose actual volume doesn't approach trailer capacity, losing the mode's cost advantage.

### Accessorial charge
A fee for a freight service beyond standard pickup and delivery — liftgate service, residential delivery, inside delivery, reweigh/reclassification, and similar add-ons.
**In use:** "The liftgate accessorial wasn't on the original quote — that's an undisclosed add-on we need to flag."
**Common misuse:** Treating a carrier's low base quote as the full cost without accounting for that carrier's typical accessorial pattern on the lane.

### Freight class (NMFC)
The National Motor Freight Classification code assigned to a commodity based on density, handling, stowability, and liability, which determines its LTL freight rate.
**In use:** "This shipment was reclassified to a higher freight class on the invoice — that needs to be checked against the actual density calculation."
**Common misuse:** Accepting a carrier's freight class reclassification on the invoice without independently verifying it against the shipment's actual density and NMFC rules.

### Fuel surcharge
A variable percentage or per-mile charge added to the base freight rate to account for fluctuating fuel costs, typically published on a recurring schedule tied to a fuel price index.
**In use:** "Fuel surcharge is at 15% this month — factor that into the total landed cost comparison."
**Common misuse:** Comparing carriers on base rate without applying each carrier's current (and potentially different) fuel surcharge percentage.

### OTIF (on-time-in-full)
A performance metric measuring whether a shipment arrived both on time (per the actual required delivery window/appointment) and complete (full quantity, no shortages/damage) — the standard for evaluating carrier and supply-chain service performance.
**In use:** "OTIF at the appointment level is 88% for this carrier, even though their internal on-time metric shows 96%."
**Common misuse:** Substituting a carrier's self-reported on-time percentage (measured against their own milestone) for true appointment-level OTIF.

### Freight audit
The process of reviewing carrier invoices against the bill of lading, contracted rates, and accessorial fee schedule to identify and recover billing errors.
**In use:** "Freight audit caught a duplicate accessorial charge on this invoice — that's a recoverable overcharge."
**Common misuse:** Treating carrier invoices as presumptively correct and skipping systematic audit, forgoing a real and recurring recovery opportunity.

### Bill of lading (BOL)
The legal shipping document that records the contract of carriage, including shipment details (weight, commodity, special handling) — the reference document for verifying invoice accuracy.
**In use:** "The invoice's liftgate charge isn't supported by anything on the BOL — dispute it."
**Common misuse:** Paying an invoice without cross-checking its line items against what the BOL actually documents.

### Lane
A specific origin-destination shipping route (e.g., Chicago to Atlanta), used as the unit of analysis for rate comparison, mode selection, and carrier performance tracking.
**In use:** "This lane's volume is too low and sporadic to justify a dedicated FTL contract."
**Common misuse:** Drawing carrier or mode conclusions from overall network averages instead of the specific lane's actual volume and cost pattern, which can differ substantially by lane.

### Carrier scorecard
A recurring performance report tracking a carrier's cost, OTIF, damage/claims rate, and other metrics, used to inform carrier selection and contract renewal decisions.
**In use:** "This carrier's scorecard shows improving cost but rising damage claims — that needs to factor into the renewal decision."
**Common misuse:** Evaluating a carrier scorecard on a single metric (usually cost) without checking whether it moved at the expense of service or damage performance.
