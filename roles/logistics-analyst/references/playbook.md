# Logistics Analyst — Playbook

## Total landed cost comparison worksheet (filled example)

| Component | Carrier X | Carrier Y |
|---|---|---|
| Base rate | $650.00 | $700.00 |
| Fuel surcharge (15%) | $97.50 | $105.00 |
| **Quoted total** | **$747.50** | **$805.00** |
| Historical expected accessorials (this lane) | $95.00 | $0.00 |
| Damage claim rate × avg claim value | 3% × $400 = $12.00 | 1% × $400 = $4.00 |
| **Total landed cost** | **$854.50** | **$809.00** |
| **Difference vs. lowest option** | +$45.50 | — (lowest) |

**Use:** Never rank on "Quoted total" alone — always carry the comparison through to "Total landed cost" using each carrier's actual historical accessorial and claims pattern for the specific lane.

## Mode breakeven reference table (illustrative — confirm against actual contracted rates)

| Shipment profile | Typical mode | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Under ~150 lbs, standard dimensions | Parcel | Below LTL minimum charge thresholds; parcel per-unit rate wins |
| ~150 lbs to ~10,000-15,000 lbs, or partial trailer cube | LTL | Consolidation with other shippers' freight is cheaper than paying for unused trailer capacity |
| Approaching full trailer weight (~44,000 lbs) or cube (~2,700 cu ft) | FTL | Per-unit cost drops once a shipment uses most of the trailer's practical capacity |
| Long-haul, non-time-critical, high volume | Intermodal (rail + truck) | Lower cost than over-the-road FTL when transit-time flexibility exists |

**Use:** Recalculate the specific breakeven for the actual lane and contracted rates in use — these ranges shift with fuel prices, contract terms, and regional capacity, and shouldn't be applied as a fixed rule across all lanes.

## OTIF calculation — filled example

| Shipment | Required delivery window | Actual delivery | Complete (full qty, no damage)? | On-time (met window)? | OTIF pass? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tue 8am-12pm | Tue 11:40am | Yes | Yes | Pass |
| 2 | Wed 8am-12pm | Wed 1:15pm (carrier logged "delivered same day" as on-time) | Yes | **No** (missed appointment window) | **Fail** |
| 3 | Thu 8am-12pm | Thu 10:20am | No (1 case damaged) | Yes | **Fail** |

**Carrier's self-reported on-time rate (same-day delivery standard): 100% (3 of 3).**
**Actual appointment-level OTIF: 1 of 3 = 33%.**

**Use:** Always recompute OTIF against the actual required appointment window and full-and-complete condition — a carrier's own "on-time" definition (often just same-day delivery) can overstate real performance substantially.

## Freight audit discrepancy checklist (per invoice)

1. Does every accessorial charge on the invoice have a corresponding entry on the bill of lading?
2. Does the freight class billed match the shipment's actual density/NMFC classification, or was it reclassified without documented justification?
3. Is the fuel surcharge percentage applied consistent with the current published rate for the invoice date?
4. Has this exact shipment been billed more than once (duplicate invoice check)?
5. If any answer above raises a discrepancy, flag and dispute before payment — log the dispute and outcome for the carrier's audit-recovery record.

> Carrier X quoted total: $747.50 (base $650 + fuel $97.50) | Historical expected accessorials: $95.00 | Expected damage cost: $12.00 | **Total landed cost: $854.50**
> Carrier Y quoted total: $805.00 (base $700 + fuel $105.00) | Historical expected accessorials: $0 | Expected damage cost: $4.00 | **Total landed cost: $809.00**
> **Recommendation: Route via Carrier Y — $45.50 lower total landed cost despite the higher quoted rate, based on this lane's accessorial and damage-claim history.**
