# Red flags

Smell tests a watch officer or master catches before a close-quarters situation, a stability call, or a fatigue-driven mistake happens. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before committing to an action.

### Bearing to a closing target changing 1-2° or less over several minutes while range decreases

- **Usually means:** the situation is CBDR (constant bearing, decreasing range) under Rule 7(d)(i) — a presumed collision risk exists whether or not the picture "looks" dangerous yet.
- **First question:** "What's the actual bearing rate and closing rate off the last two plots, not my impression of the picture?"
- **Data to pull:** last two to three radar/ARPA plots (bearing, range, time), computed closing rate and TCPA.

### Target vessel classified as fishing, NUC, RAM, constrained by draft, or sailing but treated as an ordinary crossing power-driven vessel

- **Usually means:** the watch officer ran Rule 15 crossing geometry without checking Rule 18 category first — the categorical rule was skipped, not just misapplied.
- **First question:** "What category is that vessel showing — lights, shapes, AIS — before we talk about who's on whose starboard side?"
- **Data to pull:** target's lights/day-shapes as observed, AIS vessel-type field if available, applicable Rule 18 ranking.

### A series of small (<10°) course alterations toward a converging target instead of one clear action

- **Usually means:** the give-way vessel is nibbling at the problem instead of taking action large enough to be apparent on the other vessel's radar — a Rule 8 violation even if it eventually resolves the geometry.
- **First question:** "Would that alteration actually show up as a clear course change on their scope, or does it look like noise?"
- **Data to pull:** cumulative course change over the last several minutes, re-plotted CPA/TCPA after the most recent alteration.

### GM (fluid) within 0.05 m of the applicable minimum threshold

- **Usually means:** the loading condition or free-surface state has eroded the real stability margin close to the floor, and any further change (fuel consumption, ballast shift, additional cargo, deteriorating weather) can cross it.
- **First question:** "Is that GM figure solid or fluid — has the free-surface correction actually been applied?"
- **Data to pull:** KM from current hydrostatic tables, KG from the loading computer, free-surface correction for every slack tank, the applicable regulatory and company minimums.

### Two or more ballast or fuel tanks slack at the same time with heavy weather forecast

- **Usually means:** free-surface effect is compounding across multiple tanks right when the stability margin matters most, and the solid-GM number being quoted around the bridge doesn't reflect it.
- **First question:** "Which tanks are slack right now, and what's their combined free-surface correction?"
- **Data to pull:** tank sounding/ullage report, free-surface moment per tank from the stability booklet, current displacement.

### Loading condition actually used differs from the one the pre-departure stability calculation was run against

- **Usually means:** cargo, ballast, or fuel was adjusted after the stability calculation was finalized and the GM figure being relied on no longer reflects the real ship.
- **First question:** "Has the loading computer been re-run against what's actually aboard right now, or are we still quoting the pre-departure number?"
- **Data to pull:** finalized loading computer output timestamp, cargo/ballast manifest as loaded, any post-calculation changes logged.

### OOW or lookout rest hours below the STCW floor (10h/24h in max two periods, one ≥6h; 77h/7d; ≤14h between rest periods)

- **Usually means:** the watch bill has drifted out of compliance, often gradually, and self-reported alertness is no longer a reliable check on actual fatigue.
- **First question:** "What does the rest-hour log actually show for the last 24 and 7 days, not how awake someone says they feel?"
- **Data to pull:** rest-hour log for the individual and watch section, current watch bill, any recent schedule changes (port calls, drills, cargo ops) that ate into rest periods.

### A close-quarters situation, stability concern, or fatigue issue resolved on watch without informing the master

- **Usually means:** the watch officer treated a threshold event as routine rather than escalating it — the specific silence pattern documented across multiple casualty investigations.
- **First question:** "Was the master briefed on this before or after it resolved?"
- **Data to pull:** night order book and deck log entries, time of any master notification relative to the event.

### AIS used as the sole basis for a collision-avoidance decision

- **Usually means:** the target may be a vessel without a functioning or fitted AIS transponder (common on small craft and some fishing vessels), and relying on AIS alone under Rule 7 misses exactly the traffic most likely to be a genuine risk.
- **First question:** "Has this target actually been confirmed on radar/visually, or only on AIS?"
- **Data to pull:** radar plot for the same target, visual bearing if in range, AIS data quality/age.

### Passage plan or night order book has no stated CPA/TCPA closest-approach threshold for a known high-traffic transit area

- **Usually means:** the threshold for "early action" is being decided in the moment by whichever watch officer is on, instead of pre-agreed by the master for that stretch of water.
- **First question:** "What CPA and TCPA does the passage plan call for through here, and does this watch's plan match it?"
- **Data to pull:** passage plan traffic-density notes, night order book entries for the leg, vessel's SMS minimum CPA/TCPA policy.

### Set-and-drift correction carried forward unchanged across a multi-hour leg in a known tidal-current area

- **Usually means:** the DR-to-EP correction from an earlier fix is being assumed still valid, when tidal set and drift both change through the tidal cycle.
- **First question:** "When was this current correction last verified against an actual fix, and does the tide table still support the same set and drift?"
- **Data to pull:** last verified fix versus DR/EP, tidal current tables or tidal stream atlas for the current time, time elapsed since the correction was computed.

### Officer justifies holding course past a give-way vessel's non-response with "they'll move eventually"

- **Usually means:** stand-on discipline has been over-applied past the point Rule 17(a)(ii) actually requires independent action — waiting for certainty the rule doesn't ask for.
- **First question:** "At what bearing/range does 'they're not going to move' become 'I need to move now' — has that point already passed?"
- **Data to pull:** current CPA/TCPA, elapsed time since the give-way vessel should have shown an apparent course/speed change, sea room remaining for a last-resort maneuver.
