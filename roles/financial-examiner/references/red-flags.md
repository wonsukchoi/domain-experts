### A bank's leverage ratio looks adequate but risk-based capital ratios haven't been independently recalculated
- **Usually means:** Balance sheet concentration in higher-risk-weight assets could produce a risk-based ratio below the well-capitalized threshold even while the leverage ratio looks fine.
- **First question:** What is the actual risk-weighted asset total, and does the Tier 1 risk-based ratio still clear the 8% well-capitalized threshold once recalculated?
- **Data to pull:** Asset composition by risk-weight category, Tier 1 capital figure.

### A loan's classification relies on a collateral appraisal more than 1-2 years old
- **Usually means:** The stated collateral value may no longer reflect current market conditions, understating or overstating real exposure.
- **First question:** How old is the appraisal, and has an updated valuation been obtained for this examination?
- **Data to pull:** Appraisal date, current market comparables for the collateral type.

### A borrower's debt service coverage ratio (DSCR), independently recalculated, falls below 1.0 despite the loan being carried as "Pass"
- **Usually means:** Primary repayment source (operating cash flow) is insufficient, indicating a well-defined weakness that likely warrants reclassification regardless of the bank's internal grade.
- **First question:** What updated financial data was used to recalculate DSCR, and does it match or diverge from the bank's own assumptions?
- **Data to pull:** Borrower's current financial statements, bank's DSCR calculation methodology and assumptions.

### ALLL/CECL reserve adequacy is assessed only at the aggregate portfolio level
- **Usually means:** A specific higher-risk segment could be underreserved even while the portfolio-wide ratio looks acceptable.
- **First question:** Has reserve adequacy been tested against each major segment's own loss history and current risk profile, not just the blended portfolio figure?
- **Data to pull:** Segment-level loss migration data, segment composition of the loan portfolio.

### A CAMELS composite rating is calculated as a simple average of the six components
- **Usually means:** The composite may not reflect the bank's actual risk profile if one component (commonly Capital or Asset Quality) has a severe, material weakness that should dominate the rating.
- **First question:** Does any single component show a materially weaker rating than the others, and has the composite been weighted toward that weakness rather than averaged?
- **Data to pull:** Individual CAMELS component ratings and supporting rationale.

### A finding cited in the prior examination reappears in this exam at the same severity level
- **Usually means:** The bank's corrective action process itself is inadequate — an uncorrected recurring finding is a more serious problem than the original finding alone.
- **First question:** What corrective action was the bank supposed to take on this finding since the prior exam, and why wasn't it effective?
- **Data to pull:** Prior exam's finding and required corrective action, bank's documented response/remediation actions since then.

### A loan's classification depends heavily on a secondary repayment source (collateral) rather than primary cash flow
- **Usually means:** This is itself an indicator the loan should be scrutinized closely — reliance on collateral liquidation as the effective repayment plan is a classic Substandard-level weakness.
- **First question:** Is primary repayment source (operating cash flow/DSCR) actually sufficient, or is the loan's soundness effectively dependent on collateral realization?
- **Data to pull:** DSCR calculation, collateral valuation and liquidation assumptions.

### A capital restoration or corrective action plan has no specific timeline attached
- **Usually means:** A finding without a required timeline risks indefinite deferral rather than actual remediation.
- **First question:** What is the specific deadline for this corrective action, and what Prompt Corrective Action category consequence applies if it's missed?
- **Data to pull:** PCA capital category thresholds, finding's stated (or missing) corrective action deadline.
