# Financial Examiner — Playbook

## Risk-weighted capital ratio calculation (filled example)

| Asset category | Amount | Risk weight | Risk-weighted amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cash and government securities | $50M | 0% | $0 |
| Residential mortgages | $100M | 50% | $50M |
| Commercial real estate | $250M | 100% | $250M |
| Commercial and industrial loans | $100M | 100% | $100M |
| **Total risk-weighted assets** | $500M total | | **$400M** |

| Ratio | Calculation | Result | Well-capitalized threshold | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leverage ratio | $30M ÷ $500M | 6.0% | 5.0% | Meets threshold |
| Tier 1 risk-based ratio | $30M ÷ $400M | 7.5% | 8.0% | **Below threshold — Adequately Capitalized PCA category** |

**Use:** Always calculate both the leverage ratio and the risk-based ratios — a bank can clear one while failing the other, and PCA category is determined by the most restrictive applicable ratio.

## Loan classification testing worksheet (filled example)

| Factor | Bank's original assumption | Examiner's independent test | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Collateral value | $2.2M (3-year-old appraisal) | $1.5M (current orderly liquidation value) | $500K uncollateralized exposure |
| Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR) | Assumed adequate at origination | 0.85 (recalculated with updated financials) | Below 1.0 — primary repayment insufficient |
| **Classification** | Pass | | **Reclassified to Substandard** |

## Specific reserve calculation (filled example)

| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Loan balance | $2,000,000 |
| Current collateral value | $1,500,000 |
| Uncollateralized exposure | $2,000,000 − $1,500,000 = $500,000 |
| Estimated loss severity | 40% |
| **Specific reserve required** | $500,000 × 40% = **$200,000** |

**Use:** Calculate specific reserves on the uncollateralized portion of any reclassified loan — this is in addition to, not a replacement for, the bank's general portfolio reserve.

## CAMELS component assessment table (illustrative structure)

| Component | Rating (1-5) | Key driver |
|---|---|---|
| Capital | 3 | Tier 1 risk-based ratio below well-capitalized threshold |
| Asset Quality | 3 | Reclassified Substandard loan, collateral/DSCR weaknesses found in sample |
| Management | 2 | Adequate policies, but capital planning didn't anticipate risk-weighted ratio shortfall |
| Earnings | 2 | Stable, no material concerns |
| Liquidity | 2 | Adequate funding sources |
| Sensitivity to market risk | 2 | Within acceptable interest rate risk limits |
| **Composite** | **3** | Weighted toward Capital and Asset Quality weaknesses, not a simple average of all six |

**Use:** Determine the composite by identifying which component(s) represent the most material risk to the institution — a composite of "3" here reflects the Capital and Asset Quality findings specifically, not an averaged score across all six ratings.

## Examination findings memo — filled example

> **Examination Findings — [Bank Name], [Exam Date]**
> **Finding 1 (Capital — MRA):** Tier 1 risk-based capital ratio is 7.5%, below the 8% well-capitalized threshold, despite a 6% leverage ratio appearing adequate. Bank falls into the "adequately capitalized" PCA category. Corrective action required within 60 days: capital restoration plan or capital injection.
> **Finding 2 (Asset Quality — MRA):** $2M commercial loan reclassified from Pass to Substandard based on updated collateral valuation ($1.5M current vs. $2.2M at origination) and recalculated DSCR of 0.85. Additional specific reserve of $200,000 required.
