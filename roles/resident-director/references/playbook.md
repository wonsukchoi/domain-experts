# Resident Director Playbook

Filled operational templates. Adapt names, hall sizes, and phone trees to the specific building; the structure and thresholds are the reusable part.

## 1. Incident severity tiers and response windows

| Tier | Example | Response time | Who responds | Documentation deadline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Life safety | Unresponsive/vomiting intoxication, suicidal statement with a plan, active violence, fire | Immediate — call 911/campus PD first | RA on duty + RD + campus police/EMS | Same shift, within 2 hours |
| 2 — Urgent, not life-threatening | Roommate conflict with a fear/intimidation component, suspected assault disclosure, resident in acute distress but stable | Within 30 minutes | RA on duty, RD notified within 15 min | Same shift |
| 3 — Policy violation, no immediate risk | Noise complaint, alcohol possession under 21 (no intoxication signs), guest policy violation | Within 2 hours (or next duty shift if after 2 a.m. and situation is contained) | RA on duty | Within 24 hours |
| 4 — Administrative | Maintenance request escalation, lockout, mail/package dispute | Next business day | RA or front desk | As needed, not incident-tracked |

**Fallback rule:** when in doubt about tier, treat it one tier more severe than the first instinct — the cost of an unnecessary RD notification is minutes; the cost of under-tiering a Tier 1 as a Tier 3 is the thing the whole system exists to prevent.

## 2. Duty rotation template (10-RA staff, 420-bed hall)

| Day | Primary duty | Backup duty | Coverage window |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon–Thu | 1 RA (rotating) | RD on-call | 8 p.m.–8 a.m. |
| Fri–Sat | 2 RAs (rotating pair) | RD + Area Coordinator on-call | 6 p.m.–8 a.m. |
| Sun | 1 RA (rotating) | RD on-call | 8 p.m.–8 a.m. |

Design constraints, in priority order:
1. No RA pulls duty more than 2 nights in a 7-night week absent a staffing shortage — 3+ nights/week is the burnout threshold that shows up in exit interviews.
2. Weekend duty rotates so the same RA doesn't draw both Friday and Saturday in consecutive weeks.
3. Duty the night before a final exam or the RA's own major deadline is swapped proactively, not requested reactively — build the academic calendar into the schedule at the semester start.

**Escalation tree (posted at every duty phone):**
RA on duty → RD (primary) → Area Coordinator (if RD unreachable in 10 min) → Dean of Students on-call → Campus Police (always in parallel for Tier 1, never gated behind the tree).

## 3. Roommate mediation script (no safety component)

1. **Separate intake** — 15 minutes each, same day if possible. Ask: "What's the specific behavior, when does it happen, and what have you already tried?" Not "how do you feel about your roommate" — feelings without behaviors don't produce an actionable agreement.
2. **Identify the 2–3 concrete friction points** (e.g., "guests after midnight on weeknights," "shared fridge space," "noise during the other's sleep hours") — not "we're just not compatible," which isn't mediable.
3. **Joint session, RD or RA facilitates, does not adjudicate** — each party states their friction point, the other restates it back before responding (forces listening over rehearsed rebuttal).
4. **Draft or revise the roommate/community living agreement** with specific, checkable terms: "Guests leave by 11 p.m. on weeknights" not "be more considerate about guests."
5. **Set a 2-week check-in**, not "let us know if it doesn't work" — passive follow-up is why agreements quietly fail.
6. **Fallback if agreement breaks within the 2-week window:** move to room reassignment rather than a second mediation round — a second round with no behavior change signals mediation isn't the right tool, not that it needs to be tried harder.

**If a fear/intimidation statement surfaces at any point:** stop the mediation track immediately, do not attempt to continue the joint session, and move to Tier 2 protocol (separate rooms, safety plan, and — if the statement meets the threshold — mandated reporting).

## 4. Conduct meeting structure (Tier 3 policy violation, first offense)

1. State the specific policy and the specific observed behavior (cite the incident report) before asking the resident anything — ambiguity about what's being discussed invites minimization.
2. Ask the resident's account before stating the consequence — sequencing establishes developmental framing over punitive framing.
3. Identify whether this is isolated or part of a pattern (pull prior incident history for this resident before the meeting, not during it).
4. Assign the outcome: educational (reflection paper, alcohol course, restorative conversation with an affected party) for isolated Tier 3; escalate to formal conduct process for a second occurrence or any Tier 1/2 component.
5. Confirm understanding in writing within 24 hours — a short email restating the policy, the outcome, and the consequence of recurrence. This is the record that protects both the resident (clarity) and the institution (documentation) if it recurs.

## 5. Mid-year RA staffing gap fallback (resignation or removal)

Preference order, most to least preferred:
1. Redistribute the departing RA's floor coverage across the remaining staff on that wing for 1–2 weeks while running an abbreviated internal hire process (existing waitlisted applicants from the spring selection cycle).
2. Pull a trained RA from a lower-occupancy floor in the same building rather than hiring cold — institutional knowledge of the building's culture matters more than a fully staffed floor count.
3. Only as a last resort, extend duty rotation frequency above the 2-night/week ceiling for 2–3 weeks with explicit compensating time off — document this as a temporary exception, not a new normal, or it becomes the next resignation.
