# Red flags

### Same RA logs more than 3 documented incidents on one floor in a single weekend
- **Usually means:** a floor-culture problem or a small number of repeat residents driving the volume — rarely that the RA is over-enforcing.
- **First question:** are these different residents, or the same 2–3 names recurring across the reports?
- **Data to pull:** incident log filtered by room/floor for the current semester, cross-tabbed by resident.

### A roommate conflict report contains the word "afraid" or "safety"
- **Usually means:** the conflict has crossed from a communication mismatch into intimidation or harassment territory, and mediation is no longer the right tool.
- **First question:** has a specific incident already happened, or is this an anticipated fear with no triggering event yet?
- **Data to pull:** the existing roommate agreement, and any prior documented incidents between the two residents.

### RA duty-call response times climb above roughly 15 minutes
- **Usually means:** duty coverage is thin relative to building population, or the RA on call is fatigued from consecutive duty nights.
- **First question:** how many consecutive duty nights has this RA covered in the trailing two weeks?
- **Data to pull:** duty schedule and call-log timestamps for the trailing 30 days.

### The same resident's conduct case reopens 2 or more times in a semester
- **Usually means:** the root cause (mental health, substance dependency, housing/financial insecurity) wasn't addressed at the first pass — only the symptom was sanctioned.
- **First question:** was a referral made after the first case, and did anyone confirm it was actually completed, not just offered?
- **Data to pull:** referral log and, where a release is on file, confirmation from the counseling center or CARE team.

### A parent contacts the RD directly with specific details about a student's health situation
- **Usually means:** either a genuine imminent health/safety exception applies, or a parent is attempting to route around the student's FERPA-protected privacy.
- **First question:** is there an active, imminent threat right now, or is this a routine parental concern?
- **Data to pull:** any FERPA release form on file, and the incident timeline if one exists.

### Alcohol or drug transport calls cluster on a recurring day of the week
- **Usually means:** an unofficial pregame, tailgate, or organization social event pattern feeding into the building.
- **First question:** is there a consistent triggering event on that day — game day, a specific organization's standing social?
- **Data to pull:** EMS transport log cross-referenced against the campus events calendar.

### More than 2 of 10–12 RAs resign mid-year
- **Usually means:** a duty-load, compensation, or supervisory-relationship problem across the staff, not individually unreliable people.
- **First question:** did departing RAs cite burnout, workload, or a specific incident in their exit conversation?
- **Data to pull:** exit interview notes and a duty-rotation equity report (nights per RA over the semester).

### A bias or inconsistent-enforcement complaint is filed against an RA
- **Usually means:** a real equity and liability issue — inconsistent policy application correlated with a resident characteristic is the kind of pattern that surfaces in a later institutional review.
- **First question:** pull every documented incident this RA has filed this semester — is there a pattern by whom they're written up against relative to the floor's population?
- **Data to pull:** incident log by RA, cross-tabbed against the (anonymized where possible) composition of the floor.

### A Clery-reportable crime occurs and no timely warning is issued within 24–48 hours
- **Usually means:** the campus Clery compliance officer wasn't looped in fast enough, or the RD misjudged the "ongoing threat to the community" test solo.
- **First question:** has campus police or the Clery compliance office been notified yet, and by whom?
- **Data to pull:** the Clery log and the campus safety office's incident report.

### A resident discloses assault or self-harm and says "I don't want anyone to know"
- **Usually means:** the RD is now a mandated reporter regardless of the stated wish — the disclosure content, not the resident's preference, controls the reporting decision.
- **First question:** is there an active safety risk right now that changes the immediate response?
- **Data to pull:** the Title IX office's intake procedure and the campus CARE-team referral form.

### An RA's incident reports read like conclusions rather than observations
- **Usually means:** the RA is documenting judgments ("resident was being difficult") instead of facts ("resident refused to open the door for 10 minutes, raised voice"), which weakens the report if it's ever reviewed in a hearing.
- **First question:** can every sentence in this report be traced to something the RA directly saw or heard?
- **Data to pull:** a sample of the RA's last 5 incident reports for a documentation-quality check.
