# Brownfield liability, cleanup-standard, and funding playbook

Filled worksheets for the four recurring decisions: liability protection documentation, cleanup-standard-to-reuse matching, funding stack sequencing, and institutional control disclosure.

## 1. AAI/BFPP liability protection checklist

Complete before closing on acquisition — none of these are fixable retroactively:

- [ ] Phase I ESA completed to ASTM E1527 standard, with interviews, records search, and site visit all within 180 days of acquisition date
- [ ] Historical use/chain-of-title records review completed within 1 year of acquisition date
- [ ] Environmental cleanup lien search completed
- [ ] No affiliation with any prior owner/operator who caused the contamination (required for BFPP status)
- [ ] Purchase price reflects the contamination's presence (not priced as if clean) — relevant to demonstrating good faith
- [ ] Post-acquisition compliance plan drafted: no further disposal, exercise of appropriate care, cooperation with any government response action, compliance with land use restrictions/institutional controls

If any pre-closing box is unchecked, liability protection is not established — closing without completing them means the BFPP defense may not be available if EPA or a private party later pursues a cost-recovery claim.

## 2. Cleanup-standard-to-reuse matrix

| Reuse target | Typical applicable standard | Relative remediation cost | Institutional control likely needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unrestricted residential | Most conservative (background/unrestricted) | Highest | None (fully unrestricted) |
| Restricted residential (with engineering control, e.g. vapor barrier) | Risk-based residential | Moderate | Yes — vapor mitigation system maintenance covenant |
| Commercial/industrial with engineering controls | Risk-based commercial/industrial | Lower | Yes — land use restriction excluding residential, engineering control maintenance |
| Green space/recreational (no structures) | Risk-based recreational, exposure-pathway-specific | Varies — often low for direct-contact-only pathways | Possibly — depends on groundwater/vapor pathway |

Rule: pick the reuse target FIRST, based on what the site plan actually needs at that specific footprint, then select the standard that matches — never select the cheapest standard and force the reuse plan to conform after the fact without checking whether the design actually allows it.

## 3. Funding stack sequencing worksheet

| Funding source | Typical eligibility dependency | Typical use | Sequence position |
|---|---|---|---|
| EPA Brownfields Assessment Grant | Site must be confirmed/suspected contaminated, not yet fully characterized | Phase I/II ESA costs | First — establishes contamination characterization |
| State Voluntary Cleanup Program (VCP) enrollment | Often requires completed or in-progress Phase II | Regulatory oversight + liability assurance letter | Second |
| EPA Brownfields Cleanup Grant | Often requires site ownership + approved cleanup plan (sometimes VCP enrollment) | Remediation costs | Third |
| Tax Increment Financing (TIF) | Often requires approved redevelopment plan + demonstrated but-for financing gap | Redevelopment infrastructure costs | Fourth — after cleanup plan approved |
| Historic/New Markets Tax Credits | Requires specific building/project eligibility criteria independent of contamination status | Redevelopment construction costs | Parallel track, coordinate timing with TIF/cleanup grant closing |

Rule: apply for each source only once its dependency is satisfied — applying to all sources simultaneously without checking dependency order wastes application effort on submissions that will be rejected as premature.

## 4. Institutional control disclosure checklist

- [ ] Deed restriction/land use covenant drafted and reviewed against the actual approved reuse plan
- [ ] Restriction recorded against the specific parcel/footprint, not the whole site if only part is restricted
- [ ] Restriction language explicitly stated in purchase and sale agreement
- [ ] Restriction disclosed to lender in financing application, not left for lender's own title search to discover
- [ ] Engineering control maintenance obligation (if any — vapor barrier, sub-slab depressurization) assigned to a specific responsible party with a recorded maintenance covenant
- [ ] Restriction's existence and rationale communicated to any future buyer during a resale, not just at original transaction

Undisclosed institutional controls discovered post-closing are a materially larger problem (deal disputes, lender claims) than the same restriction disclosed upfront as a normal transaction term.
