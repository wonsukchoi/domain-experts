# Red flags

Smell tests a rigger or lift inspector catches in the first pass over a lift plan — during pre-lift review or when investigating a near-miss.

### Choke angle under 120° with the sling still rated at full choker capacity

- **Usually means:** the choke-angle derate chart wasn't consulted, or the rigging was configured for convenience (available anchor point, headroom) without rechecking capacity at the resulting angle.
- **First question:** what's the actual choke angle measured at the choke point, and does the derated capacity at that angle still clear the calculated tension?
- **Data to pull:** rigging diagram with choke angle noted, sling tag choker rating, manufacturer's angle-derate table, recalculated tension.

### Two-crane (tandem) lift planned with an even load split by pick-point count

- **Usually means:** load share was assumed rather than calculated from CG and rigging-point geometry — common on asymmetric modules, vessels with off-center internals, or any load where the visual middle isn't the actual CG.
- **First question:** where is the CG relative to each pick point, and what does the beam-reaction calculation give for each crane's actual share?
- **Data to pull:** vendor weight/CG data, pick-point locations and span, load-share calculation, each crane's chart utilization at its own working radius.

### Sling or hardware D/d ratio under 4:1 at the bearing point with no derate applied

- **Usually means:** the sling's tagged WLL was used as the working capacity without checking it against the actual pin, shackle bow, or trunnion diameter it bears on.
- **First question:** what's the measured D/d ratio at every bearing point, and what's the derated effective capacity against the required tension?
- **Data to pull:** sling nominal diameter, pin/bow/trunnion diameter at each bearing point, manufacturer's D/d efficiency table, calculated per-leg tension.

### Any crane loaded above 75% of its charted capacity at the actual working radius without a documented critical-lift plan

- **Usually means:** the lift was treated as routine because the crew has done heavier picks informally before, or the chart percentage wasn't recalculated for the specific radius and configuration used that day.
- **First question:** what's the load chart utilization at the actual radius and configuration, and does a critical-lift plan exist with the sign-off the trigger requires?
- **Data to pull:** crane load chart for the exact boom length/radius/counterweight used, calculated load at that radius, critical-lift plan and sign-off record if one exists.

### Wire rope sling with visible broken wires or bird-caging still rigged for the next lift

- **Usually means:** the pre-lift inspection was a visual glance rather than a count against the numeric rejection criteria, or the sling was assumed fine because it "held on the last pick."
- **First question:** how many broken wires per lay, and where on the sling — one strand concentration or randomly distributed?
- **Data to pull:** current inspection tag/log, wire count and location, sling service history, manufacturer rejection criteria for that construction.

### Exclusion zone set by a standard barricade distance rather than the lift plan's swing/drop radius

- **Usually means:** the crew defaulted to a habitual perimeter used on smaller routine lifts instead of calculating the radius for this load's length and this boom's working radius.
- **First question:** what does the lift plan's exclusion-radius formula give for this specific load and boom configuration, and does the barricaded perimeter match it?
- **Data to pull:** load's longest dimension, maximum working boom radius, lift plan exclusion-zone calculation, as-built barricade perimeter.

### Tag lines missing, coiled on the ground, or handled by whoever happens to be nearby

- **Usually means:** tag-line duty wasn't assigned as a dedicated role in the lift plan, or the crew treats tag lines as optional on anything that isn't visibly awkward to lift.
- **First question:** who is assigned to each tag line in the lift plan, and are they in position and radio-linked before the load leaves the ground?
- **Data to pull:** lift plan tag-line assignment, radio/communication log, load geometry (rotation risk, wind exposure) that drove the assignment.

### Pick point chosen at the geometric center of an asymmetric or multi-component load

- **Usually means:** CG wasn't calculated from vendor weight and dimension data — the geometric center was used because the load "looks" balanced.
- **First question:** what's the calculated CG from vendor data, and how far is it from the pick point actually used?
- **Data to pull:** vendor weight/dimension data per component, CG moment calculation, as-rigged pick-point location, tilt reading during a test lift if one was performed.

### Synthetic sling with cuts, UV discoloration, or a missing/illegible tag still in the rigging

- **Usually means:** the sling was inspected by appearance ("still looks intact") rather than against the specific rejection criteria for fabric damage and tag legibility.
- **First question:** does the sling have a legible, current capacity tag, and does the fabric show any of the specific rejection conditions (cuts, burns, broken stitching)?
- **Data to pull:** sling inspection log, tag photo/legibility check, fabric condition against manufacturer rejection criteria.

### Below-the-hook lifting device (spreader bar, equalizer beam) used without its own documented rated capacity

- **Usually means:** the device was treated as a passive accessory rather than load-bearing rigging with its own WLL and inspection requirement.
- **First question:** what is this device's rated capacity at this configuration, and is that rating documented and current?
- **Data to pull:** device nameplate/rating documentation, most recent inspection/certification record, calculated load applied through the device.

### Lift proceeding without a pre-lift meeting or sign-off despite meeting a critical-lift trigger

- **Usually means:** schedule pressure moved the crew past the process step that exists specifically for this class of lift.
- **First question:** which critical-lift trigger applies here, and who was required to sign off before the load could move?
- **Data to pull:** critical-lift trigger checklist as applied to this lift, pre-lift meeting attendance/sign-off record, engineered lift plan document.
