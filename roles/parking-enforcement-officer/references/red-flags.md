# Red flags — what an experienced PEO checks before citing or immobilizing

Smell tests with thresholds. Format per flag: signal → usually means → first question → data to pull.

### LPR system flags a plate as scofflaw-eligible on total ticket count or debt, but individual ticket ages haven't been checked

- **Usually means:** the summary total includes tickets under 100 days old or under active dispute, which don't count toward eligibility even though they inflate the headline number.
- **First question:** "Of these tickets, how many are both unpaid *and* more than 100 days past their due date, and is any of them under dispute right now?"
- **Data to pull:** individual ticket list with issue dates and dispute status, not just the summary flag.

### A ticket contributing to a scofflaw threshold is under active adjudication

- **Usually means:** the debt is not yet final and shouldn't count toward a boot/tow trigger — immobilizing on a disputed debt is a due-process exposure for the department.
- **First question:** "Is there an open case number on this ticket, and has it been decided?"
- **Data to pull:** dispute/adjudication case status from the violations bureau system.

### Chalk marks (or any physical mark) reapplied to the same tire position across multiple visits with no individualized suspicion of a specific vehicle

- **Usually means:** the department hasn't transitioned off a legally exposed practice — under *Taylor v. City of Saginaw*, routine chalking without suspicion is a Fourth Amendment search in jurisdictions following that reasoning.
- **First question:** "Does this department have LPR or photo-timestamp equipment available, and why is chalk still the method here?"
- **Data to pull:** department policy on timing methods; equipment assignment log.

### A displayed disability placard's number returns no match, an expired record, or a different registered holder in the state registry

- **Usually means:** either a stolen/borrowed placard, an expired placard still in use, or (less often) a data-entry mismatch — not proof by itself of intentional fraud.
- **First question:** "Does the registry record match this vehicle, this driver, or neither?"
- **Data to pull:** state disability-placard registry lookup by placard number; vehicle registration.

### Citation's governing sign is missing, obscured by foliage/other signage, or mounted below the jurisdiction's minimum legibility height

- **Usually means:** the citation is challengeable regardless of whether the parking is genuinely improper — this is the single most common successful hearing defense.
- **First question:** "Would a driver approaching from either direction have been able to read this sign before parking?"
- **Data to pull:** photo of the sign in context (not cropped to just the text), with a reference object for scale/height.

### A new citation would bring a plate's total unpaid tickets close to, but not over, the scofflaw threshold when only qualifying (aged, undisputed) tickets are counted

- **Usually means:** nothing actionable yet — proximity to a threshold isn't the threshold; resist rounding up.
- **First question:** "If I recompute using only qualifying tickets, does this citation actually push it over, or just make the raw total look close?"
- **Data to pull:** recalculated qualifying count and debt including the new citation, per the ordinance's exact "or" logic.

### Driver claims to have "just left" or "just returned" to the vehicle at the moment of citation

- **Usually means:** sometimes true (grace-window edge case), sometimes a standard delay tactic — the photo timestamps resolve it either way and should be checked before arguing on scene.
- **First question:** "What do the timestamp photos actually show for this vehicle's position over the relevant window?"
- **Data to pull:** the full photo pair (session-expiration and citation-time) with timestamps, not just the final one.

### Plate returns a diplomatic, government, or otherwise flagged-exempt registration

- **Usually means:** standard enforcement (fines, boot, tow) may not apply, or a different protocol governs — proceeding as if it's an ordinary plate risks an improper immobilization with its own liability exposure.
- **First question:** "Does this plate's exemption category cover this specific violation type, or only certain enforcement actions?"
- **Data to pull:** department policy on diplomatic/exempt-plate handling; registry exemption code.

### Multiple citations issued to the same plate on the same block within a short window (same shift or consecutive days)

- **Usually means:** either a genuine repeat violator, or a documentation pattern that will read as targeting if it reaches a hearing or a complaint — both are worth a second look before the next citation.
- **First question:** "Is each of these citations independently supported by its own timestamp and sign photo, or am I relying on 'I already ticketed this car here before'?"
- **Data to pull:** citation history for the plate at that specific location; whether prior citations were paid, contested, or dismissed.

### Officer's own citation notes use "clearly," "obviously," or a driver-attitude description with no specific act attached

- **Usually means:** the record is leaning on impression rather than the documented facts the presumption of correctness actually depends on.
- **First question:** "What is the specific timestamp, photo, or registry result that supports this citation, independent of how it seemed?"
- **Data to pull:** the photo/registry evidence already on file; rewrite the note to reference it directly.
