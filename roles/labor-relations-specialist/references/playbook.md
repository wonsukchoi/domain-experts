# Labor Relations Specialist — Playbook

## Just-cause seven-factor checklist (filled example — attendance termination)

| Factor | Question | This case |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Notice | Was the employee warned of the rule and consequence? | Yes — written warning issued at 8-point threshold per policy |
| 2. Reasonable rule | Is the attendance policy itself reasonable and related to legitimate business needs? | Yes — standard attendance policy |
| 3. Investigation | Was an investigation conducted before discipline? | Yes — HR interviewed employee |
| 4. Fair investigation | Was the investigation conducted objectively? | **No** — FMLA status of 2 disputed absences was not verified |
| 5. Proof | Is there substantial evidence of the violation? | Disputed — point total included un-verified FMLA absences |
| 6. Equal treatment | Were similarly situated employees treated the same for comparable conduct? | Not assessed in this case (not the failure point) |
| 7. Proportionality | Is the penalty proportionate to the offense? | N/A — offense itself is disputed pending correct point count |

**Use:** Failing even one factor (here, factor 4 — the investigation didn't verify FMLA status before assessing points) is sufficient to overturn the discipline, regardless of how solid the other six factors are.

## Attendance point recalculation (filled example)

| Component | Points |
|---|---|
| Total points assessed | 10.5 |
| Points wrongly assessed for FMLA-qualifying absences (CBA Art. 12.3 exemption) | −2.0 |
| **Corrected point total** | **8.5** |
| Termination threshold (CBA Art. 9.4) | 10.0 |
| **Result** | 8.5 < 10.0 → termination not supported |

## Back pay calculation worksheet (filled example)

| Component | Value |
|---|---|
| Weeks since termination | 6 |
| Hours per week | 40 |
| Hourly rate | $22.00 |
| Gross back pay (6 × 40 × $22.00) | $5,280.00 |
| Less: interim earnings (temporary work during layoff) | −$800.00 |
| **Net back pay owed** | **$4,480.00** |

**Use:** Always subtract documented interim earnings from gross back pay — most remedies require this offset, and omitting it overstates the award.

## Grievance step tracking table (illustrative structure)

| Step | Party responsible | Typical deadline (confirm against CBA) | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Step 1 — Informal/supervisor | Supervisor response | 5 business days from grievance filing | |
| Step 2 — Written/HR | HR/labor relations response | 10 business days from Step 1 denial | |
| Step 3 — Department head/senior management | Senior management response | 15 business days from Step 2 denial | |
| Step 4 — Arbitration | Neutral arbitrator | Per CBA arbitration demand timeline (often 30-60 days) | |

**Use:** Calendar every deadline the moment a grievance moves to a new step — many CBAs treat a missed response deadline at any step as an automatic sustain of the grievance, regardless of the underlying merits.

## Grievance resolution memo — filled example

> **Grievance Resolution — Case [ref]**
> Issue: Termination under attendance policy (10-point threshold, CBA Article 9.4)
> Finding: 2.0 points were assessed for absences qualifying for FMLA protection under CBA Article 12.3, which exempts such absences from point accumulation. Corrected point total: 8.5, below the 10-point termination threshold.
> **Resolution: Grievance sustained. Employee reinstated with back pay.**
> Back pay: $5,280.00 gross − $800.00 interim earnings = **$4,480.00**.
