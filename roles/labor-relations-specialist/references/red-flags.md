### Discipline is being proposed without a documented prior warning for the same type of violation
- **Usually means:** The notice factor of just cause may fail — arbitrators generally expect progressive discipline unless the CBA or the offense (e.g., serious misconduct) explicitly allows skipping steps.
- **First question:** Does the CBA's discipline provision or past practice at this workplace require progressive warnings before termination for this type of violation?
- **Data to pull:** Employee's discipline history, CBA progressive-discipline language, comparable case precedent.

### Two employees committed similar conduct but received different discipline
- **Usually means:** A disparate-treatment problem — one of the most commonly successful grounds for overturning discipline at arbitration.
- **First question:** What specific facts, if any, distinguish the two cases (severity, prior record, role) that would justify different treatment?
- **Data to pull:** Both employees' discipline records, incident reports for both cases.

### An employee requested union representation during an interview and the interview continued anyway
- **Usually means:** A Weingarten rights violation, which can taint the resulting discipline regardless of what the interview uncovered.
- **First question:** Did the employee explicitly request representation, and did the interview continue after that request?
- **Data to pull:** Interview notes/recording, timeline of the representation request.

### A grievance response deadline is within 24-48 hours and investigation isn't complete
- **Usually means:** Real risk of a default loss under the CBA's automatic-sustain-on-missed-deadline provision, regardless of the case's actual merits.
- **First question:** Does the CBA allow a partial or interim response, or does it require full resolution by the deadline?
- **Data to pull:** CBA grievance procedure article, current investigation status.

### Management is changing a longstanding but unwritten practice without bargaining
- **Usually means:** If the practice meets the clear/consistent/long-duration/mutually-accepted test, this may itself be a contract violation, separate from whatever the written CBA says.
- **First question:** How long has this practice been consistently applied, and was it ever expressly agreed to or just tacitly followed?
- **Data to pull:** Historical application records of the practice, any communications acknowledging or establishing it.

### A termination is based on an attendance/points system where some absences may qualify for a legally protected exemption (FMLA, ADA, etc.)
- **Usually means:** Points may have been assessed for absences that should have been excluded under the CBA or applicable law, which can invalidate the point total supporting termination.
- **First question:** Were any of the absences contributing to the point total potentially FMLA-qualifying or otherwise legally protected, and was that verified before assessing points?
- **Data to pull:** Attendance point log with reasons for each absence, FMLA/ADA documentation status for disputed absences.

### An arbitration case is being prepared with no research into prior awards interpreting the same contract clause
- **Usually means:** The case may be argued in isolation from how an arbitrator has already interpreted this exact language at this workplace or in the industry, weakening the argument's persuasiveness.
- **First question:** Have prior arbitration awards involving this same CBA article (here or at comparable workplaches) been reviewed?
- **Data to pull:** Arbitration award archive/database for this contract clause or industry.

### A proposed penalty seems disproportionate to the offense compared to how this workplace has historically penalized similar conduct
- **Usually means:** The proportionality factor of just cause may fail even if every other factor (notice, investigation, proof) is satisfied.
- **First question:** What penalty has this workplace historically applied for comparable offenses, and does the proposed penalty align with that pattern?
- **Data to pull:** Historical penalty records for comparable offense types.
