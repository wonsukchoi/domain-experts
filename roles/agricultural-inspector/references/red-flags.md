### An aggregate defect rate passes (e.g., 9% against a 10% overall tolerance) but one defect category is elevated
- **Usually means:** A sub-tolerance (decay, serious damage, insect injury) may individually exceed its own tighter limit even though the aggregate is compliant.
- **First question:** What is the tally for each defect category separately, and does any single category exceed its sub-tolerance in the grade standard?
- **Data to pull:** The tally sheet broken out by defect category, the grade standard's sub-tolerance table for the commodity.

### A single agricultural water sample reads far higher than the facility's other recent results
- **Usually means:** Could be a genuine contamination event, or a one-off sampling artifact (runoff event, sample handling error) — needs the rolling geometric mean and statistical threshold value recomputed before treating the source as failed.
- **First question:** Does this reading, added to the rolling dataset, push the geometric mean or statistical threshold value past their respective limits?
- **Data to pull:** Full rolling water-test dataset for the source, sample collection date/method documentation.

### A pesticide residue is detected but at a low ppm value
- **Usually means:** Could be within an established tolerance for that commodity, or could be a violation regardless of the low value if no tolerance exists for that specific commodity-chemical pair.
- **First question:** Is there an established EPA tolerance (40 CFR 180) for this exact chemical on this exact commodity, and if so, what is it?
- **Data to pull:** EPA tolerance table entry for the commodity-chemical pair, pesticide application records for the field.

### A facility requests re-inspection of the same lot immediately after a failing result
- **Usually means:** An attempt to pass the lot through repeated sampling rather than a genuinely different, re-sorted product.
- **First question:** Is the sample being drawn from a newly distinguishable lot (different pack date, different field block) or the same lot presented again?
- **Data to pull:** Lot/pack-date identifiers on the containers, prior inspection tally sheet for comparison.

### A facility's temperature log shows unusually uniform or exactly rounded readings across weeks
- **Usually means:** The log may be filled in after the fact from memory or a fixed assumption rather than reflecting actual monitoring, masking real cold-chain excursions.
- **First question:** Is there an independent data logger or continuous monitoring record that can be cross-checked against the hand log for the same period?
- **Data to pull:** Independent temperature data logger export, hand-logged temperature record, monitoring equipment calibration certificate.

### Harvest occurred fewer days after the last pesticide application than the product label's preharvest interval
- **Usually means:** A preharvest interval (PHI) violation — the commodity was harvested before the labeled waiting period cleared.
- **First question:** What is the labeled PHI for this specific pesticide-commodity combination, and how many days actually elapsed between the last application and harvest?
- **Data to pull:** Pesticide application log with dates, product label PHI, harvest date records.

### A trace-back records request has passed its stated response window with no complete response
- **Usually means:** A recordkeeping violation independent of whatever the records eventually show — the facility's inability to produce Key Data Elements within the required window is itself the citable finding.
- **First question:** What time did the request go out, what is the required response window, and has a complete response been logged as received within it?
- **Data to pull:** Timestamped records request, facility's response log, list of Key Data Elements actually produced versus requested.

### A recall classification is downgraded from a more severe class to a less severe one without new evidence
- **Usually means:** Pressure from the recalling firm to minimize scope or publicity rather than a genuine change in the underlying hazard assessment.
- **First question:** What new hazard information, if any, justifies the reclassification, and who made the determination?
- **Data to pull:** Original hazard assessment supporting the initial classification, any new lab or exposure data cited for the change.

### A scale or measuring device used for weight-based grading has no current calibration certificate on file
- **Usually means:** Weight-dependent grade or mass-based determinations (e.g., mass-loading-style pack-out calculations) made on that device may not be reliable, regardless of how consistent the readings look.
- **First question:** When was this device last calibrated, and by whom, and is the certificate available on site?
- **Data to pull:** Calibration certificate and date, device service/maintenance log.
