# Adult ESL/Basic Education Instructor — Red Flags

### Attendance below 60% over a rolling 4-week window
- **Usually means:** a life-logistics conflict (childcare, shift change, transportation); second most likely — the learner already met their immediate goal (e.g., got the job) and is quietly disengaging.
- **First question:** "What changed in your schedule the last few weeks?"
- **Data to pull:** weekly attendance log; intake-form notes on employer/childcare constraints if recorded.

### Post-test administered before ~40 accumulated contact hours
- **Usually means:** program pressure to show a reportable outcome before term end, risking an understated or invalid gain measurement.
- **First question:** "How many contact hours has this learner logged since intake?"
- **Data to pull:** hours-of-instruction log against current NRS minimum-hours guidance.

### Raw score change reported without checking the EFL band crossing
- **Usually means:** the instructor or data-entry staff is unfamiliar with the NRS crosswalk, conflating score movement with a reportable outcome.
- **First question:** "Did the score cross a published EFL boundary, or move within the same band?"
- **Data to pull:** NRS CASAS/TABE-to-EFL crosswalk table, intake and post-test scaled scores.

### Placement score within 3 points of a band boundary with no intake-interview notes
- **Usually means:** the learner was placed on score alone and may be mis-banded relative to functional literacy.
- **First question:** "What's this learner's actual use-case — job, citizenship, kids' school?"
- **Data to pull:** intake interview form (if any), raw score, band-boundary table.

### Learner re-enrolling at the same level for 2+ consecutive terms despite >80% attendance
- **Usually means:** attendance isn't the barrier — either instructional content isn't matching a specific decoding/skill gap, or the learner needs a different modality (literacy-focused vs. conversational).
- **First question:** "What specific task is still hard for this learner?"
- **Data to pull:** diagnostic subscores (reading vs. listening vs. writing) if the assessment includes subtests.

### GED/HSE prep assigned to a learner below High Intermediate ABE
- **Usually means:** program pressure to move learners toward credential-track content before foundational skills are in place, setting up early attrition from frustration.
- **First question:** "What's this learner's most recent ABE reading EFL?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent CASAS/TABE reading and math scaled scores.

### Curriculum built as strictly sequential units in a program with >30% mid-term attrition
- **Usually means:** curriculum design assumes continuous attendance the program's own data shows is unrealistic.
- **First question:** "Can a learner who missed 3 weeks resume at the current unit without remediation?"
- **Data to pull:** term attrition/attendance-gap rate, curriculum unit dependency map.

### Class roster shows >50% single-employer concentration with employer-specific vocabulary as the sole content
- **Usually means:** the workplace-partnership framing has narrowed content past general transferability.
- **First question:** "Would this content help the learner in a different job?"
- **Data to pull:** unit content list, roster employer breakdown.

### Funder report shows EFL gains but no attendance-barrier notes despite <70% average attendance
- **Usually means:** the report is optimized for funder narrative rather than program improvement — real retention problems are being hidden behind the outcome number.
- **First question:** "What's driving the attendance number, and does the report say so?"
- **Data to pull:** raw attendance data vs. the submitted narrative report.

### One instructor's classes show an EFL gain rate far above the program average every term
- **Usually means:** either genuinely superior instruction, or inconsistent post-testing administration (testing only expected high scorers, or re-testing until a favorable result appears).
- **First question:** "Is post-testing applied uniformly to every enrolled learner, or only those expected to test well?"
- **Data to pull:** post-test administration rate (tested / eligible-to-test), not just the gain rate among those tested.
