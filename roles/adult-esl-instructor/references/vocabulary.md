# Adult ESL/Basic Education Instructor — Vocabulary

### NRS (National Reporting System)
The federal accountability system for WIOA Title II adult education programs; defines Educational Functioning Levels (EFLs) tied to standardized test score ranges.
**In use:** "Her post-test crossed into the next NRS level, so it counts as a reportable gain."
**Common misuse:** treating "NRS level" as synonymous with a K-12 grade level — it's a federally defined band tied to specific test scores, not a grade equivalent.

### EFL (Educational Functioning Level)
One of six ABE or six ESL bands the NRS uses to categorize learner ability and measure gains.
**In use:** "He moved from Low Intermediate to High Intermediate ESL — one EFL gain."
**Common misuse:** reporting any score increase as a "gain" without checking it crossed an EFL boundary.

### CASAS (Comprehensive Adult Student Assessment System)
One of the two federally approved standardized assessment systems (with TABE) used for adult-ed placement and outcome measurement.
**In use:** "We use CASAS Reading for ESL placement and TABE for ABE math."
**Common misuse:** assuming CASAS and TABE scores sit on the same scale — they're separate instruments with separate crosswalk tables.

### Andragogy
Knowles' theory that adult learning is self-directed, experience-based, and problem-centered, distinct from pedagogy (child-directed instruction).
**In use:** "The lesson leads with a real landlord letter, not a grammar drill — that's andragogy in practice."
**Common misuse:** using "andragogy" as a loose synonym for "adult education" broadly, rather than the specific self-directed/problem-centered instructional philosophy.

### WIOA Title II
The federal statute (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act) governing most publicly funded adult basic education and ESL programs, and the source of NRS accountability requirements.
**In use:** "Our program's Title II funding is tied to EFL completion rates."
**Common misuse:** assuming every adult ESL program, including private language schools, falls under WIOA Title II reporting — many don't.

### Level gain
An NRS-reportable outcome where a learner's post-test score crosses into a higher EFL band than their pretest; the primary federal performance metric.
**In use:** "Two-thirds of our completers achieved a level gain this program year."
**Common misuse:** conflating "level gain" with any positive score change, including movement that stays within the same band.

### Contact hours
The count of instructional hours a learner has actually attended, used to gate post-testing validity and calculate program performance per enrolled hour.
**In use:** "She hadn't hit 40 contact hours yet, so we held off on post-testing."
**Common misuse:** counting scheduled/enrolled hours instead of hours the learner actually attended.

### Standalone module
A curriculum unit designed to be taught without requiring the learner to have completed a prior unit, enabling re-entry after an absence.
**In use:** "We redesigned the unit bank to be standalone so a three-week absence doesn't cost a learner the whole term."
**Common misuse:** assuming all content can be made standalone — sequential skills like phonics decoding genuinely require prerequisite units.

### Diagnostic subscore
A breakdown of a standardized test into skill-specific components (reading, listening, writing) rather than a single composite score, used to target instruction.
**In use:** "Her composite score looked fine, but the listening subscore flagged a specific gap."
**Common misuse:** only reviewing the composite score and ignoring available subscores when a learner isn't progressing as expected.

### Crosswalk (score-to-level crosswalk)
The published table converting a raw or scaled standardized-test score into the corresponding NRS EFL band.
**In use:** "Check the CASAS-to-EFL crosswalk before you report that score as a level gain."
**Common misuse:** using an outdated crosswalk table after NRS revises the score ranges, which happens periodically.

### Life-skills curriculum
Instructional content organized around real-world adult tasks — reading a lease, understanding a pay stub, a citizenship interview — rather than academic-style graded readers.
**In use:** "The unit on reading a pay stub is life-skills content, not a grammar lesson in disguise."
**Common misuse:** labeling any non-grammar activity "life-skills" even when it doesn't map to an actual task the learner needs.

### Attrition (adult-ed context)
Learners leaving before completing a reportable outcome measure, distinct from a learner completing their goal early and self-exiting successfully.
**In use:** "Our attrition rate looks high, but a chunk of that is learners who got the job they were studying for and left satisfied."
**Common misuse:** treating all non-completion as a program failure without distinguishing goal-achieved exits from barrier-driven dropouts.
