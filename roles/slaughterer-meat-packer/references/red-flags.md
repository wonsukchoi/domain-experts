# Red flags

### Stunning effectiveness assumed from equipment operating normally without per-animal verification
- **Usually means:** an individual animal may not have been effectively stunned despite the equipment functioning correctly overall.
- **First question:** were specific insensibility signs checked for this individual animal before proceeding?
- **Data to pull:** the facility's humane handling verification protocol, per-animal verification record if documented.

### Evisceration contamination relying primarily on post-cut visual inspection
- **Usually means:** correct cutting technique — the actual primary control — may not be receiving the attention it needs, with inspection treated as sufficient backup alone.
- **First question:** does the evisceration technique itself follow the specified method to prevent intestinal/bung contamination, or is inspection catching what technique should have prevented?
- **Data to pull:** evisceration technique training/compliance record, contamination detection rate at inspection.

### Chilling verified by chiller air temperature or setpoint alone
- **Usually means:** actual carcass deep-tissue temperature may not have reached the required threshold within the specified time window, even with correct air temperature.
- **First question:** has deep-tissue/bone-adjacent temperature been directly probed, or only chiller air temperature checked?
- **Data to pull:** deep-tissue probe data if performed, chiller air temperature reading, carcass loading/position in the chiller.

### A chiller position with known reduced airflow or dense loading not flagged for extra chilling-time scrutiny
- **Usually means:** carcasses in that position may cool more slowly than the general chiller air temperature suggests.
- **First question:** does this specific position have a known airflow or loading issue, and is it accounted for in sampling/verification?
- **Data to pull:** chiller airflow/loading data by position, sampling plan coverage of known problem positions.

### Line speed increased without checking whether inspection/verification steps at each station can still keep pace
- **Usually means:** a verification step may have become nominal rather than actually effective at the new speed.
- **First question:** can each station's specific verification task (a contamination check, a defect check) actually be performed thoroughly at the current line speed?
- **Data to pull:** current line speed, time required for each station's verification task, any recent increase in missed-defect rate.

### Equipment/knife sanitation between carcasses skipped or delayed under production pressure
- **Usually means:** contamination from one carcass could spread to subsequent ones via shared equipment.
- **First question:** was the specified sanitation interval/trigger followed, or was it skipped to maintain pace?
- **Data to pull:** sanitation log/frequency, any known contamination event that should have triggered sanitation.

### A contamination event detected on one carcass with no investigation into whether preceding/subsequent carcasses were also affected
- **Usually means:** shared equipment could have spread contamination beyond the single detected carcass.
- **First question:** were carcasses processed on the same equipment before the next sanitation interval also checked?
- **Data to pull:** carcass sequence/equipment usage log, sanitation timing relative to the contamination event.

### A recurring contamination or chilling issue traced to a specific line position, shift, or worker
- **Usually means:** a systematic technique or process issue rather than isolated animal-to-animal variation.
- **First question:** does the issue correlate with a specific position, shift, or individual, or does it appear randomly distributed?
- **Data to pull:** issue traceability by position/shift/worker, pattern analysis.

### A USDA/FSIS inspection finding related to stunning, evisceration, or chilling with no documented corrective action
- **Usually means:** a regulatory finding may not have led to an actual process correction.
- **First question:** what corrective action was taken in response to this specific finding, and was it verified effective?
- **Data to pull:** the inspection finding, corrective action record, follow-up verification.

### A carcass released for further processing without documented chilling verification for that specific carcass or its sampling group
- **Usually means:** the release decision may not be based on actual verification of this carcass's chilling status.
- **First question:** does this carcass (or its representative sample) have documented deep-tissue temperature verification within the required time window?
- **Data to pull:** the carcass's chilling verification record.
