## 2. Preventive maintenance schedule by criticality (filled example)

| Asset | Criticality | Failure consequence | Inspection/maintenance cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fire suppression system | Life-safety | Catastrophic | Monthly inspection, annual full test (code-mandated minimum) |
| Primary rooftop HVAC | High | Major disruption, comfort/productivity | Quarterly |
| Emergency power generator | High | Catastrophic if needed and unavailable | Monthly test run, annual load test |
| Break room refrigerator | Low | Minor inconvenience | Reactive/as-needed only |

**Rule applied:** cycle frequency is set by consequence-of-failure, not asset cost or age alone — the generator gets tighter attention than its purchase price alone would suggest, because its failure consequence is severe.

## 3. Contractor qualification checklist (safety-critical trades, filled example)

| Criterion | Requirement | This contractor (electrical, panel upgrade) |
|---|---|---|
| Licensing | Current state electrical license, verified directly with licensing board | Verified, current |
| Insurance | General liability + workers' comp, certificates on file | Certificates current, $2M liability |
| Safety record | No major OSHA citations in trailing 3 years | Clean record confirmed |
| References | 2+ verified references for comparable-scope work | 3 references checked, all positive |
| Price | Competitive but not the deciding factor for this category | Mid-range of 3 bids |

**Rule applied:** price is checked last and isn't the deciding factor — a contractor failing any of the first four criteria is disqualified regardless of bid price for safety-critical work.
