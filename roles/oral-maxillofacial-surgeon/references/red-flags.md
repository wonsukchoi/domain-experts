# Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon — Red Flags

### Panoramic film shows ≥2 Rood's proximity signs (root darkening, canal deflection, white-line interruption, canal narrowing)
- **Usually means:** the root apex is truly adjacent to or overlapping the inferior alveolar canal in three dimensions, not just superimposed in the 2D projection.
- **First question:** has a CBCT been ordered to confirm the true spatial relationship before finalizing the surgical approach?
- **Data to pull:** CBCT cross-sectional slices at the root apex, specifically buccolingual position relative to the canal.

### Pederson difficulty score ≥7 ("very difficult")
- **Usually means:** extended surgical time and elevated complication risk; may exceed the comfort level of an inexperienced operator.
- **First question:** is the scheduled chair-time block long enough, and is the anesthesia level adequate for a 60–90 minute procedure?
- **Data to pull:** scheduled procedure duration against the historical average for score-matched cases at this practice.

### Post-operative numbness persisting beyond 8 hours past expected local-anesthetic duration
- **Usually means:** possible nerve injury (neurapraxia at minimum, axonotmesis or neurotmesis in more severe cases).
- **First question:** is sensation present but altered (paresthesia) or fully absent (anesthesia), and is it the IAN distribution or the lingual nerve distribution?
- **Data to pull:** sharp/dull and two-point discrimination testing documented at this visit, compared against a same-day baseline if available.

### Throbbing pain onset 2–4 days post-extraction after an initial pain-free interval
- **Usually means:** alveolar osteitis (dry socket), especially in smokers or oral-contraceptive users — not infection.
- **First question:** is the socket visibly empty of clot with exposed bone on exam?
- **Data to pull:** smoking status and oral-contraceptive use from the intake history.

### ASA III or higher scheduled for anything beyond local anesthesia
- **Usually means:** elevated perioperative medical risk that requires coordination before sedation is selected.
- **First question:** has the patient's treating physician provided clearance for the planned sedation level?
- **Data to pull:** most recent relevant medical records — cardiac or pulmonary clearance as applicable.

### Bleeding not controlled after 20–30 minutes of direct pressure post-extraction
- **Usually means:** possible undiagnosed coagulopathy or an anticoagulant/antiplatelet medication effect.
- **First question:** what anticoagulant or antiplatelet medications is the patient on, and when was the last dose taken?
- **Data to pull:** current medication list; INR if the patient is on warfarin.

### Swelling or trismus worsening after post-op day 3 rather than improving
- **Usually means:** possible infection rather than expected post-surgical inflammation, which typically peaks around day 2–3 and then resolves.
- **First question:** is there fever or purulent drainage present?
- **Data to pull:** temperature and direct wound inspection.

### Distoangular or horizontal impaction combined with Pell & Gregory Class III ramus relationship
- **Usually means:** the highest-difficulty combination on the Pederson index, with elevated risk of root fracture during elevation.
- **First question:** is a surgical approach (bone removal plus tooth sectioning) planned rather than simple elevation?
- **Data to pull:** full Pederson score breakdown by factor, not just the total.

### Patient on bisphosphonate or other antiresorptive therapy scheduled for extraction
- **Usually means:** elevated risk of medication-related osteonecrosis of the jaw (MRONJ), with risk magnitude depending heavily on route and duration.
- **First question:** what is the duration and route (oral vs. IV) of the antiresorptive therapy?
- **Data to pull:** full medication history and, where applicable, contact with the prescribing oncology or rheumatology provider.

### Root apex within 2mm of the maxillary sinus floor on imaging (upper posterior extraction)
- **Usually means:** elevated risk of oroantral communication at the time of extraction.
- **First question:** is a Valsalva test planned immediately post-extraction to check for sinus communication?
- **Data to pull:** panoramic or CBCT measurement of sinus-floor-to-root-apex distance.
