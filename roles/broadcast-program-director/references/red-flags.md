# Broadcast Program Director — Red Flags

### AQH share down >15% quarter over quarter while cume is within 5% of the prior book
- **Usually means:** a retention (TSL) problem — a spot-load increase, a clock change, or a talent shift is driving tune-out during or after breaks; second likeliest is a signal/technical issue reducing listenable hours.
- **First question:** has spot load per hour changed since the last clean book?
- **Data to pull:** program log minutes-per-hour history, PPM tune-out/diary clock-position report, spot inventory report from traffic.

### Cume down >10% over two consecutive books with TSL stable
- **Usually means:** a discovery/positioning problem — a competitor repositioned, added coverage (translator, HD boost), or the station's marketing/promotion spend dropped; second likeliest is a demographic shift in the market itself.
- **First question:** did a competing signal in this format change position, coverage, or promotion budget in the window?
- **Data to pull:** competitive format grid, signal/coverage change filings, promotion spend history.

### Morning-show payroll exceeds ~25% of total on-air budget while contributing under 30% of average daily cume
- **Usually means:** legacy talent priced above their current audience contribution, often protected by a long contract signed at a stronger ratings peak.
- **First question:** what's the actual cume/TSL delta comparing weeks with the host present versus fill-in weeks?
- **Data to pull:** fill-in-week ratings, talent contract terms and remaining term, cume-by-daypart breakdown.

### Spot load exceeds ~12 minutes per hour on a music-intensive format
- **Usually means:** sales added avails without a programming sign-off, or a network/syndication contract's inventory requirement grew; second likeliest is a rate-card shortfall being solved with volume instead of price.
- **First question:** what changed in the program log between this book and the last clean one?
- **Data to pull:** program log history, sales inventory report, stopset length by hour.

### Format-category share erosion appears across every competing station in the format in this market, not just this one
- **Usually means:** the format itself is cycling out of favor locally (demo aging out, category oversaturation) rather than a station-specific execution failure.
- **First question:** is every station in this format losing share this market, or only this one?
- **Data to pull:** format-tracking service's category share trend across all local competitors, national format-cycle commentary from a consultant.

### A syndicated or barter show's local avails sell below the displaced local-live daypart's historical CPM
- **Usually means:** the barter deal's cash-equivalent value no longer clears the opportunity cost of the local inventory it displaced.
- **First question:** what would this daypart's avails sell for locally at the current rate card?
- **Data to pull:** syndication/barter contract terms, local avail rate card, daypart revenue history for the prior 12 months.

### A complaint or inquiry references specific aired content tied to a sponsor relationship not on file
- **Usually means:** an undisclosed sponsorship arrangement — a payola or plugola exposure, whether cash or in-kind.
- **First question:** is there a signed sponsorship-identification log entry covering that segment?
- **Data to pull:** standards-and-practices log, traffic log for the date/time, any compensation or trade records tied to the segment.

### Listener complaint volume spikes around a specific daypart or content change
- **Usually means:** a new host, segment, or bit broke the format's implicit content expectations, or aired content close to the indecency safe-harbor boundary.
- **First question:** what time did the segment air relative to the 10p–6a safe harbor?
- **Data to pull:** program log timestamp for the segment, complaint log, standards-and-practices review notes.

### A voice-tracked or centrally programmed shift shows materially lower TSL than the live shift it replaced
- **Usually means:** the localism and spontaneity lost in the automation is costing more audience than the labor savings justify.
- **First question:** what's the TSL delta in the weeks immediately following the voice-tracking change?
- **Data to pull:** pre/post TSL by daypart, voice-tracking implementation date, the cost-savings memo that justified the change.

### E/I (educational/informational children's) programming hours log below 3 hours/week on a commercial TV affiliate
- **Usually means:** a compliance gap against the Children's Television Act's core-programming requirement, often from a schedule change that wasn't cross-checked against the quota.
- **First question:** does the quarterly children's-programming report reconcile against the actual aired log?
- **Data to pull:** FCC Children's Television Programming Report, program log cross-check for the quarter.

### A format flip is proposed within one book of a single soft ratings period
- **Usually means:** a reactive decision made before ruling out an operational cause (spot load, signal, talent absence) — the single most common way format flips destroy value they didn't need to.
- **First question:** has the operational-cause checklist been run and documented before this proposal?
- **Data to pull:** prior 3 books' AQH/cume/TSL trend, spot load history, technical/signal incident log for the period.

### A podcast or on-demand simulcast episode shows completion rate below ~40% while the broadcast TSL for the same content holds steady
- **Usually means:** the on-demand edit, ad load, or pacing isn't suited to on-demand listening habits — a distribution problem, not a broadcast programming problem.
- **First question:** where in the episode does the completion curve actually drop off?
- **Data to pull:** podcast host completion/drop-off report (IAB-standard metrics), ad placement timestamps within the episode.
