# Broadcast Program Director — Playbook

Filled templates and worked structures, not descriptions. Populate with a station's real numbers; the shapes and thresholds below are the defaults to start from.

## 1. Hot clock (CHR/Top 40, weekday middays)

A clock is the actual deliverable of a music format — this is what gets handed to a voice-tracked or live jock to execute identically every hour.

| :00–:03 | Power current (rotation A) |
|---|---|
| :03–:04 | Liner + station ID |
| :04–:07 | Recurrent (rotation B) |
| :07–:09 | Talk break / promo tease (60–90 sec) |
| :09–:13 | **Stopset 1** — 4:00 max, 3 spots + 1 promo |
| :13–:16 | Power current (rotation A) |
| :16–:19 | Gold (rotation C) |
| :19–:20 | Liner |
| :20–:23 | Power current (rotation A) — *protected: no break touches :20–:25* |
| :23–:27 | Recurrent (rotation B) |
| :27–:31 | **Stopset 2** — 4:00 max, 3 spots + 1 promo |
| :31–:34 | Power current (rotation A) |
| :34–:37 | Gold (rotation C) |
| :37–:41 | **Stopset 3** — 3:00 max, 2 spots + 1 promo |
| :41–:45 | Recurrent (rotation B) |
| :45–:49 | Power current (rotation A) |
| :49–:53 | Gold (rotation C) |
| :53–:55 | Talk break / weather |
| :55–:00 | Power current (rotation A) — *protected: no break touches :55–:00* |

Rule: total stopset time per hour ≤ 11:00; no single stopset exceeds 4:00; the :00–:05 and :55–:00 windows never carry a break, because PPM tune-out data consistently shows the steepest drop-off at stopsets bordering the top and bottom of the hour.

## 2. Rotation category structure (music format)

| Category | Plays/day (per song) | Library size (active) | Typical use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Power current | 12–14 | 6–8 songs | This week's biggest testing titles |
| Current | 6–8 | 15–20 songs | Recently added, still testing |
| Recurrent | 3–5 | 25–35 songs | Peaked in last 6–12 months |
| Gold | 1–3 | 200+ songs | Library depth, format identity |

Default: if a song's callout/testing score falls below the format's recurrent threshold for two consecutive research cycles, demote it one category rather than removing it outright — sudden removal creates a rotation gap the clock has to absorb elsewhere.

## 3. Ratings diagnostic worksheet

Formula: `AQH Persons = Cume × (TSL hours ÷ daypart hours)`; `AQH Share = station AQH Persons ÷ total market AQH Persons × 100`.

| Metric | This book | Prior book | Δ | Flag if |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cume | fill in | fill in | % | down >10% two books running |
| TSL (hrs/wk) | fill in | fill in | % | down >20% one book |
| AQH Persons | (computed) | (computed) | % | — |
| AQH Share | (computed) | (computed) | pts | down while cume flat = TSL problem; down with cume = discovery problem |

Read order: compute AQH/share first, then split the movement into the cume term and the TSL term — whichever term moved more is the actual problem, and the other is noise to ignore for this decision cycle.

## 4. TV daypart / lead-in grid (prime time, weeknight)

| Time | Show | Role in grid |
|---|---|---|
| 8:00–8:30 | Established comedy (returning, stable rating) | **Lead-in** — protects the hour |
| 8:30–9:00 | New comedy (untested) | Benefits from lead-in; watch retention vs. 8:00 |
| 9:00–10:00 | Tentpole drama (network's strongest asset) | **Tentpole** — scheduled to anchor the night, not to be protected by something else |
| 10:00–11:00 | Newsmagazine or procedural (reliable, lower ceiling) | **Hammock** — stabilizes after the tentpole, feeds the late local news lead-in |

Default: place an unproven show adjacent to (not opposite) the strongest established lead-in, and hold it a minimum of 6 episodes before a cancellation decision — one soft premiere in a new slot is not evidence, particularly opposite a competitor's own tentpole or a stunt (finale, live special) in the same hour.

## 5. Format-flip go/no-go checklist

Run every item before approving a full format change; a "no" on any of the first three sends it back to a clock/rotation/talent fix instead.

1. Has the operational-cause check (spot load history, technical/signal incidents, talent absence, syndication clearance changes) ruled out a non-format cause for the decline? **Y/N**
2. Has the decline persisted 3+ consecutive books, and does the format-category tracking service show the same erosion across other stations in that format in this market (not just this station)? **Y/N**
3. Has a clock/rotation adjustment already been tried for at least one full book with no material recovery? **Y/N**
4. Is there a stated financial model for the flip's first two books (expected cume/TSL trajectory, ad-sales transition cost, any syndication or affiliation contract penalty for dropping current programming)? **Y/N**
5. Does the new format have a named target demo and competitive gap in this specific market (not just a national trend)? **Y/N**

Only proceed to a flip when all five are Y; a flip approved on fewer is, by definition, a bet made without the checks the rest of this playbook exists to run.

## 6. Standards & practices escalation checklist

- Any sponsor-provided segment, product mention, or compensated placement: confirmed on the sponsorship-identification log before air, not after a complaint (47 CFR §73.1212).
- Any content flagged for potential indecency: confirmed air time is outside the 10p–6a safe harbor, or held for legal review before airing inside it.
- Children's core programming (E/I) hours: running weekly total checked against the 3-hour/week threshold before the quarterly FCC report is due, not compiled retroactively from the log.
