# Red flags

### Donor retention rate below 45% year-over-year
- **Usually means:** stewardship gap (no acknowledgment/impact reporting between gifts) or an acquisition-heavy strategy backfilling a leaky base; second most likely is a lapsed major donor whose relationship manager left.
- **First question:** "What's our retention rate by gift tier — is it the small-gift base or a major donor we're losing?"
- **Data to pull:** year-over-year donor retention report segmented by gift size tier, from the CRM.

### Public campaign launch planned with less than 50% of goal committed
- **Usually means:** cabinet pressure to show momentum, or an unrealistic timeline set before the prospect pool was assessed.
- **First question:** "What's our largest committed gift, and what percentage of goal does it represent?"
- **Data to pull:** pledge ledger sorted by gift size, compared against the campaign's gift range chart.

### No single gift exceeding 10% of campaign goal
- **Usually means:** solicitation has run through the broad base before the top of the gift table, or the real lead prospects haven't been screened/cultivated yet.
- **First question:** "Who are our top 5 wealth-screened prospects, and what stage are they at in moves management?"
- **Data to pull:** wealth-screening report cross-referenced with the moves-management stage tracker.

### Cost-to-raise-a-dollar above $0.20
- **Usually means:** an event or acquisition channel is being run as if it were a net-revenue source when it's actually a donor-acquisition or friends-raising-friends expense.
- **First question:** "Is this channel's real purpose net revenue, acquisition, or visibility — and is it being budgeted accordingly?"
- **Data to pull:** channel-level P&L (gross raised minus direct and staff-time cost) for the past 2 fiscal years.

### LYBUNT count up more than 15% year-over-year
- **Usually means:** a relationship-manager staffing gap, or a shift to digital-only stewardship that's under-serving mid-tier donors who expect personal contact.
- **First question:** "Which relationship manager or portfolio had these donors, and did that portfolio change hands this year?"
- **Data to pull:** LYBUNT list with prior-year assigned solicitor field.

### Average gift size falling more than 10% while donor count holds flat
- **Usually means:** major donors are being under-asked (asking a range instead of a specific capacity-based number) or a major donor has quietly reduced engagement.
- **First question:** "Pull the top 20 donors by historical gift size — are any of them giving at a materially lower amount this cycle?"
- **Data to pull:** top-20-donor trend report, 3-year lookback.

### Board giving participation below 100%
- **Usually means:** the board hasn't been asked directly by the chair or ED (a peer ask, not a mail appeal), or a board member joined for expertise/visibility without a giving expectation being set at recruitment.
- **First question:** "Did every board member receive a personal ask this fiscal year, and from whom?"
- **Data to pull:** board giving roster cross-referenced with solicitation log.

### Pledge total materially ahead of cash-in-hand relative to the payment schedule
- **Usually means:** multi-year pledges are being reported as if they were liquid, creating an operating cash gap program staff aren't aware of.
- **First question:** "What's the pledge payment schedule for the next 12 months versus the program spending plan for the same period?"
- **Data to pull:** pledge aging/payment schedule report vs. approved program budget.

### Restricted-grant renewal rate below 60% for grants up for renewal
- **Usually means:** program reporting to funders is thin or late, or the original grant proposal over-promised outcomes the program couldn't sustain.
- **First question:** "Were required interim reports submitted on time and did they show the outcomes the proposal committed to?"
- **Data to pull:** grants calendar with renewal status and report-submission dates.

### No documented planned-giving conversation with any donor 65 or older who has given 5+ consecutive years
- **Usually means:** the development team is treating loyal long-term donors purely as an annual-fund renewal, missing the highest-probability bequest pipeline in the file.
- **First question:** "Of our 5+-year consecutive donors over 65, how many have had a planned-giving conversation logged, ever?"
- **Data to pull:** donor tenure report filtered by age and consecutive-years-giving, cross-referenced with planned-giving conversation log.
