# Fundraiser playbook

Filled templates for capital-campaign sequencing, moves management, and donor communications. Populate with real numbers per campaign — the structures below are load-bearing, not illustrative.

## Gift range chart template (rule-of-thirds pyramid)

For a campaign goal $G, the standard doubling pyramid:

| Tier | Gift size | Count | Tier total | Cumulative % |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lead | 0.15 × G | 1 | 0.15G | 15% |
| 2 | 0.075 × G | 2 | 0.15G | 30% |
| 3 | 0.0375 × G | 4 | 0.15G | 45% |
| 4 | 0.01875 × G | 8 | 0.15G | 60% |
| 5 | 0.009375 × G | 16 | 0.15G | 75% |
| 6 | 0.0046875 × G | 32 | 0.15G | 90% |
| Broad base | remainder | variable | 0.10G | 100% |

Worked at G = $10,000,000:

| Tier | Gift size | Count | Tier total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lead | $1,500,000 | 1 | $1,500,000 |
| 2 | $750,000 | 2 | $1,500,000 |
| 3 | $375,000 | 4 | $1,500,000 |
| 4 | $187,500 | 8 | $1,500,000 |
| 5 | $93,750 | 16 | $1,500,000 |
| 6 | $46,875 | 32 | $1,500,000 |
| Broad base | ~$1,550 avg | ~645 | $1,000,000 |

**Test before adopting a goal:** can you name real prospects, by capacity rating, for at least the top 3 tiers (63-donor total in this example accounts for $6M, 60% of goal)? If the prospect database can't fill tiers 1-3, lower the goal or extend the timeline — don't skip to a bigger broad-base ask to close the gap.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| J. Alvarez | $500,000-$1M | Cultivation | Campus tour, Mar 3 | Invite to donor dinner | Apr 15 | not yet set |

| M. Osei | $1M+ | Stewardship | Gift received $600,000, Jan 10 | Impact report | Jun 1 | closed |

**Stage-exit rule:** a prospect moves to Solicitation only after two Cultivation-stage touches in the prior 6 months confirm both capacity and affinity — soliciting on the first meeting is the single most common cause of a declined major-gift ask.

## Ask letter (major gift, as delivered)

> Dear Margaret,
>
> Over the past year you've told me twice how much the Reading Room reading-support program mattered to your own path through college. I'd like to ask you to make that program permanent.
>
> A gift of $150,000 endows the Reading Room's tutoring coordinator position in perpetuity — the position you've watched operate on year-to-year grant funding for three years now. Your name would be on the position in the library's donor wall and in every student orientation packet describing the role.
>
> I'd like to walk you through the endowment structure in person. Are you free the week of April 14th?
>
> With gratitude,
> [Development Director]

## Stewardship letter (30 days post-gift, as delivered)

> Dear Margaret,
>
> Your $150,000 gift is now fully endowed — the Reading Room Tutoring Coordinator position carries your name starting this fall semester. Enclosed is the first cohort report: 214 students used tutoring hours last term, up from 180 the year the position ran on soft funding.
>
> You'll receive an annual impact report each September for as long as the endowment exists.

## Annual-fund LYBUNT/SYBUNT segmentation

| Segment | Definition | Typical response rate to reactivation appeal | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| LYBUNT | Gave last year, not this year | 12-18% | Phone + personal note, not mail blast |
| SYBUNT (1-2 yr lapsed) | Gave in a prior year, not last 2 years | 6-10% | Segmented mail with "we miss you" framing |
| SYBUNT (3+ yr lapsed) | Lapsed 3+ years | 2-4% | Low-cost digital only; don't spend phone time here |
| Never-lapsed | Gave every year on file | N/A — retention target, not reactivation | Standard renewal cadence |

Reactivation appeals to LYBUNT consistently outperform new-prospect acquisition mail on cost-per-dollar-raised; triage the LYBUNT list before greenlighting a new acquisition list buy.
