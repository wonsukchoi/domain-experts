# Red flags

Smell tests a structural ironworker, foreman, or safety officer catches in the first pass over an erection sequence, connection log, or near-miss.

### Crane hook released with only one bolt driven per connection

- **Usually means:** schedule pressure, or a connector treating "feels snug" as equivalent to the 2-bolt-wrench-tight minimum required before release.
- **First question:** how many bolts are actually driven wrench-tight at this connection right now, and did the signal person confirm the count before the hook released?
- **Data to pull:** connection bolt log/erection record for that member, signal person's confirmation, competent person's cantilever/eccentric-connection determination if one applies.

### Connector working above 30 ft (or past two stories) without conventional fall protection, citing the "connector exemption"

- **Usually means:** the crew conflated the 15–30 ft connector exemption with a blanket no-tie-off rule regardless of elevation.
- **First question:** what's the actual elevation above the lower level for this specific connection, and does it cross the 30 ft/two-story ceiling?
- **Data to pull:** erection drawing elevation, site fall-protection plan height bands, PFAS/positioning-device inspection log.

### Temporary guys or bracing struck before the erection plan's designated permanent lateral system is complete for that bay

- **Usually means:** the bay "looked done" (deck laid, most bolts driven) and the crew read visual completeness as structural completeness.
- **First question:** is the permanent lateral-force-resisting system and diaphragm actually complete for this bay per the erection plan, not just visually similar to a finished one?
- **Data to pull:** erection sequence/bracing plan, diaphragm weld or connection completion log, engineer-of-record sign-off if the plan requires one.

### Bolts called "torqued" with no record of which pretensioning method was used or verified

- **Usually means:** the crew snugged plus a few extra impact-wrench hits and treated that as pretension, without running the joint's specified acceptance check.
- **First question:** which method does the connection drawing specify for this joint, and where's the verification record — rotation angle, DTI gap, or calibrated-wrench torque test?
- **Data to pull:** connection detail/spec, match-marking or DTI inspection record, tension-calibrator (Skidmore-Wilhelm) log if calibrated wrench is the method.

### Controlled decking zone larger than 90 ft × 90 ft, or unsecured decking exceeding 3,000 sq ft

- **Usually means:** the crew extended the CDZ boundary toward a full floor for efficiency rather than recalculating against the numeric ceiling.
- **First question:** what are the actual measured dimensions of this CDZ and the current unsecured decking area, and do they exceed the regulatory limits?
- **Data to pull:** CDZ control-line survey/markup, decking installation log, CDZ-specific training roster for workers inside the zone.

### Column set with fewer than 4 anchor rods, or rods burned/enlarged to force a fit

- **Usually means:** a field fit-up problem was solved by grinding or burning rather than being flagged as a fabrication nonconformance, defeating the anchorage's designed 300 lb/18 in eccentric-load capacity.
- **First question:** does this column have its full designed anchor-rod count and undamaged rod/hole fit, or was a rod modified in the field?
- **Data to pull:** column base-connection drawing, field fit-up/nonconformance report, anchor-rod inspection record.

### Temporary bolts or drift pins left in a connection past the shift with no tracked plan for finish bolting

- **Usually means:** the raising gang is ahead of the bolting-up crew and nobody is tracking which connections in the bay are still temporary.
- **First question:** which connections in this bay are only pinned or snug versus finish-bolted, and is that status logged anywhere the next shift can see it?
- **Data to pull:** daily erection log/connection-status board, bolting-up crew schedule.

### Double connection at a column web forced together with bolts entered blind from below, no seat or clearance plan

- **Usually means:** the connection detail didn't account for wrench clearance where two members frame into opposite sides of the same column, and the crew improvised instead of flagging it.
- **First question:** does the connection detail specify a seat, clip, or blind-bolting device for this clearance conflict, and was it used?
- **Data to pull:** connection detail drawing, RFI/field-issue log if one was raised for this connection.

### Connector's fall-arrest or positioning-device anchorage tied to an unrated member

- **Usually means:** no independent rated anchorage was planned into the erection sequence at that location, so the crew tied off to whatever steel was closest.
- **First question:** what's the rated capacity of the anchorage point actually used, and does it meet the ≥5,000 lb (or engineered 2× safety factor) requirement?
- **Data to pull:** anchorage rating documentation, PFAS inspection tag, site fall-protection plan for that specific work area.

### Cantilevered or eccentric connection finished with only the standard 2 bolts, no competent-person review on record

- **Usually means:** the crew applied the general 2-bolt minimum without recognizing the connection's geometry as one the standard flags for an added-bolt determination.
- **First question:** has a competent person evaluated this specific connection's stability and determined whether more than 2 bolts are required before release?
- **Data to pull:** competent-person determination or erection-plan note for that connection, as-built bolt count at time of release.
