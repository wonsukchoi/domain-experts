# Red flags

Smell tests a seasoned patient representative catches in the first read of an intake. Format: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before doing anything else.

### Same complaint filed 3+ times from one unit in a quarter

- **Usually means:** a process failure on that unit (a discharge-instruction step being skipped, a rounding gap), not three unrelated "difficult patients."
- **First question:** "Pull the grievance log for this unit for the last 90 days — what category shows up more than once?"
- **Data to pull:** grievance category counts by unit and month; whether the same shift or role keeps appearing across the entries.

### Bill exceeds the Good Faith Estimate by $400 or more

- **Usually means:** either an itemized service (pathology, anesthesia) wasn't captured in the original estimate, or the estimate tool is systematically undershooting a common procedure.
- **First question:** "How many days has it been since the bill date, and is the patient self-pay or uninsured?" (PPDR eligibility depends on both.)
- **Data to pull:** GFE date/amount, bill date/amount, days remaining in the 120-day filing window, itemized charge breakdown.

### Family member requesting a status update the patient never authorized

- **Usually means:** a good-faith relative overstepping, or occasionally an active attempt to circumvent a patient's stated wishes (e.g., a contested custody or domestic-conflict situation).
- **First question:** "Is this person listed on the patient's authorized-contact list or facility directory entry?"
- **Data to pull:** admission consent form, facility directory opt-in status, any documented restriction on disclosure.

### Grievance file missing a dated written-response entry

- **Usually means:** the issue was actually resolved but never formally closed on paper — the exact gap a CMS survey audit is built to catch.
- **First question:** "When was the written response sent, and can you show me the dated copy?"
- **Data to pull:** correspondence log timestamps; compare against the organization's committed SLA (commonly 15–30 days depending on issue type).

### Registration collecting insurance information before the medical screening exam is documented complete

- **Usually means:** front-desk efficiency habits colliding with EMTALA sequencing requirements, not deliberate delay of care.
- **First question:** "Has the MSE been timestamped as complete in the chart yet?"
- **Data to pull:** ED triage/MSE completion timestamp vs. registration/insurance-collection timestamp for the same encounter.

### Medicare patient in observation status past 24 hours with no MOON on file

- **Usually means:** a NOTICE Act compliance gap, often from case management not tracking the 36-hour delivery clock once a stay crosses the 24-hour line.
- **First question:** "When did observation status start, and has the MOON been delivered and signed?"
- **Data to pull:** bed-status change log, MOON delivery/signature timestamp.

### Complaint closed same-day with no patient callback logged

- **Usually means:** the ticket was marked resolved based on the fix being made, not on the patient actually confirming satisfaction — a common way "resolved" cases quietly recur.
- **First question:** "Did anyone call the patient back after the fix, and is that call logged?"
- **Data to pull:** callback/follow-up log entry, timestamp relative to when the fix was implemented.

### Rep offers a refund or credit amount without checking delegated authority

- **Usually means:** good intentions outrunning policy, which risks a second broken promise when finance can't honor the number.
- **First question:** "What's your service-recovery fund ceiling for this case type, and does this amount fit inside it?"
- **Data to pull:** service-recovery fund policy tier, prior approvals for similar cases this quarter.

### Interpreter-line usage near zero on a unit with a high LEP patient census

- **Usually means:** staff are defaulting to family members or gesturing through language barriers instead of calling a qualified interpreter — a Title VI exposure and a consent-validity risk.
- **First question:** "What's this unit's qualified-interpreter utilization rate against its limited-English-proficiency patient census?"
- **Data to pull:** interpreter service utilization report, unit-level LEP census data, any documented family-interpreter incidents.

### HCAHPS "communication" domain scoring low while complaint volume stays flat or low

- **Usually means:** patients are dissatisfied but not complaining — a silent-dissatisfaction signal that complaint counts alone will never surface.
- **First question:** "Are nurse leader rounding logs actually being completed on this unit, or just charted as done?"
- **Data to pull:** rounding compliance rate, HCAHPS domain trend by unit, cross-reference against actual complaint volume for the same period.
