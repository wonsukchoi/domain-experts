### Highest and best use
The reasonably probable use of a property that is legally permissible, physically possible, financially feasible, and maximally productive — the basis for the value opinion, which may differ from the property's current use.
**In use:** "Zoning now allows multifamily on this lot — highest and best use analysis needs to consider that, not just value the existing single-family home."
**Common misuse:** Defaulting to valuing a property strictly based on its current improvement without testing whether a different use would be more valuable and realistically achievable.

### Sales comparison approach
A valuation method that derives value from adjusted sale prices of comparable properties, adjusting for differences in time, location, condition, size, and features.
**In use:** "Sales comparison approach reconciled to $438,000 after adjusting the three comparables."
**Common misuse:** Applying adjustment amounts that aren't derived from actual market evidence (paired-sales analysis) for the specific feature and market segment.

### Paired sales analysis
A method for extracting a specific adjustment amount by comparing two otherwise-similar sales that differ primarily in one feature (e.g., presence of a pool), isolating that feature's market-supported value contribution.
**In use:** "Paired sales analysis of two nearly identical properties, one with a pool and one without, shows the pool contributes about $15,000 in this market."
**Common misuse:** Using a generic, non-market-derived adjustment figure instead of extracting it from an actual paired comparison in the relevant market segment.

### Gross adjustment
The sum of all adjustment amounts (regardless of positive or negative direction) applied to a comparable sale, expressed as a percentage of its sale price, used as an indicator of the comparable's overall reliability.
**In use:** "Comparable 2's gross adjustment came to 6%, well within the reliable range, even though several individual adjustments were applied."
**Common misuse:** Netting positive and negative adjustments against each other to get a small "net adjustment" figure, which can mask a comparable that actually required substantial adjustment in multiple directions.

### Cost approach
A valuation method estimating value as the current cost to replace the improvements, less depreciation, plus land value.
**In use:** "Cost approach is most useful here since there are few comparable sales for this unique property type."
**Common misuse:** Relying on the cost approach for older properties with significant, hard-to-quantify depreciation, where sales comparison or income approach data is more reliable.

### Income approach
A valuation method estimating value based on a property's income-generating potential, using direct capitalization, gross rent multiplier, or discounted cash flow analysis.
**In use:** "Income approach, using a market cap rate applied to net operating income, supports a value closely aligned with the sales comparison approach."
**Common misuse:** Applying the income approach to owner-occupied residential property where it typically carries little weight, or ignoring it for genuinely investment-grade income property where it should carry significant weight.

### Reconciliation
The process of weighing the value indications from the sales comparison, cost, and income approaches into a single final value opinion, based on data reliability for the specific assignment.
**In use:** "Reconciliation weighted the sales comparison approach most heavily given strong comparable data, with the income approach as a secondary check."
**Common misuse:** Averaging the approaches together mechanically rather than reconciling them based on which approach's underlying data is most reliable for this specific property and market.

### Effective date of value
The specific date to which the value opinion applies — which can be the appraisal report's date, a retrospective (past) date, or a prospective (future) date, depending on the assignment.
**In use:** "This is a retrospective appraisal for an estate — effective date is the date of death, not today."
**Common misuse:** Using current market data and conditions to support a value opinion for a retrospective or prospective effective date that requires data from a different period.

### Appraiser Independence Requirements (AIR)
Legal requirements under mortgage lending regulations prohibiting loan production staff from selecting the appraiser or attempting to influence the value conclusion.
**In use:** "The loan officer can't have any contact with the appraiser about the target value — that's exactly what AIR is designed to prevent."
**Common misuse:** Treating appraiser independence as a professional courtesy rather than a specific legal requirement with real consequences for violations, regardless of whether the resulting value happened to be accurate.

### USPAP (Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice)
The generally accepted set of standards governing professional appraisal practice in the United States, covering ethics, competency, scope of work, and reporting requirements.
**In use:** "The report needs to document the scope of work and reconciliation logic per USPAP requirements."
**Common misuse:** Treating USPAP compliance as a formatting checklist rather than a substantive requirement governing the analysis and reporting process itself.
