### A comparable's total gross adjustment exceeds roughly 15-25% of its sale price
- **Usually means:** The comparable may not be truly comparable to the subject property, and its adjusted value indication should be weighted less heavily (or excluded) in reconciliation.
- **First question:** Is there a more similar comparable available that would require less extensive adjustment?
- **Data to pull:** Full adjustment grid for the comparable, alternative comparable sales in the same market segment.

### An adjustment amount (e.g., per-square-foot, per-bathroom, per-pool) is applied as a round, memorized figure across multiple assignments
- **Usually means:** The adjustment may not be currently supported by market evidence in this specific market segment — adjustment magnitudes shift over time and vary by submarket.
- **First question:** When was this adjustment amount last verified against actual paired-sales data in this specific market?
- **Data to pull:** Recent paired-sales analysis for the specific feature and market segment.

### A property's current improvement appears significantly underutilized relative to its zoning or realistic redevelopment potential
- **Usually means:** Highest and best use analysis may support a different (typically higher) value basis than a straightforward appraisal of the current improvement.
- **First question:** Has a highest and best use analysis (as vacant and as improved) been conducted, or was the value opinion built directly from the current use?
- **Data to pull:** Current zoning, comparable redevelopment/land sales in the area.

### The sales comparison and income approaches produce meaningfully divergent value indications for an income-producing property
- **Usually means:** One approach's underlying data (comparable sales quality vs. rent/expense reliability) may be significantly stronger than the other for this specific property, and reconciliation needs to reflect that rather than splitting the difference.
- **First question:** Which approach's underlying data is more reliable for this specific property and market — recent, comparable sales, or verified rent roll and expense data?
- **Data to pull:** Comparable sales data quality, property's actual rent roll and operating expense history.

### A loan officer or broker communicated a target value or loan amount to the appraiser before or during the assignment
- **Usually means:** A likely Appraiser Independence Requirements (AIR) violation, regardless of whether the eventual value opinion happens to be accurate.
- **First question:** Was this communication routed through an independent appraisal management function, or did it come directly from loan production staff with a stake in the transaction?
- **Data to pull:** Communication log for the assignment, lender's appraisal ordering/independence policy.

### A retrospective (past-dated) appraisal assignment uses current market comparable sales
- **Usually means:** The value opinion may not actually answer the assignment's question if it relies on market conditions not available or knowable as of the specified effective date.
- **First question:** Were the comparable sales used actually closed on or before the specified retrospective effective date?
- **Data to pull:** Comparable sale dates versus the assignment's stated effective date.

### An appraisal report's reconciled value simply averages the three approaches without explaining the weighting
- **Usually means:** The reconciliation may not reflect an actual judgment about data reliability, reading instead as a mechanical shortcut.
- **First question:** Does the report explain why each approach was weighted as it was, based on data quality and property type?
- **Data to pull:** The report's reconciliation section and its stated rationale (or absence of one).

### A comparable sale's verified transaction details (financing terms, arm's-length status) weren't checked before use
- **Usually means:** A non-arm's-length sale (family transfer, distressed sale, unusual seller-financing terms) could distort the value indication if used without adjustment or exclusion.
- **First question:** Has this specific sale been verified as arm's-length with market-standard financing terms?
- **Data to pull:** Public records, MLS remarks, or direct confirmation of the transaction's terms.
