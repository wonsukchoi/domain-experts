# Red flags

### Fingerboard/rack count off by even one stand (~90+ ft on a triple) from the driller's expected count
- **Usually means:** a miscount or double-stacked stand at a shift change; second most likely: pipe actually left downhole (a fish).
- **First question:** "was this stand physically recounted, slot by slot, or just re-estimated?"
- **Data to pull:** rig floor tally book against the driller's electronic depth/stand counter.

### H2S or multi-gas monitor alarms and no smell is noticed
- **Usually means:** concentration is already past the range where the nose is a reliable warning (olfactory fatigue); treat as real regardless of scent.
- **First question:** "what's the actual reading right now, not what did it smell like?"
- **Data to pull:** monitor log / data-logging gas detector readout for the exposure window.

### Asked to make up a connection, run elevators, or load a hole before being explicitly handed the task
- **Usually means:** scope creep from watching the task done many times, not an actual delegation.
- **First question:** "who directly handed me this task, and for this specific move?"
- **Data to pull:** none — confirm verbally with the driller, derrickhand, or shot firer before touching it.

### Blast exclusion zone flagged using yesterday's distance without recalculating today's charge weight
- **Usually means:** the crew is treating the pattern as routine instead of shot-specific.
- **First question:** "what's today's actual maximum charge weight per delay, and does the flag line match K × √W for it?"
- **Data to pull:** today's blast log / shot sheet charge-weight entry.

### Visible dust during rock cutting, crushing, or drilling with no water suppression or fitted respirator running
- **Usually means:** respirable silica exposure is already well past the OSHA action level (25 µg/m³) and likely past the PEL (50 µg/m³ 8-hr TWA).
- **First question:** "is the water system actually running, or just staged?"
- **Data to pull:** dust-control equipment status and, if available, the last area silica sampling result.

### A task-specific MSHA training hour category isn't logged for a helper assigned to that task
- **Usually means:** the helper is being worked outside their currently certified/supervised scope, which is a training-record and legal exposure issue, not just a documentation gap.
- **First question:** "does the training log show the required hours in this exact task category?"
- **Data to pull:** MSHA Part 46/48 training record for that individual and task code.

### Helper positioned inside the marked red zone while a load is suspended or a line is under tension
- **Usually means:** proximity is being used as a substitute for being explicitly called into position.
- **First question:** "did the driller or pusher call you in for this specific move, or did you move in on your own?"
- **Data to pull:** none in the moment — reposition immediately, then report if it was a standing habit, not a one-off.

### Tally or hour-log entries reconstructed from memory at end of tour instead of logged per checkpoint
- **Usually means:** entries will misstate counts or categories, and a real discrepancy from mid-shift may already be lost.
- **First question:** "was this logged at the checkpoint it happened, or written up now from memory?"
- **Data to pull:** timestamped tally book / gas-monitor log entries against the reconstructed version.

### Same stuck-pipe or lost-circulation event happening a second time on the same trip
- **Usually means:** the first event's root cause (hole condition, mud weight, tripping speed) wasn't actually resolved before resuming.
- **First question:** "what changed between the first event and resuming, or did we just try again?"
- **Data to pull:** mud log and trip-in-progress driller's report for both events.

### A blast crew member or helper is asked to remain in or re-enter the exclusion zone after loading has started
- **Usually means:** a miscommunication about clearance timing, or pressure to keep working through a scheduled clear-out.
- **First question:** "has the shot firer called the area cleared, or are we assuming it's fine because loading isn't finished?"
- **Data to pull:** the shot firer's clearance call log for that blast.

### New helper assigned to shadow a task with no MSHA-certified person actively present
- **Usually means:** the supervision requirement for that task category has quietly lapsed for the shift.
- **First question:** "who's the certified person actually on this task right now, not just scheduled for the shift?"
- **Data to pull:** shift roster against task assignments and certification currency.
