# Red flags

### Predicate's cleared intended use is narrower than the target label claim
- **Usually means:** the team picked the predicate for name recognition or same-company convenience, then wrote a broader claim after the fact.
- **First question:** "Read the predicate's actual indications-for-use statement — does it cover every claim we're about to make?"
- **Data to pull:** predicate's cleared 510(k) summary and indications-for-use statement from the FDA database.

### More than one Additional Information request already issued on this submission
- **Usually means:** the first response answered around the literal question instead of providing complete data, or the original submission had a gap the reviewer flagged once and re-flagged.
- **First question:** "Did we answer exactly what was asked, with the underlying dataset, or did we answer what we wished they'd asked?"
- **Data to pull:** the AI request letter text and the response as actually submitted, side by side.

### Marketing claim on the website or sales deck exceeds the cleared indication
- **Usually means:** marketing wrote copy from the product's capabilities rather than the cleared label, unaware the two have diverged.
- **First question:** "Is this claim on the cleared label, word for word — not implied by the label, on it?"
- **Data to pull:** current cleared indications-for-use statement and the specific marketing asset in question.

### Complaint rate on a single failure mode rises >20% quarter over quarter
- **Usually means:** a manufacturing or design change (even an unreported one) shifted a failure mode, or a new use environment is surfacing a latent issue.
- **First question:** "Has anything changed in manufacturing, suppliers, or labeling in the same window this trend started?"
- **Data to pull:** complaint database filtered by failure-mode code, trended by month, cross-referenced against the change log.

### A device change is being implemented via "letter to file" with no documented rationale
- **Usually means:** the team assumed the change was minor without running it through FDA's change-control decision tree.
- **First question:** "Could this change significantly affect safety or effectiveness — and where's the memo that says why not?"
- **Data to pull:** the change description, the 2017 FDA change-control decision tree, and the (missing) documented decision.

### EU technical file is a re-labeled version of the US 510(k) submission
- **Usually means:** the team assumed regulatory harmonization means one dossier ports across regions.
- **First question:** "Has a Notified Body actually reviewed this against MDR Annex II/III requirements, or are we assuming equivalence?"
- **Data to pull:** the MDR technical documentation checklist and the Notified Body's review correspondence.

### Clinical study protocol wasn't reviewed against the FDA-recognized consensus standard before enrollment started
- **Usually means:** the study was designed off a competitor's public study design rather than the current recognized standard, risking a data package FDA won't accept as sufficient.
- **First question:** "Which FDA-recognized consensus standard governs this device type, and did the protocol cite it before IRB submission?"
- **Data to pull:** the protocol's statistical and design section, and FDA's current list of recognized consensus standards for the device type.

### RTA checklist was completed by the same person who assembled the submission
- **Usually means:** confirmation bias — the preparer checks their own work as complete rather than adversarially.
- **First question:** "Who outside this submission's authoring team ran the RTA screen?"
- **Data to pull:** the RTA checklist sign-off record and the reviewer's role on the submission.

### A pre-submission (Q-Sub) meeting was skipped on a genuinely novel pathway question
- **Usually means:** time pressure led the team to file and hope, rather than get informal FDA feedback on a contested position.
- **First question:** "Is there a comparable cleared device with this exact technological characteristic, or is this the first of its kind?"
- **Data to pull:** predicate search results and any prior Q-Sub meeting minutes on adjacent products.

### Post-market surveillance plan wasn't updated after a labeling supplement
- **Usually means:** the supplement was treated as a documentation exercise rather than a trigger to revisit reportability criteria and complaint-handling scope.
- **First question:** "Does the current PMS plan cover the new indication's specific risk profile, or still just the original label's?"
- **Data to pull:** the PMS plan's revision history against the supplement approval date.
