# Postal Service Clerk — Red Flags

### Package is large/bulky but scale weight is under 5 lbs
- **Usually means:** cubic size likely exceeds the 1,728 cubic-inch DIM-pricing threshold, meaning the actual quotable rate is higher than a weight-only lookup suggests.
- **First question:** "Have I measured the dimensions and checked the cubic-size threshold, or am I quoting off the scale alone?"
- **Data to pull:** L × W × H measurement, DIM-weight calculation against the current divisor.

### Customer describes contents vaguely ("some liquids," "a few electronics," "personal items")
- **Usually means:** either an innocent lack of specificity, or the customer is unsure whether an item is restricted and hoping it won't come up.
- **First question:** "Can you tell me specifically what's inside — any liquids, batteries, or aerosols?"
- **Data to pull:** Itemized contents list; cross-reference against the current mailability/restricted-items reference.

### Stated declared value is significantly higher than the automatically included insurance
- **Usually means:** the customer assumes the base rate fully covers the item's value — a claims dispute waiting to happen if not corrected now.
- **First question:** "The base rate includes $100 of coverage — do you want to add insurance for the remaining value?"
- **Data to pull:** Declared value vs. class-specific included-coverage amount; additional-insurance fee schedule.

### Customer requests "the fastest and cheapest option that's trackable" without specifying what they need to prove
- **Usually means:** they haven't distinguished between proof-of-sending, proof-of-value, and simple tracking — recommending a class before clarifying risks over- or under-selling the service.
- **First question:** "Is this about proving it was sent, protecting its value if it's lost, or just knowing where it is along the way?"
- **Data to pull:** None — this is a clarifying question, not a lookup.

### Zone lookup returns a rate that seems inconsistent with the customer's stated distance (e.g., a nearby state quoting a high zone)
- **Usually means:** a zip-code entry error, or the customer is unaware that USPS zones are based on origin-processing-center distance, not straight-line geography.
- **First question:** "Can I confirm the exact destination zip code?"
- **Data to pull:** Origin and destination zip codes, zone chart lookup by zip pair (not by state or city name).

### Item is a battery-containing device (phone, laptop, power tool)
- **Usually means:** installed batteries typically have more permissive rules than loose batteries, but device type and battery chemistry (lithium-ion vs. lithium-metal) both affect eligibility — this isn't a blanket allow.
- **First question:** "Is the battery installed in the device, or shipped separately?"
- **Data to pull:** Battery type/chemistry if statable, device category, current hazmat reference entry for that battery class.

### Customer pushes back hard on a mailability refusal, insisting "I've mailed this exact thing before"
- **Usually means:** either restrictions changed since their last mailing, packaging/quantity differs from what they mailed before, or a different clerk previously let a violation through.
- **First question:** "Can you tell me exactly how it was packaged last time, and was the quantity the same?"
- **Data to pull:** Current Publication 52 entry for the item; do not let precedent override the current rule.

### Weight is exactly at or just over a price-bracket boundary (e.g., 1 lb 0.1 oz when the bracket breaks at 1 lb)
- **Usually means:** either the item genuinely qualifies for the next bracket, or the scale reading needs a second check — packaging settling, a stray finger on the scale, or a miscalibrated scale can all produce a false over-the-line reading.
- **First question:** "Let me re-weigh that to confirm."
- **Data to pull:** Re-weighed figure; scale calibration status if repeated discrepancies occur.

### Insured/registered item's declared value approaches the maximum insurable limit for the class
- **Usually means:** the item may need Registered Mail instead of standard insured Priority Mail, since Registered's insurable ceiling is substantially higher.
- **First question:** "Given the value, would Registered Mail's higher coverage limit and chain-of-custody tracking be a better fit?"
- **Data to pull:** Declared value, class-specific maximum insurable amount, Registered Mail's coverage ceiling.
