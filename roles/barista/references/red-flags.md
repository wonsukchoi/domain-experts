# Red flags

Smell tests a barista catches within the first few shots of a shift or the first sip of a complaint. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before changing anything.

### Shot pulls in under ~20s to full 1:2 yield with pale, streaky crema

- **Usually means:** channeling from poor distribution (clumps, uneven tamp) — a too-coarse grind is the second-likeliest cause, not the first.
- **First question:** "Did you WDT and distribute before tamping, or just tamp and go?"
- **Data to pull:** dose and yield weights and time from the shot log, grind setting versus yesterday's on this bag, a look at the spent puck for channel marks or craters.

### Shot runs past ~35s to reach yield and tastes harsh or astringent

- **Usually means:** grind too fine, beans past their usable freshness window, or a dirty group screen/gasket restricting flow — roughly in that order of likelihood.
- **First question:** "When was this group head last backflushed?"
- **Data to pull:** shot-time trend across the day, days-since-roast on the bag, the cleaning log.

### Same grind setting, two stations, two different shot times back-to-back

- **Usually means:** dosing or distribution inconsistency between baristas, not the grinder drifting.
- **First question:** "Is dose being weighed on a scale at both stations, or eyeballed at one of them?"
- **Data to pull:** dose-weight variance logged across stations over the last hour.

### Milk pitcher too hot to hold bare-handed before pouring

- **Usually means:** steamed past the ~150–155°F (65–68°C) serving target into scald range, likely with the sweetness flattened and an eggy off-note.
- **First question:** "Was that timed or thermometer-checked, or judged by pitcher feel?"
- **Data to pull:** sampled pitcher temperatures during the rush, any customer complaints logged as "burnt" or "off" milk taste.

### Crema disappears within a few seconds of the shot finishing

- **Usually means:** beans past their usable freshness window (well past 3–4 weeks off-roast) or a genuinely over-extracted shot.
- **First question:** "What's the roast date on this bag?"
- **Data to pull:** roast-to-use date gap, an extraction-yield spot check if a refractometer is on hand.

### New bag "tastes different" and staff keep re-pulling without changing settings

- **Usually means:** dial-in was never redone for this bag's density and roast profile — the grind setting was simply carried over from the last bag.
- **First question:** "Did we re-dial-in on this bag, or keep yesterday's grinder setting?"
- **Data to pull:** grind-setting log tied to bag and roast batch, the first test-pull's extraction yield on the new bag.

### Queue backing up during rush with pitchers being re-steamed mid-drink

- **Usually means:** a batching or fill-line problem, not understaffing — pitchers filled wrong for the combined order size force a second steam.
- **First question:** "Were pitchers filled to the line for the full batch of drinks, or per single drink?"
- **Data to pull:** average order-to-drink cycle time during the rush window, milk-dump/waste log, ticket times versus drink-ready times.

### Latte art consistently blows out — no defined pattern, foam separates from milk on pour

- **Usually means:** milk over-stretched (too airy/coarse foam for the pour) or poured from the wrong height/angle for the pattern being attempted.
- **First question:** "Was air being added right up to pull-off, or stopped around 100°F before texturing?"
- **Data to pull:** pitcher temperature at the point stretching stopped, a look at the foam's bubble size before pouring.

### Customer says "sour" on a shot logged at the correct time and ratio

- **Usually means:** channeling that happened to self-correct on time (the channel closed or reopened mid-shot) while still under-extracting, or beans genuinely too fresh (under ~4 days off roast) producing excess CO2 disruption.
- **First question:** "What's the roast date, and did the pull look even the whole way through, no streaking?"
- **Data to pull:** roast date, any visual record of the pull, an extraction-yield check if available.

### Same recipe tastes fine in the morning, bitter or sour by afternoon with no settings changed

- **Usually means:** ambient humidity or heat shifting bean moisture and grind behavior through the day, or hopper-level/static effects skewing dose as the bag empties.
- **First question:** "Has the grind setting been rechecked since opening, or is it running on the morning setting?"
- **Data to pull:** dose-weight consistency logged AM versus PM, ambient temperature/humidity if the shop tracks it.
