# Playbook

Filled templates for the three recurring operational sequences: dial-in on a new bag, milk-steaming batch order during a rush, and a periodic bean QC cupping.

## Dial-in log (per bag, per shift)

Real filled example, continued from the worked example in `SKILL.md`:

| Bag / origin | Ethiopia Yirgacheffe, washed |
| Roast date | 6 days before this log |

| Recheck window | early afternoon — denser bean, watch for drift as hopper empties |

**Rule for the log, not just the numbers:** always record what changed (grind setting *and* technique, e.g., WDT) alongside the taste and EY outcome — a setting number with no technique note is useless to the next barista if the fix was actually distribution, not grind.

## Milk-steaming batch sequence (rush order)

When three or more milk drinks are queued, texture in one pass per pitcher rather than one steam per drink:

1. **Fill to the line for the batch**: pitcher filled to the dimple/curve appropriate for total combined volume needed (e.g., a 20oz pitcher filled to ~40% for two 8oz cappuccinos plus foam headroom, not filled per-drink and re-steamed).
2. **Stretch phase (introduce air) only below ~100°F (38°C)** — a few seconds of tip-just-under-surface positioning; stop stretching once past this point even if more foam volume seems wanted, or the batch ends up over-foamed for a latte and under-foamed for the cappuccino it's meant to also serve.
3. **Texture phase (submerge deeper, no more air) from ~100°F to pull-off** — spin the milk to fold existing foam smaller without adding volume.
4. **Pull off heat at ~140°F (60°C)** by thermometer during training, by pitcher-heat/hand-tolerance and sound (steam whistle drops in pitch) once calibrated; carryover lands the poured drink around 150–155°F (65–68°C).
5. **Split the batch by drink**: pour the silkier top portion into the latte(s) first, reserve the denser foam underneath for the cappuccino(s) — one steamed pitcher correctly split serves both drink types instead of steaming twice.
6. **Discard and re-steam if the pitcher exceeds ~160°F (71°C)** at any point — past this, treat the whole batch as scalded and start over rather than serving a flattened-sweetness drink to save time.

## Cupping check (periodic bean QC, not shift dial-in)

Distinct from daily dial-in — this evaluates the bean itself against the roaster's stated profile, run weekly or on receipt of a new roast batch:

1. Grind a fixed amount (SCA protocol: ~8.25g per 150ml water, coarse drip grind) directly into cupping bowls, one bowl per bag/batch being checked.
2. Smell the dry grounds (fragrance), then pour near-boiling water and smell again at 3–5 minutes (aroma) before breaking the crust.
3. Break the crust, skim foam, and taste at progressively cooling temperatures, scoring against the SCA 100-point form categories (fragrance/aroma, flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, balance, sweetness, uniformity, clean cup, overall).
4. Compare the resulting score and flavor notes against the roaster's stated profile for that batch — a bag that cups meaningfully off the stated notes is a supplier conversation, not a dial-in problem to solve on the espresso machine.
5. Log the cupping score and date against the bag so a later "this bag tastes different" report can be checked against a baseline instead of memory.
