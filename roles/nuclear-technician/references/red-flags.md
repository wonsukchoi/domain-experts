# Red flags

### Posted general-area survey more than 24 hours old, with work, lineup change, or material movement in that area since
- **Usually means:** conditions have shifted since posting — a hot spot from a lineup change, a source moved, or waste staged nearby — and the posting no longer reflects the point of work.
- **First question:** "has anything changed at or near this location since the survey was posted?"
- **Data to pull:** survey posting timestamp, work order/clearance history and lineup change log for the area.

### Electronic dosimeter alarm triggers mid-job
- **Usually means:** pre-job dose rate was underestimated (hot spot missed in the survey) or the worker was positioned closer to the source than briefed; second most likely is instrument malfunction.
- **First question:** "what was the worker's actual position and dwell time at the moment the alarm fired?"
- **Data to pull:** EPD dose/dose-rate log, the RWP's point-of-work survey data, the pre-job stay-time calculation.

### Personnel frisker alarms above the plant setpoint (commonly background + ~300 cpm beta-gamma) on exit
- **Usually means:** actual removable contamination on skin or clothing; second most likely is ambient background drift or a detector fault.
- **First question:** "does it repeat on an immediate re-frisk at the same location with the same detector?"
- **Data to pull:** daily background log for that frisker, contamination survey of the area/item the worker last handled.

### Air sample result reaches roughly 25% of DAC for the radionuclide present
- **Usually means:** an airborne-radioactivity-generating activity (grinding, cutting, resuspension) started or intensified beyond what the RWP assumed.
- **First question:** "what work activity began or changed in this area at the time the sample was drawn?"
- **Data to pull:** continuous air monitor trend, area work-activity log, respiratory protection currently in use.

### Job's running collective dose exceeds its ALARA planning estimate by roughly 20% or more against comparable historical jobs
- **Usually means:** job scope grew mid-task, dose rates were higher than assumed, or the task is taking longer than planned.
- **First question:** "which phase of the job is consuming dose faster than budgeted?"
- **Data to pull:** job-specific dose tracking sheet vs. the original ALARA planning estimate and historical comparables.

### Item or area contamination survey shows removable activity above the site's free-release limit (commonly on the order of 1,000 dpm/100 cm² beta-gamma, radionuclide-specific)
- **Usually means:** a decontamination step was missed, or the item's radiological classification (clean vs. contaminated) was recorded incorrectly.
- **First question:** "what's this item's material and radiological history, and what was the last decon step performed?"
- **Data to pull:** item's release survey record, decontamination log.

### A worker's year-to-date dose approaches roughly 2 rem (2,000 mrem) — well below the 5 rem legal limit but above a typical administrative control level
- **Usually means:** the worker has been repeatedly assigned to high-dose jobs without dose being spread across the crew, or there's a tracking/data-entry error.
- **First question:** "has this worker's remaining annual dose allowance been checked against upcoming job assignments?"
- **Data to pull:** dose-of-record (TLD/OSL) history, work assignment schedule for the worker.

### Daily instrument source check reads outside roughly ±20% of its expected value
- **Usually means:** the instrument is drifting out of calibration, has a weak/dying battery, or is the wrong detector type for the radiation present.
- **First question:** "when was the last full calibration, and does this detector match the radiation type expected here?"
- **Data to pull:** calibration sticker/date, source-check log history for the instrument.

### RWP authorizes work with a stated stay time but no documented dose-rate-to-time calculation on the pre-job briefing form
- **Usually means:** the ALARA planning step was skipped and the stay time was estimated verbally ("should be fine") rather than calculated.
- **First question:** "what dose rate was used to derive this stay time, and where is that survey?"
- **Data to pull:** RWP worksheet, pre-job ALARA/stay-time calculation form.

### Two consecutive shifts report different dose rates at the same monitored point with no documented plant condition change
- **Usually means:** one shift's instrument is miscalibrated, or a transient condition (temporary shielding removed, source relocated) went undocumented.
- **First question:** "did anything physically change at that location between the two readings?"
- **Data to pull:** shift turnover log, radiological work history for that specific point.

### Whole-body count or bioassay requested only when a worker reports symptoms, never as routine follow-up after a confirmed skin contamination event
- **Usually means:** the intake-assessment step of the contamination response procedure was skipped because the external contamination was resolved and treated as the whole event.
- **First question:** "was an intake pathway (inhalation/ingestion/wound) ruled out, not just the external contamination decontaminated?"
- **Data to pull:** contamination event report, follow-up bioassay/WBC schedule for the affected worker.
