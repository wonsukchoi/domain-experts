# Red flags

Smell tests a solar thermal installer or design reviewer catches on a permit-set review, a site walk, or a service call. Format for each: what it usually means, the first question to ask, and the data to pull before signing off.

## 1. Storage tank sized below 1.5 gal per sq ft of collector aperture area

**Usually means:** the tank was picked off a "one tank per collector" habit rather than the site's actual aperture area — an undersized tank pushes the system into stagnation on more days than necessary.

**First question:** "What's the collector's rated aperture area, and does the tank size clear 1.5 gal/sq ft against that number?"

**Data to pull:** the collector's SRCC OG-100 aperture-area rating; the specified tank's rated volume; the recalculated minimum against the aperture area.

## 2. Glycol concentration below the freeze point needed for the site's design-low temperature, with no margin

**Usually means:** the mix was set at a round "standard" percentage instead of computed against the site's actual ASHRAE 99% design-low temperature — the failure that doesn't show until the one night it mattered.

**First question:** "What's this site's design-low temperature, and does the specified glycol concentration's freeze point clear it by at least 10°F?"

**Data to pull:** the manufacturer's freeze-point-by-concentration table for the specific fluid; the site's ASHRAE 99% design-low or local record-low figure.

## 3. Single-wall heat exchanger between the glycol loop and potable water, no AHJ variance on file

**Usually means:** the design skipped the double-wall requirement most plumbing codes impose for non-potable heat-transfer fluids — a pinhole leak lets glycol and its inhibitor package migrate into drinking water.

**First question:** "Is there a documented AHJ variance for a single-wall exchanger with this specific fluid, or does code default to double-wall here?"

**Data to pull:** the local plumbing code's adopted edition and heat-exchanger provisions; any variance paperwork on file; the heat exchanger's listing documentation.

## 4. No pressure-relief valve rated above the system's calculated stagnation pressure

**Usually means:** the relief valve was sized off "standard practice" rather than the actual fluid volume and stagnation temperature this specific collector array reaches — a valve that lifts too early dumps and concentrates glycol repeatedly; one rated too low for actual stagnation pressure risks a component failure instead of a controlled relief event.

**First question:** "What pressure does this system reach at full stagnation, and is the relief valve rated above that number?"

**Data to pull:** collector no-load stagnation temperature (manufacturer data); system fluid volume; relief valve's rated set pressure.

## 5. Expansion tank sized off a flat "2-gallon standard" number, no calculation against actual system fluid volume shown

**Usually means:** the tank wasn't checked against this system's actual glycol-loop fluid volume — an undersized tank relieves fluid out the pressure valve on the first hot, low-draw week.

**First question:** "What's the total glycol-loop fluid volume, and what acceptance volume does the specified tank actually deliver at that volume?"

**Data to pull:** measured or calculated system fluid volume (collectors + piping + heat-exchanger primary side); the expansion tank manufacturer's rated acceptance volume, not just total tank volume.

## 6. Differential controller cycling the pump on and off within a few minutes on a partly cloudy day

**Usually means:** the ON/OFF deadband is set too tight for the sensor placement and collector response time, adding pump wear without adding delivered heat.

**First question:** "What are the controller's ON and OFF differential setpoints, and do they match the manufacturer's recommended deadband for this sensor configuration?"

**Data to pull:** controller setpoint log or configuration screen; pump run-time log if available; manufacturer's recommended deadband range.

## 7. No tempering (anti-scald mixing) valve at the solar storage tank outlet

**Usually means:** the design treated the tank's setpoint as fixed the way a standard water heater's is, missing that solar can push the tank well above that setpoint on a high-solar day with no draw.

**First question:** "What's the highest temperature this tank can reach on a high-solar, low-draw day, and what's actually limiting the fixture temperature a person touches?"

**Data to pull:** tank high-limit and stagnation-adjacent temperature range; presence and setpoint of a thermostatic mixing valve at the outlet.

## 8. Glycol not tested (pH / freeze point) in 3+ years of service

**Usually means:** the fluid has been through repeated stagnation cycles and is likely acidic and corrosive to the loop's copper and brazed joints — glycol degrades under heat cycling even without a leak or dump event.

**First question:** "When was this glycol last tested, and what did the pH and freeze-point readings come back at?"

**Data to pull:** service log with prior glycol test results; a current refractometer reading and pH test strip result.

## 9. Drainback collector loop with a documented or visible low point/sag in the piping run

**Usually means:** the loop can't fully drain by gravity at that point, leaving standing water that defeats the freeze protection the drainback design exists to provide.

**First question:** "Does this run maintain a minimum 1/4 in per foot pitch back to the reservoir the entire way, with no traps or sags?"

**Data to pull:** as-built piping layout with elevations; a visual or camera inspection of the run for sags.

## 10. Tax credit or rebate claimed with no documented SRCC OG-300 system match for the installed equipment

**Usually means:** the installed collector, tank, and controller combination may not match the certified OG-300 system the incentive paperwork assumes — a component substitution after certification, even an equivalent one, can invalidate the match.

**First question:** "Does the as-installed equipment list match the OG-300 certificate's listed components exactly, or was anything substituted after design?"

**Data to pull:** the OG-300 certificate for the specified system; the as-installed equipment list and model numbers.
