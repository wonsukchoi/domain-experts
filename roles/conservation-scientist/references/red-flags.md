# Conservation Scientist — Red Flags

### Stocking rate quoted from an ecological site description average, not current-year production
- **Usually means:** the planner skipped a current-year forage clip/estimate and used a long-term or "normal precipitation year" ESD figure instead.
- **First question:** "What was this year's measured or estimated forage production, and how does it compare to the ESD's stated normal-year figure?"
- **Data to pull:** current-season clipping/double-sample data, precipitation record for the growing season vs. 30-year average.

### Proper-use factor set at a flat 50% regardless of range condition
- **Usually means:** the plan is using a textbook default instead of the site's actual condition-class-adjusted harvest rate.
- **First question:** "What range condition class does the current transect data support, and what proper-use factor does that class call for?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent line-point intercept or Parker 3-step transect data, ESD reference-condition comparison.

### Grazing lease locked at a fixed AUM rate for multiple years with no re-clip clause
- **Usually means:** the lease was written to a single year's production and doesn't adjust for drought or above-average years, creating chronic over- or under-use.
- **First question:** "Does the lease include an annual re-assessment clause, or is the AUM rate fixed regardless of yearly production?"
- **Data to pull:** lease document terms, multi-year precipitation and production trend data for the allotment.

### Prescribed-fire plan specifies a calendar date without weather-trigger parameters
- **Usually means:** the burn plan was written as an administrative schedule item rather than a fire-behavior prescription.
- **First question:** "What are the prescribed fuel-moisture, humidity, wind, and mixing-height ranges this burn must fall within?"
- **Data to pull:** the written burn prescription's parameter table, most recent spot-weather forecast.

### Two consecutive monitoring readings show declining range or forest condition
- **Usually means:** current management (stocking rate, harvest level, rotation timing) exceeds what the site can sustain, and a third "wait and see" reading will confirm what's already indicated.
- **First question:** "What changed in use level or timing between the last two readings, and does the decline track a specific pasture, season, or use type?"
- **Data to pull:** trend-monitoring transect history (minimum 2-3 reading cycles), stocking or harvest records for the same period.

### Multi-use conflict resolved by negotiating with each stakeholder separately
- **Usually means:** the allocation may not actually be internally consistent — separate deals can double-commit the same acreage or season to conflicting uses.
- **First question:** "Has the full allocation been mapped against total available AUMs/board feet/habitat capacity in one document, or only against each party's individual ask?"
- **Data to pull:** the combined allocation plan showing all uses against total site capacity, not per-stakeholder correspondence.

### Timber cruise-based allowable cut exceeds the stand's measured annual growth increment
- **Usually means:** the harvest plan is drawing down standing volume rather than harvesting at a sustained-yield rate.
- **First question:** "What is the stand's measured mean annual increment, and how does the proposed cut compare to it?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent timber cruise data, growth-and-yield model output for the stand's site index and age class.

### Wildlife habitat objective stated as a qualitative goal with no measurable threshold
- **Usually means:** the plan can't be checked against field data later, so it will default to whatever level of disturbance the other uses generate.
- **First question:** "What specific, measurable habitat metric (canopy cover %, forage/cover ratio, nesting-season closure dates) defines success for this objective?"
- **Data to pull:** the habitat objective's stated metric and baseline value, if any exists.

### Riparian buffer width set by a single fixed number regardless of stream order or slope
- **Usually means:** the plan applied a blanket buffer standard instead of a slope- and stream-order-adjusted width appropriate to sediment and shade function.
- **First question:** "What stream order and adjacent slope class is this buffer covering, and does the applied width match the agency or watershed-specific guidance for that class?"
- **Data to pull:** stream classification data, applicable state or watershed-plan buffer-width table.
