# Vocabulary

### Circulating load
The ratio of material recirculated back to a mill from a classifier, as oversize, relative to fresh feed rate — a key grinding circuit efficiency variable distinct from mill power or throughput.
**In use:** "Circulating load's running high — that's overloading the classifier, not a mill power problem."
**Common misuse:** Evaluating grinding circuit performance using only mill power draw and throughput, without checking circulating load ratio as a separate, informative diagnostic variable.

### Classifier cut point
The particle size at which a classifier separates material into finished product (finer) versus recirculated oversize (coarser), which must be set to match the target product size specification.
**In use:** "Cut point's at 150 microns — that's coarser than the 106-micron spec, so tighten it before chasing this with mill time."
**Common misuse:** Leaving classifier cut point at a default or habitual setting regardless of the current target product size, assuming mill grinding intensity alone determines final product size.

### Closed-circuit grinding
A grinding configuration where a mill's output passes through a classifier, with oversize material recirculated back to the mill rather than the mill's single-pass output being the final product.
**In use:** "This is closed-circuit — the classifier, not a single mill pass, is what actually sets final product size."
**Common misuse:** Assuming mill residence time or power alone determines final product size in a closed circuit, when the classifier is what actually determines the maximum size reporting to finished product.

### Grinding media
The balls, rods, or other charge material inside a mill that provides the actual grinding action against the feed material, sized appropriately for the target particle size reduction.
**In use:** "Switch to smaller media for this finer target — the media loaded from the last coarse job won't get us there efficiently."
**Common misuse:** Reusing whatever grinding media size happens to be loaded from a prior job rather than matching media size to the current target size reduction task.

### Critical speed (mill)
The rotational speed at which a mill's grinding media would theoretically centrifuge against the mill wall instead of cascading, used as a reference point, expressed as a percentage, for setting effective operating speed.
**In use:** "We're running at 75% of critical speed — that's the cascading regime this grind actually needs."
**Common misuse:** Operating a mill at a fixed RPM without calculating it as a percentage of critical speed, risking an inefficient grinding action for the intended grinding task.

### Sieve analysis / laser diffraction (particle size)
Direct measurement methods for verifying a material's actual particle size distribution, as opposed to inferring it from process parameters.
**In use:** "Pull a sample for laser diffraction — power draw looks normal, but that doesn't confirm the size distribution."
**Common misuse:** Judging product size adequacy from mill power draw, throughput, or elapsed processing time rather than direct particle size measurement.

### Cascading vs. cataracting (mill media motion)
Two distinct types of grinding media motion inside a rotating mill — cascading (media rolls/tumbles along the surface) and cataracting (media is thrown clear and falls with impact) — each suited to different grinding objectives.
**In use:** "We want cascading action for this fine grind, not the cataracting motion the higher speed would produce."
**Common misuse:** Assuming any media motion inside the mill produces equivalent grinding action, without considering which motion type is actually appropriate for the current grinding objective.

### Fresh feed rate
The rate at which new, unprocessed material enters a grinding circuit, distinct from the total material flow through the mill, which includes recirculated oversize.
**In use:** "Fresh feed's 100 tonnes/hour, but total mill throughput is higher once you count the circulating load."
**Common misuse:** Confusing fresh feed rate with total mill throughput, when circulating load means the mill is actually processing significantly more total material than the fresh feed rate alone suggests.

### 80% passing (particle size specification)
A common way of expressing a target particle size specification, meaning 80% of the material by weight passes through a screen of the specified size.
**In use:** "Spec is 80% passing 106 microns — that's a distribution requirement, not just an average size target."
**Common misuse:** Treating a single average particle size figure as equivalent to a full passing-percentage specification, missing that the actual distribution shape, not just an average, determines compliance.

### Classifier separation efficiency
The degree to which a classifier achieves a sharp cut at its nominal cut point size, as opposed to allowing some oversize material through and some undersize material to be misdirected.
**In use:** "Classifier's separation efficiency isn't perfect — set the nominal cut point somewhat below the actual target to account for that."
**Common misuse:** Assuming a classifier's nominal cut point setting is a perfectly sharp size cutoff, when actual separation efficiency means some material outside the nominal cut point still reports to the "wrong" stream.
