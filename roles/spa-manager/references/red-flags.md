# Red flags — signals a spa manager notices instantly

Smell tests with thresholds. Any one alone can be a fixable gap; two or more together is a pattern worth an immediate operational or compliance review.

### Room-hour utilization below 65% alongside a hiring request
- **Usually means:** the actual problem is likely scheduling fragmentation or menu-duration mismatch, not insufficient staff — hiring won't raise the room-hour ceiling and may just add idle payroll.
- **First question:** "Before we discuss headcount, what's the fragmentation loss per room per day, and has the menu-duration matrix been reviewed in the last year?"
- **Data to pull:** booking-system utilization report, gap analysis by service duration, current vs. standardized menu-duration matrix (see `references/playbook.md` §2).

### Retail attach rate under 10% with adequate foot traffic
- **Usually means:** an underdeveloped retail program (training, display, incentive) rather than a genuine lack of client interest — retail doesn't consume the room-hour constraint, so this is close to free revenue being left unclaimed.
- **First question:** "Is the gap concentrated in specific therapists or service lines, or consistent across the board?"
- **Data to pull:** retail attach rate by therapist and by service line, current training/incentive program documentation, comparison to ISPA benchmark data.

### A therapist's license renewal date has passed with no documented verification
- **Usually means:** the spa may be continuing to book and bill for services performed under a lapsed license — a direct regulatory violation regardless of the therapist's actual competence.
- **First question:** "When was this license status last verified against the state board's database, and is that check happening on a recurring schedule or only when someone remembers?"
- **Data to pull:** license renewal tracker, state board verification records, booking log for the period in question cross-referenced against license status.

### A "contractor" therapist whose schedule, room, equipment, and prices are all set by the spa
- **Usually means:** the classification doesn't meet the independent-contractor legal test regardless of what the engagement letter says — this is a misclassification exposure, not a settled cost-saving arrangement.
- **First question:** "Walk the classification checklist for this role — how many of the employee-indicating factors are true?"
- **Data to pull:** worker-classification checklist (see `references/playbook.md` §4), current engagement/contract terms, actual day-to-day scheduling and equipment-provision practice.

### Cancellation fee enforcement rate trending down over consecutive months
- **Usually means:** front-desk staff are increasingly waiving the fee case-by-case, which both erodes its economic effect and weakens its standing as a defensible, consistently applied policy.
- **First question:** "Of the fees triggered this month, what percentage were actually charged versus waived, and by whom?"
- **Data to pull:** cancellation/no-show log with charge-vs-waived status, staff member who processed each transaction, trend over the trailing 6 months.

### Marketing spend increasing while utilization is already above 80%
- **Usually means:** there's limited remaining room-hour inventory to sell into — additional demand generation at this point mostly creates frustrated clients unable to book, not incremental revenue.
- **First question:** "What's current utilization, and has a price increase or capacity expansion been evaluated instead of more marketing spend?"
- **Data to pull:** utilization trend, current price points versus comparable-market rates, marketing spend and its attributed booking lift.

### A service on the menu with no therapist currently holding the license/certification to perform it
- **Usually means:** either a scheduling gap waiting to cause a client-facing failure, or the service is being performed by someone outside their licensed scope.
- **First question:** "Who on staff is actually licensed for this specific service, and is it reflected in the booking system's service-to-therapist mapping?"
- **Data to pull:** service menu, staff licensing/certification records, booking system's service-eligibility configuration.

### High therapist turnover concentrated in the highest-utilization time slots
- **Usually means:** peak-hour scheduling burden or compensation structure isn't matching the workload those slots demand, risking a service-quality or no-show cascade during the spa's highest-value hours.
- **First question:** "What's the compensation and schedule-density difference between peak-slot therapists and off-peak therapists?"
- **Data to pull:** turnover by shift/time-slot, compensation structure by slot, exit-interview themes if any.

### Sanitation/health-code log with gaps or missing entries for treatment room turnover
- **Usually means:** either the logging practice has lapsed or the actual sanitation procedure between clients has lapsed — both are a direct inspection and liability risk.
- **First question:** "Are the gaps in the log itself, or in the actual turnover procedure being followed?"
- **Data to pull:** sanitation log for the period, state health department inspection history, staff training records on turnover protocol.
