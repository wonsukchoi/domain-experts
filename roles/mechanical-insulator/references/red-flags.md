# Mechanical Insulator — red flags

### Surface temperature within 5°F of ambient dew point on a below-ambient line
- **Usually means:** a vapor-retarder breach or a thin spot upstream of this reading; second most likely: a local thermal bridge (unshielded support, valve, flange) rather than a wall-thickness problem.
- **First question:** what's the nearest penetration, seam, or support upcurrent of this reading?
- **Data to pull:** IR scan log against the day's ambient dry-bulb/RH, and the design dew point used for the original spec.

### Staining or discoloration aligned with a jacket seam or fastener line
- **Usually means:** moisture wicking under the jacket from a breached lap or seal; second most likely: PVC jacket UV degradation if the run is outdoor or skylit.
- **First question:** does the stain track the seam/fastener line exactly, or is it randomly distributed?
- **Data to pull:** jacket inspection photos plus the seam-seal method called out on the approved submittal.

### More than 10% of hangers/supports on a below-ambient run lack a rigid insulated shield
- **Usually means:** an incomplete field install or a value-engineered substitution that wasn't caught in QC; second most likely: a support added after the original insulation pass (retrofit hanger) that never got the detail.
- **First question:** was the approved support detail actually followed, or substituted in the field without a submittal change?
- **Data to pull:** support schedule versus as-built photo log, hanger by hanger.

### Pre-1981 pipe, boiler, duct, or tank insulation, unlabeled
- **Usually means:** presumed ACM under OSHA 1926.1101 until a bulk sample clears it; treat as regulated material regardless of visual condition.
- **First question:** has a bulk sample been submitted and has the lab result come back?
- **Data to pull:** bulk-sample lab report or the building's asbestos survey/O&M plan.

### Duct liner or wrap delaminating on a supply-side run above roughly 2,500 fpm design velocity
- **Usually means:** wrong-density material specified for the velocity class — standard duct wrap erodes/delaminates under sustained high-velocity airflow that a higher-density board or liner would tolerate.
- **First question:** what design velocity did the mechanical schedule call for at this specific duct segment?
- **Data to pull:** duct design schedule and, if available, an air-balance report for the segment.

### Exposed surface above 140°F within roughly 8 ft of a walkway or platform, no guard or added insulation
- **Usually means:** the personnel-protection case was never evaluated separately from the thermal-efficiency case during design.
- **First question:** is this location inside the ASTM C1055 contact-risk zone as built, not as originally designed?
- **Data to pull:** as-built walkway/platform drawing plus a current IR surface-temperature reading.

### Vapor-retarder facing spec silent on perm rating, or facing tested above 0.1 perm on a continuously below-ambient system
- **Usually means:** the facing was selected for cost or appearance, not permeance — a common value-engineering substitution that looks identical on the submittal cover sheet.
- **First question:** what's the facing's tested perm rating per ASTM E96, and does the submittal actually show it?
- **Data to pull:** manufacturer's facing data sheet with the E96 test result.

### Visible sagging or measurable weight gain on a horizontal cold-pipe insulation run
- **Usually means:** saturated insulation from an internal condensation/vapor-drive point; second most likely: an actual leak in the piping system itself.
- **First question:** when was this section last opened, and what did the insulation look like inside at that time?
- **Data to pull:** prior maintenance log for this segment, and a moisture-meter reading if the section is accessible without full removal.

### Firestop detail at a wall/floor penetration doesn't cite a matching UL system number
- **Usually means:** a generic firestop detail was reused across penetration types that actually have different annular-space or insulation configurations than the tested assembly.
- **First question:** does this penetration's annular space and insulation type match the cited UL system's tested configuration exactly?
- **Data to pull:** UL system directory listing and the firestop submittal for this penetration.

### CUI (corrosion under insulation) found at a jacket seam or support during a turnaround inspection
- **Usually means:** the location has been cyclically wet for an extended period, not a single recent event.
- **First question:** does this staining pattern appear in prior turnaround inspection reports for the same tag number?
- **Data to pull:** prior turnaround/inspection reports for that equipment or line tag.

### Insulation thickness matches ASHRAE 90.1 minimum exactly, on a line in a documented high-humidity space (design RH consistently above ~70%)
- **Usually means:** the energy-code minimum was applied without a separate condensation-control check for this specific space's ambient condition.
- **First question:** was a condensation-control calculation run for this space's actual RH, or was the energy-minimum table used as the only reference?
- **Data to pull:** the condensation-control calc (3E Plus output or MICA table lookup) if one exists, or the design ambient condition assumed for the space.
