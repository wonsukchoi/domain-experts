# Red flags

### Thickness setting reused from a prior product lot without verifying it still hits target portion weight on this lot
- **Usually means:** portion weight may be off-target due to natural lot-to-lot product variation.
- **First question:** has actual portion weight been re-verified by scale/checkweigher on this specific lot?
- **Data to pull:** current lot portion weight measurements versus target, and whether thickness was re-verified for this lot.

### Portion weight target set at exactly the label weight rather than a calculated overweight margin
- **Usually means:** a meaningful fraction of individual portions will fall below the regulatory individual-minimum requirement.
- **First question:** what does the process's measured SD imply about the individual non-compliance rate at the current target?
- **Data to pull:** portion weight target versus label weight versus measured SD versus calculated non-compliance rate.

### Declining cut quality compensated for by adjusting thickness or speed rather than checking blade sharpness/condition
- **Usually means:** the actual root cause — blade condition — goes unaddressed while an unrelated parameter is adjusted.
- **First question:** does blade condition match what the maintenance interval schedule expects at this point?
- **Data to pull:** blade condition/sharpness check versus maintenance interval, and cut quality trend versus thickness/speed adjustments made.

### Irregular-shaped input processed with one fixed cut pattern/thickness setting regardless of piece dimension variation
- **Usually means:** avoidable yield loss at the edges of pieces that don't match the assumed uniform dimension.
- **First question:** what would yield look like if cut pattern were adapted to each piece's actual dimensions?
- **Data to pull:** actual yield achieved versus the achievable yield with an adaptive cut pattern.

### Portion weight trend drifts in one direction across a run, with repeated thickness readjustments rather than a traced cause
- **Usually means:** the underlying cause (blade wear, product characteristic change) continues degrading yield/consistency while thickness adjustments only mask the symptom.
- **First question:** does the drift correlate with blade age/condition or a specific product lot characteristic?
- **Data to pull:** portion weight trend over the run versus blade age/condition versus product lot characteristics.

### Giveaway margin never recalculated despite process improvements that could have reduced measured variability
- **Usually means:** an unnecessarily large giveaway cost is being carried forward even though current process capability would support a smaller margin.
- **First question:** how does the current measured SD compare to the SD assumption the current giveaway margin was based on?
- **Data to pull:** current measured SD versus the SD assumption the current giveaway margin was originally based on.

### A recurring underweight or yield-loss pattern for a specific product/thickness setting appears without being traced to a specific cause
- **Usually means:** a systemic process issue — blade condition, product lot variation, pattern efficiency — rather than isolated one-off batch variation.
- **First question:** does the underweight/yield trend correlate with a specific process parameter?
- **Data to pull:** underweight/yield trend log versus process parameter logs (blade condition, thickness setting, product lot) over the same period.
