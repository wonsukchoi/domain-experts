# Tutor — Red Flags

### Same wrong operation recurring across 3+ different problem types on a diagnostic
- **Usually means:** a missing prerequisite skill sitting upstream of the current unit, not a current-unit conceptual gap.
- **First question:** what specific step does every one of these problems share, and is that step tested anywhere else on the diagnostic?
- **Data to pull:** the item-by-item diagnostic breakdown by skill cluster, not just the overall score.

### Right answer but visibly slow (finger-counting, long pauses on basic facts)
- **Usually means:** an automaticity gap, not a conceptual one — the student understands the operation but hasn't drilled it to fluency.
- **First question:** does re-explaining the concept change anything, or does only timed drilling move the speed?
- **Data to pull:** time-per-item on basic-fact items versus multi-step items.

### Diagnostic overall score matches homework/quiz average within a few points
- **Usually means:** nothing informative yet — the overall score can mask a specific broken cluster sitting under an average pulled up by unrelated strong items.
- **First question:** what does the score look like broken out by skill cluster instead of as one number?
- **Data to pull:** cluster-level breakdown, not the single overall percentage.

### Session-3 retest hits 100% and the client asks to stop
- **Usually means:** short-term recall, not yet confirmed retention — a single same-day or same-week retest doesn't rule out decay by the next real assessment.
- **First question:** has a spaced-review check happened at least one full session after the skill was "closed"?
- **Data to pull:** the spaced-review log for that skill's Review 2 entry.

### A skill has been directly targeted for 4+ sessions with no diagnostic movement
- **Usually means:** the actual cause sits outside what tutoring alone can diagnose or fix — a vision/hearing issue, an undiagnosed learning difference, or an anxiety/attention issue masquerading as a content gap.
- **First question:** has this pattern been flagged to the parent as a possible referral, or is it still being billed as ordinary tutoring progress?
- **Data to pull:** session-by-session diagnostic scores for that specific skill across all sessions worked on it.

### Client requests topic order that contradicts the leverage ranking
- **Usually means:** the client is reacting to the most recent visible symptom (last week's bad quiz), not the highest-leverage underlying gap.
- **First question:** is there a deadline inside 48 hours that legitimately overrides the leverage ranking, or is this just recency bias?
- **Data to pull:** the gap-ranking worksheet showing how many current-unit problems each candidate gap blocks.

### Test-prep score jump in the first 2 sessions exceeds 15% of the total possible range
- **Usually means:** an unusually easy diagnostic, a familiarity/practice effect, or measurement noise — not a sustainable per-session rate.
- **First question:** does the score-band tracking table's target curve get revised down for later sessions to avoid setting an unsustainable expectation?
- **Data to pull:** session-by-session score history plotted against the diminishing-returns target curve.

### Parent describes the goal only as "get the grade up," with no skill named
- **Usually means:** no diagnostic has actually been run yet, or its results haven't been translated into a specific skill target the parent understands.
- **First question:** what did the diagnostic identify as the actual blocking skill, in plain language?
- **Data to pull:** the diagnostic cluster breakdown, translated into a one-sentence skill statement for the parent.

### Student solves problems correctly in session but errors reappear on independent homework
- **Usually means:** the tutor is over-scaffolding during guided practice (narrating too much of the reasoning) rather than fading support before the session ends.
- **First question:** did the exit check happen fully independently, with no tutor prompting, or was the tutor still narrating steps during it?
- **Data to pull:** the exit-check portion of the session plan log — was it scored as independent work?

### Multiple sessions pass without any spaced-review items included
- **Usually means:** the session is optimizing for covering new material over confirming retention of what's already been taught.
- **First question:** when was the last time any previously-taught skill was checked, unannounced, in a later session?
- **Data to pull:** the spaced-review log's most recent entry date for each previously-taught skill.
