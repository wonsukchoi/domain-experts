### PHQ-9 item 9 (thoughts of self-harm) > 0, regardless of total score
- **Usually means:** Passive or active suicidal ideation not otherwise volunteered in conversation; second most likely: patient minimizing verbally while endorsing on a written scale
- **First question:** "You marked thoughts of being better off dead or hurting yourself — can you tell me more about that?"
- **Data to pull:** Full C-SSRS, prior visit's item-9 score for trend, current safety-plan status

### New-onset irritability, decreased need for sleep, or rapid speech within 2–4 weeks of starting an antidepressant
- **Usually means:** Antidepressant-induced hypomanic/manic switch, unmasking previously unrecognized bipolar spectrum illness; second: activation/akathisia misread as mood elevation
- **First question:** "Has your energy or need for sleep changed in a way that's different from just feeling less depressed?"
- **Data to pull:** MDQ if not already done, family psychiatric history, timeline of symptom onset relative to drug start

### Lithium trough > 1.5 mEq/L, or GI upset/tremor/confusion regardless of last known level
- **Usually means:** Early lithium toxicity, often from dehydration, NSAID use, or a diuretic added by another prescriber; second: dosing error
- **First question:** "Any new medications, illness, vomiting, diarrhea, or reduced fluid intake in the last week?"
- **Data to pull:** Same-day trough level, BMP for renal function and sodium, current med list for interacting drugs (thiazides, NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors)

### ANC < 1000/µL on clozapine (or any unexplained fever/sore throat on clozapine)
- **Usually means:** Clozapine-induced neutropenia/agranulocytosis; second most likely: incidental viral illness — cannot be assumed without the count
- **First question:** "Any fever, sore throat, or mouth sores since your last visit?"
- **Data to pull:** Current ANC per REMS schedule, prior ANC trend, hold/discontinue decision per REMS threshold tables

### Early refill request or "lost prescription" on a controlled substance
- **Usually means:** Diversion, misuse, or an undisclosed second prescriber; second: genuine loss/theft
- **First question:** "Walk me through what happened to the prescription."
- **Data to pull:** State PDMP query, pill count if available, urine toxicology

### Abnormal involuntary movements (lip smacking, tongue thrusting, limb choreoathetosis) on a long-term antipsychotic
- **Usually means:** Tardive dyskinesia; second: acute dystonia if recent dose change (different mechanism, different urgency)
- **First question:** "How long has this movement been present, and did it start around a dose change?"
- **Data to pull:** AIMS score, medication timeline, prior AIMS score for comparison

### Weight gain ≥5% of baseline or fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL at the 12-week antipsychotic check
- **Usually means:** Metabolic syndrome developing from the antipsychotic itself; second: unrelated lifestyle change
- **First question:** "Have you noticed changes in appetite, thirst, or energy since starting this medication?"
- **Data to pull:** Full metabolic panel, waist circumference, comparison to baseline values

### Abrupt discontinuation symptoms (dizziness, "brain zaps," irritability) after stopping venlafaxine or paroxetine
- **Usually means:** Discontinuation syndrome from a short-half-life agent stopped abruptly; second: relapse of the underlying condition — distinguish by onset speed (days, not weeks)
- **First question:** "When exactly did you stop, and how quickly after did the symptoms start?"
- **Data to pull:** Exact stop date, taper schedule if any was given, symptom-onset timeline

### Co-prescription of a benzodiazepine and a full-agonist opioid
- **Usually means:** Additive respiratory-depression risk, often from two different prescribers not coordinating; second: legitimate short-term co-management with an explicit taper plan
- **First question:** "Who else is prescribing your other medications, and is there a plan to taper one of these?"
- **Data to pull:** PDMP full medication list across prescribers, respiratory risk factors (COPD, sleep apnea, alcohol use)

### Serotonin-syndrome triad emerging (mental status change + autonomic instability + neuromuscular findings) after a dose increase or drug combination
- **Usually means:** Serotonergic drug interaction (e.g., SSRI plus triptan, tramadol, or MAOI); second: unrelated infection — distinguish via Hunter Criteria and medication timeline
- **First question:** "Any new medications, supplements, or dose changes in the last 24–48 hours?"
- **Data to pull:** Full medication and supplement list, vital signs, neuromuscular exam findings (clonus, hyperreflexia)

### "Treatment-resistant" documented without two adequate trials on the chart
- **Usually means:** Premature switching driven by impatience rather than a genuinely resistant case; second: truly resistant case with poor documentation
- **First question:** "What dose, for how long, and how well did the patient actually take it?"
- **Data to pull:** Pharmacy fill history for adherence, prior dose titration notes, duration at therapeutic dose for each prior trial

### Patient requests a specific controlled substance by exact name and dose at a first visit
- **Usually means:** Doctor-shopping or diversion-seeking behavior; second: patient genuinely well-informed from prior legitimate treatment elsewhere
- **First question:** "Who prescribed this for you before, and can we get those records?"
- **Data to pull:** PDMP query, records-release request to prior prescriber, urine toxicology

### No documented informed-consent conversation for an off-label or boxed-warning prescription
- **Usually means:** Time pressure in a short visit slot led to a skipped step; second: conversation happened but wasn't charted
- **First question:** "Did we discuss what this medication is approved for versus how we're using it here?"
- **Data to pull:** Prior visit notes, current chart template's informed-consent field
