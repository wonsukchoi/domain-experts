# Playbook — visit templates, monitoring calendars, escalation ladder

## Med-management visit structure (15–20 minute follow-up)

1. **Interval history** (2–3 min): what changed since last visit — sleep, energy, appetite, concentration, function at work/school, substance use.
2. **Validated scale, same instrument as last visit** (1 min): PHQ-9, GAD-7, or condition-specific scale; compare to prior score, not to an absolute cutoff alone.
3. **Suicide risk check every visit, not just at intake** (1 min): C-SSRS screener questions, minimum "ideation" tier; escalate to full C-SSRS if any positive.
4. **Side-effect and adherence check** (2 min): specific to the drug — weight/appetite for antipsychotics, GI/tremor for lithium, sexual side effects and activation for SSRIs, sedation for benzodiazepines.
5. **Med-specific monitoring due today?** (see calendars below) — order labs before the patient leaves if due.
6. **Decision: continue / optimize dose / augment / switch**, against the adequate-trial threshold, not against how the conversation felt.
7. **Documentation**: risk tier, rationale, informed consent for any off-label or boxed-warning drug, next-visit interval tied to the decision made.

## Antidepressant start — monitoring calendar (patient <25 or new to class)

| Week | Action |
|---|---|
| 0 | Start drug; document boxed-warning discussion; safety plan given |
| 1 | Visit or telephone check — activation, akathisia, new/worsening suicidality |
| 2 | Visit — same checks; dose increase only if tolerated and sub-therapeutic |
| 4 | Visit — first point an "inadequate trial" call is valid if dose has been at a therapeutic level for ≥2 of these weeks |
| 6 | Visit — adequate-trial window closes; response/no-response decision |
| 12 | Routine follow-up cadence resumes if stable |

Patients ≥25 without acute risk factors: same drug/dose logic, but weeks 1–2 can be a telephone check instead of an in-person visit unless risk factors are present.

## Antipsychotic (second-generation) — metabolic monitoring calendar

| Timepoint | Weight/BMI | Waist circumference | BP | Fasting glucose | Fasting lipids |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | Y | Y | Y | Y | Y |
| Week 4 | Y | — | — | — | — |
| Week 8 | Y | — | — | — | — |
| Week 12 | Y | Y | Y | Y | Y |
| Annually thereafter | Y | Y | Y | Y | Y |

**Action thresholds:** weight gain ≥5% of baseline at any checkpoint, or fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL (prediabetic range), or LDL/triglyceride rise into the abnormal range → discuss switching to a lower metabolic-liability agent or refer to primary care for metabolic management before continuing the same drug unchanged.

## Lithium monitoring calendar

| Timepoint | Trough level | Renal (Cr/eGFR) | Thyroid (TSH) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before start | — | Y | Y |
| 5 days after any dose change | Y | — | — |
| Every 3 months (stable dose, stable patient) | Y | Y (every 6 mo) | Y (every 6–12 mo) |
| Any new GI/tremor/confusion symptom | Y (same day, hold next dose pending result) | — | — |

Therapeutic range: 0.6–1.2 mEq/L acute, 0.6–1.0 mEq/L maintenance. Symptoms of concern (tremor, GI upset, confusion) at any level warrant a same-day trough — don't wait for the next scheduled draw.

## Risk-tier escalation ladder

| Tier | Presentation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Low | No ideation, or passive ideation with no plan/intent/access, stable housing/support | Continue outpatient management; safety plan reviewed; standard follow-up interval |
| 2 — Moderate | Active ideation, no specific plan, or passive ideation plus a new risk factor (recent loss, intoxication, access to means) | Safety plan in writing, remove/restrict means, shorten follow-up to ≤1 week, consider involving a support person, same-day collaborating-physician consult if uncertain |
| 3 — High | Ideation with plan and intent, or a plan with access to lethal means | Do not let the patient leave unaccompanied; initiate crisis/ED evaluation same visit; document exact wording of risk statements and time of escalation |
| 4 — Imminent | Attempt in progress, disclosed, or active preparation observed | Emergency services / involuntary hold process per state law; this is not a med-management decision |

## Fallback ladder when a patient pushes back on the recommended plan

1. First position: recommended plan as above (e.g., "hold antidepressant, complete bipolar workup first").
2. If patient refuses to wait: offer the antidepressant with an explicit shortened follow-up (1 week instead of 2) and an explicit discussion that a mood stabilizer may need to be added if activation or mood elevation appears — document the informed-consent conversation and the patient's choice.
3. If patient still refuses any monitoring plan: document capacity assessment, risks discussed, and that the patient was informed of the alternative (standard workup) and declined; involve collaborating physician if available.
4. Never concede: skipping the suicide risk assessment itself, regardless of how the rest of the negotiation goes.
