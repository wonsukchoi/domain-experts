# Red flags

### Department meter usage exceeds its own weekly baseline by a large margin with no documented large mailing
- **Usually means:** personal-mail cost leakage routed through the department's meter code, or a miscoded cost center.
- **First question:** is there a documented large mailing (case filing, mass mailer, campaign) that explains it?
- **Data to pull:** meter log filtered by department, date, piece count, and destination.

### Suspicious-mail indicator present (excessive postage, oily or stained wrapper, protruding wires, restrictive endorsement with no return address)
- **Usually means:** a potentially hazardous item requiring isolation, not routine handling.
- **First question:** does the piece match two or more indicators, not just one?
- **Data to pull:** the organization's suspicious-mail screening checklist and security escalation contact.

### Certified, registered, or signed-for item handed off with no chain-of-custody signature
- **Usually means:** no recourse exists if the item is later reported missing.
- **First question:** who had physical possession at each internal handoff point?
- **Data to pull:** the chain-of-custody log for that tracking or item number.

### Same internal addressee misrouted more than a few times in a month
- **Usually means:** stale internal directory data, not repeated individual clerk error.
- **First question:** when was the directory last updated for this name or department?
- **Data to pull:** org directory change log or HR roster feed.

### Outgoing mail consistently defaults to one premium carrier or service tier regardless of weight or zone
- **Usually means:** no rate-shopping habit in place, leaving savings unclaimed shipment after shipment.
- **First question:** what would this shipment have cost at the next-cheaper tier?
- **Data to pull:** carrier rate/zone chart and the meter's cost report by service tier.

### Meter reading (postage dispensed) doesn't reconcile with the logged piece count and weight for the day
- **Usually means:** a meter miscalibration, misfire, or an unlogged piece somewhere in the day's mail.
- **First question:** does the discrepancy exceed the meter's normal calibration tolerance?
- **Data to pull:** the daily meter reconciliation report.

### Return-to-sender rate for internal mail is rising
- **Usually means:** internal directory or routing data has gone stale faster than clerks are catching it.
- **First question:** which departments or names account for most of the recent RTS volume?
- **Data to pull:** RTS log trend broken out by department over the trailing month.
