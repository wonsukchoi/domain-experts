# Vocabulary

### Meter code / cost center code
The identifier on a postage meter that attributes postage cost to a specific department or budget line.
**In use:** "That batch went out under Legal's meter code — pull the log by that code to see the full week."
**Common misuse:** Treating it as just an accounting label; it's also the audit trail that makes a usage spike or misuse pattern detectable in the first place.

### Chain of custody
A documented signature trail proving who had physical possession of a tracked item at each internal handoff point.
**In use:** "Certified letter's logged at receipt and at the department handoff — chain of custody's intact if this comes up later."
**Common misuse:** Assuming an external carrier's tracking number substitutes for it; the carrier scan proves the package reached the building, not who had it internally after that.

### Zone / rate break
The weight or distance threshold at which a shipping service's price jumps to the next pricing tier.
**In use:** "This package is two ounces under the next zone break — cheaper to ship as-is than to add the extra insert."
**Common misuse:** Assuming price scales smoothly with weight; missing the specific break points where a slightly heavier or lighter shipment would land in a cheaper tier entirely.

### Suspicious mail indicators
A defined checklist of traits (excessive postage, oily stains, protruding wires, restrictive endorsement with no return address, etc.) suggesting a potentially hazardous piece.
**In use:** "That envelope matches three indicators — isolating it and calling security, not opening it."
**Common misuse:** Treating any single odd trait alone as sufficient cause to open and inspect a piece, rather than isolating it per protocol and letting the indicator count drive the response.

### RTS (Return to Sender)
Mail routed back to its point of origin because it couldn't be delivered to the addressee.
**In use:** "RTS volume for Marketing's up again this month — probably the reorg, not a clerk problem."
**Common misuse:** Treating a rising RTS rate as a mailroom performance issue by itself, when it's frequently a symptom of stale internal directory or routing data.

### Folder/inserter
A machine that folds documents and inserts them into envelopes for outgoing mailings.
**In use:** "Run the billing statements through the folder/inserter before they go to the meter."
**Common misuse:** Confusing it with the postage meter; the two are separate machines — one prepares the envelope, the other applies postage.

### Postage meter
A device that prints prepaid postage indicia directly onto mail, tracked against a prepaid balance and, in most systems, attributable per department.
**In use:** "Meter's showing 209 lbs processed under Legal's code this week — that's what triggered the reconciliation check."
**Common misuse:** Treating meter postage as accounting-equivalent to stamps; unlike stamps, meter usage is trackable per-department in a way that makes cost-leakage patterns visible.

### Interoffice / internal mail routing
Distribution of mail to recipients within the same organization, distinct from postal delivery to an external address.
**In use:** "That's an interoffice routing issue — check the current directory before sending it back as undeliverable."
**Common misuse:** Assuming internal routing follows the same address-verification rules as postal mail; it depends entirely on an internal directory that can go stale in ways postal addresses don't.
