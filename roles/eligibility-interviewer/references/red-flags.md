# Eligibility Interviewer — Red Flags

### Household reports $0 income with rent/mortgage paid current
- **Usually means:** unreported support from another household member or outside person, savings drawdown that will run out soon, or unreported work — in roughly that order of frequency.
- **First question:** "Who's covering the rent this month?" — asked directly, not left implicit.
- **Data to pull:** bank statements for the past 60-90 days (look for recurring deposits), landlord/mortgage payment history.

### Verification document employer name doesn't match application employer name
- **Usually means:** most often a recent job change the applicant didn't think to mention, sometimes an old pay stub submitted by mistake; occasionally undisclosed additional employment.
- **First question:** "Is this your current job, or has something changed since you filled out the application?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent pay stub dated within the verification window, not just the first one submitted.

### Household composition changes between application and recertification with no life event reported
- **Usually means:** a member moved out (should reduce household size and income) or moved in (should increase household size, possibly income) without being reported — sometimes innocent oversight, sometimes benefit-maximization by omission.
- **First question:** "Is [name] still living with you and sharing food costs?"
- **Data to pull:** updated mail/lease/school-enrollment records for the household address.

### Self-employment income reported as a round number with no supporting records
- **Usually means:** genuine difficulty estimating variable self-employment income (legitimate), or a number picked to land just under the eligibility threshold (needs scrutiny).
- **First question:** "Walk me through how you got to that number — what records did you use?"
- **Data to pull:** self-employment ledger, bank deposits over the averaging period, prior-year tax return if available.

### Applicant requests expedited processing citing an emergency that isn't independently verifiable
- **Usually means:** most expedited requests are genuine (eviction notice, utility shutoff notice, zero income); occasionally a pressure tactic to bypass standard verification timelines.
- **First question:** "Do you have the eviction notice / shutoff notice with you, or can it be faxed/emailed today?"
- **Data to pull:** the triggering document itself — expedited processing standards require the hardship condition to be documented, not just stated, before waiving standard verification steps.

### Bank statement shows large, unexplained cash deposits inconsistent with reported income
- **Usually means:** gift/loan from family (not countable as income in most programs), unreported cash-based work (countable), or asset transfer relevant to asset-limit programs.
- **First question:** "Can you tell me where this deposit came from?"
- **Data to pull:** a written statement from the depositor if it's a gift/loan, to distinguish it from earned income for the record.

### Two program applications (e.g. SNAP and Medicaid) list different household compositions for the same address
- **Usually means:** the household-composition test genuinely differs by program (see playbook.md decision table) and this may be correct, not an error — but it can also indicate one application was filled out to maximize eligibility on that program's specific test.
- **First question:** confirm which household members share food preparation (SNAP test) versus who is a tax dependent (Medicaid test) — the two answers can legitimately differ for the same people.
- **Data to pull:** the household's most recent tax return, if MAGI-based eligibility is in question.

### Applicant's stated income conflicts with a wage-database or interstate data match
- **Usually means:** the data match reflects a job the applicant left recently (lag in reporting), or the applicant genuinely underreported.
- **First question:** "Our records show income from [employer] — can you tell me about that?"
- **Data to pull:** most recent pay stub or separation notice from the flagged employer to establish current status.
